## The Core Question

When working with AI CLI tools (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.) on non-code projects, what's the equivalent of a PRD that gives both the AI and the human confidence to execute autonomously until completion?

---

## Key Insight

The document *type* matters less than the document's *properties*. What makes a PRD work for code:

1. **Unambiguous specifications** — the AI knows exactly what "done" looks like
2. **Discrete deliverables** — checkable items, not vague outcomes
3. **Decision authority embedded** — the AI doesn't have to stop and ask "but what if..."
4. **Scope boundaries** — knows what NOT to do

---

## Traditional Equivalents by Project Type

| Project Type | Traditional Document |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Business/Strategic | Strategic Brief, Project Charter, Business Case |
| Marketing/Campaign | Creative Brief, Campaign Plan |
| Content/Educational | Content Architecture Document, Course Design Doc |
| Consulting/Client | Statement of Work, Engagement Brief |
| Research/Analysis | Research Plan |

---

## Universal Elements (Present in All)

1. **Problem/Opportunity** — why this exists
2. **Desired Outcome** — what success looks like
3. **Scope** — what's in, what's out
4. **Approach** — how you'll get there
5. **Dependencies/Risks** — what could derail it
6. **Timeline/Milestones** — when things happen

## How to Use These

1. **Copy the relevant template** for your project type
2. **Fill in every section** — incomplete specs = AI asking questions = breaks flow
3. **Include decision rules** — what should AI do when it hits ambiguity?
4. **Define "done" precisely** — measurable, verifiable, not "good enough"
5. **Reference source materials** — what has authority, what doesn't
