## SECTION 2: THE FOUR DIAGNOSES

### Diagnosis 1: Model Type Assessment — STRONG with caveat

**Finding: STRONG / partial MISALIGNMENT at Tier 3**

Rich is an excellent model for Tiers 1-2 (self-knowledge, voice, blind spots). He is a misaligned model for Tier 3 (production power at scale).

**Evidence:**

For Tiers 1-2, Rich's hybrid mediation works because his vulnerability stories (marriage 8.5, payroll worry, ADD, conflict avoidance) close the gap between his achievements and the audience's reality. The "I'm a fraction of a fraction of what I've helped others achieve" confession is a master-level internal mediation move: it makes Rich simultaneously impressive (helped clients generate $15B) and relatable (struggles with the same patterns as you). The chat confirms this landed: "Rich your story is excellent!!! Describes me too!!!" and "I DEFINITELY & PAINFULLY know EXACTLY what you were Experiencing."

For Tier 3, the model shifts character. When Rich shows the cockpit — 882 frameworks, 790 transcripts, ScreenPipe capturing every screen every second, multi-vault architecture, December 2025 story of hooking AI to "everything" in 90 minutes — the mediation becomes external. The audience goes from "I relate to this person" to "I could never build that." Day 3 confirmed this: Rich's actual delivery was NOT the planned Context Compound stack presentation. It was a 3.5-hour live demonstration of his advanced system — course builder deployed in real-time, three agents running simultaneously. The chat response: "Going from idea to functional website in 10 seconds is what we're talking about" and "We are watching the death of the course industry in front of our eyes." This is AWE. Awe generates admiration, not imitation.

The diagnosis: Rich is a strong model for the desire the challenge actually activates (self-knowledge). He is a partially misaligned model for the desire Tier 3 is supposed to sell (production power). The misalignment was partially masked by Rich's genuine passion — "This is what AI can do for YOUR business" — but the gap between his cockpit and a prospect's starting point was never adequately bridged.

### Diagnosis 2: Mediation Type Assessment — MISALIGNED FOR TIER 3

**Finding: CORRECTLY TYPED for Tiers 1-2 / MISALIGNED for Tier 3**

The challenge was correctly designed as hybrid mediation for Tiers 1-2. Rich's vulnerability stories activate internal mediation (I see myself in this person) while his results activate external mediation (I aspire to this outcome). This hybrid is near-ideal for the stated market.

For Tier 3, the mediation shifted to pure external during live delivery. The 3.5-hour Day 3 session became a demonstration of Rich's advanced autonomous AI infrastructure — not a structured proof that this specific cohort could build it.

**The critical Girardian distinction:**

External mediation generates ADMIRATION. The audience admires from a safe distance. They think "that's incredible" and "Rich is brilliant." They do not think "I am on my way to having this." Internal mediation generates RIVALRY-DRIVEN DESIRE — the "I could have that, and the gap between where I am and where they are is bridgeable" feeling that triggers purchase urgency.

The Day 3 chat evidence is diagnostic: "Part of me is getting excited, and the other part is getting freaked out" (Daniel Fernandes). This is a textbook external mediation response. The excitement is there. The urgency to purchase — the "I must close this gap" urgency — is mixed with fear that the gap is too large. That split response is the signature of external mediation crossing into the territory Girard called the obstacle-model problem.

**The market timing factor:** This cohort was attending in February 2026, when Claude Code + Obsidian as a production infrastructure was genuinely advanced. The audience wasn't seeing what they could build in 90 days. They were seeing what Rich had built over 2 years. The mediation type problem was compounded by the technology gap.

For existing SP list members, "Rich Schefren" triggers an immediate model response (trust, authority, desire). ### Diagnosis 4: Desire Triangle Integrity — INTACT for Tiers 1-2 / BROKEN AT SUBJECT-OBJECT LEG FOR TIER 3

**Finding: INTACT (Subject → Rich → self-knowledge identity) / BROKEN (Subject → Weaponized output)**

For the self-knowledge desire (Tier 1-2): The triangle was intact and functioning. Rich's vulnerability stories established the model. The demos (Blind Spot Audit, Voice Imprint, calibration questions) demonstrated the object. The community chat outputs (people sharing their own AI Imprint revelations) confirmed that the Subject → Object path was bridgeable.

For the production desire (Tier 3 / Weaponized): The triangle was broken at the Subject → Object leg. Here is the specific break point:

- Rich (Model) visibly desires and demonstrates his Weaponized infrastructure. He barks orders. AI produces frameworks. Course sites deploy in seconds. Three agents run simultaneously. The Model → Object connection is strong.
- The Subject (prospect) can see the Object (Weaponized production power) clearly.
- BUT: The prospect cannot see THEMSELVES at the Object. There is no demonstrated path from "I am currently at Camp 2, re-explaining myself every session" to "I am at Rich's cockpit level." The Subject → Object leg requires either (a) a peer who made that journey, or (b) a vivid, credible journey map. Neither was consistently present in the marketing.

The Day 3 credit mechanic (every dollar applies toward future programs) is a RATIONAL bridge: "This makes financial sense even if the production level is distant." It does not repair the MIMETIC bridge: "I can see myself having this." Rational bridges convert people who are already mimetically convinced. They do not create mimetic desire in people who are not.

## SECTION 3: FAILURE MODE CLASSIFICATION

| Failure Mode | Present? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **No Model** | No | Rich is a clear, credible model throughout |
| **Wrong Model Type** | Partial — Tier 3 only | Rich's cockpit became pure external mediation for Tier 3; awe without imitation |
| **Model-Market Mismatch** | Partial — new/affiliate traffic | First-timers (est. 30% of audience) showed confusion signals before internal mediation could activate |

| **Skandalon Dynamic** | Yes — secondary failure mode | No payment plan + no guarantee + $2,497 upfront creates model-obstacle dynamic for marginal buyers |
| **Invisible Object** | Partial — Tier 3 | Weaponized "production power" was shown via Rich's advanced system but not demonstrated as achievable |
| **Shattered Triangle** | No | For Tiers 1-2, the triangle is coherent and well-executed |

### Primary Failure Mode: Conflicting Models

The three-tier structure ran three mediation models simultaneously, and they pointed at different desire clusters:

**Tier 1 (OS, $997) — self-knowledge desire**
- Model: Rich's vulnerable self (marriage 8.5, procrastination, fear of irrelevance)
- Mediation: Internal (we share the same flaws)
- Object: An AI that knows your blind spots

**Tier 2 (Plus, $1,497) — identity articulation desire**
- Model: Rich's expertise extraction ("What makes you the ONLY choice?")
- Mediation: Internal-to-external transition
- Object: Being seen at your best, clearly articulated

**Tier 3 (Weaponized, $2,497) — production power desire**
- Model: Rich's cockpit (advanced, autonomous, 2-year-built infrastructure)
- Mediation: Largely external by Day 3 (awe mode)
- Object: AI that produces business assets in your voice

These three desire structures do not form a coherent desire escalation arc. They form three separate desire arcs that point in different directions. A prospect who deeply resonates with the self-knowledge desire (Tier 1) is NOT automatically escalated to the production desire (Tier 3) by the credit mechanic alone. The rational math says "deposit, not expense." The mimetic structure says "that Level 5 cockpit isn't what I'm trying to have — I want to be understood."

**The credit mechanic's limitation:** It is a price reframe, not a desire escalation device. It addresses the question "should I spend more money?" not the question "do I want what Tier 3 produces?" The two questions are independent. A prospect can be rationally convinced the deposit makes sense while still feeling mimetically drawn to Plus because the Plus desire (being articulated, seen at your best) is more intensely activated in the marketing.

### Secondary Failure Mode: Skandalon Dynamic

The no-payment-plan, no-guarantee policy combined with full-pay requirement creates a classic model-obstacle configuration.

Rich is the desire model. Rich also controls access. The terms — "full pay only, I want people who are all in" — position Rich simultaneously as the aspirational object and the gatekeeper who makes it difficult. For prospects at the margin (those whose mimetic desire is strong but whose certainty about execution is uncertain), this configuration generates the response Girard identified in the skandalon: the model simultaneously attracts and blocks, producing resentment or withdrawal rather than purchase.

Evidence: The checkout system broke during the Day 3 close. Multiple buyers emailed support to honor bonuses after checkout friction. The grandfathering conversation in Day 3 chat revealed real price sensitivity and program-relationship confusion (Force Multiplier vs. Elite, OS-tier originals getting only the new ZMOS for free, etc.).

The skandalon framing is not an argument to add payment plans — Rich made that decision deliberately for quality-of-buyer reasons. The autopsy merely notes: this cost conversions at the margin, and the Girardian reframe offered in the pre-launch autopsy (connect payment structure to PRODUCT MECHANISM rather than Rich's preference) was not implemented. "Full commitment because the system requires complete answers" is a mechanism-obstacle. "I want committed buyers" is a model-obstacle. The distinction matters for conversion rate.

## SECTION 4: THE THREE-TIER DESIRE ARCHITECTURE — Counter-Factual Redesign

The central structural problem of AI Imprint is that the three tiers were designed as a value ladder (more components = higher price = better deal) when they needed to be designed as a desire escalation arc (each tier creates desire for the next through mimetic architecture, not just through rational value comparison).

### What the Architecture Produced

The actual desire amplification across the four days:

| Day | Desire Activated | Tier Pointed At | Intensity |
|-----|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Day 1 | Self-knowledge: "I want AI that knows my blind spots" | Tier 1 | Very high |
| Day 2 | Identity articulation: "I want to be seen at my best" | Tier 2 | Very high |
| Day 3 (teaching section) | Same Tier 1-2 desires reinforced | Tiers 1-2 | High |
| Day 3 (live demo section) | Production awe: "That's incredible" | Tier 3 (awe) | Medium — awe, not desire |
| Day 3 (close) | Credit math: rational case for Tier 3 | Tier 3 (rational) | Moderate |

The architecture pointed two and a half days at Tier 1-2 desires and used rational math to convert to Tier 3. Predictive outcome: Plus over-performs, Weaponized under-performs relative to what the credit math "should" produce if the mimetic desire for Tier 3 were equally developed.

**The prediction from the pre-launch autopsy to test:** "If Plus outsells Weaponized despite the credit math, the mimetic diagnosis is confirmed."

### What the Architecture Should Have Produced

For the three-tier structure to work as a desire escalation arc, each tier must create a felt gap — a mimetically felt incompleteness — that the next tier resolves:

**Tier 1 → Tier 2 escalation (needs to be explicit):**
The Tier 1 desire is self-knowledge: "I know my blind spots." But self-knowledge without the ability to articulate it — to show up powerfully, to make others understand what makes you uniquely valuable — is frustrating. You can see the gap but you can't close it. The desire escalation line for Tier 1 → Tier 2 should be: "Knowing yourself is only valuable if you can show up as yourself." The marketing should make the prospect feel the pain of having self-knowledge that nobody else can see — and Tier 2 is the bridge.

The actual marketing DID have this: "Plus gives you the KNOWLEDGE. Not the EXECUTION. The wall is still there." This is the right diagnostic, but the DESIRE for Tier 2 required more time than "the wall is still there" to activate fully.

**Tier 2 → Tier 3 escalation (the actual structural gap):**
The Tier 2 desire is being articulated. But articulation without deployment — having the perfect positioning framework but still doing the work manually, still writing emails by hand, still building assets one at a time — is the gap. The escalation line: "You know who you are. You can articulate your value. Now imagine AI producing FROM that knowledge — not just knowing it, but acting on it."

This escalation is present in the Weaponized tier's component list, but it gets the LEAST demonstration time in the challenge. The self-knowledge desire gets calibration demos across Days 1-2. The articulation desire gets the Voice Imprint demo. The production desire gets the framework extraction (2 minutes on Day 3), the course builder demo (unplanned, emerged organically), and the credit math.

**The counter-factual: if the desire escalation architecture were redesigned:**

Day 1 ends with: Rich runs the Voice Imprint live — 3 questions, AI produces an email in Rich's voice. The audience watches. "That's what calibration does in 3 questions. Imagine 62." This creates the Tier 1 desire gap.

Day 2 introduces a SECOND demo target: not just Rich's business, but a community member's. Blind Spot Audit is run on Rich, THEN immediately on a community member who volunteered. The audience watches someone LIKE THEM get the diagnosis. This establishes an internal mediator at Day 2 — before the close.

Day 3's live demo section is restructured: before the course builder and multi-agent demos, Rich shows the community member's Weaponized output that was generated overnight using THEIR calibration data. The audience sees: "This is what [Name]'s Weaponized tier produced. A framework from their methodology. An email sequence in their voice. A strategic plan built from their blind spots." THEN the 3.5-hour live demo of Rich's system becomes aspirational rather than awe-inducing, because the bridge from "someone like me" to "Rich's level" is visible.

## SECTION 5: THE CREDIT BRIDGE ANALYSIS — Is It Working as a Desire Escalator?

### What the Credit Mechanic Actually Does

The credit mechanic is:

> "Every dollar of $2,497 applies toward any future program — Connect the Dots ($10K), Force Multiplier, anything. You're not choosing between spending $1,497 and spending $2,497. You're choosing between SPENDING $1,497 that's gone forever... and DEPOSITING $2,497 that comes back to you AND getting the full execution layer."

This is a rational reframe. It addresses the prospect's cognitive comparison of prices: "Is $1,000 extra worth it?" The answer it gives is: "The $1,000 isn't extra — it's a deposit on the next thing." This is clever and legitimate.

**What a desire escalator would look like:**

A true Girardian desire escalation device doesn't answer "is this price worth it?" It makes the prospect feel something they didn't feel before — a desire for the NEXT product that they didn't know they had until this moment. The CTD reveal at the VIP day was designed to do this (you watched Rich work on businesses for 60 minutes, now imagine 2 full days of that at a deeper level), but this comes AFTER purchase, not at the Tier 3 decision point.

**The credit mechanic as currently structured does three things:**
1. Reduces perceived net cost of Tier 3 (effective: a genuine price reframe)
2. Signals that the buyer relationship doesn't end at ZenithMind (effective: continuity framing)
3. Creates awareness that CTD/FM exist (partially effective: plants the seed)

**What the credit mechanic does NOT do:**
1. Create desire for CTD or Force Multiplier (it names them but doesn't demonstrate them)
2. Make the prospect feel the gap between Weaponized and CTD (the desire escalation function)
3. Position CTD as the thing the buyer will want AFTER they complete Weaponized

**The Girardian diagnosis:** The credit mechanic is a price mitigation device that is being asked to do the work of a desire escalation device. These are different functions. Price mitigation addresses objections. Desire escalation creates new wanting. Conflating them produces a credit mechanic that converts some marginal buyers through rational math but doesn't build the desire pipeline toward CTD that Rich needs for long-term business architecture.

### What the Credit Mechanic Would Need to Be a Genuine Desire Escalator

A desire escalation device creates a preview of the next desire at the moment of current purchase. The CTD section of the VIP briefing (live hot seats, pattern naming, the Joe Schriefer $30M → $400M proof) is the right structure but wrong placement — it's AFTER Weaponized purchase, not at the Tier 3 decision moment.

Counter-factual: At the Tier 3 decision moment (after the credit mechanic is introduced but before the cart link drops), a 60-second sequence:

> "When you finish Weaponized, your AI knows everything about you. It can produce in your voice. It can audit your business. But here's what it still doesn't know — everything around you. Your team. Your competitors. Every conversation that's happened inside your business. Every market signal. That's what Connect the Dots builds. Two days with me, your entire business mapped at the same depth we just built YOU. Joe Schriefer went from $30M to $400M by letting me do that to his business. Your Weaponized investment is already banked toward it."

This 60-second sequence does what the credit mechanic alone cannot: it makes the prospect FEEL the desire for CTD before they've finished buying Weaponized. They walk in as a ZenithMind buyer and walk out as a CTD prospect, not just because of price math but because they just had their first mimetic desire for what CTD produces.

The credit mechanic is then the rational confirmation of a desire that the sequence above already activated. Price mitigation AFTER desire activation is highly effective. Price mitigation WITHOUT desire activation is only moderately effective.

## SECTION 6: RICH'S GENUINE EMOTIONAL INVESTMENT — Does It Show Up in the Architecture?

Rich described loving "the feeling of turning people on to AI" and watching people have the epiphany of what they're now capable of. This is not marketing language — it is authentically how he experiences this work. The question is whether this genuine passion was structurally harnessed in the desire architecture or whether it was present in the man while absent in the funnel mechanics.

### Where It Showed Up (Strongly)

Day 1's actual delivery was the most authentic version of Rich's passion for this topic. The improvised stories — the girlfriend story, the current Jay Abraham collaboration with AI on the Zoom call, the December 2025 "I connected everything in 90 minutes" story — were NOT in any script version. They emerged from Rich's genuine excitement. The chat confirmed this landed: "This is the cutting edge on planet earth right now" and "I get goosebumps every time I get around this topic because it's totally changed my life."

The Day 3 session running to 3:22 AM is itself evidence of Rich's passion. He wasn't executing a sales sequence — he was demonstrating his system. The chat stayed alive and active for 3.5 hours. That does not happen in passive sales events. It happens when the desire the presenter has for the subject is contagious enough to override normal fatigue.

### Where It Was Absent (Structurally)

Rich's passion for "turning people on" created a structural tension with the funnel's intent.

When Rich is turned on, he demonstrates his system. When he demonstrates his system, the mediation shifts external — the audience watches his cockpit and experiences awe. When the audience experiences awe, they buy at lower rates than when they experience "I could do that" rivalry.

This is the Girardian paradox of the genuinely passionate model: **genuine passion for a complex, advanced system can produce EXTERNAL mediation even from an internal mediator.** Rich's authentic excitement about what his system can do is simultaneously his greatest presentation strength and his greatest conversion liability — because what he most wants to show (the full depth of what AI can do when it knows you) is precisely what creates the widest gap between model and prospect.

The counter-factual is not "show less." It is "show through peers." Rich demonstrating his cockpit + a peer demonstrating their more nascent but functional version = both forms of mediation active simultaneously. The audience gets the aspiration (Rich's level) and the imitation trigger (that person's level, which is reachable). Rich's genuine passion would then amplify both models rather than overwriting the internal mediator with pure awe.

## SECTION 8: THE NAMING — "AI Imprint" as a Desire Object

One element not analyzed in the pre-launch autopsy deserves specific attention: **the product name itself as a desire object.**

"AI Imprint" is a strong desire object name. "Imprint" implies something permanent, something left behind, something installed. It carries the connotation of branding, of marking, of making a permanent impression. "Your AI Imprint" says: after this, your AI carries you.

Contrast this with the tier names:
- ZenithMind OS — functional (operating system)
- ZenithMind Plus — additive (more)
- ZenithMind Elite / Weaponized — aspirational but vague (what is Weaponized? by what?)

The campaign name ("AI Imprint") was more mimetically loaded than any of the product names. The challenge activated the "Imprint" desire. The products were named functionally (OS, Plus) or aspirationally vague (Weaponized). This is a naming gap: the strongest desire-object language was used for the free challenge, not for the paid tiers.

A Girardian naming architecture would cascade the desire language downward:
- Challenge: "AI Imprint" (the concept, the possibility)
- Tier 1: "Your Imprint" (you, fully seen)
- Tier 2: "Your Imprint, Articulated" (you, fully shown)
- Tier 3: "Your Imprint, Deployed" (you, fully operational)

The current OS/Plus/Weaponized naming breaks the desire continuity established by "AI Imprint." The prospect experiences the AI Imprint desire during the challenge, then is asked to purchase something called "ZenithMind OS" — a functional name that doesn't carry the same desire freight. The product naming is a minor but real desire architecture gap.

## SECTION 9: COMPETITIVE MIMETIC POSITION — CONFIRMED AND EXTENDED

The pre-launch autopsy's competitive diagnosis was correct and has become more important since the event.

The AI challenge market continues to mediate tool-centric desires: "AI does the work for you," "no tech skills needed," "10x your output." These are all external-mediation desires mediated by guru-models showing impressive systems.

The AI Imprint Challenge was the only major campaign in this market mediating IDENTITY desires: "AI knows you," "AI reflects you back at a depth no human can." This is not a different value proposition — it is a different kind of desire entirely.

**Why this distinction matters for future campaigns:**

The identity desire cohort — people who want AI to know them, not just work for them — is a smaller but higher-value segment than the tool-efficiency cohort. This is the segment that becomes Connect the Dots applicants. This is the segment that sends in $6M-deal stories and 15-year-problem-solved testimonials. The AI Imprint campaign found this segment and proved the desire was real.

The competitive risk is not that someone will copy the positioning. The competitive risk is that the market's mimetic model hierarchy shifts: if another operator (Sam Woods, Perry Marshall, or an emerging figure) begins demonstrating identity-level AI use cases at the peer-model level (not guru level), they could capture the internal mediator position that the AI Imprint campaign left vacant.

The counter to this risk: occupy the internal mediator position deliberately, in advance, by building a community of peer models who demonstrate their AI Imprints publicly. Every person who shares "my AI told me I was hiding my flagship offer because I don't want to feel like I'm selling grief" (Debby Hewey) is a free internal mediator. The architecture for systematically capturing and deploying these peer moments was not present in AI Imprint — but it exists as the highest-leverage structural addition for the next campaign.

## SECTION 10: THE SIGNAL TO TRACK POST-WINDOW

These are the mimetic signals that will confirm or disconfirm this autopsy's diagnoses:

**1. Tier distribution: Plus vs. Weaponized**
The single most important post-launch data point. If Plus outsells Weaponized, the diagnosis of misaligned Tier 3 desire architecture is confirmed. The emotional desire pointed at Plus. The rational argument pushed at Weaponized. When emotion and reason diverge, emotion wins at the margin.

**2. Weaponized buyers' actual CTD conversion rate**
The credit mechanic was positioned as a desire pipeline toward CTD. What percentage of Weaponized buyers actually apply for CTD? If the application rate is low, the credit mechanic is functioning only as price mitigation, not as a desire escalator. The CTD desire was not activated at purchase — it was created as a future theoretical option.

**3. Community asset production rate**
If the majority of ZenithMind buyers complete the self-knowledge calibration (Tier 1) but do not produce deployable business assets (Tier 3 behavior), it confirms that the desire activated was Tier 1 (know yourself), not Tier 3 (produce from that knowledge). The dropout point in the curriculum is the internal mediator for the next launch: "people who started and stalled at the production layer" is the target market's lived experience that the next campaign can name.

**4. Social share language**
Rich proposed: "Three days ago, my AI was a _____. Today, my AI is a _____." The language people use reveals which desire was activated. "A stranger / a mirror" = Tier 1. "A tool / a partner who works for me" = Tier 3.

**5. CTD application volume from VIP Day**
The VIP Day was designed as a taste of CTD (live hot seats = mini version of the 2-day in-person). What was the application rate? If it was strong, the desire escalation from Weaponized → CTD was activated by the VIP session experience. If it was weak, the desire for CTD requires more than a taste — it requires the mimetic proof that someone like the prospect went through CTD and came back transformed.

## SECTION 11: VERDICT AND HIGHEST-LEVERAGE NEXT-CAMPAIGN FIXES

### The Verdict

The AI Imprint challenge ran an excellent desire architecture for Tiers 1-2 and a structurally underbuilt desire architecture for Tier 3. The campaign correctly identified a desire that no competitor is mediating (identity-level AI, not tool-efficiency AI) and proved that this desire is real and intense in the $300K+ entrepreneur market.

The primary structural failure was treating a desire escalation problem as a pricing problem. The credit mechanic is a good price mitigation device applied to a problem that needed desire architecture. No amount of rational "depositing not spending" framing closes a mimetic gap between "I want to be understood by AI" (which the challenge activated brilliantly) and "I want AI to produce business assets in my voice" (which the challenge demonstrated but did not activate as a desire the audience felt for themselves).

Rich's genuine passion for the product — the authentic "I love turning people on to AI" energy — was one of the campaign's greatest assets and a partially misaligned mediation tool for Tier 3. His passion expressed itself as cockpit demonstration. Cockpit demonstration is awe. Awe is external mediation. The structural fix is not suppressing Rich's passion but channeling it through peer-level models so that the audience's response shifts from "that's incredible" to "I want that — and I can see how to get there."

### Highest-Leverage Fixes for the Next Campaign

**Fix 1: Build an Internal Mediator Library Before the Next Campaign**
Identify 5-10 ZenithMind buyers who have gone through the system and produced deployable business assets. Capture 60-second video testimonials showing ONE output each: a framework their AI extracted, an email sequence in their voice, a strategic plan built from their blind spots. Not enthusiasm quotes — output demonstrations. These are the internal mediators the AI Imprint campaign produced but did not systematically capture. Deploy them at the Tier 2 → Tier 3 transition in the close.

**Fix 2: Restructure Day 3's Desire Architecture, Not Just Its Script**
Day 3 ran 3.5 hours because Rich's passion for demonstrating his system displaced the structured desire escalation close. The structural solution is not a shorter presentation — it is a desire-first sequence before the live demos: Community peer output → Context Compound as diagnosis → Credit mechanic as price bridge → THEN live demo as proof of what the system does, with the internal mediator's output as the anchor for what the audience will build.

**Fix 3: Redesign the Credit Mechanic as a Desire Activator**
Add a 60-second CTD desire activation sequence before the cart link drops (see Section 5 counter-factual). The credit mechanic lands harder after the audience feels something about CTD — not just knows it exists.

**Fix 4: Cascade the "AI Imprint" Language Into the Product Tier Names**
The challenge name activated the desire. The product names broke continuity. "Your Imprint / Your Imprint, Articulated / Your Imprint, Deployed" maintains the desire architecture across the tier reveal. The current OS/Plus/Weaponized naming is functional but desire-neutral.

**Fix 5: Segment the Model Architecture for New vs. Existing Traffic**
For affiliate traffic and first-timers, the four-moments structure requires more setup before internal mediation can activate. A pre-event sequence (the 15-minute video mentioned in the campaign overview, the pre-event emails) should be audited for model establishment: does a first-timer watching this understand WHY Rich is the right model before Day 1 begins? Or does Day 1 do all the model-establishment work, leaving new prospects in a catching-up state during the most important section?
