- The motivation was the transition from Wild West days to establishment days online
- This happens in every industry: "the people that are focused more on business survive and the people that were focused on the hustle die"

- It was a wake-up call: "you only have a limited amount of time to get serious about business before business comes online and is serious"

## Visibility: What Rich Wants and Doesn't Want

### What he DOESN'T want:
- Personal celebrity / being everyone's flavor
- Scripted content
- Having to hear people's opinions about his lifestyle
- The moral judgment that comes with visibility
- Recognition for its own sake ("I had my moment in the sun")

### What he's OPEN TO:
- Having a shooter (videographer) who captures natural footage
- But ONLY if the person:
  - Knows how to "work him" the right way
  - Gets him worked up just right
  - Draws out natural over-exaggeration that reads well on camera
  - Has great editing ability
  - Doesn't require scripting
- Being transparent and doing whatever he wants
- Getting notoriety to FUEL THE BUSINESS
- Transferring visibility to the BUSINESS rather than to him personally

### The tension:
- "I kind of like to do whatever the fuck I want to do and I also like to be totally transparent. I don't know that those two go totally in hand if you're being really visible."
- He'll do visibility for business reasons but doesn't personally desire recognition
- Would be happiest if the business gets the visibility, not him as a personality

### What he knows works for him:
- Interviews where the interviewer knows how to draw out the best content
- Not scripted, but natural with the right person facilitating
- When someone can get him to "over-exaggerate" in a way that plays well on camera
- The right interlocutor makes all the difference
