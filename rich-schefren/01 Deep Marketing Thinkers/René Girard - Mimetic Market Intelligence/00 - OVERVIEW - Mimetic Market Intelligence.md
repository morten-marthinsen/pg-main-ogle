# Mimetic Market Intelligence — What This Skill Does

**Created:** 2026-03-04
**Status:** Active — 6-document output, two phases

---

## The Core Idea in One Sentence

Most competitive analysis compares features and pricing. This skill goes one layer deeper — it maps what **desires** every competitor is selling, where those competitors have all unconsciously converged into saying the same thing, and where the open territory is that nobody has claimed.

The theoretical foundation is René Girard's mimetic desire theory: people don't want things independently. They want what they see other people wanting. Whoever is the most visible, credible model for a desired identity wins — not because they have the best product, but because the market has latched onto them as the aspirational target. This skill maps exactly that competitive landscape.

---

## What It Analyzes

### 1. Promise Language
The skill pulls actual quotes from every competitor's website, sales pages, social media, and public marketing. It looks at:
- **What they promise** — not features, but transformations
- **The exact words they use** — which phrases have become industry boilerplate, which are genuinely distinctive
- **How crowded each promise cluster is** — when 8 competitors all say "work less, earn more," that phrase has become invisible noise

### 2. Narrative Structure
Every competitor is telling a story — a villain, a discovery, a transformation. The skill maps:
- What story arc each competitor tells
- Whether the market has converged on a single dominant narrative (making every competitor sound like a remix of the same story)
- Where the narrative territory is genuinely unclaimed

### 3. Proof Points
How does each competitor prove their claims? The skill categorizes:
- Who they feature as success models (celebrities, peers, statistics)
- Whether proof is external-mediator style (aspirational/distant) or internal-mediator style (peer/accessible)
- Which proof formats are saturated vs. which are rare in the market

### 4. Common Enemy
Who or what does each competitor position against? The skill maps:
- What each competitor blames for the buyer's problem
- Where the market has converged on the same enemy (making the villain feel generic)
- Where an unoccupied enemy framing exists

### 5. Desire Mapping
The deepest layer. For each competitor, the skill identifies:
- **Primary desire mediated** — the core identity the buyer wants to inhabit
- **Secondary desires** — supporting wants that reinforce the primary
- **The aspirational model** — the figure the competitor holds up as what the buyer could become
- **5-10 verbatim quotes** from actual marketing showing exactly how desire is activated

## What It Produces

The skill delivers **6 documents across 2 phases**.

---

### Phase 1 — Market Research (AI produces from external research alone)

**Document 1: Anti-Mimetic Differentiator Analysis**

Maps where the market has unconsciously converged into sameness across 5 dimensions: promise language, narrative arc, offer structure, proof types, and enemy framing. Then honestly maps where the client has also converged — including quotes from the client's own marketing as evidence. Then identifies what genuinely separates the client, and what positioning territory is completely unoccupied.

*This document tells you: "Here's what everyone is saying, here's where you sound like everyone else, here's where you're genuinely different, here's what nobody is claiming."*

---

**Document 2: Competitor Desire Theft Analysis**

Profiles 10–20 competitors on their desire mediation — what they're actually selling at the identity/aspiration level, not the feature level. Identifies **Contested Desire Zones** (5+ competitors all fighting over the same desire = market is numb to it) and **Underserved Desire Zones** (desires that clearly exist in the market but that few or no competitors are explicitly activating).

*This document tells you: "Here's the desire competition map — these are the overcrowded fights you're already in, and these are the desires your buyers have that nobody is speaking to."*

---

**Document 3: Client Desire Profile**

Applies the same depth of analysis to the client that was applied to every competitor. Primary desire mediated, secondary desires, the aspirational model presented, 10+ evidence quotes from actual client marketing. Surfaces desire mediation strengths and gaps. Analyzes whether the client functions as an external mediator (aspirational/distant) or internal mediator (accessible/peer) — and whether that balance is working. Includes a side-by-side comparison table against the top 4–5 competitors.

*This document tells you: "Here's exactly what desire game you're playing right now, where you're winning it, and where you're invisible."*

---

### Phase 2 — Strategy (requires client conversation first)

Phase 1 delivers the three analysis documents and then pauses. The skill surfaces the specific questions the client needs to answer before strategy documents can be written — because Docs 4–6 require knowing what the client is willing to do, what constraints exist, and what the client's actual vision is. Strategy without that conversation produces generic advice.

---

**Document 4: Visibility and Model Strategy**

How to make the client's aspirational identity visible and accessible enough to generate sustained mimetic desire. Maps current content/channel presence against where visibility gaps are hurting desire activation. Produces a concrete plan based on what the client is actually willing and able to do.

*This document tells you: "Here's how to be seen as the model — the person your buyers want to become — in a way you'll actually execute."*

---

**Document 5: Desire Unification Strategy**

Most businesses with multiple products accidentally mediate conflicting desires — one product says "be the autonomous expert," another says "be part of a community," another says "be the systematized operator." The desires compete and weaken each other. This document maps the client's product architecture against their desire signals and builds a unified arc — one clear identity transformation that all products serve.

*This document tells you: "Here's how to make all your products feel like chapters in one story instead of unrelated items on a menu."*

---

**Document 6: Internal Mediator Deployment**

Restructures the client's social proof strategy for maximum mimetic activation. Most proof is external-mediator style (famous people, big results, credentials) — which creates admiration but not mimetic desire. The shift to internal mediation (peer-level success stories, before/afters from buyers who look like the prospect) creates "if they can do it, I can do it" desire, which converts better. Produces a specific plan for identifying, gathering, and deploying peer success stories.

*This document tells you: "Here's how to restructure your proof so it makes buyers feel the desire to become your client, not just respect you from a distance."*

---

## The Key Lesson Behind the Skill

When Rich described the origin of this skill (February 21, 2026), the insight was this:

Traditional competitive analysis asks: *"What are competitors offering?"*
This skill asks: *"What are competitors making people want to become?"*

Those are different questions with different answers. And the second answer reveals competitive territory that the first analysis cannot see — because the real competition isn't over who has the best system or the best price. It's over whose identity offer the market has adopted as the one worth imitating.

The skill doesn't tell you what to say. It tells you what the market is actually responding to at the desire level — and where the open space is.
