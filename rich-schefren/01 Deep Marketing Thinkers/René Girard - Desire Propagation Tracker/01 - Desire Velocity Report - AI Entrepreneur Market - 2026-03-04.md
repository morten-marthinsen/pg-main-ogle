# Desire Velocity Report — AI Entrepreneur Market
**Date:** 2026-03-04
**Run Type:** Baseline (First Run)
**Market:** Online entrepreneurs and business owners generating $250K–$5M/year, interested in AI-powered business growth, marketing strategy, and operational leverage
**Analyst Framework:** René Girard — Mimetic Desire Propagation
**Methodology:** Cross-platform research across X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Substack, Reddit, podcasts, YouTube, and industry reports

---

> **Girardian Framing:** This is not a trend report. Trends describe what people are *doing*. This report tracks what people are *wanting* — the mimetic contagion of aspiration, the models they are imitating, and the rivalries generating competitive desire. Every desire listed here has a *mediator* — someone whose wanting sparked the wanting of others.

## SECTION 3: DESIRE CONFLICTS

**Incompatible desires propagating simultaneously in the same market:**

### Conflict 1: "Be the Sovereign Solo Operator" vs. "Build a Real Business to Sell"

These two aspirations are structurally incompatible. The one-person empire aspiration implies minimizing dependency on any team or structure. But businesses built around a single person with no operational depth are largely unsaleable — buyers want teams and systems, not individuals. Dan Martell's own framework (Buy Back Your Time → build a team → sell for a multiple) runs directly counter to the "zero employees" fantasy. Both desires are propagating in the same cohort simultaneously, creating a split identity crisis.

**Tension level: High.** The "AI solo operator" dream is being consumed by the same entrepreneurs who have also absorbed Hormozi's "make a business valuable enough to sell" framework.

### Conflict 2: "Move Fast with AI Now" vs. "Don't Implement the Wrong Thing"

The FOMO pressure to adopt AI immediately ("you're already behind") is running headlong into the documented evidence that 60% of AI projects will be abandoned by 2026 (Gartner) and 95% of generative AI pilots fail to deliver measurable ROI (MIT). Entrepreneurs are simultaneously being told urgency is survival and that speed without strategy is waste. Both of these messages are propagating through the same media channels, creating paralysis — not action.

**Tension level: Very High.** This conflict is the primary reason the implementation gap is widening even as desire intensifies. Entrepreneurs are frozen between two fears: the fear of moving too slow, and the fear of wasting money and credibility on the wrong move.

### Conflict 3: "AI Gives Me Leverage to Do Less" vs. "I Have to Constantly Learn New AI Capabilities"

The desire for time freedom (AI as liberation from work) is being contradicted by the observable reality that AI is evolving so rapidly that staying current is itself becoming a major time commitment. The learning curve for "context engineering," multi-agent systems, and AI-native business design is steep and accelerating. Entrepreneurs want AI to give them back time, but AI is currently consuming time at the learning stage before it returns any. This is creating resentment and overwhelm underneath the aspirational surface.

**Tension level: High.** This conflict is why "AI overwhelm" is a documented, named phenomenon in this market (Entrepreneur.com, 2026).

### Conflict 4: "Humanless Business" vs. "Human Authenticity as Competitive Advantage"

The one-person AI empire desire envisions replacing all human labor with AI. Simultaneously, a strong desire is emerging for authentic human voice and perspective as the premium differentiator in an AI-commoditized content world. These cannot both be true at the same time in the same business. The entrepreneurs who want to go fully humanless will produce undifferentiated AI output; those who maintain human depth will face higher production demands. The market has not yet resolved this tension.

**Tension level: Moderate and building.**

## SECTION 4: FADING DESIRES

**What the market WAS wanting 6-12 months ago that is now losing velocity:**

### Fading Desire 1: "I Need to Learn Prompt Engineering"

**Velocity drop: Steep. Was prominent Q2-Q3 2025, now clearly declining.**

Six months ago, "prompt engineering" was the coveted skill. Dozens of courses, prompts libraries, and "prompt frameworks" were generating significant revenue. This desire is now visibly fading as the market recognizes prompt engineering as a commodity skill — something AI tools largely do automatically, and something so easily imitated that owning it provides no durable advantage. Context engineering (Desire 3 above) is replacing it in the sophisticated tier.

**Evidence:** "Context engineering is the new hotness" replacing prompt engineering discourse. The New Stack headline: "RAG Isn't Dead, But Context Engineering Is the New Hotness."

### Fading Desire 2: "I Need to Build a Funnel"

**Velocity drop: Moderate. Still present but declining in urgency.**

The classic funnel-obsession that defined the 2015-2023 era of online entrepreneurship is losing intensity. Not because funnels don't work — they do — but because the entrepreneurial conversation has moved upstream. The market is asking bigger questions about AI-native business design, and funnel optimization feels like rearranging deck chairs. The Entrepreneur.com piece "Your Current Digital Marketing Strategy Won't Hold Up in 2026. Here's the New Playbook" is symptomatic of this shift.

**Evidence:** DestineCopps.com podcast episode "What Actually Worked in 2025 (and What We're Ditching in 2026)" explicitly discusses pivoting away from high-effort funnel approaches toward different models. Search volume for "challenge funnel" declining relative to "AI agent workflow" searches.

### Fading Desire 3: "I Need to Go Viral / Hack the Algorithm"

**Velocity drop: Significant. Was dominant in 2023-2024, now secondary.**

The desire to "crack the algorithm" — build a massive audience quickly through platform-specific virality tactics — is fading as the creator economy shows that the route to monetization is community ownership, not follower count. The Creator Economy shift toward "attention to ownership" (Later.com's 2026 predictions, Digiday) signals this desire is being replaced by the desire for owned audiences and predictable revenue.

**Evidence:** "In 2026, the creator economy will move from being attention-driven to ownership-driven." — Multiple creator economy research reports. The shift from follower count obsession to subscriber/member count as the meaningful metric.

### Fading Desire 4: "Hustle Culture" / Desire to Out-Work the Competition

**Velocity drop: Structural / Generational shift.**

The "rise and grind" identity that dominated entrepreneurial culture from 2010-2022 has undergone a fundamental legitimacy collapse. It is not merely that hustle culture has "bad press" — the desire itself is weakening. 64% of Gen Z values mental health over financial growth (willing to accept lower pay for better work-life balance). Multiple Entrepreneur.com pieces in 2026 explicitly position hustle culture as actively counterproductive, not merely unhealthy. "The brands that succeed in 2026 will be the ones built on structure, not sacrifice."

**Evidence:** "Hustle Culture Is Outdated — Here's What Actually Scales a Business" (Entrepreneur.com, 2026). "Hustle Culture is Dead: Why Smart People Are Choosing Slow Growth in 2026" (MuscledMX). The framing has shifted from "hustle" to "leverage" — a fundamental vocabulary migration that signals desire migration.
