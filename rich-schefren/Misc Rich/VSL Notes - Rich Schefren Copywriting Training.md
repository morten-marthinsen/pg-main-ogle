**VSL Structure:**
![VSL Structure Image]

An empathy map is an incredibly powerful tool that gives copywriters and marketers a deeper understanding of their target audience by breaking down what prospects are thinking, feeling, saying, and doing. It's a way to visualize the real concerns, motivations, and desires of the people we're trying to reach, which can make our copy feel far more relevant and personalized.

Empathy mapping works because it dives into the psychology of a prospect rather than just demographics. When we know what our audience is experiencing on a personal level, we can create messages that speak directly to their journey, their frustrations, and their hopes. This isn't just about attracting attention—it's about making a meaningful connection.

To get the most out of empathy mapping, we break it down into four main areas:

	**Thinking**: What thoughts are occupying the prospect's mind when it comes to solving their problem?
	
	**Feeling**: What emotions are they wrestling with around this issue?
	
	**Saying**: What are they expressing verbally or online about their situation?
	
	**Doing**: What actions are they taking (or not taking) that show where they are in their journey?
	

I also take it a step further by looking at two key moments in the customer journey: before and after the purchase. Understanding what the prospect is thinking, feeling, saying, and doing at each of these stages can offer transformative insights for our copy. I also explore the influence of others in their circle—what their family, friends, or colleagues might be thinking, feeling, saying, and doing about the purchase decision. These additional layers help capture the full picture of the prospect's experience.
