# Practice Brief #05: AI Department Builder

**Type:** PRACTICE (Sandbox Competition)
**Price Point:** $2,997
**Context:** Launch
**Focus:** Build internal AI capability/team

---

## The Offer

### Product: AI Department Builder
A program to create an internal "AI function" in your company—one person or team responsible for AI implementation.

### Positioning
"Build the AI capability you'll need for the next decade—inside your own company."

### Structure
- Module 1: Defining the AI function (scope, responsibilities)
- Module 2: Selecting/developing your AI champion
- Module 3: AI tool stack selection
- Module 4: Implementation playbooks by department
- Module 5: Governance and security
- Module 6: Scaling and evolution
- Role description templates
- AI policy templates
- 90-day implementation roadmap
- Monthly office hours (6 months)

---

## Audience

### Who
- CEOs/COOs of $3M-$20M companies
- 15-100 employees
- Ready to make AI a permanent capability
- Thinking long-term, not just quick wins
- May already have someone "good with AI" on team

### Psychology
- Understands AI is not a one-time project
- Wants institutional capability, not dependency
- Thinking about who owns this internally
- Worried about security/governance
- Needs structure and templates

### Primary Objection
"We're not big enough for an 'AI department.' That's for enterprises."

---

## Competition Instructions

Create a webinar that:
1. Reframes "department" as "function" (could be one person)
2. Shows cost of NOT having internal capability
3. Addresses governance/security concerns
4. Uses long-term strategic framing
5. Creates urgency around talent/capability window

---

## Learning Goals

This brief teaches the system:
- Strategic/organizational offers
- Long-term capability building messaging
- Enterprise concerns (governance, security)
- "Function not department" reframing
- Institutional vs. individual transformation
