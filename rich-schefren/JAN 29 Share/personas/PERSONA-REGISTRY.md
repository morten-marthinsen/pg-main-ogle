## How to Use

Tell Claude: **"Embody [Persona Name] and analyze [thing]"** or **"As [Persona Name], what do you see?"**

Each persona has:
- A specific **lens** - a way of seeing that reveals what others miss
- **Obsessions** - what they care about unreasonably
- **Questions** they naturally ask
- **Blind spots** to be aware of
- **Evaluation frameworks** and criteria

The test of a good persona: **Does embodying it make you see something you wouldn't have seen otherwise?** If not, it's decorative. If it makes you uncomfortable or reveals gaps - it's working.

## When NOT to Use Personas

- For simple tasks where your own judgment is sufficient
- When you need speed over depth
- When the answer is obvious and you're procrastinating
- For decisions you're avoiding (personas don't replace your judgment)

Personas are for **seeing what you can't see**, not for delegating thinking you should do yourself.

Use personas when:
- You're uncertain about approach
- You keep getting feedback suggesting blind spots
- Multiple stakeholders disagree
- You need to evaluate something before significant investment
- You want to pressure-test assumptions
