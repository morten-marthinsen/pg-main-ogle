## Clayton Makepeace

### The Emotional Architect

**Background:** Clayton was one of the highest-paid copywriters in history, generating over $1.5 billion in sales. He specialized in health and financial markets, where proof and emotion must work together.

**Core Philosophy:** "Identify the dominant resident emotion and activate it."

Clayton believed that every prospect has a dominant emotion already living inside them - fear, anger, greed, hope. Your job isn't to create emotion; it's to identify which one is already there and pour gasoline on it.

### What Clayton Does Best

| Strength | How It Shows Up |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Emotional Triggers** | 29 resident emotions mapped with activation sequences |
| **Proof Cascades** | 13 distinct proof strategies that build overwhelming belief |
| **Headlines** | "A-Brain" targeting - hits the lizard brain first |
| **Voice** | Indignation FOR the reader, shared enemy creation |

### Clayton's Signature Techniques

**The A-Brain Test:** Does your headline trigger the lizard brain (fear, anger, greed) or the thinking brain (curiosity, logic)? Clayton always targets the lizard brain first.

**Proof Density:** Clayton doesn't put proof in one section. He layers it throughout - social proof, expert proof, demonstration proof, statistical proof - until disbelief is impossible.

**Dominant Emotion Selection:** Before writing a word, Clayton identifies which emotion is ALREADY dominant in the prospect and builds the entire piece around activating it.

### When to Use Clayton

- Health and financial markets
- Skeptical buyers who need overwhelming proof
- When you need emotional intensity
- When you want copy that feels "hard-hitting"
- When building belief is the primary challenge

## John Carlton

### The Stopping Power Specialist

**Background:** Carlton wrote the most-mailed sales letter in history. He's famous for taking complex products and making them impossible to ignore. His "Simple Writing System" has trained thousands of copywriters.

**Core Philosophy:** "Stop them cold. Make them unable to look away."

Carlton believes the biggest problem in marketing isn't persuasion - it's attention. If you can't stop someone mid-scroll, nothing else matters. His techniques are designed to create that irresistible "what the hell is THIS?" moment.

### What Carlton Does Best

| Strength | How It Shows Up |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Hooks** | "Incongruous juxtaposition" - unexpected combinations that stop the scroll |
| **Voice** | "Bar room" conversational tone, like talking to a friend |
| **Specificity** | Hyper-specific details that create believability |
| **Personality** | Strong point of view that polarizes (on purpose) |

### Carlton's Signature Techniques

**Incongruous Juxtaposition:** Put together two things that don't seem to belong together. "The one-legged golfer who outdrives pros half his age." The brain can't ignore the mismatch.

**The Bar Room Voice:** Write like you're telling a story to a friend at a bar. No corporate speak. No fancy words. Just real talk.

**A-Brain Triggers:** Similar to Clayton's lizard brain, Carlton targets primal motivations: survival, reproduction, status, belonging.

**The One-Two Punch:** Emotional hit first, then logical justification. Never lead with logic.

### When to Use Carlton

- Products that need "stopping power"
- Markets that have seen everything (jaded prospects)
- When you need a strong, distinctive voice
- Golf, fitness, self-improvement markets
- When personality should shine through

## David Deutsch

### The Story Architect

**Background:** Deutsch is the master of "A-list" copywriting - the sophisticated, story-driven approach that works on educated, skeptical buyers. His copy doesn't feel like copy; it feels like a conversation with a trusted advisor.

**Core Philosophy:** "Take them on a dual journey - rational and emotional - where both paths lead to the same conclusion."

Deutsch believes that sophisticated buyers have two decision-making systems running simultaneously. You need to satisfy BOTH the logical mind AND the emotional heart. His copy creates two parallel journeys that converge at the purchase decision.

### What Deutsch Does Best

| Strength | How It Shows Up |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Dual Journey** | Rational + emotional paths that converge |
| **Story Integration** | Copy that doesn't feel like copy |
| **Resistance-Free Selling** | "Just Talk" method that bypasses defenses |
| **Sophisticated Voice** | Trusted advisor, not salesperson |

### Deutsch's Signature Techniques

**The Dual Journey:** Every piece has two threads - one for the logical mind (evidence, reasoning) and one for the emotional heart (stories, identification). Both must arrive at the same destination.

**The Just Talk Method:** Write as if you're having a conversation with one person. Remove all "salesy" language. Let the product sell itself through genuine dialogue.

**The Crossroads Close:** At the close, present two futures - one where they buy, one where they don't. Make both vivid. Let them choose.

**10 Emotional Triggers:** Deutsch's specific emotional levers, different from Clayton's 29 - more focused on identity and transformation.

### When to Use Deutsch

- Sophisticated, educated buyers
- B2B and high-ticket offers
- When the audience resists "marketing"
- Story-driven long-form content
- When trust is the primary challenge

## Evaldo Albuquerque

### The VSL Master

**Background:** Evaldo wrote some of the most successful VSLs (Video Sales Letters) in the health market. His specialty is taking skeptical, "burned before" buyers and methodically building belief until purchase becomes inevitable.

**Core Philosophy:** "Reduce everything to the One Belief. If they believe that one thing, they'll buy."

Evaldo doesn't try to convince prospects of 10 things. He identifies the ONE belief that, if accepted, makes everything else fall into place. Then he builds the entire piece around installing that single belief.

### What Evaldo Does Best

| Strength | How It Shows Up |
|----------|-----------------|
| **VSL Structure** | The 10 Questions framework for video scripts |
| **Croc Brain Filters** | Targeting the primitive brain |
| **Gradual Belief Building** | Slow, methodical conviction |
| **One Belief Focus** | Single-minded persuasion |

### Evaldo's Signature Techniques

**The One Belief Formula:** What's the single belief that, if installed, makes purchase logical? Build everything around that.

**The 10 Questions:** A structural framework for VSLs that answers the questions prospects are unconsciously asking, in the right order.

**Croc Brain Filters:** The "crocodile brain" (primitive survival brain) filters everything before it reaches the logical brain. Evaldo's techniques get past those filters.

**The Three Box Check:** Validate ideas against three criteria before using them.

### When to Use Evaldo

- VSLs specifically
- Skeptical buyers who've been burned before
- Health and supplement markets
- When you need gradual belief building
- Products with complex mechanisms to explain
