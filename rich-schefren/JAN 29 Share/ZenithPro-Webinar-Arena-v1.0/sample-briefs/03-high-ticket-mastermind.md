# Sample Brief: High-Ticket Mastermind

## Copy This Into Your Arena

```
Brief: Create a webinar for a $15,000 mastermind for established consultants earning $300K+ who want to scale to $1M+ without adding more clients. Target audience is successful but exhausted - they've built great businesses but hit a ceiling. They're sophisticated buyers who've seen every tactic and hate being "sold to." Format is a live virtual event with application process for enrollment.

Price: $15,000
Market sophistication: 5
Format: Live
Rounds: 3
```

---

## Expected Expert Tendencies

**Joon** should excel here:
- High-ticket is his sweet spot
- Live format leverages his strengths
- Transformation positioning matches the audience

**Cage** should compete well:
- Sophisticated audience needs deep psychology
- X-Ray process works for complex desires
- Handles "seen everything" objection

**Kern** is a contender:
- Help-first addresses "hate being sold to"
- Campaign approach builds relationship
- Legitimate scarcity (application process)

**Fladlien** will likely struggle:
- His direct approach may feel too aggressive
- High-ticket needs more proof and relationship
- Sophisticated buyers resist his tactics

---

## What to Watch For

- How each handles application-based enrollment
- Approaches to the "exhausted but successful" psychology
- Different strategies for maximum sophistication

---

## Why This is a Good Test Brief

This brief has conflicting requirements:
- High price needs proof, but sophisticated buyers hate obvious proof
- Live needs energy, but exhausted buyers don't want hype
- Scaling requires belief change, but they're skeptical of promises

These conflicts make Synthesis potentially competitive.
