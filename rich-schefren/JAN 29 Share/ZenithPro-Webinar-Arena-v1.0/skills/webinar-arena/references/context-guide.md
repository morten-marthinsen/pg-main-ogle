# Webinar Arena Context Guide

## Context Variables and Expert Biases

Use this guide to understand how context affects expert selection and evaluation.

---

## Price Point

| Price | Expert Bias | Rationale |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| <$500 | Fladlien, Brunson | Direct response, urgency-based closes work well |
| $500-$2,000 | Balanced | All approaches viable |
| $2,000-$10,000 | Cage, Kern | Requires depth, relationship, trust-building |
| >$10,000 | Kern, Joon, Kennedy | Multi-touch, high-touch, precision required |

---

## Market Sophistication (Schwartz Scale)

| Level | Description | Expert Bias |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Unaware of problem | Fladlien (emotional awakening) |
| 2 | Aware of problem, not solutions | Fladlien, Brunson (direct) |
| 3 | Solution-aware, not products | Balanced |
| 4 | Product-aware, comparing options | Cage, Kern (nuanced) |
| 5 | Most aware, skeptical | Kennedy (precision), Cage (depth) |

---

## Delivery Format

| Format | Expert Bias | Rationale |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Live webinar | Brunson (energy), Cage (retention) | High energy works, retention matters |
| Automated evergreen | Fladlien (structure), Kennedy (media-agnostic) | Consistent structure, no energy loss |
| Hybrid (live + replay) | Kern (campaign thinking) | System approach |
| Live event/stage | Joon (5 Pillars) | Room control, energy management |

---

## Product Type

| Type | Expert Bias | Rationale |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Information/Course | Cage (depth proves competence) | Content-heavy, need to demonstrate value |
| Transformation/Coaching | Fladlien (emotional leverage) | Emotional connection matters |
| Service/Done-for-you | Balanced | Mix of proof and urgency |
| Physical product | Fladlien (direct, benefits) | Clear benefits, direct close |
| High-ticket consulting | Kennedy, Joon | Precision, high-touch |

## Primary Goal

| Goal | Expert Bias | Rationale |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Maximize immediate conversion | Fladlien (150 Yeses, Stack) | Direct response mechanics |
| Build long-term relationship | Cage (content depth), Kern (help-first) | Trust over transaction |
| Both | Synthesis likely strong | Need combination approach |

## When Synthesis Might Win

Synthesis is most likely to outperform pure methodologies when:

1. **Conflicting demands** - Brief needs both relationship AND urgency
2. **Mixed audience** - Some sophisticated, some not
3. **Unusual format** - Not clearly live OR automated
4. **High-ticket + mass market** - Rare combination
5. **Multi-touch required** - Kern's pre/post + someone's close

Synthesis is least likely to win when:

1. **Clear context** - Obviously matches one expert's sweet spot
2. **Simple product** - Straightforward offering
3. **Homogeneous audience** - All same sophistication level
4. **Standard format** - Clearly live webinar or automated

As the arena runs, patterns will emerge:

2. Synthesis strategy selection
