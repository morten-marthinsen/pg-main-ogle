# The Mechanism Hierarchy - Todd Brown

> **Creator:** Todd Brown
> **Based On:** E5 Method, Unique Mechanism Workshop
> **Application:** Determining appropriate mechanism sophistication for your market

---

## Overview

The Mechanism Hierarchy (E-Levels) is a classification system that helps copywriters understand the level of mechanism sophistication required to compete effectively in different market conditions.

**Core Principle:** Match mechanism sophistication to market sophistication.

---

## The E-Level Classification System

### E1: Simple Claim, No Mechanism

**Definition:** Pure benefit statement with no explanation of HOW.

**Characteristics:**
- Direct promise
- No mechanism mentioned
- No proof required
- Appeal to basic desire

**Example:** "Lose weight fast"

**When to Use:**
- Brand new market (Stage 1)
- First-mover advantage
- Unaware audience

---

### E2: Proof-Backed Claim

**Definition:** Benefit statement supported by evidence, but still no mechanism.

**Characteristics:**
- Same promise as E1
- Added proof elements
- Statistics or testimonials
- Still no HOW explanation

**Example:** "Clinically proven weight loss - 97% of users saw results"

**When to Use:**
- Early market (Stage 2)
- Competitors making similar claims
- Need credibility boost

---

### E3: Mechanism Introduced

**Definition:** Benefit is now explained through a named mechanism.

**Characteristics:**
- Promise + HOW
- Named mechanism
- Basic explanation of why it works
- Beginning of differentiation

**Example:** "Lose weight by boosting your metabolism"

**When to Use:**
- Maturing market (Stage 3)
- Competitors using E1-E2 messaging
- Need to stand out

---

### E4: New Mechanism + Paradigm Shift

**Definition:** Novel mechanism that challenges existing beliefs about the problem.

**Characteristics:**
- Completely new mechanism
- Changes how they think about the problem
- Explains why everything else failed
- Creates "aha moment"

**Example:** "Lectins punch holes in your gut lining, flooding your body with toxins that cause weight gain"

**When to Use:**
- Saturated market (Stage 4)
- Competitors using E3 mechanisms
- High skepticism

---

### E5: Category Creation + Worldview Shift

**Definition:** Creates an entirely new category and tribal identity around the mechanism.

**Characteristics:**
- Mechanism defines a new world
- Creates tribe/community
- Identity-level connection
- Complete worldview change

**Example:** "Join the Lectin-Free Movement - we're not just losing weight, we're healing from the inside out. Over 50,000 of us have discovered the truth."

**When to Use:**
- Dead market (Stage 5)
- Extreme skepticism
- Need tribal connection

## The E-Level Decision Matrix

| Market Stage | Minimum E-Level | Ideal E-Level |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Stage 1 | E1 | E1-E2 |
| Stage 2 | E2 | E2-E3 |
| Stage 3 | E3 | E3-E4 |
| Stage 4 | E4 | E4-E5 |
| Stage 5 | E5 | E5 |

## Scoring Benchmarks

Using a mechanism scoring approach with 13 dimensions:

| E-Level | Score Range | Competitiveness |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| E1 | Below 50 | Only works in Stage 1 |
| E2 | 50-70 | Can compete in Stage 2 |
| E3 | 70-90 | Competitive in Stage 3 |
| E4 | 90-110 | Strong in Stage 4 |
| E5 | 110+ | Dominant positioning |

**Scoring Benchmark:** 90+ to compete in mature markets, 110+ to dominate.

## The Three Types of Mechanisms (by Origin)

### Type A: True Unique Mechanism

**Source:** Your product actually has something proprietary.

**Examples:**
- Patented technology
- Proprietary ingredient
- Unique process

**E-Level Potential:** Can achieve any E-Level with proper positioning.

### Type B: Unspoken Mechanism

**Source:** Feature that competitors also have but don't talk about.

**Strategy:** By emphasizing what others ignore, you own it.

**E-Level Potential:** Typically E3-E4 (rarely E5 without paradigm shift).

### Type C: Transubstantiated Mechanism

**Source:** Ordinary process renamed to become extraordinary.

**Strategy:** Transform perception through naming and framing.

**E-Level Potential:** Can achieve E4-E5 with strong category creation.

## Upgrading Your E-Level

### From E1 to E2:
- Add proof elements
- Include statistics
- Feature testimonials

### From E2 to E3:
- Identify the mechanism
- Name it
- Explain how it works

### From E3 to E4:
- Create paradigm shift
- Challenge existing beliefs
- Show why others fail
- Novel mechanism required

### From E4 to E5:
- Build community element
- Create identity connection
- Establish new category
- Tribal language and belonging

## The UMP/UMS/UMD Framework

Breaking down mechanism components:

| Component | Definition | E-Level Role |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| **UMP** (Unique Mechanism Problem) | Why they've failed | Required for E4+ |
| **UMS** (Unique Mechanism Solution) | Why they'll succeed | Required for E3+ |
| **UMD** (Unique Mechanism Delivery) | How they get it | Enhances any E-Level |

**Example (Golf):**
- **UMP:** "The ball is NOT at the bottom of your swing arc"
- **UMS:** "The Perfect Strike Point technique"
- **UMD:** "The Simple Strike Sequence training"

## The Paradigm Shift Requirement (E4+)

For E4 and above, you must create a paradigm shift:

**What a Paradigm Shift Does:**
1. Challenges their existing belief
2. Reframes the problem entirely
3. Explains why everything else failed
4. Positions your mechanism as the only solution

**The Old Belief → New Belief Structure:**

```
OLD: "You're overweight because you lack willpower"
REFRAME: "Actually, willpower has nothing to do with it"
NEW: "Your gut is leaky, flooding your body with toxins that force weight gain"
```
