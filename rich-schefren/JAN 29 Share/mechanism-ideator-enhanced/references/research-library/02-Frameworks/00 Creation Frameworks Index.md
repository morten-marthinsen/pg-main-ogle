## Framework Summaries

### Stefan Georgi's RMBC Method

**Creator:** Stefan Georgi ($1B+ in copy sales)
**Acronym:** Research, Mechanism, Brief, Copy

**Core Concept:** A systematized, four-step approach to writing sales copy that removes the "creative mystery" and replaces it with a repeatable process.

**The Two-Part Mechanism Structure:**
1. **UMP (Unique Mechanism of the Problem)** - Why they've failed before
2. **UMS (Unique Mechanism of the Solution)** - Why they'll succeed now

**Key Insight:** "80% of the offers I write end up working on cold traffic. Even good copywriters can barely hit 30-40%."

**Three Criteria for Great Mechanisms:**
1. Simple enough to explain in 1-2 sentences (like a meme)
2. Provides intuitive proof (makes immediate sense)
3. Backed by empirical proof (studies, case studies, expert quotes)

### Todd Brown's E5 Method

**Creator:** Todd Brown (E5 Camp, Top One Mastermind)
**Acronym:** Examine, Engineer, Evaluate, Enhance, Expand

**Core Concept:** A five-stage method to engineer customer acquisition campaigns from scratch, with the Unique Mechanism at the core.

**The Examine Stage:**
- Identify Primary Promise
- Identify Unique Mechanism
- Identify Product USP
- Identify Functional Benefits
- Identify Dimensionalized Benefits

**Three Types of Unique Mechanisms:**
1. **True Unique Mechanism** - You actually have one (proprietary technology, ingredient, process)
2. **Unspoken Mechanism** - Competitors have it too, but nobody talks about it
3. **Transubstantiated Mechanism** - Turn something ordinary into something extraordinary (naming transforms perception)

**Key Insight:** "The Unique Mechanism gives your prospects hope that this time, with your offer, things will be different."

### Evaldo Albuquerque's One Belief System

**Creator:** Evaldo Albuquerque ($100M+ in sales, "Michael Jordan of financial copywriting")
**Book:** The 16-Word Sales Letter

**The 16-Word Formula:**
> "This NEW OPPORTUNITY is the key to THEIR DESIRE and it's only attainable through my NEW MECHANISM."

**Three Core Elements:**
1. **New Opportunity** - What makes this different
2. **Prospect's Desire** - What they truly want (significance, respect, value)
3. **New Mechanism** - How your product uniquely delivers it

**The 10 Questions:**
1. How is this different from everything else I've seen?
2. What's in it for me?
3. How do I know this is real?
4. What's holding me back?
5. Who/What is to blame?
6. Why now?
7. Why should I trust you?
8. How does it work?
9. How can I get started?
10. What do I have to lose?

**Key Insight:** "One belief. Ten questions." - Answer these and you have a complete sales letter.

### Eugene Schwartz's Market Sophistication

**Creator:** Eugene Schwartz (Breakthrough Advertising)
**Published:** 1966

**The Five Stages:**

| Stage | Market State | Mechanism Requirement |
|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | First to market | Be simple and direct - no mechanism needed |
| 2 | Competition enters | Explain how your product works |
| 3 | Market saturated | NEW MECHANISM required to make old promises fresh |
| 4 | Mechanism competition | ELABORATE on the mechanism - faster, easier, surer |
| 5 | Market dead | Identification/trust - who you are matters more |

**Key Insight:** "What this market needs now is a NEW MECHANISM: a new way to make the old promise work."

**Stage 3-4 Requirement:** In mature markets, HOW you do it matters more than WHAT you do.

### David Deutsch's Mechanism Approach

**Creator:** David Deutsch (A-List Copywriting Secrets, Boardroom veteran)

**Big Idea Creation Process:**
1. Talk it out loud to real people
2. Try to sell the product verbally first
3. Use "2nd grade math" to generate hundreds of ideas
4. Run ideas through proven templates (dozen different presentation formats)
5. Ask: "What problems are we actually solving? What are the pain points?"

**The 7-Question Big Idea Evaluator:**
- Fast evaluation to determine if an idea is worth pursuing
- Prevents wasted time on "lead balloons"

**Key Insight:** "The simple secret to coming up with lots of good ideas is to come up with lots of bad ones."

**Copy Thinking vs. Copywriting:** Don't go on autopilot when writing - consciously think through every element.

### Clayton Makepeace's Mechanism Reveal Formula

**Creator:** Clayton Makepeace ($1.5B in sales, Boardroom "A+ writer")

**The Mechanism Reveal Structure:**

1. **Name the Mechanism** - Make it concrete and specific
2. **Explain the WHY** - Create cause-and-effect chain
3. **Back with Science/Research** - Studies, patents, credible data
4. **Demonstrate Visually** - Process sidebar showing step-by-step

**Key Principle:** "Process sidebars are strong credibility devices because prospect believes it's based on real biological phenomenon, not just something you're making up."

**The Cortislim Example:**
- Named mechanism (cortisol-blocking)
- Explained why (stress hormone causes fat storage)
- Backed with research
- Result: $100M+ phenomenon in most skeptical market

### Todd Brown's Mechanism Hierarchy

**Creator:** Todd Brown (E5 Method)

**E-Level Classification (Mechanism Sophistication):**

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| E1 | Simple claim, no mechanism | "Lose weight fast" |
| E2 | Proof-backed claim | "Clinically proven weight loss" |
| E3 | Mechanism introduced | "Boost metabolism to burn fat" |
| E4 | New mechanism + paradigm shift | "Lectins punch holes in gut, causing weight gain" |
| E5 | Category creation + worldview shift | Creates entirely new category + tribal identity |

**Matching Market Sophistication:**
- Stage 1-2 markets: E1-E2 sufficient
- Stage 3 markets: E3 required
- Stage 4-5 markets: E4-E5 required to compete

### Mechanism Testing Frameworks

**The Eyes Light Up (ELU) Test** (Wes Kao)
- Watch for physical reactions during conversations
- "Eyes lighting up is the ONLY reaction that matters"
- Intellectual responses aren't helpful - watch for visceral excitement

**The Cocktail Party Test**
- Would someone at a party ask follow-up questions?
- Would they want to know more?
- Does it make them visibly interested?

**The Readability Tests**
- Can a 7th-8th grader understand it?
- Aim for Grade Level 6-8 for maximum reach
- 85% of public can understand Grade Level 8

**Simple vs Complex Test**
- Can you explain it in 1-2 sentences?
- Would they remember it tomorrow?
- Could they explain it to a friend?

### Mechanism Discovery Questions

**Core Discovery Questions:**
1. "What's the ONE thing this product does differently?"
2. "What common component is not being talked about by competitors?"
3. "What is the thing that makes your thing do what it does?"
4. "How specifically does it deliver the benefit?"
5. "Why haven't they succeeded with other solutions?"

**Market Research Questions:**
- "What common component of your product is not being talked about?"
- "What has sold well but doesn't get results?"
- "What's the most extreme way to flip this around?"

**The "Because" Formula:**
- State the benefit
- Add "BECAUSE"
- Explain the mechanism

**Formula Template:**
> "We help [target market] achieve [desired outcome] through [unique mechanism] / without [common problem]."

### Mechanism Naming Frameworks

**Naming Patterns That Work:**

| Pattern | Description | Examples |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Alliteration | Repeated consonant sounds | Firming Flavonoids, Simple Strike |
| Benefit-Embedded | Outcome in the name | Erection Muscle, Fat Flush |
| Scientific-Sounding | Technical credibility | Leptin Resistance, Muscle Confusion |
| Action-Oriented | Dynamic verb energy | Power Plane, Triple Coil |
| Proprietary Systems | Named methodology | 4-Day Cash Machine, Tripwire |

**The Transubstantiation Technique:**
- Turn ordinary into extraordinary through naming
- Example: Ordinary low-price offer → "The Tripwire" (Ryan Deiss)
- Example: Email sequence → "4-Day Cash Machine" (Frank Kern)

**Sound Symbolism Principles:**
- Each phoneme carries psychological impression
- Harder sounds (K, T) = strength, impact
- Softer sounds (L, S) = smooth, easy
- Names that trigger emotional response outperform descriptive names

## Key Principles Across All Frameworks

1. **Mechanism = Hope** - It gives prospects a reason to believe this time will be different
2. **Simplicity Wins** - If you can't explain it in 1-2 sentences, it's too complex
3. **Dual Structure** - Problem mechanism + Solution mechanism work together
4. **Market Calibration** - Mechanism sophistication must match market sophistication
5. **Proof Integration** - Every mechanism claim needs supporting evidence
6. **Proprietary Naming** - Named mechanisms become ownable and memorable
7. **Test Before Writing** - Validate the mechanism before investing in full copy
