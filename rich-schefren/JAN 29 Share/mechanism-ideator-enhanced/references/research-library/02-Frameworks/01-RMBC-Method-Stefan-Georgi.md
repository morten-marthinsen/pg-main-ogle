## Overview

The RMBC Method is a systematized, four-step approach to writing sales copy that removes the "creative mystery" and replaces it with a repeatable process. Stefan Georgi developed this method in 2014 to enable writing 12 sales letters per month.

**Key Result:** 80% of offers written using RMBC work on cold traffic (vs. industry average of 30-40%).

---

## The Four Steps

### Step 1: Research

**Purpose:** Uncover deep psychological triggers, language patterns, and pain points to eliminate guesswork.

**What You Gain:**
- Strong understanding of your prospect
- The exact language they use
- Their pain points
- What solutions they've tried (and why they failed)

**Research Sources:**
- Customer reviews and testimonials
- Competitor analysis
- Customer interviews
- Sales call transcripts
- Support ticket analysis
- Forum discussions
- Amazon reviews (for similar products)

**Time Investment:** Typically 30-40% of total project time

---

### Step 2: Mechanism

**Purpose:** Identify the "Unique Mechanism" that explains why the problem exists and why your solution is the only one that works.

**The Two-Part Mechanism Structure:**

| Component | Definition | Function |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| **UMP** (Unique Mechanism of Problem) | The true underlying cause of their failure | Explains why they haven't succeeded in the past |
| **UMS** (Unique Mechanism of Solution) | Your novel approach | Explains why they will succeed in the future |

**How UMP and UMS Connect:**
- UMP reveals they've been missing 1% of the answer
- UMS provides that missing piece
- Together they create a logical bridge from past failure to future success

**Example:**
- UMP: "You've failed to lose weight because your gut bacteria are sabotaging your metabolism"
- UMS: "Our 3-Strain Probiotic Protocol rebalances the exact bacteria responsible for fat storage"

---

### Step 3: Brief

**Purpose:** Construct a tight, logical outline that pre-sells the offer before writing final copy.

**What the Brief Contains:**
- All research synthesized
- Mechanism articulated clearly
- Logical flow of argument
- Key proof points
- Objection handling plan
- Offer structure

**The Brief Template Questions:**
1. Who is the prospect?
2. What is their primary desire?
3. What have they tried before?
4. Why did previous solutions fail (UMP)?
5. What is our solution (UMS)?
6. What proof supports our mechanism?
7. What are the main objections?
8. How do we handle each objection?
9. What is the offer?
10. What is the call to action?

**Key Benefit:** By answering all questions in the brief, you have everything needed to write copy without writer's block.

---

### Step 4: Copy

**Purpose:** Write the actual sales argument using proven templates and modular blocks.

**Copy Structure (Stefan's Framework):**
1. Lead
2. Background Story
3. Unique Problem Mechanism
4. Unique Solution Mechanism
5. Product Buildup and Reveal
6. Objection Handling
7. Close

**Writing Principles:**
- Sell with emotion first, logic second
- Keep mechanisms simple (1-2 sentences max)
- Every claim needs proof
- Match copy complexity to market sophistication

## Stefan's Three Criteria for Great Mechanisms

### Criterion 1: Simplicity

A good mechanism is like a meme - something the prospect can:
- Hear once
- Easily share with others
- Remember forever

**The Test:** Can you explain it in 1-2 sentences?

### Criterion 2: Intuitive Proof

When the prospect hears it, they should immediately think: "Yeah, that makes sense."

**The Test:** Does it feel true before you prove it?

### Criterion 3: Empirical Proof

Back up intuition with:
- Studies and research
- Case studies
- Statistics
- Expert quotes

**The Rule:** Sell with emotion first, logic second. The mechanism creates emotional belief; proof confirms it rationally.

## Step-by-Step Mechanism Creation Process

### Phase 1: Research the Problem

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What does the prospect believe is causing their problem?
2. What solutions have they tried?
3. Why did those solutions fail?
4. What do competitors claim is the cause?
5. What does research say about root causes?

### Phase 2: Find the Gap

**Questions to Answer:**
1. What are competitors NOT talking about?
2. What piece of the puzzle is missing from their understanding?
3. What new research or discovery changes the picture?
4. What assumption do they have that's wrong?

### Phase 3: Articulate the UMP

**Template:**
> "The real reason [prospect] hasn't achieved [desired outcome] is because of [root cause they don't know about]. This is called [mechanism name]."

### Phase 4: Articulate the UMS

**Template:**
> "The solution is [your unique approach], which addresses [the root cause] by [how it works]. We call this [solution name]."

### Phase 5: Test and Refine

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Can I explain it in 1-2 sentences?
- [ ] Does it feel intuitively true?
- [ ] Do I have proof to back it up?
- [ ] Is it different from what competitors say?
- [ ] Does it give them hope?

## Examples of Dual Mechanisms

### Weight Loss

**UMP:** "Your leptin receptors have become resistant due to processed foods. Your brain literally can't hear the 'stop eating' signal anymore."

**UMS:** "The Leptin Reset Protocol works by healing your receptors over 21 days, restoring your brain's ability to regulate appetite naturally."

### Financial

**UMP:** "You've missed out on gains because Wall Street algorithms front-run retail orders by milliseconds. You're always buying after they've already moved the price."

**UMS:** "Our Stealth Order System uses institutional-level order routing that masks your intentions, letting you enter positions at true market prices."

### Dating

**UMP:** "You've struggled because you've been taught to 'be nice' - which triggers a woman's 'provider' evaluation rather than her 'attraction' circuitry."

**UMS:** "The Polarity Method activates her 'romantic' brain by creating masculine-feminine tension through specific language patterns."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Mechanism too complex** - If you can't explain it simply, they won't believe it
2. **Mechanism not unique** - If competitors say the same thing, it doesn't differentiate
3. **No proof for mechanism** - Claims without evidence create skepticism
4. **UMP and UMS disconnected** - They must logically link together
5. **Mechanism doesn't create hope** - The whole point is giving them a reason to try again
6. **Skipping the research** - Guessing at mechanisms rarely works
