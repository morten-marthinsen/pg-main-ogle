# Mechanism Discovery Questions

> **Sources:** Stefan Georgi, Todd Brown, David Deutsch, Evaldo Albuquerque, various A-list copywriters
> **Purpose:** Questions to ask during research that lead to mechanism discovery

---

## Overview

The right questions unlock mechanisms. This document compiles discovery questions from top copywriters that help uncover unique mechanisms during the research phase.

**Core Principle:** 80% of copywriting is research. The mechanism usually reveals itself through systematic questioning.

---

## The Core Discovery Questions

### The Fundamental Three:

1. **"What's the ONE thing this product does differently?"**
   - Forces specificity
   - Reveals true differentiator
   - Cuts through feature lists

2. **"How specifically does it deliver the benefit?"**
   - Gets to the mechanism
   - Moves from WHAT to HOW
   - Reveals the process

3. **"Why haven't they succeeded with other solutions?"**
   - Uncovers the UMP (Unique Mechanism of Problem)
   - Explains past failure
   - Creates hope pathway

## Product Research Questions

### Understanding the Product:

- What does this product actually do?
- What's the active ingredient/component/process?
- How does it create results?
- What's the "secret sauce"?
- What can't competitors copy?
- What did the creator discover?

### Finding the Mechanism:

- What is the thing that makes your thing do what it does?
- If I had to explain how this works in one sentence, what would I say?
- What happens inside the body/system when someone uses this?
- What's the step-by-step process of how it delivers results?

### Extracting Uniqueness:

- What do you have that nobody else has?
- What's proprietary about this?
- What can you patent, trademark, or own?
- What would be impossible for competitors to claim?

## Market Research Questions

### Competitor Analysis:

- What promises are competitors making?
- What mechanisms are already in the market?
- What claims have become generic?
- What has been overused?
- What gaps exist in their messaging?

### The Unspoken Opportunity:

- **"What common component of your product is not being talked about by your competitors?"**
  - The Claude Hopkins Schlitz Beer insight
  - Everyone has it, nobody mentions it
  - Your opportunity to own it

### Market Saturation:

- How many times have they heard this promise?
- What mechanisms have they already seen?
- What do they no longer believe?
- What would feel fresh to them?

## Customer Research Questions

### Understanding Their World:

- What problem are they actually trying to solve?
- What keeps them up at night about this?
- What have they already tried?
- Why did those things fail?

### The Blame Question:

- Who or what do they blame for their situation?
- What do they think is causing their problem?
- What are they currently wrong about?

### Language Mining:

- How do they describe their problem?
- What exact words do they use?
- What phrases come up repeatedly?
- What metaphors do they use?

---

## The "Flip" Questions

### Contrarian Discovery:

- What's the common wisdom in this industry?
- What does everyone believe that might be wrong?
- What's the most extreme way to flip this around?
- What if the opposite were true?

### Reframe Questions:

- What if the problem isn't what they think?
- What if the cause is something else entirely?
- What if everything they've tried was addressing the wrong issue?
- What would change if they saw this differently?

## The Research Interview Questions

### For Product Creator/Expert:

1. Tell me the story of how you discovered this.
2. What was the breakthrough moment?
3. What did you realize that others hadn't?
4. Why does this work when other things don't?
5. If you had to explain this to a 12-year-old, how would you?
6. What's the single most important thing people need to understand?

### For Customers:

1. What did you try before this?
2. Why didn't those things work?
3. What changed when you started using this?
4. How would you explain this to a friend?
5. What surprised you most about the results?
6. What did you learn about your problem that you didn't know before?

### For Support/Sales Team:

1. What questions do customers ask most often?
2. What objections come up repeatedly?
3. What do you say that makes them buy?
4. What's the "aha moment" for customers?
5. What do happy customers say about why it works?

## The "I Didn't Know That" Method

**From David Deutsch:**

Maintain an "I didn't know that" list during research:
- Surprising facts
- Counterintuitive findings
- Little-known truths
- Things that make you stop

**These often become mechanisms** because if YOU didn't know it, prospects probably don't either.

### Questions to Generate Surprises:

- What's true about this that most people don't know?
- What would surprise people to learn?
- What contradicts common belief?
- What's the hidden truth?

## The Root Cause Drill-Down

### The "5 Whys" for Mechanisms:

1. Why do they have this problem? → Surface answer
2. Why does that cause the problem? → Deeper
3. Why does THAT happen? → Deeper still
4. Why is that the case? → Getting to root
5. Why? → Root cause / Mechanism

### Example (Weight Loss):

1. Why can't they lose weight? → "They eat too much"
2. Why do they eat too much? → "They're hungry"
3. Why are they always hungry? → "Their body doesn't feel satisfied"
4. Why doesn't their body feel satisfied? → "Their leptin signals aren't working"
5. Why aren't leptin signals working? → **"Leptin resistance from processed foods"** (MECHANISM)

## The Solution-Mechanism Questions

### How It Works:

- How specifically does this solve the problem?
- What's the process or system?
- What happens step by step?
- What makes this work when others don't?

### Why It's Different:

- How is this approach different from the standard approach?
- What's the key insight or innovation?
- What were they missing before?
- What's the missing piece this provides?

## The Proof Discovery Questions

### Validating the Mechanism:

- What evidence supports this mechanism?
- Are there studies backing this up?
- Who else has validated this approach?
- What's the scientific basis?
- What results demonstrate this works?

### Building the Proof Case:

- What credentials support this?
- What case studies exist?
- What testimonials prove the mechanism?
- What demonstrations can be done?

## The Master Discovery Process

### Step 1: Gather Information
- Interview product creator
- Review all product materials
- Study customer feedback
- Analyze competitors

### Step 2: Ask the Questions
- Work through each category
- Document all answers
- Note surprises and insights

### Step 3: Look for Patterns
- What themes emerge?
- What's repeated?
- What's unique?

### Step 4: Extract Mechanisms
- UMP: Why they've failed
- UMS: Why they'll succeed
- UMD: How they'll get it

### Step 5: Validate
- Test against discovery questions
- Check for proof availability
- Confirm differentiation

## The Formula Template

After discovery, fill in:

> "We help [TARGET MARKET] achieve [DESIRED OUTCOME] through [UNIQUE MECHANISM] / without [COMMON PROBLEM]."

**Example:**
> "We help stressed professionals lose stubborn belly fat through our Cortisol Reset Protocol without restrictive dieting or exhausting workouts."
