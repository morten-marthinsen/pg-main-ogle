## Overview

The E5 Method is a five-stage framework for engineering customer acquisition campaigns from scratch. At the core of every E5 campaign is the Unique Mechanism - the thing that gives prospects hope that this time will be different.

**Key Concept:** Marketing is "an argument that we're presenting rather than a sales pitch."

---

## The Five Stages

### E1: Examine

**Purpose:** Deep analysis of product, market, and competition before creating anything.

**What You Examine:**

| Element | Questions to Answer |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Primary Promise** | What is the one main outcome we deliver? |
| **Unique Mechanism** | What makes our product actually work? |
| **Product USP** | How is our product different from alternatives? |
| **Functional Benefits** | What does the product literally do? |
| **Dimensionalized Benefits** | How do those benefits show up in their life? |
| **Assets** | What do we have to create an irresistible offer? |

**Competitor Analysis:**
- What promises are being made?
- What claims are being used?
- What benefit statements appear?
- What mechanisms are being promoted?
- What positioning statements exist?
- What offers are in the market?

**Why This Matters:** Understanding marketplace sophistication ensures your marketing is viewed as "different, unique, new, and fresh."

---

### E2: Engineer

**Purpose:** Create the core campaign concept - the Big Marketing Idea.

**Key Engineering Elements:**
- Headline formulation
- Story flow development
- Emotional triggers identification
- Proof mechanisms integration
- Messaging architecture
- Offer design
- Logical foundation (Perfect Funnel Thesis)

**The Big Marketing Idea Components:**
1. The promise you're making
2. The mechanism that delivers it
3. The intrigue element that hooks attention

**Minimum Viable Funnel (MVF):**
- Lead capture pages
- Sales pages
- Order forms
- Each element aligned with customer decision-making psychology

---

### E3: Evaluate

**Purpose:** Assess what's working and what's not before scaling.

**Evaluation Focus:**
- Conversion rates at each step
- Drop-off points
- Message-market match
- Mechanism resonance
- Offer strength

**Key Question:** Are we wasting time on the wrong things?

---

### E4: Enhance

**Purpose:** Optimize and improve based on evaluation data.

**Enhancement Areas:**
- Headline refinement
- Mechanism clarification
- Proof strengthening
- Offer improvement
- Story optimization

---

### E5: Expand

**Purpose:** Scale what's working through traffic and reach.

**Expansion Focus:**
- Identify needle-moving traffic approaches
- Scale winning campaigns
- Expand to new traffic sources
- Create campaign variations

## The Three Types of Unique Mechanisms

### Type 1: True Unique Mechanism

**Definition:** You actually have a proprietary, differentiated mechanism.

**Examples:**
- Patented technology
- Proprietary ingredient
- Unique process or methodology
- Special combination or formulation

**How to Identify:**
- What makes your product actually work?
- What do you have that competitors don't?
- What can't be easily copied?

---

### Type 2: Unspoken Mechanism

**Definition:** A component that competitors also have, but nobody is talking about.

**The Claude Hopkins Schlitz Beer Example:**
- All beers used purified water
- Nobody talked about it
- Hopkins made it the centerpiece
- Schlitz went from #8 to #1

**How to Find:**
1. List everything your product does
2. List everything competitors' products do
3. Identify what's common but unspoken
4. Make it your unique mechanism through emphasis

**Key Insight:** "What common component of your product or service is not being talked about by your competitors?"

---

### Type 3: Transubstantiated Mechanism

**Definition:** Turn something ordinary into something extraordinary through naming and framing.

**Famous Examples:**

| Ordinary Thing | Transformed Into | Creator |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Low-price front-end offer | The Tripwire | Ryan Deiss |
| Sales-driven email sequence | 4-Day Cash Machine | Frank Kern |
| Webinar follow-up sequence | Perfect Webinar | Russell Brunson |
| Ascension model | Value Ladder | Russell Brunson |

**How to Transubstantiate:**
1. Identify a process or technique you use
2. Give it a proprietary name
3. Create a framework around it
4. Position it as your unique approach

## Unique Mechanism Workshop Framework

Todd Brown's dedicated workshop on mechanism creation teaches:

### Step 1: Product Deep Dive

**Questions to Answer:**
- What does the product actually do?
- How does it create results?
- What's the active ingredient/component?
- What's the process/methodology?

### Step 2: Market Analysis

**Questions to Answer:**
- What mechanisms are already in the market?
- What's been overused?
- What's fresh vs. stale?
- What gaps exist?

### Step 3: Mechanism Extraction

**Questions to Answer:**
- What's truly unique about how this works?
- What can we name and claim?
- What story supports this mechanism?

### Step 4: Mechanism Naming

**Principles:**
- Make it specific (not vague)
- Make it proprietary (you own it)
- Make it memorable (easy to repeat)
- Make it benefit-laden (if possible)

### Step 5: Mechanism Proof

**Build Evidence:**
- Scientific backing
- Case studies
- Expert endorsements
- Demonstrative proof

## E-Level Classification System

Todd Brown developed a sophistication ladder for mechanisms:

| Level | Description | Market Application |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **E1** | Simple claim, no mechanism | "Lose weight fast" |
| **E2** | Proof-backed claim | "Clinically proven weight loss" |
| **E3** | Mechanism introduced | "Boost metabolism to burn fat" |
| **E4** | New mechanism + paradigm shift | "Lectins punch holes in gut" |
| **E5** | Category creation + worldview | Creates new tribe/identity |

**Matching E-Level to Market:**
- Stage 1-2 markets: E1-E2 sufficient
- Stage 3 markets: E3 minimum requirement
- Stage 4-5 markets: E4-E5 required to break through
