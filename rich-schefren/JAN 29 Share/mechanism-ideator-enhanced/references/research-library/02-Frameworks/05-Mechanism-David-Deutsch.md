# The Mechanism Approach - David Deutsch

> **Creator:** David Deutsch
> **Credentials:** A-List copywriter, Boardroom veteran (6 controls simultaneously), Ogilvy & Mather trained
> **Source:** A-List Copywriting Secrets, Idea Power, davidldeutsch.com

---

## Overview

David Deutsch brings Madison Avenue sophistication to direct response. Trained under David Ogilvy, he developed a "classy hard sell" approach that combines creative big ideas with rigorous mechanism thinking.

**Core Philosophy:** "Copy thinking" vs. copywriting - consciously think through every element rather than going on autopilot.

---

## The Big Idea + Mechanism Connection

### Deutsch's Formula:
> Big Idea = Mechanism + Intrigue Element

A Big Idea isn't just a clever concept - it's a mechanism (the HOW) combined with something that creates curiosity and engagement.

---

## Deutsch's Idea Generation Process

### Step 1: Talk It Out

**Method:** Talk about the product out loud before writing.

- Talk to yourself
- Talk to other people
- Try to sell the product verbally
- Notice what resonates

**Why It Works:** When you talk, you discover what's actually compelling. Writing can mask weak ideas; speaking exposes them.

### Step 2: Find the Problems

**Before writing a single word:**
- What problems are we actually solving?
- What are the pain points?
- What keeps them up at night?

**Key Insight:** Most copywriters jump to benefits. Deutsch starts with problems because problems create emotional engagement.

### Step 3: Generate Volume

**The Secret:** "The simple secret to coming up with lots of good ideas is to come up with lots of bad ones."

- Generate hundreds of ideas
- Don't filter too early
- Use "2nd grade math" - simple combinations and variations
- Let quantity lead to quality

### Step 4: Test Against Templates

**Deutsch's Dozen Presentation Formats:**
1. Straightforward sales pitch
2. Question-based approach
3. Challenge or dare
4. "How to" framework
5. Story-based lead
6. News-style announcement
7. Proclamation/declaration
8. Problem-agitate-solve
9. Testimonial lead
10. Invitation format
11. Warning/alert style
12. Secret/discovery reveal

**Process:** Run your idea through each template format to find the strongest presentation.

## The 7-Question Big Idea Evaluator

Deutsch's tool for quickly determining if an idea is worth pursuing:

| Question | What It Tests |
|----------|--------------|
| 1. Is it new to the prospect? | Novelty |
| 2. Does it connect to a core desire? | Relevance |
| 3. Is there a clear mechanism? | Believability |
| 4. Can it be proven? | Credibility |
| 5. Is it simple enough to grasp quickly? | Clarity |
| 6. Does it differentiate from competitors? | Uniqueness |
| 7. Does it pass the "gut check"? | Instinct |

**If you can't answer YES to most:** Move on to another idea. Don't waste time on "lead balloons."

## Mechanism Requirements in Mature Markets

### The Deutsch Principle:
> "In mature markets (Stage 3+), HOW you do it matters more than WHAT you do."

**Examples:**
- Not just "lose weight" but "lose weight by balancing your hormones"
- Not just "make money" but "make money using this little-known SEC loophole"

### The Mechanism Discovery Process:

1. **State the benefit** (what prospect gets)
2. **Ask: "HOW does this product deliver that benefit?"**
3. **Keep asking "How specifically?" until you get to the unique mechanism**
4. **Name the mechanism**
5. **Combine benefit + mechanism in positioning**
6. **Explain the mechanism in copy**

## Emotional Hot Buttons

Deutsch is famous for uncovering emotional "hot buttons" that make prospects buy.

### The Research Process:

**Look for hidden benefits - hot buttons - when doing research.**

Maintain an **"I didn't know that" list:**
- Surprising facts
- Counterintuitive findings
- Little-known truths
- Things that make you stop and think

**These often become the foundation for mechanisms.**

## The Copy Thinking Approach

### Thinking vs. Writing:

Most copywriters "go on automatic pilot when they start writing." Deutsch advocates conscious thinking at every step.

**The Problem:** "We don't have the background and thinking is hard work."

**The Solution:** Force yourself to think through:
- Why does this mechanism work?
- What makes this believable?
- Why should they care about this?
- What's the emotional hook?

---

## Creating Fresh Angles

### The Approach:

> "A simple technique to make your copy stand out from competitors by giving people reasons to buy that none of your competitors are talking about."

**How to Find Fresh Angles:**
1. Research thoroughly
2. List what competitors emphasize
3. Identify what they're NOT saying
4. Find the unexplored territory
5. Make that your mechanism

## Step-by-Step Mechanism Creation

### Phase 1: Deep Research

1. Interview the product creator
2. Study the product in detail
3. Talk to customers
4. Analyze competitors
5. Look for "I didn't know that" moments

### Phase 2: Problem Identification

1. What problem does this solve?
2. Why haven't they solved it before?
3. What do they blame for their failure?
4. What's the emotional pain?

### Phase 3: Mechanism Extraction

1. HOW does this product work?
2. What's the active ingredient/component/process?
3. What makes it different from alternatives?
4. Can it be named?

### Phase 4: Idea Generation

1. Generate many ideas
2. Run through presentation templates
3. Test against 7-question evaluator
4. Select strongest candidates

### Phase 5: Refinement

1. Talk through the idea
2. Get reactions
3. Refine based on feedback
4. Lock in the Big Idea + Mechanism

## The Parris Lampropolous Connection

Deutsch worked alongside Parris Lampropolous at Boardroom. Key shared principles:

**From Parris via Deutsch:**
- Mechanism + headline + hook = three biggest response drivers
- Master fascinations/bullets - they often reveal the mechanism
- "Make people feel they absolutely had to purchase - otherwise they wouldn't sleep at night"

**The Forgiveness Technique:**
- Always make people feel freed from consequences of past actions
- Let them undo things they regret
- Position that past failure wasn't their fault

*This directly connects to mechanism - the mechanism explains why failure wasn't their fault.*

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Writing without thinking** - Going on autopilot
2. **Filtering ideas too early** - Not generating enough volume
3. **Ignoring emotional hooks** - Focusing only on logic
4. **Using competitor mechanisms** - Not finding fresh angles
5. **Skipping the "talk it out" step** - Writing before speaking
