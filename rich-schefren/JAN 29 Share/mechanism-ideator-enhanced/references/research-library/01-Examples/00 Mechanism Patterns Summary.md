## The 7 Mechanism Archetypes

### Archetype 1: The Preemptive Claim
**Pattern:** Claim what everyone does, but claim it first and most dramatically.

**Examples:**
- Hopkins/Schlitz: "Live steam sterilization" (all brewers did this)
- Hopkins/Pepsodent: "Removes the film" (all toothpaste does this)
- BluBlocker: "Blocks blue light" (first to emphasize this benefit)

**When to Use:**
- Stage 2-3 markets
- Commodity products
- When no one is educating the market

**Key Principle:**
> "The first to explain the process OWNS it."

---

### Archetype 2: The Hidden Enemy
**Pattern:** Name a specific villain causing the problem that prospects didn't know about.

**Examples:**
- Gundry: "Lectins punch holes in your gut"
- Dirty Genes: "Your genes are 'dirty' and causing disease"
- Leptin Resistance: "Hormone blocking your weight loss signals"

**When to Use:**
- Stage 4-5 sophisticated markets
- When prospects have tried multiple solutions
- When you need paradigm shift

**Key Principle:**
> "Name the enemy, explain the attack, reveal the origin."

---

### Archetype 3: The Unlikely Hero
**Pattern:** If someone with a disadvantage can succeed, the method must be powerful.

**Examples:**
- Carlton: "One-Legged Golfer" adds 50 yards
- Carlton: "55 Year-Old Golfer" hits farther than kids

**When to Use:**
- Skill-based products
- When prospects think they're not capable
- To overcome "too hard" objection

**Key Principle:**
> "The impossible success story IS the mechanism."

---

### Archetype 4: The Comparison Story
**Pattern:** Two equal starting points, different outcomes - the variable is your product.

**Examples:**
- Conroy/WSJ: "Two young men" - one becomes president
- Financial comparisons: Same market, different results

**When to Use:**
- Education/information products
- Professional services
- Long-term transformation promises

**Key Principle:**
> "Equal inputs, unequal outputs = you need what made the difference."

---

### Archetype 5: The Insider Secret
**Pattern:** What experts know (and do) that they don't share publicly.

**Examples:**
- Lampropoulos: "How Doctors Stay Well"
- Trudeau: "They Don't Want You to Know"
- Financial: "What Wall Street insiders do"

**When to Use:**
- When authority figures exist in your market
- When there's perceived information asymmetry
- When "hidden knowledge" resonates

**Key Principle:**
> "Access to insider knowledge = competitive advantage."

---

### Archetype 6: The Impending Crisis
**Pattern:** Inevitable negative event + only path to protection.

**Examples:**
- Stansberry: "End of America" - economic collapse
- Survival: Grid-down scenarios
- Financial: Market crash predictions

**When to Use:**
- Fear-based markets (financial, survival, health)
- When external threats are credible
- When urgency is needed

**Key Principle:**
> "Fear of loss + path to safety = action."

---

### Archetype 7: The Scientific Discovery
**Pattern:** New research/finding that changes everything.

**Examples:**
- Metabolic Switch: Fasting research
- Telomere: Anti-aging science
- Muscle Confusion: Exercise variation

**When to Use:**
- Health/supplement markets
- When new research exists
- When scientific credibility is needed

**Key Principle:**
> "New science = new opportunity."

## The 5 Mechanism Components

Every complete mechanism has these components:

### 1. UMP (Unique Mechanism Problem)
**The root cause of failure**

Questions to answer:
- Why have they failed before?
- What's the hidden cause?
- What do they not know?

### 2. UMS (Unique Mechanism Solution)
**How the fix actually works**

Questions to answer:
- What's the specific process?
- How does it address the root cause?
- Why is this different from alternatives?

### 3. UMD (Unique Mechanism Delivery)
**The vehicle for the solution**

Questions to answer:
- How do they access the solution?
- What form does it take?
- What's the experience of using it?

### 4. Visual Metaphors
**Mental pictures that make the mechanism memorable**

Best practices:
- Use concrete, visualizable images
- Connect abstract concepts to familiar objects
- Make it shareable ("boat with holes")

### 5. Psychological Hooks
**Emotional triggers that drive action**

Primary hooks:
- Fear (loss, danger, missing out)
- Curiosity (what's the secret?)
- Hope (this time it will work)
- Self-esteem (it's not your fault)

## The 3 Criteria Test (Georgi)

Every mechanism should pass:

### 1. Simple
Can you explain it in 1-2 sentences?

**Pass:** "Lectins punch holes in your gut lining"
**Fail:** "A multi-pathway approach addressing inflammatory cytokines through synergistic compounds..."

### 2. Memorable
Can prospects remember and share it?

**Pass:** "One-legged golfer discovered the secret"
**Fail:** "Optimized nutritional protocol"

### 3. Fascinating
Does it create a "wow" reaction?

**Pass:** "Doctors do something different for themselves"
**Fail:** "Eat healthy and exercise"

## The 13-Dimension V3 Scorecard

Score each mechanism 1-10 on:

| # | Dimension | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Novelty | How new/unknown to market |
| 2 | Specificity | Level of detail in causation |
| 3 | Scientific Credibility | Research/clinical backing |
| 4 | Visual Clarity | Can they "see" it? |
| 5 | Emotional Resonance | Gut reaction triggered |
| 6 | Enemy Clarity | Named villain + attack |
| 7 | Inevitability | "This WILL happen to you" |
| 8 | Solution Elegance | Simple yet comprehensive |
| 9 | Unique Ownership | Do YOU own this? |
| 10 | Paradigm Shift Power | Flips existing beliefs |
| 11 | False Solution Destruction | Explains why others failed |
| 12 | Market Sophistication Calibration | Matches audience stage |
| 13 | Proof Integration | Evidence for claims |

**Benchmarks:**
- 90+ to compete in mature markets
- 110+ to dominate

## Market Sophistication Calibration

### Stage 1-2 (New/Emerging Markets)
**Mechanism Requirement:** Simple claim with basic explanation
**Examples:** Early health supplements, new technology

### Stage 3 (Growing Markets)
**Mechanism Requirement:** Differentiated mechanism
**Examples:** "Better than X" positioning

### Stage 4 (Mature Markets)
**Mechanism Requirement:** Novel mechanism + paradigm shift
**Examples:** Most health, financial, weight loss markets today

### Stage 5 (Saturated Markets)
**Mechanism Requirement:** Category creation + worldview shift
**Examples:** Lectins, Dirty Genes, End of America

## The Discovery Process

### How Legendary Copywriters Find Mechanisms

**1. Deep Research**
- Interview product creators exhaustively
- Study customer reviews and feedback
- Analyze competitor messaging
- Read scientific literature

**2. Look for the Unusual**
- What's the strangest detail?
- What's the counterintuitive truth?
- What's the unexpected story?

**3. Ask the Right Questions**
- "Why does this actually work?"
- "What makes this different?"
- "Why have other approaches failed?"
- "What would a skeptic need to believe?"

**4. Test Against Criteria**
- Is it simple?
- Is it memorable?
- Is it fascinating?
- Does it pass the V3 scorecard?

## Common Mechanism Failures

### 1. Too Complex
Multi-step mechanisms with qualifiers fail. Keep it to 1-2 sentences.

### 2. Too Generic
"Boost metabolism" isn't a mechanism. "Leptin resistance" is.

### 3. Not Differentiated
If competitors can claim the same thing, it's not a mechanism.

### 4. No Enemy
Especially in Stage 4-5 markets, you need a named villain.

### 5. No Visual
If prospects can't picture it, they can't remember it.

### 6. Not Proven
Claims without proof are just hype.

## Quick Reference: Mechanism Questions

Before finalizing any mechanism, answer:

1. Can I explain this in 1-2 sentences?
2. Will prospects remember and share it?
3. Does it create a "wow" reaction?
4. Is there a named enemy?
5. Does it explain past failure?
6. Is there a clear visual metaphor?
7. Do I own this mechanism?
8. Is it calibrated to market sophistication?
9. Is there proof for every claim?
10. Does it pass the V3 scorecard (90+)?
