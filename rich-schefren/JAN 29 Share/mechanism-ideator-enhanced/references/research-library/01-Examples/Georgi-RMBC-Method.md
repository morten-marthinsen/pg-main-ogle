## Stage 1: Research

### Who Is Your Customer?
Go deep on understanding:
- How old are they typically?
- What attitudes do they have?
- What are their hopes and dreams?
- What are their victories and failures?
- What outside forces have prevented their best life?
- What are their prejudices?

### The Research Process
1. Read customer reviews (positive AND negative)
2. Study competitor copy
3. Analyze forum discussions
4. Review product documentation
5. Interview past customers if possible
6. Study related scientific research

### Research Output
A comprehensive understanding of:
- The avatar's worldview
- Their pain points (stated and unstated)
- Their desires (stated and hidden)
- What they've tried before
- Why they believe things haven't worked

## Stage 2: Mechanism

### Definition
The unique mechanism is the reason why your product/method works, especially when others don't.

**It's NOT the product itself.**
**It's the approach, ingredient, system, or process that makes the offer feel fresh, specific, and valuable.**

### Two Types of Mechanisms

**1. Mechanism of the Problem (UMP)**
The hidden cause - the REAL reason the prospect hasn't succeeded yet.

**Example:** "Leptin resistance is blocking your weight loss signals"

**2. Mechanism of the Solution (UMS)**
HOW your product actually delivers the result.

**Example:** "This compound resets leptin sensitivity"

### Three Criteria for Great Mechanisms

**1. Simple**
> "The best unique mechanisms can be explained to others in 1-2 sentences."

Multi-step, complex mechanisms typically underperform.

**2. Memorable**
> "A good mechanism is like a meme. It's something that the prospect can hear once, easily share with others, and then remember forever."

Test: Can they explain it to a friend?

**3. Fascinating**
> "If your mechanism is digestible and transferable but also boring - prospects won't care. You want them to go, 'wow, I never thought of it that way, that's fascinating!'"

Novelty matters.

---

## Stage 3: Brief

### Purpose
Take your research and your mechanism and organize it into a structured document that will guide your copywriting.

### Brief Components

**1. Avatar Summary**
- Core demographics
- Psychographics
- Emotional state
- Belief systems

**2. Mechanism Documentation**
- The problem mechanism (if applicable)
- The solution mechanism
- How they connect
- Why alternatives fail

**3. Promise Stack**
- Primary promise
- Secondary promises
- Benefit hierarchy

**4. Proof Inventory**
- Scientific studies
- Testimonials
- Expert endorsements
- Demonstration elements

**5. Objection Map**
- Common objections
- Pre-emptive answers
- Countersells needed

### Brief as Foundation
> "By answering all of the questions in the brief, you'll have everything needed to start writing the copy."

---

## Stage 4: Copy

### Why Copy Comes Last
In RMBC, copywriting is the FINAL stage, not the first.

**The Process:**
1. Research discovers the truth
2. Mechanism isolates the angle
3. Brief organizes everything
4. Copy simply translates the brief into persuasive language

### Copy Becomes Easier
> "This is the part where you finally get to write your sales copy, and the process becomes much less daunting thanks to the first three steps."

### What the Copy Does
- Translates the mechanism into compelling narrative
- Structures proof elements effectively
- Creates emotional engagement
- Drives to action

## Mechanism Deep Dive

### The Mechanism Discovery Process

**Step 1: Identify What's Different**
What can you claim that competitors can't (or haven't)?

**Step 2: Articulate the Problem Cause**
What's the hidden reason for the prospect's struggle?

**Step 3: Connect Solution to Cause**
How does your mechanism specifically address the root cause?

**Step 4: Test for Simplicity**
Can you explain it in 1-2 sentences?

**Step 5: Test for Memorability**
Will prospects remember and share it?

**Step 6: Test for Fascination**

## Lessons for Mechanism Creation

### 1. Research Is Not Optional
Skipping research is the #1 reason copy fails. RMBC makes research non-negotiable.

### 2. Mechanism Before Copy
Never write until you've identified the mechanism. Copy without mechanism is commodity copy.

### 3. Simple > Complex
If your mechanism requires a whiteboard and 10 minutes to explain, it's too complex.

### 4. Test the Three Criteria
Simple, Memorable, Fascinating. If it doesn't pass all three, keep searching.

### 5. Brief Before Writing
Organization before execution prevents sloppy, unfocused copy.

## The Three Criteria Applied

### Example: Weight Loss Supplement

**Weak Mechanism:**
"Our supplement helps boost metabolism through a proprietary blend of natural ingredients that work synergistically to support your body's natural fat-burning processes."

**Problems:**
- Not simple (too many qualifiers)
- Not memorable (sounds like everything else)
- Not fascinating (no novelty)

**Strong Mechanism:**
"Leptin resistance - a hormonal malfunction that blocks your fat-burning signals - has been sabotaging your weight loss. This compound resets your leptin sensitivity within 72 hours."

**Strengths:**
- Simple (one concept: leptin resistance)
- Memorable (hormones blocking signals)
- Fascinating (72-hour reset)
