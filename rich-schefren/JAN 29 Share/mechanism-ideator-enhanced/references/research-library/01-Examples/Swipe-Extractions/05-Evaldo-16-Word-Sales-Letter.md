## The Unique Mechanism Structure

### The Core Formula: The 16 Words

> "The secret to converting copy is to define the one belief, then answer these ten questions."

Simplified: **One Belief. Ten Questions.**

### The One Belief Statement

The foundation of every Evaldo promotion follows this template:

> "This [NEW OPPORTUNITY] is the key to [PROSPECT'S DESIRE] and it's only attainable through my [NEW MECHANISM]."

This single sentence contains three critical components:

#### 1. The New Opportunity
- What makes your product/service unique and different
- Your Unique Selling Proposition
- Cannot be something generic like "eating healthy and exercising"
- Must be something the prospect hasn't tried before

#### 2. The Prospect's Desire
- The specific benefit they want
- The outcome they're seeking
- The transformation they crave

#### 3. The New Mechanism
- HOW your product works
- The unique delivery system
- Why YOUR solution (not competitors') can achieve this
- Includes proof and evidence

---

## Example: The One Belief in Action

**For the 16-Word Sales Letter Book:**
> "Following a copy system that provides clarity, purpose, and structure (NEW OPPORTUNITY) is the key to writing converting copy in no time (DESIRE) and it's only attainable through The 16-Word Sales Letter (NEW MECHANISM)."

**For a Financial Promo (Social Security Contract):**
> "This Social Security Contract (NEW OPPORTUNITY) is the key to guaranteed income for life (DESIRE) and it's only attainable through [newsletter/research service] (NEW MECHANISM)."

The genius: Taking something ordinary (a company paying dividends from government contracts) and positioning it as a brand new opportunity.

## The Ten Questions

After establishing the One Belief, answer these questions (in order) to build your sales letter:

### Question 1: How is this different from everything else I've seen?
- Explain your offer's unique value proposition
- Establish why this isn't just another version of what they've tried

### Question 2: What's in it for me?
- Direct benefits to the prospect
- What do THEY get?

### Question 3: How do I know this is real?
- Proof elements
- Evidence
- Credibility

### Question 4: What's holding me back?
- Address objections proactively
- Remove friction points

### Question 5: Who/what is to blame for my problem?
- Identify an enemy
- Externalize the cause of their failure
- This is NOT their fault

### Question 6: Why should I trust you?
- Your credibility
- Your track record
- Your expertise

### Question 7: How does this actually work?
- The mechanism explained
- The process revealed
- The step-by-step

### Question 8: How will this work for ME specifically?
- Overcome the "but my situation is different" objection
- Make it personally relevant
- Show it works for people like them

### Question 9: What do I get?
- The offer details
- The deliverables
- The bonus stack

### Question 10: What's the catch?
- Address the "too good to be true" concern
- Explain the guarantee
- Remove final resistance

## Key Copywriting Principles from Evaldo

### The New Opportunity vs. Improvement
People don't want an IMPROVEMENT on what they've tried before (which reminds them of failure). They want a NEW OPPORTUNITY that makes past failures irrelevant.

### The Enemy Assignment
Always give the prospect someone or something to blame. This:
- Removes their guilt and shame
- Creates emotional engagement
- Positions you as the ally against the enemy

### The "Works for Me" Requirement
Even with all the proof in the world, prospects ask: "But will this work for MY specific situation?" You must answer this directly.

### The Catch Address
Every good offer triggers suspicion. Address "the catch" directly rather than hoping they won't wonder about it.

## Mechanism Analysis Notes

**Why This System Works:**
1. **Structure:** Provides clear framework when staring at blank page
2. **Completeness:** The 10 questions ensure nothing is missed
3. **Focus:** One Belief keeps the message unified
4. **Persuasion Sequence:** Questions follow natural psychological buying process
5. **Differentiation:** New Opportunity positioning avoids comparison to failed attempts

**Transferable Elements:**
- The One Belief statement can focus any offer
- The 10 Questions are a universal checklist
- New Opportunity > Improvement Offer
- Enemy identification creates emotional engagement
- Addressing "the catch" builds trust

## The Mechanism Pattern: Opportunity + Mechanism + Desire

**Structure:**
1. Establish the NEW OPPORTUNITY (never seen before)
2. Connect to the DEEP DESIRE (what they really want)
3. Reveal YOUR MECHANISM (the only way to get it)
4. Answer the 10 Questions in sequence
5. Make the offer irresistible

**Applications:**
- Financial newsletters
- Health VSLs
- Business opportunity offers
- Course/training launches
- Supplement promotions
- Info-product sales
