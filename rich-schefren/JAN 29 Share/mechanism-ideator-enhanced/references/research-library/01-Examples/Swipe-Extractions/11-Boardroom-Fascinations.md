- **Product/Offer:** Bottom Line newsletter subscriptions
- **Company/Publisher:** Boardroom Inc. (now Bottom Line Inc.)
- **Key Copywriters:** Mel Martin, Eugene Schwartz, David Deutsch, Parris Lampropoulos, Eric Betuel, Arthur Johnson
- **Estimated Performance:** $100+ million/year at peak, 1.3 billion pieces of direct mail

---

## The Unique Mechanism Structure

### The Fascination

Mel Martin's signature contribution to copywriting: The fascination is a bullet point that teases an interesting fact without revealing too much.

**Structure:**
> "[CURIOSITY HOOK] - see page [NUMBER]"

**Purpose:**
- Create curiosity gap
- Promise specific value
- Drive desire for the content
- Avoid giving away the answer

---

## The Fascination Formula

### The Pattern
Every fascination contains:
1. **The Tease:** What they'll learn
2. **The Benefit:** Why it matters to them
3. **The Withhold:** What's NOT revealed
4. **The Proof:** The page number (implies real content)

### Examples of Classic Fascinations

**Health:**
> "The common household item that could be causing your back pain... page 47"

**Finance:**
> "The one investment no financial advisor will tell you about... page 23"

**Productivity:**
> "Why working less could make you more successful - and how to do it... page 82"

## The Eugene Schwartz Contribution

### The "Read 300 Magazines" Letter

Schwartz's famous Boardroom letter promised:
> "Read 300 business magazines in 30 minutes"

**The Mechanism:**
- Benefit-driven headline
- Impossible-sounding promise
- Logical explanation (curated summaries)
- Massive time savings

### The Subhead Formula
Follow the main benefit with an explanation:
> "Get the most valuable ideas from magazines... without reading the magazines"

## Key Copywriting Principles from Boardroom

### Fascinations Sell the Content
The fascinations ARE the sales mechanism. Each bullet is a reason to buy.

### Specificity > Generality
"Page 47" beats "Inside you'll discover..."

### Curiosity Gaps Drive Action
Tell them WHAT they'll learn, not the answer itself.

### Accumulation Creates Value
50 fascinations = 50 reasons to buy. Volume matters.

### Emotion Over Information
Sell the FEELING of having the knowledge, not just the knowledge itself.

### The Newsletter Model
Recurring revenue through subscriptions > one-time product sales

## The Parris Lampropoulos Case Study

### The Challenge
Bottom Line Health stuck at 35,000 subscribers

### The Mechanism
Headline: "How Doctors Stay Well While Treating Sick Patients All Day"

**Why It Worked:**
- Universal curiosity (we all want to know what experts do privately)
- Implicit credibility (doctors know health secrets)
- Counter-intuitive (treating sick people without getting sick?)
- Specific profession identification

### The Result
250% better than control, circulation grew to 300,000+

## Mechanism Analysis Notes

**Why Fascinations Dominate Newsletter Sales:**

## The Mechanism Pattern: Fascination Accumulation

**Structure:**
1. **Hook:** Open with strongest fascinations
2. **Accumulate:** Stack dozens of curiosity bullets
3. **Categorize:** Group by topic for scanning
4. **Emotion:** Tie each to emotional benefit
5. **Proof:** Include page numbers/specifics
6. **Offer:** Make access irresistible
7. **Risk Reversal:** Guarantee satisfaction

**Applications:**
- Newsletter subscriptions
- Information products
- Course descriptions
- Book promotions
- Membership sites
- Email subject lines
- Social media hooks
