## The Unique Mechanism Structure

### The RMBC Framework

**R** = Research
**M** = Mechanism
**B** = Brief
**C** = Copy

Each step builds on the previous, creating a systematic approach to copywriting that removes guesswork.

---

## Step 1: Research (R)

### The Foundation
Before writing a single word of copy, understand:
- **The Target Audience:** Who are they? What do they want? What keeps them up at night?
- **Their Pain Points:** What problems do they face?
- **Their Desires:** What transformation do they seek?
- **Their Language:** How do they describe their problems and desires?

### Research Outputs
- Customer avatars
- Pain point inventory
- Desire mapping
- Competitor analysis
- Market awareness level

---

## Step 2: Mechanism (M)

### The Two-Part Unique Mechanism

Stefan Georgi's framework divides the mechanism into two connected parts:

#### UMP: Unique Mechanism Problem
**Why the prospect hasn't succeeded before.**

The UMP explains:
- What's been missing from previous attempts
- Why other solutions haven't worked
- The hidden reason for their failure
- The 1% they were missing that made all the difference

**Example Framing:** "The reason you haven't lost weight isn't lack of willpower - it's that every diet you've tried has ignored [HIDDEN FACTOR]."

#### UMS: Unique Mechanism Solution
**Why the prospect will succeed now.**

The UMS explains:
- How your solution addresses the missing 1%
- Why THIS approach is different
- The unique delivery system
- Why results are now possible

**Example Framing:** "This new protocol targets [HIDDEN FACTOR] directly, which is why people are seeing results even when everything else has failed."

### The Logical Connection
UMP and UMS must be logically connected:
- UMP identifies what was missing
- UMS provides what was missing
- Together they create a complete story

### Making the Mechanism "Unique"
Even commodity products can have unique mechanisms through:
- Reframing the approach
- Highlighting overlooked features
- Naming the process/system
- Revealing the "why" behind the how

---

## Step 3: Brief (B)

### The Bridge Document
The Brief takes all Research and Mechanism insights and organizes them into a structured document that contains everything needed to write the copy.

### Brief Components
- Target audience summary
- Main pain points (prioritized)
- Core desires (prioritized)
- The UMP (clearly stated)
- The UMS (clearly stated)
- Proof elements available
- Offer structure
- Objections to address
- Tone/voice guidelines

### Why the Brief Matters
- Prevents writer's block
- Ensures nothing is missed
- Creates alignment (if working with a team)
- Makes the actual writing much faster

---

## Step 4: Copy (C)

### The Execution
With R, M, and B complete, writing becomes assembly rather than creation:
- Follow the Brief structure
- Let the Mechanism drive the narrative
- Use Research insights for language and proof
- Write faster because decisions are already made

### Copy Outputs
The RMBC method applies to:
- VSLs (Video Sales Letters)
- Sales letters
- Email sequences
- Facebook ads
- Advertorials
- Landing pages
- Product descriptions

## The Schlitz Beer Example

Stefan frequently cites Claude Hopkins' Schlitz Beer campaign to illustrate mechanism:

**The Situation:** All beers used purified water, but nobody talked about it.

**The Mechanism:** Hopkins made the purification process the story:
- Described the deep wells
- Explained the filtration
- Showed the testing process

**The Result:** Schlitz went from #8 to #1 in America.

**The Lesson:** The mechanism doesn't have to be unique to your product - it just has to be the first one YOUR AUDIENCE hears about.

## Key Copywriting Principles from Georgi

### Make the Mechanism Sing
Before you can make your mechanism compelling, you have to explain exactly WHY everything else failed. When the prospect understands the problem mechanism, your solution mechanism "sings."

### Reframing Commodity Products
Any product can have a unique mechanism through reframing. You're not changing the product - you're changing how it's presented.

### The Research Investment
Most copywriters underinvest in research. The RMBC method front-loads research because it makes everything else easier and more effective.

### The Brief as Insurance
A good brief prevents the "blank page" problem and ensures consistency across long-form copy.

## The Mechanism Pattern: Problem Mechanism + Solution Mechanism

**Structure:**
1. Research the audience deeply
2. Identify the UMP (why they've failed)
3. Develop the UMS (why they'll succeed now)
4. Connect UMP → UMS logically
5. Create a Brief with all elements
6. Write copy following the Brief

**Applications:**
- Health/supplement VSLs
- Financial newsletters
- Course/training offers
- E-commerce product descriptions
- Service business proposals
- B2B solutions
