## The Mechanism

### The 16 Words
**"The secret to converting copy is to define the ONE BELIEF, then answer these ten questions."**

This is the meta-mechanism - a mechanism for CREATING mechanisms.

### The One Belief Framework
Before writing a single word, you must define what your reader needs to BELIEVE by the end of your message.

**The One Belief Formula:**
> "This NEW OPPORTUNITY is the key to their DESIRE and it's only attainable through my NEW MECHANISM."

---

## The Three Components of One Belief

### Component 1: New Opportunity
**What makes your product/service UNIQUE and DIFFERENT?**

This is your Unique Selling Proposition - the new way of achieving the desired result.

**Questions to answer:**
- What's different about this approach?
- Why hasn't the prospect seen this before?
- What makes this a "discovery" or "breakthrough"?

### Component 2: Prospect's Desire
**What specific benefit does the prospect want?**

This is the transformation the prospect is seeking.

**Questions to answer:**
- What problem do they desperately want to solve?
- What outcome would change their life?
- What do they lie awake thinking about?

### Component 3: The Mechanism
**Why is this mechanism the ONLY way to achieve the desire?**

This is how the new opportunity actually delivers the result.

**Questions to answer:**
- How does this mechanism work?
- Why do other approaches fail?
- What's the scientific/logical basis?

---

## The Ten Questions

After defining the One Belief, answer these ten questions in your copy:

### Question 1: What's in it for me?
The immediate benefit to the reader.

### Question 2: How will you make my life better?
The transformation promised.

### Question 3: Why should I trust you?
Credentials, proof, authority.

### Question 4: What makes this new and different?
The unique mechanism/opportunity.

### Question 5: How can I be sure this will work for me?
Specific proof for their situation.

### Question 6: What if I've tried everything before?
Address past failures.

### Question 7: How easy is this to implement?
Simplicity and accessibility.

### Question 8: How fast will I see results?
Timeline expectations.

### Question 9: What do I need to do right now?
Clear call to action.

### Question 10: Why should I act now?
Urgency and scarcity.

## The VSL Application

Evaldo applies this framework to Video Sales Letters (VSLs), particularly in the health supplement space.

### VSL Structure Using One Belief

1. **Hook** - Pattern interrupt that connects to the One Belief
2. **Story** - Journey to discovering the new opportunity
3. **Mechanism** - Deep explanation of HOW it works
4. **Proof** - Evidence that the mechanism delivers
5. **Offer** - The vehicle for accessing the mechanism
6. **Close** - Urgency and call to action

### Interview-Style VSL Tips
Evaldo specializes in interview-format VSLs:
- Two people discussing the topic
- One asks questions, one provides answers
- Feels like editorial content, not advertising
- Natural way to address the 10 questions

## Why This Framework Works

### 1. Single Focus
Most copy fails because it tries to say too much. One Belief forces a single, clear message.

### 2. Belief Before Logic
People buy based on belief, then justify with logic. Identify the BELIEF first, then build the logic.

### 3. New Opportunity > Better Way
"New" is more powerful than "better." A new opportunity creates curiosity; a better solution creates comparison.

### 4. Mechanism Creates Believability
Without a mechanism, a promise is just a claim. With a mechanism, it becomes believable.

### 5. Questions Mirror Objections
The 10 questions systematically address every objection the prospect might have.

## Lessons for Mechanism Creation

### 1. Define the One Belief FIRST
Before writing, answer: "What do they need to believe?"

### 2. New Opportunity > Better Solution
Position your offer as a NEW WAY, not a BETTER WAY.

### 3. The Mechanism Must Be Unique
If competitors can claim the same mechanism, you need a different one.

### 4. Questions = Structure
The 10 questions provide a complete outline for any sales message.

### 5. One Belief = One Message
Resist the temptation to add multiple beliefs. Focus creates power.

### 6. Test the Belief
Before writing, ask: "If they believed this ONE thing, would they buy?"
