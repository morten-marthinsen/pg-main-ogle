## The Science Behind Visual Metaphors

### Dual Coding Theory (Paivio, 1973)

| Encoding Type | Recall After 3 Days |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Text only | 10% |
| Text + Image | 65% |

**Mechanism:** Information encoded both verbally AND visually creates stronger memory traces with two retrieval pathways.

### Picture Superiority Effect

Images are stored as:
1. The image itself
2. A verbal label describing the image

Words are stored as:
1. The word only

**Result:** Pictures have 2x the retrieval paths, making them more memorable and retrievable.

### Conceptual Metaphor Theory (Lakoff & Johnson)

- We understand **abstract** concepts through **concrete** experiences
- Mapping goes from familiar (source) to unfamiliar (target)
- Metaphors are not decorative - they structure how we think
- The best source domains are **embodied** (physical experiences we all share)

## Category Summaries

### 1. Body-as-System Metaphors
**File:** `01-Body-As-System-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Give body parts and cells human roles and agency.

**Key Patterns:**
- Cells as workers (factories, maids, guards)
- Organs as departments (control room, shipping)
- Body parts "talking" to each other
- Scale comparisons (tennis court)

**Signature Examples:**
- "Your gut has Secret Service agents standing guard"
- "Gut surface area is the size of a tennis court"
- "Your gut is your second brain"

---

### 2. Warfare Metaphors
**File:** `02-Warfare-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame health as battle between defenders and invaders.

**Key Patterns:**
- Named enemies (invaders, saboteurs)
- Defense forces (soldiers, security)
- Attack mechanisms (breach, flood)
- Victory/defeat framing

**Signature Examples:**
- "Your immune system is at threat level red"
- "Endotoxins pour through like an invading army"
- "The Three-Prong Defense"

---

### 3. Container Metaphors
**File:** `03-Container-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame body systems as containers that can overflow, leak, or clog.

**Key Patterns:**
- Blockage (clogged pipes, dammed rivers)
- Overflow (flooding, bucket overflowing)
- Leaks (holes, breaches)
- Capacity (full tank, empty reservoir)

**Signature Examples:**
- "Your toxin bucket is overflowing"
- "Like a boat with holes - bailing vs patching"
- "Opening the floodgates of inflammation"

---

### 4. Journey Metaphors
**File:** `04-Journey-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame health transformation as a journey with paths, obstacles, and destinations.

**Key Patterns:**
- Path choices (fork in road)
- Obstacles revealed
- Guide/companion
- Destination visualization

**Signature Examples:**
- "You're at a crossroads"
- "Every obstacle has a hidden detour"
- "Your destination: the new you"

---

### 5. Mechanical Metaphors
**File:** `05-Mechanical-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame body as machine with engines, switches, and components.

**Key Patterns:**
- Engine (fuel, running, breakdown)
- Switches (on/off, toggle)
- Thermostat (set point)
- Gears (synchronized, grinding)

**Signature Examples:**
- "Your metabolic engine is running on fumes"
- "This flips your fat-burning switch to ON"
- "Your thermostat is set wrong"

---

### 6. Natural Metaphors
**File:** `06-Natural-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame health through natural cycles, growth, and renewal.

**Key Patterns:**
- Seasons (spring renewal, winter dormancy)
- Garden (soil, seeds, weeds)
- Trees (roots, branches, pruning)
- Weather (storms, sunshine)

**Signature Examples:**
- "You've been stuck in metabolic winter"
- "Time to plant your victory garden"
- "Prune the dead branches"

---

### 7. Building/Architecture Metaphors
**File:** `07-Building-Architecture-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame body as building with foundation, structure, and integrity.

**Key Patterns:**
- Foundation (solid, cracked)
- Load-bearing (walls, supports)
- Construction (materials, building)
- Collapse (structural failure)

**Signature Examples:**
- "Gut health is the foundation of overall health"
- "You're building with substandard materials"
- "The wall has been breached"

---

### 8. Financial Metaphors
**File:** `08-Financial-Metaphors.md`

**Core Concept:** Frame health as investment with returns, debt, and compound effects.

**Key Patterns:**
- Investment (ROI, deposits)
- Debt (accumulation, interest)
- Compound effects (growth, damage)
- Budget (withdrawals, balance)

**Signature Examples:**
- "Health compounds like interest"
- "You've overdrawn your sleep account"
- "The ROI on gut health is enormous"

## The Metaphor Construction Process

### Step 1: Identify Target Concept
What abstract thing needs visual explanation?

### Step 2: Select Source Category
Which metaphor family best fits?

### Step 3: Map Elements
Create 1:1 correspondences between source and target

### Step 4: Apply Pattern
Use a proven construction template

### Step 5: Test Effectiveness
- Can they see it? (Visual Clarity)
- Do they know the source? (Familiarity)
- Is it simple? (1:1 mapping)
- Does it create emotion? (Resonance)
- Would they share it? (Virality)

**Full Process:** See `09-Metaphor-Construction-Guide.md`

## The "Can You See It?" Quick Test

For every metaphor, ask:

| Test | Pass | Fail |
|------|------|------|
| Can I close my eyes and see it? | "Toxins flooding through holes" | "Increased permeability" |
| Could a 12-year-old understand? | "Weeds choking your garden" | "Microbial dysbiosis" |
| Would someone share this? | "Your gut is a tennis court" | "Your gut is important" |
| Does it create emotion? | "You're in a sinking boat" | "You have a health issue" |

## Metaphor Effectiveness Scorecard

Rate each metaphor 1-10:

| Dimension | Weight | Question |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Visual Clarity | 25% | Can they picture it? |
| Familiarity | 20% | Do they know the source? |
| Simplicity | 20% | Is mapping obvious? |
| Emotional Impact | 20% | Does it make them feel? |
| Shareability | 15% | Would they tell someone? |

**Score 70+ to pass**

## Cross-Category Combinations

Powerful mechanisms often combine metaphor systems:

**Garden + War:**
"Once you've won the war against gut inflammation, it's time to plant your victory garden."

**Building + Container:**
"The wall has been breached. Toxins are flooding through the gaps."

**Mechanical + Financial:**
"Your metabolic engine is burning fuel inefficiently - low ROI on every calorie."

**Natural + Journey:**
"You've been stuck in winter, but the path to spring is opening."
