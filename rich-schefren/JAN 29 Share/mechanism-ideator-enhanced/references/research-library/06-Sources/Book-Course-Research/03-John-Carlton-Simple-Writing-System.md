# John Carlton - Simple Writing System (SWS)

## Source Overview
- **Author:** John Carlton
- **Key Works:** Simple Writing System (SWS), Kick Ass Copywriting Secrets
- **Status:** Known as "the most ripped-off writer on the Web" and "the most respected copywriting teacher alive"
- **Relevance to Mechanisms:** 17-step process, One-Two Punch selling, Hook discovery, USP positioning

## The Simple Writing System Overview

The SWS is John Carlton's exact 17-point checklist developed over 25+ years of writing control-crushing sales letters responsible for millions in sales.

> "The Simple Writing System really is all it's cracked up to be. It's the exact 17-point checklist John Carlton developed over his 25 years of writing control-crushing sales letters."

**Core Value:** It removes all guesswork and the need to "get in the right head" from writing killer copy.

## The One-Two Punch of Selling Effectively

One of Carlton's signature concepts, from Step 12 of the SWS:

> "People always have two separate sets of reasons for buying almost anything:
> 1. The RATIONAL reasons to explain why he bought... to his dubious spouse, nosy friends, etc.
> 2. The EMOTIONAL reasons that actually drive the purchase decision."

### Application to Mechanisms

| Punch | Function | Mechanism Role |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Emotional (First Punch)** | Triggers the want | Mechanism creates hope, excitement, belief |
| **Rational (Second Punch)** | Justifies the purchase | Mechanism provides logical explanation |

**Key Insight:** The mechanism must work on BOTH levels:
- Emotionally: Creates new hope where disappointment existed before
- Rationally: Explains scientifically/logically why it works

Carlton describes bullets as "the one-two punch of emotionally-satisfying practical use."

## The "Greased Slide" Concept

Similar to Sugarman's "Slippery Slide," Carlton uses the "Greased Slide" to describe how copy should flow.

**The Goal:** Get prospects out of their "Somnambulant Blob" state (walking around in a daze).

**For Mechanisms:**
- The mechanism explanation must maintain momentum
- Don't let the reader "get off" during technical explanations
- Each sentence about the mechanism should compel reading the next

## Features vs. Benefits in Mechanisms

Carlton's distinction is crucial for mechanism presentation:

| Element | Definition | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Feature** | The physicalness or statistics | "Contains XYZ enzyme" |
| **Benefit** | What it does for someone | "Dissolves stubborn fat" |
| **Bullet** | Feature + Benefit combined | "The XYZ enzyme that dissolves stubborn fat while you sleep" |

**Application:** Every mechanism feature must be translated to a benefit. Features are the mechanism's components; benefits are what those components DO for the prospect.

## Fishing for Hooks

Carlton's approach to hooks is directly relevant to mechanism creation.

> "You gotta dig to find it. Only the best writers ever find it."

### What Makes a Great Hook (for Mechanisms):
- Something unexpected or counterintuitive
- A specific, memorable element
- What makes THIS mechanism different
- The "aha moment" in the mechanism's story

**Key Insight:** The hook is often buried in the mechanism itself--something unique about how or why it works that nobody else is talking about.

## Carlton's USP Approach

Carlton's take on USP differs from traditional definitions:

> "Carlton's USP is not about unique selling proposition in the traditional sense. It's about uniquely positioning yourself in your market to sell."

### Three Elements:
1. Know what the competition is doing
2. Uniquely fit into that landscape
3. Ensure it rings true that you fill a gap

**For Mechanisms:**
- Your mechanism IS part of your USP
- Position it against existing mechanisms in the market
- Find the gap in mechanism positioning and fill it

> "Being a clear, concise writer can actually be a USP within your market--for you to be the one guy who can clearly say what everyone else is mumbling about."

## Carlton's Writing Philosophy for Mechanisms

### 1. Write by Hand First
> "Always write by hand first, as it allows for retention and editing history."

Apply to mechanisms: First drafts of mechanism explanations should be handwritten to think through the logic.

### 2. Observe with Five Senses
> "Observe your five senses one by one to write with a clear head."

Apply to mechanisms: Make mechanism descriptions sensory and tangible, not abstract.

### 3. Constant State of Awareness
> "Be in a constant state of awareness, as if your life is a movie."

Apply to mechanisms: Notice mechanisms everywhere--how things work, why they work, what makes them interesting.

### 4. Clear Over Clever
Write mechanism descriptions so simply that anyone could understand them. Clarity always beats cleverness.

## Mechanism Application Framework (Based on Carlton)

### Step 1: Dig for the Hook
- What's the unexpected element in how this works?
- What's the story behind the discovery?
- What makes it different from everything else?

### Step 2: Extract Features and Benefits
- List every component of the mechanism (features)
- Translate each to a prospect benefit
- Combine into powerful bullets

### Step 3: Apply the One-Two Punch
- Emotional appeal: How does it make them feel?
- Rational justification: Why does it make sense?
- Both must be present in mechanism copy

### Step 4: Position as USP
- How does this mechanism fit in the competitive landscape?
- What gap does it fill?
- Why is it uniquely qualified to solve the problem?

### Step 5: Grease the Slide
- Ensure mechanism explanation maintains momentum
- No friction points or confusion
- Each sentence pulls reader to the next
