## Magnetic Marketing System

Founded in 1970, Magnetic Marketing represents a fundamental shift in customer acquisition.

### The Core Concept
> "Instead of pushing your pitch to your audience, Magnetic Marketing does the opposite--you're pulling your prospects toward your business. By offering something of value, you're reeling the audience in."

**For Mechanisms:**
- The mechanism becomes part of the "magnet"
- Valuable mechanism education attracts qualified prospects
- Lead with mechanism revelation, not product pitch

### The Attraction vs. Push Model

| Traditional (Push) | Magnetic (Pull) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Pitch product benefits | Offer valuable education |
| Interrupt and sell | Attract and engage |
| Transaction focus | Relationship focus |
| Fight for attention | Earn attention |

**Mechanism Application:**
- Share mechanism insights as valuable content
- Let mechanism education do the selling
- Position mechanism knowledge as the "magnet"

## The Ultimate Sales Letter - 28 Steps

Kennedy's sales letter methodology provides a framework for mechanism presentation.

### Key Formulas

**AIDA:**
- Attention
- Interest
- Desire
- Action

**Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS):**
> "People are more likely to act to avoid pain than to get gain."

**PAS Applied to Mechanisms:**
1. **Problem:** Define their problem clearly
2. **Agitate:** Inject emotion, make it worse, explain why previous solutions failed
3. **Solution:** Unveil the mechanism that finally solves it

## The 10 Smart Market Research Questions

Kennedy's research framework directly informs mechanism development:

1. What keeps customers awake at night?
2. What are they afraid of?
3. What are they angry about? Who are they angry at?
4. What are their top three daily frustrations?
5. What trends are occurring in their businesses or lives?
6. What do they secretly desire most?
7. Is there a built-in bias to how they make decisions?
8. Do they have their own language/jargon?
9. Who else is selling something similar to them?
10. Who has tried selling them something similar and failed?

**For Mechanism Research:**
Questions 7-10 are especially critical:
- Their decision-making bias shapes mechanism presentation
- Their language shapes mechanism naming
- Competitor mechanisms inform positioning
- Failed attempts inform why THIS mechanism is different

## Features to Benefits Translation

Kennedy's approach to features and benefits is essential for mechanism presentation.

### The Process:
1. Build a list of every separate feature of your product/mechanism
2. Translate those features to benefits
3. Identify a "hidden benefit" to use

**Mechanism Application:**
- List every component of how the mechanism works (features)
- Translate each to "what this means for you" (benefits)
- Find the hidden benefit--the unexpected positive consequence

### Example:
- **Mechanism Feature:** Uses proprietary enzyme
- **Obvious Benefit:** Dissolves fat faster
- **Hidden Benefit:** No more embarrassing moments at the beach

## Addressing Objections Directly

Kennedy recommends directly addressing product limitations:

## The Rewriting Process

Kennedy's revision approach ensures mechanism copy is optimized:

1. **First Draft:** General structure of mechanism explanation
2. **Second Pass:** Add strategy (positioning, comparison)
3. **Third Pass:** Add style (voice, personality)
4. **Fourth Pass:** Add passion (emotional connection)
5. **Fifth Pass:** Prune copy (remove unnecessary words)

## 10 Rules for Direct Marketing

Kennedy's rules inform mechanism-based marketing:

1. There will always be an offer
2. There will be reason to respond right now
3. You will give clear instructions
4. There will be tracking and measurement
5. Only no-cost brand-building
6. There will be follow-up
7. There will be strong copy
8. It will look like mail-order advertising
9. Results rule
10. You will be a tough-minded disciplinarian

**For Mechanism Marketing:**
- Mechanism must connect to a clear offer
- Urgency around mechanism access
- Clear next steps after mechanism education
- Track mechanism-related conversions
- Follow up with mechanism adopters
