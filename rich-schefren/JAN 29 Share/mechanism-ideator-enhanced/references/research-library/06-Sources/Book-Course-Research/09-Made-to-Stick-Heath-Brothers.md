# Made to Stick - Chip & Dan Heath

## Source Overview
- **Authors:** Chip Heath (Stanford GSB) and Dan Heath (former Harvard researcher)
- **Published:** 2007
- **Status:** NY Times/WSJ bestseller; named one of the best 100 business books of all time
- **Relevance to Mechanisms:** SUCCESs framework for making ideas stick--directly applicable to mechanism design

---

## Core Question

> "Why do some ideas succeed while others die?"

The Heath brothers studied what makes ideas memorable, persuasive, and actionable. Their findings provide a framework for making mechanisms "sticky."

---

## The SUCCESs Framework

Six principles that make ideas stick:

| Letter | Principle | Mechanism Application |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| S | Simple | Name and explain mechanism simply |
| U | Unexpected | Include surprising element |
| C | Concrete | Make mechanism tangible and sensory |
| C | Credible | Provide proof mechanism works |
| E | Emotional | Make them care about mechanism |
| S | Stories | Wrap mechanism in narrative |

---

## Principle 1: SIMPLE

> "Simplicity is not just about using fewer words, shorter phrases or sound bites. It is about finding the essential core of our ideas and sharing it in a form of a compact idea."

### For Mechanisms:

**Find the Core:**
- What's the ONE essential thing about how this works?
- Strip away complexity to find the memorable essence

**Compact Communication:**
- Mechanism name should be immediately understandable
- Core concept in one sentence or less
- Proverb-like simplicity

**Example Application:**
- Bad: "Our proprietary 12-phase enzymatic degradation system"
- Better: "The Fat Flush Formula"

---

## Principle 2: UNEXPECTED

> "There are 2 parts to this principle: Get Attention through the emotion of Surprise, and Keep Attention through the emotion of Interest."

### For Mechanisms:

**Break the Pattern:**
- What's surprising about how this mechanism works?
- What assumption does it challenge?
- What would make them say "wait, what?"

**Create Curiosity Gaps:**
- Reveal enough to intrigue
- Hold back enough to compel continued attention
- "Seeds of curiosity" throughout mechanism explanation

**Two-Step Process:**
1. Get attention with unexpected element
2. Keep attention with sustained interest in "how"

---

## Principle 3: CONCRETE

> "Psychologically sticky ideas are tangible, real and appeal to our senses. So don't sell the soft hands, sell the hand cream."

### For Mechanisms:

**Tangible > Abstract:**
- Make mechanism components you can "see" and "touch"
- Specific details beat vague descriptions
- Sensory language throughout

**Heath Brothers' Rule:**
> "An attribute--hand cream--is concrete, a benefit--soft hands--is abstract."

**For Mechanism Copy:**
- Describe the physical process
- Use sensory language
- Make it visual in their mind

**Example Application:**
- Abstract: "Enhances cellular metabolism"
- Concrete: "Like a tiny furnace burning through fat cells"

---

## Principle 4: CREDIBLE

> "Psychologically sticky ideas are believable--they have both 'internal credibility' (they make sense, and are not self-contradictory) and 'external credibility' (they have proof or authority to support them)."

### Two Types of Credibility:

**Internal Credibility:**
- The mechanism makes logical sense
- It's not self-contradictory
- The "how" follows from the "why"

**External Credibility:**
- Authority (experts, institutions)
- Anti-authority (regular people like them)
- Data and specifics
- Testable claims

### For Mechanisms:

- Ensure internal logic holds up
- Provide external proof (studies, experts, testimonials)
- Include testable elements ("try this yourself")
- Use specific numbers, not round ones

---

## Principle 5: EMOTIONAL

> "For people to take action, they first have to care."

### Techniques to Make People Care:

1. **Focus on Individual (Not Masses)**
   - "Mother Teresa Principle": "If I look at the mass I will never act. If I look at the one, I will."
   - Make mechanism about ONE person's transformation

2. **Use Associations**
   - Connect mechanism to things they already care about
   - Leverage existing emotional connections

3. **Appeal to Self-Interest**
   - "What's in it for me?"
   - Personal benefit of mechanism adoption

4. **Appeal to Identity**
   - Who do they want to be?
   - How does this mechanism fit that identity?

### For Mechanisms:

- Tell the story of one person transformed
- Connect to deep desires and fears
- Make it personally relevant
- Tie to their identity aspirations

---

## Principle 6: STORIES

> "A credible idea makes people believe. An emotional idea makes people care. And the right stories make people act."

### Types of Stories:

**Challenge Plot:** Underdog story, overcoming obstacles
**Connection Plot:** Building relationships, crossing gaps
**Creativity Plot:** Innovation, breakthrough discovery

### For Mechanisms:

**Origin Story:**
- How was the mechanism discovered?
- What problem was the creator trying to solve?
- What breakthrough moment occurred?

**Transformation Story:**
- Who used the mechanism?
- What was their "before"?
- What's their "after"?

**Your Story (for buyers):**
- Where are they now?
- Where will they be after using mechanism?
- What's the journey?

---

## The Curse of Knowledge

Critical concept for mechanism communication:

> "Once we know something, we find it hard to imagine what it was like not to know it. Our knowledge has 'cursed' us. And it becomes difficult for us to share our knowledge with others, because we can't readily re-create our listeners' state of mind."

### For Mechanism Creators:

- You understand your mechanism deeply
- Your prospect doesn't
- You must bridge that gap
- Test your explanation with naive audiences
- Simplify more than feels necessary

**The Challenge:** Making technical mechanisms accessible without dumbing them down.

## SUCCESs Framework for Mechanism Development

### Step 1: SIMPLE
- What's the core of how this works?
- Can you explain it in one sentence?
- What's the memorable name?

### Step 2: UNEXPECTED
- What's surprising about this mechanism?
- What assumption does it challenge?
- What creates curiosity?

### Step 3: CONCRETE
- How can you make this tangible?
- What sensory language applies?
- What metaphor makes it visual?

### Step 4: CREDIBLE
- Does the logic hold up?
- What external proof exists?
- What can they test themselves?

### Step 5: EMOTIONAL
- Who does this help (make it personal)?
- What do they deeply want?
- How does this connect to identity?

### Step 6: STORIES
- What's the discovery story?
- What's the transformation story?
- What's their potential story?

---

## Application Checklist

Before launching a mechanism, test against SUCCESs:

- [ ] **Simple:** Can anyone explain it in one sentence?
- [ ] **Unexpected:** Does it contain a surprise or pattern break?
- [ ] **Concrete:** Can they picture it? Is it sensory?
- [ ] **Credible:** Is there both internal logic and external proof?
- [ ] **Emotional:** Will they care? Is it personal?
- [ ] **Stories:** Is there narrative around the mechanism?

If any element is weak, the mechanism is less "sticky."
