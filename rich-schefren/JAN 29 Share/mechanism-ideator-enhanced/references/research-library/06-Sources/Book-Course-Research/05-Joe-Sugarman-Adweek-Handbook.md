# Joe Sugarman - The Adweek Copywriting Handbook

## Source Overview
- **Author:** Joseph Sugarman
- **Key Works:** The Adweek Copywriting Handbook (originally "Advertising Secrets of the Written Word"), Triggers
- **Status:** One of the most well-known copywriters of all time; once sold a $240,000 airplane in a single mail-order ad
- **Relevance to Mechanisms:** Psychological triggers, slippery slide technique, seeds of curiosity, axioms of copywriting

---

## The Slippery Slide Concept

Sugarman's most famous concept for keeping readers engaged.

### The Analogy
> "Picture your entire piece of copy as a giant slide. As soon as your readers get to the top of your slide (your headline), you want them to slide down (from first sentence to last) as fast as possible. How do you achieve this? Make your slide SLIPPERY."

### Application to Mechanism Copy

**The Challenge:** Mechanism explanations can create friction--they're often technical, detailed, and potentially boring.

**The Solution:** Make mechanism explanations slippery:
- Each sentence creates curiosity for the next
- Technical details wrapped in intrigue
- No stopping points or confusion

### Key Principles for Slippery Mechanism Copy:

1. **Headline's Only Job:** Get them to read the first sentence about the mechanism

2. **First Sentence's Job:** Get them to read the second sentence

3. **Every Element Serves One Purpose:** Pull them deeper into the mechanism story

4. **Use "Seeds of Curiosity":** Plant questions they need answered to keep reading

## The 17 Axioms of Copywriting

Sugarman's axioms provide a framework for effective mechanism presentation:

### Axiom 2 (Most Relevant)
> "All the elements in an advertisement are primarily designed to do one thing and one thing only: get you to read the first sentence of the copy."

**For Mechanisms:** The mechanism name, visual, headline--all exist to get them reading the explanation.

### Axiom on Emotion vs. Logic
> "Sell on emotion, but a purchase is justified with logic."

**For Mechanisms:**
- The PROMISE triggers emotional desire
- The MECHANISM provides logical justification
- Both are necessary for conversion

## Psychological Triggers as Mechanism Foundations

Sugarman identified 31 psychological triggers that convince people to buy. These can be applied to mechanism design and presentation.

### The Most Important Trigger: HONESTY

> "When Sugarman ran his mail order catalog, the reason people trusted his opinions is because he would be the first to point out the negative aspects of a product."

**Formula:** Honesty + Integrity = Credibility

**For Mechanisms:**
- Don't oversell the mechanism
- Acknowledge limitations
- Be first to address potential objections
- Honesty about what the mechanism CAN'T do builds trust

### Key Triggers Applied to Mechanisms:

| Trigger | Application to Mechanisms |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Proof of Value** | Compare your mechanism to competing mechanisms |
| **Desire to Belong** | Position mechanism as what "smart people" use |
| **Curiosity** | Hold back part of how it works to create demand |
| **Sense of Urgency** | Limited availability of mechanism access |
| **Instant Gratification** | How quickly the mechanism produces results |
| **Exclusivity** | "Proprietary" or "patented" mechanism |
| **Simplicity** | Keep mechanism explanation simple |
| **Greed** | Mechanism delivers more than expected |
| **Resolving Objections** | Address mechanism doubts preemptively |
| **Involvement/Ownership** | Let them experience the mechanism |

## Proof of Value Through Mechanism Comparison

> "A great way of showing Proof of Value is by comparing your products to other competing products. The consumer is smart enough to know there are other competitors out there, and it's your job to bring that up and show them YOUR product is the best value."

**For Mechanism Copy:**
- Don't pretend other mechanisms don't exist
- Directly compare your mechanism to alternatives
- Show why your mechanism is superior
- This builds credibility and trust

---

## Desire to Belong

> "People buy a specific product because they subconsciously want to belong to the group that already owns or uses that product."

**For Mechanism Positioning:**
- Who else uses/has used this mechanism?
- What group would they join by adopting it?
- Create identity around mechanism adoption

---

## Curiosity and Seeds of Curiosity

> "The key to curiosity is holding back part of a story in order to make people curious and create demand."

**Application to Mechanism Revelation:**
- Don't reveal everything immediately
- Create a "curiosity gap" in the mechanism story
- Promise revelation of "how it really works"
- Use phrases like "What I discovered next changed everything..."

---

## Involvement and Ownership

> "When you walk by a kiosk in a mall where they try to sell you a product, they will get you to hold the product, use the product, and talk about the product with you at length."

**For Mechanisms:**
- Let prospects experience the mechanism (demos, samples)
- Create mental ownership of the mechanism's benefits
- Walk them through using the mechanism in their mind

---

## Resolving Objections Preemptively

> "By presenting the negatives upfront, he reduced and often eliminated a major objection to a sale. You can't just resolve an objection without first raising it."

**For Mechanism Copy:**
- Anticipate mechanism skepticism
- Raise objections yourself
- Address them with honesty
- This defuses objections before they become deal-breakers

## Mechanism Presentation Framework (Based on Sugarman)

### Step 1: Create the Slippery Slide
- Headline that creates curiosity about mechanism
- First sentence that demands reading second
- Each element pulls reader deeper

### Step 2: Apply Psychological Triggers
- Lead with honesty and credibility
- Create curiosity gaps
- Offer proof of value through comparison
- Make mechanism feel exclusive

### Step 3: Sell Emotion, Justify with Logic
- Promise triggers emotional desire
- Mechanism provides rational justification
- Address both in mechanism copy

### Step 4: Seeds of Curiosity
- Plant questions about the mechanism
- Promise revelation
- Hold back key details strategically

### Step 5: Preemptive Objection Handling
- Raise mechanism skepticism yourself
- Address it honestly
- Turn objection into selling point
