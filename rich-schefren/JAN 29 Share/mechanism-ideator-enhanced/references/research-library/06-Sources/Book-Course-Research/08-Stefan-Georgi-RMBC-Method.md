## What is RMBC?

RMBC stands for:
- **R** - Research
- **M** - Mechanism
- **B** - Brief
- **C** - Copy

### The Four Pillars Explained:

**1. RESEARCH**
The foundation of high-converting copy.

> "By doing Research, you gain a strong understanding of your prospect, the language they use, their pain points, and what solutions they've tried in the past."

**Key Research Elements:**
- Prospect psychology and language
- Pain points and desires
- Previous solutions attempted
- Market and competitive landscape

**2. MECHANISM**
The central differentiator.

> "With the Mechanism, you're able to explain to the prospect why they failed to get results in the past, and how they can succeed in the future."

**Two Functions of Mechanism:**
1. Explains past failure (why old approaches didn't work)
2. Creates hope for future success (why THIS will work)

**3. BRIEF**
The blueprint for your copy.

> "With the Brief, you end up writing several pieces of the sales letter, but in bite-sized chunks."

> "The Brief's value isn't just in the questions it asks. Its value is in how you answer those questions. If you take the time to write little chunks of copy when filling out your brief, you'll be in an amazing position when it comes time to create your actual sales letter."

**4. COPY**
The actual sales argument.

> "With Copy, you have a follow-along template for producing high-converting sales letters again-and-again."

Uses proven templates and modular blocks for maximum persuasion.

## The Mechanism Deep Dive

Stefan is famous for his teaching on the "Unique Mechanism."

### Core Function:
> "The first way you differentiate your solution is by opening their eyes to a new paradigm--one that explains the truth behind their struggles. And the second way is by showing them how your product/service embraces this new paradigm. That's the Unique Mechanism in a nutshell."

### Two-Part Structure:

**Part 1: The New Paradigm**
- Reveals why they've failed before
- Introduces new understanding of the problem
- Creates "aha moment" about root cause

**Part 2: Your Solution's Embrace**
- Shows how your product operates within this paradigm
- Demonstrates why it works when others failed
- Creates logical bridge to purchase

### Mechanism as Differentiator:
> "This module clarifies how to differentiate even commodity products."

Even if your product is similar to competitors, the unique mechanism allows differentiation through HOW it works, not WHAT it does.

## The Research Phase for Mechanisms

Before developing the mechanism, thorough research is required:

### Key Research Areas:

1. **Prospect Understanding**
   - Their language
   - Their pain points
   - Solutions they've tried
   - Why those failed (in their mind)

2. **Market Analysis**
   - What competitors are saying
   - What mechanisms already exist
   - What claims have been made

3. **Product Knowledge**
   - How the product actually works
   - What's unique about the process
   - Scientific/technical elements that can be featured

## The Brief for Mechanism Integration

The Brief serves as the blueprint that integrates the mechanism into the larger sales argument.

### Brief Components (Relevant to Mechanism):

- Key mechanism elements
- How mechanism addresses objections
- Where mechanism fits in narrative arc
- Proof points for mechanism claims

> "From the Research and Brief materials, a copywriter can assemble pretty much any type of copy needed for a launch: Emails, VSL, TSL, Webinar Script, Ad copy, Landing Page, Thank you page, etc."

The mechanism developed in R-M-B phases can be adapted across all marketing assets.

## Mechanism Development Framework (Based on Stefan Georgi)

### Step 1: Deep Research
- Understand prospect psychology
- Learn their language
- Identify past solutions and why they failed
- Study competitive mechanisms

### Step 2: Identify the New Paradigm
- What truth explains their past failures?
- What don't they currently understand?
- What "aha moment" can you create?

### Step 3: Connect Your Solution
- How does your product embrace this paradigm?
- Why does it work where others failed?
- What makes the mechanism unique?

### Step 4: Document in Brief
- Write mechanism explanation in chunks
- Address objections
- Plan proof points
- Determine narrative placement

### Step 5: Execute Across Assets
- Adapt mechanism to VSL
- Adapt to emails
- Adapt to ads
- Maintain consistency

---

## Key Principles from Stefan's Teaching

### Mechanism Explains Failure AND Creates Hope
The mechanism does double duty:
- Past: "This is why nothing worked before"
- Future: "This is why it will work now"

### Research Precedes Mechanism
You can't create an effective mechanism without understanding:
- What they've tried
- Why they believe it failed
- What they think could work

### Brief Makes Copy Easier
By answering mechanism questions in the brief, the actual copywriting becomes assembly rather than creation.

### Consistency Across Assets
The same mechanism powers all marketing. Format changes; core message doesn't.
