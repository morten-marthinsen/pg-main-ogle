# Mechanical Metaphors

> **Purpose:** Document engine, gear, switch, thermostat, and machine metaphors used to explain body function, metabolism, and intervention mechanisms.

---

## Historical Context

The body-as-machine metaphor dates to Rene Descartes (17th century), who compared healthy bodies to "well-made clocks." This metaphor has evolved through industrialization:
- 1800s: Steam engine
- 1920s: Automobile needing tune-ups
- 1960s: Computer/switchboard
- Today: Software/operating system

### Why Mechanical Metaphors Work

1. **Cause-effect clarity** - Machines have clear inputs and outputs
2. **Repair framing** - Machines can be fixed
3. **Component thinking** - Problems are isolated to parts
4. **Intervention logic** - Clear action steps
5. **Familiarity** - Everyone understands machines

### Limitations to Consider

- Oversimplifies biological complexity
- May imply passive patient (machine being worked on)
- Can reduce holistic thinking
- May miss evolutionary/adaptive aspects

## Category 6: Plumbing Metaphors

### The "Water Tower" Pattern (Cardiovascular)

From academic literature:
> "To better explain cardiovascular dynamics, an analogy can be drawn to a city water supply. The heart is the city pump. The aorta is the water tower. Arteries are parallel distribution pipes. Arterioles are faucets."

| Cardiovascular | Plumbing | Function |
|----------------|----------|----------|
| **Heart** | "Pump" | Moves fluid |
| **Aorta** | "Water tower" | Maintains pressure |
| **Arteries** | "Distribution pipes" | Deliver supply |
| **Arterioles** | "Faucets" | Control flow rate |
| **Capillaries** | "Sprinkler heads" | Final delivery |

### The "Clogged Pipes" Pattern

> "Your arteries are like the plumbing in your house. Over decades, mineral deposits build up. Water flow slows. Pressure drops at the faucets. Eventually... a clog. The same thing happens with arterial plaque."
