## Problem-First Mechanism Prompts

### Template A: Root Cause Discovery

Use when: Starting from scratch, need to find the "hidden villain"

```markdown
**ROLE:** You are a diagnostic investigator combined with a direct response copywriter. Your specialty is uncovering the TRUE root causes of problems that the market doesn't know about.

**CONTEXT:**
- Product Category: [Health/Finance/Relationships/Business/etc.]
- Target Audience: [Demographics + Psychographics]
- Surface-Level Problem: [What they THINK the problem is]
- Previous Solutions Tried: [What they've already tried]

**YOUR TASK:**

Step 1: Generate 10 potential "hidden villains" - root causes that:
- The market doesn't currently know about
- Explain why all previous solutions failed
- Create an "it's not your fault" moment
- Can be named in a memorable way

Step 2: For each villain, provide:
- Scientific/logical basis for the claim
- How it connects to the surface problem
- Why other solutions don't address it
- A potential name for the villain

Step 3: Rank the villains by:
- Novelty (how unknown to market)
- Believability (can it be proven?)
- Emotional impact (does it create relief/anger/hope?)
- Solution fit (does your product actually solve it?)

**OUTPUT:** Ranked list of 10 villain concepts with full analysis
```

### Template B: "What They Don't Know" Generator

Use when: Market thinks they understand their problem, but they don't

```markdown
**ROLE:** You are an expert at identifying the gap between what people THINK causes their problem and what ACTUALLY causes it.

**CONTEXT:**
- Problem: [e.g., "Can't lose weight"]
- What they believe causes it: [e.g., "Eating too much, not exercising enough"]
- What your product/knowledge addresses: [e.g., "Gut microbiome imbalance"]

**GENERATE:**

1. **The Conventional Wisdom Statement**
   "Most people believe [problem] is caused by [common belief]..."

2. **The Evidence Against It**
   "But if that were true, then [logical inconsistency]..."

3. **The Hidden Truth Introduction**
   "The real culprit is [hidden cause], which [explanation]..."

4. **The Chain of Causation**
   "[Hidden cause] → [intermediate effect] → [intermediate effect] → [visible symptom]"

5. **The Self-Esteem Restoration**
   "This means it was never about [what they blamed themselves for]..."

6. **The Solution Preview**
   "Once you address [hidden cause], [promised result]..."

**Output each element fully developed, ready to use in copy.**
```

## Solution-First Mechanism Prompts

### Template D: How-It-Works Mechanism

Use when: Product has a clear working method, need to make it compelling

```markdown
**ROLE:** You explain HOW solutions work in ways that create "aha" moments and build unshakeable belief.

**PRODUCT/SOLUTION:** [INSERT]
**How it actually works:** [Technical explanation]
**Primary benefit:** [What it delivers]

**CREATE:**

1. **The Working Principle**
   Distill the core mechanism to ONE sentence a 12-year-old would understand:
   "[Product] works by [simple mechanism], which [benefit]."

2. **The 3-Step Process**
   Break it into exactly 3 memorable steps:
   - Step 1: [Name] - [What happens]
   - Step 2: [Name] - [What happens]
   - Step 3: [Name] - [What happens]

3. **The Demonstration Concept**
   How could you SHOW (not tell) this works?
   - Visual demonstration idea:
   - Analogy demonstration idea:
   - Before/after demonstration idea:

4. **The "That's Why" Connection**
   Connect the mechanism to past failures:
   "This is why [other approach] didn't work - it [what it missed]."
   "But [this mechanism] addresses [what others miss]."

5. **The Unique Advantage**
   What can ONLY this mechanism do?
   "Unlike [alternatives], this [unique capability]."

**OUTPUT:** Complete solution mechanism ready for copy
```

### Template E: Superpower Generator

**CONTEXT:**

5. **Address the "Can I Really?" Objection**
   Why can ANYONE develop this superpower?
   - What makes it accessible?
   - What's the proof ordinary people can get it?

## Paradigm Shift Prompts

### Template F: Belief Inversion Mechanism

Use when: Need to flip an existing belief upside down

```markdown
**ROLE:** You are a paradigm architect who reconstructs how people think about problems.

**CURRENT MARKET BELIEF:** [What everyone currently believes]
**THE TRUTH:** [What your product/research reveals]

**CREATE THE INVERSION:**

## Step 1: State the Conventional Wisdom
"Most experts agree that [belief]..."
"You've probably heard that [belief]..."
"Everyone knows that [belief]..."

## Step 2: Create Doubt
"But if [belief] were true, then why [logical problem]?"
"How do you explain [contradictory evidence]?"
"Consider this: [uncomfortable fact]..."

## Step 3: Introduce the Flip
"What if [opposite of belief]?"
"New research shows that [paradigm shift]..."
"The truth is actually the opposite: [new belief]..."

## Step 4: Rebuild the Logic
"Here's why this makes sense: [explanation]"
"Once you understand [mechanism], it becomes clear..."
"This explains why [reframe past evidence]..."

## Step 5: Create Binary Choice
"Now you have two choices:
- Keep believing [old paradigm] and continue [consequence]
- Accept [new paradigm] and finally [benefit]"

**NAME THE PARADIGM SHIFT:**
- The [Name] Effect
- The [Name] Principle
- The [Name] Revelation

**OUTPUT:** Complete paradigm shift narrative with naming
```

### Template G: "Upside Down World" Builder

Use when: Creating an E5-level mechanism that redefines the category

```markdown
**ROLE:** You create new worlds where old rules don't apply.

**THE OLD WORLD:**
- The problem is: [how it's currently defined]
- The solution path is: [what everyone tries]
- Success requires: [what's traditionally needed]
- Failure means: [how failure is explained]

**BUILD THE UPSIDE DOWN WORLD:**

1. **The Revelation Event**
   What discovery/insight flips everything?
   "[Discovery] revealed that [old world rule] is actually [opposite]..."

2. **The New Rules**
   In this world:
   - [Old success factor] actually [hinders/doesn't matter]
   - [Old failure excuse] was actually [different thing]
   - [Ignored factor] is actually [the key]

3. **The New Heroes**
   Who succeeds in this upside-down world?
   - Not: [old world winners]
   - But: [new world winners]
   - Because: [why the rules favor them now]

4. **The Navigation Guide**
   How do you succeed in this new world?
   - Stop: [old world behavior]
   - Start: [new world behavior]
   - Use: [your product/method]

5. **The Tribal Identity**
   Create belonging:
   - "People who understand this are [identity term]"
   - "We're not [old world group], we're [new world group]"
   - "Welcome to the [name] movement/revolution"

**OUTPUT:** Complete upside-down world framework
```

## Usage Notes

### When to Use Which Template

| Situation | Best Template |
|-----------|---------------|
| Starting from scratch, no mechanism idea | Template A or B |
| Have villain concept, need to develop | Template C |
| Know HOW product works, need to explain | Template D |
| Pleasure market, aspirational | Template E |
| Need to flip a belief | Template F |
| Creating category-defining mechanism | Template G |
| Health/supplement product | Template H |
| Financial product | Template I |
| Business opportunity | Template J |
| Full campaign development | Template K |
| Need mechanism + proof | Template L |

### Prompt Modification Tips

1. **Add specificity**: Replace [INSERT] with actual details
2. **Adjust tone**: Add "Use [formal/casual/urgent] tone"
3. **Add constraints**: Include word limits, format requirements
4. **Request alternatives**: Ask for "3 versions of each"
5. **Include examples**: Add "Similar to [example mechanism]"
