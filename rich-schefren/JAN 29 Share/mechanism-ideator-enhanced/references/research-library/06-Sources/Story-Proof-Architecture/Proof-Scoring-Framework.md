# Proof Scoring Framework for Mechanism Credibility

> **Purpose:** Systematic evaluation framework for scoring and optimizing proof elements across 5 dimensions.

---

## The 5-Dimension Proof Scoring System

### Overview

Every proof element should be evaluated across five key dimensions:

1. **Credibility** - How believable is the source and claim?
2. **Specificity** - How detailed and precise is the proof?
3. **Verifiability** - Can the audience confirm this independently?
4. **Emotional Impact** - Does it create feeling and connection?
5. **Mechanism Integration** - Does it reinforce the core mechanism story?

Each dimension scored 1-10, for a maximum of 50 points per proof element.

---

## Dimension 1: Credibility (1-10)

**Definition:** The degree to which the source and claim are perceived as trustworthy and believable.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Elite | World-renowned source, unquestionable authority |
| 7-8 | High | Respected institution/expert, strong credentials |
| 5-6 | Moderate | Legitimate but not prominent source |
| 3-4 | Low | Unknown source, minimal credentials |
| 1-2 | Poor | Questionable source, no authority |

### Credibility Factors

**Source Authority:**
- Institution prestige (Harvard vs. unknown university)
- Expert credentials (MD, PhD vs. no credentials)
- Publication quality (peer-reviewed vs. blog post)
- Track record (established vs. unknown)

**Claim Reasonability:**
- Within accepted scientific bounds
- Not too-good-to-be-true
- Consistent with other evidence
- Specific enough to evaluate

### Examples by Score

**Score 10:** "Published in the New England Journal of Medicine, the study conducted at Harvard Medical School..."

**Score 7:** "According to Dr. Smith, a board-certified nutritionist with 15 years of clinical experience..."

**Score 4:** "Studies have shown..." (no source cited)

**Score 2:** "Some experts believe..." (vague, unattributed)

---

## Dimension 2: Specificity (1-10)

**Definition:** The level of detail, precision, and concrete information provided.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Elite | Multiple precise details, names, numbers, dates |
| 7-8 | High | Specific numbers, clear attribution |
| 5-6 | Moderate | Some detail but missing elements |
| 3-4 | Low | Vague, general statements |
| 1-2 | Poor | No specific information |

### Specificity Elements

**Numbers:**
- Percentages ("87% saw improvement" vs "most people")
- Counts ("1,247 participants" vs "a large group")
- Timeframes ("12 weeks" vs "a while")
- Measurements ("lost 23 pounds" vs "lost weight")

**Names:**
- Researcher names
- Institution names
- Study names
- Location names

**Details:**
- Methodology description
- Before/after specifics
- Timeline clarity
- Condition specifics

### Examples by Score

**Score 10:** "In a 12-week, double-blind, placebo-controlled study at Johns Hopkins University (Smith et al., 2023), 247 participants aged 45-65 with mild cognitive impairment showed a 43% improvement in memory recall scores."

**Score 7:** "Research shows that this ingredient led to a 43% improvement in memory in clinical trials."

**Score 4:** "Studies suggest significant memory improvements."

**Score 2:** "Many people report better memory."

---

## Dimension 3: Verifiability (1-10)

**Definition:** The ability of the audience to independently confirm or check the proof.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Elite | Publicly available, easily verified |
| 7-8 | High | Can be found with some effort |
| 5-6 | Moderate | Partially verifiable |
| 3-4 | Low | Difficult to verify |
| 1-2 | Poor | Cannot be verified |

### Verifiability Factors

**Documentation:**
- Published study with DOI/citation
- Named, contactable sources
- Public records available
- Third-party confirmation possible

**Accessibility:**
- Information publicly available
- Links or references provided
- Contact information given
- Evidence not proprietary

### Examples by Score

**Score 10:** "The study (DOI: 10.1038/xyz) is available on PubMed. You can also contact the researchers directly at Harvard Medical School."

**Score 7:** "Published in the Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 2023. The full study is available online."

**Score 4:** "Based on our internal research conducted last year." (not publicly available)

**Score 2:** "Our experts have found..." (no ability to verify)

## Dimension 4: Emotional Impact (1-10)

**Definition:** The degree to which the proof creates feeling, connection, and emotional resonance.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Elite | Deeply moving, visceral response |
| 7-8 | High | Strong emotional engagement |
| 5-6 | Moderate | Some emotional resonance |
| 3-4 | Low | Minimal emotional impact |
| 1-2 | Poor | Dry, no emotional content |

### Emotional Impact Factors

**Story Elements:**
- Personal narrative
- Struggle and transformation
- Relatable circumstances
- Vivid details

**Emotional Triggers:**
- Hope generated
- Fear addressed
- Relief provided
- Connection created

**Presentation:**
- Visual imagery
- Emotional language
- Pacing and reveal
- Contrast (before/after)

### Examples by Score

**Score 10:** "When Sarah first came to me, she couldn't pick up her grandchildren without pain. She'd tried everything - three surgeries, countless medications, years of physical therapy. She'd given up hope of ever playing with them again. Six weeks after starting this protocol, I got a video of her carrying her two-year-old grandson on her shoulders, tears streaming down her face..."

**Score 7:** "John lost 47 pounds and says it changed his life. 'I can finally keep up with my kids,' he told us."

**Score 4:** "Participants reported improved quality of life."

**Score 2:** "87% showed statistical improvement in measured outcomes."

---

## Dimension 5: Mechanism Integration (1-10)

**Definition:** How well the proof element supports and reinforces the core mechanism story.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Elite | Directly proves mechanism works |
| 7-8 | High | Strong support for mechanism |
| 5-6 | Moderate | Related to mechanism |
| 3-4 | Low | Tangential connection |
| 1-2 | Poor | No mechanism connection |

### Integration Factors

**Mechanism Support:**
- Proves root cause exists
- Shows mechanism of action
- Validates solution pathway
- Reinforces paradigm shift

**Story Coherence:**
- Fits narrative arc
- Builds on previous elements
- Prepares for next elements
- Consistent with overall message

### Examples by Score

**Score 10:** "The biopsy results showed exactly what we expected - the lectin-damaged intestinal lining had regenerated. The 'holes' had closed. The inflammation markers that had been elevated for years? Back to normal. This is what happens when you address the ROOT CAUSE."

**Score 7:** "Participants who followed the gut-healing protocol showed significantly reduced inflammation markers."

**Score 4:** "The ingredient has general anti-inflammatory properties."

**Score 2:** "Customers rate the taste 4.7 out of 5 stars." (unrelated to health mechanism)

## Proof Type Scoring Benchmarks

Different proof types have different typical strengths:

### Scientific Study Proof
**Typical Profile:**
- Credibility: 8-10
- Specificity: 8-10
- Verifiability: 7-9
- Emotional Impact: 3-5
- Mechanism Integration: 7-9

**Optimization Focus:** Add emotional framing while maintaining scientific rigor.

### Transformation Testimonial
**Typical Profile:**
- Credibility: 5-7
- Specificity: 6-8
- Verifiability: 4-6
- Emotional Impact: 8-10
- Mechanism Integration: 6-8

**Optimization Focus:** Add credentials, specifics, and verifiable details.

### Expert Endorsement
**Typical Profile:**
- Credibility: 7-9
- Specificity: 5-7
- Verifiability: 6-8
- Emotional Impact: 5-7
- Mechanism Integration: 6-8

**Optimization Focus:** Make quotes more specific, add emotional language.

### Authority/Credential Proof
**Typical Profile:**
- Credibility: 8-10
- Specificity: 7-9
- Verifiability: 8-10
- Emotional Impact: 3-5
- Mechanism Integration: 5-7

**Optimization Focus:** Connect credentials to mechanism story, add emotional context.

### Popularity Proof
**Typical Profile:**
- Credibility: 6-8
- Specificity: 7-9
- Verifiability: 7-9
- Emotional Impact: 5-7
- Mechanism Integration: 4-6

**Optimization Focus:** Connect numbers to why mechanism works.

## Improving Weak Dimensions

### Boosting Credibility
- Add source credentials
- Cite specific institutions
- Reference peer-reviewed publications
- Include expert names with titles
- Add third-party validation

### Boosting Specificity
- Add exact numbers
- Include timeframes
- Name specific conditions/outcomes
- Provide methodology details
- Include before/after measurements

### Boosting Verifiability
- Provide citations (DOI, links)
- Include contact information
- Reference public records
- Offer documentation upon request
- Use named, traceable sources

### Boosting Emotional Impact
- Add personal story elements
- Include sensory details
- Show transformation journey
- Connect to universal desires/fears
- Use vivid, emotional language

### Boosting Mechanism Integration
- Explicitly connect to root cause
- Reference mechanism language
- Show how proof validates mechanism
- Use consistent terminology
- Position within narrative arc

## Proof Portfolio Analysis

### Inventory Your Proof Portfolio

List all proof elements and score each:

| # | Proof Element | Cred | Spec | Ver | Emot | Mech | Total |
|---|---------------|------|------|-----|------|------|-------|
| 1 | | | | | | | |
| 2 | | | | | | | |
| 3 | | | | | | | |
| ... | | | | | | | |

### Portfolio Quality Metrics

**Average Score:** Sum of all totals / Number of elements
- Target: 35+ average

**Dimension Balance:** Average score per dimension
- All dimensions should average 6+
- No dimension below 5 average

**Distribution Analysis:**
- % of elements scoring 40+: Target 25%
- % of elements scoring 30-39: Target 50%
- % of elements below 30: Target <25%

### Identifying Portfolio Weaknesses

**Dimension Weakness:** If any dimension averages below 6
- Focus on improving that dimension across elements

**Type Imbalance:** If certain proof types underrepresented
- Add more of that type

**Quality Gap:** If too many elements below 30
- Replace or significantly strengthen weak elements

## Proof Optimization Workflow

### Step 1: Score Current Inventory
- Score all existing proof elements
- Calculate portfolio metrics
- Identify weak elements and dimensions

### Step 2: Prioritize Improvements
- Rank elements by improvement potential
- Identify quick wins (small changes, big impact)
- Plan for elements requiring significant work

### Step 3: Strengthen or Replace
- Improve elements with potential
- Replace elements that can't be salvaged
- Add new elements to fill gaps

### Step 4: Re-Score and Verify
- Score improved elements
- Recalculate portfolio metrics
- Verify optimal distribution

### Step 5: Integrate Into Copy
- Map proof to claims (1:1 ratio)
- Distribute strategically across VSL
- Ensure narrative flow

## Quick Reference: Dimension Questions

### When Scoring Credibility:
1. Who is the source?
2. What are their credentials?
3. Is the claim reasonable?
4. Would an expert agree?

### When Scoring Specificity:
1. Are there exact numbers?
2. Are sources named?
3. Is the timeframe clear?
4. Are conditions specific?

### When Scoring Verifiability:
1. Can I find this online?
2. Is a citation provided?
3. Can I contact the source?
4. Is there documentation?

### When Scoring Emotional Impact:
1. Does this move me?
2. Can I feel the transformation?
3. Is there a story here?
4. Will the audience connect?

### When Scoring Mechanism Integration:
1. Does this prove the mechanism?
2. Does it support the root cause?
3. Does it fit the narrative?
4. Is the language consistent?
