## The Problem (45 seconds)

When you use generic words like "think," "analyze," or "create," you're essentially giving AI the linguistic equivalent of a shrug. These words travel the most worn paths in what researchers call the "semantic forest" - leading to predictable, safe, bland responses.

Think about it: When you say "analyze our quarterly performance," the AI defaults to basic statistics and obvious observations. But when you say "forensically dissect our quarterly performance," suddenly you're getting multi-layered analysis revealing causation chains, hidden correlations, and predictive indicators.

The difference? One power word.

---

## The Core Discovery (1 minute)

Here's what we've discovered: AI systems allocate computational resources based on the cognitive load signaled by your word choices. Complex, specific words signal that you want thorough processing. Generic words signal you want quick, surface-level responses.

It's like the difference between asking a master chef to "make food" versus asking them to "orchestrate a culinary symphony." Same chef, same ingredients, completely different outcome.

Our research identified over 300 power words organized into categories: analytical, strategic, creative, operational, and persuasive. Each category activates different processing pathways in AI systems.

## The Transformation Examples (1.5 minutes)

Let me give you three quick examples that show the dramatic difference:

**Example One: Problem Solving**
- Generic prompt: "Help me understand why sales are down"
- Result: Basic list - market conditions, competition, customer preferences
- Power prompt: "Diagnose the underlying pathology of our sales decline"
- Result: Root cause analysis, symptom cascade mapping, specific trigger events, targeted prescriptions

**Example Two: Innovation**
- Generic: "Give me innovative ideas for our product"
- Result: Add features, improve interface, change pricing
- Power: "Synthesize breakthrough innovations by cross-pollinating adjacent industry solutions"
- Result: Subscription pharmacy model for B2B, gaming mechanics in enterprise software, ghost kitchen model for manufacturing

**Example Three: Customer Research**
- Generic: "What do customers think about us?"
- Result: 72% satisfaction, customers like our service
- Power: "Excavate the latent emotional drivers beneath customer sentiment"
- Result: Surface satisfaction masks dependency anxiety, price complaints are proxy for value gaps, hidden driver is community not product

## The Practical Framework (1 minute)

Here's your three-step implementation framework:

**Step One:** Identify the weak link. Find the generic word limiting your prompt.

**Step Two:** Choose your power word category:
- For investigation: dissect, excavate, forensically examine
- For planning: architect, orchestrate, blueprint
- For innovation: transmute, catalyze, cross-pollinate
- For problem-solving: diagnose, untangle, navigate

**Step Three:** Layer for compound effect. Combine 2-3 power words:
- "Excavate and synthesize the patterns"
- "Dissect the problem then architect a solution"
