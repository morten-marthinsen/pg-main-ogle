## Primary Academic Sources

### Productivity Paradox and J-Curve Research

1. **Solow, Robert M.** (1987). "We'd better watch out." *New York Times Book Review*, July 12, 1987.
   - Original productivity paradox observation

2. **David, Paul A.** (1990). "The Dynamo and the Computer: An Historical Perspective on the Modern Productivity Paradox." *American Economic Review*, 80(2), 355-361.
   - Electrification analogy, 40-year lag analysis

3. **Brynjolfsson, Erik; Rock, Daniel; Syverson, Chad** (2018). "The Productivity J-Curve: How Intangibles Complement General Purpose Technologies." *NBER Working Paper No. 25148*.
   - J-curve model, complementary investment requirements

4. **Brynjolfsson, Erik & Hitt, Lorin M.** (2000). "Beyond Computation: Information Technology, Organizational Transformation and Business Performance." *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 14(4), 23-48.
   - IT productivity gains require organizational change

### Scientific Management and Process Redesign

5. **Taylor, Frederick Winslow** (1911). *The Principles of Scientific Management*. Harper & Brothers.
   - Measurement-based productivity improvement

6. **Hammer, Michael** (1990). "Reengineering Work: Don't Automate, Obliterate." *Harvard Business Review*, July-August 1990.
   - Process redesign principles

7. **Hammer, Michael & Champy, James** (1993). *Reengineering the Corporation: A Manifesto for Business Revolution*. HarperBusiness.
   - BPR methodology and case studies

### Historical Technological Transitions

8. **Mokyr, Joel** (1990). *The Lever of Riches: Technological Creativity and Economic Progress*. Oxford University Press.
   - Industrial Revolution entrepreneur patterns

9. **Goldin, Claudia & Katz, Lawrence F.** (2008). *The Race Between Education and Technology*. Harvard University Press.
   - Technology adoption and workforce transformation

10. **Gordon, Robert J.** (2016). *The Rise and Fall of American Growth*. Princeton University Press.
    - Multi-era technological disruption analysis

### Training and Workforce Development

11. **Training Within Industry Foundation** (1940-1945). TWI Report to War Manpower Commission.
    - Historical training methodology

12. **Dieckmann, J.R. & Robinson, M.** (2013). "Training Within Industry: The Foundation of Lean." *Productivity Press*.
    - TWI methodology application

### Change Management

13. **Kotter, John P.** (1996). *Leading Change*. Harvard Business School Press.
    - 8-step change model

14. **Prosci Inc.** (Multiple years). ADKAR Model Research.
    - Change management research on resistance
