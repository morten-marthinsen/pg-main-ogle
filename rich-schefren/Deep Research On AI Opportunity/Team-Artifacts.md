# AI Team Training - Team Artifacts

**4 Essential Tools for Team Members in AI Transformation**

For team members in 2-30 person companies who are learning to work with AI as part of their daily jobs.

## Artifact 1: Role Redesign Matrix

### What This Is
A personal planning tool to understand how your role will evolve with AI. It helps you see what stays human, what becomes AI-assisted, and where you'll add the most value.

### Why It Matters
Every technological disruption creates new roles by combining old skills with new capabilities. "Typesetter" became "Desktop Publisher." "Secretary" became "Executive Assistant." Your role will evolve—this helps you shape that evolution proactively.

### The Three Categories of Work

**Human Work (Keep)**
Tasks requiring:
- Judgment in ambiguous situations
- Relationship building and trust
- Creativity and original thinking
- Ethical decision-making
- Emotional intelligence
- Strategic thinking
- Physical presence or action

**Hybrid Work (Augment)**
Tasks where AI + Human > Either Alone:
- AI generates draft, human refines
- AI researches, human decides
- AI monitors, human intervenes
- AI suggests, human approves
- AI analyzes, human interprets

**AI Work (Automate)**
Tasks AI can handle with periodic oversight:
- Routine data entry and processing
- First-draft generation
- Scheduling and coordination
- Basic research and compilation
- Pattern-matching decisions
- Repetitive communications

### Role Evolution Examples

**Before: Administrative Assistant**
- 40% calendar management, scheduling
- 30% email triage and response
- 20% document preparation
- 10% relationship management

**After: Executive Support Specialist**
- 10% calendar oversight (AI handles scheduling)
- 15% email quality review (AI drafts)
- 20% strategic project coordination
- 25% relationship management and communication
- 30% research and analysis for decision support

**Before: Customer Support Rep**
- 60% answering routine questions
- 25% handling escalations
- 15% documentation and logging

**After: Customer Success Specialist**
- 10% reviewing AI responses to complex questions
- 35% handling escalations and relationship repair
- 30% proactive outreach to at-risk customers
- 25% training AI, improving response quality

### 48-Hour MVP
- Hour 0-8: List all tasks and estimate hours
- Hour 8-16: Categorize each (Human/Hybrid/AI)
- Hour 16-32: Identify one task to transition first
- Hour 32-48: Begin AI-assisted version of that task

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Complete task inventory
- Days 3-4: Full categorization and analysis
- Days 5-6: Discuss with manager/owner
- Days 7-8: Transition first 1-2 tasks
- Days 9-10: Adjust and plan next transitions

### Why It Matters

### SOP/Skill Library Maintenance Rules

1. **Create after second use** - Don't document things you've only done once
2. **Update when it breaks** - If something doesn't work, update immediately
3. **Delete when obsolete** - Outdated docs are worse than no docs
4. **Version don't duplicate** - One doc per process, update in place
5. **Share proactively** - When you create something useful, tell the team

### 48-Hour MVP
- Hour 0-12: Choose one AI-enabled process to document
- Hour 12-24: Write the one-page SOP
- Hour 24-36: Test with a colleague who hasn't done it
- Hour 36-48: Refine based on their questions

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Identify top 5 processes to document
- Days 3-5: Create SOPs for 3 processes
- Days 6-7: Team testing and refinement
- Days 8-10: Skill library entries for reusable prompts

## Artifact 3: QA/Evaluation Protocols

### What This Is
Quality assurance approaches that work without dedicated QA staff. Designed for small teams where everyone does QA as part of their job.

### Why It Matters
AI outputs need checking, but small businesses can't afford QA specialists. These protocols embed quality checks into normal workflow without creating bottlenecks.

### The 3-Tier QA System

**Tier 1: Self-Check (Every Output)**
Before considering any AI-assisted output complete, check:

```
SELF-CHECK PROTOCOL

□ ACCURACY: Does this contain any factual errors I can verify?
  • Check numbers against source data
  • Verify names, dates, specific claims
  • Confirm it matches what was asked for

□ TONE: Does this sound right for the audience?
  • Matches our company voice
  • Appropriate formality level
  • No AI-ish phrasing ("I'd be happy to help")

□ COMPLETENESS: Does this answer the full question/need?
  • Addresses all parts of the request
  • Includes necessary context
  • Nothing obviously missing

□ HARMLESSNESS: Could this cause problems?
  • No unintended promises
  • No confidential info leaked
  • No potential legal issues

Time: 30-60 seconds per output
```

**Tier 2: Peer Review (High-Stakes Outputs)**
For client-facing, financial, or permanent outputs:

```
PEER REVIEW PROTOCOL

Reviewer: _________________ Date: _________

Output type: ________________________
Prepared by: ________________________

□ SELF-CHECK COMPLETE? (verified with preparer)

PEER CHECKS:
□ Does this make sense to someone who wasn't involved?
□ Are there any red flags I see that preparer might have missed?
□ Does this represent us well?
□ Would I be comfortable if this went viral?

FEEDBACK:
□ Approved as-is
□ Minor edits needed: ________________________________
□ Major revision needed: _____________________________
□ Escalate to owner

Time: 2-5 minutes
```

**Tier 3: Owner Spot-Check (Random Sample + Red Flags)**

```
OWNER SPOT-CHECK PROTOCOL

Frequency: Weekly
Sample size: 5-10 random outputs + all escalations

REVIEW QUESTIONS:
□ Are self-checks and peer reviews actually happening?
□ What quality issues am I seeing across the sample?
□ Are there patterns in errors (same person, same process)?
□ Is quality improving, stable, or declining week over week?

ACTION ITEMS:
• Process to fix: ___________________________________
• Training needed: __________________________________
• SOP to update: ____________________________________

Date: _________ Owner initials: _____
```

## Artifact 4: Change Management Playbook (Team Version)

### What This Is
A guide for navigating the personal experience of AI transformation—managing your own adaptation and supporting colleagues through theirs.

### Why It Matters
76% of change initiatives encounter resistance. But resistance often comes from legitimate concerns. This playbook helps you understand your own reactions, adapt constructively, and support teammates.

### Understanding Your Reactions

**Common Reactions to AI (All Normal)**

| Reaction | What It Feels Like | What It Usually Means |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Excitement | "This is amazing!" | Opportunity seeking |
| Skepticism | "This won't work" | Protecting from disappointment |
| Fear | "Will I be replaced?" | Self-preservation, normal |
| Frustration | "This is harder than promised" | Learning curve, also normal |
| Resistance | "I prefer the old way" | Comfort with known |
| Fatigue | "Another thing to learn" | Bandwidth limits |

**All of these are normal.** You may cycle through several. The goal isn't to feel a certain way—it's to act constructively regardless of feeling.

### How to Raise Concerns Constructively

**Instead of:** "This AI stuff is stupid"
**Try:** "I'm struggling with X. Can we discuss solutions?"

**Instead of:** "This will never work"
**Try:** "I see potential issues with X. Have we addressed those?"

**Instead of:** "I don't have time for this"
**Try:** "I'm at capacity. Can we discuss what to deprioritize?"

**Instead of:** Silence (while internally struggling)
**Try:** "I'm finding this transition harder than expected. I'd value some support."

### How to Support Struggling Colleagues

**Do:**
- Share your own struggles (normalizes difficulty)
- Offer specific help ("I can show you the prompt I use for X")
- Acknowledge the transition is real work
- Celebrate small wins together
- Listen without judgment

**Don't:**
- Dismiss concerns ("It's not that hard")
- Compare ("I figured it out, why can't you?")
- Pressure ("Just use it")
- Report struggles to management without permission
- Make assumptions about why they're struggling
