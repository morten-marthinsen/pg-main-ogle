# AI Team Training - 12 Strategy Cards

**From Historical Disruption Winners Who Started Small**

Each card includes: Context, The Move, Mechanism, Evidence, AI Translation, Workshop Exercise, 48-Hour MVP, 10-Day Implementation

---

## Card 1: The Pilot-First Pattern

### Context + Constraints
Early textile entrepreneurs (1790s-1820s) had limited capital and couldn't afford to transform entire operations. Typical team: 5-20 workers. Risk of total loss if investment failed.

### The Move
Start with one machine, one process, one measurable outcome before committing further resources.

### Mechanism
Small experiments validated assumptions, built team confidence, and generated profits that funded expansion. Winners learned from small failures; losers bet everything and lost.

### Evidence
- Samuel Slater's first mill started with just 9 spindles (1790)
- Lowell mills expanded only after each addition proved profitable
- 70% of early mechanization failures came from over-investment before validation

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Choose ONE high-volume, low-risk process. Implement AI. Measure results. Only then consider expansion.

### Workshop Exercise
"List your top 5 time-consuming processes. Score each on: frequency (1-5), data availability (1-5), reversibility if AI fails (1-5). Your pilot is the highest total score."

### 48-Hour MVP
- Hour 0-8: Select pilot process, document current state
- Hour 8-24: Configure AI tool, test with 5 real examples
- Hour 24-48: Run full day pilot, measure time saved

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Select and scope pilot
- Days 3-5: Deploy and iterate
- Days 6-8: Train team, create SOP
- Days 9-10: Measure results, plan next use case

## Card 2: The Spreadsheet Warrior Strategy

### Context + Constraints
Early PC adopters (1983-1990) gained disproportionate advantage not from IT departments but from individual "power users" who mastered Lotus 1-2-3 or Excel before anyone else. Team size: any.

### The Move
Identify and elevate your internal AI champions who naturally experiment with new tools.

### Mechanism
Power users became force multipliers—training colleagues, solving problems, creating templates. They didn't wait for permission or formal training.

### Evidence
- VisiCalc users (1979) reported 10-20x productivity on financial analysis
- Companies with early "spreadsheet warriors" captured market share
- Power users became informal CTOs in many small firms

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Identify who on your team is already experimenting with AI. Give them time, resources, and recognition. Make them your AI champions.

### Workshop Exercise
"Who on your team has already tried ChatGPT, Claude, or other AI tools for work? What have they discovered? How can you amplify their experiments?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- Identify 1-2 AI-curious team members
- Give them explicit permission to experiment
- Ask them to document and share one discovery

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Identify champions
- Days 3-5: Provide resources and time
- Days 6-8: Have champions train others
- Days 9-10: Formalize champion role

## Card 3: The Process Redesign Imperative

### Context + Constraints
Electrification adopters (1900-1930) who simply replaced steam with electric motors saw minimal gains. Those who redesigned factories around electric power saw 30-40% productivity increases. Same technology, different results.

### The Move
Before implementing AI, redesign the process around what AI makes possible—don't just bolt AI onto existing workflows.

### Mechanism
Electric motors enabled distributed power (any machine anywhere). But gains only came when factories redesigned layouts. Same principle: AI enables new workflows, not just faster old ones.

### Evidence
- Paul David: "40 years for electrification gains" was actually faster for redesigners
- Factories built for electric power: 30-40% more productive
- Retrofitted factories: minimal gains for years

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Ask: "If we were starting this process from scratch with AI available, how would we design it?" Then design that process.

### Workshop Exercise
"Pick a process you want to AI-enable. Draw the current workflow. Now draw a new workflow that assumes AI can handle any text/data task. What's different?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- Select one process
- Document current state (steps, time, handoffs)
- Design AI-native version (fewer steps, different handoffs)
- Test the new design

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Map 3 processes in current state
- Days 3-5: Design AI-native versions
- Days 6-8: Test one redesigned process
- Days 9-10: Refine and plan rollout

## Card 4: The Training-First Approach

### Context + Constraints
TWI (Training Within Industry) programs during WWII trained 23 million workers in new manufacturing methods. Key insight: training before deployment prevented expensive mistakes.

### The Move
Train team members BEFORE deploying AI tools, not after.

### Mechanism
Untrained users create bad habits, get poor results, become skeptical, and resist adoption. Trained users start successful, build confidence, and become advocates.

### Evidence
- TWI: "Job Instruction" method reduced training time 65%
- Companies with pre-deployment training: 43% higher adoption rates
- Post-deployment training: 2x more resistance

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Invest 2-4 hours in AI fundamentals training before anyone uses AI tools for real work. Focus on prompting, limitations, and quality review.

### Workshop Exercise
"Design a 2-hour AI fundamentals training for your team. Cover: what AI can/can't do, prompting basics, quality review, and your specific use case."

### 48-Hour MVP
- Hour 0-4: Create 30-minute AI fundamentals module
- Hour 4-8: Create 30-minute prompting practice
- Hour 8-24: Deliver to pilot team
- Hour 24-48: Have them apply to real work

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Develop training curriculum
- Days 3-4: Deliver foundation training
- Days 5-7: Supervised practice
- Days 8-10: Independent use with support

## Card 5: The Complementary Investment Rule

### Context + Constraints
Computing investments (1985-2000) showed no productivity gains until companies also invested in training, process redesign, and organizational change. Technology alone never worked.

### The Move
For every dollar spent on AI tools, budget equal or greater investment in training, process documentation, and change management.

### Mechanism
Technology is necessary but not sufficient. The "productivity paradox" was actually an "investment paradox"—companies invested in hardware but not the complementary changes needed to use it.

### Evidence
- Brynjolfsson: Complementary investments account for 90% of productivity gains
- Companies with high complementary investment: 21% higher returns
- Technology-only investments: 70% failure rate

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
If AI tools cost $500/month, budget $500+/month for training, documentation, and process improvement.

### Workshop Exercise
"Calculate your AI tool budget. Now double it for complementary investments. Where will you allocate: training, process documentation, or change management?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- List all AI-related costs (tools, time, training)
- Identify gaps in complementary investment
- Allocate minimum viable complementary budget
- Create spending plan

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Full AI investment audit
- Days 3-5: Complementary needs assessment
- Days 6-8: Budget allocation and approval
- Days 9-10: Implementation plan

## Card 6: The Measurement Discipline

### Context + Constraints
Scientific Management (Taylor, 1900s) transformed productivity through measurement. Workshop owners who measured before and after saw where gains came from. Those who didn't couldn't improve.

### The Move
Measure baseline before AI, measure after AI, make decisions based on data not feelings.

### Mechanism
What gets measured gets managed. Without baseline, you can't prove ROI. Without measurement, you can't identify what's working.

### Evidence
- Taylor's time-motion studies: 200-400% productivity improvements
- Companies with measurement discipline: faster iteration, better results
- "Gut feel" adoption: higher abandonment rates

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Before implementing AI on any process, document: time required, output quantity, quality score, and cost. Measure same metrics weekly after implementation.

### Workshop Exercise
"For your pilot AI process: How long does it take now? What's the output quality (1-10)? What does it cost (time × rate)? These are your baselines."

### 48-Hour MVP
- Select pilot process
- Measure: time, steps, output quantity, quality, cost
- Document baseline
- Set measurement cadence

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Baseline measurement protocol
- Days 3-4: Measure all target processes
- Days 5-7: Implement AI with ongoing measurement
- Days 8-10: Compare and report

## Card 7: The Window Awareness Strategy

### Context + Constraints
Internet entrepreneurs (1995-2000) who acted in the first 3-5 years captured disproportionate value. Latecomers faced established competition. The window didn't stay open forever.

### The Move
Act now. The window for AI advantage is open but time-limited. Early movers compound their lead.

### Mechanism
First movers build capabilities, customer relationships, and organizational knowledge that create sustainable advantages. Windows close as technology commoditizes.

### Evidence
- Internet: 5-10 year window for category leadership
- Computing: 10-15 year window
- AI (projected): 18-36 month window for SMB advantage
- Compounding returns: early adopters get progressively further ahead

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Start this week, not "when things calm down." Every month of delay is a month competitors are building AI capabilities.

### Workshop Exercise
"What would you regret NOT doing with AI if we meet again in 2 years? What's stopping you from starting that this week?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- Identify one AI action you've been postponing
- Commit to starting in next 48 hours
- Take first concrete step
- Schedule follow-up

### 10-Day Implementation
- Day 1: Commit publicly to AI adoption
- Days 2-3: Select and start first use case
- Days 4-7: Build momentum with visible progress
- Days 8-10: Establish sustainable rhythm

## Card 8: The Owner-Led Transformation Rule

### Context + Constraints
Every successful technological transformation in small business was driven by the owner/founder, not delegated to specialists. Delegated transformations had 70%+ failure rates.

### The Move
Owner must personally use AI tools, champion adoption, and stay engaged throughout transformation—not delegate to "the AI person."

### Mechanism
Team members watch what owners do, not what they say. If owner doesn't use AI, team reads that as signal it's optional or unimportant.

### Evidence
- 90% of small business strategy failures: execution gap (owner disengagement)
- Owner-led transformations: 43% higher success rate
- Delegated transformations: frequent abandonment

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Block 30-60 minutes daily for owner AI usage. Share discoveries with team. Make AI visible in your own work.

### Workshop Exercise
"As the owner, what tasks in YOUR day could benefit from AI? Commit to personally using AI for one task this week. Share results with your team."

### 48-Hour MVP
- Owner identifies personal AI use case
- Owner configures and tests AI tool
- Owner uses for full day
- Owner shares experience with team

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Owner AI immersion
- Days 3-5: Owner demonstrates to team
- Days 6-8: Owner reviews team adoption
- Days 9-10: Owner establishes ongoing rhythm

## Card 9: The Resistance Anticipation Protocol

### Context + Constraints
Every technological transition encountered workforce resistance. Companies that anticipated and addressed resistance succeeded. Those that ignored it faced sabotage, turnover, and failure.

### The Move
Anticipate resistance sources before implementation. Create specific strategies for each source.

### Mechanism
Resistance is predictable and manageable, not a surprise to react to. Fear of job loss, loss of expertise value, change fatigue—all can be addressed with the right communication and action.

### Evidence
- 76% of change initiatives encounter resistance
- Organizations addressing resistance proactively: 72% higher adoption
- Ignored resistance: sabotage, turnover, project failure

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
Before launching AI, map who might resist and why. Create talking points for each concern. Have conversations before deployment, not after.

### Workshop Exercise
"List each team member. For each: What might they fear about AI? What's the honest answer to that fear? When will you have that conversation?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- List all team members
- Predict resistance sources for each
- Develop response for top 3 concerns
- Schedule 1-on-1 conversations

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Resistance mapping
- Days 3-4: Response development
- Days 5-7: 1-on-1 conversations
- Days 8-10: Adjust based on feedback

## Card 10: The SOP Without Overhead Principle

### Context + Constraints
Small businesses resist documentation as "overhead" but companies with documented processes implement technology 40% faster. The key: lightweight documentation that helps rather than burdens.

### The Move
Create 1-page SOPs for AI-enabled processes—enough structure to train and maintain consistency, not bureaucracy.

### Mechanism
SOPs prevent tribal knowledge, enable training, and create consistency. But they must be usable, not compliance theater.

### Evidence
- Documented processes: 40% faster AI implementation
- Over-documented processes: bureaucratic resistance
- "Just enough" documentation: optimal adoption

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
One page per AI-enabled process: What triggers it, what inputs needed, what AI does, what human reviews, what output expected.

### Workshop Exercise
"For your pilot AI process, create a 1-page SOP. Include: Trigger, Inputs, AI Prompt, Review Points, Output. Time limit: 30 minutes."

### 48-Hour MVP
- Select one AI-enabled process
- Draft 1-page SOP (30 min max)
- Test SOP with different team member
- Refine based on their questions

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: SOP template creation
- Days 3-5: Document pilot processes
- Days 6-8: Test with multiple users
- Days 9-10: Finalize and train

## Card 11: The Continuous Improvement Rhythm

### Context + Constraints
Japanese manufacturers (1950s-1980s) didn't just adopt technology—they created systems for continuous improvement (Kaizen). Small gains compounded into massive advantages.

### The Move
Establish weekly rhythm of AI improvement: What worked, what didn't, what will we try next.

### Mechanism
One-time implementations plateau. Continuous improvement compounds. Weekly attention ensures AI evolves with your business.

### Evidence
- Kaizen practitioners: sustained competitive advantage
- One-time implementations: 40-60% of value captured
- Continuous improvement: 90%+ of potential value

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
30-minute weekly AI retrospective: What prompts worked? What outputs needed fixing? What new use case should we try?

### Workshop Exercise
"Design your weekly AI retrospective. When will it happen? Who attends? What three questions will you always answer?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- Schedule first weekly AI retrospective
- Create 3-question agenda
- Run first session
- Document action items

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Design improvement rhythm
- Days 3-4: Hold first session
- Days 5-7: Implement improvements
- Days 8-10: Evaluate and refine rhythm

## Card 12: The Hybrid Role Evolution

### Context + Constraints
Every technological disruption created new hybrid roles combining old expertise with new capabilities. "Typesetter → Desktop Publisher." "Clerk → Spreadsheet Analyst." Jobs evolved, rarely disappeared.

### The Move
Redesign roles as human+AI hybrids rather than human OR AI. Define what humans do, what AI does, how they work together.

### Mechanism
Pure automation often fails. Pure human often wasteful. Hybrid roles capture AI efficiency while maintaining human judgment, creativity, and relationships.

### Evidence
- 85% of jobs in 2030 don't exist today (not elimination—evolution)
- Hybrid roles outperform pure automation on complex tasks
- Human-in-the-loop AI: higher quality, better adoption

### AI Translation for 2-30 Person Teams
For each role: What stays human? What becomes AI? What becomes human-reviewing-AI? Create explicit hybrid role definitions.

### Workshop Exercise
"For each role in your company, fill out: Human Tasks (judgment, relationships, creativity), AI Tasks (routine, data, generation), Hybrid Tasks (human + AI together). What changes?"

### 48-Hour MVP
- Map one role's current tasks
- Categorize: human, AI, hybrid
- Define new hybrid role
- Discuss with role holder

### 10-Day Implementation
- Days 1-2: Map all roles
- Days 3-5: Design hybrid versions
- Days 6-8: Discuss with team
- Days 9-10: Finalize and document
