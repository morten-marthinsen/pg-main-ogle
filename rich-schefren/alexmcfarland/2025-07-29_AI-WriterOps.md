Today I'm giving you the exact prompt system I use to reverse-engineer any competitor's content strategy.

## "Content intelligence"

Content intelligence is systematic analysis of what your competitors are actually doing that drives results—not just what they're posting.

Most people think competitor research means looking at surface-level stuff like posting frequency or topic categories. Real content intelligence digs into the psychology: what hooks are they using, what problems are they solving, how are they structuring their arguments, and what calls-to-action actually convert.

This gives you a roadmap for differentiation instead of imitation.

## The difference between guessing and knowing

Here's how most people approach competitor research:

**Weak approach:** "Let me check out their last few posts and see what topics they cover."

**Strong approach:** "Let me systematically analyze their content patterns, audience engagement triggers, and strategic positioning to identify gaps I can exploit."

The first approach gives you random inspiration. The second gives you competitive advantage.

## 4 principles of effective content intelligence

### 1\. Context over content

Don't just analyze what they're saying—analyze why they're saying it and how their audience responds. The context around content reveals strategy. The content itself just reveals execution.

### 2\. Patterns over posts

Individual posts tell you nothing. Content patterns tell you everything. Look for recurring themes, structural formulas, and engagement drivers across multiple pieces.

### 3\. Gaps over greatness

Finding what they do well is useful. Finding what they're missing is profitable. Your biggest opportunities live in their blind spots.

### 4\. Systems over screenshots

Random competitive research is basically useless. Systematic competitive research with repeatable processes gives you continuous strategic advantage.

## 1 prompt to decode any competitor's content strategy

### Intelligence Brief Generator

This prompt analyzes competitor content systematically and identifies strategic opportunities for your brand.
    
    
    You are an expert content strategist tasked with creating a comprehensive competitor-content intelligence brief. Your analysis will be based on the following inputs:
    
    <focus_topics>
    {{FOCUS_TOPICS}}
    </focus_topics>
    
    <brand_info>
    {{BRAND_INFO}}
    </brand_info>
    
    <competitor_links>
    {{COMPETITOR_LINKS}}
    </competitor_links>
    
    Your goal is to provide a detailed analysis of the competitors' content strategies, compare them with our brand, and offer actionable recommendations for differentiation and audience capture. Follow these steps to create your brief:
    
    1. Data Collection:
       Analyze the competitor links and our brand information. Extract relevant data such as recent posts, publication dates, keywords, and engagement metrics if available. List key metrics and data points extracted from each competitor and our brand.
    
    2. Key Findings Summary:
       For each competitor and our brand, provide a brief summary of their content strategy and performance, including a structured SWOT analysis. List specific strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats for each.
    
    3. Comparative Dashboard:
       Create a table comparing key metrics across competitors and our brand. Explain the significance of each metric in the table.
    
    4. Topics & Styles Analysis:
       Identify best-performing topics, analyze headline formulas, examine tone patterns, and study calls-to-action (CTAs) for competitors and our brand. Categorize topics and styles, and provide specific examples for each category.
    
    5. Gaps & Opportunities Mapping:
       Highlight content angles that competitors haven't covered, identify underserved areas, and brainstorm potential content ideas to fill these gaps. Prioritize the identified gaps based on potential impact.
    
    6. Strategic Recommendations:
       Develop 3-5 actionable moves to differentiate our brand and capture audience share. Consider both short-term and long-term impacts for each recommendation.
    
    For each step, use <thinking_process> tags inside your thinking block to show your thought process and ensure a thorough interpretation of the data. After completing your analysis, format your output as follows:
    
    1. Executive Summary
       - Provide a concise overview of key findings and recommendations
    
    2. Competitor Comparison Table
       - Present a clear, easy-to-read table comparing key metrics across competitors and our brand
    
    3. Topic & Style Breakdown
       - Detail the most successful content strategies employed by competitors and our brand
       - Include specific examples of effective headlines, tones, and CTAs
    
    4. Gap Analysis
       - Clearly identify content opportunities that competitors are missing and how our brand can capitalize on them
    
    5. Strategic Recommendations
       - List 3-5 specific, actionable recommendations
       - Explain how each recommendation will help differentiate our brand and capture audience share
    
    Ensure that your analysis is thorough, your insights are specific, and your recommendations are practical and actionable. Focus on providing valuable, data-driven insights that can directly inform content strategy decisions.
    
    Example output structure:
    
    ```
    1. Executive Summary
    [Concise overview of key findings and recommendations]
    
    2. Competitor Comparison Table
    | Metric       | Our Brand | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
    |--------------|-----------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
    | [Metric 1]   | [Value]   | [Value]      | [Value]      | [Value]      |
    | [Metric 2]   | [Value]   | [Value]      | [Value]      | [Value]      |
    ...
    
    3. Topic & Style Breakdown
    [Detailed analysis of successful content strategies]
    - Headlines: [Examples and analysis]
    - Tones: [Examples and analysis]
    - CTAs: [Examples and analysis]
    
    4. Gap Analysis
    [Identification of content opportunities]
    - Gap 1: [Description and potential]
    - Gap 2: [Description and potential]
    ...
    
    5. Strategic Recommendations
    1. [Recommendation 1]
       - Rationale: [Explanation]
       - Expected Impact: [Description]
    
    2. [Recommendation 2]
       - Rationale: [Explanation]
       - Expected Impact: [Description]
    
    ...
    ```
    
    Begin your analysis now, starting with the data collection phase. Your final output should consist only of the formatted brief and should not duplicate or rehash any of the work you did in the thinking block.

**How to use it:** Replace the variables with your specific information. The more context you provide (brand guidelines, competitor links, strategic documents), the better the analysis. But even basic URLs work for solid intelligence.

**Pro tip:** Run this monthly with the deep research option against your top 3 competitors to track strategy shifts and emerging opportunities.

## Test your competitive intelligence

Run the prompt on one competitor you know well.

Compare the analysis against your existing knowledge of their strategy. If the prompt identifies patterns you hadn't noticed or opportunities you hadn't considered, you know it's working.

If the analysis feels surface-level or obvious, add more context to your inputs and run it again.

Content intelligence is all about systematically identifying where your competition is vulnerable so you can win.
