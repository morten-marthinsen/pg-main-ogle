Two separate requests.

Like having an operations manager who actually remembers your strategy.

The Excel file was like a complete workbook:

  * 50 specific newsletter titles (not generic topics)

  * Compelling hooks for each

  * Key deliverables

  * ICP targeting

  * 7 color-coded categories

  * Summary sheet with strategic breakdown




And this involved barely any extra work on my end.

## We're not just talking about better outputs

We're talking about supercharging entire document preparation workflows.

Claude maintains context across the entire document creation process. It's thinking about structure, analyzing relationships, and making strategic connections.

My September report didn't just list what I created. It identified that 40% of my content focused on "context engineering" and recommended doubling down on that theme. 

That's strategic analysis, not just compilation.

All of this can now happen with simple requests (as long as you focus on including enough context throughout your entire system).
