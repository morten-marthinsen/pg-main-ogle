## **How this works**

The Substack notes skill I’m giving you is based on 10 psychological triggers that drive high-performing notes:

  1. **Single punch wisdom** \- Powerful quotable statements

  2. **Income proof** \- Results and specific numbers

  3. **Pattern observation** \- “I’ve noticed...” insights

  4. **Contrarian statement** \- Going against common advice

  5. **Problem and solution** \- Clear pain point + fix

  6. **Building in public** \- Behind the scenes moments

  7. **List-based tactical** \- Bullet point frameworks

  8. **Vulnerable personal stories** \- Real, relatable moments

  9. **Philosophical observations** \- Bigger picture thinking

  10. **Direct advice** \- Actionable recommendations




When you invoke this skill, Claude analyzes your content, chooses which triggers are most applicable, and generates 20 notes using those structural patterns.

The skill automatically:

  * Understands your context and voice

  * Selects the right note types for your content

  * Applies proven formulas for each type

  * Formats everything for Substack

  * Runs a quality check before delivery




You’re not getting random notes. You’re getting strategic variations designed to perform.

## **Step 2: Upload to Claude**

Open Claude and click **Settings** in the bottom left corner.

Then go to **Capabilities**.

Scroll all the way down until you see **Skills**. This is your library of Claude skills.

Hit **Upload skill** or just drag the zip file from your downloads directly into this section.

Claude will process it for a second. Then you’ll see “Substack notes skill” appear in your skills library with the description “Generate high-performing Substack notes using proven structural patterns and psychology.”

## **Step 3: Prepare your source content**

The skill works with any content you’ve already created:

  * Newsletters

  * Long-form articles

  * Voice recordings (transcribed)

  * Twitter threads

  * Workshop content

  * Even full conversation threads from Claude




You don’t need to format it. Just have it ready to paste.

**Pro tip:** If you’re using a newsletter, copy the full text—intro, main content, conclusion. The more context, the better the notes.

* * *

## **Step 4: Invoke the skill**

Start a new chat in Claude (Claude Sonnet 4.5 recommended).

Tell Claude what you want. You can be direct:

> “Generate Substack notes based off of my latest newsletter.”

Then paste your content.

Claude will automatically recognize you want Substack notes and invoke the skill. You’ll see it read the skill file first to follow best practices.

**What happens next:**

  * Claude analyzes your content

  * Chooses which of the 10 psychological triggers fit best

  * Generates notes using those patterns

  * Formats them for Substack

  * Delivers them in one output




The skill aims for 20 notes, but depending on your content, it might generate 8-15 that are highly relevant rather than forcing all 20.
