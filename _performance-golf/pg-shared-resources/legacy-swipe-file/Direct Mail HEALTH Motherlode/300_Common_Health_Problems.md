• Underactive (not producing enough thyroid hormones)
• Overactive (overproducing thyroid hormones)

2) Self-test ...
A poorly functioning thyroid can
also be identified by a constantly
low "basal" temperature--
your temperature when you
first wake up.
Buy a basal thermometer at a
drug store or online. Then, each morning for a week, put the
thermometer under your armpit. .. and take and record your
temperature when you first wake.
If your temperature is constantly below normal --say between
97.8 and 98.2, you are likely suffering from thyroid problems.
3) Medical-tests ...
Your doctor can also test your thyroid, but you really need to
know the facts about what the results mean.
The most commonly used thyroid test is the TSH Test. TSH stands
for "thyroid stimulating hormone" ... and it measures how much
of the hormone is being released by your pituitary gland.
If your TSH is high (above 5.0), it indicates that your thyroid is
underactive and your pituitary gland must make more TSH.
If your TSH is low (below 0.5), it means your thyroid is overactive.
Falling between those numbers has
always been considered "normal."
Yet lately, the American Association
of Endocrinologists and other
professional groups have recom
mended a narrower range of 0.3
to 3.0.
If the upper portion of the
"normal" range is lowered to 3.0,
approximately 20% of the U.S. population would be diagnosed
with an underactive thyroid.
The point is, you can get a TSH test done, but don't let that be
the only medical test. Your TSH may show you are "in the
normal range," but you may still feel lousy and have a thyroid
problem, like me.
It's not good enough if you want to feel great! You need to be
in the optimal range, with plenty of thyroid hormone. Not just
"normal."
If you want to get a more accurate medical reading, you should
get the TSH Test, as well as Free T4 (which measures the amount
of "unbound" T4 in your body), Free T3 (which confirms hypo
thyroidism even if your T4 levels are normal), Reverse T3 (which
shows if your production ofT3 is being blocked) and the Thyroid
Peroxidase Antibody Test (which shows if you have an auto
immune thyroid problem).
