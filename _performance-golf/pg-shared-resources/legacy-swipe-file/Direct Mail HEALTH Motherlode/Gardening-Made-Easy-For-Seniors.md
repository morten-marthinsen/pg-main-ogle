# Annoying Arits?


## Swell breakfast food


## stops 'em cold.

If you've spent any time outdoors
in the sunny South, you've probably
had a run-in with fire ants. These tiny bugs
(continued on page 8)
inflict a painful bite - and where you see one fire ant,
there are hundreds more. Luckily, the South also provides
a cheap, nontoxic solution. Grits, a Southern breakfast
staple, are an often-suggested bait for fire ants, who take
the little white morsels home to share. When they eat
grits and drink water, the grits swell inside them, and
they die. Sprinkle uncooked grits on and around ant
mounds. You'll be amazed how fast they get picked up
and taken inside.
No grits handy? Start on page 269 for more easy,
non-toxic ways to kill or repel ants. (Including cinnamon.)
