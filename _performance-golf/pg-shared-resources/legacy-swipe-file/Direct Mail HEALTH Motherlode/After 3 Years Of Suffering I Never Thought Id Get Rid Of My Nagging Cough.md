imply put, your respiratory system consists of
three parts: the nose and nasal passages, the
trachea and bronchi, and the lungs.
Air enters the respiratory system through the
nostrils where it is warmed, filtered, and moistened. The nasal
passages are lined with a membrane that secretes a sticky
fluid called mucus. Along with tiny “hairs” called cilia, the
mucus is your body’s first defense against foreign invaders.
Together, they trap dust, bacteria, and other contaminants
before they can further penetrate your body.
Next the air travels through the throat to the trachea, a
cartilage tube, also lined with ciliated mucus membrane.
Normally, what contaminants get by the nasal passages, are
caught in the trachea and moved by the cilia back to the
throat in the form of phlegm, ejected from the air passages
and sometimes swallowed.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

to COPD.
What are the symptoms of COPD? Exces-
sive coughing that produces abnormal quanti-
ties of mucous ... chronic wheezing and
