## First they hype the heck out of lutein


## for all it’s worth . . .

As soon as news stories broke about the benefits of lutein —
a potent carotenoid found in marigold petals, spinach, and other
foods — some big-name multivitamin makers began plastering
the words “Now with lutein” on the front of their labels.
Why am I so steamed about this? Because it gives
consumers the idea that lutein is the ONLY nutrient you
need to take to protect your eyes.
Folks, nothing could be further from the truth! While taking
lutein provides significant support for your macula, lens, and
retina, what about the rest of your eyes? Just for starters, there’s
your cornea, sclera, and millions of tiny nerves and blood vessels
that need to stay healthy for your eyes to function at their best.

# . . . Then they scrimp on potencies


# to fatten their profits!

Here’s the other problem that really rubs me the
wrong way. Some multivitamin makers engage in a
practice I call nutrient “dusting.” That’s where they
give you a fraction of the amount needed to actually
provide a benefit.
Multiple studies — including that Harvard University
study I mentioned earlier — have found you need at least
6 and as much as 30 milligrams of lutein each day to see
results. Yet some multivitamin makers put in as little as
0.25 milligrams — or up to 99 times less!
In fact, here’s a common trick used for listing lutein
potencies on multivitamin labels. Instead of showing it
in milligrams (mg), companies show it in micrograms
(mcg) to make it seem higher. Since a microgram is
equal to 1/1000 th of a milligram, 250 mcg is equivalent
to 0.25 mg — or a measly one-fourth of a milligram.
Why do they scrimp so much and play these dirty
tricks? Because they want to cut costs while getting the
maximum sales boost from their marketing hype.
Meanwhile, you’re lulled into a false sense of security,
thinking you’re taking enough lutein and don’t need
anything more to protect and support your precious
vision.
But if you’re taking scant amounts of lutein and
nothing else, your eyes aren’t fully protected. In fact,
I’ve identified seven ways lutein FAILS to fully protect
the health of your eyes as you age!
