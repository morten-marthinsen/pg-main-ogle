# The Most Common Leg Issues You May be Facing ...

VARICOSE VEINS occur when the
vein walls become inflexible and the
valves in the veins don't seal
properly. The result: blood leaks
backward into veins and tissue, and
pool, causing a bulging, twisted or
knotted appearance on the leg.
SPIDER VEINS are purple, red,
blue, or gray web-like streaks most
often found on the leg. Spider veins
result when a capillary becomes
damaged or weakened. Blood
becomes pooled in the capillary,
causing the threadlike streaks to
appear on the surface of the skin.
Spider veins are affected by many factors including:
heredity, obesity, hormonal changes and standing or
sitting for long periods of time.
DEEP VENOUS THROMBOSIS
is when a blood clot forms in a vein
deep inside one or both legs. This
causes ... Red skin color ... Increased
warmth ... Leg pain or tenderness ...
and Swelling (edema). DVT mainly
affects the large veins in the lower leg and thigh. The
clot can also block blood flow. So if the clot breaks
off and moves through the bloodstream, it can get
stuck in the brain, lungs, heart, or other area, leading
to severe damage or even death!
RESTLESS LEG SYNDROME is a common
cause of painful legs which
typically only eases with
constant motion and becomes
more noticeable at rest. RLS
often worsens during the early
evening or at night, often
leading to insomnia.
PERIPHERAL ARTERY DISEASE
is a nafrowing of the peripheral arteries
in the legs. PAD is similar to coronary
artery disease. This condition is caused
by narrowed and blocked arteries in
your legs. These hardened arteries
restrict the blood supply to the heart.
VENOUS INSUFFICIENCY is caused
when veins deep in the leg have a
problem sending blood from the legs
back to the heart. Normally, valves in your veins
keep your blood flowing back towards the heart so it
can't collect in one place. But the valves in varicose
veins are either damaged or missing. Causing the
veins to remain filled with blood, especially when
you're standing.
HEAVY /TIRED LEGS is caused
by stagnate blood in the veins,
making them dilate and causing
calves and ankles to swell. It also
makes them feel tired and heavy.
Without treatment, these leg veins
will enlarge and worsen with time.
Heat, long periods of standing, obesity, and improper
diet increase heavy leg symptoms.
EDEMA is swelling or
puffiness of parts of the
body caused by extra fluid
that builds up in the tissues
of your body. Edema usually
happens in the feet,
an~es
and legs, mostly due to
gravity as it pulls fluid down into your legs and feet.
Sitting or standing still for too long can cause edema
of the legs, especially in hot weather.
CRAMPS are caused by poor
circulation to the muscles in your legs.
GOUT is a disease that results from
an overload of uric acid in the body.
This overload of uric acid leads to the
formation of tiny crystals of urate that
deposit in tissues of-the body,
especially the joints. When crystals
form in the joints, it causes inflammation. Gout is
considered a chronic and progressive disease.
Chronic gout can also lead to
deposits of hard lumps of uric acid
in the tissues, particularly in and
around the joints and may cause
joint destruction, decreased kidney
function, and kidney stones.
