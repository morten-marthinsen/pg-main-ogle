Because of the extremely cold tempera
tures, Nature fitted these goats with a very
dense undercoat. It is this undercoat that
contains the ultra-fine cashmere hair.
The coarse topcoat must be sheared, and
the fine undercoat picked off by hand.
The hair is then sorted by color. The stiffer
fibers are removed by an expensive (and
confidential) procedure.
One cashmere goat yields only about 4
ounces of hair a year. The very best goat, at
his peak, might produce twice that. Under
standably, the supply of cashmere is limited.
And expensive.
The fiber itself is solid, not porous like
wool. And it's short, making it extremely dif
ficult to weave.
Why so much interest in a material that is
so much trouble to produce? Because it is the
