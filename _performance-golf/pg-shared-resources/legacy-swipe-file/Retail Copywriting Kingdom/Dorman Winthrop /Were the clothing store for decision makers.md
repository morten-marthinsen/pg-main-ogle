There are two reaso.ns why Dorman-Winthrop
is the perfect place for decision makers.
1. Our quality. Men in decision-making
positions are the most likely buyers of expen
sive clothing. That's all we sell.
2. Our huge selection. The greater the
choice, the better it is for those skilled in
decision making.
A store that has only two or three suits in
each size by a particular maker is fine for
those that have trouble deciding what they
want. With so little selection the choice is
easy.
But if decision making isn't a problem for
you, then you'd probably prefer the store
with the widest selection of colors and pat
terns in each size, by the greatest number of
top makers.
That store is us.
We buy from more of the best makers and
internationally famous designers, in the US,
Italy, France and West Germany, than any
body else does.
Our selection from these makers, in your
site, is several times larger than you're apt
to find in the other stores.
In more types and models including Euro
pean, contemporary, square shoulder, Cali
fornia and natural shoulder traditional.
But you don't have to be a great decision
maker to choose our prices. That part is easy.
Most men's stores, from small shops to
exclusive department stores, work on a 55%
to 60% profit margin on suits, coats and
slacks.
The difference between our selling price
and the wholesale price (including freight
and hanger) is only 33-1/3%. To the penny.
A suit that usually sells for $515 elsewhere,
we sell for $382. 71. A jacket that sells for $355
elsewhere, we sell for $262. 79. ·
Some alterations are included when you
buy elsewhere .. That doesn't mean they're
free. Just that the price has been padded to
cover the alterations costs.
But some customers don't need as much
alteration as others. So why charge them the
same as those that need more?
We eharge everyone just for the alterations
they need. At our cost.
And that saves you money.
Does it seem a little odd for us to cut prices
on expensive clothing for purchase by men to
whom price is no object?
Maybe. But most affluent men have devel
oped a keen sense of value.
They use their heads.
And we appeal to reason.
