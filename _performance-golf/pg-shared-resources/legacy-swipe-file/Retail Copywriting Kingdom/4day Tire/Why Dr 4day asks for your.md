Most people have better things to do with their
time than sit around a tire store.
Even though we keep our 4day Tire Stores
cleaner than the guy down the street and we give
you a place to sit and maybe a newspaper to read
(if you get there early). our customers can't wait
to leave.
Some folks are so anxious to make their getaway,
they make us feel like we're asking for a pint of
blood just because we can't let them go without
filling out a few forms.
"A FEW FOAMS!" No need to yell.
It's for your own good. Really.
Besides, there isn't a heck of a lot we can do
about it, Except for our invoice, the forms are
required by the federal government and the people ·
who make your tires,
The US Dept of Transportation (DOT) wants
every tire registered to its new owner. Just like a
pedigreed poodle.
That way, if Aunt DOT wants to send you a
Christmas card or, if she has to recall a defective
batch oftires, she'll know how to get a hold of
you.
You also have to fill out the mfr's workmanship
and road hazard guarantee form. Just about every
tire mfr has his own version of this one and
they're all pretty confusing.
We've been after the mfrs to come up with a
simple form that's easy to understand, But so far,
no luck.
I
Now then, just
one more form •.•
If you're not already buried
under all this talk about
paperwork, here's some
things we think you should
know about the invoice we
give you when you buy
ti res at 4day,
Most people don't take
time to read an invoice
once they leave the store.
It gets folded up, packed
away in the glove com
partment or sandwiched
in their wallet between
pictures of a prized Great
Dane and Mary Alice
MacAllister when she was
25 years younger.
That's a mistake, Your
invoice really isn't im
portant to you until you
leave the store. That's
when you should give
it a good going over.
If you're looking at
a 4day Tire Store in·
voice (and naturally
we hope you are) you
see that it tells you things
such as what kind of
tires you bought, what
size, how many. when you
bought them and how
much you paid
It also notes the mileage
of your car and the guaran·
teed mileage on the new
tires. That's important in
formation to have if you
should ever need an adjust·
ment.
When you decide to
purchase our 4day Guar
antee, our invoice is your
ticket to out-of.the·
ordinary service and guar·
anteed mileage that we
back ourselves.
All the terms and con·
ditions of our guarantee
are spelled out on the
front of our invoice in
words big enough for
you to read and in
language that isn't con·
fusing.
Flip it over. and our
invoice becomes a trea
sure chest of helpful in·
formation and valuable
facts and figures.
You'll find an easy to
read chart that lets you
compute the basic adjust·
ment you can expect if
one of your tires should
fail before the mileage
guarantee is used up.
Remember. 4day isn't
like the other guys. We
compute adjustments
based on the price you
paid for the tire. Not a
mysterious ,.adjustment
price", taken from a big
black book that only
dealers are allowed to
read and ends up eating
away most of the credit
you have coming.
We also give you a no
double talk comparison
of our 4day adjustment
policy and the standard
mtr•s warranty service
you'll find in most other
tire stores.
Those little boxes and
lines at the bottom of
our invoice are where
one of our operators will
record the results of your
regular 4000 to 6000
mile inspection visits.
These visits are required
to keep your 4day Guar
antee in full force and
include pressure check,
tire rotation (only if you
need it) and even flat tire
repair. No extra charge
for these services once .
you've purchased the 4day
Guarantee for an extra 4%
of the price of your tires.
In the lower right hand
corner, on the backside of
the invoice. you'll notice
a list of names and a
telephone number. The
names belong to our own·
ers who invite you to call
them, collect. at that
telephone number, any·
time you feel you weren't
treated fairly in one of our
4day Tire Stores.
There's also an address,
in case you'd prefer to
write a Jetter instead of
calling,
An invoice, DOT regis·
tration form, mfr's road
hazard and workmanship
warranty. Not exactly a
pound of flesh; but a lot
of paperwork just for a set
of tires.
But remember, those
tires are the only thing
between your family and
a safe comfortable ride.
That paperwork assures
you of some accountabil·
ity if you ever have a
gripe about your tires.
We do everything we
can to make it as painless
as possible. But next to
waiting in tire stores,
filling out paperwork is
man's least most favorite
pastime.

changed my life'

in a 4day Tire Store as just
