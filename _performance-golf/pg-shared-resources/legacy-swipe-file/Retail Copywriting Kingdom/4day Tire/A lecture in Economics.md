Every business, with the exception of a few charitable
institutions, attempts to make a profit.
Even 4day.
Any competent tire dealer knows that the most ex
pensive item in his overhead is wages.
' Problem. How .to cut significant overhead expense
without injuring the business in order to offer high
value at cut prices? · ·
The most effective method of doing that at 4day is
by staying closed on the three least busy selling days
of the week. Mondays. Tuesdays. Wednesdays.
Being open the remaining four days, Thursday
through Sunday, for a total 40 hour work week for
our store retail personnel enables us to achieve
maximum sales with a one-shift payroll.
No time·and·a-half overtime. No overlapping shifts.
In most of our stores, no costly unproductive hours.
No skilled tin~ men standing ar.ound yawning for
customers.
Does being open only 40 hours a week instead of
the usual 48, 56 or 60 hours a week limit our sales?
Our growth record says no.
Perhaps we lose a few customers during certain un
usually crowded periods. Most tire customers, though,
prefer to go to a busy store. We think we gain more
than we lose. ·
High sales volume and greater efficiency enable us
to plow the overhead savings right back into the busi
ness in the form of lower prices.
Lower prices, the laws of economics tell us, assure
us of selling more tires.
Selling more tires enables us to obtain maximum
buying discounts from the tire manufacturers. If we
buy for less we can sell for less.
But what happens_ to profit?
We're ambitious but npt greedy. We're building a
solid foundation for the biggest tire business in the
west. ·
We're satisfied with a profit margin impractical for
other ti re dealers.
There's nothing mysterious or very complicated
about it. We buy in tremendous quantities, so we can
sell at cut prices, so we can buy in even larger quan·
tities, so we can sell at even lower cut prices.
All the while taking a trifling profit on every
transaction.
In the end those trifling profits add up to a big
business. That's exactly where we're heading.
Economics isn't really a.difficult subject.
If you understand it.
