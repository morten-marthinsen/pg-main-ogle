The ABC's of UTQG
You'll see the UTQG ratings as a
number, followed by two letters.
On a Michelin XH tire, for
example, the UTQG is 330 AB.
The number, 330, stands for
relative tread life. The greater
the number, the longer it should
wear. The XH's treadwear
rating is way up there. More
about that later.
The first letter, A, represents
the tire's grade for straight
ahead braking on a wet surface.
A is best. B is well above
minimum federal requirements.
C means it passes minimum
requirements, but that's about
all.
The second letter, B, repre
sents the tire's resistance to heat
build-up. An important quality,
since heat is a tire's worst
enemy. Excessive heat can
reduce tire life by causing tread
separation or sidewall cracking.
Again, A is best, C is worst.

Michelin's longest wearing tire

An impressive 330 rating.
That doesn't guarantee you'll
get a specific number of miles
from the tire. The rating is
simply to give you an idea of
how long the tire will wear
compared to others.
Real world driving conditions
are less than perfect. A driver
who starts and stops quickly, or
drives heavier cars or with a
heavier load or at higher speeds,
or drives in hilly terrain or
makes lots of turns in city
driving, or has worn shock
absorbers or whatever, will
receive fewer miles from a tire.
What you can expect is that
the XH, rated 330, should last
over twice as long as a tire rated
160.
In addition to long wear, the
XH delivers exceptional wet
surface traction and a plush,
quiet ride.
Whal the UTQG doesn't tell you
There's more to tire quality than
UTQG ratings.
These ratings don't reflect
how well a tire sticks to the road
in a tight turn or how precisely it
responds to the driver's steering
input.

mance SPORT XGT V has a

compared to the XH or XA4.
That's because the XGT V
tread compound is designed spe
cifically for handling respon-
siveness and cornering traction.
If you want to improve the
performance characteristics of
your car, these qualities
probably are more important to
you than tread wear.
