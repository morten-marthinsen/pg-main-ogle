A tip on 4 lbs extra Inflation
An extra 2 to 4 psi of inflation
pressure can accomplish several
desirable results for you.
Your tires will wear longer.
By reducing road friction, it
improves gas mileage.
And it increases the load
carrying capability of your
vehicle.
First, of course, check your
Owner's Manual. If you've
misplaced yours, ask any 4day
store manager to show you the
Tire Guide.
Then simply add 2 to 4 psi to
whatever pressure is called for.
Never exceed the maximum
safety limit for the tire, which
you'll find in the sidewall.
Never drive with less inflation
pressure than is called out in
your Owner's Manual.
In summer or on long trips,
never bleed air out of a tire to
reduce the expanded air
pressure because the tire is hot.

What's your tire size?
Unless you spend a lot of time
combing your hair in your
chrome wheels you probably
don't know what your tire size is
off-hand.
Step outside and take a close
look at the sidewall of your tires.
You're looking for something
like 185/70SR14 or Pl85/70Rl4.
The numbers may be different
but the idea's the same.
The P means P-metric. One of
the advantages of the P-metric
tire is higher inflation pressure,
to provide better gas mileage.
No P before the numbers, like
185/70SR14, means the tire is a
European metric size.
185 is the width, in millimeters,
fro'm sidewall to sidewall.
70 is the aspect ratio. The
section height, ground to rim, is
70% of the width, sidewall to
sidewall. The lower the number.
the shorter the tire.
R means it's a radial. Some
European tire sizes may have an
SR, as above, TR, HR, VR, ZR
etc. These are speeci ratings,
explained briefly at the bottom
of the fifth column.
The next number, 14, tells you
it will fit a wheel rim 14" in
diameter.
Is a 60 better than a 70?
The first step in improving tire
performance is to lower the
aspect ratio.
A size 185/60Rl4 tire is the
same width as a 185/70Rl4 tire.
But the 60 aspect ratio sidewall
is 4.44" tall as opposed to 5.18"
for the 70-series tire.
Shorter sidewalls are also
stiffer. That generally means
quicker, more precise steering
response. Especially when
cornering.
