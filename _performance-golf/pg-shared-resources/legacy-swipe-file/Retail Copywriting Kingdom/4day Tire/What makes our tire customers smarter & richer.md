Tricks of the trade
Suppose for a moment that you
are a tire dealer.
Think of devices to get people
to come to your store.
You know one thing. Every
body loves a bargain. All right,
tell people you have bargains.
Make every tire seem like a
sensational special. Make your
ads hard to read, and be sure
they look exciting. Use big black
prices. Put the tiny sizes and
descriptions in pale dinky type,
so people won't bother to read ·
them. Sell whoopee.
Or use a gimmick. Have two
prices. During a week that you
sell at the higher price, offer the
second tire at 50% off, so people
will think it's a terrific bargain.
How we offer true tire value
Be glad you're not a 4day Tire
Store manager. If you were, you
couldn't use any tricky devices.
You'd have to use the same
size type for your prices on
larger sizes as you use for your
least expensive smaller sizes.
You couldn't raise your prices
one week and lower them the
next so you could have
"specials". You'd have to sell at
the same prices, week after
week. No whoopee.
You couldn't charge extra for
mounting. Or inflate valve stem
and balancing prices to make an
extra buck.
You couldn't adjust a tire
under your mileage guarantee at
a fictitiously high list price.
You'd have to adjust at the cur
rent printed selling price. The
4day guarantee is written to .help
the customer, not the dealer.
As a 4day Tire Store manager,
the only way you could stay in
business would be by taking a
profit that others would .consider
inadequate, working your tail off
to achieve maximum efficiency
during the 43 busiest retail hours
of the week, and then selling
about ten times as many tires
per foot of working space and
per dollar of overhead as any
other tire dealer.
