What causes blow-outs?
Most drivers call any sudden
loss of air a blow-out and assume
the problem was a defective tire.
But that's usually not the case.
The vast majority of blow-outs
are caused by underinflation or
damage in use. ·
Underinflated or overloaded
tires flex inordinately, causing
friction and heat build-up as well
as cord fatigue. The resulting ply
separation is a common explana
tion for many blow-outs.
Hitting a curb, pothole, or any
road hazard can break internal
cord plies. The damage is often
invisible and may not result in a
problem until days, weeks or
months later.
By that time you've probably
forgotten the damaging impact.
But your tires haven't forgotten.
