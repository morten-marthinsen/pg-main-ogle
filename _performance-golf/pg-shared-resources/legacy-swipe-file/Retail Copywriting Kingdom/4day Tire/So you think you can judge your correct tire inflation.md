# So you think you can judge your


# correct tire inflation by eye?

There are easy ways to waste money.
One of the easiest is to drive on tires that are
underinflated. Or overinflated.
How about yours? Have you checked them lately?
You're probably wasting money yourself, and that's
your own fault.
We use the word probably because more than half
the cars on the road have incorrect inflation pres
sure in one or more tires.
That means your tires probably are wearing out
faster than they should.
Underinflated tires give you a nice soft ride,
but less car control, particularly on turns,
and diminished traction and braking,
and reduced gasoline mileage,
and accelerated wear on the tread shoulders.
Every passenger car tire in the world is engineered
so that under a normal load, with correct inflation
pressure, the tread will lie perfectly flat.
When a tire is underinflated, the pressure no
longer is distributed equally. The edges of the tread
push harder against the road surface than the cen·
ter does.
Or vice versa if overinflated. The center of the
tread pushes harder than the edges do. The edges
wear more slowly than they should but the center
wears faster.
Although some drivers think they save money by
getting better gasoline mileage with overinflated
tires, they're actually wasting gasoline. Tires are
made from petroleum derivatives. By reducing tire
mileage, we waste petroleum.
