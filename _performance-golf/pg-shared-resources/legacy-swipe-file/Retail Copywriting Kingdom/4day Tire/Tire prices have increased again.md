As costs go up, obviously our
prices have to go up.
. What happens, though, if our
costs go down? Would our prices
go down, too? Usually that
would be a foolish question.
Costs keep going up, not down.
Even if some costs did go
down, would any business
minded red-blooded American
tire dealer even think of
reducing some prices in a
generally rising price market?
Not just reducing them for one
day or one week in a Big Sale
Extravaganza, but reducing the
everyday selling price?
Well, that would depend on the
dealer, and the kind of business
he's trying to build.
Taming tire prices
On this latest round of price
changes at 4day our costs did go
down on some sizes of some
tires, so we reduced 225 prices.
That's because we're price
cutters, on purpose.
Unlike other businesses, we
invest zero effort in finding ways
to take a little extra profit.
Instead, we establish a profit
margin that's as low as we can
get it, and then look for ways to
keep increasing our sales by
reducing our costs and prices.
Our objective is growth. If we
can do twice as much business
but gross only l '12 times as much
profit, we're better off and so
are you.
When the tire manufacturers
adjusted their prices, their new
pricing structures resulted in
slight increases as well as
decreases in some tires and
some sizes.
