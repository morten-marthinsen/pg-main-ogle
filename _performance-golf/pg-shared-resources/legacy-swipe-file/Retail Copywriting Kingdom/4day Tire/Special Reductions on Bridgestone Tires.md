How to build a better tire
A 14" tire rotates over 8000 times in a
span of just 10 miles.
As your car rolls along the road, its
weight causes the tires to squeeze,
twist and flex out of shape.
This prevents the tread from staying
in firm contact with the road and
wastes a lot of energy.
Bridgestone's solution? Rolling
Contour Optimization Theory, or
RCOT. Their RCOT tires, like the S375,
are built in a shape that more closely
resembles a rolling tire under load.
Because RCOT tires already are
closer in shape to the shape they'll
assume in service, the amount of
distortion is minimized when the tire is
in motion so more of the tire stays in
contact with the road and less energy is
consumed.
The results are impressive. Every
area of tire performance is improved
from 3-10% with the RCOT design.
