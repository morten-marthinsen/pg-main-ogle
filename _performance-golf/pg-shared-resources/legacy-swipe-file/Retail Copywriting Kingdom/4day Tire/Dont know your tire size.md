How to read your sidewalls
On the sidewall of every tire is
its size. It will look something
like this: Pl95/60Rl4.
P means P-metric. More
about that in a moment. 195 is its
. width, sidewall to sidewall, in
millimeters.
60 is the aspect ratio. The
height, from the ground to the
bottom of the rim, is 60% of the
width, sidewall to sidewall. If no
aspect ratio is indicated (as in
175Rl4), then the aspect ratio is
80.
The R means radial. The next
number, the 14, tells you it will
fit a wheel rim 14" in diameter.
And so, what's a P-metrlc tire?
Until 1976, most US-made tires
were sized in inches. 825-15
meant a 81/4'' wide tire that fit a
15" rim. That progressed to the
Alpha-Numeric system. For
example, G78-15. G referred to
' -the load carrying capacity. 78
was the aspect ratio.

How Important
are speed ratings at 55 mph?
All European passenger car
tires and many U.S. tires have
an extra letter branded on their
sidewalls. These letters (S, T, H,
V or Z) designate the tire's
speed rating.
Tire speed ratings were estab
lished by the European Tire and
Rim Technical Organization to
ensure that the tires on a vehicle
will perform safely at that
vehicle's top speed.
Many customers ask what
purpose these speed ratings
serve in the U.S. where we have
a 55 mph speed limit.
The answer is that a speed
rating is an indication of a tire's
total performance, not just its
speed capability. When compar
ing two tires of the same size,
the tire with the higher speed
category will most likely have
the better handling and traction.
That means an extra margin
of control and security at any
speed.

an industry publication, a record

were shipped in the United States
in 1991. Nearly 14 million of these
were H-, V- and Z-rated tires.
