A little more air for radials
If you've got radial tires, there
are more advantages than disad
vantages in 2 to 4 pounds of
overinflation.
Car manufacturer's recom
mendations are established for
city driving without sustained
high speed or above average
loads. That's because ride
comfort is foremost in the car
maker's mind: in this case, at
the expense of treadwear and
performance.
We recommend adding 2 to 4
pounds to the car manufactur
er's figures, depending on the
driver's needs, provided it
doesn't exceed the maximum
pressure indicated on the
sidewall.
You'll get a slightly stiffer
ride, but you'll also get an extra
margin of safety at sustained

Horse sense about underlnflotlon
As you lose air pressure, under
inflation gradually produces a
mushier ride and reduces your
control of your vehicle.
Uneven contact with the road
surface weakens skid resistance.
Also, when a tire is underinfla
ted, the sidewalls flex in and out
excessively. This builds up inter
nal heat, a tire's deadly enemy.
Even minor road hazards can
damage an underinflated tire
internally. Then, a week or a
year later one or more of your
tires mysteriously fail.
Weather affects Ure pressure
For every 10 degrees the
temperature increases, air
pressure increases by one pound.
If you carried 28 lbs at 600,
come summer you could have 32
lbs at 100°.
Even at 600 an underinflated
tire can get very hot, so always
check your air pressure after
your car has been standing for
several hours.
