## How alignment


## affects tire wear

Every tire is designed to put
a solid flat footprint on the
pavement, with car weight
distributed evenly over every
square centimeter of the foot
print.
And the tread is intended
to roll freely, straight ahead,
These four diagrams are ex·
aggerated deliberately to get
the point across visually.
If one tire is going north
east and the other northwest,
you're not only going to
you're going to burn
scrub off tread rubber when
you drag due north, but also
up more
gasoline than you should.
Incorrect camber doesn't
affect gas mileage but it does
move the center of gravity to
the outside or inside of the
tread. This not only causes
the tread to wear out too
quickly on one side, but in·
creases your chances of skid·
ding in wet weather•
