Front-wheel drive tire eaters
Generally, front tires wear faster
than rear tires. Twice as fast if
you have front-wheel drive.
That's because the front tires
are subjected to-steering,
braking and the added weight of
the engine. And, on front-wheel
drive, they also do the work of
driving the car.
As a general rule, if th~
tread
depth on the front and rear tires
differs.by more than 2/32", it's
time to rotate.
Should radials be criss crossed?
Years ago, before the use of
radials, there was only one
rotation pattern.
Bias construction tires were
rotated in a cross-over pattern

Then, along came radials. Tire
manufacturers concluded that,
because of differences in con
struction, a radial that had been
run on one side of the car should
be run in the same direction by
keeping it on the same side.
That's how we still do it at
4day.
