This year many companies our size have put aside
their long range planning.
Times are tough, they say.
Survive. Get yours now. Any way you can.
Go after today's business today. Meet last year's
figures this year.
Sell hard. Promote hard.
Think up new sales gimmicks. Dust off some of
the old ones.
Not us.
At 4day we're still thinking ahead.
Tomorrow's objectives govern our actions today.
We're building a business here.
Everything we do is aimed at constantly acquiring
new customers.
And holding them.
This month we're starting our seventh year in busi
ness. We opened our first five stores in August, 1969.
Today we're 22 stores.
During our first 12 months, we averaged $4,443
per day in sales. For the past twelve moriths, sales
were $40,973 per day.
From the beginning we've tried to be different
from all other tire dealers.
In everything we do.
Almost every tire dealer runs temporary specials
on certain tires.
We decided to be real price cutters by running per
manent specials on everything we sell, 52 weeks a
year. No tires at regular prices, ever.
To be able to do that we had to establish tight
management procedures to accomplish a higher sales
volume per-man-hour than anyone else has been able
to accomplish.
A good example is our four day work week. It en
ables us to be open only during the busiest retail
hours, and to avoid overlapping shifts and wasteful
overtime expense during the slow periods of the
week.
Also we continue to believe there is room today
for better customer-oriented service, particularly in
the tire business. Our entire organization is tuned
to treat customers better than they are treated any
where else.
It works.
Even during the energy crisis, even durir.g the
general business slump, our sales have constantly
increased.
In total sales, as well as store by store sales.
Last month we opened store number 22 in San
Leandro, California.
We manage our growth with projections which are
reviewed and adjusted every three months. These
include a One Year Plan, a Two Year Plan, and a
Five Year Plan.

Ours isn't an impulse business. Drop-in sales from

We want the discriminating customer who likes
