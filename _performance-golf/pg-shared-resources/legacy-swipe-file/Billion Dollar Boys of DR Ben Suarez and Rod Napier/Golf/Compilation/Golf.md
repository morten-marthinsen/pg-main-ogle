The Natural Golf Swing
System uses the "Natural Palm
Grip™" and Swing techniques as
its base. It was developed by a
Chicago scientist and gives every
golfer the highest probability of
hitting strong, accurate shots con-
sistently. It eliminates confusing
and conflicting instructions by pro-

golf swing.
Moe Norman, who many

What is so fascinating is

Golf Swing was guided by instinct,
Natural Golfs by science.
The 4 basic principles of

Here is how the Natural
Golf Swing was developed. In
1984, a Chicago physicist, Jack
Kuykendall had the goal of cutting
12 strokes off his handicap. He
read the hottest books, bought the
best equipment, took lessons and
practiced 5 hours a day. Two years
later, he had gotten worse.
But he was determined to
find a better way, so he abandoned
the stone age "Vardon grip"
method and used his training as a
physicist to scientifically analyze
the golf swing and developed the
single axis "Natural Golf Swing
System™."
Golf instruction has been
basically the same for some 40
years. Instead of asking what is
the best way to hit with a stick a
round ball that's on the ground,
we continue trying to copy the per-
ceptions of the best players.
Unfortunately, this method has
failed to help golfers achieve their
potential. The average golf handi-
cap has remained static for more
than twenty four decades, and
fewer than 2.5% of all male golfers
will ever achieve a handicap of five
or less.
His findings were that the
classic instructions; conventional
finger grip, feet at shoulder width,
strong body coil, etc. were pre-
venting golfers from developing a
simple, easy to repeat swing.
Using scientific analysis of
the body's mechanical move-
ments, he developed a swing that
was in sync with the laws of
physics and thus nature, "The
Natural Golf Swing System™."
The club is held in the palms of
both hands, not the fingers. This
eliminates an extra angle between
the club shaft and the arms that
occurs in the fingertip grip. The
Natural Palm Grip™ eliminates the
angle allowing a natural, powerful,
consistent golf swing.
With the Natural Golf
Swing System™ you also use a
modified stance, take a unique
address position, stand farther
from the ball, shorten your back-
swing, do not clear your hips and
face the ball at impact

As you know, a major part of the

unloading. How your shaft loads and

swing. How much you benefit from

It has always been accepted that
the faster you swing, the stiffer the
shaft; and the slower, the more
flexible. But, that alone is not true.
Since the club is a spring system
with a weight (club head) on the end,
each club has a frequency, like a
tuning fork, that is measured in
cycles per minute. The stiffer the
shaft, the higher the frequency. Just
as each club has its own frequency,
each golfer's swing has a timing that
should match the club's natural
frequency. It is this natural
frequency that determines the
response of the club head from the
time of release, or maximum load,
back to a point where the club shaft
returns to a straight position. When
the club shaft returns to the straight
position, the club head obtains the
maximum speed due to shaft flex.
The pros have known this for
years. However, they have the luxury
of visiting corporate testing ranges
and mobile vans to have their swing
examined with all the latest, high-
tech gadgets.

loss of tremendous power.
In addition, you will hit off-line
shots because the club face is not
square at impact.
Remember, this is not conjecture
or opinion, rather the facts from
scientific tests and data analysis. The
shaft must return to the straight line
position at impact for maximum
distance and accuracy.
To achieve this position, it is
critical that you use the shaft flex

In order to identify a player's

swing at which a player reaches
maximum acceleration (not to be
confused with maximum club head
speed) needs to be determined.
Various players obtain this peak
acceleration at different times. Some
players initiate it immediately at the
top of the swing, while other players
start down slowly, then accelerate

accelerate more at the bottom of the
swing with wrist action. The
different swings have different time
lapses between maximum
acceleration and ball impact. Since
the goal is to achieve the impact of
maximum speed at ball impact, the
flexed shaft needs to return straight

swing frequency is determined by
the time between maximum
acceleration and ball impact.

frequency.

experiences include worst dates,
marriage episodes and common
behavior patterns of men.

discussing some of the disappointing
behaviors of men. Personally, I had
just received a ‘termination notice’

call ... no bottle of wine ... no last
supper! A classless e-mail! I was
horrified and turned to Maria, befud­

need to write a book!’ She whipped
out her Franklin Planner and began
taking copious notes. We jotted
down over 65 coping strategies, sur­
vival tips or other eccentricities of
men before we reached our destina­
tion -just 20 minutes away!"
Sude said, “We are seasoned
veterans on the battlefields of love.
We have turned ours and other
women’s romantic mishaps into cop­
ing strategies for any reader who has
been shocked to find an army of
frogs hopping across a room where
she was certain Price Charming once
stood.”
Ewing added, “We are not men
haters. But we just found it so funny
that, although our experiences were
different, there were such common
behavior traits among men. We then
started talking to other women about
their bad dates and experiences with
men and saw more and more of
these common denominators. It real­
ly compelled us to write this book.”
