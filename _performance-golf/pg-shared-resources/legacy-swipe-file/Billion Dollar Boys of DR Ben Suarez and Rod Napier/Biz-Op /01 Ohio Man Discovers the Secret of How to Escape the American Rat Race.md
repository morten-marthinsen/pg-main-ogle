• (Canton, OH) The secret to making
a quick fortune in America has been
reduced to a simple 7 step system by
an entrepreneur from Canton, Ohio. It
requires little or no money, a minimum
of time and no elaborate plant or equip­
ment. In fact, you can do it in your
home or anywhere.
There are hundreds of rags to rich­
es stories each year in America.
However, this one is very unique.
• Most successful get-rich ventures
were spin-offs of a former business or
occupation. Or, someone was in the .
right place at the right time. You don't
usually find a guy getting rich who was
working for a big company, had no
money, had no experience outside his
specialized job at the company, and did
not even know where he was going to
start. You also don't find many people
making this much money this quick.
• The man who did it is Ben Suarez
who is a real, living, breathing human
being living in Canton, Ohio, the Pro
Football Hall of Fame city.
; As you fly into this medium sized
Midwestern town you can see that it's
a compact community surrounded by
farmland. As you drive from Akron-
Canton Airport on the I-77 Freeway
into town you can see the Pro Football
Hall of Fame just off the freeway to the
right. The Hall of Fame is situated in a
complex which also houses the city's
biggest high school, a field house, one
of the biggest natatoriums in the nation
and one of the biggest high school
football stadiums in the country, the
20,000-seat concrete-constructed
Fawcett Stadium. This is the same sta­
dium where ABC's Frank Gifford, Al
Michaels and Dan Dierdorf broadcast
the Hall of Fame Game each year.
; Suarez's neighbors here in Canton
call it a movie script. One of the neigh­
bors interviewed had this to say: "It
was unbelievable. One day he's driving
around in a rusted out Pontine station
wagon, living in an uncarpeted house
that didn't even have a color T. V., and
struggling to make ends meet like the
rest of us. The next day lie's driving in
a brand new luxury car, a luxury
motorltome. his house is like a palace,
and he's traveling all over the coun­
try."
A close friend of Suarez's com­
ments: "When he first started saying
he was going to think up a way to get
rich quick we all laughed and thought
he was crazy. Man we're not laughing
anymore. He wouldn't tell everybody
haw he was doing it for 3 years. I knew
he would eventually. Everyone who
makes a big accomplishment sooner or
later has to tell everyone how he did
it:':

For that answer I went right to the
horse's mouth, my main interview with

QUESTION: OK, Ben, so how in
the world did this all start.
' 'ANSWER: I was typical of a good
majority of Americans — working at a

chance for advancement. Big compa­
nies are immovable and impersonal.
I found out quick that moving up
had little to do with talent and hard
work and more to do with politics,
lodge membership, being a friend or
relative of a high executive, or riding
on the coattails of an executive and
hoping he ascends the organization. I
wanted out but I saw no way to escape
the proverbial rat race.
Then one Monday morning a trau­
matic last straw came. I had invented a
solution to a problem in 3 months that
the company's research division had
been trying to come up with for 5
years. I was told that morning that my
solution was being shelved because in
so many words it had "shown up" the
research division.
the whole premise on which this coun­

"Rick, I don't know how I am going to
do it or what I am going to do but I'm
going into something for myself and
become independently rich." That
point was the start of it all.

to lend you money to start a business. I
tried to start a couple small businesses
I'll never forget that afternoon

brother was the first to arrive. I was
standing in the middle of the drive

pondering my future. I started thinking
of applying elsewhere for a job. Then a
thought struck me. "Jobs, get a good
job, a better job, create more jobs," is

that could be started by renting the

I was also learning another impor­

else. When you consider preparation

the 8 hours on the job, and cleaning up

that takes 12 hours out of your day.
With 8 hours of sleep that only leaves

spent maintaining the house and car,

money, no experience or skills and the
system must make a lot of money
quickly. On the surface it sounded
impossible, to make an understatement.
But, when you're desperate you find
that necessity is indeed the mother of
invention.
Then another thing spurred me on.
I read an article by the Social Security
Administration. It related, "out of
