“Lindon’s Lazy Ironman
is a great way to get outside
and enjoy all of the amenities
Lindon City has to offer,” said
Heath Bateman, the city’s
parks and recreation director.
The challenge is being
offered through the city’s
recreation department and
the Healthy Lindon program.
It is open to all ages and will
run from June 2 through Aug.
9. Registration is now taking
place at www.lindoncity.org.
Participants do not need
to be Lindon residents. The
registration fee is $10, and
participants will receive a
Lazy Ironman shirt.
According to Bateman,
those who finish the chal-
lenge will also receive a red
apple lapel pin. The Healthy
Lindon logo is an apple.
People can earn additional
apple pins by participating in
other Healthy Lindon chal-
lenges throughout the year.
After earning five red apple
pins, participants can trade
them for a gold apple pin.
“We think it will be fun
seeing people wearing two
or three [red] apple pins,
and sometimes we will see a
gold apple pin and know that
person is really involved in
the Healthy Lindon lifestyle,”
Bateman said.
Other challenges in the
past have included a “Go for
Your Goal Olympic Chal-
lenge,” “Walk Every Park in
Lindon” and “Friends and
Family” challenge.
The purpose of the Healthy
Lindon program is to encour-
age residents to lead healthier
lifestyles, said Valarie Diehl,
program coordinator. The
program, headed by a com-
mittee of five people, has
a theme of “Every Little
Bit Counts.”
“Every little bit that you
do, whether it’s the Lazy
Ironman or healthy eating,
counts,” Diehl said.
In addition to the various
activities throughout the
year, there will be mid-day
classes beginning in the fall.
The classes will teach ways
to be more health conscious
and may include cooking,
weight training and other
healthy practices.
According to Diehl, there
are plenty of opportunities in
Lindon to stay fit and healthy.
“We have a pool and nice
trails in Lindon,” she said.
“Bike on our wonder-
ful Lindon Heritage and
Murdock trails, walk or jog
around the improved trails at
Hollow Park, Pioneer Park,
Pheasant Brook Park or the
new walking trail at Fryer
Park,” Bateman said.
For more information
about the Lazy Ironman and
other Healthy Lindon activi-
ties, visit www.lindoncity.
org and click on the Healthy
Lindon link.
