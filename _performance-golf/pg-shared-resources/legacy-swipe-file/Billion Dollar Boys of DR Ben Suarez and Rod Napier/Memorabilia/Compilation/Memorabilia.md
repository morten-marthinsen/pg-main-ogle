# Official and authentic TITANIC historical


# items now available for a limited time


# They are: 1) copies of the passenger and crew log, 2) only known photographs


# taken by Father Brown aboard Titanic, 3) exact reproduction of a newspaper


# found at the wreck site, 4) historic photos of the 4 living survivors at a first


# ever meeting in 1996, and 5) actual pieces of coal from aboard Titanic

By John Whitehead,
Media Services
(Canton, OH) Official and
authentic Titanic historical items
are finally being made available
to the public for a very limited
period of time and while certain
supplies last.
These historic items are
being made available by the U.S.
Commemorative Fine Art
Gallery, a division of SCI. SCI,
along with RMS Titanic Inc.,
financed and carried out the now
famous 1996 Titanic Expedition
and Cruise, as seen on The
Discovery Channel and a
National Geographic television
special. This expedition fully
illuminated the Titanic wreck for
the first time using newly devel-
oped million-dollar light towers.
The Expedition brought up many
artifacts from the wreck site and
attempted to raise a large part of
Titanic. The historic 1996
Expedition for the first time
allowed the public to view a
Titanic dive via two large cruise
ships.
The 1996 Expedition also
was historic because for the first
time all four of the then-living
Titanic survivors were brought
together to meet each other, and
at this meeting historic pho-
tographs were taken of these sur-
vivors.
RMS Titanic, Inc. is the
exclusive and official legal
guardian of the Titanic wreck as
determined by the U.S. Federal
Court, and SCI is the exclusive
owner of the copyrighted portrait
pictures taken of the survivors at
the 1996 Titanic Expedition.
In total, there are 5 separate
Titanic historical items being
made available to the public at
this time.
The first historical Titanic
item is a combination art repro-
duction and photo collection put
together in a dramatic wall-size
display. This historic print is
titled "Tribute to Titanic
Survivors" in honor of the 4 liv-
ing Titanic survivors in 1996 and
in recognition of their participa-
tion in the 1996 Titanic
Expedition. Along with the pho-
tos, the print contains a certified
commemorative drawing of
Titanic entitled "Fairwell to
Queenstown." For this drawing,
U.S. Commemorative Fine Art
Gallery commissioned world-
renowned Titanic historian and
artist Michael V. Ralph, who cre-
ated this special-edition, original
painting of RMS Titanic moored
off Queenstown, England, her
last port of call before embark-
ing on her fateful voyage. The 4
photos in the print are historic
portraits of 4 of the survivors;
Millvina Dean, Eleanor Shuman,
Edith Haisman (who since
passed away), and Michel
Navratil. For historical accura-
cy, each of these survivors auto-
graphs appear below their por-
trait photos, which are then
shown below the "Farewell to
Queenstown" painting. This col-
lector's piece is highly recom-
mended for framing and will
definitely be the centerpiece of
attention in any home or office.
The second historical item
being made available is a set of 4
of the only known photographs
taken aboard Titanic by a Jesuit
Priest, Father Brown, who
boarded Titanic at Southampton,
England, but for some reason
left the ship when she arrived at
her last port of call, Queenstown,
before embarking on the fateful
voyage. These 4 photos histori-
cally show key parts of Titanic
and passengers aboard Titanic.
The photos show: the main deck
and passengers; the main dining
room; the actual bridge of
Titanic, with the helmsman
wheel pictured in the center with
engine and other controls on
either side; and one of the first-
class rooms, showing a beautiful
mirrored dresser and the beds on
either side. These collector prints
are recommended for framing.
The third item now available
is an exact full-color copy of a
page of the April 10, 1912,
Southampton, England, newspa-
per that was printed the
day Titanic departed from
Southampton, England, on her
fateful voyage. This newspaper
was brought on board Titanic by
either a passenger or crew mem-
ber. This page of the newspaper
was found in relatively good
shape and readable at the Titanic
wreck site more than 80 years
later at the pitch-black, 12,500-
foot depth of the wreck. The
stories on this page will be of
great interest to history buffs as
they can actually read what was
going on in the world when
Titanic departed for her voyage
on that day in 1912. This historic
collector's copy is also recom-
mended for framing.
The fourth sensational histor-
ical item is a special edition
reproduction of the actual list of
passengers and crew members
who were aboard Titanic. Also
included in this book is informa-
tion on the number of first-class,
second-class, and steerage-class
passengers and crew members
on board; how many survived;
what happened to the survivors
in the following years; pictures
of crew members and passen-
gers; facts of the disaster; how
the wireless call for help was
sent; stories of the event from
survivors; the music played as
Titanic sank; a section drawing
of the giant Titanic; and much
more.
The final historic item is
truly sensational. It is an authen-
tic piece of actual coal that was
aboard Titanic.
Recovering the Titanic arti-
facts and coal was a costly and
difficult process. The French
Navy's deep-sea submarine that
was used has an inside diameter
of only 6.9 feet and takes two
hours to descend 2-1/2 miles to
the ocean's floor. It costs
approximately $100,000 per day
for each dangerous dive. The
titanium-alloy crew sphere of the
deep-sea submarine must with-
stand pressures of up to 6,000
pounds per square inch.
Six external lights enabled
the dive team to search the bot-
tom of the ocean for the ship's
artifacts and coal, and two robot-
ic arms were used to pick up the
items found, as the crew viewed
through portholes that measured
less than 5 inches in diameter.
Each individual piece of coal
from Titanic is attractively dis-
played in a museum-style case
with a custom-engraved brass
plaque with the name of the coal
patron honored as a conserva-
tionist.
This exceptional collection
of historical items will only
be available by the U.S.
Commemorative Fine Art
Gallery for a very limited period
of time or while certain supplies
last. The photo and art repro-
duction "Tribute to Titanic
Survivors," with the commemo-
rative drawing of Titanic entitled
