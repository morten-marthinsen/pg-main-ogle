Q: Why are L.E.D.
lightbulbs considered a
Green Technology?
A: The L.E.D. Decade
lightbulb is very environ-
mentally friendly. First, unlike
HID (High Intensity Dis-
charge) lamps, like the one you
probably use today, L.E.D.
lightbulbs contain no mercury.
In addition, the L.E.D.
Decade is RoHS (Restriction of
Hazardous Substances) com-
pliant and has been designed
to provide more than a decade
of completely maintenance-
free service.
That means no replacing
which means no waste of
money on burned out light-
bulbs.
Q: How often do you have
to replace the L.E.D. Decade
lightbulb?
A: The L.E.D. Decade does
not burn out like a standard
lamp lightbulb so individual
diodes do not need to be
replaced.
Instead, many, many years
down the road, the diodes
gradually produce lower output
levels over a very long period
of time.
And the best part is, if one
of the 90 individual L.E.D.’s
fails, it does not produce a
complete fixture outage.
Q: Why should people like
myself switch to the L.E.D.
Decade?
A: The latest studies show
that L.E.D. lights save
electricity because they are
extremely energy efficient.
In fact they are many times
more efficient that a normal
incandescent light and at least
twice that of a CFL lightbulb.
Therefore, the L.E.D.
Decade lightbulb will use about
10% of the electricity required
to burn a normal incandescent
light bulb.
This equates to the L.E.D.
Decade lightbulb lasting for a
minimum of 10 years if oper-
ated for 8 hours per day. In
that same amount of time, you
would use over 25 incan-
descent lights or 5 CFL lights.
End of interview.

Incandescent bulbs create light by
passing electricity through a metal
filament until it becomes so hot
that it glows.
Incandescent bulbs release 90% of
their energy as heat.
In a CFL, electricity is driven
through a tube containing gases.
This produces ultraviolet light that
gets transformed into visible light
by the fluorescent coating on the
inside of the tube.
A CFL releases about 80% of its
energy as heat and contains
harmful mercury.
L.E.D. lighting uses light emitting
diodes to produce light very
efficiently. A small amount of heat
is released backwards, into a heat
sink, in a well-designed product;
L.E.D.s are basically cool to the
touch.
