Q. How does evapo-
rative cooling work?
A. If you wet your arm
and blow air on it, your skin
feels cooler. That's evapora-
tive cooling. In an evapora-
tive cooler, outside air is
pulled through moist pads
where it is cooled by evapo-
ration. Evaporation is an
endothermic reaction; it
absorbs heat. So the air that
goes through the pads has
had heat absorbed out of it.
This cooler air is then circu-
lated through the room by a
blower.

50%. If using the waterless
Cooling Chamber, the air is
cooled at least 15 degrees
and humidity is not an
issue.
