# crack possession

BY CHRISTY GUTOWSKI
Daily Herald Staff Writer
Two 36-year-old men have been
charged with possession of crack
cocaine at a Prospect Heights con-
dominium complex, officials said.
Donald Hoey, of 834 E. Old Wil-
low Road, Prospect Heights, and
Charles D. Steinmetz, of 9438 Iron-
wood Drive, Des Plaines, were
arrested Tuesday and each
charged with felony unlawful pos-
session of a controlled substance,
police said.
Authorities confiscated 26.3
grams of rock cocaine, including a
small amount of powder cocaine,
and related drug paraphernalia
after searching the men while offi-
cers investigated a possible bur-
glary in the Willow Heights condo-
minium complex.
Police said they found 13.8 grams
of cocaine on Hoey and 12.5 grams
of cocaine on Steinmetz.
Police arrested Hoey shortly
after spotting him walking out of a
door near an apartment that had
been broken into, police reports
showed. Hoey later admitted to the
breaking into the unit, which
belongs to his ex-girlfriend, police
said.
Police said they have not
charged Hoey with burglary.
Steinmetz was taken into custody
two hours later when he showed up
at the complex, police said.
Both suspects were being held
Wednesday in Cook County jail.
Hoey was being held on $20,000
bond and Steinmetz on $15,000
bond, officials said.
Both men were scheduled to
appear July 12 in court in Rolling
Meadows.

# Mount Prospect man


# charged in boat death

BY LARRY SMITH
Daily Herald Staff Writer
A 25-year-old Mount Prospect man
was charged Wednesday in the
death of a 16-year-old girl in a boat-
ing accident.
Jacek Wolski, 914 S. Edward St.,
was charged in Chicago with invol-
untary manslaughter and recklessly
operating a boat under the influence
of alcohol, a Cook County state's
attorney's spokesman said.
Wolski was operating a 12- to 14-
foot boat near Diversey Harbor in
Chicago Monday when the engine
stalled just as the boat was leaving
the harbor.
During the attempt to restart the
engine, the boat dipped into Lake
Michigan, began to take on water
and sink, said officer David Stran-
berg, of the Chicago Police Marine
Unit.
There were seven people on the
boat, which was built to handle four
passengers. There were only two life
preservers on board, police said
The 16-year-old girl, Inga
Olszewskaya of Chicago, was the
only one of the seven passengers
who couldn't make it back to shore,
which was 50 to 75 yards away,
