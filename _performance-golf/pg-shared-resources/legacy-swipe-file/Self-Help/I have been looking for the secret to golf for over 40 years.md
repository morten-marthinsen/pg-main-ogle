Buddy’s discovery is not some
radically different stance, grip or
swing. Instead, it is a simple approach
to the traditional swing that allows
you to easily strike the ball with a
square club face with a natural free
flow ing swing.
Buddy gives you the secret to
unlocking power you never thought
you had. (I-have seen Buddy, who is
54 years old, 5’8” tall and 135 pounds,
effortlessly hit 270+ yard drives.)
He gives you a simple method to
achieve much greater accuracy and
control.
Most importantly, he gives you the
ability to consistently hit great shots
and eliminate those wayward shots
that can destroy a good round.
It takes only minutes to learn and
requires no special athletic ability or
equipment.
After reading through the book
only once, I hit some of the best shots
of my life.
At clinics, Buddy has seen ama­
teurs dramatically improve their
swings after just a few minutes.
I saw Buddy explain his technique
to a fellow Senior tour player and
straighten out his swing almost imme­
diately.
Forget all the junk you have stored
in your mind. (Keep the arm straight,
tuck your elbow, pull down, drive the
knees, keep the head still, etc )
Paralysis by analysis is the term.
With all these swing thoughts it is
a wonder any of us can even hit the
ball, and when we hit a bad shot we
usually have no idea as to why.
Buddy shows you how to quit
swinging mechanically. He instead
shows you how to use your own natur­
al ability and natural swing. You are
not trying to exactly copy his swing or
some other pro’s.
While there certainly will be close
similarities, you are not trying to force
your mind and body to do something
they cannot.
First, following his advice on the
basics, particularly grip, alignment and
square, Buddy puts you in perfect
position to let your swing flow power­
fully and naturally.
Then CENTER-LINE is the key to
everything else.
Buddy gives you two movements
and two thoughts, one to start the
backswing and one to start the forward
swing, that make everything else hap­
pen automatically and naturally.
You do this and naturally and
smoothly make your backswing.
That’s all you think about. You let
everything else happen naturally.
Then you do this to start the for­
ward swing and everything else fol­
lows.
Yes, it is really that easy.
