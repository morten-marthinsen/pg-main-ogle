## The brain in your belly

You've probably said things like, "''ve got a gut feeling ... " or "I can't stomach the
thought ... " countless times in your life. But you may not have realized just how
right you were.
Expressions like these reflect the fact that the digestive system has a brain all its
own, one that is intimately connected to the rest of the nervous system, yet also an
independent locus of command.
Scientists have discovered that this "brain in the belly" - known in the scientific
world as your enteric nervous system, or ENS- contains over 100,000,000
neurons. That's more than the number of nerve cells in the spinal cord.
The complex network of neurons and neurochemicals that make up your ENS
can sense and control events in the digestive tract. It can also, remarkably, sense and
respond to events in other parts of the body.
What's more, researchers have found that the flow of neural traffic from the ENS
to the head-brain is much greater than from the head-brain to the ENS: It's not your
head that tells your digestive system what to eat and how to process it ... its the
other way around!
