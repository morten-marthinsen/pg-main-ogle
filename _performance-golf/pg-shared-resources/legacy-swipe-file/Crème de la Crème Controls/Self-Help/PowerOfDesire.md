Let me give you an example that will show you how this amazing Conquering Force

Once, a university professor came to me and told me he wanted to become a black
belt in karate (this was when I was teaching karate). He told me it had been his goal for
over 15 years, and for the first time, he was finally doing something about it.
He wanted to know how long it would take to make his goal a reality. I told him if he
wanted to achieve that distinguished rank in karate, he would have to dedicate at least
one hour a day for four years.
The professor was clearly disappointed. After a moment, he said, "I had no idea it
would take that kind of effort." He walked away, and he never again pursued karate.
He had committed his wish to paper and somehow hoped it would magically happen,
but he hadn't bothered to learn what it takes, let alone start on the path. Once he
discovered how much work, commitment, and time would be required to achieve a black
belt, he lost interest and gave up.
One week later, Mark Porath, a 14-year old boy, approached me and asked the same
question. When I told him it would take two hours a day and five years of his life, he
said, "Is that all? When can I start?"
Five years later, Mark was one of the best black belts I had ever seen. He went on to
become state champion, winning nearly every tournament he entered. Mark never wrote
down that he wanted to be a black belt- he just wanted it with all his heart and went
after it, not letting obstacles stand in his way including a major physical challenge.

# What made the difference

Mark Porath and the professor both said they wanted to become black belts. They

To figure out why, let me tell you more about Mark's situation.
