# How to TRANSCEND all LIMITATIONS

When you are inspired by some great purpose or some extraordinary project,
all of your thoughts break their bonds. Your mind transcends limitations, your
consciousness expands in every direction, and you find yourself in a new and a great
and a wonderful world. Dormant forces, faculties, and talents come alive, and you
discover yourself to be a greater person by far than you ever dreamed yourself to be.
All you have to do is become inspired by moving into spirit, into the higher
frequencies. And when you go there into that state of inspiration, you don't ever
think about money. You don't ever think about what ails you, or how others respond
to you.
For example, everywhere the Buddha went he had followers as well as people who
were critical of him. His critics would goad him and insult him, and every time he
would respond with love and kindness.
One day one of his students said to him, "For days and days, these people have
been so critical and so ugly in their reactions toward you, and always you still react
with kindness and love. How do you do that? How does that work?"
The Buddha responded, "If someone offers you a gift and you do not accept that
gift, to whom does it belong?" He stayed in spirit, he stayed inspired, and each time
he responded with love, he nullified the lower vibrations of his critics.
As another example, in 19th-century England, a man named George Mueller
started and ran several orphanages. What made these orphanages unique is that
Mueller decided to depend on God alone to provide for the needs of his children.
He would not tell people if he was in need of money. No matter how pressing the
need, George simply renewed his prayers and stayed in spirit, and either money or
food always came in time to save the situation.
One morning the plates and cups and bowls on the table were empty. There was no
food in the larder and no money to buy food. The children were standing, waiting for
their morning meal, when Mueller said, "Children, you know we must be in time for
school." Lifting his hand he said, "Dear Father, we thank Thee for what Thou art
going to give us to eat."
There was a knock on the door. The baker stood there ... and said, "Mr. Mueller, I
couldn't sleep last night. Somehow I felt you didn't have bread for breakfast and the
Lord wanted me to send you some. So I got up at 2 a.m. and baked some fresh bread
and have brought it." Mueller thanked the man. No sooner had this transpired when
there was a second knock at the door. It was the milkman. He announced that his milk
cart had broken down right in front of the orphanage and he would like to give the
children his cans of fresh milk so he could empty his wagon and repair it.
