# Clifton, Chancellor


# Wed At Texarkana

Lila Marlene Chancellor
of Texarkana, Texas,
became the bride of John
Scott Clifton of Texarkana
in an evening ceremony
Aug. 27, at First United
Methodist Church in Tex­
arkana. The bride is the
daughter of Mr. and Mrs.
G W “Bill" Chancellor of
Laurel The groom is the
son of Mr. and Mrs. Robert
Scotty Clifton of Pine Bluff,
Ark.
The double-ring ceremo­
ny was performed by Dr.
Bdwin Dodson before a
background of palms,
magnolia foliage and can­
delabra. David Dean of
Texarkana served as or­
ganist. Vocalists were Pearl
Martindale and Buddy
Crawford, both of Tex­
arkana.
The bride was given in
marriage by her father. She
wore a gown of crystal
white gossamer silk or­
ganza embroidered with
silk Venise and English
laces. The bodice of English
net was overlaid with lace
and scrolled with pearls.
The open portrait neckline
was encircled with lace.
The back of the bodice
featured a deep yoke of
English net outlined with
lace.
Bishop sleeves were of
sheer organza appliqued
with lace and had wide cuffs
of English net at the wrists.
'Fhe A line skirt was edged
at the hemline with Venise
lace and formed a cathedral
train. Vertical rows of lace
on the train ended in a deep
ruffle of crystal pleating.
The bride’s camelot cap
was overlaid with silk
Venise lace and beaded with
seed pearls. The cap held a
cathedral-length veil of silk
illusion appliqued with
matching motifs and edged
with lace.
The bride carried a
hand-crocheted lace fan
decorated with ivory silk
roses and peach carnations
and tied with peach and
cream ribbon. Crocheted
wedding bells and tiny silk
flowers cascaded in ribbons
from the fan. Wedding
flowers were designed and
handmade by the bride’s
mother. The bride also car­
ried a linen handkerchief
handmade for her by Mrs.
Т.К. Byers of Orangeburg,
S.C.
Patricia Craven of
Memphis served as maid of
honor. Bridesmaids were
Billie McCord and Katy
Chancellor of Laurel, the
bride’s sisters; and Carol
Perkins of Texarkana, Tex­
as.
Attendants wore identical
gowns of peach silk chiffon
over taffeta. Gowns had
fitted bodices forming a
“V" and tiny rolled straps.
Sheer floats ended in a point
at the front and back. Tea-
length skirts had
handkerchief hemlines.
Attendants carried
hand-crocheted fans deco­
rated with peach silk roses,
peach and cream ribbons
falling in knotted stream­
ers. They wore circlets of
tiny silk flowers in their
hair.
Robert Scotty Clifton of
Pine Bluff served his son as
best man. Groomsmen were
Paul Clifton of Texarkana,
Texas, the groom’s brother;
Bill Parks of Lake Charles,
La.; and Chuck Milstead of
Natchez. Ushers were Greg
Williams of Reform, Ala.;
and Mike Chancellor of
Laurel, the bride’s brother.
