# Mealtime

by Habeeb Salloum
“Spring arrives with trees still bare,
The farmers work with special care
To tap the sweet natural fare,
It’s maple syrup time.”
These poetic words by Elaine McDougall were
on my mind as I made my way to attend The
World’s Largest One-Day Maple Syrup Festival
in Elmira, Ont. which proclaims itself Ontario’s
maple sugar capital. Soon we were in the midst
of a sugar-maple forest with sap dripping into
buckets attached to almost every tree. In a few
minutes we were in the sugar shack, listening to
a syrup maker relating the story of maple syrup
while he kept an eye on the boiling sap.
Cultivating this natural sweet was inherited
by the early settlers from the aboriginals who
lived in southern Ontario and Quebec, and the
northeastern region of the U.S.A. In all these
areas of North America, the most robust of
the seven species of sugar maple trees, Acer
saccharum (the true maple sugar tree) is found.
Canada accounts for 70 per cent of the world’s
maple-syrup production, and 90 per cent comes
from Quebec.
As I walked away after purchasing a gallon,
the words of the friendly saleswoman rang in my
ears: “Enjoy our syrup. It’s Ontario’s liquid gold.
It’s the best!”
Maple Flavoured Baked Chicken
Serves 8
2 tablespoons ground mustard seeds
8 cloves garlic, crushed
5 tablespoons maple syrup
2 teaspoons powdered ginger
1 1/2 teaspoon salt
4 tablespoons olive oil
4 pounds chicken drumsticks
Flour for dredging
2 eggs whisked with 1 tablespoon water
2 1/2 cups breadcrumbs
Preheat oven to 400 degrees.
Place mustard, maple syrup, garlic, lemon
juice, thyme, pepper, cumin, ginger, salt and
2 tablespoon of the oil in a large bowl then
thoroughly combine. Add chicken and coat
evenly. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator
for one hour, turning the chicken over every 15
minutes.
Combine breadcrumbs and remaining 2
tablespoons of the oil. Dredge the drumsticks
with the fl our, then lightly coat with egg. Coat
each drumstick in the breadcrumbs and place in
a well-greased casserole, side by side. Cover.
Bake until golden for about 1 1/2 hours.
Serve with mashed potatoes.
