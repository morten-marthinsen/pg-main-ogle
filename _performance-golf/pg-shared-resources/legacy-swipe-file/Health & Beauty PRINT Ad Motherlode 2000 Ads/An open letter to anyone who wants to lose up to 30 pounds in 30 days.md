Guitarist Robert Cray takes off his
shoes, his socks and his blues man
label for his debut on Rykodisc The
result is a soulfest Cray assembled
his band in a Nashville studio and
produced a fine rhythm and blues
record that showcases his sweet
voice more than his playing. As if to
give the record an extra measure of
credence, Cray dips into the soul
vaults to bring new life to Mack
Rice's "24-7 Man" and "Won't You
Give Him (One More Chance)," a
1964 hit for Soloman Burke. After
13 years with Mercury Records, Cray
headed to the independent Rykodisc
and used the occasion to give full
voice to the soul roots that have
always been a part of his music. His
six originals are right at home with
the soul covers.
"Borders" (Red House
Records) - Cliff Eberhardt
Songwriter Cliff Eberhardt
explores boundaries on his second
record for Minnesota-based Red
House Records. There's a rawness
to his voice and a sparseness to his
guitar playing that sharpens the
emotion of his well-written lyrics.
Eberhardt also offers a haunting
Civil War account of the ravages of
battle on those with no reason to
fight in "The Wrong Side of the
Line." There are some quirky ele-
ments that mar "Borders," such as
the lyric bridge in "Lines," But it's a
heartfelt record sung with passion.
