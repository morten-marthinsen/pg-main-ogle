and swirl it around in the mouth for better
absorption.

5. Go for a workout
Exercise fills the body with endorphins – the
happy chemicals – and yields a natural glow.
Sweating during a workout is good for your
skin as it escorts out toxins.
6. Don’t worry, be happy
When you worry, it shows on your face; it
creates worry lines or wrinkles. Think happy
thoughts to bring out the beauty from within
and instant radiance.
7. De-stress daily
Stress is ageing so learn how to manage it.
Find the time to relax and de-stress. Book a
pampering treatment after a stressful day to
help you unwind and get a good night’s sleep.
8. Fight wrinkles with supplements
The best way to ward off wrinkles naturally
is to use antioxidant supplements. The
most important skin nutrient, vitamin A, is
responsible for improving collagen and elastin
production which keeps the skin plump and
supple. Vitamin C is found in many anti-ageing
creams as it is essential for the production
of collagen. Vitamin E makes a great natural
moisturiser. Zinc is essential for skin repair
and reproduction. Essential fatty acids help
keep the skin soft and reduce inflammation.
Look for a good antioxidant formula to get the
maximum benefit from their synergistic effect.

9. Avoid skin enemies
Avoid refined sugar at all costs as it leads to
premature ageing and causes acne and dull
complexion. Cut down your caffeine intake.
Caffeine is a diuretic which dehydrates the skin
causing it to lose elasticity and look sallow.
Limit your alcohol intake. Drinking too much
alcohol dehydrates the body and generates
toxins which damage the skin. Smoking
prematurely ages the skin and increases wrinkle
formation. Kick the habit, and your skin will
thank you for it.
10. Try beauty acupuncture
Acupuncture facial is the latest anti-ageing
treatment that has a huge A-list celebrity
following. It increases the flow of oxygen and
nutrients to the face reducing the signs of
ageing and bringing a youthful glow to the face.
It is a great natural alternative to Botox! ¨
