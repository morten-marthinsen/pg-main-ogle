Millions of North Americans spend their nights interrupted by their loved ones
heavy snoring and loud gasps for air. They lie awake listening to their sleep
partner’s breathing abruptly end and anxiously wonder if and when it will start up
again. This serious life-threatening breathing-disorder is known as Obstructive
Sleep Apnea.
Being a victim of sleep apnea himself, Patrick Strong DD, FCAD knew that this
was no way for people to spend their nights. A local denturist for nearly 30 years,
Patrick became quickly fascinated by this condition afflicting both men and
women and started treating sleep-disordered patients twelve years ago with
dental sleep appliances.
Although dental sleep appliances were effective and gaining popularity,
they also had one major drawback: their high breakage rate. Unsatisfied
with the 50% breakage rate that came with dental sleep appliances,
Patrick was determined to find a better, durable solution for sleep
deprived patients. While researching, Patrick noticed that about half
the people who suffer from sleep apnea also grind their teeth at
night, which was helping wear down and fracture their dental
appliance. With the support of his wife, Mary-Lou Strong,
Patrick eagerly put his ideas to work himself.
