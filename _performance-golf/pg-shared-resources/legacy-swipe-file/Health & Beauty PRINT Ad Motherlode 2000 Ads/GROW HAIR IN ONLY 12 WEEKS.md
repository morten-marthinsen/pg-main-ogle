FIGHT BACK: STRATEGIES FOR SAVINGS
You can find bargain feres if you're willing to shop around. Tom Parsons,
the publisher of Best Fares, a monthly roundup of travel discounts, offers
these tips (prices were current at press time):
WATCH FOR CROSS-PROMOTIONS. "There's not an airline I can think
of that doesn't have promotional coupons out there right now." Coupons
tor reduced fares turn up everywhere. Parsons says he recently found a
$129 Northwest Airlines companion coupon in a Golf magazine he was
reading aboard a Continental flight
SPUT CITIES. Instead of an expensive non-stop flight, take connecting
flights through a low-cost city in between. For example: Absent a fare war,
a non-stop flight from Dallas-Fort Worth to St. Louis is $326. But you
could fly Southwest from Dallas-Fort Worth to Little Rock for $79, then
from Little Rock to St Louis for another $79—and save $168.
CONSIDER ALTERNATIVE CITIES. The standard airfare from Chicago
to Cincinnati is about $260. But a ticket from Chicago to Louisville (less
than two hours' drive from Cincinnati) might run as tow as $42.
INFORM YOUR TRAVEL AGENT. Your determination to save money
counts for little unless you let your travel agent know what you're willing
to go through to cut costs. Tell the agent whether you would consider
alternative airports, non-direct flights and so on. And request a full check
^smaller, upstart airlines' schedules and prices; they sometimes don't
show up on the computerized schedule the agent scans in the office.
