Shocking Discovery
Recently, radiologists and
radiographers using extremely
strong imaging technology began
noticing vast fields of bright white
objects on magnetic resonance
imaging (MRI) scans.
These unidentified bright
objects, or UBOs, are suspected
harbingers of age-related cognitive
decline.
Researchers believe these
objects may indicate that large
portions of the brain have become
oxygen-starved.
The presence of these bright
spots can go unnoticed for years.
Even then, the signs emerge
only very slowly over time, and are
often confused with ‘just getting
older.’
UBOs have been found in 40
to 50 percent of healthy adults over
the age of 50.
Epidemic Proportions?
More alarming, recent studies
published in the Current Cardiology
Reports and Postgraduate Medical
Journal estimate this memory-
robbing phenomenon may be
present in up to 95 percent of
adults over 50.
According to Dr. Arnold
Bresky,a Preventive Gerontologist
in the field of Behavioral
Neurology, “This is not a good
sign at all. A healthy brain needs
a constant, uninterrupted supply
of oxygen.”
The human brain weighs three
pounds (one to three percent of
total body weight) explains Dr.
Bresky, but uses 15-20% of the
body’s total inhaled oxygen.
“When oxygen is restricted in
the brain,” adds Dr. Bresky, “bad
things can happen.”
Blood Flow ‘Cut Off
at the Knees’
In one study, those with
UBOs were found to have just
39.7 percent of the volume of
blood flow in affected regions of
their brains compared with
others.
Other serious outcomes for
thoseaffected are a two-to-three-
fold increase in risk of having
automobile incidents.
Healthy people with less than
optimum blood flow to the brain
may also have a greater risk of
more serious health concerns.
In one study, those with
UBOs showed a 46 percent
increase in reduced lifespan.

The connection between low
brain oxygen and mind and
memory problems was first
made years ago by US brain
researcher, Joshua Reynolds.
Reynolds suspected that the
mental ‘hiccups and cognitive
pauses’ of aging were signs of a
potentially correctable state trig-
gered by low oxygen levels due
to reduced blood flow.
So he looked for a safe, effec-
tive way to help improve blood

He and fellow researchers

greater amounts of oxygen.

has profound and eye-opening

seen as an encouraging

take on debt.

borrowing can fuel higher
consumer spending, which
accounts for 70 percent of
economic activity.
The February decline
in credit card borrowing
marked the third decrease
in the past four months.
