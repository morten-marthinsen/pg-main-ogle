By Paul Turenne
THIS is one seniors’ bus tour that has nothing to do with
golf, gambling, or the Grand Canyon.
Last year, Age and Opportunity hosted its first-ever
seniors’ housing tours, where people were driven by bus to
different seniors’’ residences to have a first-hand look at
their potential new homes. There were four tours last year,
and they were such a success that the organization has
planned another 13 for this year, with the first one of the
season scheduled for April 27 in St. Boniface and Southdale.
“The purpose is to expose seniors to different housing
options; to give them an idea of the alternatives that are
available to them when they move out of their home,” said
Christa Durhack, outreach co-ordinator with Age and
Opportunity’s St. Vital Senior Centre.
Seniors’ housing ranges from fully independent apart-
ment complexes to assisted living residences where work-
ers do chores for you to subsidized housing and life lease
blocks. The tour does not include personal care homes.
“People definitely need to look into their options,” said
Durhack. “They need to know they’re not necessarily going
to get in tomorrow because some places have waiting lists.
They need to ask themselves whether or not they need
assistance. They need to know if they’re looking for some-
thing permanent or transitional. It also depends what their
income level is. The places I choose are open to varying lev-
els of income.”
Last year Durhack ran two tours, and they were so pop-
ular she added two more. At the end of the year she had a
list of 100 people waiting to sign up for future tours. “There
is a need for this. There are a lot of questions and there is
a lot of curiosity about what’s out there,” she said.
Many of the people who attend the tours either have no
family members in the city to tour residences with on their
own, or they have no way of getting around to see the build-
ings, said Durhack. Many people are also intimidated or shy
about touring the facilities.
“This way they have someone to go with and someone to
ask their questions for them.”
With the exception of one tour in East Kildonan, all of
the tours will take place in the south end of the city, tour-
ing residences in St. Boniface, St. Vital, Fort Garry, Fort
Richmond, and Southdale.
Each tour can accommodate 24 people and costs $15,
which includes a lunch at one of the residences.
The April 27 tour will include stops at Chez Nous, Cather-
ine Place, Saint Peter’s and Paul’s Manor, and Villa Beliv-
eau. Call 956-5734 for more information or to register for
a tour.
