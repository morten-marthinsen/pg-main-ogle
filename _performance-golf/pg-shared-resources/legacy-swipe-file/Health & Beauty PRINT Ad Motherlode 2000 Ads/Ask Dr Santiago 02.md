“A smile is the light in your window that tells others that there
is a caring, sharing person inside.”
-Denis E. Waitley
Several years ago, when makeover shows first started appearing on televi-
sion, you may have noticed an increasing interest in cosmetic dentistry. Den-
tists certainly did! Hair, make-up, and clothing aside, there is simply nothing
as powerful or as emotionally uplifting as a beautiful smile. You may be con-
sidering a smile enhancement of your own, in hopes of looking more healthy
and youthful. But, what you may not know is that it takes an artistic touch to
create a pleasing, natural-looking smile.
Consider what a smile is for a moment…adults have between 28 and 32 teeth
(depending on the number of wisdom teeth), and in an attractive smile, every
tooth falls perfectly into place. The size, shape, and color of each individual
tooth has a role in the overall appearance of one’s smile, and therein lies the
complexity of cosmetic dentistry. A cosmetic dentist designs and builds a
smile much like an architect– planning in advance the ways various treatment
options will work together in concert. Porcelain veneers, cosmetic bonding,
crowns, bridges, orthodontics , gum lifts and whitening treatments are a few of
the “building blocks” available, and modern technology ensures both gentle
treatment and realistic results.
Creating a beautiful smile is truly a science and an art. In addition to being
beautiful, a smile must also function properly. A cosmetic dentist must also be
a restorative expert during the treatment process, identifying existing func-
tional problems and correcting them. The upper and lower teeth must fit to-
gether in a very specific way, and the margin for error is small. If you have a
restoration or tooth that breaks, this may be a consequence of a functional
problem. In fact, poor alignment can also lead to jaw pain and headaches.
These are some of the issues that your dentist will take into account when de-
signing your unique treatment plan.
The ideal way to create a beautiful and natural smile is to preserve as much of
your natural teeth as possible. Your dentist will suggest the treatment plan
that minimizes preparation or removal of natural, healthy tooth structure.
The resulting smile will look better and will last longer, giving you years
of enjoyment. So, if you hide your teeth when you speak, or hesitate to laugh,
consider the freedom you could experience with cosmetic dentistry.
You won’t regret it. I don’t! Every day I appreciate the beautiful smile dentistry
has given me!
