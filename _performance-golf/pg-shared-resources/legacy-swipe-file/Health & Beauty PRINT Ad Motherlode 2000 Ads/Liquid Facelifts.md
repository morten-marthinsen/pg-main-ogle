# LIQUID FACELIFTS


# Look younger and refreshed without surgery.

Dr. Jenkins
By PAULA VESS
Regional Marketing Group
SHELBY - Going under the knife
is no longer a requirement in
the search for younger looking
skin. Non-surgical cosmetic
treatments that restore a more
youthful appearance are more
accessible than ever.
These treatments can be
performed in the physician’s
office with no anesthetic
required, and are less expensive
than surgical
procedures.
The effects are
temporary,
however, and must
be maintained
every few months.
At Cleveland
Plastic and Hand
Surgery plastic
surgeon Nelson Jenkins, MD,
offers two of the most popular
methods of cosmetic, non­
surgical treatments - botulinum
toxin type A injections, and
dermal filler injections.
These two types of injections
can be used independently or
in combination to plump up
and smooth out crow’s feet,
frown lines, parentheses around
the mouth, and more. “These
treatments are sometimes
referred to as liquid facelifts,”
said Dr. Jenkins. “They are
popular non-surgical treatments
for people who want to look
younger or simply less stressed
and more refreshed.*’
Injectable botulinum toxin type
A is used to improve the look
of moderate to severe wrinkles
between the eyebrows, on the
forehead, and around the eyes.
During a treatment session, Dr.
Jenkins uses a thin needle to
inject the botulinum toxin into
the specific muscles that cause
these lines. These treatments are
recommended for adults between
the ages of 18 and 65.
A dermal filler is a substance
Dr. Jenkins injects just below
the skin’s surface to smooth
away wrinkles caused by aging,
usually around the mouth and
nose. There may be a little
swelling or bruising after a
dermal filler treatment. To
minimize these effects, keep
the head elevated and do not
exercise for 24 hours, and apply
ice to the treated area.
These types of quick, easy, less
expensive facial rejuvenation
treatments are gaining
popularity, especially among
busy women. “Each treatment
session takes only about 20 - 30
minutes,” said Dr. Jenkins, “and
there’s practically no down time
so busy women can have the
treatments and get back to their
medications, prescription and

all medical conditions you have,
and allergies.
Anyone who has had an allergic
reaction to any botulinum toxin
product or is allergic to the
ingredients in the particular
product Dr. Jenkins uses, should
not take the treatment. Anyone
who has a skin infection at the
planned injection site should not
have a treatment.
People who have a history of
cold sores, fever blisters,
or canker sores must be
pretreated with anti-viral
medication before having dermal
filler injections.
