Popcorn on the cob
Popcorn, our fastest-growing snack
food, has added a new punch to its pop:
microwave popcorn on the cob.
A quick zap in the microwave turns
a plain, yellow ear into fluffy white
popcorn and, according to retailers,
makes bigger, fluffier and tastier ker­
nels without oil or salt.
Some of the brands popping up in
supermarkets, gourmet shops and vid­
eo outlets include Popping-Corn on
the Cob, packaged six to seven ears per
jar ($9.95 to $12.98), yielding an aver­
age 36 to 42 cups of popped corn.
Squire Boone Village of New Albany,
Ind., already has sold a million jars.
Corny Brothers Popcorn on the Cob
(three ears per package; $3.95) and
Texas Microwave Popcorn on the Cob
(two ears per package; $1.99) are two
other brands.
