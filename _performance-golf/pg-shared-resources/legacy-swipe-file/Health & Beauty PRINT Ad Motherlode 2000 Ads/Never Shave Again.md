Yes! People who do manual
labor, such as construction
work, have long been known
for wearing down their
fingerprints, but you may
be surprised to learn that
many other occupations also
have a similar effect,
although for different
reasons. Musicians such as
guitarists can abrade their
prints into oblivion; typists
may wear them down from
so much keyboarding. Even
surgeons sometimes rub off
their prints from washing
their hands repeatedly.The
fingerprints don’t disappear
permanently, but they may
not be recognized by new
high-tech equipment, such
as security devices, at least
not while you’re still doing
the same kind of work.
