What Is Peripheral Neuropathy?
Neuropathy has several different
causes.Among the most common are
diabetes and side effects from chemo
therapy. Medical news today classi-
fies peripheral neuropathy as damage
to nerve axons leading to pain and
numbness in the hands and/or feet.
Pain from peripheral neuropathy can
be debilitating and is often described
as a tingling or burning sensation.
Studies have shown that upwards of
50% of individuals suffering from
diabetes will also suffer from some
form of associated neuropathy. Sen-
sation changes in the limbs can be
dangerous in that they can lead to the
formation of ulcerations which can get
infected and may even lead to ampu-
tation of the limb.
