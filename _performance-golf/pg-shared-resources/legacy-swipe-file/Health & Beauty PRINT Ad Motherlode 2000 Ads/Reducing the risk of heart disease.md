# Heart disease remains a killer

If you're like most of us, you're probably looking
for ways to defend yourself against the risk of
developing heart disease. That's because coronary
heart disease remains the #1 threat to our health
in the U.S. With many Americans suffering from
poor dietary habits, obesity, and the effects of
smoking, there's never been a greater need for
new ways to reduce the risk. A diet rich in fruit
and vegetables, plus regular exercise, are a great
start towards living a heart-healthier lifestyle. Now,
emerging science suggests that there is even more
you can do.

# Omega-as and inflammation

While not conclusive, some scientific research
shows that consuming omega-3 fatty acids (or
omega-3s, for short) may reduce the risk of
coronary heart disease. 1·3 Omega-3s may provide
these benefits to your heart health by addressing
inflammation.•·•
Emerging science suggests that inflammation Is
one of the possible causes of coronary heart
disease. 1·9 Inflammation is a natural part of a
healthy, functioning immune system because it
helps us fight the daily challenges to our health.
However, when inflammation gets out of balance
and becomes chronic and excessive, it can result
in a range of health problems, including an increased
risk of coronary heart disease. 7 ·•.1• "Inflammation
is becoming recognized as one of the important
contributing factors in heart disease," said
Dr. David Madsen, Director of Scientific Affairs at
Nature Made and an expert in nutritional science.
Epidemiology studies have shown that those who
consume foods containing omega-3 fatty acids
have lower markers of inflammation than those
who. do not.' ..

# How to get your daily omega-as

Omega-3s are found primarily in cold-water fish
(particularly salmon and mackerel), as well as in
certain seeds and nuts. Since 2000, the American
Heart Association has recommended that healthy
adults eat at least two or more servings of fish
a week to obtain the heart-healthy benefits of
omega-3s.
