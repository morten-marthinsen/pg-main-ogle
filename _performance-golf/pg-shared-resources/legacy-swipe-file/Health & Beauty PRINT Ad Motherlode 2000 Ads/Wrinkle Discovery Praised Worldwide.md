## Flight canceled

A passenger jet made an
emergency landing at BWI
Thurgood Marhsall airport
Monday afternoon after hitting
a bird shortly after takeoff, an
airline spokesman confirmed.
Delta Airlines Flight 684
from Baltimore to Atlanta,
Ga., a Boeing MD-88 carrying
137 passengers, landed without
incident. A company spokes­
man described the incident as a
precaution.
The 1:30 p.m. flight was
eventually cancelled and all
passengers were rebooked on
other planes.

## Man hurt in fall

A 43-year-old man fell 20 feet
Tuesday morning when his
scissor lift fell over outside the
new BW! Hilton under con­
struction in Linthicum, county
police and firefighters said.
Mark Hawkins of Glen
Burnie hurt his head and broke
his right leg. He was taken
by ambulance to the Shock
Trauma Center at University
Hospital in Baltimore.
Lt. Alex Makris, fire depart­
ment spokesman, said his inju­
ries were not life threatening.
Medics responded to 1739
W. Nursery Road shortly be­
fore 8:30 a m. Police said Mr.
Hawkins, an electrician with
Enterprise Electric, was work­
ing on some lights outside the
hotel when his lift rolled into
an uncovered hole.
County police and Maryland
Occupational Safety and Health
are investigating.
