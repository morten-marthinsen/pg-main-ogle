# Highmark pays Westmoreland


# $50,000 plus for overpayments

Claims adjustments cover three-year
period and total $50,935.65.
By DAVID HUNT
For The Valley Independent
dhunt@tribweb.com
Highmark Blue Cross Blue Shield has cut
a check for more than $50,000 to West-
moreland County to settle overpayments
dating back three years.
Controller Carmen Pedicone said the
recently recontracted health insurance
company gave the county $50,935.65 in
claims adjustments that had been building
since the county discontinued a previous
contract in 2003. The money has been
deposited to the general fund.
While such adjustments are a normal
part of doing business with an insurer,
county officials said they were unsure if
Highmark would have paid the money as
quickly had the company not been given a
new contract.
"I would have definitely pursued it," Pedi-
cone said. "I think we would have gotten it,
but I think we got it a little sooner."
Highmark spokesman Michael Weinstein
said the payment was being worked out.
"This is the sort of thing that sometimes
goes on with larger customers," he said. "1
think it's more a finalization of the
process." '
By a 2-1 vote of the commissioners in
December, Highmark 'joined UPMC and
HealthAmerica as the third health insurer
for Westmoreland County's 2,000 employ-
ees. Highmark did not have a Westmore-
land contract in 2004 and 2005. Almost 700
employees have opted for the Highmark
insurance plan, according to county human
resources records.
Health insurance is projected to cost the
county nearly $14.2 million this year. Com-
missioners Ibm Ceraso and Phil Light, who
voted in favor of Highmark, said they
believe adding the carrier will cut down on
the overall cost.
Board Chairman Tom Balya, who cast the
dissenting vote, said Highmark has proven
in its past business with the county that it
is a more expensive carrier.
Adding controversy to the Highmark deci-
sion, lobbyists for the company flew Ceraso
to the Masters Golf Tournament in Augus-
ta, Ga., last April. Before the vote, Ceraso
announced that he was repaying the $3,000
cost of the trip. He abstained from voting
on the contract, but was called on to break
the tie as Light and Balya deadlocked.
