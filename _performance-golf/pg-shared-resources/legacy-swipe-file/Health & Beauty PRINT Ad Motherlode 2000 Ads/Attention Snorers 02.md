# If so, be aware!

Mass-produced anti-snoring mouth guards, sprays, herbal capsules, pillows,
head/jaw bands or nasal sprays may put you at great medical and dental risk.
These products are only designed to treat basic snoring. Snoring is one of several signs of a life-threatening medical
disorder known as obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) that often goes undetected. OSA occurs when your
airway becomes completely blocked while sleeping, causing breathing to stop. If undiagnosed,
OSA may lead to hypertension, stroke or heart attack.
Anti-snoring mouth guards may cause serious dental problems, including loosening or loss
of teeth or restricted jaw movement. These products are NOT medically approved to treat
snoring and sleep apnea. Some claim to be made from FDA-approved materials; however,
the mouth guard itself is not medically approved.
Over-the-counter solutions are generally available at nutrition/wellness centres, grocery
stores or mall kiosks. Or they can be ordered over the phone or on the Internet. No
prescription is required. To purchase, you do not have to consult a medical or dental
practitioner before use. This means your potential health issues could be overlooked,
with serious consequences.
