ANDY Cunningtonbegan working as a grill cook at the Grant
Park McDonald’s in 1972.
The Southland Park resident now owns four of the fast-food restau-
rants in East Kildonan and Transcona, and was recently honoured by the
chain for his charitable efforts.
At the recent McDonald’s Restaurants worldwide convention in Flori-
da, Cunnington and his wife Myrnawere taken by surprise when they
were awarded an Outstanding Contributor Award. It is given by the chain
to franchisees who have dedicated themselves to supporting causes in
their communities.
“I still keep looking at it and polishing it every day,” said Cunnington.
“To be recognized for the things you’re involved in and for the time and
effort you put into getting a good reputation for your business, it really
feels good and it makes a big difference.”
The Cunningtons are involved with McDonald’s-related charities and
events like Ronald McDonald House and its various charities, as well as
McHappy Day and World Children’s Day. They also support organizations
like the Special Olympics, as well as giving frequently to community
initiatives like fundraising events for Girl Guides and Scouts, and school
events like band trips or exchanges.
“Everybody’s looking for silent auction prizes nowadays,” said Cun-
nington.
