SACRAMENTO, Calif. – Kim Moore,

Hearing improvements have
different levels of severity. When
people think of hearing loss, they often
think of deafness or severe hearing
impairment. Though mild loss may
not seem troublesome , any level of
hearing loss can cause difficulty in
communicating. Mild hearing loss
should never be ignored and should be
taken seriously. The key to minimizing
the amount of hearing loss we experience
is to be aware that we are suffering from
a certain amount of hearing loss. The
damage caused by ignoring mild loss
depends upon the age of the sufferer
and the cause of the hearing loss. The
most serious danger of ignoring mild
hearing loss is the possibility that it is a
symptom of a progressive problem.
The severity of hearing loss is
measured in decibels (db) needed to
make a sound loud enough to begin to
be heard. This is graphed on what is
called an audiogram. There are four
levels of hearing loss on the audiogram.

unilateral. A bilateral hearing loss

hearing loss. Sensorineural hearing
loss is caused by damage to the
cochlea or auditory nerve. Noise-
induced sensorineural hearing loss
is an example of a hearing loss that
can be a mild hearing loss. Most
people that experienced noise-
induced hearing loss have a higher
degree of damage to their hearing
in the higher frequencies, which is
perceived by the individual as “I can
hear fine, I just can’t understand the
words clearly.”
In normal face to face
conversation, people with mild
hearing loss do not typically have
difficulty understanding the other
person. However, if the other person
is farther away or if there is any
background noise, the mild hearing
loss interferes with understanding
the conversation. Having mild
hearing loss can also make it difficult
to pinpoint the location of sounds.
People with mild hearing loss are
