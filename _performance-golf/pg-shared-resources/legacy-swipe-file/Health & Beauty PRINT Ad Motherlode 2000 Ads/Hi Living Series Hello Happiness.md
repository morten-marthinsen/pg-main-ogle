8. Get plenty of sleep
We have become a sleep-deprived nation. So, is
it any wonder that we feel irritable, frustrated
and miserable? Getting into bed at 10pm and
taking a power nap whenever possible can do
wonders for your mood and outlook on life.
After a good night’s sleep you feel mentally,
emotionally and physically more resilient to
tackle anything.
9. Tune out unhappy chatter
The way you talk to yourself has a profound
impact on your emotional state. Whenever you
catch yourself talking to yourself in a negative,
critical or judgemental manner, change the
tone of your inner voice. Imagine you have a
remote control and press the mute button, or
make it sound like Mickey Mouse so that you
cannot take it seriously. Over the next few days,
experiment with the tone of your inner voice
and the content of your internal dialogue until
you feel better within.
10. Visualise positive images
The mental movies we run in our mind can
affect us just as powerfully as a big screen
movie. This is because the brain cannot
differentiate between what is real and what is
vividly imagined. Therefore, when you visualise
positive images in your mind you feel good.
Practise creating happy movies and you will
experience happy feelings more often! ¨

5. Look good
When you look good, you feel good and vice
versa. Raid your wardrobe for that million-

the mirror. Replay all the compliments you got.
And remember, you were not complimented
on the dress but on how great you look. In the
words of Samantha Jones (from Sex and the
City), “YOU rock that dress!”
6. Meditate

stress, meditation helps balance the body, mind
and spirit making it possible to live in the present
and appreciate the here and now moments.
