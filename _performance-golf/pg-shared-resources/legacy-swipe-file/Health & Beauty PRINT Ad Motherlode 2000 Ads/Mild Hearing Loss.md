## Hearing the Facts by: Mark Moore


# Hale & Hearty

Hearing improvements have different levels of severity. When
people think of hearing loss, they often think of deafness or severe
hearing impairment. Though mild loss may not seem troublesome ,
any level of hearing loss can cause difficulty in communicating. Mild
hearing loss should never be ignored and should be taken seriously.
The key to minimizing the amount of hearing loss we experience is to
be aware that we are suffering from a certain amount of hearing loss.
The damage caused by ignoring mild loss depends upon the age of
the sufferer and the cause of the hearing loss. The most serious
danger of ignoring mild hearing loss is the possibility that it is a
symptom of a progressive problem.
The severity of hearing loss is measured in decibels (db) needed
to make a sound loud enough to begin to be heard. This is graphed
on what is called an audiogram. There are four levels of hearing loss
on the audiogram. Profound hearing loss is anything over 80 db,
severe hearing loss is between 60 and 80 db, moderate hearing loss is
between 26 and 59 db, and mild hearing loss is between 10 and 25
db. The level of hearing loss can vary between the two ears.
Mild hearing loss can affect one or both ears. If hearing loss affects
only one ear, the hearing loss is called unilateral. A bilateral hearing
loss is a hearing loss that affects both ears. A mild hearing loss may
be caused by a blockage of the ear canal such as earwax or fluid
build up from an ear infection. Hearing loss from a blockage is called
conductive hearing loss. Sensorineural hearing loss is caused by
damage to the cochlea or auditory nerve. Noise-induced
sensorineural hearing loss is an example of a hearing loss that can be a
mild hearing loss. Most people that experienced noise-induced
hearing loss have a higher degree of damage to their hearing in the
higher frequencies, which is perceived by the individual as “I can hear
fine, I just can’t understand the words clearly.”
In normal face to face conversation, people with mild hearing loss
do not typically have difficulty understanding the other person.
However, if the other person is farther away or if there is any
background noise, the mild hearing loss interferes with understanding
the conversation. Having mild hearing loss can also make it difficult to
pinpoint the location of sounds. People with mild hearing loss are
inclined to increase the volume of the television and other
electronics. This can be more of annoyance to others in the
household than the person with the hearing loss.
People living with mild hearing loss are at a disadvantage. They
easily blame others for not speaking clearly, too fast or reducing the
volume too much. They avoid denial of the hearing loss by avoiding
difficult situations that create problems they can’t control. It’s usually
family and friends of the sufferer that is very aware of their hearing
problem and will apart pressure even when the sufferer is not
motivated to get help or treatment for their hearing problem.
Early detection is critical. It is very important that we do all we can
to avoid any further damage to our ears and subsequent hearing loss.

# Mild Hearing Loss (Early Detection is Critical)

If mild hearing loss is ignored it becomes a symptom of a progressive
problem that usually contributes to auditory deprivation and can lead
to permanently losing the ability to understand speech.
The first step to early detection is if you or you suspect a family
member might be suffering with mild hearing loss, or having any
problems hearing, you or they will need to have a complete
audiological evaluation performed at McDonald Hearing Aid Center.
McDonald Hearing Aid Center has developed many ground breaking
industry treatments to help people hear better. If an individual is
found to have permanent mild hearing loss, the treatment is the use of
hearing aids.
Hearing aids will:
1) Potentially reverse auditory deprivation (stopping hearing loss
from progressively getting worse).
2) Improve their ability to communicate easier with family/friends
and co-workers especially when they’re in background noise or
across the room.
Mild hearing loss if not addressed will dramatically and negatively
impact a person’s quality of life.
