# Haunted by Headaches


# Nine-year-old girl overcome by grueling Headaches finds Peace in the least-likely of places.

Mayfield, Kentucky—Brooke Dunaway
was just four-years-old when she first felt
her head-splitting migraine headaches
that would haunt her childhood.
The pain was so intense, nine-year-old
Brooke says, that her parents often had
to turn off the lights before eventually
rushing her to the local emergency room
for a shot that would dull the pain.
“The pain would start on the left side
of my head, and it would make me cry a
lot,” Brooke said. “I would cry for my
mom and dad because it hurt so bad.”
The weekly occurrence became com-
monplace for Brooke’s parents, James
and Teresa, but that didn’t stop them
from trying to find an answer.
They took Brooke to three different
hospitals for evaluation, but after each
visit, disappointed doctors would tell
the Dunaway family they simply didn’t
know what was wrong.
“It just tore my heart apart,” said
Brooke’s father James. “When a kid that
young is that sick, and there’s nothing you
can do about it, you can’t help but feel terri-
ble for them.”
The Dunaway’s continued search led them
to everything from experimental diets to
pain-killing pills. James said just as the fami-
ly had become “fed up” with the pills and the
“zombie-like” effect they had on his little
girl, the Dunaway’s found an answer at a
HealthSource Chiropractic™ and
Progressive Rehab™ office near their home.
“We took her to HealthSource for chiro-
practic adjustments and all of a sudden her
migraines went away,” James said. “She’s
like a different person now. She’s got a life!”
Brooke is now back to enjoying life as an
average third grader, and can often be found
playing sports and riding her bicycle with
friends.
Although she’s a long way from the pain
that riddled her and her family for so long,
Brooke said she never forgets to remind her
parents when it’s time to see her favorite
HealthSource doctor.
“I like going (to HealthSource) because
everybody is real nice and they make me
feel better,” she said. “I’m not afraid of my
migraines any more.”
