CUMBERLAND — Hovering near 95 percent of their
total student capacity, Braddock and Washington middle
schools could use more room. Both facilities were toured
by members of the District I Delegation to the Maryland
General Assembly and Allegany County Board of Educa­
tion on Monday.
The delegates also toured Beall High School in Frost­
burg.
“We double up on a lot of our rooms. If a room’s open we
make use of it,” said Jenean Fazenbaker, guidance coun­
selor at Braddock Middle.
She said several of the school’s former closets have even
been turned into tiny classrooms and used for special edu­
cation.
Principal Danny Carter said Braddock has a total capaci­
ty for 764 students, with a current enrollment of 729.
Although utilization of the school was given a good rating
in a 2002 Comprehensive School Facilities Utilization Study,
the same study noted that Braddock had not received any
major renovations since the school was built in 1964.
The building’s physical condition was listed as poor and
improvement costs were estimated at approximately $5.6
million.
Similar in design to Braddock, Washington Middle School
was also built in 1964, but Washington Middle received
some upgrades in 2000 after Oldtown School was closed
due to consolidation.
“We had to create six classrooms that were built after the
consolidation, but there’s been no other renovations,” said
Principal Harry Smith.
He said the two-story school only has two narrow stair­
wells.
“They’re only half as wide as the stairwells at Braddock,”
said Smith. “The stairwells are a concern because when we
have fire drills it takes longer for students to leave the sec­
ond floor.”
Washington Middle was also given a poor rating for phys­
ical condition and overcrowding in the 2002 facilities utiliza­
tion study. The study recommended relief of the overcrowd­
ing by rezoning students to Braddock Middle School and
making capital improvements as necessary.
Costs to improve Washington Middle’s physical condition
were estimated at approximately $3 million.
“We have no current plans for renovations at Braddock
and Washington middle schools,” said Dr. William AuMiller,
superintendent of schools for Allegany County.
“Our current focus is on the schools in the western part
of the county,” he said, but added that the two middle
schools would be first and second on a list of facilities
scheduled for improvements as funding becomes available.
District I Delegation Chair George Edwards felt the
school tours were worthwhile.
“Now that we’ve had a thorough look at these schools we
can tell that some are in worse shape than others,” he said.
“In some there’s more of an overcrowding issue. The mid­
dle schools were built later and of better materials than
(Beall) high school which, in my opinion, is in worse shape.
But I’m not saying that these other facilities don’t need
work. They do.”
