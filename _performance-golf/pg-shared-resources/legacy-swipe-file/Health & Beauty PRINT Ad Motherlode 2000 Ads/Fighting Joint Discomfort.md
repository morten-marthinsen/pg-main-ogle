# Joint Health Basics

While there is no cure for joint discomfort, there are ways to
lessen its effects and promote or maintain joint health.
Here’s a closer look.
Joints are mechanical in nature and can become worn
with use as the smooth layer of cartilage cushioning the
joint bones breaks down over time. That effect can be
made worse at the cell-level where radical oxidants –
structures similar to the free radicals thought to be
responsible for a laundry list of aging issues from
weakening immune systems to hair loss – can hasten the
breakdown of cartilage.
According to Dr. Luke Bucci, Ph.D., CCN, C(ASCP), CNS,
and Vice President of research for Schiff Nutrition,
maintaining the cartilage you have, lubricating areas where
cartilage has worn away and staving off these radical
oxidants are key to ensuring joint health.
“As in so many other matters of health, eating a well-
balanced diet, maintaining a healthy weight and taking the
right supplements all play roles in joint health and slowing
down cartilage wear,” explains Dr. Bucci. “Participating in a
regular exercise program that promotes strength and
flexibility is important, too.”
