# Breast reconstruction helps one woman heal

By PAULA VESS
Regional Marketing Group
SHELBY - Nancye Weitman is
nearing the end of a very long and
tough journey.
“She has one more surgery to
complete the process,” said Nelson
Jenkins, MD, a plastic surgeon
with Cleveland Plastic and Hand
Surgery. “It will probably take
about two more months for her to
be completely finished.”
Late last year, Weitman
discovered a small lump on her
left breast. “It was toward the top
of my breast. You could see it sort
of sticking up on the surface,” she
said. “There’s no family history
(of breast cancer). Weight was
the only risk factor I had. I was
sure it couldn’t be cancer.”
The results of a biopsy showed
otherwise - it was cancer.
Weitman and her husband,
Joseph, moved from south
Florida to Cleveland County when
they retired in 2010. Barely three
years later, she found herself in
need of some major medical care.
“My daughters tried to get me to
come back to Florida, or at least
go to Wake Forest because of the
medical school’s reputation.”
She chose to stay here,
her new home. Her surgeon
referred her to Dr. Jenkins to
discuss her surgery options.
“I did my research and looked
him up online. He had lots of
good reviews and good word of
mouth,” Weitman said.
This part of Weitman’s journey
started with a consultation, which
her husband attended with her.
“When patients first come in
there’s a lot of confusion. They are
usually in a gray area and haven’t
decided what they want to do,”
said Dr. Jenkins. “I talk with them
about all their options. Then most
patients go back to their surgeon
to make a final decision.”
“I started out thinking I
wanted to have a lumpectomy,”
Weitman said. “You think you
want to remain a woman, and
you wonder if having a breast
removed will change that.”
However, after doing some
research, talking with Dr. Jenkins
and considering all of her options,
Weitman decided to have a double
mastectomy ~ to remove the
entire breast on the affected side
