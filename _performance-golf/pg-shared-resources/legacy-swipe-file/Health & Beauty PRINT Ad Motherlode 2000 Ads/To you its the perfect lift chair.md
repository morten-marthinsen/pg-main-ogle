## BEAN SALAD

You can substitute fresh sugar
snap peas for half or all of the
carrots. The dressing is also great
for sprucing up an everyday
green salad.
1 bunch green onions
1 lb baby carrots
2 (15-oz) cans kidney beans,
about 2½ cups drained, not
rinsed
Dressing:
2 Tbsp grated fresh ginger
3 Tbsp toasted sesame oil or
extra-virgin olive oil
3 Tbsp soy sauce
3 Tbsp rice vinegar (like
Kikkoman)
½ tsp salt
1. Trim green onions and thinly slice
crosswise to yield 1 cup. Place in a
fi ne mesh strainer and run hot water
over them for 45 to 60 seconds.
Drain; place in large mixing bowl.
2. Slice carrots into 1⁄8- to ¼ -inch-
thick coins to yield about 3½ cups
and add to green onions. Add beans.
3. Add ginger, sesame oil, soy sauce
and rice vinegar. Toss well. Add salt.
Taste salad before serving and re-
season with vinegar for more punch.
Serve at room temperature or
chilled. Makes 8 cups. Serves 6 to 8.
