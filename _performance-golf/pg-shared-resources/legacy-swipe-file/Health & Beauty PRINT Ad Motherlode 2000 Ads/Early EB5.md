# By Martha Bebee

TO EMPHASIZE the power of the
senior citizen, which I mention so
often in this column, I could give you
no better example than the "Senior
Power Day" at Lansing, Mich., where
50,000 came by bus from every area of
the state to meet some of their re-
presenatives.
The issues were reform of the en-
tire health care system, economic se-
curity and job opportunities for the
older American.
The group has asked for controlled
costs in all levels of health care, in-
cluding hospitals, doctors, nurses,
laboratory and prescriptions. It is out
to defeat all proposals to increase out-
of-pocket Medicare costs and to
strengthen the aging network in its
programs that will help older people
live independently in their own homes.
As for employment, it has asked for
the removal of age barriers, retaining
the older worker and more jobs of
which seniors are capable.
The 50,000 people also asked that
buses be provided in each of their
counties for recreation, as well as ser-
vice, that the Social Security cost of
living adjust ment be restored and that
geriatric education be mandatory for
every health care professional.
Before any criticism for writing
about Michigan gets back to me, let
me say that the same power shown
there can happen here as well, and
that victories in other states can well
make a big difference in your future
here in Anderson.
A Milwaukee group has won a
major victory in the health-care field.
A medical center there now accepts
the Medicare reinbursement as its full
payment for "in-and-out" patient care
and in all areas of its services, with no
deductibles or co-payments. Over 100
doctors have .signed up with this pro-
gram.
This giant step was made by grass-
root seniors, who cared enough to get
behind their leaders and programs.
Let's get busy, Indiana.

# Police investigate


# salon burglary

The Apex Beauty Salon was
broken into early today and two of-
fices were ransacked, according to
Anderson Police reports.
A video cassette recorder, a
number of curling irons and possi-
bly some cash were taken, accord-
ing to police.
Police said the building, at 10th
and Fletcher, was entered through
a first floor window. The down-
stairs area was not damaged, but
the two upstairs offices were
"completely torn apart," police
said.
An investigation is pending until
a more complete inventory can be
made.
