Dr. Price’s beloved mother, Frances also
known as "Tutu" to everyone who knew and
loved her, touched dozens of lives at the House
of Hope for troubled teens. She was devoted,
generous, and strong. She had even battled
thyroid cancer and won.
When the doctor got the call about his
mother that she had fallen from a 2 foot bench
and broken her hip, his heart sunk because as
an orthopedic surgeon, he knew that 25% of all
patients die within a year of injury and 3 out
of 4 elderly patients never fully recover. Sadly,
the statistic became a reality when she passed
away IO months later from complications
due to her broken hip. Dr. Charles Price
is internationally known for his clinical
and academic achievements in the field of
orthopedics, so the loss of his mother in this
way, weighed especially heavy on him.

## Why Bone Health Can Be


## the Difference


## Between Life And Death

What makes fractures in people over 50 so
dangerous is not the fracture itself but the issues
surrounding it. One broken bone often leads to
another as the body steals from healthy bones
to repair the damaged ones. The body becomes
weaker with lack of activity and appetite,
making it more susceptible to illnesses and
infections such as pneumonia and delirium.
As an orthopedic surgeon, Dr. Price knew that
trying to repair the break in an operating room
is difficult due to the frailty of the older bones.
Charles T. Price, MD (far right) and a team
of orthopedic surgeons, from the Institute
for Better Bone Health, have discovered
that an organic form of silicon could be a
key to real bone health.

## The Doctor's Battle Continues

To add to his heartbreak a few months after
his mother passed away, Dr. Price learned his
nightmare wasn’t over. When his high school
sweetheart and wife of 40 years, Pam, at the
age of 64, started showing signs of arthritis, her
doctor recommended a bone density test. They
were surprised to find the results displayed
bone mineral density lower than normal.
That's when he decided enough was enough!
this condition was not going to take his wife,
too. With that, he hit the ground running, in
search of answers. What he found in his two
years of research was startling, even to him.

## Silicon - The Missing Link

His research with bone graft materials
uncovered a forty year old paper published by
Edith Carlisle at UCLA that proved the mineral
silicon plays a major role in bone health. In
fact, our bodies need silicon and bone can't
form without it. Edith Carlisle had proven this
fact long ago!
This was all starting to fall into place!
Regions of the world with diets rich in natural
silicon, such as in China and India, have the
lowest frequency of hip fractures, while here in
North America, we have much lower amounts
of silicon in our diets and hip fractures are
more common.
Dr. Price discovered that an organic form of
silicon can actually improve the structure and
mineralization of bone - and yet some forms
of silicon are difficult for the body to absorb -
and retain. So, the question remained - how
would patients combine the right nutrients in
the right amounts?
