# Anniversaries celebrated


# Wilson

GENEVA - Mr. and Mrs. Everett “Ab” Wilson
of Geneva celebrated their 50th wedding
anniversary June 4. A weekend family reunion
was hosted by their children at Holiday Inn.
The couple have been active in United Church
of Geneva where a friendship hour was hosted
in their honor June 4.
“Ab” and Nora were married by the Rev. Hollis
Spurlock June 4, 1950, in Greenup, Ky.
They have four children, Scott of Burbank,
Calif., Jennifer Cook of Madison, Jeffery of
Geneva and Kelly of Stow. They also have 15
grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.
Following his retirement as staff lieutenant
with the Ohio State Highway Patrol in 1974 Mr.
Wilson was transportation director for Geneva
and Jefferson Schools and was a driver for
Massucci Ford. Mrs. Wilson was formerly
employed at the Pottery Gift Shop and as a sub­
stitute teachers’ aide and secretary for Geneva
Schools.
MR. and MRS. EVERETT WILSON

# Bartone

CONNEAUT- Mr. and Mrs. Anthony Bartone
are celebrating their 50th wedding anniversary.
Anthony and Marion (Schultz) were married
July 7, 1950, in New York.
The couple have two daughters and sons-in-
law, Dianne L. and Ted Mayberry and Tona L.
and Scott Humphrey. They also have three
grandchildren and two great-grandchildren.
Mr. Bartone was formerly a carpenter and
employed with The Pittsburgh & Conneaut
Dock Co. Mrs. Bartone was formerly employed
as a secretary at Astatic and Emco Wheaton.
MR. and MRS. ANTHONY BARTONE

# Greenwood

ASHTABULA - In observance of their 55th
wedding anniversary Mr. and Mrs. Dixon C.
Greenwood were among those honored at a Mass
celebrated by Bishop Thomas J. Tobin June ll in
St. Columba Cathedral, Youngstown.
Dixon and Mary Alice (Borges) were married
June 19, 1945, at St. Mary’s Cathedral in
Portland, Ore., where Mr. Greenwood was serv­
ing in the U.S. Army.
The couple have two children, Gerald and Mary
Elizabeth Greenwood of Arlington, Va.
Mr. Greenwood retired in 1979 after 32 1/2
years at Union Carbide Corp. Metals Division
where he was safety engineer and supervisor of
operations. He is a member emeritus of Catholic
Charities and director of Ashtabula County
Safety Council and a member of Ashtabula
County Amateur Radio Club. Mrs. Greenwood
was a teacher for 20 years prior to her retire­
ment in 1976. She was also Democratic presid­
ing judge for elections for 30 years. Both are
members of St. Joseph Church and pastoral care
