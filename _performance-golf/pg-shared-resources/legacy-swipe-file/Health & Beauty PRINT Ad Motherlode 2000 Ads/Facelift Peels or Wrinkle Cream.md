# Festival of Lights volunteers needed

BLUEFIELD — The city’s
Festival of Lights Commission is
looking for a few volunteers to
help decorate City Park for the
upcoming Holiday of Lights, offi­
cials said Tuesday.
The Holiday of Lights is the
kick-off event for the city’s
“Millennium Celebration.”
Commission Co-Chair Sherry
Ciccolini said volunteers are
needed to help complete the 1999-
2000 displays as well as help in
setting them up.
Volunteers donating 2,000 min­
utes (33 1/2 hours) will receive a
certificate from the city.
The event’s work schedule:
— Nov. 6, beginning at 9 a.m., an
all-day session to set up displays.
— Nov. 9, 10 and 11, 6-9 p.m.,
work at the city’s old freight sta­
tion on Bluefield Avenue. •
— Nov. 13, an all-day work day at
City Park.
— Nov. 16,17 and 18, 6-9 p.m. at
the old freight station.
