Q. What Is Invisalign and How Does It Work?
Invisalign takes a modern approach to straightening
teeth, using a custom-made series of aligners. The
aligner trays are made of smooth, comfortable and
virtually invisible plastic worn over your teeth. Wear-
ing the aligners gradually and gently shifts your teeth
into place; the aligners are changed every 2-3 weeks.
There are no metal brackets and no wires to tighten;
Invisalign is metal-free. The aligners are removed for
eating and oral hygiene and worn at least twenty-
two hours per day. Almost two million people have
benefitted from Invisalign care.
Q. What are some differences between
traditional orthodontics and Invisalign?
Invisalign aligners are clear and removable; traditional
braces are metallic and fixed. The average length of
treatment for Invisalign is nine months to one year
compared to 2-3 years for traditional orthodontics. In-
visalign care is appropriate for about 80% of diagnosed
malocclusions/poor teeth alignment. Invisalign offers
far less risk to patients who are vulnerable to tooth
decay and periodontal disease, the approach allows
for total oral hygiene access (and…straight teeth are
engineered to be self-cleansing and better balanced to
not wear down long-term.)
Q. Does Invisalign hurt?
I like this one. Nope. There aren’t any sharp edges
or points and the clear aligners are easily adjusted
or smoothed (in the unlikely event an adjustment
is necessary.) When a new aligner is fitted, there’s a
measure of pressure or tightness that lasts for about a
day. And what’s really awesome is being able to clearly
track your progress each visit just by looking up at the
screen and the Invisalign website. The start-to-finish
process is established and visibly accessible from start
to finish of care.
Q. What about the cost?
Only your doctor can determine the cost of your
specific care. And the variables include the result you
want, the care you’ll need, and how long it will take.
In the U.S., the cost of Invisalign treatment ranges
from $3,500-$8,000 (same as traditional orthodontics),
with the national average at about $5,000. For very
simple cases (5 aligners and less) the cost can be as
little as $2,500 or less. Many dental insurance plans
cover Invisalign just as they would traditional ortho-
dontics. Interest-free financing for up to 2-years can
reduce monthly payments to slightly more than $200
per month. Flexible Spending Accounts can also be
very helpful in fitting the care within your budget.
