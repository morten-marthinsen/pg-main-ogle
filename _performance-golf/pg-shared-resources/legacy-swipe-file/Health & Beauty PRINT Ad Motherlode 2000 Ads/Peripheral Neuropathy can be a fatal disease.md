Early signs of PN are: tingling,
numbness,pain,discoloration,
burning or cold fingers or
toes. Another less frequent
symptom is unexplained falls
or loss of balance. The lack of
oxygen and nutrients that the
blood brings to the tissues of
the feet or hands results in
the toes or fingers becoming
discolored. They start out
turning a darker shade and
eventually turn a slight purple.
If allowed to continue, they
eventually turn black. At this
point the tissues have died.
This is known as gangrene.
If the gangrene enters the
body cavity, a condition
known as septicemia will set
in. This condition will poison
the blood stream causing a
shutdown and death of all the
organs, and death is the end
result.
What are the major causes
of Peripheral Neuropathy?
Diabetes is a major cause of
PN. Because of the body’s
inability to use glucose
correctly, the glucose will be
converted to yellow fat which
will adhere to the walls of the
capillaries in the feet or hands
robbing the tissues of oxygen
and nutrients.
The second cause of
Peripheral Neuropathy is
chemotheraphy. Technicians
are supposed to watch for
tingling, pain, numbness in
the feet, toes or fingers and
back off the treatment until
the symptoms disappear. If
they’re not careful, the toxic
effects of the chemo can
damage nerves.
