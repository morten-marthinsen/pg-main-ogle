# Board president: Closure of St. Luke's


# emergency department 'a possibility'

By GREG JORDAN
Bluefield Daily Telegraph
BLUEFIELD — Princeton
Community Hospital has been
speaking with the West
Virginia Health Care Authority
about the possibility of closing
the emergency department of
St. Luke's Hospital.
"That's a possibility. We're
looking at all kinds of possibili-
ties," said PCH Board
President Tom Lilly. "Nothing
has been decided by the board
of directors, and any specula-
tion about what would happen
would be premature."
Princeton Community
Hospital has to look at the
finances of St. Luke's and see
what the profitable parts are,
Lilly said.
"Even though it's a hospital,
it's still a business," he said.
Lilly said he was not aware of
any time frame for making a
decision about St. Luke's emer-
gency department.
"We'll just have to see what
the administration recom-
mends and we'll go from
there," he said.
Lilly said that he had not
been part of discussions with
state health officials. Wayne
Griffith, CEO of Princeton
Community Hospital was
paged by the hospital and
returned a message stating he
was in a meeting and would be
available Friday. Assistant Vice
President Mike Harold was not
available Thursday evening.
