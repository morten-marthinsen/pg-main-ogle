Why do authorities require bicyclists to ride with the fl ow
of traffi c as opposed to against it? Aren’t we cyclists safer
when we can see the traffi c coming toward us?
—Steve Mitchell, Lake Forest, Ill.
No. Imagine yourself on a motorcycle, instead. Would you feel
safer riding against the traffi c? And when you ride against traf-
fi c, your closing speed
with an approaching car
is additive. (Say you’re
riding at 15 mph, and
a car is heading toward
you at 30 mph; your clos-
ing speed is 45 mph.)
Regardless, statistics
show that wrong-way
(riding against traffi c) bi-
cyclists are several times
as likely to get hit by a
car as right-way (riding
with traffi c) cyclists.
