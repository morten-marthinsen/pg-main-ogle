# Sometimes when shopping is a real zoo, it can be fun

By Jennifer Lowe
The Orange County Register
Shopping during the holidays can
be a real zoo.
At The Think Shop, it's supposed
to be.
The zoo-inspired shop, temporar-
ily set up in South Coast Plaza's
Crystal Court, is operated by the
Santa Ana Zoo. Proceeds from
sales benefit the zoo and the zoo's
10-year master plan for redevelop-
ment, which is explained in a dis-
play at the rear of the store.
Organizers selected the store's
name so customers would think
about animals and other environ-
mental concerns from the moment
they walk in.. . past a large, moss-
covered elephant displayed in the
window.
Though small and tucked into the
mall's third level, the store offers
shoppers unusual items — almost
all with an animal theme — priced
from a few dollars to a few hundred
dollars.
"You walk around and see so
many of the same things (in the
mall)," customer Ginny Metz of
Irvine said, as she carried a large
cardboard box decorated with fish.
"When you find something unusu-
al, it's nice."
A majority of the store's items
were shipped over from the zoo's
gift ship, but some also came from
the Bowers Museum gift shop in
Santa Ana, or were ordered espe-
cially for the store.
Though the store has been open
only a few weeks, its hotter items
— decorative ornaments, natural
lotions and children's hand pup-
pets — are going fast. Some mer-
chandise is being reordered, but
many things won't be, since The
Think Shop will be open just
through the holidays, manager
Joyce Dalessi said.
For children, the store has ev-
erything from family science kits
to study tidepools ($29.95) to T-
shirts with animal faces and
matching socks ($20) to a wooden
dinosaur swing ($100).
Christmas shoppers can pick
from a variety of ornaments, in-
cluding brightly painted animals
($9.95, and a portion of the pur-
chase benefits an animal fund), a
basket filled with tiny bird orna-
ments made in Guatemala ($17),
and hand-made teddy bears ($20
and $30).
Among the more unusual items
are exotic hand lotions, shampoos
and conditioners ($6.50-$10.2S), the
purchase of which benefits rain
forests; a bright bracelet made
from chopsticks ($65); and hand-
carved woodenpins painted to look
like leaves ($24). . - ^
And those stuck for/gift ideas
might consider adopting a zoo ani-
mal (starting at $25); the money
helps pay for thè animars care at
the zoo. Among the choices: spot-
nosed guenon, bald eagle, Western
pond turtle and California king
snake.
