OU MIGHT HAVE HEARD
that digital phone service can
save you money, but the logistics
are confusing. Here's how it works:
Basically, VoIP technology con-
verts your voice into little data pack-
ets and sends them over the Internet
like e-mail—instead of through a
phone line. Calls are cheaper because
real estate on the Internet is unlim-
ited, while phone lines are finite and
controlled by a few big companies.
Sound quality for digital calls is
getting better, but it will only be as
good as your Internet connection.
High-speed broadband is a must.
Keep your land line or cell in case
of emergencies—since connecting
to the Internet requires electricity,
you'll lose your phone service if the
power goes out. Also, some VoIP
services do not provide 911 calling.
A lot of companies offer digital
calling, so your choice will depend
on how—and how much—you want
to use your phone. Each service
comes with its own pros, cons and
"bonus features," like instant mes-
saging, video conferencing and file-
transfer software (great for sending
photos). Visit Pomde.com for a list
of services, features and links.
