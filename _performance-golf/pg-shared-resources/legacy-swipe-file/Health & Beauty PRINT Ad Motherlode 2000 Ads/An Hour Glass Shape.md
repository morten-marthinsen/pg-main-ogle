# Stay heart-healthy during the holidays with recipe modifications

By PAULA VESS
Regional Marketing Group
SHELBY - With the holidays come
family dinners, celebrations at
work and parties with friends.
That means food.
"During the holidays most of
us just throw in the towel," said
Sara Smart, MPH, RD, LDN, a
clinical dietitian in the Cardiac
Rehab Department at Cleveland
Regional Medical Center. "What
we need to do is try making our
favorite recipes healthier."
You don't have to make drastic
changes. A few simple changes can
make almost any dish healthier.
"Make changes one ingredient
at a time," said Smart. "Don't try
to cut out or reduce fat, sugar and
salt all at once."
For some recipes, especially
cakes and baked goods, it may
not be possible to remove all the
fat, salt or sugar. "Reducing the
fat in a recipe by one tablespoon
saves you 135 calories and -15
grams of fat," she stated.
Once you've prepared the dish
with the change, write down what
you did and the effect it had on
the dish. Did you like the way
it tasted? How was the texture?
Make notes about what you liked
and didn't like.
If you are satisfied with the re-
sult. you can try reducing another
ingredient next time. If you did
not like the result, make a note of
that and try reducing a different
ingredient next time.
