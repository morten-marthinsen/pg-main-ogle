Not all endangered animals can
be returned to nature now. Here
are two species of big cat being
safeguarded in zoos so their
descendants eventually maybe free.

## Tiger

Intent on his prey, a 400-pound
3-year-old named Globus (above)
pounces after ducks that alight in
the moat of his two-acre enclosure.
The Siberian tiger, stalking instincts
intact, arrived at the Twirl Cities'
Minnesota Zoo last year from a
Russian preserve. Because of
poaching and habitat loss, release
is not in the cards for Globus or
other captive tigers. Safe in zoos,
they're insurance in casetheir
wild cousins become extinct

## Cheetah

Zoos and other conservation groups
hope to eventually release captive-
bred cheetahs into the wilds of
Africa. But habitat for wild cheetahs
is shrinking in the face of human
encroachment Because many
endangered species face the same
threat zoos have put a new emphasis
on protecting native habitat

# Think You've Got Problems?

Sharon Meyer is a nurse and mother
from a small town in east Texas. She's
now married to a doctor and lives in
Marietta, GA.
Interviewer: You were — by your
own admission — a very lonely and
overweight child. Is that right?
Sharon: Yes... I remember an incident
that occurred while I was growing up
in Texas.
One Sunday evening, two of my cous-
ins enticed me into a small fenced-in
cemetery near my Aunt Ellen's house.
They had taken a chain and padlock
from Grandpa's shed. Once I was inside
they ran back out and locked the gate.
Interviewer: What happened?
Sharon: Well, I was too heavy to climb
over the fence. So they knew I'd be stuck
there for the night! Fortunately, Aunt
Ellen rescued me about 9:30 that night.
Interviewer: Any other traumatic
incidents?
Sharon: Sort of. When I was nineteen
I married this insurance salesman
named Earl. He was heavy, too.
Earl used to take me out at night
with his friends and their wives. As
the evening wore on, he'd start calling
me "... Thunderthighs" and his "...
Big-Fat Momma."
Earl loved it. He actually thought it
was funny. It never occurred to him that
his remarks hurt me. They hurt so much
I'd actually cry myself to sleep at night.
Please understand. Fm not a "whiner."
I had tried to lose the weight before
this. But it was hopeless. First I'd lose
8 pounds. Then gain back 10! Three
years and twelve diets later, I finally
went to my doctor for help.
Interviewer: What did he say?
Sharon: Well, that's the really strange
part. I thought for certain he'd prescribe
diet pills. But he didn't. Instead he
handed me this sealed package and said,
"Try this. It's gonna help you. But
follow the directions carefully..."
Let me tell you something. This was
no diet! No way. I couldn't believe the
food they let me have.
I was allowed to eat three regular
meals every day. And three additional
snacks. Every day.
And it was all good wholesome food.
Lots of variety! Lots of choices!
I was flabbergasted! And delighted!
When I went back to the doctor for my
checkup, I discovered I'd lost 13pounds!
I could hardly believe it! In fact, during
the following 19 1/2 weeks I lost an
additional 54 pounds! This was the first
time in my life I'd ever lost that much
weight. And kept it offl!
