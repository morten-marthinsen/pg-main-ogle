# Q: How can I avoid


# thunder thighs?

Cathy Pelican, Edison, NJ.
Mia: You told me you
need strong legs for
volleyball but don't
want your inner thighs
any larger than they
are now. First thing: The leg curls
and leg presses you've been doing
have little effect on the inner thigh.
Leg curls focus on the hamstrings; leg
presses work mainly the glutes, quads
and hamstrings. For the thighs, try
exercises that recruit the hip adduc-
tors (inner-thigh muscles that draw
the legs inward) and hip abductors
(those on the outside thigh that push
legs away from body). Here's one:
• Lateral lunges. Stand with feet
parallel and wider than shoulder-
width apart. Hold light free weights,
use a barbell or keep hands on hips.
Leading with the hip, push off to one
side, stepping out with the leg. Turn
toes slightly outward so leg is turned
outward when you land on the ball of
the foot. Squat on lunging leg (knee
bent) until thigh is parallel to floor,
keeping body "tall." The other leg
should be extended but relaxed. Push
back up to starting position, pause,
repeat with other leg. Do 10-15 reps.
