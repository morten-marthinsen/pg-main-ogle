What is "Low-Priced''?
A "low-priced" option is generally con
sidered to be one selling for 501'! or less. Un
less the option is quite close to expiration, it
is usually well out of the money. Its low price
reflects the low probability of it being in-the
money at expiration.
Under the new margin rules now in effect,
these low-priced options offer even higher
potential returns than before.
