DEAR INVESTOR:
H this letter helps prevent one case of Speculative Fever,
it will be worthwhile.
People buying cats and dogs look upon stocks and the
stock market as a page full of names. They have lost track
of a very simple fact--a stock represents a company.
The stock is no better than the company. A company is no
better than its ability to earn a profit. A growing profit is
necessary for capital gain in the long run.
Some stocks have gone up in price much faster than their
earnings can be expected to grow in the foreseeable future.
(Some big name blue chips included.) These are not good
investments now.
Some stocks are priced cheap relative to their company's
profit growth. They are good investments now for sound
reasons. Growth in a company is not happenstance. It is
planned for, worked for-it is measurable. You don't have
to guess or "take a flier.',
Insist on growth facts about any company you invest in.
When you have facts about sound, growing companies,
you can invest with a clear head, regardless of "highs,"
"lows" or "dips" of the Stock Market.
You may be thinking, "I understand Standard & Poor's
policy about buying stocks-buy each stock on its individ
ual merits regardless of temporary market conditions. But
what do you think about The Stock Market?"
Standard & Poor's believes today's stock market is funda
mentally sound if one allows for future growth and infla
tion. Of course, there will be corrections and some may be
deep, but S&P believes the Stock Market averages, now
"high," inevitably will go much higher over the years
ahead.
Not all stocks, of course, will participate in the growth
ahead. Many will stand still or decline. Stocks representing
companies with g1·owing profits will lead the march to new
highs.
TO SUM UP-
1. Keep remembering that a stock represents a company
-keep up with the changes within each company
you buy.
2. When any stock you own loses its potential for future
gain-sell it. A dead stock is no protection against
inflation.
3. Hold some cash in reserve.
4. Avoid Speculative Fever. Don't buy cats and dogs.
5. If you have extra cash to invest, go right ahead and
buy value-growing value, stocks that have NOT
been bid up into the hereafter. Which stocks? Specifi
cally, we suggest you buy one or more stocks in
Standard & Poor's Master List. When you do this, we
believe you are making sense, buying corporate
profits instead of the crowd's fashion of the moment.
