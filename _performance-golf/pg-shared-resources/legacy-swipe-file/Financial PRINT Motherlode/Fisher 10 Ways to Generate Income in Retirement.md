## The Big Switch—Saving to Spending

Congratulations! After decades of saving for retirement, it’s
now time to switch gears from saving to spending. However,
after working with tens of thousands of people who are close
to retirement or retired, we’ve learned that deciding how to
generate income in retirement is one of the most stressful,
complicated and confusing aspects of retirement life. Even
if you have accumulated a large nest egg—in excess of
$500,000—making the wrong income moves could put your
entire retirement at risk. That’s why we urge you to call for
your free copy of The Definitive Guide to Retirement Income.

## A Complicated Balancing Act

Generating a retirement income requires you to balance a
variety of elements, many of them beyond your control, such
as inflation, stock market volatility, interest rate trends and
your and your spouse’s expected longevity. Too conservative
in your investments and you risk having inflation strip you
of purchasing power. Too aggressive and you risk losing
your money. Take too much out early in retirement and risk
running out of money in your later years. Too stingy with
your withdrawals and you might be cheating yourself out of
enjoyable experiences. It’s a tough proposition with lots of
emotional components. And that’s often a recipe for disastrous
decision making.
