"What makes stock prices go up and down?"
There are a thousand factors that can make a
stock go up or down.
Example: A company launches a new
product. People think the product will suc
ceed, and that the company's profits will
increase. So, they buy the stock. If enough
people buy, the price goes up.
Minimum commissions are set by the ex
changes, and they are probably lower than
you might think.
Example: The minimum commission per
mitted by the New York Stock Exchange
on the purchase or sale of 100 shares of
stock worth $1,000 is $17.
As a matter of policy, Merrill Lynch charges
its customers only the minimum rates per
mitted, and not a penny more. Our commis
sion on the preceding example: $17.
"How much money do I need
to start investing?"
You can buy some stocks for literally pennies
