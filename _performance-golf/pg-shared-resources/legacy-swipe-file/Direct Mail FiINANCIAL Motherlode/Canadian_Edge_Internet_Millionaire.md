It all started when Sergey Brin and Larry Page happened to meet at Stanford University.

campus in Palo Alto, pondering attending graduate school. Brin ran into Page and offered to

prototype together at Stanford.
Search Juggernaut is Born
Hosted on university servers, the fledgling search engine started up. But after one year,
Stanford decided the project was taking up way too much bandwidth. Brin and Page switched
servers to keep their dream alive. And the founders named their search engine Google in 1997.
Google got its name from a misspelling of the mathematical term googol, which is the
number 1 followed by 100 zeros. Brin and Page used the term deliberately. Their vision called
for one search engine to organize an infinite amount of data on the World Wide Web.
Despite the founders' vast knowledge base, none of the major Internet portals were
impressed. And the so-called "professionals" on Wall Street in the investment banking business
purposely ignored Google. So Brin and Page began struggling for financial support in Silicon
Valley.

month out in that garage. After establishing a prototype model, they began searching for quali

## NOW is the Time to Buy Canadian Income Trusts

In a nutshell, Canadian Income Trusts are a brilliant structure that allows a business
to avoid taxes. That's one obvious reason they're so incredibly popular with wealthy global
investors.


The other big reason is that by Canadian law, nearly all the earnings from a trust business
must flow right through to its investors, which in this case means you. You no longer have to
settle for the breadcrumbs we call "dividends" here in the South 50!
Here's how trusts work up north: A trust uses what it receives from its product sales to
pay general expenses and service any debt, and it also sets aside a little bit for exploration or
development.
Trusts are very careful to set aside no more than 15% of their gross profits, so at least
85% normally goes directly to your mailbox every month. In other words, you usually get a
check for about 85% of the true earnings. And sometimes it's near 100%!
As an investor, your life doesn't get any smoother than that. Canadian income trusts
aren't structured for the benefit of their CEOs, but their shareholders. What an amazing
concept!
There's been a lot of talk and hand wringing about Canadian Income Trusts being taxed
in the fo.ture. That negative hype is bunk! The fact is some of my favorite Canadian Income
Trusts are 100% exempt from ANY taxes. Period.
And there are key loopholes (namely tax pooling for trusts and retirement accounts for
stockholders) affecting other trusts that most Wall Street analysts have predictably missed.
Even if Canadian Income Trusts are taxed in the future, these companies will still be pay
