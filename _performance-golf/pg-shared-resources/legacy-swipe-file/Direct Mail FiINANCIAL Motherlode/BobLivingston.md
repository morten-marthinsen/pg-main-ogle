lost the war, and by 1923 the exchange rate soared to one trillion marks to one U.S. dollar.
Why didn't the German government act to slow inflation? First, it was a shaky, fragile
government, especially after the assassination of Walter Rathenau, the foreign minister.

working, so the printing presses continued to run. Doesn't that sound familiar?

up to three times a day. His wife would meet him at work, take the money, and run to the
shops to exchange it for food, clothes, soap, etc.-anything that had value. He said it took
a wheel barrow full of money to buy anything. A loaf of bread cost 200 billion marks.
Incredibly, Germany is not the only country in which we can draw experience from.
