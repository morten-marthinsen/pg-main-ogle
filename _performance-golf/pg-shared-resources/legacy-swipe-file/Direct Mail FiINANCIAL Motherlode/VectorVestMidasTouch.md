worth, how safe it was and whether it was okay to buy. I
went into my office and began writing formulas to give me
the answers I needed. By the end of the day, I had written
my formulas for calculating the value of any stock, and a
new world of investing was opened up to me.
The first rule of picking stocks is to
buy stocks that are undervalued.
As time went by, I developed additional formulas
addressing stock safety. When you buy a stock, you
need to know about its financial track record. Has the
company produced consistent, predictable earnings
quarter after quarter and year after year, or has its
financial performance been erratic and unpredictable?
Consistently rising earnings is the engine that drives a
stock's price higher and higher over the years.
The second rule of picking stocks is to
favour stocks with consistently rising earnings.
Finally, I learned the hard way that the only time
you want to buy a stock is when it's rising in price. If
you really want to lose money in the stock market, just
listen to your broker. He11 tell you to buy stocks that
are going down in price. There's hardly a worst thing
you can do.
What you want to do is to buy stocks after they have
gone down in price and have started to go back up.
This is the secret to buying low and selling high and
making your fortune in the stock market.
The third rule in picking stocks is to
buy stocks that are rising in price.
Don't invest and hope
We all know you can't buy just any stock and hope
it will go up in price. All stocks need to be analyzed
before you buy or sell them. Hunches, guesswork,
