# Product Examination Process

This lesson covers the product examination process—the final step in the examination triad. After examining prospects and competitors, we now examine your product.

## The Examination Triad

1. **Prospects** ✓ (completed)
2. **Competitors** ✓ (completed)
3. **Product** (this lesson)

---

## Approach: Think Like a Consultant

Think about this process as if you were a consultant brought in to help design an effective marketing campaign. Even though this is likely your own product or service, you want to:
- Put on fresh eyes
- Think like a consultant
- Operate like a consultant
- Dive deep into your product

---

## What We're Looking For

Now that we understand:
- What your prospects want (from the prospect examination)
- What your competitors are saying to the marketplace (from the competitor examination)

We examine your product to determine:

### 1. What Are You Promising?
- What is the result, outcome, transformation, or payoff your product or service can deliver?
- You'll learn more about developing a full-blown primary promise later
- For now, begin thinking about this as you look at your product

### 2. What Are the Benefits You're Claiming?
- All the sub-benefits and secondary benefits
- All the ways your product improves your prospect's life
- All the ways it eliminates pain
- All the ways it gives them more pleasure
- All the things it helps them gain, do, be, and save

**Critical:** These must be benefits your prospect wants to experience and would value. It's only a benefit if the prospect cares about it.

### 3. How Does It Work?
- Is there a mechanism?
- Is there a method, process, framework, system, formula, or algorithm?
- What is responsible for delivering the result?

Behind every product or service that works, there is always a mechanism. It might be:
- Common or unique
- An ingredient, formula, set of steps, process, or combination

## The 10-Point Product Analysis

### 1. Primary Promise
What is the promise your product or service can present?
- The outcome
- The transformation
- The result

### 2. Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
Is there a unique benefit that only your product or service offers?
- A benefit none of your competitors are offering?
- Something only you can claim?

**Note:** More than likely the answer will be no, and that's okay. But look for it.

### 3. Proof Points
Is there any proof to back up your claims and benefit statements?
- Evidence, stats, or studies linked to performance?
- Reason why claims are true?
  - If you claim it lasts longer—why does it last longer?
  - If you claim it's crystal clear—why is it crystal clear?

### 4. Credibility Factors
What aspects of your journey and history make you credible?

Consider:
- Experience in the field
- Media appearances (radio, podcasts, conferences)
- Methodology used by notable companies, vendors, or recognized figures
- Work with wildly popular people
- Endorsements
- Case studies and testimonials
- Degrees and certifications
- Time in the industry
- Notable achievements in the industry

These establish credibility and demonstrate why prospects should listen to you.

### 5. Mechanism / Delivery Mechanism
How does your product or service work to deliver the result?

Ask yourself:
- What is it about my product/service that allows it to deliver the result?
- Why is it able to deliver the result?
- What does it contain?
- What is it built on?
- What is it powered by?

**Examples by product type:**
- **Teaching/Training:** What's the method, process, system, or framework?
- **Software:** Is there a mathematical formula? An algorithm? A multi-point analysis?

### 6. Track Record
Is there any substantial track record behind your product or service?
- Results achieved (e.g., 99 out of 100 people got extraordinary results)
- Speed of results (works within 24 hours, 48 hours)
- Historical performance (976 winning trade recommendations over 10 years)

Track record demonstrates you're credible and that your stuff works.

### 7. Performance Metrics
Numbers, data, and statistics related to the performance of your product or service.

**Difference from Track Record:**
- **Track Record:** "I've gotten 99 out of my last 100 clients top Google rankings"
- **Performance Metric:** "We're able to get search engine rankings within 24 hours"

### 8. Interesting Points / Gems
Are there any interesting aspects or stories behind your product?
- How it was created
- Why it was created
- What you first stumbled on
- Unique elements of your journey

These can serve as interest points in your marketing.

### 9. Features
Facts about your product or service that your prospect would care about.

**Note:** Not general features like "I wear a blue shirt in my videos"—features that make prospects care more.

**Examples:**
- Videos are HD
- Training is accessible from any mobile device
- Specific technical specifications that matter to buyers

### 10. Benefits
The most important element (besides primary promise and delivery mechanism).

What are all the different ways your product or service improves your prospect's life?

**If you've only identified two benefits, you haven't dug deep enough.** Keep asking:
- What is the benefit of this?
- What else does this improve?
- What else does this save?
- What else does this help them gain, experience, enjoy, do, feel, see?

---

## The Three Types of Benefits

### 1. Functional Benefits
The typical, surface-level benefits most marketers think about.

**Examples:**
- You'll lose weight
- You'll be more efficient
- You'll have more free time
- You'll make more money
- You'll have better cash flow
- You'll be done with print jobs quicker

### 2. Dimensionalized Benefits (Experiential Benefits)
What does the functional benefit actually look like in the prospect's life?

**Example Transformation:**

| Functional | Dimensionalized |
|------------|-----------------|
| "You will generate sales every day" | "Every day when you wake up and check your email, you will see multiple new sales notifications" |

Dimensionalized benefits paint a picture of what they can actually expect to experience. It's real, tangible, and they can see it in their mind's eye.

### 3. Emotional Benefits
How does the dimensionalized benefit make them feel?

**Example Progression:**
1. **Functional:** "You will generate sales every day"
2. **Dimensionalized:** "Every morning when you log on to your email, you'll see dozens of new sale notifications"
3. **Emotional:** "You'll feel excited, inspired, motivated. You'll finally feel on track. You'll feel successful. You'll feel a surge of energy knowing your business is growing."

**Or the inverse (eliminating negative emotions):**
"You'll never again have to feel frustration or disappointment knowing that another day passed with zero money coming into your business."

---

## Benefit Worksheet Process

### Step 1: List Functional Benefits
Write out all functional benefits in the first column.

### Step 2: Dimensionalize Each Benefit
In the next column, create a tangible, experiential picture of each functional benefit.

### Step 3: Add Emotional Benefits
In the third column, describe the emotional impact and payoff of the dimensionalized experience.

### Step 4: Rank by Importance
Go back and rank benefits in order of importance to your prospect:
1. Which is most important/valuable?
2. Which would they want first or most?
3. Continue ranking from most to least important

**Note:** This ranking is somewhat subjective. Use what you learned in the prospect examination to understand what emotions they want to feel and what experiences they want in their "after state."

---

## Summary: The Complete Examination Process

After completing all three examinations, you now have:

| From Prospects | From Competitors | From Product |
|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| What they want | What promises work | Your promise |
| Their emotions | What mechanisms to avoid | Your benefits (functional, dimensionalized, emotional) |
| Their beliefs | Their offers | Your mechanism |
| Their now/after states | | Your proof, credibility, track record |

### How It All Connects

You're looking at your product through the filter of:
1. **What your prospects want** (so you present benefits they care about)
2. **What competitors are saying** (so you can differentiate)

This allows you to:
- Present your product in a way that shows prospects they can experience what they want
- Differentiate from competitors

**Remember:** This is about information collection. You'll learn how to put it all together and assemble everything correctly in later lessons.

---

You've now completed the product examination process—the final piece of the examination triad.
