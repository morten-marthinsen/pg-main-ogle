# Prospect Examination Process

This lesson covers the prospect examination process—the single most important stage of the entire examination process. Everything you do, say, show, present, and demonstrate in your marketing messages must come from your prospect.

## The Examination Triad

1. **Prospects** (this lesson) — Everything stems from here
2. **Competitors** — Examined through the lens of what we learned about prospects
3. **Product** — Examined to find alignment with prospect desires and differentiation from competitors

---

## Core Principle: Everything Flows from the Prospect

You can never know enough about your prospects. The better you understand:
- What moves them
- What motivates them
- What they really want and don't want
- How they're feeling

...the easier it becomes to craft a message that resonates and moves them to buy.

---

## The Six Examination Tools

1. Prospect Awareness Pyramid
2. WEB Analysis (the most important)
3. Now and After Grid
4. Ideal Client Outcome
5. Magic Wand Exercise
6. Promise Exposure Spectrum

## Getting Started: General Demographics and Behaviors

Before diving into the tools, develop a general understanding of your target audience's demographics and behaviors.

**Note:** This is NOT the typical detailed avatar approach (giving them a name, specific family details, etc.). This is a general understanding.

### Demographics to Consider
- Gender (does it slant 80% one way?)
- Income level (higher or lower?)
- Age range (older, younger?)
- Marital status
- Education level
- Political affiliations (if relevant)

### Behaviors to Consider
- Common purchases in the market
- Experts they currently follow
- Movies or TV shows they gravitate toward
- Periodicals, magazines, trade journals, blogs they read
- Favorite books

**Important:** We're not looking for exact science. Don't get stuck on whether your market is "30-40" or "35-40"—knowing they're "middle of the road, 30-40ish" is valuable enough.

## Tool 1: The Prospect Awareness Pyramid

This tool helps you understand what your prospects already know and think about:
1. The problem you solve
2. The different solutions available

### The Five Levels of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)

```
                    ┌─────────────────┐
                    │   Most Aware    │ ← Smallest segment, easiest to convert
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │  Product Aware  │
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │ Solution Aware  │
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │  Problem Aware  │
                    ├─────────────────┤
                    │    Completely   │ ← Largest segment, hardest to convert
                    │     Unaware     │
                    └─────────────────┘
```

### Understanding Each Level

#### Level 1: Completely Unaware
- Unaware they have a problem
- Not in the market for any solution
- **Example:** People without back pain aren't looking for chiropractors

#### Level 2: Problem Aware
- Aware they have a problem
- Unsure what to do about it
- Not actively looking at solutions
- **Example:** Someone with back pain, unsure if they'll do anything about it

#### Level 3: Solution Aware
- Aware of their problem
- Actively looking at different solution categories
- Haven't picked a specific type yet
- **Example:** Deciding between chiropractor, massage therapy, or physical therapy

#### Level 4: Product Aware
- Aware of problem and solutions
- Narrowed down to a specific category
- Looking for the best option within that category
- **Example:** Decided on chiropractic, now finding the best chiropractor

#### Level 5: Most Aware
- Aware of problem, solutions, category, AND your product
- Typically existing customers or tribe members
- **Example:** Knows your practice and is ready to buy

### Key Insights About the Pyramid

**Your market IS the entire pyramid.** The question isn't "which one is my market?"—it's "which segment will I target?"

#### The Trade-offs:

| Going UP the Pyramid | Going DOWN the Pyramid |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Smaller universe | Bigger universe |
| Easier to convert | Harder to convert |
| Shorter marketing needed | Longer/more complex marketing needed |
| Less scale opportunity | More scale opportunity |

#### The Cascade Effect:
A campaign created for a lower level works for all levels above it:
- Campaign for Completely Unaware → works for ALL levels
- Campaign for Solution Aware → works for Solution, Product, and Most Aware
- Campaign for Most Aware → ONLY works for Most Aware

### Matching Message to Awareness Level

| Awareness Level | Appropriate Message Example |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Completely Unaware | ❌ "Best chiropractor in West Palm Beach" won't work |
| Problem Aware | ✓ "The natural way to alleviate back pain" |
| Solution Aware | ✓ "Chiropractic vs. Physical Therapy: Pros and Cons" |
| Product Aware | ✓ "Best chiropractor in West Palm Beach" |
| Most Aware | ✓ Direct offers, familiar messaging |

### Recommendation
**When in doubt, target Problem Aware.** It's low on the pyramid (good scale) but significantly easier to convert than Completely Unaware.

## Tool 2: The WEB Analysis

The most valuable and frequently used tool for understanding prospects.

### W = Wants and Desires

#### Key Principles

**1. Wants are more compelling than needs.**
Just because someone needs something doesn't mean they want it.
- What they need: strict diet, aggressive exercise
- What they want: easy weight loss

**2. We channel existing desire—we don't create it.**
We're not convincing people they should want something. We tap into desires that already exist.

**3. Prospects want results, not products.**
Nobody buys vitamin D tablets to have another bottle in their cabinet. They want stronger bones, healthier cardiovascular system—the benefits.

**4. People buy solutions to existing problems.**

#### Defining "Problem"

A problem can be:
1. **Existing pain, discomfort, or frustration** they want alleviated (moving AWAY from pain)
2. **Unfulfilled desire or want** (moving TOWARD pleasure)

**Examples:**
| Type | Example | Motivation |
|------|---------|------------|
| Pain/Discomfort | Hip pain, debt, arthritis | Moving away from pain |
| Unfulfilled Desire | Making more money, greener lawn | Moving toward pleasure |

#### People Are Driven by Deeper Emotional Benefits

**Vic Schwab's Five Categories of Desires:**

**What people want to GAIN:**
- Health, time, money, popularity
- Improved appearance, comfort, leisure
- Pride of accomplishment, advancement
- Increased enjoyment, self-confidence, personal prestige

**What people want to BE:**
- Good parents
- Social, hospitable, up to date, creative
- Proud of possessions
- Influential over others
- Efficient, first in things
- Recognized as authorities

**What people want to DO:**
- Express their personality
- Not be dominated by others
- Satisfy curiosity
- Emulate admirable people
- Appreciate beauty
- Acquire and collect things
- Win others' affections
- Improve themselves

**What people want to SAVE:**
- Time, money, work
- Discomfort, worry, doubts
- Risk, personal embarrassment

**What people want to AVOID:**
- Trouble, criticism, effort
- Misfortune, worry, doubt
- Risk, embarrassment

#### The Secret Desires Behind Stated Desires

| What They Say They Want | The Secret Desire (Feeling) |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Earn more money, launch a business | Feel more successful |
| Live healthier, have more energy | Feel less mortal, in control |
| Learn something, master a skill | Feel independent, in control of destiny |
| Save money, build nest egg | Feel more secure financially |
| Lose weight, look younger | Feel more attractive |
| Make friends, get dates | Feel wanted, desired |
| Become famous, earn praise | Feel respected |
| Sleep better, eliminate pain | Feel comfortable, at peace |
| Indulge desires, have the best | Feel worth it, feel like they've arrived |

**Key insight:** People buy emotions. They buy feelings. They buy for the feelings they believe they'll experience through the purchase.

---

### E = Emotions and Feelings

How does your prospect feel RIGHT NOW about their problem, and how do they WANT to feel?

**Core emotions that motivate:**
- Greed and fear
- Anger
- Envy
- Embarrassment
- Belonging
- Guilt
- Inadequacy
- Escape
- Lust

**Remember:** These aren't black and white. Under "fear" you might have: disgusted, disappointed, nervous, anxious. Under "anger" you might have: frustrated, upset.

**Questions to answer:**
1. What emotions are they feeling now, coming into your campaign?
2. How do they want to feel after their problem is resolved?

---

### B = Beliefs and Identifications

Your prospect is NOT a blank canvas. They have existing beliefs coming into your marketing campaign.

#### What to Understand

**General beliefs they hold:**
- Any beliefs you need to be aware of that the market as a whole holds?

**Beliefs about the fundamental problem or solution:**
- Do they believe solutions are kept from them?
- Do they believe the problem was created by an industry?
- Do they believe nobody knows the right solution yet?

**Beliefs about the marketplace:**
- Is it filled with charlatans?
- Is it scammy?
- Are people just out to make money?
- Is it good for society?

**Beliefs about your type of product/service:**
- Should it be priced at a certain point?
- Are most of these products garbage?
- Do information products withhold the best stuff?

**Beliefs about others selling in your marketplace:**
- How do they view other vendors, experts, companies?
- Example: Many view attorneys as "shady" (right or wrong)
- Example: Online marketing space—many believe there are fake gurus, fake testimonials

#### Why Beliefs Matter

Their beliefs dictate how they see the world. When you make a claim, you're making it to someone with prior beliefs that impact how they view, understand, and absorb what you say.

**Example - Alternative Health:**
Most believers think the pharmaceutical industry is the enemy. Don't praise Big Pharma in your opening.

**Example - Financial Publishing:**
Typical prospect is 55+ conservative male. Don't positively reference left-leaning politicians.

## Tool 3: The Now and After Grid

Your prospect is experiencing a problem RIGHT NOW and wants to reach an AFTER state.

```
         NOW STATE                    AFTER STATE
    ┌─────────────────┐          ┌─────────────────┐
    │ Problems        │          │ Problems solved │
    │ Frustrations    │   ───►   │ Positive results│
    │ Pain            │          │ Pleasure        │
    │ Absence of want │          │ Desire fulfilled│
    └─────────────────┘          └─────────────────┘
```

**Your job:** Show prospects you can bring them from NOW to AFTER with a better solution—more effectively, efficiently, faster, easier, with less work than anything else.

### The Grid Categories

| Category | NOW | AFTER |
|----------|-----|-------|
| **Have** | What negatives do they have? (pains, debts, dead lawn) | What do they want to have? (income, properties, gorgeous lawn) |
| **Average Experience** | What does their daily experience look like? What symptoms? | What do they want their experience to look like? |
| **Status** | How do they currently view themselves? How do others view them? | What status do they want? |
| **Feeling** | What emotions do they feel now? | What emotions do they want to feel? |

### Example: Dead Lawn Problem

| Category | NOW | AFTER |
|----------|-----|-------|
| Have | Dead lawn, HOA letters, fines | Gorgeous, green, lush lawn |
| Experience | Jump in car quickly to avoid neighbors, embarrassment | Neighbors congratulating them, winning community award |
| Status | Homeowner with embarrassing property | Pride of ownership, impressive home |
| Feeling | Embarrassed, frustrated | Proud, confident |

## Tool 4: Ideal Client Outcome

Imagine your greatest success story—your absolute best client.

### Questions to Answer

1. **What results did they get from working with you?**
   - From learning from you
   - From training under you
   - From coaching/consulting with you

2. **What other transformations happened because of those results?**

### Example: Weight Loss Client

**Core Results:**
- Dropped 40 pounds
- Dropped 18 inches
- Dropped multiple dress/waist sizes
- Now fits into clothes from their 20s

**Secondary Transformations:**
- Became more social
- Goes to more parties and outings
- No longer embarrassed at the beach
- Met more friends/romantic interests
- Comes across as more confident
- Gets more respect from coworkers

**If you don't have an actual example, imagine it.** Write down exact results plus secondary benefits.

## Tool 5: The Magic Wand Exercise

If you could wave a magic wand and grant your prospect ANY outcome related to the problem you address, what would it look like?

**Key framing:** Not what would YOU grant them—what would THEY ask for?

This is blue sky thinking. The perfect world scenario.

**Note:** You'll likely need to back this down when crafting your actual message, but it's a great starting point.

## Tool 6: Promise Exposure Spectrum Analysis

Understanding what promises your prospects have already heard from competitors.

### Why This Matters

Differentiation in marketing is key. There are only two ways to effectively differentiate:
1. **Different Promise** — Promise something different from competitors
2. **Different Mechanism** — How your product works to deliver the result is different

If you don't differentiate on either, you're offering a commodity.

### The Five Stages of Market Sophistication

Markets evolve over time. Every market starts at Stage 1 and progresses.

#### Stage 1: Promise
- First to market only needs to state the promise
- "Take this pill and you'll lose weight"
- Works when no one else has made this promise
- **Rare for most businesses today**

#### Stage 2: Promise Expanded
- Competitors have copied the promise
- Now expand/enlarge the claim
- "Lose 10 pounds" → "Lose 20 pounds" → "Lose 30 pounds and 10 inches"
- **Limit:** Can't expand forever without becoming unbelievable or lying

#### Stage 3: Promise + Unique Mechanism
- Introduce HOW your product delivers the result
- "Take this pill with Garcinia Cambogia, which prevents fat absorption"
- A new mode, method, system, framework, or process
- **This is where most coaches/consultants/experts can thrive**

#### Stage 4: Promise + Expanded Mechanism
- Mechanism gets knocked off
- Now expand/enlarge the mechanism
- "Pharmaceutical grade Garcinia Cambogia"
- "Brazilian rainforest Garcinia Cambogia"
- **More common with physical products**

#### Stage 5: Prospect Experience
- No longer about promise or mechanism
- Focus on prospect's frustration/experience
- "Sick and tired of dieting, exercising, and not losing weight?"
- "Sick and tired of a sluggish metabolism?"

### The Three-Step Analysis Process

1. **Determine the primary promise** of your marketing funnel
2. **Review competitors** — Are they making that promise?
3. **If yes, determine what stage** your prospects are at

### Quick Reference

| Stage | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Your promise hasn't been made yet |
| 2 | Promise made but not being expanded on |
| 3 | Promise expanded but not presented with YOUR unique mechanism |
| 4 | Promise + mechanism presented but not expanded on |
| 5 | Promise + mechanism already expanded |

**For experts, coaches, consultants, agencies:** You can live, breathe, and thrive in Stage 3 (Promise + Unique Mechanism). It's much easier to create a unique mechanism on the fly for information/service businesses than for physical products.

## Summary: The Complete Prospect Examination

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Awareness Pyramid** | Know what they already know; match message to awareness level |
| **WEB Analysis** | Understand Wants, Emotions, and Beliefs |
| **Now and After Grid** | Map their current state to desired state |
| **Ideal Client Outcome** | Envision the best possible results |
| **Magic Wand Exercise** | Blue sky—what would they ask for? |
| **Promise Exposure Spectrum** | Know where to differentiate |

### Ongoing Practice

Consistently return to:
- The WEB Analysis
- The Now and After Grid

These keep your finger on the pulse of your market.

---

**Remember:** Everything in your marketing comes from this examination. You can never know too much about your prospects.
