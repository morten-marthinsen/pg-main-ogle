step and concept so that it's crystal clear to you. Crafting the Purple Offer consists of eight steps
and let me give you a quick overview before I go into details. The first step is you're going to
define the number one problem, outcome, and desire of the target market that you're selling to or
selling your product or service to.

You're then going to formulate those three things into a promise. You're going to gather any proof you might have and this is not only testimonials or reviews but also
other forms of proof that I will show you. You're going to then craft a unique mechanism which when
you do it the way that I show you how to do it, it'll take just a few minutes and you won't get stuck
on this at all. Next you're going to pick out the features you want to use to describe this product
or service and I'll show you how to pick them and how to formulate them. You're then going to go into
step number six which are the benefits of each feature.

So even though you have features and you
explain what those are, we're going to give them another layer of depth when we describe the benefits. Step number seven is when you describe life before, during, and after someone using your product or service
and that's the experience that they have with it. And finally step number eight is you're going to pick
either urgency or scarcity when you give people a reason to buy now and to act today. Step number one
is the problem outcome and desire of the target audience, the target market, and the customers you
have in mind for the product or service. The problem is an unresolved problem that we're looking for and
you're going to ask yourself what is the number one challenge frustration that people are experiencing
that they're trying to solve or fix.

The next one is the unachieved outcome and that is what is
their number one outcome that they need or want to experience as a result of solving their problem. And the third is the unfulfilled desire. When your customers get the outcome that they wanted,
what is the number one desire that will be satisfied in them? So you might have a number of problems that
your target market or buyers are trying to solve and you should actually have an idea of what those,
what several of those problems may be. But you are going to pick one of them and you're going to pick
the one that people feel the most, that people struggle with the most and have the biggest emotional
attachment to.

And you're going to make sure that that is also a problem that your product or service
actually solves. So that's how you decide which one to pick. Now same thing with outcome and desire. There may be multiple outcomes, there may be multiple desires that your target market are trying to
experience or have satisfied in them. And when you do this in the worksheet, I would encourage you to
write them all down, but you're going to select one of them from each category.

Step number two is the
promise that your product or service will help your buyers resolve their number one problem, achieve their
number one outcome, and experience their number one desire. These are the three things that you just laid
out in step number one. And you're going to use a very simple formula to express this for now and we'll modify
this for the sales page later. But you will simply say that with the whatever name of your product or
service that is you're selling, the promise you'll make to people is that they will no longer struggle
with the unresolved problem. Instead, they will finally get their unachieved outcome and they'll experience
true and lasting unfulfilled desire.

So you're going to simply fill in the blanks with those three
categories as you formulate this promise. Step number three is are you going to gather any proof
you have. And when people think of proof, most of the time they think of testimonials or reviews. And these are important and you should ideally have testimonials and or reviews of your product
service. But even if you don't, and even if you do, there are other forms of proof that I want you to
gather and make sure that you do have.

And these are things like third-party data. There may be
research on, let's say you're selling a supplement, there may be research on the ingredients for that

third-party research, third-party data, anything that is science-based that you can use as proof

seem to be confused about it.

But let me show you just how you're going to use this. So first of all,
there are three types of mechanisms. You have the actual mechanism where your offer or your product
or service I should say actually has something unique about it that no one else has. There may
be an unspoken mechanism which is that how things work may be common but no one talks about it or no
one is calling it out and or no one is describing it publicly necessarily. The third form is the
transubstantiated mechanism and that is when you turn something ordinary into something extraordinary.

Now the way we approach this in the purple notebook files is that we actually single out the unspoken
mechanism but we turn it into the spoken mechanism. And that is simply that you're going to gather a
description and write out how your product or service works from beginning to end. You're going to describe
what goes into it, what happens when people use it, how it works, how it all fits together, and you're going to
give it a name. And don't get stuck on this. This does not have to be perfect.

It does not have to be

Step number five are the features of your product or service. And these are
the facts about your product or service and how they work. So you have the deliverables which is what

Once you have that you
move on to step number six which are the benefits of each feature. So you're going to describe, functionally

aspect of that benefit. So how that dimensionalized benefit is going to make a prospect feel.

Step number seven is the experience of your product or service. And that is you're going to describe
what life is like before your product or service. What the experience of the product or service is. What
life is like during that experience. And then what life is like after someone has experienced your product or
service.

The final step is step number eight and that is when you're going to select either urgency or
scarcity to use as part of your offer. An urgency is simply a timing or deadline based urgency. That is
that your product or service is only available for a certain number of hours or days or between certain
dates. Or you can select scarcity which you make it you make the availability quantity based. That is
you can only buy there are only a hundred of your product available.
