# Active Campaign Basic Setup

**Date:** 2018-06-01 | **Duration:** 4m | **Folder:** VCTO

---

Okay, so in this video we're going to go through the basic setup of a brand new active campaign
account. When you first log in, you'll see this sample dashboard that has kind of different placeholders
here to give you an idea of what your dashboard will look like as things start happening within
your account. There's also the whole getting started process here that will walk you through creating a
list, importing your contacts, creating your first campaign, etc. Right now, all we're going to do is walk through the basic settings that you want to make sure
you set up. So down here on the left, click on settings.

You've got your account information with your username and you want to put in your phone
number. You can have a signature that will be attached to the bottom of different emails that you
send out. You also want to make sure you set your time zone and your language. And then under addresses, this will be important for when you're creating lists. So you want to go ahead and add an address here.

Okay. So just fill in the basic address and then click add. So now when I go later on to set up my list, this will be available as an address. Users. Again, if you're going to have additional users in your account, you can set up new users
here.

Add them to different groups such as administrators, editors, etc. Deals, we're not going to be focusing on too much. Deals are primarily if you're running, if you have an offline sales process and you need
to kind of track the status of deals as they move through the negotiation process and things
like that. You can also connect your existing email accounts so that whenever you're communicating with
different deals through your main email system, everything will be tracked inside of that
contact record as well. Under notifications, again, this applies mostly to deals, but when and how you want to be notified
related to deals.

In this section under the email deliverability, for the most part, you're going to let them
manage deliverability and authentication for you. If for some reason you're having problems with deliverability, then you can choose to
manage your own email authentication. You have to go through and set up some DNS records and other things like that. The dates and time formats, most of this doesn't need to change unless you're in a different
country or need this setup to be different. Under the developer settings, you'll have your URL and your API key.

You'll need this when integrating with something like ClickFunnels to connect your account to
ClickFunnels. So this is where you'll get that information. And then tracking, you can enable site tracking. This will require you to copy and paste some code onto your website so that you can track
and trigger different things when somebody visits certain pages. You also have to specify which domains you want this tracking to be available on.

So you just put in your domain name here. You'll copy this code. And we'll come back to this when we're doing the analytics and tracking sections. Site messages. So ActiveCampaign allows you to essentially actually pop up messages to people as they're browsing
your site based on different things that they've done in your system.

You can create rules on this, but this just sets where you want those notifications to
appear. Top left, top right, bottom left, bottom right. Conversions can be tracked. You can use conversion tracking. Again, we'll set this up a little bit later in the tracking section.

Saved responses. So if you have a certain response that you send to prospects over and over again. This is different than a can of emails where you'd be sending an email manually, but you
need to respond with something that you send over and over, you can create responses here. And then calendar, if you want to integrate with your calendar, you can click add calendar,
enter a name for it, determine which this is basically going to be based on tasks, right? So if you have your automations creating tasks for people to do certain things at certain times,
you can create a calendar for that and then you can grab the feed for that calendar and integrate it
with your Google calendar or other things.

So that's the basic setup stuff. And then again, like I said, as we're moving deeper into building campaigns and things like
that, we'll go through setting up how to create your first list and things like that. So that's it for the overview of ActiveCampaign Setup.
