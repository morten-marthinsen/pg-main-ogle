In this lesson, we're going to talk about the key elements of your transaction maximization
pages. That's what goes on these different pages. Now, everything that we're going to
talk about in this lesson applies to the reverse add-on offer page, the soft add-on offer page,
and the mid-tier add-on offer page. It does not apply to the thank you gift offer page,
which we're going to talk about in the next lesson. So let's go through the different key
elements of these pages.

The first is that all of your transaction maximization pages must maintain
a continuity of branding. That just means that they need to look and feel the same. They need to
have the same coloring, the same logo, the same header if possible. They need to look and feel
like they are part of a sequence, not as if they are disconnected pages that don't relate to each
other. As well, your transaction maximization pages need to convey that the transaction is still in
progress.

A mistake that a lot of marketers make, even with the general type of upsell sequence that
you see is after the initial offer is conveying to the customer that the transaction is done. They kind of say, hey, thank you for your order. In the meantime, take a look at this, right? And that's a mistake that I made for a long time. Well, you want to convey that the transaction is still
in progress, that the transaction is still occurring, that they are still in the middle of the ordering
process.

Even though when they submit the order form for the customer captivation offer, we've got
their credit card, we've processed their order. And in essence, the order is complete. We want to convey
