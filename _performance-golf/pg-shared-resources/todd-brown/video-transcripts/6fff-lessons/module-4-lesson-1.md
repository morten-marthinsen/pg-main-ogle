customer captivation offer. Now, taking a peek at our funnel diagram, you can see that the

offer is.

Well, a customer captivation offer is a superior, high-value, low-barrier offer
designed to attract quality customers and transform them into raving fans. So let's break this down a
little bit. Number one, it is superior. Superior to what? Well, as we've already talked about in
module one, it's superior to the offers that are currently out in the marketplace.

It's superior
to competitors' offers. It's high-value because it provides stellar quality content deep in a
particular subject area. Low-barrier offer because it is on the lower-priced side. It's designed to
attract quality customers because it's not a rock-bottom pricing. It's not a $7 report or $9 report or $12
report.

We're going to go over the pricing structure in just a bit. And it's designed to transform them
into raving fans because of the fact that you're giving such a stellar, over-the-top, superior offer
and then you're delivering on that offer. And so let's go over the different elements of a customer
captivation offer. Well, number one, as you already know, you're seeing the common theme here that the offer
is built on your hook and promise, right? So you already know that on the lead magnet offer page, what gets
offered is now what we're going to be talking about on your customer captivation offer page.

And we're going to cover this in great detail in an upcoming lesson, how you actually do this and how
you present your message. But the customer captivation offer is built off of the back of your hook and
promise. It's based on your hook and promise. The customer captivation offer is narrow in focus and
scope. And what that means is that you're not trying to deliver a wide variety of information
about your particular topic.

A customer captivation offer needs to be narrow in scope. And the reason
why it needs to be narrow in scope is because a wide scope offer that is cheap gives prospects the
feeling that there's no depth to the product. For example, if you talked about, you know,
how to work out to lose weight in your, in your, um, in your first product and your customer
captivation, uh, product, well, that's too general, too wide at this price point for prospects to
believe that there's any depth and quality to the subject, because they know that if you were selling
it at a low price point, that it can't, it's either not that good or it's not that deep. And so
customer captivation offer is narrow in focus and scope. And so in terms of, let's say, using this
example of working out to lose weight, we would pick a particular area of that, um, of that subject.

For example, maybe it is, um, the cardio portion of your exercise program to lose weight. And so again,
it's narrow in scope and focus, or maybe it's on abdominal, the abdominal training portion of the
routine to lose weight. And so it needs to be narrow in focus and scope. The price point ranges
between 19 and $29. And you'll understand more about why this is so critical later on when we get
to the, uh, transaction maximization sequence, but your customer captivation offer is priced between 19
and $29.

And so it's not ultra cheap, meaning it's not one of these $7, $9 or $12 offers that attracts
low, uh, low quality, uh, customers. This is low enough so that it's low barrier of entry, but high
enough so that it is weeding out the really low value customers. Your customer captivation offer also
offers a lopsided value proposition. And that just means that what you are offering, what you're going to
deliver, what you're going to give to them compared to the 19 to $29 price point is lopsided in their
favor in terms of value. In other words, if you were offering an ebook for $29, well, that's certainly
not a lopsided value proposition.

But if for example, your customer captivation offer included a, let's say

going to learn in the customer captivation, uh, um, offer, or really your front end product
is also lopsided in their favor. Your customer captivation offer has one or more high value premiums,
or what is commonly referred to as bonuses. And so bonuses can often drive the sale. The key with
the bonuses is we're going to talk about in just a little bit is that they're high value.

They're not
