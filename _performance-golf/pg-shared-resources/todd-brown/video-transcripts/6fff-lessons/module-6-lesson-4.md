# Module 6 Lesson 4

**Date:** 2014-03-01 | **Duration:** 3m | **Folder:** 6FFF Lessons

---

In this lesson, we're going to talk about maximization offer number three, the mid-tier
add-on. And you can see that this offer falls right after the soft add-on. Now, whether a
customer takes the soft add-on or not, they're going to be brought to the mid-tier add-on offer. So let's talk about the elements of the mid-tier add-on offer. Well, just like before, it's got
to be congruent, it's got to add value, and it can no way imply that the customer captivation
offer is incomplete.

Now, the mid-tier is the typical upsell. This is what you typically
see from the average marketer online, only in most sequences you see this far too early. Now, this offer is priced between $297 and $497 with a three-pay option. So customers are
given the option of paying three payments or paying one payment. Now, let's talk about
this three-payment option for a second.

And so let's say the offer is for $297. That's the
offer. Let's say it's for $297. That's what you are charging as a single payment. Well, when
you do the three-pay option, what you want to do is you want to make the total slightly
higher than the single pay.

So if the single pay is $297, you would have the three-pay option
at maybe something like $109. So that the three-pay option, it's less obviously per payment, but
the total is more than the $297. Now, in a lot of cases, even if the three-pay option is more
total-wise than the single pay, you will get a substantial number of people that will take the
three-pay option over the single pay option. But again, this is priced at $297 to $497 with a three-pay
option. Now, this is presented as either a VSL or a long-form sales letter, and this does have a
delayed add-to-cart button.

Obviously, if it's a long-form sales letter, it's not going to have a
delayed add-to-cart button. But if it's presented as a VSL, which is what I recommend you do, it will
be presented with a delayed add-to-cart button where the add-to-cart button will pop up when the
offer is presented. Now, I want you to recognize a couple of things, and I want you to recognize
why this offer converts as well as it does at this particular point in the sequence. Now, an individual that has taken the customer captivation offer, the reverse add-on, and the
soft add-on has proven themselves to be a buyer. And so it makes adding this, especially with a three-pay
option to their order, a lot more likely.

And the reason why we present it with a delayed add-to-cart
button is because at the higher price point, it requires a bit more marketing to make the sale
happen. It requires a longer presentation because odds are there are going to be more features,
advantages, and benefits to present. There are going to be more objections, if you will,
to overcome. There's going to be more marketing that needs to happen at this price point compared
to a $49 price point or a $67 price point. And so because of that, we want to communicate what we
want to communicate without displaying the price right away.

And so that is maximization offer number
three.
