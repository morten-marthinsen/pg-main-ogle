# Module 12 Lesson 1

**Date:** 2014-03-01 | **Duration:** 6m | **Folder:** 6FFF Lessons

---

In this lesson, we're going to talk about how to begin thinking about your six-figure funnel
formula back-end marketing. The back-end is this section right over here on the map. The back-end is all the marketing, all the products, all the offers that you make to
existing customers. And this is where all of the profit is at in your business. This is where all
the big money is in your business.

Remember, the purpose of the front-end funnel is for maximum
customer acquisition. It's the back-end where all the money is in your business. And this is why,
like I said earlier, the real business we're in, the real business of direct response starts after
the first sale. The first sale is all about acquiring a customer. That's why we generate the
first sale.

We don't generate the first sale to generate a profit. We generate that first sale
to create a customer, to plug that customer into your back-end. And that's where all the
money is. That's where all the profit is in the ongoing and frequent offers made to existing
customers. Now, the big mistake that a lot of marketers make when thinking about their back-end
and approaching their back-end is thinking that if they make too many offers, if they make more
offers to existing customers, that customers will get annoyed, that customers will get pissed off
with them.

Well, the reality is most marketers don't make enough offers to existing customers. And you should be a lot more afraid of customers getting bored with you or just going elsewhere and
looking to fulfill their desire for more from a competitor, right? The reality is that most of the time
you cannot present your customers with enough offers. That's the key to the back-end. The key is
being prolific with product and offer creation, putting more and more and more offers out in front
of your existing customers.

Of course, those offers need to be great and they need to deliver a stellar
product and a stellar experience, right? That's how you turn a customer into a one-time customer from a
one-time customer into a lifetime customer. You need to put out enough offers to satiate their desire
for more. And believe me, their desire for more far exceeds what most marketers think. This is why Dan
Kennedy said that in most cases, it's giving enough offers to satiate the desire, the feeling that rabid
customers want to experience.

He said, while they're in heat, you must sell them as hard and as fast as
possible. Now, what is it that you offer or what are the back-end products that you create? Well, here's something
that I first heard from my buddy, Russell Brunson. He said, people will pay more for the same content
delivered via a different medium. And what he meant was that individuals will pay more money, more and
more money for the same content expanded via a different format.

So for example, they might pay $19 for a
book. They may pay $97 for a set of CDs on the same topic. They might pay $397 for a set of DVDs, CDs,
and manuals. They might pay $997 for a live online coaching program that comes with webinars and comes
with a private Facebook group. And they may pay $3,000 for a live weekend event all on the same topic.

So when it comes to your back-end and the product creation, I want you to think about how can you
take your initial content and expand it bigger and deeper via different mediums. Now, there are
a few different categories when it comes to back-end products. The first category is what we would call
manual labor. This is things like consulting, coaching, and done-for-them services. These are
considered manual labor because they require your time and effort and ongoing activity.

Then we have
non-manual labor. That's things like online information products, offline information products,
and software tools. Then we have big paydays. These are things like seminars and conferences,
boot camps. And then we have what's called the toll booth position.

And this is all about making
affiliate offers and joint venture offers. And so you can begin to think about your back-end
within these different categories. And then you can drill deep. Now, when it comes to optimizing your
back-end, what it's all about is realizing that not all customers are created equal. And therefore,
not all marketing to those customers should be created, implemented, and executed equally.

Your best customers, those who purchase from you more often at higher price points,
they must be segmented for better and more frequent offers. The reality is that your best
customers, those who have proven to be buyers, should be presented with offers more often than
average customers. That is the key. The key is to identify your best customers and invest more and
more of your time creating offers and products for those customers, marketing to them differently than
the way you market to the average customer. Now, to have a rocking back-end, like I said earlier,
you must be prolific with product and offer creation.

You have to create more and more products,
more and more offers. That's the name of the business. Remember that the more offers you have,
the higher your customer lifetime value will go. And the higher your customer lifetime value goes,
the more profit you make. That's what the back-end is all about.

It's thinking about what is it that
this customer purchased on the front-end and what can we give them after that to deliver more value
along the same theme as what they bought in their initial purchase. And then doing that more and more
and more.
