In this lesson, I just want to very quickly make sure you understand what a second chance
offer is. I kind of explained it already in the prior lesson, but I really just want to make sure
that you grasp what it is and why it's so valuable. And so a second chance offer is really nothing more than just an additional opportunity for
your new customer to take advantage of any of the different add-on offers that they skipped
during their initial transaction. So basically, compared to the typical quote-unquote upsell sequence where an individual comes in,
buys a product, and then is presented with one-time offers, if you will, they only have
the opportunity to take advantage of the offers once as they're going through the ordering
process. What the six-figure funnel formula does is present add-on offers to new customers, not one-time
offers, but add-on offers to new customers, and takes into account the fact that lots of
customers might want to experience your initial product, your customer captivation product first
before investing any more money.

And so what the second chance offer sequence does is it gives them the ability to consume
some of or all of your initial product to gain trust in you, to help you establish credibility,
and then it gives them the opportunity to go back and take advantage of any of the special
offers that they might have passed on when they were initially going through their ordering
process. Now, keep in mind, the thing that is really cool about this sequence, like I mentioned
in the prior lesson, is that it picks up where they left off. And so it will only make these second chance offers to customers based on what offers they've
skipped. And so if they haven't skipped any of the offers in the transaction maximization sequence, if
they've taken them all, well then, of course, they won't be exposed to any second chance
offers. But if they stopped at a certain point in the transaction maximization sequence, what
we want to do is we want to pick up where they left off and give them a second chance
to grab it.

And then what will happen is it will continue, they will continue to be able to get the additional
add-on offers based on what it is, again, that they originally skipped. And like I said, all this does is give you just an amazing rare, it's rare in the online
marketing world, it gives you this rare opportunity to further monetize buyers, to raise the front
end transaction value of customers coming into your funnel simply by leveraging the second
chance offer. In other words, without second chance offers, you're leaving a tremendous amount of money
on the table, right? You're losing out on all of the dollars and cents, the transactions that you'll get from
these second chances when you're only offering add-ons or upsells once. And so that's what the second chance offer is all about.

That's what the whole amplification autoresponder chain does. And really, let me just tell you kind of a behind the scenes thing a little bit. It's called an amplification autoresponder because it really amplifies the transaction
value. It amplifies, it grows the average dollar value of a new customer for you. And so you understand the long-term value in doing that.

Remember, the more money that the average customer spends with you, the more money you
can afford to invest into traffic to generate a new customer. And all else being equal, the marketer who is able to invest the most amount of money
to acquire a single new customer will win the game. Think of it like this. If your competitors are only using one-time offers and they're not leveraging the second
chance offer part of the six-figure funnel formula, and therefore they're not getting
the additional revenue and sales and profits, so to speak, from the second chance offers,
and you are, well, maybe their average new customer is worth $50, but your average new customer
is worth $80. That means you can afford to spend $30 more to acquire every single customer than they
can.

That means that you'll be able to use certain traffic channels and traffic sources that
they won't be able to because it may be too expensive for them. But for you, because of your second chance offers, you're making more money per new customer,
and therefore you're able to do things that competitors aren't able to do. And when you're able to do that, thanks to your second chance offers and this whole transaction
maximization sequence, man, you'll dominate in your market.
