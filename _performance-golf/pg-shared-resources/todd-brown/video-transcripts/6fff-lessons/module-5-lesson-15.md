# Module 5 Lesson 15

**Date:** 2016-11-28 | **Duration:** 7m | **Folder:** 6FFF Lessons

---

In this lesson, we're going to be talking about engineering the perfect minimum viable
funnel or MVF for short. So, what is a minimum viable funnel? Well, minimum viable funnel is simply a marketing campaign that has the minimum number of elements
that we need to evaluate the big four core elements of the funnel. We always start with a minimum viable funnel when creating a new marketing campaign. The reason why we do this is because we want to test the core elements of the funnel.

Before we create all of the extra pieces, if you will, or all of the enhancement pieces
that you're going to learn about during the enhanced stage, we want to test the big idea. We want to test the lead. We want to test the marketing argument, and we want to test the offer. As you're going to learn more about in a later module, these are the four factors that can
radically impact your conversion rate. These are the factors that can double, triple, quadruple your conversion rate, or these are
the factors that can crush your conversion rate.

And so, before we create all of the other pieces, like the upsell sequence, like the email
follow-up sequence, like all the additional elements, before we begin to enhance and optimize
any piece of the marketing campaign, we want to make sure that we've nailed these four core
elements. That's why we always start when we are putting together and engineering a new marketing campaign,
a new marketing funnel with a minimum viable funnel. Now, in order to test these four elements, there are actually four pieces to the minimum
viable funnel. The first piece is what we call the traffic captivation page. Now, this is our lead capture page, but as you're going to learn in our next lesson, this
is different from the typical lead magnet squeeze page.

You're going to understand how to construct this in our next lesson. For now, I want you to understand that this is the very first piece of the minimum viable
funnel. The next piece in your MVF is the marketing and sales vehicle. This is your marketing page, your sales page, and this is, as you're going to learn in our
next lesson, this might be your single video sales letter page. This might be a hybrid page, a long form sales letter page.

This is the page or pages where we are presenting the marketing message as well as the offer and
ultimately presenting the call to action. The next piece of your MVF is the order form. And this is so that we can process the order. Again, you're going to understand how we do this, how we construct this page in a later lesson. Here is really where the rubber meets the road.

A lot of marketers make the mistake of trying to test a marketing campaign without the order
form, without actually processing the order, or actually, I should say, without having the
prospect enter their payment details. This is a mistake because today there is such a significant order form abandonment rate that
if we don't put the order form in place, we're not going to get an accurate measurement of
how effective this minimum viable funnel is. Ultimately, how effective the four core pieces of a high conversion funnel are. And then the final piece of the minimum viable funnel is the thank you page. And this is really here by necessity.

This is the page that is after the order is complete. This is where you can deliver the product. You can have a simple thank you message. We're actually not even going to be talking about this page in this module because this
is really just personal preference. This is, again, you can have a thank you page.

You can have a product delivery page. This is post order. Now, again, it's important for you to understand that in the minimum viable funnel, we do not have
any kind of prospect follow-up sequence. So even though, as you're going to learn in our next lesson, we're collecting the lead,
the opt-in, if you will, on the traffic captivation page, we don't start when setting up a minimum
viable funnel with any kind of prospect follow-up, with any kind of email follow-up sequence. As well, you'll notice that in the minimum viable funnel, there is no upsell sequence or
add-on offer sequence or downsell sequence.

We're not going to work on that at this point when we're creating the minimum viable funnel
because there's no value, there's no use in having an upsell sequence or an add-on
offer sequence until we have tested the core offer, until we've tested the big idea, the lead,
the marketing argument, and the offer. Imagine a marketer spending a whole bunch of time creating a bump offer for the order form,
creating an upsell sequence, one or two or even three upsells. And then they roll out this new marketing funnel and they see that their core offer doesn't
convert well. They just wasted a whole bunch of time creating the upsell sequence when the main offer doesn't
even work. And if they would have saved that time in creating the upsell sequence and put that time into
testing the minimum viable funnel, they would have figured out what they need to do in order
to make the core offer work.

That's what we want to focus on. Again, we're using the minimum viable funnel to test the four core pieces of a high conversion
marketing funnel so that you can understand how we're doing this. The big idea is tested on the traffic captivation page. We're testing it to see whether the big idea resonates enough with the market to generate
the opt-in. And again, you're going to learn more about this in our next lesson, as well as how to
judge the viability and effectiveness of that page.

When we get to the evaluate stage of the E5 camp, you'll also notice that we're then
testing the lead and the marketing argument in the marketing and sales vehicle. And we're testing the offer with the marketing and sales vehicle, as well as the order form. And so those three pages or three pieces of the minimum viable funnel are what allow us
to test the most important four elements of your funnel. Those are the elements that can double, triple, quadruple response. Those are the four elements that can crush response.

These are the four elements that we always look at and judge and evaluate when creating
and ultimately testing a new funnel. And so in the rest of this module, I'm going to walk you through the first three pages in
the minimum viable funnel. I'm going to explain the traffic captivation page, the marketing and sales vehicle, as well
as the order form. And then of course, after we complete the evaluation stage and get to the enhanced stage,
that's when you'll learn how to properly structure all of your prospect follow-up, your upsell,
add-on offer, downsell sequence, et cetera. For now, continue on to lesson 16.
