# Module 6 Lesson 2

**Date:** 2014-03-01 | **Duration:** 6m | **Folder:** 6FFF Lessons

---

In this lesson, we're going to talk about maximization offer number one, the reverse
add-on. Looking at the map, you can see that this is the first add-on offer that comes immediately
after the customer captivation offer. So immediately after a prospect places their order for the
customer captivation offer, they are brought to the reverse add-on offer page. Now let's talk about
some of the elements of the reverse add-on offer. Like with all add-on offers, this offer needs to
be congruent, right?

It needs to be congruent with the customer captivation offer. This can't be just
a random upsell or random add-on offer. It needs to be tied, linked with whatever it is that you
are offering to prospects in the customer captivation offer. Whatever topic, whatever area,
whatever element it is that you're covering in there, the add-on offer needs to be congruent. Now what you offer these new customers needs to add value to the customer captivation offer.

It
should either improve results that they get with the customer captivation offer, or it should provide
them with the next step after the customer captivation offer. The key is that your reverse
add-on offer in no way, shape, or form implies that the customer captivation offer is incomplete. In other words, one of the biggest mistakes that you can make with any kind of upsell, but especially
the reverse add-on, is to imply that, well, this is really what you need, or this gives you the
complete story, or this is everything that you were after. No, all you'll do is piss off new
customers. And so it needs to be congruent with the initial offer.

It needs to add value to the
initial offer, and it can no way imply that that initial offer is incomplete or will not deliver on
what was promised. Now the reason why this add-on offer is huge, and why this thing converts
at a rate higher than any other type of upsell that I've ever seen as the first upsell or first
upgrade offer after an initial customer captivation offer is because it leverages typical consumer
add-on psychology. The reverse add-on offer is an offer that is priced up to 80% of the customer
captivation offer. In other words, it's at a lower price point than the original customer
captivation offer. It's not at a price point higher, and it's certainly not at a price point
three times higher.

See, remember, keep in mind, think about the way it works in the real world,
the real world of commerce. Anytime a consumer is presented with the opportunity to upgrade their
order or to add something on to their order. It's never more than what the original purchased item
was priced at. It's always a fraction of what the original item is priced at. If somebody goes
through the drive-thru at McDonald's and they're asked if they want to biggie size it or upsize it
or supersize it, whatever it's called, the upsizing it is always a lower price point than the main meal
that they ordered.

If a man were to go in and purchase a new suit and spend $2,000 on a new
suit, when he's then offered the opportunity to add a tie to it for $80 or a beautiful shirt for $150
or a pair of shoes for $200, those things are always a fraction of the $2,000 suit. They're never offered
something that is multiples of the initial offer. And so this type of offer becomes brain-dead
simple. It becomes a no-brainer for your new customers to take advantage of as long as it's
congruent and it adds value and it's something that they want, right? You're tapping into what it is
that they desire most.

And so again, it is priced up to 80%. It's 80% of the price of the customer
captivation offer. Now, the reverse add-on is presented as a video sales letter and it does not
have a delayed add to cart button. So it's presented as a VSL or a short form sales letter
and the add to cart button is immediately displayed. So compared to the customer captivation offer,
the customer captivation offer has a delayed add to cart button.

It pops up when the offer is presented
and I already explained the psychology behind why we do that. In this case, because of the way it's
priced, because it's a reversed add-on, it becomes much more of an impulse purchase. It becomes much
easier for the prospect or the new customer to make a decision. And so we have the add to cart button
displayed immediately. Now, let me give you a simple example of a reverse add-on.

And this was
an offer that you were presented with when you became a Six Figure Funnel Formula client. The initial launch offer price for the Six Figure Funnel Formula is $297. Now, the very first add-on
offer was priced at $199. That's 67% of the initial offer. It's also congruent with the Six Figure Funnel
Formula because it's all about next level marketing funnel maps.

It in no way takes away from anything
that the Six Figure Funnel Formula promised or delivers. And it adds value because it gives
clients the ability to learn new marketing funnels once they reach a higher level. And so this is a
great example of a reverse add-on offer at work.
