# Module 6 Lesson 1

**Date:** 2014-03-01 | **Duration:** 6m | **Folder:** 6FFF Lessons

---

In this lesson, we're going to talk about the purpose of the transaction maximization sequence. So looking at the overall funnel map here, you can see where this sequence falls. So at the
beginning of your funnel, you've got the lead magnet offer page, the lead generation page,
this is where we are generating a lead. And those leads are immediately going to the customer
captivation offer. Well, once a prospect takes this offer, they become a customer and they're
immediately plugged into this transaction maximization sequence.

So what is this sequence? Well, it's a strategic series of add-on offers that tap into existing consumer psychology and
eliminate the common friction that's found in the typical upgrading or upsell process. This is not
your typical upsell sequence where you simply put out higher and higher priced offers in front of
people. This is a strategically designed sequence of offers in a specific order that each do a
specific thing, which I'm going to break down for you in the rest of this module, all designed to
tap into existing consumer psychology. So it makes the upgrading process, the upselling process,
as close to frictionless as possible.

Now the entire purpose, the whole objective of having this
sequence as part of your funnel is to maximize front end transaction value. See, we have a low
barrier of entry offer, the customer captivation offer to ultimately plug new customers into this
transaction maximization sequence. And that's where this sequence takes over and it methodically
increases the average front end transaction, the average amount of money that a new customer spends
with you. Now the front end transaction value is the amount of money, again, new customers spend with
you during their first transaction. And ultimately the overall objective of having this sequence as part of
your funnel is to maximize that front end transaction value so that you can afford to invest more and
more to acquire new customers.

Now we're going to be talking about this in a later module,
but what I want you to understand is that it's this sequence, it's these upgrade offers that increase
the transaction value, that increase the average amount of money that a new customer spends with you
so that a new customer is worth more to you so that you can spend more to acquire a new customer. And the higher your front end transaction value goes, not only the more money that you can invest to
acquire a new customer, to get a new customer, to generate a sale, but it gives you a massive advantage
in the marketplace because as your front end transaction value goes up using a strategic sequence,
like what you're about to learn in the rest of this module, and you can afford to invest in marketing
channels, in media, in certain advertising that your competitors won't be able to do because they
don't have the same size front end value that you do. Now what's really important for you to understand
is that it's this entire sequence from the customer captivation offer to the reverse add-on,
the soft add-on, the mid-tier add-on, all the way through to the thank you page gift offer,
all of which I'm going to break down for you. Like I said, in this module, it's this entire
sequence from beginning to end that is considered the front end. It's not just the customer
captivation offer.

Now, and this is a huge lesson for you, the biggest players in direct response
marketing don't view their front end, don't view this entire sequence as an expense. In other words,
they don't view their front end marketing as an expense. They view their front end as an investment
in customer acquisition, as an investment in future profits and business growth. And so they view this
entire sequence from beginning to end as their front end. And their goal with this entire sequence
is to acquire maximum new customers at break even.

In other words, what I'm trying to say here is that
the money that you generate from the customer captivation offer, the reverse add-on, the soft
add-on, the mid-tier add-on, and the thank you page gift offer all contribute towards the front end. The total value of what you generate from this entire sequence is what allows you to acquire new
customers. In other words, you're going to invest what it is that you generate on average per new
customer through this entire sequence back into the front end to acquire new customers. So if,
for example, and we're going to go through each of these, like I said, each of these offers,
price points, everything, elements, what these are, what these do, etc. But let's say on average from
customer captivation offer all the way through to the thank you page gift offer, your average new
customer is worth $150 to you.

Well, what that tells you is that you can now and should be investing
$150 to acquire a single new customer. That means that you can spend $1,500, let's just say,
right? You can spend $1,500 to acquire 10 new customers because on average, those 10 new customers
are worth $150 each, meaning they're worth $1,500. So if you invest $1,500, that means you're
acquiring new customers at breakeven, you're acquiring new customers for free. And as you're
going to learn later on, the profit, the money in your business takes place on the back end.

And
we're going to be talking about the back end in module 12. For now, what I want you to understand
is the goal of this to increase and maximize transaction value so that you can use the full
transaction value to acquire maximum new customers.
