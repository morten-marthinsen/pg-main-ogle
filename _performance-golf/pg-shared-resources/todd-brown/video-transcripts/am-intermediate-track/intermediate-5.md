Hey, Todd here again at Acquire and Monetize, and in this video training, we're going to cover
how to launch a brand new front-end marketing campaign. You're going to learn how to launch
that front-end campaign as what's called a minimum viable funnel or an MVF. Now,
launching a new campaign as a minimum viable funnel is a way to evaluate the core pieces
of your campaign without wasting a lot of time or a lot of money. See, a big mistake
that a lot of new marketers make, new marketers, new entrepreneurs, is they create a new campaign,
a new marketing campaign, and they create the entire campaign. They'll create all of the
follow-up emails, all the follow-up sequences.

They'll create all the upsell offers, the downsell
offers, the bump offer. They'll create the products and all of the offers for those things. They'll
create all of those pieces before they test the core elements of their campaign. Now, this is a
mistake because why waste time on creating the upsell offers, the downsell offers, the back end,
or the bump offer, the email sequences, the customer email sequence before you test the campaign? Why waste time doing that if your main offer doesn't convert well?

No one's ever going to see
the upsell or the downsell or the customer email sequence. So before we create a full-blown campaign,
we want to test the most essential core elements of your campaign as a minimum viable funnel. The core
elements are as follows. We want to test the idea that you've come up with a compelling, interesting,
unique idea, the headline. We want to test the lead and make sure that it's setting the emotional
hook.

We want to test and evaluate the marketing argument, and we want to test the offer. And we're
able to do that and test these core components with a minimum viable funnel. Now, a minimum viable
funnel typically consists of the following pages, and this by now should look familiar to you. We've
got a lead capture page, we've got a sales page, we've got an order page, and then we've got a thank
you page. Now, we're able to test the big idea and the headline right on or with the opt-in page.

We're
able to test and evaluate the quality of the lead and marketing argument with the sales page, and of
course, the offer both at the end of this page as well as on the order form. This is really important
that we're able to test these elements because without these elements working dialed in, nothing
else matters. Now, you'll notice that in a minimum viable funnel, there is no email follow-up. There's no
email follow-up for leads for the folks that enter their contact information in your lead capture page,
and there is no email follow-up over here attached to this thank you page. As well, there are no upsell
offers after the order form.

We are starting with the minimum viable funnel in order to evaluate
these elements over here. Now, one other thing that I want to point out to you is that one of the
things that you want to do, you want to make sure that if you are testing out a message,
a marketing message, and an offer, and an idea before you have the product done. So, let's say
you do not have the product done yet, and you still want to create a minimum viable funnel to test out
your core elements. You can do that, but here's what you need to do. You still need to have an order
page.

You still need to have your prospects enter their details, their payment details, and you still
need to have them press the submit button, bringing them to the thank you page. But you do not want to
charge their credit card, and you want to let them know that you didn't charge the credit card on this
thank you page. The reason why you want to get the order page submitted, why you want to include this as part of the
test is because most marketers don't realize there is a tremendous order form abandonment rate that is
typically seen with lots of campaigns. And we need to take that into account when we are evaluating
the minimum viable funnel. And so, you want to get them to fill out their information, send them over to
the thank you page, don't process the order, right?

Don't take any payment, and then let them know that
over here. And so, this is how you would start or test all of your campaigns. In other words, these are
the pieces that you would put together and then roll out to the market. So, you don't need to wait
until you have your upsells and downsells and email follow-up sequences to get this campaign out into the
wild and evaluated. Now, the way that we evaluate the minimum viable funnel, there are two ways.

If you are
just getting started in your business and you don't have an existing email list of prospects,
then you're going to go out to cold traffic and you're going to test cold traffic right to your
lead capture page. But if you have a house list, an existing email list already, then you're not going
to start with cold traffic. You're going to start with your house list and you're going to send them
direct to the sales page. You're going to bypass the lead capture page over here. And so, cold traffic,
if you don't have a list, house traffic first, if you do have a list.

And what we're looking for,
if you are starting with cold traffic, we're looking for a 60% return on investment. That means
that we're looking for 60 cents back on the dollar from your MVF. Remember, we're talking about an MVF
here. And so, all we're looking for when testing an MVF to cold traffic is we are looking for greater
than 60% ROI, more than 60 cents back for every dollar. As long as we can get 60 cents back per or more
per dollar, then we can enhance that campaign.

your house list first is because you're not risking any additional capital on cold traffic. And so,
with your house list, you're sending straight to the sales page. And what you're looking for is a 2%
or greater conversion.

So, you're looking to generate two or more sales for every 100 people
that you send there. As long as you get a 2% or more conversion rate, you can then go on to test that
minimum viable campaign with cold traffic. At which point, again, you're looking for that then 60% ROI.
