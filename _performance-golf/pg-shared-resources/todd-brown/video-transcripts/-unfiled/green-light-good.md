It's based on a powerful, big marketing idea. So how do you know if you have the right marketing idea that's big enough and strong enough to build a game-changing marketing campaign around?

Well, you take part in the A&M Greenlight Rally Sessions that happen every single week. Let me start off by showing you how to join and then I'll walk you through exactly how these sessions work. From inside your A&M portal, simply scroll down to the bottom of any page and under News and Announcements,

This way, we can conduct a timely and effective session for you and for everyone joining. Next, when it's time to join, simply click here and you'll be able to join the live session at the appropriate time. And remember, even if you don't have a big marketing idea for review that specific week,

Once you're inside the private Greenlight Rally channel in the A&M community,
you'll be able to submit your idea to the A&M advisors. Now, when you submit your big marketing idea,
this needs to be a written version of a 90-second elevator pitch of your marketing idea. This isn't just a headline. Just imagine if you were walking down the road or talking to somebody in an elevator
and you had 90 seconds or less to share your big marketing idea. That's what you want to submit in written form.

Once you do, the trained A&M advisors will evaluate your marketing idea on the core components necessary
in order to be strong enough to build a marketing campaign around. First, we'll look at, is your marketing idea emotionally compelling? Which means, does it have a primary promise? Something big, bold, audacious, true, and believable. Then, does it convey your primary promise and deliver it by a unique mechanism?

Remember, it's the unique mechanism that gives your prospects hope that this time
they'll get the results they've always wanted. Next, is your big marketing idea focused around one core emotion,
what is known as the dominant resonant emotion? Third, is it focused around one storyline? You'll be noticing a trend here. A great big marketing idea is singular and focused.

Is your big marketing idea timely, based on something happening now or about to happen? Big marketing ideas aren't built on yesterday. They're built on right now and what's happening in the future. Next, is your big marketing idea intellectually interesting? Does it contain an intellectually interesting component,
something that gives the prospect the feeling of discovery or an aha moment
and piques their attention?

Is it focused on one single good idea? Again, back to that singular component of a great marketing idea. Is your big marketing idea unique and different from what's already been said
and heard around the market? And last but not least, if you have already written your headline,
is your headline pithy and tight as can be? Pithy means concise and powerfully forceful.

Is it stated with focus and powerful verbs? Is it using the correct verbiage for the market? Again, this might seem like a lot at first,

your big marketing idea is strong enough to build a winning campaign around. Don't worry though, your A&M advisors will guide you,
and they'll be responsible for making sure that they evaluate your big marketing idea. After you get your evaluation, they'll either say simply,
