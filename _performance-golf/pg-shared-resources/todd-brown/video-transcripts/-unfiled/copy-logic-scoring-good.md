from dozens of other marketers to make your marketing message superior and powerful
when it comes to generating new sales and customers. Well, that's exactly what the CopyLogic scoring sessions are all about.

Let me explain how they're going to work and how to get started. So first off, if you watch the video on the Greenlight Rally sessions, that's where your
new marketing campaigns start. It's where your ideas get evaluated to make sure they're strong enough to build a Grand
Slam marketing campaign around. Once you get the green light from the A&M advisors, you'll be able to write the start of your copy
for your new campaign. Then you could submit it to the CopyLogic scoring session.

Now, let me explain how to join, and then I'll explain how these sessions work. From inside any page within your A&M portal, simply scroll down to the bottom where you'll
see news and announcements. Click on the next upcoming session for details and instructions. You'll notice a couple important things. First of all, in order to have your own copy reviewed as part of the CopyLogic session,
you must submit your copy in advance.

We do this in order to create the most effective and efficient experience for all of the A&M members. To do so, simply click the link on the session. Then when it's time to join the live session, click here. It's also really important to remember, even if you're not submitting copy to be reviewed

sessions every week. They are the fastest and most effective way at you building your marketing chops so that

Let's look at exactly how the A&M CopyLogic scoring sessions work. Remember, your campaign all starts with a solid big marketing idea. And you want to make sure that you have a big marketing idea by going to those green light
rally sessions. Once your big marketing idea gets the thumbs up at the green light rally session, then
you'll start writing the start of your marketing message, which will include your headline and
your lead, which is really just the first couple paragraphs of your marketing message. You'll then submit them the night before the CopyLogic scoring session.

And this is how it'll work. When it's your turn, step one, you'll introduce your copy. This is simply explaining who your market is, who your target prospect is, and a little bit
about your product or solution. Step two, you'll then present your headline. Now, the headline will be scored by the A&M advisors, as well as your A&M fellow community
members.

It'll be scored on a scale of one to four. A score of one indicates you definitely would not read beyond the headline, you meaning the
prospect. A score of two indicates the prospect would probably not read on. A score of three means you probably would read on, but with some doubts or skepticism. And a score of four means you definitely would read on with a high level of interest.

The goal here is for your headline to score a 2.8 or higher to move on. At step three, after you've been scored, it'll then be time for all the A&M advisors and community
members to give you improvements. This isn't a time for opinions, discussion, or criticisms. This will simply be the collective genius and IQ of all the marketers coming together to give
you specific improvements to make your headline even stronger so it converts better and is
more compelling. In order to move on to step four of the CopyLogic scoring process that day, your headline needs
to have scored 2.8 or higher.

If it has, you'll go on to step four, which is now when you'll get a score of your lead. Remember, your lead is the first couple paragraphs of your marketing message. It'll be presented to the group and once again scored on a scale of one to four. The goal here is for you to achieve a score of 3.0 or higher. This means your prospects would probably read on, maybe with some doubt or skepticism, or
if it's really good, that they would definitely read on with a high level of interest.

After you've received the score for your lead, you'll also again get the opportunity to leverage
the collective genius and IQ of the A&M advisors and your fellow A&M members as you gather improvements

session. That's how the CopyLogic scoring process works inside of A&M.
