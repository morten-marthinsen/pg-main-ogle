Now you're ready to take everything you've put in the Send Offer Worksheet and write
a Send Offer Presentation. The Send Offer Presentation is the actual text that you create that represents your
offer. But let's get your mind right before you write anything. Answer these questions for me. By the time your prospect sees your Send Offer, what do they want?

I mean by the time they click your ad, opt in, read your headline, and read or listen
to your marketing message, what do they want? Think about it. What do they have a strong desire for right now? I'm going to give you a second to think. Now if you said the unique mechanism, then you're right.

I'll repeat that because it's important and a lot of our clients don't really appreciate
the significance of this. Your prospect already wants your unique mechanism. They want it. They want it very badly. So let's not focus on talking about all the stuff they're going to get from you.

Instead, let's offer them a way to get your unique mechanism. Now here's my next question for you to think about. In the mind of your prospect, are any of your deliverables worth the price that you're asking
for them? What do you think? The answer is no.

Your prospect doesn't want training. They don't want PDFs or books. They don't want audios. They don't wake up in the morning and say, boy, I really hope I can buy some cheat sheets
today. What they really want is the result, the payoff that they get from using your deliverables.

So what this means is that you must not skimp on benefits during your send-off or presentation. The benefits are what they want to hear. In reality, what happens is that with every benefit they hear, your offer grows in value
in their mind. So when they finally hear your price, if all of that value that has been building up in
their mind is worth it, then they'll buy. I'll give you an example of how I focus on benefits instead of deliverables, features,
and why.

Take a look at this core deliverable. When I write about it, I'm going to spend as little time as I can on the name, the feature,
and the why. In fact, I'll write all three of those things in one sentence if I can, but the benefit,
