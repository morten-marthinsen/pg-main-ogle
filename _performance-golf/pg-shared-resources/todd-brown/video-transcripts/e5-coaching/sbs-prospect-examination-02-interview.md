When I call a prospect, the first thing I do is make sure that they are a prospect and not an existing client or customer or even a lead. The reason I do that is that I don't want them to be telling me things that they think I want to hear. However, it's still okay if you want to talk to a lead or a Facebook group member or even a customer. Just make sure when you do that that you can tell they're being sincere. So when I start the conversation, I have a mind map in front of me that's based on the prospect examination worksheet.

And the first thing I do is I ask them what their biggest pain or frustration or problem is with regards to the solutions that I provide. So, for example, recently I asked a prospect, so when it comes to getting your book actually published, what is your main frustration? Now, since I'm recording the session, I don't worry about taking notes. I just keep an eye on the mind map in front of me. If they start talking about the things they want, I keep an eye on that part of the mind map, and then I lead them down the other branches later in the conversation.

So, for example, if they say that they want to avoid being ripped off by publishers, I'll ask, well, why is that?
