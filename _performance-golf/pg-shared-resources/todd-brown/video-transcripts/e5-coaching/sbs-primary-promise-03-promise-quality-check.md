# SBS - Primary Promise - 03 Promise Quality Check

**Date:** 2021-06-04 | **Duration:** 3m | **Folder:** E5 COACHING

---

In this video, we will quickly look at a few bad examples of primary promises. Read this promise here. Become a top 10 bestseller, become an authority, and close more prospects into clients. Now, what's wrong with it? I'll give you a few seconds to think about it.

The problem here is that this statement actually makes three promises. One, becoming a bestseller. Two, being an authority. And three, closing prospects. It's too much.

Now, you may be thinking, yeah, but my course teaches how to do all three. Or my coaching, I will be able to help them with all three. The thing is that the more promises you make, the more you're going to have to prove,
and the longer your message must be. Another issue is that there are a lot of people, if you think about it,
who want to be a bestselling author, but they don't all want to close clients. So, by making multiple promises, you can actually decrease your audience.

So, limit your primary promise to a single specific promise. Now, as a final comment on this particular issue, you can often combine things into a single promise. For example, if you said this, close three times as many prospects into clients each week as a bestselling authority,
then you're actually promising only one thing, that you're going to close more clients. And the part about bestselling authority becomes how that happens. It becomes a hint at your unique mechanism.

Now, look at this second statement. Generate a tsunami of leads and turn them into loyal customers who beg to buy your product or service for sky-high ticket prices. Now, first of all, the words tsunami and beg and sky-high are hyperbolic and they're flashy. And second of all, they are not very specific. What do you mean by tsunami?

How much is sky-high? In the third example, go from average to awesome, I have no idea what that means. But more importantly, if you had a promise like this, your prospects would decide what you mean for you. Are you talking about getting golfers from a 26 handicap down to a 19 or from a 16 to a 6? Are you talking about playing your instrument at Carnegie Hall?

Or are you talking about just getting your first paying gig? Your promise needs to be specific enough so it leaves little or nothing to interpretation by your prospects. The next promise says, have more energy every day. Now, again, that's very vague. Everyone hearing that promise will have a completely different interpretation of what you mean by it.

So, what do you think it means? I mean, having more energy every day. Does it mean getting the kids off to school in the morning and not needing a nap? If I made the promise that you would wake up every morning completely refreshed without an alarm clock
and stay that way until your head hits the pillow at night, you would know exactly what I mean. So, what I want you to do is look at your primary promise and check that it's specific,
that it's not hyperbolic or flashy,
that it's not vague,
that it's measurable,
and that it solves a problem or delivers a desired result.
