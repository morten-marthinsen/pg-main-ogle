# Choosing Your Core Campaign Message Delivery Asset

**Date:** 2021-02-20 | **Duration:** 4m | **Folder:** E5 COACHING

---

In this lesson, I very quickly want to cover how to choose your core campaign message delivery
asset. What I'm talking about here is choosing the right communication tool to present your E5
message and offer. Should you use a webinar? Should you use a video? Should you use a long
form sales letter or a short form sales letter?

The reality is, is that all of those tools can be
valuable just in the right place at the right time. The way we determine this is really based
on price point. So for lower ticket price products, you can use a short form sales letter, a long
form sales letter, or a video sales letter. For mid tier price products, you want to go with a
video sales letter or a long form sales letter, or even a multi-part video sequence. For higher
ticket products, especially high ticket products, where the investment is thousands of dollars,
you can also use a video sales letter and or a webinar.

Now really you can use a webinar as well
for mid tier. You certainly don't want to use a webinar or a multi-part video sequence for
lower ticket price points. It's just unnecessary. The big difference between higher ticket priced
products, especially high tier in the multiple thousands of dollars compared to mid tier is that
with mid tier, if you use a video sales letter in the end, you're going to present the offer for the
full product with the price point, give a call to action so that they can buy it right now and send
them direct to an order form. Whereas with multi thousand dollar products, when it comes to high
ticket or higher tier price products, the call to action or the offer will typically be for a phone
call, especially when it has to do with an intimate type of product like coaching or consulting, you're
going to need to use the phone in all likelihood.

And so at the end of a video sales letter or at the end
of a webinar, the offer is going to be for a phone call where the prospect can learn more about the
offer. And it's on the phone call that you'll present the full offer, all the deliverables, the
features, all the benefits, the advantages, the price, the terms, all of that kind of stuff. Now, one big
recommendation that I have for you is that you focus on only one or two delivery assets so that you
can become efficient and effective. You can get better at them. You and your team can become more
efficient at creating these things and publishing them rather than using a variety of different
delivery assets and constantly changing up what it is that you do where there's lots of
inefficiencies.

I highly recommend that you pick one or two. Now, I'll tell you my favorite and my
recommendation for you, at least as of the recording of this lesson, is the video sales letter and the
long form sales letter. You'll notice that the video sales letter appears in every category, and that's
because you can deliver a full blown E5 message and offer for lower ticket price products, for mid
tier, for higher ticket using a video. The only difference, again, between mid tier and high ticket
is the offer. When you're doing high ticket, going to a phone call.

When you're doing mid tier or low
ticket, you are going to the offer, right? You're presenting the offer price, sending them over to the
order form. Now, for low ticket, I tend to favor short form or long form sales letters rather than
video. I actually like the written sales letters where the prospect can actually see the low price
point because in those instances, the price point isn't a dissuading factor. If anything, it adds to
the conversion, meaning it gives them a reason to feel good about moving forward.

Whereas at the end of
a video sales letter that we're presenting the price, that's the only time that they're seeing the
price. And throughout the video sales letter, they have no idea what the price is. So with low ticket,
it's cool to just be upfront in terms of to show the price right at the bottom of the sales letter. That's why I favor short form, long form sales letters for lower ticket. I like video sales
letters for mid tier and for high ticket.

I like video sales letters and or webinars again, both with
a phone call as the call to action in the end. So there you go. That's how you choose your core
campaign message delivery asset.
