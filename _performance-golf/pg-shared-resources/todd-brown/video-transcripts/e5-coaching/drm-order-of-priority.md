# DRM Order Of Priority

**Date:** 2021-02-11 | **Duration:** 5m | **Folder:** E5 COACHING

In this lesson, we're going to quickly cover the order of priority with every marketing campaign. What I'm talking about here is what is most important when it comes to creating a campaign. There are really three core elements that I want to make you aware of, and I want you to understand
which is more important than the others and why. So in the order of priority, the top three go like
this. Number one, the single most important element or the single greatest priority, if you will,
with every marketing campaign that you engineer and put out into the marketplace, the number one
most important thing is the audience.

This is referred to as the list. Who are you talking to? And this is critical because it does not matter how good your marketing message is. It doesn't matter
how great your copy is. It doesn't matter how great your offer is.

It doesn't matter if you've
nailed design and every other detail. You could have the most complex, the most beautiful, the most
magnificent marketing campaign. But if you're putting it out in front of the wrong people, people
that aren't interested, people that don't care, people that don't have the problem that you're
addressing, people that don't have the desire that you are tapping into, well, the campaign is going to
fail. It is all about first and foremost, identifying who is the right audience, who are the people that
have the desire that you're tapping into, that have the problem that you can solve, that care about the
issue that you are presenting. Number one, single most critical element is that you've got the right
audience.

Now, in line with this, I would add to it that it's also about understanding the audience. In this context, what we're talking about here is that you're targeting the right people, right? Because again, target the wrong people with a great marketing campaign, doesn't matter, it's going to
fail, right? But this is about, so this is about targeting the right people, being out in front of the
right audience. This is also about understanding that audience and understanding what makes that audience
tick, right?

It becomes difficult, nearly impossible without luck to create a campaign that resonates
on a deep emotional level with what it is that these people are struggling with, what it is that they
want, and so on. If you don't understand the audience, you're going to hear me say that, number one,
in terms of priority, right? It's all about the audience who you're talking to. You're going to hear me

number one. The second thing in order of priority, which surprises a lot of new entrepreneurs, new folks
to the marketing world or the world of direct response marketing, and that is the offer.

What is
the offer? What are you actually offering to give the prospect? What are they getting and what do they
need to give in order to get it? This is the second most critical element. This is another element that

Now in this context, right, what we're talking about is

second priority.

And the third priority is copy. This is the actual words that you use, the message
that you put forth. Now what's interesting here, what surprises a lot of marketers, a lot of entrepreneurs
