# 3 Foundations Of Direct Response Marketing

**Date:** 2021-02-11 | **Duration:** 4m | **Folder:** E5 COACHING

---

In this lesson, we're going to cover the three foundations of direct response marketing. These are the three elements that all of direct response marketing, the type of marketing that
we use, is based on. And I'm sharing this with you because I want you to understand
what it is that we're doing, what it is that we're using, what is kind of undergirding this
entire method of direct response marketing. So the first of the three foundations or three
core principles is psychology. Now, in this context, when I say psychology, I'm talking
about the psychology of the marketplace, specifically your audience, your prospects.

In this context,
what I mean is, what is your market thinking? What is your market feeling? What does your
market want? What are their desires? What don't they want?

What are the problems that they're
having? And how do they feel about those problems? It's the psychology of your audience. More than
anything else, it's critical that you understand the psychology of your audience, that you understand
what makes them tick, that you understand the problems that they're having, the issues,
the obstacles that they're facing, all things that we're going to talk about as they relate to
your product, to your service, to your solution, because everything begins from their psychology. Really, everything that we need to do and say and show and present to lead a prospect to buy,
to invest with you, or everything that we need to do and say and show and present to lead a buyer,
a client, a customer to continue to buy from you starts, first and foremost, with an understanding
of what they're feeling, what they're thinking, what they want, what they don't want, and so forth.

All of that falls under the category of psychology. The second of the three foundations is communication. Of course, this is all about what is it that we're saying? What is it that we're communicating? What is it that we are presenting to your marketplace to ultimately drive them to buy?

Now, this communication obviously is what we say in terms of our copy, our headlines, our calls to
action. This is also the offers that we put together. These are really, this drives everything
that we say and present and show. We're talking about all of our communication, and all of our
communication needs to stem from psychology, right? So first and foremost, we are understanding the
psychology of your marketplace.

We're understanding the psychology of your audience, and then using that
or knowing that or really based on that psychology, we are then designing our message, our communication. And the third of the three foundations of direct response is arithmetic. Sometimes I refer to this as
math, meaning in this context that the beauty of direct response marketing is that it's based on data. It's based on metrics. So in this game of using direct response marketing to grow your business,
we're not reliant on guesses or assumptions or your feelings or your opinions.

We are using data. This is a data-driven business. This is a numbers-driven business, a metrics-driven business. And so it's the
combination of these three foundations that make up direct response. Understanding the market and who
they are and what they want, communicating accordingly, knowing what it is that they want,
knowing what it is that they need to hear and see to move to buy, and then using the numbers,
using the data to make decisions about what's working and what's not working, what needs to be optimized,
what needs to be improved, what needs to be scrapped, and so forth.

It's key that you understand and never
lose sight of these three foundational elements. We're going to continue to go back to them.
