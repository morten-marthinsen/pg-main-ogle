# Understanding Financial Campaign Metrics

**Date:** 2021-02-17 | **Duration:** 18m | **Folder:** E5 COACHING

---

In this lesson we're going to cover your financial campaign metrics. Now you remember there are two
categories of marketing metrics. We already talked about your performance campaign metrics,
the numbers associated with how each step or stage or page in your campaign is performing
and how those metrics are used to improve the results of a campaign. Now we're going to talk
about your financial campaign metrics. These are the metrics that tell us the viability of a campaign.

They tell us how well we're doing turning a dollar of traffic into a dollar or more of sales of
profits. They tell us ultimately what we should be paying for traffic, what traffic is worth to us,
what a lead is worth to us, what it's costing us to get a lead. This is all about the viability from
a financial perspective of your campaign. Now there are six core financial campaign metrics that we're
going to look at and all of these metrics fall under one of two columns. We're either talking
about the cost side or we're talking about the revenue side.

For example we're going to look at
cost per visitor. This is of course what does it cost you to get a single visitor to your marketing
campaign. Next is the cost per lead. So if you're using any kind of traffic captivation page, an opt-in
page, a lead generation page, if you're using two-step marketing where you're generating the lead first
we're going to look at what is our cost per lead. How much are we paying to acquire a lead?

How much
are we paying to get somebody to enter their email address for example? And then we're going to look
at cost per acquisition, CPA. This is one of the most valuable numbers that you'll be using going
forward with all of your media buying really when it comes to assessing the viability of every single
campaign. This is how much does it cost us to acquire a single new customer? What are we paying
on average to acquire a new buyer?

Now this is the cost side of the equation. Then we have the revenue
side of the equation. Just like we have cost per visitor, now we want to look at the other column which
is revenue per visitor. This tells us really what is the average visitor worth to us. So when we say
revenue per visitor, of course we know that some visitors are going to come to your campaign and
they're not going to enter their email address.

They're not even going to move forward. They're
going to bounce. That's okay. We're not talking about an actual revenue number per visitor. We're
really talking about value per visitor.

What is the average visitor to your campaign worth? Now you should
see right off the bat that knowing what it's costing you to generate a visitor and what a visitor is worth
to you tells us whether your campaign is viable or not. If it costs you $5 to get a visitor and every
visitor is only worth $2 to you, well we know that that campaign has an issue that needs to be fixed
regardless of the performance metrics or regardless of how well the different pages are performing,
there is an issue because it's costing us more than what a visitor is worth. Also we've got cost per
lead. We've got of course revenue per lead.

Are we looking at the cost based on a lead? Are we looking at the cost based on a buyer? Are we looking at the revenue based on a visitor? Are we looking at the revenue based on a lead? Or are we looking at the revenue based on a buyer?

All pulling from the same numbers, all giving us insight into the viability of a campaign. So
average order value is again, the average amount of money that a buyer spends with you when they go
