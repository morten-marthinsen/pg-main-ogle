In this lesson, we're going to cover how to identify your campaign constraint. Now, as a reminder, the campaign constraint is the weakest link, the weakest stage,
the weakest step in your marketing campaign, which when fixed will have the largest positive
impact on the overall campaign performance. Really another way to think about this is
think about your campaign as a chain. You know that in every chain, there is a series of links. Well, there is always in every chain, a weakest link.

The weakest link in a chain is what determines
the strength of the chain. So it doesn't matter whether every link in the chain can withstand,
let's say a hundred pounds of pressure. If there's one link that can only withstand 80 pounds of
pressure. Well, when that chain experiences more than 80 pounds of pressure, that chain is going
to snap specifically at that weakest link. Now, it doesn't matter if we go in and we strengthen
all of the other links.

If we make all the other links able to withstand 110 pounds of pressure,
let's say it doesn't matter. That chain is still going to snap once that pressure exceeds 80 pounds. Why? Because it's the weakest link that determines the strength of the chain. And so we want to
identify what is that weak link?

What is the root cause of the problem? Now, like in every system,
