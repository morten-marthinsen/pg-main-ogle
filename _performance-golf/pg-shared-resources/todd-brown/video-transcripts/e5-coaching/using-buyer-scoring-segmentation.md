# Using Buyer Scoring Segmentation

In this lesson, we're going to cover how to use buyer scoring segmentation for more aggressive,
more effective back-end monetization. So let's start with what is buyer scoring. This is actually really simple. This is where you assign a value to each buyer after they complete a campaign and obviously
participate in a transaction. And the value is assigned based on either the total amount of money that they've spent
with you and or the volume of offers purchased.

So what do I mean in this context by the volume of offers purchased? Well, let's say you have a campaign that has a core offer, the offer right before the order
form. It also has a bump offer and maybe it has two upsell offers or add-on offers after that. Well, you could base the buyer scoring value or the point that you assign to either the
total amount of money that a buyer has spent with you through that campaign, or you can
base it on whether the buyer has purchased only the core product, the core product and
the bump, the core product, the bump and upsell one, or the core product, the bump and both
upsells. Now, the value of buyer scoring is that it allows you to identify your hot buyers for
custom communication and more aggressive monetization, meaning it allows you to identify
your hot buyers so that we can communicate with them differently than we communicate with
the rest of the buyers or maybe the average buyers or the below average buyers.

Remember, right? The better that you match your marketing message to your audience's interests and behaviors,
the better your sales conversion rates will be. Buyer scoring gives us insight into who is hot, into their behavior, into the demonstration
of being a rabid buyer, rabid interest. Therefore, we have the chance to follow up with those people differently, to give them
a different path, a unique path, a different, maybe even a better opportunity to spend more
money with you. Now, let me give you some examples of how this can work.
