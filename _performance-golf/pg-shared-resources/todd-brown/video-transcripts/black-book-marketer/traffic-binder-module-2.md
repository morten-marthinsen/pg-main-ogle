# Traffic Binder Module 2

**Date:** 2016-04-21 | **Duration:** 2m | **Folder:** Black Book Marketer

---

Welcome to Traffic Binder Method 2, the forever traffic build-out that drives constant sales. What you want to do right off the bat after you go through Method 1 and get together all your potential partners,
you'll really want to have a system in place that could rank them after you talk to them and email back and forth with them. So, I gave you five key categories I look for with this endorsed traffic ranker I set up for you
to let you plainly see the key players in your market, in your niche, that can send a lot of traffic to you. What you'll want to do is the 80-20 rule. Get the 20% that are the best and spend 80% of your time trying to acquire them to send your emails.

Here are the five categories I use to rank the traffic that a partner can send you. I rank type of traffic, the list size, the email open rate, the click-through rate,
and sometimes the customers on the list if that's available. Now, here's the simple scale I use to rank each category of each partner. Great, good, okay, and not good. Category 1, type of traffic.

Essential synergistic traffic is great. Identical traffic is great. Synergistic traffic is good. Out-of-the-box traffic is good. Category 2 is list size.

The list size can vary depending on the niche, but this is what I go with. 20,000 prospects or more, great. 10,000 prospects or more, good. 5,000 prospects or more, okay. Less than 5,000, not good.

Category 3, email open rate. This is the amount of people that open the email on the total send of the list. 5 to 7% open rate, that's okay. 8 to 11% open rate, that's good. 12% or more, that's great.

Category 4, click-through rate. This is calculated on the entire list size and then the clicks. For example, if you have a list of 20,000, 1% click-through rate would be 200, and that would
only be okay. 2% click-through rate would be 400, and that would be good. 3% click-through rate would be 600, and that would be great.

Now, this one's optional, and it's very hard to get, especially when you first meet a partner. But it's calculated from the list size to the customer base. So, if you have 20,000 people and 200 are customers, that's okay. If you have 20,000 people and 400 to 600 are customers, that's good. And if you have 20,000 people on your list and over 600 are customers, that's great.

That's it. That's how you rank all the partners on your list, and you'll want to put the top 20% up
front and really focus your energy on them.
