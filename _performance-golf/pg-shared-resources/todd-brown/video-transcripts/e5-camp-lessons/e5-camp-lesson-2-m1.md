In this lesson, we're going to talk about the second step of the examination stage of
the E5CAMP framework. We're going to talk about conducting the competitor analysis. Now, in the last lesson, we talked about analyzing your prospects using the web analysis,
demographic profile, behavior profile, etc. This is all about analyzing your competitors
from a marketing perspective. Now, in this context, I'm not talking about competitive analysis to
uncover your competitors' traffic sources or the different steps or stages in their marketing
funnel.

I'm talking about analyzing your competitors from a pure marketing perspective. We want to
understand how they're positioning their product, how they're presenting their product, what it is
that they're offering, etc. Now, in this context, at this stage, what we're really looking for is two
things. One, we're looking to see what it is that your competitors are doing so we know what not to
do. In other words, we're looking to see the positioning statements, the claims, the benefits,
the mechanisms, all the things that I'm going to show you in just a second that they're doing
and using and conveying because we don't want to do and convey and use the same thing.

We want to be
able to come at your market from a different angle, right, with a different perspective, a different
hook, a different promise, etc. In order to do that, we need to know what it is that your competitors
are doing. The second reason why we are doing this analysis is because we want to look for gaps in the
marketplace. We want to see where your competitors are missing the mark, so to speak. What is it that
they're not giving?

What is it that they're not touching on? What is it that they're not offering
that we can come along and fill in that gap with our marketing? Now, we're going to do this with what
I call the 11-point competitor analysis. Now, you want to use this analysis with the top five to seven
direct response competitors in your marketplace. Now, there are two things that I want to point out
here that are very important.

Number one, this is direct response competitors. That's competitors
that are using direct response marketing. We're not looking to analyze the competitors that are using
brand, institutional, name recognition marketing. We're only looking to do this analysis with direct
response competitors, competitors that are using direct response marketing. Now, you might not have
five to seven competitors depending on your market.

You might only have two or you might have three or
you might have none. If you have none, then you don't need to do this analysis and you can skip this. But at the most, we only want to analyze the top five to seven. That's five to seven in terms of
popularity in your marketplace. The second thing that I want you to understand is that when I'm talking
about competitors, I'm not only talking about the individuals that sell or market the same type of
product or service that you do, I'm talking about anybody that your prospects could see as a potential
alternative to your product or service.

For example, what I mean by that is that if a massage therapist
was marketing their services for the alleviation of low back pain, well, there are a lot of other
competitors besides just other massage therapists, right? Chiropractors, physical therapists, over-the-counter
painkillers, over-the-counter creams, surgery, home inversion tables, and so on. Those are all
competitors because remember, prospects aren't looking for products or services. Prospects are looking for a
result. They're looking for an outcome.

And if prospects can perceive or view over-the-counter
painkillers, an inversion table, physical therapy as a potential way of alleviating back pain, that is
considered a competitor. So again, you're looking for the top five to seven competitors as seen by
your prospects that are using direct response. And for the top five to seven, you're going to fill in
the competitor analysis worksheet based on these 11 points. The first point is the hook. What is the
hook or the angle?

Now for right now at this stage, we're just in stage one, right? We're just in the

direct response competitors is what is the hook or the angle that they're using to get the market's
