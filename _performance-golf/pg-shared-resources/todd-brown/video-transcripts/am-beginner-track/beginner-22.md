Hey, Todd here again at Acquire and Monetize. Now, in this video lesson, we're going to talk about the three types of paid traffic. All paid traffic falls under one of these three umbrellas. And it's important when you're getting started that you understand the difference between these three and the pros and the cons. Now, I'll explain all three, and then I'll give you the recommendation for where you should begin.

So the first type of paid traffic is what's called performance-based. Now, this is when you pay based on the volume of times your ad gets displayed. You may pay based on the number of times that your ad gets clicked on. You may even pay based on the number of leads that you get and or the number of sales that you get. There's a variety of different performance-based approaches.

Sometimes you might hear what's called CPM. That's based on a thousand impressions. Other times you might hear CPC. That's cost per click. Both of those are performance-based.

The beauty of performance-based is, as it says, you're paying for performance. So you're only paying when you get a click. You're only paying when your ad gets displayed. Or you're only paying when you get a lead or when you get a sale. Then we've got fixed cost.

Now, fixed cost advertising is when you pay a flat fee up front with no guarantee of any performance. This is, for example, if you were to pay $1,000, let's just say, to have an ad sent out to, let's say, Newsmax's list of subscribers. There's no guarantee you're going to get any clicks. There's no guarantee the emails are going to get open. There's no guarantee you're going to get any leads or any sales.

It's a flat fee. That's what's called fixed cost. And then, of course, the third type of paid traffic is typically what's called a revenue share. This is when you pay a commission for each sale produced. This is typically what you see within the affiliate marketing world, sometimes referred to as joint venture marketing.

This is, for instance, where you would pay a percentage of each sale when an affiliate sends you a sale. So, if you were selling a $100 product, you might have a 50% revenue share or a 50% commission level. And that means for every sale that your partner gives you, you'd pay out 50%. Or, in this case, on a $100 item, you'd pay out $50. In this case, there is no risk because you are only paying out money after you collect money.

And usually, if you're paying out a percentage, meaning of 50% or 25% or 75%, you are first collecting a larger amount of money, and then you're paying out a smaller amount as a commission. This is why revenue shares, affiliate marketing, joint ventures, which all fall under this umbrella, are extremely enticing and extremely exciting for product owners. Now, with that being said, I recommend that when you're getting started, that you begin with performance-based. And the reason why I recommend that you begin with performance-based, which is something that you can utilize with Facebook advertising, with Google AdWords, is because it gives you maximum control. First of all, you can begin with a smaller budget.

So, unlike fixed cost, where you are laying out a certain amount, which is typically a much larger amount than what you'd have to or what you could get started with with performance-based, with performance-based, you can choose your budget. You can also set a daily budget. You can set a monthly budget. You can set a max budget. So, if there's a certain amount of money that you're willing to invest, you can predetermine that in advance, and you will not spend more.

The advertising platform will turn off your ads once you've reached the limit. The other thing is that with performance-based, you get metrics right away. And so, you can make changes as you go. It's kind of like if you've got a $500 budget, for example. Well, using performance-based, you can take 100 of that, and you can test your campaign.

Something that we're going to be talking about in one of the later tracks in Acquire and Monetize. You can start off by testing that $100 and seeing what kind of ROI response, conversions, and sales that you get. And then based on those metrics, based on that performance, you can go back and make changes to your campaign, and then you can take another $100 of your $500 budget, and you can test some additional traffic and see how you've improved performance. And so, what that means is that you have the ability, the opportunity to improve the performance of your campaign before you go through and spend your entire budget. And then once you've seen the performance and you've gotten the performance dialed in, you could then increase your budget over time.

And with performance-based, you could increase your budget, you could pay more per click as a click is worth more to you. All of which you're going to learn later on in the intermediate track, and then certainly more so in the advanced track. And so, now you understand the three different types of paid traffic, and why I recommend that you begin with performance-based. With that being said, go on to the next video training.
