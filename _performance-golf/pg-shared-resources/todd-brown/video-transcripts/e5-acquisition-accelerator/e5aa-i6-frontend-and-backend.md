# E5AA: I6: Frontend and Backend

**Date:** 2020-02-22 | **Duration:** 5m | **Folder:** E5 ACQUISITION ACCELERATOR

---

Direct response-driven entrepreneurs approach marketing very differently from the way the typical mom-and-pop entrepreneur does for their business. The typical mom-and-pop looks at their marketing with one objective, to generate a sale, to generate a sale at a particular margin. For the typical mom-and-pop, there's no difference in the way they approach marketing to get new customers versus marketing to existing customers. There's no difference for them in terms of what their objective is when it comes to making the first sale or the fifth sale or the 15th sale. Direct response-driven entrepreneurs know better, and therefore, that we approach our business, specifically our marketing, really with two different categories, what's called the front-end and the back-end.

These are two different components, two different areas when it comes to the growth of your business. And therefore, two different types of marketing campaigns. This is actually very simple. Number one, the front-end is all of the marketing campaigns that are designed to turn prospects into new buyers. Front-end marketing campaigns are campaigns that are going out to non-buyers, to individuals that have not bought anything from you.

They could be absolutely cold, not know anything about you, not know anything about your business, your product. Maybe they're on your email list. Maybe they're not on your email list. It does not matter. The front-end is all the marketing that we do to generate a new buyer.

And the aim is to generate a buyer. The aim of front-end marketing is to acquire a new buyer, really to acquire the maximum number of new customers, new clients, new patients, new buyers as possible. We're using the front-end to separate the buyers from the masses in your marketplace. And so the front-end is all of the marketing that we do to generate the first sale for a new customer, right? To turn a prospect into a buyer.

That is a one-time activity for each prospect. It's that first transaction. The back-end is all of the marketing that we do with existing buyers. It's all the marketing that we do with our existing customers, clients, patients, etc., all designed to create additional transactions. And so the back-end is all about marketing to existing buyers that you've got a relationship with, that already know you, like you, and trust you.

The back-end is infinitely easier than the front-end, which is why we require a different approach on the front-end to generate a new buyer than we do to generate a second sale or a third sale or a fourth sale from an existing buyer. This is really, really important for you to understand because this, as you're going to learn, impacts the way that we craft our offers. It impacts the way that we craft our messaging, all the things that you're going to learn with the E5 method. But I just want you to differentiate, and I want you to be aware of this differentiation in your mind, that the front-end, the objective is customer acquisition, maximum new customers. Our aim, when we roll out your first E5 campaign, is for you to be able to acquire the most amount of customers at the right cost every single day, day in and day out, without fail.

That is the objective of the front-end. Customer acquisition, meaning that first transaction, and doing it at scale, maximum number of new customers. The back-end is all about customer monetization. Our back-end campaigns, the type of campaigns that we create, the type of offers that we make, the type of products that we put out as part of those offers, are all there to deliver more value to existing customers and to increase the value of a customer. We call that customer monetization, to generate the second transaction, the third transaction, fourth transaction, and so on.

Now, it's also important for you to understand, before we wrap up this little video, that the most expensive activity in any business is customer acquisition. The most expensive activity in your business is the front-end, is acquiring customers. That's the nature of the beast, right? If you run any kind of traffic, or you pay for any kind of traffic, there's a cost, there's an expense to that. And so, that expense is all handled on the front-end when we're acquiring customers.

And you're going to understand all of this as you learn and master the E5 method. The back-end is where all the profit is in your business. Again, the front-end, single transaction, first transaction. Back-end, second transaction, and on. So, the back-end can consist of multiple transactions, and it's where the profit is made.

So, just think, front-end, maximum customer acquisition, right? Most expensive activity, back-end, where we turn customers into more and more valuable assets to your business by delivering more and more value to them, and where you bank more and more profits. That's the difference between the front-end and the back-end. And so, with that, go on to the next lesson. So, with that, go on to the next lesson.
