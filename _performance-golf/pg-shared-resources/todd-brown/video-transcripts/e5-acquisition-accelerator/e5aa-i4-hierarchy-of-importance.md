# E5AA: I4: Hierarchy Of Importance

**Date:** 2020-02-22 | **Duration:** 5m | **Folder:** E5 ACQUISITION ACCELERATOR

---

For every direct response marketing campaign, every E5 campaign, there's what's called the
hierarchy of importance. This is the order of priority when it comes to different elements
or different aspects of your campaign. There are some parts of your campaign that can make
or break the campaign success. And then there are other parts that can have a very little
impact or very little difference, very little change. And so I want to share with you the
top three.

Number one, in order of importance in this hierarchy of importance is the list
or your audience. It's critical that you are targeting the right people, that you are presenting
an offer to people that are interested in what it is that you are offering, that are interested
in the benefits, that have an existing desire for some results, some change, some solving
of a problem. There is nothing more important than getting your audience right, than targeting
the right people. Great copy, a great offer, a great product, great marketing, a great team,
none of those things will overcome the wrong audience. If you were selling, for example,
a training program, let's say on mixed martial arts, how to become a mixed martial arts,
a UFC warrior.

And you were talking to an audience of elderly men and women. Well, unless they were a
ferocious group of elderly men and women, odds are no matter how great your offer is, no matter how great
the copy is, no matter how great the marketing campaign is, no matter what you do, you are going to
suffer. Why? Because you are talking to the wrong audience. The right audience, an audience of
aspiring UFC fighters, for example, in this example, who are in the market looking for a training
program that desperately want to learn how to do mixed martial arts successfully, right?

You're going
to have a much greater level of success, even if you change nothing within the marketing campaign
itself compared to the audience of elderly men and women. So number one is that you are targeting
the right people. Legendary advertising copywriter, Gary Halbert, once asked his copywriters, his
audience of readers, he said, if we were going to compete in a restaurant, we were going to open up
a burger joint and we were going to go head to head. He said, I would only need one thing to beat
everybody. And he said, so what do you think that one thing is?

And most people responded
with either location or great pricing or great meat, you know, great tasting burgers. And he said,
wrong. Absolutely not. All I need to beat you all is a starving crowd. What Gary was talking about
was the power and importance of the audience.

You get the audience right and everything else
becomes infinitely easier. Now the second priority or the second element in this hierarchy of
importance is the offer. What you are actually offering to prospects. What are you giving them
and what do they need to give you in order to get it? Now you're going to learn the entire E5
methodology for structuring what's called a sin offer.

But what I want you to understand,
what I want you to see right off the bat is this, that the offer comes before the copy. Comes before the messaging. So many marketers today, so many entrepreneurs today invest so much
time, energy, effort, resources, and money trying to dial in the perfect copy, the perfect headline,
the perfect opening sentence, the perfect fourth paragraph on the third page, right? Like the second
sentence of the second paragraph on the fifth page of, you know, of this copy or whatnot, trying to dial
that in and they invest a tremendous amount of time, energy, and effort in trying to get that right,
endlessly editing and tweaking that. And they spend very little time on the offer, constructing the
absolute best offer.

Yet the offer, the right offer can make up for weak copy, but great copy will never
make up for a weak offer. Just like a great offer and great copy will never make up for the wrong
audience, but the right audience perfectly dialed into what it is that you are offering can make up
for a weak, a weaker offer, and certainly weak copy. So in the second part or the second piece of this
hierarchy of importance is the offer, that you get the offer right. I'm going to show you exactly how to do
that. I'm going to show you the elements, but I want you to be fully aware of the importance of having
the right offer.

A great offer can move mountains. Great copy without a great offer cannot. And so list
or audience, offer, then copy. So there you go. That is the hierarchy of importance.
