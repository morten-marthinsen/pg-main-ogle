# The Enormous Marketing Value of a Unique Mechanism

## Differentiation is Critical Today

Today, it's important for you to understand that **differentiation is critical**. You have to have something different to say. What prospects want to know across the board in the overwhelming majority of markets is: **What makes yours different?** And then ultimately: **What makes yours better?**

You're going to learn how we use "different" to get attention, and we use "better" or "superior" to produce the conversion, to produce the sale.

### Nobody Seeks Out "More of the Same"

Nobody wants more of the same. Nobody's going to try another product or service that is:
- Similar to what they've already tried
- Similar to what they've already experienced
- Similar to what they already know
- Similar to what they're already using

**Nobody wants an approach that they already know about, that they already understand, that they already have experience with. They want different.**

### Different is Better Than Better

**When it comes to getting attention, different is better than better. Different gets attention. Better doesn't always get attention.**

You're going to hear me talk about the unique mechanism being **different and better**:
- **Different** (for attention)
- **Better** (for conversion)

But differentiation is critical.

## Why Traditional Differentiation No Longer Works

### Experience Alone is No Longer Effective

If all you are doing is bringing the same thing to the market that others are bringing, and hoping that because of your:
- Personality
- Credibility
- Track record
- Credentials

...they'll choose you - **no good. Not today, not anymore.**

There was a time when:
- Your track record was valuable
- How long you've been in business was valuable
- "We've been in business for 50 years" was a sought-after point of differentiation

Thanks to the internet and technology companies, that is no longer a sought-after point of differentiation today.

### What Prospects Want to Know

**The number one thing that prospects want to know is:**
1. What's different about your thing?
2. Why is that difference better for me?
3. Why does that difference make it better for producing the result?

## The Three Key Advantages of Unique Mechanisms

### 1. Differentiation with a Unique Benefit (USP) is Rare

We already discussed that unique selling propositions are rare in today's marketplace.

### 2. A Unique Mechanism Gives You the Ability to Differentiate with Something New

What prospects, what people are attracted to is **new**. Nobody wants what's old. Nobody wants what's dated. They want what's new.

Just by having a unique mechanism, **it is naturally new to the prospect.**

I don't mean new as in you just created this yesterday. I mean **new to the prospect** - new as in:
- It's not something that they've heard about
- It's not something that they've experienced
- It's not something that they've tried
- It's not something that they've deployed for themselves

Therefore, it is new to the prospect.

#### People Want What's New

This is why people ask when they get together: "So what's new?"

Nobody wants to know:
- What's old
- What's still the same as the last time I spoke to you

Nobody wants to know that. Nobody cares about that. They want to know what's new.

**It's a weird thing in most markets:** People don't even want what's old, even when it is tried and true. After 20 years (two decades) in this direct response world, I can tell you that **people are more enamored with new than they are with old - even if old is tried and true.**

### 3. A Unique Mechanism Allows You to Differentiate While Promising the Same Result

One of the beautiful things about the unique mechanism is that **you don't have to figure out a new promise to make to your prospects.**

You don't have to try to come up with some whizzbang promise of:
- Outcome
- Transformation
- Result

**You don't need to do that** because we're not going to differentiate based on the promise.

In most cases, you don't have a USP. So because you don't have a USP, we're not going to differentiate based on the promise.

#### How It Works

We are able to:
1. Identify what is the outcome that your prospects want
2. Identify what is the result that they want
3. Identify what is the change that they want
4. Identify what is the problem that they are trying to solve
5. Identify what it looks like for them when it is solved

We are able to differentiate you from everybody else, **even if everybody else is making that same promise.**

**You can promise the same result, and with a unique mechanism, we still differentiate.** We still present yours as:
- Exciting (because it's new)
- Different
- Superior

## The Unique Mechanism Answers "Why This?"

Ultimately, what I want you to understand: when people say "Why this? Why yours?" - what they're ultimately looking for, what they ultimately want to hear, **what ultimately produces the conversion** is that you've got something that is:

1. **Certainly different from the others**
2. **Superior to the others at giving the prospect what it is that they want**

That's what they want to hear from you. That's what produces the conversion.

### The Foundation of Marketing

At the root of marketing, you have a solution to solve their problem - whether it is:
- An unresolved conflict
- An unmet or unfulfilled need or desire

You've got something to solve their problem.

But what they want to know next is: **"Well, why yours?"**

There are a lot of people, a lot of products, a lot of services that are also promising to relieve that problem.

### The Answer That Produces Conversion

**The answer is:**
1. Ours is different
2. Here's how it's different
3. Here's why it's different
4. Here's why it produces better results for you

**Boom. That's the answer.** That's the answer that leads to the conversion.

When you can't answer that, when you don't have the answer to that, when the answer is "because I've been doing this for 20 years" - nobody cares today that you've been doing it for 20 years.

**What they care about is:**
- How does your thing produce better results for me?
- How is your thing going to get me the outcome, the result, the transformation that I want?
- Easier?
- Faster?
- Bigger results?
- More consistently?

One of those, two of those, a combination of those - **that's what they want to know.**

They want to understand how and why your thing can give them the outcome better than anything else. **The unique mechanism answers that question.**

## A Unique Mechanism Gives Your Prospects Hope

This is where a unique mechanism creates this excitement, this emotional response in the prospect - **because it gives them a feeling of hope.**

**"Maybe this time, maybe with your mechanism..."**

Maybe with:
- This thing
- This framework
- This system
- This approach

That I haven't tried yet, that I haven't heard of before, that I haven't been introduced to before, that is different from all the others that I've tried and failed with...

**"Maybe this time, this'll work. Maybe this is what I've been missing all along. Maybe this is the gap, the missing piece."**

### Why People Buy and Keep Buying

In the majority of your prospects' minds, the reason why people buy and they buy and buy (in many markets, especially when it comes to information products, courses, training programs, coaching, consulting) is this belief:

**"I'm missing something. There is one thing that if I knew it, it would change everything for me."**

That is an inherent belief that people have. That's why people buy. They believe: "This could be the thing. This could be the one thing that I'm missing. This could be the answer. This could fix it all."

When it is different from everything else that you've tried, and through your education-based marketing content (which I'm going to teach you), you are able to prove why it is superior - **it gives them hope.**

It actually gives them hope from the opening of the campaign. When you introduce them to: "There's a new and different way for you to get the result that you want, and this result produces better results" - **that immediately creates hope.**

Hope that:
- This could be it
- This could be the answer

And so it's a very powerful emotion that is stirred from the introduction of the unique mechanism.

## Marketing to More Sophisticated Markets

A unique mechanism allows you to market to more sophisticated markets.

### Understanding Market Sophistication

Markets where:
- There are a lot of competitors
- Prospects have been exposed to a lot of marketing messages
- Prospects have heard a lot of claims
- Prospects have heard a lot of promises

These prospects are more sophisticated.

Those folks, typically the masses of those folks, only respond to what we call **more sophisticated marketing messages.**

### Sophisticated vs. Unsophisticated Marketing Messages

**Unsophisticated marketing message:** A message that is based purely on a claim or promise
- "Lose weight"
- "Grow greener grass"
- "Lose up to 10 pounds a week"

These are unsophisticated marketing messages that only appeal to unsophisticated, low-awareness markets.

**The reality:** If you're in a competitive and saturated marketplace, your prospects have heard most of the claims already.

**What moves them is not:**
- More claims
- More exaggerated claims
- More blown-up claims

**What moves them is:**
- Claims
- Promises
- **Backed by a unique mechanism**
- A different approach to produce that result

## Education-Based Marketing Content (EBM)

The unique mechanism allows you to use what's called **education-based marketing content** (EBM content).

### How EBM Content Works

When you have a unique mechanism, what you are able to do is:

1. **Introduce** this new and different way for your prospect to get the result that they want
2. **Educate** the prospect on this new and different way to get the result that they want
3. **Show** how and why it works
4. **Demonstrate** how and why it works superior or better than all the other methods

Maybe it's:
- Faster
- Easier
- Produces more consistent results
- Produces more results

You're able to educate. **We're not talking about the product.** We're not talking about your offer. We're not talking about you. We're not pitching your product. We're not talking about the features of your product.

**We're educating the prospect on a new and different way to get the result that they want.**

All we're doing is:
- Educating them
- Showing them why it is not only different, but why it is superior
- Teaching them

We're not pitching. We're not selling. **We're educating.**

### The Unique Aspect of EBM

We're educating in a unique way. What I mean by that is **we're educating about one thing, and that is:**
- The unique mechanism
- The value of the unique mechanism
- The value of why the unique mechanism is the superior option for the prospect

**We're not pitching. We're not selling. We're not talking about a product.**

The prospect's sales filter is down because we're educating. But the way we are educating - because we're educating about the unique mechanism - **we lead the prospect to want the unique mechanism.**

Once they want the unique mechanism, that's when we segue into your offer, which is the only place that they can get the unique mechanism.

**Before you ever even go into the offer, they already want the mechanism.** And the offer is the way that they get the unique mechanism.

## The Unique Mechanism vs. The Product

**One critical point:** The unique mechanism and the product are not the same thing.

- **The product is what contains the unique mechanism**
- **The product is what delivers the unique mechanism**
- **The product is how the prospect can learn or experience or benefit from the unique mechanism**

But the unique mechanism and the product are not the same thing.

### Example: E5 Method vs. E5 Coaching

- **E5 method** = the overall unique mechanism (the overall framework)
- **E5 coaching** = the product

When I talk about the E5 method, I'm not talking about the product. I'm not talking about everything that you get with E5 coaching. I'm not talking about:
- My team
- My curriculum
- A coach
- Your questions being answered Monday through Friday
- Your own coach

When I'm talking about the E5 method, I'm educating on this unique and different approach, this superior approach to generating clients consistently day in and day out.

**That's not the same as me talking about E5 coaching.**

### Why This Distinction Matters

This is a tremendously important point because when we use education-based marketing, **we are not talking about your product or your service.** We're not talking about:
- Your coaching
- Your consulting
- Your course
- Your book
- The offer or the deliverables in the offer itself

**We're talking about the mechanism.**

We're able to talk about this mechanism. We're able to educate on this mechanism and:
- What makes it so valuable
- What makes it so effective
- What makes it so different

Then, once we've led them to see: "I understand why that mechanism is the way to go. I understand why that approach, why that framework, why that system is the right way for me to [achieve the desired result]..."

**At that point, when we segue into the offer, they see the value already of the mechanism.** The offer and the product or service in the offer is how they get it.

---

This is a subtle point that I want to make sure that you grasp. The unique mechanism and the product are related but distinct - and understanding this distinction is critical to effective marketing.
