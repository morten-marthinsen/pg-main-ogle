### Step 1 - AGGRESSIVE Examination

#### A. Prospects

- Psychology, communication, arithmetic (math)
- Understand, communicate, economics

##### W.E.B. Analysis

**1. Wants and Desires**

- What result/transformation/outcome are they looking for? Not looking for?
  - What DO they want... VS what DON'T they want
  - Priority problem they want solved
  - What kind of ideal perfect "wave a magic wand" solution do they want
  - You must understand prospects and competitors BEFORE you can understand your product
  - Not a one-time thing, keep your finger on the pulse because markets evolve
    - What was once a desire may no longer be desirable...
    - What was once a great offer may no longer be a "good" offer...
    - What was once a unique mechanism may just be a mechanism

**2. Emotions/Feelings**

- Positive or negative state
- Labels < general understanding applies to as many people as possible
- How do they feel about other solutions? Vendors? (scammers, trustworthy)

**3. Beliefs/Identifications**

- What are the closely-held concepts your prospects live by? (They're not a blank canvas)
- Problems, solutions
- Power rank them by "biggest"/most pressing

##### Prospect Awareness Pyramid

*(Eugene Schwartz - Stages of Awareness)*

- What do prospects already know and think?
- What are they already aware of?
- How aware are they of the problem we're solving and the different solutions out there

**Levels of Awareness:**

1. **Completely Unaware** - no back pain / have some pain but they don't think of it as an issue
2. **Problem Aware** - having pain and it's an issue (not sure how to SOLVE it)
3. **Solution Aware** - know about yoga, chiro, etc.
4. **Product Aware** - choose the right chiro
5. **Most Aware** - know problem, solutions, messages, offers, etc.

**Key Insights:**

- As you go UP in awareness, the total addressable market SHRINKS
  - Higher on the pyramid - EASIER to convert
  - Lower on the pyramid - HARDER to convert
    - Campaigns created for HIGHER levels will work for levels below... but campaigns created at the bottom will NOT work for levels above
    - You can identify LEVEL of Awareness through "keyword research"
- **Unaware Stage** -> You START with an IDEA to pull them in, not an offer, claim, or problem because the person reading/watching isn't there yet
  - **Agora Example:** Started with an IDEA about left-wing politics (Hilary Clinton + Big Pharma)... and then they were selling Olive Leaf Extract

##### Promise Exposure Spectrum (Marketplace Sophistication)

- What promises have my prospects been exposed to?
- Promises expand (get bigger):
  1. **Promise:** How to make money online
  2. **Promise expanded/enlarged:** How to make $10,000 a month online
  3. **Promise expanded/enlarged x2:** How to make $30,000 a month online
     - Promises get to the point where they're hard to believe and ignored
  4. **Promise + (commoditized) mechanism:** How to make $10,000 a month with Facebook ads
  5. **Promise + unique mechanism:** How to make $10,000 a month with the "Triple Stack System"
     - Promising a "proprietary" approach: 3-step process; Purified ingredient; formula; framework (ie. unique)
  6. **Prospect diagnosis** - Where are my prospects at right now? They can be at any or all levels... but this spectrum applies to the market as a whole

### Step 2 - Understanding The Unique Mechanism

> Does this change everything?

#### How a Product Works to Deliver a Result

- To understand a unique mechanism... you must first understand an "ordinary/commoditized" mechanism = solution is available elsewhere in other products (ie. Nature's Bounty - Magnesium)
- Commodities lead to price shopping
- **Unique** = how and why it is able to fulfill the promise. It's unique and only delivered by you

#### 2 Meaningful Points of Differentiation

(increases desire and demand)

##### i. USP = RESULT

Unique BENEFIT only offered by your product or service and not available through any other competitors (benefit your competitors are NOT)

- Or you're the only one talking about it even if multiple people do it
- (RARE: ex: AI Swing Coach - fix your swing with an ai coach)
- Selling a promise they can NOT get from any other product or service

##### ii. UMS = PROCESS

How your product delivers the result

- Can be the same promise others offer
- It's "Unique" to the prospect (not necessarily to the market)
- Allows you to make the SAME promise, yet still differentiate your solution (unique "how to")

**3 Types of Mechanisms:**

1. **Actual**
   - When you actually have a unique mechanism in the product or service
   - When things get patented, those are unique mechanisms

2. **Unspoken**
   - An "unspoken mechanism" is not unique to your product or service, but you're the only one who's talking about it
   - Typically a temporary mechanism, since competitors will start using it too, once they see you using it

3. **Transubstantiated**
   - Ie Trip Wire = turning the ordinary (low ticket offer) into the extraordinary (tripwire)
   - Comes from Roman Catholic wine into blood

#### 2 Aspects To Your Unique Mechanism

**1. Clarity on how and why it works**

- Far more important
- **The Real Secret is NOT In The Unique Mechanism, But Is In The ARGUMENT for the Unique Mechanism**
  - Rock solid airtight emotional and logical argument (prosecutor vibes)
- Being new and different isn't enough (that gets attention), it needs to be superior (gets conversion)
- The power is in the argument (the how and why it works)
- Only claim that which you can prove

**2. The Name**

- Comes from the interrogation
  - Step 1 - how and why it works
  - Step 2 - name based on that
- Interrogating you and your competitors' mechanisms
  - **Action** - see slides
- Argument creates desire to remember the name, versus the name being memorable on its own
- **Promotional/benefit-driven names versus scientific, credible, formal names**
  - It doesn't sound exciting, it sounds legit
  - Nitrogen infused keel

## Understanding The Argument

1. Sound logical argument as the foundation of a marketing message
2. The saying: "Copywriting is salesmanship in print" IS DEAD!
3. **75% marketing 25% selling**
   - Education based marketing = marketing argument

### What is the Argument?

- A set of premises (claims) leading to a conclusion
- If someone believes all the premises they should believe the conclusion
  - There's a belief and there's a reason(s) for that belief
- A string of disconnected claims/benefits/promises is NOT an argument

**Conclusion Formula = Claim + Benefit + Proof**

- People are unlikely to believe something you say without proof
- Need to give RTB (reason[s] to believe it) -> people expect support and explanation
  - The reason why is
  - And that's because
  - Because (Bencivenga's MOST powerful word)
  - The reason why is because
    - What follows "because" serves as proof/support for the claim
- **Cohesion Markers (Discourse markers or Bucket Brigade)**
  - That's why...
  - Because...
  - The reason why is...
  - That's because...
- You don't need RTB for every claim or a promo becomes bloated

### BE CONSCIOUS OF CLAIMS

- Premises imply the conclusion
  - EX: I'm going to work from the library today
  - No you're not.
  - Why not?
  - Because today's Sunday and the library is not open on Sundays

### Coherence Markers + Bucket Brigade/Transitions

- Connects sentences versus them being disjointed

### Claims + Proof + Benefits (CPB) = Buying Belief

- **Buying belief = golden thread = north star = conclusion = marketing campaign thesis**
  1. Buying belief = When prospect switches to believing the mechanism is a superior/the only option
     - Stage 5 messages lead prospect to believe your solution is the ONLY solution
  2. Everything a prosecutor does is to lead the jury to ONE conclusion ("guilty")
  3. Only when the prosecutor has made his entire case to prove the ONE conclusion do they make the "offer" aka closing argument with the CTA to find the defendant "guilty"
  4. **SOCIAL PROOF:** Must follow claims and it should SPEAK to the claim
     - Do NOT put "testimonials" before the OFFER
     - Do put "proof" before the offer and make sure each "proof" piece supports a claim. Otherwise, do not put it in.
     - **NEVER MAKE A CLAIM BIGGER THAN YOUR BIGGEST PROOF POINT**

## Stage 4 Marketing Message

1. One of a superior solution (different and better) -> superior mechanism, superior solution to an existing recognized problem
2. Making an argument for the superiority of the mechanism
   - Argument that PROVES the superiority of the mechanism for producing different results for the prospect
3. **Always start with your "Thesis Statement"**
   - Statement of superiority
     - Only have to be superior in one area
       - Something can get fast results but not be easy, or be easy but not get fast results
     - Nobody Googles the second fastest way to do anything
     - **The [SUPERIOR] way to [PRIMARY PROMISE] is with [UNIQUE MECHANISM]**
       - Educating them on the difference and superiority of the unique mechanism to get the result they want
       - Ex: The fastest way to learn copywriting is with Todd Brown's E5 Method... because it's been used and tested in over 100 markets

   - Primary promise (of outcome)
   - Unique Mechanism
   - Difference in CPB chunks to prove the superiority of your unique mechanism

4. **Theory of constraints** - find and fix the biggest constraint. Fixing the biggest will then naturally reveal the next biggest constraint

## Stage 5 Marketing Argument

1. Not merely a different and better/superior solution but an **accurate diagnosis requiring a new solution**
2. Taking the problem they THINK they have and reframing it as a symptom of a deeper underlying problem or this (NEW) thing is the real problem
3. Change/reframe their perspective so the problem they thought they had is not the real problem they have and there's only one solution to that different problem and that's our unique mechanism
4. The reason why other solutions aren't as effective is because they're addressing the symptoms/other problems not the REAL root problem

### Diagnostic Copy

- An accurate diagnosis in this context is one which demonstrates a new problem (ie. a deeper root cause creating the symptoms)
- Go to a new doctor, he gives a different diagnosis and solves the problem
  - First doctors said it was sciatica, new doctor says it's a bulging disk
  - No matter how many solutions you try to fix sciatica will ever fix their problem because it's not the real problem
- Instilling the belief that only by addressing the new problem can the primary promise be experienced. And only with the unique mechanism can it happen

### Engineering a Moment of Truth

- Creating a change in perspective creates the conditions for a change in behavior
- **Conflict drives choice, choice drives change.**
- To change what people do, you have to change what people see/perceive/believe (perspective)
- Can't continue without experiencing cognitive dissonance
- Creating an internal conflict that makes inaction emotionally painful
- A conflict between what someone wants (their goal) and what they believe to be true about either themselves or their surroundings
  - **Action** - rest of this slide quote

### Options (After the Moment of Truth)

1. **Give up how I've been looking for the answer/solution** - Keep looking for old not root cause solutions (sciatica fixes)
2. **Give up what I want** - Give up on outcome (being pain free)
3. **Give up what I believe to be true about the problem** - Replaces old belief with new one

### Key Principles

- "People think the problem is X, but it is really Y"
- This reframed problem demonstrates why the audience hasn't found the answer/solution yet despite wanting it...

## The Power of a Catalyst

1. The catalyst CREATES a new problem (Ex: New Golf Ball? Putter Rules? Longer Drivers?)
2. An agent that provokes or speeds up significant change or action
3. Is there something happening now or about to happen which gives an advantage to acting on new (reframed) problem earlier than later
4. In this context, the catalyst is the reason for why the new (reframed) problem is now an issue
5. **Ex:** New law passed has negative impact on investment opportunity
   - It's the catalyst that brought about the new problem
   - Ie Facebook iOS slap
6. **Opposite example** (creates a new opportunity) can happen but not as part of Stage 5 because Stage 5 is reframing the PROBLEM:
   - Ex: New law passed about legal cannabis creates new opportunity
   - Builds urgency
