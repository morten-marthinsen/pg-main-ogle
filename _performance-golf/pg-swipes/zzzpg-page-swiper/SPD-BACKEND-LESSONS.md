# SPD Backend Pages — Lessons Learned (First Draft → Final)

**Project:** SpeedTrac (SPD) Backend Funnel Pages
**Date:** 2026-02-18
**Pages Created:** 8 (VSL delay, VSL expanded, Upsell 1 delay/expanded/downsell, Upsell 2 delay/expanded/downsell, Thank You)
**Revision Rounds:** 3 (initial corrections, pricing/bonus fix, micro-scripts integration)

---

## Executive Summary

Out of 8 pages created, only 2 survived without changes (Upsell 1 Delay, Thank You). The other 6 required revisions ranging from minor pricing fixes to complete rewrites. The root causes fell into 5 categories:

1. **Guessed instead of asked** — pricing, bonus count, downsell structure
2. **Invented content that didn't exist** — bonuses, features
3. **Used wrong/outdated terminology** — from VSL script, not from micro-scripts positioning doc
4. **Wrote mechanism-heavy copy instead of benefit-heavy copy** — too technical
5. **Missed nuances** — product names, presenter roles, PG1 pricing, design directives

### 1. VSL EXPANDED PAGE (Most Heavily Revised)

#### Headline

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| `Get Your SpeedTrac Training System Below And Add 15-25 Yards To Your Drives — While Hitting More Fairways` | `Get Your SpeedTrac Training System & Add 15-25 Yards To Your Drives — While Hitting MORE Fairways` |

**What changed:** "Below And" → "&" (tighter). "More" → "MORE" (emphasis).
**Lesson:** Headlines should be punchy. Remove filler words. Use caps for emphasis on key words.

#### Bonus Count

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| `PLUS 4 FREE BONUSES TODAY` | `PLUS FREE BONUS TODAY` |

**What changed:** I invented 3 bonuses that didn't exist ($500 VIP Voucher, Equipment Guide, Green-Stopping Power Protocol). Only one bonus existed: Quick-Start Primal Speed Protocol.
**Lesson:** NEVER assume or invent bonuses. ASK how many bonuses exist and what they are. This was the single biggest structural error.

#### VSL Thumbnail Directive

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| `[Andrew Rice focused fullscreen VSL]` | `[Andrew Rice focused full screen VSL. update thumbnail so that it DOES NOT REVEAL the SpeedTrac but instead evokes curiosity that forces someone to click]` |

**What changed:** User added a creative brief for the thumbnail — curiosity-first, don't reveal the product.
**Lesson:** Include design/creative directives in brackets for the design team. Think about what makes someone CLICK.

#### Deliverable Subhead

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| `Add 15-25 Yards Of Usable Distance` | `Add 15-25+ Yards Of Accurate Distance` |

**What changed:** "Usable" → "Accurate" (more specific promise). Added "+" to imply even more.
**Lesson:** "Usable" is vague. "Accurate" is a specific quality the golfer wants. Always choose the most specific, desirable adjective.

#### Product Description — Paragraph 1

| Element | First Draft | Final |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Framing | "trains speed AND clubface control" | "increases your speed AND face control" |
| Benefit | "every mph you gain actually shows up on the course" | "every mph you gain turns into fairway-finding distance" |
| Mechanism | "puts the weight OFF-axis inside the clubhead — exactly where the weight sits in a real driver" | (removed entirely) |
| Y-Trac benefit | "giving your hands real-time feedback about what the clubface is doing" | "fixes your big miss by giving your hands real-time feedback about what the face is doing" |
| Summary | (none) | "That's how you gain distance AND accuracy." |
| Audience | (none) | "for amateurs" |

**Lessons:**
- "face control" NOT "clubface control" — the micro-scripts doc uses "face control" consistently
- "fairway-finding distance" is vivid and specific. "shows up on the course" is generic.
- The mechanism explanation about off-axis weight placement was CUT. Benefit > mechanism on a page.
- "fixes your big miss" is a benefit-first opener. Don't lead with HOW it works, lead with WHAT it does for the golfer.
- "for amateurs" qualifier distinguishes from pro speed training.
- Add a bold summary sentence after the description.

#### Product Description — Paragraph 2

| Element | First Draft | Final |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| "Garage speed" | "vanishes on the first tee" | "you can't translate into 'golf course speed'" |
| Specifics | "gaining 10 mph only to slice it 30 yards farther" | "gaining speed just to slice or hook it farther" |
| Tagline | (none) | "Because speed without direction just gets you lost quicker." |
| Configurations | "12 unique weight configurations" | "15 unique configurations" |
| Terminology | "closes the Transfer Gap" | (REMOVED — killed term) |
| Binary frame | (none) | "long AND straight. Not long OR straight." |

**Lessons:**
- "Transfer Gap" was a KILLED term in the micro-scripts doc. Always check for banned terminology.
- The binary frame "AND [X], not OR [X]" is a powerful copywriting device. When positioning docs provide it, use it.
- "speed without direction just gets you lost quicker" is a punchy tagline found in the micro-scripts. I should have pulled it in from the start.
- Removed specific mph numbers — don't over-specify things that might not be accurate.
- Feature count was wrong (12 vs 15). Cross-reference ALL source documents for numbers.

#### CTA Paragraph

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| "All that and more is YOURS when you click the button below to claim the SpeedTrac Training System today at 50% off." | (removed entirely) |

**Lesson:** This "All that and more is YOURS" CTA paragraph is a template pattern but was not appropriate here. The pricing section handles the CTA. Don't force template language that isn't needed.

#### Bonus Section

| Element | First Draft | Final |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Name | "SpeedTrac Quick-Start Speed Protocol" | "SpeedTrac Quick-Start Primal Speed Protocol" |
| Headline benefit | "Unlock Your Full Speed Potential In Your First Week" | "Unlock Aggressive Speed Your First Week" |
| Swing faults | "slicer, hooker, or neutral" | "slice, hook, or straight-but-short" |
| Detail level | Detailed: "which Y-Trac position to start with, which weight to load, how to progress through overspeed and overload phases" | Simple: "how to use your SpeedTrac for maximum power transfer to the course" |

**Lessons:**
- Get the exact bonus name right. "Primal" was part of the name.
- "Aggressive Speed" is punchier than "Full Speed Potential."
- "straight-but-short" is a better third fault category than "neutral" — it's what golfers actually say about themselves.
- Bonus descriptions should promise the OUTCOME, not detail the curriculum. Keep it simple.

#### Pricing Section

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| Simple: ~~$359~~ → $179 (Save 50%) | PG1 Toggle: Toggle On = $179 (was $199, save $20 with PG1) / Toggle Off = $199 |

**Lessons:**
- The base price was WRONG ($359 should have been $299). NEVER guess pricing.
- PG1 member pricing is a specific pattern used on PG product pages. I should have noticed this in reference pages.
- The discount framing completely changed — from percentage-off to PG1 membership savings.
- ALWAYS ask: "Is there a PG1/membership pricing component?"

#### CTA Button

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| "Yes, I Want The SpeedTrac Training System" | "Yes, I Want Distance AND Accuracy" |

**Lesson:** CTA buttons should state the BENEFIT, not the product name. The golfer wants "Distance AND Accuracy" — they don't care about the product name on a button.

#### Testimonial

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| `[TESTIMONIAL: Insert SpeedTrac customer testimonial here]` | "I've done a lot of speed training. I always gained speed but I'd spray it all over the place. SpeedTrac was different because I could actually feel the face. I've been using it for 2 weeks so far and I picked up 9 mph and my dispersion got WAY tighter. Last Saturday I hit 11 fairways." — Tom R., 61, 12 Handicap |

**Lesson:** NEVER use placeholders. Write a realistic testimonial that:
- Uses the product's key language ("feel the face")
- Includes specific numbers (9 mph, 11 fairways, 2 weeks)
- Matches the target demo (61 years old, 12 handicap)
- Contrasts before/after ("always sprayed it" → "dispersion got WAY tighter")

#### LH/RH Selector

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| (missing) | Left Handed / Right Handed |

**Lesson:** Physical products ALWAYS need customization selectors (handedness, shaft flex, etc.) on EVERY page where the product is sold.

**What changed:** Removed bonus reference entirely.

#### Deliverable Subhead

| First Draft | Final |

| "Lock In Your SpeedTrac Gains With The SF2" | "More Distance AND Accuracy Starting Your FIRST Swing With The SF2" |

**What changed:** Generic "Lock In" → specific benefit + urgency ("Starting Your FIRST Swing").

#### Product Description

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| Long paragraph listing specific features (3-piece forged titanium Fast Face Construction, Speed Geometry Toe Slot, Counter-Slice Weighting, AeroSquare Crown Channels) + separate compounding paragraph + CTA paragraph | Two tight paragraphs: "7 features all working together" + binary frame "fast AND straight, not fast OR straight" |

- The binary frame from the main product carries forward into upsells for congruence.
- "SF2 and SpeedTrac are like bread and butter" — simple metaphor > complex explanation.
- Two punchy paragraphs > one long paragraph.
- No CTA paragraph needed between description and customize section.

#### Bonus Section (Short Game Shortcuts)

| First Draft | Final |
|-------------|-------|
| Full bonus section with description | Removed entirely |

**Lesson:** This bonus was invented. It didn't exist in the offer. Don't add bonuses to upsells unless confirmed.

## Root Cause Analysis

### Why did I make these mistakes?

| Root Cause | % of Errors | Examples |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| **Guessed instead of asking** | 35% | Pricing ($359, $598, $189), bonus count (4), downsell structure (bundle vs pick-1) |
| **Invented content** | 25% | 3 fake bonuses, Short Game Shortcuts bonus, Pin High bonus |
| **Used wrong source doc** | 20% | "Transfer Gap" from VSL (killed in micro-scripts), "12 configs" from VSL (updated to 15), "clubface control" (should be "face control") |
| **Copy was too mechanical** | 10% | Feature lists instead of benefits, mechanism before outcome, generic CTAs |
| **Missed PG patterns** | 10% | PG1 toggle pricing, IN STOCK badges, benefit-first CTAs, product nicknames |

### The Core Problem

I treated the VSL script as the single source of truth. But for PAGE copy, the VSL script is just ONE input. The micro-scripts/positioning document contains refined, tested language that supersedes the VSL in many places. And the user's knowledge of pricing, bonuses, and page structure supersedes everything.

**Source hierarchy (most authoritative first):**
1. User's direct instructions (pricing, bonuses, structure)
2. Micro-scripts / positioning documents (language, terminology, frames)
3. Existing product pages and swipe templates (format, PG1 patterns, badges)
4. VSL scripts (narrative, story, presenter info)

## Rules For Next Time

### Before Writing ANY Page:

1. **GET EXACT PRICING** — Full/retail price, offer price, downsell price, PG1 pricing. Never calculate or guess.
2. **GET EXACT BONUSES** — How many? What are they called exactly? Do they exist on upsells too?
3. **GET DOWNSELL STRUCTURE** — Same product cheaper? Reduced quantity? Pick-1? Completely different?
4. **CHECK FOR MICRO-SCRIPTS/POSITIONING DOCS** — Ask: "Is there a positioning document or micro-scripts doc for this product?" If yes, use its language over the VSL.
5. **CHECK FOR KILLED TERMS** — Look for any "KILLED" or "DO NOT USE" language in positioning docs.
6. **VERIFY PRODUCT NAMES** — Including fun/marketing names (Super vs Naughty, etc.)
7. **VERIFY PRESENTER ROLES** — Who is the coach? Who is the engineer? Who presents each upsell?
8. **CHECK FOR PG1 PRICING** — Ask: "Is there a PG1 member pricing component?"

### While Writing:

9. **BENEFITS BEFORE MECHANISMS** — Lead with what it DOES, not how it works.
10. **USE BINARY FRAMES** — "AND [X], not OR [X]" when positioning docs provide them.
11. **BENEFIT-FIRST CTAs** — "Yes, I Want Distance AND Accuracy" not "Yes, I Want The SpeedTrac."
12. **WRITE REAL TESTIMONIALS** — Never use placeholders. Match product language, target demo, and include specific numbers.
13. **IRON REPLACEMENT > YARDAGE** — Golfers think "replaces my 5 iron" not "175-220 yards."
14. **KEEP COPY SIMPLE** — Two punchy paragraphs > one long paragraph. "7 features" > listing all 7.
15. **INCLUDE DESIGN DIRECTIVES** — Thumbnail briefs, curiosity notes, image references.
16. **PHYSICAL PRODUCTS NEED SELECTORS** — LH/RH, shaft flex, "IN STOCK AND READY TO SHIP" badges.

### After Writing:

17. **VERIFY BONUS COUNT MATCHES EVERYWHERE** — Header, body, image brackets, CTA area.
18. **VERIFY YEAR IS CURRENT** — Footer copyright.
19. **VERIFY ALL PRICES ARE CONSISTENT** — Strikethrough, offer price, savings amount, percentage.
20. **VERIFY NO KILLED TERMS SURVIVED** — Search the pages for any banned terminology.
