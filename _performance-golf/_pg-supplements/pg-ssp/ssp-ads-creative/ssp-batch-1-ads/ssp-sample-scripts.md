## Script 1: "The Pop Test"

**Concept**: Self-diagnosis creates immediate awareness
**NeuroCopy Framework**: FOCUS + FEAR (Pattern Interrupt → Perception Shift)
**Duration**: 38 seconds
**Setting**: Backyard or garage, casual clothes, morning light

---

### SCRIPT

**[0:00-0:03] HOOK**
*(Influencer looks directly at camera, speaks with genuine curiosity)*

"Do me a favor. Stand up and make a backswing motion. Just turn your shoulders."

**[0:03-0:06] PATTERN INTERRUPT**
*(Pause. Let them process.)*

"Did you hear that? That pop... that crackle... that grinding? That's not supposed to be there."

**[0:06-0:15] PERCEPTION SHIFT (The Problem Reframe)**
*(Tone shifts to informative, like sharing a discovery with a friend)*

"I used to think that was just... getting older. Part of the deal. But turns out, it's actually a protein building up in your joints. Starting around 45, this stuff accumulates and literally glues your swing stiff from the inside out."

**[0:15-0:25] CONTEXT (The Mechanism)**
*(Holds up product bottle, but keeps talking naturally - doesn't "present" it)*

"That's why stretching doesn't fix it. That's why lessons don't fix it. You're not fighting your mechanics. You're fighting biology. This is what's been helping me unlock it."

*(Glances at bottle)*

"It's called Swing Smooth Pro. Formulated by a doctor who works with PGA Tour players. Three ingredients that dissolve that protein buildup."

**[0:25-0:35] PERMISSION (The Timeline)**
*(Sets bottle down, looks back at camera)*

"Five days you start feeling less ache. Eight weeks your swing actually glides instead of grinds. I'm at week six, and that backswing? No crackle. Just smooth."

**[0:35-0:38] CTA**
*(Casual point to link)*

"Link's in my bio if you want to try it."

**[ON SCREEN DURING 0:25-0:35]:**
*These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

---

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **The Hook**: The "do this right now" hook is powerful because it creates immediate participation. The viewer literally feels the problem in their own body.

- **Physical Sensation Language Used**: "pop," "crackle," "grinding," "glues your swing stiff," "glides instead of grinds"

- **Perception Shift**: FROM "This is just aging" TO "This is a specific protein I can address"

- **Why It Works**: FOCUS is captured through pattern interrupt (asking them to move). FEAR is triggered through naming the specific problem. Permission is granted through the timeline.

- **Influencer Freedom**: They can modify the hook to fit their experience. If they don't hear a pop, they can say "If you feel a stuck point halfway through..." or "That tightness that makes you stop short..."

## Script 2: "The Morning After"

**Concept**: Sensory memory of what pain-free felt like
**NeuroCopy Framework**: EMOTION + RELIEF (Desire-Focused)
**Duration**: 42 seconds
**Setting**: Kitchen, morning, coffee in hand, natural light through window

---

### SCRIPT

**[0:00-0:04] HOOK**
*(Influencer is standing at kitchen counter, coffee in hand, looks at camera like remembering something)*

"When's the last time you played 18 holes... and woke up the next day feeling... normal?"

**[0:04-0:08] EMOTIONAL GROUNDING**
*(Slight pause, looks down, then back)*

"Not hobbling out of bed. Not stiff for the first two hours. Just... getting up. Making coffee. Ready to go again."

**[0:08-0:18] PAIN STATE (The Before)**
*(Tone shifts slightly, like confessing)*

"For me, it had been years. Every round came with a price. That lower back thing. The shoulder that takes forever to loosen up. I just accepted it. Figured that's what golf after 50 looks like."

**[0:18-0:28] CONTEXT (The Discovery)**
*(Picks up product bottle from counter)*

"Then I found out about something called stuck joints. Basically, there's a protein that builds up starting in your mid-40s. It literally glues your joints stiff. And there's a formula that dissolves it."

*(Looks at bottle)*

"Swing Smooth Pro. Created by Scottie Scheffler's doctor. Three ingredients backed by clinical research."

**[0:28-0:38] TRANSFORMATION (The After)**
*(Sets down bottle, looks back at camera, genuine)*

"This morning? I played yesterday. 18 holes. Walked the whole thing. And this morning..."

*(Gestures around kitchen)*

"...making coffee. Moving around. No stiffness. No aches. I forgot what that felt like."

**[0:38-0:42] CTA**
*(Small smile)*

"If you want to remember what that feels like too, link's in my bio."

**[ON SCREEN DURING 0:28-0:38]:**
*These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

---

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **The Hook**: Opens with a question that triggers sensory memory. Everyone can remember what pain-free felt like - this makes them FEEL the absence.

- **Physical Sensation Language Used**: "hobbling out of bed," "stiff for the first two hours," "lower back thing," "shoulder that takes forever to loosen up"

- **Emotional Contrast**: Clear BEFORE (accepted pain, paid the price) vs. AFTER (making coffee, no stiffness, forgot what it felt like)

- **Why It Works**: EMOTION is front and center. This script leads with desire/relief rather than fear. It paints the after state so vividly that the viewer starts wanting it.

- **Key Moment**: "I forgot what that felt like" - This is the emotional peak. The influencer should deliver this with genuine surprise/gratitude, not as a sales pitch.

- **Influencer Freedom**: They should share their own "before" experience. What specific pain did THEY accept? Where did THEY hurt? The more specific and personal, the better.

## Script 3: "Scottie's Secret"

**Concept**: Insider access to elite-level care
**NeuroCopy Framework**: AUTHORITY + ASPIRATION
**Duration**: 45 seconds
**Setting**: Golf course (putting green or driving range), or golf cart, wearing golf attire

---

### SCRIPT

**[0:00-0:05] HOOK**
*(Influencer is at a golf course, speaks like sharing insider information)*

"You know how Scottie Scheffler moves? That effortless rotation. Zero restriction. Turns out his doctor just made his joint formula available to everyone."

**[0:05-0:12] AUTHORITY ESTABLISHMENT**
*(Conversational, like explaining to a friend)*

"Dr. Troy Van Biezen. He's been working with Scottie since Scottie was 14. Twenty-plus years with PGA Tour players. Helped guys win over a hundred tour events."

**[0:12-0:22] THE REFRAME**
*(Tone shifts to more personal, relatable)*

"But here's the thing - he didn't make this for tour pros. He made it for us. Recreational golfers. Over 45. The ones who are losing rotation every year and wondering why."

**[0:22-0:32] MECHANISM (Quick)**
*(Holds up product)*

"Swing Smooth Pro. Three ingredients that target the protein that's literally gluing your joints stiff. One dose a day. Five days, you feel the difference. Eight weeks, your swing actually glides."

**[0:32-0:40] TRIBAL CLOSE**
*(Sets product aside, looks at camera with slight smile)*

"Same science the tour pros get. Same doctor. Made for guys like us who just want to stop fighting their own body."

**[0:40-0:45] CTA**
*(Casual)*

"Link's in my bio. Might be the closest we ever get to having a tour doctor in our corner."

**[ON SCREEN DURING 0:32-0:40]:**
*These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.*

---

### DIRECTOR'S NOTES

- **The Hook**: Name-drops Scottie immediately. "Scottie Scheffler" is a pattern interrupt for any golfer. Then promises access to his "secret."

- **Authority Transfer**: Dr. Troy's credentials flow naturally: Scottie since 14 → 20+ years → 100+ tour wins. Let the authority speak for itself.

- **The Pivot**: "He didn't make this for tour pros. He made it for us." This is critical. It prevents the viewer from thinking "that's for elites, not me." It's PERMISSION to believe this applies to them.

- **Physical Language Used**: "effortless rotation," "zero restriction," "joints stiff," "swing glides"

- **Tribal Element**: "Guys like us who just want to stop fighting their own body" - This creates in-group identity. We're recreational golfers. We're not tour pros. But now we have access.

- **Why It Works**: AUTHORITY is the lead trigger, but it's wrapped in ASPIRATION. The viewer isn't just buying a supplement - they're buying access to elite-level care. They're joining a tribe of golfers who refuse to accept decline.

- **Influencer Freedom**: They can add their own "before" if it feels natural: "I've been fighting my body for years..." But the core of this script is the authority transfer, so that should stay intact.

## General Notes for All Scripts

### What Makes These Work

1. **Physical Sensation Language**: Every script includes specific body feelings - "grinding," "hobbling," "stiff for two hours," "glides instead of grinds"

2. **Specific Moments**: "Making coffee the morning after," "Week six," "14 years old" - Specificity creates believability

3. **Conversational Tone**: None of these read like scripts. They read like someone sharing a discovery with a friend.

4. **FATE Model Applied**:
   - Script 1 (Pop Test): FOCUS (pattern interrupt) → FEAR (problem revelation)
   - Script 2 (Morning After): EMOTION (desire/relief) → PERMISSION (you deserve this)
   - Script 3 (Scottie's Secret): AUTHORITY (Dr. Troy) → TRIBE (golfers like us)

5. **PCP Sequence**:
   - PERCEPTION: Each script reframes the problem (it's not aging, it's a protein)
   - CONTEXT: Each explains why THIS solution works (mechanism + authority)
   - PERMISSION: Each grants permission to act (timeline, tribal belonging, access)

### Compliance Notes

- FDA disclaimer must appear on screen for 3-5 seconds minimum
- Place during the transformation/results section, NOT over the CTA
- Do NOT make medical claims ("cures," "treats," "heals")
- Stick to structure/function claims ("supports joint comfort," "promotes flexibility")

### Things to Avoid

- Reading word-for-word (these are guides, not teleprompter scripts)
- "Infomercial" energy or overselling
- Rushed delivery - let pauses happen naturally
- Forgetting to show the product (it should appear at least once)
- Hiding the FDA disclaimer or making it unreadable

## Quick Reference: Which Script for Which Influencer

| Influencer Type | Best Script | Why |
|----------------|-------------|-----|
| **Golfer who's felt the decline** | Script 1 (Pop Test) | They can demonstrate the problem authentically |
| **Lifestyle/wellness influencer** | Script 2 (Morning After) | Emotional storytelling is their strength |
| **Golf instructor or serious player** | Script 3 (Scottie's Secret) | Authority and aspiration resonate with their audience |
| **Older influencer (55+)** | Script 2 (Morning After) | The "years of acceptance" resonates more |
| **Performance/fitness focused** | Script 1 (Pop Test) | The mechanism explanation appeals to their curiosity |

## Adaptation Guidelines

Influencers should feel free to:
- Use their own words while keeping the structure
- Add their personal "before" story
- Adjust the hook to match their experience
- Film in their authentic setting (doesn't have to be a golf course)
- Extend or shorten sections based on what feels natural

Influencers should NOT:
- Skip the mechanism explanation (this is what makes it credible)
- Make medical claims (stick to the script's language)
- Forget the FDA disclaimer
- Oversell or add hype language not in the script
- Hide the product until the very end (it should feel organic, not a "reveal")
