# Performance Golf Brand Guidelines

This skill covers two areas: **Brand Voice & Copy** and **Visual Identity & Design**. Both work together to create a cohesive brand experience.

---

# PART 1: Brand Voice & Copy

## Brand Foundation

**Idea Worth Rallying Around:** Love Your Game

**Promise:** Your path to playing better golf.

**Mission:** Transform the games, and lives, of millions of golfers with the world's simplest personalized golf improvement system.

**Core Values:**
- **Transformation** – Meaningful long-term change, not quick fixes
- **Empowerment** – Simple tools plus confidence to succeed
- **Accessibility** – Golf improvement within reach for everyone

**Identity Transformation:**
- FROM: "I'm a frustrated golfer stuck in scattered confusion"
- TO: "I'm someone who loves their game and gets better every time I play"

## The Enemy & The Weapon

**Enemy: THE SCATTERED PLAYBOOK**
- Random YouTube tips that contradict each other
- Magazine drills with no context
- Buddy's advice from his lesson last week
- Standing over the ball with 12 swing thoughts
- Creates paralysis and confusion

**Weapon: ONE CONNECTED SYSTEM (PG1)**
- Personalized path for THEIR game
- Step-by-step clarity (right thing, right time, right order)
- Adapts as they improve
- Eliminates guesswork

**Linguistic Pattern:**
"You've been [struggling with symptom]. Not because you [lack talent]. But because The Scattered Playbook has been [keeping you confused]. Once you see it, you can replace it. And [PG1's personalized system] is designed specifically to defeat The Scattered Playbook."

## Voice: Brixton

Brixton loves golf, even when he swears he's hanging up his sticks. His hunger to wring the most from life shows in his relentless pursuit of improvement. He practiced tirelessly to find the quickest path to a better swing—now he wants to share it.

**Brixton's priority:** Make it clear PG's system is personalized (right recommendations, right time, right order) without leaning too hard on tech jargon. Let enthusiasm shine—nailing the next flush shot is addictive.

### Voice Pillars

**UNWAVERING** – Calm, Driven, Methodical (NOT: Stern, Emotionless, Dismissive)
- Speak with confidence, frame progress as attainable, meet challenges with encouragement

**CONSTRUCTIVE** – Personalized, Pragmatic, Clarifying (NOT: Clinical, Distant, Robotic)
- Speak human to human, translate data into advice, get personal

**UPLIFTING** – Encouraging, Inspiring, Aspirational (NOT: Rah-rah, Idealized, Insincere)
- Speak joy, don't overhype, remind them it's about more than golf

## Signature Phrases

| Category | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| Primary | Love your game • Your path to playing better golf • Make progress permanent |
| Transformation | Less guesswork. More fireworks • The better you get, the better it feels • Where progress gets personal |
| Emotional | That magical feeling • Once-in-a-lifetime shots should happen more often • Lower your handicap. Raise your fist |
| System | Your straight shot to better golf • To play better golf, you need a clear path • Good game. Great day |

## 11-Point Copy Checklist

Run through before publishing:

1. **Name the transformation** – FROM frustration TO confidence, not just "improvement"
2. **Name the specific emotion** – frustrated, embarrassed, confident, fired up (not vague)
3. **Include physical sensations** – tight chest, death grip, shoulders climbing
4. **Put them in a specific moment** – Saturday morning, first tee, playing with clients
5. **Make progress feel attainable** – real gains, not fantasy outcomes
6. **Emphasize personalization without tech jargon** – "your personalized path" not "AI-powered algorithm"
7. **Lead with feeling, then show data** – emotion first, proof second
8. **Name The Scattered Playbook as the enemy** – call out conflicting YouTube tips
9. **Position PG1 as the weapon** – One Connected System defeats scattered chaos
10. **Use Brixton's voice** – encouraging without rah-rah, confident without dismissive
11. **Focus on loving the game** – joy, confidence, looking forward to next round

## Forbidden Patterns

❌ **Don't overhype** – No "CRUSH 50 YARDS!!!" Focus on attainable progress
❌ **Don't use tech jargon** – "AI-powered ML algorithms" → "Your personalized path"
❌ **Don't be stern/dismissive** – Stay encouraging, never talk down
❌ **Don't use golf clichés** – "Take your game to the next level" is dead
❌ **Don't promise perfection** – Progress, not perfection
❌ **Don't be robotic** – Clinical = bad. Brixton = encouraging + real
❌ **Don't skip the feeling** – Lead with emotion. Data justifies. Never flip it
❌ **Don't focus on pros** – This is for everyday golfers with real lives

## Quick Copy Examples

**Subject Lines:**
- ✓ "Your straight shot to better golf"
- ✓ "Make progress permanent this season"
- ✓ "Less guesswork. More fireworks."

**Social Posts:**
- ✓ "That feeling when your drive splits the fairway exactly like you pictured it."
- ✓ "Progress you can feel. Results you can measure. Golf you can love."
- ❌ "CRUSH YOUR DRIVES LIKE NEVER BEFORE!!!"
- ❌ "Our AI-powered ML algorithms optimize your swing plane"

**Product Description Structure:**
1. Emotional hook (the magical moment)
2. The benefit (what it does for their game)
3. How it works (personalized, data-driven, clear)
4. Call to action (confident, attainable)

# PART 2: Visual Identity & Design

## Brand Concept: There's A Story In Every Swing

Our visual identity captures the cinematic quality of golf's magical moments—the flush iron, the twenty-foot putt, the drive that makes your playing partners stare. Drawing inspiration from film's ability to transform ordinary moments into something memorable, we frame golf with precision, drama, and beauty.

The visual system should feel: **Modern, technical, yet approachable. Confident without being cold. Premium without being elitist.**

## Color System

**Primary Colors:**
- **Performance Orange** (#FD3300) – Our signature. Vibrant, passionate, high-performance.
- **Dark Orange** (#DB2C00) – Deeper variant for emphasis and hierarchy.
- **Black** (#1D1A1A) – Strong, confident, grounding.
- **UI Gray** (#7B726C) – Interface elements and supporting text.

**Neutrals:** Stone (#B3AAA3), Pebble (#DFD9D5), Sand (#ECE9E4), Fog (#F4F2F0), Mist (#FCFAFA)

**Secondary Colors (use sparingly):** Hi-Vis (#E4F222), Grass (#BCE9B1), Forest (#2E4734), Sky (#B2C6EB), Indigo (#4F41D5), Polo (#C5A6CA)

**Color Proportion:** 60% neutrals, 30% Performance Orange/Dark Orange, 10% secondary colors and black

**Accessibility:** All combinations must meet WCAG 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text.

## Typography System

**Repro** (Primary) – Friendly, flexible sans serif. The backbone of visual communication.
- Bold Uppercase → IMPACTFUL TITLES
- Regular Sentence Case → Informative body copy

**GT Super Text** (Secondary) – Expressive serif with warmth and conversational tone.
- Book Sentence Case → Human, approachable moments

**Repro Mono** (Tertiary) – Technical counterpoint for data-driven precision.
- Regular Uppercase → METRICS, STATS, DETAILS

**System Fallbacks:** DM Sans (for Repro), Times New Roman (for GT Super Text), DM Mono (for Repro Mono)

## Logo Usage

**Symbol** (PG icon) – Tight spaces: social avatars, app icons, watermarks
**Logotype** (wordmark) – Broader applications: marketing materials, signage, packaging
**Combination Mark** (symbol + wordmark) – High-visibility: product launches, campaigns, partnerships

**Logo Rules:**
- Maintain clear space around the logo
- Ensure strong contrast with background
- NEVER: Distort, rotate at odd angles, apply unapproved colors, or crowd the mark

## Design Checklist

Before publishing any designed asset, verify:

✅ **Color:** Using approved palette? Proper 60/30/10 proportion? Accessible contrast?
✅ **Typography:** Correct typeface for context? Proper hierarchy? Fallbacks specified?
✅ **Logo:** Right variant for the use case? Adequate clear space? Strong contrast?
✅ **Tone:** Does it feel modern, technical, yet approachable? Premium but not elitist?
✅ **Alignment:** Does the visual support the brand promise—"Your path to playing better golf"?

## Final Test

Before publishing anything, ask:

**For copy:** Does this sound like Brixton? Does it help everyday golfers love their game?

**For design:** Does this look like Performance Golf? Modern, confident, approachable—not cold or elitist?

If yes to both, you're on brand.
