## **3\. The Enemy & The Weapon** {#3.-the-enemy-&-the-weapon}

### **The Enemy: THE SCATTERED PLAYBOOK** {#the-enemy:-the-scattered-playbook}

This is the pattern we're fighting against. Not instructors, not other companies—the SYSTEM that keeps golfers confused.

**What it is:**

* Random YouTube tips that contradict each other  
* Magazine drills with no context  
* Buddy's advice from his lesson last week  
* Collection of "fixes" that don't connect  
* Standing over the ball with 12 swing thoughts

**What it does:**

* Creates paralysis and confusion  
* No clear path forward  
* They collect information but can't apply it  
* Try everything, progress nowhere  
* More effort \= more confusion

**Physical Reality They FEEL:**

* Standing over the ball paralyzed with 12 different swing thoughts  
* That death grip on the club, tension creeping up your forearms  
* Shoulders climbing, chest tightening before you even start your backswing  
* Walking back to the cart frustrated after another chunked wedge  
* Making excuses to skip Saturday morning because they dread it

### **Our Weapon: ONE CONNECTED SYSTEM (PG1)** {#our-weapon:-one-connected-system-(pg1)}

**What it does:**

* Personalized path for THEIR game  
* Step-by-step clarity (right thing, right time, right order)  
* Adapts as they improve  
* Eliminates guesswork  
* Clarity replaces chaos

**The Transformation:**

* FROM: Scattered confusion, trying everything, paralysis over the ball  
* TO: Clear personalized path, knowing exactly what to work on next, confidence

### **The Linguistic Pattern** {#the-linguistic-pattern}

Use this structure when addressing The Scattered Playbook:

"You've been \[struggling with symptom\]. Not because you \[lack talent\]. But because The Scattered Playbook has been \[keeping you confused with conflicting advice\]. Once you see it, you can replace it. And \[PG1's personalized system\] is designed specifically to defeat The Scattered Playbook."

**Example:**

"You've been struggling with inconsistency. Not because you lack talent or aren't trying hard enough. But because The Scattered Playbook has been keeping you confused—random YouTube tips, magazine drills, buddy recommendations that all contradict each other. Once you see it, you can replace it. And PG1's One Connected System is designed specifically to defeat The Scattered Playbook."

Brixton loves golf, even if half the time, he swears he's hanging up his sticks the minute he gets home. His hunger to wring the most out of life manifests in his relentless pursuit of being as good as he can be, both on and off the course.

### **Voice Pillars** {#voice-pillars}

#### **01\. UNWAVERING** {#01.-unwavering}

To keep players positive and motivated, we channel the certainty that this new standard of golf instruction will help everyone play better and love their game. Our role is to instill confidence and eliminate self-doubt and frustration.

**We are:** Calm, Driven, and Methodical  
**We are not:** Stern, Emotionless, or Dismissive

**How to apply:**

* **Speak with confidence** \- Focus on empowering statements to reinforce a can-do mindset. "You have what it takes and we'll guide you through."  
* **Frame progress as attainable** \- Highlight what's achievable and how it will feel, rather than presenting flawless or fantastical outcomes.  
* **Meet challenges with encouragement** \- Recognize difficulties but stay optimistic: every practice and every game move them closer to where they want to be.

#### **02\. CONSTRUCTIVE** {#02.-constructive}

Our game improvement system is personalized, data-driven, and clearly mapped out. We put player progress front and center, using tech to support our recommendations and human coaching to ensure progress is permanent. Our role is to provide clarity and dispel confusion.

**We are:** Personalized, Pragmatic, and Clarifying  
**We are not:** Clinical, Distant, or Robotic

**How to apply:**

* **Speak human to human** \- Don't spit numbers, stats, and tech jargon at users. Instead, couple them with approachable, conversational language.  
* **Translate data into advice** \- Connect information to clear suggestions so data feels relevant and educational, rather than abstract or confusing.  
* **Get personal** \- Emphasize that every user's path to better golf is built around their root flaw, their goals, and their evolution to reinforce how our plan is uniquely tailored to their needs.

#### **03\. UPLIFTING** {#03.-uplifting}

Progress is the path, but not the only point. Mastering new skills creates more magical moments, and the exhilaration they elicit is reflected in our voice. Our role is to create joy and banish disappointment.

**We are:** Encouraging, Inspiring, and Aspirational  
**We are not:** Rah-rah, Idealized, or Insincere

**How to apply:**

* **Speak joy** \- Infuse copy with the feeling our customers crave to remind them of the high they'll get from a straight, powerful drive that hits the fairway IRL just as it did in their mind.  
* **Don't overhype it** \- Celebrate real achievements with authentic affirmations. Be wary of clichés. When talking about what's yet to come, avoid exaggerated or vague claims.  
* **Remind them it's about more than golf** \- Time with your buddies. Fresh air on a gorgeous day. A break from the stress of daily life. When you play better, you relax more, and that improves your mental health, your physical health, and everything else in the game of life.

### **Writing Principles** {#writing-principles}

**Tip 01: Think about what you're trying to say**  
 Before you write, define what you want to say. It'll keep you focused on the message. No one wants to work hard to grasp what you're saying.

**Tip 02: Put the reader first**  
 Write with your audience in mind. Who's going to read this copy, what do they need to know, why should they care?

**Tip 03: Structure matters**  
 Lead with the most important point, then build supporting details in a logical order. Say what matters, then stop. Clarity beats complexity.

**Tip 04: Embrace the crappy first draft**  
 There's an old saying that the real writing is in the re-writing. Trim, refine, and polish for maximum impact.

**Tip 05: Cut the fluff**  
 Remove unnecessary words, filler phrases, jargon, and corporate-speak. It can clutter writing and make it feel awkward, unclear, or unnatural.

**Tip 06: Vary sentence length**  
 Short sentences add punch. Longer ones create flow and depth. Used together, they keep the reader engaged.

**Tip 07: Be consistent**  
 Personality, style, and terminology should align with your brand voice and remain uniform across all communications.

**Tip 08: Use you and we**  
 Speak directly to the reader and make it conversational. You makes your writing feel personal. We builds trust.

**Tip 09: Use active voice**  
 Passive voice sounds stilted. Keep writing natural and engaging e.g. "we'll get back to you soon" instead of "your inquiry will be replied to."

**Tip 10: Read it out loud**  
 Always read your copy out loud first. If it sounds flat or robotic, tweak the rhythm until it feels natural and alive.

We use four distinct type styles to create different voices:

**Repro Bold Uppercase** — IMPACTFUL TITLES  
 When we need maximum impact and clarity.

 For clear, accessible information delivery.

**GT Super Text Book Sentence Case** — Conversational Tone  
 When we want to feel human and approachable.

**Repro Mono Regular Uppercase** — TECHNICAL DATA  
 For precision, metrics, and performance details.

#### **Core Type Specifications** {#core-type-specifications}

**Headline:** Repro Bold, 172pt / 87% leading / \-3% tracking  
**Subheadline:** Repro Medium, 43pt / 104% leading / \-3% tracking  
**Body Serif:** GT Super Text Book, 43pt / 140% leading / \-2% tracking  
**Body:** Repro Regular, 43pt / 122% leading / \-1% tracking  
**Detail:** Repro Mono Regular, 22pt / 135% leading / 0% tracking

#### **Scaling Grid System** {#scaling-grid-system}

All typography scales proportionally from a baseline (typically 15px). This ensures visual balance and consistent hierarchy across every application, from mobile screens to billboard installations.

#### **System Alternatives** {#system-alternatives}

When brand typefaces aren't available, use these substitutes:

* **Times New Roman Regular** (for GT Super Text)  
* **DM Sans Black / Medium** (for Repro)  
* **DM Sans Mono Regular** (for Repro Mono)

 Symbol and logotype together create our most branded expression.

**Symbol Only** — Tight spaces where full brand name isn't necessary (social avatars, app icons, watermarks)

**Logotype Only** — Broader applications where impact and recognition matter most (marketing materials, signage, packaging)

#### **Logo Guidelines** {#logo-guidelines}

**Clear Space:** Always maintain adequate clear space around the logo to preserve visibility and impact

**Contrast:** Ensure strong contrast when pairing with brand colors

**Never:** Distort, rotate at odd angles, apply unapproved colors, or crowd the mark

### **Brand Architecture** {#brand-architecture}

As we expand into new products and services (like PG-1), the symbol can integrate into the typographic language to create sub-brand identifiers. This maintains consistency while allowing each offering its own identity within the Performance Golf family.

## **6\. Audience Intelligence** {#6.-audience-intelligence}

### **Who We Serve** {#who-we-serve}

We don't define our audience by age, gender, or zip code. Whether they're 25 or 55, seasoned or just starting out, weekend range hitters or weekly 18-hole regulars, if they're an everyday golfer swinging a club, they belong here.

What unites them is the shared drive to have more fun playing golf—and you do that by playing better golf.

### **Audience Mindset** {#audience-mindset}

**Identity-Driven**  
 Golf is more than a hobby; it's a reflection of who they are. A good round means a good day. A bad one lingers.

**Overloaded by Information**  
 They're overwhelmed by all the contradictory advice, yet can't stop searching for the holy grail on YouTube. They need a system that simplifies and guides.

**Externally and Internally Motivated**  
 They want to impress their peers, but they also want to impress themselves. Every golfer has someone they want to show off for. And every golfer has a version of themselves they're chasing.

**Resistant to Change**  
 They want to get better, but they're looking for the easiest way to do it. It could be a new club or new tech, but it has to be simple.

**Hungry for Progress**  
 They crave improvement they can feel and measure. Not theory. Not fluff. They want results that show up on the course and in their self-confidence.

## **8\. Storytelling & Content Strategy** {#8.-storytelling-&-content-strategy}

### **How to Use Stories to Shift Beliefs** {#how-to-use-stories-to-shift-beliefs}

Stories bypass logical resistance and install beliefs at an emotional level. Use these story types strategically to shift each of the 10 core beliefs.

#### **Story Type 1: Universal Story (Parable)** {#story-type-1:-universal-story-(parable)}

A relatable anecdote that reinforces the belief through analogy.

**Best for:** Method belief, Core belief, Real Problem belief

**Example:**  
 "Two golfers join with the same handicap. One researches the perfect swing on YouTube for 3 months, collecting tips. The other just follows ONE clear system. 6 months later, only one has transformed. One Connected System beats scattered tips."

#### **Story Type 2: Personal Story (Hell to Heaven)** {#story-type-2:-personal-story-(hell-to-heaven)}

Your journey from struggle to success showing you understand their pain.

**Best for:** Core belief, Help belief

**Structure:**

* I was where you are  
* I tried what you've tried  
* I discovered \[key insight\]  
* Now I'm on the other side  
* I built this to help others cross

**Example:**  
 "18 months stuck at the same handicap. Tried every YouTube channel. Bought every training aid. Scores stayed flat. Then I realized: I wasn't missing information. I was drowning in The Scattered Playbook..."

#### **Story Type 3: Client Story (Case Study)** {#story-type-3:-client-story-(case-study)}

Someone like them who got the result. The more similar, the more powerful.

**Best for:** Core belief, Method belief, Time belief

**Structure:**

* They were where you are  
* Same doubts  
* Made the decision  
* Here's what happened  
* Here's where they are now

**Example:**  
 "Jake \- 18 handicap, busy executive. Tried lessons for years. His problem wasn't talent—it was The Scattered Playbook. 90 days with PG1: Clear path, 14 handicap, texting buddies for tee times instead of making excuses."

#### **Story Type 4: External Authority** {#story-type-4:-external-authority}

Research, data, or authority that validates the approach.

**Best for:** Method belief, Real Problem belief

**Examples:**

* "Research shows information overload decreases performance..."  
* "When elite coaches train athletes, they don't give scattered tips..."  
* "Studies on skill acquisition show personalized systems beat generic advice..."

#### **Story Type 5: Internal Data** {#story-type-5:-internal-data}

Your results across multiple clients showing patterns.

**Best for:** Time belief, Money belief, Method belief

**Example:**  
 "Across 47 golfers, those who followed ONE clear system saw 2.8x faster improvement than those collecting random tips. Average time to transformation: 12 weeks."

#### **Story Type 6: Other Area (Transfer)** {#story-type-6:-other-area-(transfer)}

Show them they already believe this in another domain.

**Best for:** Method belief, Real Problem belief

**Examples:**

* "You didn't learn to drive by collecting random Formula 1 tips. Why would you improve your golf game that way?"  
* "You already know this from cooking—you can't follow 12 different recipes at once. Same with golf—you need ONE clear system."

### **3-Layer Story Structure** {#3-layer-story-structure}

When crafting transformation stories, use this structure:

**Layer 1: Surface**

* Story feels like someone else's problem (strategic distance)  
* Sensory details lock attention  
* Reader thinks "just a story" — defenses stay down

**Layer 2: Mirror**

* Pattern-matches the reader's actual struggle  
* Different form, identical function  
* Recognition is FELT, never stated  
* Reader cares without knowing why

**Layer 3: Transformation**

* Character's revelation becomes reader's revelation  
* Show life after the belief change  
* LINGER HERE — this is where installation happens

**Example Structure:**

*Layer 1 \- Surface:* Story about a restaurant owner struggling with scattered advice  
 "Marcus had tried every system. Fifteen different consultants..."

*Layer 2 \- Mirror:* Pattern matches golfer's experience  
 "He'd collect strategies but couldn't connect them. Each new expert contradicted the last."

*Layer 3 \- Transformation:* The belief installation  
 "Marcus realized: The problem wasn't that I needed more advice. The problem was the scattered advice itself."

## **Final Reminders** {#final-reminders}

This is a living document. When in doubt, ask yourself:

**Does this help everyday golfers love their game?**

If the answer is yes, you're on brand.

### **Balance is Everything** {#balance-is-everything}

* Emotion \+ Data \= Compelling  
* Personal \+ Universal \= Relatable  
* Confident \+ Humble \= Trustworthy  
* Technical \+ Human \= Accessible

### **The Brand Promise** {#the-brand-promise}

We give everyday golfers a clear path to playing better golf. Every word, every color, every design choice should ladder back to this promise.

### **The Ultimate Goal** {#the-ultimate-goal}

Not just lower scores. Not just better ball-striking.

**Help them LOVE THEIR GAME again.**

When progress feels possible and personal, they don't just play better.  
 They start to love their game in a whole new way.
