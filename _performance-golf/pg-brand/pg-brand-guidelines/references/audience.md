# Audience Intelligence

## Who We Serve

We don't define our audience by age, gender, or zip code. Whether they're 25 or 55, seasoned or just starting out, weekend range hitters or weekly 18-hole regulars—if they're an everyday golfer swinging a club, they belong here.

What unites them: the shared drive to have more fun playing golf. And you do that by playing better golf.

---

## Audience Mindset

**Identity-Driven**
Golf is more than a hobby; it's a reflection of who they are. A good round means a good day. A bad one lingers.

**Overloaded by Information**
Overwhelmed by contradictory advice, yet can't stop searching for the holy grail on YouTube. They need a system that simplifies and guides.

**Externally and Internally Motivated**
They want to impress their peers AND themselves. Every golfer has someone they want to show off for. Every golfer has a version of themselves they're chasing.

**Resistant to Change**
They want to get better, but they're looking for the easiest way. It could be a new club or new tech, but it has to be simple.

**Hungry for Progress**
They crave improvement they can feel and measure. Not theory. Not fluff. Results that show up on the course and in their self-confidence.

**Cautiously Optimistic**
They believe improvement is possible, but past experiences make them skeptical. They've tried YouTube, gadgets, lessons. Some helped. Most didn't. But they haven't given up.

**Progress that Can be Felt**
They love the feeling of progress: hitting one flush shot, sinking a long putt, unlocking a new level. Those magic moments keep them coming back.

**Time-Constrained but Committed**
Busy careers, families, and relationships. But golf is their thing, and they'll make time for what works. They just want to be sure their time pays off.

## Persona: Ari (The Everyday Golfer)

To Ari, golf is more than a game. It's how they unwind, spend time with friends, challenge themselves, and prove something personal. They think about golf often, counting down to the next range session or tee time.

They're smart, successful, and confident in most things. But golf humbles them. And they respect that. They obsess over how to get better. They want to know how to overcome setbacks.

They've tried the tips. Bought the clubs. Watched the videos. Even dabbled with tech like a launch monitor. Some of it helped. Most of it didn't. What they want now is a path that's clear, trustworthy, and built for them.

Ari isn't chasing perfection. They're chasing progress. That buttery iron off the fairway. Another pure shot right down the middle. A round where everything feels right. They don't just want to play golf. They want joy, exhilaration, and camaraderie. They want more wow moments. They want to feel like they're better every time they hit the course.

### Ari's Goals
- Improve their game consistently
- Get personalized guidance
- Feel confident on the course
- Have more magical moments
- Make golf more enjoyable

### Ari's Frustrations
- Conflicting advice everywhere
- Wasting time on what doesn't work
- Inconsistent performance
- Lack of clear improvement path
- Expensive lessons that don't stick

---

## Writing for Ari

When writing copy, imagine you're talking to Ari:

**DO:**
- Acknowledge their frustration without condescension
- Speak to their intelligence—they're successful in other areas
- Validate that they've tried hard already
- Offer a clear, simple path forward
- Focus on the feeling of progress, not just scores
- Remember they have limited time

**DON'T:**
- Talk down to them
- Assume they don't know anything about golf
- Promise unrealistic outcomes
- Use excessive tech jargon
- Focus only on low handicap players
- Ignore the emotional side of the game
