## 1. Brand Strategy Summary

As a reminder of why the tactics and strategies in this document matter and where they fit our model, this section summarizes what we mean when we say **brand building**.

### Current Model

Today we drive all of our traffic, revenue, and interest as a company by pushing out media spend. We are actively going out and spending money to acquire customers. That is the work Performance Golf has been built on leading up to where we are today.

### The Shift: Brand Gravity

To reach the next level of business growth and brand, we need to deploy new tactics **in addition to** the performance marketing we do today. The strategies in this roadmap create **brand gravity**—they pull people into our brand organically and through different media tactics than paid media alone. This approach has worked for many other brands that are much larger than ours.

### Why It Matters

- **Diversifies our marketing mix** and reduces dependence on paid media only.
- **Brings in new demos and audiences** that currently don’t buy through our paid acquisition strategies.
- **Makes current ad spend more efficient** by creating a brand halo.
- **Measured differently:** Paid acquisition shows return (or not) on spend immediately. Brand strategies compound over time and compound together. We will look up in 3, 6, 9, and 12 months and see better ad efficiency and more organic traffic—and it will be because of these initiatives.

### The Goal

Be in all the right places at all the right times—surrounding the customer with authentic touchpoints within the brand. When they decide to check out Performance Golf (organic search, direct nav, or our social platforms), they are met with a brand experience that matches their expectations in the game-improvement space and the brands they connect to. These initiatives build community, brand, and lifetime value through consistency of look, feel, message, and positioning—and through trusted sources and platforms, not just the ads we push out.

*Alignment: This work supports our mission (“Love Your Game”) and positions Performance Golf as the answer to the Scattered Playbook—One Connected System (PG1).*

## 2. Process

These are the steps we follow to create and maintain the roadmap for Brand, give visibility to the CEO, and enter work into ClickUp.

### High-Level Steps

1. **Finalize Strategies**
2. **Layout Steps**
3. **Create boards for each section**
4. **Enter into ClickUp under each section**
5. **Create crossover view that combines all into one brand dashboard**

### More Detail on Each Step

**1. Finalize Strategies**  
We go through each brand area (Partnerships, Influencers, Brand Collaborations, Events, PR/Podcasts, Organic Content, Social Media Calendar, Website Rebrand, Email Branding, GTM Campaigns) and lock strategy and scope. This document is the place where we capture and refine those strategies.

**2. Layout Steps**  
Within each area we document the strategy summary and break down the concrete steps and tactics—what gets done and in what order. That creates the task-level detail we need for execution and for ClickUp.

**3. Create Boards**  
In ClickUp we create one board per brand area (e.g., Partnerships, Influencers, Brand Collabs, Events, etc.). Each board will hold the tasks for that area.

**4. Enter into ClickUp**  
We add all steps and tactics as tasks under the correct boards, assign owners, and add target dates where known (using placeholders where dates are TBD). Everything is tagged so it can roll up into the CEO view.

**5. Crossover CEO View**  
We create a single ClickUp view (e.g., tagged “Brand” or “Brand Halo”) that pulls all brand-area work into one place. The CEO and team can sort by date, section, owner, completion, etc., for full visibility without moving between boards.

**Strategy summary:** We use other platforms, brands, and people that have audiences we don’t yet reach—or that, when we show up on their channels, transfer brand equity to us. Partnerships are authentic connections with brands or people that align to our mission and add value: their audience and following, their ability to create content (extending our content team), or another product/service that amplifies the game-improvement journey and our mission to help people love their game. These are not simply affiliate relationships; they go deeper and amplify our reach and position in the market.

#### Influencer Program

**Strategy:** Performance Golf has traditionally used influencers like a casting agency—matching demographics for paid creative. The strategy moving forward is to build a **modern influencer program**: macro and micro influencers who align with our brand and are incentivized through base retainers and/or affiliate commissions. We empower them to create authentic content for their audiences (not scripts we write and edit). The goal is an army of influencers who love Performance Golf, align with “Love Your Game,” and create content on their own channels that drives back to our platforms. We also establish a process to identify trending organic content from influencers and pull it into paid advertising.

#### Brand Collaborations

**Strategy:** Because of the size of the Performance Golf platform, reach, and revenue, many brands in adjacent categories are open to brand collaborations. We use these to share audiences and amplify both brands. We are pursuing partners in **apparel, hats, club covers, bags, and shoes** to start. Target: **one large brand collaboration per quarter**, mapped to the GTM calendar—whether large-scale partnerships or limited-time drops around key moments. A dedicated owner (e.g. partnerships manager) manages the relationship and activation components.

**Branded swag layer:** Where possible we use these collaborations to create branded swag for our team, influencers, coaches, employees, and VIP customers—saving on cost, gaining brand clout from the partner, and enabling collaboration posts.

- **ClickUp Board** = one per Brand Area (e.g. Partnerships, Events, PR/Podcasts, etc.). Sub-areas such as Celebrities, Influencer Program, and Brand Collaborations can live as lists or folders under the Partnerships board.
- **ClickUp Task** = each tactic or step row in the tables above.
- **Custom fields:** Owner, Target date. Tag every task with **Brand** or **Brand Halo** so it appears in the crossover CEO view.
- The crossover view combines all brand-area work into one place, sortable by date, section, owner, and completion for CEO and team visibility.
