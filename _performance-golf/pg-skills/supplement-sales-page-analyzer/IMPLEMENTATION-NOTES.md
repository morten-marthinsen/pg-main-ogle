## Research Methodology

### Phase 1: Company & URL Discovery

**Objective:** Identify actual sales pages (not homepages) for target supplement companies.

**Process:**

**Step 1: Initial Company Research**
- Visit company website
- Navigate to product pages (not homepage)
- Identify primary sales pages for hero products
- Look for dedicated landing pages separate from main site
- Check for campaign-specific URLs

**Step 2: URL Documentation**
- Record exact URLs (copy/paste, don't type)
- Verify each URL is accessible
- Note any redirects or variants
- Document page type (product page, landing page, VSL page)
- Flag any pages requiring email signup or gating

**Step 3: Page Type Classification**

**Primary Sales Page:** Main product purchase page on company site
**Landing Page:** Standalone conversion page (often separate domain)
**VSL Page:** Video sales letter focused page
**Checkout Page:** Final purchase page (usually not analyzed unless unique)

**Prioritize: Primary Sales Pages and Landing Pages**

### Phase 2: Systematic Page Analysis

**Objective:** Extract all conversion-relevant elements from each page.

**Analysis Checklist (Use for Each Page):**

#### 1. Headline & Hook Analysis

**Elements to Extract:**
- Primary headline (exact copy)
- Subheadline (exact copy)
- Pre-headline if present
- Headline structure (question, claim, benefit, curiosity)
- Emotional hook used (fear, hope, curiosity, pride, etc.)
- Unique mechanism mentioned in headline
- Word count and reading level

**Documentation Format:**
```
Primary Headline: [EXACT COPY]
Subheadline: [EXACT COPY]
Structure: [Question/Claim/Benefit/etc.]
Hook Type: [Fear/Hope/Curiosity/etc.]
Unique Element: [What makes it stand out]
```

#### 2. Problem/Solution Framework

**Elements to Extract:**
- How problem is introduced (agitation level)
- Specific pain points mentioned
- Problem amplification techniques
- Villain identification (what caused the problem)
- Solution positioning (how product solves it)
- Mechanism explanation (why it works)
- Differentiation from alternatives

**Analysis Questions:**
- How much time spent on problem vs. solution?
- What emotions are triggered by problem description?
- Is there a "villain" (big pharma, aging, toxins, etc.)?
- How is the mechanism made unique?

#### 3. Social Proof Strategy

**Types to Document:**
- Customer testimonials (video vs. text vs. image)
- Number of customers served
- Star ratings and review counts
- Expert endorsements
- Media mentions
- Celebrity/influencer associations
- Clinical study references
- Before/after transformations

**For Each Proof Element:**
- Type of proof
- Placement on page
- Specific/generic ("Sarah from Texas" vs. "verified customer")
- Emotional vs. factual
- Visual proof (photos, videos, screenshots)

**Documentation Format:**
```
Social Proof Type: Customer Testimonials
Count: 12 testimonials (3 video, 9 text+photo)
Placement: Throughout page, major cluster at 60% scroll
Specificity: All named with location and photo
Emotional/Factual Balance: 70% emotional benefits, 30% specific results
Standout Element: Video testimonial from functional medicine doctor
```

#### 4. Scarcity & Urgency Tactics

**Elements to Identify:**
- Time-based scarcity (countdown timers, deadlines)
- Inventory scarcity (limited stock claims)
- Access scarcity (exclusive offers, membership)
- Seasonal scarcity (limited run, special batch)
- Price scarcity (sale ending, price increase coming)

**Assessment Criteria:**
- Real vs. artificial scarcity
- Urgency placement (above fold, near CTA, multiple times)
- Language used ("only X left", "sale ends", "limited time")
- Visual urgency elements (timers, red text, etc.)

#### 5. Scientific Backing & Credibility

**Elements to Extract:**
- Clinical studies mentioned (specific or generic)
- Ingredient research citations
- Scientific mechanism explanations
- Doctor endorsements or formulation
- Lab testing and quality certifications
- Comparison to prescription alternatives
- Graphs, charts, data visualization

**Credibility Signals:**
- Third-party testing (NSF, USP, etc.)
- Manufacturing certifications (GMP, etc.)
- Media coverage from credible sources
- University affiliations
- Published research references

#### 6. Pricing Psychology

**Elements to Document:**
- Price presentation format ($47 vs. $1.56/day)
- Anchor pricing strategy (compare to expensive alternative)
- Bundle pricing and discounting
- Subscription vs. one-time pricing
- Free trial or sampling offers
- Money-back guarantee terms
- Payment plan options

**Psychological Tactics:**
- Strikethrough pricing (was $97, now $47)
- Bulk discount incentives
- "Most popular" package highlighting
- Total value calculation ("$300 value for $47")
- Free bonuses with purchase
- Risk reversal language

#### 7. Call-to-Action Analysis

**For Each CTA on Page:**
- Exact button text
- Button color and contrast
- Size and prominence
- Placement on page (above fold, 25%, 50%, 75%, bottom)
- CTA type (add to cart, claim offer, start trial, etc.)
- Surrounding text/context
- Visual attention draws (arrows, highlighting)

**CTA Pattern Analysis:**
- How many CTAs total?
- Primary CTA text pattern
- Progressive CTA changes throughout page
- Urgency language in CTA
- Action vs. benefit focused

#### 8. Visual & UX Design

**Page Structure:**
- Above-the-fold content
- Overall page length (scroll depth estimate)
- Section count and flow
- White space usage
- Color psychology
- Image vs. text ratio
- Video integration

**Visual Proof Elements:**
- Before/after images
- Product demonstration videos
- Ingredient close-ups
- Manufacturing/sourcing imagery
- Lifestyle imagery
- Infographics and data viz

**Mobile Optimization:**
- Mobile-first design elements
- Touch-friendly CTAs
- Vertical scroll optimization
- Mobile-specific content

#### 9. Trust Signals

**Elements to Document:**
- Security badges (SSL, Norton, McAfee, etc.)
- Money-back guarantee prominence and terms
- Certification badges (organic, non-GMO, etc.)
- "As seen on" media logos
- BBB rating or similar
- Customer service information
- Transparent ingredient sourcing

**Trust Building Techniques:**
- Company story and mission
- Founder visibility and credibility
- Transparency about sourcing/manufacturing
- FAQ addressing concerns
- Contact information accessibility

#### 10. Copywriting & Messaging

**Voice & Tone:**
- Reading level (conversational vs. scientific)
- Personal vs. corporate voice
- Urgency level (aggressive vs. educational)
- Emotional vs. logical appeal balance

**Language Patterns:**
- "You" vs. "I/we" focus
- Question usage frequency
- Storytelling elements
- Jargon vs. plain language
- Power words and trigger words
- Sentence and paragraph length

**Target Audience Signals:**
- Demographic indicators (age, gender, lifestyle)
- Psychographic indicators (values, concerns, aspirations)
- Problem awareness level assumed
- Solution sophistication assumed

**Pattern Categories to Track:**

**Track:**

- "Mushroom Coffee That Boosts Energy Without The Jitters"

- "Colostrum That Actually Survives Your Stomach Acid"

**Track Most Effective Types:**
1. Video testimonials with specificity
2. Before/after transformations

5. Media mentions
6. Star ratings

**Pattern Analysis:**

- What's the typical count of each type?
- Where are they placed for maximum impact?

### Phase 4: Scoring Methodology

**Objective:** Quantify page effectiveness for ranking.

**Scoring Framework (1-10 for Each Category):**

**Score 9-10:**
- Above fold immediately compelling
- Clear value proposition within 3 seconds
- Logical flow with momentum building
- Multiple strategic CTAs throughout
- Zero friction points identified

**Score 7-8:**
- Strong opening but could be tighter
- Value prop clear but takes 5+ seconds
- Good flow with minor pacing issues
- CTAs present but could be optimized
- Few minor friction points

**Score 5-6:**
- Decent opening but lacks punch
- Value prop present but not immediately clear
- Adequate flow but missing momentum
- CTAs adequate but not strategic
- Some friction points present

**Score 3-4:**
- Weak opening, slow to engage
- Value prop unclear or buried
- Poor flow, feels disjointed
- CTAs weak or poorly placed
- Multiple friction points

**Score 1-2:**
- Fails to engage quickly
- Value prop missing or confusing
- No clear flow or structure
- CTAs ineffective or missing
- Significant friction throughout

## Quality Standards

### Verification Checklist

Before delivering analysis:

**URL Verification:**
- [ ] All URLs tested and accessible
- [ ] No broken links included
- [ ] Redirects documented
- [ ] Inaccessible pages flagged

**Accuracy Standards:**
- [ ] All quotes are exact (copy/paste)
- [ ] No paraphrasing of key elements
- [ ] All claims verifiable by visiting URLs
- [ ] Screenshots or archives for any questionable elements

**Completeness Standards:**
- [ ] Minimum 10 pages analyzed
- [ ] All scoring categories completed
- [ ] Pattern analysis comprehensive
- [ ] Recommendations actionable

**Insight Quality:**
- [ ] Non-obvious insights included
- [ ] Competitive intelligence valuable
- [ ] Immediately applicable
- [ ] CEO-level quality

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1:** Analyzing homepages instead of sales pages
- **Why it's wrong:** Homepages rarely optimized for direct conversion
- **Fix:** Navigate to actual product sales pages

**Mistake 2:** Paraphrasing instead of exact quotes
- **Why it's wrong:** Loses nuance of effective copy
- **Fix:** Copy/paste all headlines, CTAs, key phrases exactly

**Mistake 3:** Generic observations without specifics
- **Why it's wrong:** Not actionable or valuable
- **Fix:** Always tie observations to specific examples with URLs

**Mistake 4:** Including outdated or inactive pages
- **Why it's wrong:** Wastes time and reduces credibility
- **Fix:** Verify all URLs before analyzing

**Mistake 5:** Missing mobile analysis
- **Why it's wrong:** Most traffic is mobile
- **Fix:** Test every page on mobile device

**Mistake 6:** Ignoring why elements work
- **Why it's wrong:** Can't replicate without understanding mechanism
- **Fix:** Always explain the psychological/strategic reasoning

**Mistake 7:** Not cross-referencing patterns
- **Why it's wrong:** Misses the most valuable insights
- **Fix:** Track which elements appear across multiple top performers

## Success Metrics

**A successful analysis delivers:**

1. **Immediate Actionability:** Can start implementing insights today
2. **Specific Examples:** Every point backed by exact copy from real pages
3. **Non-Obvious Intelligence:** Reveals patterns not visible before research
4. **Competitive Advantage:** Provides edge over competitors
5. **Replicable Mechanisms:** Clear path to adaptation for new pages

**Quality Verification Question:**

*"If I give this analysis to a copywriter right now, can they create a high-converting supplement sales page without additional research?"*

If answer is no, analysis is incomplete.
