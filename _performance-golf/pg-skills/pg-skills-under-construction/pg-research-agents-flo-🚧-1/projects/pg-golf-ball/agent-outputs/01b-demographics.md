## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The golf ball market is experiencing a demographic transformation. While the traditional profile skews older (average age 43.5) and male (72%), the fastest-growing segment is 18-34 year-olds, who are now the LARGEST group of on-course players at 6.3 million. This generational shift has implications for golf ball marketing: younger players are more value-conscious, digitally-native, and motivated by wellness/mental health rather than business networking.

## KEY INSIGHTS FOR PRODUCT STRATEGY

### Insight 1: The Youth Market is Real and Growing
The 18-34 segment is now the LARGEST. They're not brand-loyal yet. First-mover advantage exists for brands that speak their language (value, performance, no BS).

### Insight 2: The "Good Enough" Anxiety
85%+ of golfers have 10+ handicaps. They can't feel the difference in balls, but they WANT to believe premium matters. This creates an opportunity for a ball positioned as "the smart choice for improving golfers."

### Insight 3: Female Golfers Are the Growth Story
60% of net new golfers since 2019 are female. This segment is underserved in golf ball marketing (everything is still "tour performance" male imagery).

### Insight 4: Mental Health > Status
Gen Z plays golf for mental health and self-care, NOT business networking or status. Ball marketing focused on "tour trust" may not resonate with this growing segment.

### Insight 5: Price Threshold Matters
The gap between $25/dozen (Kirkland) and $55/dozen (Pro V1) is massive. There's room for a "credible premium" at $35-40 that delivers perceived value without the Pro V1 price shock.
