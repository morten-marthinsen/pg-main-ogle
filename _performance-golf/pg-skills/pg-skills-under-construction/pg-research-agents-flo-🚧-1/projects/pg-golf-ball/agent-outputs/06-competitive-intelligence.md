## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The golf ball market is dominated by Titleist with a stranglehold on the premium segment (~50% market share), but the DTC disruption wave has created viable pathways for challengers. The competitive landscape reveals a critical opportunity: every major brand positions on either "tour performance" OR "value disruption" — nobody owns the "improvement journey" territory. Titleist owns tour credibility. Kirkland owns value perception. Vice/Snell own DTC sophistication. Performance Golf can own the third axis: **the trusted recommendation for golfers working on their game**.

### 2025 Pro V1 Positioning (Just Launched)

From Titleist's January 2025 announcement:

> "The golf ball has to do everything. It's never just about maximizing one area – golfers need performance on every single shot. Each of our golf ball models is optimized for distance off the tee, and with 2025 Pro V1 and Pro V1x in particular, we've realized a speed gain while unlocking even better iron and wedge performance. **Key Messaging Themes:**
1. **25 Years of Innovation** — heritage/credibility play
2. **Complete Performance** — not just one attribute
3. **Tour Validation** — 102 of top 150 tour players
4. **Continuous Improvement** — "always trying to improve"

### Titleist Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability | Evidence | Opportunity |
|---------------|----------|-------------|
| **Price Perception** | $58/dozen creates barrier | "Premium performance, honest price" |
| **Tour-Centric Messaging** | Alienates recreational golfers | "Made for improvers, not tour pros" |
| **Status Anxiety** | "Am I good enough?" feeling | "This ball doesn't check your handicap" |
| **Durability Issues** | "New ProV1 scuffs too easily" (forum) | "Built to last your round" |
| **Corporate Distance** | No relationship with customers | "From the people who know your game" |

### Why Titleist Dominates

1. **Tour Validation Loop:** Tour players use → amateurs aspire → market leadership
2. **Consistency Reputation:** "Every ball is identical" quality control
3. **Heritage Story:** 25 years of Pro V1, 92 years of Titleist
4. **Category Creation:** Invented the solid-core premium ball category
5. **R&D Investment:** Continuous improvement cycle

|---------|-------|----------|

**Key Findings from MyGolfSpy Testing (2025):**
- New Version 3.5 shows improvement over previous versions
- Compression variability remains an issue

- Cover durability inferior to Pro V1

**The Kirkland Phenomenon:**
- Original 2016 K-Sig achieved cult status
- "Pro V1 alternative for half the price" narrative

**Kirkland Strengths:**

- "Good enough" for recreational golfers

**Kirkland Weaknesses:**
- Quality control inconsistency

- No golf expertise behind the product

## SECTION 11: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE SUMMARY

### Key Findings

1. **Titleist Owns Tour, But Not Improvement**
   - Their messaging explicitly targets tour players and aspirants
   - Recreational/improvement golfers feel excluded

2. **DTC Brands Compete on Value, Not Differentiation**
   - Vice, Snell, Cut, OnCore all compete in same territory
   - No clear positioning separation beyond price/credentials

3. **Kirkland Created Value Benchmark**
   - $15/dozen established as "value" price point
   - But quality inconsistency undermines trust

4. **Nobody Owns the Improvement Journey**
   - "For golfers getting better" is unclaimed
   - "Coach's recommendation" is unowned
   - "End the search" is available

5. **The Relationship Gap is Real**
   - NGF research shows golfers feel like "just another customer"
   - PG already has relationship advantage

### Strategic Implications

| Finding | Implication for PG |
|---------|-------------------|
| Tour territory locked | Don't try to compete on tour credibility |
| Value territory crowded | Don't compete on price alone |
| Improvement unclaimed | OWN this positioning |
| Relationship missing | LEVERAGE existing customer relationship |
| Coach authority unused | POSITION as "the ball your coach recommends" |
