## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The global golf ball market represents a $1.4-1.5 billion opportunity with strong growth fundamentals. The U.S. market alone is ~$540 million, dominated by Titleist (Acushnet) with a stranglehold on the premium segment. However, the DTC disruption wave (Vice, Snell, Kirkland) has proven that brand loyalty CAN be cracked with the right value proposition. For Performance Golf, the realistic opportunity lies in the "credible premium" segment — a $50-100M SAM of golfers who want tour-level performance without Pro V1 pricing.

## SECTION 10: KEY FINDINGS & STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

### Finding 1: The Market is Larger Than It Appears
The $540M U.S. market masks the true opportunity: 400-500 million balls lost annually creates constant repurchase demand. Golf balls are a consumable, not a durable good.

### Finding 2: Titleist Dominance is Real but Not Impenetrable
Titleist owns the premium segment, but DTC brands have proven the formula can be cracked. Kirkland's success showed that golfers WILL switch for value.

### Finding 3: The $35-45 Price Gap is Underserved
There's a clear gap between "cheap" ($25) and "premium" ($55). This "credible premium" zone is where Performance Golf can compete.

### Finding 4: Brand Extension is Viable
Vice, Snell, and others built brands from scratch. Performance Golf has an existing audience of improvement-focused golfers — a significant advantage.

### Finding 5: The Improvement Narrative is Untapped
Every ball is positioned on "tour performance" or "value." Nobody owns the "ball for getting better" positioning.
