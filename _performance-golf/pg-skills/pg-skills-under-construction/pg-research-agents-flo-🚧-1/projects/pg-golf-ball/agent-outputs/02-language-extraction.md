## KEY LINGUISTIC PATTERNS FOR COPYWRITING

### Pattern 1: The Self-Worth Qualifier
Golfers consistently state their handicap or skill level BEFORE asking about balls, as if to establish whether they "deserve" to ask the question.

**Copywriting Implication:** Address the "am I good enough" anxiety directly. Don't make golfers prove their worthiness.

### Pattern 2: The Rationalization Math
Golfers do mental math to justify premium ball purchases: "cost per month," "cost vs. round price," "balls lost vs. balls retired."

**Copywriting Implication:** Provide the rationalization FOR them. Make the math easy.

### Pattern 3: The Brand Skepticism
Deep skepticism toward Titleist claims ("Titleist would say that"), but simultaneous respect for the Pro V1 as the standard.

**Copywriting Implication:** Position against Titleist claims while respecting Pro V1 performance. "You don't need to pay Titleist prices for Titleist performance."

### Pattern 4: The Consistency Argument
Widespread agreement that playing ONE ball consistently is more important than playing the "best" ball inconsistently.

**Copywriting Implication:** Build commitment/loyalty messaging. "The ball you'll actually play every round."

### Pattern 5: The DTC Curiosity
Strong interest in DTC alternatives (Vice, Snell, Kirkland) with active comparison shopping behavior.

**Copywriting Implication:** DTC buyers are research-oriented. Provide data, comparisons, proof.
