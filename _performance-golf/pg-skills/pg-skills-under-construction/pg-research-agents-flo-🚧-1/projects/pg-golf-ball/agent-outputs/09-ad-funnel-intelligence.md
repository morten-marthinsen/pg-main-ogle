## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Golf ball advertising is dominated by tour validation (Titleist), innovation claims (Callaway/TaylorMade), and value disruption (DTC brands). Analysis of competitor strategies reveals a critical gap: no brand leads with improvement-focused messaging or coach credibility. Performance Golf's funnel advantage lies in its existing customer relationship and content integration capabilities. Recommended approach: Lead with content-first advertising (improvement tips featuring the ball), leverage email to warm audience, and use subscription as the primary conversion model for maximum LTV.

## PART I: COMPETITOR ADVERTISING ANALYSIS

### 1.1 Titleist Advertising Strategy

**2025 Campaign: "Excellence in Process"**

**Core Messaging:**
> "Where there's excellence in process, there's excellence in performance."
> "More speed off the tee, more control with irons, more spin with wedges."

**Creative Themes:**
1. **25 Years of Innovation** — Heritage/credibility play
2. **Tour Player Stories** — "Excellence in Process" featuring Ludvig Åberg, Justin Thomas
3. **Technical Performance** — "Spin Slope" technology explanation
4. **Process Journey** — Behind-the-scenes R&D content

**Channel Mix:**
- YouTube (primary video content)
- Tour validation (PGA Tour presence, player bags)
- Premium print (Golf Digest, Golf Magazine)
- Retail merchandising (premium displays)
- Pro shop presence

**Ad Formats:**
- 30-second brand spots
- 5+ minute documentary content
- Player testimonial videos
- Technical explainer content

**Key Insight:** Titleist spends on BRAND, not direct response. Their ads build the "aspirational" narrative that makes golfers feel they should play Pro V1. This creates the worthiness anxiety PG can address.

### 1.2 Vice Golf Advertising Strategy

**Positioning:** "Premium quality, DTC savings"

**Historical Approach:**
> "They would literally cut out the middleman — like, with a machete." — MyGolfSpy describing early Vice ads

**Current Strategy (2025 Evolution):**
- Moving from pure DTC to retail distribution
- Facebook/Instagram as primary paid channels
- Email marketing focus (analyzed by DTC Newsletter)
- Bold visual branding (distinctive packaging, colors)

**Key Messaging:**
- "Top Performance, Half the Price"
- "German Engineering"
- "Born on Tour"

**DTC Email Strategy Analysis (from DTC Newsletter):**
- Welcome series optimization needed
- Abandoned cart flows
- Subscription push messaging
- Sleek product design photography

**Channel Mix:**
- Facebook/Instagram (primary paid)
- Email marketing (retention/conversion)
- YouTube (product reviews, comparisons)
- Influencer partnerships
- Retail expansion (PGA Tour Superstore)

**Key Insight:** Vice built the DTC golf ball playbook but is now pivoting to retail, which dilutes their "cut out the middleman" story. PG can claim the DTC + relationship territory Vice is abandoning.

**Positioning:** "Designed by the Pro V1 creator"

**Core Message:**

### 1.5 Kirkland (Costco) "Advertising"

**Strategy:** Essentially no paid advertising

**How They Generate Awareness:**

### 3.1 DTC Marketing Funnel Structure

**Standard DTC Funnel:**
```
AWARENESS → CONSIDERATION → CONVERSION → RETENTION → ADVOCACY
```

**Golf Ball-Specific Modifications:**

| Stage | Standard DTC | Golf Ball Adaptation |
|-------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Awareness** | Paid social ads | Content marketing + paid |
| **Consideration** | Landing page | Comparison content + quiz |
| **Conversion** | Product page | Subscription offer |
| **Retention** | Email flows | Instruction integration |
| **Advocacy** | Referral program | Playing partner sharing |

### 3.2 Landing Page Best Practices

**From DTC Landing Page Analysis:**

**Key Elements:**
1. **Single Focus** — Sell one product/bundle, not entire catalog
2. **Problem-Solution** — Lead with the problem, position ball as solution
3. **Social Proof** — Reviews, testimonials, usage data
4. **Value Proposition** — Clear positioning statement
5. **Risk Reversal** — Guarantee or trial offer
6. **Urgency/Scarcity** — Limited offers, subscription incentive

**PG-Specific Landing Page Elements:**

| Element | Standard | PG Advantage |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **Headline** | Product benefit | "The ball your coach recommends" |
| **Social Proof** | Customer reviews | Instructor testimonials + customer reviews |
| **Authority** | Certifications | PG teaching authority |
| **Risk Reversal** | Money-back guarantee | "3-round trial" |
| **Conversion** | Single purchase | Subscription primary, one-time secondary |

## PART V: CREATIVE RECOMMENDATIONS

### 5.1 Ad Creative Directions

**Direction 1: "The Improvement Tip" Format**

**Structure:**
1. Open with instruction tip (3-5 seconds)
2. Demonstrate with PG Ball visible
3. Mention ball as supporting element
4. CTA: "Get the ball your coach plays"

**Example Script:**
> "Here's the one change that'll help you hold more greens. [Demonstrates chip shot with PG Ball visible] This tip works even better when you're playing a ball designed for improvers. The PG Ball — from the instructors who've helped millions of golfers get better."

---

**Direction 2: "Search is Over" Format**

**Structure:**
1. Open with overwhelm (1,308 balls on USGA list)
2. Show confusion/frustration
3. Present PG Ball as the answer
4. CTA: "Stop searching. Start playing."

**Example Script:**
> "1,308 golf balls on the USGA conforming list. You've tried a dozen. Read fifty reviews. Still not sure. Here's your answer: The PG Ball. The ball your coaches at Performance Golf play. Your search is over."

---

**Direction 3: "You Qualify" Format**

**Structure:**
1. Address worthiness anxiety directly
2. Reframe qualification criteria
3. Include diverse golfers
4. CTA: "Made for golfers who are getting better"

**Example Script:**
> "You don't need to shoot 70 to deserve a premium ball. You just need to be working on your game. Taking lessons. Hitting the range. Trying to get better. The PG Ball — made for golfers who are committed to improvement, not just their handicap."

---

**Direction 4: "Coach's Recommendation" Format**

**Structure:**
1. PG instructor speaks directly to camera
2. Personal recommendation framing
3. Shows ball, briefly explains why
4. CTA: "The ball I play. Now it's yours."

**Example Script:**
> [PG Instructor to camera] "After 20 years of teaching and testing every ball on the market, this is what I play. The PG Ball. It's designed for golfers like the ones I teach every day — people who are serious about getting better. This is my recommendation. Now it's yours."

## PART IX: KEY INSIGHTS

### 9.1 Advertising Insights

1. **Nobody owns improvement messaging** — All ads are tour-focused or value-focused
2. **Content-first works for golf** — Instructional content with ball integration is untapped
3. **Credibility > Budget** — Snell proves founder/expert credibility beats ad spend
4. **Vice is abandoning DTC purity** — Creates opening for relationship-focused DTC

### 9.2 Funnel Insights

1. **Existing audience is the moat** — 500K+ email list = immediate distribution
2. **Subscription should be primary offer** — Golf balls are consumable with predictable cycle
3. **Content integration reduces CAC** — Ball in instruction videos = free awareness
4. **Quiz as consideration tool** — Addresses "which ball for me" confusion

### 9.3 Creative Insights

1. **Lead with the tip, follow with the ball** — Content-first creative outperforms product-first
2. **Instructor testimonials > Customer reviews** — Authority positioning is key
3. **"Your search is over" messaging resonates** — Addresses eternal searcher fatigue
4. **Anti-worthiness messaging is differentiated** — Nobody else says "you qualify"
