### 3. What are Claude's typical shortcuts on market research that I must AVOID?

- **Surface-level competitive analysis** (listing features vs. capturing VOICE and positioning)
- **Assuming golf ball buyers think like golf instruction buyers** (different purchase psychology)
- **Focusing on technical specs** (compression, layers, urethane) without understanding emotional drivers
- **Missing the "switching cost" psychology** — why golfers are loyal to their ball
- **Generic "premium vs value" segmentation** instead of identity-based portraits
- **Ignoring the disconnect** between what golfers SAY matters and what actually drives purchase
- **Treating brand loyalty as irrational** instead of understanding its roots

## FAILURE PRE-MORTEM

### If this research disappoints, what's the most likely reason?

1. **Competitor feature comparison** instead of understanding consumer psychology
2. **Missing the "brand prestige" factor** — golfers choose balls for identity reasons, not just performance
3. **Focusing on specs** (spin rate, compression) when golfers can't actually feel the difference
4. **Not understanding WHY golfers stay loyal** to Titleist Pro V1 even when cheaper alternatives test similarly
5. **Generic market sizing** without actionable segmentation

### What would make an expert product strategist say "this research is amateur"?

- Can't articulate WHY a golfer would SWITCH from their current ball
- Doesn't understand the "good enough" problem — most balls perform similarly for amateurs
- No clear answer to "What's the believable reason Performance Golf can make a great ball?"
- Competitive analysis that lists features without capturing the EMOTIONAL positioning
- No insight into the golf ball purchase MOMENT — where/when/why decisions happen
