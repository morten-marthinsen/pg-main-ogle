## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The high-ticket golf school market is fragmented with no dominant player in the $5,000-$10,000 "permanence-focused" segment. Key findings:

1. **No competitor emphasizes extended support** — Most offer 0-30 days post-immersive
2. **Celebrity instructors command premium** but don't deliver more results
3. **Technology is table stakes** — TrackMan, video analysis expected everywhere
4. **Logistics flexibility is emerging** — Bird Golf's multi-location model is gaining traction
5. **Spouse/partner integration is underserved** — Opportunity for resort-style positioning

## 7. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### 7.1 Positioning Strategy

**Primary Differentiator:** Extended support (6-12 months post-immersive)
- NO competitor offers this
- Addresses #1 buyer complaint ("didn't stick")
- Creates ongoing relationship vs. one-time transaction
- Higher perceived value justifies premium price

**Secondary Differentiator:** Root cause methodology
- "One flaw causing 4 symptoms"
- Contrasts with "tips and tricks" approach
- Scientific, mechanistic credibility
- Reduces complexity (one thing to fix, not 10)

**Tertiary Differentiator:** Body-first approach
- "Your body is your 15th club"
- Holistic (physical + technical)
- Resonates with aging demographic
- Unique angle in market

### 7.2 Pricing Strategy

Recommended positioning: **Upper-Mid Premium**
- Foundation: $2,500-$3,000 (entry, compete with Jim McLean)
- Transformation: $5,000-$7,000 (core, compete with Bird Golf)
- Elite: $12,000-$15,000 (premium, compete with Butch Harmon value)

### 7.3 Location Strategy

Multi-location approach (Bird Golf model):
- Reduces #1 objection (logistics/travel)
- Southeast focus aligns with sales data (Phoenix, Savannah, Florida, Alabama, NC)
- Partner with resort properties for spouse amenities
