**What the data says:** Logistics objections outnumber price objections **26 to 1**.


**The implication most analysis would miss:**

This isn't a value problem. It's a COMMITMENT problem.

The prospect isn't asking "Is this worth $6,000?" They're asking:
- "Can I actually make this work in my life?"
- "What will my wife say?"
- "I've got things scheduled through October..."

**The A-List reframe:** We're not selling golf instruction. We're selling a LOGISTICS-SOLVABLE transformation.

1. We've already solved the logistics (6 locations, spouse activities, flexible dates)
2. The deposit is refundable if life intervenes

**The A-List insight:** The 12-month support isn't a feature. It's the ENTIRE differentiation.

### Portrait 1: THE FRUSTRATED INVESTOR (45% of Market)

**Meet Frank:**

Frank is 62, retired airline pilot. He hit age 65 and "they forced me to retire from that one." Now he flies part-time for a small corporate company—two weeks on, two weeks off.

He's been playing golf for 40 years. He's got a $3,000 driver, a membership at a semi-private club, and a handicap that stubbornly refuses to drop below 18.

Here's what Frank has tried:
- Local pro lessons ($200/hour × 20 sessions = $4,000)
- Three different "swing methods" from YouTube
- Two online subscriptions (Performance Golf, Me and My Golf)
- A weekend clinic in Arizona three years ago
- New clubs every 2-3 years ("Maybe the equipment will help")

Total golf improvement investment: Probably $15,000+.
Current handicap: Same as five years ago.

**What Frank says on the sales call:**
> "I've done those before. How many people to a class?"
> "They were, you know, they were half that."
> "I've tried lessons before and they didn't really work for me."

**What Frank is REALLY thinking:**
"I'm not stupid. I'm successful in my career. Why can't I figure out this damn game? Maybe it's not possible for me. Maybe I'm just going to be a 18-handicap forever."

**The identity threat:** Frank's competence is on trial. He's used to being good at things. Golf exposes him.

**What would make Frank say YES:**
- Mechanism that explains WHY nothing has worked (not his fault)
- Differentiation from "every other golf school"
- Proof that change is PERMANENT (not another 2-week improvement)
- Logistics that don't require negotiating with his wife for a week

**Copy that lands for Frank:**
> "You've done everything right. You've invested in equipment, instruction, practice. And yet... here you are. That's not a reflection on you—it's a reflection on the instruction model. Here's what's been missing..."

## PART 3: THE A-LIST BIG IDEAS

### What Makes a Big Idea A-List vs. B-List?

B-List Big Ideas are:
- Benefit statements ("Improve your golf game forever")
- Generic promises ("The breakthrough you've been waiting for")
- Claims that could apply to any competitor

A-List Big Ideas are:
- **Specific** — Describes THIS offer's unique mechanism
- **Surprising** — Not what they expected to hear
- **Inevitable** — "Of COURSE that's true" in retrospect
- **Differentiating** — Only WE can say this
