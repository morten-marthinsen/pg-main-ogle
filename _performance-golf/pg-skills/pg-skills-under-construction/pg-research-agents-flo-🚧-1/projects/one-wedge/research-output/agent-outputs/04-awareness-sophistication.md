## SCHWARTZ AWARENESS LEVELS

### Level 1: UNAWARE (5% of market)
**Who they are:** Golfers who chunk chips but don't recognize it as a solvable problem

**Characteristics:**
- Attribute bad shots to "having an off day"
- Blame the course conditions, not themselves
- Haven't connected chunking to equipment or technique
- May joke "I've never been good at chipping" without trying to fix it

**Language Markers:**
- "That's just golf"
- "Everyone has bad days"
- "Chipping is hard for everyone"

**Marketing Approach:** NOT TARGET - Awareness building cost too high

---

### Level 2: PROBLEM AWARE (25% of market) ★ PRIMARY TARGET
**Who they are:** Golfers who know they have a chunking problem but don't know solutions exist

**Characteristics:**
- Explicitly identify short game as weakness
- Feel frustrated but don't know where to start
- Have vague sense that "better players don't have this problem"
- May have tried random tips but nothing systematic

**Language Markers:**
- "My short game is killing me"
- "I can't chip to save my life"
- "Something is wrong with my wedge play"
- "I need help with my short game"

**Marketing Approach:**
- Lead with problem articulation ("Do you chunk chips?")
- Agitate the emotional pain
- Position ONE.1 as "the solution you didn't know existed"

**Copy Entry Points:**
> "If you've ever chunked a chip 3 feet or skulled one over the green... you know the frustration."

---

### Level 3: SOLUTION AWARE (40% of market) ★ SECONDARY TARGET
**Who they are:** Golfers who know solutions exist but haven't found one that works

**Characteristics:**
- Have tried lessons, tips, drills
- Own "game improvement" equipment
- Watch YouTube instruction videos
- Read golf magazines/websites for fixes
- Skeptical of new solutions but still hoping

**Language Markers:**
- "I've tried everything"
- "YouTube tips don't work for me"
- "Lessons helped temporarily"
- "Looking for something that actually works"

**Marketing Approach:**
- Acknowledge solution exhaustion
- Differentiate from "tips and techniques"
- Position ONE.1 as "equipment solution, not technique solution"
- Overcome skepticism with demonstration

**Copy Entry Points:**
> "Tired of chipping tips that don't stick? What if the problem isn't your technique—it's your wedge?"

---

### Level 4: PRODUCT AWARE (20% of market)
**Who they are:** Golfers who know about game-improvement wedges but haven't heard of ONE.1

**Characteristics:**
- Know about Cleveland CBX, high-bounce wedges
- Have considered "forgiving" wedge options
- May own or have tried game-improvement wedges
- Compare products before purchasing

**Language Markers:**
- "I've looked at Cleveland CBX"
- "Which wedge is most forgiving?"
- "I need high bounce"
- "What wedge stops chunking?"

**Marketing Approach:**
- Direct comparison to known products
- Highlight ONE.1's unique "Auto-Glide Sole"
- Focus on differentiated mechanism
- Use testimonials from similar products' failures

**Copy Entry Points:**
> "You've heard of forgiving wedges. Now meet the first wedge engineered specifically to eliminate chunking."

---

### Level 5: MOST AWARE (10% of market)
**Who they are:** Golfers who know about ONE.1 or Performance Golf products

**Characteristics:**
- On Performance Golf email list
- Have seen ONE.1 ads
- May own other PG products
- Waiting for right offer or more proof

**Language Markers:**
- "I saw the ONE Wedge ad"
- "Performance Golf makes that, right?"
- "What's the difference from the old ONE wedge?"
- "When does it go on sale?"

**Marketing Approach:**
- Offer, urgency, guarantee
- New model advantages (ONE.1 vs ONE)
- Bundle deals
- Customer testimonials from ONE users

**Copy Entry Points:**
> "The ALL-NEW ONE.1 Wedge: Everything you loved about ONE, now with even more anti-chunk forgiveness."

## MARKET SOPHISTICATION ANALYSIS

### Sophistication Stage Framework

| Stage | Characteristics | Primary Approach |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| 1 | Market is new, basic claims work | Simple promise |
| 2 | Claims copied, need to enlarge | Bigger promise |
| 3 | Claims exhausted, need mechanism | Unique mechanism |
| 4 | Mechanisms copied, need proof | New proof type |
| 5 | Everything exhausted, need identification | Identity/tribe |

### Wedge Market Sophistication Assessment

**Current Stage: LATE STAGE 3 / EARLY STAGE 4**

**Evidence:**

**Stage 3 Indicators (Mechanism Focus):**
- Competitors lead with technology names (ZipCore, Spin Gen, Hydropearl)
- "How it works" is central to marketing
- Groove patterns, sole grinds, CG placement emphasized
- Feature comparisons dominate reviews

**Stage 4 Indicators (Proof Focus):**
- "Tour-used" proof is primary for Vokey/Callaway
- Customer testimonials emerging as differentiator
- Before/after demonstrations increasing
- Independent reviews carry weight

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS

### ONE.1 Positioning Strategy

**Given:** Late Stage 3 / Early Stage 4 sophistication

**Optimal Approach:**
1. **New Mechanism** (Auto-Glide Sole as unique tech)
2. **Specific Claim** ("Anti-chunk" not "forgiving")
3. **Demonstration Proof** (Video showing sole gliding through turf)
4. **Identity Bridge** ("For golfers who've tried everything")

### Message Architecture by Awareness Level

**Problem Aware (25%):**
```
HOOK: "Do you chunk chips?"
AGITATE: Pain of embarrassment, score destruction
SOLVE: "There's now a wedge designed to prevent it"
```

**Solution Aware (40%):**
```
HOOK: "Tired of chipping tips that don't work?"
DIFFERENTIATE: "It's not your technique. It's your wedge."
MECHANISM: "The Auto-Glide Sole accelerates through any lie"
PROOF: "Watch it in action..."
```

**Product Aware (20%):**
```
HOOK: "You know about forgiving wedges. This is different."
COMPARE: "Unlike CBX, ONE.1's 4-way camber prevents digging"
DEMONSTRATE: "See the difference in action"
OFFER: "Try it risk-free"
```
