## DIG METHODOLOGY

Each dig follows the three-layer model:
1. **SURFACE** → What they say (stated problem)
2. **DEEPER** → What it means (implied consequences)
3. **DEEPEST/IDENTITY** → Who they are/become (existential impact)

---

## DIG #1: THE CHUNKING CRISIS

**Seed Quote:**
> "I proceed to death march thru the rest of that round thinning and bladeing more chips then I ever have in my life"

### Layer 1 - SURFACE
**What they say:** "I'm chunking/thinning/blading chips during my round"
- The ball goes nowhere (chunk) or rockets over the green (blade)
- It happens on easy shots within 30 yards
- It happens repeatedly, not just once

### Layer 2 - DEEPER
**What it means:**
- **Score destruction:** Each chunk costs 1-3 strokes (chip to chip to putt to putt)
- **Round ruination:** One bad sequence mentally ruins the next 16 holes
- **Playing partner awareness:** Others notice, comment, or awkwardly look away
- **Self-trust erosion:** "If I can't do THIS, what CAN I do?"

### Layer 3 - DEEPEST (Identity)
**Who they become:**
> "I am someone who can't be trusted with a simple shot"

- **Competence questioning:** "Maybe I'm just not meant to be good at this"
- **Investment questioning:** "Why did I spend $3,000 on clubs if I can't chip?"
- **Social identity threat:** "My playing partners are starting to pity me"
- **Future visualization:** "Will I EVER be able to chip without fear?"

**Core Identity Wound:** Loss of control over something that should be controllable.

- **Isolation tendency:** "Maybe I should just play alone"

**Core Identity Wound:** Social exposure of perceived inadequacy.

**What they say:** "I'm good at hard things but bad at easy things"

### Layer 2 - DEEPER

### Layer 3 - DEEPEST (Identity)

- **Unfairness narrative:** "Other people don't struggle like this"

**Core Identity Wound:** Unrealized potential made visible.
