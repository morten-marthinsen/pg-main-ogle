## Mental Models Library

### Systems Thinking
**Theory of Constraints:** Every system has ONE bottleneck limiting throughput. Find it, fix it, repeat.

**Feedback Loops:** Identify reinforcing loops (growth/decline) vs balancing loops (stability). Most problems are stuck balancing loops preventing growth.

**Stock and Flow:** Distinguish accumulations (assets, systems) from rates (activities, effort). Build stocks, not just flows.

**Emergence:** System behavior exceeds sum of parts. Look for unexpected patterns and interactions.

### Strategic Frameworks
**Jobs-to-be-Done:** What progress are people trying to make? Frame solutions around progress, not features.

**Blue Ocean Strategy:** Create uncontested market space instead of competing in red oceans.

**Disruption Theory:** Start with overlooked segments, improve faster than incumbents can respond.

**Platform Thinking:** Create value through connections. Network effects = exponential leverage.

### Decision Models
**Opportunity Cost:** What's the highest value alternative you're giving up? Make this explicit.

**Reversible vs Irreversible:** Two-way doors (easily reversed) vs one-way doors (hard to undo). Move fast on two-way doors.

**Regret Minimization:** What would 80-year-old you choose? Removes short-term noise.

**Expected Value:** Probability × Impact. Calculate this for major decisions.

### Leverage Principles
**80/20 Analysis:** Which 20% of efforts drive 80% of results? Double down on these.

**Compound Effects:** Small improvements that multiply over time. Seek geometric returns, not linear.

**Network Effects:** Value increases with users/connections. Build in network effects when possible.

**Zero Marginal Cost:** Can you scale without proportional cost increases? This is true leverage.

### Cognitive Frameworks
**First Principles:** Break down to fundamental truths. Rebuild from there without inherited assumptions.

**Inversion:** Work backwards from failure scenarios. What would guarantee failure? Avoid that.

**Second-Order Thinking:** Then what happens? And then what? Think through consequences 2-3 levels deep.

**Map vs Territory:** Your model isn't reality. Stay humble about what you know.

## Portfolio Analysis Framework

### Impact/Effort Matrix

**Quick Wins** (High Impact, Low Effort)
- Do immediately for momentum
- Usually process improvements or removing friction
- Example: Fixing broken automation that's failing daily

**Strategic Projects** (High Impact, High Effort)  
- Plan carefully with clear phases
- Often build new revenue streams or transform operations
- Example: Building affiliate program from scratch

**Fill-ins** (Low Impact, Low Effort)
- Delegate or batch process
- Nice-to-haves that don't move the needle
- Example: Internal team training on existing processes

**Time Wasters** (Low Impact, High Effort)
- Eliminate or dramatically simplify
- Often disguised as "important" but drain resources
- Example: Building elaborate systems no one will use

### Scoring Methodology

**Impact Score (1-10):**
- Revenue potential (direct or indirect)
- Strategic importance (position, capabilities)
- Compound effects (improves over time?)
- Problem severity (cost of not solving)

**Effort Score (1-10):**
- Time investment required
- Resource needs (people, tools, money)
- Complexity level (dependencies, unknowns)
- Organizational lift (change management)

**Portfolio Decision Rules:**
1. Always start with Quick Wins (build momentum)
2. Plan Strategic Projects in parallel (long-term bets)
3. Delegate Fill-ins ruthlessly (don't do yourself)
4. Eliminate Time Wasters immediately (protect focus)

**If not 4/5 "yes" → Redesign the approach or reject the initiative.**

## Extraction Intelligence Patterns

### Meeting Transcript Signals

**Commitment Indicators:**
- "Let's definitely..." = Strong decision
- "I'll take care of..." = Ownership claimed
- "By [specific date]..." = Timeline commitment
- Repeated mentions = High importance
- Action verb + owner name = Executable decision

**Exploration Indicators:**
- "We should consider..." = Idea stage
- "What if we..." = Innovation/brainstorming
- "Has anyone thought about..." = Seeking input
- Questions without answers = Uncertainty/blocks
- Multiple perspectives = Complex decision ahead

**Red Flag Indicators:**
- "We'll fix that later" = Tech debt accumulating
- "Someone should..." (no owner) = Accountability vacuum
- Interrupted topics = Unresolved tensions
- Passive voice = Avoiding ownership
- "We talked about this before" = Recurring unresolved issue

### Slack Chaos Patterns

**High-Priority Signals:**
- Recurring topics = Unresolved systemic issues
- Long threads (10+ messages) = Complex problem or strong interest
- Multiple participants = Cross-functional importance
- @mentions of leadership = Escalation or decision needed
- Emoji reactions cluster = Quick consensus forming

**Research Phase Signals:**
- Links and resources shared = Information gathering
- Screenshots and examples = Showing not telling
- "Has anyone tried..." = Seeking validation
- Comparative analysis = Evaluation mode

**Momentum Signals:**
- Volunteering = Natural champions emerging
- "I can handle..." = Ownership self-selecting
- Time-based language = Urgency and commitment
- Follow-up questions = Deep engagement

## Usage Notes

This skill is designed for backend operations leaders, strategic thinkers, and operators who need to transform chaotic inputs into clear direction. It excels at:

- Parsing meeting recordings and extracting decisions
- Analyzing multiple competing initiatives for priority
- Designing leverage strategies for time-trapped operators  
- Routing complex problems to appropriate resources
- Challenging assumptions and reframing problems

It is NOT designed for:
- Direct operational execution (route those to Operations Engine)
- Detailed SOP creation (route to Operations Engine)
- Team communications drafting (route to Operations Engine)
- Simple tactical questions (handle directly without full analysis)

The skill's power comes from its multi-expert perspective system and comprehensive mental models library. Use it when you need strategic clarity, not when you need task completion.
