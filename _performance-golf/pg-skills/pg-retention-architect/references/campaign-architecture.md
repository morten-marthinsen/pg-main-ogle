## 2026 Email/SMS Landscape (Current State)

### Critical Trends
- **Email volume increased 60% YoY** - You must send more to stay visible
- **Open rates are unreliable** (artificially inflated by iOS privacy) - Focus on click rates instead
- **Click rates are the new north star**: 1-1.5% average, 2-3% good, 5%+ great
- **iOS 18 AI summarization** - All images need descriptive alt text for AI to parse correctly
- **Spam complaints must stay under 0.3%** (3 per 1,000 emails) - Monitor with Google Postmaster Tools
- **Average order value declining**: $130 → $94 per email conversion (SMS similar)

### Success Thresholds (Investigate If Breached)
- Click rates below 1%
- Open rates below 18%
- Spam complaints above 0.08%
- Bounce rate above 5%
- Inactive percentage above 25%
- Unsubscribe-to-action ratio above 15%

## SMS Strategy & Best Practices

### SMS Core Principles
- **Open rate: 98%** (vs. email's 30%)
- **Engagement window: 2 hours** (vs. email's 24-48 hours)
- **Character limit: 160** (emojis consume space)
- **Cost consideration**: MMS costs 3x more than SMS (test ROI)

### SMS Timing Rules
- **Best sending times**: 11am-3pm in recipient's local time zone
- **Legal sending window**: 8am-8pm in recipient's local time zone (TCPA compliance)
- **Frequency limit**: 1-2 SMS per week standard, 3 maximum
- **Spacing**: Days 1, 3, 5 for most campaigns (except final day can be back-to-back)

### SMS Copy Structure (160 characters)
```
[First Name], [Product] is back! 24hrs only: Get 20% off with code [CODE]. Shop now: [link]

Elements required:
1. Personalization (first name)
2. Value proposition
3. Urgency element
4. Clear CTA with link
```

### SMS vs. Email Integration
- **Email drives 81% of revenue, SMS drives 19%** (but SMS boosts both)
- Send SMS follow-up ONLY to those who didn't open email
- Use email for detailed info, SMS for urgent actions
- Map customer journey to prevent overlap
- SMS for high-intent moments: abandoned carts, back-in-stock, VIP early access
- Keep tone conversational and intimate ("like a friend texting you")

### SMS High-Performance Use Cases
1. Abandoned cart recovery (link directly to cart)
2. Back-in-stock alerts
3. VIP early access (before public launch)
4. Flash sales (time-sensitive offers)
5. Drop launches (limited quantity)
6. Order/shipping updates

## Advanced Segmentation Framework

### RFM Model (20/60/20 Value Buckets)

**Core principle**: "If you have a $20 customer, why are you sending the same offers you give your $100 customer?"

| Segment | Definition | Strategy |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Top 20% (High-Value)** | High recency + frequency + monetary | Exclusive offers, no discounts, most frequent communication, VIP treatment, early access |
| **Middle 60% (Core)** | Average spenders | Standard campaigns, moderate discounts, product education, move them to top 20% |
| **Bottom 20% (Low-Value)** | Discount shoppers, infrequent buyers | Heavy discount focus during sales, lighter communication, avoid premium product pushes |

### Segmentation by Customer Type

**1. Prospects/Non-Buyers**
- Engaged but haven't purchased
- Focus: Education, social proof, first-purchase incentive
- Frequency: Moderate (don't burn out)

**2. Recent Buyers (Last 30 Days)**
- Just converted
- Focus: Product care, usage tips, avoid heavy discounting
- Goal: Second purchase, reduce buyer's remorse

**3. Active Customers (31-90 Days)**
- In buying cycle
- Focus: Cross-sells, new arrivals, exclusive offers
- Frequency: Higher (they're engaged)

**4. At-Risk (91-120 Days)**
- Slipping away
- Focus: Re-engagement, preference updates, soft incentives
- Frequency: Reduced (50% of standard)

**5. Inactive/Lapsed (120+ Days)**
- No engagement for 4+ months
- Focus: Win-back sequence → sunset
- Frequency: Re-engagement only

**6. Discount Shoppers**
- Only buy on sale
- Focus: Sale announcements, clearance, BFCM
- Strategy: Accept their behavior, don't over-invest

**7. High-Frequency Buyers**
- Purchase often
- Focus: New products, restocks, loyalty rewards
- Treat similar to high-value segment

### Data Collection for Better Segmentation

**Zero-Party Data** (customer intentionally shares):
- Quizzes and surveys (benefit-focused, not data-extraction)
- Preference centers (content types, frequency)
- Product/category preferences
- Birthday/anniversary dates
- Style/fit preferences

**First-Party Data** (behavior-based):
- Purchase history
- Browse/cart abandonment patterns
- Email/SMS engagement (clicks, opens)
- Product category interest
- Buying cycle patterns
- Geographic location

**Third-Party Data** (external identification):
- Anonymous visitor identification
- Use ONLY for targeted flows (cart abandonment, browse abandonment)
- Do NOT add to newsletter list without consent
- Monitor deliverability closely

## Retention Automation Library

### Welcome Series (6 emails + 2 SMS, 14 days)

**ENHANCED with Send It Podcast best practices**

| Timing | Channel | Focus | Goal |
|--------|---------|-------|------|
| **Immediate** | Email 1 | Welcome + incentive delivery | Set expectations, deliver promised offer |
| **+30-60 min** | SMS 1 | Code reminder + nudge | "Hey [Name], your 15% off code is ready: FRESH15 👉 [link]" |
| **Day 2** | Email 2 | Brand story + founder narrative | Emotional connection, the "why" behind brand |
| **Day 3** | Email 3 | Urgency + last chance | FOMO, social proof, countdown, "48 hours left" |
| **Day 4** | Email 4 | How to use / best practices | Value delivery, education, long-term engagement |
| **Day 7** | Email 5 | Social proof + UGC | Community integration, customer photos/reviews |
| **Day 10** | Email 6 | Perks, loyalty, referral | Ongoing relationship, "join the club" |
| **Day 14** | SMS 2 | Exclusive offer for subscribers | First purchase conversion push |

**Email 1 - The Warm Welcome**
- Hero headline (benefit-driven, match brand promise)
- Value proposition (1-2 lines, why this brand is different)
- Incentive delivery (big button, clear code)
- Social proof block (star rating, short reviews)
- CTA repetition (don't assume they scroll)

**Email 2 - Founder Story**
- Opening hook (relatable problem)
- 3-act narrative: problem → obsession → breakthrough
- Values + product tie-in (subtle, not pushy)
- Founder photo or lab image
- CTA to "explore collection" (invite, don't sell)

**Email 3 - Urgency & Last Chance**
- Time pressure headline
- Countdown visual (bar or graphic)
- Reminder of what they'll miss
- UGC images (2-3 customer photos)
- Urgent CTA with final hours messaging

**Email 4 - How to Use**
- Instructional, helpful tone
- Niche-specific tips (e.g., "3 ways to sleep cooler tonight")
- Product education (get more from purchase)
- CTA to full guide or complementary products

**Email 5 - UGC & Community**
- Feature happy customers
- "See how others styled/used theirs"
- Build belonging and connection
- Soft CTA to join community or share their experience

**Email 6 - Perks & Loyalty**
- Introduce loyalty program
- Referral incentive ("Get $10, give $10")
- Ongoing value proposition
- "Join the club" or "Start earning rewards"

**Pro Tips:**
- Keep emails short (no fluff, no novels)
- Design hierarchy: Big headline, small body, clear button
- Repeat CTA (don't assume scrolling)
- SMS = 1 action only (tap, not a pitch)
- Test subject lines, CTA copy, font size (18-20pt recommended)

## Assessment Funnel Architecture

### Funnel Stages
```
TRAFFIC → QUIZ ENTRY
Hook: Problem discovery angle ("What's REALLY sabotaging your [result]?")
Promise: Quick diagnosis (60 seconds, 5 questions)

QUIZ → RESULTS
Diagnosis: Identify specific problem type
Amplification: Show consequences of not fixing

RESULTS → BOOKING
Bridge: "Free consultation to fix your [diagnosed problem]"
Scarcity: Limited analyst availability

BOOKING → CONFIRMATION
Commitment: Expectation setting, no-show prevention
Gift: Value delivery for showing up
```

### Post-Assessment Sequences

**Non-Completers (started but didn't finish):**
- Day 1: "You were so close..." reminder
- Day 3: Different angle on same problem
- Day 7: Final invitation

**Completers Who Don't Book:**
- Immediate: Results delivery + booking CTA
- Day 2: Educational content based on results
- Day 4: Success stories matching their diagnosis
- Day 7: Soft booking reminder
- Day 10: Case study with similar problem
- Day 14: Special incentive for completers

**Booked But No-Show:**
- 1 hour after: "We missed you" + easy reschedule
- Day 2: Value of what they missed + reschedule
- Day 7: Final reschedule attempt

## Clearance Campaign Framework (6 Phases)

Story-driven inventory liquidation that maintains brand credibility.

| Phase | Discount | Story Angle | Urgency Source |
|-------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| 1: Emergency | 60-70% | Behind-scenes crisis | Disposal deadline |
| 2: Back by Demand | 65-70% | "You broke our website" | Overwhelming response |
| 3: Warehouse Clean-Out | 70-75% | Discovered inventory | Space for new stock |
| 4: Employee Pricing | 75-80% | Internal policy extension | Limited time privilege |
| 5: Demo/Returns | 80-85% | Lightly used units | Cosmetic imperfections |
| 6: Manufacturing Overrun | 85-90% | Production error | Partner liquidation |

### Clearance Rules
- Never clearance your Product of the Month
- 2-4 week spacing between phases
- Different products can run different phases simultaneously
- Always include soft mentions of primary offers
- Track clearance buyers separately (different nurture path)

### Clearance Buyer Graduation (Convert to Full-Price)
```
Day 7: Success check-in ("How's it working?")
Day 14: Amplification content (get more from purchase)
Day 30: Success story + complementary product at insider pricing
Day 60: Premium product positioning
Day 90: VIP/high-ticket invitation
```

### Campaign Spacing Rules
- Minimum 2 days between campaign endings and new campaign starts

- Never overlap two "urgent" campaigns
- Content weeks buffer between heavy promotional periods

## High-Ticket Promotion Framework

### Structure (3-Day Dedicated Push)
```
Day 1: Problem/Opportunity Introduction
- Establish the gap they're experiencing
- Introduce the transformation available
- Soft scarcity mention

Day 2: Solution Deep Dive + Social Proof
- How it works
- Who it's for (qualification)
- Results from others like them

Day 3: Urgency + Final Call
- Deadline reinforcement
- Consequence of waiting
- Clear next step
```

### Supplementary Mentions
Throughout the month, include soft high-ticket mentions in:
- Content email PS lines
- Post-purchase sequences
- Assessment result pages
- Success story emails

### Urgency Drivers (Pick One)
- Limited spots: "Only X spots remaining this month"
- Calendar scarcity: "Coach has X dates left in Q2"
- Seasonal deadline: "Last chance before [season change]"
- Special access: "VIP booking window closes [date]"

## List Health Protocol

### Engagement Segments
| Segment | Definition | Send Frequency |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| Highly Engaged | Opened/clicked in 30 days | Full sending |
| Engaged | Opened/clicked in 60 days | Standard |
| At Risk | No engagement 61-90 days | Reduced (50%) |
| Disengaged | No engagement 91-120 days | Re-engagement only |
| Inactive | No engagement 120+ days | Sunset sequence |

### Re-engagement Triggers
- Day 60: Soft re-engagement (value-focused, no hard sell)
- Day 75: Preference update request
- Day 90: Direct re-engagement + incentive
- Day 105: Final notice ("Should we say goodbye?")
- Day 120-150: Sunset sequence → Remove

### Health Monitoring
- **Use Google Postmaster Tools** (free) to monitor spam complaint trends
- Focus on 7-day rolling average (not single-campaign spikes)
- High sending volume makes 0.3% threshold easier to maintain
- Track contextual data (product clicks) to understand interest patterns
