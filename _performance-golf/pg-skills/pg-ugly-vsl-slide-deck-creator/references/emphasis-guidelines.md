# Emphasis Guidelines for Ugly VSL Slide Decks

## Core Principle

Emphasis is a tool to make specific content "pop" - use it sparingly or it loses its impact. If every slide has emphasis, nothing stands out.

**CRITICAL RULE: Only emphasize the POSITIVE.** Bold, red, and all other emphasis go exclusively on benefits, outcomes, promises, and desirable results. Never emphasize negative, fear-based, or problem-focused words. The negatives do their job in plain text; the visual "highlight reel" of the deck should be 100% aspirational.

## Types of Emphasis

### 1. Bold

**When to use:**
- Major benefits being promised
- Key positive outcomes or transformations
- Product names or important terms
- Powerful positive action words
- Numbers that represent gains (distance, savings, results)

**Examples:**
- "You'll finally **break 80** consistently..."
- "Adding **20-30 yards** to your drives..."
- "In just **7 days**..."
- "**Hit fairways consistently**..."

**What NOT to bold:**
- Pain points ("struggling," "broken," "wrong")
- Negative outcomes ("wasted money," "fighting against you")
- Fear-based words ("never," "failing," "painful truth")

**Markup:** `**text to bold**`

### 2. Bold + Red (#B11800)

**When to use:**
- The BIGGEST positive moments — peak promises and transformation results
- Aspirational outcomes that represent the ultimate benefit
- High-impact positive numbers or results

**Rule:** Red text is ALWAYS also bolded. Never use red without bold. Red is reserved for the biggest positive peaks, not for problems.

**Examples:**
- "**{red}Straight drives down the center of the fairway{/red}**..."
- "**{red}20-30 extra yards{/red}** added to every drive..."
- "A total value of **{red}$696.00{/red}**..."

**What NOT to make red:**
- Problems or pain points (these stay plain text)
- Negative qualities or bad outcomes
- Words like "not," "wrong," "broken"

**Markup:** `**{red}text in red{/red}**`

### 3. Underline

**When to use:**
- Very sparingly - only for positive words that absolutely need extra emphasis
- When you want something to pop but bold isn't enough
- Single words rather than phrases
- Unique terms or coined phrases (product/mechanism names)

**Examples:**
- "I call it the __AutoCorrect__ swing..."
- "This is the __only__ method that delivers..."

**Markup:** `__text to underline__`

## Frequency Guidelines

| Emphasis Type | Frequency |
|---------------|-----------|
| Bold | Every 5-10 slides (when warranted) |
| Bold + Red | Every 15-25 slides (biggest positive moments) |
| Underline | Every 30-50 slides (rare, special cases) |

## What NOT to Emphasize

- Transitional words ("and", "but", "so")
- Common phrases that don't add impact
- Multiple items on the same slide (pick ONE thing to emphasize)
- Generic positive words ("great", "good", "nice")
- ANY negative, fear-based, or problem-focused language
- Pain points, flawed solutions, bad outcomes

## Decision Framework

Ask yourself:
1. Is this word/phrase a POSITIVE benefit, outcome, or promise?
2. Would the slide lose impact without this emphasis?
3. Have I used this type of emphasis recently? (If yes, skip it)
4. Is this the MOST important positive thing on this slide?

If you can't answer "yes" to all four questions, don't add emphasis.

## Examples from Real VSLs

### SF2 Driver VSL (Hank Haney)
- Bold on benefit: "**20-30 yards**"
- Bold on outcome: "**straight drives**"
- Bold on credibility number: "**182,000** golf lessons"
- Plain text on negatives: "fails to deliver" (no emphasis)
- Plain text on problems: "NOT the fastest OR the easiest way" (no emphasis on the negative framing)
