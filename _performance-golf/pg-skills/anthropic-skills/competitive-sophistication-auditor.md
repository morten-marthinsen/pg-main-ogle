# Competitive Sophistication Auditor

Maps what the market has already been TOLD to find what's fresh.

**Core Question:** "What claims and mechanisms are saturated, and what's the next frontier?"

**Input:** Competitor advertising, promotions, messaging (manual paste)
**Output:** Competitive Sophistication Audit (claim/mechanism map + stage assessment)
**Workflow:** Used in both Workflow B and Workflow C, after Market Psychology Analyzer

---

## THE SOPHISTICATION MODEL

Schwartz's 5 Stages of Market Sophistication:

| Stage | Market State | What Works | Example |
|-------|--------------|------------|---------|
| **1** | First to market | Simple, direct claim | "Lose weight" |
| **2** | Second wave | Enlarged claim | "Lose 30 pounds in 30 days" |
| **3** | Mechanism introduction | Explain HOW | "The keto mechanism that burns fat 24/7" |
| **4** | Elaborate mechanism | More sophisticated proof | "The 3-phase metabolic reset backed by Stanford research" |
| **5** | Identification/status | For people like you | "For busy executives who refuse to sacrifice health" |

**Key Insight:** You can't go backward. A Stage 4 market won't respond to Stage 1 claims. But you CAN leapfrog (Stage 3 → Stage 5) or introduce a NEW category (reset to Stage 1 in a sub-niche).

## ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Gather Competitive Intelligence

Request from user (or paste available):

**Essential:**
- Current competitor ads (Facebook Ad Library, YouTube, Google)
- Competitor sales pages/VSLs
- Email sequences if available
- Headlines and hooks currently running

**Valuable:**
- Historical promotions (if available)
- Market leader messaging evolution
- Failed/pulled promotions (if known)

**Nice to Have:**
- Performance signals (what's scaling?)
- Longevity signals (how long has it run?)
- A/B test variations

---

### Step 2: Extract Claims

Document every major claim in the competitive set:

```
CLAIM INVENTORY
===============

CLAIM: [The promise/statement]
Source: [Competitor name]
First Seen: [Approximate date if known]
Saturation: [Fresh / Moderate / Saturated]
Type: [Direct / Enlarged / Mechanism / Elaborate / Identity]

[Repeat for all major claims]
```

**Claim Types:**

| Type | Schwartz Stage | Pattern |
|------|----------------|---------|
| Direct | 1 | "Get [result]" |
| Enlarged | 2 | "Get [bigger/faster/easier result]" |
| Mechanism | 3 | "Get [result] because [how]" |
| Elaborate | 4 | "Get [result] through [sophisticated how with proof]" |
| Identity | 5 | "For [type of person] who wants [result]" |

---

### Step 3: Extract Mechanisms

Document every mechanism that's been introduced:

```
MECHANISM INVENTORY
===================

MECHANISM: [The "how" explanation]
Source: [Who introduced it]
Type: [Scientific / Proprietary / Borrowed Authority / etc.]
Status: [Novel / Established / Exhausted]
Proof Backing: [What evidence supports it]

[Repeat for all mechanisms]
```

**Mechanism Type Reference:**

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Scientific | Based on research/studies |
| Proprietary | Developed by creator (named method) |
| Borrowed Authority | Associated with famous person/institution |
| Physiological | How the body works |
| Psychological | How the mind works |
| Systemic | Hidden structures/forces |
| Simplification | Reduces to one thing |
| Contrarian | Inverts conventional wisdom |

**Status Assessment:**

| Status | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| Novel | <6 months in market, few competitors using |
| Established | Widely recognized, still working |
| Exhausted | Overused, audience skeptical, diminishing returns |

```
[ ] Still works [ ] Exhausted
Evidence: [What you observed]

STAGE 4 (Elaborate mechanism):
[ ] Still works [ ] Exhausted
Evidence: [What you observed]

[ ] Still works [ ] Exhausted
Evidence: [What you observed]

CONFIDENCE: [High / Medium / Low]
```

**Stage Determination Rules:**

1. **Find the ceiling:** What's the most sophisticated approach currently working?
2. **Find the floor:** What simple approaches have stopped working?
3. **The stage is the ceiling** — that's where the market's expectations sit

### Step 6: Next Frontier Assessment

Synthesize into actionable direction:

**The Next Frontier Question:**
"Given the current sophistication stage and what's been claimed, what's the most promising untapped angle?"

**Evaluation Criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Freshness | Can we credibly claim "new"? |
| Proof Availability | Can we back it up? |
| Desire Match | Does it hit the market's key desire? |
| Difficulty of Copy | Can competitors quickly match? |
| Mechanism Credibility | Will they believe the "how"? |

## USAGE INSTRUCTIONS

### Input Requirements

**Minimum viable input:**
- 5+ competitor ads/promotions from current market
- General sense of what's been running for a while vs. what's new

**Better input:**
- 10-20 competitor pieces across multiple competitors
- Historical perspective (what worked 6-12 months ago)

- Comprehensive competitive set
- Ad spy tool data (Facebook Ad Library, etc.)
- Historical archive

1. Gather competitive intelligence

## SOPHISTICATION STAGE STRATEGY GUIDE

Quick reference for what works at each stage:

| Stage | What Works | What's Exhausted | Proof Needed |
|-------|------------|------------------|--------------|
| 1 | Direct claims | Nothing yet | Minimal |
| 2 | Bigger/faster/easier | Simple direct claims | Moderate |
| 3 | New mechanism explaining HOW | Claim escalation alone | Mechanism credibility |
| 4 | Sophisticated mechanism + heavy proof | Simple mechanisms | Substantial |
| 5 | Identity/status positioning | All direct benefit claims | Social proof, aspiration |

**Stage Transition Signals:**
- **1→2:** First competitor matches your claim
- **2→3:** Claim escalation wars, diminishing returns on "more"
- **3→4:** Multiple mechanisms competing, need differentiation
- **4→5:** Mechanism fatigue, audience wants "people like me"
- **5→Reset:** Category disruption, new sub-niche creation
