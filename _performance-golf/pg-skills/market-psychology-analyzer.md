# Market Psychology Analyzer

Maps current market psychology to identify Big Idea opportunities.

**Core Question:** "What latent emotion/belief is waiting to be crystallized into urgency?"

**Input:** Market research (manual paste: trends, forums, competitor ads, cultural signals)
**Output:** Market Psychology Snapshot (structured assessment for Big Idea generation)
**Workflow:** Used in both Workflow B (Existing Product) and Workflow C (Market-First)

---

## THE FIVE PSYCHOLOGY DIMENSIONS

Every market, regardless of vertical, has the same five psychological states. Only the *data proxies* differ.

```
DIMENSION 1: FEAR/ANXIETY ........... What are they worried about?
DIMENSION 2: DESIRE/ASPIRATION ...... How badly do they want transformation?
DIMENSION 3: FRUSTRATION/PAIN ....... How urgent is the problem?
DIMENSION 4: HOPE/RECEPTIVITY ....... Are they open to new solutions?
DIMENSION 5: SKEPTICISM/JADEDNESS ... How burned are they by past failures?
```

**Why These Five?**

| Dimension | Big Idea Implication |
|-----------|---------------------|
| High Fear | Fear-based ideas land; "name the danger" works |
| High Desire | Aspiration-based ideas land; "transformation" works |
| High Frustration | Pain amplification works; "why everything failed" works |
| High Hope | New mechanisms welcomed; breakthrough positioning works |
| High Skepticism | Massive proof required; anti-hype positioning works |

### Step 1: Gather Input Data

Request the following from user (or paste available research):

**Quantitative Signals:**
- Search trends (Google Trends data)
- Market reports/statistics
- Sales data or indicators
- Sentiment metrics if available

**Qualitative Signals:**
- Forum discussions (Reddit, niche communities)
- Review patterns (Amazon, Trustpilot, etc.)
- Social media sentiment
- Competitor ad messaging
- Content consumption patterns
- Cultural/news context

### Step 2: Score Each Dimension

For each dimension, assess on 1-10 scale with evidence:

```
DIMENSION: [Name]
SCORE: [1-10]
DIRECTION: [Rising / Stable / Falling]
EVIDENCE:
- [Quantitative signal + interpretation]
- [Qualitative signal + interpretation]
- [Additional evidence]
CONFIDENCE: [High / Medium / Low]
```

**Scoring Guide:**

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Low intensity — not a primary driver |
| 4-6 | Moderate — present but not acute |
| 7-8 | High — significant and actionable |
| 9-10 | Extreme — crisis-level, dominant force |

---

### Step 3: Narrative Archaeology

Beyond scores, reconstruct the *story* the market is telling itself.

**Extract:**

| Element | Question |
|---------|----------|
| **Dominant Narrative** | What story is the market/media telling? |
| **Counter-Narrative** | What's the emerging challenger story? |
| **Unspoken Belief** | What do they believe but won't say publicly? |
| **Normalcy Bias** | What are they ignoring or minimizing? |
| **Aspiration Gap** | Who do they want to be vs. who they are? |

**Sources:**
- Media coverage trends
- Bestseller lists (what books are selling?)
- Podcast popularity
- Influencer content themes
- Forum discussion patterns
- Comment sentiment on relevant content

---

### Step 4: Awareness Level Assessment

Map to Schwartz spectrum:

| Level | Definition | Evidence Pattern |
|-------|------------|------------------|
| **Unaware** | Don't know they have a problem | No problem-related searches |
| **Problem-Aware** | Know something's wrong, not what | General symptom/frustration searches |
| **Solution-Aware** | Know solutions exist, evaluating | Category/method searches |
| **Product-Aware** | Know your product, comparing | Brand searches, competitor comparisons |
| **Most Aware** | Ready to buy, need trigger | Deal/offer searches, cart abandonment |

**Assessment Questions:**
- Do they KNOW something is wrong?
- Do they know the SPECIFIC threat/opportunity?
- Do they know what to DO about it?
- Are they thinking about YOUR solution category?

---

### Step 5: Timing Factors

**Assess:**

| Factor | Current State |
|--------|---------------|
| **Seasonal** | Where in annual cycle? Peak/trough? |
| **Cultural/News** | What's happening now that amplifies/dampens? |
| **Demographic** | Any cohort transitions? Age-related timing? |
| **Competitive** | Recent launches saturating attention? Fresh terrain? |

## USAGE INSTRUCTIONS

### For Workflow B (Existing Product)

1. Gather market research relevant to the product's target audience
2. Paste research data (trends, forums, ads, cultural signals)
3. Request: "Analyze market psychology for [audience/niche]"
4. Receive Market Psychology Snapshot
5. Proceed to Skill 3 (Competitive Sophistication Auditor)

### For Workflow C (Market-First / No Product Yet)

1. Define target market/audience broadly
2. Gather general market research for that space
3. Request: "Analyze market psychology for [market]"
4. Receive Market Psychology Snapshot with opportunity identification
5. The "Holy Grail Synthesis" section becomes the brief for product development

### Minimum Input Requirements

At minimum, provide:
- [ ] Target market/audience definition
- [ ] 2-3 sources of current market sentiment (forums, trends, or ads)
- [ ] Any known timing factors

More data = higher confidence scores.

### What This Skill Does NOT Do

| Does NOT | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Analyze the product | That's Skill 1's job |
| Map competitor messaging | That's Skill 3's job |
| Generate Big Ideas | That's Skill 5's job |
| Score/rank ideas | That's Skill 6's job |

This skill answers: **"What is the psychological state of this market right now?"**
