In 2000, Tiger won the U.S. Open at Pebble Beach by 15 shots and shot a final round bogey free 67..

I recently went to Pebble to see if I could do better than Tiger.

So we filmed an experiment called Perfection at Pebble.

The rules were simple...
 
✅ Target score: 66. 
✅ Constraint: fewer than 18 mulligans.

A mulligan means you get to retake a shot. 

So the question was, how many do-overs would it take me to match what Tiger did without any?

That constraint changed everything.

This became less about ball striking and more about how you navigate yourself around a golf course. 

Recovering from imperfect shots. 
Staying patient when momentum wanted to swing the other way. 
Making clear decisions when the margin for error disappeared.

Pebble forces you to think and choose lines deliberately. 

To manage nerves with the ocean, wind, and history pressing in from every angle.

Throughout the round, I talk through every decision. 

How I plot each hole, how I manage emotion shot by shot. 

Every mulligan is shown. 
Every moment of pressure is left in.

The whole theme of the video is talking strategy. 

How to navigate a course in a smarter way. 

Almost like you're navigating yourself around the business world.

How many mulligans do you think it took?
