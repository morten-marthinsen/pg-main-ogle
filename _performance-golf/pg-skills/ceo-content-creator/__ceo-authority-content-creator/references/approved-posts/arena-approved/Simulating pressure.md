Most people perform worse under pressure for one reason:

They've never practiced under it.

I've always believed practice should be harder than the real thing.

Not just a little harder, but significantly harder.

Because when the real moment comes, I want it to feel easy by comparison.

I've done this my whole life, no matter the context.

In golf, I'd chip balls off the green instead of on it. 

When I was the number one sales rep at my company, I was constantly roleplaying in front of my CEO.

Getting out of my comfort zone as often as possible, so that I'd be ready for anything that comes my way.

Most people do the opposite. 
They practice in conditions that feel safe, and constantly avoid discomfort.

Then they wonder why they freeze when the moment actually matters.

But when you train under adversity, you've already felt it. You've already performed through it.

So when it's game time, you're ready.

If you want to perform better under pressure, stop protecting yourself from it.

Simulate it. 
Train in it. 
Get uncomfortable on purpose.

That's the only way the real thing ever starts to feel easy.
