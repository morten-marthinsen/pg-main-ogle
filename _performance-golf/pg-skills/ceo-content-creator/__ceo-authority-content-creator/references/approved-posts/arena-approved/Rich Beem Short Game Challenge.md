How does a scratch golfer's wedge game compare to a major champion's?

I decided to find out.

I went head-to-head with Rich Beem at The Hay, the nine-hole par three course at Pebble Beach designed by Tiger Woods. 

A course that strips the game down to wedges, creativity, impeccable distance control, and nerve. 

For context, Rich has won multiple times on tour, including the 2002 PGA Championship where he went toe to toe with Tiger Woods and beat him on one of golf's biggest stages. 

On the first tee, Rich looked over and asked, "Brixton, how many shots do you want?"

I smiled and said, "Rich, I don't think I'm going to need any."

That moment set the tone for the whole round.

This wasn't about bravado. 
It was curiosity. 

I genuinely wanted to see how all my practice would hold up against someone who's proven he can deliver when it matters most.

Let's just say there were moments I surprised myself.

And moments I got a very clear reminder of what separates a major champion from the rest of us.
