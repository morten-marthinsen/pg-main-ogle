Some golfers you admire for their stats.

Rocco Mediate I admired for something else:
His presence.

Loose, confident, just completely himself. 

He looked like he was having more fun than anyone else out there, even when everything was on the line.

As an 11-year-old, I used to picture myself walking down the fairway like him.

Yesterday, that picture turned real.

Rocco and I teed it up at TPC Scottsdale Stadium Course, the same course that hosts the Waste Management Phoenix Open. 

We played it at about 5,500 yards in a two-man scramble with one challenge in mind: 

Could we break 60?

For context, this is a guy who has made 38 hole-in-ones. 

Who nearly beat Tiger Woods at the 2008 U.S. Open, taking him all the way to a Monday playoff. 

Who kept his Tour card for more than 35 straight years without ever losing it.

35 years. Think about how hard that is. 

All the travel and pressure. 

The young guys coming up every year trying to take your spot. And he just kept showing up.

Throughout the round, I picked his brain on everything:

- What he was thinking in those moments against Tiger. 
- How he managed nerves when the stakes were highest. 
- How he stayed loose when most players tighten up.

There's a lot in this video. 

Strategy. Stories. A few shots you won't believe.
