I was playing the best golf of my life.
A +3 handicap. Winning tournaments.

So naturally, I thought:
"How do I get even better?"

I did what most serious golfers do:
- Filmed my swing thousands of times
- Watched every YouTube breakdown
- Took tons of lessons

I wasn’t short on effort.
But I was drowning in information.
And I got five shots worse.

Everything changed when I finally worked with a coach who could see the real problem.

He didn’t give me ten things, or try to fix everything at once.

He said:
“Work on on your root swing flaw. Just this. Then we move on.”

That moment flipped a switch for me.

I started studying how people actually learn.
Things like language apps and flight simulators.

The training systems used by some of the best masters in the world.

The pattern in all of them was clear:
The best learning systems never ask you to be ten things at once.

They give you one system, based on your goal, your learning style, and the time you actually have.

You lock it in.
Then they progress you forward.

Most golfers do the opposite.

They jump from tip to tip, or practice in perfect conditions with no pressure.
Then they wonder why nothing holds up on the course.

Then pressure.
Then recall under stress.
