This is why random golf tips don’t work:

1️⃣ Every golfer is unique

You have a different build, mobility, and learning style.
Maybe you have a short torso and long arms, or your back limits your rotation.

What works for someone else may work against you.

2️⃣ Not addressing your one root swing flaw

Most golfers have four to five visible issues in their swing.
But there is always one root flaw driving everything else.

3️⃣ Order matters more than effort

The key is identifying the root flaw before working on anything else
Work on secondary flaws first and you’re treating symptoms, not the cause.

4️⃣ The right fix cleans up everything else. 

When you fix the root issue, you often clean up three other problems automatically.

The swing gets simpler, timing improves and compensations disappear.

5️⃣ There's no real feedback loop

Watch a tip, try it once, then move on.
Without clear feedback, you never know if you’re actually improving.

6️⃣ Too many swing thoughts

Random tips stack ideas on top of ideas.
Under pressure, your brain can’t perform with too much information.


Golf is the one game where you can actually practice and get worse.
