Want to start something but feel stuck?

This is what you need to do:

You've probably been there. 

Perhaps it's starting a business, or a new workout.

Maybe you have an idea you keep trying refine over and over in your head,
And you don't get started because you want to have the perfect plan first.

I did that for three years with Performance Golf. 

I had the idea, even told people about it. 
But I kept waiting. Kept convincing myself the plan wasn't ready.

Then at the end of January 2016, someone close to me passed away.

It hit me in a way I didn't expect. 

I remember thinking, life is going to go fast. 
This is only going to get harder. People I love are going to keep passing away. 

And here I am, sitting on an idea I've been talking about for three years.

In that moment, I got clear on something I hadn't been clear on before:

➡️ My why.

- Why would I work an extra 50 hours a week on top of my job? 
- Why would I put myself through the uncertainty? 
- Why would I risk failing publicly after telling everyone what I was going to do?

The answer became simple: 

I knew that life was going to go fast.

So I wanted to live it on my terms, and see if I could find a way to make an impact, free up time and spend it with the people that matter to me.

Not just work for the next thirty years.

On January 30th, 2016, I went from three years of talking to all in the next day.

Since then, there have been countless hard moments. 
Days where I questioned everything. 

But every time, I anchor back to that why. 

It's the thing that gets you started. And it's the thing that keeps you going when nothing else will.

If you've been thinking about starting something but feeling stuck, here's what helped me identify my why:

✅ Find thirty minutes this week
✅ Turn off your phone
✅ Close every browser

No distractions, just you and a blank page.

Ask yourself: why does this actually matter to me?

Your plan doesn't need to be perfect. 
Your vision will expand as you go. 

But "your why" needs to be clear. 
That's what turns years of thinking into action.
