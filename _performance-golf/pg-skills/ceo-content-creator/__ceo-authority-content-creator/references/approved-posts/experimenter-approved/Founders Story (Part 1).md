Most people never find out what they're capable of.

I realized this when I was four years old.

I remember crying, and my mom asked what was wrong. 

I said, "Life's going to go fast. Wouldn't it be a shame to not reach your potential?"

That's a strange thing for a kid to say. But I felt it. 

Not in an inspirational way, but in a sobering one.

I sensed that most people never find out what they're capable of. 

The problem isn't that they lack talent. 
Time just runs out before they push hard enough, long enough, or intelligently enough.

I was a late bloomer. I had a speech impediment. 

I was overlooked. Whenever I started something new, I was always the worst.

So I became obsessed with one question early on: how does someone go from overlooked to undeniable?

I studied stories:

→ Tom Brady was the seventh-string quarterback at Michigan. 
→ Aaron Rodgers was overlooked by college scouts and almost every NFL team. 
→ Michael Jordan got cut his sophomore year. 

How did they go from there to becoming the greatest?

I wanted to understand what actually creates that inflection point. 
And whether it could be figured out instead of left to chance.

Golf became my proving ground. So did my sales job. So did business.

When someone said something would take five years, I asked how it could be done in five months. 

I failed. I adjusted. I tried again.

That obsession followed me everywhere.

I worked 100-hour weeks for five years. Nights, weekends, early mornings. 
I kept my full-time job until my side business reached $29 million in annual sales.

I documented everything. 
What worked, what stalled, what created momentum, what turned effort into leverage.

That journey eventually became Performance Golf.

But this isn't just about building a company.

My team put together a short documentary on the first part of the story. 
The part before scale and before any results.

It's about chasing potential before time runs out.

If you, too, have ever felt like a slow starter, or if you've ever believed there was more in you but couldn't find the switch. 

This is where it started for me,
And where it could start for you.
