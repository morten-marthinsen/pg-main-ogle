"You're not allowed to do that."
That's what an executive told me when I pitched an idea years ago.

I wanted to hire someone internationally to support my sales role. 
I thought it would make me more efficient.

But I was shut down immediately.

That moment stuck with me.

I've always believed that when everyone goes one direction, real leverage is found by relentlessly testing paths to see if you can set a new standard. 

Not to be different, but to find if another level existed in terms of what was actually possible.

I couldn't do it there. So I made a little promise to myself.
One day, I'd do it my way.

Years later, when I started my own company, I ran the test.

I hired two guys in the US at $80,000 salaries. 
And one person in Asia at $15 an hour. 

Same expectations and accountability for all.

I put him through a three-month sales bootcamp. Full training and coverage.

He outperformed both of them combined.

That was just the beginning of the experiment.

We kept testing. US versus Asia. Over and over. The international team kept performing.

So I kept building a division of my company abroad for five and a half years.

Sales, video editors, customer service.
120 people across the business.

For all those years, I knew them as just names on a screen.

I trusted the process and measured the results...
But I had never met them.

Until last week.

I flew 9,813 miles and walked into a room expecting a meeting. 
But it felt like coming home.

I met people who bought their first homes because of this job.
People who told me this company changed the trajectory of their entire family.

These aren't just 'international hires'. These are lives.

I don't lower the bar because someone is 9,000 miles away. 

This team didn't just meet that bar, but shattered it.

We may have built Performance Golf to help golfers transform their games,
But transformation works both ways.

For the customer, the team, and the families building futures quietly.

If there's a lesson here, it's this:
Don't default to how things have always been done. 
Don't accept "you're not allowed to do that" as a ceiling. 

Test. Measure. Constantly look to set a new standard on what's possible.

To our entire team abroad:

Thank you for believing before you ever met me, and for proving that excellence has no borders.

One of the most meaningful experiences of my life.
And we're only getting started.
