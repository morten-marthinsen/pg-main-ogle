## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. The Core Thesis is VALIDATED
The Product Concept Doc's claim that "fear of chunking causes tension and deceleration" is **GOLD-STANDARD validated** by:
- PGA PhD research explicitly identifies "fear of club digging into ground" as psychological trigger
- 51% of yips-affected golfers report chipping yips specifically
- "Yips are exacerbated by anxiety but not necessarily caused by it" - meaning equipment CAN help

### 2. The Market is Larger Than Expected
- **20% of all golfers** face yips in some form (PhD research)
- Problem affects ALL skill levels - 7.7 handicap golfer: "I feel like a fraud"
- Average 23 years playing before onset = experienced golfers, not beginners

### 3. Cleveland is the Direct Competitor
Cleveland CBX4 marketing:
- "Chunk a little less" (WEAK claim)
- "Elite forgiveness"
- "If you're a digger, you'll love these"

**Opportunity:** ONE.1 can out-message with "Stop chunking. Period."

### 4. Ego Barrier is Real and Addressable
Multiple sources show golfers have stigma about "game improvement" equipment:
- "Swallowing my pride"
- "Put the ego aside"

**Solution:** Reframe as "smart equipment choice" not "game improvement crutch"

### 5. Emotional Language is Rich and Consistent
Across all sources, consistent patterns:
- **Shame:** "I feel like a fraud"
- **Frustration:** "Cost me dearly"
- **Fear:** "Fear of club digging"
- **Relief:** "I was amazed"
