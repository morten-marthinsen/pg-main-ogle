## 1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### Primary Competitor: Cleveland CBX4 ZipCore

**Positioning:**
- "Elite forgiveness"
- "Chunk a little less"
- All RTX6 face technology in cavity back form

**Strengths:**
- Established brand with OEM distribution
- Full technology stack (HydraZip, UtiliZip grooves)
- Multiple sole designs (V, S, C by loft)
- 12-14° bounce across all models = strong anti-dig

**Weaknesses:**
- "Chunk a little less" is a WEAK claim - implies you'll still chunk
- Positioned as "game improvement" which carries stigma
- No emotional connection in messaging
- Appearance concerns at address (larger head)

**Price Point:** ~$150-170 retail

### Secondary Competitors:

| Brand | Model | Positioning | Weakness |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Callaway | Mack Daddy CB | "For the everyday golfer" | Generic, no emotional hook |
| Ping | Glide 4.0 | Precision engineering | Too technical, not accessible |
| TaylorMade | Hi-Toe 3 | Full-face grooves | Tour-focused, intimidating |

### Competitive White Space:

**Cleveland says:** "Chunk a little less"
**Callaway says:** "For the everyday golfer"
**No one says:** "STOP chunking. Restore your confidence."

**Opportunity:** Own the EMOTIONAL outcome, not just the technical feature.

## 2. TARGET CUSTOMER PROFILE

### Primary Segment: The Frustrated Achiever

**Demographics:**
- Handicap: 7-18 (mid to high)
- Age: 35-60
- Experience: 10-30+ years playing
- Income: $75K+ (can afford premium equipment)
- Plays: 15-40 rounds/year

**Psychographics:**
- Takes golf seriously but isn't obsessive
- Has invested in lessons and equipment over the years
- Knows what good golf feels like, frustrated by inconsistency
- Competitive with self and playing partners
- Willing to try new approaches if logic is sound

**Emotional State:**
> "I feel like a fraud because I shouldn't be struggling with this still"

- Embarrassment about short game
- Tension standing over chip shots
- Mental replay of past chunks
- Avoiding certain shots/clubs

**Behavioral Patterns:**
- Researches equipment online (Reddit, YouTube, forums)
- Reads reviews before buying
- Tries tips/drills but reverts to old patterns
- Has considered "game improvement" equipment but ego resists

### Secondary Segment: The Equipment Seeker

**Profile:**
- Higher handicap (18-30+)
- Newer to game OR returning after break
- Less ego about equipment choices
- More price sensitive
- Actively seeking "easy to hit" solutions

**Language:**
> "More forgiving than my other clubs"
> "I may use an 8 iron from 15 yards out"

## 3. CUSTOMER JOURNEY MAP

### Stage 1: Awareness (Problem Recognition)
**Trigger Events:**
- Chunk a critical shot in competitive round
- Embarrassing miss in front of playing partners
- Scorecard analysis showing strokes lost around green
- Spouse/friend comments on short game

**Current Behavior:**
- Searches "how to stop chunking chip shots"
- Watches YouTube instructional videos
- Tries technique changes (weight forward, hands ahead)
- Blames self, not equipment

**ONE.1 Opportunity:**
Interrupt the "blame yourself" cycle with: "What if it's not your technique?"

### Stage 2: Consideration (Solution Research)
**Questions Asked:**
- "Is there a wedge that's easier to hit?"
- "Do forgiving wedges sacrifice spin?"
- "Will my friends judge me for using a 'game improvement' wedge?"
- "Does this actually work or is it marketing?"

**Current Behavior:**
- Reads reviews on Plugged In Golf, GolfWRX, Reddit
- Watches comparison videos
- Considers Cleveland CBX, Callaway CB, chippers
- Hesitates due to ego concerns

**ONE.1 Opportunity:**
Address ego directly: "The smartest players know equipment matters."
Provide social proof from converted skeptics.

### Stage 3: Decision (Purchase)
**Decision Factors:**
1. Will it actually help?
2. What do other people like me say?
3. Risk mitigation (return policy, guarantee)
4. Price vs. perceived value

**Barriers:**
- "I've tried other things that didn't work"
- "Maybe I just need more practice"
- "What if I'm beyond help?"

**ONE.1 Opportunity:**
Strong guarantee, testimonial-heavy landing page, specific use case examples.

### Stage 4: Post-Purchase (Experience)
**Success Metrics:**
- First time ball "pops out" of bad lie
- Round without a chunk from greenside
- Playing partner comments on improvement
- Confidence increase = enjoyment increase

**Advocacy Triggers:**
- Dramatic before/after experience
- Story worth telling ("I didn't believe it would work...")
- Visible to playing partners who ask questions
