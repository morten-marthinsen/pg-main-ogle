## 2. OBJECTION LANGUAGE

### Objection 1: "It's a technique problem"
**Frequency:** Very common

**How customers express it:**
- "Weight on front foot should fix it"
- "I just need more practice"
- "Phil's video will help"
- "It's mental, not equipment"

**Counter-narrative:**
"Yes, technique matters. But when your equipment DIGS, perfect technique doesn't help. The ONE.1 eliminates the dig so your technique can work."

### Objection 2: "Game improvement wedges are for hackers"
**Frequency:** Common among 10-15 handicaps

**How customers express it:**
- "I had to put the ego aside"
- "Swallowing my pride"
- "If these improve my short game I won't have any issues keeping them"

**Counter-narrative:**
"Smart golfers choose equipment that works. Tour players have perfect technique - you don't need equipment designed for them."

### Objection 3: "I've tried everything"
**Frequency:** Common among frustrated golfers

**How customers express it:**
- "Watched the videos"
- "Tried the drills"
- "Nothing works for me"

**Counter-narrative:**
"Most solutions address technique. The ONE.1 addresses the equipment gap that technique can't fix."

### Objection 4: "Will I lose spin?"
**Frequency:** Common concern

**How customers express it:**
- "Do forgiving wedges sacrifice spin?"
- "I need to check the ball"

**Counter-narrative:**
Cleveland validated this already: "You don't have to give up spin for forgiveness." ONE.1 can make same claim with data.

## 3. DECISION DRIVERS

### What makes golfers BUY:

**Rational Drivers:**
1. Clear explanation of WHY it works (sole design)
2. Comparison to current wedge/competitor
3. Risk mitigation (guarantee, return policy)
4. Price competitive with alternatives

**Emotional Drivers:**
1. Belief that this time it will actually work
2. Validation that problem isn't their fault
3. Social proof from similar golfers
4. Permission to try "game improvement"

**Trust Signals:**
1. Real golfer testimonials (not pros)
2. Long-term testing mentions ("after a month...")
3. Skeptic → believer narratives
4. Specific shot descriptions (not generic praise)

## 4. LANGUAGE DO's AND DON'Ts

### DO Use:
- "Pop out" / "pops up"
- "Accelerates through"
- "Anti-dig"
- "Confidence"
- "Stop chunking" (not "chunk less")
- "Any lie"
- "Amazed"
- "Finally"

### DON'T Use:
- "Game improvement" (stigma)
- "Easy" (sounds like it's for beginners)
- "Forgiving" alone (too generic)
- "Hack-proof" (insulting)
- "Beginner" or "high handicap"
- "Miracle" (skepticism trigger)

### Tone:
- Direct, confident, not desperate
- Validation, not condescension
- Specific, not vague
- Empathetic but not pitying

Based on VOC patterns, ideal testimonials should follow this structure:

> "I was skeptical. I've tried [technique/drills/equipment] before with no luck. But after [time period] with the ONE.1, I'm a believer. [Specific shot example]. I [emotional outcome]."

**Example:**

Last week I hit it from deep rough and it just popped out - landed soft, checked up. My playing partner asked what I was using."

**Example:**

**Example:**
