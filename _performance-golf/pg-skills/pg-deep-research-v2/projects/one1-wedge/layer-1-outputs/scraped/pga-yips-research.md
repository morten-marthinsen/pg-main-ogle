# PGA Research: Unknotting the Chipping Yips
**Source:** https://www.pga.info/discover/latest/news/unknotting-chipping-yips/
**Author:** Gordon Morrison PhD (Irish PGA Professional, Masters in Sports Psychology, PhD in Motor Control/Psychology)
**Date:** 21st July 2025
**Scrape Date:** 2026-01-16

## Key Research Findings

### Study 1: Prevalence Survey (417 golfers self-identified as yips-affected)
- **67%** experienced putting yips (35% putting only)
- **51%** reported chipping yips (19% chipping only)
- **32%** experienced BOTH putting and chipping yips
- **12%** reported full swing yips
- **76%** reported yips occurred just before striking the ball
- **Average 23 years** of playing before problem emerged

### What Triggers Chipping Yips (from interviews with 10 golfers)

**Environmental/Task Related:**
- Sudden start to problem (5/10)
- Gradual start (5/10)
- Lie of ball makes problem worse (4/10)
- Yips most often occurs during competition (7/10)

**Motor Behavior:**
- Jerking during movement (6/10)
- Club and body changing height during stroke (5/10)
- Acceleration and deceleration problems (2/10)
- Twisting club at impact (2/10)

**Psychological Aspects:**
- Negative thoughts/loss of concentration (9/10)
- Low confidence on chip shots (6/10)
- Experiencing somatic anxiety (4/10)
- Experiencing cognitive anxiety (3/10)
- **Fear of club digging into ground** (2/10)
- Cause has psychological nature (5/10)

**Coping Attempts:**
- Avoiding chipping (5/10)
- Using a chipper (2/10)
- Some success chipping with eyes closed (3/10)

### Key Quote from Researcher
> "One of my conclusions here was that the yips are exacerbated by anxiety but not necessarily caused by it."

### Study 2: 3D Movement Analysis
- Yips-affected golfers showed **greater variability in hand and club movements** during downswing
- Particularly in y-axis (forward thrust)
- Reduced accuracy, poorer strike quality, less consistent movement patterns
- Erratic movement with sudden changes in direction vs. smooth swings in unaffected golfers

### Study 3: Dual-Tasking Intervention Results
- 7 out of 9 participants showed significant improvements
- Smoother movement patterns and improved ball striking
- Technique: Humming or speaking during chip shots to reduce conscious overthinking

### Study 4: Pre-Shot Routine Intervention (6 months)
Results:
- Reduced frequency and severity of yips
- Improved movement quality and shot accuracy
- Enhanced concentration, reduced cognitive anxiety, increased confidence

### Equipment Observations from Research
> "Unlike professional musicians, where only 1% experience similar movement issues, around 20% of golfers face the yips in some form."
