## ***Pull vs. Push: Science***

## **Executive Summary**

Research evidence supports that **pulling motions are biomechanically superior to pushing motions** in golf, including putting. When weight is positioned **forward/on the face** of a putter (forward CG), it creates conditions that favor a pulling motion and naturally improve stability and consistency. Here's the comprehensive scientific evidence:

---

## **1\. FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS: WHY PULLING IS SUPERIOR TO PUSHING**

### **The Truck and Trailer Principle (Core Physics Concept)**

From “Rotary Swing” Golf's research on golf physics:

**Pushing (Rear CG) Problems:**

* When you push an object, it tends to rotate around its own center of gravity  
* The pushed object tries to move away from the line of force  
* Example: Backing up a trailer \- extremely difficult to control because pushing causes rotation  
* In a putter: When the CG is behind the shaft axis, pushing forces cause the face to want to rotate open

**Pulling (Forward CG) Advantages:**

* When you pull an object, it aligns itself with the center of gravity of the pulling force  
* Pulled objects naturally straighten and track straight  
* Example: A truck pulling a trailer down the highway \- the trailer stays perfectly straight with no effort  
* In a putter: When the CG is forward/on the face, pulling forces keep the face stable and square

---

## **2\. PUTTER-SPECIFIC PHYSICS: FORWARD CG vs REAR CG**

### **How Center of Gravity (CG) Position Affects Torque**

From *Dave Tutelman* on extensive putter design research:

**Forward CG Benefits:**

1. **Reduces Torque**: When force is applied in front of the CG, it creates a stable clubface during the stroke  
2. **Face Stability**: Striking the ball directly in front of the CG prevents putter rotation  
3. **Self-Squaring**: Forward CG naturally squares the face at impact

**Rear CG Problems:**

1. **Increases Torque**: When force is applied with CG behind the shaft, it creates rotational forces  
2. **Face Rotation**: The putter wants to open during the stroke  
3. **Requires Compensation**: Golfer must apply counter-torque with hands to keep face square

### **The Zero-Torque Revolution**

Modern putter companies (LAB Golf, Axis1) have revolutionized putting with forward CG placement:

**LAB Golf DF3:**

* Places CG to eliminate torque completely  
* "Lie Angle Balance" technology \- shaft axis crosses directly through CG  
* Result: Face stays square regardless of hand action

**Axis1 Putters:**

* Patents on placing CG on the center of the striking face and in-line with shaft axis  
* Eliminates both static and dynamic torques

**TaylorMade Spider FCG (Forward CG):**

* Forward CG (closer to clubface) increases toe-hang, which is ideal for golfers that push putts  
* Forward CG reduces face opening tendency  
* Research shows: When it's a back CG with a moderate forward press putting style, the face tends to open to the right in reaction to the CG

---

## **3\. BIOMECHANICS: BIG MUSCLES & PULLING MOTIONS**

### **Why "Big Muscles" Work Better with Pulling**

From biomechanics research and golf instruction:

**The Big Muscle Principle:**

* Modern putting instruction emphasizes using shoulders, back (lats), and core \- not hands/wrists  
* These large muscles work best with pulling motions, not pushing  
* Large muscles produce smoother, more consistent motion with less twitchy movement

**The Pendulum Stroke & Pulling:**

* Ideal putting stroke is a "rocking of the shoulders" (pendulum motion)  
* This creates a pulling action from the lead shoulder  
* Large muscles \= more stable, less affected by pressure/adrenaline  
* Results in repeatable, consistent stroke

---

## **4\. MUSCLE ACTIVATION STUDIES: PULLING VS PUSHING**

### **Scientific Evidence from Human Biomechanics Research**

From NIH/PMC study "Human Body Mechanics of Pushing and Pulling":

**Pulling Advantages (Task-Specific):**

1. **Lower perceived exertion** (RPE) in some pulling tasks  
2. **Higher muscle activation** for pulling \= more direct force application  
3. **Better control** when pulling toward center of mass

**Pushing Disadvantages:**

* Creates "off-axis forces" that must be stabilized  
* Requires more complex muscle coordination  
* Less natural alignment with body mechanics

**Golf-Specific Application:**

* The putting stroke is a subtle, precision movement  
* Pulling engages larger, more stable muscle groups (shoulders, lats)  
* Pushing engages smaller, twitchier muscles (wrists, forearms)  
* Result: Pulling is more consistent and repeatable under pressure

---

## **5\. FORWARD CG PUTTERS: PRACTICAL BENEFITS**

### **How Forward Weight Distribution Helps Golfers**

**1\. Minimal Forward Shaft Lean Required:**

* Rear CG putters require forward press to prevent face opening  
* Forward CG putters work with neutral or minimal forward press  
* **Benefit**: Simpler, more consistent setup

**2\. Natural Face Squaring:**

* Forward CG creates restoring force if face becomes unsquare  
* LAB Golf calls this "Directed Force" technology  
* **Benefit**: Face returns to square automatically

**3\. Reduced Hand Manipulation:**

* Forward CG eliminates need to counter-torque with hands  
* Golfer can focus purely on stroke path, not face control  
* **Benefit**: More putts holed from 10 feet and in

**4\. Better with "Pulling" Stroke:**

* Forward CG putters naturally favor a pulling motion  
* Engages shoulders and lats (big muscles)  
* **Benefit**: More consistent stroke, especially under pressure

**5\. Forgiveness on Mis-Hits:**

* Forward CG reduces twisting even on off-center strikes  
* Ball maintains line and speed  
* **Benefit**: Fewer 3-putts from longer distances

| **Face Stability** | Self-squares | Requires hand compensation |
| **Muscle Usage** | Large muscles (shoulders, lats) | Small muscles (wrists, hands) |
| **Consistency** | High (physics-based stability) | Lower (requires perfect timing) |

| **Under Pressure** | More stable (big muscles) | Less stable (small muscles) |
| **Physics Advantage** | Pulling alignment | Fighting natural rotation |

## **7\. WHY FORWARD CG \= PULLING MOTION**

### **The Mechanical Connection**

**When CG is Forward/On Face:**

1. The golfer's force is applied behind the CG  
2. This creates a pulling relationship (CG being pulled forward)  
3. Physics: Pulled objects track straight and stable  
4. The stroke becomes a pulling motion of the lead shoulder

**When CG is Rear/Back:**

1. The golfer's force is applied in front of the CG  
2. This creates a pushing relationship (CG being pushed)  
3. Physics: Pushed objects rotate and become unstable  
4. The stroke requires pushing with trail hand to square face

---

## **8\. SCIENTIFIC EVIDENCE SUMMARY**

### **Pulling is Superior Because:**

1. **Physics**: Pulling keeps objects aligned and tracking straight (truck/trailer principle)  
2. **Torque**: Forward CG eliminates face-opening torque  
3. **Muscles**: Pulling engages large, stable muscles (shoulders, lats, core)  
4. **Consistency**: Large muscles are less affected by pressure and adrenaline  
5. **Simplicity**: No need to counter-torque with hands  
6. **Natural**: Body mechanics favor pulling over pushing for precision tasks

### **Forward CG Putters are Better Because:**

1. **Torque Reduction**: Minimal to zero torque around shaft axis  
2. **Face Stability**: Self-squares the face without hand manipulation  
3. **Pulling Motion**: Naturally favors biomechanically superior pulling action  
4. **Big Muscles**: Forces use of shoulders/lats instead of wrists/hands  
5. **Forgiveness**: Reduces twisting on off-center strikes  
6. **Consistency**: Physics-based stability rather than feel-based timing

## ***Forward CG: Benefits & Drawbacks***

## **MAIN BENEFITS OF FORWARD CG PUTTERS**

### **1\. Better Roll Quality**

* Ball gets into a forward roll faster (less skid/bounce)  
* Creates immediate topspin off the face  
* More predictable ball behavior on various green speeds

### **2\. Reduced Torque / Increased Stability**

* Less twisting on off-center hits  
* Face stays more square through impact  
* Reduces the severity of mishits

### **3\. Improved Feel & Feedback**

* More solid, blade-like feel even in mallet designs  
* Better distance control through consistent feedback  
* Some golfers report more "connected" sensation

### **4\. Biomechanical Advantage (Pulling Motion)**

* Promotes pulling the putter back rather than pushing  
* Engages larger, more stable muscle groups (shoulders, back)  
* More consistent face angle at impact  
* Reduces hand/wrist manipulation

### **5\. Faster Adaptation to Different Greens**

* Consistent roll characteristics across various green speeds  
* Less adjustment needed between practice green and course

---

## **MAIN DRAWBACKS OF FORWARD CG PUTTERS**

### **1\. Adjustment Period / Learning Curve**

* Feels significantly different from traditional putters  
* Requires relearning distance control (especially on lag putts)  
* Some golfers struggle to adapt to the new sensation  
* "Too light" or "too heavy" feeling depending on design

### **2\. Different Sound/Feel at Impact**

* Often softer, muted sound (less satisfying "click" for some)  
* Can feel "dead" or "mushy" to golfers used to traditional putters  
* Less auditory feedback to gauge strike quality

### **3\. Potential for "Too Much" Roll**

* Ball can roll out farther than expected on fast greens  
* Requires recalibration of distance perception  
* Downhill putts can be more challenging initially

### **4\. Limited Availability & Higher Cost**

* Fewer models available compared to traditional putters  
* Often premium pricing ($300-$500+)  
* Limited customization options in some lines

### **5\. Not for Everyone's Stroke Type**

* May not suit golfers with very wristy/hands-dominant strokes  
* Some players with strong arc strokes prefer traditional heel-toe weighting  
* Face-balanced forward CG putters don't suit all stroke paths

### **6\. Aesthetic/Visual Concerns**

* Some designs look unconventional or "ugly" to traditionalists  
* Offset/onset shaft positions can be visually distracting at address  
* May not appeal to golfers who want classic blade appearance

### **7\. Less Forgiveness on Toe/Heel Hits (Compared to Extreme MOI Mallets)**

* While forward CG reduces twisting, it doesn't maximize perimeter weighting  
* High-MOI mallets with rear weighting may be more forgiving on severe mishits  
* Trade-off: feel/roll quality vs. maximum forgiveness

## ***MOI (moment of inertia)***

**Moment of Inertia \= Resistance to Twisting (at impact)**

* **High MOI** \= Putter head is **hard to twist** when you miss the sweet spot  
* **Low MOI** \= Putter head **twists easily** on off-center hits

There are **TWO main MOI measurements** for putters:

### **1\. VERTICAL AXIS MOI (Most Common)**

* **What it measures:** Heel-to-toe stability (left-right twisting)  
* **Why it matters:** Keeps face square on off-center hits  
* **This is what manufacturers advertise**

### **2\. HORIZONTAL AXIS MOI (Less Discussed) ←**Rare but has been marketed ([https://argolf.com/blogs/tips-and-instructions/how-does-moi-affect-a-putter-s-forgiveness](https://argolf.com/blogs/tips-and-instructions/how-does-moi-affect-a-putter-s-forgiveness))

* **What it measures:** Front-to-back stability (high-low hits)  
* **Why it matters:** Consistent ball speed on strikes above/below center  
* **Rarely mentioned in marketing**

## ***MOI (moment of inertia) vs. Forward CG***

***\* MOI and Forward CG in putters have an inverse relationship: moving CG forward (toward the face) typically reduces MOI, as mass concentrates closer to the rotation axis, lowering resistance to twisting.***

| ASPECT | FORWARD CG | HIGH MOI |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **What it controls** | Face ROTATION **during stroke** | Face TWIST **on mishits** |
| **When it matters** | Throughout the entire stroke motion | At moment of impact only |
| **Problem it solves** | Face opening/closing (torque) | Face deflection on toe/heel hits |
| **Physics principle** | Reduces rotational torque | Resists twisting force |
| **Axis of control** | Vertical axis (face open/closed) | Vertical axis (heel-toe stability) |
| **Biomechanical benefit** | Promotes pulling motion | N/A \- purely physics |
| **Where weight goes** | Toward the face | To perimeter (heel/toe) |
| **Type of stability** | DYNAMIC (during motion) | STATIC (at impact)  |

## ***Face Down Face Balanced***

## **THE 4 MAIN PUTTER BALANCE TYPES**

### **1\. FACE-BALANCED**

**What It Is:**

* When you balance the putter on your finger (under the shaft), the face points straight up to the sky  
* Center of Gravity (CG) is mostly below or in line with the shaft axis

**Characteristics:**

* Face stays square throughout the stroke  
* Minimal face rotation during swing  
* Works best with **straight-back, straight-through** putting strokes

**Common Designs:**

* Center-shafted mallets  
* Some heel-shafted mallets with heavy rear weighting  
* High-MOI spider-style putters

**Popular Examples:**

* TaylorMade Spider (most models)  
* Odyssey White Hot (many mallets)  
* Ping Fetch models  
* **Most modern mallet putters**

### **2\. TOE-HANG (Toe-Down Balanced)**

**What It Is:**

* When balanced on your finger, the toe hangs down toward the ground

**Characteristics:**

* Face wants to rotate open on backswing, closed on follow-through  
* Natural arc to the putting stroke  
* Requires more face rotation through impact

**Common Designs:**

* Traditional blade putters with heel-mounted shafts  
* Anser-style putters  
* Classic offset designs

**Popular Examples:**

* Scotty Cameron Newport  
* Ping Anser  
* TaylorMade TP Collection (blades)  
* **Most traditional blade putters**

### **3\. SLIGHT TOE-HANG (Moderate Balance)**

**What It Is:**

* When balanced, toe hangs down  
* Middle ground between face-balanced and full toe-hang

**Characteristics:**

* Works with **slight arc strokes**  
* Some natural face rotation but not extreme  
* Versatile for different stroke types

**Common Designs:**

* Modified Anser designs  
* Some mid-mallets  
* Plumber's neck putters with moderate offset

**Popular Examples:**

* Odyssey Stroke Lab (some models)  
* Cleveland Huntington Beach  
* Ping Vault 2.0 (some models)

---

### **4\. TOE-UP / FACE-DOWN ←**(RARE/SPECIALIZED)

**What It Is:**

* When balanced on your finger, the toe points UP (face points DOWN toward the ground)  
* Opposite of traditional toe-hang  
* CG is positioned significantly behind the shaft axis

**Why It Exists:**

* Designed for **extreme arc strokes**  
* Players who have very strong hand/wrist rotation  
* Theoretical benefit: Face naturally wants to close aggressively
