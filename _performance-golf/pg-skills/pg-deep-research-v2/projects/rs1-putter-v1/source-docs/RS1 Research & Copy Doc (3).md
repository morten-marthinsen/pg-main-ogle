| Popularity | Material | Pros | Cons |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |

| Less common | Carbon steel | Softer feel | Likely lower MOI; rust potential |

Putter **SOLE (bottom)**:

| Popularity | Material | Pros | Cons |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Most common | Stainless Steel | Durable glide; consistent | Limits MOI compared to multi-material given weighting (if this sole is not paired with the aluminum body) |
| Relatively common recently | Tungsten (*adjustable weights*) | Places mass in a good spot for MOI; customization | Expensive; complexity; can lose weights |
| Less common | Brass / Copper | Softer metal reduces sole bounce for more consistent roll | Dents or softens; higher maintenance |

Putter **SHAFT**:

| Popularity | Material | Pros | Cons |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Most common (80%+) | Steel | Feel familiarity; durability; cheaper; doesn’t dampen feel | Feel preference; heavier |
| Growing in popularity | Graphite (Carbon) | Lighter; vibration dampening | Expensive; durability; may feel too light |
| Emerging (under 5%) | Multi-material (steel \+ graphite) | Best of both worlds \- feel, weight, stability | May be most expensive option; unproven; feel may be different |

→ STEEL shafts do come up as having less torque/twisting than GRAPHITE shafts (con for carbon/graphite).

  ## **THE THREE KEY BENEFITS FOR YOUR PUTTING**

  ### **1\. STRAIGHTER STARTING LINE**

**What happens:** Thicker shaft \= less face rotation during stroke

**Lab data testing:**

* Stability Shaft: More consistent starting direction with smaller standard deviation  
* Steel shaft: More variation in face angle at impact

**In your game:** More putts start on your intended line, meaning more makes inside 10 feet.

---

### **2\. BETTER DISTANCE CONTROL**

**What happens:** Less shaft twist \= more consistent energy transfer

**The physics:** When the shaft twists, energy is lost in the deformation. A stiffer shaft means:

* More energy goes into the ball  
* Less variation on mishits  
* Better speed consistency

**In your game:** Fewer three-putts because your distance control is tighter.

---

### **3\. IMPROVED FEEL ON OFF-CENTER HITS**

**What happens:** Thicker shaft dampens the "bad feedback" from mishits

**The reality:** Nobody hits every putt on the sweet spot. A thicker, stiffer shaft:

* Reduces face twisting on toe/heel hits  
* Maintains ball speed better across the face  
* Gives you more forgiveness

**In your game:** Mishits still go a reasonable distance and stay closer to your line.

---

## **THE WEIGHT EFFICIENCY BONUS**

Stiffness increases faster than weight.

The engineering:

* Torsional stiffness increases with diameter⁴  
* Weight only increases with diameter² (roughly)

**What this means:** You can get a MUCH stiffer shaft without adding proportional weight. This is why carbon fiber putter shafts work so well \- larger diameter for massive stiffness gains, but lighter than steel.

## **ONSET vs OFFSET Putter Shafts: Complete Comparison**

### **What Are They?**

**Offset Putters**: The shaft is positioned ahead of (in front of) the putter face. The offset can be "full shaft" (equal to shaft width) or "half shaft" (half the shaft width). This is the most common design in putters.

**Onset Putters**: The shaft enters the head behind the putter face, meaning the face is ahead of (closer to the target than) the leading edge of the shaft. These are relatively rare and considered unconventional.

---

## **PROS AND CONS**

### **OFFSET PUTTERS**

**Pros:**

* **Promotes forward hands at impact** \- Encourages hands to stay slightly ahead of the ball through the stroke  
* **Reduces wrist breakdown** \- Helps maintain firmer wrists and prevents excessive hand action  
* **More forgiving** \- The design makes it easier to maintain consistency  
* **Promotes upward strike** \- Helps get the ball rolling smoothly with slight loft  
* **Most common design** \- Easy to find and widely available

**Cons:**

* **Affects aim to the left** \- The more offset, the more it causes right-handed golfers to aim left.  
* **Can obscure vision** \- The shaft can block your view of the ball and alignment  
* **Not ideal for straight-back-straight-through strokes** \- Better for slight arc putters  
* **May not suit dominant eye positioning** \- Can interfere with eye dominance alignment

### **ONSET PUTTERS**

**Pros:**

* **Unobstructed vision** \- You can see the entire top edge of the putter blade without the shaft blocking your view  
* **Improved alignment for some golfers** \- Most people aim further right with onset putters, which can correct leftward aiming tendencies  
* **Better for dominant eye positioning** \- Particularly good for right-handed golfers with a dominant left eye ←May only be 20%-30% generally  
* **Cleaner visual appearance** \- Simplifies the look at address  
* **Prevents excessive forward shaft lean** \- Good for golfers who don't want hands ahead

**Cons:**

* **Unconventional feel** \- Takes adjustment if you're used to traditional designs  
* **Less forgiving** \- Doesn't promote the same hand-forward position that helps consistency  
* **May encourage wrist breakdown** \- Without the offset structure, easier to get "handsy"
