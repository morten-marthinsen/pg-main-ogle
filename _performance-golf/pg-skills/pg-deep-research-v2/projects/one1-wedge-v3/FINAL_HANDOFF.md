## 3.8 RISK FACTORS

### Risk 1: Chipper Comparison Despite Positioning
**Description:** Despite "not a chipper" positioning, media and customers may still categorize ONE.1 with chippers, transferring stigma.
**Mitigation:** Lead with wedge credentials. Ensure all visual marketing shows standard wedge aesthetics. Create comparison content showing differences from true chippers.

### Risk 2: "Just Another Forgiving Wedge" Perception
**Description:** Market saturation with forgiveness claims may cause ONE.1 to blend in with CBX4, Callaway CB, etc.
**Mitigation:** Lead with mental game/confidence positioning - unique in market. Emphasize AUTOMATIC ground contact vs. forgiveness on mishits (different mechanism, different benefit).

### Risk 3: Price Sensitivity in Target Segment
**Description:** High handicappers often price-conscious; may resist premium pricing for "game improvement" equipment.
**Mitigation:** Strong guarantee reduces perceived risk. ROI messaging (cost per stroke saved). Payment plans. Value comparison to lessons.

### Risk 4: Influencer/Review Credibility
**Description:** Low-handicap reviewers may not experience the benefits, leading to lukewarm reviews.
**Mitigation:** Target mid-to-high handicap reviewers specifically. Provide longer test periods. Share testimonial data to support reviews. Accept that tour-focused publications may not be target audience.
