# Micro-Skill 2.6-H: Now-After Grid

**Layer:** 2 - Intelligence
**Purpose:** Dimensionalize the customer transformation across Have/Experience/Status/Feeling to create vivid before/after contrast
**Dependency:** 2.2-E WEB Analysis complete, Layer 1 extraction complete
**Parallel:** Can run simultaneously with 2.6 pattern detection skills
**Version:** 1.0
**PRD Reference:** [[RESEARCH-PRD#Section 4.2]]
**E5 Reference:** Todd Brown's Prospect Examination - Tool 3: Now and After Grid

---

## CRITICAL: The Grid Creates Copy That SHOWS, Not Tells

The Now and After Grid transforms abstract benefits into CONCRETE, VIVID pictures of transformation.

Your job: Show prospects you can bring them from NOW to AFTER with a better solution—more effectively, efficiently, faster, easier, with less work than anything else.

**Without this grid:** Copy says "you'll be more confident"
**With this grid:** Copy says "you'll step up to that chip shot, visualize exactly where you want it to land, and execute without a single tremor in your hands"

---

## The Four Grid Categories

```
         NOW STATE                    AFTER STATE
    ┌─────────────────┐          ┌─────────────────┐
    │     HAVE        │          │     HAVE        │
    │ (What negatives │   ───►   │ (What positives │
    │  do they have?) │          │  will they have)│
    ├─────────────────┤          ├─────────────────┤
    │   EXPERIENCE    │          │   EXPERIENCE    │
    │ (What is daily  │   ───►   │ (What will daily│
    │  life like?)    │          │  life look like)│
    ├─────────────────┤          ├─────────────────┤
    │    STATUS       │          │    STATUS       │
    │ (How do they/   │   ───►   │ (How will they/ │
    │  others view?)  │          │  others view?)  │
    ├─────────────────┤          ├─────────────────┤
    │    FEELING      │          │    FEELING      │
    │ (What emotions  │   ───►   │ (What emotions  │
    │  now?)          │          │  after?)        │
    └─────────────────┘          └─────────────────┘
```

## Grid Building Process

### Step 1: Build the HAVE Category

**NOW - What negatives do they currently have?**
```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Pain quotes (direct negatives)
  - Solutions tried quotes (what they've accumulated that doesn't work)
  - Root cause quotes (the problems they have)

question_prompts:
  - What tangible problems do they have?
  - What negative conditions exist?
  - What's wrong with their current situation?
  - What do they have too much or too little of?
```

**AFTER - What positives will they have?**
```yaml
construction_approach:
  - Direct inverse of NOW items
  - Dimensionalize: What does it LOOK like to have this?
  - Use hope quotes for verbatim language

question_prompts:
  - What tangible positives will they have?
  - What will they possess that they don't now?
  - What will they have that solves the NOW problems?
```

### Step 2: Build the EXPERIENCE Category

**NOW - What does daily experience look like?**
```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Pain quotes describing moments
  - Emotional quotes about specific situations
  - Behavioral descriptions

question_prompts:
  - What does a typical golf round feel like?
  - What moments are worst?
  - What does the actual experience look like in detail?
  - Walk through a scenario step by step
```

**AFTER - What will daily experience look like?**
```yaml
construction_approach:
  - Same scenarios, transformed
  - Vivid, sensory detail
  - Show the movie of the new experience

question_prompts:
  - What does the same scenario look like after transformation?
  - What do they see, feel, hear in the new state?
  - How does the rhythm of their day/round change?
```

### Step 3: Build the STATUS Category

**NOW - How do they/others view them?**
```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Identity statements in quotes
  - Social comparison quotes
  - Reputation/label quotes

question_prompts:
  - How do they see themselves relative to this problem?
  - How do others perceive them?
  - What's their reputation?
  - What labels have they been given?
```

**AFTER - How will they/others view them?**
```yaml
construction_approach:
  - New identity statements
  - New social standing
  - New reputation

question_prompts:
  - How will they see themselves after transformation?
  - How will others perceive them?
  - What new reputation will they have?
  - What will they be known for?
```

### Step 4: Build the FEELING Category

**NOW - What emotions do they feel?**
```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Direct from 2.2-E WEB Analysis current emotional state
  - Emotion words in pain quotes

list_with_intensity:
  - Primary emotions (HIGH)
  - Secondary emotions (MEDIUM)
  - Underlying emotions (LOW but present)
```

**AFTER - What emotions will they feel?**
```yaml
construction_approach:
  - Direct from 2.2-E desired emotional state
  - Dimensionalize each emotion
  - Show what the emotion looks like in practice

format:
  - Emotion name
  - What it looks like (dimensionalized)
  - Contrast to NOW emotion
```

---

## Dimensionalization Guidelines

**DON'T just state the benefit:**
- "You'll be more confident"
- "You'll save strokes"
- "You'll feel better"

**DO paint the picture:**
- "That quiet inner knowing when you step up to a chip - no tremor in your hands, no tightness in your grip - just clear visualization and clean execution"
- "Those 'routine pars' everyone else seems to save? Now you save them too. Two, three times a round. Watch what happens to your handicap."
- "Walking off the green with a smile instead of a grimace. Your buddies notice. They stop making the chunk jokes. New respect."

**Dimensionalization Test:**
Can the reader SEE it in their mind? Can they FEEL it? If yes, it's dimensionalized. If no, go deeper.

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All four categories complete (Have, Experience, Status, Feeling)
- [ ] NOW states backed by verbatim evidence
- [ ] AFTER states fully dimensionalized (not just stated)
- [ ] Each AFTER item explicitly contrasts with NOW item
- [ ] Experience category tells a visual "movie"
- [ ] Status category addresses both self-view and others' view
- [ ] Feeling category uses specific emotions, not generic
- [ ] Transformation summary is one sentence that captures the arc
- [ ] Copy applications provided (headlines, story beats, contrast language)

---

## Failure Protocol

```
IF NOW state too shallow:
  - Return to pain quotes for more specific moments
  - Ask "what does this actually look like?"
  - Seek scenario-based descriptions

IF AFTER state not dimensionalized:
  - Don't accept simple benefit statements
  - Force sensory detail: "What do they see? Feel? Hear?"
  - Write the movie scene, not the summary

IF contrast unclear:
  - Explicitly state FROM → TO for each item
  - Ensure they're direct inverses
  - Check that language creates tension between states
```

## Notes

- The Now-After Grid is one of the most powerful copy creation tools
- EXPERIENCE is often the most powerful category - it creates empathy
- STATUS addresses identity, which is deeply motivating
- Use this grid to "show don't tell" in all copy
- Return to this grid when writing any marketing copy for this product
- The grid should make the reader feel SEEN (NOW) and HOPEFUL (AFTER)
