---

## CRITICAL: Understanding Offers Enables Superior Positioning

An offer is: **What does the prospect get, and what do they need to give to get it?**

We examine competitor offers to create a **SIN offer** - **Superior, Irresistible, No-brainer**.

To create a superior offer, you MUST know what competitors are offering. This skill maps:
- Deliverables (what they get)
- Features (facts about the product)
- Price and Terms (what they pay)
- Bonuses and Premiums (extras included)
- Guarantee / Risk Reversal (how risk is reduced)

## Analysis Process

### Step 1: Identify Competitor Offers to Analyze

From 2.4-A competitor list, identify products with direct offers (not just awareness marketing).

```yaml
offer_sources:
  - Brand product pages (retail flow)
  - Direct-to-consumer sites
  - Amazon listings
  - Retailer product pages (Golf Galaxy, PGA Superstore)
  - Direct response marketing (infomercials, long-form funnels)
```

### Step 2: Extract Deliverables

What does the customer actually receive?

```yaml
deliverables_to_document:
  primary: "Core product delivered"
  included: "Items bundled with purchase"
  customization: "Options available (loft, shaft, grind, etc.)"
```

### Step 3: Extract Features

Facts about the product that customers would care about.

```yaml
feature_categories:
  technical: "Specs, technology, construction"
  presentation: "How features are positioned"
  differentiating: "Features highlighted as unique"
```

### Step 4: Extract Price and Terms

What do they pay and how?

```yaml
price_elements:
  retail_price: "Standard listed price"
  sale_price: "Promotional pricing if applicable"
  price_positioning: "Premium, mid-tier, value?"

terms_elements:
  payment_options: "Full, financing, split-pay?"
  shipping: "Free? Threshold? Speed?"
```

### Step 5: Extract Bonuses and Premiums

What extras do they include?

```yaml
bonus_categories:
  included_bonuses: "Always included with purchase"
  promotional_bonuses: "Limited-time or conditional"
  value_adds: "Access, content, community"
```

### Step 6: Extract Guarantee / Risk Reversal

How do they reduce purchase risk?

```yaml
guarantee_analysis:
  guarantee_type: "Money-back? Defects only? Performance?"
  return_policy: "Days? Conditions? Used/unused?"
  risk_reversal_strength: "STRONG / MEDIUM / WEAK"

strength_criteria:
  STRONG: "Full refund, extended period, no questions, includes used product"
  MEDIUM: "Standard return window, some conditions"
  WEAK: "Defects only, short window, unused only"
```

### Step 7: Analyze Offer Landscape

Synthesize findings into strategic insights.

```yaml
landscape_analysis:
  price_tiers: "Map competitors by price segment"
  guarantee_gaps: "Where is risk reversal weak?"
  bonus_gaps: "What value-adds are missing?"
  terms_opportunities: "What payment/delivery innovations possible?"
```

### Step 8: Extract SIN Offer Intelligence

Identify opportunities for Superior, Irresistible, No-brainer positioning.

```yaml
sin_framework:
  SUPERIOR: "What would objectively beat the market?"
  IRRESISTIBLE: "What creates urgency and value perception?"
  NO_BRAINER: "What removes all risk and makes decision easy?"
```

## Failure Protocol

```
IF offer data incomplete:
  - Visit competitor product pages directly
  - Check Amazon listings for pricing
  - Search for promotional offers
  - Document what's available, note gaps

IF competitor doesn't sell direct:
  - Analyze retailer offerings
  - Note that guarantee comes from retailer, not brand
  - This is still competitive intelligence

IF no direct response competitors found:
  - That itself is intelligence - opportunity for DR approach
  - Document standard retail-only landscape
  - Note opportunity for differentiated offer structure
```

## Notes

- This analysis is for COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE, not price matching
- The goal is to identify gaps in competitor offers, not replicate them
- Strong guarantees are often the biggest differentiator in commoditized markets
- Bonuses add perceived value at low cost - look for content-based opportunities
- Risk reversal is especially powerful for products with performance skepticism
- Direct response competitors (Square Strike) often have the most aggressive offers - learn from them
