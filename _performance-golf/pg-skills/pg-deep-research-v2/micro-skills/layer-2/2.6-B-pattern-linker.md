## Collaborative Filtering Logic

### The Netflix/Amazon Pattern
"People who bought X also bought Y"
Applied to research:
"People who expressed X also expressed Y"

### Why It Works
- Reveals hidden psychological connections
- Shows non-obvious associations
- Uncovers bundled desires/fears
- Identifies complementary messaging

---

## Pattern Types to Link

### Topic Links
People discussing topic A also discuss topic B
- Cross-category connections
- Related concern patterns
- Bundled interests

### Emotional Links
People expressing emotion A also express emotion B
- Emotional cascades
- Compound feelings
- Hidden drivers

### Behavioral Links
People who do/want A also do/want B
- Activity correlations
- Purchase patterns
- Usage combinations

### Language Links
People who use phrase A also use phrase B
- Vocabulary clusters
- Mindset indicators
- Tribal language

---

## Detection Process

### Step 1: Quote Clustering
Group quotes by author where possible:
- Same Reddit username
- Same forum member
- Same video commenter

### Step 2: Co-occurrence Calculation
For each pattern pair:
1. How often do they appear together?
2. How often does each appear alone?
3. Is co-occurrence higher than expected?

```
Co-occurrence Score = (A&B) / sqrt(A * B)
Higher than expected = potential link
```

### Step 3: Non-Obvious Filtering
Eliminate obvious connections:
- "Distance" and "drives" - obvious
- "Price" and "value" - obvious
- "Lost balls" and "aggressive play" - NON-OBVIOUS ✓

### Step 4: Insight Generation
For each non-obvious link:
- What does this reveal?
- What's the psychological connection?
- How could this inform positioning?

## "Lift" Calculation

How much more likely is co-occurrence than random?

```
Lift = Actual Co-occurrence Rate / Expected Co-occurrence Rate
Expected = (Frequency A / Total) * (Frequency B / Total)

Lift > 2.0 = Strong association
Lift 1.5-2.0 = Moderate association
Lift < 1.5 = Weak or random
```

- **No insight:** Reporting links without interpretation
- **Missing positioning:** Links without "so what"
