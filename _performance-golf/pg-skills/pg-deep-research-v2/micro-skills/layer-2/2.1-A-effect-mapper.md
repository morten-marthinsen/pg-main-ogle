# Micro-Skill 2.1-A: Effect Mapper

**Layer:** 2 - Intelligence
**Purpose:** Map ALL effects of a product across consequence chains
**Dependency:** Product brief + Layer 1 category data

## Effect Categories

### First-Order Effects (Direct Results)
What the product directly does/delivers:
- Physical performance
- Measurable outcomes
- Immediate functional benefits

### Second-Order Effects (Results of Results)
What happens because of first-order effects:
- Downstream consequences
- Enabled behaviors
- Changed situations

### Emotional Effects (How It Feels)
The emotional experience created:
- During use
- After use
- In anticipation

### Identity Effects (Who You Become)
What using this product says about you:
- Self-perception
- Personal identity
- Aspirational state

### Social Effects (How Others Perceive)
How others see/respond to the user:
- Status signals
- Social interactions
- Group belonging

### Negative Effects (What It Eliminates)
What problems/negatives are removed:
- Fears eliminated
- Frustrations avoided
- Anxieties resolved

---

## Mapping Process

### Step 1: Capture Known Effects
From product brief:
1. List all claimed/known benefits
2. Categorize into first-order effects
3. Note what company already emphasizes

### Step 2: Research Category Effects
From Layer 1 category data:
1. What effects do similar products claim?
2. What effects do users mention?
3. What effects appear in reviews/discussions?

### Step 3: Trace Consequence Chains
For each first-order effect:
1. Ask: "What happens because of this?"
2. Follow the chain 2-3 levels deep
3. Note emotional and identity implications

### Step 4: Map Negative Effects
For each problem the product solves:
1. What negative is eliminated?
2. What fear is resolved?
3. What frustration is avoided?

### Step 5: Identify Hidden Effects
Look for effects that are:
- Mentioned by users but not by companies
- Logical consequences but not stated
- Present in data but not emphasized

---

## Consequence Chain Mapping

### Template

```
First-Order Effect: [Direct result]
    ↓
Second-Order Effect: [What that enables/causes]
    ↓
Emotional Effect: [How that feels]
    ↓
Identity Effect: [What that means about the person]
    ↓
Social Effect: [How others respond]
```

### Example: Golf Ball

```
First-Order: More consistent distance
    ↓
Second-Order: More predictable club selection
    ↓
Emotional: Less anxiety over approach shots
    ↓
Identity: "I know my game"
    ↓
Social: Playing partners respect my consistency

First-Order: Softer feel
    ↓
Second-Order: Better feedback around greens
    ↓
Emotional: Confidence in short game
    ↓
Identity: "I have touch"
    ↓
Social: Not embarrassed by putting/chipping

First-Order: Lower price point
    ↓
Second-Order: Less hesitation when ball is lost
    ↓
Emotional: Freedom to play aggressively
    ↓
Identity: "I'm smart about money"
    ↓
Social: Not seen as wasteful/pretentious
```

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All first-order effects captured
- [ ] Consequence chains traced (2-3 levels)
- [ ] All six effect categories populated
- [ ] Sources noted (claim vs research vs inferred)
- [ ] Confidence notes included
- [ ] Gaps identified

---

## Failure Modes

- **Too narrow:** Only listing what company claims
- **Too shallow:** Not tracing consequence chains
- **Missing negatives:** Ignoring what product eliminates
- **No identity effects:** Missing "who you become"
- **Ungrounded:** Making up effects without evidence
