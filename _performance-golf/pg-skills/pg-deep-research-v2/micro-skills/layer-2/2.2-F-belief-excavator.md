# Micro-Skill 2.2-F: Belief Excavator

**Layer:** 2 - Intelligence
**Purpose:** Systematically excavate pre-existing beliefs about marketplace, vendors, solution types, and industry that filter all marketing messages
**Dependency:** Layer 1 extraction complete, 2.2-E WEB Analysis started
**Parallel:** Enhances 2.2-E WEB Analysis - can run concurrently
**Version:** 1.0
**PRD Reference:** [[RESEARCH-PRD#Section 4.2]]
**E5 Reference:** Todd Brown's WEB Analysis - B = Beliefs and Identifications

---

## CRITICAL: Beliefs Are the Filter Through Which All Messages Pass

Your prospect is NOT a blank canvas. They have pre-existing beliefs that impact how they see, understand, and absorb what you say.

When you make a claim, you're making it to someone with prior beliefs that affect reception. Miss this, and your copy fails before it starts.

**Four Categories of Beliefs to Excavate:**
1. Beliefs about the fundamental problem/solution
2. Beliefs about the marketplace itself
3. Beliefs about your TYPE of product/service
4. Beliefs about vendors/sellers in the space

## Excavation Process

### Step 1: **Excavate** Beliefs About the Problem/Solution

**Ruminate** on what they believe CAUSES their problem and FIXES it.

```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Root cause quotes (why they think they chunk)
  - Solutions tried quotes (what they believe works)
  - Attribution language ("it's because...", "the reason is...")

questions_to_answer:
  - What do they believe is the root cause?
  - What do they believe will solve it?
  - Who/what do they blame?
  - What have they tried based on these beliefs?
```

### Step 2: Extract Beliefs About the Marketplace

What do they believe about the golf equipment industry?

```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Competitor quotes (how they talk about brands)
  - Marketing skepticism quotes
  - Industry commentary

questions_to_answer:
  - Do they trust equipment marketing?
  - How do they view innovation claims?
  - Who do they consider credible?
  - What's their skepticism level?
```

### Step 3: Extract Beliefs About Solution Type

What do they believe about game improvement clubs, forgiveness, etc.?

```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Equipment discussion quotes
  - Stigma language
  - Identity statements about equipment choices

questions_to_answer:
  - Is there stigma around "game improvement"?
  - What do they believe forgiveness costs them?
  - What identity concerns exist?
  - What social pressures affect equipment choices?
```

### Step 4: Extract Beliefs About Vendors

What do they believe about DTC, unknown brands, non-big-brand sellers?

```yaml
extraction_sources:
  - Brand discussion quotes
  - DTC/online shopping quotes
  - Credibility questions in forums

questions_to_answer:
  - What establishes vendor credibility?
  - What raises red flags?
  - What proof do they need?
  - What's the trust threshold?
```

### Step 5: **Scrutinize** Accuracy of Each Belief

For each belief, **calibrate** whether it's accurate, partially accurate, or false.

```yaml
accuracy_categories:
  ACCURATE: "Belief reflects reality - align with it"
  PARTIALLY_ACCURATE: "Contains truth but incomplete - nuance needed"
  FALSE: "Belief is wrong - disrupt it with evidence"
```

### Step 6: **Synthesize** Strategy for Each Belief

**Distill** the optimal approach:

```yaml
strategy_options:
  DISRUPT: "Belief blocks our message - need to change it"
  ALIGN: "Belief helps or is neutral - agree and use"
  AVOID: "Belief is dangerous to trigger - stay away from it"
```

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All four belief categories covered
- [ ] Minimum 3 beliefs per category extracted
- [ ] Verbatim evidence for each belief
- [ ] Belief strength assessed (STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK)
- [ ] Accuracy assessed for each
- [ ] Strategy defined (disrupt/align/avoid)
- [ ] Copy implications documented
- [ ] Language guidance provided (use/avoid)

---

## Failure Protocol

```
IF beliefs not appearing in quotes:
  - Look for implicit beliefs in explanations
  - Analyze language choices for underlying assumptions
  - Check solutions_tried for belief patterns
  - Search forum discussions about equipment/brands

IF beliefs contradict each other:
  - Document both - market may be segmented
  - Note which is more common
  - Strategy may need to address both

IF stigma beliefs strong:
  - DO NOT ignore - these kill sales
  - Explicit differentiation required
  - Language choices critical
```

## Notes

- Beliefs are often invisible to the prospect - they don't know they have them
- Stigma beliefs are especially powerful - ego and identity are at stake
- Don't fight accurate beliefs - align with them
- Disrupting false beliefs requires PROOF, not assertion
- The golf equipment market has strong brand loyalty beliefs
- "Game improvement" stigma is real - language matters enormously
- Skepticism about marketing is healthy - work with it, not against it
