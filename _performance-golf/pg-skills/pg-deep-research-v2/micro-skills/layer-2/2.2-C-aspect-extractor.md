## Aspect Categories

### Pain
Suffering, problems, negative current state:
- Physical pain points
- Process frustrations
- Outcome disappointments
- Current state dissatisfaction

**Markers:** "hate," "frustrated," "struggle," "can't," "problem," "annoying"

### Desire
Wants, wishes, aspirational states:
- What they want to achieve
- Outcomes they seek
- States they aspire to
- Goals they have

**Markers:** "want," "wish," "would love," "if only," "dream," "goal"

### Frustration
Blocked progress, unmet expectations:
- Things that should work but don't
- Expectations not met
- Progress hindered
- Repeated failures

**Markers:** "why can't," "doesn't work," "supposed to," "should," "keeps happening"

### Hope
Optimism, potential, future vision:
- Belief things can improve
- Openness to solutions
- Positive expectations
- Willingness to try

**Markers:** "maybe," "could," "hoping," "think it might," "give it a shot"

### Fear
Anxiety, worry, risk perception:
- What might go wrong
- Consequences they dread
- Risks they perceive
- Outcomes they avoid

**Markers:** "worried," "afraid," "don't want," "scared," "what if," "might"

### Identity
Self-concept, who they are/want to be:
- How they see themselves
- Who they want to become
- Labels they accept/reject
- Self-image concerns

**Markers:** "I'm the type," "I'm not a," "I consider myself," "I'd never," "that's not me"

## Extraction Process

### Step 1: Aspect Detection
For each quote:
1. Scan for aspect markers
2. Identify which aspects present
3. Note strength of each

### Step 2: Multi-Aspect Handling
Same quote can express multiple aspects:
```
"I'm frustrated (FRUSTRATION) that I keep losing balls,
but I'm hoping (HOPE) this cheaper ball will let me
play more aggressively (DESIRE) without feeling like
I'm wasting money (PAIN)."
```

Tag ALL aspects present, not just dominant one.

### Step 3: Aspect Details
For each aspect found:
1. Note specific expression
2. Rate intensity (weak/moderate/strong)
3. Identify trigger/cause

### Step 4: Distribution Analysis
After all quotes tagged:
1. Count aspect frequency
2. Calculate co-occurrence patterns
3. Note dominant aspects by segment

## Intensity Rating

### Strong
- Explicit emotional language
- Multiple markers present
- Central to quote
- High SQS correlation

### Moderate
- Some emotional language
- Clear aspect presence
- Not central focus
- Supporting element

### Weak
- Implied aspect
- Subtle markers
- Minor element
- Background presence

---

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All quotes aspect-tagged
- [ ] Multi-aspect quotes handled
- [ ] Intensity rated for each aspect
- [ ] Triggers identified
- [ ] Distribution calculated
- [ ] No forced tagging

---

## Failure Modes

- **Over-tagging:** Finding aspects that aren't there
- **Under-tagging:** Missing obvious aspects
- **Single-aspect bias:** Only tagging dominant aspect
- **Intensity inflation:** Everything rated "strong"
- **Missing triggers:** Aspects without causes

---

## Notes

- A quote can have 1-6 aspects
- Don't force aspects that aren't present
- Intensity matters for prioritization
- This feeds 2.2-D Aspect Connector
- Multi-aspect quotes are especially valuable
