## Timing Signal Types

### Rising Problems
Problems getting worse over time:
- Pain intensifying
- Frustration growing
- Need becoming urgent

**Timing Implication:** Good time to solve this problem

### Shifting Attention
Cultural focus moving:
- New topics emerging
- Old topics fading
- Discourse changing

**Timing Implication:** Align with where attention is going

### Competitive Gaps
What competitors abandoned:
- Topics they stopped emphasizing
- Positions they vacated
- Segments they ignored

**Timing Implication:** Opportunity to claim vacated territory

### Market Readiness
Audience prepared to accept new ideas:
- Previous solutions failed
- Skepticism about old ways
- Openness to alternatives

**Timing Implication:** Market is primed for new positioning

---

## Assessment Process

### Step 1: Rising Problem Analysis
From trend data:
1. Which problems are intensifying?
2. How urgent is the trend?
3. What's the trajectory?

### Step 2: Attention Shift Analysis
From emerging/fading topics:
1. Where is conversation going?
2. What's gaining mindshare?
3. What's losing relevance?

### Step 3: Competitive Window Analysis
From competitive data:
1. Any positions being abandoned?
2. Any claims being overused (fatigue)?
3. Any gaps opening up?

### Step 4: Market Readiness Assessment
From sentiment and volume:
1. Is there frustration with current options?
2. Is there openness to alternatives?
3. What conditions favor change?

### Step 5: Timing Recommendation
Synthesize signals into:
- NOW: Immediate opportunity
- SOON: Opportunity developing
- WAIT: Not yet ready
- AVOID: Timing unfavorable

## Timing Window Assessment

| Assessment | Criteria | Action |
|------------|----------|--------|
| **NOW** | Trends aligned, gap open, market ready | Act immediately |
| **SOON** | Trends developing, conditions improving | Prepare, monitor |
| **WAIT** | Conditions not yet favorable | Hold, watch signals |
| **AVOID** | Timing unfavorable, trends against | Don't pursue now |
