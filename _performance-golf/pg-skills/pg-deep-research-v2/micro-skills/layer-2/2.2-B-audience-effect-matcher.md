**Layer:** 2 - Intelligence
**Purpose:** Match audience segments to effects with intensity scoring
**Dependency:** 2.2-A Audience Identifier + 2.1-B Validated Effects

## Matching Process

### Step 1: Build Match Matrix
Create grid of:
- Rows: Audience segments
- Columns: Validated effects
- Cells: Intensity + Evidence

### Step 2: Evidence Search
For each segment-effect pair:
1. Search quotes from that segment
2. Filter for effect-related language
3. Assess intensity of caring

### Step 3: Intensity Scoring
Rate how much each segment cares about each effect:

| Rating | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Life-changing** | Mentioned with strong emotion, multiple quotes, central to their experience |
| **Important** | Mentioned regularly, clear priority, affects decisions |
| **Nice-to-have** | Mentioned occasionally, not central, not decision-driver |
| **Irrelevant** | Not mentioned, no connection to segment priorities |

### Step 4: Competitive Analysis
For each high-intensity match:
1. Is this segment being spoken to?
2. Is this effect being marketed to them?
3. What's the gap?

### Step 5: Mismatch Identification
Find the Jolie patterns:
- Effect exists ✓
- Segment cares intensely ✓
- Nobody marketing to them ✓
= OPPORTUNITY

---

## Intensity Evidence

### Life-Changing Indicators
- Quotes express transformation
- Multiple sources confirm
- High SQS quotes
- Emotional language (frustration, relief, joy)
- Decision-driver language

### Important Indicators
- Regular mentions
- Clear preference statements
- Comparison language
- Upgrade/switch mentions

### Nice-to-Have Indicators
- Occasional mentions
- Not central to discussion
- "Would be nice" language
- Not a deal-breaker

### Irrelevant Indicators
- No mentions in segment
- When mentioned, dismissed
- Different priorities evident

## Opportunity Scoring

Calculate opportunity score (1-10) for each match:

| Factor | Weight | Criteria |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Intensity | 30% | Life-changing = 10, Important = 6, Nice-to-have = 3 |
| Competitive gap | 30% | Underserved = 10, Partial = 5, Crowded = 2 |
| Segment size | 20% | Large = 10, Medium = 6, Small = 3 |
| Evidence strength | 20% | Strong = 10, Moderate = 6, Weak = 3 |

**Opportunity Score = Weighted sum**

---

## The Jolie Pattern Detection

Specifically look for:
1. **Functional effect exists** (validated in 2.1-B)
2. **Secondary/emotional effect matters to segment** (life-changing intensity)
3. **Different segment than primary target** (not who brand talks to)
4. **Higher emotional stakes** (more intense than primary audience)
5. **Nobody speaking to them** (competitive gap)

When all five criteria met → Flag as Jolie Pattern Opportunity
