## Saturation Categories

### DEAD
- Everyone says it
- No one believes it anymore
- Zero differentiation value
- Market is fatigued

**Criteria:**
- 80%+ advertisers claim it
- Market language shows skepticism
- "They all say that" sentiment

### CROWDED
- Many competitors claiming
- Marginal differentiation
- Hard to stand out
- Requires proof overwhelm

**Criteria:**
- 50-79% advertisers claim it
- Some market belief remains
- "Need to prove it" sentiment

### PARTIAL
- Some competitors claiming
- Segment-specific ownership
- Room for different framing
- Can enter with differentiation

**Criteria:**
- 20-49% advertisers claim it
- Clear segment ownership
- Adjacent segments open

### EMERGING
- Few competitors claiming
- Starting to appear
- First mover advantage possible
- Risk of trend-chasing

**Criteria:**
- <20% claim it
- Growing frequency
- Market interest rising

### OPEN
- No one claiming
- Data shows market cares
- White space opportunity
- Requires validation

**Criteria:**
- 0% currently claim it
- Layer 1 data shows demand
- Unaddressed need

---

## Scoring Process

### Step 1: Claim Saturation Analysis
For each claim from 2.4-A:
1. Calculate advertiser coverage
2. Assess placement prominence
3. Note trend (growing/stable/declining)

### Step 2: Market Belief Assessment
From Layer 1 data:
1. Do people believe this claim?
2. Is there skepticism?
3. What's the sentiment?

### Step 3: Segment Analysis
For each claim:
1. Which segments care about this?
2. Are specific segments underserved?
3. Is there segment-specific opportunity?

### Step 4: Cross-Reference
Compare:
- What competitors say (2.4-A)
- What market wants (Layer 1)
- What gaps exist (2.3)

### Step 5: Opportunity Scoring
For each claim/position:
- Saturation penalty (higher = worse)
- Market demand bonus (higher = better)
- Gap opportunity bonus (if applicable)

---

## Market Belief Indicators

### High Belief
- People repeat the claim positively
- Recommendations based on claim
- "I bought because of X"
- Emotional investment in claim

### Medium Belief
- Some positive, some skeptical
- "Works for some people"
- Conditional endorsements
- "If you need X, then..."

### Low Belief
- Widespread skepticism
- "They all say that"
- "Marketing BS"
- "Can't trust those claims"

## Opportunity Score Calculation

| Factor | Weight | Scoring |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Saturation | 30% | DEAD=0, CROWDED=3, PARTIAL=6, EMERGING=8, OPEN=10 |
| Market demand | 30% | Based on Layer 1 quote count and intensity |
| Segment alignment | 20% | Clear segment=10, Mixed=5, Unclear=2 |
| Differentiation potential | 20% | Unique angle possible=10, Marginal=5, None=2 |

## Notes

- DEAD doesn't mean claim is wrong - just not differentiating
- OPEN requires market validation before committing
- This output is crucial for Layer 3 opportunity ranking
- White space + high demand = highest opportunity
