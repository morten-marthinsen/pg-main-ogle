## CRITICAL: The Magic Wand Reveals What They REALLY Want

The Magic Wand Exercise uncovers desires prospects might not articulate directly. It asks:

**"If you could wave a magic wand and grant your prospect ANY outcome related to the problem you address, what would it look like?"**

Key framing: Not what would YOU grant them - what would THEY ask for.

This is BLUE SKY thinking. The perfect world scenario. You'll likely need to back this down when crafting your actual message, but it's a powerful starting point.

## Extraction Process

### Step 1: Frame the Magic Wand Question

Articulate the specific question for this product/problem.

```yaml
question_template: |
  "If [target audience] who struggle with [problem] could wave a magic wand
  and instantly have any outcome related to [domain], what would they ask for?"

example: |
  "If golfers who struggle with chunking could wave a magic wand and instantly
  have any outcome related to their short game, what would they ask for?"
```

### Step 2: Extract PRIMARY Wishes from Hope Quotes

These are the biggest, most frequently expressed desires.

```yaml
extraction_signals:
  - "I wish I could..."
  - "If only I..."
  - "I'd give anything to..."
  - "My dream would be..."
  - "In a perfect world..."
  - Inverse of strongest pain quotes
  - Aspirational language

categorize_by:
  - Elimination (remove the negative)
  - Achievement (gain the positive)
  - Mastery (reach excellence)
  - Freedom (escape constraints)
  - Confidence (emotional state)
  - Status (social position)
```

### Step 3: Extract SECONDARY Wishes

These are the less frequently mentioned but still important desires.

```yaml
look_for:
  - Less frequent hope quotes
  - Wishes mentioned in passing
  - Desires that follow from primary wishes
  - Social/emotional wishes (not just functional)
```

### Step 4: Identify IMPLICIT Wishes

These are wishes they don't say directly but are implied in their language.

```yaml
excavation_techniques:
  - What does their frustration imply they want?
  - What does their identity struggle imply they want?
  - What would resolving their emotional state require?
  - What are they not saying but clearly wanting?

example: |
  If they say "I've been 'the bad chipper' for years" - the implicit wish
  is "I want to shed that identity" or "I want to prove I can change"
```

### Step 5: Categorize and Rank Wishes

```yaml
ranking_criteria:
  by_frequency:
    - How often is this wish expressed?
    - How many variations appear?

  by_emotional_intensity:
    - How much emotional charge in the language?
    - How deeply does this connect to identity/fear?

  by_impact:
    - If granted, how much would it change their life?
    - What secondary benefits would follow?
```

### Step 6: Calibrate Against Reality

For each wish, assess what the product can actually deliver.

```yaml
calibration_levels:
  CAN_DELIVER_FULLY:
    - Wish aligns perfectly with product capability
    - Can promise this without qualification

  CAN_DELIVER_SUBSTANTIALLY:
    - Wish can be significantly addressed
    - Need to calibrate promise language
    - "Dramatically improve" not "guarantee"

  ASPIRATIONAL_ONLY:
    - Wish is blue-sky beyond what any product can deliver
    - Use as direction/inspiration, not promise
    - Acknowledge the aspiration, deliver what's realistic
```

### Step 7: Create Messaging Applications

Translate wishes into usable copy elements.

```yaml
applications:
  headline_inspiration:
    - What if [magic wand wish]?
    - Imagine [magic wand wish]
    - Finally: [magic wand wish]
    - The [product] that [magic wand wish]

  promise_calibration:
    - Magic wand version (aspirational)
    - Realistic version (deliverable)
    - Bridge language (connects aspiration to reality)

  aspiration_anchoring:
    - State the wish they have
    - Acknowledge it's the ceiling
    - Deliver what you can promise
    - Show how that gets them closer
```

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] Magic wand question clearly framed
- [ ] Primary wishes extracted (minimum 4)
- [ ] Secondary wishes extracted (minimum 4)
- [ ] Implicit wishes identified (minimum 2)
- [ ] Each wish has verbatim evidence or extraction explanation
- [ ] Wishes categorized by type
- [ ] Wishes ranked by frequency and emotional intensity
- [ ] Reality calibration complete for each wish
- [ ] Messaging applications provided
- [ ] Bridge language connects aspiration to reality

---

## Failure Protocol

```
IF wishes too surface-level:
  - Push for the emotional core
  - Ask "what would having that FEEL like?"
  - Look for implicit wishes in frustration

IF wishes unrealistic:
  - Don't discard - use as aspiration anchor
  - Document the gap between wish and reality
  - Create bridge language that acknowledges and delivers

IF wishes too few:
  - Return to hope quotes
  - Invert pain quotes
  - Look at Now-After Grid AFTER states
  - Consider implicit desires in identity struggles
```

## Notes

- The Magic Wand Exercise is about THEIR wishes, not your capabilities
- Blue-sky thinking reveals what really matters to them
- You'll back down from magic wand wishes to realistic promises
- But the aspiration-to-reality bridge is powerful copy territory
- Use wishes to understand emotional priorities, not just functional ones
- The most powerful wishes often aren't the obvious ones
