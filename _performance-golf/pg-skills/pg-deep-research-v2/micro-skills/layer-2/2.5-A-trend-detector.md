# Micro-Skill 2.5-A: Trend Detector

**Layer:** 2 - Intelligence
**Purpose:** Detect volume and sentiment trends over time
**Dependency:** Layer 1 data with timestamps

---

## Trend Types

### Volume Trends
How much is something being discussed?
- Rising: More mentions over time
- Stable: Consistent volume
- Falling: Fewer mentions over time

### Sentiment Trends
How are attitudes changing?
- Improving: More positive over time
- Stable: Consistent sentiment
- Declining: More negative over time

### Topic Trends
What's gaining/losing attention?
- Emerging topics: New discussions appearing
- Fading topics: Previously active now quiet
- Persistent topics: Consistently discussed

---

## Analysis Process

### Step 1: Time Period Definition
Standard comparison:
- Recent period: Last 30 days
- Prior period: 30-60 days ago
- Baseline: 60-90 days ago

### Step 2: Volume Comparison
For each topic/theme:
1. Count mentions in recent period
2. Count mentions in prior period
3. Calculate change ratio

```
Change Ratio = Recent / Prior
> 1.0 = Rising
= 1.0 = Stable
< 1.0 = Falling
```

### Step 3: Sentiment Comparison
For each topic/theme:
1. Average sentiment in recent period
2. Average sentiment in prior period
3. Calculate sentiment delta

### Step 4: Significance Assessment
Is the change meaningful?
- Large sample sizes in both periods
- Consistent pattern
- Not explained by seasonal factors

### Step 5: Confidence Rating
Based on:
- Sample size
- Pattern consistency
- Source diversity

## Seasonal Considerations

### Golf-Specific Seasonality
- Spring: Golf season starting (Northern hemisphere)
- Summer: Peak season
- Fall: Season winding down
- Winter: Off-season, equipment purchases for next year

Adjust interpretation for seasonal patterns.

### Economic Factors
- Recession indicators: More value-consciousness
- Holiday periods: Gift-buying patterns
- New product launches: Spikes around releases

---

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] Time periods clearly defined
- [ ] Both volume and sentiment analyzed
- [ ] Magnitude calculated with formula
- [ ] Confidence rated with justification
- [ ] Emerging/fading topics identified
- [ ] Seasonal factors considered

---

## Notes

- Trends require sufficient data in both periods
- Small samples produce unreliable trends
- Always consider external factors
- Output feeds 2.5-B Timing Assessor
