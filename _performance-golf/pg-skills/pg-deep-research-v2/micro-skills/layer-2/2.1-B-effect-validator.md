## Validation Categories

### Validated
Effect appears clearly in Layer 1 data:
- Multiple quotes support it
- Different sources confirm it
- Consistent pattern

### Unvalidated (Potential Opportunity)
Effect is logical but NOT appearing in data:
- Could be opportunity to introduce
- Could be overlooked by market
- Could be wrong hypothesis

### Contradicted
Effect is contradicted by data:
- Quotes say opposite
- Evidence suggests effect doesn't exist
- Should be removed from consideration

---

## Validation Process

### Step 1: Evidence Search
For each effect in the effect map:
1. Search tagged quotes for relevant language
2. Filter by SQS (prioritize 6+)
3. Count mentions across sources

### Step 2: Evidence Assessment

**Strong Evidence (validated with confidence):**
- 10+ quotes across 3+ sources
- High SQS quotes support it
- No contradicting evidence

**Moderate Evidence (validated with caution):**
- 5-10 quotes across 2+ sources
- Mix of SQS levels
- Minor contradicting evidence

**Weak Evidence (tentatively validated):**
- 3-5 quotes, limited sources
- Lower SQS average
- Proceed with caution

**No Evidence (unvalidated):**
- <3 quotes
- Not appearing in data
- Flag as potential opportunity

**Contradicting Evidence (invalidated):**
- Quotes explicitly contradict
- Opposite pattern emerging
- Remove from consideration

### Step 3: Quote Attachment
For each validated effect:
1. Select 5-10 representative quotes
2. Include range of sources
3. Include highest SQS examples

### Step 4: Opportunity Flagging
For unvalidated effects:
1. Note the gap
2. Assess if plausible
3. Flag as potential opportunity
4. Recommend validation approach

---

## Evidence Search Patterns

### For First-Order Effects
Search for:
- Direct benefit mentions
- Performance descriptions
- Feature references

### For Emotional Effects
Search for:
- Feeling words
- Emotional language
- Experience descriptions

### For Identity Effects
Search for:
- "I am" statements
- Self-description
- Aspirational language

### For Social Effects
Search for:
- Playing partner mentions
- Social situation descriptions
- Status/perception language

### For Negative Effects
Search for:
- Frustration descriptions
- Problem mentions
- Relief language

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All effects validated against data
- [ ] Evidence strength categorized
- [ ] Representative quotes attached
- [ ] Unvalidated effects flagged
- [ ] Contradictions identified
- [ ] Opportunity signals surfaced

---

## Interpretation Protocol

Per architecture error-reduction principles:

**A. Quantification Phase (Automated)**
- Count quotes per effect
- Calculate source distribution
- Measure SQS averages

**B. Detection Phase (Automated with thresholds)**
- Apply evidence thresholds
- Binary: evidence exists or doesn't
- Flag patterns meeting criteria

**C. Assessment Phase (Human-reviewable)**
- Assign confidence levels
- Attach evidence chain
- Note counter-evidence
- Flag for review where needed

---

## Notes

- This skill TESTS hypotheses from 2.1-A
- It does NOT create new effects
- Unvalidated effects are opportunities to explore
- Contradicted effects should be removed
- Output feeds audience-effect matching (2.2-B)
