## CRITICAL: The Three Levels of Benefits

Most marketers stop at functional benefits. That's why their copy falls flat.

**Three Levels:**
1. **Functional Benefits** - Surface level ("you'll lose weight")
2. **Dimensionalized Benefits** - Experiential ("every morning when you step on the scale...")
3. **Emotional Benefits** - Feeling ("you'll finally feel in control")

Each functional benefit must be TRANSFORMED through all three levels. The emotional benefit is what people actually BUY.

## Dimensionalization Process

### Step 1: **Excavate** All Functional Benefits

From product brief and feature list, **extract** surface-level benefits.

```yaml
functional_benefit_sources:
  - Product features translated to outcomes
  - Marketing claims simplified
  - What the product DOES for the customer

characteristic: "States the benefit but doesn't paint a picture"
```

### Step 2: **Distill** Each Benefit into Dimensionalized Form

**Ruminate** on how to transform functional into experiential by painting the movie.

```yaml
dimensionalization_questions:
  - What does this benefit LOOK like in their life?
  - What do they SEE when experiencing this benefit?
  - What do they HEAR?
  - What do they FEEL physically?
  - What SCENARIO illustrates this benefit?
  - What's the CONTRAST between before and after?

technique: |
  Don't tell them what they get.
  SHOW them what it looks like.
  Make them SEE it happening.
```

### Step 3: Extract Emotional Benefit

Identify the FEELING behind the dimensionalized benefit.

```yaml
emotional_benefit_questions:
  - How do they FEEL when experiencing this?
  - What emotion does this create?
  - What emotional state does this resolve?
  - What deeper desire does this fulfill?

common_emotional_benefits:
  - Confidence
  - Control
  - Freedom
  - Pride
  - Joy
  - Relief
  - Safety
  - Belonging
  - Accomplishment
```

### Step 4: Create Copy-Ready Versions

For each benefit, write usable copy at each level.

```yaml
versions_needed:
  functional_only: "One sentence, surface level"
  dimensionalized: "One paragraph, experiential"
  emotional: "One sentence, feeling-focused"
  full_stack: "Complete benefit stack, all three levels"
```

### Step 5: Link to Verbatim Evidence

Connect benefits to actual quotes from research.

```yaml
evidence_connection:
  - Which hope quotes support this emotional benefit?
  - Which pain quotes does this benefit address?
  - What language do they use for this desire?
```

### Step 6: Rank by Emotional Power

Prioritize benefits by how strongly they resonate emotionally.

```yaml
ranking_criteria:
  - How central is this emotion to their transformation?
  - How frequently does this desire appear in quotes?
  - How differentiated is this benefit from competitors?
  - How deeply does this connect to identity/fear?
```

## Dimensionalization Examples

**DON'T:**
"You'll generate sales every day." (Functional only)

**DO:**
"Every morning when you log on to your email, you'll see dozens of new sale notifications." (Dimensionalized)

"You'll feel excited, inspired, motivated. You'll finally feel on track. You'll feel successful." (Emotional)

---

**DON'T:**
"You'll lose weight." (Functional only)

**DO:**
"Picture stepping on the scale Monday morning and seeing that number drop again. Another pound. Another step toward your goal. You reach for the jeans you haven't worn in two years, pull them on, and they fit." (Dimensionalized)

"You'll feel in control for the first time in years. Like you've finally figured it out. Like the future is yours." (Emotional)

- [ ] Benefits ranked by emotional power
- [ ] Verbatim evidence linked where available

## Failure Protocol

```
IF functional benefits too few:
  - Return to product features
  - Consider indirect benefits
  - Think about cascading effects of primary benefits

IF dimensionalization flat:
  - Add sensory detail: what do they SEE/HEAR/FEEL?
  - Create specific scenarios
  - Use before/after contrast
  - Show the movie, don't summarize it

IF emotional benefit generic:
  - Ask "why does that matter to them?"
  - Connect to WEB analysis emotions
  - Link to identity and deeper desires
  - Be specific: "confident" not "good"
```

- The emotional benefit is what people actually BUY
- Dimensionalization makes benefits believable and real
- Functional benefits provide logical justification AFTER emotional resonance
- Always lead with emotion, prove with experience, support with function
- The best copy stacks all three levels seamlessly
- Use this worksheet when writing ANY copy for this product
