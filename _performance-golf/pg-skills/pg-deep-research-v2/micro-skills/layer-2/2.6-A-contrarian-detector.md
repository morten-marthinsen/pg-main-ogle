## Contrarian Logic

### Why Consensus Creates Opportunity
When everyone believes X:
- The belief becomes invisible/unquestioned
- Counter-evidence is dismissed
- Contrarian position feels bold/fresh
- "Finally someone says what I was thinking"

### The 80/20 Rule
Strong contrarian opportunities exist when:
- 80%+ express same belief
- BUT silent minority exists
- AND belief has cracks in evidence

---

## Detection Process

### Step 1: Consensus Identification
Scan for uniform beliefs:
- "Everyone knows that..."
- "Of course you should..."
- "Nobody would..."
- "It's obvious that..."

### Step 2: Agreement Rate Calculation
For each consensus belief:
1. Count supporting statements
2. Count contradicting statements
3. Calculate agreement rate

```
Agreement Rate = Supporting / (Supporting + Contradicting)
```

### Step 3: Minority Voice Search
Look for dissenters:
- "Am I the only one who thinks..."
- "Unpopular opinion but..."
- "I might be crazy but..."
- "Everyone disagrees but..."

### Step 4: Evidence Crack Detection
Look for weak spots in consensus:
- Qualifiers ("I guess...", "I think...")
- Exceptions acknowledged
- Doubt language
- "If you have the money" type conditions

### Step 5: Opportunity Assessment
Is contrarian position viable?
- Is there a minority who would agree?
- Is there evidence to support contrarian view?
- Would contrarian position differentiate?

---

## Consensus Types

### Performance Consensus
"Premium balls really do perform better"
- Check: Do they actually for all golfers?
- Crack: "At my swing speed, I'm not sure..."

### Value Consensus
"You get what you pay for"
- Check: Is this always true?
- Crack: "Kirkland kind of proved otherwise..."

### Identity Consensus
"Serious golfers play premium balls"
- Check: What defines serious?
- Crack: "I'm serious but I'm also smart about money..."

### Behavior Consensus
"You should get fitted for your ball"
- Check: Does everyone actually do this?
- Crack: "I've never been fitted and I play fine..."

## Opportunity Strength Rating

| Strength | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| **HIGH** | 80%+ agreement, vocal minority, evidence cracks, differentiation clear |
| **MEDIUM** | 70-80% agreement, some minority voice, limited cracks |
| **LOW** | 60-70% agreement, weak minority, consensus may be justified |

---

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] Consensus patterns identified
- [ ] Agreement rates calculated
- [ ] Minority voices surfaced
- [ ] Evidence cracks detected
- [ ] Contrarian positions articulated
- [ ] Risks assessed

---

## Failure Modes

- **Forcing contrarian:** Not every consensus has a viable opposite
- **Ignoring risk:** Contrarian can backfire if minority too small
- **Missing consensus:** Only finding obvious ones
- **Overconfidence:** Contrarian isn't automatically right

---

## Notes

- Contrarian only works if minority actually exists
- Must have evidence to support contrarian view
- Position as liberation, not criticism
- "Finally someone says what I've been thinking"
- Output feeds Layer 3 opportunity synthesis
