## Identification Signals

### Self-Identification Language
Look for:
- "As a [X], I..."
- "I'm the type of golfer who..."
- "For someone like me..."
- Handicap self-reporting
- Experience level mentions

### Context Markers
Look for:
- Where they play (public vs private)
- How often they play
- Who they play with
- What they spend on golf
- What matters to them

### Psychographic Patterns
Look for:
- Values expressed
- Priorities stated
- Trade-offs mentioned
- Emotional triggers
- Identity statements

---

## Segment Discovery Process

### Step 1: Pattern Mining
Scan high-SQS quotes for:
1. Self-identification language
2. Context clues
3. Value statements
4. Emotional patterns

### Step 2: Cluster Formation
Group quotes by:
1. Similar self-identification
2. Similar values/priorities
3. Similar emotional triggers
4. Similar contexts

### Step 3: Segment Definition
For each cluster:
1. Name the segment (descriptive, not generic)
2. Define key characteristics
3. Identify emotional profile
4. Estimate relative size

### Step 4: Validation
For each segment:
1. Minimum 10 quotes
2. Consistent pattern
3. Distinct from other segments

## Common Segment Types (Golf Example)

### By Skill Level
- Beginners (just starting, high anxiety)
- Improvers (working on game, investing time)
- Consistent players (stable handicap, established habits)
- Low handicappers (serious, performance-focused)

### By Spending Pattern
- Budget-conscious (minimize cost, maximize rounds)
- Value-seekers (best performance per dollar)
- Premium buyers (pay for best, justify with quality)
- Mixed (premium on some, value on others)

### By Play Style
- Weekend warriors (play for fun, social)
- Competitive amateurs (play to win, track progress)
- Golf tourists (travel for golf, experiential)
- Grinding improvers (practice-focused, dedicated)

### By Emotional Driver
- Anxiety-driven (fear of embarrassment, loss)
- Joy-driven (pure enjoyment, no pressure)
- Achievement-driven (scores, improvement)
- Social-driven (relationships, belonging)

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] At least 3 distinct segments identified
- [ ] Each segment has 10+ quote evidence
- [ ] Emotional profiles defined
- [ ] Size indicators assigned
- [ ] Overlap noted
- [ ] No generic segment names ("Golf Enthusiasts")

---

## Failure Modes

- **Too broad:** "Golfers who care about price" - not specific enough
- **Too narrow:** Segment of 3 quotes - not enough pattern
- **Missing emotion:** Only demographics, no psychographics
- **Generic names:** "Committed Improvers" (Brandy-speak)
- **Forced segments:** Creating segments that don't exist in data

---

## Notes

- Segments should emerge from data, not be imposed
- Name segments descriptively, not generically
- Emotional profile is crucial for later matching
- Size indicators are relative, not absolute
- Output feeds 2.2-B Audience-Effect Matcher
