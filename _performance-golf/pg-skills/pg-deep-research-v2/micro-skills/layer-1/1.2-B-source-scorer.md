## Scoring Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Activity Indicators (1-10)

| Signal | Low (1-3) | Medium (4-6) | High (7-10) |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Volume** | <1 post/month | 1-4 posts/month | 1+ posts/week |
| **Recency** | Last post 30+ days | Last post 7-30 days | Last post <7 days |
| **Thread Depth** | One-liners only | Some discussion | Multi-paragraph exchanges |

**Composite Activity Score:** Average of three signals

### Dimension 2: Quality Indicators (1-10)

| Signal | Low (1-3) | Medium (4-6) | High (7-10) |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Emotional Intensity** | Neutral, factual only | Some emotion | Strong feelings expressed |
| **Specificity** | Generic statements | Some details | Named products, dollar amounts, stories |
| **Debate Presence** | Everyone agrees | Some disagreement | Active debates, strong opinions |

**Composite Quality Score:** Average of three signals

### Dimension 3: Relevance Indicators (1-10)

| Signal | Low (1-3) | Medium (4-6) | High (7-10) |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Direct Product Discussion** | Tangential only | Some product talk | Product is central topic |
| **Buyer Language** | Spectator language | Mixed | Decision-making language |
| **Problem Awareness** | Unaware stage | Problem aware | Solution seeking |

**Composite Relevance Score:** Average of three signals

### Dimension 4: Advanced Indicators (1-10)

These are the big tech-level differentiators:

| Signal | Low (1-3) | Medium (4-6) | High (7-10) |
|--------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Velocity/Momentum** | Declining activity | Stable | Growing activity |
| **Engagement Depth Ratio** | Low comments-to-views | Average | High engagement rate |
| **Creator Authority** | Unknown/new | Established | Recognized expert |
| **Sentiment Polarity** | Uniform sentiment | Some variance | Strong polarity (disagreement) |

**Composite Advanced Score:** Average of four signals

---

## Composite Scoring Formula

```
Composite = (Activity × 0.20) + (Quality × 0.30) + (Relevance × 0.30) + (Advanced × 0.20)
```

**Weights rationale:**
- Quality and Relevance weighted highest because they predict content value
- Activity ensures freshness but isn't sufficient alone
- Advanced indicators add differentiation layer

---

## Tier Classification

| Tier | Composite Score | Recommendation |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Tier 1** | 7.5+ | Must include - high priority scraping |
| **Tier 2** | 5.5-7.4 | Include - standard scraping depth |
| **Tier 3** | 4.0-5.4 | Include if budget allows - lighter scraping |
| **Exclude** | <4.0 | Do not scrape - insufficient signal |

## Scoring Process

### Step 1: Data Collection
For each source, gather observable data:
- Visit source or use API
- Capture metrics visible on page
- Sample content for quality indicators

### Step 2: Signal Extraction
For each dimension, extract specific signals:

**For Activity:**
- Count posts in last 30 days
- Check date of most recent post
- Sample 5 threads for depth

**For Quality:**
- Read 10 posts/comments for emotional language
- Check for specificity markers (numbers, names, stories)
- Look for disagreement in comments

**For Relevance:**
- Assess how directly product category is discussed
- Look for buying decision language
- Evaluate problem-awareness level

**For Advanced:**
- Compare recent activity to older activity (velocity)
- Calculate engagement ratios where visible
- Research creator/moderator reputation
- Assess comment sentiment distribution

### Step 3: Score Assignment
Assign 1-10 score for each signal based on criteria tables

### Step 4: Composite Calculation
Apply weighted formula

### Step 5: Tier Assignment
Classify each source into tier

### Step 6: Output Generation
Produce scored_sources.json with full breakdown

## Platform-Specific Scoring Notes

### YouTube Channels
- Volume = videos per month
- Depth = average comment count
- Authority = subscriber count + verification

### Reddit Subreddits
- Volume = posts per day
- Depth = comments per post average
- Authority = moderator activity + community rules

### Forums
- Volume = threads per week
- Depth = replies per thread
- Authority = member tenure, post count

### Review Sites
- Volume = reviews per product
- Depth = review length (verified vs brief)
- Authority = verified purchase ratio

### Social Accounts
- Volume = posts per week
- Engagement = likes/comments ratio
- Authority = follower count + verification

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] All sources scored on all four dimensions
- [ ] Score breakdown explains each rating
- [ ] Tier classification complete
- [ ] Recommendations provided for each source
- [ ] Platform coverage maintained (don't exclude all sources from any platform type)

---

## Failure Modes

- **Inflated scores:** Giving high scores without evidence
- **Activity bias:** High activity doesn't mean high quality
- **Missing advanced signals:** Not checking velocity or authority
- **Uniform scoring:** Everything clustering at 5-6 suggests shallow evaluation

---

## Notes

- Scoring is based on observable signals, not guesses
- When signal is unclear, note uncertainty in breakdown
- This output goes to 1.3-A Source Validator for review
- Checkpoint 1 requires human approval of scored sources before scraping
