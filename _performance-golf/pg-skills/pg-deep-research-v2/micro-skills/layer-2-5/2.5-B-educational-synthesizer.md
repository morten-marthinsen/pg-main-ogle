## Purpose

This skill creates **educational pairs** - sequences that explain WHY a problem exists and HOW to solve it. These pairs are critical for:
1. Educational marketing content
2. Authority building
3. Mechanism explanation
4. Objection handling (when prospects believe wrong root cause)

---

## CRITICAL: Full Analysis Requirement

**The 25-pair minimum is the OUTPUT, not the INPUT.**

This skill MUST:
1. **Analyze ALL ROOT_CAUSE quotes** to identify the most common "why it hurts" explanations
2. **Analyze ALL COMPETITOR_MECHANISM quotes** to identify the most common "how it works" solutions
3. **Analyze ALL SOLUTIONS_TRIED quotes** to identify what prospects have already tried
4. **Tally counts** for each explanation type and solution type
5. **Select TOP pairs** that represent the BIGGEST patterns

**Goal:** Identify the BIGGEST Why and BIGGEST How based on data analysis:
- Which root cause explanation appears MOST frequently?
- Which mechanism/solution is MOST commonly mentioned?
- Which WHY→HOW sequences are MOST relevant to the product?

The 25+ pairs should represent the STRONGEST, most data-supported educational sequences.

## Sequence Types

### Type 1: Mechanical Cause → Equipment Solution
- WHY: Technical explanation of what causes the problem
- HOW: Equipment feature that addresses the mechanical cause
- Example: "Open face at impact" → "Heel-weighted CG closes face"

### Type 2: Mental Barrier → Confidence Restoration
- WHY: Psychological explanation of why confidence is lost
- HOW: How the product restores confidence
- Example: "Fear of right side causes tension" → "Visually confidence-inspiring design"

### Type 3: Failed Solution → Working Solution
- WHY: Why common solutions (lessons, practice) don't work
- HOW: Why equipment approach succeeds
- Example: "Lessons didn't fix it" → "Equipment removes the trigger"

### Type 4: Market Insight → Opportunity
- WHY: Market-wide problem (96% want to fix slice)
- HOW: Product addresses underserved need
- Example: "96% slice, only 8% draw" → "Draw-bias technology for the masses"

### Type 5: Effort Problem → Forgiveness Solution
- WHY: Overswinging/effort causes inconsistency
- HOW: Forgiving design accommodates effort variations
- Example: "Swinging too hard opens face" → "Larger MOI forgives mishits"

## Matching Algorithm

### Step 1: Identify WHY Quotes
From ROOT_CAUSE bucket, identify quotes that explain:
- Mechanical causes (swing path, face angle)
- Mental causes (fear, anxiety, tension)
- Equipment mismatches (wrong shaft, wrong loft)
- Market gaps (what's missing)

### Step 2: Identify HOW Quotes
From HOPE, COMPETITOR_MECHANISM, and SOLUTIONS_TRIED buckets, identify quotes that explain:
- Equipment solutions that work
- Mechanism explanations
- Success stories with clear mechanism link
- What finally fixed the problem

### Step 3: Create Logical Sequences
Match WHY to HOW where:
- The HOW directly addresses the WHY
- The sequence creates an "aha moment"
- The narrative is copy-ready

### Step 4: Prioritize Educational Value
GOLD pairs have:
- Clear cause-effect relationship
- Quotable language
- Authority source (research, expert)
- Directly applicable to product mechanism
