# Micro-Skill 3.1-C: Proactive Objection Handler

**Layer:** 3 - Opportunity Surfacing
**Purpose:** Proactively identify every possible objection and prepare CPT-validated responses for each opportunity/angle suggested
**Dependency:** All Layer 2 analysis complete, 3.1-A Opportunity Scorer output
**Version:** 1.0
**PRD Reference:** [[RESEARCH-PRD#Section 4.3]]
**E5 Reference:** Todd Brown's 25 Core Copywriting Principles - Principle 25: Address All Objections

---

## CRITICAL: The Best Time to Handle an Objection Is BEFORE It Comes Up

**Principle 25:** "All the reasons they may have for not buying, all doubts, questions, and possible objections need to be addressed in your marketing."

Every opportunity, gap, or angle suggested MUST include proactive objection handling. If you make a claim they might doubt, address it HEAD ON.

For every suggestion, you must provide:
1. **Claim** - What you're asserting
2. **Proof** - Supporting evidence
3. **Benefits Stacked** - Why this matters to them
4. **Objections Answered** - Every possible pushback addressed

## Objection Excavation Process

### Step 1: **Excavate** All Possible Objections

For each opportunity/suggestion, **ruminate** on every possible pushback.

```yaml
objection_sources:
  - Beliefs from 2.2-F (resistance beliefs)
  - Skepticism patterns from quotes
  - Solutions tried (why those didn't work fear)
  - Competitor exposure (what they've heard before)
  - Category stigmas (game improvement = beginner)
  - Price/value concerns
  - Trust/credibility gaps
  - Personalization fears ("will it work for ME?")

objection_categories:
  belief_based: "Pre-existing beliefs that contradict your claim"
  skepticism: "General distrust of marketing/claims"
  identity_stigma: "Fear of what using product says about them"
  credibility: "Doubts about brand/company"
  performance_concern: "Worry about trade-offs"
  personalization: "Doubt it applies to their situation"
  price: "Value/cost concerns"
  general_resistance: "Generic reasons not to act"
```

### Step 2: **Calibrate** by Depth

**Scrutinize** to separate surface-level from deep-seated objections.

```yaml
depth_levels:
  SURFACE: "Easy to address with simple proof"
  MODERATE: "Requires explanation and evidence"
  DEEP: "Requires belief disruption or identity work"

critical_objections:
  - Any objection 50%+ of market has
  - Any objection that kills sale instantly if not addressed
  - Any identity/stigma objection
  - Any objection rooted in strong pre-existing belief
```

### Step 3: **Synthesize** CPT Response for Each

For every objection, **distill** a complete response using CPT structure.

```yaml
cpt_structure:
  CLAIM: "What you're asserting in response"
  PROOF: "Evidence supporting your response"
  BENEFIT: "Why your answer benefits them"

response_components:
  acknowledge: "Show you understand their concern"
  disrupt_or_address: "Challenge or directly answer"
  bridge: "Connect to your solution"
  proof: "Provide evidence"
  risk_reversal: "Remove remaining concern"
```

### Step 4: Determine Handling Type

Assign appropriate handling strategy.

```yaml
handling_types:
  BELIEF_DISRUPTION: "Must change a false belief"
  ACKNOWLEDGE_AND_DIFFERENTIATE: "Accept concern, show why you're different"
  EXPLICIT_DIFFERENTIATION: "Directly contrast with category/competitor"
  TURN_WEAKNESS_TO_STRENGTH: "Reframe potential negative as positive"
  DIRECT_ADDRESS_WITH_PROOF: "Head-on answer with evidence"
  SCOPE_AND_PROVE: "Limit claim, then prove limited claim"
  PERSONALIZE_AND_GUARANTEE: "Address individual concern with risk reversal"
  VALUE_REFRAME: "Change how they think about price/value"
```

### Step 5: Specify Copy Placement

Where should each objection be addressed?

```yaml
placement_strategy:
  EARLY: "Objections that kill interest if not addressed immediately"
  MID: "Objections that arise during consideration"
  LATE: "Objections that surface before purchase decision"

principle: "Address objections BEFORE they come up, not after"
```

### Step 6: Stack Objection Summary

Create overview of objection landscape.

```yaml
summary_elements:
  - Total objections count
  - Distribution by category
  - Critical objections (must address)
  - Copy structure recommendation
  - Proof requirements summary
```

## Objection Handling Principles

### Principle 1: Address BEFORE It Comes Up

Don't wait for them to think of the objection. Preempt it.

```yaml
wrong: "If you're thinking this might be a gimmick..."
right: "I know what you might be thinking - another equipment promise. Let me address that head on..."
```

### Principle 2: Acknowledge, Don't Dismiss

Show you understand their concern before addressing it.

```yaml
wrong: "That's not a valid concern..."
right: "That's a fair concern. Here's why it doesn't apply here..."
```

### Principle 3: Be Specific in Responses

Vague responses don't overcome objections.

```yaml
wrong: "This is different from other products"
right: "Unlike Cleveland's ZipCore which still allows digging, the ONE.1's beveled leading edge physically cannot dig in - here's why..."
```

### Principle 4: Stack Proof

Multiple proof points are more convincing than one.

```yaml
single_proof: "Testimonials show it works"
stacked_proof: "The physics show it works, the testimonials confirm it, and if you don't experience it yourself, full refund."
```

### Principle 5: End with Risk Reversal

The ultimate objection handler is removing risk.

```yaml
pattern: |
  Even if you're still skeptical, here's the deal:
  [Strong guarantee]. If [doesn't work for you],
  [consequence removed]. The only risk is [not trying].
```

## Failure Protocol

```
IF objections too few:
  - Return to belief excavation
  - Consider category stigmas
  - Think about trust gaps
  - Review competitor exposure

IF responses weak:
  - Add more proof elements
  - Strengthen risk reversal
  - Be more specific in addressing
  - Ensure acknowledge + proof structure

IF critical objections not addressed:
  - Sales will fail
  - Return to this skill and complete
  - Flag in output for human review
```

## Notes

- Every claim you make creates potential objections
- The biggest objections are often the ones they don't voice
- Belief-based objections require more than just proof - they require disruption
- Identity/stigma objections are silent killers - people won't tell you this is why they didn't buy
- Strong guarantees handle many objections at once
- If you don't address an objection, it sits in their mind and kills the sale
