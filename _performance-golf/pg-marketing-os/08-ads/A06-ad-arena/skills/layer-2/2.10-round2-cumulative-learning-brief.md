# 2.10: Round 2 Cumulative Analytical Brief

## Purpose
Generate the Cumulative Analytical Brief combining Round 1 and Round 2 learnings. Identify persistent strengths across rounds. Identify persistent weaknesses across rounds. Note techniques that produced the biggest improvements. This feeds into Round 2 (FINAL) as the final learning input.

## Inputs
- 2.5-round1-learning-brief output (Round 1 learnings)
- 2.9-round2-scoring output (Round 2 scores and improvement tracking)
- 2.6-round2-persona-evaluation output (Round 2 evaluations with technique absorption notes)

## Process
```
STEP 1: Load Round 1 Analytical Brief and Round 2 results
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.5-round1-learning-brief.md
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.9-round2-scoring.md
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.6-round2-persona-evaluation.md

STEP 2: Identify persistent strengths
  - Which concepts scored well in BOTH rounds?
  - Which criteria are consistently strong across personas and rounds?
  - Which techniques produced reliable improvements?

STEP 3: Identify persistent weaknesses
  - Which weaknesses survived both Round 1 and Round 2?
  - These are the DEEPEST issues -- the ones critique-revision couldn't fix
  - These are what Round 2 (FINAL) must target most aggressively

STEP 4: Track technique effectiveness
  - Which techniques from Round 1 Analytical Brief produced the biggest improvements?
  - Which techniques were absorbed but didn't improve scores?
  - This informs Round 2 (FINAL) guidance

STEP 5: Generate persona-specific Round 2 (FINAL) guidance
  FOR EACH persona:
    - What worked in Round 2 (keep doing)
    - What persistent weakness remains (target this in Round 2 (FINAL))
    - Voice preservation reminder (this is the FINAL round -- maintain perspective)
    - "This is your BEST evaluation -- maximum quality expected"

STEP 6: Campaign-level insights
  - What is the Arena learning about what makes effective ads for THIS campaign?
  - Are there upstream issues (weak hooks, generic scripts, mismatched visuals)?
  - What patterns are emerging across all concepts?
```
