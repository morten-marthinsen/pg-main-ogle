# 2.5.4: Tone Consistency Check

## Purpose
Verify all variants for a given concept maintain consistent tone and voice. Hook swaps should feel like they belong to the same campaign even though they use different entry points. CTA variants should maintain brand voice. Platform adaptations should adapt tone appropriately (not randomly). Check against Soul.md voice constraints if loaded.

## Inputs
- All variants per concept (from Layer 1 and Layer 2 outputs)
- Soul.md voice constraints (from 0.7, if loaded)
- Persona voice specimens (from 0.7)
- Variant inventory (from 2.4)

## Process
```
FOR EACH concept:

STEP 1: ESTABLISH the tone baseline.
  - From the base copy: what is the established tone?
  - Dimensions to evaluate:
    a. Formality level (casual / conversational / professional / formal)
    b. Energy level (calm / moderate / energetic / intense)
    c. Vocabulary register (simple / moderate / sophisticated)
    d. Sentence rhythm (short punchy / mixed / flowing)
    e. Emotional register (empathetic / authoritative / urgent / playful)
  - From Soul.md (if loaded):
    f. Voice register constraints
    g. Anti-voice patterns (FORBIDDEN)
    h. Pacing signature

STEP 2: EVALUATE each hook swap for tone consistency.
  - Does this hook sound like it belongs to the same campaign?
  - A dramatic hook paired with a calm body is INCONSISTENT
  - A humorous hook paired with a serious body is INCONSISTENT
  - Different hook TYPE is expected; different TONE within the same
    campaign voice is a violation

STEP 3: EVALUATE each CTA variant for tone consistency.
  - Does the CTA maintain the campaign voice?
  - An aggressive CTA on a soft-sell body is INCONSISTENT
  - A casual CTA on a formal body is INCONSISTENT

STEP 4: EVALUATE platform adaptations for APPROPRIATE tone shifts.
  - Platform adaptations SHOULD shift tone (TikTok = more casual)
  - But the shift should be intentional and consistent:
    - TikTok adaptation of a formal brand = slightly less formal, not slang
    - YouTube adaptation of a casual brand = slightly more polished, not stiff
  - RANDOM tone shifts are violations
  - APPROPRIATE platform-native shifts are expected

STEP 5: CHECK against Soul.md anti-voice patterns (if loaded).
  - Any variant using anti-voice patterns = VIOLATION
  - Any variant exceeding emotional ceiling = VIOLATION
  - Any variant below emotional floor = VIOLATION

STEP 6: PRODUCE the Tone Consistency Audit.
```
