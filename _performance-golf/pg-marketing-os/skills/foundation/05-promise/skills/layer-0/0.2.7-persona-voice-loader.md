### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `strategic` arena mode, prioritize **teaching and philosophy specimens** that show each writer's THINKING process, not just their selling voice.

For promise specifically, prioritize specimens that demonstrate how each writer **constructs and calibrates promises**. The promise skill needs to find the biggest promise that can be credibly backed by available proof — the sweet spot between exciting and believable. The best specimens for this skill show:

1. **Promise construction** — How does the writer build a specific, measurable, exciting promise?
2. **Proof calibration** — How does the writer ensure promises don't exceed what can be proven?
3. **Specificity techniques** — How does the writer make promises concrete rather than vague?
4. **Promise escalation** — How does the writer push promises as far as proof allows without crossing the line?

Ogilvy's ads are strongest here for research-backed credible promises — every Ogilvy claim is anchored to specific evidence. Bencivenga's proof-first philosophy (particularly Maxim 10 and Maxim 12) constrains promise to proof ceiling and shows how the strongest promises are the ones that can be MOST proven. Makepeace's controls demonstrate promise escalation techniques where the promise pushes right to the edge of proof without exceeding it.
