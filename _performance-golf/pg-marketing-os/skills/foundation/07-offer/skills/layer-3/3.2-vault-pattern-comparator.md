# Skill 3.2: Vault Pattern Comparator

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 3 (Validation & Selection)
**Type:** Empirical Comparison
**Dependencies:** All Layer 1 + Layer 2 outputs, vault_offer_patterns.json (0.2)
**Output:** vault_comparison.json
**Persona:** Dr. Alena Vasquez (Evidence Validation) + Marcus Webster (Pattern Synthesis)

---

## Purpose

Compare the constructed offer against empirical patterns from the TIER1_EXTRACTIONS vault — 139+ real-world direct response offers. This isn't about copying what worked. It's about stress-testing our offer against what the market has actually seen, identifying where we're aligned with proven patterns, where we're innovating, and where we might have blind spots.

---

## The Principle

**"Innovation without awareness of the baseline is just ignorance. Know what works, then choose where to differ — and know WHY you're differing."**

---

## Comparison Process

### Step 1: Structural Comparison

```
COMPARE our offer structure against vault patterns:

METRIC 1: Value Stack Size
  Our offer: [X] core components + [Y] bonuses
  Vault average: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Vault range: [min - max]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / ABOVE / BELOW
  Implication: [what this means for market expectations]

METRIC 2: Bonus Count
  Our offer: [X] bonuses
  Vault average: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / ABOVE / BELOW
  Implication: [are we over or under the market norm?]

METRIC 3: Value-to-Price Ratio
  Our offer: [ratio]
  Vault average: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / ABOVE / BELOW
  Implication: [is our ratio competitive?]

METRIC 4: Guarantee Type
  Our offer: [type]
  Vault most common: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / DIFFERENT
  Implication: [are we meeting or breaking market expectations?]

METRIC 5: Payment Structure
  Our offer: [structure]
  Vault most common: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / DIFFERENT
  Implication: [are we using proven payment approaches?]

METRIC 6: CTA Count
  Our offer: [count]
  Vault average: [from vault_offer_patterns]
  Assessment: ALIGNED / ABOVE / BELOW

COMPILE: structural_comparison_table
```

### Step 2: Presentation Flow Comparison

```
COMPARE our offer presentation sequence against vault patterns:

OUR FLOW:
  [List our presentation sequence from value_demonstration_script]

VAULT MOST COMMON FLOW:
  [From vault_offer_patterns → presentation_flow_patterns]

DEVIATIONS:
  For each point where our flow differs from the vault pattern:
    - What did we do differently?
    - Why? (strategic choice or oversight?)
    - What's the risk of deviating here?
    - What's the upside of deviating here?

TRANSITION LANGUAGE COMPARISON:
  Compare our transition phrases to vault transition_language library
  Are we using proven transitions or untested ones?
  Identify any vault transition phrases we should consider adopting
```

### Step 3: Language Pattern Comparison

```
COMPARE our offer language against vault language patterns:

VALUE DEMONSTRATION LANGUAGE:
  Our "if all it did was..." phrasing vs vault exemplars
  Assessment: Stronger / comparable / weaker than vault top performers

PRICE PRESENTATION LANGUAGE:
  Our anchor-to-reveal sequence vs vault patterns
  Assessment: More sophisticated / standard / less effective

URGENCY/SCARCITY LANGUAGE:
  Our urgency phrases vs vault urgency library
  Our scarcity phrases vs vault scarcity library
  Assessment: Fresh and genuine / standard / overused

GUARANTEE LANGUAGE:
  Our guarantee statement vs vault guarantee patterns
  Assessment: Stronger risk reversal / standard / weaker

CONSEQUENCE AMPLIFICATION LANGUAGE:
  Our pain reminders vs vault consequence patterns
  Assessment: More visceral / standard / generic

FOR each category:
  NOTE standout vault examples we could learn from
  NOTE where our language is superior to vault patterns
  NOTE where our language falls short
```

### Step 4: Exemplar Benchmarking

```
FROM vault_offer_patterns → top 10 exemplars:

FOR each of the top 3 exemplars most relevant to our niche/offer type:

  DIRECT COMPARISON:
    Their dream outcome vs ours:
      [Exemplar] | [Ours] | Winner and why

    Their value stack vs ours:
      [Exemplar components] | [Our components] | Assessment

    Their bonus strategy vs ours:
      [Exemplar bonuses] | [Our bonuses] | Assessment

    Their guarantee vs ours:
      [Exemplar guarantee] | [Our guarantee] | Assessment

    Their price architecture vs ours:
      [Exemplar pricing] | [Our pricing] | Assessment

  WHAT WE CAN LEARN:
    Specific techniques or approaches from this exemplar
    that could strengthen our offer

  WHERE WE'RE STRONGER:
    Specific areas where our offer exceeds this exemplar
```

### Step 5: Anti-Exemplar Check

```
FROM vault_offer_patterns → anti-exemplars (bottom 5):

FOR each anti-exemplar:

  SIMILARITY CHECK:
    Does our offer share ANY characteristics with anti-exemplars?

    Red flags:
    - Similar sparse value stacks
    - Similar generic naming
    - Similar weak guarantees
    - Similar vague outcomes
    - Similar missing components

  FOR each similarity found:
    FLAG with specific description
    RECOMMEND remediation
```

### Step 6: Innovation Assessment

```
IDENTIFY where our offer INTENTIONALLY differs from vault patterns:

FOR each deviation:
  CLASSIFY:
    STRATEGIC INNOVATION: We're doing something different because our
    analysis/framework suggests a better approach
    → Document the reasoning

    ACCIDENTAL DEVIATION: We didn't realize we were breaking a pattern
    → Evaluate whether to align with the pattern or keep the deviation

    MARKET ADVANCEMENT: The vault represents older patterns and we're
    evolving beyond them
    → Document why this is an advancement, not just different

INNOVATION RISK ASSESSMENT:
  For each strategic innovation:
    What's the UPSIDE if it works?
    What's the DOWNSIDE if it doesn't?
    Is there a way to test before full commitment?
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. All 6 structural metrics compared with vault data
2. Presentation flow compared with deviations documented
3. Language compared across 5 categories
4. At least 3 exemplars benchmarked against our offer
5. Anti-exemplar similarity check performed
6. Every deviation classified as strategic/accidental/advancement
7. Blind spots identified (things the vault has that we forgot)
8. Comparison is honest — not skewed to make our offer look better
