# Layer 2.3 — Benefit Dimensionalizer

## Purpose

Create multi-layered benefits for each CPB chunk across three dimensions: functional, dimensionalized, and emotional. Benefits answer the prospect's unspoken question: "So what does this mean for ME?" They transform claims from abstract facts into personal relevance.

**Primary Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter — Master Copy Composite
**Secondary Persona:** Jake Torres — Viral Content Architect

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Understand the Three Benefit Dimensions

Benefits are NOT features. They are what the feature/claim MEANS for the prospect's life.

**Functional Benefit:**
- What the claim directly enables them to DO
- Concrete, tangible, measurable
- Example: "So you can lower your blood pressure without medication"
- Example: "So you can add 15-20 yards to your drive"
- Example: "So you can generate an additional $2,000-$5,000 per month"

**Dimensionalized Benefit:**
- How the functional benefit changes their DAILY EXPERIENCE
- Vivid, sensory, situational
- Takes the functional benefit and paints a picture of what life looks like
- Example: "Imagine walking into your doctor's office and seeing the surprise on her face when she reads your numbers"
- Example: "Picture yourself on the first tee, knowing you're about to outdrive everyone in your foursome"
- Example: "Think about what it would feel like to check your account and see money coming in while you slept"

**Emotional Benefit:**
- How achieving this makes them FEEL
- Identity-level transformation
- Example: "Finally feel in control of your health — not at the mercy of a broken system"
- Example: "Experience the quiet confidence of knowing your game is at a level most golfers only dream about"
- Example: "Feel the freedom of knowing your financial future doesn't depend on a single paycheck"

### Step 2: Draft Benefits for Each Chunk

For each CPB chunk, using the claim as the foundation:

1. **Read the claim** — what is being asserted?
2. **Ask "So what?"** — the answer is the functional benefit
3. **Ask "What does that look like?"** — the answer is the dimensionalized benefit
4. **Ask "How does that make them feel?"** — the answer is the emotional benefit

### Step 3: Apply Benefit Transition Phrases

Benefits should flow naturally from proof. Use transition phrases:
- "So you can..." (functional)
- "So you will..." (functional/dimensionalized)
- "Which means..." (functional → dimensionalized)
- "Imagine..." (dimensionalized)
- "Picture..." (dimensionalized)
- "Think about what it would feel like to..." (dimensionalized → emotional)
- "Finally..." (emotional)
- "For the first time..." (emotional)

### Step 4: Connect Benefits to Promise Architecture

Every benefit should echo some aspect of the primary or supporting promises. Benefits are where the promise comes alive in specific, tangible terms.

- **Primary promise echo:** At least 2-3 chunks should have benefits that directly reference the primary promise
- **Supporting promise coverage:** Remaining chunks should connect to supporting promises
- **No orphan benefits:** Every benefit should connect back to the promise architecture

### Step 5: Apply Niche-Specific Benefit Language

Benefits must sound like what the PROSPECT would say, not what a marketer would write:

| Niche | Avoid | Use |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Health | "Optimize your cellular function" | "Feel 10 years younger, with energy to play with your grandkids" |
| Wealth | "Maximize your ROI potential" | "Watch your account grow while you sleep" |
| Golf | "Improve your biomechanical efficiency" | "Hit it longer and straighter than you have in years" |
| Self-improvement | "Unlock your cognitive potential" | "Wake up excited to attack the day instead of dreading it" |

### Step 6: Score Each Benefit Triplet

| Dimension | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Functional clarity | 0.25 | 1-10 | Is the functional benefit specific and tangible? |
| Dimensionalized vividness | 0.25 | 1-10 | Can the prospect visualize this in their life? |
| Emotional resonance | 0.25 | 1-10 | Does this connect to deep desires/fears? |
| Promise connection | 0.15 | 1-10 | Does this echo the promise architecture? |
| Prospect language | 0.10 | 1-10 | Would the prospect say this, not a marketer? |

**Minimum threshold:** Weighted average ≥ 7.0/10

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Every chunk has all three benefit dimensions (functional + dimensionalized + emotional)
- [ ] All benefit triplets score ≥ 7.0 weighted average
- [ ] At least 2 chunks directly echo the primary promise
- [ ] Benefits use prospect language, not marketer language
- [ ] Dimensionalized benefits contain sensory/visual elements
- [ ] Emotional benefits connect to identity-level transformation
- [ ] Transition phrases used naturally (not forced)
- [ ] No "feature masquerading as benefit" (benefits answer "So what?" not "What is it?")
- [ ] Aggregate all_above_threshold = true

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **Feature as benefit:** "You get a 7-module system" — that's a feature. "You get a simple step-by-step path that eliminates guesswork" — that's a benefit.
- **Generic emotional:** "You'll feel great" — too vague. "You'll feel the quiet confidence of knowing your health is on the right track for the first time in years" — specific.
- **Missing dimensionalization:** Going straight from functional to emotional without painting the picture of daily experience — the middle layer is where belief becomes vivid.
- **Marketer voice:** "Experience unprecedented results" — no prospect talks like this. "Finally see the numbers your doctor said were impossible" — that's how they think.
- **Promise orphan:** Benefits that don't connect back to any promise — they float disconnected from the campaign's core assertion.
