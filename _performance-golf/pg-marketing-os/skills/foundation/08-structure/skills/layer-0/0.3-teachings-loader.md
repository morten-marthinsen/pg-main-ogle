## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Index E5 Campaign Argument Principles

From M5A (Engineering Your Campaign Argument), extract:
- **Core principle:** The campaign argument proves the campaign thesis, NOT the product
- **Mindset:** Think like a prosecutor, not a salesperson
- **Function:** Education-based marketing content demonstrates mechanism value
- **Scope:** The argument transforms attention → engagement → belief
- **Boundary:** Education proves thesis, not showcases expertise

### Step 2: Index CPB Chunk Framework

From M5C (Structure of the Campaign Argument), extract:
- **CPB Chunk definition:** Claim + Proof + Benefit = one unit of argument
- **Assembly process:**
  1. Determine your CPB chunks (from prospect gap questions)
  2. Order your CPB chunks (logical sequence)
  3. Fill your CPB chunks (claims, proof, benefits)
- **Core insight:** CPB chunks are the delivery mechanism for education-based content

### Step 3: Index Prospect Gap Questions

From M5D (Determining and Ordering), extract the **9 core gap questions:**

| # | Gap Question | Gap Type |
|---|-------------|----------|
| 1 | What kind of results can I expect? | results |
| 2 | How is this different from everything else? | differentiation |
| 3 | Why is this better than the common mechanisms? | superiority |
| 4 | How and why does this work? | mechanism |
| 5 | How easy is it? | ease |
| 6 | How fast does this work? | speed |
| 7 | Is it safe? Is it predictable? | safety |
| 8 | Will this work for me? | personalization |
| 9 | How can I get started using this? | access |

**Ordering rules:**
- "How can I get started?" ALWAYS comes last
- All other questions can be ordered based on logical flow or strategic emphasis
- Better to have more gap questions than fewer (excess can be trimmed)

### Step 4: Index Chunk Filling Rules

From M5E (Filling Your CPB Chunks), extract:

**Claim Rules:**
1. Avoid statements that sound like opinion — use facts and specifics
2. All claims must be true — no fabrication
3. All claims should be specific — specificity is persuasive

**8 Types of Proof Points:**
1. **Demonstration** — Show it working (most powerful)
2. **Reason Why / Because** — "The reason why is..." (most versatile)
3. **Strong Examples** — Specific examples showing results
4. **Before and Afters** — Transformation evidence
5. **Studies / Test Results** — Research backing claims
6. **Statistics** — Data supporting claims
7. **Authoritative Proof** — Quotes from respected sources
8. **Logic Flow** — "If X, then Y" reasoning

**Benefit Presentation:**
- Functional: What it does for them
- Dimensionalized: How it changes their daily experience
- Emotional: How it makes them feel
- Transitions: "So you can...", "So you will...", "Imagine..."

### Step 5: Index Assembly Framework

From M6A (Finishing Your Campaign Argument), extract:

**Simple Segue Template:**
> "Fact is, there's no better way right now for you to [PRIMARY PROMISE] than with [UNIQUE MECHANISM]."

**Assembly Steps:**
1. Paste CPB chunks into document after simple segue
2. Add CPB chunk connectors where desired (not between every chunk)
3. Add SIN segue and SIN offer

**Connector Examples:**
- "And not just that..."
- "And here's more..."
- "And it gets better..."
- "What makes this special?"
- "Here's the amazing part..."

**SIN Segue Template:**
> "By now you see how and why [MECHANISM] is so effective at [RESULT]. And that's why today I want to give you the opportunity to put it to work for you with [PRODUCT NAME]. Let me tell you what it will do for you, how quickly it will work, and everything you get today."

**Read-Aloud Rule:** Always read the campaign argument aloud. Adjust anything that sounds written rather than spoken.

### Step 6: Index Makepeace Logical Argument Framework

From Stage5Bootcamp-Part4, extract:
- **Marketing split:** 75% education/argument, 25% selling/offer
- **Argument structure:** Premises/claims + evidence/proof = conclusion
- **Critical distinction:** A string of claims is NOT an argument — reasons must follow
- **Dual appeal:** People buy on emotion BUT rationalize with logic — need BOTH
- **"Reason why" primacy:** Most powerful and versatile proof method
- **Interrogation method:** Question the mechanism deeply to uncover all claims and supporting reasons
- **Coherence markers:** Words/phrases connecting sentences into continuation of thought
- **Common mistakes:** Generic language, opinion-based claims, "copywriter mode" voice, skipping interrogation

### Step 7: Build Teaching Index

Assemble all indexed principles into a structured object that microskills can query by topic.
