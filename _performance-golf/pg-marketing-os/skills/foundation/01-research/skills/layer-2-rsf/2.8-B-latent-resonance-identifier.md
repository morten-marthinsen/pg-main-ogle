## CONSTRAINTS

```
1. NEVER fabricate emotional states — ALL latent emotions must be INFERRED from
   evidence in classified_quotes.json, belief_excavation.json, or market_narrative.json.
2. NEVER produce fewer than 3 expressed-vs-latent gaps.
3. NEVER assign high confidence (>0.8) to a latent emotion without multiple
   converging evidence points (quotes + behavioral patterns + narrative context).
4. ALWAYS distinguish between EXPRESSED emotions (what they say) and LATENT emotions
   (what they feel but don't say). This distinction is the core value of this skill.
5. ALWAYS produce at least 5 "finally someone said it" candidates.
6. MUST include identity tensions — the gap between who they want to be and who
   they fear they are. This is the deepest layer of resonance.
7. MUST include cultural timing indicators — what collective moods or events
   amplify or suppress specific emotional territories right now.
8. NEVER confuse LATENT with ABSENT. A latent emotion is PRESENT but UNEXPRESSED.
   An absent emotion is not there. Evidence of latent emotion: it shows up in
   language patterns, behavioral choices, and what people DON'T say.
9. ALWAYS provide evidence chains for each latent emotion — the trail of
   inference from expressed data to latent conclusion.
10. MUST produce resonance_summary as a narrative paragraph that downstream
    skills can use directly as creative generation context.
```

## Purpose

Identify the audience's latent emotional landscape — the feelings, beliefs, fears, and desires that are PRESENT but UNEXPRESSED in the audience's conscious messaging. The gap between what people SAY they feel and what they ACTUALLY feel is where resonance lives.

**Why this matters for creativity:** Resonant surprise works by naming something the audience feels but hasn't heard articulated. The "finally someone said it" response — that moment of recognition when someone puts words to your unspoken experience — produces disproportionate emotional impact (up to 400% amplification per gift-giving psychology research). This skill maps the territory of unspoken truths that, when named, would produce that recognition response.

**Downstream consumer:** Skill 05 (Big Ideas) uses this as the resonance anchor for directed incongruity — ensuring creative surprises connect to real, felt emotions rather than being clever-but-empty.

## Processing Logic

### Step 1: Map the Expressed Emotional Layer

```
FROM classified_quotes.json:

FOR each emotional_tag in the corpus:
  COUNT frequency
  MEASURE average intensity_score
  IDENTIFY which buckets it appears in (PAIN, HOPE, etc.)

BUILD expressed_emotion_map:
  {emotion: frequency, average_intensity, bucket_distribution}

RANK by frequency × intensity = expressed_prominence

FROM belief_excavation.json:
  ADD current_beliefs and emotional_beliefs to expressed layer

RESULT: Complete map of what the audience SAYS they feel
        Ranked by how prominently they express it
```

---

### Step 1.5 (RSF v2.0): Calculate Emotional Momentum

```
PURPOSE:
Track whether emotions are increasing, stable, or declining over time.
This determines temporal relevance of resonance targets.

PREREQUISITE:
classified_quotes.json must contain temporal_recency field (v4.0+)
IF temporal_recency not present: WARN and skip momentum calculation

MOMENTUM CALCULATION LOGIC:

FOR each emotion (expressed AND latent) identified:

  STEP 1: Count quotes by temporal bucket

  current_quotes = quotes mentioning emotion WHERE temporal_recency = "current"
  recent_quotes = quotes mentioning emotion WHERE temporal_recency = "recent"
  historical_quotes = quotes mentioning emotion WHERE temporal_recency IN ("aging", "old")

  STEP 2: Calculate frequency ratios

  # Normalize by total quotes in each bucket to account for uneven distribution
  current_frequency = current_quotes / total_current_quotes_in_corpus
  recent_frequency = recent_quotes / total_recent_quotes_in_corpus
  historical_frequency = historical_quotes / total_historical_quotes_in_corpus

  STEP 3: Determine trend

  current_to_historical_ratio = current_frequency / historical_frequency
    # Handle division by zero: if historical = 0, treat as "increasing"

  IF current_to_historical_ratio >= 1.25:
    trend = "increasing"
    temporal_relevance = "high"
  ELSE IF current_to_historical_ratio >= 0.75:
    trend = "stable"
    temporal_relevance = "evergreen"
  ELSE:
    trend = "declining"
    temporal_relevance = "declining"

  STEP 4: Calculate trend confidence

  total_data_points = current_quotes + recent_quotes + historical_quotes

  IF total_data_points >= 20:
    trend_confidence = 0.9
  ELSE IF total_data_points >= 10:
    trend_confidence = 0.7
  ELSE IF total_data_points >= 5:
    trend_confidence = 0.5
  ELSE:
    trend_confidence = 0.3
    # Low confidence — trend may not be reliable

  STEP 5: Calculate intensity trends (optional enhancement)

  current_intensity_avg = AVG(emotional_intensity) for current_quotes
  historical_intensity_avg = AVG(emotional_intensity) for historical_quotes

  IF current_intensity_avg > historical_intensity_avg + 1:
    # Emotion is not just more frequent, but more intense
    # This strengthens the "increasing" signal
    BOOST trend_confidence by 0.1

  STEP 6: Record in emotional_momentum section

  ADD to output:
  {
    emotion: [emotion name]
    expressed_or_latent: [type]
    trend: [increasing/stable/declining]
    trend_confidence: [0-1]
    evidence: {
      current_frequency: [normalized count]
      recent_frequency: [normalized count]
      historical_frequency: [normalized count]
      current_intensity_avg: [avg]
      historical_intensity_avg: [avg]
    }
    temporal_relevance: [high/evergreen/declining]
    momentum_notes: [explanation of trend]
  }

RESULT: Complete emotional momentum map
        Used to weight FSSIT candidates and inform generation
```

### Applying Momentum to FSSIT Candidates

```
AFTER generating FSSIT candidates (Step 4):

FOR each FSSIT candidate:

  LOOKUP target_latent_emotion in emotional_momentum

  IF found:
    SET emotional_momentum = trend
    SET temporal_relevance = temporal_relevance

    # Adjust recognition strength based on momentum
    IF trend = "increasing":
      momentum_adjusted_strength = MIN(predicted_recognition_strength + 1, 10)
    ELSE IF trend = "stable":
      momentum_adjusted_strength = predicted_recognition_strength
    ELSE IF trend = "declining":
      momentum_adjusted_strength = MAX(predicted_recognition_strength - 1, 1)
      # Flag declining emotions for human review
      ADD note: "Target emotion showing declining momentum — validate relevance"

  ELSE:
    SET emotional_momentum = "unknown"
    SET temporal_relevance = "unknown"
    momentum_adjusted_strength = predicted_recognition_strength
    ADD note: "No momentum data available for target emotion"

SORTING PRIORITY for FSSIT candidates:

PRIMARY: momentum_adjusted_strength (descending)
SECONDARY: predicted_recognition_strength (descending)
TERTIARY: temporal_relevance = "high" first, then "evergreen", then "declining"

RESULT: FSSIT candidates ranked by temporally-adjusted resonance potential
```

### Step 2: Detect Latent Emotions Through Gap Analysis

```
ANALYSIS METHOD 1: LANGUAGE PATTERN DETECTION
FOR each expressed emotion:
  EXAMINE the language used in quotes:
  - Is there hedging? ("I guess I'm frustrated" → deeper emotion underneath)
  - Is there deflection? ("It's not a big deal, but..." → it IS a big deal)
  - Is there humor/sarcasm? ("Haha, yeah I've basically given up" → real resignation)
  - Is there anger masking hurt? ("These companies are scammers" → "I feel foolish for trusting")

  IF hedging/deflection/masking detected:
    INFER latent emotion beneath expressed one
    RECORD evidence chain

ANALYSIS METHOD 2: ABSENCE DETECTION
FROM the complete emotional spectrum expected for this audience:
  IDENTIFY emotions that SHOULD be present but are NOT expressed:
  - Shame (often present but rarely named)
  - Envy of others who succeed (felt but socially unacceptable to express)
  - Grief for lost time/money/identity (felt but rarely framed this way)
  - Relief-seeking (desire to stop trying rather than succeed)

  FOR each absent emotion:
    SEARCH for indirect evidence:
    - Behavioral indicators (quitting, avoiding, reducing engagement)
    - Circumlocution (talking AROUND the emotion without naming it)
    - Projection (attributing the emotion to others or to external causes)

  IF indirect evidence found:
    CLASSIFY as latent emotion
    RECORD evidence chain

ANALYSIS METHOD 3: INTENSITY MISMATCH
FROM transformation_pairs.json:
  IDENTIFY pairs where:
  - Pain emotional_contrast_score is very high (>8)
  - BUT expressed language is understated or clinical

  This mismatch suggests the audience FEELS more than they SAY.
  The gap between behavioral intensity and verbal expression = latent territory.

ANALYSIS METHOD 4: NARRATIVE CONTEXT
FROM market_narrative.json:
  EXAMINE customer_journey.stuck_point:
  - What beliefs do they hold at the stuck point?
  - What emotions MUST be present given these beliefs?
  - Which of those emotions are NOT in the expressed data?

  EXAMINE hidden_truth:
  - If the hidden truth were told to the audience, what emotional
    response would it trigger?
  - Which of those triggered emotions are currently latent?

RESULT: List of latent emotions with evidence chains and confidence scores
```

### Step 3: Identify Identity Tensions

```
FROM belief_excavation.json + now_after_grid.json:

IDENTITY TENSION = gap between:
  a) WHO THEY WANT TO BE (aspirational identity)
  b) WHO THEY FEAR THEY ARE (feared identity)

FOR each tension:
  EXTRACT from current_beliefs:
    - Self-efficacy beliefs ("I'm capable/not capable")
    - Comparison beliefs ("Others can do this, why can't I?")
    - Identity beliefs ("I'm the kind of person who...")

  EXTRACT from now_after_grid:
    - Current state identity markers
    - Desired state identity markers
    - The GAP between them

  SCORE tension_intensity (1-10):
    - How much does this gap cause suffering?
    - How frequently does it surface in quotes?
    - How central is it to their sense of self?

COMMON IDENTITY TENSIONS (market-agnostic patterns):
  - "Competent person" vs. "Someone who can't figure this out"
  - "Smart investor" vs. "Someone who keeps getting fooled"
  - "Dedicated practitioner" vs. "Someone who can't commit"
  - "Independent thinker" vs. "Someone who needs hand-holding"

RESULT: Identity tension map with intensity scores
```

### Step 4: Generate "Finally Someone Said It" Candidates

```
FOR each expressed-vs-latent gap AND each unnamed emotion AND each identity tension:

GENERATE a statement that would:
  a) Name the latent emotion DIRECTLY
  b) Use language the audience would recognize as THEIR experience
  c) Produce a recognition response: "Yes. That's exactly it."

CANDIDATE GENERATION RULES:
  - Use second person ("You've probably..." not "People often...")
  - Be specific enough to feel personal, general enough to be widespread
  - Name the emotion, don't explain it (showing > telling)
  - Include the RELIEF of having it named (not just the pain of having it)

SCORE predicted_recognition_strength (1-10):
  - How widespread is this latent emotion? (prevalence)
  - How deeply felt is it? (intensity)
  - How unexpressed is it? (latency — higher latency = stronger recognition)
  - How well does this statement capture it? (precision)

SCORE narrative_reorganization_potential (1-10):
  - Does this statement EXPLAIN why past attempts failed? (+2-3)
  - Does it REMOVE self-blame by providing external cause? (+1-2)
  - Does it create "I always knew it" recognition? (+2-3)
  - Does it ONLY address current state without past relevance? (-2)

  NR SCALE:
    1-3: Resonates with present but doesn't reorganize past
    4-6: Explains some past failures — partial narrative shift
    7-10: Explains entire domain history — "THAT'S why nothing worked"

  CRITICAL: DOMAIN-SPECIFIC NARRATIVE
    NR applies to their story WITHIN THE DOMAIN the product addresses:
    - Financial: their investment/retirement history
    - Golf: their swing/game history
    - Health: their diet/wellness history
    - Relationships: their relationship history

    The FSSIT doesn't need to change their whole worldview —
    just their domain-specific worldview.

DESCRIBE past_explaining_power:
  - How specifically does this statement reorganize their DOMAIN-SPECIFIC biographical narrative?
  - What story does it tell about their past IN THIS DOMAIN that makes their struggle make sense?
  - What domain-specific self-concept does it enable? ("I'm not bad at golf, I was missing X")

  DOWNSTREAM IMPACT (CRITICAL):
    High-NR FSSIT candidates (≥7) become PRIORITY starting points for Big Idea generation.
    Big Ideas built from high-NR FSSITs inherit narrative reorganization as foundational,
    not as a validation check. This is why accurate NR scoring here matters.

SPECIFY tone_requirements:
  - Must this be said with empathy? Authority? Solidarity?
  - What tone would cause it to backfire?

SPECIFY risk_if_mishandled:
  - What happens if this is said accusatorily instead of empathetically?
  - What happens if the audience feels exposed rather than understood?

MINIMUM: 5 candidates with recognition_strength >= 6
TARGET: 10 candidates across different emotional territories
```

### Step 5: Assess Cultural Timing

```
FROM market_config.yaml + general cultural context:

ASSESS current_collective_mood:
  - What is the dominant emotional state of this audience RIGHT NOW?
  - What recent events or trends have shaped this mood?
  - Is the audience in a period of hope, fatigue, anger, resignation, seeking?

IDENTIFY active_cultural_tensions:
  - What broader cultural forces affect this audience's receptivity?
  - Trust in institutions? Economic anxiety? Political climate?
  - Industry-specific events (scandals, breakthroughs, trend shifts)?

FOR each latent emotion identified in Steps 2-4:
  ASSESS timing_fit:
  - Would naming this emotion be AMPLIFIED by current conditions?
  - Would it be SUPPRESSED or feel tone-deaf?
  - Is there a temporal window of opportunity?

GENERATE timing_opportunities:
  - Which emotional territories are amplified by current conditions?
  - What messages would land NOW that wouldn't have 6 months ago?

GENERATE timing_risks:
  - Which emotional territories are risky right now?
  - What messages would feel tone-deaf in current climate?
```

### Step 6: Generate Resonance Summary

```
SYNTHESIZE a 3-5 sentence narrative:

FORMAT:
"This audience expresses [expressed emotions] but beneath the surface feels
[latent emotions]. The deepest identity tension is between [aspirational self]
and [feared self]. The strongest 'finally someone said it' territory is
[highest-recognition candidate]. Current cultural conditions [amplify/suppress]
[specific emotional territories]."

This summary is consumed directly by Skill 05 as context for resonance anchoring.
```

  - High-confidence (>0.8) items have 3+ converging evidence points

- MEDIUM (0.5-0.8): 2 evidence types, some behavioral support
- LOW (0.3-0.5): 1 evidence type, plausible inference but limited confirmation
  → Include with note: "confidence: low — inferred from limited evidence"
- BELOW 0.3: Do not include — insufficient evidence

FSSIT CANDIDATE CONFIDENCE:
- If based on HIGH-confidence latent emotion: include at full strength
- If based on MEDIUM-confidence: include with note "validate before use"
- If based on LOW-confidence: include only if recognition_strength >= 7
  (strong predicted response can compensate for uncertain underlying emotion)

WHEN UNSURE about cultural timing:

- Note: "cultural_timing_uncertain — recommend human validation"
