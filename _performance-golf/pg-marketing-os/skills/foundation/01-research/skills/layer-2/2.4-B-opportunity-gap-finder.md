# Skill 2.4-B: Opportunity Gap Finder

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** Strategic Analysis
**Dependencies:** competitor_analysis.json, mechanism_inventory.json, market_narrative.json
**Output:** opportunity_gaps.json

---

## Purpose

Identify white space opportunities in the market where customer needs are unmet, competitor positioning is weak, or mechanisms are underdeveloped. These gaps become the foundation for Big Idea differentiation.

---

## Gap Categories

```
4 TYPES OF OPPORTUNITY GAPS:

1. POSITIONING GAPS
   - Unoccupied market positions
   - Angles no one is taking
   - Frames no one is using

2. MECHANISM GAPS
   - Unexploited mechanism types
   - Underdeveloped explanations
   - Missing scientific backing

3. AUDIENCE GAPS
   - Underserved segments
   - Ignored sub-populations
   - Overlooked journey stages

4. MESSAGING GAPS
   - Unspoken truths
   - Untapped emotions
   - Missing connections
```

## Analysis Logic

### Step 1: Positioning Gap Analysis

```
FROM competitor_analysis.positioning_landscape:

MAP all competitor positions:
- What frames do they use?
- What categories do they occupy?
- What angles do they take?

IDENTIFY unoccupied territory:
- What positions exist that no one owns?
- What frames could apply but aren't used?
- What categories could be created?

FOR each potential gap:
  EVALUATE:
  - Is it strategically valuable?
  - Why hasn't anyone taken it?
  - What would it take to own?

CLASSIFY by potential and difficulty

EXAMPLES:
- No one positions as "execution training" (all say "education")
- No one owns the "trading athlete" frame
- No one focuses on "nervous system" angle
```

### Step 2: Mechanism Gap Analysis

```
FROM mechanism_inventory:

CATALOG all mechanisms in use:
- What do competitors claim?
- What frameworks exist?
- What scientific bases are cited?

IDENTIFY mechanism opportunities:

UNEXPLOITED TYPES:
- Neurological/biological mechanisms
- Environmental mechanisms
- Behavioral automation mechanisms
- Identity-based mechanisms

UNDERDEVELOPED EXPLANATIONS:
- Mechanisms mentioned but not explained
- Mechanisms without proprietary names
- Mechanisms without proof structure

MISSING SCIENTIFIC BACKING:
- Areas where science exists but isn't cited
- Research that could be leveraged
- Credentialing opportunities

FOR each gap:
  ASSESS development potential
  IDENTIFY what's needed to fill it
```

### Step 3: Audience Gap Analysis

```
FROM awareness_map + market_narrative:

SEGMENT the audience:
- By awareness level
- By journey stage
- By psychographic profile
- By specific pain point
- By demographic

FOR each segment:
  ASSESS current competitive attention:
  - Who targets this segment?
  - How well are they served?
  - What needs remain unmet?

IDENTIFY underserved segments:
- Groups competitors ignore
- Stages of journey with no solutions
- Specific pains with no direct address

ESTIMATE segment size and potential
```

### Step 4: Messaging Gap Analysis

```
FROM all Layer 2 data:

IDENTIFY unsaid truths:
- What do quotes reveal that no one says?
- What's the elephant in the room?
- What would be "taboo" to say but true?

ASSESS each potential truth:
- Why isn't anyone saying this?
- What's the risk of saying it?
- What's the reward of saying it?

IDENTIFY untapped emotions:
- What emotions appear in quotes but not in competitor copy?
- What feelings are underexploited?
- What emotional angle is fresh?

IDENTIFY missing connections:
- What dots aren't being connected?
- What cause-effect relationships are unexplained?
- What patterns are unacknowledged?
```

### Step 5: Gap Scoring

```
FOR each identified gap:

SCORE on:
1. Potential (1-10): How valuable if captured?
2. Feasibility (1-10): How achievable?
3. Defensibility (1-10): How hard to copy?
4. Alignment (1-10): How well does it fit our strengths?

CALCULATE priority_score:
priority = (potential × 0.35) + (feasibility × 0.25) +
           (defensibility × 0.20) + (alignment × 0.20)

RANK all gaps by priority_score
```

### Step 6: Strategic Synthesis

```
IDENTIFY primary gaps:
- Top 3-5 highest priority gaps
- Across different categories for diversification

IDENTIFY combination opportunities:
- Gaps that can be combined for stronger positioning
- Complementary gaps that reinforce each other

IDENTIFY gaps to avoid:
- Low potential, high difficulty
- Already being moved toward by competitors
- High risk, low reward messaging

COMPILE strategic_recommendations
```

---

## Gap Identification Heuristics

```
SIGNALS OF STRONG GAPS:

POSITIONING:
- Multiple quotes expressing frustration with current options
- No competitor dominates the space
- Adjacent markets have proven the angle

MECHANISM:
- Science exists that no one is citing
- Customer language suggests missing explanation
- Complexity can be simplified with new frame

AUDIENCE:
- Specific complaints from segment with no response
- Large segment with generic solutions only
- Journey stage with high drop-off

MESSAGING:
- Customer quotes say what no ad says
- High emotional intensity with low market acknowledgment
- Obvious truth that feels "forbidden"
```
