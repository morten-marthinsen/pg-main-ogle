# Skill 2.3-C: Market Narrative Builder

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** Narrative Synthesis
**Dependencies:** theme_synthesis.json, competitor_analysis.json, web_analysis.json
**Output:** market_narrative.json

---

## Purpose

Synthesize all intelligence into a cohesive market narrative that explains the current state of the market, why customers struggle, what they believe, and where opportunity exists. This narrative becomes the foundation for Big Idea development.

---

## Core Framework

```
MARKET NARRATIVE COMPONENTS:

1. THE MARKET REALITY
   - What is actually happening
   - Current state of affairs
   - Observable conditions

2. THE CUSTOMER JOURNEY
   - Where they start
   - What they try
   - Why they fail
   - Where they get stuck

3. THE HIDDEN TRUTH
   - What nobody is telling them
   - Why existing solutions fail
   - The real root cause

4. THE PATH FORWARD
   - What would actually work
   - Why it's different
   - How transformation happens

This narrative explains the ENTIRE market story.
```

## Synthesis Logic

### Step 1: Establish Market Reality

```
FROM theme_synthesis + competitor_analysis:

IDENTIFY current market state:
- What does the data say is happening?
- What are the observable conditions?
- What statistics define this market?

ARTICULATE market_reality:
- headline: Single sentence market truth
- current_state: 2-3 sentence description
- key_statistics: Numbers that prove the state
- observable_conditions: What anyone can see
- market_trajectory: Where it's heading

EXAMPLE:
headline: "Most traders know what to do but can't make themselves do it"
current_state: "The trading education market is saturated with strategy
content, yet 90%+ of traders remain unprofitable. Knowledge is abundant;
execution is rare."
```

### Step 2: Map Customer Journey

```
FROM web_analysis + belief_analysis + awareness_map:

MAP entry_point:
- How do people enter this market?
- What triggers their search?
- What do they believe initially?
- What's their emotional state?

MAP attempts (typically 3-5):
FOR each common solution type:
  - Why do they try it?
  - What happens when they do?
  - Why does it fail for them?
  - How do they feel after?

MAP stuck_point:
- Where do most people end up?
- How long do they stay stuck?
- What characterizes this state?
- What do they believe now?

MAP critical_moment:
- What creates the fork in the road?
- What options do they perceive?
- What determines the path?
```

### Step 3: Uncover Hidden Truth

```
FROM all Layer 2 analyses:

IDENTIFY the layers:

SURFACE PROBLEM:
- What do customers THINK is their problem?
- This is what they search for solutions to
- Quote evidence from belief_analysis

DEEPER PROBLEM:
- What is actually causing their symptoms?
- This is one level deeper
- Often misidentified as the root cause

ROOT CAUSE:
- What is the actual fundamental issue?
- Why do all their attempts fail?
- This is the hidden truth

WHY NOBODY SAYS THIS:
- Why isn't this commonly known?
- Who benefits from the confusion?
- What prevents this truth from spreading?

COMPILE evidence from quotes and patterns
```

### Step 4: Define Path Forward

```
FROM mechanism_inventory + villain_analysis:

ARTICULATE what would actually work:

REQUIRED SHIFT:
- What belief must change?
- What understanding must develop?
- What capability must be built?

WHAT MUST CHANGE:
- Specific behavior or approach change
- This is the "instead of X, do Y"

MECHANISM CATEGORY:
- What type of solution addresses root cause?
- Not specific product, but category

TRANSFORMATION PATH:
- How does change actually happen?
- What is the sequence?

EXPECTED OUTCOME:
- What does success look like?
- What do they achieve?
```

### Step 5: Synthesize Narrative

```
COMPILE into cohesive narrative:

ONE PARAGRAPH VERSION:
Write a complete narrative in 4-6 sentences that:
- States the market reality
- Describes the customer journey
- Reveals the hidden truth
- Points to the path forward

KEY TENSION:
- What is the core conflict?
- What must be resolved?

RESOLUTION DIRECTION:
- How is the tension resolved?
- What is the answer?
```

### Step 6: Extract Strategic Implications

```
FROM narrative components:

DERIVE strategic direction:

POSITIONING DIRECTION:
- Where should solution be positioned?
- What category/frame?

MESSAGING ANGLE:
- What angle enters the conversation?
- What hook captures attention?

PROOF REQUIREMENTS:
- What must be proven?
- What evidence is needed?

OBJECTION ANTICIPATION:
- What will they doubt?
- What concerns will arise?
```

---

## Narrative Quality Criteria

```
STRONG NARRATIVE CHARACTERISTICS:

1. COHERENCE
   - All elements connect logically
   - No contradictions
   - Flows naturally

2. EVIDENCE-BACKED
   - Each claim supported by data
   - Quotes available for each assertion
   - Patterns, not opinions

3. EMOTIONALLY RESONANT
   - Describes real experiences
   - Captures actual feelings
   - Rings true to the audience

4. STRATEGICALLY USEFUL
   - Provides clear direction
   - Enables differentiation
   - Suggests messaging approach

5. UNIQUE INSIGHT
   - Says something not obvious
   - Reveals hidden truth
   - Provides "aha" moment
```
