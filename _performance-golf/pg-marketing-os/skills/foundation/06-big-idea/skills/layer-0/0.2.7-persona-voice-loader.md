### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `strategic` arena mode, prioritize **teaching and philosophy specimens** that show each writer's THINKING process, not just their selling voice.

For Big Idea specifically, prioritize specimens that demonstrate how each writer **generates creative concepts and synthesizes strategic elements into compelling campaign theses**. The Big Idea skill needs to fuse root cause, mechanism, promise, and FSSIT into a single organizing concept that is simultaneously surprising (schema-violating) and deeply resonant. The best specimens for this skill show:

1. **Creative conceptualization** — How does the writer arrive at the ONE idea that organizes the entire campaign?
2. **Big Idea expression patterns** — What structural patterns do their most powerful ideas follow?
3. **Hook-power thinking** — How does the writer create ideas that STOP people and reorganize their understanding?
4. **Strategic synthesis** — How does the writer connect multiple strategic elements into a single coherent thesis?

Schwartz's process essay ("I Write With My Ears") is essential here — it reveals exactly how he generates Big Ideas by listening to the market and finding the gap between what they want and what they've heard. His 204-headline compendium shows Big Idea expression patterns across dozens of campaigns. Halbert's newsletters show hook-power thinking — how to create ideas with enough gravitational pull to stop someone mid-scroll. Bencivenga's Maxim 12 provides the strategic framework for how all elements connect into a Persuasion Equation.
