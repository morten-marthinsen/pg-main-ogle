# Skill 2.7: Transformation Operators

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Generation)
**Type:** Ideation Engine
**Dependencies:** candidate_architectures.json (from 2.2), layer1-output.json (from 1.6), layer0-output.json (from 0.6)
**Output:** transformed_candidates.json

---

## Purpose

Apply systematic Transformation Operators to candidate Big Ideas to generate higher-schema-distance variants. These operators are cognitive manipulation techniques that increase CNI, BCI, and/or LNI scores by restructuring claims, contradicting beliefs, and introducing novel linguistic frames. Each operator has specific psychological mechanics that predict which SDS index it primarily affects.

---

## Theoretical Foundation

### Why Transformation Operators?

The FSSIT (Finally Someone Said It) phenomenon requires ideas that feel both **revelatory** and **recognizable**. Pure brainstorming produces either:
1. **Obvious ideas** (low schema distance) — "Eat less, move more"
2. **Alien ideas** (excessive schema distance) — Claims so bizarre they trigger rejection

Transformation Operators provide **systematic pathways** from obvious ideas to FSSIT ideas by applying specific cognitive manipulations with predictable effects on schema distance indices.

### Operator Theory

Each operator transforms a **base claim** into a **transformed claim** by manipulating one or more of:
- **Reference class** (what category the claim belongs to)
- **Causal direction** (what causes what)
- **Hidden variables** (what factors are introduced)
- **Scale** (magnitude or scope)
- **Temporal position** (when things happen)
- **Attribution** (who/what is responsible)

---

## The Six Transformation Operators

### Operator 1: INVERSION

**Definition:** Reverse the causal direction or claim polarity of a market belief.

```yaml
operator_inversion:
  mechanism: "Take what the market believes is cause→effect and flip it"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+2 to +4 points"
    reason: "Directly contradicts core market beliefs"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "LNI (Linguistic Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Inverted framing creates novel perspective"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[A] causes [B]"
    output: "[B] causes [A]" OR "[A] is caused by [B]" OR "[A] prevents [B]"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "Eating too much causes weight gain"
      market_belief_contradicted: "Calories in > calories out = weight gain"
      transformed_claim: "Weight gain causes you to eat too much"
      mechanism_explanation: "Hormonal disruption from fat cells increases hunger signals"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+1 (competitors don't claim this)"
        BCI: "+3 (contradicts foundational CICO belief)"
        LNI: "+1 (inverted framing)"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Stress causes belly fat"
      market_belief_contradicted: "Stress is a result of being overweight"
      transformed_claim: "Belly fat causes chronic stress"
      mechanism_explanation: "Visceral fat produces cortisol independently"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+1"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "Poor sleep makes you tired"
      market_belief_contradicted: "Tiredness is just lack of sleep"
      transformed_claim: "Fatigue causes poor sleep quality"
      mechanism_explanation: "Cellular energy depletion prevents deep sleep cycles"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+3"
        LNI: "+1"

  operator_constraints:
    - Inversion must be mechanistically defensible (not just wordplay)
    - Must cite scientific basis for the reversal
    - Cannot contradict laws of physics or established biology
    - Must create "aha" moment, not confusion

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify core causal claim in candidate Big Idea"
    step_2: "Identify market belief about cause→effect direction"
    step_3: "Reverse the causal direction"
    step_4: "Find mechanistic justification from deep research"
    step_5: "Test for 'aha' resonance vs. confusion"
```

### Operator 2: HIDDEN VARIABLE INTRODUCTION

**Definition:** Introduce a previously unrecognized variable that explains both problem and solution.

```yaml
operator_hidden_variable:
  mechanism: "Reveal a factor the market doesn't know about that explains everything"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "CNI (Claim Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+2 to +4 points"
    reason: "Competitors haven't named this variable"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Existing beliefs don't account for this variable"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[Problem] exists because of [known cause]"
    output: "[Problem] exists because of [hidden variable] that affects [known cause]"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "Diets fail because people lack willpower"
      hidden_variable: "Leptin resistance"
      transformed_claim: "Diets fail because leptin resistance blocks your brain's fullness signals"
      mechanism_explanation: "Leptin is the satiety hormone; resistance means your brain never gets the 'full' signal"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+3 (competitors don't mention leptin)"
        BCI: "+2 (contradicts 'willpower' belief)"
        LNI: "+1 (new term introduced)"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Energy declines with age"
      hidden_variable: "NAD+ depletion"
      transformed_claim: "Energy declines because NAD+ levels drop 50% by age 50"
      mechanism_explanation: "NAD+ is required for cellular energy production"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+3"
        BCI: "+1"
        LNI: "+2"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "Joint pain comes from wear and tear"
      hidden_variable: "Collagen type mismatch"
      transformed_claim: "Joint pain comes from producing the wrong TYPE of collagen"
      mechanism_explanation: "Type II collagen for joints requires specific nutrients most people lack"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+3"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+1"

  operator_constraints:
    - Hidden variable must be scientifically real (not invented)
    - Must explain both the problem AND why solutions haven't worked
    - Must be nameable and memorable
    - Must be addressable by the product/mechanism

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify what the market believes causes the problem"
    step_2: "Search deep research for upstream/hidden factors"
    step_3: "Select hidden variable with strongest evidence base"
    step_4: "Craft transformation that 'reveals' the hidden variable"
    step_5: "Verify the variable is addressable by the mechanism"
```

### Operator 3: CATEGORY VIOLATION

**Definition:** Place the subject in an unexpected category or reference class.

```yaml
operator_category_violation:
  mechanism: "Reframe what 'type of thing' the subject is"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "LNI (Linguistic Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+2 to +4 points"
    reason: "Unexpected category creates novel framing"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Violates category expectations"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[Subject] is a type of [Expected Category]"
    output: "[Subject] is actually a type of [Unexpected Category]"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "Belly fat is a cosmetic problem"
      expected_category: "Appearance issue"
      violated_category: "Organ dysfunction"
      transformed_claim: "Belly fat is a sick organ that's poisoning your body"
      mechanism_explanation: "Visceral fat produces inflammatory cytokines and hormones"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+3 (organ = dramatic recategorization)"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Your gut processes food"
      expected_category: "Digestive system"
      violated_category: "Second brain"
      transformed_claim: "Your gut is a 'second brain' that controls your mood, weight, and energy"
      mechanism_explanation: "Enteric nervous system contains 100M+ neurons; produces 95% of serotonin"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+1"
        BCI: "+1"
        LNI: "+3"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "Wrinkles are signs of aging"
      expected_category: "Age markers"
      violated_category: "Nutrient deficiency symptoms"
      transformed_claim: "Wrinkles are nutrient deficiency symptoms, not age markers"
      mechanism_explanation: "Collagen production requires specific amino acids most diets lack"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+2"

  operator_constraints:
    - Category violation must be mechanistically defensible
    - New category must create useful implications
    - Must not feel like mere wordplay
    - Must survive the "so what?" test (new category must matter)

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify what category the market places the subject in"
    step_2: "Brainstorm alternative categories that are mechanistically valid"
    step_3: "Select category that creates most useful implications"
    step_4: "Craft transformation with vivid category violation"
    step_5: "Verify category violation survives 'so what?' test"
```

### Operator 4: TEMPORAL SHIFT

**Definition:** Move the critical moment or cause to an unexpected point in time.

```yaml
operator_temporal_shift:
  mechanism: "Change WHEN the important thing happens"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +3 points"
    reason: "Contradicts beliefs about timing/causation sequence"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "CNI (Claim Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Competitors focus on conventional timing"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[Effect] happens at [Expected Time]"
    output: "[Effect] already happened at [Earlier Time]" OR "[Effect] happens at [Later Time]"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "Weight gain happens when you eat"
      expected_timing: "At meal time"
      shifted_timing: "While you sleep"
      transformed_claim: "Most weight gain happens while you sleep, not while you eat"
      mechanism_explanation: "Insulin sensitivity drops at night; late eating stores as fat during sleep"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2 (contradicts 'eating causes weight gain' timing)"
        LNI: "+1"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Muscle loss happens in old age"
      expected_timing: "After 60"
      shifted_timing: "Starting at 30"
      transformed_claim: "You've been losing muscle since age 30—it just shows up at 60"
      mechanism_explanation: "Sarcopenia begins at 30 at ~1% per year; compounds to visible loss by 60"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+1"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+1"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "Brain fog happens when you're tired"
      expected_timing: "End of day"
      shifted_timing: "First thing in morning"
      transformed_claim: "Brain fog starts the moment you wake up—before you even feel tired"
      mechanism_explanation: "Overnight glucose depletion leaves brain cells in energy deficit at waking"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+1"
        LNI: "+1"

  operator_constraints:
    - Temporal shift must be mechanistically defensible
    - Must create actionable implications (if it happens then, what do I do?)
    - Cannot be trivially obvious or universally known
    - Must feel like genuine insight, not pedantic correction

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify conventional timing beliefs about the problem"
    step_2: "Search for evidence of earlier/later causation points"
    step_3: "Select temporal shift with strongest evidence and implications"
    step_4: "Craft transformation emphasizing the timing surprise"
    step_5: "Verify shift creates actionable insight"
```

### Operator 5: SCALE SHIFT

**Definition:** Change the magnitude, scope, or scale of the claim dramatically.

```yaml
operator_scale_shift:
  mechanism: "Make the claim much bigger or much smaller than expected"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "CNI (Claim Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +3 points"
    reason: "Extreme scale claims are less common"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Contradicts intuitions about scale"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[Effect] is [Medium Scale]"
    output: "[Effect] is [Extreme Scale] (bigger or smaller)"

  scale_shift_directions:
    amplification:
      description: "Make effect much larger than expected"
      example_pattern: "It's not just X, it's [10X]"

    minimization:
      description: "Make cause much smaller than expected"
      example_pattern: "It only takes [tiny amount] to cause [huge effect]"

    inversion:
      description: "Flip which thing is bigger"
      example_pattern: "[Small thing] matters more than [big thing]"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "Your gut affects your health"
      expected_scale: "Minor contributor"
      shifted_scale: "Primary controller"
      transformed_claim: "Your gut controls 70% of your immune system"
      mechanism_explanation: "GALT (gut-associated lymphoid tissue) contains majority of immune cells"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+0"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Sleep matters for health"
      expected_scale: "Important factor"
      shifted_scale: "Single most important factor"
      transformed_claim: "One night of bad sleep does more damage than a week of bad eating"
      mechanism_explanation: "Single night sleep deprivation causes insulin resistance comparable to 6 months poor diet"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+1"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "Small habits compound"
      expected_scale: "Gradual improvement"
      shifted_scale: "Microscopic trigger"
      transformed_claim: "30 seconds each morning can reverse 10 years of damage"
      mechanism_explanation: "Specific breathing pattern triggers vagus nerve reset"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+1"
        LNI: "+1"

  operator_constraints:
    - Scale shift must be provable (not hyperbole)
    - Must cite specific evidence for the extreme scale
    - Cannot violate physical possibility
    - Must feel revelatory, not exaggerated

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify conventional scale beliefs about the claim"
    step_2: "Search for evidence supporting extreme scale"
    step_3: "Select scale shift with strongest evidence base"
    step_4: "Craft transformation emphasizing the scale surprise"
    step_5: "Verify scale claim is defensible with proof"
```

### Operator 6: VILLAIN REFRAME

**Definition:** Redirect blame from the prospect to an external enemy.

```yaml
operator_villain_reframe:
  mechanism: "It's not your fault—it's THEIR fault"

  primary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "LNI (Linguistic Novelty Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +2 points"
    reason: "Villain naming creates memorable language"

  secondary_sds_impact:
    index_affected: "BCI (Belief Contradiction Index)"
    typical_increase: "+1 to +3 points"
    reason: "Contradicts self-blame beliefs"

  transformation_formula:
    input: "[Problem] happens because you [personal failing]"
    output: "[Problem] happens because [Villain] is [doing something to you]"

  villain_types:
    industry_villain:
      description: "A profit-motivated industry actively harming people"
      examples: "Big Pharma, The Diet Industry, The Sugar Industry"

    belief_villain:
      description: "A false belief that's been propagated"
      examples: "The Calorie Myth, The Low-Fat Lie, The Exercise Fallacy"

    hidden_saboteur:
      description: "Something disguised as good that's actually bad"
      examples: "The 'Healthy' Foods Destroying Your Gut, The Supplement That Blocks Absorption"

    systemic_villain:
      description: "A system or policy causing harm"
      examples: "The Food Pyramid, FDA Guidelines, Medical Establishment"

  application_examples:
    example_1:
      base_claim: "You're overweight because you eat too much"
      personal_blame: "Lack of willpower"
      villain_introduced: "The Sugar Industry"
      transformed_claim: "The Sugar Industry engineered foods to override your fullness signals"
      mechanism_explanation: "Sugar + fat combination triggers dopamine without satiety signaling"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+1"
        BCI: "+2"
        LNI: "+2"

    example_2:
      base_claim: "Your cholesterol is too high because of your diet"
      personal_blame: "Eating the wrong foods"
      villain_introduced: "The Statin Industry"
      transformed_claim: "The Statin Industry invented 'bad cholesterol' to sell you drugs"
      mechanism_explanation: "LDL is transport mechanism, not inherently harmful; inflammation is real issue"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+3"
        LNI: "+1"

    example_3:
      base_claim: "You can't lose weight because you don't exercise enough"
      personal_blame: "Laziness"
      villain_introduced: "The Fitness Industry Lie"
      transformed_claim: "The Fitness Industry sold you a lie—exercise barely moves the needle on weight loss"
      mechanism_explanation: "Exercise accounts for only 5-10% of caloric expenditure; diet is 80%+"
      sds_increase:
        CNI: "+2"
        BCI: "+3"
        LNI: "+1"

  operator_constraints:
    - Villain must be defensibly culpable (not pure conspiracy)
    - Must cite evidence for villain's role
    - Villain must be external to the prospect (NEVER prospect's fault)
    - Must create clear enemy vs. solution dynamic

  application_process:
    step_1: "Identify where market places blame on the prospect"
    step_2: "Search for external factors that better explain the problem"
    step_3: "Select villain that is both emotionally satisfying and defensible"
    step_4: "Craft transformation that shifts blame clearly"
    step_5: "Verify villain claim can be supported with evidence"
```

## Process Logic

### Step 1: Analyze Each Candidate for Transformation Opportunities

```
FOR each candidate in candidate_architectures:

  IDENTIFY transformation_opportunities:

    SCAN core_concept for:
      - Causal claims that could be inverted
      - Missing explanatory variables
      - Category assumptions that could be violated
      - Timing assumptions that could be shifted
      - Scale assumptions that could be extreme
      - Self-blame elements that could become villain-blame

    SCAN root_cause_reframe for:
      - What_they_think beliefs that could be inverted
      - Hidden variables not yet named
      - Category violations for the root cause

    SCAN mechanism for:
      - Scale claims that could be more extreme
      - Temporal claims about when mechanism works
      - Villain elements that could be strengthened

  RECORD all opportunities with operator type
```

### Step 2: Apply Operators to Each Candidate

```
FOR each candidate:

  SELECT operators to apply:
    - REQUIRED: At least 2 operators per candidate
    - PREFERRED: 3-4 operators for maximum SDS boost
    - CONSTRAINT: Don't stack too many (diminishing returns, coherence risk)

  FOR each selected operator:

    IDENTIFY base_claim to transform

    APPLY operator transformation_formula

    DEVELOP mechanism_justification:
      - Search deep_research for supporting evidence
      - Cite specific quotes, studies, or data points
      - Verify transformation is mechanistically defensible

    PREDICT SDS impact:
      - Estimate CNI_delta based on competitor analysis
      - Estimate BCI_delta based on belief contradiction count
      - Estimate LNI_delta based on linguistic novelty

    RECORD transformation with full documentation
```

### Step 3: Reconstruct Transformed Architecture

```
FOR each candidate:

  REBUILD transformed_architecture:

    core_concept:
      - Integrate all transformations into coherent narrative
      - Maintain logical consistency
      - Preserve mechanism-proof connection

    root_cause_reframe:
      - Update what_they_think if inverted or villain-reframed
      - Update what_it_really_is if hidden variable introduced
      - Maintain why_nothing_worked coherence

    mechanism:
      - Update if scale_shift or temporal_shift applied
      - Ensure mechanism still addresses transformed root cause
      - Maintain proof connection

  VERIFY coherence:
    - All elements still connect logically
    - No contradictions between transformed elements
    - Proof still supports the claims
```

### Step 4: Score Transformation Quality

```
FOR each candidate:

  SCORE aha_factor (0-10):
    - Does the transformation create genuine surprise?
    - Does it feel like "finally someone said it"?
    - Does it shift understanding vs. just wordplay?

  SCORE defensibility (0-10):
    - Can every transformation be supported with evidence?
    - Are mechanism justifications scientifically valid?
    - Could this survive skeptical scrutiny?

  SCORE memorability (0-10):
    - Will the transformed claim stick in memory?
    - Is it repeatable (can someone tell a friend)?
    - Does it have vivid, concrete language?

  CALCULATE overall_score = (aha_factor + defensibility + memorability) / 3

  FLAG candidates with overall_score < 6.0 for revision
```

---

## Constraints

- NEVER apply an operator without mechanistic justification
- NEVER apply more than 4 operators to a single candidate (coherence risk)
- NEVER apply conflicting operators (e.g., two different inversions on same claim)
- NEVER produce a transformation that cannot be defended with evidence
- ALWAYS maintain logical coherence across transformed elements
- ALWAYS cite evidence sources for each transformation
- ALWAYS predict SDS impact with specific rationale
- ALWAYS apply at least 2 operators per candidate

---

## Quality Criteria

1. All 5 candidates have 2-4 transformations applied
2. Every transformation has mechanistic justification with evidence
3. Every transformation has predicted SDS impact with rationale
4. All transformed architectures maintain logical coherence
5. No transformation relies on pure wordplay (must have substance)
6. Overall transformation_quality score ≥ 6.0 for all candidates
7. Operator usage is diverse (not all same operator)
