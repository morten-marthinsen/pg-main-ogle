## OVERVIEW

This file captures learnings from client feedback on Big Idea generation for golf equipment (SF2 Driver). These patterns should be applied to future golf promotions and similar sports/equipment niches.

---

## LEARNING 1: Specificity Over Cleverness

### What Was Tried
"The 20 Yards You're Already Owed" — entitlement framing with recovery psychology.

### Feedback Received
"Too tongue-in-cheek." The IDEA was good (recovery/regaining lost distance), but the EXPRESSION was too playful/clever.

### The Pattern
Golf audience responds to direct, specific language — not clever wordplay. "Already owed" sounds like a marketing phrase. "Your slice stole from you" sounds like truth.

### How To Apply
| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "The 20 yards you're already owed" | "Regain the 30 yards your slice stole" |
| "Time to collect" | "Get them back" |
| Entitlement metaphors | Direct recovery language |
| Abstract cleverness | Specific, visceral statements |

### Rule
**In golf copy, be direct. Clever = skepticism. Direct = credibility.**

## LEARNING 2: Contrarian Hooks Work When Tight

### What Was Tried
"The Worse Your Slice, The More This Works" — counterintuitive inversion.

### Feedback Received
This one worked. It's tight, immediately graspable, and inverts expectations without requiring explanation.

### The Pattern
Contrarian hooks work in golf when:
1. The inversion is immediately understandable (no explanation needed)
2. It's tight (short phrase, punchy)
3. It resolves logically (the mechanism backs it up)

Contrarian hooks fail when:
1. They require setup to understand
2. They sound like clickbait
3. They can't be logically explained

### Variants That Work
- "The Worse Your Slice, The More This Works" (PRIMARY — tightest)
- "The Worse Your Slice, The More Distance You Gain" (adds surprise element)
- "The Worse Your Slice, The Bigger The Fix" (transformation emphasis)

### Rule
**Contrarian hooks must be graspable in one read. If it needs a paragraph to land, it's not tight enough.**

## LEARNING 3: Specificity Beats Abstraction

### What Was Tried
Abstract recovery language ("already owed," "collect," "recovery").

### Feedback Received
"30 yards" + "first shot" is better than abstract ownership language.

### The Pattern
Golf audience is analytical. They respond to:
- Specific numbers (30 yards, not "distance")
- Specific moments (first shot, not "immediately")
- Specific actions (stole, regain, not "owed")

### How To Apply
| Abstract | Specific |
|----------|----------|
| "Distance you're owed" | "30 yards your slice stole" |
| "Results immediately" | "On your first shot" |
| "Improvement" | "8 strokes off handicap" |
| "The equipment change" | "The moment you switch drivers" |

### Rule
**Numbers + moments + actions. Never abstract concepts.**

## LEARNING 4: Equipment-As-Instant-Fix Is Underexplored

### What Was Tried (New)
"Eliminate your slice before you even pick up your club."

### Feedback Received
This is intriguing. The paradox (fix before you swing) creates curiosity while directly connecting to the "no swing change" promise.

### The Pattern
Golf equipment copy usually focuses on what happens AFTER the swing. But the root cause is BEFORE the swing — it's the equipment itself.

Framing the equipment change as the MOMENT of transformation is:
- Surprising (paradoxical)
- True (equipment mismatch IS the problem)
- Intriguing (how can slice be fixed before swing?)
- Differentiating (nobody else says this)

### How To Apply
- "Your slice is gone the moment you switch drivers"
- "Fix your slice without swinging — just switch drivers"
- "The fix happens before the swing"
- "No swing change required — because the driver IS the change"

### Rule
**For equipment promotions, the equipment change CAN be the climactic moment — not just the enabler.**

## LEARNING 5: Avoid Conspiracy in Golf

### What Was Tried (v1)
"The Equipment Lie" — implying industry conspiracy.

### Feedback Received
"Conspiracy framing doesn't work in golf like it does in health/finance."

### The Pattern
Golf culture trusts:
- Pro shops
- Equipment manufacturers
- The game's traditions

Golf culture is skeptical of:
- "Industry lies" framing
- "They don't want you to know"
- Anti-establishment messaging

### Why It Fails
Golfers respect the game. They don't see equipment companies as villains — they see them as innovators. Conspiracy framing feels disrespectful to a game they love.

### What Works Instead
- Physics explanations (not conspiracies)
- Design intent ("built for tour pros, not you")
- Mismatch framing (not deception framing)

### Rule
**In golf, blame physics — not people. Explain mismatch — don't imply conspiracy.**

## LEARNING 6: Generic Terms Kill Golf Copy

### What Was Tried
"Equipment" (generic term for driver).

### Feedback Received
"Too general — we're selling a DRIVER."

### The Pattern
Golf audience knows their equipment. They know a driver is different from a hybrid is different from an iron. Generic terms signal:
- Lack of expertise
- Mass-market messaging
- Not written by golfers

### How To Apply
| Generic | Specific |
|---------|----------|
| "Equipment" | "Driver" |
| "Club" | "SF2" |
| "Golf gear" | "460cc titanium head" |
| "Your game" | "Your drives" |

### Rule
**Name the specific equipment. Generic terms = generic credibility.**

## SUMMARY: GOLF BIG IDEA CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any golf Big Idea, verify:

- [ ] **Specific, not clever:** Direct language, no wordplay
- [ ] **Numbers + moments:** Specific yards, specific swings
- [ ] **Tight contrarian:** If counterintuitive, graspable in one read
- [ ] **No conspiracy:** Physics mismatch, not industry villains
- [ ] **Equipment-specific:** Driver, not "equipment" or "club"
- [ ] **Direct action verbs:** Stole, regain, eliminate — not abstract nouns

---

## APPLICATION TO FUTURE GOLF PROMOTIONS

When running Big Idea skill for golf:

1. **Check headline candidates against "tongue-in-cheek" filter**
   - Read aloud. Does it sound like clever marketing? Revise.
   - Test: Would a golf purist roll their eyes? Remove.

2. **Force specificity pass**
   - Replace every abstract term with a number or moment
   - "Distance" → "30 yards"
   - "Immediately" → "on your first shot"

3. **Tighten contrarian hooks**
   - If it takes a paragraph to explain, it's not tight enough
   - Goal: graspable in one read

4. **Apply physics framing**
   - Root cause is physics mismatch, not industry deception
   - Equipment was designed for tour pros (fact)
   - Your swing doesn't match tour swing (fact)
   - No conspiracy — just mismatch
