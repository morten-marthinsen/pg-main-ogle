# 2.6 Niche Expression Matching

## Purpose
Match the expression method to the NICHE. Different niches have different "native" expression patterns—health uses syndromes, golf uses simple reframes, finance uses villain/expose. This microskill ensures the derived root cause is expressed in the way that WORKS for the target niche.

---

## The Matching Principle

**Expression method must match niche expectations.**

An expression that works brilliantly in health copy will feel bizarre in golf copy. The same root cause can be expressed different ways—choose the one your niche expects.

---

## Niche Expression Map

### HEALTH / WELLNESS / SUPPLEMENTS

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Named Syndrome + Villain
SECONDARY: Metaphor (to explain mechanism)
AVOID: Simple reframe (feels too casual)

PATTERN:
"You have [SYNDROME NAME] caused by [VILLAIN].
Here's how it works: [MECHANISM with metaphor].
That's why [FAILED SOLUTIONS] never worked."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Sleep deprivation prevents metabolic reset"
HEALTH EXPRESSION: "Shallow Sleep Syndrome—when you don't reach
deep restorative stages, your body can't complete its nightly reset.
Leptin and ghrelin hormones never rebalance..."
```

---

### GOLF / SPORTS INSTRUCTION

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Simple Reframe
SECONDARY: Metaphor (to clarify)
AVOID: Named syndrome (feels medicalized), Villain (no external enemy)

PATTERN:
"There's only ONE reason [problem]: [SIMPLE TRUTH].
It's like [METAPHOR].
That's why [everything makes sense now]."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Misconception about swing arc"
GOLF EXPRESSION: "The biggest misconception in golf: the ball is NOT
at the bottom of your swing. Your low point shifts during the swing.
It's like fumbling the snap—everything downstream goes wrong."
```

---

### WEIGHT LOSS

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Villain + Named Syndrome
SECONDARY: Metaphor (hormones, metabolism)
AVOID: Simple reframe (problem too emotional)

PATTERN:
"It's not your fault. [VILLAIN/HORMONES] have been working against you.
You have [SYNDROME] that makes [fat storage] inevitable.
That's why [willpower-based approaches] fail."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Hormonal transition causes fat storage"
WEIGHT LOSS EXPRESSION: "Three 'queen hormones'—insulin, cortisol,
estrogen—switched to procreation mode after puberty. They've
hardwired your body for fat storage. It's a ticking time bomb
of the female metabolism. You're not failing—you're fighting evolution."
```

---

### FINANCE / INVESTING

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Villain + Expose (Hidden Truth)
SECONDARY: Simple reframe
AVOID: Named syndrome (feels too medical)

PATTERN:
"[VILLAINS—Wall Street, Banks, Elites] don't want you to know this.
The REAL reason [your investments struggle] is [HIDDEN TRUTH].
While [they] do [X], ordinary investors are left [Y]."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Currency devaluation erodes savings"
FINANCE EXPRESSION: "The Fed has been printing money at unprecedented
rates. Wall Street insiders know where to put their money. But ordinary
Americans are watching their purchasing power evaporate. The real threat
isn't the stock market—it's the dollar in your pocket."
```

---

### BUSINESS / ENTREPRENEURSHIP

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Simple Reframe + Hidden Truth
SECONDARY: Dual Problem
AVOID: Villain (audience sees themselves as agents), Syndrome

PATTERN:
"You've been told [conventional business wisdom].
But here's what actually drives [success metric]: [INSIGHT].
Most businesses fail at [X] because they misunderstand [Y]."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Focusing on acquisition instead of retention"
BUSINESS EXPRESSION: "Everyone talks about getting more customers.
But here's the math most entrepreneurs miss: A 5% increase in retention
produces 25-95% more profit than the same increase in acquisition.
You're focused on the front door while the back door is wide open."
```

---

### SELF-IMPROVEMENT / PRODUCTIVITY

```
PRIMARY EXPRESSION: Simple Reframe + False Belief
SECONDARY: Metaphor
AVOID: Villain (internal focus), Syndrome (not medical)

PATTERN:
"You believe [FALSE BELIEF ABOUT PRODUCTIVITY].
But here's what actually matters: [REFRAMED TRUTH].
That's why [hustle culture / tips / hacks] never stick."

EXAMPLE ROOT CAUSE: "Identity doesn't match behavior"
SELF-IMPROVEMENT EXPRESSION: "You don't lack motivation—you're trying
to ACT like a productive person instead of BECOMING one. Every time
you force a new habit, your identity pulls you back to baseline.
The goal isn't behavior change. It's identity change."
```

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Identify the Niche

```
NICHE IDENTIFICATION:
Primary Niche: [health | golf | weight_loss | finance | business | self_improvement]
Sub-Niche: [supplements | instruction | investing | etc.]
Audience Sophistication: [low | medium | high]
```

---

### Step 2: Look Up Expression Pattern

From the map above, identify:
- PRIMARY expression method
- SECONDARY expression method (can combine)
- Methods to AVOID

---

### Step 3: Evaluate Existing Expression Candidates

If you've already developed expressions in Layer 2 microskills, evaluate fit:

```
EXPRESSION FIT EVALUATION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Expression: [2.1 Simple Reframe]                │
│ Fit for [Niche]: PRIMARY / SECONDARY / AVOID    │
│ Adjust?: YES/NO                                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Expression: [2.2 Named Syndrome]                │
│ Fit for [Niche]: PRIMARY / SECONDARY / AVOID    │
│ Adjust?: YES/NO                                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Expression: [2.3 Villain]                       │
│ Fit for [Niche]: PRIMARY / SECONDARY / AVOID    │
│ Adjust?: YES/NO                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Step 4: Select or Combine

```
SELECTION MATRIX:
Niche: [Name]
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

Selected Primary Expression: [Method]
Reason: "[Why this fits the niche]"

Selected Secondary Expression: [Method or None]
How Combined: "[How they work together]"

Avoided: [Method]
Reason: "[Why this doesn't fit]"
```

---

### Step 5: Niche-Calibrate the Language

Adjust tone, vocabulary, and intensity:

| Niche | Tone | Vocabulary | Intensity |
|-------|------|------------|-----------|
| Health | Concerned, hopeful | Medical-adjacent, body terms | Medium-high |
| Golf | Conversational, direct | Swing terms, golf lingo | Medium |
| Weight Loss | Empathetic, validating | Emotional, body terms | High |
| Finance | Urgent, insider | Money terms, enemy language | High |
| Business | Smart, peer-to-peer | Metrics, strategy terms | Medium |
| Self-Improvement | Wise, challenging | Psychology lite, mindset | Medium |
