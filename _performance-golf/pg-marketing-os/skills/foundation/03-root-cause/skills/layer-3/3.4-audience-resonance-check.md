# 3.4 Audience Resonance Check

## Purpose
Validate that the expressed root cause will RESONATE with the target audience—that they'll recognize themselves, feel understood, and accept the reframe. A true root cause that doesn't resonate is useless in copy.

---

## The Resonance Principle

**Resonance = Recognition + Relief + Receptivity**

The audience must:
1. **Recognize**: "Yes, that's me / my problem"
2. **Feel Relief**: "It's not my fault"
3. **Become Receptive**: "Tell me more about the solution"

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Profile the Target Audience

Confirm who this root cause is speaking to:

```
AUDIENCE PROFILE:
Primary Audience: "[Description]"
├── Demographics: [age, gender, location if relevant]
├── Psychographics: [mindset, beliefs, values]
├── Problem Stage: [aware | struggling | desperate | skeptical]
└── Sophistication: [beginner | intermediate | experienced | jaded]

What They've Tried: "[Previous solutions]"
What They Believe: "[Current beliefs about problem]"
What They Feel: "[Emotional state]"
```

---

### Step 2: Test Recognition

Will they immediately recognize this root cause as relevant to them?

```
RECOGNITION TEST:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

1. SURFACE RECOGNITION
   "Does this describe a problem they consciously have?"
   └── [Yes, exactly | Mostly | Partially | Not really]

2. LANGUAGE MATCH
   "Is this stated in language they would use?"
   └── [Their exact words | Close | Translated | Foreign]

3. SPECIFICITY MATCH
   "Is this specific enough to feel personal, not generic?"
   └── [Feels like it's about me | Somewhat personal | Generic | Too broad]

4. EXPERIENCE VALIDATION
   "Does this match their actual lived experience?"
   └── [Exactly | Mostly | Somewhat | Not at all]

RECOGNITION SCORE: [1-10]
```

---

### Step 3: Test Emotional Response

What emotions will this root cause evoke?

```
EMOTIONAL RESPONSE TEST:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

Expected Emotional Sequence:
1. Initial Reaction: [curiosity | skepticism | recognition | defensiveness]
2. Processing: [relief | vindication | anger | hope | doubt]
3. Resolution: [receptivity | acceptance | resistance | rejection]

POSITIVE INDICATORS (want these):
□ "That's exactly what I've been experiencing"
□ "Finally someone understands"
□ "It's not my fault after all"
□ "So THAT'S why nothing worked"
□ "What can I do about it?"

NEGATIVE INDICATORS (avoid these):
□ "That's not my problem"
□ "I already knew that"
□ "Sounds like a sales pitch"
□ "That's too good to be true"
□ "They're trying to scare me"

EMOTIONAL RESPONSE SCORE: [1-10]
```

---

### Step 4: Test Relief & Blame Removal

Does the root cause remove self-blame?

```
BLAME REMOVAL TEST:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

1. Who/what was blamed BEFORE?
   └── [Self | Willpower | Lack of effort | Bad luck | Unknown]

2. Who/what is blamed NOW (after root cause)?
   └── [External factor | Hidden mechanism | False belief | Industry | Circumstance]

3. Is blame fully removed from audience?
   └── [Yes, completely | Mostly | Partially | Still feels like their fault]

4. Does this create relief or additional burden?
   └── [Relief—they can stop blaming themselves | Neutral | Burden—new thing to worry about]

BLAME REMOVAL SCORE: [1-10]
```

---

### Step 5: Test Receptivity to Solution

Does the root cause create receptivity to the mechanism?

```
RECEPTIVITY TEST:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"
Mechanism: "[Name]"

1. Does knowing the root cause make them WANT a solution?
   └── [Urgently | Yes | Somewhat | Not really]

2. Does the root cause point TOWARD the mechanism naturally?
   └── [Obvious next step | Clear connection | Requires explanation | Forced]

3. Is the audience in a state to receive the solution?
   └── [Ready and open | Open but skeptical | Needs more persuasion | Resistant]

4. Is the root cause overwhelming or actionable?
   └── [Actionable—they can do something | Neutral | Overwhelming—feels hopeless]

RECEPTIVITY SCORE: [1-10]
```

---

### Step 6: Simulate Audience Internal Dialogue

Write out what the audience is thinking as they hear this:

```
INTERNAL DIALOGUE SIMULATION:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

When they hear Part 1 (What they think the problem is):
"[Their internal response]"

When they hear Part 2 (What the real problem is):
"[Their internal response]"

When they hear Part 3 (Why nothing worked):
"[Their internal response]"

DIALOGUE QUALITY: [Positive progression | Neutral | Losing them]
```

**Positive Example**:
```
Part 1: "Yeah, that's exactly what I've tried..."
Part 2: "Wait, really? I never thought of it that way..."
Part 3: "Oh my god, that explains everything. So what do I do?"
```

**Negative Example**:
```
Part 1: "I guess that's sort of what I've done..."
Part 2: "Hmm, not sure I buy that..."
Part 3: "This sounds like every other sales pitch."
```

## Quality Gates

Before proceeding:

- [ ] Total resonance score is 7.0+ (minimum for effective copy)
- [ ] Recognition score is 7.0+ (they must see themselves)
- [ ] Blame is fully or mostly removed
- [ ] Internal dialogue shows positive progression
- [ ] Audience ends receptive, not resistant

---

## Threshold Guidelines

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 8.5+ | Strong | Resonance is excellent—proceed |
| 7.0-8.4 | Good | Resonance solid—proceed with confidence |
| 5.0-6.9 | Weak | Some disconnect—adjust expression |
| Below 5.0 | Poor | Won't resonate—major revision needed |
