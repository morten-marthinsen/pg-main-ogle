# Skill 2.12: Visceral Response Validator

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Scorecard Optimization)
**Type:** Dimension Evaluation + Optimization
**Dimension:** 12 of 12 — "CAN YA FEEL IT??"
**Dependencies:** gate_1_report.json, All Layer 2 outputs (2.1-2.11)
**Output:** visceral_response_scores.json

---

## Purpose

Evaluate and optimize each mechanism candidate on VISCERAL RESPONSE: Does this mechanism produce a GUT-LEVEL emotional response? Not intellectual agreement — PHYSICAL sensation. The best mechanisms make the prospect's body respond before their mind catches up. This is the final, holistic dimension that integrates all others.

---

## The Question

**"Does hearing about this mechanism make you FEEL something in your body?"**

---

## Scoring Rubric

```
1-3 (INTELLECTUAL ONLY): Prospect thinks "that's logical" but feels nothing.
     - Makes sense on paper but doesn't move them
     - They'd agree it's smart but wouldn't ACT on it
     - No physical sensation, no emotional charge

4-6 (MILD RESPONSE): Prospect feels slight interest or curiosity.
     - "Hmm, that's interesting" with a lean forward
     - Some engagement but could walk away easily
     - Mild positive feeling but no urgency

7-8 (STRONG RESPONSE): Prospect feels genuine excitement or relief.
     - "Wait, this could actually work for me"
     - Physical lean-in, quickened attention
     - They want to know more RIGHT NOW
     - Emotional charge: hope, excitement, or determined anger

9-10 (VISCERAL): Prospect's body responds before their mind processes.
     - Goosebumps, gut clench, breath catch, eyes widen
     - "Holy shit" moment — can't NOT respond
     - Feels like being punched with possibility
     - The kind of response that makes them screenshot and send to someone
```

---

## The Visceral Triggers

```
WHAT CREATES BODY-LEVEL RESPONSE:

1. RECOGNITION — "That's ME they're describing" (goosebumps)
2. REVELATION — "I never saw it that way before" (breath catch)
3. VINDICATION — "I KNEW something was wrong!" (fist clench)
4. HOPE SPIKE — "Wait, this could actually work" (chest opens)
5. FEAR SPIKE — "Oh shit, this is happening to me right now" (gut drop)
6. ANGER — "They've been doing this to us?" (jaw tightens)
7. RELIEF — "Thank god, there's an answer" (shoulders drop)
8. AWE — "That's incredible" (wide eyes)
```

## Evaluation Logic

### Step 1: Holistic Mechanism Review

```
FOR each candidate:

  COMPILE the complete mechanism presentation:
    - Name (from 1.2)
    - Root cause connection (from 1.3)
    - Analogy (from 1.4)
    - Proof elements (from 1.5)
    - Scorecard optimizations (from 2.1-2.11)

  READ it as a WHOLE — not analytically, but as a prospect would:
    - First impression of the name
    - First read of the explanation
    - Reaction to the proof
    - Feeling after the doomsday scenario
    - Feeling after the super power statement

  NOTE: This is a GUT CHECK, not an analysis.
  What does the WHOLE package FEEL like?
```

### Step 2: Identify the Emotional Peak

```
FOR each candidate:

  WHERE in the mechanism presentation is the emotional peak?

  IS IT:
    - The NAME itself? (rare but powerful)
    - The ROOT CAUSE revelation? ("That's why nothing worked!")
    - The EXPLANATION? ("Oh my god, that makes sense")
    - The PROOF? ("Wait, this is real?")
    - The DOOMSDAY? ("I need to act NOW")
    - The SUPER POWER? ("I could actually have that?")
    - The COMBINATION? (everything building together)

  IDENTIFY the peak moment and its trigger type (recognition,
  revelation, vindication, hope, fear, anger, relief, awe)

  IF NO clear peak exists → score ≤ 5
  IF peak exists but mild → score 6-7
  IF peak is strong and specific → score 8-9
  IF peak is overwhelming → score 10
```

### Step 3: The "Stop Scrolling" Test

```
FOR each candidate:

  SIMULATE: Prospect is scrolling social media, mildly bored.
  They see this mechanism presented in an ad or post.

  DO THEY:
    A) Keep scrolling (score 1-3)
    B) Pause briefly, then scroll (score 4-5)
    C) Stop and read the whole thing (score 6-7)
    D) Stop, read, and click (score 8-9)
    E) Stop, read, screenshot, and send to a friend (score 10)

  WHAT MAKES THEM STOP?
    - The name alone?
    - The root cause hook?
    - The doomsday implication?
    - The super power promise?

  RECORD what stops them and why
```

### Step 4: Optimize If Below Threshold

```
IF current_score < 7:

  TECHNIQUE 1: Amplify the Peak
    IDENTIFY the strongest emotional moment (from Step 2)
    AMPLIFY it — make it sharper, more specific, more personal:
    → Add personal pronoun ("YOUR cortisol")
    → Add present tense ("is happening right NOW")
    → Add specificity ("every single morning at 6am")

  TECHNIQUE 2: Add a Trigger Layer
    IF mechanism lacks emotional trigger:
    ADD one of the 8 visceral triggers that's authentic:
    → RECOGNITION: Describe their exact experience so precisely
       they think you're reading their mind
    → VINDICATION: Name the thing they suspected was true
    → FEAR SPIKE: Quantify what they're losing RIGHT NOW

  TECHNIQUE 3: Compress the Impact
    IF emotional elements are spread too thin:
    COMPRESS into a single devastating paragraph:
    "Here's what's happening: [root cause] is [doomsday], and the
     reason [failure narrative]. But [mechanism] means for the first
     time you can [super power]."
    → ALL emotional elements in one hit

  TECHNIQUE 4: Add Physical Language
    REPLACE abstract emotion words with body sensations:
    "feel better" → "feel the tension drain from your shoulders"
    "get excited" → "feel your pulse quicken with possibility"
    "feel relief" → "feel the weight lift off your chest"
    → Forces the reader's body to mirror the sensation

  RE-SCORE after optimization
```
