# Skill 2.9: Belief Compatibility Checker

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Scorecard Optimization)
**Type:** Dimension Evaluation + Optimization
**Dimension:** 9 of 12 — CURRENT BELIEF COMPATIBILITY
**Dependencies:** gate_1_report.json, upstream_context.json, explanation_structures.json
**Output:** belief_compatibility_scores.json

---

## Purpose

Evaluate and optimize each mechanism candidate on BELIEF COMPATIBILITY: Does this mechanism FIT what the prospect already believes about the world? A mechanism that requires the prospect to abandon core beliefs faces massive resistance. The best mechanisms EXTEND existing beliefs rather than contradicting them.

---

## The Question

**"Does this mechanism FIT what the prospect already believes about how things work?"**

---

## Scoring Rubric

```
1-3 (CONTRADICTS): Requires prospect to abandon core beliefs.
     - "Cholesterol is actually good for you" — violates mainstream belief
     - Requires paradigm shift before mechanism makes sense
     - Prospect's reaction: "That can't be right"

4-6 (NEUTRAL): Neither confirms nor contradicts existing beliefs.
     - Mechanism is plausible but not intuitive
     - Requires some explanation before it "clicks"
     - Prospect's reaction: "Maybe, I'd need to see more"

7-8 (COMPATIBLE): Fits within existing belief framework.
     - Extends something they already believe
     - Feels like a missing puzzle piece
     - Prospect's reaction: "That makes sense"

9-10 (CONFIRMS + EXTENDS): Validates beliefs AND adds new dimension.
     - "You were right that [belief] — here's WHY it works that way"
     - Makes them feel vindicated AND educated
     - Prospect's reaction: "I KNEW IT! And now I understand why..."
```

---

## Evaluation Logic

### Step 1: Map Prospect's Current Beliefs

```
FROM upstream_context.audience_context:

  EXTRACT existing beliefs about:

  THE PROBLEM:
    - What do they believe causes their problem?
    - What have they been told by others?
    - What do they SUSPECT but can't prove?

  SOLUTIONS:
    - What do they believe works? (even if it hasn't for them)
    - What are they skeptical of?
    - What have they given up on?

  THE WORLD:
    - Do they trust science? Nature? Authority? Systems?
    - Are they mainstream or contrarian?
    - Do they believe in quick fixes or hard work?

  COMPILE belief_inventory:
    - core_beliefs (deeply held, hard to change)
    - peripheral_beliefs (held loosely, changeable)
    - suspicions (things they suspect but haven't confirmed)
```

### Step 2: Assess Mechanism-Belief Alignment

```
FOR each candidate:

  FROM explanation_structures.core_logic:

  COMPARE mechanism claims against belief_inventory:

  FOR each core_belief:
    DOES the mechanism:
      CONFIRM it? → Strong positive signal
      CONTRADICT it? → Strong negative signal (resistance point)
      IGNORE it? → Neutral

  FOR each peripheral_belief:
    DOES the mechanism:
      CONFIRM it? → Mild positive
      CONTRADICT it? → Mild negative (addressable)
      EXTEND it? → Strong positive (best case)

  FOR each suspicion:
    DOES the mechanism:
      CONFIRM the suspicion? → VERY strong positive ("I KNEW IT!")
      → This is the GOLD STANDARD for belief compatibility
      → Makes prospect feel validated AND informed
```

### Step 3: Identify Resistance Points

```
FOR each candidate:

  WHERE will the prospect push back?

  RESISTANCE TYPES:
    WORLDVIEW RESISTANCE:
      - Mechanism contradicts how they think the world works
      - Example: "Your body doesn't need food to heal" (contradicts basic belief)

    IDENTITY RESISTANCE:
      - Mechanism threatens their self-image
      - Example: "Your mindset is wrong" (implies they're defective)

    EXPERIENCE RESISTANCE:
      - Mechanism contradicts their lived experience
      - Example: "Exercise is the key" (they've tried and failed)

    AUTHORITY RESISTANCE:
      - Mechanism contradicts trusted authority
      - Example: "Your doctor is wrong" (challenges trusted advisor)

  FOR each resistance point:
    CLASSIFY severity: blocking / addressable / minor
    IDENTIFY bridge needed to overcome
```

### Step 4: Optimize If Below Threshold

```
IF current_score < 7:

  TECHNIQUE 1: Lead with Confirmed Belief
    START explanation with something they already believe
    THEN extend into the mechanism:
    "You already know [belief they hold]... what you don't know is
     the REASON it works that way: [mechanism]"

  TECHNIQUE 2: Validate Before Redirecting
    IF mechanism requires slight belief shift:
    "You're RIGHT that [their belief]. But there's a deeper layer
     that explains why [their solution] didn't work for you..."

  TECHNIQUE 3: Confirm Suspicions
    IF mechanism aligns with their suspicions:
    MAKE THIS EXPLICIT in the explanation
    "If you've ever suspected that [suspicion] — you were right."

  TECHNIQUE 4: Bridge Through Agreement
    FOR each resistance point:
      FIND common ground first
      THEN build the bridge
      "Like you, I used to believe [shared belief]... until I discovered
       that [mechanism] explains why it wasn't working"

  TECHNIQUE 5: Reframe Contradiction as Extension
    "It's not that [their belief] is wrong — it's that there's
     a missing piece that makes it actually work: [mechanism]"

  RE-SCORE after optimization
```

## Output Schema

```yaml
output:
  belief_compatibility_scores:
    for_each_candidate:
      - candidate_name: string
        current_score: integer[1-10]
        belief_analysis:
          beliefs_confirmed: [string]
          beliefs_contradicted: [string]
          suspicions_validated: [string]
          resistance_points:
            - belief: string
              resistance_type: enum[worldview, identity, experience, authority]
              severity: enum[blocking, addressable, minor]

        optimization_applied: boolean
        optimized_version:
          belief_bridge: string        # How to connect mechanism to existing beliefs
          validation_statement: string # What "I KNEW IT" moment to trigger
          new_score: integer[1-10]
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. Prospect's belief inventory mapped (core, peripheral, suspicions)
2. Every mechanism-belief conflict identified with severity
3. No "blocking" resistance points unaddressed in optimized versions
4. Top candidates validate at least one prospect suspicion
5. Optimization uses "confirm + extend" technique, never "you're wrong"
