# Skill 2.13: Delivery/Tangible Evaluator

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Scorecard Optimization)
**Type:** Dimension Evaluation + Optimization
**Dimension:** 13 of 13 — DELIVERY TANGIBILITY
**Dependencies:** gate_1_report.json, explanation_structures.json, emphasis_strategy.json
**Output:** delivery_tangible_scores.json

---

## Purpose

Evaluate and optimize how concretely and compellingly each mechanism candidate demonstrates the TANGIBLE ACTION the product performs. This is the 3rd element of the 3-Part Framework: the "Action That Delivers the Benefit." The strongest mechanisms don't just explain WHY they work — they show WHAT the product physically, tangibly, demonstrably DOES that produces the result.

This is NOT the same as Ease of Use (2.5). Ease of Use asks "is it simple?" Delivery Tangibility asks "can the prospect SEE and FEEL what the product specifically does that's different?"

---

## The Question

**"Can the prospect SEE what this product specifically DOES that delivers the mechanism's benefit?"**

---

## The 3-Part Framework Context

```
FROM SOURCE TEACHINGS (Steal Our Winners):

Element 3: DELIVERY/TANGIBLE — "The Action That Delivers the Benefit"

This is the CONCRETE, DEMONSTRABLE thing the product does differently.

WEAK delivery: "Our supplement supports healthy cortisol levels"
  → What does it DO? How? What's the tangible action?

STRONG delivery: "7 forms of magnesium, each targeting a different
  biological pathway — so your body can actually ABSORB it instead of
  flushing 90% down the toilet like single-form supplements"
  → SPECIFIC action. DEMONSTRABLE difference. TANGIBLE innovation.

THE LITMUS TEST:
  Could you FILM this working?
  Could you DEMONSTRATE it in person?
  Could a skeptic VERIFY the tangible claim?
  Is there a SPECIFIC action, ingredient, step, or feature
  that does the heavy lifting?
```

---

## Scoring Rubric

```
1-3 (WEAK): No tangible action visible. Mechanism is purely conceptual.
     - "Supports healthy function" — what does it DO?
     - "Optimizes your system" — how? with what?
     - "Uses advanced technology" — show me
     The product could be ANYTHING. No specific action specified.

4-6 (MODERATE): General action stated but not specific or demonstrable.
     - "Contains powerful ingredients" — which ones? doing what?
     - "Uses a 3-step process" — what happens in each step?
     - "Targets the root cause" — HOW does it target it?
     You know it does SOMETHING but can't picture the specific action.

7-8 (STRONG): Specific, demonstrable action clearly stated.
     - "6 seconds of targeted stretching that releases the psoas muscle"
       → Specific action, specific target, specific duration
     - "Chelation compound binds to heavy metals and carries them out
       through your kidneys within 4-6 hours"
       → Specific substance, specific action, specific pathway, specific timeline
     - "Three proprietary grip adjustments that redirect the clubface
       2.3 degrees at impact"
       → Specific number, specific action, specific measurable result

9-10 (EXCEPTIONAL): Tangible action so vivid you can almost feel it working.
     - "7 forms of magnesium, each one calibrated to a different absorption
       pathway — glycinate for your brain, citrate for your gut, taurate
       for your heart — so every cell gets exactly the form it needs"
       → Multiple specific innovations, each mapped to a specific benefit,
         combined into an unmatchable whole
     - "The micro-vibration pad sends 47 pulses per second directly into
       the nerve bundle — you'll feel the tingling within 8 seconds, and
       the pain signal literally can't get through"
       → Specific frequency, specific target, specific sensation,
         specific timeline, specific mechanism of action
```

## Evaluation Logic

### Step 1: Assess Current Delivery Tangibility

```
FOR each viable candidate:

  READ explanation_structures.core_logic (especially "what_it_does_to_you")
  READ explanation_structures.level_3_walkthrough (steps 4-6)

  EXTRACT the delivery element:
    - What specific ACTION does the product perform?
    - What specific SUBSTANCE/TOOL/FEATURE does it use?
    - What specific TARGET does it act upon?
    - How FAST does the action occur?
    - How MEASURABLY does it produce results?

  EVALUATE demonstrability:
    FILMABLE: You could record a video of this working
      (e.g., "6-second stretch", "grip adjustment", "apply the patch")
    DESCRIBABLE: You could describe specific components
      (e.g., "7 forms of magnesium", "3 neural frequencies")
    ABSTRACT: The delivery is conceptual, not tangible
      (e.g., "supports function", "optimizes systems")

  SCORE based on rubric above
```

### Step 2: Check Emphasis Context

```
FROM emphasis_strategy:

IF primary_emphasis == DELIVERY_TANGIBLE:
  → This is a PRIMARY dimension. Target score: 8-10.
  → Optimization is CRITICAL. Weak delivery = mechanism failure.
  → The delivery element should dominate the copy.

IF primary_emphasis != DELIVERY_TANGIBLE:
  → This is a SUPPORTING dimension. Target score: ≥ 6.
  → Delivery should be mentioned but doesn't need to be the star.
  → Basic specificity is sufficient.

SET target_score accordingly.
```

### Step 3: Identify Tangibility Gaps

```
IF current_score < target_score:

  DIAGNOSE what's missing:

  MISSING SPECIFIC ACTION:
    → What VERB describes what the product does?
    → Replace "supports" with "binds to", "releases", "redirects",
      "pulses into", "dissolves", "activates"
    → The action should be PHYSICAL, not metaphorical

  MISSING SPECIFIC SUBSTANCE:
    → WHAT is doing the action?
    → Name the ingredient, compound, tool, feature, component
    → "Our formula" → "the chelation compound (EDTA)"
    → "Our system" → "the 47-pulse micro-vibration pad"

  MISSING SPECIFIC TARGET:
    → What is being acted UPON?
    → Name the body part, cell type, system, pathway
    → "your body" → "the inflamed nerve bundle in your lower back"
    → "your mind" → "the prefrontal cortex's decision-making center"

  MISSING SPECIFIC TIMELINE:
    → How fast does the action occur?
    → "quickly" → "within 8 seconds"
    → "over time" → "across 21 days of consistent use"

  MISSING SPECIFIC MEASUREMENT:
    → How much change occurs?
    → "improvement" → "2.3-degree realignment"
    → "better sleep" → "47 more minutes of deep sleep per night"
```

### Step 4: Optimize If Below Target

```
IF current_score < target_score:

  REWRITE the delivery description with:
    - At least 1 specific action verb (physical, not abstract)
    - At least 1 named substance/feature/component
    - At least 1 specific target
    - At least 1 timeline OR measurement (ideally both)

  CHECK that the optimized delivery:
    - Connects BACK to the root cause (addresses the reframe)
    - Connects FORWARD to the science (executes the explanation)
    - Could stand alone as compelling (even without full context)

  RE-SCORE the optimized version
  SET optimization_applied = true
  RECORD what_changed
```

### Step 5: Categorize Delivery Type

```
FOR each candidate:

  IDENTIFY delivery category:

  INGREDIENT: The mechanism's power comes from a specific substance
    - Examples: "7 forms of magnesium", "EDTA chelation compound"
    - Strongest when: ingredient is novel, combination is unique

  ACTION: The mechanism's power comes from a specific physical movement
    - Examples: "6-second stretch", "3 grip adjustments", "30-second drill"
    - Strongest when: action is simple, repeatable, measurable

  DEVICE: The mechanism's power comes from a specific tool/technology
    - Examples: "micro-vibration pad", "neural frequency generator"
    - Strongest when: technology is proprietary, demonstrable

  PROCESS: The mechanism's power comes from a specific sequence of steps
    - Examples: "3-phase morning protocol", "5-step trading system"
    - Strongest when: sequence is logical, each step is tangible

  COMBINATION: The mechanism's power comes from a unique blend
    - Examples: "4-3-1 ratio", "triple-action formula"
    - Strongest when: combination logic is explained

  FEATURE: The mechanism's power comes from a product characteristic
    - Examples: "slow-release capsule", "nano-particle absorption"
    - Strongest when: feature solves known product limitation
```

## Emphasis-Adjusted Scoring Weight

```
This dimension's WEIGHT in the final scorecard (3.1) adjusts based on emphasis:

IF DELIVERY_HEAVY emphasis:
  → Weight multiplier: 1.5x (this dimension matters MORE than average)
  → Minimum acceptable score: 8

IF SCIENCE_HEAVY emphasis:
  → Weight multiplier: 0.8x (still matters, but science carries the load)
  → Minimum acceptable score: 6

IF ROOT_CAUSE_HEAVY emphasis:
  → Weight multiplier: 0.7x (delivery is backdrop, root cause carries)
  → Minimum acceptable score: 6

IF BALANCED emphasis:
  → Weight multiplier: 1.0x (equal weight)
  → Minimum acceptable score: 7
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. Every candidate has a delivery tangibility score
2. Delivery type categorized for every candidate
3. At least 3 of 5 specificity elements identified (action, substance, target, timeline, measurement)
4. Candidates below target_score receive optimization
5. Optimized versions meet target_score (or flag as structurally impossible)
6. Integration check confirms delivery connects to root cause AND science
7. Emphasis context properly adjusts target expectations
