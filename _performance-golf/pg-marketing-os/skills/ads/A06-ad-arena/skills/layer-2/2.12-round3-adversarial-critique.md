# 2.12: Round 3 Adversarial Critique (FINAL)

## Purpose
Highest standard critique. Focus on remaining weaknesses that survived 2 rounds of critique-revision. These are the deepest, most persistent issues. The Critic MUST still find weaknesses even in high-scoring evaluations -- if the Critic finds zero weaknesses, that itself is a degradation signal.

## Inputs
- 2.11-round3-persona-evaluation output (all 7 FINAL evaluations per concept)
- 2.7-round2-adversarial-critique output (R2 critiques for persistence check)

## Process
```
STEP 1: Load Round 3 evaluations and Round 2 critiques
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.11-round3-persona-evaluation.md
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.7-round2-adversarial-critique.md

STEP 2: FOR EACH concept:
  FOR EACH persona evaluation (7 total):

    A. CHECK if Round 2 weakness was addressed
    B. IDENTIFY remaining/new weakest element
      - These are the DEEPEST issues -- they survived 2 rounds
      - They represent genuine limitations, not surface-level problems

    C. MAP to specific criterion
    D. PROVIDE actionable fix direction
      - This fix direction goes to the revision phase AND to synthesis
      - Quality of fix direction matters more in Round 3 because these
        inform the final hybrid concepts

    E. ASSESS severity and persistence
      - Is this weakness persistent (appeared in R1 and R2)?
      - Is it structural (cannot be fixed by this concept) or refinable?

CRITICAL RULES:
  - Round 3 critique is the HIGHEST standard
  - If the Critic finds ZERO weaknesses: FLAG this as a degradation signal
  - Even 9.0+ evaluations have some element that could improve
  - Focus on deepest issues that 2 rounds of revision couldn't fix
  - These critiques inform both final revision AND synthesis
  - Effort level: high
```
