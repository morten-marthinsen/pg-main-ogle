# 2.5: Round 1 Learning Brief

## Purpose
Generate the Learning Brief for Round 1: extract the winner's techniques, provide persona-specific feedback for each non-winner, identify scoring gaps, and include voice preservation notes for each persona. The Learning Brief distributes TECHNIQUES, not PERSPECTIVE -- personas absorb winner techniques but maintain their own evaluative lens.

## Inputs
- 2.1-round1-persona-evaluation output (all evaluations)
- 2.4-round1-scoring output (scores and rankings)

## Process
```
STEP 1: Load scoring results
  - READ: layer-2-outputs/2.4-round1-scoring.md
  - IDENTIFY: Winner per concept and overall

STEP 2: EXTRACT winner's techniques (per concept)
  - What SPECIFIC evaluation technique did the winner use?
  - What improvement suggestions were most insightful?
  - What comparison to specimens was most effective?
  - What criterion did the winner score highest on, and WHY?
  - Express these as TECHNIQUES, not as "be more like [winner persona]"

STEP 3: Generate persona-specific feedback (for non-winners)
  FOR EACH non-winning persona:
    - What technique from the winner could strengthen YOUR evaluation?
    - How to integrate that technique WITHOUT losing your perspective?
    - What criterion gap between you and the winner should you target?

STEP 4: Voice Preservation Notes (CRITICAL)
  FOR EACH persona:
    - "Absorb [technique]. Maintain YOUR evaluative lens."
    - "The DR Strategist learning from The UGC Native's authenticity insights
       doesn't make the DR Strategist abandon conversion focus -- it integrates
       authenticity INTO the conversion evaluation."
    - Specific note per persona on what to KEEP vs. what to ABSORB

STEP 5: Identify scoring gaps
  - Which criteria have the biggest score gaps across personas?
  - Which criteria are consistently weak (potential upstream issue)?
  - Which concepts improved most from critique-revision cycle?

STEP 6: Compile Learning Brief for Round 2 distribution
```
