# 1.4: Proof-Based Angle Mining

## Purpose

Extract strategic angles from Proof Inventory (Skill 02) and Research data. These angles LEAD WITH evidence — data, studies, testimonials, demonstrations — making proof the entry point.

## Inputs

- Proof Inventory (from Skill 02 if available)
- Research data points (from Research Handoff)

## Process

1. **Load strongest proof elements**
   - If Proof Inventory (Skill 02) exists:
     - Top-ranked proof by category
     - Knockout proof (standout elements)
     - Proof by position (opening-worthy)
   - If Proof Inventory doesn't exist:
     - Extract data points from Research
     - Extract any studies/citations from brief

2. **Identify proof that can LEAD**
   - Which proof is dramatic enough to open with?
   - Which proof creates immediate credibility?
   - Which proof is counter-intuitive enough to stop scroll?

3. **Derive proof-based angles that:**
   - **Lead with data:**
     - "73% of [audience] don't know this..."
     - "[Surprising statistic] reveals..."
   - **Lead with study:**
     - "After 30 days, participants..."
     - "[Institution] discovered..."
   - **Lead with social proof:**
     - "50,000 customers have already..."
     - "[Number] people can't be wrong..."
   - **Lead with demonstration:**
     - "Watch what happens when..."

4. **Plain language angle statements**
   - State the proof-first strategic idea
   - NOT the proof itself (that's the hook)

5. **Source attribution**
   - Which proof element is featured?
   - Where did this proof come from?
   - Why is this proof strong enough to lead?
