# 1.3: Identity-Based Angle Mining

## Purpose

Extract strategic angles from Research hope quotes, FSSIT candidates (Skill 06), and Promise (Skill 05). These angles tap into identity, aspiration, and belonging — who the prospect wants to BECOME.

## Inputs

- Research hope quotes (from 0.1-upstream-packages.md — top 10-15)
- FSSIT candidates (from Big Idea if available)
- Promise package (from Skill 05)

## Process

1. **Extract hope quotes and aspiration language**
   - Load top 10-15 hope quotes from research
   - What do prospects WANT to become?
   - What identity shift are they seeking?
   - What tribe/group do they want to belong to?

2. **Load FSSIT candidates (if available)**
   - From Skill 06 Big Idea output
   - Identity tensions the FSSIT exploits
   - Aspirational identities activated

3. **Cross-reference with Promise**
   - Promise ceiling from Skill 05
   - What identity shift can we DELIVER?
   - What transformation is calibrated and believable?

4. **Derive identity-based angles that:**
   - **Tap into who prospect wants to BECOME**
     - "People like you who've already made the shift..."
     - "Join the [identity group]..."
   - **Use belonging/tribe language**
     - "If you're a [current identity] ready to become [aspirational identity]..."
   - **Reference identity shifts product enables**
     - "From [current state] to [aspirational state]..."

5. **Plain language angle statements**
   - NO promise language in the angle (angles lead TO promise)
   - Focus on identity/aspiration, not features/benefits

6. **Ensure within promise ceiling**
   - Don't promise identity shifts we can't deliver
   - Calibration check against Skill 05 promise ceiling
