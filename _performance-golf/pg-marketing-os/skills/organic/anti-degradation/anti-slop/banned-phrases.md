# BANNED PHRASES — ANTI-SLOP ENFORCEMENT
## Content That Signals Generic AI Output
## Version 1.0 — March 2026

---

## PURPOSE

This file contains phrases, patterns, and structures that signal generic, AI-generated content. These are banned from all Organic Marketing Engine outputs.

**Why This Exists:**
- Generic content destroys trust
- AI-flavored writing signals low effort
- These patterns make content forgettable
- Slop wastes distribution on worthless content

---

## CATEGORY 1: OPENING CLICHÉS

These openings are banned. Never start content with:

```
# Direct Bans
"In today's fast-paced world..."
"In today's digital age..."
"In this day and age..."
"Have you ever wondered..."
"Let me tell you a story..."
"Picture this..."
"Imagine if..."
"What if I told you..." [Exception: genuinely surprising reveal]
"Here's the thing..."
"So, here's the deal..."
"Let's dive in..."
"Let's unpack this..."
"Let's break this down..."
"Without further ado..."
"I'm going to share..."
"Today I want to talk about..."
"Allow me to explain..."
"Let me start by saying..."
"First things first..."
```

**Why Banned:** These are throat-clearing. They delay value. They signal "AI wrote this."

## CATEGORY 5: AI-SIGNATURE PATTERNS

These patterns strongly signal AI generation:

```
# Pattern Bans

1. Starting with "I"
   Bad: "I believe that..."
   Better: "Here's why this matters..."

2. Excessive use of "certainly" or "absolutely"
   Bad: "This is certainly important..."
   Better: "This matters because..."

3. Triple adjective stacking
   Bad: "A powerful, transformative, life-changing approach"
   Better: "An approach that [specific outcome]"

4. Excessive em-dashes
   Bad: "This strategy — which is incredibly effective — helps you..."
   Better: "This strategy helps you [specific thing]."

5. The "Here's the thing" pattern
   Bad: "Here's the thing: success requires effort."
   Better: Just state the point.

6. Over-qualification
   Bad: "While this may not work for everyone, and results may vary, in my experience..."
   Better: Make a clear statement.

7. The list of three with "and more"
   Bad: "This helps with productivity, creativity, efficiency, and more!"
   Better: Be specific about what it helps with.

8. Excitement escalation
   Bad: "This is exciting. Actually, this is REALLY exciting!"
   Better: Let content speak for itself.
```

## CATEGORY 7: FORBIDDEN STRUCTURES

### The "Most People" Setup
```
Bad: "Most people think X, but actually Y..."
Why: Overused. Feels manipulative.
Better: Just state Y with evidence.
```

### The "Secret" Reveal
```
Bad: "The secret nobody tells you..."
Why: Rarely an actual secret. Clickbait pattern.
Better: "Here's what actually works..."
```

### The "Mistake" List
```
Bad: "The 5 mistakes everyone makes..."
Why: Generic. Assumptive.
Better: Be specific about a particular mistake and solution.
```

### The "Simple" Promise
```
Bad: "This one simple trick..."
Why: Clickbait. Rarely simple.
Better: "Here's how to [specific outcome]"
```

## SLOP DENSITY CALCULATION

```
Slop Density = (Banned Phrase Count / Total Sentences) × 100

Thresholds:
0%      = Excellent (Target)
1-2%    = Acceptable
3-5%    = Warning — Revision Required
>5%     = Blocked — Full Rewrite
```

---

## ANTI-SLOP SCAN PROTOCOL

Every content output runs through:

1. **Tokenize** content into sentences
2. **Check** each sentence against all banned phrases
3. **Check** for banned patterns
4. **Flag** specific violations with line numbers
5. **Calculate** slop density
6. **Decision:**
   - Density ≤ 2%: PASS
   - Density 3-5%: WARN + specific rewrites required
   - Density > 5%: BLOCK + full rewrite required

## WHAT TO USE INSTEAD

### Instead of Generic Openings:
- Start with a specific claim or observation
- Open with a relevant statistic or fact
- Begin with a provocative question (not "Have you ever wondered")
- Jump into the story ("I was standing in line when...")
- Make a bold statement

### Instead of Generic Closings:
- End with a specific action they can take RIGHT NOW
- Close with a memorable line worth screenshotting
- Finish with an open loop that drives to next piece
- End with a question that prompts genuine reflection

### Instead of Filler:
- Cut it entirely
- Get to the point faster
- Replace with specific detail

### Instead of Generic Metaphors:
- Use concrete, specific language
- Create original comparisons from the topic itself
- Be literal — it's often more powerful
