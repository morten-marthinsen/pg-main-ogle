# 1.4 Backstory Development

## Purpose
Build believable backstory that explains WHY this persona has authority in their niche. Create origin story, credentials, journey, and distinctive perspective that grounds their expertise.

## Model Assignment
**Model:** claude-opus-4-20250514

## Input Requirements
- Identity concepts (from 1.3)
- Niche ownership map (from 1.1)
- Archetype assignments (from 1.2)

## Execution Protocol

### MUST
- Create origin story: how they discovered their niche, what problem/experience led them here
- Define credentials: education, experience, achievements that establish authority
- Map journey: key turning points, failures, breakthroughs that shaped their perspective
- Articulate distinctive perspective: what they believe that others don't, contrarian views
- Ensure backstory is SPECIFIC with details, names, places (even if fictional, must feel real)
- Connect backstory to current niche authority (why they're qualified to teach/share)
- Create emotional depth (struggles, victories, motivations)

### NEVER
- Create generic "been doing X for 10 years" backstories
- Skip origin story (most important for parasocial connection)
- Make backstory too perfect (no struggles or failures)
- Create identical journey patterns across personas
- Ignore how backstory establishes authority in specific niche

## Output
**Filename:** `backstory-development.md`

**Format:** Markdown backstory narratives

**Minimum Size:** 6KB

**Required Sections:**
1. Per-Persona Backstories (origin story, credentials, journey, turning points)
2. Authority Mapping (how backstory establishes niche credibility)
3. Distinctive Perspectives (what contrarian/unique views emerged from journey)
4. Emotional Depth Assessment (struggles, motivations, human elements)
5. Backstory Differentiation (how journeys differ across personas)

## Quality Gates
- [ ] Each backstory includes origin story with specific details
- [ ] Credentials and experience clearly establish niche authority
- [ ] Journey includes failures/struggles (not just successes)
- [ ] Distinctive perspective articulated (not generic expert view)
- [ ] Backstories are differentiated (not formulaic)

## Handoff
Feeds into: 1.5 Personality Framework (backstory shapes personality traits and values)

**CONCEPT CHECKPOINT:** Human reviews backstories before Phase B naming begins.
