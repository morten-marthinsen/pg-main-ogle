# 1.4 Partnership Framework

## Purpose
Design sponsorship/affiliate strategy per persona. Define brand alignment criteria, create rate cards, establish disclosure requirements. Ensure partnerships maintain audience trust.

## Model Assignment
**Model:** claude-opus-4-20250514

## Input Requirements
- Revenue model mapping (from 1.1)
- Persona Bibles (voice, values, niche)
- Audience readiness (from 1.2)
- Teaching summary (partnership frameworks)

## Execution Protocol

### MUST
- Define partnership types per persona: sponsorships, affiliate partnerships, brand ambassadorships, product collaborations
- Create brand alignment criteria: what brands fit persona's values and audience needs?
- Design rate cards: what to charge for sponsored content, affiliate commissions, ambassadorships
- Establish disclosure requirements: how persona discloses partnerships while maintaining trust
- Define partnership vetting process: how to evaluate partnership opportunities
- Create partnership pitch templates: how persona approaches or responds to brands
- Document "never partner" list: brands/products persona won't promote regardless of payment
- Include network-level partnership opportunities (all personas promote different angles)

### NEVER
- Accept partnerships outside persona's niche or values
- Skip brand alignment criteria (destroys audience trust)
- Create rate cards without market research
- Ignore disclosure requirements (legal and ethical obligation)
- Design identical partnership frameworks across personas
- Forget "never partner" list (some things shouldn't be promoted)

## Output
**Filename:** `partnership-framework.md`

**Format:** Markdown partnership strategy

**Minimum Size:** 5KB

**Required Sections:**
1. Per-Persona Partnership Types (sponsorships, affiliates, ambassadorships, collaborations)
2. Brand Alignment Criteria (what brands fit persona's values and audience)
3. Rate Cards (pricing for sponsored content, affiliate structure, ambassador fees)
4. Disclosure Requirements (how persona maintains transparency and trust)
5. Partnership Vetting Process (evaluation criteria for opportunities)
6. Partnership Pitch Templates (how to approach or respond to brands)
7. "Never Partner" Lists (brands/products persona won't promote)
8. Network-Level Partnership Opportunities (multi-persona partnerships)

## Quality Gates
- [ ] Partnership types defined per persona
- [ ] Brand alignment criteria explicit (not just "pays well")
- [ ] Rate cards researched and realistic
- [ ] Disclosure requirements clear and comprehensive
- [ ] Vetting process prevents misaligned partnerships
- [ ] "Never partner" lists protect audience trust
- [ ] Network-level opportunities identified
