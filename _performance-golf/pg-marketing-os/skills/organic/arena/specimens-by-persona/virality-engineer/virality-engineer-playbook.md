## 1. WHEN TO DEPLOY THE VIRALITY ENGINEER

### Primary Deployment Scenarios

The Virality Engineer leads when the objective is **maximum reach** — getting content in front of the largest possible audience in the shortest possible time.

**Deploy When:**
- Launching a new brand, product, or offer that requires massive awareness
- Breaking into a new audience segment that doesn't yet know Marcus exists
- Creating tentpole/hero content designed for maximum distribution
- Optimizing underperforming content that has strong substance but weak hooks
- Building top-of-funnel volume to feed downstream conversion systems
- Competing for attention during high-noise periods (product launches, cultural moments)
- Testing new platforms where organic reach is still high

**Do Not Lead With When:**
- Building deep community loyalty (lead with [[the-community-builder]])
- Creating premium brand positioning (lead with [[the-brand-purist]])
- Producing educational deep-dives (lead with [[the-value-architect]])
- Nurturing existing audience relationships (lead with [[the-storyteller]])
- But even in these cases, the Virality Engineer should review hooks and format selection before publication.

### The 70/30 Rule

Even when the Virality Engineer is not the lead persona, apply the 70/30 rule:
- **70%** of the content's DNA comes from the lead persona's philosophy
- **30%** comes from the Virality Engineer's hook and format optimization

No content should leave the Arena without the Virality Engineer reviewing the first 3 seconds and the sharing mechanics. See [[arena-protocol]] for full synthesis process.

## 2. THE VIRAL ENGINEERING FRAMEWORK

### The 7-Step Process: Concept to Viral Hit

This is the systematic process for engineering viral content, distilled from [[kane-viral-content-model]], [[berger-stepps-framework]], and the five specimen case studies in [[virality_engineer_specimens]].

```
RESEARCH → FORMAT → HOOK → BUILD → TEST → OPTIMIZE → DISTRIBUTE
 Step 1     Step 2   Step 3  Step 4  Step 5   Step 6      Step 7
```

---

### Step 1: RESEARCH — Reverse-Engineer What's Working

**Time Investment:** 40% of total effort (non-negotiable)

Before creating anything, study what is already winning on your target platform. Kane's VCM framework insists that research is the superpower — and the five specimens prove it. Nas Daily studied Facebook video patterns for months before finding the 1-minute formula. Duolingo's team analyzed thousands of TikToks before developing the chaotic owl strategy.

**Process:**
1. **Platform Search:** Enter topic keywords into your target platform's search. Identify videos/posts with view counts 10x above the creator's average.
2. **Creator Deep Dive:** Visit the profile of every high-performing piece. Look at their top 10 pieces. What format patterns repeat?
3. **GSB Analysis (Gold/Silver/Bronze):**
   - **Gold:** Top 10% of content by views — what do they have in common?
   - **Silver:** Average performers (use mode, not mean) — what's the baseline?
   - **Bronze:** Bottom 10% — what went wrong?
   - **Critical Comparison:** What is present in Gold but ABSENT from Bronze? That delta is your performance driver.
4. **Performance Driver Documentation:** Write down the specific elements that separate Gold from Bronze:
   - Hook structure (what are the first 3 seconds doing?)
   - Visual treatment (pacing, cuts, text overlays)
   - Emotional arc (what do you FEEL watching Gold vs. Bronze?)
   - Format mechanics (length, structure, CTA placement)
5. **Reference Library:** Save 20-50 Gold-tier reference videos organized by format type. These become your pattern library.

**Output:** A documented set of performance drivers and a reference library of proven formats.

**Teaching Reference:** [[kane-viral-content-model]], Section 4: Content Testing Methodology

---

### Step 2: FORMAT — Select the Proven Structure

**Rule:** Format selection matters more than execution quality.

A mediocre execution of a proven format will outperform brilliant execution of an unproven format. Kane's data across tens of billions of views confirms this repeatedly. Trahan's Penny Challenge (specimen VE-005) uses the quest series format — a format with proven viral mechanics on YouTube. Duolingo (VE-002) uses the mascot-in-unexpected-situations format — a format validated by millions of views from multiple brand accounts.

**Format Selection Criteria:**
1. **Consistent High Views:** The format produces hundreds of thousands to millions of views consistently, not just once
2. **Non-Celebrity Success:** Multiple non-celebrity creators succeed with this format (eliminates personality-dependent formats)
3. **Organic Performance:** Success driven by organic reach, not paid amplification (check likes-to-view ratio — above 1% suggests organic)
4. **Replicable Structure:** The format has identifiable structural elements that can be replicated with different content
5. **Platform Alignment:** The format works WITH the target platform's algorithmic reward system

**Proven Viral Formats by Platform:**

| Platform | Proven Formats | Specimen Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **YouTube** | Quest/Challenge Series, Levels Concept, Testing Viral Hacks, Watch Me Work | VE-005 (Quest Series) |
| **TikTok** | Trend-Jack + Brand Twist, Visual Metaphor, Two Characters/One Lightbulb, Man on Street | VE-002 (Trend-Jack) |
| **Instagram Reels** | Hook-Optimized Short Form, Multi-Variant Hook Testing, Transformation Reveals | VE-003 (Hook Testing) |
| **Facebook** | 1-Minute Stories, Bold Claim + Fast Cuts, Universal Theme in Specific Story | VE-001 (1-Minute Video) |
| **Email** | Forward-Worthy Newsletter, Gamified Referral Engine, Curiosity-Gap Subject Lines | VE-004 (Referral Engine) |

**Output:** One selected format with its structural blueprint documented.

**Teaching Reference:** [[kane-viral-content-model]], Section 3: Format Selection

### Step 3: HOOK — Engineer the First 3 Seconds

**Rule:** If you lose them in 3 seconds, nothing else matters.

The hook is the single highest-leverage variable in all of content creation. Kane's Hook Point system treats the first 3 seconds as the gate — everything downstream is irrelevant if the gate fails. Specimen VE-003 (Kane's client testing) demonstrates this empirically: testing 10-20 hook variations per concept and measuring 3-second retention isolates the hook as THE determining factor in virality.

**Hook Creation Process:**

**A. Select Hook Type**

Choose from the proven Hook Point taxonomy (from [[kane-hook-point-system]]):

| Hook Type | Structure | Best For | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity Gap** | Open loop that MUST be closed | Educational, how-to | "The one thing every millionaire does at 5AM..." |
| **Shock/Pattern Interrupt** | Unexpected visual or statement | Awareness, entertainment | "I just deleted my $2M business. Here's why." |
| **Challenge/Controversy** | Creates tension demanding resolution | Thought leadership | "Everything you know about AI is wrong." |
| **Transformation Reveal** | Before/after demanding "how?" | Case studies, results | "$0 to $47K in 30 days using one tool." |
| **Authority + Promise** | Credential + value | Expert content | "After 80,000 songs and 1.1B streams..." |
| **Question Hook** | Direct question to viewer | Engagement, relatability | "What would you do with an extra $10K/month?" |
| **Statistic Hook** | Surprising data point | Data-driven content | "98% of AI tools are useless. Here are the 2%." |

**B. Create 10-20 Variations**

For every concept, produce multiple hook variations using different types from the taxonomy above. Do not publish the first hook you write — it is statistically unlikely to be the best one.

**C. Apply Kane's 10 Elements Test**

Score each hook variation against:
1. Minimal words (magazine-cover headline test)
2. Authentic (true to brand)
3. Subverts expectations (challenges conventional thinking)
4. Easy to understand (no jargon, instant comprehension)
5. Sparks thought (creates mental engagement)
6. Creates curiosity (demands more)
7. Original (not seen elsewhere)
8. Relatable + Unique (familiar category + unexpected feature)
9. Grasped in 3 seconds (non-negotiable)
10. Offers solution (promises to solve a problem)

Hooks scoring below 7/10 on this checklist get cut.

**Output:** 10-20 hook variations ready for testing, each scoring 7+ on Kane's 10 Elements.

**Teaching Reference:** [[kane-hook-point-system]], Sections 2-3: Hook Point Creation Formula + Ten Elements

### Step 4: BUILD — Construct the Content Around the Format

With format selected and hooks ready, build the content body.

**Content Body Architecture:**

```
HOOK (0-3 sec) → COMMITMENT (3-10 sec) → ESCALATION (10-80% mark) → CLIMAX (80% mark) → CTA (final 10%)
```

**Hook (0-3 seconds):** Deployed from Step 3.

**Commitment (3-10 seconds):** Expand on the hook's promise. Add specificity. Confirm this is worth the viewer's time. If the hook is "I made $47K in 30 days using AI" — the commitment beat is "And I'm going to show you the exact tool, the exact process, and the exact results, step by step." This locks in the viewer because the commitment matches their expectation.

**Escalation (10% to 80% mark):** This is the body. Each beat should escalate in value, tension, or surprise. Apply McKee's progressive action/reaction: each revelation should be more interesting than the last. Nas Daily (VE-001) uses fast cuts every 3-5 seconds to maintain pacing. Trahan (VE-005) uses obstacle escalation — each challenge harder than the previous one. The key principle from [[kane-viral-content-model]]: never give the viewer a moment where they could disengage.

**Climax (80% mark):** The highest emotional intensity point. For educational content, this is the biggest insight. For entertainment, this is the peak moment. For transformation content, this is the full reveal. Place it at 80% — close enough to the end that viewers who reach it will finish, deep enough to reward the full journey.

**CTA (Final 10%):** Engineered sharing prompt. Not "please like and subscribe" — but a specific, benefit-framed sharing mechanism. Nas Daily's "Tag someone who needs to see this" (VE-001). Morning Brew's tiered referral system (VE-004). The CTA should make sharing feel like a gift to the recipient, not a favor to the creator.

**Output:** Complete content piece built around the selected format, with multiple hook versions ready for testing.

---

### Step 5: TEST — Deploy Hook Variations and Measure

**The 10-Hook Protocol (from specimen VE-003):**

1. **Produce 10 hook variations** for the same content body. Only the first 3 seconds change.
2. **Publish all 10 within 48 hours** on the target platform.
3. **Measure 3-second retention rate** as the primary metric for each variation.
4. **Document results** in the Hook Performance Database:

| Hook # | Hook Type | Hook Text | 3-Sec Retention | Completion Rate | Shares | Winner? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Curiosity Gap | "..." | X% | X% | X | Y/N |
| 2 | Shock | "..." | X% | X% | X | Y/N |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

5. **Identify the winner** — the variation with the highest 3-second retention rate.
6. **Analyze the losers** — what specifically failed? Visual treatment? Emotional angle? Specificity?
7. **Update the Hook Performance Database** with learnings for future rounds.

**Statistical Validity Note:** 10 variations published to an audience of 1,000+ provide enough signal to identify a clear winner. For smaller audiences, increase the number of variations or extend the testing window.

**Output:** One validated winning hook with documented data on why it outperformed.

---

### Step 6: OPTIMIZE — Push the Winner, Learn from Losers

**Winner Optimization:**
- Push the winning version with paid amplification if budget allows (Duolingo's team amplifies top organic performers with paid spend — 700% increase in purchase intent when users see organic + paid per TikTok research)
- Repost the winner at optimal times for the platform's second-wave distribution
- Create derivative versions of the winner for other platforms (cross-platform amplification per [[cross-platform-amplification]])

**Loser Analysis:**
- GSB-analyze the hooks: what separates Gold (winner) from Bronze (worst performers)?
- Document the performance driver delta
- Feed learnings into the next round's hook creation
- Build a "What Doesn't Work" reference alongside the "What Works" reference

**Iteration Loop:**
```
Test → Identify Winner → Push Winner → Analyze Losers → Update Database → Create Better Hooks → Test Again
```

This loop runs weekly. After 8-12 weeks, the Hook Performance Database contains enough data to predict hook performance before testing — dramatically reducing the number of variations needed per round.

---

### Step 7: DISTRIBUTE — Engineer the Spread

Content does not go viral by being published. It goes viral by being engineered for spread. Each platform has specific distribution mechanics (see Section 5), but the universal distribution engineering principles are:

**A. Embed Sharing Mechanics**
- Tag CTAs (VE-001: "Tag someone who needs this")
- Referral incentives (VE-004: tiered referral rewards)
- Duet/stitch-ability (VE-002: content designed for participatory formats)
- Forward-worthy framing (VE-004: content designed to be the forwarding message)

**B. Cross-Platform Funnel**
- Create platform-native teasers for every piece of long-form content (VE-005: 3 teasers per YouTube episode)
- Each teaser must create a curiosity gap resolvable only by consuming the full piece
- Short-form platforms (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) handle awareness
- Long-form platforms (YouTube, Email, Blog) handle depth and conversion

**C. Comment Section Engineering**
- Design content that provokes comments (bold claims, debates, "agree or disagree?" framings)
- Actively respond to comments to keep threads growing (VE-002: Duolingo's social team replies to hundreds)
- Comments signal engagement to algorithms, extending reach

**D. Fan Content Amplification**
- When audience creates derivative content (recreations, reactions, commentary), share and celebrate it
- This signals to the audience that creating content about your content is rewarded
- Each fan creation is a new distribution node (VE-005: thousands of Penny Challenge fan videos)

## 3. HOOK TESTING PROTOCOL

### How to Test 10-20 Variations Efficiently

This is the operational protocol for implementing the hook testing methodology from specimen VE-003, adapted for Marcus's workflow.

### Pre-Production (30 minutes)

1. **Select one content concept** with strong substance (educational insight, story, demonstration).
2. **Write 10 hook variations** using the hook type taxonomy from Step 3 above.
3. **Score each hook** against Kane's 10 Elements. Cut any below 7/10.
4. **Select 10 surviving hooks** (if fewer than 10 survive scoring, write more).

### Production (60-90 minutes)

5. **Record one content body** — the segment from second 4 to end. This is identical across all variations.
6. **Record 10 hook openings** — the first 3 seconds for each variation. Change clothes/angle/setting between takes to create visual distinction.
7. **Assemble 10 complete videos** — each using a different hook + the same body.
8. **Create 10 text overlays and thumbnails** — each matching its hook variation.

### Publication (10 minutes per variation)

9. **Publish all 10** within a 48-hour window on the target platform.
10. **Use identical captions and hashtags** to isolate the hook as the only variable.
11. **Track publication time** for each to account for time-of-day effects.

### Measurement (24-72 hours post-publication)

12. **Collect 3-second retention data** from platform analytics.
13. **Document full metrics** in the Hook Performance Database.
14. **Identify winner** (highest 3-second retention).
15. **Analyze losers** — what specifically is different about the bottom 3 vs. the top 3?
16. **Update Hook Performance Database** with pattern learnings.

### Post-Test Actions

17. **Push the winner** — repost, amplify, or create derivative cross-platform versions.
18. **Archive all data** for future reference.
19. **Feed learnings into next week's hook creation.**

### Efficiency Notes

- **Batch Production:** Record all 10 hooks in a single session. Change shirt/angle between takes.
- **Template Body:** The content body should be modular — designed so any hook can be attached without re-editing.
- **Automation:** Use scheduling tools to publish all 10 variations at predetermined times across the 48-hour window.
- **Weekly Cadence:** Run this protocol every week. After 12 weeks, you have 120+ data points — a statistically significant dataset for predicting hook performance.

## 4. STEPPS OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

### Score Content Against All 6 Berger Principles

Before publishing any content, run it through this checklist based on [[berger-stepps-framework]]. Scoring each principle 0-3 (0 = absent, 1 = weak, 2 = present, 3 = strong).

**Target minimum: 12/18 for Virality Engineer-led content.**

---

### S — Social Currency (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | Sharing this content says nothing about the sharer |
| 1 | Sharing this content mildly signals the sharer's taste or interests |
| 2 | Sharing this content makes the sharer look smart, informed, or culturally aware |
| 3 | Sharing this content is a STATUS MOVE — the sharer looks like an insider with exclusive knowledge |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Frame content as insider knowledge ("What the top 1% know...")
- Create "smart person" content people want to be SEEN sharing
- Add exclusive or surprising data points that make the sharer look informed
- Use the "inner remarkability" principle — find what makes this genuinely surprising

**Specimen Reference:** VE-002 (Duolingo: sharing = "I'm in on the joke"), VE-004 (Morning Brew: sharing = "I curate smart content")

---

### T — Triggers (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | No environmental or temporal trigger links to this content |
| 1 | Weak trigger association (general topic area) |
| 2 | Clear trigger — audience will be reminded of this content by a common experience |
| 3 | Strong, frequent trigger — audience encounters the trigger daily or multiple times per day |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Tie content to daily experiences (morning routine, commute, work frustration)
- Create recurring series associated with specific times ("Monday AI Briefing")
- Build association between a common object/experience and your brand
- Use temporal triggers (seasons, events, industry cycles)

**Specimen Reference:** VE-004 (Morning Brew: daily inbox notification = daily trigger), VE-005 (Penny Challenge: seeing a penny IRL = series trigger)

---

### E — Emotion (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | Content is informational with no emotional charge |
| 1 | Low-arousal emotion (contentment, mild satisfaction) |
| 2 | Medium-arousal emotion (interest, curiosity, moderate surprise) |
| 3 | HIGH-arousal emotion (awe, excitement, anger, anxiety, inspiration) |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Aim for high-arousal positive emotions: awe, excitement, inspiration, amusement
- High-arousal negative emotions (anger, anxiety) can drive sharing but require careful brand alignment
- Lead with the emotional moment, not the information
- Use the "so what" test: does this make someone FEEL something, or just think something?

**Specimen Reference:** VE-001 (Nas Daily: awe + inspiration), VE-005 (Trahan: anxiety + excitement + inspiration)

---

### P — Public (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | Consumption is private — no visible engagement behavior |
| 1 | Some visible engagement possible (likes) but not designed for it |
| 2 | Designed for visible engagement (comments, duets, shares show on timelines) |
| 3 | Creates behavioral residue — visible artifacts of engagement (challenge recreations, screenshots, fan content) |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Design for participatory formats (duets, stitches, quote tweets, challenge recreations)
- Create screenshot-worthy moments (quotable lines, data visualizations, frameworks)
- Include "tag someone" or "show this to" prompts that create visible sharing
- Build content that generates fan/derivative content

**Specimen Reference:** VE-002 (Duolingo: duet/stitch-able format), VE-005 (Trahan: fan challenge recreations)

---

### P — Practical Value (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | No actionable value — pure entertainment or opinion |
| 1 | General advice ("work hard, be consistent") |
| 2 | Specific, actionable insight ("Here's the exact tool and settings I used") |
| 3 | Save-worthy, reference-quality value ("This single piece replaces hours of research") |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Include at least one actionable takeaway even in entertainment content (VE-005: practical negotiation tactics inside entertainment series)
- Use specific numbers, tools, and steps — not general advice
- Create save-worthy density: checklists, frameworks, templates
- Frame sharing as helping a friend ("Know someone who needs this?")

**Specimen Reference:** VE-004 (Morning Brew: actionable business intelligence), VE-001 (Nas Daily: useful info wrapped in entertainment)

---

### S — Stories (0-3)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 0 | No narrative structure — list format or pure instruction |
| 1 | Weak narrative framing (anecdote tacked on) |
| 2 | Clear narrative arc with character and resolution |
| 3 | Compelling story where the message is INSEPARABLE from the narrative |

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Wrap every lesson in a story — not a lecture
- Create characters (yourself, a client, an example) that the audience can root for
- Build tension before revealing the insight (McKee's violation of expectation)
- Use the Heath brothers' three story types: Challenge Plot, Connection Plot, Creativity Plot

**Specimen Reference:** VE-005 (Trahan: entire series is a Challenge Plot story), VE-001 (Nas Daily: complete narrative arc in 60 seconds)

## 5. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC VIRAL MECHANICS

### What Goes Viral on Each Platform and Why

Each platform's algorithm rewards different signals. The Virality Engineer optimizes for the platform's specific physics, not for generic "good content."

---

### TikTok

**Algorithm Rewards:** Completion rate, rewatch rate, comment volume, share rate
**Viral Window:** First 1-2 hours determine distribution trajectory
**Optimal Length for Virality:** 15-45 seconds (completion rate maximized)

**What Goes Viral:**
- Pattern interrupts in the first frame (visual incongruity, unexpected situations)
- Trending sounds with original twist (format arbitrage)
- Content that provokes comments (hot takes, "agree?", debates)
- Duet/stitch-able content (enabling participatory engagement)
- Fast-paced delivery with high information density

**What Doesn't:**
- Slow builds without early payoff (algorithm kills before the value arrives)
- Over-produced content that feels like advertising
- Content requiring context or prior knowledge
- Long-form without extreme retention engineering

**Specimen Alignment:** VE-002 (Duolingo TikTok strategy)
**Teaching Reference:** [[tiktok-algorithm-2026]]

---

### Instagram Reels

**Algorithm Rewards:** Completion rate, saves, shares (DM shares weighted heavily), comment volume
**Viral Window:** First 3-6 hours
**Optimal Length for Virality:** 15-60 seconds

**What Goes Viral:**
- Hook-optimized short form with text overlays
- Save-worthy frameworks and checklists
- Transformation reveals (before/after)
- Trend-surfing content (sounds + original perspective)
- Carousel + Reel hybrid strategies

**What Doesn't:**
- Pure entertainment without save-worthy value (TikTok beats Instagram for pure entertainment)
- Content without text overlays (many viewers watch without sound)
- Generic motivational content without specificity
- Content that only makes sense with audio

**Specimen Alignment:** VE-003 (Kane's hook testing methodology)
**Teaching Reference:** [[instagram-algorithm-2026]]

---

### YouTube

**Algorithm Rewards:** Click-through rate (CTR), average view duration, session time (total minutes watched in a sitting)
**Viral Window:** First 48 hours for initial push; evergreen discoverability for months/years
**Optimal Length for Virality:** 10-20 minutes (long enough for significant session time, short enough for completion)

**What Goes Viral:**
- Thumbnails with dramatic facial expressions + curiosity-provoking text
- Titles that create curiosity gap without being clickbait (must deliver on promise)
- Series content that drives multi-episode binge sessions
- Quest/challenge formats with escalating stakes
- Practical value content that becomes a reference resource

**What Doesn't:**
- Weak thumbnails (50% of YouTube performance is the thumbnail per Kane)
- Titles that reveal the answer (kills curiosity gap)
- Content with no clear reason to click vs. watching something else
- Single-topic content without escalation or narrative arc

**Specimen Alignment:** VE-005 (Trahan's Quest Series)
**Teaching Reference:** [[youtube-algorithm-2026]]

---

### Facebook

**Algorithm Rewards:** Completion rate, share rate, comment volume (especially meaningful comments, not just emojis)
**Viral Window:** First 1-2 hours
**Optimal Length for Virality:** 60 seconds for video; any length for text posts that drive discussion

**What Goes Viral:**
- 1-minute videos with bold opening claims (VE-001 format)
- Universal themes told through specific stories
- Content that provokes tagging behavior
- Community-oriented content (challenges, shared experiences)
- Content from pages that consistently drives engagement (page-level quality signal)

**What Doesn't:**
- Link posts (algorithm deprioritizes external links)
- Content that requires niche knowledge
- Longer video without proportionally stronger retention engineering
- Content that generates negative engagement (hides, reports)

**Specimen Alignment:** VE-001 (Nas Daily 1-minute formula)
**Teaching Reference:** [[facebook-algorithm-2026]]

---

### Email

**Algorithm Equivalent:** Open rate (subject line hook), read-through rate (content quality), forward rate (shareability), click rate (CTA effectiveness)
**Viral Mechanic:** Human-powered referral loops, not algorithmic distribution

**What Goes Viral:**
- Curiosity-gap subject lines that MUST be opened
- Content so useful it gets forwarded as a gift to colleagues
- Gamified referral programs with escalating rewards
- Consistent delivery schedule creating habitual opening behavior
- Each edition containing at least one "did you know" surprising insight

**What Doesn't:**
- Sales-forward newsletters that feel like advertisements
- Inconsistent delivery (kills the habit loop)
- Generic content that doesn't warrant forwarding
- Subject lines that reveal the content (no curiosity gap)

**Specimen Alignment:** VE-004 (Morning Brew referral engine)
**Teaching Reference:** [[baer-talk-triggers]], [[eyal-hook-model]]

## 6. THE VIRALITY SCORING SYSTEM

### From [[the-virality-engineer]] Persona File

Every piece of content must be scored against the five virality dimensions before publication. This is the Virality Engineer's core evaluation system.

```
EA: Emotional Activation   (1-10)
SC: Social Currency         (1-10)
PI: Pattern Interrupt       (1-10)
PF: Platform Fit            (1-10)
SH: Shareability            (1-10)

TOTAL: ___/100 (sum of all five, each weighted x2)
TARGET: 70+ minimum for publication
```

---

### Dimension Definitions and Scoring Criteria

#### EA: Emotional Activation (1-10)

Does this content make people FEEL something?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Informational only. No emotional charge. Viewer processes intellectually but feels nothing. |
| 4-5 | Mild emotional response. Viewer is interested or mildly amused but not moved to act. |
| 6-7 | Clear emotional response. Viewer feels curiosity, surprise, or inspiration. May share if convenient. |
| 8-9 | Strong emotional response. High-arousal state — awe, excitement, righteous anger. Viewer feels compelled to share. |
| 10 | Overwhelming emotional response. Content creates a physiological reaction — goosebumps, laughter, tears, urgency. Viewer MUST share. |

**STEPPS Alignment:** E — Emotion (high-arousal focus)
**Optimization:** Prioritize high-arousal emotions. Awe, excitement, amusement, and inspiration drive 2-3x more sharing than contentment or satisfaction. See [[berger-stepps-framework]], E_emotion.

---

#### SC: Social Currency (1-10)

Does sharing this make the SHARER look good?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Sharing says nothing about the sharer. Generic content with no identity signal. |
| 4-5 | Sharing mildly signals interest area ("they're into AI"). Low identity investment. |
| 6-7 | Sharing signals intelligence, taste, or cultural awareness. "They know interesting stuff." |
| 8-9 | Sharing is a status move. "They found this FIRST" or "They're on the cutting edge." Insider knowledge. |
| 10 | Sharing is a defining identity statement. "This IS who I am." Content becomes part of the sharer's personal brand. |

**STEPPS Alignment:** S — Social Currency
**Optimization:** Frame content as insider knowledge, exclusive access, or early-adopter intelligence. Use "What the top 1% know..." framing. Make sharing feel like a flex, not a favor.

---

#### PI: Pattern Interrupt (1-10)

Does this stop the scroll?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Content looks like everything else in the feed. Nothing visually or conceptually distinct. |
| 4-5 | Mild distinction. Something slightly different but not enough to stop a fast scroll. |
| 6-7 | Clear pattern interrupt. Visual or conceptual incongruity that causes a pause. |
| 8-9 | Strong pattern interrupt. Viewer physically stops scrolling and focuses. "Wait, what?" |
| 10 | Extreme pattern interrupt. Content is so unexpected, incongruent, or visually striking that it is impossible to scroll past. Duolingo owl level. |

**STEPPS Alignment:** Not directly mapped in STEPPS, but supported by E (Emotion through surprise)
**Optimization:** Lead with the most unexpected element — visual, verbal, or conceptual. Test multiple hook types (Step 3) to find the maximum interrupt for your audience.

---

#### PF: Platform Fit (1-10)

Is this content native to the platform where it will be published?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Cross-posted from another platform with no adaptation. Wrong aspect ratio, wrong length, wrong tone. |
| 4-5 | Basic adaptation (correct aspect ratio) but doesn't leverage platform-specific features. |
| 6-7 | Good platform fit. Uses platform features (sounds, text overlays, series) and matches audience expectations. |
| 8-9 | Strong platform fit. Optimized for the platform's specific algorithmic signals (completion rate, CTR, session time). |
| 10 | Perfect platform fit. Content could ONLY work on this platform. Uses platform mechanics as a core part of the content (TikTok duets, YouTube series, email referral loops). |

**STEPPS Alignment:** P — Public (observable, shareable within platform's mechanics)
**Optimization:** Study platform-specific viral mechanics (Section 5). Never cross-post without platform-native adaptation. The same concept should be re-engineered for each platform.

---

#### SH: Shareability (1-10)

If someone watches/reads this, will they send it to another specific person?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | No sharing impulse. Content consumed and forgotten. |
| 4-5 | General "I liked that" but no specific sharing target comes to mind. |
| 6-7 | Viewer thinks of 1-2 specific people who would enjoy this. May share if frictionless. |
| 8-9 | Viewer immediately knows WHO to send this to and WHY. Sharing is an active impulse, not passive. |
| 10 | Content is inherently MULTIPLEX shareable — the viewer can think of 5+ different people/groups to share with, each for a different reason. |

**STEPPS Alignment:** All STEPPS contribute to shareability; this dimension is the aggregate result.
**Optimization:** Build in explicit sharing mechanisms (tag CTAs, referral incentives, forward-worthy framing). Ask: "When they see this, who will they send it to?" If the answer is "nobody specific," the shareability score is too low.

---

### Scoring Process

**Pre-Publication Scoring:**
1. Score each dimension independently (1-10)
2. Multiply each by 2 (range becomes 2-20 per dimension)
3. Sum all five for total score out of 100

**Score Interpretation:**
- **80-100:** Publish immediately. Allocate distribution budget. This has breakthrough potential.
- **70-79:** Publish. Strong viral potential. Monitor performance and learn from results.
- **60-69:** Optimize the weakest dimension before publishing. One weak dimension is dragging the score.
- **50-59:** Significant optimization needed. Likely 2+ dimensions need rework. Do not publish until score reaches 70+.
- **Below 50:** Start over. The concept or execution is fundamentally misaligned with viral mechanics.

## 7. COMMON FAILURE MODES

### What Kills Virality (and How to Fix It)

---

### Failure Mode 1: Hook Optimism
**Symptom:** Publishing the first hook you write because it "feels right."
**Why It Fails:** Kane's data shows creator intuition about hooks is wrong more than half the time. Your first hook is statistically unlikely to be your best.
**Fix:** Never publish without testing 10+ hook variations (Step 5). Let data choose the hook, not instinct.

### Failure Mode 2: Platform Blindness
**Symptom:** Creating "content" without specifying the platform, then cross-posting everywhere.
**Why It Fails:** Each platform rewards different signals. A great TikTok is a terrible YouTube video and vice versa.
**Fix:** Choose the platform FIRST, then engineer content for that platform's specific physics (Section 5).

### Failure Mode 3: Substance Without Hook
**Symptom:** Incredible value buried behind a weak opening. "I spent hours on this content and it got 200 views."
**Why It Fails:** The value is irrelevant if nobody survives the first 3 seconds. Content without a hook is a tree falling in an empty forest.
**Fix:** Invest 50% of production energy on the first 3 seconds. The hook is not the intro — the hook IS the content. Everything else supports it.

### Failure Mode 4: Virality Without Strategy
**Symptom:** Chasing views as an end goal. Content goes viral but generates no followers, no subscribers, no customers.
**Why It Fails:** Views without conversion infrastructure are vanity metrics. Virality is a distribution mechanism, not a business outcome.
**Fix:** Always connect viral content to a conversion pathway — follow CTA, subscribe CTA, referral loop, or funnel to longer-form content where the business lives.

### Failure Mode 5: Formula Fatigue
**Symptom:** Same format, same hook type, same structure for every piece. Audience begins to predict and ignore.
**Why It Fails:** Pattern interrupts only work if they interrupt a pattern. When your hook becomes the expected pattern, it stops working.
**Fix:** Rotate hook types within a consistent format. Keep the structure but vary the execution. Kane's testing methodology naturally combats this by surfacing new winning hooks regularly.

### Failure Mode 6: Ignoring the Losers
**Symptom:** Publishing a winner and discarding the losers without analysis.
**Why It Fails:** The losers contain the performance driver data. The contrast between Gold and Bronze reveals what matters.
**Fix:** Every test round should produce a "Learnings" document that captures why losers failed and updates the Hook Performance Database.

### Failure Mode 7: Authenticity Sacrifice
**Symptom:** Content goes viral but audience says "this doesn't feel like you." Brand identity eroded by format-chasing.
**Why It Fails:** Short-term virality at the cost of long-term brand destroys the compounding trust advantage.
**Fix:** Apply Kane's authenticity checklist: Does this Hook Point support who we are and why we exist? Can we deliver on the promise? Is this aligned with our values? This is where [[the-brand-purist]] provides essential guardrails.

## 8. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER PERSONAS

### How the Virality Engineer Collaborates in the Arena

The Virality Engineer does not operate in isolation. In the [[arena-protocol]], the VE collaborates with and is challenged by six other personas. Here is how the integration works:

---

### Virality Engineer + [[the-storyteller]]
**Collaboration:** The Storyteller provides narrative depth; the VE provides hook optimization and format selection.
**Synergy:** A compelling story with a weak hook dies unseen. A strong hook with a weak story gets views but no loyalty. Together: maximum reach WITH maximum emotional impact.
**Tension:** The Storyteller wants narrative integrity; the VE wants to front-load the most viral moment. Resolution: Open with the hook (VE), then deliver the story (Storyteller), with the climax at the 80% mark serving both personas.

### Virality Engineer + [[the-value-architect]]
**Collaboration:** The Value Architect provides substantive, save-worthy content; the VE ensures it reaches people.
**Synergy:** Educational content with strong hooks and format optimization reaches 10-100x more people without sacrificing depth.
**Tension:** The Value Architect wants comprehensiveness; the VE wants compression. Resolution: Create long-form value content (Value Architect) with short-form teasers (VE) that funnel audiences to the full piece.

### Virality Engineer + [[the-brand-purist]]
**Collaboration:** The Brand Purist ensures viral content doesn't dilute positioning; the VE pushes for maximum reach.
**Synergy:** Viral content that is perfectly on-brand builds awareness AND positioning simultaneously.
**Tension:** The VE might chase a trend that doesn't align with brand identity. The Brand Purist vetoes off-brand virality. This tension is healthy — it prevents Failure Mode 7 (authenticity sacrifice).

### Virality Engineer + [[the-community-builder]]
**Collaboration:** The Community Builder turns viral reach into community depth; the VE provides the top-of-funnel volume.
**Synergy:** Viral content fills the community pipeline. Community engagement provides the comment volume and fan content that amplifies future viral reach.
**Tension:** The VE optimizes for breadth; the Community Builder optimizes for depth. Resolution: VE leads for awareness content, Community Builder leads for engagement content, both review each other's work.

### Virality Engineer + [[the-volume-machine]]
**Collaboration:** The Volume Machine provides publishing velocity; the VE provides quality optimization per piece.
**Synergy:** High volume + optimized hooks = statistical inevitability of viral hits. More at-bats with better swings.
**Tension:** The Volume Machine wants to publish NOW; the VE wants to test 10 hooks first. Resolution: Batch content production — Volume Machine pace for body content, VE hook testing for the opening 3 seconds.

### Virality Engineer + [[the-algorithm-hacker]]
**Collaboration:** The Algorithm Hacker provides platform-specific technical optimization; the VE provides content-level optimization.
**Synergy:** Technically optimized posting (timing, hashtags, engagement pods) + content-optimized hooks and formats = maximum algorithmic amplification.
**Tension:** Minimal — these two personas are natural allies. The Algorithm Hacker handles distribution mechanics; the VE handles content mechanics.
