# TikTok Growth Trajectories Playbook

> The operational manual for scaling a TikTok account from 0 to 100K+ followers.
> Derived from analysis of 15 real creator trajectories in the companion specimen collection.
> Not motivation. Not theory. Executable strategy for each growth phase.
> Cross-references: [[tiktok_growth_trajectories_specimens]], [[tiktok_viral_formats_playbook]], [[tiktok_series_hooks_playbook]], [[One Million Followers - Brendan Kane]], [[Contagious - Jonah Berger]]

## 1. TikTok Growth Phases

Every successful TikTok account moves through five distinct phases. Each phase has different rules, different metrics that matter, and different content strategies that work.

### Phase 1: TESTING (0 — 1K followers)
**Duration:** 1-4 weeks
**Primary objective:** Find what resonates. This is a data-gathering phase, not a growth phase.

**Rules:**
- Post 1-3x daily (minimum 1x)
- Test 3-5 different format types across 15-20 videos
- Each video must have a clear, testable hook in the first 3 seconds
- Do NOT commit to a niche yet — let the data tell you where to go
- Track completion rate above all other metrics (it's the algorithm's #1 signal)

**What the algorithm does at this phase:**
- Shows your content to small test audiences (200-500 people)
- Measures completion rate, share rate, and comment rate
- If a video performs above threshold, it gets pushed to larger audiences in waves
- Your follower count is irrelevant to distribution at this phase

**Metrics that matter:**
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Completion rate | >30% | Algorithm's primary distribution signal |
| Views per video | >500 | Confirms you're not shadowbanned |
| Best-performing format | Identify it | This is your format-market fit signal |

**Action items:**
1. Create 5 videos in Format A, 5 in Format B, 5 in Format C
2. After 15 videos (one week), rank formats by completion rate
3. Double down on the top 1-2 formats
4. Kill formats below 20% completion rate

### Phase 2: FORMAT-MARKET FIT (1K — 10K followers)
**Duration:** 2-8 weeks
**Primary objective:** Lock in the format that grows you. Iterate relentlessly on your best-performing format.

**Rules:**
- Post 1-2x daily in your winning format
- Iterate on hook structures — test 5+ hook variations
- Begin developing a visual/audio identity (consistent thumbnail style, intro pattern, or signature element)
- Start one series to test follower conversion
- Engage in comments on EVERY video (algorithm rewards creator engagement in first hour)

**What the algorithm does at this phase:**
- Begins categorizing your content for specific audience segments
- Establishes your "content type" in its recommendation engine
- Consistency in format helps the algorithm KNOW who to show your content to
- Inconsistency confuses the algorithm and reduces distribution

**Metrics that matter:**
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Follower conversion rate | >0.5% of views | Format is attracting followers, not just viewers |
| Profile visits | >5% of views | People are curious enough to check your profile |
| Top video views | >10K on at least 1 | Confirms the algorithm is testing you with larger audiences |
| Series Part 2/Part 1 ratio | >1.5x | Series format is working |

**Action items:**
1. Refine your top format into a repeatable template
2. Launch a 3-5 part series to test follower conversion
3. Create a recognizable profile (clear bio, consistent aesthetic, pinned best video)
4. Engage in comments within first 60 minutes of posting

### Phase 3: MOMENTUM (10K — 100K followers)
**Duration:** 1-6 months
**Primary objective:** Scale content velocity and optimize for shareability. This is where growth compounds or plateaus.

**Rules:**
- Post 1-2x daily (consistency > frequency)
- Alternate between series content (follower conversion) and standalone content (reach)
- Begin cross-platform presence (repurpose clips to Reels and Shorts)
- Study your analytics weekly — identify which sub-topics within your niche perform best
- Collaborate or duet with creators at your level and slightly above

**What the algorithm does at this phase:**
- Your content gets shown to established audience segments
- The algorithm tests you against competitors in your niche
- Consistent posting signals reliability — the algorithm favors reliable content sources
- This is when the "For You Page" machine either locks you in or locks you out

**Metrics that matter:**
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Avg views per post | >50% of follower count | Algorithm distributing beyond your followers |
| Share rate | >2% | Content is spreading through viewer networks |
| Follower growth rate | >500/week | Compound growth trajectory |
| Video-to-video consistency | <30% variance | Stable performance = algorithm trust |

**Critical decisions at this phase:**
1. **Niche depth vs. breadth:** Go deeper into your best sub-topic, or broaden to adjacent topics?
   - **Recommendation:** Go deeper until you hit 50K. Breadth too early dilutes algorithm categorization.
2. **Series vs. standalone ratio:** How much series content vs. standalone?
   - **Recommendation:** 70% standalone (reach) / 30% series (follower conversion)
3. **Collaboration timing:** When to start duets/stitches with other creators?
   - **Recommendation:** Start at 10K. Target creators between 10K-100K (your level). Don't reach for 1M+ creators yet.

### Phase 4: BREAKOUT (100K — 500K followers)
**Duration:** 1-6 months (varies wildly — some hit 500K in weeks, others take 6 months)
**Primary objective:** Turn a breakout moment into sustained growth. One viral video is not enough — the system that follows matters more.

**Rules:**
- When a video breaks out (10x your average views), post a follow-up within 24 hours
- The follow-up should be in the SAME format as the breakout video
- Convert breakout viewers to followers with a pinned series or best-of collection
- Increase posting frequency temporarily (2-3x daily for 1-2 weeks after breakout)
- Do NOT change your content strategy because of one viral video. Stay the course.

**What the algorithm does at this phase:**
- After a breakout, the algorithm tests your OTHER content with the new audience
- If your other content performs well with the breakout audience, it distributes EVERYTHING more widely
- If the breakout was a one-off and other content doesn't match, the algorithm won't sustain distribution
- This is why format consistency matters — every video must serve the same audience

**Metrics that matter:**
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Post-breakout follower retention | >80% (don't lose them) | Breakout viewers becoming actual followers |
| Next 5 videos avg views | >2x pre-breakout avg | Algorithm sustaining increased distribution |
| Profile view to follow conversion | >10% | Profile is optimized for conversion |
| New audience demographics | Study them | Understand WHO the breakout reached |

**The breakout response playbook:**
1. **Hour 1-6:** Post nothing. Let the breakout video run. Engage in comments.
2. **Hour 6-24:** Prepare a follow-up video in the same format. Don't rush quality.
3. **Hour 24-48:** Post follow-up. Pin the breakout video to your profile.
4. **Week 1-2:** Post at 2x normal frequency in the breakout format.
5. **Week 3-4:** Return to normal cadence. Assess if new audience stuck.

### Phase 5: SCALE (500K+ followers)
**Duration:** Ongoing
**Primary objective:** Maintain growth rate while diversifying content and revenue.

**Rules:**
- Content quality must increase with audience size (expectations rise)
- Begin delegating production (editor, thumbnail designer, content calendar manager)
- Diversify formats cautiously — test new formats at 10-20% of posting volume
- Launch products, services, or brand deals that align with your content
- Build cross-platform presence seriously (YouTube, Instagram, newsletter)

**What the algorithm does at this phase:**
- Your content is shown to massive audiences by default
- But competition for attention at this scale is fierce
- The algorithm measures relative performance (your video vs your average), not absolute
- A video getting 1M views can underperform if your average is 3M

**Metrics that matter:**
| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Engagement rate | >3% | Audience is active, not passive |
| Revenue per 1K followers | Track and optimize | Growth must translate to business |
| Cross-platform growth | Growing on 2+ platforms | Platform risk mitigation |
| Content delegation ratio | >50% delegated by 1M | Creator burnout is the real ceiling |

## 2. Algorithm Favor Signals

The TikTok algorithm is a machine that optimizes for user retention on the platform. Every piece of content is evaluated on how well it keeps people scrolling. Here are the signals it measures, ranked by importance:

### Primary Signals (Highest Weight)

**1. Completion Rate**
The percentage of viewers who watch the entire video. This is the single most important metric.
- >70% completion = algorithm aggressively distributes
- 40-70% = moderate distribution
- <40% = limited distribution
- **Implication:** Shorter videos with high completion > longer videos with low completion

**2. Rewatch / Loop Rate**
How many viewers watch the video more than once. Auto-looping short videos get this for free.
- Loop rate >1.3x = strong signal
- Creates exponential view counts on short-form content
- **Implication:** Design loop-friendly content (seamless endings that connect to beginnings)

**3. Share Rate**
Viewers sending the video to others via DM, text, or other platforms.
- Share rate >2% = strong viral signal
- Shares are weighted more heavily than likes
- **Implication:** Create content people want to SEND to specific people ("tag your friend who...")

### Secondary Signals (Medium Weight)

**4. Comment Rate**
Comments signal active engagement beyond passive viewing.
- Comments with replies (conversations) weighted more than standalone comments
- Creator responses in first 60 minutes boost signal significantly
- **Implication:** Ask questions, create debate, respond to every comment in the first hour

**5. Save Rate**
Saves (bookmarks) indicate content with reference value.
- Highest save rates: educational, recipes, financial tips, how-to content
- Saves signal long-term value to the algorithm
- **Implication:** Create content worth returning to. Lists, tips, and tutorials get saved.

**6. Profile Visits After Viewing**
Viewers clicking through to your profile after watching.
- Signals that the content created curiosity about the creator
- Leads to viewers watching older content (compound distribution)
- **Implication:** End videos with hooks that make people want to see more ("check my other videos on this topic")

### Tertiary Signals (Lower Weight but Still Important)

**7. Follow Rate**
New followers gained from the video.
- Higher follow rate = algorithm shows your content to similar audiences
- **Implication:** Give clear reasons to follow in content or bio

**8. Video Information Alignment**
Hashtags, captions, audio, and visual elements that help the algorithm categorize your content.
- Consistent use of niche hashtags helps accurate distribution
- Trending audio gets temporary algorithm boost
- **Implication:** Use 3-5 relevant hashtags per video. Include 1 broad + 2-3 niche.

**9. Account-Level Signals**
Your overall account health: posting consistency, engagement history, community guidelines adherence.
- Consistent posting (same times, same days) builds algorithm trust
- Guideline violations reduce distribution temporarily or permanently
- **Implication:** Post consistently, stay within guidelines, never delete underperforming videos (the algorithm reads deletion as a negative signal)

## 3. Content Velocity Requirements

How much content you need to post at each growth phase:

### The Velocity-Quality Matrix

```
                HIGH QUALITY
                    ^
                    |
         SLOW      |    THE GOAL
         DEATH     |    (Phase 4-5)
                    |
   <----------------+---------------->
                    |              HIGH VELOCITY
         NOT       |    THE PATH
         STARTING  |    (Phase 1-3)
                    |
                    v
                LOW QUALITY
```

**The Path (Phase 1-3):** Post frequently at "good enough" quality. Volume creates more at-bats, more data, and faster format-market fit.

**The Goal (Phase 4-5):** Increase quality while maintaining velocity. This requires systems, delegation, or efficient production processes.

### Minimum Velocity by Phase

| Phase | Minimum Posts/Week | Ideal Posts/Week | Purpose |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|---------|
| Testing (0-1K) | 7 | 14-21 | Maximize data collection speed |
| Format-Market Fit (1K-10K) | 5 | 7-10 | Refine winning format through iteration |
| Momentum (10K-100K) | 5 | 7 | Consistency builds algorithm trust |
| Breakout (100K-500K) | 5 (normal), 10-14 (post-breakout) | 7 | Capitalize on breakout momentum |
| Scale (500K+) | 3-5 | 5-7 | Quality expectations rise; delegate production |

### Production Time Budgets

If your production time per video exceeds these thresholds, your process needs optimization:

| Format Type | Max Production Time | How to Speed Up |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Face-to-camera / Storytime | 15 minutes | No editing. Record in one take. |
| Text-on-screen | 10 minutes | Use templates. Batch create. |
| Greenscreen commentary | 10 minutes | One take + trim |
| Transition / edit-heavy | 45 minutes | Batch film multiple transitions in one session |
| GRWM / DITL | 20 minutes | Film during actual routine. Minimal editing. |
| Series part | 20 minutes | Pre-outline the full series. Record in batches. |

### Batch Production System
The most efficient creators use batch production:
1. **Planning day (1 hour/week):** Outline 7+ video concepts
2. **Filming session (2-3 hours/week):** Record all 7+ videos in one sitting
3. **Editing session (1-2 hours/week):** Edit all videos (if editing required)
4. **Daily posting (5 minutes/day):** Upload pre-made content at optimal times

This separates creative work from distribution work, preventing the daily "what do I post today?" paralysis.

## 4. Niche Selection

### The Niche Selection Matrix

The ideal niche sits at the intersection of three factors:

```
        YOUR EXPERTISE
             /\
            /  \
           /    \
          / IDEAL \
         /  NICHE  \
        /    HERE    \
       /______________\
AUDIENCE            CONTENT
DEMAND              SUPPLY GAP
```

**Your Expertise:** What can you talk about with genuine authority, enthusiasm, or experience?
**Audience Demand:** Is there an audience actively seeking this content? (Check TikTok search volume)
**Content Supply Gap:** Are competitors doing it poorly, or is there an unfilled angle?

### High-Growth Niches on TikTok (Ranked by Ceiling)

| Niche | Follower Ceiling | Monetization Potential | Competition Level |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Personal Finance | 10M+ | Very High (courses, coaching) | High |
| Comedy / Entertainment | 50M+ | Medium (brand deals, live) | Very High |
| Cooking / Food | 15M+ | High (cookbooks, brand deals, products) | High |
| Health / Fitness | 10M+ | High (programs, supplements) | Very High |
| Education / Science | 10M+ | Medium (courses, speaking) | Medium |
| Beauty / Skincare | 15M+ | Very High (brand deals, own line) | Very High |
| True Crime | 5M+ | Medium (podcasts, books) | Medium |
| Tech / AI | 5M+ | High (consulting, courses) | Medium-Low |
| Lifestyle / Aspiration | 10M+ | High (brand deals, products) | High |
| Music / Art | 10M+ | Medium (streams, merchandise) | High |
| Corporate / Work | 3M+ | Medium-High (consulting, courses) | Low |
| Parenting | 5M+ | High (brand deals, books) | Medium |

### Niche Differentiation Strategies

Once you've selected a niche, differentiate within it:

**1. Format Differentiation**
Same niche, different delivery. (Everyone does face-to-camera finance tips. You do cinematic B-roll finance tips.)

**2. Audience Differentiation**
Same topic, different audience. (Everyone does general fitness. You do fitness for people over 50.)

**3. Angle Differentiation**
Same audience, different perspective. (Everyone does motivational cooking. You do scientific cooking.)

**4. Persona Differentiation**
Same content, different character. (Everyone is gentle and supportive. You're blunt and confrontational.)

**5. Production Differentiation**
Same everything, better production. (Everyone is phone-quality. You're cinematic.)

The strongest position is **two-factor differentiation** — different in format AND angle, for example.

## 5. Hashtag Strategy Evolution

Hashtags serve different functions at different growth phases.

### Phase 1-2 (0-10K): Discovery Hashtags

**Goal:** Help the algorithm categorize your content accurately.

**Strategy:**
- 3-5 hashtags per video
- 1 broad niche tag (#fitness, #cooking, #finance)
- 2-3 specific sub-niche tags (#mealprep, #budgeting, #hiitworkout)
- 1 format tag (#storytime, #pov, #grwm)
- Do NOT use #fyp, #foryou, #viral — they add zero value

**Why:** The algorithm uses hashtags to determine which audience segments to test your content with. Accurate hashtags = accurate testing = faster format-market fit.

### Phase 3 (10K-100K): Competitive Hashtags

**Goal:** Appear alongside established creators in your niche.

**Strategy:**
- 4-6 hashtags per video
- 1 broad niche tag
- 2 competitive niche tags (tags your successful competitors use)
- 1-2 trending topic tags (when relevant)
- 1 series-specific tag (if running a series)

**Why:** At this phase, you're competing for audience attention in established feed categories. Using the same hashtags as successful competitors tells the algorithm to test you with their audience.

### Phase 4-5 (100K+): Brand Hashtags

**Goal:** Build searchable content library and brand identity.

**Strategy:**
- 3-5 hashtags per video
- 1-2 brand-specific tags (your series name, your catchphrase)
- 1-2 niche tags
- 1 trending tag (when naturally relevant)

**Why:** At scale, your content is distributed based on account authority more than hashtags. Brand tags make your content searchable and create a category YOU own.

### Hashtag Anti-Patterns
- Using 15+ hashtags (looks spammy, doesn't help)
- Using only broad tags (#funny, #trending) with no niche specificity
- Using irrelevant trending tags to "game" the algorithm (penalized)
- Inconsistent hashtag usage (confuses algorithm categorization)
- Never updating hashtags as your content evolves

## 6. When to Pivot

### Pivot Triggers

Act on these signals:

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| 20+ videos, no video >1K views | Wrong format or wrong niche | Test 3 completely different formats |
| High views but zero follower growth | Content is entertaining but not follow-worthy | Add series content and CTAs |
| Declining views over 2+ weeks | Algorithm losing interest or audience fatigue | Test new hook structures and posting times |
| One niche topic 3x outperforms others | Sub-niche opportunity | Double down on that specific topic |
| Audience demographics don't match target | Attracting wrong people | Adjust content language, visuals, and topics |
| Burnout on current format | Unsustainable content strategy | Simplify production or shift to lower-effort format |

### Pivot vs. Patience Decision Framework

```
Is the data clear? (20+ videos in current format)
├── NO → Keep going. Too early to judge.
└── YES → Is any format showing promise? (>30% completion rate)
    ├── YES → Iterate on the promising format. Don't pivot — refine.
    └── NO → Is your niche validated? (Competitors succeeding?)
        ├── YES → The niche works. Your execution needs work. Study competitors.
        └── NO → Pivot to a different niche entirely.
```

### The 30-Day Pivot Protocol
If you've decided to pivot:

**Week 1:** Study 10 successful creators in your new target niche. Document their formats, hooks, posting patterns, and audio choices.

**Week 2:** Create 10 test videos in 2-3 formats inspired by (not copied from) your research. Post 2/day.

**Week 3:** Evaluate. Which format got highest completion rate? Double down on it. Create 10 more.

**Week 4:** Commit. If the data supports the new direction, go all in. Update bio, pinned videos, and content calendar.

### What Creators Get Wrong About Pivoting
1. **Pivoting too early:** 5-10 videos is not enough data. The algorithm needs 20+ signals to properly test your content.
2. **Pivoting too late:** 3 months of flatline performance is too long. If nothing is working after 50 videos, something fundamental needs to change.
3. **Pivoting format AND niche simultaneously:** Change one variable at a time. If you change both, you won't know which change worked.
4. **Pivoting because of one bad video:** Individual videos are noisy signals. Judge by patterns across 10+ videos.
5. **Pivoting away from a winning format because they're bored:** Your audience doesn't experience your content the same way you do. What feels repetitive to you is fresh to every new viewer.

## 7. Common Mistakes (and How to Fix Them)

### Mistake 1: Optimizing for the Wrong Metric

**The error:** Chasing likes instead of completion rate and shares.
**Why it's wrong:** Likes are the weakest algorithm signal. Completion rate and shares are 5-10x more important for distribution.
**The fix:** Design every video to be watched to the end. Front-load the hook. Create a payoff that rewards staying.

### Mistake 2: Inconsistent Posting

**The error:** Posting 5 videos one week, then nothing for 2 weeks.
**Why it's wrong:** The algorithm builds trust through consistency. Inconsistency resets your distribution momentum.
**The fix:** Use batch production (film 7+ videos in one session). Schedule posts in advance. Never post zero times in a week.

### Mistake 3: Copying Competitors Verbatim

**The error:** Recreating top-performing videos from other creators.
**Why it's wrong:** The algorithm de-prioritizes duplicate content. And your audience can tell it's copied.
**The fix:** Study the STRUCTURE of what works, then execute with your own angle, personality, and content.

### Mistake 4: Overproduction

**The error:** Spending 3+ hours per video in Phase 1-3.
**Why it's wrong:** In early phases, volume and iteration matter more than polish. One 3-hour video gives you 1 data point. Six 30-minute videos give you 6.
**The fix:** Set a time cap per video. 30 minutes max in Phase 1-2. Increase production quality gradually as audience grows.

### Mistake 5: Ignoring the First 60 Minutes

**The error:** Posting a video and walking away.
**Why it's wrong:** The algorithm heavily weights early engagement signals. Creator engagement in comments during the first hour significantly boosts distribution.
**The fix:** Be present for 30-60 minutes after every post. Reply to every comment. Ask questions. Create conversations.

### Mistake 6: Deleting Underperforming Videos

**The error:** Removing videos that didn't perform well to "clean up" the profile.
**Why it's wrong:** The algorithm reads deletion as a negative signal. And underperforming videos can resurface weeks later through algorithmic re-testing.
**The fix:** Never delete. If a video embarrasses you, unlist it from your profile (if platform allows) but don't delete.

### Mistake 7: Niche Hopping

**The error:** Posting about finance Monday, cooking Tuesday, fitness Wednesday.
**Why it's wrong:** The algorithm cannot categorize you. It doesn't know WHO to show your content to. No audience segment ever gets enough content to build loyalty.
**The fix:** Pick one niche. Stay in it for a minimum of 30 days and 30 videos before evaluating.

### Mistake 8: Ignoring Audio Strategy

**The error:** Using random music or no audio strategy at all.
**Why it's wrong:** Audio is the most powerful trigger mechanism on TikTok. Trending audio gets algorithmic boost. Original audio builds brand recognition.
**The fix:** Be intentional. Use trending audio for reach. Original audio for brand. Never use random audio with no strategic purpose.

### Mistake 9: No Clear CTA

**The error:** Creating great content with no direction for the viewer's next action.
**Why it's wrong:** Without a CTA, viewers watch, enjoy, and scroll. With a CTA, they follow, save, share, or watch the next part.
**The fix:** Every video should have one clear CTA: "Follow for Part 2," "Save this for later," "Share with someone who needs this."

### Mistake 10: Treating TikTok Like Other Platforms

**The error:** Posting horizontal content, long intros, polished corporate aesthetics, or YouTube-style thumbnails.
**Why it's wrong:** TikTok rewards native content that FEELS like TikTok. Platform-alien content gets scrolled past instantly.
**The fix:** Study the platform for 30 minutes daily. Internalize the native aesthetic. Record vertically. Hook immediately. No intros.

## 8. Growth Timeline Expectations

Based on the 15 case studies in the specimen collection, here are realistic expectations:

### The Reality Curve

```
Expected:
Followers
  ^    /
  |   /
  |  /
  | /
  |/
  +----------> Time

Reality:
Followers
  ^           *
  |          *
  |         *
  |        *
  |  * * *
  |*
  +----------> Time
  (flat)  (breakout)  (compound)
```

Most growth is NOT linear. It's flat, flat, flat — then breakout — then compound.

### Realistic Timelines (Median from Case Studies)

| Milestone | Median Time | Fastest | Slowest |
|-----------|------------|---------|---------|
| 0 to 1K | 2 weeks | 3 days | 2 months |
| 1K to 10K | 4 weeks | 1 week | 4 months |
| 10K to 100K | 3 months | 2 weeks | 12 months |
| 100K to 500K | 4 months | 1 month | 12 months |
| 500K to 1M | 3 months | 2 weeks | 12 months |

### What Determines Speed
1. **Niche clarity** — clear niche = faster algorithm categorization = faster distribution
2. **Format-market fit** — found quickly = faster growth. Found slowly = longer flat phase.
3. **Posting velocity** — more videos = more at-bats = more chances for breakout
4. **Hook quality** — strong hooks = higher completion = more algorithm favor
5. **Cultural timing** — being in the right niche at the right moment = accelerator (luck factor)

### The Patience Threshold
If you've posted 50+ videos with no video exceeding 5K views, something is fundamentally misaligned. At 50 videos, the algorithm has had enough data to surface your content if it resonates. Options:
1. Format pivot (same niche, new delivery)
2. Niche pivot (new topic entirely)
3. Hook audit (same content, completely new first 3 seconds)
4. Platform audit (check for shadow ban, guideline violations, account issues)

## 9. Platform-Specific Growth Tactics

### TikTok LIVE
- Available at 1K followers
- Going live 1-2x/week boosts overall account distribution
- Live viewers convert to followers at 2-3x the rate of video viewers
- Best live strategy: Q&A about your niche expertise
- Optimal live time: 30-60 minutes (enough to appear in live feeds)

### TikTok SEO
- TikTok is increasingly a search engine (40% of Gen Z uses TikTok for search over Google)
- Optimize captions for search terms your audience would type
- Use keyword-rich hashtags that match search queries
- Create content that answers specific questions ("How to...", "What is...", "Best way to...")
- Long-tail search terms have less competition and more consistent views

### TikTok Creator Marketplace
- Available at 10K followers (typically)
- Brand deals can fund better production quality
- Select brand partnerships that align with your niche (misaligned brand deals confuse the algorithm)
- Sponsored content should match your organic content style EXACTLY

### Feature Adoption Advantage
- TikTok consistently boosts early adopters of new features
- When a new feature launches (new editing tool, new format, new interaction), use it within 48 hours
- Early adoption signals to the algorithm that you're an engaged creator
- This applies to: new effects, new editing features, new format options, TikTok Shop, series feature
