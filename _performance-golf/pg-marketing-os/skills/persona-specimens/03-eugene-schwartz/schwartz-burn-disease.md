## Eugene Schwartz's Own Commentary on This Ad

"We paid the author well over a million dollars. I wrote it as a special favor for the author because he had done a special favor for me. He gave me back the use of my right hand after I had my stroke. I paid $125 for the layout. It's as crude as can be. I never thought the thing would sell. Layout, letter, very crude, very small type. A lot of violations. I couldn't get anybody to run it, so I started my own business to run it.

'Burn Disease Out of Your Body Laying Flat on Your Back, Using Nothing More Than the Palm of Your Hand' are not my words. They're the author's words. I wrote seven paragraphs of this letter. But I had the ability to let this man speak for himself. And he still speaks to millions of Americans. We are mailing more in January than we mailed for the first eight years of the mailing. And it goes against very, very strong, strong, strong wonderful copy."

## Context

Originally written in 1979, this promotion ran for two decades in direct mail and as a print ad. The ad promised readers relief from heart disease, obesity and a variety of other ailments if they sent away for a book of special massage techniques.

## Schwartz Techniques Visible

- **20-year control:** One of the longest-running direct response ads in history
- **Geographic/cultural authority:** "Chinese Medicine" -- exotic origin creates mystique
- **Violent action verb:** "Burn Disease Out" -- visceral, physical language
- **Ease mechanism:** "Laying Flat on Your Back" -- zero effort required
- **Simplicity mechanism:** "Using Nothing More Than the Palm of Your Hand" -- no equipment, no cost, no complexity
- **Author's voice preserved:** Schwartz credits the AUTHOR'S words, not his own -- letting the passionate expert speak
- **Crude layout intentional:** Proves that copy strength > design quality
- **Personal story connection:** Schwartz wrote it as a favor -- personal connection to the product
- **Advertorial format:** Disguised as health editorial

## CRITICAL LESSON FOR PERSONA VOICE

Schwartz explicitly said he "wrote seven paragraphs" but "had the ability to let this man speak for himself." This reveals Schwartz's core philosophy: the copywriter's job is to find the most powerful language that ALREADY EXISTS in the product/author/market and CHANNEL it, not invent new language. The verbatim customer/author voice IS the copy.
