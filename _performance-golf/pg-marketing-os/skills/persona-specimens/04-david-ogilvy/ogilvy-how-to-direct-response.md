**Headline:** How direct response advertising can increase your sales and profits

**Subhead:** Even if your company has never used direct response, read what Ogilvy & Mather has learned from half a billion coupons

Ogilvy & Mather has created more than $1,000,000,000 worth of direct response advertising — for such varied clients as American Express Company, Sears Roebuck, Shell Oil, Reader's Digest, the American Express Company, Subscription Television, Shell Oil, Sears, Roebuck and many others.

We have learned — sometimes the hard way — that direct response advertising can help sell $1,000,000 in products as well as $1 packets of seeds.

Here are 18 of the things we have learned.

**1.** Ogilvy & Mather has identified this single most powerful form of direct response advertising — ads or commercials that generate orders or inquiries via mail, phone, or coupon. We have identified the three most successful forms of direct response:

**1. Direct response can be your "secret weapon" in new product introductions.** Ogilvy & Mather used direct mail in its introduction of Zippo's new lighter... to build a bank of interested consumers; to test offers and premiums.

The first "Guinea" was in production.

**2. Direct response can build repeat sales.** Ogilvy & Mather helped a leading beverage company use direct mail to build a list of retail outlets where consumers had redeemed coupons. Periodic promotions are now directed at these outlets to keep the product on the shelves.

3. It can build sales fast one-by-one across your board. Today Mercedes-Benz direct mail promotions are pulling almost 30 percent response — and those respondents are turning into buyers.

**4. It is your most profitable customer acquisition program** — using continuity, subscription and automatic shipment programs designed to acquire new customers and generate consistent repeat business.

These programs combine sophisticated list selection with proven creative techniques that build sales month after month.

**5. It is an indispensable tool for sales force support.** Effective direct mail and phone follow-up can multiply your salesmen's productivity and pinpoint their time and effort.

**4. How to build a bank of localized leads.**
When your product can't be sold through retail outlets, direct mail is the ideal medium for distributing information that leads to sales. Ogilvy & Mather created direct mail for the Bank of Montreal's "FirstBank" mortgage fund leads to Merrill Lynch.

**5. Direct response can make advertising more accountable.** We use a test-and-track approach to build responses for a direct response by phone. This produces carefully documented results.

Ogilvy & Mather has also improved response by putting coupons in newspaper advertising.

**6. Direct response is an indispensable marketing tool.** A company that doesn't use direct response ignores one of the key ingredients in marketing.

**Techniques that work best**

**7. A challenge coupon.** Ogilvy & Mather has developed a technique we call the "challenge coupon." We use it for breakthroughs. Our tests show that these ads pull up to 25% more coupons than conventional ads.

**8.** On-page coupons enveloped? use its results.

**9.** "The 'close'" is crucial in direct response — be specific, tell the reader exactly what to do.

Never use a "bingo card" if you want a good response because a big, beautiful letter is better.

**10. How to make long copy succeed.** Tests prove that long copy — 4 times as long — will out-pull short copy in direct response by huge margins. Offering guarantees turn readers to action. More buyers respond.

**11. The way you position your offer can make the difference between profit and loss.** We have run new advertisements against a successful control and produced cost-per-inquiry reductions ranging from 25% to 70%.

All these new advertisements improved the positioning of the offer.

**A new profit opportunity**

**11. A sleeper.** One of the most successful and fastest-growing forms of direct response is telemarketing.

Today, more and more major companies — GM, Ford, Xerox, Kodak, Quaker Oats, P&G — are using telemarketing.

It pays to look before you leap into direct response. Ogilvy & Mather can measure telephone response more precisely than print response. Telemarketing has helped develop new customers for mail and print, extended the marketing of some companies, and helped develop new lifelong customers into lifelong clients.

**Ogilvy & Mather has developed a revolutionary new way of selling direct.**

**12. It pays to demonstrate.** Product advertising uses demonstrations to prove value. On television, we ask the consumer to actually send for something. This is particularly effective if a product lends itself to Avedon camera art or live in-store demonstrations.

**13. Asking the reader to quiz himself or herself.** "Take this test. Find out what you know." Ogilvy & Mather's test ads have helped to treble response.
