## Headline

"The amazing story of a Zippo that worked after being taken from the belly of a fish."

## Context & Background

Ogilvy references this ad in his book "Ogilvy on Advertising" as an example of using storytelling in an advertisement. Zippo's unique selling proposition was that "no matter how old or in what condition your lighter is in, Zippo will fix it for you."

Ogilvy positioned them brilliantly as the lighter that lasts forever and this story was a brilliant demonstration of that positioning. Their strong guarantee helped justify their higher priced lighters which priced as high as $175 at the time for the 14K gold edition.

## Key Specimen Value

- Storytelling as proof: the story IS the demonstration of the USP
- Extreme example creates memorability: belly of a fish is unforgettable
- Story format makes the guarantee feel real rather than corporate
- Price justification through durability narrative
- Ogilvy himself cited this as an exemplar of storytelling in advertising

## OCR Extracted Body Copy (2026-02-15)

**Header:** ZIPPO — THE LIGHTER THAT WORKS

**Photo caption:** THE FISH THAT SNAPPED UP THE ZIPPO LIGHTER WAS A GREAT NORTHERN PIKE, ONE OF AMERICA'S SCRAPPIEST GAME FISH.

**Headline:** The amazing story of a Zippo that worked after being taken from the belly of a fish

Mr. Harry Best, recently retired fish and game protector for the New York State Conservation Department, told this remarkable (but true) story:

"A party, just west of Cleveland on Oneida Lake, was fishing for Great Northern Pike in Three Mile Bay. They caught a Pike that weighed about 18 pounds. When they cleaned the Pike, in the stomach was one of your lighters.

"The Pike must have picked it off the bottom or could have grabbed it before it got to the bottom. The lighter was in bad shape which showed it had been in the water a considerable time. It was the Zippo for the first time."

Even for a fisherman, it's quite a yarn. But then there are thousands of other Zippo lighters which have been through adventures that would have made Baron Munchausen's hair stand on end.

The Zippo man is not surprised that the lighter worked. He makes every one of his lighters to work. Not just for weeks, months or years, but forever.

Whether you buy your Zippo lighter in a store or hear it is a gift, the Zippo maker gives you this same incredible guarantee: No matter how old it is or its condition, if a Zippo ever fails to work, *he'll fix it free!*
