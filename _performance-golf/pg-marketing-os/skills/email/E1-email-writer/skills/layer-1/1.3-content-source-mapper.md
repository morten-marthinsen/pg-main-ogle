# 1.3-content-source-mapper.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Type-Specific Architecture)
> **Skill:** E1-email-writer
> **Purpose:** Map available content assets to template slots (what content goes in which section)
> **Inputs:** `structural_template` from 1.2, `email_context` from 0.1 (upstream packages or brief), blueprint content direction
> **Outputs:** `content_map` with specific content assigned to each template section

---

## FUNCTION

Map available content assets (from upstream packages, brief, or blueprint content direction) to the structural template's section slots. This is the PLANNING step that determines WHAT goes into each section BEFORE generation begins. Layer 2 generators use this map to know what content to deploy where.

**Key Principle:** Content mapping prevents two failure modes: (1) the body section running out of content mid-generation, and (2) the bridge/CTA section cannibalizing body content. By pre-mapping content to slots, each section has its assigned material and generators can focus on CRAFT, not content discovery.

---

## CONTENT SOURCE INVENTORY

### Available Content Sources (by mode)

**Mode A (Full Pipeline):**

| Source | Content Type | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Research FINAL_HANDOFF | Audience quotes, pain language, objections, desires | Opening hooks (QA, NR), body content (all types) |
| Root cause package | Three-part structure, villain, reframe | Body content (CT, ST), bridge pivot |
| Mechanism package | Mechanism name, uniqueness, explanation | Body content (QO, LB), bridge connection |
| Promise package | Primary promise, promise ceiling | Bridge, CTA |
| Big idea package | Central idea, mechanism angle | Opening (CT), body (QO, ST) |
| Proof inventory | Testimonials, studies, results | Body (TM), body content (all), bridge evidence |
| Blueprint content direction | Specific topic/angle for this email | All sections -- primary guide |

**Mode B (Brief-Only):**

| Source | Content Type | Best For |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Project brief | Product info, audience, pain points, outcomes | All sections |
| Blueprint content direction | Specific topic/angle for this email | All sections -- primary guide |

## MAPPING PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 1.3-content-source-mapper:

  STEP 1: Inventory Available Content
    1. LIST all available content sources (per mode)
    2. FOR each source: CATALOG key content pieces (topics, quotes, claims, proof)
    3. ASSESS content richness per source (sparse/adequate/rich)

  STEP 2: Read Content Direction
    1. LOAD email_context.assignment.content_direction
    2. This is the PRIMARY guide for what this email is about
    3. IF content_direction is sparse:
       DERIVE topic from blueprint campaign phase + position

  STEP 3: Map Content to Template Sections
    FOR each template section (opening, body, bridge, CTA):

      OPENING:
        BASED ON body_type:
          CT: Map a specific contrarian claim from content sources
          QO: Identify a relevant quote (from research quotes, famous quotes, etc.)
          TM: Select a specific testimonial and attribution details
          QA: Frame a reader question (from research FAQ/objections)
          LB: Create setup framing (why this list topic matters)
          ST: Identify story elements (character, setting, conflict)
          NR: Select a specific criticism/objection to display

      BODY:
        BASED ON body_type and content_direction:
          - IDENTIFY 3-5 content pieces for the body section
          - SEQUENCE them logically (building argument, narrative, or list)
          - VERIFY enough content exists for body word allocation
          - FLAG gaps where content needs to be generated (not just curated)

      BRIDGE:
        - IDENTIFY the bridge connection point (what lesson/insight connects to product)
        - LOAD relevant bridge phrases from transition-phrase-library.md (if available)
        - MAP the pivot sentence concept (where content becomes pitch)

      CTA:
        - EXTRACT primary action (from blueprint)
        - IDENTIFY emotional appeal for CTA (from content direction or promise)
        - LOAD CTA pattern details from cta-pattern-library.md (if available)

  STEP 4: Validate Coverage
    1. VERIFY every template section has assigned content
    2. IDENTIFY any GAPS (sections without sufficient content)
    3. FOR gaps: DETERMINE if content can be generated or if upstream is needed
    4. CALCULATE estimated content-to-pitch ratio based on mapping
    5. VERIFY ratio is within 70-80/20-30

  STEP 5: Assemble content_map
  STEP 6: Return content_map
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Content direction missing and cannot be derived | HALT -- Check blueprint |
| CRITICAL | Body section has insufficient content | HALT -- Source additional content or flag for E0 |
| CRITICAL | Estimated ratio violates 70-80/20-30 | HALT -- Rebalance content mapping |
| DEGRADED | Bridge content thin | WARN -- Bridge generator will need to be creative |
| DEGRADED | Mode B with sparse brief | WARN -- Content quality ceiling reduced |

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. **Mapping pitch content to the body section** -- Body is for CONTENT (70-80%). Pitch goes in bridge and CTA only.
2. **Leaving body content to "figure it out later"** -- Every body section must have assigned content BEFORE generation starts.
3. **Ignoring content direction** -- Blueprint content direction is the PRIMARY guide, not a suggestion.
4. **Overloading the opening** -- Opening is 3 lines max. Don't map body-level content to the opening.
5. **Mapping without checking ratio** -- Must estimate ratio BEFORE generation to catch violations early.
