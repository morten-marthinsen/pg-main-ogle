# 2.2-body-generator.md

> **Layer:** 2 (Generation)
> **Skill:** E1-email-writer
> **Purpose:** Generate core content section following body type structure. This is the 70-80% content section.
> **Inputs:** `opening_output` from 2.1, `structural_template.sections.body` from 1.2, `content_map.section_mapping.body` from 1.3, `structural_specimens` from 0.2
> **Outputs:** Body text following body type structure, comprising the majority of the email's content

---

## FUNCTION

Generate the core content body of the email following the assigned body type's structural template. This is the LARGEST section of the email (55-70% of total word count) and must be pure CONTENT -- not pitch. The body delivers value, builds argument, tells story, or presents proof depending on the body type. The reader must get genuine value from the body section alone, even if they never click the CTA.

**Key Principle:** The body is the VALUE DELIVERY section. A reader who reads only the body and never clicks should still feel their time was well-spent. This is what keeps them opening future emails. Pitch belongs in bridge and CTA, not body.

## BODY-TYPE-SPECIFIC GENERATION PROTOCOLS

### CT (Contrarian): Stack-and-Dismiss

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. PRESENT conventional wisdom (what most people believe) -- 2-3 sentences
  2. FIRST DISMISSAL with evidence or logic -- 3-5 sentences
     - Cite specific evidence, study, or logical flaw
     - Don't just assert "they're wrong" -- SHOW why
  3. SECOND DISMISSAL (optional, for longer CT emails) -- 3-5 sentences
     - Different angle from first dismissal
     - Builds cumulative case
  4. BUILD contrarian case -- what's actually true -- 3-5 sentences
     - This is the PAYOFF of the contrarian argument
     - Must feel revelatory, not just "different opinion"

TONE: Confident, intellectually playful, slightly aggressive
PACING: Escalating certainty -- start questioning, end declaring
```

### QO (Quote-Opener): Riff on Quote

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. WHY THIS QUOTE MATTERS -- 2-4 sentences
     - Don't just paraphrase the quote
     - Add context, history, or personal perspective
  2. HOW IT APPLIES TO READER'S SITUATION -- 3-6 sentences
     - Make it specific to niche and audience
     - Use concrete examples or scenarios
     - Reader should think "that's exactly my situation"
  3. LESSON EXTRACTION -- 2-4 sentences
     - The specific, actionable takeaway
     - What the reader should do/think differently
     - This sets up the bridge

TONE: Thoughtful, analytical, authority-building
PACING: Expanding understanding -- from quote to universal truth
```

### TM (Testimonial): Testimonial Block

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. TESTIMONIAL -- verbatim or near-verbatim -- bulk of body
     - Keep the person's actual language
     - Preserve specific details (numbers, timeframes, emotions)
     - If edited, preserve voice and key specifics
  2. BEFORE/AFTER CONTRAST (if available) -- 2-4 sentences within testimonial
     - What life was like before
     - What changed
     - How it is now

TONE: Let the testimonial speak -- minimal editorial intervention
PACING: The testimonial sets its own pace -- follow the speaker's energy
SPECIAL RULE: Body is SHORTER for TM (testimonial does the work, not commentary)
```

### QA (Q&A): Question + Substantive Answer

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. DISPLAY THE QUESTION -- formatted, set apart
     - Use quote formatting or italics
     - Keep question intact (don't reword)
  2. ANSWER SUBSTANTIVELY -- 4-8 sentences
     - Don't give a one-line answer
     - Provide genuine depth and value
     - Include reasoning, not just conclusion
  3. ADD VALUE BEYOND SIMPLE ANSWER -- 2-4 sentences
     - "But here's what most people miss..."
     - Expand beyond the literal question
  4. EXTRACT BROADER LESSON -- 2-3 sentences
     - What this means for the reader generally
     - The principle behind the specific answer

TONE: Helpful, conversational, teacher-student dynamic
PACING: From specific question to broader understanding
```

### LB (List-Based): Numbered/Bulleted List

```
STRUCTURE:
  - 3-7 ITEMS (sweet spot: 5)
  - Each item: 1-3 sentences
  - Items BUILD on each other (not random collection)
  - First item: accessible, engaging (hook them into the list)
  - Middle items: building value, depth
  - Last item: strongest or most surprising (payoff)

FORMAT OPTIONS:
  Numbered: When order matters or count is the hook
  Bulleted: When items are equal weight

TONE: Authoritative, organized, actionable
PACING: Each item is a mini-payoff -- reader gets value per item
SPECIAL RULE: Each list item must be self-contained but connected
```

### ST (Story): Narrative Development

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. CHARACTERS AND SETTING -- 2-3 sentences
     - Who is involved? Where are we?
     - Specific sensory details
  2. CONFLICT/TENSION -- 3-5 sentences
     - What's at stake? What went wrong?
     - Build emotional investment
  3. TURNING POINT OR REVELATION -- 2-4 sentences
     - The moment everything changed
     - This is the narrative climax
  4. RESOLUTION OR CLIFFHANGER -- 2-3 sentences
     - How it ended (or where it's heading)
     - If cliffhanger: "And that's when I discovered..."

TONE: Conversational, vivid, emotionally engaging
PACING: Rising tension -- slow build to turning point, then release
SPECIAL RULE: Story must feel REAL, not manufactured for selling purposes
```

### NR (Negative Response): Demolition

```
STRUCTURE:
  1. ADDRESS CRITICISM DIRECTLY -- 2-3 sentences
     - Don't dodge the criticism
     - Show you're taking it seriously (before demolishing it)
  2. DISMANTLE WITH LOGIC, HUMOR, OR EVIDENCE -- 4-6 sentences
     - Choose the right weapon for the criticism:
       - Logic: for factual errors
       - Humor: for absurd criticisms
       - Evidence: for sincere but wrong criticisms
  3. TURN INTO TEACHING MOMENT -- 3-5 sentences
     - What this criticism reveals about common misconceptions
     - Why the critic's perspective is actually understandable
     - The broader lesson for the reader

TONE: Entertaining, confident, slightly combative (NEVER mean or personal)
PACING: Address → Demolish → Teach -- escalate from defense to offense to wisdom
SPECIAL RULE: Never punch down. Never be cruel. Be entertaining and educational.
```

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 2.2-body-generator:

  STEP 1: Load Inputs
    1. READ opening_output from 2.1 (body must continue from opening's tone)
    2. READ structural_template.sections.body from 1.2
    3. READ content_map.section_mapping.body from 1.3
    4. HOLD structural_specimens in active context

  STEP 2: Study Specimen Bodies
    FOR each loaded specimen:
      1. ISOLATE the body section
      2. ANALYZE structure, pacing, paragraph length
      3. NOTE how content flows between sub-sections
      4. IDENTIFY body-type-specific patterns
    INTERNALIZE the body structure for this body type

  STEP 3: Generate Body Section by Section
    FOR each section defined in template structure:
      1. LOAD assigned content piece from content_map
      2. FOLLOW body-type-specific structure (from protocols above)
      3. MAINTAIN tone established by opening
      4. ENFORCE paragraph brevity (max 3 sentences, prefer 1-2)
      5. ENSURE section delivers genuine VALUE (not pitch)

  STEP 4: Body-Level Quality Check
    [ ] All structural sections present (per body type protocol)
    [ ] Body word count within allocated range (55-70% of total)
    [ ] All paragraphs are 3 sentences or fewer
    [ ] Body is CONTENT, not pitch (zero selling language)
    [ ] Body continues tone from opening
    [ ] Body builds logically (escalating argument, deepening understanding, or rising tension)
    [ ] Zero slop words
    [ ] Niche-appropriate language throughout

  STEP 5: Ratio Pre-Check
    1. ESTIMATE content-to-pitch ratio with current body
    2. Body should be 100% content (pitch goes in bridge/CTA)
    3. IF any pitch language detected in body: FLAG for revision

  STEP 6: Output
    WRITE body text to output file
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Body sections missing from template structure | HALT -- Add missing sections |
| CRITICAL | Pitch language detected in body section | HALT -- Revise to pure content |
| CRITICAL | Paragraph exceeds 3 sentences | HALT -- Break into shorter paragraphs |
| CRITICAL | Body word count outside allocation | HALT -- Edit to fit range |
| WARNING | Body doesn't build logically | Restructure section order |
| WARNING | Tone shift from opening | Revise for consistency |

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. **Pitch in the body** -- Body is 100% content. ANY selling language is a violation.
2. **Wall-of-text paragraphs** -- Max 3 sentences. Most paragraphs should be 1-2 sentences.
3. **Generic content** -- Body must be niche-specific and audience-appropriate. No "one-size-fits-all" advice.
4. **Missing structural sections** -- If the body type protocol says 4 sections, there must be 4 sections.
5. **Body that doesn't flow from opening** -- Opening sets the tone; body continues it.
6. **Slop words** -- Zero tolerance in body content.
