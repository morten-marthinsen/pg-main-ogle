# Specimen: Ben Settle — 24 (not very) mindblowing tips for tripling your success (List-Based)

## Metadata
- **Author:** Ben Settle
- **Body Type:** LB (List-Based)
- **Campaign Function:** DR (Daily Relationship)
- **Source:** Ben Settle Email Players, Wednesday, March 21, 2018
- **Subject Line:** "24 (not very) mindblowing tips for tripling your success"
- **Word Count:** ~324
- **Structural Quality:** Pure rapid-fire tip stacking -- no elaboration, no breathing room, just 24 items delivered at maximum speed. The anti-hype subject line ("not very mindblowing") is classic Settle contrarian positioning. The final list item IS the CTA, which eliminates the need for a separate bridge section entirely.

---

## Full Email Text

<!-- [OPENING — One-sentence setup, then immediately into the list] -->
If you want to triple (at least) your sales, income, and all-around success in a fast way, print this email out and apply ye the following 24 tips to your business (starting today):

<!-- [BODY — 24-item numbered rapid-fire tip list, no elaboration on any item] -->
1. Be an investor-minded buyer, not an opportunity-minded buyer

2. Embrace repetition i.e. seek a deep vs wide knowledge of what you want to learn

3. Ignore 99% of anything you hear being taught on Facebook

4. Buy information (i.e. you use and benefit from info you spend money on), don't pay for it (i.e. you never use or benefit from info you spend money on)

5. Eliminate all neediness from your marketing

6. Do something every day to make yourself better today than you were yesterday

7. Ruthlessly curate your daily content (garbage in, garbage out is a real phenomenon)

8. Think and problem-solve, don't mindlessly swipe copy

9. Always be more consistent than the other guy

10. Don't just build a list, build an audience

11. Read 10 great books on a subject 10 times vs 100 books 1 time

12. Don't Virtue Signal

13. Avoid being cheap

14. Ignore social proof when making buying decisions

15. Master the principles before executing the tactics

16. Create outcome independence with everything you do

17. Make goals you can control (i.e. eating right and exercising) vs those you can't (i.e. losing x amount of weight by y date)

18. Create your own media so you don't have to rely on anyone else's

19. Respect your list's time

20. Don't treat your email list like a booty call

21. Practice storytelling via writing emails each day to your list selling something

22. Don't hide your contempt for anyone you don't want buying from you

23. Read this list 10 times, and think about how to apply each to your business

<!-- [CTA — Final list item IS the call to action, no separate bridge needed] -->
24. To get a deep knowledge of how to write emails people love reading and buying from, go to:

http://www.EmailPlayers.com

Ben Settle

## Structural Analysis
- **List Type:** Rapid-fire tip list (no elaboration, maximum density)
- **List Count:** 24 items
- **List Device:** Numbered (1-24)
- **Opening Hook:** Single-sentence command ("print this email out and apply ye the following 24 tips") -- no story, no setup, just a direct instruction that frames the list as actionable
- **Bridge Type:** List-item-as-CTA -- item #23 says "read this list 10 times" (meta-engagement), then item #24 seamlessly becomes the product pitch. No separate bridge paragraph exists.
- **Key Structural Move:** The CTA buried as the final list item. By making the product link item #24 in a numbered sequence, it feels like "just another tip" rather than a pitch. The reader's eye is already in list-scanning mode, so the link gets processed with the same momentum as the prior 23 items. Also notable: item #23 ("read this list 10 times") is a self-referential engagement device that makes the reader pause and re-engage right before the pitch.
