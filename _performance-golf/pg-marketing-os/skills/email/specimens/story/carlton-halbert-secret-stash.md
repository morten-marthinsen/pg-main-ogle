## Structural Analysis

### Body Type Pattern: ST (Story)
1. **One-line hook**: "This is pretty shocking." — pure curiosity, no context.
2. **Character setup** (2 paragraphs): Establishes Gary Halbert as a character — genius but disorganized. Carlton's personal relationship adds credibility.
3. **Discovery beat**: "His sons... have uncovered a treasure-trove" — the emotional peak of the story.
4. **What was found**: Brief description of recordings, name-dropping (J. Abraham) to increase desire.
5. **Anti-launch framing**: "This isn't a launch -- it's just a chance to check out" — defuses sales resistance.
6. **Multiple CTAs**: Three separate pushes ("Just click", "Just see what's up", "Go here now") — each with different framing.

### Key Techniques
- **Ellipsis pacing** — Carlton's signature "..." creates conversational pauses and builds suspense
- **Short paragraphs** — no paragraph exceeds 3 lines, many are single lines
- **Personality injection** — "greedy little heart fluttering", "freakin' stunner-deal" — unmistakably Carlton
- **Scarcity without pressure** — "Very few folks know", "tight lid on how many" — curiosity-based urgency, not deadline-based
- **Self-insertion** — "I'm in these recordings, too" — adds personal endorsement credibility
- **Anti-launch frame** — explicitly saying "this isn't a launch" reduces sales resistance while still selling
- **Casual CTA language** — "Just see what's up" and "Just do it" feel like peer advice, not sales commands
