## Structural Analysis

### Body Type Pattern: CT (Contrarian)
1. **Bold contrarian claim** (2 paragraphs): "Buy what everyone else is selling" — immediately challenges conventional wisdom.
2. **Crowd dismissal** (2 paragraphs): "The thundering herd always wants to buy things that are already soaring" — creates us-vs-them framing.
3. **Personal proof** (~10 paragraphs): Specific stocks, specific entry prices, specific % gains. Heavy on numbers.
4. **Third-party proof stack** (~15 paragraphs): Cascading list of contrarian wins with escalating returns (154% → 8,214%). The escalation is key — each example is bigger than the last.
5. **Bridge/exclusion**: "Unfortunately, you are NOT on the list" — creates FOMO.
6. **Product introduction**: Million-Dollar Contrarian Portfolio, Dr. Weiss investing $1M of own money.
7. **Deadline urgency**: "MUST CLOSE THIS WEEK" — hard deadline with permanent consequences.

### Key Techniques
- **Escalating proof cascade** — starts at 154% and climbs to 8,214%, creating momentum and increasing desire with each data point
- **Repetitive structure** — "The crowd hammered X... But contrarian investors could have grabbed Y%" — same pattern repeated 8+ times creates rhythmic certainty
- **"Not on the list" bridge** — telling someone they're excluded from something creates immediate desire to be included
- **Skin in the game** — "Dr. Weiss... $1 million of his own personal money" — principal risk sharing builds trust
- **Specific numbers throughout** — $9.75 silver, $3.57 Silver Wheaton, 411%, 777% — specificity builds credibility
- **Content-to-pitch ratio** — approximately 75% proof content / 25% pitch, matching the email engine target
