## Headline Unit Definition

A complete headline unit includes:
- **Main Headline:** The primary attention-capturing line (5-15 words typical)
- **Subheadline:** The supporting/expanding line that adds credibility, specificity, or curiosity (10-30 words typical)
- **Deck Copy:** (Optional) Additional context lines that further develop the promise

The subheadline is NOT optional for analysis. Many headlines only work BECAUSE of their subheadline support.

### Gold #1: "End of America" — Existential Question + Authority Stack

**Source:** Porter Stansberry / Stansberry Research
**Niche:** Financial/Macro | **Format:** Magalog | **Quality Score:** 95/100
**Type:** Existential Question + Authority Validation

```
VERBATIM HEADLINE UNIT:

HEADLINE: Will This Be The End of America As We Know It?

SUBHEADLINE: The day of reckoning has arrived...

DECK COPY / AUTHORITY STACK:
"What is the government not telling you about the stability of our financial
system?" — Governor Chris Christie
"The U.S. is on a path toward becoming the next Greece or Spain." — 60 Minutes
"In 2012 or 2013, we're going to have a major currency crisis in the US." — Jim Rogers
"There is a significant risk of the standard of living going down for this
country over the next century." — Meredith Whitney

CAUTION: This presentation is controversial and may be offensive to some.
If you do not want to discover the truth about the problems with our economy
and what the next several years may have in store, please leave this page now.
```

**Pattern DNA:**
- **Question technique:** Forces reader to consider unthinkable possibility
- **Patriotic hook:** "America" identity creates immediate emotional stake
- **Authority stack:** Multiple diverse authorities (political, media, financial) create overwhelming proof
- **Warning label:** "Controversial/Offensive" disclaimer creates forbidden fruit appeal
- **Schema distance:** 9/10 — Violates "America is stable" assumption
- **Why it works:** The question format makes the reader answer internally. The authority stack then validates that the answer might be "yes."

## Subheadline Support Patterns

1. **Authority Stack:** Multiple quotes from credible sources
2. **Specificity Injection:** Numbers, percentages, timeframes
3. **Proof Preview:** "Research proves...", "Studies show..."
4. **Curiosity Extension:** "INSIDE: Discover how..."
5. **Urgency Creation:** Time-bound language
6. **Testimonial Hook:** Direct quote from transformed user

---

## Generation Calibration Notes

When generating headlines, these specimens calibrate:

1. **Promise magnitude:** These headlines make BIG promises backed by proof
2. **Specificity level:** Numbers are specific but believable (not round)
3. **Authority integration:** Authority is front-loaded, not buried
4. **Emotional trajectory:** Fear → hope, anger → revenge, curiosity → access
5. **Schema distance:** 6-9 range — surprising but not confusing
6. **Subheadline necessity:** Every strong headline has stronger support

**Key Insight:** The subheadline often does the HEAVY LIFTING. The headline captures attention; the subheadline builds the promise. Generate them as a unit, not separately.
