# 3.1-power-word-injector.md

> **Layer:** 3 (Refinement)
> **Skill:** 10-headlines
> **Purpose:** Enhance headline candidates with emotional power words
> **Inputs:** `top_5_candidates` from Layer 2
> **Outputs:** Power-word-enhanced versions of each candidate

---

## FUNCTION

Analyze the top 5 headline candidates and inject POWER WORDS that amplify emotional impact. Power words are high-arousal terms that trigger immediate emotional responses — they make headlines FEEL more urgent, more important, more compelling.

**Key Principle:** Power words are spice, not the meal. One or two strategically placed power words elevate a headline. Too many make it feel hypey and untrustworthy.

---

## POWER WORD TAXONOMY

### Category 1: FEAR/URGENCY Words

```
WORDS: Warning, Danger, Alert, Crisis, Emergency, Devastating, Terrifying,
       Alarming, Shocking, Exposed, Risky, Deadly, Fatal, Hidden, Secret,
       Banned, Forbidden, Suppressed

USE WHEN: Fear-based positioning, threat awareness, protection messaging

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "The Problem With Your Retirement Plan"
  AFTER:  "The HIDDEN Problem With Your Retirement Plan"
```

### Category 2: GREED/OPPORTUNITY Words

```
WORDS: Free, Bonus, Exclusive, Limited, Secret, Insider, Millionaire,
       Fortune, Wealth, Profit, Jackpot, Windfall, Breakthrough, Discovery,
       Revealed, Exposed

USE WHEN: Opportunity positioning, gain framing, insider access

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "How to Invest in the Coming Market Shift"
  AFTER:  "How to PROFIT From the Coming Market Shift"
```

### Category 3: CURIOSITY/INTRIGUE Words

```
WORDS: Strange, Weird, Unusual, Bizarre, Surprising, Shocking, Unexpected,
       Little-known, Underground, Mysterious, Controversial, Forbidden,
       Counterintuitive, Paradox

USE WHEN: Curiosity-driven headlines, pattern interrupts, contrarian angles

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "The Reason Your Diet Keeps Failing"
  AFTER:  "The WEIRD Reason Your Diet Keeps Failing"
```

### Category 4: AUTHORITY/PROOF Words

```
WORDS: Proven, Guaranteed, Certified, Doctor-approved, Research-backed,
       Scientific, Studies show, Evidence, Clinical, Harvard, Nobel,
       Expert, Official, Verified

USE WHEN: Credibility building, skeptical audiences, bold claims

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "A New Way to Lower Blood Pressure"
  AFTER:  "A PROVEN Way to Lower Blood Pressure"
```

### Category 5: EASE/SPEED Words

```
WORDS: Simple, Easy, Quick, Fast, Instant, Automatic, Effortless,
       Painless, Lazy, Overnight, Minutes, Seconds, Finally, Now

USE WHEN: Reducing perceived effort, speed promises, simplicity angles

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "How to Learn a New Language"
  AFTER:  "The SIMPLE Way to Learn a New Language"
```

### Category 6: TRANSFORMATION Words

```
WORDS: Transform, Revolutionary, Breakthrough, Miracle, Amazing, Incredible,
       Stunning, Remarkable, Extraordinary, Life-changing, Game-changing,
       Ultimate, Complete, Total

USE WHEN: Transformation promises, major benefit claims, paradigm shifts

EXAMPLE:
  BEFORE: "A New Approach to Weight Loss"
  AFTER:  "A REVOLUTIONARY Approach to Weight Loss"
```

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Analyze Candidate Weaknesses

```
FOR each of top_5_candidates:

  REVIEW scores from 2.5:
    IF emotional_impact < 8:
      FLAG for power word injection
    IF curiosity < 8:
      FLAG for intrigue word injection
    IF clarity low BUT emotion high:
      SKIP power words (don't add complexity)

  IDENTIFY insertion opportunities:
    - Adjective slots (before nouns)
    - Verb replacements (weak → strong)
    - Opener positions (first word impact)
```

### Step 2: Select Power Words

```
FOR each flagged candidate:

  MATCH power word category to headline type:
    IF warning/fear headline → Fear/Urgency words
    IF opportunity headline → Greed/Opportunity words
    IF curiosity headline → Curiosity/Intrigue words
    IF authority headline → Authority/Proof words
    IF ease/speed headline → Ease/Speed words
    IF transformation headline → Transformation words

  SELECT 1-2 power words max per headline
  AVOID using same word twice in a headline unit (headline + sub)
```

### Step 3: Injection Methods

```
METHOD A: Adjective Insertion
  "The Reason Your Diet Failed" → "The SHOCKING Reason Your Diet Failed"
  Insert power adjective before key noun

METHOD B: Verb Upgrade
  "How to Improve Your Golf Swing" → "How to TRANSFORM Your Golf Swing"
  Replace weak verb with power verb

METHOD C: Opener Upgrade
  "Learn the Secret to Wealth" → "FINALLY: Learn the Secret to Wealth"
  Add power word as opener/flag

METHOD D: Emphasis Marking
  "The 17-Cent Circulation Miracle" → "The AMAZING 17-Cent Circulation Miracle"
  Add power word with caps for emphasis
```

### Step 4: Quality Validation

```
FOR each enhanced headline:

  CHECK:
    [ ] Power word feels natural, not forced
    [ ] Headline is still clear (no confusion added)
    [ ] Power word matches emotional trajectory
    [ ] Not more than 2 power words per headline
    [ ] Doesn't feel "hypey" or scammy

  IF any check fails:
    - Try different power word
    - Try different placement
    - Remove power word if no good fit
```

**Why:** Adds conspiracy/hidden angle to curiosity

**Original:** "A Doctor's Approach to Heart Health"
**Enhanced:** "A Harvard Doctor's PROVEN Approach to Heart Health"
**Words Added:** Harvard (specificity) + PROVEN (authority)
**Why:** Doubled credibility without feeling hypey

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. **Power Word Stacking**
   - BAD: "The SHOCKING AMAZING REVOLUTIONARY Secret to..."
   - One or two words max, not a pile

2. **Mismatched Emotion**
   - Don't add fear words to hope headlines
   - Don't add ease words to warning headlines

3. **Forced Insertion**
   - If there's no natural slot, don't force it
   - Some headlines are complete without power words

4. **Credibility Destruction**
   - "MIRACLE" and "GUARANTEED" in same headline = scam signal
   - Match power level to proof available
