# Layer 4.2 — Emotional Sale Auditor

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Type:** Emotional Outcome Gate
**Dependencies:** full draft lead text (from Layer 3 output), emotional_arc (from 1.4), teaching_index.e5_framework (from 0.3), teaching_index.georgi_formula (from 0.3), mechanism_elevator_pitch (from 2.2), open_loops (from 2.3)
**Output:** emotional_sale_audit.json

---

## Purpose

Audit whether the lead achieves its core purpose — the emotional sale. By the end of the lead, the prospect must arrive at: *"This sounds amazing. This sounds perfect. Now prove to me that it works and will work for me."* This is the DEFINING test of the lead. A lead can have all four E5 elements, pass Georgi compliance, and score well on anti-slop — and still FAIL if it does not produce the emotional sale. The emotional sale is not a checkbox. It is the gestalt outcome of every lead element working together. This auditor evaluates that gestalt.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Read the Lead as a Skeptical Prospect

```
ASSUME the perspective of a real prospect:
  - They clicked the headline out of mild curiosity or hope
  - They are NOT pre-sold — they are cautiously interested at best
  - They have been burned before by similar promises
  - They are ready to click away at any moment
  - They do NOT trust the author yet

READ the full_draft_lead.full_text from beginning to end
in one continuous pass — do NOT analyze sentence by sentence yet.

AFTER the full read, record FIRST IMPRESSION:
  - What is the prospect's emotional state at the END of the lead?
  - Did the lead create DESIRE for what the mechanism promises?
  - Does the prospect want to KEEP READING?
  - Does the prospect feel this could work for THEM specifically?

RECORD prospect_state_at_end: string (free-form emotional assessment)
```

### Step 2: Score Dimension 1 — Emotional Excitement

```
EVALUATE: Does the lead create genuine excitement about the mechanism?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: Prospect is viscerally excited — heart rate up, leaning forward, can't stop reading
  8-9: Strong excitement — the mechanism sounds genuinely compelling and desirable
  6-7: Moderate interest — the prospect is intrigued but not emotionally gripped
  4-5: Mild curiosity — the prospect might continue but isn't invested
  2-3: Flat — the lead reads like information, not emotional ignition
  1: Dead — the prospect has no emotional response and would click away

KEY SIGNALS OF EXCITEMENT:
  - The mechanism sounds like something the prospect has NEVER heard of
  - The promise attached to the mechanism feels personally relevant
  - The language creates sensory or emotional imagery
  - The pacing builds energy (not monotone information delivery)
  - There is at least one "wow" moment in the lead

RECORD: emotional_excitement: integer[1-10]
RECORD: excitement_evidence: string (quote the specific text that creates or fails to create excitement)
```

### Step 3: Score Dimension 2 — Desire Activation

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect WANT what the mechanism promises?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect CRAVES the outcome — it feels like exactly what they've been searching for
  8-9: Strong desire — the promise resonates deeply with their current pain/aspiration
  6-7: Moderate desire — they'd like it but don't feel urgency
  4-5: Abstract interest — sounds nice but doesn't feel personal
  2-3: Disconnected — the promise doesn't land emotionally
  1: Irrelevant — the prospect doesn't see themselves in this picture

KEY SIGNALS OF DESIRE:
  - The promise connects to a FELT need, not just an intellectual one
  - The lead paints the after-state vividly enough to be wanted
  - The four E5 elements combine to make the mechanism feel desirable
  - Simple + Easy + Quick + Reliable = "I want this NOW"

RECORD: desire_activation: integer[1-10]
RECORD: desire_evidence: string
```

### Step 4: Score Dimension 3 — Safety Signal

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect feel this is reliable/safe (not dangerous/risky)?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: Complete safety — prospect would tell a friend about this with confidence
  8-9: Strong safety — credibility insertions and reliability language create trust
  6-7: Adequate safety — no red flags but no strong reassurance either
  4-5: Uncertain — prospect has some concerns about legitimacy
  2-3: Suspicious — the lead triggers "too good to be true" without addressing it
  1: Alarm bells — sounds like a scam or dangerous advice

KEY SIGNALS OF SAFETY:
  - Brief credibility insertion that establishes authority without lecturing
  - Predictable/reliable element communicates consistency
  - Tone is confident but not hyperbolic
  - Any skepticism-addressing language present (per Georgi DO list)
  - The mechanism sounds grounded, not magical

RECORD: safety_signal: integer[1-10]
RECORD: safety_evidence: string
```

### Step 5: Score Dimension 4 — Proof Hunger

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect want to KEEP READING to see if it really works?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect is desperate to see the proof — they NEED this to be real
  8-9: Strong pull forward — they are actively seeking validation
  6-7: Curious enough to continue — open loops and teases are working
  4-5: Might continue if nothing else to do — no strong pull
  2-3: Could stop here — the lead hasn't created enough investment
  1: Would stop — no reason to continue reading

KEY SIGNALS OF PROOF HUNGER:
  - Open loops are functioning — the prospect has unanswered questions they CARE about
  - The emotional sale has been made but the logical sale has NOT — creating the gap
  - Forward signaling ("what I'm about to share," "pay close attention") creates anticipation
  - The attention lock at the end creates urgency to continue
  - The prospect thinks: "If this is real, it changes everything for me"

RECORD: proof_hunger: integer[1-10]
RECORD: proof_hunger_evidence: string
```

### Step 6: Score Dimension 5 — Self-Relevance

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect think "this could work for ME"?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect feels this was written specifically for their situation
  8-9: Strong personal relevance — they see themselves in the problem and the promise
  6-7: General relevance — applies to "people like them" but not specifically THEM
  4-5: Abstract relevance — intellectually applicable but not emotionally personal
  2-3: Distant — sounds like it's for someone else
  1: Irrelevant — the prospect does not identify with anything in the lead

KEY SIGNALS OF SELF-RELEVANCE:
  - Problem callout triggers self-identification (Clemens principle working)
  - Promise speaks to their specific desires, not generic outcomes
  - Mechanism sounds accessible to THEM (Simple/Easy element working)
  - Speed claim feels relevant to their timeline and patience
  - No barriers to entry visible (age, skill, equipment, etc.)

RECORD: self_relevance: integer[1-10]
RECORD: self_relevance_evidence: string
```

### Step 7: Evaluate the No-Premature-Proof Criterion

```
CRITICAL CHECK: Has the lead tried to PROVE anything?

SCAN the full_draft_lead for:
  - Statistics, studies, or research citations used as PROOF (not as hooks)
  - Detailed testimonials or case studies
  - Scientific explanations of WHY the mechanism works
  - Before/after comparisons with specific data
  - Expert endorsements deployed as evidence (vs. brief credibility insertion)
  - Any language that says "here's the proof..." or "studies show..."

DISTINGUISH between:
  ACCEPTABLE: Brief credibility insertion ("developed by a Harvard-trained physician")
  VIOLATION: Proof deployment ("in a study of 1,247 patients, 94% saw results within...")

  ACCEPTABLE: Social proof hint ("thousands have already discovered...")
  VIOLATION: Detailed testimonial ("John from Texas lost 47 pounds in 6 weeks...")

  ACCEPTABLE: Authority mention ("leading researchers have identified...")
  VIOLATION: Research walkthrough ("a 2019 study published in The Lancet found...")

The lead makes the EMOTIONAL sale.
The campaign argument makes the LOGICAL/PROOF sale.
These must NOT be confused.

RECORD: no_premature_proof: boolean (true = clean, false = proof leaked into lead)
RECORD: proof_violations: array of { location: string, text: string, type: string }
```

### Step 8: The "This Sounds Amazing" Test

```
THE DEFINING TEST:

Read the full lead one final time.

At the end, ask THE question:
  "Does a skeptical prospect — someone who has been burned before,
   who doesn't trust easily, who is ready to click away —
   arrive at this emotional state:

   'This sounds amazing. This sounds perfect.
    Now prove to me that it works and will work for me.'?"

This is a YES or NO answer.

IF YES:
  - The emotional sale is achieved
  - The lead has done its job
  - The campaign argument can now do ITS job (proving it works)

IF NO:
  - Identify WHERE the emotional arc breaks down
  - Identify WHAT is missing (which dimension failed)
  - Identify WHETHER the lead tried to do the argument's job (proof leakage)
  - The lead must be remediated before assembly

RECORD: this_sounds_amazing_test: enum[PASS, FAIL]
RECORD: failure_point: string (if FAIL — where the arc breaks)
```

### Step 9: Calculate Overall Achievement Score

```
overall_achievement = WEIGHTED AVERAGE:
  emotional_excitement × 0.25
  desire_activation × 0.25
  safety_signal × 0.15
  proof_hunger × 0.20
  self_relevance × 0.15

GATE DECISION:
  IF no_premature_proof = false → FAIL (regardless of scores)
  IF this_sounds_amazing_test = FAIL → FAIL (regardless of scores)
  IF overall_achievement >= 7.0 AND no_premature_proof = true AND this_sounds_amazing = PASS → PASS
  IF overall_achievement >= 5.0 AND < 7.0 AND no violations → PASS_WITH_WARNINGS
  IF overall_achievement < 5.0 → FAIL

NOTE: Premature proof is an automatic FAIL because it means
the lead is doing the campaign argument's job. This is a structural
error, not a quality issue.
```

### Step 10: Generate Remediation Notes (If Not PASS)

```
IF gate_result ≠ PASS:

  FOR each dimension scoring below 7.0:
    GENERATE specific remediation:
      - Which emotional dimension is weak
      - What the prospect SHOULD be feeling at that point
      - What element or technique is missing or underperforming
      - Which upstream microskill should re-execute

  IF no_premature_proof = false:
    LIST each proof violation with:
      - Location in lead
      - The text that constitutes proof
      - What it should be replaced with (brief tease or credibility hint)
      - Reminder: "Proof belongs in campaign argument, not the lead"

  IF this_sounds_amazing_test = FAIL:
    DIAGNOSE the failure:
      - Is the lead too informational? (solving with logic, not emotion)
      - Is the lead too vague? (no mechanism to get excited about)
      - Is the lead too aggressive? (pushing too hard, triggering skepticism)
      - Is the lead too timid? (not making bold enough promises)
      - Is the lead too long? (losing momentum before the emotional peak)
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Intellectual sale disguised as emotional sale:** The prospect "understands" the mechanism is good but doesn't FEEL excited about it — information delivery is not emotional selling
- **Proof leakage rationalized as credibility:** Inserting a full study citation and calling it a "credibility element" — if it attempts to PROVE the mechanism works, it is proof, not credibility
- **Generous self-relevance scoring:** Rating self-relevance high because the niche matches — the lead must make the prospect feel THIS specific mechanism is for THEM, not just that the topic is relevant
- **Confusing curiosity with desire:** The prospect being mildly curious is NOT the emotional sale — the emotional sale requires DESIRE ("I want this") combined with PROOF HUNGER ("now show me it works")
- **Skipping the gestalt read:** Scoring dimensions analytically without first reading the full lead as a prospect would experience it — the first-impression read is essential, not optional
- **Emotional inflation:** Scoring high because the language is intense ("AMAZING! INCREDIBLE!") rather than because the emotional arc actually builds genuine feeling — intensity is not the same as effectiveness
