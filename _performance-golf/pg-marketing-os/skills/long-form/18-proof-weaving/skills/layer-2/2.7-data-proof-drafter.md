# Microskill 2.7: Data Proof Drafter

**Layer:** 2 (Drafting)
**Purpose:** Write weaponized statistics that prove through data (Stansberry style)

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Data proof works differently than testimonial proof. Instead of emotional stories, data proof uses NUMBERS AS WEAPONS — statistics that are undeniable, often from authoritative sources, arranged in a sequence that leads to an inevitable conclusion. The reader's own logic forces them to accept the claim.

---

## DATA PROOF TYPES

### Type 1: The Rhetorical Cascade

**Data arranged as rhetorical questions:**

```
"How can anyone call this a recovery?

When 75% of Americans are living paycheck to paycheck?
When 49% depend on government benefits?
When workforce participation is the lowest since 1970?
When median household income has been stagnant for 20 years?

These aren't fringe statistics. This is the government's own data."
```

**Structure:**
- Rhetorical setup question
- Data point 1 (shocking)
- Data point 2 (compounding)
- Data point 3 (pattern establishing)
- Data point 4+ (momentum building)
- Source validation

---

### Type 2: The Government's Own Data Weapon

**Turn authority sources against official narrative:**

```
"Don't take my word for it. Look at what the government admits:

Bureau of Labor Statistics: Lowest workforce participation since 1970.
Federal Reserve: Money supply expanded 400% in just 6 years.
Census Bureau: 49% of Americans now receive government benefits.
Treasury Department: National debt exceeded $31 trillion.

This isn't conspiracy theory. This is official government data."
```

**Structure:**
- Credibility setup ("their own data")
- Named source + specific stat (repeat 4-6x)
- Authority validation close

---

### Type 3: The Single Devastating Stat

**One number that changes everything:**

```
"One statistic tells you everything you need to know:

400%.

That's how much the money supply has expanded in just 6 years.

In the 200 years before that? The money supply grew gradually, predictably, sustainably.

Then, in 6 short years, they expanded it 400%.

That's not policy. That's insanity. And you're about to pay the price."
```

**Structure:**
- Setup
- The number (isolated, emphasized)
- Context (historical baseline)
- Contrast (new reality)
- Implication (consequence)

---

### Type 4: The Comparison Killer

**Data that eliminates alternatives:**

```
"Here's what the data tells us about conventional approaches:

Average weight loss on calorie restriction: 8.4 lbs (most regained within a year)
Average weight loss on popular diet programs: 11.2 lbs (similar regain rates)
Average weight loss on prescription medications: 15.7 lbs (with significant side effects)

Now look at this approach:

Average weight loss: 27.3 lbs
Regain rate: Less than 15% (vs. 80%+ for other methods)
Side effects: None documented.

The numbers don't lie. This works better."
```

**Structure:**
- Alternative 1 data (weak)
- Alternative 2 data (weak)
- Alternative 3 data (weak with downside)
- Your approach data (strong)
- Contrast emphasis
- Conclusion

---

### Type 5: The Visual Data (Chart/Graph Support)

**Data designed to be visualized:**

```
"Let me paint you a picture.

For 200 years — from 1810 to 2008 — the monetary base grew from essentially zero to about $800 billion. Slow, steady, sustainable.

Then, in just 6 years — from 2008 to 2014 — it exploded to over $3.5 trillion.

If you saw this on a chart, it would look like a hockey stick. 200 years of flat growth, then a vertical line straight up.

That's not normal. That's not sustainable. That's a crisis waiting to happen."
```

**Structure:**
- Visual setup ("let me paint a picture")
- Historical baseline (long period, small growth)
- Contrast period (short period, massive growth)
- Visual metaphor ("hockey stick")
- Implication

## DATA PROOF RULES

### Rule 1: Source Your Data

**WEAK:** "Statistics show..."
**STRONG:** "According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics..."

**Hierarchy of data credibility:**
1. Government sources (BLS, Fed, Census)
2. Academic institutions
3. Major research organizations
4. Industry associations
5. Internal/proprietary data (lowest)

### Rule 2: Use Specific Numbers

**WEAK:** "A significant increase"
**STRONG:** "A 400% increase"

**WEAK:** "Most Americans"
**STRONG:** "75% of Americans"

### Rule 3: Provide Context

**Raw number:** "$31 trillion national debt"
**With context:** "$31 trillion national debt — that's $94,000 for every man, woman, and child in America"

### Rule 4: Build Momentum

**Single stat:** Has impact but can be dismissed
**Data cascade:** Creates inevitability, overwhelms objection capacity

### Rule 5: Connect to Consequence

**Data alone:** "400% money supply expansion"
**With consequence:** "400% money supply expansion — which historically has ALWAYS led to currency collapse"

## DATA INTENSIFICATION TECHNIQUES

### Technique 1: The "Can't Be Dismissed" Frame

```
"You can argue with opinions.
You can debate interpretations.
But you can't argue with numbers.

And the numbers are devastating."
```

### Technique 2: The "Hidden in Plain Sight" Frame

```
"This data isn't hidden. It's not classified. It's not secret.

It's right there on the Federal Reserve website. The Treasury Department publishes it.

They're counting on the fact that you won't look.

But now you're looking."
```

### Technique 3: The "If-Then" Logic Chain

```
"If the money supply has expanded 400%...
And if history shows that massive money printing leads to inflation...
And if inflation has already hit multi-decade highs...

Then the conclusion is inescapable: this is just the beginning."
```

### Technique 4: The "Ask Yourself" Reflection

```
"Ask yourself:

If the economy is really recovering, why is workforce participation at a 45-year low?

If inflation is under control, why are grocery prices up 30% in two years?

If debt doesn't matter, why does servicing that debt now cost more than the entire defense budget?

The data tells a different story than the headlines."
```
