# Microskill 1.5: Sequencing Strategy Selector

**Layer:** 1 (Architecture)
**Purpose:** Select the optimal proof sequencing strategy for the campaign

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

How proof is SEQUENCED matters as much as what proof is used. Different strategies create different persuasion architectures. This microskill selects the optimal strategy based on inventory, market sophistication, and copy format.

---

## AVAILABLE SEQUENCING STRATEGIES

### Strategy 1: Proof-First (Whitaker Style)

**Architecture:**
```
PROVE → Scale → Explain → CTA
```

**Flow:**
1. **Lead:** Dramatic transformation proof (Ken on TV)
2. **Scale:** "Not just one person — thousands"
3. **Authority:** Who made this possible
4. **Mechanism:** Why it works
5. **More Proof:** Proof cascade
6. **Close:** Scale callback

**When to Use:**
- [ ] Have dramatic, TV-worthy proof element
- [ ] Transformation is visually/emotionally striking
- [ ] Proof is strong enough to lead with
- [ ] Market sophistication Stage 2-3

**When NOT to Use:**
- Authority needs establishing first
- Proof is moderate (not dramatic)
- Market is Stage 4-5 (skeptical of proof-first)

---

### Strategy 2: Authority-to-Social (Sinatra Style)

**Architecture:**
```
Authority → Framework → Clinical → Social → Risk Reversal
```

**Flow:**
1. **Authority:** Expert credentials establish right to speak
2. **Framework:** Expert's system/approach introduced
3. **Clinical:** Scientific/mechanism validation
4. **Social:** Scale proof, customer validation
5. **Risk Reversal:** Guarantee as final proof

**When to Use:**
- [ ] Have strong expert/authority figure
- [ ] Market is Stage 3-4 (needs credibility first)
- [ ] Product requires expertise positioning
- [ ] Mechanism is sophisticated

**When NOT to Use:**
- No strong authority figure available
- Market is Stage 1-2 (wants results, not credentials)
- Proof is more compelling than authority

---

### Strategy 3: Scale Cascade (Progressive)

**Architecture:**
```
Individual → Pattern → Scale → Universal
```

**Flow:**
1. **Individual:** Single compelling story
2. **Pattern:** "She's not alone — hundreds have..."
3. **Scale:** "In fact, over 40,000 patients..."
4. **Universal:** "This works for virtually anyone..."

**When to Use:**
- [ ] Have individual testimonial + large customer base
- [ ] Can show pattern across demographic variety
- [ ] Scale numbers are impressive
- [ ] Market needs fluke-removal

**When NOT to Use:**
- No scale data available
- Individual proof is weak
- Market doesn't care about others (B2B)

---

### Strategy 4: Testimonial Parade (Wave Pattern)

**Architecture:**
```
Wave 1 → Mechanism → Wave 2 → Offer → Wave 3
```

**Flow:**
1. **Wave 1:** 2-3 testimonials in lead
2. **Mechanism:** Explanation (with embedded proof)
3. **Wave 2:** 3-4 testimonials in proof section
4. **Offer:** Value proof (worth it testimonial)
5. **Wave 3:** 2 testimonials in close + callbacks

**When to Use:**
- [ ] Testimonial inventory is 25%+ of proof
- [ ] Testimonials are high quality with variety
- [ ] Market is Stage 2-3 (testimonial-responsive)
- [ ] Product has emotional benefits

**When NOT to Use:**
- Limited testimonials available
- Market is Stage 4-5 (testimonial-skeptical)
- Product benefits are technical/analytical

---

### Strategy 5: Data Cascade (Stansberry Style)

**Architecture:**
```
Question → Data → Rhetorical Cascade → Visual → Conclusion
```

**Flow:**
1. **Question:** Surface concern with data
2. **Data Point 1:** Shocking statistic
3. **Rhetorical:** "How can it be normal when..."
4. **Data Cascade:** Stack 5-9 data points
5. **Visual:** Chart/graph makes tangible
6. **Conclusion:** "The patterns are clear..."

**When to Use:**
- [ ] Data inventory is strong (5+ data points)
- [ ] Market is Stage 4-5 (analytical)
- [ ] Product is financial/analytical
- [ ] Credibility comes from data, not testimonials

**When NOT to Use:**
- Testimonials are stronger than data
- Market is Stage 1-2 (wants stories, not numbers)
- Product benefits are emotional

---

### Strategy 6: Balanced (Default)

**Architecture:**
```
Authority → Proof → Mechanism → Proof → Close
```

**Flow:**
1. **Authority:** Credibility establishment
2. **Early Proof:** 1-2 elements for credibility
3. **Mechanism:** Explanation
4. **Heavy Proof:** Cascade in proof section
5. **Close:** Callbacks and scale

**When to Use:**
- Mixed inventory (no single type dominates)
- Market is Stage 3 (moderate sophistication)
- Standard format (VSL, sales letter)
- No extreme proof strengths

**When NOT to Use:**
- Have clear proof advantage to exploit
- Market demands specific approach
- Format requires specific architecture

## STRATEGY SELECTION ALGORITHM

```
EVALUATE inventory:
  IF transformation_proof_score > 8.5 AND has_dramatic_visual:
    → Consider PROOF_FIRST

  IF authority_credentials_score > 8.0 AND market_stage >= 3:
    → Consider AUTHORITY_TO_SOCIAL

  IF scale_proof_available AND scale_number > 10000:
    → Consider SCALE_CASCADE

  IF testimonial_count >= 8 AND testimonial_quality_avg > 7.0:
    → Consider TESTIMONIAL_PARADE

  IF data_point_count >= 5 AND market_stage >= 4:
    → Consider DATA_CASCADE

  ELSE:
    → Use BALANCED

CROSS-CHECK with:
  - Human preference (from 0.5)
  - Copy format requirements
  - Brand guidelines
```
