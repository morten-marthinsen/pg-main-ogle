**Skill:** 17-close

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 0 — Foundation & Loading
**Position:** Fourth microskill in Layer 0
**Persona:** Alex Rivera (Primary), Dr. Liu (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

Presents the close approach to the human operator for confirmation before any writing begins. The close is the FINAL persuasive section of the promotion — once written, it completes the entire copy pipeline. This checkpoint ensures the human has visibility and control over three strategic decisions: (1) which primary closing theme will drive the close section, (2) which P.S. strategy will use the second-most-read real estate, and (3) what close-specific details are needed (phone numbers, URLs, security badges, additional bonuses).

This microskill compiles the recommended close approach based on niche, campaign format, emotional state at handoff, and teaching index analysis. It presents a structured recommendation with rationale, then offers three response options: CONFIRM (accept the recommendation), MODIFY (adjust themes or strategy), or PROVIDE (supply specific close requirements or fill data gaps flagged by the validator). The human checkpoint is BLOCKING — Layer 1 cannot begin until the human responds.

This is the last checkpoint before close writing begins. After this, the system executes through Layer 1-4 autonomously (with gate remediation as needed). The human's response here shapes the entire close section.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The human-facing close approach presentation system
- A strategic recommendation engine — selects primary theme, P.S. strategy, CTA target count
- A rationale provider — explains WHY the recommended approach fits this promotion
- A data gap resolver — surfaces validator issues for human action
- A close-specific detail collector — phone numbers, URLs, security badges, additional bonuses
- A BLOCKING checkpoint — Layer 1 waits for human response
- The last human touchpoint before autonomous close execution

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A close writer — this presents the approach, not the prose
- A data validator (that is 0.3 — this presents validation results to the human)
- An autonomous decision-maker — it recommends but the human decides
- A teaching loader (that is 0.2)
- A theme executor (that is Layer 1 and Layer 2)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Determine Recommended Primary Closing Theme

1. Analyze the following factors to select the recommended primary closing theme:

   **Factor A: Niche and Product Type**
   - Health/Supplement → Crossroads (health decisions are visceral fork-in-road moments) or Don't Go Alone (expert guide through health crisis)
   - Finance/Investment → Logical Restatement (financial audiences respect logic chains) or Reasons Why (numbered, systematic appeal)
   - Golf/Sports → USP Close (highly competitive market — differentiation critical) or But WAIT There's More (digital products benefit from bonus stacking)
   - Survival/Preparedness → Don't Go Alone (dangerous world, need expert) or Crossroads (preparedness is a life-or-death fork)
   - General/Info → Simple Restatement (when offer is strong) or Reasons Why (condensed selling points)

   **Factor B: Campaign Format**
   - VSL → Crossroads, Don't Go Alone, or USP (these themes work with spoken narrative)
   - Magalog → Logical Restatement, Reasons Why, or Simple Restatement (these work in print)
   - Email → Simple Restatement or Reasons Why (shorter format needs efficiency)
   - Advertorial → Logical Restatement or USP (editorial formats favor logic and differentiation)

   **Factor C: Emotional State at Handoff**
   - High desire, seeking reassurance → Crossroads or Don't Go Alone (resolve final hesitation)
   - Intellectually convinced → Logical Restatement (cap the argument chain)
   - Excited by value → But WAIT There's More (pile on bonuses at peak excitement)
   - Skeptical but interested → Reasons Why or USP (give concrete reasons to trust)

   **Factor D: Audience Sophistication**
   - Low sophistication → Simple Restatement, Crossroads (direct, emotional)
   - Medium sophistication → Reasons Why, Don't Go Alone (balanced logic and emotion)
   - High sophistication → Logical Restatement, USP (respect intelligence, differentiate)

2. Select primary theme based on highest alignment across all four factors
3. Write rationale explaining the selection (2-3 sentences)

### Step 2: Select Secondary Themes

4. Choose 0-2 secondary themes that complement the primary:
   - **Crossroads + Logical Restatement** — emotion and logic (powerful combination)
   - **USP Close + But WAIT There's More** — exclusivity and bonus stacking
   - **Don't Go Alone + Crossroads** — expert guide at life's fork
   - **Reasons Why + Simple Restatement** — numbered reasons then clean summary
   - **Logical Restatement + USP** — facts leading to exclusive conclusion

5. Secondary themes are MIXED into the primary — they enhance, not replace
6. Write brief rationale for each secondary theme selection (1 sentence each)

### Step 3: Select P.S. Strategy

7. Analyze available assets to determine P.S. strategy:

   **Available Makepeace P.S. Techniques:**
   - **(a) Fast-reply bonus** — Requires: a bonus that can be tied to urgency ("Order within 24 hours and also receive...")
   - **(b) Call-in bonus** — Requires: phone order capability AND a phone-exclusive bonus
   - **(c) Additional FREE bonus revealed** — Requires: at least 1 unrevealed bonus in inventory
   - **(d) Most powerful testimonials** — Requires: at least 2 strong testimonials in proof inventory
   - **(e) Additional reasons to act NOW** — Requires: urgency justification (supply, demand, price, time)
   - **(f) Restate guarantee** — Always available; best when guarantee is particularly bold

8. Match technique availability to actual assets:
   - If strong testimonials available → technique (d) recommended
   - If unrevealed bonuses available → technique (c) recommended
   - If branded guarantee is bold → technique (f) recommended
   - If urgency is credible → techniques (a) or (e) recommended
   - If phone orders supported → technique (b) available

9. Select 1-3 P.S. entries:
   - Single P.S. = concentrated power (best ammo in one shot)
   - P.S. + P.P.S. = two-technique combination
   - P.S. + P.P.S. + P.P.P.S. = full cascade (for long-form formats only)

10. Write rationale for P.S. strategy selection

### Step 4: Set CTA Target Count and Style

11. Determine CTA target count based on format:
    - VSL: 8-10+ CTAs (longer format, more ask opportunities)
    - Magalog: 6-8 CTAs (including sidebar CTAs and order form references)
    - Email: 6-7 CTAs (tighter format, every CTA counts)
    - Advertorial: 6-8 CTAs (editorial pacing with clear action points)

12. Determine CTA style guidance:
    - Health: Mix urgency + compassion ("Please, if your health means anything to you...")
    - Finance: Mix logic + greed ("Doesn't it make sense to..." / "Start collecting profits...")
    - Golf: Mix excitement + social proof ("Join thousands of golfers who..." / "Start shaving strokes...")
    - General: Mix across all emotion types for variety

13. Estimate close word count based on format benchmarks:
    - VSL: 1000-1800 words
    - Magalog: 600-1500 words
    - Email: 200-500 words
    - Advertorial: 400-800 words

### Step 5: Identify Close-Specific Details Needed

14. Compile list of close-specific details the human may need to provide:
    - **Phone Number:** If campaign supports phone orders (enables Makepeace P.S. technique: call-in bonus)
    - **Order URL:** The specific URL or button destination for online orders
    - **Security Badges:** Which security verifications to reference (Norton, McAfee, BBB, SSL)
    - **Additional Bonuses for P.S.:** Any unrevealed bonuses available for P.S. surprise reveal
    - **Checkout Page Details:** What happens after the click (order form, shopping cart, scheduler)
    - **Shipping Details:** If physical product, any free shipping or expedited options
    - **Customer Support Contact:** For guarantee confidence ("Call us anytime at...")

15. Flag which details are ESSENTIAL (needed for close) vs. OPTIONAL (would enhance close)

### Step 6: Surface Validator Issues

16. Review validation report from 0.3
17. For BLOCKED readiness:
    - Present critical issues prominently — close cannot proceed without resolution
    - Clearly state what the human must provide
18. For CONDITIONAL readiness:
    - Present advisory issues with recommended resolutions
    - Ask human if they can fill advisory gaps
19. For GO readiness:
    - Note clean validation — no data gaps to resolve
    - Focus presentation on strategic choices (theme, P.S., CTA count)

### Step 7: Compose and Present Checkpoint

20. Assemble the checkpoint presentation in structured format:

### Step 8: Process Human Response

21. **If CONFIRM:**
    - Lock in recommended theme, P.S. strategy, and CTA plan
    - Proceed to Layer 1 with confirmed approach
    - Log: human confirmed recommended approach

22. **If MODIFY:**
    - Accept human's theme/strategy modifications
    - Validate modifications against teaching index (ensure modified theme exists)
    - Update approach accordingly
    - Proceed to Layer 1 with modified approach
    - Log: human modified approach with specific changes

23. **If PROVIDE:**
    - Accept human-provided data (phone numbers, URLs, bonuses, etc.)
    - Re-run validator (0.3) with new data to update readiness
    - If readiness improves to GO: present updated approach for CONFIRM/MODIFY
    - If readiness still BLOCKED: request additional data
    - Log: human provided data, re-validation result

24. Store checkpoint response for downstream reference:
    - Theme selection (primary + secondary)
    - P.S. strategy
    - CTA target count
    - Close-specific details provided
    - Any human direction or preferences stated

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Theme Recommendation Misaligned with Format
- **Symptom:** Recommending "But WAIT There's More" for a 300-word email close
- **Cause:** Format constraints not weighted heavily enough in theme selection
- **Prevention:** Step 1 Factor B (Campaign Format) is a hard constraint, not a suggestion
- **Recovery:** Human selects MODIFY and chooses a format-appropriate theme

### Failure Mode 2: P.S. Strategy Requires Unavailable Assets
- **Symptom:** Recommending "most powerful testimonials" P.S. when no testimonials in proof inventory
- **Cause:** Asset availability not checked before P.S. technique selection
- **Prevention:** Step 3 explicitly matches technique requirements to actual assets
- **Recovery:** Human provides testimonials via PROVIDE, or MODIFY selects a different P.S. technique

### Failure Mode 3: Human Provides Invalid Modification
- **Symptom:** Human requests a closing theme that does not exist (e.g., "Scarcity Close")
- **Cause:** Human unfamiliar with the 7 Makepeace theme names
- **Prevention:** Checkpoint presentation lists all available themes
- **Recovery:** Map human's intent to nearest Makepeace theme (Scarcity → Reasons Why with urgency focus); confirm with human

### Failure Mode 4: BLOCKED Readiness Not Resolved
- **Symptom:** Human confirms approach but critical data is still missing
- **Cause:** Human selected CONFIRM instead of PROVIDE, ignoring BLOCKED status
- **Prevention:** If readiness is BLOCKED, CONFIRM is not available — only PROVIDE or MODIFY
- **Recovery:** Re-present checkpoint with BLOCKED status emphasized; require PROVIDE before CONFIRM

### Failure Mode 5: Checkpoint Timeout Without Response
- **Symptom:** Human has not responded to checkpoint
- **Cause:** Human is not actively monitoring the pipeline
- **Prevention:** No timeout — checkpoint waits indefinitely (per agent protocol)
- **Recovery:** System remains in HUMAN_CHECKPOINT state until response received; no autonomous bypass
