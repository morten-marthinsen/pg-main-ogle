# Microskill 1.1 — Closing Theme Selector

**Skill:** 17-close
**Microskill:** 1.1-closing-theme-selector
**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 — Strategic Classification
**Position:** First microskill in Layer 1
**Persona:** Marcus Webster (Primary), Legendary Copywriter (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

Selects the primary closing theme from Clayton Makepeace's 7 closing themes — the strategic backbone that determines the entire close section's approach. The closing theme is NOT a template; it is a persuasion architecture that shapes how the close builds its final argument. A Crossroads close paints two futures and forces a binary choice. A Logical Restatement caps an argument chain with an irrefutable conclusion. A "But WAIT There's More" close overwhelms resistance with escalating value.

The theme selection draws from four strategic factors: niche and product type, campaign format, emotional state at handoff from Skill 16, and audience sophistication level. The human checkpoint (0.4) has already confirmed or modified the recommended theme — this microskill executes the confirmed selection with full strategic detail, adding secondary theme mixing instructions and theme-specific constraints that downstream writers (Layer 2) must follow.

This is the first creative decision in the close pipeline. Everything that follows — benefit summary ordering, CTA emotional mapping, P.S. content, crossroads writing, Cialdini integration — flows from the theme selected here.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The closing theme selection engine from 7 Makepeace themes
- A multi-factor strategic analyzer (niche, format, emotion, sophistication)
- A secondary theme mixer — selecting 0-2 complementary themes for blending
- A theme-specific constraint generator — what each theme demands from downstream writers
- The creative direction setter for the entire close section
- A theme combination optimizer — ensuring primary + secondary themes amplify rather than conflict

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A theme writer (that is 2.3 — this selects, 2.3 writes)
- A benefit summary designer (that is 1.2)
- A CTA planner (that is 1.3)
- A human checkpoint (that is 0.4 — theme is already confirmed here)
- A close assembler (that is 4.4)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Confirm Theme Selection

1. Read the human-confirmed primary closing theme from checkpoint response
2. If human modified the recommendation, accept the human's choice as final
3. If human confirmed the recommendation, proceed with the recommended theme
4. Validate that the selected theme is one of the 7 Makepeace themes:
   - (1) Crossroads
   - (2) Logical Restatement
   - (3) Reasons Why
   - (4) Don't Go It Alone
   - (5) Simple Restatement
   - (6) "But WAIT... There's More"
   - (7) USP Close

### Step 2: Generate Primary Theme Execution Profile

5. For the selected primary theme, generate a full execution profile:

**IF CROSSROADS (Theme 1):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Three options presented to prospect
    option_a: "Do nothing — let [problem] continue unchecked"
    option_b: "Keep doing what you've been doing — same results"
    option_c: "Take action now — experience [promise]"
  emotional_register: visceral, sincere, empathetic
  key_requirement: Dimensionalize the RISK of options A and B
  week_13_integration:
    - Crossroads two-paths ("fork in the road" metaphor)
    - Dream vs. nightmare feelings
    - Decision ownership ("I can't make this decision for you")
  word_count_allocation: 200-400 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must write all three options with vivid consequences
    - 3.2 must write future pacing connected to crossroads
    - 3.4 Cialdini must leverage "since you agree..." before crossroads
  sincerity_requirement: MUST feel genuinely helpful, not manipulative
```

**IF LOGICAL RESTATEMENT (Theme 2):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Chain of agreed-upon facts leading to conclusion
    fact_chain: "Here are the facts: 1)... 2)... 3)..."
    conclusion: "Given all this, doesn't it make sense to..."
  emotional_register: rational, confident, irrefutable
  key_requirement: Each fact must be something prospect already agreed to
  week_13_integration:
    - Decision ownership (respect the logical conclusion)
  word_count_allocation: 150-300 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must reconstruct argument chain from structure package
    - 3.4 Cialdini integration is CENTRAL to this theme
    - CTA 1.3 should include logic-based asks
  sincerity_requirement: Facts must be genuinely established earlier in copy
```

**IF REASONS WHY (Theme 3):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Numbered reasons to act now
    count: 5-7 reasons (human may specify)
    reasons_include: savings, bonuses, proof, scarcity, exclusivity, guarantee
  emotional_register: systematic, urgent, compelling
  key_requirement: Each reason must be distinct and impactful
  week_13_integration:
    - Emphasize demand (reason: high demand)
    - Branded guarantee (reason: bold guarantee)
  word_count_allocation: 200-400 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must write numbered reasons with urgency
    - Each reason should connect to a different emotional appeal
    - CTAs can follow individual reasons
```

**IF DON'T GO IT ALONE (Theme 4):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Expert guide positioning
    positioning: "This is NO TIME to go it alone"
    guide_credentials: [from product introduction]
    danger_without_guide: [specific dangers in this niche]
  emotional_register: authoritative, protective, urgent
  key_requirement: World must feel genuinely dangerous without expert help
  week_13_integration:
    - Decision ownership (with expert encouragement)
  word_count_allocation: 150-300 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must establish danger and position spokesperson as essential
    - Authority proof needed from proof inventory
    - CTAs should frame action as "joining" the expert
```

**IF SIMPLE RESTATEMENT (Theme 5):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Clean restatement of offer components
    elements: premium benefits, deliverables, guarantee, price
    format: bullet points transitioning to close ask
  emotional_register: clear, direct, confident
  key_requirement: Offer must be strong enough to close itself
  week_13_integration:
    - Checkout process clarity
  word_count_allocation: 100-250 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 writes a clean summary that transitions to CTA
    - 2.1 benefit summary carries extra weight with this theme
    - Works best when combined with strong secondary theme
```

**IF "BUT WAIT... THERE'S MORE" (Theme 6):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Escalating bonus revelations
    base_value: [value stack from offer]
    additions: each bonus revealed after previous settles
    goal: overwhelm resistance with positive greed
  emotional_register: excited, generous, escalating
  key_requirement: Multiple bonuses available for stacking
  week_13_integration:
    - Congratulate on space left (after bonus reveal)
    - Emphasize demand (increase urgency after value peaks)
  word_count_allocation: 200-500 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must pace bonus reveals with building excitement
    - Each bonus needs fascination-level description
    - CTAs punctuate each bonus addition
    - P.S. can reveal final "secret" bonus
```

**IF USP CLOSE (Theme 7):**
```
execution_profile:
  structure: Unique selling proposition as closing argument
    usp_statement: "I'm the ONLY [expert] with [exclusive capability]"
    exclusivity: "You can ONLY get this through [product name]"
    scarcity_tie: exclusivity creates natural urgency
  emotional_register: exclusive, authoritative, differentiated
  key_requirement: USP must be genuinely unique and provable
  week_13_integration:
    - Emphasize demand (exclusive access + high demand = urgency)
  word_count_allocation: 150-300 words for theme section
  downstream_demands:
    - 2.3 must establish genuine uniqueness
    - Positioning statement from Skill 15 is central
    - CTAs should reference exclusivity
```

### Step 3: Configure Secondary Theme Mixing

6. For each confirmed secondary theme (0-2), generate mixing instructions:

   **Mixing Rules:**
   - Secondary themes are WOVEN INTO the primary, not appended as separate sections
   - The primary theme controls structure; secondary themes add emotional layers
   - A secondary theme should occupy 15-25% of the theme section
   - Two secondary themes should not exceed 40% combined

7. Define integration points — where does each secondary theme appear:
   - Before the primary theme pivot (setup)
   - During the primary theme body (enrichment)
   - After the primary theme conclusion (reinforcement)

8. Check for theme conflicts (these combinations should be avoided):
   - Crossroads + Simple Restatement (crossroads demands drama; simple restatement is low-drama)
   - But WAIT There's More + Logical Restatement (bonus stacking is emotional; logical restatement is rational)
   - If conflict detected: recommend human reconsider via feedback, but proceed if human confirmed

### Step 4: Generate Theme-Specific CTA Emotional Map

9. Based on the primary theme, recommend emotional distribution for CTAs (feeds into 1.3):

   - **Crossroads:** Fear (3), Hope (2), Urgency (2), Logic (1), Compassion (1)
   - **Logical Restatement:** Logic (3), Greed (2), Social Proof (1), Urgency (2), Exclusivity (1)
   - **Reasons Why:** Greed (2), Urgency (2), Logic (2), Fear (1), Social Proof (1), Exclusivity (1)
   - **Don't Go It Alone:** Fear (2), Authority (2), Urgency (2), Hope (2), Compassion (1)
   - **Simple Restatement:** Greed (2), Logic (2), Urgency (1), Fear (1), Hope (1)
   - **But WAIT There's More:** Greed (3), Excitement (2), Urgency (2), Fear (1), Social Proof (1)
   - **USP Close:** Exclusivity (3), Urgency (2), Greed (2), Fear (1), Logic (1)

10. Note: These are recommendations, not mandates. CTA planner (1.3) makes final assignments.

### Step 5: Set Close Section Word Count Targets

11. Based on primary theme + format, set word count targets for each close section:

| Section | VSL | Magalog | Email | Advertorial |
|---------|-----|---------|-------|-------------|
| Benefit Summary | 200-400 | 150-300 | 80-150 | 100-200 |
| Guarantee Close | 150-300 | 100-250 | 60-120 | 80-180 |
| Closing Theme | 200-500 | 150-400 | 80-200 | 100-300 |
| CTA Sequence | 300-600 | 200-400 | 100-200 | 150-300 |
| Urgency/Scarcity | 100-200 | 80-150 | 40-80 | 60-120 |
| Future Pacing | 150-300 | 100-200 | 60-120 | 80-150 |
| Checkout Process | 80-150 | 60-100 | 40-60 | 50-80 |
| P.S. Section | 150-400 | 100-300 | 60-150 | 80-200 |
| **Total** | **1000-1800** | **600-1500** | **200-500** | **400-800** |

12. Adjust targets based on theme — themes that are word-heavy (Crossroads, But WAIT) get more allocation

### Step 6: Package Theme Selection Output

13. Compile the full theme selection package:
    - Primary theme with execution profile
    - Secondary themes with mixing instructions
    - CTA emotional map recommendation
    - Word count targets by section
    - Theme-specific constraints for downstream
    - Sincerity requirements

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Theme Mismatch with Format
- **Symptom:** "But WAIT There's More" selected for a 300-word email close
- **Cause:** Human selected a theme that conflicts with format constraints
- **Prevention:** Step 5 word count targets reveal the mismatch; checkpoint should have caught this
- **Recovery:** Flag concern to human via feedback bus; if human insists, adapt theme to shorter format (fewer bonus reveals)

### Failure Mode 2: Secondary Themes Conflict
- **Symptom:** Crossroads + Simple Restatement — one demands drama, other demands simplicity
- **Cause:** Secondary theme selection did not check compatibility
- **Prevention:** Step 3 conflict check identifies incompatible pairs
- **Recovery:** Drop the conflicting secondary theme; notify human of the adjustment

### Failure Mode 3: Theme Demands Unavailable Assets
- **Symptom:** "Don't Go It Alone" selected but spokesperson has no established authority in copy
- **Cause:** Theme requires authority positioning that wasn't built in earlier skills
- **Prevention:** Execution profile checks for prerequisite content (authority established upstream)
- **Recovery:** Shift to "Don't Go It Alone LITE" — reference credentials from product introduction rather than requiring full authority narrative

### Failure Mode 4: Over-Constraining Downstream Writers
- **Symptom:** Theme constraints so rigid that writers cannot exercise creative judgment
- **Cause:** Execution profile treated as a script rather than a direction
- **Prevention:** Constraints specify REQUIREMENTS and EMOTIONAL REGISTER, not word-for-word copy
- **Recovery:** Loosen constraints to "must include" minimums rather than "must follow" scripts

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Crossroads for Health Supplement VSL

**Context:** Male vitality supplement, VSL, emotional state: high desire seeking final reassurance.

**Theme Selection Output:**
```yaml
theme_selection:
  primary_theme:
    theme_number: 1
    theme_name: "Crossroads"
    execution_profile:
      structure: "Three options — do nothing / keep status quo / take action"
      emotional_register: "visceral, sincere, empathetic"
      key_requirement: "Dimensionalize risk of inaction with health-specific consequences"
      week_13_integration:
        - "Fork in the road metaphor — risky road vs. EASY road"
        - "Dream vs. nightmare feelings — vitality vs. decline"
        - "Decision ownership — I can't decide for you"
    rationale: "Health decisions are inherently crossroads moments. The prospect is literally choosing between continued decline and renewed vitality. This theme maps naturally to the life-or-death stakes of the health niche."
    word_count_allocation: "300-400 words"
  secondary_themes:
    - theme_number: 4
      theme_name: "Don't Go It Alone"
      mixing_instructions:
        integration_point: "Before crossroads pivot — establish expert authority"
        proportion: "20% of theme section"
        technique: "Brief spokesperson positioning before presenting the three paths"
      rationale: "Reinforces that the prospect has a trusted guide for this health decision"
  cta_emotional_map:
    recommended_distribution:
      fear: 3
      hope: 2
      urgency: 2
      logic: 1
      compassion: 1
```

### Example 2: Reasons Why for Financial Newsletter Magalog

**Context:** Investment newsletter, magalog format, human modified to Reasons Why (7 reasons).

**Theme Selection Output:**
```yaml
theme_selection:

      week_13_integration:
        - "Branded guarantee naming — reason to trust"
```

**Context:** Golf instruction course, VSL, competitive market.

```yaml
      week_13_integration:
        - "Emphasize demand — exclusivity plus demand creates urgency"

    - theme_number: 6
      theme_name: "But WAIT... There's More"
      mixing_instructions:

      rationale: "Digital products benefit from bonus stacking; exclusivity + bonuses = overwhelming value"

      urgency: 2
      greed: 2
      fear: 1
      logic: 1
```
