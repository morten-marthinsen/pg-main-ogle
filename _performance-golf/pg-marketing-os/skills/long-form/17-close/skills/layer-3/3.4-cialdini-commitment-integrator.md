## PURPOSE

Weaves Robert Cialdini's commitment/consistency principle throughout the close section. Cialdini's research found that people are powerfully motivated by their innate desire for commitment and consistency — once they've agreed to something, they feel compelled to act in ways consistent with that agreement. Since any prospect who has read to the close section has implicitly agreed with the main argument, this psychological leverage can be subtly deployed to reduce closing resistance.

The key word is SUBTLE. Makepeace and his protégés emphasize that this technique works when the prospect doesn't feel manipulated. It's a nudge, not a shove. The prospect should feel that ordering is simply the natural, consistent next step — not that they've been tricked into a corner.

This microskill takes the drafted close sections (benefit summary, guarantee, theme, CTAs, urgency, future pacing, P.S.) and integrates commitment/consistency language at strategic points.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Map the Implicit Agreement Chain

1. Identify what the prospect has implicitly AGREED TO by reading this far:
   - They agree the PROBLEM is real (root cause section)
   - They agree the OLD SOLUTIONS don't work (failure explanation)
   - They agree the MECHANISM is the real answer (mechanism section)
   - They agree the PRODUCT delivers the mechanism (product introduction)
   - They agree the OFFER is fair (they've read the offer section)
   - They agree they WANT the result (they're still reading)

2. Map these as the "agreement chain" — each point builds on the previous

3. Identify which agreements are strongest (most explicitly proven in the copy)

### Step 2: Identify Integration Points

4. Scan the drafted close sections for optimal integration points:

**HIGH-VALUE integration points:**
- Just before a major CTA (leverage agreement right before asking for action)
- In the closing theme section (especially crossroads or logical restatement)
- In the P.S. (where a "since we agree" nudge can be very effective)

**MEDIUM-VALUE integration points:**
- In the benefit summary (subtle reminder they've seen this value)
- After the guarantee (they've seen the proof, they know it works)

**LOW-VALUE / AVOID:**
- In the urgency section (urgency should stand on its own)
- In the checkout instructions (too mechanical for psychology)

5. Select 2-4 integration points (more than 4 becomes heavy-handed)

### Step 3: Write Commitment/Consistency Language

6. For each integration point, write a subtle commitment reference using one of these Makepeace-approved patterns:

**Pattern 1: The "We've Covered" Recap**
"In the short time we've had together, I've shown you why [fact 1 they agreed to]... and I've demonstrated exactly why [fact 2]... and I've proven that [fact 3]..."

**Pattern 2: The Implicit Agreement Nudge**
"If you've read this far, you already know that [key claim]. You've seen the evidence. You've heard from real people who've experienced the results. So the only question left is..."

**Pattern 3: The Consistency Lever**
"Look, you and I both know that [agreed-upon fact]. And since we agree on that, doesn't it make sense to [take action]?"

**Pattern 4: The "You Wouldn't Still Be Here" Recognition**
"The fact that you're still reading tells me something important — you're serious about [desired outcome]. And serious people don't let opportunities like this pass them by."

**Pattern 5: The Logical Inevitability**
"Given everything you've learned today — about [mechanism], about [root cause], about why [old solutions] don't work — the logical next step is clear..."

7. For each integration point, write 2-4 sentences using the appropriate pattern
8. Calibrate tone: CONVERSATIONAL, not academic or manipulative

### Step 4: Calibrate Subtlety

9. Review each integration for subtlety using these tests:

**THE AWARENESS TEST:** If the prospect became consciously aware of the technique, would they feel manipulated? If YES → too heavy-handed, soften.

**THE NATURAL TEST:** Does the language feel like a natural part of the conversation? If it feels like a "technique" → rewrite more naturally.

**THE RESPECT TEST:** Does the language respect the prospect's intelligence? If it feels patronizing ("well, OBVIOUSLY you agree...") → rewrite with respect.

**THE FREQUENCY TEST:** Are there more than 4 commitment references in the close? If YES → remove the weakest ones. Less is more.

10. Revise any integration that fails a subtlety test

### Step 5: Integrate into Close Sections

11. Insert the commitment/consistency language at the selected integration points
12. Ensure surrounding text flows naturally (no jarring insertion feeling)
13. Verify each integration enhances rather than disrupts the section's primary function

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Heavy-Handed Manipulation
- **Symptom:** "Since you've read all 47 pages, you OBVIOUSLY agree with everything I've said, so just buy it!"
- **Cause:** Writer treated Cialdini as a hammer instead of a nudge
- **Prevention:** Step 4 subtlety tests (especially Awareness and Respect tests)
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with softer framing: "If you've read this far, you already sense..."

### Failure Mode 2: Too Many Integrations
- **Symptom:** Every other paragraph references "since you agree..." — feels like a broken record
- **Cause:** Writer over-deployed the technique
- **Prevention:** Step 2 limits to 2-4 integration points, Step 4 Frequency Test
- **Recovery:** Remove weakest integrations, keep only the 2 strongest

### Failure Mode 3: Academic Tone
- **Symptom:** "As Dr. Robert Cialdini has demonstrated in peer-reviewed research..."
- **Cause:** Writer explained the technique instead of USING it
- **Prevention:** Step 8 requires conversational tone — never cite Cialdini or psychology explicitly
- **Recovery:** Rewrite in first-person conversational voice

### Failure Mode 4: Disconnected from Argument
- **Symptom:** "Since we agree..." but the specific agreement isn't something proven in the copy
- **Cause:** Agreement chain not properly mapped from actual promotion content
- **Prevention:** Step 1 maps agreements from the actual argument made in this promotion
- **Recovery:** Replace with agreement that was actually established in the copy

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Financial Newsletter Close — 2 Integrations

**Integration 1 (In closing theme, before CTA):**
"In the short time we've spent together, I've shown you why the dollar crisis is locked in... I've documented how it will hammer unsuspecting investors... and I've demonstrated that my system has averaged 47% gains — on ALL trades — even in the worst markets. Since we see eye-to-eye on all of this, doesn't it make a lot more sense to send for your FREE report today — rather than risk everything by going it alone?"

**Integration 2 (In P.S.):**
"P.S. — Look, you've made it this far because you're not the type of person who buries their head in the sand. You're someone who takes action when the evidence is clear. The evidence IS clear. All that's left is one simple step — call 1-800-236-0407 right now."

**Subtlety Score:** NATURAL — both integrations feel like logical conversation, not technique.

### Example 2: Health Supplement Close — 3 Integrations

**Integration 1 (After benefit summary):**
"You've seen the research. You've heard from real men who've experienced the transformation. You already know that [mechanism] is the real answer. The only question is — are you ready to experience it for yourself?"

**Integration 2 (Before final crossroads):**
"The fact that you're still watching tells me something important about you. You're not the type to give up on your health. You're not the type to settle for less. And you're definitely not the type to let an opportunity like this pass by without at least giving it a shot."

**Integration 3 (In P.P.S.):**
"P.P.S. — Everything I've shared with you today is real. The science is real. The results are real. And the guarantee means you risk absolutely nothing. The only thing standing between you and your second prime... is clicking that button."

**Subtlety Score:** NATURAL — commitment language woven into emotional context.
