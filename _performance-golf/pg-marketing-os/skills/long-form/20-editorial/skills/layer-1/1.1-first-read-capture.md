# Microskill 1.1: First-Read Capture

**Layer:** 1 (Blind Read Assessment)
**Purpose:** Read copy without analysis, capture uncontaminated gut reactions

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE: BLIND READ FIRST

The most valuable editorial insight comes from the FIRST read — before methodology analysis contaminates the experience. Once you start analyzing, you can never recapture the reader's fresh perspective.

**RULE:** Read the ENTIRE copy straight through without stopping. Do NOT make notes during reading. Only capture impressions AFTER completing the full read.

---

## FIRST-READ PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Clear Mental State

Before reading:
- No analysis mode
- No "looking for problems" mode
- Read as if you're the target customer
- Read at natural pace (don't skim)

### Step 2: Complete Uninterrupted Read

- Read from headline to final P.S.
- Do NOT stop to make notes
- Do NOT highlight problems yet
- Just experience the copy

### Step 3: Immediate Capture

Within 60 seconds of finishing, capture:

```yaml
first_read_capture:
  # Raw gut reactions - don't overthink

  stopping_power:
    question: "Did the opening grab me?"
    gut_reaction: "[yes strongly | yes somewhat | not really | no]"
    first_memory: "[what do you remember from the opening?]"

  engagement:
    question: "Did I want to keep reading?"
    gut_reaction: "[couldn't stop | kept going | some drag | struggled]"
    where_did_interest_peak: "[section or moment]"
    where_did_interest_drop: "[section or moment if any]"

  believability:
    question: "Did I believe this?"
    gut_reaction: "[completely | mostly | somewhat | skeptical]"
    most_believable_moment: "[what felt most credible]"
    least_believable_moment: "[what raised doubt]"

  desire:
    question: "Did I want what they're offering?"
    gut_reaction: "[must have | interested | maybe | not really]"
    desire_peak_moment: "[when did want feel strongest]"

  clarity:
    question: "Did I understand the offer?"
    gut_reaction: "[crystal clear | mostly clear | somewhat confused | confused]"
    any_confusion_points: "[what was unclear if anything]"

  action_impulse:
    question: "Did I feel urged to act?"
    gut_reaction: "[ready to buy | considering | thinking about it | not moved]"
    what_stopped_you: "[if not ready, what held you back]"

  overall_impression:
    quality_feel: "[elite | professional | decent | needs work | rough]"
    one_word_description: "[single word that captures the feeling]"
    would_you_share: "[yes | maybe | no]"
```

## SECTION-BY-SECTION IMPRESSIONS

Capture quick impressions by section (still from gut, not analysis):

```yaml
section_impressions:
  lead:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    memorable: "[what stuck with you]"

  story:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    felt: "[engaging | okay | slow | boring]"

  root_cause:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    convinced: "[yes | somewhat | no]"

  mechanism:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    understood: "[clearly | mostly | barely | lost]"

  proof:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"

  product:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    wanted: "[yes | maybe | not really]"

  offer:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
    value_feel: "[great deal | fair | expensive | confused]"

  close:
    impression: "[strong | okay | weak]"
```

## CRITICAL MOMENTS LOG

Flag specific moments that stood out (good or bad):

```yaml
critical_moments:
  high_points:
    - moment: "[describe the moment]"
      section: "[where it occurred]"
      why_notable: "[what made it work]"

    - moment: "[next high point]"
      # ...

  low_points:
    - moment: "[describe the moment]"
      section: "[where it occurred]"
      why_problematic: "[what felt off]"

    - moment: "[next low point]"
      # ...

  confusion_points:
    - moment: "[where you got confused]"
      what_was_unclear: "[description]"

  friction_points:
    - moment: "[where reading felt like work]"
      what_caused_friction: "[description]"
```

## FIRST-READ SCORE

Based purely on gut reaction, estimate quality:

```yaml
first_read_score:
  estimated_grade: "[A | B | C | D | F]"
  confidence: "[high | medium | low]"

  scoring_rationale:
    if_a: "Compelling throughout, believed it, wanted it, ready to act"
    if_b: "Good overall, some strong moments, few weak spots"
    if_c: "Mixed - some good, some problematic, needs work"
    if_d: "Struggled to finish, significant issues"
    if_f: "Couldn't finish or fundamentally broken"
```

## DUPLICATE CONTENT DETECTION (Learning #51 — MANDATORY)

**CRITICAL:** Source files (especially from Google Docs exports) often contain duplicate content from track changes/suggestions. These must be detected and flagged.

```yaml
duplicate_detection:
  check_for:
    - "Repeated paragraphs (exact or near-exact)"
    - "Repeated sentences within 20 lines of each other"
    - "Content appearing as both 'original' and 'suggested' versions"
    - "Concatenated text that looks like merge artifacts"

  detection_method:
    step_1: "Scan for exact paragraph duplicates"
    step_2: "Scan for near-duplicates (80%+ similarity within 20 lines)"
    step_3: "Flag content that appears in two formatting styles"
    step_4: "Check for run-on text without proper paragraph breaks"

  action_on_detection:
    exact_duplicates: "Auto-flag for removal in Layer 4"
    near_duplicates: "Flag for human review"
    merge_artifacts: "Flag for cleanup"

  output:
    duplicates_found:
      - location: "[section/line range]"
        type: "[exact | near | merge_artifact]"
        first_occurrence: "[text snippet]"
        second_occurrence: "[text snippet]"
        recommended_action: "[remove | review | merge]"

  quality_gate: "Zero duplicate content acceptable in final output"
```

### Example Duplicate Pattern

```
DETECTED DUPLICATE:
Location: Root Cause section, lines 45-50 and 52-55
Type: Near-duplicate (export artifact)

First occurrence:
"That said, the real problem though…
And the thing nobody really talks about…
Is what's happening with the toe of the club through impact."

Second occurrence:
"The real problem though… and the thing nobody really talks about…
is what's happening with the toe of the club through impact."

Recommended action: Remove second occurrence (reformatted duplicate)
```
