# Microskill 2.1: Makepeace Lens

**Layer:** 2 (Multi-Expert Critique)
**Purpose:** Evaluate copy through Clayton Makepeace's flow and momentum principles

---

## MAKEPEACE EXPERTISE PROFILE

**Master of:** Emotional momentum, reader engagement, relentless forward motion
**Signature Approach:** Every sentence must PULL the reader to the next
**Core Philosophy:** "Boring is the only unforgivable sin in copywriting"

---

## MAKEPEACE 10-POINT CRITIQUE CHECKLIST

### 1. Opening Velocity
**Question:** Does the opening create immediate forward motion?

```yaml
opening_velocity:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  quote: "[First 2-3 sentences]"
  diagnosis: "[What's working or not working]"
  makepeace_principle: "The first sentence must be so compelling they HAVE to read the second"
```

---

### 2. Sentence-Level Pull
**Question:** Does each sentence create desire to read the next?

```yaml
sentence_pull:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  sample_weak_sentences:
    - quote: "[sentence that doesn't pull]"
      location: "[section]"
      problem: "[why momentum dies]"
  makepeace_principle: "Every sentence is a promise that the next will reward"
```

---

### 3. Paragraph Momentum
**Question:** Do paragraphs build energy or drain it?

```yaml
paragraph_momentum:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  energy_mapping:
    opening_paragraphs: "[building | flat | draining]"
    middle_paragraphs: "[building | flat | draining]"
    closing_paragraphs: "[building | flat | draining]"
  momentum_killers:
    - location: "[where energy dies]"
      cause: "[what killed it]"
  makepeace_principle: "Momentum is like a ball rolling downhill—once it stops, you have to push it again"
```

---

### 4. Section-to-Section Flow
**Question:** Do transitions maintain or kill momentum?

```yaml
section_flow:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  transitions_reviewed:
    - from: "[section A]"
      to: "[section B]"
      verdict: "[smooth | jarring | dead stop]"
      quote: "[transition text]"
  makepeace_principle: "The reader should never feel the 'bump' between sections"
```

---

### 5. Reader Engagement Hooks
**Question:** Are there regular engagement devices throughout?

```yaml
engagement_hooks:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  hooks_found:
    count: "[number]"
    types: "[questions | open loops | teasers | curiosity gaps]"
    distribution: "[well-spaced | clustered | sparse]"
  missing_hook_zones:
    - location: "[section]"
      current_length_without_hook: "[word count]"
  makepeace_principle: "Every 200-300 words needs a fresh reason to keep reading"
```

---

### 6. Emotional Escalation
**Question:** Does emotional intensity build appropriately?

```yaml
emotional_escalation:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  emotional_arc:
    opening_intensity: "[1-10]"
    mid_intensity: "[1-10]"
    close_intensity: "[1-10]"
  flat_spots:
    - location: "[where emotion flatlines]"
      current_emotion: "[what reader feels]"
      needed_emotion: "[what they should feel]"
  makepeace_principle: "Emotion should rise like a fever, not flatline like a patient"
```

---

### 7. Urgency Build
**Question:** Does urgency accumulate naturally toward the close?

```yaml
urgency_build:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  urgency_checkpoints:
    early_copy: "[urgency level 1-10]"
    mid_copy: "[urgency level 1-10]"
    pre_close: "[urgency level 1-10]"
    close: "[urgency level 1-10]"
  manufactured_vs_authentic:
    verdict: "[authentic | manufactured | mixed]"
    evidence: "[what makes it feel real or fake]"
  makepeace_principle: "Urgency is earned through the copy, not announced at the end"
```

---

### 8. Boredom Detection
**Question:** Are there any sections that would lose readers?

```yaml
boredom_detection:
  assessment: "[NONE | MINOR | SIGNIFICANT]"
  boring_zones:
    - location: "[section]"
      word_count: "[length]"
      problem: "[why it's boring]"
      quote: "[sample of boring text]"
  makepeace_principle: "If you're bored writing it, they're bored reading it"
```

---

### 9. Energy Recovery
**Question:** After low points, does energy recover quickly?

```yaml
energy_recovery:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  low_points_identified: "[count]"
  recovery_speed:
    - low_point: "[description]"
      words_to_recovery: "[count]"
      recovery_quality: "[strong | weak | none]"
  makepeace_principle: "You can dip the energy, but you must bring it back fast"
```

---

### 10. Close Energy Level
**Question:** Does the close have maximum momentum?

```yaml
close_energy:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  close_momentum: "[accelerating | steady | decelerating]"
  reader_state_at_cta: "[urgent | interested | tired | lost]"
  makepeace_principle: "The close should feel like the copy is racing toward the finish line, not limping"
```

## EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

Every critique point MUST include:
- **Quote:** Exact text from the copy
- **Location:** Section or paragraph reference
- **Problem:** Specific diagnosis
- **Fix:** Concrete recommendation

**NO VAGUE CRITIQUES.** "Flow could be better" is unacceptable. "Paragraph 3 of the mechanism section kills momentum with a 47-word sentence explaining cellular biology—break into 3 punchy sentences" is acceptable.
