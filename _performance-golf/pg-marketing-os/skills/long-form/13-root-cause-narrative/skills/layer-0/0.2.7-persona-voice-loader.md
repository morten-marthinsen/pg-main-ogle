### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `generative_full_draft` arena mode, prioritize **actual selling copy specimens** that show each writer's voice, vocabulary, rhythm, and persuasion techniques in the specific type of copy this skill generates.

For root cause narratives, prioritize specimens showing blame externalization and villain construction. The root cause narrative is where the prospect's worldview gets shattered and rebuilt — "what you thought was causing the problem is wrong, and here's the REAL cause." This requires specimens that demonstrate: (1) how to articulate a false belief so the reader recognizes it as their own, (2) how to introduce a counter-intuitive truth without losing credibility, (3) how to name and characterize a villain that the reader can direct their frustration toward, and (4) how to make the reader feel absolved of blame. Makepeace's health controls (Shameless Sobs, 17-Cent Life Saver) show industry-villain framing — pharmaceutical companies and medical establishment as the antagonists. Schwartz's ads show sophisticated problem revelation calibrated to market awareness. Clemens' VSLs show the "it's not your fault" pattern executed at scale. Bencivenga's maxims show proof-first problem validation where the evidence itself creates the worldview shift.
