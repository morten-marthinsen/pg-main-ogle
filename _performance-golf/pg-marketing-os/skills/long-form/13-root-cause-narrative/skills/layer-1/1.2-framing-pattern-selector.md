# Skill 13.1.2: Framing Pattern Selector

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** 10 — Root Cause Narrative
**Layer:** 1 (Strategic Classification)
**Position:** Second classification microskill — determines the FRAME through which the root cause is presented
**Type:** Pattern Selection
**Dependencies:** communication_type_classification.json (from 1.1), checkpoint_response.json (from 0.4), tier1_intelligence_index.json (from 0.2)
**Output:** framing_pattern_selection.json

---

## PURPOSE

Select the primary and secondary framing patterns that determine HOW the root cause is presented to the reader. While the communication type (from 1.1) determines the VOICE and TONE, the framing pattern determines the LENS — whether the root cause is framed as a villain's fault, a scientific misunderstanding, a deliberate conspiracy, a simple oversight, an inversion, or a forgotten history. Framing patterns can stack (most TIER1 controls use 2), and the selection drives evidence requirements and villain utilization downstream.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A strategic frame selector that chooses the narrative lens for root cause presentation
- A compatibility engine that ensures the selected frame works with the communication type
- A stacking advisor that identifies which frames layer effectively together
- An evidence requirements mapper that tells downstream microskills what proof is needed

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A communication type classifier (that is 1.1) — framing and communication type are separate axes
- A writer — it selects the frame, not the prose
- An emotional arc designer (that is 1.3) — framing is structural, emotional arcs are experiential
- A villain creator — it uses the villain from upstream; it determines how the villain is framed

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load the 6 Framing Patterns

```
FROM tier1_intelligence_index.framing_patterns, LOAD all 6 frames:

FRAME 1: villain_driven (65% of TIER1 controls)
  Definition: Root cause is caused, perpetuated, or hidden by a named villain
  When dominant: When a clear villain exists with motive and evidence of harmful actions
  Evidence required:
    - Villain identity (name, type, role)
    - Villain motive (why they perpetuate the problem)
    - Villain actions (what they have done)
    - Victim impact (how the reader is harmed)
  Compatible with: ALL other frames (villain can stack with anything)
  TIER1 examples:
    - Gundry: Food industry knew lectins were harmful, pushed them anyway for profit
    - Stansberry: Federal Reserve deliberately devalues currency
    - Montezuma: Pharmaceutical industry treats symptoms to maintain repeat customers

FRAME 2: science_driven (40% of controls)
  Definition: A misunderstood biological, chemical, or physical process is the real cause
  When dominant: When the root cause IS a process, not a person; educated audiences
  Evidence required:
    - Scientific basis (research, studies, biological processes)
    - Specific mechanism (named enzyme, hormone, process)
    - Why it was misunderstood (complexity, recency of discovery)
  Compatible with: villain_driven, conspiracy_driven, ignorance_driven
  TIER1 examples:
    - Gundry: Lectin biology, Citrate Lyase enzyme, gut microbiome science
    - Sears: Telomere biology, cellular aging science
    - FloraSource: Microbiome diversity science

FRAME 3: conspiracy_driven (30% of controls)
  Definition: An organized, deliberate effort to keep the reader ignorant
  When dominant: When villain actions are coordinated, institutional, ongoing
  Evidence required:
    - Conspiracy evidence (meetings, policies, documented decisions)
    - Motive (financial, political, competitive)
    - Scope (how widespread the suppression is)
  Compatible with: villain_driven, science_driven, historical_driven
  TIER1 examples:
    - Stansberry: Federal Reserve monetary policy as coordinated devaluation
    - Gundry: 1950s Harvard conference where food industry "didn't care"
    - FloraSource: Pharmaceutical industry suppressing microbiome research

FRAME 4: ignorance_driven (25% of controls)
  Definition: The root cause was simply unknown, overlooked, or misunderstood — no malice
  When dominant: Lower sophistication markets; new discoveries; no clear villain
  Evidence required:
    - Discovery narrative (how the truth was found)
    - Why it was overlooked (complexity, paradigm blindness)
    - New evidence (what changed)
  Compatible with: science_driven, historical_driven
  NOT compatible with: villain_driven, conspiracy_driven (contradicts "no malice" premise)
  TIER1 examples:
    - Sears: Telomere science too new for mainstream medicine to adopt

FRAME 5: inversion_driven (15% of controls)
  Definition: The conventional "solution" IS the root cause — doing the "right" thing made it worse
  When dominant: Stage 4-5 markets where audience has followed conventional wisdom faithfully
  Evidence required:
    - Conventional approach named specifically
    - Evidence it makes things WORSE (not just "doesn't work" — actively harmful)
    - Mechanism of harm (how the "solution" causes damage)
  Compatible with: villain_driven, science_driven, conspiracy_driven
  TIER1 examples:
    - Gundry: "Healthy" whole grains and tomatoes ARE the cause of gut damage
    - Haney: Standard grip/stance corrections create new compensations that worsen the slice

FRAME 6: historical_driven (10% of controls)
  Definition: Ancient wisdom was lost, suppressed, or forgotten; modern science confirms it
  When dominant: Markets where historical/traditional remedies are relevant
  Evidence required:
    - Historical source (ancient practice, traditional knowledge)
    - Modern validation (recent science confirming the old wisdom)
    - Why it was lost (modernization, institutional bias)
  Compatible with: science_driven, conspiracy_driven, ignorance_driven
  TIER1 examples:
    - FloraSource: Traditional fermented foods supported microbiome diversity — modern diet destroyed it
    - Montezuma: Ancient digestive practices before pharmaceutical era
```

### Step 2: Score Each Frame Against Campaign Data

```
FOR each framing_pattern:

  CALCULATE villain_alignment (0-10):
    IF villain_driven:
      IF villain.name specific AND villain.type known AND villain.role defined: 9-10
      IF villain data partial: 5-8
      IF no villain data: 1-3
    IF ignorance_driven:
      IF no clear villain OR villain is "biological_process": 8-10
      IF strong villain present: 2-4 (contradicts "no malice" frame)
    [Score each frame based on how well the villain data supports it]

  CALCULATE evidence_alignment (0-10):
    COMPARE frame's evidence requirements against available_evidence_types:
    COUNT requirements that can be fulfilled
    SCORE: (fulfilled / total_requirements) * 10

  CALCULATE communication_type_compatibility (0-10):
    DEFINE compatibility matrix:
      conspiracy_reveal → conspiracy_driven (10), villain_driven (9), science_driven (5)
      scientific_explanation → science_driven (10), ignorance_driven (7), inversion_driven (6)
      direct_confrontation → villain_driven (9), inversion_driven (8), science_driven (5)
      data_presentation → science_driven (8), conspiracy_driven (7), villain_driven (6)
      metaphor_driven → science_driven (8), villain_driven (7), historical_driven (6)
      conspiracy_expose → conspiracy_driven (10), villain_driven (9), historical_driven (5)
      opposite_of_truth → inversion_driven (10), villain_driven (7), conspiracy_driven (6)
      hidden_truth → ignorance_driven (9), science_driven (8), historical_driven (7)
      not_your_fault → villain_driven (9), science_driven (7), ignorance_driven (7)
      suppressed_science → conspiracy_driven (9), science_driven (9), villain_driven (7)
    LOOKUP: compatibility_matrix[selected_communication_type][frame]

  CALCULATE reframe_technique_alignment (0-10):
    DEFINE alignment matrix:
      not_your_fault → villain_driven (9), science_driven (8), ignorance_driven (7)
      conspiracy → conspiracy_driven (10), villain_driven (8)
      hidden_truth → ignorance_driven (9), science_driven (8), historical_driven (7)
      opposite_of_truth → inversion_driven (10), villain_driven (7)
      suppressed_science → conspiracy_driven (9), science_driven (9)
      conspiracy_expose → conspiracy_driven (10), villain_driven (9)
    LOOKUP: alignment_matrix[reframe_technique][frame]

  CALCULATE niche_frequency (0-10):
    LOOKUP: cross_vertical_patterns.by_niche[niche]
    IF frame is dominant in niche: 8-10
    IF frame is secondary in niche: 5-7
    IF frame is rare in niche: 2-4

  FRAME_SCORE = (villain_alignment * 0.25) + (evidence_alignment * 0.20) +
                (communication_type_compatibility * 0.25) +
                (reframe_technique_alignment * 0.15) + (niche_frequency * 0.15)
```

### Step 3: Select Primary and Secondary Frames

```
RANK all 6 frames by FRAME_SCORE descending

SELECT primary_frame:
  - Highest scoring frame
  - Must score >= 6.0
  - IF villain_driven scores >= 7.0 AND villain data is complete:
    STRONGLY PREFER villain_driven as primary (65% of TIER1 controls use it)

SELECT secondary_frame:
  - Second highest scoring frame IF score >= 5.0
  - Must be COMPATIBLE with primary (check compatibility list)
  - IF incompatible: skip to next compatible frame
  - IF no compatible frame scores >= 5.0: secondary = null

VALIDATE stack:
  IF primary = villain_driven AND secondary = ignorance_driven:
    FLAG: These are incompatible (villain implies malice, ignorance implies no malice)
    RESOLVE: Drop secondary or choose next compatible frame
  IF primary = ignorance_driven AND secondary = conspiracy_driven:
    FLAG: Same incompatibility
    RESOLVE: Drop secondary or choose next compatible frame
```

### Step 4: Map Evidence Requirements

```
BASED ON selected frames:

COMPILE evidence_requirements:
  FROM primary_frame.evidence_required: [list]
  FROM secondary_frame.evidence_required: [list]  # if secondary exists
  DEDUPLICATE

FOR each requirement:
  CHECK: Is this requirement fulfillable from available_evidence_types?
  SET: requirement_status = "available" OR "missing" OR "partial"

COMPILE evidence_map:
  requirements: [object]
  fulfilled_count: integer
  total_required: integer
  fulfillment_percentage: float
  missing_requirements: [string]

IF fulfillment_percentage < 50%:
  ADD to warnings: "Frame evidence requirements significantly unmet.
                    Evidence integration calibrator will need creative sourcing."
```

### Step 5: Define Frame-Specific Narrative Guidance

```
BASED ON selected frames:

COMPILE frame_guidance:
  primary_frame:
    name: string
    narrative_role: string  # how this frame shapes the story
    phase_emphasis:         # which phases this frame most affects
      - phase_number: integer
        emphasis: enum[primary_driver, supporting, minimal]
    villain_usage:
      IF villain_driven: "Villain is central — named, motivated, evidenced"
      IF conspiracy_driven: "Villain is institutional — documented, systematic"
      IF science_driven: "Villain may be secondary — process is the star"
      IF ignorance_driven: "No villain — ignorance itself is the antagonist"
      IF inversion_driven: "The 'solution' is the villain"
      IF historical_driven: "Modern institutions are the villain by omission"

  secondary_frame:
    name: string
    stacking_technique: string  # how secondary layers on primary
    phase_emphasis: [object]

  combined_approach:
    opening_emphasis: string  # which frame leads
    transition_point: string  # where frame shifts or layers
    closing_emphasis: string  # which frame delivers the final punch
```

### Step 6: Compile Selection Output

```
COMPILE framing_pattern_selection:
  primary_frame: string
  secondary_frame: string  # or null
  primary_score: float
  secondary_score: float
  frame_stack_rationale: string
  evidence_requirements:
    requirements: [object]
    fulfilled_count: integer
    missing: [string]
  frame_guidance: object  # from Step 5
  villain_type_match: boolean  # does villain data support the frame
  all_frame_scores: [object]  # transparency

WRITE to: layer-1-outputs/framing_pattern_selection.json
HANDOFF to: 1.3-emotional-arc-designer (parallel), 1.4-narrative-sequence-mapper
```

---

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Primary frame scores >= 6.0** — below this threshold indicates poor frame fit
2. **Villain-driven strongly preferred when villain data is complete** — 65% of elite controls use it; deviating requires strong rationale
3. **Frame stack is compatible** — no incompatible frame pairs (villain + ignorance, ignorance + conspiracy)
4. **Evidence requirements mapped** — every requirement tagged as available, missing, or partial
5. **Frame guidance actionable** — Layer 2 writers must be able to use the guidance to shape their prose
6. **Communication type compatibility verified** — frame must work with the classified communication type
7. **Rationale documented** — why this frame stack, what TIER1 controls use it, why alternatives were rejected

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| No frame scores above 6.0 | HIGH | All scores < 6.0 | Use highest scorer with warning; may indicate upstream data problems |
| Incompatible stack selected | MODERATE | Compatibility check fails | Drop secondary frame or select next compatible option |
| Villain data incomplete for villain_driven | MODERATE | villain_driven selected but villain.name generic | Flag for human review; may need to fall back to science_driven |
| Evidence requirements severely unmet | MODERATE | fulfillment_percentage < 40% | Re-evaluate frame selection; consider frames with lower evidence requirements |
| Communication type conflicts with best frame | LOW | High frame score but low type compatibility | Document conflict; communication type takes priority (it was classified first) |
| Inversion_driven selected without clear inversion evidence | MODERATE | Frame selected but no evidence that conventional solution causes harm | Flag; inversion_driven requires STRONG counter-evidence |
