# Skill 13.3.2: Reframe Stacking Optimizer

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** 10 — Root Cause Narrative
**Layer:** 3 (Refinement & Polish)
**Position:** Second refinement microskill — layers multiple reframe techniques for maximum worldview shift
**Type:** Copy Refinement
**Dependencies:** All Layer 2 outputs, tier1_intelligence_index.json (from 0.2), checkpoint_response.json (from 0.4)
**Output:** reframe_stack_report.json

---

## PURPOSE

Optimize the layering of multiple reframe techniques throughout the root cause narrative so that the reader's worldview shifts progressively and irreversibly. A single reframe creates a crack in the reader's existing belief system. A stacked set of 2-3 compatible reframes SHATTERS it. This microskill ensures the narrative deploys the primary reframe technique (from Skill 11) plus at least one compatible secondary technique, layered at the optimal narrative points for cumulative worldview transformation.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A reframe architect that layers multiple compatible techniques for cumulative impact
- A stacking optimizer that determines the SEQUENCE in which reframes deploy for maximum effect
- A coherence checker that ensures stacked reframes complement rather than contradict each other
- A TIER1-pattern follower that replicates proven stacking patterns from elite controls

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A reframe selector — the primary reframe comes from Skill 02; this skill adds and layers
- A first-draft writer — Layer 2 already deployed the primary reframe; this skill optimizes the stack
- An emotional arc designer (that is 1.3) — reframes affect worldview; emotional arcs affect feelings
- A single-pass tool — it reads the existing narrative and ADDS or STRENGTHENS reframe layers

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Reframe Taxonomy

```
FROM tier1_intelligence_index.reframe_taxonomy, LOAD all 6 techniques:

TECHNIQUE 1: not_your_fault (35% frequency)
  Mechanism: Removes self-blame by attributing cause to external agent
  Worldview shift: "I'm broken/lazy" → "I was set up to fail by [external cause]"
  Deployment point: Phase 4 (reveal) and Phase 5 (failure explanation)
  Signature language: "It's not your fault," "You didn't fail — the system failed you,"
    "You were set up to fail," "Anyone would have the same result because..."
  TIER1 example: Gundry — "It's not your willpower. It's lectins."

TECHNIQUE 2: conspiracy (25% frequency)
  Mechanism: Reveals organized effort to keep reader ignorant for profit
  Worldview shift: "I'm uninformed" → "I was deliberately kept in the dark"
  Deployment point: Phase 6 (villain) and Phase 4-5
  Signature language: "They knew," "They didn't care," "This was deliberate,"
    "You were never supposed to find out," "The industry has known since..."
  TIER1 example: Gundry — "The food industry has known since 1950"

TECHNIQUE 3: hidden_truth (15% frequency)
  Mechanism: Positions root cause as overlooked information, not suppressed
  Worldview shift: "Nobody knows the answer" → "The answer has been there all along"
  Deployment point: Phase 3-4 (authority → reveal)
  Signature language: "What most people don't realize," "Hidden in plain sight,"
    "The research exists — it just hasn't reached the mainstream"
  TIER1 example: Sears — telomere science available but unknown to mainstream

TECHNIQUE 4: opposite_of_truth (10% frequency)
  Mechanism: Conventional wisdom is exactly WRONG — not just incomplete, but inverted
  Worldview shift: "I know what to do" → "What I 'know' is the opposite of truth"
  Deployment point: Phase 2 (false belief) and Phase 4 (reveal)
  Signature language: "The exact opposite," "Everything you've been told is backwards,"
    "The conventional wisdom isn't just wrong — it's 180 degrees wrong"
  TIER1 example: Gundry — "The foods you think are healthiest are destroying your gut"

TECHNIQUE 5: suppressed_science (10% frequency)
  Mechanism: Real peer-reviewed research exists but has been buried by interests
  Worldview shift: "The science isn't there yet" → "The science IS there — it's been buried"
  Deployment point: Phase 4 (reveal) and Phase 6 (villain)
  Signature language: "Published research shows," "The studies exist,"
    "Peer-reviewed evidence that [villain] doesn't want you to see"
  TIER1 example: FloraSource — microbiome research buried by pharmaceutical interests

TECHNIQUE 6: conspiracy_expose (5% frequency)
  Mechanism: Names specific conspirators with documented actions and dates
  Worldview shift: "Something feels wrong" → "Here's WHO did it, WHEN, and HOW"
  Deployment point: Phase 6 (villain deep dive)
  Signature language: "In [year], [named person] at [institution] decided..."
    "The documents show..." "Internal memos reveal..."
  TIER1 example: Stansberry — names specific Fed officials and policy decisions
```

### Step 2: Identify Current Reframe State

```
SCAN existing narrative text from Layer 2 outputs:

FOR each phase:
  IDENTIFY which reframe technique(s) are currently present
  TAG each instance:
    - technique: which of 6 techniques
    - location: phase and approximate position
    - strength: strong (explicit deployment), moderate (implied), weak (barely present)
    - language: the specific text that deploys the reframe

COMPILE current_reframe_state:
  primary_reframe: string  # from Skill 02
  primary_deployments: [location, strength, language]
  secondary_reframes_found: [technique, location, strength]
  current_stack_depth: integer  # count of distinct techniques present
  gaps: [phases where no reframe is present]
```

### Step 3: Determine Optimal Stack

```
FROM stacking_matrix, IDENTIFY compatible techniques:

  not_your_fault stacks well with:
    → conspiracy (35% stack: "It's not your fault — they did this deliberately")
    → opposite_of_truth (25%: "It's not your fault — you were told the opposite of truth")
    → hidden_truth (20%: "It's not your fault — the truth was hidden from you")
    → suppressed_science (15%: "It's not your fault — the science was buried")

  conspiracy stacks well with:
    → not_your_fault (most common pair)
    → suppressed_science ("They buried the science")
    → conspiracy_expose ("Here's who did it specifically")
    → villain presence required

  hidden_truth stacks well with:
    → not_your_fault
    → opposite_of_truth
    → suppressed_science
    → lower-intensity villain okay

  opposite_of_truth stacks well with:
    → not_your_fault
    → conspiracy ("The opposite was pushed deliberately")
    → hidden_truth ("The truth was the opposite all along")

  suppressed_science stacks well with:
    → conspiracy ("They suppressed the science on purpose")
    → not_your_fault
    → conspiracy_expose ("Here's who suppressed it")

  conspiracy_expose stacks well with:
    → conspiracy (expose is the detailed version)
    → suppressed_science (expose the suppressors)

INCOMPATIBLE STACKS:
  hidden_truth + conspiracy_expose → contradicts: "overlooked" vs. "deliberately suppressed"
  ignorance (in framing) + conspiracy → contradicts: "nobody knew" vs. "they deliberately hid"

DETERMINE optimal secondary reframe:
  FROM primary reframe, LOOKUP compatible techniques
  RANK by: frequency in TIER1 + compatibility score + villain alignment
  SELECT highest ranking compatible technique NOT already present

IF current_stack_depth < 2:
  MUST add at least 1 more technique
IF current_stack_depth == 2:
  EVALUATE whether a 3rd technique would strengthen or dilute
  ADD 3rd ONLY if it significantly enhances the worldview shift
IF current_stack_depth >= 3:
  VERIFY all techniques are coherent and not redundant
```

### Step 4: Layer Reframes into Narrative

```
FOR each reframe technique in the stack:

  DETERMINE deployment sequence (order matters):

  TIER1 STACKING PATTERNS:

  Pattern A — Gundry Triple Stack:
    Layer 1 (Phase 4): not_your_fault — "It's not your willpower"
    Layer 2 (Phase 4-5): opposite_of_truth — "The 'healthy' foods are the cause"
    Layer 3 (Phase 6): conspiracy — "The food industry knew since 1950"
    Effect: Relief → shock → anger — progressive escalation

  Pattern B — Montezuma Triple Stack:
    Layer 1 (Phase 4): not_your_fault — "Your body isn't broken"
    Layer 2 (Phase 5): conspiracy — "Pharmaceuticals profit from your symptoms"
    Layer 3 (Phase 4-5): hidden_truth — "The real answer was there all along"
    Effect: Relief → anger → hope — emotional sandwich

  Pattern C — Stansberry Double Stack:
    Layer 1 (Phase 4-5): conspiracy — "Federal Reserve manipulates currency"
    Layer 2 (Phase 6): conspiracy_expose — "Here's who, when, and how"
    Effect: Suspicion → confirmed outrage — escalating specificity

  APPLY to current narrative:
    FOR each technique to add or strengthen:
      1. IDENTIFY optimal deployment point (which phase, which paragraph)
      2. WRITE reframe language matching the technique's signature patterns
      3. ENSURE the reframe BUILDS on what came before (not restarts)
      4. CHECK that the reframe serves the emotional arc at that point

  LAYERING PRINCIPLE:
    Each reframe must DEEPEN the worldview shift, not repeat it.
    Layer 1 creates the crack ("It's not your fault")
    Layer 2 widens the crack ("They did this on purpose")
    Layer 3 shatters the wall ("Here's exactly who and how")
    The reader's old worldview should be IRRETRIEVABLE by Phase 6
```

### Step 5: Verify Stack Coherence

```
CHECK stack coherence:

  □ All techniques are compatible (no contradictions)
  □ Reframes build progressively (each deepens the shift)
  □ No redundant deployments (same technique restated without adding depth)
  □ Deployment sequence matches emotional arc (check phase targets)
  □ Minimum 2 techniques present (mandatory requirement)
  □ Maximum 4 techniques present (beyond 4, diminishing returns)

  □ Primary reframe is the STRONGEST deployment (most prominent, most text)
  □ Secondary reframe(s) support rather than compete with primary

  □ Worldview shift is progressive:
    Entry worldview: [what they believe before Phase 1]
    Exit worldview: [what they believe after Phase 7]
    The shift should be COMPLETE — reader cannot return to entry worldview

  IF any coherence check fails:
    IDENTIFY the failing point
    ADJUST: remove conflicting technique OR rewrite deployment to resolve
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Minimum 2 reframes stacked** — single reframe creates a crack; stack shatters the worldview
2. **Stack is coherent** — no contradictory techniques (e.g., hidden_truth + conspiracy_expose)
3. **Progressive deepening** — each reframe layer deepens the worldview shift, not repeats it
4. **Primary reframe is dominant** — the primary technique from Skill 02 gets the most prominent deployment
5. **Deployment points match emotional arc** — reframes appear at phases where they amplify the designed emotional target
6. **Worldview shift is complete** — the reader's exit worldview must be fundamentally different from entry worldview
7. **Stack matches TIER1 patterns** — the combination should map to a proven TIER1 stacking pattern or have documented rationale for deviation

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Stack depth < 2 | HIGH | Fewer than 2 distinct techniques deployed | Add secondary reframe from compatible stacking matrix |
| Contradictory techniques stacked | HIGH | hidden_truth + conspiracy_expose, or ignorance + conspiracy | Remove conflicting technique; replace with compatible one |
| Reframes repeat rather than deepen | MODERATE | Same shift language at multiple points without escalation | Rewrite later deployments to build on earlier ones |
| Primary reframe overshadowed by secondary | MODERATE | Secondary technique gets more text and prominence | Strengthen primary deployment; ensure it remains the dominant voice |
| Worldview shift incomplete | HIGH | Reader could still hold entry worldview after Phase 7 | Strengthen deployment at Phase 4-5; ensure the break is irreversible |
| Stack depth > 4 | LOW | More than 4 techniques, causing incoherence | Trim to strongest 2-3; too many techniques dilutes each one |
| Reframe language too on-the-nose | MODERATE | "This is a not_your_fault reframe" — meta-language | Rewrite in natural narrative voice; remove framework language |

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Gundry — Triple Stack (not_your_fault + opposite_of_truth + conspiracy)

```
REFRAME STACK:

Reframe 1: not_your_fault (PRIMARY)
  Phase 4 deployment: "It's not your willpower. It's not your discipline. It's not
    your metabolism. The real cause is a protein in the foods you were TOLD to eat."
  Worldview shift: "I'm lazy/weak" → "I was poisoned by approved foods"

Reframe 2: opposite_of_truth
  Phase 4-5 deployment: "The very foods on the USDA food pyramid — the foods
    every doctor and magazine told you to eat — are the exact foods causing
    your gut damage, weight gain, and inflammation."
  Worldview shift: "I know what's healthy" → "What I thought was healthy is the opposite"

Reframe 3: conspiracy
  Phase 6 deployment: "The food industry has known about lectin damage since 1950.
    At a landmark conference, researchers presented the evidence. The industry's
    response: they didn't care. Profits mattered more than your health."
  Worldview shift: "Nobody knew" → "They knew and chose profit over my health"

Stack pattern: Pattern A (Gundry)
Stack coherence: TRUE
Stacking sequence: [not_your_fault → opposite_of_truth → conspiracy]

Worldview progression:
  Entry: "I can't lose weight because I lack willpower and discipline"
  Phase 4: "It's not me — it's lectins in foods I thought were healthy"
  Phase 5: "Everything I tried was wrong because it included lectins"
  Phase 6: "The food industry KNEW and didn't care about my health"
  Exit: "I was the victim of a profit-driven food system. Now I know the real cause."
```
