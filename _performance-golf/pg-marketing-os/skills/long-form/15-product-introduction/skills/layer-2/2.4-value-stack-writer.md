# Microskill 2.4 — Value Stack Writer

**Skill:** 15-product-introduction

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 — Draft Generation
**Position:** Fourth microskill in Layer 2 (final in Layer 2)
**Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter (Primary), Jake Torres — Viral Content Architect (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

Writes the value stack section — the bonuses, their dollar values, descriptions, and total value statement. This section builds the perceived value of the offer dramatically above the actual price, so that when the price is revealed in Layer 3, it feels like a fraction of what the prospect expected to pay. The value stack transforms "buying a product" into "getting an incredible deal."

This microskill uses the value stacking technique and bonus introduction pattern selected in Layer 1 (microskill 1.4) to structure how bonuses are presented. Each bonus must have a name, a specific value, and a brief description that connects it to the mechanism or the product's primary outcome.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A value stack writer that presents bonuses with names, values, and descriptions
- A value perception builder that makes the total offer feel dramatically underpriced
- An executor of the value stacking technique and bonus pattern from 1.4
- A mechanism-connector that ties each bonus to the core problem or outcome

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A bonus creator or designer (that is Skill 06 — confirmed in 0.4)
- A price reveal writer (that is 3.1 — value stack sets UP the price reveal)
- A component reveal writer (that is 2.3 — components prove the mechanism; bonuses add value)
- A guarantee or scarcity writer (that is 3.2)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Check for Bonus-Free Path

1. If bonus_count == 0 AND bonus_introduction.pattern == "bonus_free_authority_play":
   - Execute the AUTHORITY PLAY path (Step 1A)
   - Skip Steps 2-6
   - Go directly to Step 7

**Step 1A: Authority Play (No Bonuses)**
2. Write a brief section that reinforces the product's intrinsic value:
   - "I'm not going to pad this with a bunch of bonuses you don't need."
   - OR: The product's authority speaks for itself (Gundry pattern)
   - OR: The guarantee IS the bonus (Australian Dream pattern — "Empty Jar Guarantee")
3. Word count: 30-75 words (brief — confidence, not apologetic)
4. TIER1 examples:
   - Gundry Total Restore: Zero bonuses. Entire value is Dr. Gundry's authority + 16 ingredients + clinical data. The offer doesn't NEED bonuses.
   - Australian Dream: Zero bonuses. The Empty Jar Guarantee IS the offer sweetener.
   - Resurge: Zero traditional bonuses. Implicit value of "$5,000 worth of John's fitness systems" is positioned as guarantee backup, not bonus.
5. Go to Step 7.

### Step 2: Write Value Stack Introduction

6. Based on selected bonus_introduction pattern, write the transition into the value stack:

**IF STACKED REVEAL:**
```
"But [Product Name] is just the beginning of what you're getting today..."
OR: "When you claim your [Product Name] today, you're not just getting [core product]. You're also getting..."
```

**IF BUT-WAIT ESCALATION:**
```
"And if you [order/claim/start] today, I'm also going to include something special..."
Each subsequent bonus gets increasingly enthusiastic introduction.
```

**IF PRE-PRICE BONUS STACK:**
```
"Before I tell you the investment, let me show you everything that's included..."
All bonuses revealed BEFORE any price language appears.
```

7. Use TIER1 transition language as reference:
   - "But that's not all you're getting today..." (Golf VSLs)
   - "I'm also going to include..." (Montezuma)
   - "When you sign up today, you don't just get X... you also get..." (Superbrain)
   - "Just look at what you get..." (no!no! PRO)
   - "Your FREE gifts are yours to keep just for trying..." (no!no! PRO)

### Step 3: Write Each Bonus Section

8. For EACH bonus in sequence_order, write a mini-section:

**Bonus Section Template:**
```
[Bonus Number/Label]: [Bonus Name] — [Value Statement]

[2-3 sentence description connecting bonus to mechanism or core outcome]

[Why this bonus matters — connects to the prospect's problem or desire]
```

9. Each bonus MUST include:
   - A specific, memorable NAME (not "Bonus 1: PDF Guide")
   - A stated DOLLAR VALUE (not "valuable" — actual number)
   - A brief DESCRIPTION connecting to the mechanism or outcome
   - A RELEVANCE statement (why this bonus helps the prospect)

10. TIER1 bonus examples:

**BBW — Stacked Reveal:**
```
"Bonus #1: The Warren Buffett Pyramid — Value: $97
A step-by-step wealth building framework modeled after Warren Buffett's compound growth strategy..."

"Bonus #2: 7 Lazy Millionaire Habits — Value: $97
The daily routines that separate the wealthy from everyone else..."

"Bonus #3: Quick Cash Manifestation Guide — Value: $107
How to generate an extra $500-$1,000 within the first 30 days..."

"Bonus #4: 500 Billionaire Brain Wave Success Stories — Value: $97
Real results from people just like you..."
```

**PSS — Pre-Price Stack with Dollar Values:**
```
"Bonus #1: Distance Amplifier Drill — Value: $97
The single drill that adds 15-25 yards to your drives within 10 minutes..."

"Bonus #2: Private VIP Day Credit — Value: $300
A $300 credit toward a one-on-one coaching day..."

"Bonus #3: Short Game Rescue Sequence — Value: $97
Three drills that eliminate the chunk-and-thin pattern around the greens..."

"Bonus #4: Putting Fundamentals Masterclass — Value: $250
The putting system used by 4 major champions..."

"Bonus #5: $1,000 in Equipment Coupons — Value: $1,000
Real savings on clubs, balls, accessories, and apparel..."
```

### Step 4: Apply Value Stacking Technique

11. Based on selected primary_technique:

**IF DOLLAR VALUE PARADE:**
- Present running total after each bonus
- "That's [Bonus 1 value] right there..."
- "Plus [Bonus 2 value]..."
- "We're already at [running total]..."
- End with dramatic total: "That's a total value of [sum] — and you're getting ALL of it today."

**IF CATEGORY DIVERSIFICATION:**
- Emphasize the VARIETY of what's included
- After all bonuses: "Video training. Coaching credit. Digital tools. Guides. And real savings. Everything you need from every angle."

**IF CONTINUITY TROJAN HORSE:**
- Present the subscription bonus as exceptionally valuable
- Frame the trial as a GIFT: "You're also getting a FREE 14-day pass to [membership/app]..."
- Mention the ongoing value AFTER the trial detail
- "After your free trial, you can continue for just $[amount]/month — or cancel anytime with one click."
- TIER1: 357 (Game Improvement App, 14-day free → $29/mo), SSS (Scratch Club 14-day → $19/mo)

**IF WORTH ENTIRE COURSE ANCHOR:**
- Single out ONE bonus as being worth the entire purchase:
- "Honestly, [Bonus Name] alone is worth more than what you'll invest in [Product Name] today."
- TIER1: Superbrain ("Overcome Procrastination bonus — worth the entire course alone"), PSS ("$1,000 in coupons alone is worth more than your entire investment")

12. If secondary_technique exists, layer it on top of primary

### Step 5: Write Total Value Statement

13. Sum all bonus values into a total:
    - "[Bonus 1] at $[value]"
    - "[Bonus 2] at $[value]"
    - "..."
    - "That's a total value of $[total] — and you're getting everything today."

14. The total value must be DRAMATICALLY higher than the actual price:
    - Ideal ratio: 5:1 to 15:1 (total value : actual price)
    - If ratio < 3:1: Flag — "Value stack may feel insufficiently dramatic"
    - If ratio > 20:1: Flag — "Value stack may strain credibility"

15. If total value includes continuity trojan horse:
    - State the trojan horse value separately (ongoing value)
    - "Plus ongoing access to [membership] worth $[monthly × 12]/year"

### Step 6: Write Value Stack Summary

16. Write a 2-4 sentence summary that:
    - Restates the total value
    - Creates excitement ("All of this is yours today")
    - Transitions to price reveal (if pre_price placement)
    - OR transitions to next section (if post_price placement)

17. Summary patterns:
    - "When you add it all up, you're looking at over $[total] in total value. And it's ALL included when you claim your [Product Name] today."
    - "That's [total] worth of [training/resources/tools] — yours free when you take action today."

### Step 7: Handle Placement Relative to Price

18. Based on bonus_introduction.placement:

**IF PRE-PRICE:**
- Value stack appears BEFORE any price language
- Ending transitions to price reveal: "Now, you might be wondering what your investment will be..."
- This is the Principle 5 execution: value ALWAYS before price

**IF POST-PRICE:**
- Value stack appears AFTER initial price reveal
- Opening acknowledges price was just revealed: "And that's not all..."
- Creates "but wait" momentum that makes the deal feel even better

**IF NO_BONUSES:**
- Step 1A authority play already handled this
- Transition directly to price section

### Step 8: Verify Value Stack Quality

19. Quality checks:

**VALUE DRAMATICALLY EXCEEDS PRICE:**
- Total bonus value should be 5-15x the actual price
- If not, flag for review

**BONUSES FEEL RELEVANT, NOT PADDED:**
- Each bonus connects to the mechanism, outcome, or prospect's problem
- No bonus should feel like random filler

**NAMES ARE SPECIFIC AND MEMORABLE:**
- "The Warren Buffett Pyramid" = GOOD
- "Bonus eBook" = BAD
- "Distance Amplifier Drill" = GOOD
- "Training Video" = BAD

**VALUES ARE STATED FOR EACH:**
- Every bonus has a specific dollar value
- No vague "valuable" or "priceless" language

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Total value exceeds price | Ratio >= 5:1 (or authority play for no-bonus path) |
| Every bonus named | Specific, memorable name (not generic) |
| Every bonus valued | Specific dollar amount stated |
| Every bonus described | 2-3 sentences connecting to mechanism or outcome |
| Bonuses feel relevant | Each bonus relates to the core problem/solution |
| Value stacking technique applied | Selected technique from 1.4 faithfully executed |
| Bonus pattern followed | Selected pattern (stacked, but-wait, pre-price, authority) executed |
| Total value statement present | Clear summation of all bonus values |
| Placement correct | Pre-price or post-price per strategy map |
| Transition to price section | Clear connection to price reveal (or next section) |

---

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Bonuses Feel Like Random Padding
- **Symptom:** Bonuses don't connect to the product or problem — feels like filler
- **Cause:** Bonus descriptions are generic, not mechanism-connected
- **Prevention:** Each bonus description must reference mechanism or core outcome
- **Recovery:** Rewrite descriptions to connect each bonus to the prospect's problem

### Failure Mode 2: Value-to-Price Ratio Too Low
- **Symptom:** Total bonus value is only 2x the price — deal doesn't feel dramatic
- **Cause:** Bonus values understated or too few bonuses
- **Prevention:** Step 5 ratio check with flag if below 5:1
- **Recovery:** Re-assess bonus values (are they understated?) or flag to Skill 06

### Failure Mode 3: Generic Bonus Names
- **Symptom:** "Bonus PDF" or "Training Video" — unmemorable, generic
- **Cause:** Bonus names not given specific, proprietary titles
- **Prevention:** Step 8 name specificity check
- **Recovery:** Rename with specific, evocative titles

### Failure Mode 4: Value Stack After Price (When Pre-Price Required)
- **Symptom:** Prospect sees price before value is established — sticker shock
- **Cause:** Placement not aligned with strategy map
- **Prevention:** Step 7 enforces placement per 1.4 strategy map
- **Recovery:** Reorder sections: value stack → price reveal

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Dollar Value Parade — Pre-Price (PSS Pattern)

**Value Stack Text:**
"But the Pendulum Swing Sequence is just the beginning. When you claim your access today, you're also getting:

**Bonus #1: The Distance Amplifier Drill — a $97 value**
A single drill that adds 15 to 25 yards to your drives within your first practice session. This is the same technique I use with my Tour-level students to unlock stored power they didn't know they had.

**Bonus #2: Private VIP Coaching Day Credit — a $300 value**
A $300 credit toward a private, one-on-one coaching day at our facility. This is the kind of personalized instruction that typically costs $500 per session — and you're getting $300 of it included free.

**Bonus #3: Short Game Rescue Sequence — a $97 value**
Three drills that eliminate the chunk-and-thin pattern around the greens. My students save an average of 4 strokes per round after learning this sequence.

**Bonus #4: The Putting Fundamentals Masterclass — a $250 value**
The same putting system used by four major champions. Distance control, green reading, and stroke mechanics — all in one comprehensive masterclass.

**Bonus #5: $1,000 in Equipment Savings — a $1,000 value**
Real coupons and discounts on clubs, balls, accessories, and apparel from top brands. One coupon alone is worth more than your entire investment today.

When you add it all up, that's over $1,744 in total value — the complete swing sequence, private coaching credit, short game rescue, putting mastery, and real equipment savings. ALL of it is yours today."

**Metrics:**
- Bonus count: 5
- Total value: $1,744
- Actual price: $47-$159
- Ratio: 11:1 to 37:1
- Technique: dollar_value_parade + worth_entire_course_anchor (Bonus #5)
- Pattern: pre_price_bonus_stack
