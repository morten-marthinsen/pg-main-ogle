## PURPOSE

Writes the branded guarantee section AND the justified scarcity section. These two elements work in tandem to execute the third critical emotional shift from TIER1 analysis: "What if it doesn't work?" transforms into "What if I miss out?" The guarantee eliminates downside risk first, establishing safety. Then scarcity creates urgency by introducing the risk of NOT acting. This order is essential — safety before urgency.

Two TIER1 principles govern this section:
- Principle 6: Guarantees are NAMED AND BRANDED. Generic "money-back guarantee" is never acceptable.
- Principle 7: Scarcity must be JUSTIFIED. Every scarcity claim is backed by a real reason.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A branded guarantee writer that transforms policy into product feature
- A justified scarcity writer that creates urgency with real reasons
- The "safety then urgency" sequence executor
- A guarantee naming engine (when branded name not provided)
- A justification enforcer that rejects naked urgency

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A guarantee designer (that is Skill 06 — type and duration confirmed in 0.4)
- A scarcity strategist (type selected in 1.4)
- A price reveal writer (that is 3.1 — guarantee follows price)
- A close writer (that is 3.4 — this sets UP the close)
- A manipulative urgency creator — all scarcity must have genuine justification

## PROCESS

### PART A: GUARANTEE WRITING

### Step 1: Determine Guarantee Branded Name

1. Check branded_name_status from 1.4:

**IF CONFIRMED (human or upstream provided):**
- Use the provided branded name
- Verify it is NOT generic ("money-back guarantee" alone is unacceptable)

**IF NEEDS_GENERATION (skill must create):**
- Generate branded name based on:
  - Guarantee strength (extreme, strong, moderate, standard)
  - Product name or mechanism name
  - Outcome promise
  - Duration
- Naming patterns from TIER1:
  - Outcome-based: "Greens In Regulation Guarantee" (357 — golf outcome)
  - Action-based: "Flush It Or It's Free" (SSS — dare/challenge)
  - Object-based: "Empty Jar Guarantee" (Australian Dream — use it all, still refund)
  - Quantity-based: "Triple-Back-Guarantee" (Montezuma — 3x refund)
  - Duration-based: "90-Day 100% Ironclad Money Back Guarantee" (BBW — strength + duration)
  - Promise-based: "Unlock Your Pendulum Swing Or It's Free" (PSS — outcome or free)

2. The branded name must be:
   - Memorable (can be repeated by the prospect to a friend)
   - Specific to this product (not generic to any product)
   - Indicative of the guarantee's extreme nature (if applicable)

**IF GENERIC_REJECTED:**
- The provided name was too generic
- Generate a specific branded name using patterns above
- Log: "Original guarantee name '[name]' was generic. Generated '[new name]' instead."

### Step 2: Write the Guarantee Section

3. Structure the guarantee section based on strength level:

**EXTREME GUARANTEE:**
```
Pattern: Bold claim + branded name + specific terms + emotional safety net

"Here's my [Branded Guarantee Name]:

[Specific terms — what happens if the product doesn't work]

[Duration — how long the guarantee lasts]

[What the prospect gets back — and anything EXTRA]

[Emotional statement — I'm so confident because...]"

TIER1 examples:
  Montezuma — Triple-Back-Guarantee:
    "If Montezuma's Secret doesn't transform your confidence in the bedroom within 60 days, I don't want your money. In fact, I'll give you TRIPLE your money back. That's how confident I am."

  Australian Dream — Empty Jar Guarantee:
    "Use the entire jar. If you're not amazed at the relief, send back the empty jar for a full refund. The risk is entirely on us."

  PSS — Unlock Your Pendulum Swing Or It's Free:
    "If the Pendulum Swing Sequence doesn't add measurable distance and consistency to your game, you don't pay a cent. Period."

  SSS — Flush It Or It's Free:
    "If this doesn't dramatically simplify your ball striking within 60 days, flush it. It's free."
```

**STRONG GUARANTEE:**
```
Pattern: Confidence statement + branded name + specific terms + no questions

"You're protected by our [Branded Guarantee Name]:

Try [Product Name] for a full [duration]. If you're not completely satisfied — for ANY reason — simply [return/contact] and you'll receive a full refund. No questions asked. No hassle. No hard feelings."

TIER1 examples:
  Gundry — 90-Day Satisfaction Promise:
    "Try Total Restore for a full 90 days. If you don't notice a dramatic difference in your digestion, energy, and overall wellbeing, simply contact our team for a full, courteous refund."

  BBW — 90-Day 100% Ironclad Money Back Guarantee:
    "You have a full 90 days to try the Billionaire Brain Wave. If it doesn't work for you — for any reason — you get every penny back."
```

**MODERATE GUARANTEE:**
```
Pattern: Standard protection + duration + simplicity

"You're covered by a [duration] [Branded Name]. If [Product Name] isn't everything I've promised, you get a full refund."

TIER1 examples:
  Resurge: "60-day money-back guarantee — if Resurge doesn't deliver the deep sleep transformation I've described, you get every penny back."
  no!no! PRO: "60-day satisfaction guarantee with free return shipping."
```

**STANDARD GUARANTEE:**
```
Pattern: Trial period + easy cancellation

"Try [Product Name] free for [trial period]. If it's not for you, cancel with one click."

TIER1 example:
  Superbrain: "15-day free trial. Cancel anytime with one click."
```

### Step 3: Add Guarantee Amplification

4. After the core guarantee, add 1-2 amplification sentences:

**Risk Reversal Amplification:**
- "The risk is entirely on me." / "You risk nothing."
- "If it doesn't work, you lose nothing. If it DOES work, you gain everything."
- "You're either thrilled with the results or you pay nothing. There is no third option."

**Confidence Amplification:**
- "I can make this guarantee because [specific reason — clinical data, track record, etc.]"
- "In [X] years of [offering/selling], our return rate is less than [low %]."
- "I wouldn't make a guarantee like this if I wasn't absolutely certain."

5. The guarantee section should feel like a FEATURE of the product, not a legal policy:
   - It should make the prospect think "wow, they're really confident"
   - Not "here are the refund terms"

### PART B: SCARCITY WRITING

### Step 4: Verify Scarcity Justification

6. Check scarcity justification_status from 1.4:

**IF JUSTIFIED:**
- Scarcity has a real reason provided — proceed with writing
- The justification must appear IN the copy (not just in metadata)

**IF NEEDS_JUSTIFICATION:**
- Scarcity type was selected but no reason was provided
- Generate justification based on scarcity type:
  - quantity_limited → manufacturing capacity, batch size, ingredient sourcing
  - price_increasing → supplier cost increases, raw material costs, exchange rates
  - suppression_takedown → industry opposition, competitor pressure, controversial mechanism
  - time_limited_window → launch pricing, first-time customer offer, promotional window
  - real_world_supply_constraint → tariffs, geopolitical disruption, supply chain issues

**IF NO_SCARCITY:**
- No scarcity type selected
- Skip Part B entirely
- Transition directly to future pacing

7. CRITICAL: Naked urgency without justification is NEVER acceptable
   - "Order now before time runs out!" → REJECTED (no reason)
   - "Order now — our supplier has informed us that ingredient costs are increasing 40% on our next production run" → ACCEPTED (real reason)

### Step 5: Write the Scarcity Section

8. Based on scarcity type, write the justified urgency:

**QUANTITY LIMITED:**
```
"Here's something you should know: [Product Name] is produced in small batches due to [justification — rare ingredients, manufacturing complexity, quality control requirements].

[Specific claim: e.g., 'The first 500 orders will receive this special pricing' or 'We have a strict limit of 6 bottles per household']

Once this batch is gone, we simply cannot guarantee when the next batch will be ready — or that it will be available at this price."

TIER1: Montezuma — "First 500 orders will receive an even more shocking discount... strict limit of 6 bottles per person/household."
TIER1: 357 — "We have a limited quantity of 357s ready to ship."
```

**PRICE INCREASING:**
```
"I should let you know that this pricing won't last. [Justification — supplier costs, ingredient price increases, next production run pricing].

[Specific claim: e.g., 'The upcoming cost price will be $[higher price] per bottle' or 'This introductory pricing is only available through this presentation']"

TIER1: Resurge — "The cost of key nutrients is going up substantially on our next order."
TIER1: Superbrain — "The yearly price is a one-time special offer only for today."
```

**SUPPRESSION/TAKEDOWN:**
```
"I have to be honest with you — I don't know how much longer [this site/this video/this information] will remain available. [Justification — industry pressure, controversial mechanism, powerful opposition].

[Specific claim: 'This is the only place you can access [Product Name]' or 'Multiple attempts have been made to take this down']"

TIER1: BBW — "I don't know how much longer this site will remain online... this site is the only place you can get the Billionaire Brain Wave."
```

**TIME-LIMITED WINDOW:**
```
"This offer is only available [justification — to first-time customers, through this specific presentation, for the next 24 hours].

[Specific claim with timeframe or condition]"

TIER1: Gundry — "This offer is only available to first-time customers through this video presentation, and it's only available for the next 24 hours, or until supplies run out."
TIER1: Montezuma — "I've reserved your supply for the next 7 minutes."
```

**REAL-WORLD SUPPLY CONSTRAINT:**
```
"As you may have heard, [real-world event] has disrupted [relevant supply chain]. [Justification — tariffs, manufacturing delays, shipping disruptions, geopolitical factors].

[Specific impact on product availability]"

TIER1: 357 — "As you know, tariffs, production times, and manufacturing availability have all been disrupted recently."
```

### Step 6: Order the Sections Correctly

9. MANDATORY ORDER: Guarantee FIRST, then Scarcity
   - Guarantee establishes SAFETY (zero risk)
   - Scarcity creates URGENCY (risk of missing out)
   - The prospect must feel SAFE before feeling URGENT
   - Reverse order (urgency before safety) feels manipulative

10. Section flow:
    - Price reveal (from 3.1) → Guarantee section → Scarcity section → Future pacing (3.4)

### Step 7: Verify Quality

11. Quality checks:

**GUARANTEE NAMED AND BRANDED:**
- Guarantee has a specific branded name (not just "money-back guarantee")
- Name is memorable and product-specific

**GUARANTEE IS A FEATURE:**
- Guarantee reads as a product feature, not a legal policy
- Confidence amplification present

**SCARCITY JUSTIFIED:**
- Every scarcity claim has a stated REASON in the copy
- No "limited time offer" without explaining WHY it is limited

**ORDER CORRECT:**
- Guarantee appears BEFORE scarcity
- Safety established BEFORE urgency

**NO NAKED URGENCY:**
- No "act now," "don't miss out," "limited time" without justification
- Every urgency phrase has a reason attached

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Guarantee has branded name | Specific, memorable, product-relevant name |
| Guarantee is not generic | "Money-back guarantee" alone is never acceptable |
| Guarantee reads as feature | Feels like a product advantage, not legal boilerplate |
| Guarantee strength appropriate | Matches promise level (extreme claims → extreme guarantee) |
| Scarcity has justification | Real reason stated in copy for every urgency claim |
| No naked urgency | Zero instances of urgency without stated justification |
| Order is guarantee → scarcity | Safety before urgency, never reversed |
| Confidence amplification | At least 1 sentence amplifying guarantee confidence |
| Specific claim present | Scarcity includes specific quantity, timeframe, or condition |
| Transition to close | Section ends ready for future pacing / binary choice |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Generic Guarantee Name
- **Symptom:** "30-day money-back guarantee" — forgettable, interchangeable
- **Cause:** Branded naming skipped or defaulted to generic
- **Prevention:** Step 1 requires branded name check with generation fallback
- **Recovery:** Generate branded name using TIER1 patterns

### Failure Mode 2: Scarcity Without Reason
- **Symptom:** "Don't miss out! Limited time offer!" — feels manipulative
- **Cause:** Justification not included in copy
- **Prevention:** Step 4 justification check with zero-tolerance for naked urgency
- **Recovery:** Add specific justification from confirmed data or generate from type

### Failure Mode 3: Urgency Before Safety
- **Symptom:** Reader feels pressured before feeling protected — distrust
- **Cause:** Scarcity section placed before guarantee
- **Prevention:** Step 6 mandatory order enforcement
- **Recovery:** Reorder: guarantee → scarcity

### Failure Mode 4: Guarantee Reads Like Legal Disclaimer
- **Symptom:** "Subject to terms and conditions. Refund processed within 30 business days."
- **Cause:** Written as policy instead of feature
- **Prevention:** Step 3 feature framing + Step 3 confidence amplification
- **Recovery:** Rewrite in confident, personal voice with branded name emphasis

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Extreme Guarantee + Quantity Scarcity (Montezuma Pattern)

**Guarantee:**
"Here's my Triple-Back-Guarantee:

Try Montezuma's Secret for a full 365 days. If it doesn't transform your confidence, your vitality, and your performance in the bedroom — I don't want your money. Send back the bottles, and I'll refund every penny. No questions. No hassle.

But I'm going even further. If you're not satisfied, I'll give you TRIPLE your money back. That's right — I'll pay you three times what you invested. That's how confident I am in this formula.

I can make this guarantee because in testing with 39 couples, 38 reported dramatic results. The science is overwhelming, and the results speak for themselves."

**Scarcity:**
"Now, there is one thing I need to tell you. Due to the difficulty of sourcing these rare ingredients — some of which come from remote regions most supplement companies have never visited — we can only produce Montezuma's Secret in limited batches.

The first 500 orders through this page will receive the special pricing you see below. After that, the price goes up to $149.99 per bottle — the original planned retail price.

There is also a strict limit of 6 bottles per person, per household. This isn't a marketing tactic — it's a supply constraint."

### Example 2: Strong Guarantee + Time Window (Gundry Pattern)

**Guarantee:**
"You're protected by what I call the Total Restore Promise:

Try Total Restore for a full 90 days. Take it every morning. Give your gut the support it needs. And if you don't notice a dramatic difference in your digestion, your energy, and your overall sense of wellbeing — simply contact our team for a full, courteous refund. No interrogation. No hoops to jump through.

After treating over ten thousand patients and formulating dozens of supplements, I would never put my name on something I wasn't completely confident in."

**Scarcity:**
"This offer is only available to first-time customers through this video presentation. It's available for the next 24 hours, or until our current supply runs out — whichever comes first.

I'm offering this special pricing because I want as many people as possible to experience what Total Restore can do. But the economics of producing sixteen premium ingredients simply don't allow me to offer this price indefinitely."

### Example 3: Extreme Guarantee + Suppression Scarcity (BBW Pattern)

**Guarantee:**
"You're covered by a 90-Day 100% Ironclad Money Back Guarantee.

Listen to the Billionaire Brain Wave for 90 days. If your life doesn't change — if you don't start seeing opportunities, ideas, and financial shifts you've never experienced before — you get every penny back. No questions. No explanation required. One email and it's done.

I'm not worried about refunds. The results speak for themselves."

**Scarcity:**
"I have to be honest with you about something. I don't know how much longer this site will remain online.

The research behind the Billionaire Brain Wave challenges some very powerful interests. Dr. Summers faced opposition from institutions that didn't want this kind of technology becoming public. Since his passing, those pressures haven't gone away.

This site is the only place you can access the Billionaire Brain Wave. I can't guarantee it will be here tomorrow."
