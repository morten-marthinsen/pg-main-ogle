**Skill:** 15-product-introduction

**Position:** First microskill in Layer 3
**Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter (Primary), Alex Rivera — Strategic Integration (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

Constructs the price reveal section using the price architecture selected in Layer 1 (microskill 1.4). The price reveal is where the prospect's "this costs money" feeling must transform into "this is a steal." The section must build value perception through anchoring BEFORE revealing the actual price, then minimize the actual price through per-day breakdowns, comparisons, or emotional justification.

Principle 5 from TIER1 analysis governs this entire section: Value is ALWAYS built before price exists. No control analyzed ever reveals price before establishing perceived value. The price reveal is a MOMENT OF DELIGHT, not a moment of cost.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A price psychology constructor that makes the actual price feel dramatically low
- An anchor-point builder that establishes high reference prices before revealing the real one
- A per-day minimizer that reframes total cost as trivial daily investment
- A multi-buy tier presenter for products with quantity options
- The executor of "costs money" → "this is a steal" emotional shift

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A price setter (prices confirmed in 0.4 from Skill 11)
- A value stack builder (that is 2.4 — value must be established BEFORE this microskill)
- A guarantee writer (that is 3.2 — guarantee follows price reveal)
- An offer architect (that is Skill 11)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Verify Value Precedes Price

1. Confirm that value stack (2.4) has been completed BEFORE this microskill executes
2. Confirm that if bonus placement is pre_price, value stack appears before this section
3. If price would appear before value: HALT — violates Principle 5
4. Calculate the value-to-price setup:
   - Total value established: $[bonus total + product intrinsic value anchoring]
   - Actual price about to be revealed: $[actual_price]
   - Gap magnitude: The wider the gap, the more effective the reveal

### Step 2: Write Price Introduction

5. Write the transition from value/components into the price section:

**IF FOLLOWING VALUE STACK (pre-price bonuses):**
```
"Now, you might be wondering... what is your investment for [Product Name] and everything I've just described?"
OR: "So what's the damage? What's this going to cost you?"
```

**IF FOLLOWING COMPONENT REVEAL (authority play, no bonuses):**
```
"Now, a formula this comprehensive — with sixteen clinically researched ingredients — you might expect to pay..."
OR: "You're probably wondering what it costs to get access to [Product Name]."
```

6. TIER1 transition language:
   - "Not $10,000 or $5,000 or $1,000 or even $100..." (BBW)
   - "They cost over $400 to produce..." (Montezuma)
   - "If you were to get this level of training with [Authority], you'd expect to pay upwards of $[high anchor]..." (Superbrain)
   - "How much would it be worth to live in the body of your dreams? $10K? $20K? $50K?" (Resurge)

### Step 3: Execute Primary Price Architecture

7. Write the price reveal using the selected primary architecture:

**IF DESCENDING ANCHOR CASCADE:**
```
Writing pattern:
  Anchor Point 1 (highest): "You might expect to pay $[highest]..." or "$[highest] would be completely reasonable..."
  Anchor Point 2: "But you won't pay $[high]. You won't even pay $[medium-high]."
  Anchor Point 3: "Not $[medium]. Not even $[medium-low]."
  ...continue descent...
  REVEAL: "Your total investment today is just $[actual_price]."

Structure: 4-6 anchor points, each approximately 50% of the previous
  Example cascade: $10,000 → $5,000 → $1,000 → $100 → $49 → $39
  Example cascade: $400 → $300 → $200 → $149.99 → $99 → $69
  Example cascade: $599 → $297 → $49

TIER1 examples:
  BBW: "Now, Dr. Summers recommended I sell this for $10,000 per person... Not $10,000 or even $5,000. Not $1,000 or $100. Just $49."
  Montezuma: "They cost over $400 to produce... You won't pay $400 today. You won't even pay $300. Or $200. Your cost is just $69 per bottle."
  Resurge: "If I were to follow the expert recommended price, I would charge you $599 for each bottle. The upcoming cost price will be $297 per bottle. But today, you won't pay anywhere near that."
  Superbrain: "You'd expect to pay upwards of $2,500... But I'm going to tear that price into shreds... and then tear those shreds into shreds."
```

**IF COMPARISON ANCHOR:**
```
Writing pattern:
  Establish what prospect ALREADY spends: "Right now, you're probably spending $[competitor price] on [inferior alternative]..."
  OR: "The average [person] spends $[annual cost] per year on [category]..."
  REVEAL: "For less than [fraction of that], you get [Product Name] — which actually addresses the root cause."

TIER1 examples:
  Montezuma: "Prescription alternatives cost $10-$60 per pill. Montezuma's Secret works out to less than $2 per day."
  Gundry: "Buying all 16 ingredients individually would cost you hundreds of dollars per month."
  357: "$399 is the public website price. But through this presentation, you won't pay anywhere near that."
```

**IF RHETORICAL VALUE QUESTION:**
```
Writing pattern:
  Ask 6-8 consecutive "What would it be worth to you..." questions
  Each question targets a different benefit/outcome
  Build emotional value before ANY number appears
  Then reveal price as trivial compared to emotional value

TIER1 examples:
  Superbrain: "What would it be worth to you to read three times faster? To remember every name? To learn any skill in half the time? To overcome procrastination permanently? To have the brain of a 25-year-old at any age? To think more clearly than you ever have? To give your children or grandchildren the same advantage? To never forget a meeting, birthday, or important detail again?"
  Resurge: "How much would it be worth to live in the body of your dreams? $10K? $20K? $50K?"
  Montezuma: "What would it be worth to YOU to escape the humiliating nightmare your weak, limp erections have caused?"
```

**IF PRICE-PER-DAY MINIMIZER:**
```
Writing pattern:
  State total price first
  Immediately reframe as daily cost
  Compare to trivial daily expense (coffee, parking, lunch)

TIER1 examples:
  Gundry: "That works out to as little as $1.27 per day. Less than a cup of coffee."
  Superbrain: "Less than $3 per day. Skip two or three nights out to dinner this month."
  Montezuma: "Less than two dollars per day for the vitality and confidence of a man half your age."
```

**IF NON-MONETARY JUSTIFICATION:**
```
Writing pattern:
  State that this ISN'T about money
  Explain the real motivation (mission, promise, moral obligation)
  Then reveal price as a consequence of accessibility, not profit

TIER1 examples:
  BBW: "I'm not doing this for money. I already have more money than I had the day before I started using this. I'm doing this to honor Dr. Summers' final request — to make this available to as many people as possible."
  Resurge: "This isn't about money. It's about having lived through the endless pain, frustration, and helplessness of watching someone you love suffer — and knowing you have the power to help."
```

### Step 4: Apply Secondary Architecture (If Present)

8. If secondary_architecture selected in 1.4, layer it on top of primary:
   - Most common layering: descending_anchor_cascade + price_per_day_minimizer
   - Pattern: Cascade down to actual price → then minimize further with per-day
   - Example: "$400 → $300 → $200 → $69 per bottle... that's less than $2 per day"

### Step 5: Present Multi-Buy Tiers (If Applicable)

9. If multi_buy_tiers present in confirmed pricing:

```
Writing pattern:
  Present tiers from MOST EXPENSIVE (per unit) to BEST VALUE:

  "Choose your [Product Name] package:"

  Option 1 (1 unit): "$[price] per [unit]"
    - Basic option, highlighted as "starter"

  Option 2 (3 units): "$[price] per [unit] — SAVE $[amount]"
    - "Most Popular" label
    - Per-unit savings highlighted

  Option 3 (6 units): "$[price] per [unit] — BEST VALUE, SAVE $[amount]"
    - Best savings
    - Often includes exclusive bonus

TIER1 examples:
  Montezuma: 1 bottle ($69), 3 bottles ($177, save $30), 6 bottles ($294, save $120 + FREE shipping)
  Gundry: Similar multi-tier with 1/3/6 options and per-unit savings
  Resurge: 1 bottle ($49), 3 bottles ($117), 6 bottles ($204)
```

10. Highlight the BEST VALUE tier (usually middle or largest)
11. Add exclusive bonus or free shipping to the largest tier

### Step 6: Write Price Summary Statement

12. After the price reveal, write a summary that locks in the "deal" feeling:
    - Restate total value vs. price
    - Include per-day if applicable
    - Transition to guarantee section

```
"So to recap: You're getting [Product Name] with [total value statement]... all for just $[actual_price]. That's [per_day] per day. Less than [trivial comparison]."
```

### Step 7: Verify Price Reveal Quality

13. Quality checks:

**ANCHORING EFFECTIVE:**
- At least one anchor point significantly higher than actual price
- Prospect should think "I expected to pay more"

**PER-DAY INCLUDED (if applicable):**
- Total price broken to daily cost
- Trivial comparison included (coffee, parking, etc.)

**MULTI-BUY CLEAR (if applicable):**
- All tiers presented with per-unit pricing
- Best value tier highlighted
- Savings quantified

**NO PRICE BEFORE VALUE:**
- Confirm that value (bonuses, components, authority) is established upstream
- First price number appears AFTER value context

**EMOTIONAL SHIFT ACHIEVED:**
- "Costs money" → "this is a steal"
- Reader should feel they are SAVING money, not spending it

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Value before price | Value fully established upstream before any price number |
| Anchoring present | At least 1 anchor point higher than actual price |
| Actual price revealed | Clear, unambiguous final price stated |
| Per-day included | If daily-use product, per-day price with comparison |
| Multi-buy presented | If tiers available, all tiers with per-unit savings |
| Architecture followed | Selected price architecture faithfully executed |
| Emotional shift | Reader feels they are getting a deal, not being charged |
| No premature price | Price number does not appear before value context |
| Transition to guarantee | Section ends with natural transition to guarantee/scarcity |
| TIER1 grounded | Price reveal approach matches elite control patterns |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Sticker Shock — Price Too Abrupt
- **Symptom:** Reader sees "$49" without context and thinks "another thing asking for money"
- **Cause:** Anchoring insufficient or absent
- **Prevention:** Step 3 requires anchoring BEFORE actual price in every architecture
- **Recovery:** Add 2-3 anchor points before the actual price

### Failure Mode 2: Per-Day Missing for Daily Product
- **Symptom:** "$49" feels like $49 instead of "$1.27 per day"
- **Cause:** Per-day minimizer not applied to supplement/daily product
- **Prevention:** If product has daily usage, per-day is REQUIRED
- **Recovery:** Add per-day calculation with trivial comparison

### Failure Mode 3: Multi-Buy Tiers Confusing
- **Symptom:** Reader unsure which option is best value
- **Cause:** Tiers presented without clear savings or "best value" label
- **Prevention:** Step 5 requires savings quantification and best-value highlight
- **Recovery:** Add "BEST VALUE" label and per-unit savings to each tier

### Failure Mode 4: Cascade Too Long — Reader Fatigues
- **Symptom:** Too many anchor points (8+) — reader gets impatient
- **Cause:** Every possible anchor included instead of 4-6 key points
- **Prevention:** Limit cascade to 4-6 anchor points per TIER1 pattern
- **Recovery:** Trim to strongest anchor points with ~50% descent between each

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Descending Anchor Cascade + Per-Day (Montezuma Pattern)

"Now, each bottle of Montezuma's Secret costs over $400 to produce when you factor in the rare ingredients, the GMP-certified manufacturing, and the third-party lab testing.

You won't pay $400 today. You won't even pay $300. Or $200.

Originally, we planned to offer Montezuma's Secret for $149.99 per bottle — which is still a fraction of what men spend on prescription alternatives that cost $10 to $60 per pill.

But because you're watching this presentation right now, your price is not $149.99. It's not even $99.

Your investment today is just $69 per bottle.

That's less than two dollars per day for the vitality and confidence of a man half your age.

Choose your package:
- **1 Bottle (30-day supply):** $69 per bottle
- **3 Bottles (90-day supply):** $59 per bottle — SAVE $30 — *Most Popular*
- **6 Bottles (180-day supply):** $49 per bottle — SAVE $120 + FREE Shipping — *Best Value*"

**Architecture:** descending_anchor_cascade + price_per_day_minimizer
**Anchors:** $400 → $300 → $200 → $149.99 → $99 → $69
**Per-day:** <$2/day

### Example 2: Rhetorical Value Questions (Superbrain Pattern)

"Before I tell you the investment, I want to ask you something.

What would it be worth to you to read three times faster than you do right now? To absorb and retain everything you read — every book, every article, every report?

What would it be worth to remember the name of every person you meet? To never walk into a room and forget why you're there?

What would it be worth to learn any new skill — a language, an instrument, a professional certification — in half the time it takes everyone else?

What would it be worth to overcome procrastination permanently? To have the focus and drive of the most productive people you know?

$2,500? $5,000? More?

If you worked with Jim privately, you'd invest upwards of $2,500 for a single session. His celebrity clients pay significantly more.

But we're going to tear that price into shreds. And then tear those shreds into shreds.

Your investment in Superbrain today is just $399 for lifetime access."

**Architecture:** rhetorical_value_question + descending_anchor_cascade
**Questions:** 4 value questions before any number
**Anchor:** $2,500 → $399

### Example 3: Non-Monetary Justification (BBW Pattern)

"I'm not doing this for money. I already have more money than I had the day before I started using the Billionaire Brain Wave. My bank account proves this works.

I'm doing this because it was Dr. Summers' final request. Before he passed, he asked me to make this accessible to everyone — not just the wealthy elite who could afford his private sessions.

That's why I'm not charging $10,000 — even though that's what his research team recommended. I'm not charging $5,000. Or $1,000. Or even $100.

Your investment today is just $39.

That's it. One payment. Lifetime access."

**Architecture:** non_monetary_justification + descending_anchor_cascade
**Mission:** Honoring Dr. Summers' request
**Anchors:** $10,000 → $5,000 → $1,000 → $100 → $39
