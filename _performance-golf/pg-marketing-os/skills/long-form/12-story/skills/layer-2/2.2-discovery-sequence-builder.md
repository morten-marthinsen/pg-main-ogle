# Layer 2.2 -- Discovery / Experiment Sequence Builder

## Purpose

Build the discovery or experiment sequence -- the HEART of the story. This is Carlton's "Action" section -- the fulfillment of the setup's tease. The discovery sequence is where suspense lives, where curiosity intensifies, and where the prospect becomes emotionally invested in the protagonist's journey. For discovery stories, this means: Finding Yoda, the Cry for Help/Breakdown, and the buildup to Yoda revealing the secret. For proof stories/live experiments, this means: the data cascade with specific results, the escalation narrative, the compounding evidence. For straight-to-expert stories, this means: the expert teaching sequence that reframes the prospect's understanding. Regardless of story type, the discovery sequence MUST maintain tension with at least 2 strategic tension points. If the sequence goes flat, the prospect stops reading.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Map the Discovery Sequence Beats for the Classified Story Type

Using the story beats from Layer 1 and the classified story type, map the specific beats that belong to the discovery sequence. The discovery sequence begins AFTER the setup/inciting incident and ends BEFORE (or at the start of) the mechanism revelation.

**Beat mapping by story type:**

```
Standard Discovery Sequence:
  Beat 1: Finding Yoda
    - Protagonist encounters the person/source who holds the secret
    - The encounter must feel natural -- not contrived
    - Yoda is introduced through ACTION, not credential dump
    - Example: "Among them was Ashraf Hadi and five other guys who
      were from a tiny Malaysian Island called Langkawi..."

  Beat 2: The Clue / Observation
    - Protagonist notices something unusual about Yoda or Yoda's world
    - The clue is an anomaly that creates curiosity
    - The protagonist doesn't yet understand what it means
    - Example: "As the weeks went on, I came to realize these six guys
      didn't have an ounce of fat on them..."

  Beat 3: The Cry for Help / Emotional Breakdown
    - Protagonist's vulnerability breaks through
    - Creates empathy and lowers Yoda's guard
    - This is the EMOTIONAL turning point -- desperation makes
      the protagonist open to receiving
    - Example: "I told him all about my wife's condition, her suicidal
      thoughts... 'If you know anything that can help her, please tell me.'"

  Beat 4: The Build to Revelation
    - Yoda agrees to share / the door opens
    - Tension is HIGHEST here -- the reader knows something important
      is about to be shared
    - This beat ends at the threshold of the mechanism revelation (2.3)
    - Example: "Ashraf smiled, patted me on the shoulder and told me
      to wait where I was..."

Accidental Discovery Sequence:
  Beat 1: Encounter with the Guide
    - The guide appears unexpectedly in the accident's aftermath
    - The guide offers something without being asked
    - Example: "A small elderly man came into my life and changed it forever..."

  Beat 2: The Initial Effect
    - The protagonist experiences something unexpected from the guide's offering
    - The effect creates wonder but isn't fully understood
    - Example: "Within an hour, I began to feel different. My body felt
      energized and the pain slightly dulled..."

  Beat 3: Escalating Effects + Pursuit
    - Each interaction increases the effect -- results compound
    - The protagonist becomes desperate to learn more
    - Example: "The next morning, my stomach was flatter again... I begged
      him for the name of this weird grape..."

  Beat 4: The Search for Answers
    - Protagonist takes the clue and begins researching
    - Creates suspense: will they find the answer?
    - Example: "The minute I returned home, I began researching the
      ingredients from Ashraf's recipe..."

Straight-to-Expert Sequence:
  Beat 1: Expert Assessment
    - The expert evaluates the protagonist's situation
    - Uses a dramatic assessment device (test, evaluation, shocking diagnosis)
    - Example: "He sent me home with a futuristic sleep assessment device
      that looked like something right out of Star Trek..."

  Beat 2: The Shocking Reframe
    - Expert tells protagonist to "throw everything you know out the window"
    - Counter-intuitive information shatters assumptions
    - Example: "About halfway through our first appointment, he told me
      to throw everything I thought I knew about sleep out the window..."

  Beat 3: The Teaching Sequence
    - Expert begins explaining the real cause and the real solution
    - This overlaps with mechanism revelation (2.3) for this story type
    - The teaching must feel like REVELATION, not lecture
    - Example: "Over the next hour or so, he laid out exactly why..."

Proof Story / Live Experiment Sequence:
  Beat 1: First Results (Data Point 1)
    - The experiment produces its first documented result
    - Must include: DATE + SPECIFIC RESULT + CONTEXT
    - The first result should be impressive but not the biggest
    - Example: "On February 1, 2007, this small group had a chance to
      make more than $497 per minute..."

  Beat 2: Data Cascade (Data Points 2-5+)
    - Results multiply and ASCEND -- each data point bigger than the last
    - Must include dates, percentages, dollar amounts (SPECIFICS)
    - The cascade creates momentum: "but it didn't stop there..."
    - Example: "On February 9, it happened again -- $251 per minute.
      On February 27 -- $552 per minute..."

  Beat 3: Escalation Narrative ("Imagine If...")
    - Bridge from documented results to prospect's imagination
    - "Had you been among this small group..." / "Just imagine..."
    - Calculate compounding: "You could have transformed $5,000 into $55,595..."
    - This is where the reader begins placing THEMSELVES in the story

  Beat 4: Ordinariness + Transparency
    - Prove the participants were ordinary: "not rocket scientists"
    - Admit losses/limitations (transparency paradox): "We won 74% of the time"
    - Show the system is simple: "Step 1, Step 2, Step 3"
    - Example: "Most of our beta-testers were ordinary people just like
      you and me. They weren't born on easy street."

  Beat 5: Re-investment Compounding
    - Show what happens when profits are reinvested
    - Creates the "wealth snowball" effect
    - Example: "Had you invested $5,000 on Feb 27, reinvested on Apr 24,
      then again on Sep 12 -- you could have turned $5,000 into $55,595."
```

### Step 2: Engineer Strategic Tension Points (Minimum 2)

The discovery sequence MUST contain at least 2 strategic tension points -- moments where the reader's uncertainty, curiosity, or emotional investment SPIKES. Without tension points, the sequence becomes a flat recitation of events.

**Tension Point Types:**

```
Type 1: The Obstacle
  Definition: Something blocks the protagonist's path to the discovery.
  Effect: Reader thinks "oh no, will they get the answer?"
  Examples:
    - "The elder said no." (erect_on_demand)
    - "Outsiders were not allowed to know." (fat_flusher)
    - "She said, 'How DARE you! That's a secret!'" (elixir_of_eros)
  Placement: Between the clue and the revelation

Type 2: The Emotional Breakdown
  Definition: The protagonist's vulnerability erupts, creating raw empathy.
  Effect: Reader thinks "I've felt that way too..."
  Examples:
    - "Karen began to cry... she was in tremendous emotional pain" (erect_on_demand)
    - "My gorgeous wife of 19 years began crying like a baby..." (montezumas_secret)
    - "I told her about my shitty life..." (elixir_of_eros)
  Placement: Before Yoda opens the door

Type 3: The Unexpected Complication
  Definition: A complication arises that threatens to derail the discovery.
  Effect: Reader thinks "what's going to happen now?"
  Examples:
    - "The herbs were local... unless I wanted to move to Peru" (erect_on_demand)
    - "I gained 10 pounds... I thought the batteries were dying" (fat_flusher)
    - "I still did not get fully erect" (erect_on_demand -- initial attempt fails)
  Placement: After partial success, before final breakthrough

Type 4: The Data Escalation Point (Proof Stories)
  Definition: A specific data result that dramatically exceeds expectations.
  Effect: Reader thinks "this can't be real... can it?"
  Examples:
    - "260.87% gains in less than 57 days" (makepeace)
    - "$35,320 in just 18 days" (makepeace)
    - "This happened 20 different times in just 12 months" (makepeace)
  Placement: Mid-cascade, when results first break through "believable" range

Type 5: The Cliffhanger Bridge
  Definition: A pause in the narrative that forces the reader forward.
  Effect: Reader thinks "don't stop now..."
  Examples:
    - "What he told me next changed everything..." then pause
    - "But I didn't stop there..." then new section
    - "But it didn't stop there. In fact, that was just the beginning." (makepeace)
  Placement: At the peak of a data cascade or before a major revelation
```

**Tension mapping requirements:**
- Minimum 2 tension points in the discovery sequence
- Maximum 5 tension points (beyond that, the sequence becomes melodramatic)
- Tension points must be SPACED -- not clustered together
- Each tension point must escalate or shift emotional state
- At least one tension point must create DOUBT (will it work? will they get the answer?)

### Step 3: Implement Open Loops Within the Sequence

The discovery sequence should contain 1-2 internal open loops that sustain attention WITHIN the story itself. These are distinct from the lead's open loops -- they operate within the narrative.

**Internal open loop patterns:**

```
The Deferred Answer:
  "I'll tell you what that ingredient was in just a moment --
   but first, you need to understand something..."
  Effect: Creates a micro-gap the reader needs to close

The Unexplained Observation:
  "I noticed something strange about these men...
   something I couldn't explain at the time..."
  Effect: Creates a mystery within the narrative

The Foreshadowing:
  "I had no idea that what happened next would change
   my life forever..."
  Effect: Tells the reader something big is coming

The Partial Revelation:
  "He pulled out a small leather pouch filled with what
   looked like red grapes... nothing I ever saw before..."
  Effect: Shows the mechanism ingredient without naming it

The Result Without Explanation:
  "Within an hour, I began to feel different..."
  (But WHY? The reader needs to find out)
  Effect: Shows effect before cause -- forces continued reading
```

### Step 4: Build the Proof Story Elements (If Applicable)

For proof stories / live experiments, the discovery sequence requires specific Makepeace-pattern elements. Each must be present and properly executed.

**Makepeace Proof Story Element Checklist:**

| Element | Definition | Template | Quality Test |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Data Cascade | Specific results with dates, ascending | "On [DATE]... [RESULT]... On [DATE]... [BIGGER]..." | Are at least 3 data points present? Do they ascend? |
| Escalation Narrative | "Imagine if you had invested..." | "Had you been among this small group... you would have..." | Does it bridge data to the reader's imagination? |
| Ordinariness Proof | Participants were ordinary people | "Most weren't rocket scientists..." | Does the reader feel "I could do this too"? |
| Transparency Paradox | Admit losses/limitations | "We won 74%... the flip side is we lost 26%..." | Does the admission INCREASE rather than decrease trust? |
| Simplicity Proof | Even a 12-year-old can do it | "Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..." | Does the system sound genuinely simple? |
| Re-investment Compounding | Show wealth building over time | "$5,000 becomes $55,595 through 3 re-investments..." | Are the compounding numbers specific and documented? |
| Specificity as Proof | Document EVERY result | Full trade list with dates and percentages | Are winners AND losers shown? |
| Exclusivity Frame | Limited spots / under the radar | "Only 1 out of every 100 readers will be able to join..." | Does scarcity feel natural, not manipulative? |

**Data cascade construction rules:**
- First data point: impressive but believable
- Middle data points: ascending, with variety in result type
- Peak data point: the most impressive result, often with a "can you believe it?" frame
- Losers documented: at least 2-3 losses shown (transparency paradox)
- Dates are SPECIFIC: "February 1, 2007" not "early February"
- Amounts are SPECIFIC: "$28,043" not "almost $30,000"

### Step 5: Calibrate Pacing and Rhythm

The discovery sequence is the LONGEST section of the story. Pacing must vary to prevent fatigue.

**Pacing pattern for discovery sequences:**

```
Opening: MODERATE pace
  - Introduce Yoda / first data point
  - Reader is orienting to the new character/situation

Rising: ACCELERATING pace
  - Clues discovered / data points accumulate
  - Sentences get shorter, momentum builds
  - "And then... and then... and then..."

Tension Point: SLOW DOWN
  - At the obstacle/breakdown/complication
  - Longer sentences, more emotional detail
  - Time feels like it stops for the reader

Climax approach: RAPID pace
  - The door opens / data cascade peaks
  - Very short sentences, fragments
  - "That's when it happened."

Pre-revelation pause: BREATH
  - A single moment of stillness before 2.3 takes over
  - "What he said next changed everything..."
  - The reader is holding their breath
```

**Sentence rhythm techniques:**
- Long sentence for scene-setting, followed by short sentence for impact
- Sentence fragments after emotional peaks ("Humiliating." / "Incredible.")
- Single-word paragraphs for maximum punch ("Wow." / "Finally.")
- Ellipses for pacing control ("And then... something happened.")
- Line breaks between sentences for breathless pacing (VSL-style)

### Step 6: Verify Sequence Integrity

**Continuity check:** Does the sequence flow naturally from the setup (2.1)?
- The inciting incident from 2.1 should be the launching point for 2.2
- Characters introduced in 2.1 should remain consistent
- Emotional state should progress from 2.1's ending state into 2.2's opening

**Momentum check:** Does the sequence build toward the mechanism revelation (2.3)?
- The sequence should END at the threshold of the revelation
- The reader should be DESPERATE to learn what Yoda reveals / what the data proves
- The final beat of the sequence should be a cliffhanger or emotional peak

**Story type compliance:** Does the sequence match the classified story type's format?
- Discovery stories: Yoda encountered, clues found, breakdown occurs
- Proof stories: Data cascaded, escalation narrated, ordinariness proven
- Expert stories: Expert assessment completed, shocking reframe delivered

### Step 7: Score the Discovery Sequence

| Dimension | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Tension point effectiveness | 0.25 | 1-10 | Are 2+ tension points present and emotionally effective? |
| Suspense maintenance | 0.20 | 1-10 | Does the reader feel compelled throughout the sequence? |
| Story type compliance | 0.15 | 1-10 | Does the sequence match the classified type's format? |
| Pacing and rhythm | 0.15 | 1-10 | Does pacing vary appropriately -- no flat stretches? |

**Minimum threshold:** Weighted average >= 7.0/10

## Anti-Patterns

- **The Flat Sequence:** "He went to Malaysia. He met some firefighters. They were thin. He asked them why. They told him." -- Zero tension, zero suspense, zero emotional investment. This is a timeline, not a story. Fix: Add obstacles, emotional breakdowns, observations that create mystery, and pacing that builds.

- **The Credential Dump Yoda:** "I met Dr. James Robert Thompson III, MD, PhD, FACP, who graduated summa cum laude from Harvard Medical School in 1982, completed his residency at Johns Hopkins, published 47 peer-reviewed papers, and was named the AMA's Top Physician in 2018..." -- Yoda is introduced through resume, not action. Fix: "The old man pulled out a small leather pouch filled with what looked like red grapes..." (action reveals character).

- **The Monotone Data Cascade:** "We made 50%. Then we made 60%. Then we made 70%. Then we made 80%." -- The data points are ascending but lifeless. Fix: Add dates, context, escalation language ("but that was just the beginning"), the reader's hypothetical ("had YOU been among this small group..."), and the "but it didn't stop there" bridges.

- **The Missing Transparency:** A proof story that shows ONLY winners -- no losses admitted. This destroys credibility. The Makepeace control shows EVERY trade, winners AND losers. Fix: "We won 74% of the time... the flip side is we lost 26%. Losses can and WILL happen. I'm not perfect, and anybody who tells you they ARE is a liar."

- **The Melodramatic Breakdown:** "I fell to my knees, tears streaming down my face, my body wracked with sobs as I clutched the doctor's desk and screamed 'WHY ME?' to the heavens above..." -- Over-written, melodramatic, strain believability. Fix: "Karen began to cry. I thought she was just afraid... but that's not why she was crying. Karen was crying because she was in tremendous emotional pain." (Understated emotion is more powerful.)

- **The Premature Revelation:** The discovery sequence reveals the full mechanism before 2.3 gets its chance. "And that's when Ashraf explained that milk thistle detoxifies BPA, which is an endocrine-disrupting chemical that causes leptin resistance..." -- This is the mechanism revelation. It belongs in 2.3. Fix: End the sequence at the THRESHOLD: "Ashraf smiled, patted me on the shoulder... 'The secret you've been searching for.'"

- **The Skipped Ordinariness Proof:** A proof story with results that seem only attainable by experts, without showing that ordinary people achieved them too. Fix: "Most of our beta-testers were ordinary people just like you and me. They weren't rocket scientists or Harvard graduates. They didn't have millions when they started."
