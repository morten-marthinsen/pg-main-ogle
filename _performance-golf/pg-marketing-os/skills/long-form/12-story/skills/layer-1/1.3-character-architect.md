# Layer 1.3 — Character Architect

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Story Architecture)
**Type:** Character Design & Constellation Mapping
**Dependencies:** upstream_data (from 0.1), vault_story_intelligence (from 0.2), teaching_index (from 0.3), story_type_classification (from 1.1), story_beat_map (from 1.2)
**Output:** character_architecture.json

---

## Purpose

Design the protagonist and supporting character constellation that serves the classified story type and beat structure. The protagonist is the most critical character in the story — they are the vehicle through which the prospect EXPERIENCES the mechanism's discovery and validates its effectiveness. The prospect must either SEE THEMSELVES in the protagonist (everyman proxy) or TRUST the protagonist's experience (authority figure).

Per Carlton: "The story is about your READER, not you. Readers want to see THEMSELVES in your story." Every character design decision must serve this principle. The protagonist's vulnerability, credibility, name, and emotional starting state must be calibrated so the prospect's brain automatically maps the protagonist's experience onto their own life.

Supporting characters serve specific narrative functions: the yoda/guide delivers the mechanism, the villain/antagonist embodies the root cause, the authority validator provides credibility, and the supporting witness provides social proof. Each character must have a clear function — no character exists without a narrative purpose.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Select Protagonist Type Based on Story Classification

```
LOAD story_type_classification.selected_type
LOAD story_type_classification.niche_alignment.niche

SELECT protagonist type from taxonomy:

PROTAGONIST TYPES:

1. EVERYMAN_PROXY:
   Definition: An ordinary person who mirrors the prospect's demographics, struggles,
               and emotional state. The prospect sees THEMSELVES in this character.
   Best for: standard_discovery, accidental_discovery, underdog_vulnerability, transformation_story
   Niche fit: health (weight loss, ED, energy), relationships, self_improvement
   Key characteristic: The protagonist IS the prospect, just further along the journey
   Identification mechanism: "This person is JUST LIKE ME — same problems, same frustrations,
                              same failed attempts. If THEY found a solution, maybe I can too."
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Leptitox protagonist: firefighter, married, wife struggling with weight
     - Elixir of Eros protagonist: young man, rejected by women, socially awkward
     - Erect on Demand protagonist: married man, ED destroying his relationship

2. AUTHORITY_FIGURE:
   Definition: An expert, doctor, researcher, or credentialed professional the prospect
               trusts because of their expertise, not because they mirror the prospect.
   Best for: straight_to_expert, revelation_story, doctor-fronted campaigns
   Niche fit: health (doctor-fronted), wealth (analyst-fronted), sports_instruction (coach-fronted)
   Key characteristic: The protagonist's CREDENTIALS make their story credible
   Identification mechanism: "This person KNOWS things I don't. If THEY say it works,
                              it must be real. I trust their expertise."
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Resurge protagonist: celebrity sleep specialist with clinical credentials
     - Montezuma's Secret protagonist: doctor with medical authority
     - Jack Crooks (Makepeace): 23 years currency trading experience, Bloomberg appearances

3. RELUCTANT_DISCOVERER:
   Definition: A person who did NOT set out to find a solution but stumbled onto one
               through circumstance, accident, or necessity.
   Best for: accidental_discovery, standard_discovery (when discovery was unplanned)
   Niche fit: health, relationships, self_improvement
   Key characteristic: The discovery's ACCIDENTAL nature makes it more believable
   Identification mechanism: "This person wasn't selling anything — they just found something
                              by accident. That makes it MORE credible, not less."
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Fat Flusher protagonist: accidentally discovered African plant remedy
     - Leptitox protagonist: firefighter sent to Malaysia for work (not seeking a cure)

4. BETA_TESTER_GROUP:
   Definition: A COLLECTIVE protagonist — a group of ordinary people who tested
               the mechanism with documented results. Not a single character but a group.
   Best for: proof_story_live_experiment
   Niche fit: wealth/investment, sports_instruction, health (clinical trial framing)
   Key characteristic: The GROUP validates what no single person could — it works for
                       many different people, not just one lucky individual.
   Identification mechanism: "487 ordinary people tested this, and MOST of them got results.
                              I'm an ordinary person too. This could work for me."
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Makepeace Speed Profits: "487 beta-testers" — described as "ordinary people just like you"
   NOTE: The group still needs a NARRATOR (usually the authority who ran the experiment),
         but the protagonist role is SHARED across the group.

5. PATIENT_ZERO:
   Definition: The first person to try the mechanism — the original test case whose
               experience validates the approach before anyone else tried it.
   Best for: transformation_story, origin_story, proof_story
   Niche fit: health, self_improvement, sports_instruction
   Key characteristic: Being FIRST creates urgency and pioneering credibility
   Identification mechanism: "This person was brave enough to try it first, and look what
                              happened. I don't have to be the guinea pig — I can learn from them."

6. PROTAGONIST_COUPLE:
   Definition: A relational pair — husband/wife, partners, parent/child — where the
               mechanism affects the RELATIONSHIP, not just one person.
   Best for: underdog_vulnerability (when shame affects relationship), health (when condition
             affects partner), relationships
   Niche fit: health (ED, weight loss affecting marriage), relationships
   Key characteristic: The RELATIONAL STAKES raise the emotional intensity — it is not
                       just about the protagonist's body, it is about their marriage/love.
   Identification mechanism: "This isn't just about one person — it's about their relationship.
                              That's what I'M worried about too."
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Leptitox: protagonist + wife Grace (weight affecting their marriage)
     - Erect on Demand: protagonist + wife (ED destroying intimacy)
```

### Step 2: Design Protagonist Profile

```
BUILD protagonist profile:

  name:
    IF story_assets.protagonist_name exists: USE provided name
    ELSE: SELECT a name that matches:
      - Niche demographics (age-appropriate, culturally appropriate)
      - Prospect demographics (name the prospect would identify with)
      - Everyman quality (not exotic or unusual — should feel "normal")
    NAMING RULES:
      - Use first name only (familiarity, not formality)
      - Avoid names that signal wealth, status, or exoticism (unless authority type)
      - For authority_figure type: include title (Dr., Professor) to signal credentials
      - For everyman_proxy: common, relatable name for the target demographic

  prospect_mirror_elements:
    Design minimum 3 elements that create identification:
    FOR EACH mirror element:
      element: What specific trait, situation, or experience mirrors the prospect
      connection: HOW this creates identification ("prospect thinks: 'that's me'")

    MIRROR ELEMENT CATEGORIES:
      - Demographic mirror: Same age range, gender, life stage, occupation type
      - Struggle mirror: Same problem, same failed attempts, same frustrations
      - Emotional mirror: Same feelings — shame, frustration, hopelessness, skepticism
      - Situational mirror: Same life situation — marriage affected, career impacted, health declining
      - Attempt mirror: Same things tried and failed — diets, medications, programs, investments

    MINIMUM REQUIREMENT: At least 1 struggle mirror AND 1 emotional mirror.
    The prospect MUST think: "This person understands what I'm going through."

  vulnerability:
    The protagonist's PRIMARY vulnerability — the thing that makes them human, relatable,
    and emotionally accessible. This is NOT weakness. It is honest, courageous admission
    of a problem that the prospect also experiences.

    VULNERABILITY TYPES:
      - Physical vulnerability: health condition, weight, appearance, performance failure
      - Emotional vulnerability: shame, embarrassment, fear of being found out
      - Relational vulnerability: relationship strained or failing because of the problem
      - Financial vulnerability: money fears, retirement anxiety, investment losses
      - Social vulnerability: judgment from others, isolation, feeling different

    DESIGN RULE: The vulnerability must be SPECIFIC, not generic.
      BAD: "He was struggling with his health"
      GOOD: "He watched his wife push away her dinner plate again, knowing the 14 diets
             they'd tried in the last 3 years had crushed her spirit"

    Per Carlton (via Halbert): "Put some empathy into this damn thing."
    The vulnerability is WHERE empathy lives in the story.

  credibility:
    Why the prospect should trust this protagonist's experience.
    This is NOT about credentials (unless authority type) — it is about WHY the
    protagonist's journey is RELEVANT and TRUSTWORTHY.

    CREDIBILITY TYPES:
      - Experiential credibility: "I went through exactly what you're going through"
      - Professional credibility: "I have credentials/expertise in this area"
      - Reluctant credibility: "I didn't want to believe it either, but the evidence..."
      - Accidental credibility: "I wasn't looking for a cure — I stumbled onto it"
      - Institutional credibility: "I work at / trained at [institution]"
      - Duration credibility: "I've been doing this for 23 years" (Makepeace/Jack Crooks)

  emotional_starting_state:
    The protagonist's emotional state at the BEGINNING of the story.
    This must match or be slightly more intense than the prospect's current state.

    STARTING STATE OPTIONS:
      - desperate: "Nothing has worked and I'm running out of options"
      - ashamed: "I can't believe this is happening to me"
      - frustrated: "I've tried everything and nothing works"
      - skeptical: "I didn't believe this would be any different"
      - curious: "Something unusual caught my attention"
      - determined: "I was going to find the answer no matter what"
      - professional: "In my 23 years of research, I'd never seen anything like..."

    MATCH TO STORY TYPE:
      standard_discovery -> desperate or frustrated
      accidental_discovery -> frustrated or curious
      straight_to_expert -> curious or skeptical
      proof_story -> professional or determined
      underdog_vulnerability -> ashamed or desperate
      transformation_story -> frustrated or desperate
      revelation_story -> curious or professional
      warning_story -> concerned or determined
      origin_story -> determined or frustrated
```

### Step 3: Design Supporting Character Constellation

```
DETERMINE required supporting characters based on story_type and story_beat_map:

FOR EACH required character role, design the character:

1. YODA_GUIDE:
   Required for: standard_discovery, accidental_discovery, straight_to_expert
   Function: The person who holds the key knowledge and reveals the mechanism
   Design requirements:
     - Name (specific, culturally appropriate for the story's setting)
     - Credentials or reason they possess the knowledge
     - Introduction point (which beat introduces them)
     - How they earned the protagonist's trust
     - The moment they share the secret (which beat)
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Ashraf Hadi (Leptitox): Malaysian firefighter from Langkawi Island
     - Shelley (Elixir of Eros): woman in Athens hostel who knew the technique
     - Celebrity sleep specialist (Resurge): medical authority with credentials

2. AUTHORITY_VALIDATOR:
   Required for: any story type where external credibility strengthens the narrative
   Function: A credentialed person who validates the mechanism's legitimacy
   Design requirements:
     - Name and credentials (must be verifiable or believable)
     - What they validate (which aspect of the mechanism)
     - Introduction point (usually at verification beat)
     - How their validation advances the narrative
   NOTE: Can be same as YODA_GUIDE in straight_to_expert stories

3. VILLAIN_ANTAGONIST:
   Required for: warning_story, conspiracy-adjacent stories, any story with root_cause.villain_profile
   Function: Embodies the root cause or the force working AGAINST the prospect
   Design requirements:
     - Identity (industry, institution, substance, or named entity)
     - What they are doing to harm the prospect (connected to root_cause)
     - How they are exposed in the story (which beat)
     - The prospect's emotional response to the villain (anger, alarm)
   TYPES:
     - Industry villain: "Big Pharma," "Wall Street," "the diet industry"
     - Substance villain: EDCs, toxins, hidden ingredients
     - Institutional villain: government agencies, regulatory bodies (if cover-up angle)
     - Named villain: specific person or entity causing harm
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Leptitox: EDCs (endocrine disrupting chemicals) as the invisible villain
     - Weiss Betrayal 2012: named financial/political villains

4. SUPPORTING_WITNESS:
   Required for: stories needing social proof within the narrative
   Function: Another person who experienced the mechanism and can testify to its effectiveness
   Design requirements:
     - Name (if applicable)
     - Their result/experience (specific, not vague)
     - How their testimony appears in the story (conversational mention, quoted, referenced)
     - Introduction point (usually after mechanism reveal, during verification/transformation)

5. BETA_TESTER (for proof_story type):
   Required for: proof_story_live_experiment
   Function: Representative members of the beta-test group whose individual results are highlighted
   Design requirements:
     - Group size (specific number: "487 beta-testers")
     - Demographic description: "ordinary people just like you and me"
     - Individual spotlight results (if any individual results are highlighted)
     - Ordinariness proof: "They weren't rocket scientists or Harvard graduates"
   VAULT EXAMPLE:
     - Makepeace Speed Profits: "487 beta-testers" — ordinary people, started small,
       not from money, not experts. "Most started with just a few hundred bucks."

6. SPOUSE_PARTNER:
   Required for: protagonist_couple type, or when relational stakes are central
   Function: The person whose relationship with the protagonist creates emotional stakes
   Design requirements:
     - Name
     - Relationship to protagonist
     - How the problem affects them / the relationship
     - Their role in the emotional arc (do they doubt? support? suffer alongside?)
     - Their reaction to the mechanism's results (validation beat)
   VAULT EXAMPLES:
     - Grace (Leptitox): wife struggling with weight, suicidal thoughts
     - Wife (Erect on Demand): relationship strained by ED

7. SKEPTIC_CONVERTED:
   Required for: stories targeting skeptical audiences (Stage 4-5 markets)
   Function: A character who initially doubts the mechanism but becomes a believer
   Design requirements:
     - Why they were skeptical (mirrors prospect's skepticism)
     - What converted them (specific evidence or experience)
     - Their conversion moment (which beat)
     - How their conversion serves as permission for the prospect to believe
```

### Step 4: Map Characters to Story Beats

```
FOR EACH designed character:
  IDENTIFY:
    introduction_beat: Which beat introduces this character (beat_id from story_beat_map)
    active_beats: Which beats this character appears in
    function_by_beat: What the character does in each beat they appear in
    exit_beat: When this character's active role concludes (if applicable)

  VALIDATE:
    - Protagonist appears in ALL beats (they are the continuous thread)
    - Yoda/guide is introduced BEFORE the mechanism revelation beat
    - Villain is introduced BEFORE or AT the root cause/threat beat
    - Authority validator appears at or near the verification beat
    - Supporting witness appears AFTER the mechanism reveal (not before)
    - Beta testers appear throughout the data cascade (proof_story type)
    - Spouse/partner appears at vulnerability beat and again at transformation/payoff beat
    - No character appears without a function (no decorative characters)

  CHARACTER DENSITY CHECK:
    - Total unique characters: should be 2-5 for most story types
    - proof_story may have group + narrator = 2 primary entities
    - Too many characters (6+) creates confusion — consolidate or remove
    - Too few characters (1) creates loneliness — add at least yoda/guide or validator
```

### Step 5: Design Character Constellation Dynamics

```
MAP the relationships between characters:

  PROTAGONIST <-> YODA_GUIDE:
    Relationship type: Teacher/student, elder/seeker, mentor/mentee
    Trust arc: How does protagonist come to trust the guide?
    Key moment: The reveal scene — when the guide shares the secret
    Emotional dynamic: Gratitude, respect, relief

  PROTAGONIST <-> VILLAIN:
    Relationship type: Victim/oppressor (often unconscious until revelation)
    Awareness arc: When does protagonist realize the villain's role?
    Key moment: Root cause revelation — the villain is exposed
    Emotional dynamic: Anger, alarm, determination to escape

  PROTAGONIST <-> SPOUSE/PARTNER:
    Relationship type: Shared suffering, shared hope
    Stakes: What happens to the relationship if the mechanism doesn't work?
    Key moment: Transformation beat — partner sees the change
    Emotional dynamic: Love under pressure -> love renewed

  PROTAGONIST <-> SUPPORTING_WITNESS:
    Relationship type: Fellow traveler, corroborating voice
    Function: Provides "it's not just me" validation
    Key moment: Witness shares their experience
    Emotional dynamic: Solidarity, reinforced belief

  CONSTELLATION COHERENCE:
    - Every character relationship must serve the story's persuasion function
    - The constellation must create a complete narrative world
    - The prospect should be able to map themselves into the protagonist position
      and understand every other character's role from that perspective
    - No relationship should feel forced or artificial
```

### Step 6: Validate Prospect-Protagonist Identification

```
CRITICAL VALIDATION: Will the prospect see THEMSELVES in the protagonist?

FOR everyman_proxy, reluctant_discoverer, patient_zero, protagonist_couple:
  CHECK identification strength:
    [ ] Prospect shares at least 2 demographic traits with protagonist
    [ ] Prospect experiences the same core struggle as protagonist
    [ ] Prospect has felt the same emotional state the protagonist starts in
    [ ] Protagonist's failed attempts mirror the prospect's failed attempts
    [ ] Protagonist's vulnerability is something the prospect would relate to
    [ ] Protagonist is NOT so specific that large segments of the audience are excluded
    [ ] Protagonist is NOT so generic that no one feels personally identified

FOR authority_figure:
  CHECK trust formation:
    [ ] Authority's credentials are genuinely impressive (not inflated)
    [ ] Authority's expertise is relevant to the mechanism
    [ ] Authority's tone is accessible (not condescending or academic)
    [ ] Authority demonstrates empathy toward people like the prospect
    [ ] Authority has a personal stake or passion (not just professional interest)

FOR beta_tester_group:
  CHECK group identification:
    [ ] Group is described as "ordinary people just like you and me"
    [ ] Group demographics include the prospect's demographic
    [ ] Individual results span a range (not just best-case scenarios)
    [ ] Group size is specific (not "many people" — use exact numbers)

IDENTIFICATION SCORE (assess 1-10):
  10: Prospect will immediately think "that's ME" or "I trust this person completely"
  8-9: Strong identification — most of the target audience will connect
  6-7: Moderate identification — some audience segments will connect
  4-5: Weak identification — protagonist feels distant from prospect
  1-3: No identification — protagonist is unrelatable or untrustworthy

MINIMUM THRESHOLD: 7/10 identification score required.
IF below threshold: Redesign protagonist mirror elements and vulnerability.
```

### Step 7: Compile Character Architecture

```
BUILD complete character_architecture object:
  - Protagonist profile (type, name, mirror elements, vulnerability, credibility, starting state)
  - Supporting character profiles (each with role, function, introduction point)
  - Character-to-beat mapping (who appears where)
  - Constellation dynamics (relationships between characters)
  - Identification validation (score and assessment)
  - Carlton compliance (story is about the reader, empathy present, characters serve persuasion)

VERIFY:
  - Protagonist type matches story type (per Step 1 taxonomy)
  - All required characters for the story type are present
  - No character exists without a narrative function
  - Characters are introduced at appropriate beats
  - Identification score >= 7/10
  - Carlton's empathy requirement is met through protagonist's vulnerability
```

- [ ] Protagonist type selected based on story type classification (not arbitrary choice)
- [ ] Protagonist has minimum 3 prospect-mirror elements with specific connections documented
- [ ] At least 1 struggle mirror AND 1 emotional mirror present in mirror elements
- [ ] Protagonist vulnerability is SPECIFIC, not generic (detailed description, not vague label)
- [ ] Protagonist credibility type documented with supporting evidence
- [ ] Protagonist emotional starting state matches or is slightly more intense than prospect's state
- [ ] Identification score >= 7/10 with documented assessment
- [ ] All required supporting characters for the story type are present (yoda for discovery, beta_tester for proof, etc.)
- [ ] Every supporting character has a documented narrative function (no decorative characters)
- [ ] Every character is mapped to specific story beats from the beat map
- [ ] Character constellation dynamics documented (relationships, trust arcs, key moments)
- [ ] Total character count is appropriate (2-5 for most stories, no more than 6)
- [ ] Carlton's empathy requirement met through protagonist vulnerability design
- [ ] Story is about the READER (protagonist mirrors reader), not the copywriter
- [ ] For proof_story type: beta_tester group described as "ordinary people just like you"
- [ ] For discovery types: yoda/guide introduced BEFORE mechanism revelation beat
- [ ] Villain/antagonist connected to root_cause.villain_profile (if applicable)

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Perfect Protagonist** | Protagonist has no vulnerability, no struggles, no relatable flaws | No identification — the prospect cannot see themselves in someone who has never struggled; Carlton/Halbert: "Put empathy into this damn thing" |
| **Generic Everyman** | "John was a regular guy with regular problems" — no specific details | Specific identification requires specific details; vague characters create no emotional connection |
| **Credential Inflation** | Authority figure's credentials are exaggerated or unverifiable | Destroys trust when the prospect senses inflation; use real credentials or don't use authority type |
| **Orphan Characters** | Characters introduced who never appear again or serve no function | Every character must advance the narrative; orphan characters waste words and confuse the reader |
| **Missing Yoda** | Discovery story has no guide/expert who reveals the mechanism | The yoda figure is the DELIVERY VEHICLE for the mechanism; without them, the mechanism appears from nowhere |
| **Invisible Villain** | Root cause exists but no character or entity embodies it | Per root_cause.villain_profile: the villain gives the prospect someone/something to blame; invisible villains create no emotional charge |
| **Protagonist Confusion** | Protagonist switches mid-story or it is unclear who the main character is | The prospect must map onto ONE character throughout; switching protagonists breaks identification |
| **Group Without Individuals** | Beta-tester group referenced but no individual humanity shown | Even group protagonists need specific human details — "ordinary people" must FEEL ordinary through specific descriptions |
| **Spouse as Prop** | Spouse/partner mentioned but has no agency, reaction, or emotional arc | Relational characters must react, feel, change — they create relational stakes, not set dressing |
