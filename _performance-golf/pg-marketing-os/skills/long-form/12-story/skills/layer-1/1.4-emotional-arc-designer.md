**Version:** 1.0

**Dependencies:** upstream_data (from 0.1), vault_story_intelligence (from 0.2), teaching_index (from 0.3), story_type_classification (from 1.1), story_beat_map (from 1.2), character_architecture (from 1.3)
**Output:** emotional_arc_design.json

---

## Purpose

Design the emotional progression that transports the prospect through the story from their current state (post-lead curiosity/attention) to the story's terminal state: **"believes the mechanism is real and wants it."** The emotional arc is the invisible architecture that makes the story FEEL inevitable — each beat moves the prospect ONE emotional step closer to visceral belief in the mechanism.

This is NOT the lead's emotional arc (which ends at "emotionally sold, wanting proof"). This is the STORY's emotional arc, which goes FURTHER: it takes the prospect from "wanting proof" through an experiential journey that delivers EMOTIONAL proof. By the end of the story, the prospect should not merely believe the mechanism intellectually — they should FEEL its reality in their gut, because they lived it through the protagonist's experience.

Per Carlton: "In the end, your reader should FEEL something: happiness, alarm, astonishment, or greed." The emotional arc designer is the system that ensures this FEELING is precisely engineered, not left to chance.

Per Carlton: "The concept of 'transporting' is critical — you're driving the story, and it's your responsibility to keep it on the road." The emotional arc is the road map for that transportation.

## Persona Deployment

**Primary Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter — Master Copy Composite
Designs emotional progressions from decades of direct response intuition. Knows how to build from curiosity to conviction without ever letting the reader plateau. Controls the emotional temperature beat by beat — knowing exactly when to increase intensity, when to dip for contrast, when to hit the emotional peak, and when to transition from story-emotion to reader-relevance.

Cross-references emotional arc designs against vault extraction patterns. Validates that the designed progression matches empirically successful arcs from TIER1 controls. Identifies where elite copywriters place emotional peaks, how they manage transitions, and what the terminal emotional state looks like in proven stories.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Define Starting and Ending Emotional States

```
STARTING STATE:
  The prospect arrives at the story having just read the lead.
  Their emotional state is: POST-LEAD CURIOSITY + INITIAL BELIEF
  - The lead delivered the emotional sale (per E5: "This sounds amazing")
  - The prospect is primed with curiosity about the mechanism
  - The lead teased the story — the prospect EXPECTS a narrative
  - They are: curious, conditionally hopeful, wanting proof

  starting_state = "post-lead curiosity — prospect expects the story the lead teased,
                    is conditionally hopeful but still needs emotional proof"

  NOTE: This is DIFFERENT from the lead's starting state (which was raw attention).
  The prospect has already been through the lead's emotional arc. The story
  starts at a HIGHER emotional baseline.

ENDING STATE (NON-NEGOTIABLE):
  The story MUST end with the prospect in this state:
  "BELIEVES the mechanism is real and WANTS it."

  This means:
  - EMOTIONALLY CONVINCED: They have felt the mechanism work through the protagonist's
    experience. This is not intellectual agreement — it is gut-level conviction.
  - WANTING THE MECHANISM: Desire is activated. They don't just believe it's real —
    they want to apply it to their own life.
  - TRANSPORTED: They have been INSIDE the story. Per Carlton, they were "transported"
    to the world the story created.
  - READY FOR SEGUE: When the copy says "And here's what that means for YOU," the
    prospect is ALREADY thinking about their own life.

  ending_state = "believes mechanism is real, wants it — prospect has experienced
                  emotional proof through the protagonist's journey and desires
                  the mechanism for themselves"

CRITICAL DISTINCTION from Lead Arc:
  Lead ending: "This sounds amazing. Now prove it works for me."
  Story ending: "I BELIEVE this works. I FELT it through the protagonist. I WANT it for myself."

  The story goes BEYOND the lead. It does not just promise — it delivers EMOTIONAL proof.
  The prospect's state after the story should be FURTHER along the belief spectrum
  than after the lead.

FAILURE STATES (the arc MUST NOT end here):
  - "Interested" = TOO WEAK (that's mid-arc, not end-state)
  - "Curious to know more" = TOO WEAK (curiosity should be resolved by story's end)
  - "Intellectually agrees" = WRONG TYPE (intellectual agreement is not emotional conviction)
  - "Entertained" = WRONG FUNCTION (entertainment is a byproduct, not the goal)
  - "Skeptical but intrigued" = INCOMPLETE (skepticism should be dissolved by story's end)
```

### Step 2: Select Arc Shape Based on Story Type and Character

```
LOAD story_type_classification.selected_type
LOAD character_architecture.protagonist.type
LOAD story_beat_map.tension_curve.shape

SELECT emotional arc shape:

ARC SHAPE: TENSION_TO_RELIEF
  Pattern: Build tension through struggle/obstacle -> release through discovery/results
  Emotional progression: anxiety -> desperation -> discovery -> relief -> conviction -> desire
  Visual: /\/↓ (ascending tension, then relief dropping into conviction)
  Best for: standard_discovery, accidental_discovery
  When to use: When the protagonist's struggle creates tension that the mechanism resolves
  Peak moment: The "cry for help" or deepest obstacle — maximum tension before relief
  Resolution feeling: "FINALLY — an answer. This is what I've been looking for."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Happiness (relief) + Astonishment (at the mechanism)

ARC SHAPE: SHAME_TO_DIGNITY
  Pattern: Deep shame/embarrassment -> rock bottom -> discovery -> tentative hope -> full restoration
  Emotional progression: shame -> empathy -> desperation -> surprise -> hope -> dignity -> desire
  Visual: \._/↑ (descent into shame, then ascent to dignity)
  Best for: underdog_vulnerability, transformation_story (shame-driven)
  When to use: When the protagonist's core experience is shame/embarrassment the prospect shares
  Peak moment: The CONTRAST between rock bottom and first signs of change
  Resolution feeling: "If THEY overcame that shame, I can too. I DESERVE this dignity."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Happiness (dignity restored) + Greed (I want that feeling)

ARC SHAPE: CONFUSION_TO_CLARITY
  Pattern: Confusing/contradictory information -> expert guidance -> clear understanding -> conviction
  Emotional progression: confusion -> frustration -> encounter -> understanding -> clarity -> conviction
  Visual: ~~/↑ (chaos resolving into clean ascent)
  Best for: revelation_story, straight_to_expert
  When to use: When the mechanism contradicts conventional wisdom and needs explanation
  Peak moment: The "everything clicks" moment — when confusion resolves into crystal clarity
  Resolution feeling: "NOW I understand. The root cause was [X] all along. This makes PERFECT sense."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Astonishment (at the hidden truth) + Greed (I want this clarity)

ARC SHAPE: SKEPTICISM_TO_BELIEF
  Pattern: Doubt -> grudging interest -> evidence accumulation -> conversion -> conviction
  Emotional progression: skepticism -> curiosity -> impressed -> surprised -> convinced -> desire
  Visual: _..../↑ (flat skepticism gradually building to belief)
  Best for: proof_story_live_experiment, authority-fronted stories for Stage 4-5 markets
  When to use: When the prospect is highly skeptical and needs cumulative evidence
  Peak moment: The data point or result that breaks through the skepticism barrier
  Resolution feeling: "I didn't want to believe it, but the EVIDENCE is overwhelming. This is real."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Astonishment (at the results) + Greed (I want those results)

ARC SHAPE: FEAR_TO_HOPE
  Pattern: Identified threat -> escalating danger -> escape route discovered -> safety/hope
  Emotional progression: concern -> alarm -> fear -> anger -> discovery -> hope -> determination
  Visual: \↓_/↑ (descent into fear, then ascent through hope)
  Best for: warning_story, conspiracy-adjacent stories
  When to use: When the story's primary function is to establish a threat and offer escape
  Peak moment: The moment the escape route / protection mechanism is revealed
  Resolution feeling: "Thank God there's a way out. I need to act NOW to protect myself."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Alarm (at the threat) + Greed (for the protection mechanism)

ARC SHAPE: CURIOSITY_TO_CONVICTION
  Pattern: Intriguing setup -> escalating fascination -> mechanism reveal -> comprehensive proof -> conviction
  Emotional progression: curiosity -> fascination -> surprise -> impressed -> conviction -> desire
  Visual: /↑ (steady ascending engagement)
  Best for: proof_story_live_experiment (data cascade), origin_story
  When to use: When the story's power comes from accumulating evidence/results
  Peak moment: The comprehensive results reveal — when the full weight of evidence lands
  Resolution feeling: "The results speak for themselves. This is undeniably real. I want in."
  Carlton emotion delivered: Greed (for the results) + Astonishment (at the scale)

MATCH arc shape to story type:
  standard_discovery -> tension_to_relief (primary) or confusion_to_clarity (secondary)
  accidental_discovery -> tension_to_relief
  straight_to_expert -> confusion_to_clarity
  proof_story_live_experiment -> skepticism_to_belief (primary) or curiosity_to_conviction (secondary)
  origin_story -> curiosity_to_conviction
  transformation_story -> shame_to_dignity (if shame-driven) or tension_to_relief (if struggle-driven)
  revelation_story -> confusion_to_clarity
  warning_story -> fear_to_hope
  underdog_vulnerability -> shame_to_dignity
  hybrid -> combine dominant and secondary arc shapes

VALIDATE: Arc shape is compatible with:
  - Story type classification
  - Protagonist type (shame_to_dignity requires everyman with vulnerability)
  - Tension curve from beat mapper (emotional arc must align with structural tension)
  - Prospect's post-lead state (starting point of this arc)
```

### Step 3: Map Emotional States to Each Story Beat

```
LOAD story_beat_map.beats

FOR EACH beat in the sequence:
  ASSIGN specific emotional state from this taxonomy:

  EMOTIONAL STATE TAXONOMY (STORY-SPECIFIC):

  Pre-Discovery States (before mechanism is revealed):
    - post_lead_curiosity: "I'm curious about what was teased in the lead"
    - identification: "This protagonist is JUST LIKE me — same problems"
    - empathy: "I feel for this person — I understand their pain"
    - vulnerability_shared: "This person is being brave enough to share their shame"
    - desperation: "Things are terrible — I feel their desperation as my own"
    - shame: "I've felt this exact shame — this person GETS it"
    - confusion: "I've been told conflicting things — what's the truth?"
    - alarm: "This is worse than I thought — I need to pay attention"
    - anger: "Someone is RESPONSIBLE for this — I'm furious"
    - rock_bottom: "It can't get worse than this — I feel the hopelessness"
    - surprise_encounter: "Something unexpected just happened — what is this?"

  Discovery/Revelation States (when mechanism enters the narrative):
    - curiosity_intensified: "What is this secret? I NEED to know"
    - fascination: "This is genuinely interesting — I'm absorbed"
    - surprise: "I didn't expect THAT — this changes everything"
    - hope_emerging: "Could this actually work? I'm starting to believe"
    - relief: "Finally — someone understands the REAL cause of my problem"

  Post-Discovery States (after mechanism is revealed):
    - understanding: "NOW I get it — the root cause was [X] all along"
    - impressed: "These results are remarkable — this actually works"
    - growing_conviction: "The evidence is building — this is real"
    - excitement: "This could change my life — I want this"
    - conviction: "I BELIEVE this is real — I've seen/felt the proof"
    - desire: "I WANT this mechanism for myself — how do I get it?"
    - greed: "I want THOSE results — I need to be part of this"
    - urgency: "I need to act NOW — I can't miss this opportunity"

  Segue States (transitioning from story to reader):
    - personal_relevance: "This isn't just their story — this is about MY life"
    - activated_desire: "I believe the mechanism is real and I WANT it for myself"

  MAPPING RULES:
    - Each beat must have ONE primary emotional state assigned
    - States must PROGRESS forward (no backtracking unless intentional dip)
    - Pre-discovery states occupy the first 40-60% of the story
    - Discovery states occupy 10-20% (the pivot point)
    - Post-discovery states occupy the remaining 20-40%
    - The segue state is ALWAYS the final beat
    - Emotional states must align with tension levels from the beat map

  FOR EACH BEAT, DOCUMENT:
    emotional_state: The primary emotion the prospect feels
    prospect_internal_monologue: What the prospect is THINKING at this moment
    protagonist_emotional_state: What the protagonist is feeling (prospect feels WITH them)
    transported_feeling: What the prospect PHYSICALLY feels (tension in chest, relief, excitement)
    carlton_emotion: Which of Carlton's four emotions is present (happiness, alarm, astonishment, greed)
```

### Step 4: Design the Peak Emotional Moment

```
IDENTIFY the single most emotionally intense moment in the story:

PEAK MOMENT CHARACTERISTICS:
  - Highest emotional intensity in the entire story
  - The moment where the prospect makes the EMOTIONAL DECISION to believe
  - Usually occurs at 60-80% through the story
  - The moment that would make the prospect physically react (lean forward, gasp, nod)
  - The moment they would tell someone else about ("you won't BELIEVE what happened...")

PEAK MOMENT BY STORY TYPE:

  standard_discovery:
    Peak = Root cause revelation — when EVERYTHING clicks
    "Finally, it all made sense. The reason nothing had worked was [root cause].
     And THIS [mechanism] is the first thing that actually addresses it."
    Prospect feeling: "OH. So THAT'S why nothing worked. And THIS is the answer."

  accidental_discovery:
    Peak = The moment the accidental discovery is validated by evidence
    The accident led to the mechanism, and now PROOF confirms it works.
    Prospect feeling: "This wasn't just luck — there's REAL science behind this."

  straight_to_expert:
    Peak = Expert's paradigm-shifting revelation
    "What the expert revealed next shocked even me..."
    Prospect feeling: "If an expert with THOSE credentials says this, it must be true."

  proof_story_live_experiment:
    Peak = Comprehensive results reveal — the full data cascade
    "Over 12 months, 20 different opportunities, an average of 53.07% profit per trade,
     74% win rate — and every single result documented, winners AND losers."
    Prospect feeling: "These numbers are REAL. This isn't cherry-picked. I WANT in."

  origin_story:
    Peak = Breakthrough moment — when the mechanism first worked
    Prospect feeling: "They finally cracked it. And it WORKS."

  transformation_story:
    Peak = Full transformation revealed — the complete before-to-after
    Prospect feeling: "If they went from THAT to THIS, imagine what I could achieve."

  revelation_story:
    Peak = Hidden truth fully exposed with irrefutable evidence
    Prospect feeling: "I can't believe this was hidden from me. Now I understand."

  warning_story:
    Peak = Consequence projection — what happens if you DON'T act
    Prospect feeling: "I can't let that happen to me. I NEED the escape route."

  underdog_vulnerability:
    Peak = CONTRAST between rock bottom and triumph — the dignity restoration
    Prospect feeling: "From THAT shame to THIS dignity? I deserve that transformation too."

PEAK MOMENT REQUIREMENTS:
  - Must connect to a SPECIFIC beat from the beat map (identify peak_beat_id)
  - Must trigger the STRONGEST Carlton emotion (happiness, alarm, astonishment, or greed)
  - Must be achievable in the story's word count
  - Must be the moment where the story's persuasion function is FULFILLED
  - The prospect should feel compelled to tell someone about this moment
```

### Step 5: Design Emotional Transitions Between Beats

```
FOR EACH consecutive beat pair (beat N -> beat N+1):
  DESIGN the emotional transition:

  TRANSITION ELEMENTS:
    from_state: Emotional state at beat N
    to_state: Emotional state at beat N+1
    transition_trigger: What specific story element causes the shift
      (character action, revelation, evidence, obstacle, relief)
    transition_type: How the shift occurs
      - gradual: Slow build from one state to the next
      - sudden: Sharp shift (for surprise, shock, or revelation moments)
      - contrasting: Deliberate dip-then-rise (for shame_to_dignity or tension_to_relief)
    intensity_change: How much the emotional intensity changes
      - escalating: Intensity increases (most common in ascending arcs)
      - de-escalating: Intensity decreases (for relief, resolution, settling)
      - spike: Sharp increase (for shock moments)
      - dip: Sharp decrease (for contrast before a peak)
    transition_word_count: Approximate words needed for this transition

  TRANSITION RULES:
    - Every transition must have a TRIGGER (no transitions happen "because it's time")
    - Gradual transitions: minimum 50-100 words to develop naturally
    - Sudden transitions: can happen in a single sentence but must be EARNED
      (sudden transitions only work after sufficient tension has been built)
    - Contrasting transitions (dips): the dip must serve the peak
      (you go DOWN only so the UP is more powerful)
    - Maximum 2 consecutive escalating transitions before a contrast/rest moment
      (unrelenting escalation creates fatigue, not excitement)

  PACING ALIGNMENT:
    - Emotional transitions must align with tension levels from the beat map
    - High-tension beats should have escalating or spike transitions
    - Medium-tension beats should have gradual or stabilizing transitions
    - Low-tension beats should have de-escalating or settling transitions
    - The peak emotional beat should align with the peak tension beat (from 1.2)
```

### Step 6: Verify "Transported" State at Key Moments

```
Per Carlton: The concept of "transporting" is critical.

AT EACH MAJOR BEAT (minimum 3 checkpoints across the story):
  VERIFY the prospect is "transported":

  TRANSPORTING CHECKLIST:
    [ ] Prospect can visualize the scene (sensory details planned)
    [ ] Prospect feels WHAT the protagonist feels (emotional mirroring active)
    [ ] Prospect has temporarily forgotten they are reading copy (immersion achieved)
    [ ] Prospect would be "afraid to exhale, for fear of missing a delicious detail"
    [ ] Prospect's internal monologue matches the protagonist's emotional state

  TRANSPORT FAILURE INDICATORS:
    - Prospect is analyzing the copy instead of experiencing the story
    - Prospect is thinking "this is well-written" instead of "what happens next?"
    - Prospect is aware of being sold (the story feels like a pitch, not a narrative)
    - Prospect has mentally "stepped back" from the story (detachment)

  IF transport is at risk at any checkpoint:
    IDENTIFY the cause:
      - Excessive exposition (explaining instead of showing)
      - Pacing too slow (reader's attention wandering)
      - Emotional state unrealistic (transitions feel forced)
      - Language too formal/academic (breaks conversational flow)
    RECOMMEND remediation for Layer 2 construction
```

### Step 7: Design the "And Here's What That Means for YOU" Transition

```
Per Carlton: "Don't try any clever transitions back into your sales pitch.
             Instead, you merely say: And here's what that means for YOU..."

DESIGN the story-to-reader transition:

  TRANSITION REQUIREMENTS:
    - Occurs at the FINAL beat of the story (segue_to_reader)
    - Shifts the emotional state from protagonist-focused to prospect-focused
    - The prospect STOPS experiencing the protagonist's journey
      and STARTS seeing how it applies to THEIR life
    - Must feel natural, not jarring (the transition should feel inevitable)

  TRANSITION DESIGN:
    pre_transition_state: The prospect's emotional state at the end of the story's climax
      (conviction, desire, or excitement)
    transition_sentence: The bridge sentence that shifts focus
      Structure: "And here's what [this story/discovery/experiment] means for YOU..."
      Or: "Now, the reason I'm telling you this is because..."
      Or: "And the best part? You don't have to [go through what protagonist went through]..."
    post_transition_state: "activated_desire"
      The prospect is now thinking about THEIR OWN life with the mechanism applied.

  TRANSITION VALIDATION:
    - The prospect's belief in the mechanism (earned through the story)
      CARRIES OVER into their belief about their own situation
    - The emotional proof delivered by the story becomes PERSONAL proof
    - The desire activated by the protagonist's transformation becomes
      the prospect's OWN desire for transformation
    - The transition does not RESET the emotional state — it REDIRECTS it
```

```

COMPARE designed arc against vault exemplar:
  - Starting state comparison
  - Ending state comparison
  - Arc shape alignment
  - Peak moment placement (similar position in story?)
  - Number of emotional stages

  similarity_notes: How this arc resembles the vault pattern
  intentional_differences: Where and why this arc departs from vault
  risk_assessment: How risky are the departures

  FLAG: "Designed arc terminates at weaker emotional state than proven vault pattern.
         Consider intensifying the post-discovery emotional progression."
```

### Step 9: Compile Complete Emotional Arc

```
BUILD complete emotional_arc_design object:
  - Starting state (post-lead curiosity)
  - Ending state (believes mechanism is real, wants it)
  - All progression stages with emotional states, prospect thoughts, and carlton emotions
  - Peak moment design with trigger, position, and intensity
  - All transitions with triggers, types, and intensity changes
  - Arc shape and rationale
  - Transport verification checkpoints
  - Story-to-reader transition design
  - Vault comparison

FINAL VALIDATION:
  - Arc ENDS at "believes mechanism is real and wants it" (NOT "interested" or "curious")
  - Per Carlton: reader FEELS something (happiness, alarm, astonishment, or greed)
  - Every stage has a trigger connecting it to a specific beat and character action
  - The peak moment is identified and placed at 60-80% through the story
  - The "And here's what that means for YOU" transition is designed
  - The emotional arc aligns with the tension curve from the beat map
```

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Starting state is "post-lead curiosity" (not raw attention — the lead has already been read)
- [ ] Ending state is "believes mechanism is real and wants it" (NOT "curious," "interested," "intrigued," or "intellectually agrees")
- [ ] The ending state represents a STRONGER belief position than the lead's ending state
- [ ] Arc shape matches the classified story type (validated in Step 2)
- [ ] Every progression stage maps to a specific beat from the beat map (beat_id assigned)
- [ ] Every stage has a documented prospect internal monologue (not just an emotion label)
- [ ] Every stage has a protagonist emotional state (the prospect feels WITH the protagonist)
- [ ] Every transition has a specific trigger (no "and then they feel..." transitions)
- [ ] Peak moment identified and placed at 60-80% through the story
- [ ] Peak moment triggers the strongest Carlton emotion (happiness, alarm, astonishment, or greed)
- [ ] At least 3 transport verification checkpoints designed across the arc
- [ ] "And here's what that means for YOU" transition designed at the final stage
- [ ] Per Carlton: reader FEELS something by story's end (carlton_emotion documented)
- [ ] Per Carlton: "transporting" is planned — reader feels IN the story world
- [ ] Emotional states progress forward (no backtracking except intentional dips)
- [ ] Intentional dips are justified (serve contrast, empathy, or peak amplification)
- [ ] Emotional arc aligns with tension curve from beat map (peak emotion = peak tension)
- [ ] Vault emotional arc studied and compared (differences documented and justified)
- [ ] If shame_to_dignity arc: shame stage comes BEFORE hope stage (proper sequencing)
- [ ] No emotional stage repeats across consecutive stages (always forward motion)
- [ ] Total stages achievable within the story's estimated word count

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Curiosity Ending** | Arc ends at "curious to learn more" or "interested in the mechanism" | The story's job is to deliver EMOTIONAL PROOF; ending at curiosity means the story failed its core function — the prospect must BELIEVE, not just wonder |
| **Intellectual Agreement** | Arc ends at "intellectually understands the mechanism" without emotional conviction | Understanding is not believing. The prospect must FEEL the mechanism is real, not just comprehend the explanation |
| **Plateau Arc** | Prospect reaches "interested" at stage 2 and stays "interested" for 5 stages | No forward emotional motion; the story feels flat and the reader disengages; every beat must move the prospect forward |
| **Emotional Whiplash** | Arc jumps from "shame" to "greed" to "alarm" with no logical progression | Random emotional shifts feel manipulative; the prospect's brain rejects incoherent emotional journeys |
| **Peak at Opening** | The most emotionally intense moment is the first beat | Nothing that follows can match the opening intensity; the story deflates instead of building |
| **Missing Dip** | Shame-to-dignity arc has no rock bottom — goes straight from mild concern to triumph | The dip creates CONTRAST that makes triumph feel earned; without the valley, the peak is diminished |
| **Transport Breaking** | Story shifts to exposition, feature-listing, or data-dumping mid-arc | Any moment that reminds the prospect they are reading copy DESTROYS the transported state; proof must be woven into narrative, not bolted on |
| **No Segue** | Story ends at the protagonist's triumph without connecting to the reader's life | Per Carlton: "And here's what that means for YOU" — the story must complete the thought, or the prospect absorbs it without acting |
| **Proof Bolting** | Emotional arc is interrupted by a dense proof/evidence section that breaks immersion | Proof must emerge FROM the narrative (through verification beats, data cascades, expert validation), not be inserted as a separate section |
| **Generic Emotions** | Stages labeled "engaged" and "more engaged" and "very engaged" instead of specific emotional states | Emotional states must be SPECIFIC: "shame," "identification," "hope," "conviction" — not intensity levels of the same vague feeling |
| **Ignoring Protagonist** | Emotional arc designed without reference to protagonist's emotional journey | The prospect FEELS what the protagonist feels; the protagonist's emotional journey IS the prospect's emotional journey; you cannot design one without the other |
