# Layer 0.1 — Upstream Package Loader

## Purpose

Load and parse all required upstream skill packages (mechanism, structure, promise, root cause, proof inventory, lead, headline) into working memory for story engineering. Extract the specific fields needed for story construction: the mechanism name/type/uniqueness claim that the story must emotionally prove, the campaign thesis the story must support, the primary promise the story's payoff must deliver, the root cause's three-part structure that provides counter-intuitive story fuel, proof elements for natural weaving into narrative, the lead's discovery story tease status (what was teased vs. what must now be told), and the lead's open loops that the story must close or advance.

The story skill sits downstream of ALL other skills. Unlike the lead (which only teases), the story TELLS. This loader must capture not just raw data but the specific narrative obligations created by upstream packages — what the lead promised the reader would learn, what the mechanism requires the prospect to emotionally validate, and what the structure expects the story to accomplish within the campaign argument flow.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Load Mechanism Package

Extract for story use:

- `mechanism.name` — the named mechanism the story must make FEEL real (not just explained — emotionally validated through protagonist's experience)
- `mechanism.type` — determines story type suitability (e.g., supplement mechanisms favor discovery stories; trading systems favor proof/experiment stories)
- `mechanism.explanation_text` — reference for story's mechanism revelation beat (unlike the lead, the story CAN explain the mechanism through narrative)
- `mechanism.e5_mechanism_type` — E1-E5 classification affects how the mechanism is revealed in the story (E5 = brand new concept requires more setup; E1 = known concept requires differentiation)
- `mechanism.uniqueness_claim` — the "new and different" element the story must dramatize through the protagonist's discovery or experiment
- `mechanism.proof_elements` — credibility anchors to weave naturally into narrative (not bolt on)
- `mechanism.how_it_works` — detailed mechanism operation for the story's revelation/teaching moment

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- The mechanism name becomes the "secret" or "discovery" the protagonist encounters
- The uniqueness claim becomes the counter-intuitive twist in the story
- The explanation text becomes raw material for the "Yoda reveals secret" beat
- Proof elements become verification/studying beats in discovery stories OR data cascade fuel in proof stories

### Step 2: Load Structure Package

Extract for story placement and integration:

- `campaign_thesis.statement` — what the campaign argument will prove (story must emotionally support this thesis)
- `argument_strategy.type` — prosecution/revelation/education/demonstration (affects story tone and function)
  - Prosecution: story shows the DAMAGE of the old way (villain emphasis)
  - Revelation: story shows the DISCOVERY of the new way (Yoda emphasis)
  - Education: story shows the UNDERSTANDING of why it works (verification emphasis)
  - Demonstration: story shows the RESULTS of using the mechanism (transformation/payoff emphasis)
- `flow_architecture.story_placement` — WHERE in the campaign flow the story appears
- `flow_architecture.simple_segue` — what the story must transition INTO (argument, proof section, offer)
- `downstream_handoffs.for_story_writing` — specific data the structure skill prepared for the story
  - `story_function` — what the story must accomplish for the argument
  - `story_placement_in_flow` — before/after/within argument chunks
  - `emotional_entry_point` — emotional state of the prospect when they reach the story
  - `emotional_exit_target` — emotional state the story must deliver the prospect to

### Step 3: Load Promise Package

Extract for story payoff engineering:

- `primary_promise.statement` — the BIG promise the story's transformation/payoff must deliver emotionally
- `primary_promise.emotional_frame` — the emotional context that shapes the protagonist's journey
- `supporting_promises` — additional benefits the story can dramatize through secondary results
- `promise_ceiling` — maximum defensible promise level (story results must not exceed this)
- `primary_promise.specificity_level` — how specific the story's results should be

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- The primary promise becomes the RESULT the protagonist achieves (transformation/payoff beat)
- The emotional frame shapes the protagonist's starting emotional state (what they felt BEFORE)
- Supporting promises become secondary results mentioned in the payoff section
- Promise ceiling prevents story results from being unbelievable or over-claimed

### Step 4: Load Root Cause Package

Extract for counter-intuitive story fuel:

- `root_cause.expression` — the root cause statement the story must reveal or dramatize
- `root_cause.three_part_structure.what_they_think` — conventional wisdom the protagonist initially believes (setup/context beat)
- `root_cause.three_part_structure.what_it_really_is` — the real cause the protagonist discovers (revelation beat)
- `root_cause.three_part_structure.why_it_matters` — the significance that drives the protagonist to act (motivation beat)
- `root_cause.villain_profile` — for antagonist/villain character design in the story (if villain-based argument)
- `root_cause.counter_intuitive_element` — the surprising twist that makes the story interesting (the "I never would have guessed" moment)

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- The three-part structure maps directly to the story's emotional arc: protagonist believes the conventional wisdom (setup) -> discovers the real cause (revelation) -> understands why it matters (payoff)
- The counter-intuitive element is the ENGINE of story interest — it's what makes the reader lean in
- The villain profile becomes the antagonist the protagonist overcomes or the system they escape
- Root cause addressed is typically the CLIMAX of a discovery story

### Step 5: Load Proof Inventory

Extract for natural narrative proof integration:

- `summary.overall_strength` — determines how much proof can be woven into the story (high proof = proof story viable; low proof = discovery story more appropriate)
- `rankings.knockout_proof` — potential centerpiece proof element for the story
- `proof_by_type.authority_proofs` — expert/credential elements for the "Yoda" character
- `proof_by_type.social_proofs` — testimonial/beta-tester elements for proof stories
- `proof_by_type.statistical_proofs` — data cascade elements for proof/experiment stories
- `proof_by_type.demonstration_proofs` — experiment/case study elements for proof stories
- `proof_by_type.logical_proofs` — scientific/research elements for verification beats
- `proof_by_type.specificity_proofs` — specific details that add believability to the narrative

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- Authority proofs shape the Yoda/expert character's credibility
- Social proofs become the "ordinary people just like you" element in proof stories
- Statistical proofs become the data cascade in Makepeace-style proof stories
- Demonstration proofs become the experiment/beta-test results
- Specificity proofs add sensory detail and believability to the narrative

### Step 6: Load Lead Package

Extract for story-lead integration (CRITICAL — this is the unique upstream dependency for the story skill):

- `lead_components.discovery_story_tease` — what the lead teased about the discovery story (the story must DELIVER on this tease without repeating it)
- `lead_components.open_loops` — all open loops created by the lead that the story is responsible for closing
  - `tangible_teases` — specific teases the reader is waiting to have resolved
  - `discovery_teases` — hints about a discovery the story must now tell
  - `mechanism_teases` — hints about how the mechanism works that the story can reveal
  - `counter_intuitive_teases` — counter-intuitive claims the story must now explain
- `lead_components.protagonist_introduced` — whether the lead already introduced a protagonist (story must continue, not restart)
- `lead_components.emotional_state_at_handoff` — the emotional state the reader is in when they reach the story
- `lead_components.credibility_established` — what credibility was already established (story should not repeat)
- `downstream_handoffs.for_body_copy.discovery_story_teased` — boolean flag indicating whether a discovery story was teased
- `downstream_handoffs.for_body_copy.open_loops_to_close` — specific loops the body (and story within it) must address
- `lead_type` — the lead type used (affects story integration approach)

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- The Georgi framework is absolute here: the lead TEASES, the story TELLS
- If `discovery_story_teased` = true, the story MUST deliver a discovery story (or the reader feels cheated)
- Open loops from the lead create OBLIGATIONS the story must fulfill
- The protagonist introduction status determines whether the story introduces a new character or continues an existing one
- The emotional state at handoff is the STARTING STATE of the story's emotional arc
- Credibility already established means the story should not repeat those elements (no redundancy)

### Step 7: Load Headline

Extract for emotional continuity:

- `main_headline` — the headline's emotional promise that flows through lead into story
- `subheadline` (if present) — additional context
- Headline's implied promise — what the reader expects to receive
- Headline's mechanism reference — what mechanism was named or hinted at

### Step 8: Load Story Elements Research (from Deep Research)

Extract factual story raw material from the 2.7-A-story-elements-researcher output:

- `discovery_narrative` — [FACTUAL] discovery/origin elements from client brief + [RESEARCHED] elements found through research
  - `discoverer` — name, title, credentials, backstory (if available)
  - `location` — place, geographic detail, institution
  - `circumstances` — type (accidental/deliberate/personal_desperation/inherited_knowledge), description, trigger_event, aha_moment
  - `artifacts` — physical objects, foreign elements, sensory details
- `founder_journey` — [FACTUAL] founder/protagonist journey elements
  - `personal_struggle` — problem faced, emotional impact, who else affected
  - `vulnerability_moment` — lowest moment, what almost stopped them, specific incident
  - `turning_point` — what changed, first success, reaction
  - `permissions` — can share publicly, names to change, details to omit
- `authority_figures` — [FACTUAL] expert/Yoda figures with credentials and endorsements
- `testimonial_stories` — [FACTUAL] or [FROM_VOC] testimonials with full story arcs (before → transformation → after)
- `proof_data` — quantified results, study references, timeframes
- `warning_threat_data` — statistics and expert warnings (if warning story type)
- `competitive_story_analysis` — what story types competitors use, underutilized angles
- `story_type_recommendation` — recommended story type based on available material with material assessment scores
- `creative_license_summary` — count of [FACTUAL], [ENHANCE], [CREATIVE], [MISSING] elements
- `authenticity_rating` — HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW based on factual grounding available

**Story-Specific Extraction Notes:**
- Elements tagged [FACTUAL] are ANCHORS — story MUST preserve these exactly
- Elements tagged [ENHANCE] can be improved narratively while maintaining factual core
- Elements tagged [CREATIVE] require flagging in output — reasonable fill allowed but marked for review
- Elements tagged [MISSING] CANNOT be fabricated — if critical for story type, recommend different story type
- The `story_type_recommendation` provides initial classification guidance but does NOT override mechanism/proof-based analysis
- The `authenticity_rating` indicates how much creative license will be needed

**Critical Principle:** The story skill does NOT fabricate discovery narratives, founders, or proof data. This loader provides the FACTUAL raw material that makes stories honest and grounded.

### Step 8B: Load Campaign Brief

Extract strategic anchors for story alignment:
- `campaign_thesis` — the central argument (story must emotionally support this)
- `tone_direction` — clinical/emotional/urgent/conversational (shapes story voice)
- `avatar_summary` — who we're writing for (shapes protagonist relatability)
- `market_sophistication` — Schwartz stage (affects how overtly the story can "sell")
- `proof_approach` — how proof should be deployed (affects proof weaving in narrative)
- `promise_ceiling` — maximum defensible promise (story payoff must not exceed)

**Why this matters for story:** Without the brief's strategic anchors, the story may drift from campaign positioning. A Stage 5 audience requires a radically different story tone than Stage 2.

### Step 8C: Load Lead Assembled Prose (CRITICAL FOR CONTINUITY)

Load the ACTUAL WRITTEN PROSE from the lead section:
- `lead_full_text` — the complete lead as written (not just metadata)
- This enables the story to:
  - Continue the exact voice and rhythm established in the lead
  - Avoid repeating phrases, metaphors, or proof elements already used
  - Pick up narrative threads at the exact point the lead left off
  - Maintain consistent character/protagonist voice

**Why this matters:** Without reading the actual lead prose, the story starts "fresh" — as if it's a separate document. This is a primary cause of the "stitched together" feeling in assembled drafts. The story must READ the lead to CONTINUE the lead.

### Step 8D: Load Context Reservoir (if available)

If a context reservoir exists for this project, load for enriched story generation:
- `voc_quotes` — authentic audience language for dialogue and internal monologue
- `fssit_intelligence` — unspoken feelings to crystallize through story
- `expression_anchoring` — which phrases resonate (use in key revelation moments)
- `counter_intuitive_core` — the surprising truth the story must dramatize
- `arena_intelligence` — why choices were made (informs emphasis in narrative)

### Step 9: Build Story Context Object

Merge all extracted data into a single `story_context` object with the following structure:

1. **Mechanism Layer** — what the story must emotionally prove
2. **Structure Layer** — where the story fits and what it must accomplish
3. **Promise Layer** — what result the story must deliver
4. **Root Cause Layer** — what counter-intuitive truth the story reveals
5. **Proof Layer** — what evidence can be woven into the narrative
6. **Lead Integration Layer** — what was teased vs. what must be told, open loops to close
7. **Headline Layer** — emotional continuity thread
8. **Story Elements Layer** — [FACTUAL] raw material from Deep Research for authentic story construction
9. **Narrative Obligations** — synthesized list of everything the story MUST accomplish

### Step 10: Derive Narrative Obligation Map

From the merged context, build a `narrative_obligations` object that explicitly states:

- What the lead teased that the story must now tell
- What open loops the story must close
- What emotional state the reader arrives in
- What emotional state the story must deliver them to
- What mechanism the story must make feel real
- What proof elements are available for natural weaving
- What promise the story's payoff must deliver
- What counter-intuitive twist drives the story's interest
- Whether the protagonist was already introduced or needs to be created
- What [FACTUAL] story elements must be preserved exactly
- What [ENHANCE] elements can be improved narratively
- What [CREATIVE] elements require flagging for review
- What story type the available material best supports (cross-referenced with mechanism/proof analysis)

## Error Handling

### Missing Required Package — Mechanism
```
FATAL: Cannot load story context without mechanism-package.json.
The story must emotionally prove the mechanism works.
Run 04-mechanism first. Story engineering cannot proceed.
```

### Missing Required Package — Structure
```
FATAL: Cannot load story context without structure-package.json.
The story must integrate with the campaign argument flow.
Run 08-structure first. Story engineering cannot proceed.
```

### Missing Required Package — Lead
```
FATAL: Cannot load story context without lead-package.json.
The story expands what the lead teased. Without knowing what was teased,
the story cannot fulfill its narrative obligations.
Run 11-lead first. Story engineering cannot proceed.
```

### Missing Optional Package — Warning Template
```
WARNING: [package_name] not loaded. Story engineering can proceed
but [specific_impact] will be degraded. Recommend running [skill_number]
before finalizing story-package.json.
```

### Missing Story Elements Research — Warning
```
WARNING: story_elements_research.md not loaded from Deep Research output.
Story engineering can proceed but:
- Factual grounding will be LIMITED — story may require more creative license
- Authenticity rating cannot be calculated — human review strongly recommended
- Story type recommendation from research not available — relying solely on mechanism/proof analysis
- [FACTUAL] elements not available — all story elements treated as [CREATIVE] requiring review

IMPACT: Stories generated without story elements research will be flagged as
AUTHENTICITY_RATING: UNKNOWN. Client review mandatory before publication.

RECOMMENDATION: Run Deep Research with Section 8 (Story Elements) completed,
or provide story_elements_research.md manually before finalizing story-package.json.
```

## Handoff

Pass `story_context` to:
- **0.4-input-validator.md** (for threshold validation of all loaded data)
- Available to all Layer 1+ microskills via shared context
- **1.1-story-type-classifier.md** (primary consumer — uses mechanism type, proof strength, lead tease status, story_elements.story_type_recommendation, and niche to classify optimal story type; cross-references story elements authenticity rating)
- **1.2-story-beat-mapper.md** (uses narrative obligations to map required beats; incorporates [FACTUAL] elements as beat anchors)
- **1.3-character-architect.md** (uses protagonist status, Yoda/authority proof elements; pulls [FACTUAL] discoverer/founder details from story_elements.discovery_narrative and story_elements.founder_journey)
- **1.4-emotional-arc-designer.md** (uses starting/target emotional states from lead handoff; maps vulnerability_moment from story_elements.founder_journey if available)
- **2.1-setup-context-builder.md** (uses root cause what_they_think for protagonist's initial belief; incorporates [FACTUAL] location/circumstance details from story_elements)
- **2.3-mechanism-revelation-constructor.md** (uses mechanism explanation_text and root cause revelation; weaves in [FACTUAL] authority_figures and discovery circumstances)
- **2.4-transformation-payoff-builder.md** (uses promise and proof for payoff construction; incorporates [FACTUAL] testimonial_stories and proof_data from story_elements)

**Story Elements Integration Notes:**
- [FACTUAL] elements are HARD CONSTRAINTS — microskills MUST use them exactly as provided
- [ENHANCE] elements are SOFT CONSTRAINTS — microskills can improve narrative quality while preserving factual core
- [CREATIVE] elements generate FLAGS in output — creative license taken, marked for human review
- [MISSING] elements CANNOT be fabricated — if a beat requires a missing element, flag for story type reconsideration or client input
