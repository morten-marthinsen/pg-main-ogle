# 1.1 — Reframe Selector

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U3 — Downsell Writer
**Microskill:** 1.1 — Reframe Selector
**Layer:** 1
**Model:** sonnet
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (U0 reframe_type recommendation, U2 reframe_suggestions, declined upsell context, downsell product), `layer-0/0.2-specimen-calibration.md` (reframe patterns)
**Output:** `layer-1/1.1-reframe-selection.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Select the most effective reframe type from 4 options (Core Extract, Payment Plan, Lite Version, Different Format) based on upstream recommendations, product analysis, price differential, and likely buyer objection. The reframe type determines the entire creative direction of the downsell. "Same thing cheaper" is NOT a reframe and will FAIL this microskill.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Load upstream reframe inputs** from `0.1-input-context.md`:
   - U0 `reframe_type` recommendation (if provided — Mode A only)
   - U2 `reframe_suggestions` (if provided — specific angle suggestions from the upsell writer)
   - Declined upsell: product name, price, angle, bonuses, what was included
   - Downsell: product name, description, price
   - FE: product name, mechanism, promise

2. **Analyze the 4 reframe types against this specific product situation:**

   **Core Extract** — "Just the essential component"
   - Viability check: Can the upsell product be broken into components? Is there one piece that stands alone as the most valuable?
   - Best when: upsell has multiple modules/components, one is clearly the highest-impact piece, buyer objection was likely scope/overwhelm
   - Example: "Can't commit to the full 30-day program? Start with just the Quick-Start Guide — the single most impactful piece."
   - Score viability 1-10 for this product.

   **Payment Plan** — "Same product, spread payments"
   - Viability check: Is the product unchanged? Is price genuinely the barrier? Can payments be structured sensibly?
   - Best when: product-market fit is clearly right (buyer wants it), price is the only objection, product cannot be meaningfully split
   - Caution: This is the LEAST creative reframe. Use only when product fit is obvious and the other 3 do not apply. It is the closest to "same thing cheaper" — the reframe is in the commitment structure, not the product.
   - Score viability 1-10 for this product.

   **Lite Version** — "Reduced scope, same mechanism"
   - Viability check: Can the product scope be genuinely reduced while preserving the core mechanism? Is there a meaningful "starter" or "essentials" version?
   - Best when: scope/time commitment was the objection, product has natural tier points (7-day vs 30-day, basic vs advanced)
   - Example: "The 7-day version instead of the 30-day program."
   - Score viability 1-10 for this product.

   **Different Format** — "Same content, different delivery"
   - Viability check: Can the content be delivered differently? Does a format change genuinely reduce friction?
   - Best when: format/time/learning style was the objection, content is format-agnostic
   - Example: "Audio versions instead of video + audio," "PDF guide instead of video course."
   - Score viability 1-10 for this product.

3. **Weigh selection factors** (in priority order):
   - **(a) U0 reframe_type recommendation** (highest weight if provided — U0 analyzed the entire offer stack strategically)
   - **(b) U2 reframe_suggestions** (second weight — U2 writer knows what angle the upsell used and can suggest a complementary reframe)
   - **(c) Product analysis** (can the product actually be split/scoped/reformatted?)
   - **(d) Price differential** (how far below upsell price is the downsell? Large gaps suggest Core Extract or Lite Version; small gaps suggest Payment Plan)
   - **(e) Likely buyer objection** (inferred from product type, price level, and position — was it price? scope? format? overwhelm?)

4. **Select the winning reframe type.** Choose the type with the highest viability score, weighted by upstream recommendations.

5. **CRITICAL VALIDATION — "Same thing cheaper" check:**
   - After selection, explicitly answer: "Is this reframe genuinely a different angle, or is it just the upsell at a lower price?"
   - Core Extract: PASSES if a specific component is isolated. FAILS if it is just "fewer bonuses."
   - Payment Plan: PASSES only if the product is unchanged and price is genuinely the sole barrier. Note: this is borderline by design.
   - Lite Version: PASSES if scope is genuinely reduced with a different value proposition. FAILS if it is just "less of the same."
   - Different Format: PASSES if format change is genuine. FAILS if it is cosmetic.
   - If the check fails, re-evaluate. If no reframe type genuinely works, escalate to U0 — the downsell product may need to be reconceived.

6. **Write the reframe angle statement.** One sentence that captures the specific reframe angle. This becomes the creative brief for Layer 2 generation.
   - Example: "Instead of the full 30-day Advanced Metabolic Reset, offer just the 7-Day Quick-Start Protocol — the foundation module that delivers the first measurable results."

7. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

- "Same thing cheaper" is NOT a reframe. It is the single most common downsell failure (Pattern 4 in UPSELL-ENGINE-CLAUDE.md). Every selection must pass the explicit validation check in step 5.
- Payment Plan is the weakest reframe type by design. It should only be selected when: (a) the product genuinely cannot be split, scoped, or reformatted, AND (b) price is clearly the sole barrier, AND (c) U0 recommended it. If Payment Plan is selected without U0 recommendation, provide strong rationale.
- U0 recommendation carries the highest weight because U0 analyzed the entire offer stack. Only diverge from U0 with explicit justification (e.g., "U0 recommended Core Extract but the product has no separable components").
- The reframe angle statement must be specific enough to guide Layer 2 generation. "Offer a smaller version" is insufficient. "Offer just the 7-Day Quick-Start Protocol — the foundation module that delivers the first measurable results" is sufficient.
- If no reframe type genuinely works for this product, do NOT force a selection. Escalate to U0 for product reconception. A forced reframe produces worse output than acknowledging the gap.
- Reframe changes the OFFER angle, not the mechanism or promise. The mechanism and root cause remain identical to the front-end. Only the product scope, format, or commitment structure changes.

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| "Same thing cheaper" selected | Validation check in step 5 fails — no genuine angle change | Re-evaluate all 4 types. If none work, escalate to U0 for downsell product reconception. |
| All viability scores below 4 | No reframe type fits the product well | Escalate to U0. The downsell product may need to be reconceived or the upsell angle needs adjustment. |
| Diverging from U0 without rationale | Selected type differs from U0 recommendation but no explanation given | Add explicit rationale for divergence. U0 recommendation is the default unless overridden with product-specific evidence. |
| Reframe angle statement too vague | Statement does not specify what is being offered differently | Rewrite with specifics: product name, component name, format type, or scope boundary. |
| Payment Plan selected as path of least resistance | Selected because "it's the easiest" rather than "it's the best fit" | Re-score all 4 types. Payment Plan requires affirmative justification, not default selection. |
