# 2.1 — ARO Draft Generator

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U3 — Downsell Writer
**Microskill:** 2.1 — ARO Draft Generator
**Layer:** 2
**Model:** opus
**Input:** `layer-1/1.1-reframe-selection.md` (reframe type, angle statement), `layer-1/1.2-congruence-map.md` (exact mechanism name, root cause, promise, placement directives), `layer-0/0.2-specimen-calibration.md` (ARO proportions, tone patterns, Acknowledge templates), `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (product details, pricing, position, mode flag)
**Output:** `layer-2/2.1-aro-draft.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Generate the full 300-1000 word downsell page following the ARO structure (Acknowledge, Reframe, Offer + Choice). This is the core creative generation step. The buyer DECLINED the upsell — they said no. The downsell acknowledges that no, reframes the offer from a genuinely different angle, and presents an alternative with a clean binary choice. This is NOT a second sales pitch, NOT a CAIRO upsell, and NOT a PAS sales page.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Read all Layer 1 outputs completely** before writing a single word:
   - `1.1-reframe-selection.md` — the selected reframe type and angle statement (this is your creative brief)
   - `1.2-congruence-map.md` — the exact strings that MUST appear in the draft (mechanism name, root cause language, promise maintenance strategy, placement directives)

2. **Internalize the 5 Laws of Upsell** as they apply to downsell:
   - Law 1: This is NOT a sales page. It is a post-DECLINE page. Even more sensitive than post-purchase.
   - Law 2: Congruence is everything. The mechanism, root cause, and promise thread through unchanged.
   - Law 3: Speed kills. 300-1000 words. Buyer patience is at its LOWEST. Every word must earn its place.
   - Law 4: Yes-or-No architecture. This is the SIMPLEST decision in the funnel. Binary choice, zero friction.
   - Law 5: Descending commitment, ascending value. Less commitment than the upsell, more value per dollar.

3. **Determine word count target based on position:**
   - Downsell 1 (after Upsell 1 decline): 500-1000 words. Full ARO with developed Reframe section.
   - Downsell 2 (after Upsell 2 decline): 300-600 words. Compressed ARO. Tighter Reframe. Buyer is at maximum fatigue.
   - Position is in `0.1-input-context.md`. If position is unclear, default to Downsell 1.

4. **Generate the ACKNOWLEDGE section:**
   - **Target:** Downsell 1: 50-100 words. Downsell 2: 50-75 words.
   - **Open with validation.** The very first sentence must respect their "no." Not challenge it, not question it, not guilt-trip it. Validate it.
   - **Template patterns** (adapt, do not copy verbatim):
     - "I completely understand. The [Upsell Product] isn't for everyone, and there's absolutely nothing wrong with starting with just the [FE Product]."
     - "That's a perfectly reasonable decision. The [FE Product] is a powerful [mechanism type] on its own."
     - "No problem at all. [FE Product] with [Mechanism Name] is going to deliver real results on its own."
   - **Name what they declined** — reference the upsell product by name, briefly. Show you heard them.
   - **Affirm the FE purchase** — remind them they already have something valuable. Name the mechanism.
   - **Create a soft bridge** — end with a natural transition to the Reframe, not a hard pivot. "But before you head to your download page, I want to make sure you know about one other option..."
   - **FORBIDDEN in Acknowledge:**
     - "Wait!" / "Hold on!" / "Before you go!" (pressure)
     - "You're missing out on..." / "Most people regret..." (guilt)
     - "Are you sure?" / "Last chance" / "One more thing" (FOMO)
     - Any sentence that makes the reader feel bad about their decision

5. **Generate the REFRAME section:**
   - **Target:** Downsell 1: 250-500 words. Downsell 2: 150-300 words.
   - **This is the creative core.** The reframe is the hardest part of downsell copy. It must present a genuinely different angle — not just the upsell at a lower price.
   - **Execute the selected reframe type** per the angle statement from `1.1-reframe-selection.md`:

     **If Core Extract:**
     - Isolate the single most valuable component of the upsell product
     - Position it as the "essential starting point" — not the "lesser version"
     - Frame: "The [Upsell] includes [X components]. But if you could only have ONE, it would be [this one]. Here's why..."
     - The component must be named specifically

     **If Payment Plan:**
     - Same product, restructured commitment
     - Frame: "Same [Upsell Product], but instead of $[upsell price] today, spread it across [X] payments of $[downsell payment]."
     - This is the shortest Reframe because the product doesn't change — only the commitment structure

     **If Lite Version:**
     - Reduced scope, same mechanism at work
     - Frame: "The [Upsell] is the full [scope]. But many of our [audience] get tremendous results starting with just the [lite scope]."
     - Position the lite version as "faster to start" or "easier to implement," not "less effective"

     **If Different Format:**
     - Same content, different delivery vehicle
     - Frame: "The [Upsell] comes as [format A]. But if you prefer [format B], we have you covered."
     - Position the format as a legitimate preference, not a downgrade

   - **Include maximum 1 proof element.** Downsell gets LESS proof than upsell (which allows 2). Choose the single strongest: one testimonial quote, one stat, one authority claim. Weave it in naturally — no proof cascade.
   - **Reference the mechanism by EXACT name** (from congruence map). At least 1 appearance in Reframe.
   - **Reference the root cause language VERBATIM** (from congruence map). At least 1 appearance.
   - **End with a clear transition to the Offer.** The reader should feel they understand what they are being offered and why it is different from what they declined.

6. **Generate the OFFER + CHOICE section:**
   - **Target:** Downsell 1: 100-200 words. Downsell 2: 75-150 words.
   - **Product name and one-sentence description** — clear, simple, specific
   - **Price presentation placeholder** — leave a marked placeholder for 2.2 integration: `[PRICE PRESENTATION — from 2.2]`
   - **Guarantee** — mirror the FE guarantee. "Same [X]-day money-back guarantee applies."
   - **Binary CTA:**
     - YES: "Yes, I'll Take [Downsell Product Name] — Just $[Price]"
     - NO: "No thanks, I'll continue with just my original purchase"
   - **The NO option must be CLEAN.** Zero guilt. Zero manipulation. Zero shame. The NO option is the cleanest in the entire funnel. Test: would you be comfortable clicking this NO button yourself? If not, rewrite.
   - **FORBIDDEN in NO option:**
     - "No, I don't want to [desirable outcome]" — guilt
     - "No, I'm fine being [undesirable state]" — shame
     - "No, I'll pay full price later" — pressure
     - Any framing that makes declining feel like a mistake

7. **Apply structure-level quality checks before writing output:**
   - Total word count within 300-1000? (position-specific range)
   - All 3 ARO sections present with distinct headers?
   - Mechanism name appears in at least 2 sections?
   - Root cause language appears verbatim at least once?
   - Acknowledge section has ZERO guilt, ZERO pressure?
   - Reframe is genuinely different from upsell angle (not "same thing cheaper")?
   - Maximum 1 proof element in entire draft?
   - Binary CTA present with clean NO option?
   - If any check fails, revise BEFORE writing output.

8. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

1. **ARO structure is MANDATORY.** All 3 sections (Acknowledge, Reframe, Offer + Choice) must appear in order with distinct headers. Do not merge sections. Do not skip sections. Do not reorder.
2. **NOT CAIRO. NOT PAS.** This is a post-DECLINE page, not a post-purchase page (CAIRO) or a pre-purchase page (PAS). If you notice yourself writing a Congratulate section, STOP — the buyer said NO, not YES. If you notice problem agitation, STOP — the buyer already bought the FE.
3. **The buyer said NO. Respect that.** Every word in the Acknowledge section must honor their decision. The Reframe presents an alternative — it does not re-argue for the upsell.
4. **Mechanism name must appear by EXACT name** from the congruence map. Minimum 2 appearances. "This system" / "this protocol" are not acceptable substitutes.
5. **Root cause language must appear VERBATIM** from the congruence map. Minimum 1 appearance.
6. **Maximum 1 proof element.** Even less than the upsell (which allows 2). The downsell is about simplicity and alternative angle, not proof.
7. **Word count 300-1000 is a HARD GATE.** Under 300 = incomplete ARO. Over 1000 = violating Law 3 (speed kills). If outside range, fix before writing output.
8. **Position determines length.** Downsell 1 = 500-1000w. Downsell 2 = 300-600w. Do not write a 900-word Downsell 2 or a 350-word Downsell 1.
9. **Tone is warm, understanding, zero-pressure.** Not conviction, not urgency, not persuasion. Scan for: "limited time," "act now," "don't miss out," "you can't afford to," "imagine your problem," "are you tired of." Flag and rewrite any instances.
10. **This is the reference draft for Arena, not the final output.** Arena competitors will generate their own ARO pages. This draft provides the structural foundation and content direction, not a template to be varied.
11. **Never invent products.** If the downsell product is not specified in the handoff, ask the human. Do not create a product that does not exist.
12. **Price presentation is a placeholder.** The 2.2 microskill will build the detailed price presentation. Leave a clear `[PRICE PRESENTATION — from 2.2]` marker. Do not build the full price presentation here.

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| CAIRO structure generated instead of ARO | Scan for Congratulate/Amplify/Intrigue/Reason sections | Delete draft. Re-read U3 AGENT.md. Regenerate with ARO: Acknowledge → Reframe → Offer. The buyer said NO, not YES. |
| PAS structure generated | Scan for problem statement, agitation language, solution reveal | Delete draft. The buyer already bought the FE. They do not need problem awareness. Regenerate with ARO. |
| Guilt/pressure in Acknowledge | Scan for: "missing out," "regret," "last chance," "before you go," "Wait!" | Rewrite Acknowledge section completely. Zero means zero. |
| "Same thing cheaper" reframe | Reframe section offers the upsell at a lower price without a different angle | Return to 1.1 for reframe type re-evaluation. The reframe angle statement was not strong enough. |
| Mechanism name missing or paraphrased | Exact string search from congruence map finds 0-1 matches | Insert mechanism name at placement directives from congruence map. Must appear in 2+ sections. |
| Root cause language absent or paraphrased | Verbatim string search fails | Insert verbatim root cause language at placement directive from congruence map. |
| Word count outside position-specific range | Word count check fails | Trim Reframe (usually the longest section) if over. Expand Reframe if under. Do not pad with filler or trim Acknowledge (sets tone). |
| Over-proving (2+ proof elements) | Count proof elements exceeds 1 | Remove weakest proof element. Keep the single strongest. |
| Manipulative NO option | NO text uses guilt, shame, or "you don't want" framing | Replace with: "No thanks, I'll continue with just my original purchase." |
