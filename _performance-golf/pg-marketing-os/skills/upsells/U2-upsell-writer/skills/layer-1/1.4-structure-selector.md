# 1.4 — Structure Selector

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U2 — 1-Click Upsell Writer
**Layer:** 1
**Microskill:** 1.4 — Structure Selector
**Purpose:** Integrate all Layer 1 analysis into a complete generation blueprint — selecting CAIRO variant, word allocation, opening pattern, transition mechanics, and price presentation template for Layer 2 draft generation.

---

## INPUT

- `layer-1/1.1-congruence-map.md` (congruence bridge, mechanism placement map, extension logic)
- `layer-1/1.2-position-analysis.md` (position, psychological state, CAIRO proportions, opening pattern)
- `layer-1/1.3-proof-inventory.md` (1-2 selected proof elements with placement)
- `layer-0/0.2-specimen-calibration.md` (specimen-derived proportion targets, tone patterns, price patterns)
- `layer-0/0.3-format-classification.md` (format, word count target, format-specific requirements)

## PROCESS

1. **Select CAIRO variant.** Combine position analysis (1.2) with specimen calibration (0.2) to select the generation variant:
   - **Standard Upsell 1 (575-1250w):** Default for first upsell position with standard product complexity. Balanced CAIRO proportions. Congratulate opening.
   - **Long Upsell 1 (825-1600w):** For first upsell position with complex product (multiple components, unfamiliar product type, high price >$97). Expanded Reason section to build logical case. Expanded Offer section for detailed value stack.
   - **Compressed Upsell 2 (400-825w):** For second upsell position. Shortened proportions across all sections. Faster to offer. Warning/confession or insider reveal opening.
   - Cross-reference: does the format classifier's word count target align with this variant's range? If not, adjust variant selection to match the word count target.

2. **Set final word count allocation per CAIRO section.** Take the selected variant's proportion ranges, adjust to hit the format classifier's word count target, and produce a specific allocation:

   Example for Standard Upsell 1, 900w target:
   | Section | Words | % |
   |---------|-------|---|
   | C | 75w | 8% |
   | A | 225w | 25% |
   | I | 100w | 11% |
   | R | 275w | 31% |
   | O | 225w | 25% |
   | **Total** | **900w** | **100%** |

   Ensure: C is the smallest section (8-12%), A and R are the largest (25-35% each), I is a bridge (8-15%), O is substantial but not dominant (20-30%).

3. **Confirm opening pattern selection** from 1.2 and refine:
   - **Standard congratulate** → draft the opening beat: "Congratulations — you just [specific action verb] [specific product/mechanism name]..."
   - **Warning/confession** → draft the opening beat: "Before you go to your confirmation page, I need to be honest with you about something..."
   - **Insider reveal** → draft the opening beat: "You've just accessed something that less than [X]% of our [audience] ever see..."
   - The opening beat is a GUIDE for Layer 2, not final copy. It sets the tone and approach.

4. **Plan transition mechanics between CAIRO sections.** Each transition must feel seamless, not segmented:
   - **C → A (loop-close transition):** Congratulate opens a loop ("but there's one thing that could make this work even better..."). Amplify closes it by painting the picture of what the FE product delivers. The loop connects purchase validation to benefit visualization.
   - **A → I (intrigue bridge):** Amplify builds the emotional high of future-paced benefits. Intrigue pivots with "But here's what we've noticed about the people who get these results FASTEST..." or "And there's one additional element our most successful [audience] have in common..." The bridge introduces the upsell concept without naming it yet.
   - **I → R (story pivot):** Intrigue creates the curiosity. Reason satisfies it with a story. "Here's why we created this..." or "When we first developed [mechanism name], we noticed something unexpected..." The pivot moves from curiosity to explanation.
   - **R → O (offer turn):** Reason explains why the upsell exists. Offer makes the ask. "So we packaged this into [upsell product name]..." or "That's why I want to offer you..." The turn moves from story to deal with a clean, logical handoff.

5. **Select price presentation template** based on specimen patterns and product context:
   - **Value anchor:** "Worth $[higher amount]" — always present. Source: retail price, component values, or competitor pricing.
   - **Per-day breakdown:** "$[price] / [days] = less than $[daily amount] a day" — always present. Use 30-day default unless product has a different natural usage period.
   - **Comparison anchor:** "Less than [familiar daily expense]" — recommended. Match to audience: golfers = "a single sleeve of Pro V1s," health = "a cup of specialty coffee," finance = "a single stock trade commission."
   - **Payment plan option:** If upsell price > $97, recommend a payment plan variant: "Two easy payments of $[amount]." Specimen data: payment plans outperformed flat pricing by $38/order.
   - Select from templates: flat price, payment plan, or both (with flat as primary and payment plan as alternative).

6. **Integrate proof placement.** From 1.3, map the 1-2 proof elements into the word allocation:
   - Proof in Amplify → that section may run toward the upper end of its word range
   - Proof in Reason → that section absorbs the proof naturally within its story
   - Proof in Intrigue → the proof creates the intrigue itself ("we noticed something in our data from 14,000 users...")
   - Do NOT add word count for proof. Proof is woven INTO the section's existing allocation.

7. **Compile the generation blueprint.** This is the single document Layer 2 uses to generate the full CAIRO draft. It contains everything needed — structure, proportions, tone, transitions, proof placement, price template, opening pattern, and congruence constraints.

8. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

1. This microskill runs LAST in Layer 1. It depends on outputs from 1.1, 1.2, and 1.3. Do not execute until all three are complete.
2. The generation blueprint must be SELF-CONTAINED. Layer 2 should be able to generate a complete CAIRO draft from this file alone (plus persona voices). If the blueprint requires reading additional Layer 1 files to be understood, it is incomplete.
3. Word count allocations must sum to a total within the format classifier's target range AND within the 500-2000 hard boundaries.
4. Transition mechanics must be SPECIFIC, not generic. "Smooth transition from A to I" is not a transition mechanic. "Amplify builds to peak benefit, then pivots with 'But here's what our most successful users have in common...'" is a transition mechanic.
5. Price presentation template must include all 4 mandatory elements: value anchor, actual price, per-day breakdown, comparison anchor. Missing any one = incomplete blueprint.
6. Proof integration must NOT increase section word counts beyond the allocated range. Proof is woven into existing allocation, not added on top.
7. The opening beat is a GUIDE, not final copy. It sets direction for Layer 2. Layer 2 (and Arena competitors) may write different opening language while maintaining the selected pattern.
