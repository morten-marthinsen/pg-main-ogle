# 2.3 — Price Presentation Builder

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U2 — 1-Click Upsell Writer
**Layer:** 2
**Microskill:** 2.3 — Price Presentation Builder
**Purpose:** Build the complete price presentation block including value anchor, actual price, per-day breakdown, comparison anchor, guarantee, binary CTA, and 5-7 price variant suggestions for split testing.

---

## INPUT

- `layer-1/1.4-structure-selection.md` (price presentation template, comparison anchor, payment plan recommendation)
- `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (upsell price, FE price, guarantee terms, audience)
- `layer-0/0.2-specimen-calibration.md` (price presentation patterns from specimens)
- `layer-2/2.2-bonus-stack.md` (total bonus value for value stack math — if available)

## PROCESS

1. **Establish the 4 mandatory price presentation elements:**

   **(a) Value Anchor — "Worth $[higher amount]":**
   - Source the anchor from one of: retail price (if upsell product has a standalone price), component values (sum of individual parts), competitor pricing (what a comparable solution costs), coaching equivalent (what this guidance costs in a 1-on-1 context)
   - The anchor must be CREDIBLE. It should be a price the buyer would find plausible, not aspirational. "Worth $297" for a $47 product is credible if it's a video course with multiple modules. "Worth $2,997" for a $47 PDF is not credible.
   - Specimen pattern: most effective anchors are 3-5x the actual price (e.g., $197 anchor for $47 price, $97 anchor for $27 price)

   **(b) Actual Price — "Yours today for just $[price]":**
   - Present with contrast language: "just," "only," "today"
   - Position immediately after the anchor so the gap is visually obvious
   - If payment plan is recommended (from 1.4): present BOTH options
     - Primary: "Just [X] easy payments of $[amount]" (specimen data: payment plans outperformed flat pricing by $38/order)
     - Alternative: "Or one payment of $[flat price]"
   - If payment plan is NOT recommended: present flat price only

   **(c) Per-Day Breakdown — "Less than $[daily] a day":**
   - Calculate: upsell price / usage period in days
   - Default usage period: 30 days (unless product has a different natural period — e.g., 90-day supply → divide by 90)
   - Round to a psychologically appealing number (e.g., $0.90 → "less than a dollar a day," $2.23 → "less than $2.25 a day")
   - Always use "less than" framing to make the number feel even smaller

   **(d) Comparison Anchor — "Less than [familiar expense]":**
   - Match to the campaign audience:
     - Golf: "less than a single sleeve of Pro V1s" / "less than a bucket of range balls" / "less than one golf lesson"
     - Health: "less than your daily coffee" / "less than a single supplement bottle at the store" / "less than one co-pay"
     - Finance: "less than one stock trade commission" / "less than a single financial advisor meeting" / "less than lunch at your desk"
     - Personal development: "less than a single self-help book" / "less than one month of that streaming service you never use"
     - Technology: "less than one month of cloud storage" / "less than a single SaaS subscription"
   - The comparison must be: (1) familiar to the audience, (2) something they spend on without thinking, (3) clearly worth LESS than the upsell product's value

2. **Build the guarantee presentation:**
   - **Option 1 — Mirror FE guarantee:** "Same [X]-day money-back guarantee applies. If [upsell product] doesn't [specific result], email us for a full refund." This is the default and strongest option because it leverages existing trust.
   - **Option 2 — Upsell-specific guarantee:** "Try [upsell product] for [X] days. If you don't see [specific result], we'll refund every penny." Use this only if the upsell product has a meaningfully different guarantee period or condition.
   - **Guarantee placement:** Immediately before the binary CTA. The guarantee removes the last objection before the decision.
   - **Guarantee tone:** Matter-of-fact, not defensive. "Same 60-day guarantee applies" — not "We're SO confident you'll love this that we're offering an IRONCLAD guarantee..."

3. **Build the binary CTA:**
   - **YES button:** "Yes, Add [Upsell Product Name] To My Order — Just $[Price]"
     - Must include: product name + price
     - Action verb: "Add" (not "Buy" — they're adding to an existing order, not making a new purchase)
     - Format: button (primary CTA, visually prominent)
   - **NO link:** "No thanks, I'll pass on this special offer" OR "No thanks, I'll continue with just the [FE Product Name]"
     - Must be: clean, respectful, zero guilt
     - Format: text link below the button (secondary, less prominent)
   - **FORBIDDEN NO options:**
     - "No, I don't want to [desirable outcome]" — guilt manipulation
     - "No, I'm fine being [undesirable state]" — shame manipulation
     - "No, I'll pay full price later" — false urgency/scarcity manipulation
     - "No, I don't care about [important thing]" — values attack
   - Test: read the NO option aloud. Does it sound like something a reasonable person would comfortably click? If not, rewrite.

4. **Generate 5-7 price variant suggestions** for split testing:
   - Specimen insight: 11 of 18 specimens contain multiple price test points. Price testing is standard practice in upsell optimization.
   - Variant types:
     - **Price point variants:** Test 2-3 different price points (e.g., $27 vs $37 vs $47)
     - **Payment plan variants:** Flat price vs 2 payments vs 3 payments
     - **Anchor variants:** Different anchor amounts (e.g., $97 anchor vs $197 anchor)
     - **Framing variants:** Per-day vs per-use vs per-month breakdown
     - **Bundle variants:** With bonuses vs without bonuses (stripped price)
   - For each variant, provide: the variant description, the price presentation copy, and what hypothesis it tests.

5. **Assemble the complete price presentation copy block** — ready to insert into the CAIRO Offer section:

   ```
   [Value anchor sentence]

   [Actual price with contrast]

   [Bonus stack — from 2.2, or placeholder]

   [Total value vs your price]

   [Per-day breakdown]

   [Comparison anchor]

   [Guarantee]

   [YES CTA button]

   [NO text link]
   ```

6. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

1. **All 4 mandatory elements must be present.** Value anchor + actual price + per-day breakdown + comparison anchor. Missing any one = incomplete price presentation. Do not proceed to integration without all four.
2. **NO guilt-trip CTAs.** This is a HARD constraint. The NO option must be clean and respectful. Read it aloud. If it makes you feel bad for clicking it, rewrite it. "No thanks, I'll pass" is acceptable. "No, I don't want to improve my life" is forbidden.
3. **Value anchor must be credible.** 3-5x the actual price is the sweet spot. Under 2x = weak anchoring. Over 10x = absurd anchoring that undermines trust.
4. **Per-day breakdown always uses "less than" framing.** "$2.24 a day" becomes "less than $2.25 a day." The "less than" prefix makes the number feel smaller.
5. **Guarantee is mandatory.** An upsell page without risk reversal is asking for a leap of faith from someone who just gave you money. Mirror the FE guarantee at minimum.
6. **Payment plan recommendation from specimen data: payment plans outperformed flat pricing by $38/order.** If the upsell price is > $67, strongly recommend including a payment plan option. If < $67, flat price is usually sufficient.
7. **Split test variants must be genuinely different**, not cosmetic rewording. "Just $47" vs "Only $47" is not a meaningful variant. "$47 flat" vs "2 payments of $27" IS a meaningful variant.
8. **This microskill runs in parallel with 2.2** (Bonus Stack Builder). Its output is integrated into the CAIRO draft after both 2.2 and 2.3 are complete.
