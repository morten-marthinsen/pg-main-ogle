# 1.1-word-reduction-auditor.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Audit Pass 1)
> **Skill:** CK-03-checkout-editorial
> **Purpose:** Can any word be cut without losing meaning?
> **Outputs:** `word_reduction_report` object with specific cuts and word savings

---

## FUNCTION

Perform a ruthless word-by-word audit of every piece of checkout copy. Checkout is the shortest copy in the entire funnel -- every word must earn its place. If a word can be removed without changing the meaning, it must be removed. This is not about rewriting -- it is about cutting. The goal is maximum clarity with minimum words.

---

## WORD REDUCTION PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE CK-03/1.1-word-reduction-auditor:
  INPUT: checkout_copy_elements (from 0.1)
  OUTPUT: word_reduction_report object

  STEP 1: AUDIT TRUST COPY
    FOR each trust copy element:
      - Read the copy aloud (mentally)
      - Ask: can any word be removed without losing meaning?
      - Ask: is any phrase redundant with another signal?
      - Ask: can a shorter phrase convey the same trust?
      - Document each proposed cut:
        Original: "Your information is protected with 256-bit SSL encryption"
        Revised: "256-bit SSL encryption"
        Words saved: 5
        Meaning preserved: Yes -- the badge icon conveys "protected"

  STEP 2: AUDIT FIELD LABELS AND HELPER TEXT
    FOR each field:
      - Label: is it 1-3 words? Can it be shorter?
      - Helper text: is it necessary? Does the label already convey this?
      - Placeholder: is it necessary or redundant with label?
      - Cut unnecessary helper text:
        Original: "Please enter your email address for order confirmation and receipt"
        Revised: "For your receipt" (label already says "Email address")
        Words saved: 7

  STEP 3: AUDIT ERROR MESSAGES
    FOR each error message:
      - Is it as short as possible while still guiding?
      - Does it have filler words? ("Please note that..." can become "Please...")
      - RULE: Never cut error message specificity. "Please enter your 5-digit zip code"
        must keep "5-digit" -- that is guidance, not filler.
      - RULE: Do not cut "Please" -- it sets guiding tone.

  STEP 4: AUDIT ORDER SUMMARY COPY
    - Product description: can it be 5 words instead of 10?
    - Savings copy: "You save $50.00" -- minimal already
    - Labels: "Shipping", "Total" -- minimal already
    - Look for any verbose descriptions

  STEP 5: AUDIT BUTTON TEXT AND PROGRESS INDICATORS
    - Button: "Complete Order" (2 words) -- minimal
    - Progress: "Contact" -> "Payment" -- minimal
    - Look for verbose navigation text

  STEP 6: COMPILE REDUCTION REPORT
    a. List every proposed cut with original and revised
    b. Calculate total words before and after
    c. Calculate reduction percentage
    d. Verify no cut changes meaning
    e. Flag any cut where meaning preservation is uncertain

  IF a cut changes meaning:
    DO NOT MAKE THE CUT. Flag as "reviewed, retained" with reason.

  IF total copy is already minimal (< 100 words):
    DOCUMENT that copy was reviewed and no cuts possible.
    Do NOT rubber-stamp -- show the analysis.
```

---

## COMMON CUTS

| Pattern | Before | After | Typical Savings |
|---------|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Redundant with visual | "Your information is protected with 256-bit SSL encryption" | "256-bit SSL encryption" | 5 words |
| Unnecessary helper | "Please enter your email address for order confirmation" | "For your receipt" | 5 words |
| Verbose guarantee | "If you are not completely satisfied with your purchase, simply return for a full refund" | "Not satisfied? Full refund." | 9 words |
| Verbose confirmation | "Thank you for your purchase. Your order has been confirmed." | "Order confirmed. Check your email for details." | 3 words |
| Filler in error | "Please note that your card number appears to be invalid" | "Please check your card number -- it should be 16 digits" | 2 words (and more specific) |

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Audit skipped (rubber stamp) | HALT -- perform actual word-by-word review |
| CRITICAL | Cut changes meaning | RESTORE original -- cuts must preserve meaning |
| DEGRADED | Checkout already extremely minimal | Document analysis, confirm no cuts possible |
| DEGRADED | Uncertain whether cut preserves meaning | FLAG for human review, do not apply cut |
