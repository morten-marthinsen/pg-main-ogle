## Search 3: Motor Learning Research — Root Cause Diagnosis + Personalized Training

### Key Findings

**DISC-09: Blocked vs Random Practice (Motor Learning Foundation)**
- "Random practice is superior to blocked practice because it allows for greater variability of practice, which leads to better transfer and retention of skills"
- "Blocked practice leads to better performance in acquisition but results in poorer retention"
- **PG1 IMPLICATION:** YouTube tip-chasing = blocked practice (one tip, repeat). PG1's adaptive plan = structured random practice (varied drills targeting root flaw). Motor learning research validates PG1's approach over the YouTube model.
- Source: Multiple peer-reviewed (Shea & Morgan 1979; Ogden 2020)

**DISC-10: Specificity of Learning Hypothesis**
- "Any skill acquired in a particular context or environment will only be effective in that context or environment"
- "Learning is most effective when practice sessions include the environment and movement conditions that closely resemble those required during performance"
- **PG1 IMPLICATION:** Validates PG1's on-course features and context-aware coaching over generic range drills.
- Source: Wulf & Lewthwaite, 2016

**DISC-11: Four Motor Learning Mechanisms (PMC 2022)**
- Use-dependent, Instructive, Reinforcement, Sensorimotor adaptation
- "The primary behavioral driver of use-dependent motor learning is the amount of practice completed by the learner"
- "Reinforcement learning is primarily mediated by reward-based dopamine signaling"
- **PG1 IMPLICATION:** PG1's progress tracking and flaw resolution scoring leverages reinforcement learning mechanisms — showing improvement activates dopamine-mediated learning.
- Source: PMC/NIH (2022)
