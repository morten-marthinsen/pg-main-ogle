# 1.7 Element Scoring

## Purpose
Score each proof element on 5 dimensions (Specificity, Credibility, Relevance, Novelty, Emotional Impact) to enable quality assessment, gap analysis, and ranking. Scoring must be consistent and calibrated.

---

## Input Requirements

From 1.6 Sub-Type Matching:
- Sub-typed proof elements
- Category and sub-type assigned
- Raw text and extracted components

---

## The 5 Scoring Dimensions

Each element scored 1-10 on each dimension:

```
DIMENSION OVERVIEW:

1. SPECIFICITY   → How precise and concrete?
2. CREDIBILITY   → How believable is the source?
3. RELEVANCE     → How directly does it support the claim?
4. NOVELTY       → How fresh/unused in this market?
5. EMOTIONAL IMPACT → Does it create feeling?
```

---

## Execution Protocol

### Dimension 1: SPECIFICITY (1-10)

**Question:** How precise and concrete is this proof?

```
SCORING RUBRIC:

10 - Exact & Verifiable
├── Specific numbers: "27.4 pounds", "$12,847", "97.3%"
├── Named sources: "Dr. Jane Smith, Stanford University"
├── Dates and timeframes: "Published March 2023"
├── Verifiable details: DOI, patent number, address

8-9 - Very Specific
├── Rounded but precise: "about 30 pounds", "nearly $10,000"
├── Named but partial: "Sarah M. from Texas"
├── General timeframe: "in 6 weeks"

6-7 - Moderately Specific
├── Approximate figures: "over 20 pounds", "thousands of dollars"
├── First name only: "Mark says..."
├── Vague timeframe: "in a few weeks"

4-5 - Somewhat Vague
├── Ranges: "10-30 pounds", "weeks to months"
├── Qualitative: "significant weight loss"
├── Anonymous: "One customer said..."

2-3 - Mostly Vague
├── Non-quantified: "a lot of weight", "great results"
├── No attribution: "People are saying..."
├── No timeframe

1 - Completely Vague
├── Generic: "amazing results", "life-changing"
├── No specifics at all
└── Unverifiable claims
```

**Category-Specific Markers:**

| Category | High Specificity Signals |
|----------|-------------------------|
| SOCIAL | Name + location + exact result |
| AUTHORITY | Full citation, journal name, sample size |
| DEMONSTRATION | Exact before/after numbers |
| MECHANISM | Named pathway, specific percentage |
| DATA | Precise figure with source |
| RISK_REVERSAL | Exact duration, clear conditions |

---

### Dimension 2: CREDIBILITY (1-10)

**Question:** How believable is the source/claim?

```
SCORING RUBRIC:

10 - Highest Authority
├── Peer-reviewed journal (high impact factor)
├── Government agency (FDA, CDC, NIH)
├── Major university research
├── Verified third-party testing

8-9 - Strong Authority
├── Peer-reviewed (any journal)
├── Recognized expert with credentials
├── Independent lab testing
├── Established media coverage

6-7 - Moderate Authority
├── Company-funded but disclosed research
├── Industry certifications
├── Named customer with photo
├── Professional endorsement

4-5 - Limited Authority
├── Internal company data
├── Anonymous testimonials
├── Unverified claims
├── Self-reported results

2-3 - Weak Authority
├── No source cited
├── Vague "studies show"
├── Clearly biased source
├── No attribution

1 - No Authority
├── Fabricated or suspected fabrication
├── Contradicted by evidence
└── Completely unverifiable
```

**Source Credibility Hierarchy:**
```
TIER 1: Peer-reviewed, government, major institution
TIER 2: Independent third-party, verified experts
TIER 3: Named + verified customers, professional bodies
TIER 4: Company data, anonymous testimonials
TIER 5: Unattributed, vague, suspected fabrication
```

---

### Dimension 3: RELEVANCE (1-10)

**Question:** How directly does this support the claim/promise?

```
SCORING RUBRIC:

10 - Direct Proof
├── Proves the exact claim being made
├── Same mechanism, same outcome, same audience
├── No inference required

8-9 - Very Relevant
├── Proves closely related claim
├── Same mechanism, similar outcome
├── Minimal inference needed

6-7 - Moderately Relevant
├── Adjacent claim
├── Similar mechanism or similar outcome
├── Some inference required

4-5 - Tangentially Relevant
├── Related topic but different mechanism
├── Requires significant inference
├── Analogy-based connection

2-3 - Weakly Relevant
├── Only topically related
├── Different mechanism and outcome
├── Connection is a stretch

1 - Irrelevant
├── No meaningful connection to claim
├── Wrong audience, wrong mechanism, wrong outcome
└── Includes as padding
```

**Relevance Assessment Questions:**
1. Does this prove what we're actually claiming?
2. Is the audience in the proof similar to our audience?
3. Is the mechanism in the proof the same as ours?
4. Is the outcome in the proof what we're promising?

---

### Dimension 4: NOVELTY (1-10)

**Question:** How fresh/unused is this proof in this market?

```
SCORING RUBRIC:

10 - Never Seen
├── First-ever study on this mechanism
├── Proprietary data no one else has
├── Unique testimonial story
├── New discovery/finding

8-9 - Very Fresh
├── Recent research not yet widely used
├── Uncommon angle on common proof
├── Unique framing of results

6-7 - Moderately Novel
├── Known but underused in this market
├── Fresh application of known proof
├── Recent update to older proof

4-5 - Somewhat Familiar
├── Commonly used in market
├── Standard proof type
├── Seen in competitor copy

2-3 - Overused
├── Industry cliche
├── Seen in most competitor promotions
├── "Me too" proof

1 - Completely Exhausted
├── Everyone uses this exact proof
├── Audience is numb to it
└── Actively creates skepticism
```

**Novelty Assessment:**
- Have you seen this exact proof elsewhere?
- How often does this type appear in competitor copy?
- Will the audience think "I've heard that before"?

---

### Dimension 5: EMOTIONAL IMPACT (1-10)

**Question:** How does this make the reader FEEL?

```
SCORING RUBRIC:

10 - Powerful Emotional Response
├── Creates strong hope, relief, excitement
├── Transformation story that resonates deeply
├── "If they can do it, so can I" moment
├── Moves reader to action

8-9 - Strong Emotional Resonance
├── Clear emotional trigger
├── Reader identifies with the situation
├── Creates desire or relief

6-7 - Moderate Emotional Engagement
├── Some emotional connection
├── Interesting but not moving
├── Mild hope or curiosity

4-5 - Slight Emotional Response
├── Intellectually interesting
├── Low emotional engagement
├── Informational rather than emotional

2-3 - Minimal Emotion
├── Dry facts only
├── No story or human element
├── Reader feels nothing

1 - Emotionally Flat
├── Boring, forgettable
├── No human connection
└── Pure data without context
```

**Emotional Impact Signals:**
- Transformation narratives (high)
- Personal stories with struggle (high)
- Raw statistics without context (low)
- Technical mechanism explanations (low unless "aha" moment)

---

## Composite Score Calculation

### Basic Composite

```python
composite_score = (
    specificity * 0.25 +
    credibility * 0.25 +
    relevance * 0.20 +
    novelty * 0.15 +
    emotional_impact * 0.15
)
```

**Rationale:**
- Specificity + Credibility = Believability (50% weight)
- Relevance = Connection to claim (20% weight)
- Novelty + Emotion = Differentiation & Engagement (30% weight)

## Quality Tier Classification

Based on composite score:

| Tier | Score Range | Description |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Strong** | 7.0 - 10.0 | Use prominently in copy |
| **Medium** | 4.0 - 6.9 | Supporting proof, proof stack |
| **Weak** | 1.0 - 3.9 | May need improvement or omission |

---

## Quality Gates

Before scoring complete:

- [ ] All 5 dimensions scored (relevance may be null pending context)
- [ ] Rationale provided for each score
- [ ] Composite calculated
- [ ] Quality tier assigned
- [ ] No score inflation (be conservative)

---

## Scoring Calibration Notes

**Avoid Inflation:**
- When in doubt, score lower
- 10s should be rare and exceptional
- Most elements will be 5-7 range

**Be Consistent:**
- Same type of proof should score similarly
- Use anchors from previous scoring
- Document rationale for reference

**Context Matters:**
- Relevance requires promise/claim context
- Novelty requires market knowledge
- Emotional impact varies by audience
