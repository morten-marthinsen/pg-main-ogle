|---------|-------------|

## Prospect Vocabulary by Emotional State

### When They're in PAIN (Use to Mirror)
- "I'm at my 147th attempt"
- "Every tool produces the same garbage"
- "Fixing AI output takes longer than writing from scratch"
- "My content sounds like everyone else's ChatGPT output"
- "I can't get it to match my voice no matter what I try"
- "The promised time savings is a lie"

### When They're HOPEFUL (Use to Inspire)
- "What if there's a way to make AI actually sound like me?"
- "I just want near-publish-ready from the first draft"
- "A system that works every time, not random prompting"
- "Copy that converts without 2 hours of editing"
- "Something that makes my content stand out from the AI crowd"

### When They're SEARCHING (Use in Ads/Headlines)
- "How to make AI copy sound human"
- "Best AI copywriting system"
- "AI copy that actually converts"
- "Fix AI writing quality"
- "Why does all AI copy sound the same?"

### When They're SKEPTICAL (Use to Overcome)
- "I've tried everything -- why would this be different?"
- "Another AI writing tool? Pass."
- "Show me before and after, not marketing promises"
- "How is this not just another prompt pack?"
- "Prove it works with real examples, not testimonials"

## Key Linguistic Insights for Copy Team

1. **The market speaks in negatives.** They describe what they DON'T want more than what they DO want. Mirror this: "No more AI slop" > "Better AI output."

2. **"Sounds like" is the dominant evaluation frame.** "Sounds human" = good. "Sounds like AI" = bad. "Sounds like ChatGPT" = death. Use the auditory metaphor.

3. **They have a developed vocabulary for the problem but NOT for the solution.** They can describe AI slop in 20 ways but struggle to articulate what good AI copy would actually look like. This is a massive opportunity to NAME the solution.

4. **"System" and "method" > "tool" and "software."** The market has tool fatigue. They want a methodology. Position accordingly.

5. **Self-deprecating humor resonates.** "Am I the only one who..." and "Please tell me it's not just me..." are common patterns. The market bonds over shared frustration.

6. **Price sensitivity is LOW for one-time purchases but HIGH for subscriptions.** $27 one-time faces almost no price objection. $27/month would face significant resistance. Frame the one-time nature aggressively.

7. **Before/after proof is the single most persuasive content type.** The market evaluates mechanisms visually. Side-by-side comparisons of AI output with vs. without specimens will be the strongest sales asset.
