## Section 9: Gap Analysis

### What's Missing or Thin

1. **Post-purchase emotional proof.** The corpus is pre-purchase forum data. Emotions like Vindication, Relief, and Confidence have low frequencies because golfers haven't experienced PG1 yet. This is expected and will be filled by Founders testimonials. **Impact on Layer 3:** The proof stack section of the founders page will rely on borrowed proof (competitor user results, coaching testimonials) until PG1-specific proof exists.

2. **STATUS dimension quotes are partially inferred.** Golfers don't often post publicly about social shame. The STATUS dimension (7/10 contrast) is the weakest, with evidence partially inferred from quotes that mention embarrassment in passing. **Impact on Layer 3:** STATUS-based copy angles (playing partners noticing, identity shift) are directionally correct but less quote-supported than other dimensions.

3. **HOPE quote concentration.** A small number of HOPE quotes carry disproportionate weight. HOPE-006 (airjammer, root cause AI), HOPE-003 (OhYeahPlayer, instant feedback), and HOPE-001 (josephlee, feel vs. real) appear repeatedly across multiple artifacts. This reflects corpus reality -- there are fewer articulate hope quotes than pain quotes -- but it means the "after" side of the copy has less verbatim variety than the "before" side. **Impact on Layer 3:** The mechanism section will need to lean more on educational framing and less on direct quotes.

4. **Beta user proof gap (carried from Gate 2).** No specific PG1 handicap improvement results exist. The proof inventory (from Layer 2) flagged this as a critical gap. **Impact on Layer 3:** The founders page must use borrowed proof, mechanism logic, and risk reversal to compensate.

5. **Guarantee / risk reversal not yet defined (carried from Gate 2).** This was a Gate 2 warning and remains unresolved. **Impact on Layer 3:** The pricing section will need this defined before copy can be written.

### Contradictions Between Artifacts

**None significant.** All six artifacts tell the same story from different angles:
- 2.5-A (emotional contrast) and 2.5-D (transformation grid) agree on the same NOW/AFTER states
- 2.5-B (educational frames) and 2.5-C (beliefs) agree on the same root causes of failure
- 2.5-E (language) and 2.5-F (categorization) agree on the same emotional hierarchy (Frustration > Confusion > Determination > Skepticism)
- The master narratives in 2.5-C and 2.5-D are complementary and mutually reinforcing

### Recommendations for Layer 3

1. **Use EXPERIENCE dimension as the primary copy driver** -- it is the master transformation (9/10 contrast) and produces immediate Day 1 impact.
2. **Lead the emotional arc with Frustration + Determination** -- the target golfer hasn't quit yet. Honor their commitment while reframing their approach.
3. **The X-ray metaphor and drug/addiction metaphor are the two strongest language assets.** Build the mechanism section around "diagnosis" and the problem section around "the tip-hopping cycle."
4. **Prioritize the root-cause belief alignment** (B-ALIGN-01) -- this is the single strongest psychological anchor and PG1's home base.
5. **Address the "nothing works for me" belief** (B-DISRUPT-01) explicitly -- it is the master gatekeeper belief. If this isn't disrupted, no other copy will land.
6. **Plan for a lean proof stack.** Until Founders generate testimonials, use the educational pairs (2.5-B) and the X-ray/diagnosis mechanism to build logical belief. The proof will be in the reasoning, not in the results.
