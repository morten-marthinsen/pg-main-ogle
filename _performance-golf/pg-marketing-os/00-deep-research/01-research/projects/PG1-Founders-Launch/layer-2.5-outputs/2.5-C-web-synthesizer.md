## WANTS -- Ranked by Frequency and Intensity

### GAIN (Things They Want MORE Of)

**W-01: Consistency -- Predictable, Repeatable Results** | Intensity: 10/10
The single most wanted outcome in the entire corpus. Not "get better" -- golfers want to stop being wildly unpredictable. The gap between their best round and their worst round is what destroys them.

> "My ball striking is wildly inconsistent" -- PAI-364, r/GolfSwing
> "Two steps forward, three steps back -- never consistent" -- market_config
> "I was incredibly inconsistent and had to spend an hour on the range before every round just to find some kind of contact" -- PAI-362, r/GolfSwing

**Copy angle:** Lead with consistency, not peak performance. "What if every round felt like your best round?" The promise is not shooting 72 -- it is closing the gap between their 85 and their 105.

**W-02: Confidence That Their Approach Is Correct** | Intensity: 9/10
Golfers want to stand over the ball knowing what they are doing, not cycling through 7 competing swing thoughts. The confidence deficit is not about ability -- it is about uncertainty.

> "I need to understand before I commit to it because if I don't understand then there's no point I feel." -- GGTTH, GolfWRX
> "A slump that is almost a year long, that is showing absolutely zero signs of getting better has certainly drained my confidence" -- GGTTH, GolfWRX

**Copy angle:** Position PG1 as certainty. "You'll know exactly what to work on, why, and in what order." The opposite of YouTube guesswork.

**W-03: A Clear Improvement Path (Not More Information)** | Intensity: 9/10
The most specific want: stop giving me tips. Give me a plan. Golfers are drowning in information and starving for sequence and priority.

> "Essentially the perfect golf swing is out there for everyone to understand and implement. Where I struggle without assistance is identifying what issues exist in MY swing and in what order things need addressed." -- dg_1983, GolfWRX
> "One day it's swing mechanics, then short game, then putting, then 'mental stuff.' It feels like I'm doing a lot of golf without a clear plan" -- PAI-190, r/golf

**Copy angle:** "One root flaw. One clear plan. One step at a time." This is the inverse of the information avalanche.

**W-04: Access to Elite Coaching Without Elite Pricing** | Intensity: 8/10
The coaching they need costs $150-$300/hour or $500-$650/year for premium apps. They want world-class instruction at a sustainable price.

> "I've been playing for about 3 years, have had close to $1,000 worth of lessons over the past 2 years with 4 different instructors." -- Warik, TheSandTrap
> "Sportsbox... love it, but it's $$$$$... when I signed up it was 500, now 650 per year" -- kiddoc, GolfWRX

**Copy angle:** Value anchoring against cumulative lesson/app spending. "$299 lifetime replaces the $1,000+/year you're spending now."

## SYNTHESIS -- The Master Psychological Portrait

**Primary Want:** Consistent, predictable improvement from a clear, personalized plan -- not more information, tips, or data.

**Dominant Current Emotion:** Deep frustration from sustained effort without proportional results, compounded by confusion from information overload and eroded by repeated failures of instruction, apps, and self-help approaches.

**Key Belief to Disrupt:** "Nothing works for me -- I've tried everything." Reframe: You've tried the same broken approach (generic tips, fragmented instruction, data without diagnosis) multiple ways. You have never tried starting with an accurate root-flaw diagnosis.

**Key Belief to Align With:** "There is ONE root cause behind my inconsistency." This belief exists naturally in the market and is the psychological foundation for PG1's entire mechanism.

**Master Narrative:**
These golfers want one thing above all: to stop being wildly inconsistent despite years of genuine effort. They are trapped in a devastating cycle -- consuming instruction that confuses more than it helps, investing in apps that measure without diagnosing, and practicing hard on the wrong things because nobody has identified their actual root swing flaw. Their deepest frustration is not that they lack talent or effort, but that all their effort has been misdirected. They believe the answer exists (they see others improve) but have lost faith that any specific product or coach will deliver it for them. Underneath the skepticism, however, lives a powerful enabling belief: there is ONE thing at the root of everything, and if they could just find it, everything would change. PG1's founders page must validate that belief, demonstrate that the system can find their specific root flaw, and give them the emotional permission to hope one more time -- with evidence, not hype.
