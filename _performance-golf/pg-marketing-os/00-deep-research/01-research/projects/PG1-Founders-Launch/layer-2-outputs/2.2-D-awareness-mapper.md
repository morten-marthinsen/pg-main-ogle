### Primary Awareness Level: SPLIT — Problem-Aware (30%) + Solution-Aware (40%)

The PG1 target audience is primarily spread across Levels 2 and 3, with the center of gravity in the Solution-Aware segment. This has critical messaging implications: the founders launch must not sell on "solutions exist" (they already know that) — it must differentiate PG1 from everything they have already tried.

**Confidence:** Medium-High. The split between Level 2 and Level 3 is genuine — roughly 30/40. No single level dominates above 50%.

## Level 1: UNAWARE (~5%)

### Characteristics
- Golfers who are stuck but attribute it to "golf is just hard" or "I need more practice"
- Do not frame their situation as a problem with a solution
- Not actively searching for improvement tools
- May not realize their struggles are diagnosable or fixable

### Quote Evidence

> "Golf is an endless source of kicks in the nuts. I thinks its that way for most of us, people play for 50+ years and still stink. Keep at it and don't put so much pressure on yourself." — kmay__, GolfWRX

> "I don't get frustrated. I get depressed... In reality, the problem with me getting any better is that I lack ability... As much as I want to think I can conquer this game intellectually, it's still a physical game." — Marty2019, TheSandTrap

> "I haven't got the first clue about swing paths etc etc and I'm not sure if I really want too." — Arthur Wedge, Golf Monthly

### Language Indicators
- "Golf is just hard"
- "That's the way it is for most people"
- "I'm just not athletic enough"
- "It's just a game" (defensive minimization)
- No mentions of specific solutions, apps, or instruction products

### PG1 Relevance
Low for founders launch. These golfers are not in the buying window. They need an awakening event — typically a confrontation with their own stagnation. The docu-series "Confession" episode (Brixton admitting PG was part of the problem) could serve this function for PG customers who have normalized being stuck.

## Level 2: PROBLEM-AWARE (~30%)

### Characteristics
- Know they are stuck and frustrated about it
- Actively describe their problem but do not know the cause
- Ask "why" questions: "Why can't I improve?" "What am I doing wrong?"
- Have not yet identified a specific solution category
- Emotional intensity is highest at this level — they feel the pain acutely but cannot name it

### Quote Evidence

> "I am completely obsessed with golf... But often I get out on the course, top my driver, shank my 5 iron, zig zagging down the fairway... It is so frustrating to be completely obsessed with something and put so much effort and money into it only to fail miserably." — westozhacker, GolfWRX

> "I started playing golf at 14 and still play today at 74... I have read scores of golf instruction books, watched hundreds of golf videos, and taken untold lessons. But I never get any better. What's my problem?" — u/Jehoshephat, Reddit

> "After trending in the right direction score and handicap wise since I started playing I've now hit a slump that I can't get out of. Scores are rising, handicap is rising. Confidence is gone. Game is gone." — GGTTH, GolfWRX

> "My swing never really feels comfortable and I get overwhelmed by how many things I have to remember to do in my setup." — westozhacker, GolfWRX

> "I can't seem to shake making 3-4 triples per round which are absolutely soul destroying." — DrGolfer, Golf Monthly

> "I started golf 6 months ago and now I am ready to quit... I understood what my instructor said, I know what I should do, but I just do not know how to do it." — golftlist, TheSandTrap

### Language Indicators
- "I don't know what I'm doing wrong"
- "Why can't I improve?"
- "What's my problem?"
- "I'm stuck at [handicap]"
- "Nothing is working"
- "I feel like I'm getting worse"
- Heavy frustration language without naming specific solutions
- Questions oriented toward understanding the problem, not comparing products

### Key Problems Recognized (But Not Named)
- Inconsistency despite effort
- Information overload causing paralysis
- Range performance not translating to the course
- Emotional drain from the improvement cycle
- Spending money without results

### PG1 Strategy for Level 2
**Entry point:** Agitate the problem by naming it with precision — "root swing flaw." These golfers have the pain but no vocabulary for what is causing it. PG1's greatest gift to Level 2 prospects is a NAME for their problem.

**Movement tactic:** "What if everything you've been working on is a symptom — not the cause? What if there's one root flaw in your swing that's creating every other issue?"

**Critical note:** Problem-Aware golfers are the docu-series audience. The six-piece content series exists to move them from Level 2 to Level 3 by introducing the root flaw concept before they ever see the product.

### PG1 Strategy for Level 3
**Entry point:** Acknowledge their journey. "You've tried the tips. You've taken the lessons. You've downloaded the apps." This creates instant trust by demonstrating understanding.

**Movement tactic:** Explain WHY nothing worked — not because the solutions were all bad, but because none of them started with the right diagnosis. "Every solution you've tried has treated symptoms. PG1 starts with the cause."

**Differentiation is everything at this level.** The Solution-Aware golfer's default response to any new product is "how is this different from everything else I've tried?" The answer must be specific, concrete, and immediately understandable:
1. AI trained on 2M+ swings identifies your ONE root flaw (not 50 metrics)
2. Six elite PGA coaches designed the system (not random YouTube instructors)
3. Your plan adapts as you improve (not a static video library)
4. It is a coaching system, not a data app

**This is the founders launch PRIMARY audience.** The existing PG customer base — email list, previous buyers — is overwhelmingly Solution-Aware. They have tried PG's other products (training aids, courses). They know solutions exist. They need to understand why PG1 is categorically different.

## Awareness Journey Map

### Typical Path for PG1's Target Audience

```
Stage 1: UNAWARE
"Golf is just hard. I'm not that good."
   ↓ Trigger: A particularly bad round, or realizing their handicap hasn't moved in years

Stage 2: PROBLEM-AWARE
"Something is wrong. I'm putting in effort but not improving. Why?"
   ↓ Trigger: Encounter with the "root flaw" concept (docu-series, keynote, ad)

Stage 3: SOLUTION-AWARE
"I've tried lessons, YouTube, apps. Nothing works. What else is out there?"
   ↓ Trigger: Specific recommendation, PG1 ad, friend mention, docu-series

Stage 4: PRODUCT-AWARE
"PG1 looks interesting. Is it different from what I've tried? Is it worth it?"
   ↓ Trigger: Keynote viewing, proof stack, testimonials, founders offer

Stage 5: MOST AWARE
"I want in. Where do I buy?"
   ↓ Trigger: Clear CTA, 1,000-member urgency, Founders page ATF
```

### Sticking Points (Where People Get Stuck)

**Sticking Point 1: Problem-Aware Cycling**
- Location: Between Level 2 and Level 3
- What happens: Golfers stay in the "I'm stuck but I don't know why" state for months or years. They keep playing, keep being frustrated, but do not take action because they have not framed their problem as solvable.
- Duration: Can last years (the 74-year-old who has been the same handicap for 58 years)
- Quote evidence: "I started playing golf at 14 and still play today at 74... But I never get any better."
- PG1 intervention: The "root flaw" concept is the unlock. It reframes "golf is just hard" into "you have one specific, identifiable problem."

**Sticking Point 2: Solution-Aware Skepticism Loop**
- Location: Within Level 3
- What happens: Golfers try solution after solution, get burned, and become increasingly skeptical. Each failure makes the next purchase harder. They are stuck in a cycle of "maybe this one will work" → disappointment → "nothing works for me."
- Duration: 1-5 years typically
- Quote evidence: "I've went for groups of lessons with three or four different coaches over the last few years, spending an absolute fortune in the process, trying to find one that will improve me with time. Has it been worth it? Absolutely not."
- PG1 intervention: Must break the "another golf app" pattern by leading with diagnosis, not features. "This is not another tip, lesson, or app. This is the first system that starts by finding YOUR root flaw."

**Sticking Point 3: Product-Aware Price Hesitation**
- Location: Between Level 4 and Level 5
- What happens: Golfers believe PG1 might work but hesitate at the price — not because $199-$299 is objectively high, but because they have been burned before and do not trust their own judgment ("Am I about to waste money again?")
- Duration: Days to weeks
- Quote evidence: "I downloaded a handful of them, but I never got around to signing up for all of the different subscriptions."
- PG1 intervention: Value anchoring ($2,000-$3,000/yr industry estimate), founders-exclusive benefits (not just a discount), lifetime option ($299 one-time = never pay again), and the 1,000-member cap creating real urgency.

## Entry Point Strategy for the Founders Launch

### Primary Entry: Solution-Aware (Level 3) — Target ~40% of audience

**Why:** PG's existing email list is overwhelmingly Solution-Aware. These golfers have already bought PG products (training aids, courses, clubs). They know solutions exist. They have tried things. They need to understand why PG1 is categorically different from everything else.

**How the funnel moves them:**
1. Docu-series validates their frustration and introduces the root flaw framework
2. Keynote presents PG1 as the specific solution to the problem they have been unable to solve
3. Founders page converts them with proof, offer clarity, and urgency

### Secondary Entry: Problem-Aware (Level 2) — Target ~30% of audience

**Why:** A significant portion of PG's list has not tried competing apps — they have only tried lessons and YouTube. They are stuck but have not entered the "golf app shopping" phase. The docu-series is designed to move these golfers from "I'm stuck and I don't know why" to "I know what the problem is and PG1 can fix it."

**How the funnel moves them:**
1. "The Confession" names the problem and validates their frustration
2. "The War Room" and "$3 Million Bet" show PG taking the problem seriously
3. "The Coaches" and "The Breakthrough" demonstrate the solution in action
4. Keynote provides the complete argument
5. Founders page converts

### Tertiary Entry: Product-Aware (Level 4) — Target ~20% of audience

**Why:** Some golfers will encounter the founders page directly — via a forward from a friend, a social media link, or a PG email that skips the docu-series. The page must work for these buyers too.

**How the page serves them:**
1. ATF: Clear value prop, price, CTA (for fast-action Level 5 buyers who already watched the keynote)
2. BTF: Complete sales argument including problem agitation, root flaw concept, PG1 mechanism, proof stack, coach credentials, offer breakdown, guarantee, urgency

### Recommended Primary Messaging Angle

**Enter at Level 3 (Solution-Aware), move to Level 4 (Product-Aware), close at Level 5 (Most Aware).**

The messaging sequence should be:

1. **Acknowledge their journey** — "You've tried the tips, the lessons, the apps. We know."
2. **Explain why nothing worked** — "Because none of it started with YOUR root flaw diagnosis."
3. **Introduce the mechanism** — "PG1 uses AI trained on 2M+ swings to identify the one root flaw behind your inconsistency."
4. **Prove it works** — Proof stack: AI accuracy, coach credentials, beta results, investment size
5. **Present the offer** — Founders Club, 1,000 members, $199/yr or $299 lifetime
6. **Close on identity and emotion** — "Be one of the first 1,000. Love your game again."
