## What Proof Types Matter Most to Golfers (From Research)

### Proof Hierarchy for Golf Improvement Products

Based on analysis of 1,824 quotes and competitive landscape research, here is what golfers respond to most when evaluating a new improvement tool:

**Tier 1 — Decisive Proof (changes purchase decision)**
1. **Specific results from real golfers:** Handicap drops, score improvements, specific timeframes. Golfers want to see someone like them who improved. "From 14 to 10 in a year" is more powerful than any technology spec.
2. **Personal demonstration / "show me it works":** Seeing the product in action on a real swing, not a demo. The Breakthrough on Hole 7 docu-series piece targets this directly.
3. **Founder credibility / personal story:** Brixton's story of hitting a wall at +3 and dropping 5 shots after root flaw diagnosis is top-tier proof. It's personal, specific, and demonstrates the exact mechanism PG1 claims.

**Tier 2 — Credibility Builders (strengthens conviction)**
4. **Technology specifics with real numbers:** 2M+ swings, 96% accuracy, 77+ flaws. These aren't generic claims — they're specific enough to feel real. Golfers who've been burned by vague promises respond to specificity.
5. **Elite coaching credentials (named):** "6 PGA coaches" means nothing. "Dr. [Name], who coached [Tour player] for 15 years" means everything. Named credentials with track records are essential.
6. **Investment scale ($5M+):** Communicates "this isn't a weekend project." Separates PG1 from the 400+ apps built by small teams.
7. **Celebrity/influencer adoption:** Alonzo Mourning using PG1 creates aspirational proof. But it must be real usage, not paid endorsement — golfers are cynical about sponsorships.

**Tier 3 — Supporting Proof (reduces friction)**
8. **Company track record (1.4M golfers):** Establishes that PG isn't new to golf improvement.
9. **Technology partner credibility (MyFitnessPal developer):** Answers "will the app actually work?"
10. **Price anchoring ($2K-3K value, $350-2K/lesson):** Makes the founders pricing feel like a steal.
11. **Guarantee / risk reversal:** Not mentioned in brief. Strongly recommended based on the trust deficit in this market.

**What Golfers Do NOT Respond To (Based on Research):**
- Generic "personalized" claims without proof
- Technology jargon without translation to outcomes
- Vague "AI-powered" positioning
- Unverified testimonials
- Screenshots of data dashboards (they've seen enough data)
- Comparisons to other apps by name (feels defensive)

## Proof Gaps (Critical)

### Gap 1: Specific Beta User Results (CRITICAL)
- **Claim that needs proof:** "PG1 Pulse identifies your root flaw and helps you improve"
- **Proof needed:** 3-5 specific case studies with handicap before, handicap after, timeframe, and named individuals
- **Current status:** No specific beta user results with handicap improvements cited in keynote or brief. "5x more likely to complete" speaks to engagement, not improvement.
- **Recommendation:** This is the single most important proof gap. Before the founders page goes live, collect and document 3-5 beta user transformations. Even small improvements (e.g., "dropped 4 strokes in 6 weeks after fixing root flaw") are powerful. The UGC carousel planned for the page must prioritize RESULTS, not just excitement.
- **Workaround if gap persists:** Lead with Brixton's personal story (root flaw diagnosis dropped him 5 shots) as the proxy proof. Acknowledge newness honestly: "We built the most advanced diagnostic system in golf. Now we're looking for the first 1,000 golfers to prove it."

### Gap 2: Individual Coach Credentials (HIGH PRIORITY)
- **Claim that needs proof:** "6 elite PGA coaches"
- **Proof needed:** Individual names, certifications, teaching track records, notable students, years of experience
- **Current status:** "6 elite PGA coaches" is stated but no individual credentials are in the research data
- **Recommendation:** Each coach should have a brief bio with: name, photo, PGA certification level, years teaching, notable students/achievements, and their specific area of expertise within PG1. "Coach [Name]: 25-year PGA Master Professional, coached 3 state champions, specializes in short game diagnostics" is infinitely more powerful than "6 elite coaches."

### Gap 3: Named Third-Party Validation (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
- **Claim that needs proof:** "Industry insiders valued PG1 at $2,000-3,000/yr"
- **Proof needed:** Named individuals who made this assessment
- **Current status:** Unnamed "industry insiders"
- **Recommendation:** If possible, attach a name or organization to this valuation. "Todd Brown, who has helped launch over 50 digital products, said..." Even one named source transforms this from claim to proof.

### Gap 4: Guarantee / Risk Reversal (RECOMMENDED ADDITION)
- **Claim that needs proof:** "This will work for you"
- **Proof needed:** A guarantee that removes risk from the purchase decision
- **Current status:** No guarantee mentioned in brief or keynote
- **Recommendation:** Given the high trust deficit in this market (golfers have been burned by apps, lessons, and promises), a money-back guarantee is strongly recommended. Options: 30-day money-back guarantee, "if you don't see improvement in your root flaw within 60 days, we'll refund you," or similar. The research consistently shows that golfers fear wasting money on another solution that doesn't work. A guarantee directly addresses this.

### Gap 5: Before/After Visual Proof (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
- **Claim that needs proof:** "SwingScan AI identifies your root flaw with precision"
- **Proof needed:** Side-by-side before/after swing videos showing the root flaw diagnosis, the plan, and the improvement
- **Current status:** Planned in docu-series (The Breakthrough on Hole 7) but not yet as structured proof on the founders page
- **Recommendation:** Create 2-3 visual proof pieces that show: (1) the SwingScan AI identifying a root flaw, (2) the personalized plan generated, (3) the golfer's swing after working the plan. This is "show, don't tell" proof that's more powerful than any metric.
