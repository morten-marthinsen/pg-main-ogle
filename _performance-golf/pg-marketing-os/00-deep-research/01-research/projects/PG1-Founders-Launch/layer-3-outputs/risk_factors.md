## OPP-01: Root Flaw Diagnosis — Risk Assessment

### MARKET RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 3/5 | **Composite:** 6/25
- **Description:** Demand for root cause diagnosis is overwhelmingly validated (240+ organic root cause quotes, 652 pain quotes describing undiagnosed symptoms). The risk is not demand — it's whether the audience can FIND PG1 through the noise of 400+ golf apps.
- **Mitigation:** The docu-series + keynote launch bypasses app store discovery entirely. PG1 reaches its audience through PG's existing 1.4M customer base via email, not through app store search. For the Founders launch, market discovery risk is near-zero because the audience is already known and reachable.

### COMPETITIVE RISK
- **Likelihood:** 3/5 | **Impact:** 4/5 | **Composite:** 12/25
- **Description:** The "root cause diagnosis" positioning is currently uncontested, but Sportsbox AI, GOATCode, or a new entrant could pivot to claim diagnosis language within 6-12 months. The mechanism is harder to copy (2M+ swings, 96% accuracy, 77+ patterns), but the positioning language is easy to appropriate.
- **Mitigation:** Move fast. The Founders launch locks in 1,000 early adopters who become proof and advocates. Build a wall of testimonials and case studies (handicap drops attributed to root flaw identification) within the first 90 days. File trademark considerations for "SwingScan" and "Root Swing Flaw" as proprietary terminology. Every month of head start deepens the moat.

### EXECUTION RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 5/5 | **Composite:** 10/25
- **Description:** The diagnosis promise creates an extremely high bar. If SwingScan AI delivers a diagnosis that feels generic or inaccurate to the first 1,000 founders, the backlash will be severe — because the PROMISE is specificity. "Your ONE root flaw" cannot afford to feel like "generic swing advice repackaged."
- **Mitigation:** The 7-day free trial lets users experience the diagnosis before paying. Post-purchase onboarding must deliver the diagnosis moment within the first session — not buried behind 10 minutes of setup. Coach Chat provides human backup if the AI diagnosis needs contextualizing. Product team must monitor early user feedback obsessively and patch diagnostic gaps in real-time.

### MESSAGING RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 3/5 | **Composite:** 6/25
- **Description:** The "root flaw" language could feel clinical or intimidating. "Flaw" has negative connotations. Some golfers may resist the idea that they have ONE problem (their experience feels like MANY problems). The medical/diagnosis metaphor could feel cold if not delivered with warmth.
- **Mitigation:** Always pair "root flaw" with resolution language: "Your root flaw — and the clear plan to fix it." The docu-series builds emotional warmth before the clinical language arrives. Brixton's personal story (his instructor found HIS one root flaw, he dropped 5 shots) humanizes the concept. Test "root swing flaw" vs. "root cause" in early ad creative to optimize.

### TIMING RISK
- **Likelihood:** 1/5 | **Impact:** 2/5 | **Composite:** 2/25
- **Description:** Timing is highly favorable. AI skepticism has peaked (creating demand for an AI product that actually delivers), subscription fatigue is at maximum (lifetime pricing arrives at the right moment), and golf participation remains elevated post-COVID. No seasonal risk — the launch targets PG's email list, not cold traffic dependent on golf season.
- **Mitigation:** No significant mitigation needed. The timing window is open. The only timing risk is DELAY — every month without launching gives competitors time to enter the diagnosis space.

**OPP-01 Overall Risk Profile:** LOW-MODERATE. The biggest risks are execution (diagnosis must deliver on its promise) and competitive (the positioning language can be copied). Both are mitigable with speed and quality.

## OPP-02: End the Search — Risk Assessment

### MARKET RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 2/5 | **Composite:** 4/25
- **Description:** The desire to end the search is universally validated (47+ "I just want" quotes, 80+ subscription fatigue quotes, 70+ multiple-instructor quotes). This is an evergreen emotional territory — it does not depend on trends or timing.
- **Mitigation:** None needed. Market demand is structural, not cyclical.

### COMPETITIVE RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 3/5 | **Composite:** 6/25
- **Description:** Shot Scope already uses anti-subscription positioning ("no subscription ever") for hardware. A competitor could adopt lifetime pricing for a coaching/AI app. However, the "end the search" positioning is broader than pricing — it encompasses the emotional promise of "this is the last thing you'll try," which requires delivering on diagnosis, not just removing a recurring charge.
- **Mitigation:** The "end the search" promise is credible only when paired with OPP-01 (root flaw diagnosis). Competitors can copy lifetime pricing but cannot copy the diagnostic engine without years of development. The moat is the mechanism, not the pricing model.

### EXECUTION RISK
- **Likelihood:** 3/5 | **Impact:** 4/5 | **Composite:** 12/25
- **Description:** "The last golf improvement purchase" is a bold promise. If founders don't experience meaningful improvement within 60-90 days, the "end the search" positioning collapses into "just another thing I tried." Retention is the execution challenge — the diagnosis must lead to real, felt improvement.
- **Mitigation:** Onboarding must deliver a tangible "aha" moment within the first week (diagnosis reveal). Week 2-4 must show measurable progress against the root flaw. Monthly Coach Chat check-ins maintain engagement. The adaptive plan keeps the experience evolving. 30-day check-in email with "here's how your root flaw has improved" data visualization.

### MESSAGING RISK
- **Likelihood:** 2/5 | **Impact:** 3/5 | **Composite:** 6/25
- **Description:** "The last thing you'll try" can sound arrogant or trigger the "yeah, right" response from a jaded audience. The audience has been promised "this changes everything" by multiple apps and instructors. Another absolute claim risks immediate dismissal.
- **Mitigation:** Don't SAY "the last thing you'll try." Let the STRUCTURE deliver it: lifetime pricing (never pay again), diagnosis-first approach (fundamentally different), Founders community (belonging, not purchasing). The "end the search" sentiment should be FELT through the experience and pricing, not stated as a headline.

### TIMING RISK
- **Likelihood:** 1/5 | **Impact:** 2/5 | **Composite:** 2/25
- **Description:** Subscription fatigue is peaking — ideal timing for a lifetime offer. No seasonal dependency. No cultural risk.
- **Mitigation:** None needed.

**OPP-02 Overall Risk Profile:** LOW-MODERATE. The biggest risk is execution (improvement must be real and felt within 60-90 days). The structural elements (lifetime pricing, Founders community) mitigate messaging and competitive risks.
