## Awareness Distribution

### Level 5 — Most Aware (5%)

**Who they are:** Golfers who have already encountered PG1 Pulse — perhaps through the free 7-day trial on the current site, through PG internal communications, or through early beta access. They know the product, know what it does, and just need the offer.

**Indicators:**
- Have visited shop.performancegolf.com/pages/pg1-app
- May have tried the free 7-day trial
- Follow PG closely enough to know about the app pre-launch
- Active PG community members who have heard about PG1 internally

**Research Note:** This is a small group. The founders launch is designed to expand this group massively — the docu-series moves people from Level 3/4 to Level 5.

---

### Level 4 — Product Aware (20%)

**Who they are:** Golfers who know that golf coaching/analysis apps exist and can name specific ones. They've compared Skillest, Arccos, V1 Golf, or others. They're familiar with the category and may have tried one or more apps. They don't know PG1 Pulse specifically.

**Indicators:**
- Ask "what's the best golf app for swing analysis?"
- Compare specific products by name in forums ("Skillest vs V1 Golf")
- Read app reviews before downloading
- Understand the difference between GPS apps, stat-tracking apps, and coaching apps
- May have subscriptions to one or more golf apps
- Discuss specific features (video upload, AI analysis, coach chat)

**Research Implications:**
- These golfers need DIFFERENTIATION — why PG1 is categorically different from what they've tried
- They'll compare PG1 to known competitors immediately
- Mechanism distinction is critical (root flaw diagnosis vs. video markup vs. stat tracking)
- They respond to specific proof, not general claims

---

### Level 3 — Solution Aware (40%)

**Who they are:** The largest segment. Golfers who know they need help improving and know that various solutions exist — lessons, apps, training aids, online coaching, YouTube videos — but they're not comparing specific products. They're still figuring out WHAT TYPE of solution will work for them. Many have tried generic approaches and are looking for something different.

**Indicators:**
- Search "how to actually improve at golf" (not "best golf app")
- Ask "are golf lessons worth it?" rather than "which golf app should I use?"
- Describe frustration with multiple solution types, not specific products
- Post "I've tried everything" without naming specifics
- Looking for a new APPROACH, not just a new product
- Respond to "what if the whole approach is wrong?" framing

**Research Implications:**
- These golfers need SOLUTION EDUCATION — what makes AI root flaw diagnosis different from everything they've tried
- The keynote's "intelligence layer" analogy is built for this group
- The combination lock metaphor explains why previous approaches didn't work
- They need to understand the CATEGORY difference (personalized coaching system vs. golf app)
- Hope-forward messaging resonates: "there IS a path, you just haven't found it yet"

---

### Level 2 — Problem Aware (30%)

**Who they are:** Golfers who feel the pain deeply — stuck at the same handicap, frustrated every round, possibly considering giving up — but haven't crystallized what solution they need. They know they have a problem but may blame themselves ("maybe I just don't have talent") or external factors ("maybe golf is just too hard"). They're not actively shopping for solutions.

**Indicators:**
- Post "why can't I get better at golf" (not "what app should I use")
- Express frustration without solution-seeking language
- Say things like "I've accepted I'll never break 90"
- Describe emotional relationship with the game — love/hate
- May have stopped actively seeking help after multiple failures
- Respond to problem validation: "it's not you, it's the approach"

**Research Implications:**
- These golfers need PROBLEM-SOLUTION BRIDGING — the docu-series content is designed for this
- They need to hear that the SYSTEM was broken, not them
- The "root flaw" concept is revelatory for this group — "you mean there's ONE thing causing all of this?"
- Emotional language is critical — match their frustration, then offer the bridge
- They won't click on "best golf app" content, but they'll engage with "why you're stuck and it's not your fault"

---

### Level 1 — Unaware (5%)

**Who they are:** Golfers who don't realize their approach to improvement is flawed. They think they just need to "play more" or "practice harder" without recognizing that their method of improvement (random tips, no diagnosis, no sequencing) is the actual problem. They may be relatively new to serious improvement efforts.

**Indicators:**
- Believe "just hitting more balls" is the answer
- Haven't considered that their improvement method might be wrong
- Attribute poor play to not playing enough, weather, equipment, or luck
- May be newer golfers who haven't hit the plateau yet
- Don't engage with instruction content — just play and hope

**Research Implications:**
- This is a small group and NOT the primary target for the founders launch
- The docu-series "Confession" piece may reach some of these golfers
- Not worth significant research investment for this launch
- They would need symptom-based awakening content ("Are you doing THIS at the range? It's why you're stuck.")

### Messaging Approach

**Lead with solution education and mechanism differentiation, anchored in problem validation.**

The market is educated enough to know solutions exist (Level 3 dominant) but frustrated enough to be skeptical of new promises (Level 2 influence is strong). The messaging must:

1. **Validate the problem first** — "You're not broken. The approach is broken." (Bridges L2 → L3)
2. **Introduce the root flaw mechanism** — "There's ONE swing flaw causing everything else. Nobody diagnosed it." (Educates L3)
3. **Differentiate from known solutions** — "This isn't another tip library or stat tracker. This is a diagnostic system." (Separates from L4 comparisons)
4. **Present proof of mechanism** — AI specifics, coach credentials, user results (Converts L3-4 → L5)
5. **Close with offer** — Founders Club, 1,000 members, locked pricing (Converts L5 to buyer)

The docu-series arc maps perfectly to this awareness journey:
- "The Confession" → Level 2 validation (even WE were part of the problem)
- "The War Room" → Level 2-3 bridge (here's why we had to build something new)
- "The $3 Million Bet" → Level 3 education (here's the scale of what we built)
- "The Coaches" → Level 3-4 bridge (here's who's behind it)
- "The Breakthrough on Hole 7" → Level 4 proof (here's it working in real life)
- "72 Hours" → Level 4-5 urgency (here's your chance to get in)
- Keynote → Level 5 conversion (full argument + offer)

## Research Recommendations

### 1. Weight Problem Articulation Queries Heavily (30% of total)
- **Rationale:** 30% of the target market is at Level 2 — they feel the pain but haven't found the bridge. The docu-series STARTS with these people ("The Confession" is for them). We need their exact language.
- **Query Focus:** Frustration language, "why" questions, emotional posts about being stuck, giving up, hating golf

### 2. Prioritize Solution Exploration Queries (35% of total)
- **Rationale:** 40% at Level 3 means the largest segment is trying to figure out what type of solution will finally work. The keynote's "intelligence layer" analogy targets this group directly.
- **Query Focus:** "What actually works," comparison of approaches (not products), coaching vs. apps vs. self-study, method-level discussion

### 3. Include Robust Competitor Comparison Queries (20% of total)
- **Rationale:** 20% at Level 4 are actively comparing products. They'll encounter PG1 in the context of Skillest, Arccos, V1 Golf, etc. We need to know what they love and hate about these products.
- **Query Focus:** App-specific reviews, "worth it" threads, comparison threads, churn stories, feature wishlists

### 4. Extract Emotional Language from Level 2 Sources Heavily
- **Rationale:** The most powerful copy comes from Level 2 language — raw frustration, near-quitting moments, the "moment of despair." This language fuels the docu-series, the keynote emotional beats, and the founders page.
- **Extraction Focus:** First-person frustration narratives, "I almost quit" stories, emotional vulnerability, identity language

### 5. Investigate "Root Cause" Awareness Specifically
- **Rationale:** The PG1 mechanism depends on golfers resonating with "root flaw." Do they already think in root-cause terms, or is this new framing? Research should answer: does the market already say "one thing causing everything" or do they describe problems as a bundle of unrelated issues?
- **Query Focus:** "Root cause swing problems," "one thing ruining my golf swing," "the one change that fixed everything," "it was all because of one thing"

### 6. Research Price Anchoring from Level 4 Sources
- **Rationale:** Level 4 golfers have price reference points from competing apps. Understanding their mental models for subscription pricing ($X/month, $X/year) helps calibrate the founders offer value anchoring.
- **Query Focus:** App pricing discussions, "is X worth $Y," subscription fatigue, golf spending priorities

### 7. Cross-Reference Awareness Levels Post-Scraping
- **Rationale:** This distribution is a BASELINE estimate. After quote extraction, validate whether the 40% Level 3 estimate holds or if the market is more polarized (more Level 2 + Level 4, less Level 3).
- **Action:** Tag quotes by awareness level during extraction, recalculate distribution in Layer 2
