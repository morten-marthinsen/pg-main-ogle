# Skill 1.0-B: Prospect Awareness Mapper

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 1 (Infrastructure)
**Type:** E5 Analysis Tool
**E5 Reference:** Tool 1 - Awareness Pyramid
**Dependencies:** market_config.yaml, context_expansion.json
**Output:** awareness_baseline.json

---

## Purpose

Diagnose where the target market sits on the 5-level awareness pyramid BEFORE research begins. This baseline guides query generation and source prioritization - different awareness levels require different research approaches.

---

## The 5 Awareness Levels (Eugene Schwartz Framework)

```
LEVEL 5: MOST AWARE
├── Know your product
├── Know what it does
├── Just need the offer/deal
└── Research Focus: Competitor comparison, offer analysis

LEVEL 4: PRODUCT AWARE
├── Know products like yours exist
├── Don't know your specific product
├── Need differentiation
└── Research Focus: Competitor messaging, mechanism differentiation

LEVEL 3: SOLUTION AWARE
├── Know solutions exist
├── Don't know about specific products
├── Need education on options
└── Research Focus: Solution comparison, buyer criteria

LEVEL 2: PROBLEM AWARE
├── Know they have a problem
├── Don't know solutions exist
├── Need problem-solution bridge
└── Research Focus: Problem language, pain articulation

LEVEL 1: UNAWARE
├── Don't know they have a problem
├── Need awakening
├── Hardest to reach
└── Research Focus: Symptom language, identity triggers
```

## Execution Logic

### Step 1: Gather Awareness Signals

```
AWARENESS SIGNAL GATHERING:

From market_config and context_expansion, identify:

1. MARKET MATURITY SIGNALS
   - How long has this market existed?
   - How many competitors are there?
   - Is this a new or established category?

2. EDUCATION LEVEL SIGNALS
   - How sophisticated is the market language?
   - Do they use technical terminology?
   - Do they compare specific products?

3. SOLUTION FAMILIARITY SIGNALS
   - Do they discuss specific solutions?
   - Are they comparing options?
   - Have they tried alternatives?

4. PROBLEM ARTICULATION SIGNALS
   - How clearly do they define their problem?
   - Do they use precise language?
   - Are symptoms vs root causes distinguished?

5. COMPETITOR AWARENESS SIGNALS
   - Do they name specific competitors?
   - Are they familiar with different approaches?
   - Do they understand mechanism differences?
```

### Step 2: Analyze Market Mode

```
MODE-BASED ANALYSIS:

Mode A (Existing Product):
- Likely higher awareness (people know it exists)
- Research competitor positioning
- Focus on differentiation language
- Estimate: Typically Level 3-5

Mode B (New Product):
- May have lower awareness of THIS solution
- Research problem language heavily
- Focus on education gaps
- Estimate: Typically Level 2-4

Industry Factors:
- Established industries (finance, health) → Higher awareness
- Emerging categories → Lower awareness
- Commoditized markets → Level 5 dominant
- Novel solutions → Level 2-3 dominant
```

### Step 3: Estimate Distribution

```
DISTRIBUTION ESTIMATION:

Based on signals, estimate percentage at each level:

Example for Established Market (Day Trading):
- Level 5 (Most Aware): 15%
  - Know multiple specific courses/systems
- Level 4 (Product Aware): 30%
  - Know education products exist, comparing
- Level 3 (Solution Aware): 35%
  - Know they need training, don't know specifics
- Level 2 (Problem Aware): 15%
  - Know they're losing money, don't know what to do
- Level 1 (Unaware): 5%
  - Don't realize their approach is flawed

Example for Emerging Market (New Biohacking Tool):
- Level 5 (Most Aware): 5%
  - Early adopters who follow the space
- Level 4 (Product Aware): 10%
  - Know products like this exist
- Level 3 (Solution Aware): 25%
  - Know they want optimization, exploring options
- Level 2 (Problem Aware): 40%
  - Feel suboptimal, don't know solutions exist
- Level 1 (Unaware): 20%
  - Don't realize they could perform better

Each level needs:
- percentage: Estimated %
- indicators: What signals suggest this %
```

### Step 4: Determine Research Implications

```
RESEARCH IMPLICATIONS:

Based on primary awareness level:

LEVEL 5 DOMINANT:
- Query Emphasis: Competitor comparisons, pricing, offers
- Source Priority: Review sites, comparison pages
- Messaging: Lead with offer, differentiation

LEVEL 4 DOMINANT:
- Query Emphasis: Product comparisons, mechanism questions
- Source Priority: Forums, review sites
- Messaging: Lead with unique mechanism

LEVEL 3 DOMINANT:
- Query Emphasis: Solution exploration, "how to" questions
- Source Priority: Educational content, forums
- Messaging: Lead with solution education

LEVEL 2 DOMINANT:
- Query Emphasis: Problem articulation, "why" questions
- Source Priority: Support forums, emotional discussions
- Messaging: Lead with problem agitation

LEVEL 1 DOMINANT:
- Query Emphasis: Symptom language, identity triggers
- Source Priority: General communities, aspirational content
- Messaging: Lead with awakening, symptom connection
```

### Step 5: Generate Recommendations

```
RECOMMENDATIONS:

For each awareness level in distribution, provide:

1. QUERY RECOMMENDATIONS
   - What types of queries to prioritize
   - Language patterns to use

2. SOURCE RECOMMENDATIONS
   - Where this level congregates
   - What content they consume

3. EXTRACTION RECOMMENDATIONS
   - What to look for in quotes
   - What validates this level

Example:
- recommendation: "Prioritize competitor comparison queries"
  rationale: "35% at Level 4 means significant comparison shopping"

- recommendation: "Include 'beginner' and 'getting started' queries"
  rationale: "15% at Level 2 need education-focused content"
```

### Step 6: Validate and Output

```
VALIDATION:

- [ ] Distribution sums to 100%
- [ ] Primary level identified with confidence score
- [ ] Each level has indicators
- [ ] Implications are specific, not generic
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable

Save to: awareness_baseline.json
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. **Grounded in signals** - Not guessing, based on market evidence
2. **Distribution is realistic** - Not 100% in one level
3. **Implications are actionable** - Directly inform query generation
4. **Recommendations are specific** - Not generic advice

---

## Error Handling

| Error | Response |
|-------|----------|
| Insufficient market signals | Default to industry typical, flag uncertainty |
| Can't determine distribution | Use 20% each level, flag for validation |
| Conflicting signals | Document conflict, recommend research to resolve |
