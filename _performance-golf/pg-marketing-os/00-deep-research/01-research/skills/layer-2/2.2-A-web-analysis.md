# Skill 2.2-A: WEB Analysis (E5 Tool)

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** E5 Method - World Exclusion Bucket
**Dependencies:** theme_synthesis.json, scored_quotes.json, market_config.yaml
**Output:** web_analysis.json

---

## Purpose

Execute the WEB (World Exclusion Bucket) Analysis from Todd Brown's E5 Method. Identify the narrow, specific sub-world within the broader market that the product serves. This creates targeting precision and messaging resonance.

---

## WEB Framework

```
THE WORLD EXCLUSION BUCKET:

Your prospect exists in a specific sub-world within the broader market.

Key distinctions:
- NOT everyone interested in [market]
- NOT even everyone with [problem]
- ONLY people in [specific situation/context/phase]

A properly defined WEB:
- Is narrow enough to create recognition ("That's me!")
- Is large enough to support business goals
- Has specific characteristics that matter
- Creates natural exclusion of non-ideal prospects
```

## Analysis Logic

### Step 1: Map Market Landscape

```
IDENTIFY broad market:
- What is the overall category?
- Who potentially enters this market?
- What are the main segments?

FROM market_config and theme_synthesis:
  EXTRACT market category
  IDENTIFY observable segments

SEGMENTS might include:
- By experience level
- By capital level
- By time commitment
- By goal type
- By approach type
```

### Step 2: Identify Primary WEB

```
ANALYZE quote corpus for clustering:

LOOK FOR:
- Self-identification patterns
- Shared characteristics
- Common contexts
- Similar phases/stages

IDENTIFY who speaks most in our data:
- What are their common traits?
- What situation are they in?
- What phase of journey?
- What specific problem variant?

FORMULATE primary_web:
{
  name: [Descriptive name]
  description: [2-3 sentence definition]
  defining_characteristics: [What makes them them]
  exclusion_criteria: [Who they are NOT]
}

VALIDATE with quote_evidence
```

### Step 3: Extract Characteristics

```
FOR the primary WEB:

DEMOGRAPHIC PATTERNS:
- Age range (if evident)
- Career/life stage
- Geographic indicators
- Economic indicators

PSYCHOGRAPHIC PATTERNS:
- Beliefs about [market]
- Values expressed
- Identity elements
- Aspirations

SITUATIONAL PATTERNS:
- Current life circumstances
- Time in the problem
- Resources available
- Support structures

BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS:
- What they've tried
- How they engage
- Where they spend time
- How they make decisions
```

### Step 4: Identify Language Markers

```
EXTRACT language that defines the WEB:

SELF-IDENTIFICATION:
- How they describe themselves
- Labels they use
- "I'm a [type of person]"

IN-GROUP VOCABULARY:
- Terms only insiders use
- Jargon and abbreviations
- Shared references

RECOGNITION TRIGGERS:
- Phrases that create "that's me!" reaction
- Situations that resonate
- Problems that define them
```

### Step 5: Map WEB Journey

```
UNDERSTAND the journey within WEB:

ENTRY POINTS:
- How do people enter this WEB?
- What triggers entry?
- What do they believe upon entry?

TYPICAL PATH:
- What happens first?
- What phases do they go through?
- Where do they get stuck?

EXIT POINTS:
- How do people leave the WEB?
- Success exits (they solve it)
- Failure exits (they give up)
- Transition exits (they become different WEB)
```

### Step 6: Generate Marketing Implications

```
FROM WEB analysis:

TARGETING CRITERIA:
- Specific identifiers to target
- Platform behaviors
- Content preferences

MESSAGING HOOKS:
- What will resonate?
- What creates recognition?
- What builds trust?

EXCLUSION STATEMENTS:
- "This is NOT for [type]"
- "Don't [action] if [condition]"
- Qualification language
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. **Specificity** - WEB is narrow enough to resonate
2. **Evidence** - Multiple quotes support each characteristic
3. **Distinctness** - WEB is clearly different from broader market
4. **Usability** - Clear targeting and messaging implications
