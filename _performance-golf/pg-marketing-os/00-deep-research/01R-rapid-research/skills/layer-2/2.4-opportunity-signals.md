# Skill 2.4: Opportunity Signals

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Analysis)
**Type:** Opportunity Detection
**Dependencies:** quotes_validated.json, patterns.md
**Output:** opportunity_signals.md

---

## PURPOSE

Surface unmet needs, underserved segments, positioning gaps, and messaging gaps visible in the evidence. Each signal must be grounded in quote evidence (3+ quotes minimum). These are inputs for Big Idea development, positioning strategy, and campaign angles.

---

## INPUT

```yaml
input:
  quotes_validated: quotes_validated.json
  patterns: patterns.md
```

---

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Identify Unmet Needs

```
SCAN quotes for expressed desires without stated solutions:

UNMET NEED indicators:
  - "I wish there was..." / "I'd pay anything for..."
  - "Why doesn't anyone..." / "Someone should..."
  - Complaints about existing solutions with no positive alternatives mentioned
  - Gaps between hope expressions and solutions tried expressions

FOR each potential unmet need:
  - State the need clearly
  - Count supporting quotes
  - Note if any existing solutions partially address it
```

### Step 2: Identify Underserved Segments

```
LOOK for audience subsets with distinct pain/hope patterns:

SEGMENT indicators:
  - Demographic mentions: age, gender, experience level
  - Situational mentions: business golf, competitive play, casual
  - Identity mentions: "as a beginner...", "for us older guys..."
  - Geographic/contextual mentions

FOR each potential segment:
  - Define the segment
  - Note their unique pain/hope expressions
  - Assess if current market adequately serves them
```

### Step 3: Identify Positioning Gaps

```
ANALYZE the competitive landscape implied by quotes:

POSITIONING GAP indicators:
  - "Every product does X but none do Y"
  - Complaints about category conventions
  - Requests for combined solutions
  - Frustration with either/or tradeoffs

FOR each potential positioning gap:
  - State what's missing in current positioning
  - Identify the opportunity for differentiation
  - Note quote evidence
```

### Step 4: Identify Messaging Gaps

```
LOOK for emotional territories not being addressed:

MESSAGING GAP indicators:
  - Strong emotional expressions without matching solutions language
  - Pain language that doesn't match typical marketing language
  - Hope language that marketers aren't using
  - Objections that aren't being preemptively addressed

FOR each potential messaging gap:
  - State the emotional territory
  - Note how current marketing misses it
  - Suggest the messaging opportunity
```

## OUTPUT

**File:** `opportunity_signals.md` saved to project folder

**Contents:**
- 3-5 opportunity signals with evidence
- Type classification for each
- Implications and potential actions

---

## CONSTRAINTS

1. NEVER report an opportunity without 3+ supporting quotes.
2. NEVER speculate beyond what the evidence shows.
3. ALWAYS ground signals in specific quotes, not general impressions.
4. NEVER suggest opportunities that require capabilities beyond rapid research scope.
5. ALWAYS classify signal type — different types require different responses.

---

## SIGNAL PRIORITIZATION

```
HIGH priority signals:
  - 10+ supporting quotes
  - 4+ distinct sources
  - Clear, actionable implication
  - Not already being exploited by competitors (based on evidence)

MEDIUM priority signals:
  - 5-9 supporting quotes
  - 2-3 distinct sources
  - Actionable but may require validation

LOW priority signals:
  - 3-4 supporting quotes
  - May be noise, not signal
  - Worth noting but not acting on without more evidence
```

---

## ERROR HANDLING

| Error | Response |
|-------|----------|
| No clear opportunities emerge | Report "No strong opportunity signals — market may be well-served" |
| All signals are low-evidence | Report with caveat, recommend full research |
| Opportunity conflicts with evidence | Do not report — must be evidence-supported |

---

## ANTI-DEGRADATION RULE REFERENCES

- **Rule 8:** Do not synthesize during assembly — opportunities are identified here, assembly just compiles.
- **Rule 9:** Flag what you didn't do — opportunity signals from rapid research are preliminary.

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

```markdown
# opportunity_signals.md

## Opportunity Signals

Generated: 2026-03-21
Based on: 162 quotes from 5 sources

---

## 1. The Simplicity Gap

**Type:** positioning_gap

**Signal:** Golfers are overwhelmed by complex swing mechanics advice and desperately want "one simple thing" to focus on. Current market offers comprehensive systems, not focused solutions.

**Evidence Strength:** strong (15 quotes)

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "Just tell me ONE thing to focus on. I can't remember 15 swing thoughts."
> — overwhelmed_golfer, Reddit

> "Every instructor adds more stuff. I need less, not more."
> — keep_it_simple, GolfWRX

> "The best round I ever had was when I only thought about one thing."
> — one_thought, Reddit

> "Why does every program have to be so complicated? Just give me the one thing that matters."
> — simple_golfer, Reddit

**Implication:** There's an opportunity to position as "the anti-complexity" solution. While competitors pile on features and lessons, a focused "one thing" positioning could cut through.

**Potential Action:** Lead with "The One [Thing/Move/Fix]" as the Big Idea frame. Emphasize simplicity over comprehensiveness.

---

## 2. The Social Golfer Segment

**Type:** underserved_segment

**Signal:** Business golfers and those who play socially (not competitively) have distinct pain around embarrassment and pressure, but most marketing speaks to competitive improvement.

**Evidence Strength:** strong (18 quotes)

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "I don't care about my handicap. I just don't want to embarrass myself with clients."
> — biz_golfer, Reddit

> "I'd pay anything to just feel comfortable on the first tee with my boss."
> — corporate_hacker, GolfWRX

> "I don't need to win. I need to not be the worst one there."
> — social_golfer, Reddit

**Implication:** Marketing that speaks to "confidence in social golf situations" rather than "lower your score" may resonate with a segment that's being ignored.

**Potential Action:** Consider a campaign angle targeting "client golf" or "first tee confidence" — this is an emotional territory competitors aren't addressing.

---

## 3. The Training Aid Graveyard Objection

**Type:** messaging_gap

**Signal:** Significant skepticism about new products due to history of failed training aid purchases. Current marketing doesn't preemptively address this objection.

**Evidence Strength:** moderate (8 quotes)

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "I've got a garage full of swing trainers. None of them helped."
> — gadget_graveyard, Reddit

> "Another $50 gimmick that'll end up in the closet."
> — skeptical_buyer, GolfWRX

> "I've spent hundreds on training aids. Why would this one be different?"
> — been_burned, Reddit

**Implication:** Copy MUST address this objection head-on. Acknowledging the "graveyard" and differentiating is critical for conversion.

**Potential Action:** Include "this isn't another gadget for your garage" messaging. Lead with proof/testimonials from former skeptics.

---

## 4. The Grip Revelation

**Type:** unmet_need

**Signal:** Multiple golfers discovered grip issues were their root cause after years of other fixes. There may be demand for a "grip-first" diagnostic or solution.

**Evidence Strength:** moderate (6 quotes)

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "I spent $800 on a new driver before someone told me my grip was completely wrong."
> — grip_convert, GolfWRX

> "Three years of lessons and nobody ever looked at my grip."
> — missed_basics, Reddit

**Implication:** Either a grip-focused product or a diagnostic that starts with grip fundamentals could address this unmet need.

**Potential Action:** Consider grip as a mechanism component, or as a lead magnet angle ("The 5-Second Grip Test").

---

## 5. The YouTube Backlash

**Type:** messaging_gap

**Signal:** Strong frustration with conflicting YouTube advice. Opportunity to position against the "YouTube confusion" and offer clarity.

**Evidence Strength:** moderate (7 quotes)

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "I've watched probably 500 YouTube videos and I'm worse than when I started."
> — youtube_addict, Reddit

> "One video says keep my head down, another says that's a myth. I don't know who to believe."
> — confused_viewer, Reddit

**Implication:** Anti-YouTube positioning could resonate. "Cut through the noise" or "one voice, one system" messaging.

**Potential Action:** Use YouTube confusion as a villain in copy. Position product as the antidote to information overload.

---

| 2 | The Social Golfer Segment | underserved_segment | strong | high |
| 3 | Training Aid Graveyard Objection | messaging_gap | moderate | high |
| 4 | The Grip Revelation | unmet_need | moderate | medium |
| 5 | The YouTube Backlash | messaging_gap | moderate | medium |
```
