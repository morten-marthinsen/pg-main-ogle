# Skill 2.2: Hypothesis Validation

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Analysis)
**Type:** Hypothesis Testing
**Dependencies:** quotes_validated.json, market_config.yaml (for hypotheses)
**Output:** hypothesis_verdicts.md

---

## PURPOSE

Test each hypothesis from intake against the full quote dataset. Assign verdicts: VALIDATED (strong evidence), INVALIDATED (counter-evidence dominates), or INCONCLUSIVE (insufficient evidence). If no hypotheses were provided, run in open-scout mode and surface the top 5 emergent findings.

---

## INPUT

```yaml
input:
  quotes_validated: quotes_validated.json
  market_config: market_config.yaml  # Contains hypotheses array
```

---

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Hypotheses

```
FROM market_config.hypotheses:

IF hypotheses.length > 0:
  mode = "hypothesis_testing"
  hypotheses_to_test = market_config.hypotheses (max 3)

ELSE:
  mode = "open_scout"
  hypotheses_to_test = []
```

### Step 2: Test Each Hypothesis (Hypothesis Mode)

```
FOR each hypothesis:

  SCAN all quotes for evidence:

  SUPPORTING evidence:
    - Quotes that directly confirm the hypothesis
    - Quotes that describe the hypothesized pattern
    - Quotes that express the hypothesized sentiment

  CONTRADICTING evidence:
    - Quotes that directly contradict the hypothesis
    - Quotes that show opposite patterns
    - Quotes that express contrary sentiment

  COUNT:
    - supporting_quotes = number of supporting quotes
    - supporting_sources = number of distinct sources for supporting quotes
    - contradicting_quotes = number of contradicting quotes
    - contradicting_sources = number of distinct sources for contradicting quotes
```

### Step 3: Assign Verdict

```
VERDICT CRITERIA:

VALIDATED:
  - supporting_quotes >= 10 AND
  - supporting_sources >= 3 AND
  - supporting_quotes > (contradicting_quotes * 2)

INVALIDATED:
  - contradicting_quotes >= 10 AND
  - contradicting_sources >= 3 AND
  - contradicting_quotes > (supporting_quotes * 2)

INCONCLUSIVE:
  - Neither VALIDATED nor INVALIDATED criteria met
  - OR total relevant quotes < 10

FOR each verdict, capture:
  - verdict: VALIDATED | INVALIDATED | INCONCLUSIVE
  - confidence: high (clear evidence) | medium (mixed evidence) | low (thin evidence)
  - supporting_evidence: [top 3 supporting quotes]
  - contradicting_evidence: [top contradicting quotes, if any]
  - reasoning: brief explanation of verdict
```

### Step 4: Open Scout Mode (If No Hypotheses)

```
IF mode == "open_scout":

  INSTEAD of testing hypotheses:

  1. Review pattern analysis from 2.1
  2. Identify 5 most significant findings from the data:
     - Strongest pain patterns
     - Surprising insights
     - Gaps in existing solutions
     - Dominant emotional themes
     - Unexpected audience segments

  FOR each finding:
    - finding: statement of the finding
    - evidence_strength: strong | moderate | weak
    - supporting_quotes: 3-5 quotes
    - implication: what this means for marketing/product

  OUTPUT as "Open Scout Findings" instead of hypothesis verdicts
```

## OUTPUT

**File:** `hypothesis_verdicts.md` saved to project folder

**Contents:**
- Verdict for each hypothesis (or Open Scout Findings)
- Evidence counts and representative quotes
- Summary table

---

## CONSTRAINTS

1. NEVER validate a hypothesis with < 10 supporting quotes.
2. NEVER validate a hypothesis from a single source — need 3+ sources.
3. NEVER ignore contradicting evidence — always report it.
4. NEVER invent supporting quotes — evidence must exist in quotes_validated.json.
5. ALWAYS report INCONCLUSIVE if evidence is thin — don't stretch for a verdict.

---

## VALIDATION THRESHOLDS

```
STRONG VALIDATION (high confidence):
  - 15+ supporting quotes
  - 4+ distinct sources
  - < 3 contradicting quotes

MODERATE VALIDATION (medium confidence):
  - 10-14 supporting quotes
  - 3+ distinct sources
  - < 5 contradicting quotes

WEAK VALIDATION (low confidence):
  - 10+ supporting quotes BUT
  - Only 2 sources OR
  - 5-8 contradicting quotes

INVALIDATION requires same thresholds but for contradicting evidence
```

---

## ERROR HANDLING

| Error | Response |
|-------|----------|
| Hypothesis too vague to test | Mark INCONCLUSIVE, note "hypothesis too broad" |
| No relevant quotes found | Mark INCONCLUSIVE, note "no relevant evidence" |
| Equal support/contradiction | Mark INCONCLUSIVE, note "mixed evidence" |
| < 10 total relevant quotes | Mark INCONCLUSIVE, recommend more research |

---

## ANTI-DEGRADATION RULE REFERENCES

- **Rule 6:** Do not bias scraping toward hypotheses — this skill tests AFTER collection.
- **Rule 10:** Recommend upgrade when evidence is thin — INCONCLUSIVE = thin evidence.

```markdown
## Hypothesis Validation Results

## Hypothesis 1: "Most amateur golfers blame equipment when the real issue is grip"

**Verdict: VALIDATED**
**Confidence: medium**

### Evidence Summary

- Supporting quotes: 14 from 4 sources
- Contradicting quotes: 3 from 2 sources

### Supporting Evidence

> "I spent $800 on a new driver before someone told me my grip was completely wrong. Fixed my grip, old driver works fine now."
> — grip_convert, GolfWRX

> "Everyone's talking about what club to buy when they can't even hold it right. Grip is 90% of the game IMO."
> — teaching_pro, Reddit

> "Turns out my slice had nothing to do with my shaft flex. It was my grip pressure the whole time."
> — former_slicer, Reddit

### Contradicting Evidence

> "Got fit for the right clubs and it made a huge difference. Sometimes it really is the equipment."
> — club_fitter, GolfWRX

### Reasoning

Strong pattern of quotes indicating grip correction solved problems that equipment purchases did not. Multiple sources confirm the pattern. Some contradicting evidence exists but is significantly outweighed.

---

## Hypothesis 2: "Golfers are frustrated by conflicting advice from different instructors"

**Verdict: VALIDATED**
**Confidence: high**

### Evidence Summary

- Supporting quotes: 18 from 5 sources
- Contradicting quotes: 1 from 1 source

### Supporting Evidence

> "One video says keep my head down, another says that's a myth. I don't know who to believe anymore."
> — confused_newbie, Reddit

> "My instructor says one thing, YouTube says another, my playing partner has yet another opinion. I'm paralyzed."
> — info_overload, GolfWRX

> "I've watched probably 500 YouTube videos and I'm worse than when I started. Too many conflicting tips."
> — youtube_addict, Reddit

### Contradicting Evidence

None significant.

### Reasoning

Strong, consistent pattern across multiple sources. High quote count with minimal contradiction. Clear emotional intensity in the supporting quotes. High confidence validation.

---

## Hypothesis 3: "Price is the main barrier to golf lessons"

**Verdict: INCONCLUSIVE**
**Confidence: low**

### Evidence Summary

- Supporting quotes: 4 from 2 sources
- Contradicting quotes: 6 from 3 sources

### Supporting Evidence

> "At $150/hour, lessons aren't really an option for most of us."
> — budget_golfer, Reddit

### Contradicting Evidence

> "It's not the cost, it's the time commitment. I'd pay more if I could see faster results."
> — time_poor, GolfWRX

> "I've spent money on lessons. The issue is they don't stick."
> — lesson_skeptic, Reddit

### Reasoning

Mixed evidence. Some price sensitivity exists but multiple quotes suggest other factors (time, effectiveness, habit formation) are equal or greater barriers. Insufficient evidence to validate or invalidate.
```

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT (Open Scout Mode)

```markdown
# hypothesis_verdicts.md

## Open Scout Findings

Generated: 2026-03-21
Mode: open_scout
Total quotes analyzed: 162

*No hypotheses provided — surfacing top findings from evidence base.*

---

## Finding 1: Inconsistency Is The Dominant Pain

**Evidence Strength: strong**

The single most common theme across pain quotes is unpredictable results — golfers who can hit good shots but can't reproduce them. This appears in 28 quotes from 4 sources.

**Supporting Quotes:**

> "I'll hit 3 beautiful drives in a row, then shank the 4th into the woods."
> — frustrated_golfer22, Reddit

> "The maddening thing is I KNOW I can do it. I've done it before."
> — weekend_warrior, GolfWRX

**Implication:** Messaging should focus on consistency/reliability rather than distance or technique improvement.

---

## Finding 2: Social Embarrassment Drives Urgency

**Evidence Strength: strong**

Fear of embarrassment in front of others is a powerful emotional driver — particularly on the first tee and in business golf settings. 18 quotes across 3 sources.

**Implication:** Lead generation could target business golfers or those with upcoming golf events.

---

## Finding 3: YouTube Information Overload

**Evidence Strength: moderate**

Significant frustration with conflicting advice from YouTube, instructors, and peers. 15 quotes across 3 sources.

**Implication:** Positioning as "the one simple thing" or "cut through the noise" could resonate.

---

## Finding 4: Training Aid Skepticism

**Evidence Strength: moderate**

History of failed training aid purchases has created skepticism. 8 quotes mentioning "garage full of gadgets."

**Implication:** Must overcome objection about "another gimmick" — proof/testimonials critical.

---

## Finding 5: Desire for Simplicity

**Evidence Strength: moderate**

Repeated theme of wanting "one thing" to focus on rather than complex swing thoughts. 12 quotes across 4 sources.

**Implication:** Simple, focused mechanism likely to resonate over comprehensive systems.
```
