## Structural Analysis

### Body Type Pattern: LB (List-Based)
1. **News hook** (2 paragraphs): "I just added a 2nd group" — makes this feel like an update, not a pitch.
2. **Setup line**: "Which means 3 things:" — clean transition into the list.
3. **3-item numbered list**: Each item is a benefit of the split (spots open, customization for entrepreneurs, customization for writers).
4. **Value expansion** (4 paragraphs): Credentials, mastermind benefits, community language.
5. **Scarcity CTA**: "only a couple of spots available" + link.
6. **Loss framing**: "you risk being left out for up to a year if you dither" — opportunity cost language.

### Key Techniques
- **Update framing** — "Just double-checking" and "2nd group added" makes this feel like news, not marketing
- **Numbered list with natural flow** — items 2 and 3 are grammatically connected ("so we now have one group... and one group..."), making the list feel conversational rather than choppy
- **Specific scarcity** — "at least 3 new people" is more credible than "limited spots"
- **Risk reversal** — "You risk zip by checking it out" vs. "you risk being left out for up to a year" — asymmetric risk framing
- **Charter member appeal** — "bragging rights to being a charter member" — appeals to early-adopter identity
- **Carlton signature touches** — "Stay frosty," sign-off, ellipsis pacing, parenthetical asides
