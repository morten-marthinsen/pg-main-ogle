## Structural Analysis

### Body Type Pattern: TM (Testimonial)
1. **Testimonial block** (3 paragraphs): Chad Kettner's testimonial leads the entire email. Full attribution (name, title).
2. **Separator**: "* * * * *" — visual break between testimonial and Clayton's voice.
3. **Brief reaction** (1 line): "This is Clayton again with a quick reminder:" — minimal personal commentary.
4. **Bullet summary** (6 lines): Condensed offer recap using bullet-style stacking.
5. **Urgency close**: "BUT ONLY if you take action quickly" — hard deadline.
6. **Simple CTA**: Single link.

### Key Techniques
- **Testimonial-first structure** — the social proof comes BEFORE any selling. Reader processes the endorsement before encountering the pitch.
- **Third-person endorsement** — Chad says "you should buy" directly. This is more persuasive than any self-promotion.
- **Minimal seller voice** — Clayton adds only ~100 words of his own. The testimonial does the heavy lifting.
- **Stacked benefits** — rapid-fire bullet recap assumes the reader already knows the offer; this is a reminder email, not a first touch.
- **"Copy cub" language** — "I've taught my copy cubs -- all of whom are now earning million-dollar paydays" — creates aspirational identity.
- **Price anchoring** — $600 savings, $1,293 in bonuses, $97/month entry point — multiple price frames.
- **Loss framing** — "you WILL pay at least $600 more" — emphasizes cost of inaction over benefit of action.
