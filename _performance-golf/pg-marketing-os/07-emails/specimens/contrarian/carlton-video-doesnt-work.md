## Structural Analysis

### Body Type Pattern: CT (Contrarian)
1. **Conventional wisdom stated** (3 lines): "Video doesn't work." — quotes what "everyone" says.
2. **Mock agreement** (2 lines): "They offer proof... Proof. Right?" — appears to validate before dismantling.
3. **Pivot/reframe** (1 line): "Uh... not exactly." — the contrarian turn.
4. **Real explanation** (3 paragraphs): "If your video sucks, it will bomb. Same as a sucky sales letter." — shifts blame from the medium to the execution.
5. **Contrarian proof** (4 paragraphs): Low-rent video can crush expensive production. Expert endorsement (Andy Jenkins).
6. **Self-credential bridge**: "And you're hearing this from a fairly well-respected copywriter" — positions himself as unlikely advocate for video.
7. **Double CTA**: Link appears twice with different framing (first time: "for free", second time: "from a trusted colleague").

### Key Techniques
- **Quote-the-crowd-then-demolish** — states the conventional wisdom in quotes, then systematically destroys it
- **Short punchy lines** — "Proof. Right?" / "Uh... not exactly." — creates conversational rhythm
- **Parenthetical asides** — "(from modest to grandiose)", "(because it's done by amateurs)" — Carlton's signature voice device
- **Self-aware credentialing** — "And you're hearing this from a fairly well-respected copywriter, remember" — acknowledges his authority without being heavy-handed
- **Anti-perfection argument** — "crappiest camera, weakest old-as-dirt computer" — the contrarian claim within the contrarian claim (bad equipment = good results)
- **Ellipsis pacing** — "..." used 12+ times to create pauses, build suspense, and simulate spoken rhythm
