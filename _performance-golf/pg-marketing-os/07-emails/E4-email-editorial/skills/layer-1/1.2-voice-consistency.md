# Layer 1.2 -- Voice Consistency Check

## Purpose

Verify persona voice consistency at TWO levels: (1) within each individual email (no mid-email voice shifts), and (2) across the entire sequence (all emails sound like the same writer). Voice drift is one of the most common AI email failures -- the model shifts register, formality, vocabulary, or personality between emails without realizing it.

---

## Input Requirements

```yaml
from_layer_0:
  loaded_sequence:
    emails: [object]  # all parsed emails with body text
  quality_baseline:
    per_email_scores: [object]  # baseline voice consistency scores (criterion 5)

campaign_context:
  persona_voice: string  # the persona voice these emails were generated in
  niche: string  # health, finance, golf, personal-dev, technology
```

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Establish Voice Fingerprint

Read emails 1-3 (the sequence opening) to establish the voice baseline:

```
VOICE FINGERPRINT DIMENSIONS:
  1. FORMALITY REGISTER
     - Casual (slang, contractions, incomplete sentences)
     - Conversational (natural speech, moderate formality)
     - Semi-formal (professional but warm)
     - Formal (no contractions, complete sentences, professional)
     Score: 1-5 scale (1=very casual, 5=very formal)

  2. VOCABULARY LEVEL
     - Simple (8th grade reading level)
     - Moderate (10th-12th grade)
     - Advanced (college level)
     - Specialized (industry jargon heavy)
     Note: What specific vocabulary markers appear?

  3. PERSONALITY MARKERS
     - Humor: [none | occasional | frequent | dominant]
     - Confidence: [tentative | moderate | confident | bold]
     - Warmth: [distant | neutral | warm | intimate]
     - Edge: [none | mild | moderate | sharp]
     Note: What specific personality traits are displayed?

  4. SENTENCE PATTERNS
     - Average sentence length (words)
     - Use of fragments: [none | occasional | frequent]
     - Use of questions: [none | occasional | frequent]
     - Paragraph length pattern

  5. SIGNATURE PHRASES
     - Recurring transition words/phrases
     - Sign-off style
     - CTA language patterns
     - Bridge phrase patterns
```

### Step 2: Check Within-Email Voice Consistency

For EVERY email:

```
FOR email IN loaded_sequence.emails:

  1. READ the complete email from opening to sign-off
  2. COMPARE voice in opening vs. middle vs. closing
  3. CHECK for these within-email voice breaks:

     REGISTER SHIFT:
       Opening is casual, but middle becomes formal
       Content section sounds different from CTA section
       Bridge section shifts to a "marketing voice"

     VOCABULARY SHIFT:
       Simple language in content, complex in pitch
       Technical terms appear suddenly without buildup
       Niche-inappropriate language in any section

     PERSONALITY SHIFT:
       Humor disappears in pitch section
       Confidence level changes between sections
       Warmth drops when discussing the product

  4. SCORE within-email consistency: 1-10
  5. IF score < 7: Identify the specific break point(s)
  6. CLASSIFY severity:
     - Score < 5 → P1 (voice completely breaks within email)
     - Score 5-6 → P2 (noticeable shift, reader feels it)
     - Score 7 → P3 (minor wobble, most readers won't notice)
     - Score 8+ → No issue
```

### Step 3: Check Across-Sequence Voice Consistency

Compare ALL emails against the voice fingerprint established in Step 1:

```
FOR email IN loaded_sequence.emails:

  1. MEASURE email against voice fingerprint:
     - Formality: How far does this email deviate from baseline?
     - Vocabulary: Any new vocabulary not seen in fingerprint?
     - Personality: Do humor/confidence/warmth/edge match?
     - Sentence patterns: Similar lengths and structures?
     - Signature phrases: Consistent use of recurring phrases?

  2. CALCULATE voice deviation score (0-10):
     0 = identical to fingerprint
     10 = completely different voice

  3. FLAG emails with deviation > 3:
     - Deviation 4-5 → P3 (minor drift, addressable)
     - Deviation 6-7 → P2 (noticeable -- reader may sense different writer)
     - Deviation 8+ → P1 (different persona entirely)

  4. IDENTIFY specific deviation dimensions:
     "Email 7 is [+2 formality, -1 warmth] vs. baseline"
```

### Step 4: Check for Cross-Niche Contamination

Using niche-adaptation-guide.md, verify no cross-niche language has leaked in:

```
FORBIDDEN PATTERNS (from niche-adaptation-guide.md):
  - Health language in finance emails ("toxin," "detox," "healing")
  - Finance language in health emails ("portfolio," "returns," "invest")
  - Golf language in non-golf emails ("swing," "fairway," "driver")
  - Guru language in any niche ("unlock your potential," "manifest")
  - Tech buzzwords outside tech ("disrupt," "leverage," "optimize")

IF cross-niche contamination detected:
  Severity: P2 (niche contamination damages credibility)
```

### Step 5: Compile Voice Consistency Report

---

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Remediation |
|---------|-------------|
| Fewer than 3 emails in sequence (can't establish fingerprint) | Use email 1 only as fingerprint. Note reduced confidence. |
| Persona voice not specified in campaign context | Infer voice from content. Note that without explicit persona, voice checking is approximate. |
| All emails have high deviation (no clear baseline) | Flag as systemic P1 issue: "Sequence lacks consistent voice -- may need regeneration." |
| Niche guide unavailable | Skip cross-niche contamination check. Log limitation. |
