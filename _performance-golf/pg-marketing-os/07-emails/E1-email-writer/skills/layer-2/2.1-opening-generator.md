> **Layer:** 2 (Generation)
> **Skill:** E1-email-writer
> **Purpose:** Generate type-specific opening hook (first 3 lines)
> **Inputs:** `structural_template.sections.opening` from 1.2, `content_map.section_mapping.opening` from 1.3, `structural_specimens` from 0.2
> **Outputs:** Opening hook text (1-3 lines) ready for email assembly

---

## FUNCTION

Generate the opening hook for this email following the body-type-specific pattern. The opening is the MOST CRITICAL section of any email -- it determines whether the reader continues or deletes. Every body type has a different opening technique. The opening must create immediate engagement within 3 lines maximum.

**Key Principle:** The opening is NOT a summary of the email. It is a HOOK -- a pattern interrupt that makes the reader want to keep reading. The best openings create curiosity, tension, surprise, or recognition in the first sentence.

---

## BODY-TYPE-SPECIFIC OPENING PATTERNS

### CT (Contrarian): Bold Provocative Claim

```
PATTERN: Open with a statement most readers would disagree with or find surprising.
         No preamble. No "I want to talk about..." Lead with the contrarian claim.

EXAMPLES (structural pattern, not actual copy):
  "Everything your doctor told you about cholesterol is wrong."
  "The best salespeople never close."
  "Your morning routine is killing your productivity."

GENERATION RULES:
  1. First sentence IS the contrarian claim -- no build-up
  2. Claim must be specific (not "everything is wrong" vagueness)
  3. Claim must be defensible (you'll defend it in the body)
  4. Max 3 lines for entire opening
  5. No questions -- CT opens with STATEMENTS
```

### QO (Quote-Opener): Formatted Quote Block

```
PATTERN: Open with a formatted, attributed quote set apart visually.

FORMAT:
  "[Quote text here.]"
  -- [Author Name], [Context/Title]

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Quote must be visually separated (blank line above and below)
  2. Attribution is REQUIRED (author + context)
  3. Quote must be relevant to email topic
  4. Quote must be from a credible or interesting source
  5. Max 3 lines for entire opening (quote + attribution)
  6. If quote is long, trim to essential phrase
```

### TM (Testimonial): Attribution Line

```
PATTERN: Open with who is speaking and why you're sharing their story.

EXAMPLES:
  "[Name] from [City], [State] just sent me this..."
  "I got this email from [Name] last week and had to share it..."
  "[Name], a [brief context], wrote in to say..."

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Name and location/context required
  2. Brief setup (1-2 sentences max)
  3. Creates anticipation for the testimonial
  4. Keep it SHORT -- the testimonial is the star, not the intro
```

### QA (Q&A): Reader Question Setup

```
PATTERN: Open with attribution of the reader who asked the question.

EXAMPLES:
  "Reader [Name] wrote in and asked..."
  "[Name] from [City] sent me this question last week..."
  "Got a great question from [Name] that I think a lot of you are wondering about..."

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Attribution (name, optionally location)
  2. Signal that a question is coming
  3. Create interest in the question itself
  4. Max 3 lines -- then display the question
```

### LB (List-Based): Setup/Promise

```
PATTERN: Open with why this list matters and what the reader will get from it.

EXAMPLES:
  "There are [N] things I wish someone had told me when I first started..."
  "Most people get [topic] wrong for [N] preventable reasons."
  "Here are the [N] [things] that separate [desirable group] from [undesirable group]:"

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Number in the opening (creates specific expectation)
  2. Why this list matters to the reader
  3. Promise of value (what they'll learn)
  4. Max 3 lines -- then into the list
```

### ST (Story): Story Hook

```
PATTERN: Open with a specific detail that creates immediacy and pulls into the narrative.

EXAMPLES:
  "The other day I was sitting in a coffee shop when..."
  "Last Tuesday, at exactly 3:47 PM, everything changed for..."
  "Years ago, before I knew anything about [topic], I made a mistake that..."

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Specific detail (time, place, sensation) -- NOT vague
  2. Creates immediacy ("The other day...", "Last week...", "Years ago...")
  3. Implies conflict or tension (something is about to happen)
  4. Max 3 lines -- then into the narrative
  5. DO NOT give away the ending in the opening
```

### NR (Negative Response): Troll/Critic Display

```
PATTERN: Open by displaying the troll or critic comment verbatim.

EXAMPLES:
  "Someone on [platform] left this comment:"
  "I got this email yesterday from someone who clearly disagrees:"
  "Here's what [Name] had to say about [topic]:"

FOLLOWED BY: The actual criticism/troll comment, formatted and set apart.

GENERATION RULES:
  1. Brief setup (1 sentence) introducing the criticism
  2. Display the criticism verbatim or near-verbatim
  3. Criticism must be specific enough to demolish
  4. Set criticism apart visually (quote formatting)
  5. Max 3 lines for setup + first line of criticism
```

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 2.1-opening-generator:

  STEP 1: Load Inputs
    1. READ structural_template.sections.opening (from 1.2)
    2. READ content_map.section_mapping.opening (from 1.3)
    3. HOLD structural_specimens in active context

  STEP 2: Study Specimen Openings
    FOR each loaded specimen:
      1. ISOLATE the opening (first 1-3 lines)
      2. IDENTIFY what makes it work (hook mechanism)
      3. NOTE word count, sentence structure, rhythm
    INTERNALIZE the opening pattern for this body type

  STEP 3: Generate Opening
    1. FOLLOW the body-type-specific pattern (from above)
    2. USE the content assigned to opening section (from content_map)
    3. APPLY tone constraint from routing_decision
    4. ENSURE max 3 lines
    5. ENSURE hook mechanism is present (curiosity, tension, surprise, or recognition)

  STEP 4: Quality Check
    [ ] Opening is 3 lines or fewer
    [ ] Opening creates immediate engagement (would reader keep reading?)
    [ ] Opening follows body-type-specific pattern
    [ ] Opening uses assigned content (not random)
    [ ] Opening has zero slop words
    [ ] Opening tone matches body type requirement
    [ ] Opening doesn't give away the email's conclusion

  STEP 5: Output
    WRITE opening text to output file
    INCLUDE: opening text, word count, hook mechanism identified, body type compliance
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Opening exceeds 3 lines | HALT -- Compress opening |
| CRITICAL | Opening has no hook mechanism | Regenerate with stronger hook |
| CRITICAL | Opening doesn't match body type pattern | Regenerate following correct pattern |
| WARNING | Opening uses assigned content loosely | Tighten alignment to content map |

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. **Burying the lead** -- The hook must be in the FIRST sentence, not the third.
2. **Generic openings** -- "I wanted to reach out..." / "In today's email..." / "I hope you're doing well" are BANNED.
3. **Giving away the conclusion** -- Opening creates TENSION, not resolution.
4. **Exceeding 3 lines** -- This is a structural constraint, not a suggestion.
5. **Wrong body type pattern** -- A CT email must open with a bold claim, not a question (that's QA).
