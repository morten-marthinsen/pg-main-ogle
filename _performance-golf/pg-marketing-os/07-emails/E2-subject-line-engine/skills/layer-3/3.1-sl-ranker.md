# 3.1-sl-ranker.md

> **Layer:** 3 (Selection & Output)
> **Skill:** E2-subject-line-engine
> **Purpose:** Score all finalist SL candidates against 7 SL-specific criteria and produce final ranking
> **Outputs:** `sl_ranking` object with scored and ranked SLs ready for output packaging

---

## FUNCTION

Score every finalist SL candidate (post-Arena, human-selected top 10-15) against 7 SL-specific criteria with detailed evidence for each score. Produce a weighted ranking that identifies the top 5-10 SLs for the output package. This is the final quality validation before packaging -- no SL should leave this step without rigorous scoring.

---

## THE 7 SL-SPECIFIC SCORING CRITERIA

| # | Criterion | Weight | Definition |
|---|-----------|--------|------------|
| 1 | **Curiosity Gap Strength** | 20% | Does the SL create genuine need-to-know? Is the gap calibrated -- strong enough to compel but resolvable? |
| 2 | **Emotional Trigger Clarity** | 15% | Is there one clear emotional pull? Can you name the specific emotion in one word? |
| 3 | **SL-Body Alignment** | 15% | Does the SL accurately represent the email content? Would the reader feel satisfied after opening? |
| 4 | **Word Economy** | 15% | Maximum impact per word. 6-7 word sweet spot. Every word earns its place. No filler. |
| 5 | **Formula Execution** | 10% | Does the SL properly execute its assigned formula? Pattern mastery visible? |
| 6 | **Niche Calibration** | 10% | Is vocabulary, tone, and reference set appropriate for the target audience? |
| 7 | **Originality** | 10% | Does it avoid cliche? Would a subscriber notice this SL among 50 others in their inbox? |
| 8 | **Reuse Potential** | 5% | Could this SL pattern work for future similar emails? Structural transferability? |

## SCORING PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 3.1-sl-ranker:
  INPUT: Arena-selected SLs (from Layer 2.5/human selection), email_context, niche_calibration
  OUTPUT: sl_ranking object

  1. LOAD all human-selected SL candidates (top 10-15 from Arena)

  2. FOR each SL candidate:

     a. SCORE Curiosity Gap Strength (20%):
        - What is the implicit question or gap?
        - How strong is the pull to find the answer?
        - Is the gap resolvable by reading the email?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     b. SCORE Emotional Trigger Clarity (15%):
        - What emotion does this SL target?
        - Is it one clear trigger or multiple competing?
        - How immediately does the emotion register?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     c. SCORE SL-Body Alignment (15%):
        - Re-read the email body
        - What does this SL promise or imply?
        - Does the email DELIVER on that promise?
        - Would the reader feel satisfied or tricked?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence
        - AUTO-REJECT if below 7.0

     d. SCORE Word Economy (15%):
        - How many words? (Count exactly)
        - Can ANY word be removed without losing meaning?
        - Is every word pulling maximum weight?
        - Is it in the 6-7 word sweet spot?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     e. SCORE Formula Execution (10%):
        - What formula category is this?
        - Does it follow the formula's structural pattern?
        - Is the execution sharp or loose?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     f. SCORE Niche Calibration (10%):
        - Does vocabulary match the target audience?
        - Any cross-niche contamination?
        - Would this audience recognize this language as "theirs"?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     g. SCORE Originality (10%):
        - Has this exact pattern been used thousands of times?
        - Would a subscriber notice this among 50 inbox SLs?
        - Is there a fresh element (word, angle, twist)?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

     h. SCORE Reuse Potential (5%):
        - Could this pattern generate variants for future emails?
        - Is the structure transferable beyond this specific content?
        - Score 1-10 with specific evidence

  3. CALCULATE weighted score:
     weighted_total = (curiosity * 0.20) + (trigger * 0.15) + (alignment * 0.15)
                    + (economy * 0.15) + (formula * 0.10) + (niche * 0.10)
                    + (originality * 0.10) + (reuse * 0.05)

  4. RANK by weighted_total (descending)

  5. FLAG quality issues:
     - Any criterion below 6.0 = critical gap
     - Alignment below 7.0 = auto-reject
     - Overall weighted below 8.0 = below threshold
     - Word count > 10 = length flag

  6. SELECT top 5-10 for output package:
     - All must score >= 8.0 weighted
     - All must have alignment >= 7.0
     - All must be <= 10 words
     - Diverse formula categories preferred

  7. ASSEMBLE sl_ranking object
  8. RETURN sl_ranking
```

## SCORING CALIBRATION NOTES

### What Separates 8.0 from 9.0
- **8.0:** Strong SL that would get good open rates. Clear curiosity, clean execution, niche-appropriate.
- **9.0:** Exceptional SL that would stop a subscriber who was about to delete everything. The kind of SL you screenshot and save. Fresh, precise, emotionally compelling, and perfectly aligned.

### Common Pitfalls in Scoring
- Overscoring alignment (assuming the SL matches without re-reading the email)
- Underscoring originality (familiar patterns CAN be original with fresh execution)
- Ignoring word economy (a strong concept in too many words still loses points)
- Conflating curiosity with confusion (obscure SLs score LOW on curiosity)

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Zero candidates above 8.0 threshold | Return to Arena for additional rounds or new formula selection |
| CRITICAL | All candidates fail alignment check | Return to Layer 2 with stricter alignment constraints |
| CRITICAL | Fewer than 5 candidates pass all checks | Expand selection pool from Arena |
| DEGRADED | Low formula diversity in top 5-10 | Weight diversity in final selection |
| DEGRADED | All candidates same word count | Note for future generation |
