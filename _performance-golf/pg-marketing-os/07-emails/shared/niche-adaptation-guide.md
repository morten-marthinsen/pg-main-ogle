# Niche Adaptation Guide

**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** 2026-02-21
**Purpose:** Calibrate email tone, language, and structural choices by vertical

---

## WHY THIS FILE EXISTS

Ben Settle's emails target the internet marketing / copywriting niche. The structural patterns are UNIVERSAL, but the tone, vocabulary, and cultural references must be calibrated for each vertical. This guide prevents cross-niche contamination.

---

## THE 5 VERTICALS

### 1. Health / Supplements

**Tone:** Authoritative but warm. Doctor-next-door, not clinical lecturer.
**Register:** Mid-formal. No slang. Medical terms used but immediately explained.
**Content-to-pitch ratio:** 75-80% content / 20-25% pitch (lean toward content — health buyers need more trust)

**Adapt These:**
- Stories → patient transformation stories, research discovery narratives
- Quotes → medical authority quotes, study findings
- Lists → symptom checklists, ingredient benefits
- Contrarian → "what your doctor won't tell you" angle
- Q&A → reader health questions, "I tried X and it didn't work"

**Avoid:**
- Cure/treat/diagnose language (compliance)
- Dismissing conventional medicine entirely
- Fear-mongering without solutions
- Overly casual tone about serious conditions

**Bridge Adaptation:**
- "Subscribe" → "Get yours" / "Try it risk-free"
- "Goes to print" → "Limited batch" / "While supplies last"
- "Email Players" → [Supplement Name]

**Subject Line Calibration:**
- "How to" works exceptionally well (educational frame)
- "The [X]" works well (authority frame)
- Edgy/profane does NOT work — damages credibility
- Number/list converts well (specificity = trust)

### 2. Finance / Trading

**Tone:** Confident authority. Data-driven but accessible. Insider knowledge positioning.
**Register:** Mid-formal to formal. Financial terminology used precisely.
**Content-to-pitch ratio:** 70-75% content / 25-30% pitch (financial buyers are more action-oriented)

**Adapt These:**
- Stories → market prediction stories, investment wins/losses
- Quotes → Warren Buffett, Munger, Dalio — borrowed authority
- Lists → "5 stocks to watch" / "3 mistakes killing your portfolio"
- Contrarian → "why Wall Street is wrong about [X]"
- Q&A → reader portfolio questions, market confusion

**Avoid:**
- Guaranteed returns language (compliance)
- "Risk-free" investment claims
- Overly casual tone about people's money
- Predictions without caveats

**Bridge Adaptation:**
- "Subscribe" → "Get access" / "Join"
- "Goes to print" → "Research window closes" / "Offer expires"
- Personal investment stories build trust

**Subject Line Calibration:**
- "Why [Z]" works exceptionally well (contrarian insights)
- Name-drop converts well (Buffett, Soros, institutional names)
- Curiosity/secret works well (insider knowledge frame)
- "How to" works for actionable strategies

### 3. Golf / Sports Performance

**Tone:** Buddy-on-the-course. Enthusiastic peer, not lecturing pro.
**Register:** Informal. Sports slang welcome. First-name basis.
**Content-to-pitch ratio:** 70-80% content / 20-30% pitch (varies by product type)

**Adapt These:**
- Stories → round stories, lesson stories, "what I saw at the range"
- Quotes → tour pros, teaching legends
- Lists → "5 drills for more distance" / "3 swing thoughts that work"
- Contrarian → "why your instructor is wrong about [X]"
- Q&A → reader swing questions, equipment confusion

**Avoid:**
- Overly technical biomechanics (save for mechanism section)
- Talking down to weekend golfers
- Equipment brand bashing (partnership damage)
- Unrealistic improvement claims without timeframes

**Bridge Adaptation:**
- "Subscribe" → "Get started" / "Try it on the range"
- "Goes to print" → "Enrollment closes" / "Group starts [date]"
- Specific yardage gains are powerful proof points

**Subject Line Calibration:**
- Specific numbers convert well ("Add 30 yards")
- "How to" works well for technique content
- Story hooks convert well (golfers love stories)
- Edgy can work if it's playful, not crude

### 4. Personal Development

**Tone:** Wise friend who's been through it. Aspirational but grounded.
**Register:** Mid-informal. Conversational but not flippant about growth.
**Content-to-pitch ratio:** 75-80% content / 20-25% pitch (PD buyers value the content itself)

**Adapt These:**
- Stories → personal transformation stories, client breakthroughs
- Quotes → philosophers, business leaders, spiritual teachers
- Lists → habits, mindset shifts, daily practices
- Contrarian → "why positive thinking is holding you back"
- Q&A → reader struggles, mindset questions

**Avoid:**
- Guru positioning (creates resistance)
- Dismissing people's current struggles
- Empty inspirational platitudes
- "Just believe in yourself" without actionable steps

**Bridge Adaptation:**
- "Subscribe" → "Start here" / "Begin the work"
- Trust-building is paramount — longer warm-up in sequences
- Testimonials emphasize inner transformation, not just external results

**Subject Line Calibration:**
- "Why [Z]" converts well (paradigm shifts)
- Confession/admission works well (vulnerability builds trust)
- Provocation/identity works well ("Are you a [A] or a [B]?")
- "The [X]" works for concept-naming

---

### 5. Technology / SaaS

**Tone:** Smart colleague. Technical competence without jargon overload.
**Register:** Mid-informal. Industry terms okay, buzzwords not.
**Content-to-pitch ratio:** 70-75% content / 25-30% pitch (tech buyers are solution-oriented)

**Adapt These:**
- Stories → implementation stories, "we built X and here's what happened"
- Quotes → industry leaders, customer testimonials
- Lists → feature comparisons, "5 ways to solve [problem]"
- Contrarian → "why [popular tool] is the wrong choice for [use case]"
- Q&A → technical questions, implementation confusion

**Avoid:**
- Buzzword soup (synergy, leverage, disrupt, paradigm)
- Assuming technical knowledge level
- Feature-dumping without benefit translation
- Dismissing competitors by name (partnership risk)

**Bridge Adaptation:**
- "Subscribe" → "Try it free" / "Start your trial"
- Free trials and demos are the natural CTA, not subscriptions
- Case study emails convert exceptionally well

**Subject Line Calibration:**
- "How to" dominates (tech buyers want solutions)
- Number/list converts well (specificity + scanability)
- "The [X]" works for product/framework naming
- Edgy/profane works if brand voice supports it

## CROSS-NICHE CONTAMINATION RULES

```
FORBIDDEN:
  - Health language in finance emails ("toxin," "detox," "healing")
  - Finance language in health emails ("portfolio," "returns," "invest")
  - Golf language in non-golf emails ("swing," "fairway," "driver")
  - Guru language in any niche ("unlock your potential," "manifest," "vibration")
  - Tech buzzwords outside tech ("disrupt," "leverage," "optimize")

IF cross-niche language detected:
  HALT — Remove and replace with niche-appropriate vocabulary
```

- Negative Response (NR) should be used sparingly in all niches — it's personality-dependent
- Story (ST) is universally strong but requires genuine stories, not fabricated
- List-Based (LB) is the safest body type across all niches
- Testimonial (TM) effectiveness depends entirely on proof quality
