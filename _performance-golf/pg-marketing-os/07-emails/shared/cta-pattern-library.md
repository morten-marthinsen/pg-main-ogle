# CTA Pattern Library

**Version:** 1.1
**Created:** 2026-02-21
**Source:** Structural analysis of 2,684 Ben Settle emails
**Purpose:** Call-to-action patterns for email generation

---

## WHY THIS FILE EXISTS

Email CTAs are NOT long-form sales letter CTAs. They are short, conversational, and often deliberately casual. The CTA in a Ben Settle email is typically 1-3 sentences — never a full close. The CONTENT does the selling; the CTA just points.

---

## THE 5 CTA PATTERNS

### Pattern A: Soft Redirect (Most Common — ~60% of emails)

The CTA is just a transition phrase + URL. No selling in the CTA itself.

**Structure:**
```
[Transition phrase]:

[URL]

[Name]
```

**Specimens:**
```
Subscribe here in time while you can:

www.emailplayersbook.com

Ben Settle
```

```
Go ye here:

www.emailplayersbook.com

Ben Settle
```

**When to use:** Daily Relationship (DR), Content/Podcast (CP), Autoresponder (AR)
**Pitch ratio:** ~10% of email (1-2 lines)

---

### Pattern B: Benefit Reminder + Redirect (~20% of emails)

One sentence reminding what they'll get, then URL.

**Structure:**
```
[1-sentence benefit reminder]

[Transition phrase]:

[URL]

[Name]
```

**Specimens:**
```
This month's issue shows you exactly how to write emails that sell without being "salesy."

Subscribe here while there's still time:

www.emailplayersbook.com

Ben Settle
```

```
If you want 13 ways to make your emails impossible to ignore, this is for you.

Details here:

www.emailplayersbook.com

Ben Settle
```

**When to use:** Deadline Urgency (DU), late-sequence launch emails
**Pitch ratio:** ~15% of email (2-4 lines)

---

### Pattern C: Blatant Pitch Meta (Ben Settle specialty — ~5% of emails)

The pitch is TRANSPARENTLY a pitch. The entertainment is in the self-awareness.

**Structure:**
```
[Meta-acknowledgment of pitching]

[Brief product description]

[URL]

[Name]
```

**Specimens:**
```
And now for the shameless, blatant, in-your-face sales pitch part of this email:

Email Players teaches you how to write one email per day that keeps your list engaged, responsive, and buying. Each month you get a new issue with specific techniques, templates, and examples.

Subscribe before the deadline here:

www.emailplayersbook.com

Ben Settle
```

**When to use:** Blatant Pitch (BP) function ONLY
**Pitch ratio:** ~25-30% of email (but the meta-framing makes it feel lighter)

---

### Pattern D: Urgency Stack (Launch close-day emails — ~10% of emails)

Multiple urgency elements stacked before URL.

**Structure:**
```
[Urgency statement 1]

[Urgency statement 2 — different angle]

[Transition phrase]:

[URL]

[Name]

P.S. [Final urgency or bonus reminder]
```

**Specimens:**
```
This sale ends today at midnight.

After that, the price goes back up and the bonuses disappear.

Last call:

www.example.com/product

Ben Settle

P.S. Remember, you also get [bonus] which alone is worth the price of admission.
```

**When to use:** Final day of Product Launch (PL) or Affiliate Launch (AL)
**Pitch ratio:** ~20-25% of email

---

### Pattern E: PS-Heavy (Launch emails with bonus stacking — ~5% of emails)

Minimal in-body CTA but substantial PS section.

**Structure:**
```
[Minimal transition phrase]:

[URL]

[Name]

P.S. [Bonus 1 mention]

P.P.S. [Bonus 2 mention or testimonial]

P.P.P.S. [Deadline reminder]
```

**Specimens:**
```
Details here:

www.example.com/product

Ben Settle

P.S. I almost forgot — you also get [bonus name] when you grab this before the deadline. This alone would cost you [price] if you bought it separately.

P.P.S. [Customer name] told me last week that [specific result from product]. If that doesn't get your motor running, I don't know what will.
```

**When to use:** Mid-sequence launch emails where bonuses are being revealed
**Pitch ratio:** ~20% (but split between body and PS)

## CTA PHRASE VARIETY RULES

In any email sequence of 5+ emails:

1. **Never use the same transition phrase twice in a row**
2. **Rotate across at least 3 categories** from transition-phrase-library.md
3. **Match CTA pattern to campaign function:**

| Campaign Function | Primary Pattern | Secondary Pattern |
|-------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Daily Relationship (DR) | A (Soft Redirect) | B (Benefit Reminder) |
| Deadline Urgency (DU) | B (Benefit Reminder) | D (Urgency Stack) |
| Product Launch (PL) | A→B→B→D→D | Escalating pattern |
| Affiliate Launch (AL) | A→B→D | Shorter escalation |
| Blatant Pitch (BP) | C (Meta Pitch) | — |
| Content/Podcast (CP) | A (Soft Redirect) | — |
| Autoresponder (AR) | A (Soft Redirect) | B (Benefit Reminder) |

---

## LAUNCH SEQUENCE CTA ESCALATION

```
Day 1 (1 email):     Pattern A — Soft, establish the product exists
Day 2 (2 emails):    Pattern A + B — Still soft, add benefit reminder
Day 3 (2 emails):    Pattern B + B — Benefit-forward, building desire
Day 4 (2-3 emails):  Pattern B + D — Urgency entering
Day 5 (5-8 emails):  Pattern D + D + D — Full urgency, deadline day

FINAL EMAIL (always):
  Pattern D with shortest possible urgency:
  "Last call. Link here: [URL]"
  (Under 120 words total email)
```

## CTA TRANSFORMATION PRINCIPLE

**Source:** Ann Handley (Everybody Writes) — adapted for email

Before selecting a CTA pattern, apply this transformation: **replace invisible verbs with visible, outcome-hinting actions.**

| Before (Invisible) | After (Outcome-Hinting) | Why It Works |
|---------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| "Shop now" | "Wave goodbye to winter skin" | Reader sees the result, not the action |
| "Buy our product" | "Transform your mornings" | Outcome implicit in the verb |
| "Learn more" | "Discover your unfair advantage" | Curiosity + specific benefit |
| "Click here" | "See the 3 patterns they all share" | Content extension, not interruption |
| "Sign up" | "Start your 30-day experiment" | Low-commitment framing + specificity |

**The Rule:** The CTA verb phrase should make the reader feel like clicking is the natural next step in the content — not a departure from it. The best CTAs feel like the final sentence of the content section, not the first sentence of the pitch.

**Application:** After selecting Pattern A-E above, check whether the transition phrase uses an invisible verb. If so, transform it. This is especially high-impact for Pattern A (Soft Redirect) and Pattern B (Benefit Reminder), which together cover ~80% of emails.

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. ❌ **Long-form closes in emails** — Emails are NOT sales letters. No "Imagine yourself 30 days from now..." paragraphs
2. ❌ **Multiple CTAs in body** — One CTA point per email (PS section is the exception)
3. ❌ **Feature dumps in CTA** — The content section does this work, not the CTA
4. ❌ **Generic urgency** — "Act now!" or "Limited time!" without specific reason
5. ❌ **Identical CTA across sequence** — Must vary phrase, pattern, and emotional angle
