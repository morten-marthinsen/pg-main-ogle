# Email Quality Rubric

**Version:** 1.1
**Created:** 2026-02-21
**Purpose:** Scoring criteria for individual email quality and campaign-level quality

---

## INDIVIDUAL EMAIL SCORING (9 Criteria)

### 1. Opening Hook Strength (15%)

The first 3 lines determine whether the email gets read or deleted.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Impossible to stop reading. Visceral curiosity or emotional pull. Reader MUST continue. |
| 7-8 | Strong opening. Creates genuine interest. Most readers continue. |
| 5-6 | Adequate opening. Some interest generated. Many readers continue. |
| 3-4 | Weak opening. Generic or predictable. Easy to abandon. |
| 1-2 | No hook. Could be any email from anyone. Immediate delete risk. |

**What makes a 9-10:**
- Specific, unexpected detail in first sentence
- Emotional tension or curiosity established immediately
- Pattern interrupt — doesn't read like a "marketing email"
- Reader's internal monologue: "Wait, what?"

---

### 2. Content-to-Pitch Ratio (15%)

Target: 70-80% content / 20-30% pitch.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Perfect ratio (75/25). Reader feels they got VALUE. Pitch is barely noticeable. |
| 7-8 | Good ratio (70-80/20-30). Content dominates. Pitch is natural. |
| 5-6 | Acceptable ratio (60-70/30-40). Slightly pitch-heavy but not egregious. |
| 3-4 | Poor ratio (50-60/40-50). Feels like a sales email with some content. |
| 1-2 | Failed ratio (<50/50+). This is a sales letter, not an email. |

**Measurement:** Count words in content sections vs. pitch sections. Bridge sentence counts as pitch. Transition phrase + URL + sign-off counts as pitch.

---

### 3. Transition/Bridge Smoothness (15%)

The moment content becomes pitch. THE critical skill in email writing.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Seamless. Reader doesn't notice the transition. Clicking feels like the natural next step. |
| 7-8 | Smooth. Transition is visible but not jarring. Bridge feels earned. |
| 5-6 | Adequate. Transition works but feels like a gear shift. |
| 3-4 | Clumsy. Reader feels the "now I'm being sold to" moment. |
| 1-2 | Broken. Content and pitch feel like two different emails stitched together. |

**What makes a 9-10:**
- Pivot sentence connects content theme to product naturally
- Bridge phrase matches email tone (casual content → casual bridge)
- Reader's train of thought continues through the transition
- No "But wait, there's more!" energy shift

---

### 4. Body Type Compliance (10%)

Does the email follow its assigned structural template?

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Perfect structural execution. Every element of the body type present and properly ordered. |
| 7-8 | Strong compliance. All key elements present, minor structural variations. |
| 5-6 | Partial compliance. Some elements present but structure loosened. |
| 3-4 | Weak compliance. Template barely recognizable. |
| 1-2 | No compliance. Email doesn't match assigned body type. |

**Per-type checklists:**
- **CT (Contrarian):** Bold claim present? → Argument stacked? → Dismissals clear? → Bridge follows?
- **QO (Quote-Opener):** Quote formatted? → Riff connected? → Lesson extracted? → Bridge follows?
- **TM (Testimonial):** Attribution present? → Testimonial blocked? → Reaction brief? → CTA follows?
- **QA (Q&A):** Attribution present? → Question displayed? → Answer substantive? → Bridge follows?
- **LB (List-Based):** Setup present? → List numbered/bulleted? → Wrap-up concise? → CTA follows?
- **ST (Story):** Story opener hooks? → Narrative develops? → Lesson extracted? → Bridge follows?
- **NR (Negative Response):** Troll displayed? → Demolition entertaining? → Lesson extracted? → Bridge follows?

---

### 5. Voice Consistency (10%)

Does the email maintain consistent persona voice throughout?

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Voice is unmistakable and consistent from first word to last. |
| 7-8 | Voice is strong with minor wobbles. |
| 5-6 | Voice is present but inconsistent. Some sections feel different. |
| 3-4 | Voice is weak. Could be written by anyone. |
| 1-2 | No discernible voice. Generic AI output. |

---

### 6. Engagement / Entertainment Value (10%)

Would the reader enjoy this email even if they never click?

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Reader would forward this to a friend. Genuinely entertaining/insightful. |
| 7-8 | Reader smiles, nods, or feels something. Good content. |
| 5-6 | Reader reads through. Not boring but not memorable. |
| 3-4 | Reader skims. Functional but flat. |
| 1-2 | Reader deletes. Nothing interesting or valuable. |

---

### 7. CTA Clarity and Strength (10%)

Is it crystal clear what the reader should do and why?

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | CTA feels inevitable after the content. One clear action. Emotionally compelling. |
| 7-8 | CTA is clear and well-positioned. Reader knows exactly what to do. |
| 5-6 | CTA exists but doesn't feel urgent or compelling. |
| 3-4 | CTA is buried or confusing. Multiple actions compete. |
| 1-2 | CTA is missing, unclear, or disconnected from content. |

---

### 8. Paragraph Brevity (5%)

No paragraph longer than 3 sentences. Most should be 1-2 sentences.

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Every paragraph 1-2 sentences. White space creates rhythm. Scannable. |
| 7-8 | Mostly 1-2 sentences. Occasional 3-sentence paragraph. |
| 5-6 | Mix of short and medium paragraphs. Some 4+ sentence blocks. |
| 3-4 | Multiple long paragraphs. Wall of text feeling. |
| 1-2 | Dense paragraphs throughout. Not formatted for email reading. |

---

### 9. Niche Appropriateness (10%)

Is the tone, vocabulary, and cultural reference set appropriate for the target audience?

| Score | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| 9-10 | Reader feels "this person gets me." Language matches their world exactly. |
| 7-8 | Appropriate with minor calibration needs. No cross-niche contamination. |
| 5-6 | Generally appropriate but some language feels off. |
| 3-4 | Noticeable mismatch. Language from wrong niche leaking in. |
| 1-2 | Wrong niche entirely. Health language in finance email, etc. |

---

## WEIGHTED SCORING

```
Individual Email Score =
  (Opening Hook × 0.15) +
  (Content-Pitch Ratio × 0.15) +
  (Bridge Smoothness × 0.15) +
  (Body Type Compliance × 0.10) +
  (Voice Consistency × 0.10) +
  (Entertainment Value × 0.10) +
  (CTA Clarity × 0.10) +
  (Paragraph Brevity × 0.05) +
  (Niche Appropriateness × 0.10)
```

## QUALITY THRESHOLDS

| Threshold | Score | Action |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Excellent** | 8.5+ | Ready for human review |
| **Good** | 7.5-8.4 | Minor revision, then human review |
| **Acceptable** | 7.0-7.4 | Revision required before human review |
| **Below Standard** | 6.0-6.9 | Significant rework — return to generation |
| **Failure** | Below 6.0 | Complete regeneration required |

**Minimum for Arena acceptance:** 7.5 overall weighted score
**Minimum for human presentation:** 7.0 overall weighted score
**Target for final output:** 8.0+ overall weighted score

## CAMPAIGN-LEVEL SCORING (5 Additional Criteria)

These apply to the complete email sequence, not individual emails.

### C1. Body Type Variety
No same type twice in a row. In any 5-email window, at least 3 different body types.

### C2. Emotional Arc
Sequence has clear emotional progression (trust → education → desire → urgency).

### C3. Cross-Email Continuity (Scheherazade Principle)
Later emails reference earlier content (backward callbacks) AND plant hooks for the next email (forward teases). In sequences of 5+, at least 60% of non-final emails contain a forward hook. 100% of emails after the first contain a backward reference.

### C4. Urgency Escalation
Launch sequences show clear escalation from no urgency to maximum urgency.

### C5. Subject Line Diversity
Subject lines use at least 3 different formula categories across the sequence.
