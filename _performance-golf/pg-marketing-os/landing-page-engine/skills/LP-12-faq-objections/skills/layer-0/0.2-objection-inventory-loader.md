# 0.2 — Objection Inventory Loader

**Skill:** LP-12-faq-objections
**Position:** Layer 0, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Mine, inventory, and deduplicate all possible purchase objections from the upstream data. This is the raw material that Layer 1 will classify and sequence. The inventory must contain at minimum 8 distinct objections covering at least the 3 mandatory categories (price/value, efficacy/results, trust/guarantee).
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `packages-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `objection-inventory.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  packages_loaded: "[from packages-loaded.md -- all extracted fields]"
  sources_to_mine:
    - audience_profile: "[primary pain, failed solutions, desires, demographics]"
    - research_quotes: "[any objection-relevant quotes from research]"
    - conversion_intelligence: "[objection signals, price sensitivity, trust deficit]"
    - comparison_landscape: "[competitors, alternatives the audience considers]"
    - offer_summary: "[price, guarantee, bonuses -- what they're being asked to commit to]"
    - product_specifics: "[ingredients, formulation, access method, delivery format]"
    - vertical_norms: "[what objections are universal in this vertical?]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

1. **Mine objections from 7 sources.** Go through each source systematically and extract every possible objection a prospect might hold at the point of purchase:

   **Source 1: Audience Pain Profile**
   - What has the audience tried before that failed? (Generates "does this actually work?" and "how is this different?" objections)
   - What are they skeptical about? (Generates trust objections)
   - What are they afraid of? (Generates risk/guarantee objections)

   **Source 2: Research Quotes (if available)**
   - Scan any verbatim audience quotes for objection language: "I'm not sure if...", "What if...", "My concern is...", "The thing that stops me is..."
   - Quotes are the best source because they use the audience's own words

   **Source 3: Conversion Intelligence**
   - What are the industry-level purchase barriers?
   - What does the objection signal data reveal?
   - Is price sensitivity high or low in this vertical?
   - Is there a trust deficit (new brand, no reviews, unproven claims)?

   **Source 4: Comparison Landscape**
   - What alternatives does the audience know about?
   - What is the main competitive comparison they will make?
   - What is the most common "why not just use X instead?" question?

   **Source 5: Offer Specifics**
   - What is the price? (Generates "is it worth it?" and "why so expensive?")
   - What is the guarantee? (Generates "what if I don't like it?")
   - Is there a subscription/commitment? (Generates "am I locked in?")
   - Are there bonuses? (Generates "what do I actually get?")

   **Source 6: Product Specifics**
   - Physical product: ingredients, dosing, interactions, side effects, shipping, returns
   - Info product: access method, time commitment, skill level, format, refund process
   - Software: features, integrations, learning curve, support, cancellation

   **Source 7: Vertical Norms**
   - Health/supplement: safety, interactions, pregnancy/nursing, children, dosing, "does it work?", ingredients transparency
   - Info product: time commitment, "can a beginner do this?", results timeline, "is this just another course?"
   - Finance: risk, regulatory, "is this legal?", historical performance, "is this a scam?"
   - Ecomm/DTC: shipping time, returns, product quality, "is this the same as the cheaper version?"

2. **Deduplicate.** Multiple sources may surface the same objection. Merge duplicates but note which sources surfaced it (more sources = stronger objection signal).

3. **Tag each objection** with:
   - Category (price_value | efficacy_results | trust_guarantee | timing | comparison | technical_logistics)
   - Strength (1-10 — how likely is this to prevent a purchase?)
   - Source(s) that surfaced it
   - Surface question (how a prospect would phrase this)
   - Hidden objection (the real concern behind the question)

4. **Verify minimum thresholds:**
   - Total objections: >= 8
   - Price/value category: >= 1 objection
   - Efficacy/results category: >= 1 objection
   - Trust/guarantee category: >= 1 objection
   - If any threshold not met: expand mining (re-read sources with fresh focus on the missing category)

5. **Rank by strength.** Order all objections by strength score (10 = most likely to prevent purchase). This ranking informs Layer 1's sequence planner.

6. **Write `objection-inventory.md`** with all objections, tags, and ranking.

## Quality Criteria
- All 7 sources mined (not just 2-3)
- Objections are specific to THIS product, not generic objections that apply to anything
- Hidden objection identified for every item (surface question is not enough)
- Strength ranking is honest — the hardest objections are ranked highest, not buried
- Mandatory categories all have at least 1 objection
- Total count >= 8 distinct, non-duplicate objections

## Failure Modes
- Mining only 1-2 sources -> Must mine all 7. Shallow mining produces a shallow FAQ.
- All objections are technical/logistics (no hard objections) -> Re-mine with focus on price, efficacy, trust. These are the objections that prevent purchases.
- Inventing objections that do not exist for this product/audience -> Every objection must trace to a source. If no source surfaced it, it may not be a real concern.
- Strength scores clustered at 5 (everything rated "medium") -> Differentiate. Some objections ARE stronger than others. If price is the #1 barrier, rate it 9-10, not 5.
- Fewer than 8 after deduplication -> Expand mining. Consider edge-case objections, vertical-specific concerns, or objections surfaced by competitor pages.
