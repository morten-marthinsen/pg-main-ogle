# 2.5 — Urgency Support Writer

**Skill:** LP-13-urgency-scarcity
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 5
**Purpose:** Write the supporting urgency copy that JUSTIFIES all urgency claims, plus natural urgency framing (cost of delay), and close/P.S. callbacks. This microskill ALWAYS runs regardless of which urgency types are selected. It is the connective tissue that makes urgency believable — without justification support copy, urgency claims are naked assertions that the reader will dismiss.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `justification-plan.md` (1.2), `placement-strategy.md` (1.3), all relevant Layer 2 copy files (2.1-2.4 as applicable), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `urgency-types-selected.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `urgency-support-copy.md`

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Read all Layer 2 copy written so far.** This microskill writes SUPPORT copy — it must be consistent with the urgency claims already written in 2.1-2.4.

**Step 2: Read the justification plan.** Every support paragraph must trace back to a justification documented in 1.2.

**Step 3: Read the placement strategy.** Natural urgency callbacks have specific target sections identified in 1.3.

**Step 4: Read audience profile.** Cost-of-delay framing (natural urgency) must be specific to this audience's situation, not generic "every day you wait" language.

---

## Execution Logic

### Part 1: Justification Support Paragraphs

1. **Write the master justification paragraph** (100-250 words for Type A, 30-80 words for Type B):

   This is the single most important paragraph in LP-13. It is the paragraph that EXPLAINS why the urgency/scarcity exists. Without it, every deadline, stock level, and price change is just a claim.

   **Type A structure:**
   - Open with transparency signal: "Let me be straightforward about why this [deadline/limitation] exists."
   - State the constraint: specific, factual, no marketing language
   - Explain the constraint: why it exists, what business/operational reality creates it
   - Frame it: this is NOT a pressure tactic — it is a real constraint that affects the offer
   - Close with the implication for the reader: what this means for their decision

   **Type B structure:**
   - 2-3 sentences maximum
   - State the constraint + why it exists + what it means for the buyer
   - Placed near the urgency element it supports (below countdown, near stock level)

   Example (Type A):
   > "I want to be straightforward about the pricing. The $67 launch price is real, and so is the deadline. Here's why: we're launching [Product] at a price that barely covers our costs because we need something more valuable than profit right now — we need proof. We need 1,000 customers using [Product] and reporting their results. Once we hit that number, the price goes to $97, which reflects the actual value of what's inside based on the ingredient costs alone. This isn't a marketing tactic. It's a launch strategy. And it has an expiration date: [specific date]."

   Example (Type B):
   > "We produce [Product] in batches of 5,000 to preserve ingredient potency. Current batch: [number] remaining. Next batch ships in 6-8 weeks."

### Part 2: Natural Urgency Copy (if Type 6 selected)

2. **Write natural urgency callbacks** for each target section identified in placement-strategy.md:

   For each callback:
   - Target section ID and name
   - 1-2 sentences that describe the specific cost of delay for this audience
   - Must be woven into narrative (not a separate urgency block)
   - Must reference specific, concrete consequences (not vague "things get worse")

   **Cost-of-delay patterns by vertical:**

   | Vertical | Natural Urgency Pattern |
   |---------|----------------------|
   | Health | Progressive condition: "Every night of poor sleep adds to cortisol accumulation. Research shows 14 days of chronic sleep deprivation increases cortisol by 37%." |
   | Finance | Compounding loss: "Every month you delay, you lose approximately $[amount] in potential returns. Over a year, that compounds to $[larger amount]." |
   | Personal Dev | Opportunity cost: "The skills gap between those using AI and those who aren't is widening every week. Six months from now, the gap will be significantly harder to close." |
   | Golf/Sports | Performance window: "Your swing compensations become more ingrained with every round. The longer the pattern persists, the harder it is to correct." |

   Write 2-4 callback sentences, each targeted to a specific section:

   **Root cause callback:**
   "[Specific consequence that gets worse over time, tied to the root cause the section discusses]"

   **Mechanism callback:**
   "[How the delay specifically undermines the mechanism/solution being explained]"

   **Story callback (if applicable):**
   "[Character reference or audience reference about the cost of waiting]"

### Part 3: Consequence of Delay Statement

3. **Write the consequence-of-delay statement** (2-4 sentences):
   - What SPECIFICALLY happens if the reader does nothing?
   - Not vague "you'll miss out" — specific, concrete, measurable if possible
   - This appears in the urgency section and may be referenced in P.S.

   Example:
   > "If you wait: the launch price ends on Monday, and the price goes to $97. But more importantly, every week you continue with [problem] costs you [specific consequence — hours, dollars, health impact]. The product will still be available at $97. The question is whether the cost of another [timeframe] of [problem] is worth $30."

### Part 4: Close and P.S. Integration Copy

4. **Write P.S. urgency integration** (2-4 sentences):
   - Designed to be the urgency component of the P.S. section (LP-15 Assembly integrates this)
   - Must include: the core urgency claim + the justification in compressed form + the consequence
   - The P.S. is one of the most-read sections on any long-form page — this is high-impact placement

5. **Write close section urgency integration** (1-2 sentences):
   - Designed to be woven into the final close/CTA section
   - Must be brief and work alongside the emotional close without overpowering it
   - Example: "This pricing, this guarantee, this offer — all of it is available right now, today, at $67. After [date], the price is $97."

## Quality Criteria
- Master justification paragraph explains the WHY behind urgency (not just asserts the urgency)
- Transparency signal present ("Let me be straightforward" or similar honest framing)
- Natural urgency callbacks are specific to the vertical and audience (not generic "every day you wait")
- Consequence of delay statement names specific, concrete consequences
- P.S. integration compresses the urgency argument into 2-4 high-impact sentences
- Close section integration is brief enough to complement (not overpower) the emotional close
- All support copy consistent with claims written in 2.1-2.4
- All claims traceable to justification-plan.md
- Voice direction maintained — support copy matches page tone

## Failure Modes
- Master justification paragraph that restates the urgency claim without explaining why -> The justification IS the explanation. "The price goes up on Monday because the launch period ends" is not a justification — it's circular. "The price goes up on Monday because we priced the launch below cost to acquire our first 1,000 customers for proof" is a justification.
- Natural urgency callbacks that are generic ("every day counts") -> Must name the specific consequence for this specific audience. "Every day counts" is forbidden. "Every night of disrupted sleep adds to your cortisol burden" is specific.
- P.S. integration that introduces new urgency claims not present in the main urgency section -> P.S. is a callback, not a new argument. It compresses existing claims; it does not add new ones.
- Consequence of delay statement that is only about price/offer -> If there's natural urgency (health, financial, competitive), the consequence should reference the REAL cost of delay, not just "you'll pay more."
- Support copy that sounds like a salesperson making excuses for high-pressure tactics -> The tone must be HONEST, not defensive. "I'm not trying to pressure you" is defensive. "Here's why this deadline exists" is honest.
