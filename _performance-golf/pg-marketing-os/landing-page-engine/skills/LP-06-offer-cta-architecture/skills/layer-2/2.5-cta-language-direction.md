# 2.5 — CTA Language Direction

**Skill:** LP-06-offer-cta-architecture
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 5
**Purpose:** Generate 3+ specific emotional appeal directions for each CTA placement. This is the critical handoff to LP-14 (CTA Copy Optimizer) — LP-14 writes the final button text and surrounding copy FROM these directions. A direction must specify: emotional appeal type, personalization vector, and specific language territory. "Write a good CTA" is not a direction. "Desire/Confidence: communicate ownership of daily energy starting today, include product name and savings amount" IS a direction.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `cta-placement-map.md` (2.3), `guarantee-architecture.md` (1.4), `offer-type-classification.md` (1.1), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1 — audience, product)
**Output:** `cta-language-directions.md`

## The 6 Emotional Appeals — Language Direction Templates

### 1. Desire/Confidence
**Core emotion:** Ownership, certainty, "this is mine"
**Language territory:**
- "Get My [Product/Benefit] — [Timeframe]"
- "Start [Specific Outcome] Today"
- "Claim My [Product Name] [Offer Detail]"

**Personalization vectors:** Product name, specific benefit, timeframe, "my" possessive

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Communicate ownership of [product name] with 'Get My' possessive. Include primary benefit and 'Today' urgency."
- "Frame as the start of the outcome: 'Start [specific benefit].' First person possessive."

### 2. Transformation
**Core emotion:** Aspiration, identity shift, becoming
**Language territory:**
- "Start My [Outcome] Transformation"
- "Begin My Journey to [Desired State]"
- "Take the First Step Toward [Identity]"

**Personalization vectors:** Desired outcome, identity aspiration, journey metaphor

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Frame as identity transformation. The CTA is not buying a product but becoming the person who [desired identity]. Use 'Start My' or 'Begin My.'"

### 3. Consequence
**Core emotion:** Loss aversion, missing out, regret
**Language territory:**
- "Don't Miss [Specific Offer/Savings]"
- "This [Offer Type] Won't Last — [Specific Deadline]"
- "Before This [Limitation] Is Gone"

**Personalization vectors:** Specific savings, deadline, scarcity element

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Frame the cost of NOT acting. Reference the specific savings amount ($[X]) and the deadline. Tone: informative urgency, not panic."

### 4. Belonging
**Core emotion:** Social validation, community, "people like me"
**Language territory:**
- "Join [Number] [People Who Outcome]"
- "See Why [Number] Choose [Product]"
- "Become Part of the [Community/Movement]"

**Personalization vectors:** Customer count, community identity, shared outcome

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Lead with social proof number. Frame joining as validation that this is the right choice. '[Number] [audience description] already [outcome].'"

### 5. Risk Reversal
**Core emotion:** Safety, zero risk, nothing to lose
**Language territory:**
- "Try [Product] Risk-Free — [Duration]"
- "Protected by [Guarantee Name]"
- "[Duration] to Decide — No Questions Asked"

**Personalization vectors:** Guarantee name, duration, claim ease

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Lead with risk removal. Reference the branded guarantee by name. Include duration. Frame as: 'you have [X days] to decide with zero risk.'"

### 6. Specificity
**Core emotion:** Precision, deal, smart decision
**Language territory:**
- "Claim My [Product] — Save [Amount] Today"
- "Get [Quantity] for Just $[Price] — [Savings Detail]"
- "Lock In [Specific Offer] Before [Deadline]"

**Personalization vectors:** Exact savings, exact price, exact quantity, exact deadline

**Example directions for LP-14:**
- "Maximum specificity. Include: product name, exact price, exact savings amount, and exact offer detail. The CTA should read like a deal summary."

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** For each CTA from the placement map (2.3), review:
- The assigned emotional appeal categories (from 1.3 strategy)
- The trigger context (what the visitor just read)
- The conversion intent (impulse, educated, convinced, final capture)

**Step 2:** Generate 3+ specific language directions per CTA. Each direction must contain ALL of:
1. **Appeal type** — named from the 6-appeal framework
2. **Personalization vector** — specific data (product name, benefit, savings, audience identity)
3. **Language territory** — specific enough for LP-14 to draft from (not "write something compelling")

**Step 3:** For each CTA, also specify:
- Inline risk reversal: Y/N — if Y, what text appears beneath or beside the button?
- Sub-text direction: Any supporting text below the button (e.g., "Free shipping on all orders" or "Cancel anytime")

**Step 4:** Verify no direction uses forbidden generic language:
- "Buy Now" - FORBIDDEN
- "Get Started" - FORBIDDEN (unless paired with specific benefit)
- "Submit" - FORBIDDEN
- "Click Here" - FORBIDDEN
- "Act Now" - FORBIDDEN (unless with specific reason)
- "Order" alone - FORBIDDEN

**Step 5:** Cross-check that personalization is present in EVERY direction. The +202% lift from personalized CTAs requires that every direction includes at least one of: product name, specific benefit, savings amount, audience identity, or guarantee duration.

## Quality Criteria
- Every CTA has 3+ specific language directions
- Every direction names the emotional appeal type
- Every direction includes a personalization vector
- Every direction is specific enough for LP-14 to draft copy (not vague)
- Zero forbidden generic CTA language
- Inline risk reversal specified where appropriate
- Generic language check passes

## Failure Modes
- Product name or benefit not available -> Use category-level personalization: "your [category] solution" or "your daily [benefit] supply." Flag as `needs_product_details`.
- Appeal categories from 1.3 don't fit the trigger context -> Override with context-appropriate appeals. Document: "Strategy assigned [X] appeal but trigger context better suits [Y] appeal. Overriding for this placement."
- Fewer than 3 distinct directions possible for a CTA -> Ensure at least 3 by combining appeal types: Desire+Specificity hybrid, Transformation+Belonging hybrid, etc.
