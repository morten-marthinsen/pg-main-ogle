# 2.2 — Ingredient Deep-Dive Writer

**Skill:** LP-09-benefits-features
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Write ingredient deep-dive sections for Type A supplement/health pages. Each ingredient gets a dedicated sub-section with dose, mechanism of action, clinical evidence, and felt benefit. This is the education layer that transforms skepticism into belief through specificity and transparency.
**Effort Level:** max
**Dependencies:** `ingredient-strategy.md` (1.3), `dfwbp-structure-plan.md` (1.2), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `packages-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `ingredient-deep-dives.md`

## Pre-Execution Gate

**This microskill runs ONLY when:**
- At least one section is classified as `ingredient_deep_dive` in 1.1
- ingredient-strategy.md status is ACTIVE (not N/A)
- At least one ingredient has `CONFIRMED` dose status

**If no ingredients have confirmed doses:** Write `ingredient-deep-dives.md` with content: "BLOCKED — all ingredient doses are DOSE_MISSING. Cannot write accurate deep-dives. Escalated to human." Do NOT write ingredient deep-dives with unconfirmed doses.

---

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Load BIOpt specimen** (LP-SPEC-10) for ingredient section structure. This is the gold standard for supplement ingredient deep-dives — 23 sections, mechanism-led, with clinical citations.

**Step 2: Review ingredient strategy cards** from 1.3. Each card provides the scaffold (dose, mechanism, citation, felt benefit, presentation priority).

**Step 3: Calibrate language level** based on audience health literacy:
- Low literacy: Simple analogies, no scientific terms, "in other words" translations
- Medium literacy: Some scientific terms with explanations, mechanism described functionally
- High literacy: Scientific terms acceptable, mechanism described precisely, citations in full format

**Step 4: Read hero threading anchor.** Ingredient sections must feel like part of the same page as the hero. If the hero promised "deeper sleep in 7 days," every ingredient must connect back to sleep.

## Generation Execution

### Per-Ingredient Deep-Dive Structure

Write each ingredient in this format:

```markdown
## [Ingredient Name] — [Dose per serving]

[1-2 sentence hook: Why this ingredient matters. Connect to the audience's pain point or desire. Open with the BENEFIT, not the ingredient name.]

**How it works:**
[Mechanism of action in 2-3 sentences. Plain language — accessible to the target audience. Use analogies where helpful. Explain WHAT the ingredient does in the body and WHY that produces the benefit.]

**The evidence:**
[Specific clinical citation in format: "[Journal/Source Name], [Year]: [Key finding with specific number]." If multiple studies, list the strongest 1-2. If CITATION_GAP: "Currently under active research — [what existing evidence suggests]." Never fabricate citations.]

**What you feel:**
[The felt benefit in customer language — what changes in their daily life. Specific, experiential, picturable. Connect to the primary desire from the audience profile.]
```

### Presentation Order

Follow the order from ingredient-strategy.md (1.3):
1. Hero ingredient (most directly connected to headline promise) — gets the longest treatment
2. Supporting ingredients — standard treatment
3. Secondary ingredients — condensed treatment or combined into a "complete formula" closing

### Section Opening and Closing

**Section opening (before first ingredient):**
```markdown
## What's Inside [Product Name] — And Why Every Ingredient Matters

[1-2 sentences: Frame the section. Not "here are the ingredients" but "here's WHY these specific ingredients, at these specific doses, produce [the promised benefit]."]
```

**Section closing (after last ingredient):**
```markdown
### The Complete Formula

[2-3 sentences: Tie all ingredients together into a single coherent benefit story. Reference the hero promise. Reinforce why the COMBINATION matters, not just individual ingredients.]
```

### Condensed Format (Type B or Hybrid Below-Fold)

If the page type is Hybrid or if a section needs a condensed ingredient treatment:

```markdown
### [Ingredient Name] ([Dose])
[1 sentence mechanism] — [1 sentence felt benefit]. [Citation if available.]
```

## Quality Criteria
- Every ingredient with confirmed dose has a deep-dive section
- Doses match exactly what the brief states (not estimated, not rounded)
- Mechanism of action is in plain language appropriate to audience health literacy
- Clinical citations include journal/source name + year + numerical finding
- Felt benefits are in customer language (not clinical outcomes)
- Section opening frames WHY, not just WHAT
- Complete formula close ties all ingredients to the single hero promise
- Tone matches hero threading anchor
- Zero forbidden words from anti-slop list

## Failure Modes
- Writing a deep-dive for an ingredient with DOSE_MISSING -> BLOCKED. Skip that ingredient and note in output.
- Mechanism written as "helps support" without explaining HOW -> The mechanism must explain the actual process. "Helps support sleep" is not a mechanism. "Reduces cortisol by 28% by modulating the HPA axis — your body's stress thermostat" is a mechanism.
- Fabricating a clinical citation -> SEVERITY 10 FAILURE. If no study exists in the proof assets, flag as CITATION_GAP. Never invent journal names, years, or findings.
- All ingredients have the same "what you feel" language -> Each ingredient must produce a DISTINCT felt benefit. If two ingredients both "help you sleep," differentiate: one reduces time-to-sleep, the other increases deep sleep duration.
- Section reads like a supplement facts panel -> This is copy, not a spec sheet. Each ingredient must tell a micro-story: hook -> mechanism -> evidence -> felt change.
