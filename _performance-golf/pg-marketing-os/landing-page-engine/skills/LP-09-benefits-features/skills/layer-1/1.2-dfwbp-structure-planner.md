# 1.2 — D-F-W-B-P Structure Planner

**Skill:** LP-09-benefits-features
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Plan the complete D-F-W-B-P chain for every deliverable, ingredient, or feature that will appear in benefit sections. This is the structural scaffold that ensures no element reaches Layer 2 without all five components mapped. Proof gaps are identified HERE, not discovered during writing.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `packages-loaded.md` (0.1), `benefit-section-classification.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `dfwbp-structure-plan.md`

---

  product_data:
    ingredients: "[list with doses if supplement]"

## Execution Logic

1. **List every distinct element** that needs a D-F-W-B-P chain:
   - For supplement pages: one chain per ingredient
   - For SaaS/info pages: one chain per feature
   - For all pages: one chain per major deliverable (product, bonuses, components)

2. **For each element, plan all 5 components:**

   **D (Deliverable):** What does the customer get? This is the tangible thing.
   - Must be concrete and specific
   - Examples: "7-form magnesium complex," "real-time analytics dashboard," "12-week golf training program"

   **F (Feature):** What is the technical attribute that matters?
   - Must be specific enough to differentiate from alternatives
   - Examples: "500mg KSM-66 ashwagandha (full-spectrum root extract)," "cross-channel attribution across 12+ ad platforms," "video swing analysis with AI feedback"

   **W (Why):** Why does this feature matter to the customer?
   - This is the BRIDGE — most often the missing component
   - Must connect Feature to felt Benefit
   - Must explain the mechanism or logic, not just assert importance
   - Examples: "Most supplements use cheap oxide forms your body can't absorb. KSM-66 is the most clinically studied form with 22+ human trials."

   **B (Benefit):** What outcome does the customer actually experience?
   - Must be in the customer's language, not technical/clinical language
   - Must be specific and picturable (not "feel better" or "improved health")
   - Examples: "Fall asleep in under 20 minutes instead of tossing for an hour," "Cut wasted ad spend by $200-500/day within the first week"

   **P (Proof):** What evidence makes this real?
   - Source from proof-architecture.json first
   - Must include: source name, year, and key numerical finding
   - If unavailable: flag as `PROOF_GAP` with reason
   - Examples: "2019 Nutrients journal study: multi-form magnesium improved intracellular levels 2.4x vs. single-form"

3. **Identify all PROOF_GAPs** — elements where no proof data is available from upstream packages:
   - Count total gaps
   - For each gap: state what type of proof would be ideal (clinical study, customer data, expert endorsement, usage statistic)
   - Escalation: if >30% of elements have PROOF_GAPs, flag for human review before proceeding to Layer 2

4. **Map each element to its section** from the classification (1.1). Verify every section has at least one D-F-W-B-P chain assigned.

5. **Write `dfwbp-structure-plan.md`** with the complete plan.

## Quality Criteria
- Every ingredient/feature/deliverable has a planned D-F-W-B-P chain
- D and F components sourced from brief data (not invented)
- W (Why) bridge is planned with enough specificity to guide generation (not just "because it's important")
- B (Benefit) is in customer language, not clinical/technical
- P (Proof) is sourced from upstream data or explicitly flagged as PROOF_GAP
- Every benefit section has at least one element assigned
- PROOF_GAP rate calculated and escalation triggered if >30%

## Failure Modes
- Element has no Feature data in brief -> Flag as `needs_data` on F component. The element can still proceed but Feature will be generic.
- Proof assigned from proof-architecture.json but it's a testimonial, not a study -> Testimonials are valid proof. Record type as "testimonial" not "clinical study."
- Why bridge is just "because it works" -> Insufficient. The Why must explain the mechanism or logic. If the model cannot articulate the Why, flag for human input.
- All Benefits sound the same -> Each Benefit must be distinct. If multiple elements produce the same felt outcome, differentiate by dimension (sleep vs. energy vs. mood for different magnesium forms).
