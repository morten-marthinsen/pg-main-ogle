# 3.2 — Loop Audit (Type A Only)

**Skill:** LP-07-hero-section
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Systematically identify, count, and verify all curiosity loops in the final lead copy. Confirm 3+ loops are open at the end of the lead. Confirm 0 loops are closed within the lead. This is a binary pass/fail gate.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `lead-final.md` (3.1 — revised lead based on human selection notes) OR `lead-draft.md` if approved as-is
**Output:** `loop-audit.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  lead_text: "[full text of the final/revised lead]"
  page_type: "[type_a | hybrid]"
  planned_loops: "[3 loops identified in 1.2 — verify these were implemented]"
```

---

## Loop Detection Protocol

**Step 1: Read the lead completely without marking anything.**

**Step 2: Identify all questions the reader is left with at the end of the lead.**
For each question: Can it be answered from information within the lead alone?
- YES (answerable from lead) → Loop is CLOSED → FAIL condition
- NO (requires reading further) → Loop is OPEN → count it

**Step 3: Map each open loop to its type** (from the 6 loop types in AGENT.md):
- Story Loop
- Revelation Loop
- Mechanism Loop
- Villain Loop
- Proof Loop
- Identification Loop

**Step 4: Find the sentence that OPENS each loop.** Quote it exactly.

**Step 5: Identify where each loop RESOLVES** (which page section, NOT the lead).

**Step 6: Check for CLOSED loops within the lead.**
A closed loop occurs when:
- A question is raised AND answered in the same paragraph
- The mechanism is named AND explained
- The root cause is stated AND the solution is given
- The villain is named AND indicted
- A promised proof is mentioned AND delivered

**Step 7: Tally.** PASS requires ≥3 open loops AND 0 closed loops.

---

## Partial-Loop Detection (Difficult Cases)

Some loops appear to be open but are actually "soft closed" — partially answered in a way that reduces urgency to continue reading.

**Soft close patterns to catch:**
- "There's a reason [X] happens... [explains the reason fully in the next sentence]" → CLOSED
- "Most people don't know about [X]... but it's actually [explains X]" → CLOSED
- "The real cause is [names root cause]... [doesn't explain, just names]" → depends on whether naming is enough to satisfy curiosity (usually: naming alone = still open)

**Judgment rule:** If a reasonably intelligent reader could stop reading the lead and feel they've gotten the core information → loop is closed. If they feel they've been tantalized but still need to scroll → loop is open.

---

## Loop Strengthening Guidance (If Fewer Than 3 Open Loops)

If audit reveals only 1-2 open loops, provide specific fix guidance:

**For Story Lead — add loops by:**
- Adding a "what you don't know yet" sentence before a key revelation
- Inserting a bridge: "But to understand why that works, you first need to know something about [topic]..."
- Delaying the story's key moment: "I'll come back to what [character] discovered in just a minute. But first..."

**For Promise Lead — add loops by:**
- Adding specificity to the mechanism tease: "It involves [process name] — something I'll explain in full detail below"
- Adding a proof tease: "And there's clinical data behind this that will probably surprise you"

**For Probing Question Lead — add loops by:**
- Adding a counterintuitive reframe without explaining it: "The reason none of those worked has nothing to do with [what they think]"
- Adding a promise of specific future content: "What I'm going to show you is exactly how [character/audience] fixed this — and why it works when nothing else does"

## Quality Criteria
- Every open loop has an exact quote identified (not "there's a loop about the mechanism")
- Every loop has its resolution section identified
- Partial/soft closes explicitly reviewed
- Result is binary PASS or FAIL with specific revision guidance if FAIL

## Failure Modes
- Lead has no explicit loop-opening language → Likely a closed-loop lead (everything explained) — FAIL, revise with specific additions
- Loop count is 2 → FAIL — add one more loop. Use strengthening guidance above.
- Loop is opened and partially answered → Judge: does partial answer satisfy or intensify curiosity? If satisfies → revise to withhold
