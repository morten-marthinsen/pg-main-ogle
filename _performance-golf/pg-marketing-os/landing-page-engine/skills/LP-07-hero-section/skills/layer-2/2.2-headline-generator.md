# 2.2 — Headline Generator (3-Lens System)

**Skill:** LP-07-hero-section
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Generate ≥9 headline candidates using the 3-lens system. Score all candidates. Identify the top 5 for human selection. This is the highest-leverage creative output in the entire Landing Page Engine.
**Effort Level:** max
**Dependencies:** `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `headline-type-confirmed.md` (1.1), `copy-assets.md` (0.2)
**Output:** `headline-candidates.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  headline_type: "[T1-T7 — confirmed in 1.1]"
  specimens_loaded: "[from specimens-loaded.md — hold verbatim in context]"
  copy_assets:
    headline_territory: "[from copy-assets.md]"
    mechanism_name: "[if available]"
    root_cause_anchor: "[if available]"
    primary_promise: "[if available]"
    audience_pain_quotes: "[verbatim quotes from research if available]"
    fssit_candidates: "[if available from CopywritingEngine research]"
  audience_profile: "[from page-brief.json]"
  vertical: "[from page-brief.json]"
  anti_voice: "[from page-brief.json — what to avoid]"
```

---

## Pre-Generation Protocol (Execute Before Writing Any Headline)

**Step 1: Load specimens into active context**
Hold the 3 loaded headline specimens verbatim. These are the structural attractors.
Before writing Headline 1, explicitly read each specimen and extract: What specific technique does this headline use? How does it create curiosity without explaining? How specific is the claim?

**Step 2: Read copy assets**
Read all available copy assets. If mechanism name exists → it must appear in at least 2 headline candidates. If research pain quotes exist → extract 2-3 verbatim phrases that could anchor a Lens 2 candidate.

**Step 3: Review anti-slop word list**
Before writing: mentally scan the forbidden words list. Commit to producing zero instances.

**Step 4: State the core promise in one sentence**
Before generating: write "The core promise of this product is: [statement]." This anchors all candidates to the actual offer.

---

## Generation Execution

### LENS 1: The Converter (Direct Response Formula)

**Perspective:** Direct response optimization. What proven formula works best for this headline type + vertical combination?

**Instruction:** Apply the confirmed headline type (T1–T7) using classic DR formulas. Be maximally specific — numbers, timeframes, named mechanisms, named credentials. No vague claims.

**Generate 3 candidates:**

```
Lens1-A: Apply T[X] formula at maximum specificity
Lens1-B: Apply T[X] formula with social proof anchor (if applicable)
Lens1-C: Apply T[X] formula with mechanism name (if available) or credentialed discovery framing
```

**After generating:** Score each against 7 criteria. Note: Converter lens usually scores highest on Specificity and Believability, lower on Freshness.

### LENS 2: The Empath (Voice of Customer)

**Perspective:** Mirror the audience's own language. Build the headline from how THEY describe the problem and desired outcome, not how the brand describes the product.

**Instruction:** Use verbatim phrases from research pain quotes where available. If no research quotes, infer from the pain profile language in page-brief.json. The test: would the target reader feel "finally someone said what I'm thinking"?

**Generate 3 candidates:**

```
Lens2-A: Mirror the primary pain — their exact language + promise of resolution
Lens2-B: Mirror the failed solution experience — "you've tried X, here's why it failed"
Lens2-C: Mirror the identity/desire — speak to who they want to become, not just what they want to have
```

**After generating:** Score each. Empath lens usually scores highest on Relevance and Voice Match, sometimes lower on Specificity.

### LENS 3: The Skeptic (Credibility First)

**Perspective:** A suspicious, burned-before reader. Every word is questioned. The headline must earn its claim.

**Instruction:** Lead with proof, specifics, or counterintuitive truths. Avoid anything that sounds like marketing. The test: would this headline make a cynical 55-year-old woman think "I've never heard it put that way before — tell me more"?

**Generate 3 candidates:**

```
Lens3-A: Counterintuitive truth — contradict what the audience believes (safely, accurately)
Lens3-B: Proof-first — open with a specific study, statistic, or named discovery
Lens3-C: Authority anchor — named expert or institution + specific finding
```

**After generating:** Score each. Skeptic lens usually scores highest on Believability and Freshness, sometimes lower on Curiosity Gap.

---

## Scoring Rubric

Score every candidate on all 7 criteria. Calculate weighted score.

| Criterion | Weight | 1 (Poor) | 5 (Average) | 10 (Excellent) |
|-----------|--------|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Specificity** | 25% | Generic claim, no numbers/names | Partial specifics | Specific number/name/timeframe/mechanism |
| **Relevance** | 20% | Could be for anyone | Partially targeted | Perfect self-selection for target audience |
| **Curiosity Gap** | 20% | Fully explained (closed loop) | Slight mystery | Tantalizing incomplete — reader MUST know more |
| **Believability** | 15% | Immediately dismissed | Requires some proof | Sounds immediately credible to skeptic |
| **Pull** | 10% | No urgency to read | Some draw | Compelling reason to stop and read now |
| **Freshness** | 5% | Identical to 5+ competitors | Somewhat unique | Original angle no competitor is using |
| **Voice Match** | 5% | Completely off-brand/tone | Partially aligned | Perfect alignment with voice-direction |

**Weighted score formula:**
`score = (S × 0.25) + (R × 0.20) + (C × 0.20) + (B × 0.15) + (P × 0.10) + (F × 0.05) + (V × 0.05)`

**Disqualification rules:**
- Specificity < 6.0 → DISQUALIFIED (generic)
- Believability < 6.0 → DISQUALIFIED (unbelievable claim)
- Any forbidden word from anti-slop list → DISQUALIFIED (revise and re-submit)

**Minimum to qualify for top-5 presentation:**
- Weighted score ≥7.0
- Both specificity and believability ≥6.0
- Zero forbidden words

---

## Refinement Round

After initial 9+ candidates are scored:

1. Identify the highest-scoring candidate from each lens (3 winners)
2. Each lens refines its winner by absorbing the best TECHNIQUE (not voice) from the other lenses' winners:
   - Lens 1 winner: absorb Empath's audience language technique OR Skeptic's proof anchor
   - Lens 2 winner: absorb Converter's specificity technique OR Skeptic's counterintuitive framing
   - Lens 3 winner: absorb Converter's DR formula structure OR Empath's emotional relevance
3. Generate 1 refined candidate per lens
4. Score refined candidates
5. Optional: Generate 1–2 hybrid candidates by combining best phrase-elements from multiple lenses

---

## Top 5 Selection

From all scored candidates (9 initial + 3 refined + 1-2 hybrids = 13–14 total):
- Rank all qualifying candidates by weighted score
- Select top 5 qualifying candidates
- Ensure top 5 includes diversity: at least 2 different headline types, at least 2 different lenses represented
- If top 5 are all from one lens → force inclusion of highest-scorer from other lenses (diversity requirement)

## Quality Criteria
- ≥9 candidates generated in Round 1 (3 per lens)
- All 9+ scored on all 7 criteria
- All forbidden words checked and absent
- Top 5 include ≥2 different headline types and ≥2 lenses
- Each top-5 candidate has a "why this works" explanation

## Failure Modes
- Fewer than 5 candidates qualify (too many disqualified) → Generate additional candidates with revised approach, not just minor variations
- All 5 candidates are from one lens → Force-include highest qualifiers from other lenses
- All candidates above 7.0 but very similar to each other → Generate more radical variations before presenting top 5
