# 1.1 — 7-Signal Page Type Classifier

**Skill:** LP-00-brief-classifier
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 1
**Purpose:** Run all 7 classification signals against the product brief and tally Type A vs. Type B signals. This is the primary classification mechanism — no classification happens without this file being executed.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `brief-load.md` (Layer 0 output)
**Output:** `classification-signals.md`


## The 7 Signals — Evaluation Criteria

For each signal, evaluate the brief and declare: `type_a`, `type_b`, or `neutral` with specific evidence from the brief.

### Signal 1: Offer Complexity
**Type A indicator:** The offer requires explanation to be valued — bonuses, stacked value, mechanism-dependent promise, high-ticket justification
**Type B indicator:** Simple single-product purchase. Value is self-evident from product name, image, and basic description.
**Neutral:** Could go either way

Evidence extraction question: *"Does a cold prospect need education before they can understand why this offer is worth buying?"*

### Signal 2: Price Point & Purchase Psychology
**Type A indicator:** AOV ≥$50 (single unit) or subscription ≥$45/month; OR product requires risk-removal conversation (high skepticism category)
**Type B indicator:** AOV $20–$60 range, single purchase, impulse-buyable with social proof
**Neutral:** $45–$70 range where both could apply

Evidence extraction question: *"At this price point, does the visitor need persuasion or just reassurance?"*

### Signal 3: Claim Complexity
**Type A indicator:** Claims that require a mechanism to be believed ("This compound activates the AMPK pathway to..."). A cold prospect would be skeptical without explanation.
**Type B indicator:** Claims that are self-evident or verifiable through reviews ("Customers say it helped their energy in 2 weeks")
**Neutral:** Claims that are moderately complex but reviewable

Evidence extraction question: *"Would a skeptical cold-traffic visitor believe the core claim without educational content?"*

### Signal 4: Audience Awareness Stage
**Type A indicator:** Audience awareness stage 1–3 (Unaware, Problem-Aware, Solution-Aware). They need education.
**Type B indicator:** Audience awareness stage 4–5 (Product-Aware, Most Aware). They know what they're looking for.
**Neutral:** Stage 3 (Solution-Aware) — borderline

Evidence extraction question: *"Does this audience already know products like this exist and work, or do they need convincing?"*

### Signal 5: Traffic Source
**Type A indicator:** Cold traffic — Facebook/Instagram ads (interest targeting), YouTube pre-roll, native ads, cold email, affiliate traffic
**Type B indicator:** Warm/hot traffic — Google Shopping, branded search, organic keyword search, influencer link traffic, email list (warm subscribers), retargeting
**Neutral:** Mixed traffic or unknown source

Evidence extraction question: *"Is the primary traffic source cold (no prior product awareness) or warm/hot (prior awareness or intent)?"*

### Signal 6: Competitive Landscape
**Type A indicator:** Market differentiation is through mechanism/story/angle (not price or formulation comparison). Competitors also use long-form. Category is supplement DR, financial, personal development.
**Type B indicator:** Market differentiation through formulation, price, brand, reviews, certifications. Competitors have ecomm PDPs. Category is clean DTC, food/bev, beauty.
**Neutral:** Category has both types of competitors

Evidence extraction question: *"Do the best-performing competitors in this category use long-form sales pages or ecomm product pages?"*

### Signal 7: Category Norm
**Type A indicator:** Industry standard is long-form sales page. Examples: health supplements DR (Agora, Clickbank), information products, financial newsletters, coaching programs.
**Type B indicator:** Industry standard is product page. Examples: DTC brands (Glossier, AG1 modern site), Amazon-adjacent supplements, Shopify-first brands, food/beverage brands.
**Neutral:** Category has both types in top performers

Evidence extraction question: *"What do the top-converting pages look like in this specific market niche?"*

## Quality Criteria
- All 7 signals evaluated
- Each signal has specific evidence from the brief (not generic)
- Tally accurate
- No signal left blank or "skipped"

## Failure Modes
- Brief too vague to evaluate a signal → Mark as `neutral` with note "insufficient information in brief"
- Signal contradicts others → Mark the contradiction in the notes, let 1.3 resolve
