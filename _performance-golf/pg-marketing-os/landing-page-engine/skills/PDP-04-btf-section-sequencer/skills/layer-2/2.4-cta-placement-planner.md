# 2.4 — CTA Placement Planner

**Skill:** PDP-04-btf-section-sequencer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Map all mid-page CTA placements and buy-box return points throughout the BTF sequence. PDP pages need at least 2 mid-page CTA placements (not counting the sticky ATC bar). These are NOT hard-sell interruptions — they are natural conversion opportunities placed at points where a visitor who has been convinced by preceding sections can act without scrolling back to the buy box. This microskill plans WHERE CTAs go. The sticky ATC bar is planned separately in 2.5.
**Effort Level:** medium (opus)
**Dependencies:** `optional-decisions.md` (1.4 — locked section list with positions), `section-ordering.md` (1.2 — zone map), `word-count-plan.md` (2.1 — for scroll depth estimation)
**Output:** `cta-placement-plan.md`

---

```yaml

  zone_map: "[from section-ordering.md — scannable/authority/education/comparison/decision zones]"
```

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Calculate cumulative scroll depth at each section boundary:
```
For each section position N:
  cumulative_words_after_N = sum(word_count_targets[1..N])
  scroll_percentage_after_N = cumulative_words_after_N / total_btf_word_count * 100
```

**Step 2:** Identify natural CTA placement opportunities using scroll depth milestones from AGENT.md:
- **25% scroll depth:** First mid-page CTA opportunity — after scannable/authority zone
- **50% scroll depth:** Second mid-page CTA opportunity — after education zone
- **75% scroll depth:** Third mid-page CTA opportunity — after reviews/evidence zone

**Step 3:** For each CTA placement opportunity, determine:
- **After which section** the CTA should appear (the section that naturally concludes a persuasion arc)
- **CTA type:** What kind of CTA is appropriate at this point
  - `add_to_cart` — Direct purchase CTA (button linking to buy box or adding to cart)
  - `scroll_to_buy_box` — Anchor link that scrolls to above-fold buy box
  - `learn_more` — Soft CTA that scrolls to next section (for undecided visitors)
  - `email_capture` — Secondary conversion for visitors not ready to buy

**Step 4:** Apply CTA placement principles:
- **First CTA (25% zone):** Place after the last scannable/authority section. CTA type: scroll_to_buy_box or add_to_cart. This catches visitors who were convinced by proof and don't need education.
- **Second CTA (50% zone):** Place after the education zone (Problem/Solution/Why or Ingredients). CTA type: add_to_cart. This catches visitors who needed the mechanism/ingredient story.
- **Third CTA (75% zone):** Place after Reviews or The Proof. CTA type: add_to_cart. This catches visitors who needed volume social proof or data.
- **Final CTA:** After FAQ (last section) — implicit since visitor returns to buy box or sticky bar

**Step 5:** Ensure minimum 2 mid-page CTA placements (not counting sticky bar). If scroll depth math only supports 1 natural placement (very short PDP), force a second CTA at a reasonable position.

**Step 6:** For each CTA, specify:
- Position (after section #)
- CTA type
- Button text suggestion
- Visual treatment (inline button, banner, floating element)
- Rationale (why here — what persuasion arc concludes at this point)

## Quality Criteria
- At least 2 mid-page CTA placements mapped (not counting sticky bar)
- Each CTA has a specific type, button text suggestion, visual treatment, and rationale
- CTA positions correspond to natural persuasion arc conclusions (not randomly placed)
- No scroll gap > 40% between consecutive CTA opportunities
- First CTA within the first 30% of the page
- CTA types are appropriate for their position (early = scroll_to_buy_box or add_to_cart; late = add_to_cart)

## Failure Modes
- Fewer than 2 mid-page CTAs -> HALT. Add CTA placements until minimum met.
- CTA placed in the middle of a section (not after) -> Reposition to section boundary.
- All CTAs clustered in one zone (e.g., all in last 25%) -> Redistribute across page depth.
- CTA type is "learn_more" for the final CTA -> Switch to add_to_cart. The final CTA should be a direct conversion opportunity.
- No rationale for CTA position -> HALT. Each placement must justify why THIS section boundary is a natural conversion moment.
- Gap > 40% between CTAs -> Add intermediate CTA to reduce gap.
