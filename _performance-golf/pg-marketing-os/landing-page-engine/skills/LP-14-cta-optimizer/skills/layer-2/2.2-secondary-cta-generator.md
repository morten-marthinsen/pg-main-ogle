# 2.2 — Secondary CTA Generator

> Version: 1.0
> Layer: 2
> Model: sonnet
> Input: cta-type-plan.md, primary-cta-candidates.md, cta-architecture-loaded.md
> Output: secondary-cta-candidates.md

**Skill:** LP-14-cta-optimizer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Generate position-specific CTA variants for every CTA position on the page EXCEPT the primary/terminal CTA (handled by 2.1). Each position gets 2-3 variants tailored to its placement context — what the reader just finished reading, what emotional state they are in, and what page section follows.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `cta-type-plan.md` (1.1), `primary-cta-candidates.md` (2.1), `cta-architecture-loaded.md` (0.2)
**Output:** `secondary-cta-candidates.md`

## Execution Logic

1. **Read `cta-type-plan.md`** to get the full position-appeal mapping.

2. **Read `primary-cta-candidates.md`** to understand the primary CTA language and tone. Secondary CTAs must be consistent with the primary but not identical.

3. **For each secondary CTA position, generate 2-3 variants:**

   **Above-Fold CTA** (if distinct from primary):
   - Appears before reader has consumed any content
   - Must be the LOWEST friction CTA on the page
   - Cold traffic: softer language ("See How It Works" / "Watch the Video")
   - Hot traffic: direct but not aggressive ("Get Started" / "Start My Trial")
   - Generate 2-3 variants matching the assigned appeal from cta-type-plan.md

   **Mid-Page CTA** (appears after problem/mechanism section):
   - Reader has just learned WHY they have the problem
   - Consequence Awareness or Specificity appeals work strongest here
   - Can reference the mechanism or root cause just explained
   - Generate 2-3 variants
   - Example: "Stop the Real Cause of [Problem]" / "Fix [Mechanism Name] Now"

   **Post-Proof CTA** (appears after clinical data or authority endorsement):
   - Reader has just seen evidence — credibility is high
   - Social Proof or Confidence appeals work strongest here
   - Can reference the proof just presented
   - Generate 2-3 variants
   - Example: "Join [X] Customers Who Saw Results" / "Get the Same Formula Doctors Recommend"

   **Post-Testimonial CTA** (appears after customer stories):
   - Reader has just heard from peers — belonging is activated
   - Social Proof or Empath appeals work strongest here
   - Can reference the testimonial just read
   - Generate 2-3 variants
   - Example: "Get Results Like [Customer Name]" / "Join the Community"

   **Post-Offer/Pricing CTA** (appears after value stack or pricing reveal):
   - Reader has just seen what they get and what it costs
   - Value or Confidence appeals work strongest here
   - Should reference the value stack or savings
   - Generate 2-3 variants
   - Example: "Get All [X] Items — Just [Price]" / "Add the Complete Package"

4. **Consistency check:** Each secondary CTA should be distinct from the primary CTA in wording but consistent in:
   - Voice/tone (same register as primary)
   - Promise reference (same core promise from LP-07)
   - Offer reference (same single product/offer)

5. **Score each secondary CTA** using the same 6-criteria rubric as primary CTAs, but with adjusted weights:
   - Threading weight increased to 20% (position context matters more)
   - Freshness weight decreased to 5% (consistency with primary is more important)

6. **Write `secondary-cta-candidates.md`** with all position-specific variants.

## Quality Criteria
- Every CTA position from cta-type-plan.md has 2-3 variants
- Each variant is position-appropriate (references what the reader just consumed)
- Voice/tone consistent with primary CTA candidates
- All button text 3-8 words
- Variants are distinct from each other (not minor rewording of the same CTA)
- Appeal assignments from cta-type-plan.md honored

## Failure Modes
- All positions get the same CTA text -> Each position must be tailored to its context. A post-proof CTA and a post-testimonial CTA should feel different.
- Secondary CTAs inconsistent with primary in tone -> If primary uses "my" possessive, secondaries should too. If primary is formal, secondaries should match.
- Position context ignored -> "Get Started" works above-fold but is oddly generic after a detailed proof section. Adapt to context.
- More than 8 words in button text -> Hard limit. Revise to fit.
