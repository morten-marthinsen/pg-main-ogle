# 2.2 — Content Density Auditor

> **Skill:** LP-16-mobile-audit
> **Layer:** 2 (Mobile Audit)
> **Model:** opus
> **Input:** `assembled-page-load.md`, `mobile-criteria.md`
> **Output:** `content-density-audit.md`
> **Covers Dimensions:** Content Density (primary), Navigation (partial)

---

## PURPOSE

Scan every section of the assembled page and identify elements that create walls of text, scroll traps, or poor scannability on mobile screens. A paragraph that reads fine on a 1440px desktop monitor becomes a 12-line wall of text on a 375px phone screen. This auditor catches those failures before they cost conversions.

**POSITIONAL REINFORCEMENT:** You are evaluating content as it renders on a 375px mobile screen. A paragraph of 45 words becomes roughly 3 lines on mobile — that is the maximum before you flag it. Desktop readability is irrelevant. Mobile scannability is everything.

---

## PROCEDURE

### Step 1: Section-by-Section Text Analysis

For EVERY section in the assembled page, evaluate:

| Metric | Threshold | Flag When |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Paragraph length | ~45 words / ~3 mobile lines max | Any paragraph > 45 words without a break |
| Section word count | < 500 words ideal | Any section > 500 words without collapse/accordion recommendation |
| Visual breaks | Every 2-3 paragraphs | Section has 4+ consecutive paragraphs without image, icon, divider, or whitespace |
| List format | Bullets for 3+ items | Items listed with inline commas instead of bullets |
| Line count (mobile estimate) | Calculate at 375px, 16px font | Any continuous text block > 8 mobile lines |

### Step 2: Identify Scroll Traps

A "scroll trap" is a section where the user must scroll through extensive text with no visual reward, no CTA, and no clear endpoint. Scroll traps cause abandonment on mobile.

**Scroll trap indicators:**
- Section > 500 words with no images, no CTAs, no visual breaks
- Section > 3 mobile screen heights with no interaction point
- Multiple consecutive text-heavy sections (compounding density)

### Step 3: Evaluate Content Collapse Needs

For pages with long sections:

| Page Type | Collapse Trigger | Collapse Method |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Type A | Any section > 500 words | Accordion (show first 2-3 lines, expand on tap) |
| Type B | Ingredient/science sections > 500 words | Accordion or tab interface |
| Hybrid | Education sections below fold > 500 words | Accordion with section headers as labels |

### Step 4: Check Visual Break Rhythm

Count the ratio of visual elements to text blocks across the full page:
- **Healthy ratio:** At least 1 visual break (image, icon, divider, badge, CTA button) per 3 text blocks
- **Unhealthy ratio:** 5+ consecutive text blocks with no visual element

### Step 5: Navigation Impact Assessment

Dense content impacts navigation:
- Can the user quickly scan to find what they want?
- Are section headers visible and descriptive?
- Is there a clear "what comes next" signal at each section boundary?

## CRITICAL RULES

1. **Measure at 375px width, 16px body font.** This gives approximately 7-8 words per line on mobile. A 45-word paragraph = ~6 lines = readable. A 90-word paragraph = ~12 lines = wall of text.
2. **Every flagged section needs a specific fix.** "Reduce text" is not a fix. "Break the 120-word ingredient description in Section 5 into 4 separate accordion panels, one per ingredient, each <=40 words" is a fix.
3. **Consecutive dense sections compound.** Section 5 at 400 words followed by Section 6 at 450 words is worse than either alone — the user has been scrolling through text for 6+ screen heights with no visual reward.
4. **"Unspecified" visual breaks = assume zero.** If the assembled page does not specify images, dividers, or icons within a section, assume the section is pure text for density calculation purposes.
5. **Type A pages are at higher risk** for content density failures because they are naturally longer (4,000-18,000 words). This does not mean they get a pass — it means they need MORE aggressive content collapse and visual break recommendations.
