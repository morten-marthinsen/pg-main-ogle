# 2.4 — Before/After Caption Writer

**Skill:** LP-10-social-proof-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Write transformation captions for before/after image pairs. Each caption tells a specific person's story — the before state, the after state, the timeframe, and one sentence in their own voice. Before/after captions are among the most conversion-impactful proof elements because they combine visual evidence with narrative context.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `proof-slot-analysis.md` (1.1), `proof-slots-loaded.md` (0.2), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `before-after-captions.md`

## Caption Structure

Every before/after caption follows this structure:

```
[NAME], [AGE or QUALIFIER] — [TIMEFRAME]

BEFORE: [Specific before state — 1 sentence]
AFTER: [Specific after state with measurable result — 1 sentence]

"[1 sentence in the person's voice — the most emotionally resonant moment]"
```

### Type A (Long-Form Sales Page) Captions
- 40-60 words per caption
- Written as mini-stories with emotional texture
- Before state should be vivid and relatable
- After state must include a measurable result
- Quote should capture the turning point or emotional impact

**Example:**
```
Sarah M., 47 — 12 weeks

BEFORE: "Avoided mirrors. My joints ached every morning. I'd tried 3 different programs and quit them all by week 2."
AFTER: "Down 23 lbs. Morning walks are back. My daughter says I look 10 years younger."

"Week 3 was when I stopped saying 'if this works' and started saying 'when I hit my goal.'"
```

### Type B (Ecomm/PDP) Captions
- 20-35 words per caption
- Scannable — name, result, timeframe in one line
- No extended narrative
- Quote optional (include only if powerful and short)

**Example:**
```
Michael T., 52 — 8 weeks
Lost 19 lbs | Waist: 38" → 34" | "My doctor couldn't believe the bloodwork."
```

---

## Execution Rules

1. **Every caption MUST include a specific measurable result.** Acceptable specifics:
   - Weight: "Lost 23 lbs" (not "lost weight")
   - Measurement: "Waist 38" to 34""
   - Timeframe: "In 8 weeks" (not "in a few weeks")
   - Performance: "Handicap 15 to 9" (not "improved my game")
   - Metric: "Cholesterol dropped 40 points" (not "healthier numbers")

2. **Before state must be relatable** — the target reader should recognize their own experience.

3. **After state must be aspirational but believable** — the target reader should think "I could do that."

4. **The quote must be human** — conversational, specific to a moment, not a polished endorsement.

5. **Never fabricate before/after data.** If specifics are unavailable, write placeholder captions with sourcing flags.

6. **Match demographics to the target audience** — the people in before/afters should look like the reader's peers.

---

## Execution Steps

1. **For each before/after slot,** check content source status:
   - If content available: Write caption using real data
   - If `requires_sourcing`: Write placeholder with specific sourcing requirements

2. **Write each caption** following the Type A or Type B structure.

3. **Verify specificity** of every caption — must contain at least one measurable result.

4. **Verify variety** across all captions — different demographics, different outcome types, different timeframes.

5. **Verify emotional range** — not all captions should be dramatic transformations. Include at least one "quiet" success story (consistency, confidence, quality of life).

## Quality Criteria
- Every before/after slot has a caption (real or placeholder)
- Every caption contains at least one measurable result (number, metric, timeframe)
- Before states are relatable and specific (not generic "struggled with health")
- After states are aspirational and believable (not hyperbolic "completely transformed")
- Quotes sound human — conversational, moment-specific, not polished marketing language
- Demographic variety across captions (age, gender, location where applicable)
- Word counts within range (Type A: 40-60, Type B: 20-35)

## Failure Modes
- Vague before/after states ("Was unhealthy, now healthy") -> Both states must be specific and vivid.
- All captions showing dramatic transformations -> Include at least one moderate/quiet success story for believability.
- Fabricating before/after metrics for sourcing-required slots -> Write placeholders with clear sourcing flags. Never fabricate.
- Quotes that sound like marketing copy instead of real people -> Real quotes are specific to a moment, conversational, and often grammatically imperfect.
- All captions with same timeframe -> Vary timeframes to show both quick wins and sustained results.
