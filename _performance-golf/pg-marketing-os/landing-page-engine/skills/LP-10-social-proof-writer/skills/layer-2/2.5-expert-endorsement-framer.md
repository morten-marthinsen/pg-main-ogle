# 2.5 — Expert Endorsement Framer

**Skill:** LP-10-social-proof-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 5
**Purpose:** Write the credential context and framing copy for expert endorsements, clinical study references, and media mentions. The framing answers: "Why should I care what THIS person/institution says?" A named expert without context is wasted authority. The framing makes the authority FELT, not just stated.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `proof-slot-analysis.md` (1.1), `proof-slots-loaded.md` (0.2), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1), `specimen-proof-copy.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `expert-endorsements.md`

## Expert Framing Structure

### Expert Endorsement Format

```
[CREDENTIAL LINE]
[Full name], [Degree(s)] — [Primary institutional affiliation], [Relevant role/title]
[1-2 sentence relevance statement — why this expert's opinion matters for THIS product]

[ENDORSEMENT STATEMENT]
"[The expert's actual endorsement — verbatim or accurately paraphrased]"

[OPTIONAL: VERIFICATION SIGNAL]
[Link to publication / "Board-certified by [body]" / "Published in [journal]"]
```

**Example:**
```
Dr. Sarah Chen, MD, PhD — Board-Certified Endocrinologist
Associate Professor, Johns Hopkins School of Medicine
Lead researcher on the 2024 magnesium absorption study published in the Journal of Clinical Nutrition

"In 30 years of practice, I've never seen a magnesium formulation achieve this level of bioavailability.
The 7-form approach solves the absorption problem that single-form supplements cannot."

Published: Journal of Clinical Nutrition, Vol. 119, March 2024
```

### Clinical Study Reference Format

```
[STUDY HEADLINE — 1 line summarizing the finding]
[Study source: Journal name, date, sample size]
[Key finding — 1-2 sentences, specific numbers]
[Relevance to product — 1 sentence connecting study to product claim]
```

**Example:**
```
Double-Blind Study Confirms 43% Greater Absorption Rate
Published: Journal of Clinical Nutrition, March 2024 | N=312 participants | 12-week trial

Key finding: Participants using 7-form magnesium complex showed 43% greater serum magnesium
levels compared to single-form magnesium oxide at identical dosages.

This is the research behind [Product Name]'s multi-form formulation.
```

### Media Mention Format

```
[MEDIA LOGO INDICATOR] — [Publication Name]
"[Pull-quote from the article — the most relevant sentence]"
[Context: What the article was about — 1 sentence]
```

**Example:**
```
[Inc. Magazine Logo]
"[Brand] has quietly become the fastest-growing supplement company in America."
— Inc. 5000 Annual List, 2024 (Ranked #847)
```

## Credential Chain Rules by Vertical

| Vertical | Must Include | Adds Credibility | Damages Credibility |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Health/Supplement | Degree + institution + board certification | Published research, clinical trial experience | "Wellness influencer" without credentials |
| Finance | License type + firm + years + track record | AUM, specific fund performance | Unregistered advisor, no verifiable track record |
| Golf | Playing credentials + teaching credentials | Tour player coached, handicap impact data | Self-proclaimed expert without verifiable results |
| Info Products | Verifiable revenue/student outcomes + years | Named students with results, media features | Unverified income claims, bought testimonials |
| B2B/SaaS | Company role + company credibility + experience | Named clients, measurable results delivered | Title inflation at unknown companies |
| Technology | Technical credentials + publications + affiliations | Patents, conference keynotes, peer citations | Blog posts as "publications" |

---

## Execution Rules

1. **Full credential chain required.** Never write "Dr. Smith recommends it." Write "Dr. [Full Name], [Degrees] — [Institution], [Role], [Why relevant]."

2. **Relevance statement required.** Every expert framing must answer: "Why should the reader care what THIS expert thinks about THIS product?" The connection must be explicit, not implied.

3. **Never inflate credentials.** Use verifiable information only. If an expert's specific institution is unknown, note it as `requires_verification` rather than guessing.

4. **Quote accuracy.** Endorsement statements must be verbatim from inventory or clearly marked as paraphrased. Never fabricate expert quotes.

5. **Study references must include source, sample size, and key finding with specific numbers.** "Studies show..." is forbidden. Name the study.

6. **Media mentions must include the publication name and the specific claim/ranking.** "As seen in..." without specifics is forbidden.

---

## Execution Steps

1. **For each expert endorsement slot,** load the expert data from proof inventory.
2. **Build the full credential chain** — degree, institution, role, relevance.
3. **Write the relevance statement** — why THIS expert matters for THIS product.
4. **Format the endorsement** with the quote and optional verification signal.
5. **For each clinical study slot,** extract the study headline, source, sample size, key finding, and product relevance.
6. **For each media mention slot,** format with publication name, pull-quote, and context.
7. **If expert data is incomplete** (`requires_sourcing` or partial data): Write what is available and flag missing elements.

## Quality Criteria
- Every expert endorsement has a complete credential chain (degree + institution + role)
- Every expert has a relevance statement connecting them to the product
- Clinical studies include journal, date, sample size, and specific finding with numbers
- Media mentions include publication name and specific claim
- Zero credential inflation ("world-renowned" without verification, "leading expert" without institution)
- Zero unattributed studies ("studies show..." without citation)
- Quotes are clearly marked as verbatim, paraphrased, or requires_sourcing

## Failure Modes
- Writing "Dr. Smith recommends it" without credential context -> Full credential chain + relevance statement required.
- "Studies show that..." without naming the study -> Name the journal, date, sample size. If unknown, flag as requires_sourcing.
- Inflating credentials to sound more impressive -> Use only verifiable information. Unknown credentials = flag, not fabricate.
- Expert endorsement without relevance statement -> The reader must know WHY this expert's opinion matters. Not all MDs are relevant to all products.
- Media mention as "As featured in..." without the specific claim -> Include the pull-quote and context. The publication name alone is insufficient.
