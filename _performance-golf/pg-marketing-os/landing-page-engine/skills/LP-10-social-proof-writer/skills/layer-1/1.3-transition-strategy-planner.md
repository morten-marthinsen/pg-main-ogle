# 1.3 — Transition Strategy Planner

**Skill:** LP-10-social-proof-writer
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 3
**Purpose:** Plan the narrative transitions INTO and OUT OF each proof block. Each proof block needs a unique bridge that connects the preceding section to the proof and the proof to the next section. This planning step ensures no repeated transitions and contextually appropriate bridge copy.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `proof-slot-analysis.md` (1.1), `proof-slots-loaded.md` (0.2), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1), `specimen-proof-copy.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `transition-plan.md`

---

## Execution Logic

1. **List all proof blocks requiring transitions** from proof-slot-analysis.md.

2. **For each proof block, establish context:**
   - What section comes BEFORE this proof block? (narrative, mechanism, product intro, etc.)
   - What section comes AFTER this proof block? (more narrative, offer, CTA, another proof block, etc.)
   - What is the wave zone? (early, education, accumulation, close)
   - What proof types are IN this block? (testimonials, expert endorsements, before/afters, etc.)

3. **Apply transition strategy by wave zone:**

   | Wave Zone | IN Transition Strategy | OUT Transition Strategy |
   |-----------|----------------------|----------------------|
   | **Early** | Authority bridge — establish credibility context before proof | Curiosity bridge — proof validates, now reader wants to know HOW |
   | **Education** | Evidence bridge — science/mechanism claims need proof | Understanding bridge — proof makes the mechanism real, now explain the product |
   | **Accumulation** | Momentum bridge — "and it's not just [previous person]..." | Decision bridge — overwhelming proof propels toward buying decision |
   | **Close** | Final validation bridge — "one more thing before you decide..." | Action bridge — proof is done, time to act |

4. **Apply page-type rules:**
   - **Type A:** Transitions are narrative. Written as prose that flows from the previous section's voice. 1-3 sentences. Conversational register.
   - **Type B:** Transitions are structural. Section headers, not prose paragraphs. Brief, scan-friendly. May be implicit (no explicit transition needed if the visual layout signals the proof section).
   - **Hybrid:** Zone 1-2 use Type B transitions. Zone 3+ use Type A transitions.

5. **Plan unique angles for each transition** — no two IN transitions or OUT transitions should use the same technique:

   **IN Transition Techniques:**
   - The Humble Redirect: "I could keep explaining. But these people say it better than I ever could."
   - The Evidence Callback: "Remember the [mechanism/study] I mentioned? Here's what happened when real people tried it."
   - The Challenge: "Skeptical? Good. So were they."
   - The Volume Reveal: "In the last [timeframe], [exact number] people have tried [product]. Here's what they report."
   - The Story Tee-Up: "Let me introduce you to [Name]. [One sentence about who they are.]"
   - The Pattern Interrupt: "[Specific result]. [Specific result]. [Specific result]. See a pattern?"

   **OUT Transition Techniques:**
   - The Momentum Builder: "And [Name] isn't alone. But before I share more stories, there's something important you need to know about [next section topic]."
   - The Reader Mirror: "If any of those stories sound like yours, you're probably wondering [question that next section answers]."
   - The Pivot to Action: "The only difference between [Name] and you is that [Name] made a decision. Here's what that decision looks like:"
   - The Proof Summary: "[Count] people. [Count] results. One product. Now let me show you exactly what's inside."
   - The Next Wave Tease: "Those are the results. But there's one thing every single person above did first..."

6. **Verify uniqueness** — ensure no two proof blocks share the same IN technique or the same OUT technique.

7. **Write `transition-plan.md`.**

## Quality Criteria
- Every proof block has a planned IN and OUT transition
- No two blocks share the same IN technique or OUT technique
- Transition strategies are contextually appropriate (wave zone + preceding/following section content)
- Page type rules applied (Type A = prose, Type B = structural)
- LP-05's transition directions are incorporated (not overridden without rationale)
- Tone planned per transition (varies across the page — not monotone)

## Failure Modes
- All transitions planned as the same technique -> Diversify. Use the technique list above and assign different approaches to different blocks.
- Ignoring LP-05's transition direction -> LP-05 provides guidance. Use it as a starting point. Diverge only with explicit rationale documented.
- Planning prose transitions for Type B sections -> Type B proof sections use structural headers, not narrative paragraphs. Check page type before planning.
- Transition plan doesn't reference preceding/following section content -> The whole point of transitions is CONTEXT. Each transition must bridge FROM something specific TO something specific.
