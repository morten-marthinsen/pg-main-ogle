# 3.4 — Anti-Slop Check

**Skill:** PDP-05-social-proof-review-system
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Scan every piece of generated text in the review system for AI-telltale language — words, phrases, and structural patterns that signal machine authorship and destroy the illusion of authentic customer voice. A single "exceeded my expectations" in a review damages the credibility of the entire system. This is the authenticity health check — would a human reader suspect any of this content was AI-generated?
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `input-verification.md` (0.1), `featured-reviews.md` (2.1), `reviewer-personas.md` (2.3), `qa-section-architecture.md` (2.4), `review-card-template.md` (2.5), `volume-audit.md` (3.1), `specificity-check.md` (3.2), `badge-verification.md` (3.3)
**Output:** `anti-slop-check.md`

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Collect ALL generated text that requires anti-slop scanning:
- Featured review headlines (from 2.1)
- Featured review body text (from 2.1)
- Featured review pros (from 2.1)
- Featured review cons (from 2.1)
- General pool review samples (if specified in 1.1)
- Q&A questions (from 2.4)
- Q&A brand answers (from 2.4)
- Q&A community answers (from 2.4)
- Merchant response templates (from 2.5 / 1.1)
- Keyword filter chip labels (from 2.2 — brief check, less critical)

**Step 2:** Run the 10-point AI Telltale Word/Phrase Scan. Each item is a HARD FAIL if found anywhere in generated content:

| # | Telltale | Why It Fails | Common Locations |
|---|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| 1 | "revolutionary" | AI superlative — real customers don't talk this way | Review body, headlines |
| 2 | "game-changing" or "game changer" | AI superlative — overused in AI copy | Review body, headlines |
| 3 | "exceeded my expectations" or "exceeded expectations" | AI-telltale phrasing — real people say "better than I thought" or describe specific surprise | Review body |
| 4 | "I was skeptical but" or "I was skeptical at first" | AI cliche opener — real skeptics describe WHAT made them skeptical | Review body, opening lines |
| 5 | "transform" / "transformative" / "transformed my" | AI verb — real people say "changed," "fixed," "helped" | Review body, headlines |
| 6 | "unlock" / "unlocked" / "unlocking" | AI verb — no customer says "this product unlocked my potential" | Review body |
| 7 | "journey" (in product context) | AI filler — "my skincare journey" is AI; "I've been dealing with acne for 3 years" is human | Review body |
| 8 | "blown away" | AI superlative — overused in AI reviews | Review body, headlines |
| 9 | "truly" / "absolutely" / "simply" (as intensifiers) | AI intensifiers — real reviews use specifics instead of empty amplifiers | Review body, headlines, pros |
| 10 | "seamless" / "seamlessly" | AI smoothness word — real customers describe specific experiences, not abstract smoothness | Review body, Q&A answers |

**Step 3:** Run the structural pattern scan. These are patterns, not words — harder to detect but equally damaging:

| # | Pattern | Detection Method | Why It Fails |
|---|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| S1 | Uniform sentence length | Measure sentence length variance across each review. If standard deviation < 3 words across 4+ sentences -> FAIL | Real writing has wild variance (5-word sentence followed by 25-word sentence) |
| S2 | All reviews same tone | Compare emotional register across all featured reviews. If all are enthusiastic/positive with no variation -> FAIL | Real review sets include grudging praise, matter-of-fact reports, excited gushing — variety is human |
| S3 | Parallel structure across reviews | Check if multiple reviews use same sentence pattern (e.g., all start with "I've been using X for Y months and...") -> FAIL | AI defaults to templated openings |
| S4 | Perfect grammar in all reviews | If every review has zero grammatical informality (no fragments, no dashes, no ellipses, no colloquialisms) -> FAIL | Real reviews have imperfect grammar, informal punctuation, fragments |
| S5 | Identical con format | If all review cons follow exact same structure ("The only downside is...") -> FAIL | Real cons vary wildly in how they're expressed |
| S6 | Three-paragraph structure | If all reviews have exactly 3 paragraphs or sentences -> FAIL | Real reviews are messy: 1 sentence, 7 sentences, 2 run-on sentences |
| S7 | "As a [identity], I..." opener | If 2+ reviews open with identity framing ("As a nurse," "As a mom of three") -> YELLOW FLAG | Real reviews sometimes do this, but AI over-indexes on it |
| S8 | Escalating praise pattern | If review follows problem→skepticism→trial→amazement arc identically to other reviews -> FAIL | This is the default AI review template |

**Step 4:** Run the Q&A content scan:
- Brand answers: check for corporate boilerplate language
  - FAIL phrases: "We appreciate your question," "At [Brand], we pride ourselves on," "Thank you for reaching out"
  - PASS example: direct answer with specific product information, no preamble
- Community answers: check for AI-sounding helpfulness
  - FAIL phrases: "Great question!", "I'd recommend," "Hope this helps!"
  - PASS example: casual, direct, possibly incomplete — the way real customers answer in forums
- Q&A questions: check for AI-structured curiosity
  - FAIL: "Could you elaborate on the mechanism by which..." (too formal)
  - PASS: "Does this actually work for bad knees or just minor stuff?" (casual, specific)

**Step 5:** Run the merchant response scan:
- Check merchant response templates for corporate boilerplate
- FAIL phrases: "We're sorry to hear about your experience" (alone), "We take customer satisfaction seriously," "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- PASS example: specific acknowledgment of the stated issue + concrete resolution action + direct contact
- If merchant response is a template, it should feel like a real person responding, not a PR department

**Step 6:** Run the keyword filter chip scan (brief):
- Check chip labels for AI-generic terms
- FAIL: "Quality," "Experience," "Performance" (too abstract)
- PASS: "Taste," "Shipping Speed," "Joint Pain," "Capsule Size" (specific to product)

**Step 7:** Calculate per-review slop scores. For each featured review:

| Dimension | Weight | Score Logic |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Word/phrase telltales | 40% | 0 found = 10, 1 found = 0 (instant fail), per yellow flag -2 |
| Structural patterns | 30% | 0 patterns = 10, per pattern detected -3.3 |
| Tone uniqueness | 15% | Distinct from all other reviews = 10, similar to 1 other = 6, similar to 2+ = 2 |
| Grammar naturalness | 15% | Has informal elements (fragments, dashes, colloquialisms) = 10, perfect grammar throughout = 4 |

Per-review slop score minimum: 8.0/10.
ANY review with a hard-fail telltale word/phrase = automatic 0/10 for that review.

**Step 8:** Calculate Q&A slop scores:

| Content Type | Pass Criteria |
|-------------|--------------|
| Brand answers | 0 corporate boilerplate phrases, direct answers, specific product info |
| Community answers | Casual tone, possibly incomplete, no "Hope this helps!" |
| Questions | Casual phrasing, specific to product, not overly formal |

Q&A slop score: 10 - (2 * count of AI-telltale instances found). Minimum: 7.0/10.

**Step 9:** Calculate overall anti-slop score:

| Metric | Weight | Calculation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Featured review avg slop score | 40% | Average of all featured review slop scores |
| Structural pattern violations | 25% | 10 - (2.5 * count of cross-review patterns detected) |
| Q&A slop score | 20% | From Step 8 |
| Merchant response + chip labels | 15% | 10 - (3 * count of boilerplate/generic instances) |

Overall anti-slop score = weighted average. Minimum passing: 8.0/10.

**Step 10:** If any hard-fail telltale found, document specific location, surrounding context, and rewrite guidance:
- What phrase was found
- Which review / Q&A / merchant response
- What the surrounding sentence says
- Suggested replacement phrasing that sounds human

**Step 11:** Document all findings, per-review scores, pattern detections, Q&A scan results, and the overall anti-slop result.

## Quality Criteria
- All generated text scanned for 10 hard-fail AI telltale words/phrases
- 8 structural patterns checked across all reviews (sentence length variance, tone uniformity, parallel structure, grammar perfection, con format, paragraph structure, identity openers, praise escalation)
- Per-review slop scores calculated on 4 weighted dimensions
- Every featured review scores >= 8.0/10 on slop check
- Q&A content scanned for corporate boilerplate (brand answers), AI helpfulness (community answers), and formality (questions)
- Merchant response templates scanned for corporate boilerplate
- Keyword chip labels checked for product specificity vs generic abstraction
- Overall anti-slop score >= 8.0/10
- Every FAIL includes specific location, surrounding context, and human-sounding rewrite guidance
- ANY hard-fail telltale word = automatic HALT regardless of overall score

## Failure Modes
- Any hard-fail AI telltale word/phrase found anywhere in generated content -> HALT. Rewrite the containing sentence with product-specific, informal language.
- 2+ structural patterns detected across featured reviews -> HALT. Vary sentence length, tone, structure, and openings across reviews.
- All featured reviews have perfect grammar (no fragments, no dashes, no colloquialisms) -> HALT. Add natural informality — fragments, parenthetical asides, imperfect punctuation.
- All featured reviews follow same emotional arc (problem->skepticism->trial->amazement) -> HALT. Vary arcs (some matter-of-fact, some grudging, some enthusiastic from sentence 1).
- Brand Q&A answers contain "We appreciate your question" or equivalent -> HALT. Rewrite as direct, specific answer without preamble.
- Community Q&A answers contain "Hope this helps!" or equivalent -> HALT. Rewrite as casual customer response.
- Merchant response is only "We're sorry to hear about your experience" -> HALT. Add specific issue acknowledgment + concrete resolution.
- Any featured review slop score below 8.0/10 -> HALT. Address all flagged dimensions.
- Overall anti-slop score below 8.0/10 -> HALT. Address all FAIL items before proceeding to Layer 4.
- "As a [identity], I..." opener appears in 3+ reviews -> HALT. Vary review openings — lead with outcome, product detail, or timeline instead.
