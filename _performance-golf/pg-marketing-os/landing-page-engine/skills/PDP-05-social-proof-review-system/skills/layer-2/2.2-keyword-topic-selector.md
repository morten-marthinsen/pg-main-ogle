# 2.2 — Keyword Topic Selector

**Skill:** PDP-05-social-proof-review-system
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Finalize the 5-8 keyword filter chip labels by analyzing research data frequency, mapping to product attributes, ensuring specificity, and validating coverage. This microskill takes the preliminary candidates from 1.4 and produces the FINAL chip labels that will appear on the page.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `input-verification.md` (0.1), `research-load.md` (0.2), `keyword-filter-spec.md` (1.4)
**Output:** `keyword-topics-final.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  input_verification: "[from 0.1 — product category, product attributes, key claims]"
  research_load: "[from 0.2 — verbatim quotes with keyword frequency, top concerns, top praises]"
  keyword_filter_spec: "[from 1.4 — preliminary candidates, selection criteria, FORBIDDEN labels]"
  scope: "[from 0.1 — full | standard | minimal | generation_only]"
  product_category: "[from 0.1]"
  product_attributes: "[from 0.1 — list of product features/attributes]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Check scope. If `scope = minimal` or `scope = generation_only`, SKIP. Document: "Keyword topics not applicable — filters omitted per scope." Output stub and exit.

**Step 2:** Compile keyword frequency data from research load (0.2):
- Extract ALL mentioned product topics from verbatim quotes
- Count frequency of each topic across all quotes
- Rank by frequency (descending)
- Note: a "topic" is a PRODUCT ATTRIBUTE, not a sentiment word

**Step 3:** Map research topics to chip candidate labels:
- For each high-frequency topic, determine the clearest 1-3 word chip label
- Labels must be INSTANTLY understandable to a shopper (no jargon, no abbreviations)
- Labels must describe a PRODUCT ATTRIBUTE or EXPERIENCE DIMENSION, not a sentiment

Examples of mapping:
| Research Topic | Good Chip Label | Bad Chip Label |
|---------------|-----------------|----------------|
| People talk about taste frequently | "Taste" | "Delicious" |
| Many mention it helped them sleep | "Sleep Quality" | "Works Great" |
| Shipping complaints appear often | "Shipping Speed" | "Delivery" |
| Texture/consistency mentioned | "Texture" | "Feel" |
| Side effects discussed | "Side Effects" | "Safety" |
| Battery life a common topic | "Battery Life" | "Battery" |
| Fit/sizing discussed frequently | "Fit & Sizing" | "Size" |

**Step 4:** Apply selection criteria from 1.4:
- RELEVANCE: keyword appears in 5%+ of research quotes (or estimated review mentions)
- SPECIFICITY: label is a product attribute, not generic praise
- COVERAGE: selected chips collectively cover 60%+ of review content themes
- BALANCE: include at least 1 concern-related topic (side effects, shipping, price, etc.)
- NO OVERLAP: each chip covers a distinct territory

**Step 5:** Validate against FORBIDDEN labels (from 1.4):
- "Quality" -> reject (too vague)
- "Great" -> reject (sentiment)
- "Love it" -> reject (sentiment)
- "Amazing" -> reject (sentiment)
- "Product" -> reject (meaningless)
- "Review" -> reject (meta)
- Any single-word adjective describing sentiment -> reject

**Step 6:** Finalize chip set:
- Rank candidates by frequency × specificity
- Select top 5-8 (based on scope: 7-8 for full, 5-6 for standard)
- Verify balance: at least 1 concern/negative topic included
- Verify no overlap between selected chips
- Verify coverage: count what % of research quotes are addressed by at least one chip

**Step 7:** Generate review-matching keywords for each chip:
- Each chip label maps to multiple search terms used for filtering
- Example: chip "Taste" matches reviews containing: taste, flavor, delicious, bitter, sweet, yummy, gross, chalky
- Example: chip "Battery Life" matches: battery, charge, charging, dies, lasts, power, hours
- These keyword sets are used by the frontend to filter reviews when a chip is active

**Step 8:** Document final chip selection with rationale.

## Quality Criteria
- 5-8 chips selected (count matches scope: 7-8 full, 5-6 standard)
- All chips are product-specific attributes (no generic praise words)
- At least 1 concern-related chip included (side effects, shipping, price, etc.)
- Coverage metric calculated (60%+ target)
- Matching keyword sets provided for each chip (for frontend filtering)
- No FORBIDDEN labels present
- No overlap between chips
- Rejected candidates documented with rationale
- Display order specified

## Failure Modes
- Generic label like "Quality" in final selection -> HALT. Replace with specific attribute.
- Zero concern-related chips -> HALT. Add at least 1 concern topic (price, shipping, side effects, etc.).
- Fewer than 5 chips for full/standard scope -> HALT. Add from category defaults.
- Coverage below 40% -> FLAG. Consider adding broader chips or re-analyzing research data.
- Two chips with overlapping territory (e.g., "Taste" and "Flavor") -> HALT. Merge into single chip.
- No matching keywords provided -> HALT. Each chip needs a keyword set for filtering logic.
