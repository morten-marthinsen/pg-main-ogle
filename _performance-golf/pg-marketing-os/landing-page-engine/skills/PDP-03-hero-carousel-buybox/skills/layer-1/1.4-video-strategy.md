# 1.4 — Video Strategy

**Skill:** PDP-03-hero-carousel-buybox
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Specify which carousel slides get video overlays and define the highlight reel specification. Video in carousel increases engagement and conversion when placed correctly. This microskill determines video placement, content, duration, and playback behavior.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** 1.2 carousel-architecture.md
**Output:** `video-strategy.md`

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read `carousel-architecture.md` to identify which slides are candidates for video overlays.

**Step 2:** Mandatory video positions:
- **Slide 1 (Outcome):** 30-second highlight reel — this is the primary video that plays when users tap the play button on the hero product shot
- **Slide 10 (Testimonials):** Testimonial montage — 45-60 seconds of customer testimonial clips

**Step 3:** Optional video positions (include if product has supporting video content):
- **Slide 4 (Enjoyment):** Usage/lifestyle clip — 15-20 seconds showing product in use
- **Slide 5 (How It Works):** Mechanism animation — 20-30 seconds explaining the science
- **Slide 8 (Credibility):** Expert endorsement clip — 30-45 seconds of expert speaking

**Step 4:** Define Slide 1 Highlight Reel specification:
- Duration: 30 seconds max
- Structure: 3-5 quick cuts
  - Cut 1 (5s): Product beauty shot + outcome text overlay
  - Cut 2 (5s): Problem → solution transition
  - Cut 3 (10s): Key ingredient/mechanism visual
  - Cut 4 (5s): Real user testimonial clip
  - Cut 5 (5s): CTA overlay ("Shop Now" or product + price)
- Audio: Background music, no voiceover required (most mobile users have sound off)
- Captions: Burned-in text overlays for each cut (sound-off friendly)
- Autoplay: NO — user must tap play button. Muted autoplay only on hover (desktop).

**Step 5:** Define Slide 10 Testimonial Montage specification:
- Duration: 45-60 seconds
- Structure: 3-4 customer clips, each 10-15 seconds
- Requirements per clip: Customer face visible, specific result mentioned, natural (not scripted-sounding)
- Diversity: Mix of demographics matching target audience
- Captions: Burned-in subtitles mandatory
- End frame: Product + star rating + "Read Reviews" CTA

**Step 6:** For each optional video position, specify:
- Whether to include (based on available assets/content)
- Duration target
- Content brief (what the video should show)
- Sound-off requirements (text overlays/captions)

**Step 7:** Specify universal video UX rules:
- Play button: Centered, semi-transparent white circle with play triangle, 64px diameter
- Gallery overlay: Video plays in gallery overlay view (not inline thumbnail)
- Controls: Standard play/pause, progress bar, mute/unmute, fullscreen
- Mobile: Tap to play in overlay, NOT inline (preserves thumbnail grid)
- Back-button behavior: Closes overlay, returns to carousel (NOT browser back)

**Step 8:** Write `video-strategy.md`.

## Quality Criteria
- Slides 1 and 10 have mandatory video specifications
- Each video has specific duration, structure (per-cut breakdown), and content direction
- All videos are sound-off friendly (captions or text overlays)
- No autoplay without user consent (tap to play only)
- Gallery overlay playback specified (not inline)
- Back-button behavior specified (closes overlay, not browser back)
- Asset requirements listed for production team
- File exists after execution

## Failure Modes
- No video assets available for any slide → Still write video specifications as production briefs. Note "asset needed" status.
- Slide 1 video omitted → HALT. Highlight reel is mandatory for Slide 1.
- Slide 10 video omitted → HALT. Testimonial montage is mandatory for Slide 10.
- Video specified as autoplay with sound → Remove autoplay with sound. Muted autoplay on desktop hover only.
- Video durations exceed spec → Trim to specified max duration.
- carousel-architecture.md not found → HALT, run 1.2 first.
