# 1.2 — Carousel Story Architecture

**Skill:** PDP-03-hero-carousel-buybox
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Design the 10-thumbnail carousel following the NLS persuasion story arc. Each slide has a specific story position, persuasion function, copy direction, and visual direction. This is the single most important microskill in PDP-03 — the carousel IS the above-fold selling system.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** 0.2 pdp-reference-load.md, 0.3 specimen-load.md, 1.1 ksp-inventory.md
**Output:** `carousel-architecture.md`

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read `ksp-inventory.md` to get the ranked KSPs and their story position mappings.

**Step 2:** Read `pdp-reference-load.md` to get the 10-Thumbnail Story Architecture with per-position requirements.

**Step 3:** Read `specimen-load.md` to identify carousel reference specimens.

**Step 4:** For each of the 10 slides, design the following in order:

### Slide 1: OUTCOME
- **Purpose:** Reconfirm the promise the user was pre-sold on from the ad/referral
- **Copy direction:** Outcome headline (2-6 words max), positioned center-bottom over product shot. Must be a benefit statement, not a product name. Example: "Calm Focus. All Day." NOT "Premium Nootropic Supplement"
- **Visual direction:** Hero product shot (main SKU, lifestyle angle), play button overlay → 30-second highlight reel video
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 2: PROBLEM SIGNS
- **Purpose:** Self-identification — visitor sees their symptoms/frustrations and thinks "that's me"
- **Copy direction:** Visual checklist of 4-6 problem signs. Use audience language from research, not clinical terms. Each item is a checkbox + short phrase. Example: "Brain fog by 2pm" NOT "Cognitive decline"
- **Visual direction:** Split layout — checklist on one side, subtle problem imagery on other. Clean, scannable.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 3: INGREDIENTS
- **Purpose:** Answer "what's in this" in human terms before the user has to scroll
- **Copy direction:** Product centered with ingredient visuals orbiting around it. Each ingredient gets "X for Y" naming. Example: "Ashwagandha for Stress Relief" NOT just "Ashwagandha 300mg"
- **Visual direction:** Product at center, 4-6 ingredient callouts radiating outward with small icons. Clean, premium feel.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 4: ENJOYMENT
- **Purpose:** Build desire through lived experience — what it's like to actually use this product
- **Copy direction:** Lifestyle copy — short phrase about the experience. Example: "Mixes smooth. Tastes like chocolate." NOT "Advanced formulation with..."
- **Visual direction:** Real person using product in natural setting. Aspirational but believable. NOT studio shot.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 5: HOW IT WORKS
- **Purpose:** Make the solution feel logical — bridge from "what's in it" to "why it works"
- **Copy direction:** 4-step flow: Problem → Key Ingredient → Mechanism → Benefit. Each step gets 3-5 words max. Use arrows or numbered steps.
- **Visual direction:** Infographic-style with flow arrows, clean icons per step. NOT a wall of text.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 6: WHAT TO EXPECT
- **Purpose:** Set expectations and prevent premature doubt — "when will I feel results?"
- **Copy direction:** 3-phase timeline with specific timeframes. Example: "Week 1: Calmer Mornings → Week 3: Deeper Sleep → Month 2: Sustained Energy"
- **Visual direction:** Vertical timeline with milestone markers, clean typography. Progress visualization.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 7: HOW TO USE
- **Purpose:** Remove friction — show how easy the product is to use
- **Copy direction:** 3-step instruction: dose → timing → method. Example: "1. Take 2 capsules 2. With breakfast 3. Daily for best results"
- **Visual direction:** 3 numbered icons with short text below each. Clean, simple, no clutter.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 8: CREDIBILITY
- **Purpose:** Signal legitimacy — answer "can I trust this?"
- **Copy direction:** Study reference, expert endorsement, or authority badge. Use the strongest credibility asset available. Example: "Clinically studied ingredients" with study citation, or "Formulated by Dr. [Name], [Credentials]"
- **Visual direction:** Clean authority layout — badge, seal, or expert photo with credentials. Premium, not cluttered.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 9: COMPARISON
- **Purpose:** Collapse the choice into an obvious verdict — prevent comparison shopping on competitor sites
- **Copy direction:** 3-column comparison table: [Product] vs [Competitor Category 1] vs [Competitor Category 2]. 5 rows of feature/benefit comparisons. Checkmarks vs X marks. NEVER name real competitors — use category terms ("Leading Brand," "Store Brand")
- **Visual direction:** Clean table layout, product column highlighted/shaded, checks in green, Xs in gray.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

### Slide 10: TESTIMONIALS
- **Purpose:** Transfer belief through social proof and create FOMO
- **Copy direction:** 1-2 real user quotes with name, location, and star rating. Play button overlay → testimonial montage video. Quotes should include specific results, not generic praise.
- **Visual direction:** Customer photo (or avatar) + quote bubble + star rating. Play overlay for video.
- **KSP mapped:** #[from inventory]

**Step 5:** Run CAROUSEL-POSITION-CHECK (from ANTI-DEGRADATION.md):
- All 10 positions present: Y/N
- Each has purpose: Y/N
- Each has copy direction: Y/N
- Each has visual direction: Y/N
- IF any N → fill missing elements before proceeding

**Step 6:** Verify copy direction quality — each slide's copy direction must be:
- **Specific:** Not "add a headline about benefits" but exact phrases or phrase patterns
- **Actionable:** A designer/developer could build the slide from the direction alone
- **Product-relevant:** Maps to actual product KSPs, not generic category claims

**Step 7:** Write `carousel-architecture.md`.

## Quality Criteria
- Exactly 10 slides, each matching an NLS story position
- Every slide has all four elements: story position, persuasion function, copy direction, visual direction
- Copy directions are specific phrases or phrase patterns (not "add benefit text")
- Visual directions describe specific imagery (not "add lifestyle image")
- At least 2 slides have video overlays (Slide 1 and Slide 10 minimum)
- Comparison slide (9) uses category terms, never competitor brand names
- KSP mapping shows no orphan KSPs and no unmapped positions
- CAROUSEL-POSITION-CHECK passes all fields as Y
- File exists after execution

## Failure Modes
- Fewer than 10 slides → HALT. NLS story architecture requires all 10 positions.
- Slide without copy direction → HALT. Every slide must have specific copy direction.
- Copy direction is vague ("add benefit image") → Rewrite with specific content.
- Comparison slide names real competitors → Remove brand names, use category terms.
- ksp-inventory.md not found → HALT, run 1.1 first.
- KSP gaps leave story positions empty → Flag for human input on missing data.
