# 2.10 — Identity Matching Writer

**Skill:** PDP-07-btf-section-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 10
**Purpose:** Write the Identity Matching ("Who Is This For?") section — a 3-column persona grid that helps visitors self-select by showing them which type of person benefits most from this product. This is the final self-selection signal before the decision zone (Reviews, FAQ). Each column represents a distinct customer persona with specific demographics, psychographics, and a key benefit matched to their identity.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `per-section-briefs.md` (1.1 — identity_matching brief), `data-validation.md` (1.2 — GREEN or YELLOW), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3 — Section 5.15 template)
**Output:** `section-identity-matching.md`

---

  section_brief: "[identity_matching brief from per-section-briefs.md]"

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read the Identity Matching template from PDP-BEST-PRACTICES-REFERENCE.md Section 5.15. Key pattern: 3-column persona grid, specific demographic/psychographic matching.

**Step 2:** Define 3 distinct customer personas from brief data. Each persona must be:
- **Distinct:** Clearly different from the other two (not variations of the same person)
- **Specific:** Named with a concrete identity, not abstract
- **Relatable:** Visitor can immediately say "that's me" or "that's not me"

**Persona Naming Pattern:**
- Not: "Health-Conscious Adults" (too vague)
- Better: "The Busy Professional" / "The Active Parent" / "The Health Optimizer"
- Best: Use a specific scenario: "You're over 40 and your energy crashes by 2pm"

**Step 3:** For each persona, write:
- **Persona Label:** 3-5 word identity (e.g., "The Busy Professional")
- **"This is for you if...":** 3-4 specific bullets using second person
  - Each bullet is a specific situation, behavior, or frustration
  - Not: "You want better health" (too generic)
  - Better: "You rely on 3+ cups of coffee just to get through the afternoon"
- **Key Benefit:** The single most relevant product benefit for this persona
  - Matched to this persona's specific need (not generic)
- **Optional Testimonial Snippet:** 1-line customer quote from someone matching this persona

**Step 4:** Ensure personas are diverse:
- Different life stages, situations, or motivations
- Cover the product's primary use cases
- Do not all focus on the same benefit/outcome

**Step 5:** Write section header:
- Not: "Who Is This For?" (acceptable but generic)
- Better: "Is [Product Name] Right for You?" or "Who Benefits Most"

**Step 6:** Ensure self-contained:
- Visitor should understand each persona without reading any other section
- Bullets reference specific situations, not product features

**Step 7:** Verify word count against target (+/-10%).

**Step 8:** Write `section-identity-matching.md`.

## Quality Criteria
- Exactly 3 personas, each distinct
- Persona labels are specific identities (not demographics)
- "This is for you if" bullets use second person and specific situations
- Key benefit per persona is matched (not generic across all 3)
- Optional testimonial snippets match persona profiles
- Personas cover diverse use cases / life stages
- Self-contained (no references to other sections)
- Word count within +/-10% of PDP-04 target

## Failure Modes
- Fewer than 3 personas -> HALT. Grid requires 3 columns for visual balance and coverage.
- All personas are essentially the same person -> Rewrite with genuinely distinct life stages/situations.
- Bullets are generic goals ("You want more energy") -> Rewrite as specific situations ("Your energy crashes every day at 2pm")
- No persona data in brief -> Use product positioning + audience profile to infer 3 personas. Flag as data gap.
- Key benefit is identical across all 3 personas -> Differentiate. Each persona should see a different reason to buy.
