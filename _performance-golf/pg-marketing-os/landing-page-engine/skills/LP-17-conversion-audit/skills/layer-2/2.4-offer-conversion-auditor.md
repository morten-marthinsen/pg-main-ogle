## EXECUTION

**POSITIONAL REINFORCEMENT:** You are evaluating offer presentation and conversion architecture. Score 4 points. Trace the exact flow from value establishment to price reveal. Count every CTA instance. Verify anchoring with quoted evidence.

### Step 1: Map the Offer Flow
Before scoring, map the complete offer/conversion flow from the assembled page:

1. Identify all sections that establish VALUE (benefits, proof, mechanism, story, results).
2. Identify all sections that reveal PRICE (pricing block, ATC with price, subscription options).
3. Identify the GUARANTEE section (text, placement, branding).
4. Identify every CTA instance (text, section, position).
5. Identify price anchoring techniques (crossed prices, per-day calculations, "value of X").

### Step 2: Score Point 13 — Value Before Price
Read the criterion from `configured-checklist.md` Point 13.

**Evaluation process:**
1. Identify the FIRST section that establishes substantive value (not just the headline — a full section that builds the case for why this product matters).
2. Identify the FIRST section where the actual price number appears.
3. Confirm value section appears before price section.
4. For Type B with price above fold: the above-fold price display is acceptable IF the page also has a substantive pricing section below fold where the full offer is presented. The assessment checks whether the full value case is built before the primary pricing/offer section.

**PASS (Type A):** "Value established in Sections 2-8 (benefits, mechanism, proof, story). Price first appears in Section 9 (pricing block). Value precedes price by 7 sections."
**PASS (Type B):** "Above-fold shows price ($49.99) but primary offer section is Section 6 after benefits (Section 2), ingredients (Section 3), reviews (Section 4), and FAQ (Section 5). Full value case built before primary pricing decision point."
**FAIL:** "Price appears in Section 2 immediately after hero. No benefit, proof, or mechanism content precedes the pricing section."

### Step 3: Score Point 14 — Guarantee Quality
Read the criterion from `configured-checklist.md` Point 14.

**Evaluation process:**
1. Locate the guarantee in the assembled page. Quote it verbatim.
2. Check three criteria:
   - **Branded name:** Does the guarantee have a unique name? "The Complete Confidence Guarantee" = YES. "Money-back guarantee" = NO.
   - **Specific duration:** Is the duration explicitly stated? "365 days from purchase" = YES. "Guaranteed" without duration = NO.
   - **Clear terms:** Are the terms specific? "Call, email, or chat — no questions asked, full refund within 48 hours" = YES. "If not satisfied, return for refund" = VAGUE.
3. All three criteria must be met for PASS.

**PASS:** "'The Iron-Clad Results Guarantee: Try it for 60 days. If you don't notice a difference in your energy, sleep, and mood, call us at 1-800-XXX-XXXX for a full refund. No hassle, no questions.'"
**FAIL:** "'30-day money-back guarantee.' — No branded name, no specific terms, generic language."

**Reference:** conversion-data-reference.md notes longer guarantees reduce returns (counterintuitive) and branded guarantees outperform generic.

### Step 4: Score Point 15 — CTA Count
Read the criterion from `configured-checklist.md` Point 15.

**Evaluation process:**
1. Count every CTA instance in the assembled page from the inventory in `assembled-page-load.md`.
2. Map each CTA to its position:
   - Early: Hero section, above-fold, first 25% of page
   - Mid: After proof block, after benefits, middle of page
   - Pre-close: After guarantee, after urgency, final sections
3. Verify minimum 3 placements covering early, mid, and pre-close positions.
4. For Type B: sticky ATC bar counts as a continuous CTA (satisfies the "always available" requirement).

**PASS:** "5 CTA instances: Section 1 (hero — early), Section 4 (after proof — mid), Section 7 (after benefits — mid), Section 10 (after guarantee — pre-close), Section 12 (final — pre-close). Covers early, mid, and pre-close."
**FAIL:** "2 CTA instances: Section 1 (hero) and Section 8 (final). Missing mid-page CTA. Only covers early and pre-close."

**Reference:** Specimens show CTA count range of 2-8+ (non-interactive pages). conversion-data-reference.md: personalized CTAs +202%.

### Step 5: Score Point 16 — Price Anchoring
Read the criterion from `configured-checklist.md` Point 16.

**Evaluation process:**
1. Locate the pricing section in the assembled page.
2. Check for anchoring techniques:
   - **Retail/compare price:** Original price crossed out, savings displayed
   - **Alternative cost:** "Compared to $X/month for [alternative]..."
   - **Per-day calculation:** "$2.33 per day" or "$0.83 per serving"
   - **If-purchased-separately value:** "Total value: $397. You pay: $97"
   - **Bundle savings:** "Save 25% when you buy 3"
3. At least ONE anchoring technique must be present for PASS.
4. Quote the specific anchoring text.

**PASS:** "'Regular price: $89.99. Today: $59.99 — Save 33%.' Retail price anchor with savings calculation."
**FAIL:** "Price shown as '$59.99' with no anchor, no comparison, no per-day calculation. The price exists in isolation."

### Step 6: Write Offer/Conversion Audit
Compile all evaluations into `offer-conversion-audit.md`.

## QUALITY CRITERIA

- Complete offer flow mapped before scoring
- All 4 points explicitly scored with specific evidence
- Point 13 includes exact section identification for value and price
- Point 14 includes verbatim guarantee quote and assessment of all three criteria (name, duration, terms)
- Point 15 includes complete CTA inventory with position mapping
- Point 16 includes identification of specific anchoring technique with quoted text
- Every FAIL has specific fix, expected lift with source, and routing

---

## FAILURE MODES

- Guarantee text not found in assembled page -> Score Point 14 as FAIL. Fix recommendation: create a guarantee section. This is a critical conversion element.
- Pricing not clearly delineated -> If the assembled page does not have a clear pricing section, note this and assess the most prominent price mention.
- Conflating CTA visibility with CTA count -> A sticky bar is ONE continuous CTA, not multiple. Count unique CTA instances by section for Point 15.
- Accepting "See Pricing" as price anchoring -> Navigation to pricing is not anchoring. Anchoring requires a reference price or comparison within the pricing section itself.
