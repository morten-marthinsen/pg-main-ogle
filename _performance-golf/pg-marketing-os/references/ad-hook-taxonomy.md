## 1. Meta-Framework: How Hooks Work

### The 3-Second Decision Window

The human brain evaluates three things in the first 0.5-3 seconds of encountering an ad:

1. **Novelty** — Is this new/unexpected? (Pattern interrupt detection)
2. **Relevance** — Is this for me? (Identity/pain matching)
3. **Effort** — Is this easy to consume? (Cognitive load assessment)

If all three pass, the viewer stays. If any one fails, they scroll.

### Three Psychological Pathways

Every hook operates through one (or a combination) of three core psychological mechanisms:

**Pathway A: Information Gap (Curiosity)**
- Creates cognitive tension by presenting incomplete information
- The brain finds unresolved information genuinely uncomfortable
- The viewer must keep watching to "close the loop"
- Strongest driver of watch time and completion rates

**Pathway B: Pattern Interrupt (Surprise)**
- Disrupts the brain's prediction engine
- Forces shift from passive to active attention
- Counterintuitive statements, unexpected visuals, or tonal breaks
- Strongest driver of initial stop/thumb-stop rate

**Pathway C: Direct Solution (Relevance)**
- Immediately identifies a specific pain point the viewer recognizes
- Triggers self-referential processing ("Wait, that's ME")
- No curiosity gap needed — the relevance IS the hook
- Strongest driver of qualified engagement and downstream conversion

### Cognitive Biases Exploited by Hooks

| Bias | How Hooks Use It | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| Loss aversion | Negative/warning hooks outperform positive ones | "Stop doing X right now" |
| Peak-end rule | Frontloading intensity creates stronger ad memory | High-energy first 3 seconds |
| Social proof | Numbers/testimonials reduce perceived risk | "Join 1M+ customers" |
| Curiosity gap | Withholding information creates compulsion to watch | "The one thing nobody tells you about..." |
| Authority bias | Expert positioning bypasses skepticism | "Harvard study reveals..." |
| FOMO | Scarcity/exclusivity triggers action urgency | "Only 5 left in stock" |
| Bandwagon effect | Popularity signals reduce decision friction | "TikTok made me buy this" |

## 2. Master Hook Taxonomy (32 Types)

### Category A: Curiosity & Information Gap Hooks

#### A1. The Question Hook
**What it is:** Opens with a question the target audience genuinely wants answered.
**Why it works:** The brain seeks closure. Unanswered questions create cognitive tension that drives continued viewing.
**Best for:** All verticals. Universal.
**Best platforms:** All platforms, especially Facebook/Instagram feed.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Can anyone learn to sing? Or is it something you're just born with?" (30 Day Singer, YouTube)
- "Are you an entrepreneur looking to grow your business?" (generic B2B)
- "What if I told you there's a way to lose weight without exercising?" (health/supplement)
- "How would your life change if you achieved financial freedom?" (finance)
- "What's the biggest mistake you're making in your fitness routine?" (fitness)

---

#### A2. The Curiosity Gap / Tease Hook
**What it is:** Hints at valuable or surprising information without revealing it.
**Why it works:** Creates an "itch" the viewer needs to scratch. Reveals just enough to spark interest while withholding the payoff.
**Best for:** All verticals. Works especially well with unique mechanisms.
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Everything you knew about [topic] is 100% wrong."
- "This is why your [action] isn't working, and it's not what you think."
- "The secret hack nobody is telling you about [industry]."
- "Find your DNA relatives across all DNA test kit services." (YourRoots)

---

#### A3. The Surprising Fact / Statistic Hook
**What it is:** Opens with an unexpected statistic or counterintuitive data point.
**Why it works:** Surprise triggers attention and pauses scrolling behavior. Numbers convey authority and credibility.
**Best for:** Health, finance, technology, B2B. Any vertical with quantifiable claims.
**Best platforms:** All platforms. Especially strong on YouTube (pre-roll) and Facebook.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Did you know that 70% of your audience prefers to learn about your product using video?"
- "Here's why 60% of small businesses fail within the first five years."
- "9 out of 10 dentists recommend [product]."
- "73% of ecommerce video ads fail within the first three seconds."

---

#### A4. The Cliffhanger / Suspense Hook
**What it is:** Builds tension by presenting problems while promising solutions, teasing just enough to spark curiosity.
**Why it works:** Suspense activates the brain's narrative completion drive. Viewers need to see the resolution.
**Best for:** Storytelling-heavy verticals (personal development, health transformations).
**Best platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Don't scroll just yet -- what's coming next will redefine the future of smartphones."
- "Hang tight... this one change will revolutionize your fitness journey."
- "Wait until the end to see how this transforms [problem]."
- "This mistake could destroy your marketing campaign in 5 seconds."

---

#### A5. The Belief Reversal / Contrarian Hook
**What it is:** Challenges a widely accepted belief to force viewers to reevaluate their assumptions.
**Why it works:** Disrupts the brain's prediction engine. Forces active engagement as the viewer processes the contradiction.
**Best for:** Health (myth-busting), finance (conventional wisdom), personal development.
**Best platforms:** All platforms. Particularly effective on TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Everything you thought you knew about budgeting is outdated."
- "Forget everything you know about sales -- this new strategy changes everything."
- "Most people have been using email marketing wrong this entire time."
- "You're not going to agree with me, but I think personal trainers are overrated."

---

#### A6. The Secret / Insider Knowledge Hook
**What it is:** Promises exclusive, uncommon, or hidden information.
**Why it works:** Exclusivity appeals activate curiosity. Audiences value rare knowledge and feel privileged to receive it.
**Best for:** Finance ("the rich know this"), health ("doctors don't tell you"), golf ("the pro secret").
**Best platforms:** YouTube (longer format allows the reveal), Facebook, TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Here is what dermatologists use but rarely share with patients." (47% hook rate in testing)
- "Here's what no one tells you about creating videos for marketing."
- "The one ingredient your skincare routine is missing."
- "The dangerous truth about popular skincare brands no one is telling you."

**Performance data:** In one 50-hook test, the "Industry Secret" hook achieved 47% hook rate -- the highest of all types tested.

---

### Category B: Authority & Social Proof Hooks

#### B1. The Social Proof / Numbers Hook
**What it is:** Leverages social validation by showing how many people have benefited from the product.
**Why it works:** Bandwagon effect. People trust products already vetted by large groups.
**Best for:** All verticals. Essential for retargeting campaigns.
**Best platforms:** Facebook/Instagram, TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Over 1,000 customers can't be wrong. Here is why they switched."
- "Join our 1 million happy customers who transformed their skin."
- "This trending fitness gadget has over 10,000 reviews -- let's see if it's worth the hype."

---

#### B2. The Authority / Expert Hook
**What it is:** Positions the product using expert opinions, endorsements, or institutional credibility.
**Why it works:** Authority bias. Expert positioning bypasses skepticism and builds instant trust.
**Best for:** Health (doctors, researchers), finance (analysts, economists), technology (engineers).
**Best platforms:** YouTube (allows credential building), Facebook.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "I tested 10 versions of [product] so you don't have to."
- "We put two popular [products] to the test -- watch which one actually works."
- "Harvard researchers found that those who ate the most anthocyanin-rich foods were 34% less likely to have a heart attack." (Mitolyn)

---

#### B3. The Skeptic Conversion Arc Hook
**What it is:** Opens with the creator's own skepticism, then reveals they were proven wrong.
**Why it works:** Mirrors the viewer's own skepticism, creating identification. The conversion arc creates a narrative the viewer wants to follow.
**Best for:** DTC products, supplements, technology products.
**Best platforms:** TikTok (UGC format), Instagram Reels, Facebook.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "I thought this was another TikTok scam. I was wrong. Here's what happened after 30 days." (39% hook rate)
- "I was today years old when I learned this [fact/tip]."
- "I tested the viral face mask to see if it really works."

---

#### B4. The Testimonial / Personal Experience Hook
**What it is:** Creates emotional connection through sharing relatable stories and achievable results.
**Why it works:** Mirror neurons. We process others' experiences as potential versions of our own.
**Best for:** Health transformations, personal development, financial success stories.
**Best platforms:** Facebook, Instagram, TikTok (UGC style).

**Verbatim examples:**
- "This is how I achieved a 50% increase in my conversion rate using content hooks."
- "This is how I went from [bad state] to [good state] in just 30 days."
- "This is how I lost 10 pounds by cutting out unhealthy habits."

---

### Category C: Pain Point & Problem Hooks

#### C1. The Direct Pain Point Hook
**What it is:** Addresses a specific physical or emotional problem the viewer experiences.
**Why it works:** Immediate self-identification. The viewer's brain goes "That's me!" and engagement becomes involuntary.
**Best for:** Health (specific symptoms), personal development (specific struggles), SaaS (specific workflow problems).
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "POV: You've tried every acne cream and your skin is still breaking out." (35% hook rate)
- "Struggling with slow Wi-Fi? We have the solution."
- "Tired of your [object] doing [pain point]? We have the solution."
- "Tired of buying the wrong hair loss products that only work for a short time?"
- "Struggling to stay consistent with workouts?"

---

#### C2. The Common Mistake / Warning Hook
**What it is:** Highlights a widespread mistake or bad practice the audience unknowingly follows.
**Why it works:** Loss aversion. Fear of doing something wrong is stronger than desire to do something right.
**Best for:** All verticals. Particularly strong in health, finance, and SaaS.
**Best platforms:** All platforms. Strong on YouTube pre-roll.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Stop doing [common habit] right now! Do this instead."
- "The #1 mistake people make when buying [product]."
- "Here's the number one mistake you're making about video creation."
- "Stop making this one mistake that's killing your conversion rate."
- "If your [object] sounds like this, unplug it immediately."

---

#### C3. The Negative / "Don't Do This" Hook
**What it is:** Uses negative framing to create urgency by highlighting what's going wrong.
**Why it works:** Loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain motivation. Warning language triggers protective instincts.
**Best for:** Health (harmful habits), finance (money mistakes), SaaS (wasted spend).
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "You are wasting your money on [competitor/tool]. Here is why."
- "Don't fall for these common [industry] myths that are holding you back."
- "Most people use [product] wrong -- here is why it's not protecting you."
- "4 things you're NOT doing to improve [goal]."

---

#### C4. The Problem-Solution Hook
**What it is:** Names a specific problem and immediately promises a solution.
**Why it works:** Reduces cognitive load. Viewer doesn't have to wonder "is this for me?" -- it's instantly clear.
**Best for:** SaaS/B2B ("Tired of manually reconciling spreadsheets?"), supplements, technology.
**Best platforms:** Facebook, YouTube.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Disappointed with your DNA results? Upload them to Genomelink!" (Genomelink)
- "Close your books in two days instead of two weeks." (B2B SaaS)
- "Struggling with [pain point]? Click to see the 3-step fix."

---

### Category D: Transformation & Results Hooks

#### D1. The Before/After Hook
**What it is:** Shows the transformation the product enables through visual or narrative contrast.
**Why it works:** Aspirational visualization. Viewer sees the gap between current state and possible future.
**Best for:** Health/fitness, beauty, home improvement, personal development.
**Best platforms:** Instagram, Facebook, TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Before vs. After: Swipe to see $18k well spent."
- "Transform your skin in just 7 days."
- "Get your dream body without sacrificing your favourite foods."

---

#### D2. The Outcome Tease Hook
**What it is:** Reveals an aspirational result paired with an unconventional or surprising method.
**Why it works:** Aspiration + curiosity about "how." The viewer needs to know the strategy behind the result.
**Best for:** Business/marketing, personal development, fitness.
**Best platforms:** All platforms. Strong on YouTube.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "We got 1,000 signups for our conference with zero ads. Here's what we did instead."
- "This is how I went from 0 to 100k followers in 6 months without spending a dime."
- "What we did to sell this [product/service] in under 3 days."

---

#### D3. The Experiment / Experience Hook
**What it is:** Frames content as results from personal testing or real-world experimentation.
**Why it works:** Demonstrates credibility. Viewer benefits from someone else's trial-and-error.
**Best for:** DTC products, SaaS tools, supplements, fitness programs.
**Best platforms:** YouTube (longer format for methodology), TikTok (quick results).

**Verbatim examples:**
- "I published 1 video every day on YouTube for 30 days. Here's what I learned."
- "I tried every popular fitness app so you don't have to."
- "Here are 3 reasons why I fired my personal trainer for this app." (31% hook rate)

---

### Category E: Urgency & Scarcity Hooks

#### E1. The FOMO / Scarcity Hook
**What it is:** Leverages limited availability to compel immediate action.
**Why it works:** FOMO triggers the brain's loss prevention system. The potential of missing out feels like an actual loss.
**Best for:** E-commerce, supplement offers, course launches.
**Best platforms:** Facebook, Instagram.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Only 5 left in stock! Order now before it's too late."
- "Last Chance to Save $100 during the Anniversary Sale!" (Hurom)
- "Don't miss out! Limited-time offer."
- "Need a last-minute gift? We are open until [time]."

---

#### E2. The Price / Value Hook
**What it is:** Leads with affordability or extraordinary value proposition.
**Why it works:** Reduces the primary purchase barrier. Anchors value before other considerations.
**Best for:** E-commerce, DTC, supplements, SaaS (free trial).
**Best platforms:** Facebook, Google Ads.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "I found the perfect product for [pain point] and it's under $20."
- "I can't believe this only costs $10 -- here's why it's worth every penny."
- "Rental car for just $22 a day." (Hopper)
- "How to [achieve goal] for FREE."
- "Get 20% off your first order and free shipping!"

---

#### E3. The Deadline / Time-Pressure Hook
**What it is:** Creates urgency through time-bound opportunities.
**Why it works:** Decision paralysis is broken by a deadline. The finite window forces action.
**Best for:** Finance (market timing), e-commerce (sales), course launches.
**Best platforms:** Facebook, email (landing pages).

**Verbatim examples:**
- "This opportunity closes at midnight."
- "Demo today, see 80-day payback." (B2B SaaS)
- "Book now -- join 500+ companies saving 40%."

---

### Category F: Engagement & Platform-Native Hooks

#### F1. The Audience Call-Out Hook
**What it is:** Directly addresses a specific demographic or persona.
**Why it works:** Self-identification. When the viewer hears their identity called out, it feels personal.
**Best for:** All verticals. Essential for cold traffic targeting.
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "If you are a [target audience], stop scrolling!"
- "Hey entrepreneurs over 40..."
- "Attention: anyone who's tried every diet and nothing worked."
- "Dear Fellow American..." (Agora Financial -- national identity call-out)

---

#### F2. The Algorithmic Call-Out / Platform-Native Hook
**What it is:** References the platform's algorithm or the viewer's feed as part of the hook.
**Why it works:** Breaks the fourth wall. Creates a sense of destiny/serendipity about seeing the ad.
**Best for:** DTC, personal development, "woo-woo" verticals.
**Best platforms:** TikTok (strongest), Instagram Reels.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "If you're seeing this on your For You Page, the universe is trying to tell you..." (41% hook rate -- highest tested)
- "The algorithm brought you here for a reason."
- "TikTok Made Me Buy This." (text overlay format)

**Performance data:** 41% hook rate in testing -- the single highest-performing hook type measured.

---

#### F3. The Reply / Comment Response Hook
**What it is:** Mimics platform-native comment/reply format. Displays a skeptical or curious comment, then addresses it.
**Why it works:** Feels like organic content, not advertising. Social proof through the "conversation."
**Best for:** DTC products, supplements, beauty.
**Best platforms:** TikTok (comment bubble), Instagram Reels.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Okay, I keep getting this comment. Let me show you the raw results, no editing." (38% hook rate)
- "Where did you get that outfit?" (reply bubble format)
- "Is this product really worth the hype?" (TikTok-style response)

---

#### F4. The Rapid-Fire Q&A Hook
**What it is:** Addresses the audience's most common questions quickly and efficiently.
**Why it works:** Combines social proof (many people asked) with curiosity (what are the answers?).
**Best for:** Products with FAQ-heavy purchase journeys. Strong for retargeting.
**Best platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Rapid fire replies to the most asked questions on home renovation projects."
- "Rapid fire replies to FAQs about our meal delivery service."
- "People always ask me, how do you [problem]? Well, here's what I know..."

---

### Category G: Visual & Sensory Hooks

#### G1. The Pattern Interrupt / Visual Surprise Hook
**What it is:** Uses unexpected visuals, odd compositions, or disruptive imagery to break scrolling patterns.
**Why it works:** The brain pauses on novelty. When something doesn't fit the expected feed pattern, attention is involuntary.
**Best for:** All verticals. Essential for high-competition feeds.
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- Post-it note covering product, then removed to reveal (Savannah Sanchez format)
- Sunglasses text reflection technique
- Blurred-to-focus text reveal
- Product filmed from bizarre angle or in unexpected context
- "(Starts with silence or random scene) This clip has nothing to do with marketing, but it'll change how you pitch forever."

---

#### G2. The Demonstration / "Show Don't Tell" Hook
**What it is:** Shows the product solving the problem rather than talking about it.
**Why it works:** Visual proof is processed faster than verbal claims. Demonstrations bypass skepticism.
**Best for:** Physical products, cleaning products, beauty, kitchen gadgets, supplements (mixing).
**Best platforms:** Facebook, TikTok, YouTube.

**Verbatim examples:**
- Squatty Potty unicorn ice cream demonstration (140M+ views, Harmon Brothers)
- BlendTec "Will It Blend?" series
- Purple Mattress raw egg test
- Dollar Shave Club razor comparison
- "This texture play will calm your mind." (ASMR-adjacent)

---

#### G3. The Unboxing / Reveal Hook
**What it is:** Creates anticipation through the act of opening, unpacking, or revealing a product.
**Why it works:** Gift-opening psychology. The brain's reward system activates during reveals.
**Best for:** E-commerce, DTC, subscription boxes, tech products.
**Best platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Unboxing the viral [product] to see if it's worth the hype."
- Custom jewelry with photo inside -- genuine reaction filmed (Felicity)
- Nathan James furniture: unbox-to-styled workflow in one continuous shot

---

#### G4. The Oddly Satisfying / ASMR Hook
**What it is:** Uses mesmerizing visual or auditory textures to create involuntary engagement.
**Why it works:** Taps into the brain's fascination with smooth, repetitive, visually pleasing actions. Nearly impossible to scroll past.
**Best for:** Beauty, food, cleaning products, art supplies.
**Best platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "The most oddly satisfying paint pour you've ever seen."
- Product texture close-ups with satisfying sounds
- Slow-motion product application with macro lens

---

### Category H: Humor & Entertainment Hooks

#### H1. The Comedy / Absurdist Hook
**What it is:** Uses humor, absurdity, or entertainment to earn attention before introducing the product.
**Why it works:** Entertainment value creates goodwill. Viewers share funny content, amplifying reach organically.
**Best for:** DTC brands with personality (Dr. Squatch, Poo-Pourri, Dollar Shave Club), mass-market consumer products.
**Best platforms:** YouTube (longer format for sketches), TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "This is where your ice cream comes from. Creamy poop of a mystic unicorn." (Squatty Potty)
- "Our blades are f***ing great." (Dollar Shave Club)
- "Your butt napkins, my lord." (Poo-Pourri)
- "You're not a dish. You're a man." (Dr. Squatch)

**Critical note:** Harmon Brothers formula = infomercial structure + modern humor + absurdist visuals. Entertainment must serve the sale. Ratio: 80% content, 20% promotion.

---

#### H2. The Reverse Psychology / Disqualifier Hook
**What it is:** Tells the viewer NOT to buy or NOT to click, triggering reactance.
**Why it works:** Psychological reactance. Telling people "don't" activates the desire to do exactly that.
**Best for:** Confidence products, premium products, health/fitness.
**Best platforms:** TikTok, Facebook.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Don't buy this hair oil if you hate having shiny hair." (34% hook rate)
- "Don't buy this pre-workout unless you want to crush new PRs consistently."
- "Don't click unless you're serious about growth."
- "I bet you can't pull off these advanced marketing strategies."
- "Ignore this if you're happy with your current skincare routine."

### Category I: List & Structure Hooks

**What it is:** Offers quick, digestible information in numbered format.
**Why it works:** Numbers set expectations for length and effort. The brain knows "3 tips" is manageable.
**Best for:** All verticals. Especially educational content, SaaS, personal development.
**Best platforms:** All platforms.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "3 mistakes most people make when booking a rental car." (Hopper)
- "5 ways to boost your website's traffic today."
- "3 simple steps to improve your conversion rate."
- "4 strategies top brands use for quick growth."

---

#### I2. The Superlative / Bold Claim Hook
**What it is:** Uses "best," "fastest," "most advanced," "world's first" to position the product.
**Why it works:** Superlatives create a mental benchmark. The viewer wants to verify or refute the claim.
**Best for:** Technology, SaaS, premium products.
**Best platforms:** YouTube, Facebook.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Finally: The world's first [product feature]."
- "The fastest way to lose weight in just 7 days."
- "The most advanced smartphone."

---

### Category J: Special Format Hooks

#### J1. The Taboo / Discomfort Hook
**What it is:** Leverages avoided or uncomfortable topics to create intrigue.
**Why it works:** Taboo topics attract attention precisely because they are rarely discussed openly.
**Best for:** Health (embarrassing conditions), personal development (failures), finance (debt).
**Best platforms:** Facebook, TikTok.

**Verbatim examples:**
- "Bum's the word." (Anusol hemorrhoid cream)
- "This is what smoking does to your body."
- "The one thing nobody wants to admit about [topic]."

---

#### J2. The "Trending / Viral" Hook
**What it is:** References current viral trends, challenges, or cultural moments.
**Why it works:** Currency = relevance. Piggybacking on existing attention gives free momentum.
**Best for:** Fashion, beauty, DTC consumer products, food/bev.
**Best platforms:** TikTok (trend-native), Instagram Reels.

**Verbatim examples:**

---

#### J3. The Handwritten / Lo-Fi Hook
**What it is:** Uses handwritten messages, sticky notes, or deliberately amateur visuals.
**Why it works:** Breaks the pattern of polished ads. Feels personal, authentic, and human.
**Best for:** DTC, beauty, supplements, personal brands.
**Best platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Stories.

**Verbatim examples:**
- Handwritten note: "Don't miss out! Only 3 left -- grab yours now!"
- Post-it note over product with question
- Whiteboard explanation for complex concepts

## 3. Structural Dimensions

### 3.1 The Three-Layer Hook Structure

Every video hook operates across three simultaneous channels in the first 1-3 seconds:

| Layer | What It Is | Key Principle |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **Text Hook** | Words on screen (overlay, caption, headline) | 3-7 words. Sharpen the promise. |
| **Visual Hook** | What you see (motion, composition, subject) | Big gestures, direct eye contact, sudden motion, or something out of place. |
| **Audio Hook** | What you hear (voice, music, sound effect) | Trending audio, unexpected sound effects, snappy voiceover, or SILENCE. |

**Critical insight from Pilothouse's 3-3-3 Framework:** All three layers must be DISTINCT from each other to signal differentiation to Meta's visual recognition systems. Two video ads with the same hook across all three layers will cannibalize each other's performance.

### 3.2 Hook Delivery Mode Matrix

| Delivery Mode | Description | When to Use | Platform Strength |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Statement-first** | Bold declarative claim opens the ad | Authority positioning, bold claims | YouTube, Facebook |
| **Question-first** | Opens with a question | Problem-aware audiences, educational | All platforms |
| **Visual-first** | Arresting image or motion before any text/audio | Pattern interrupt, demonstration | TikTok, Instagram Reels |
| **Audio-first** | Sound effect, dramatic music, or silence | Scroll environments with sound on | TikTok |
| **Text-first** | On-screen text before voiceover starts | Sound-off environments (85% of FB) | Facebook, Instagram Feed |
| **Demonstration-first** | Product in action before any explanation | Physical products, beauty, cleaning | All platforms |
| **Face-first** | Person looking directly at camera, speaking | UGC, testimonials, authority | TikTok, Instagram Reels |

### 3.3 Hook Intent Classification

| Intent | Purpose | Examples |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Pattern Interrupt** | Stop the scroll. Doesn't need to be relevant yet. | Odd visual, loud sound, "STOP" |
| **Native Blending** | Look like organic content so the viewer doesn't realize it's an ad | UGC format, reply bubbles, trending format |
| **Direct Qualification** | Immediately filter for the right audience | "If you're a [persona]..." |
| **Emotional Trigger** | Activate a feeling before rational processing | Transformation reveals, pain imagery |
| **Value Promise** | Lead with what the viewer will get | "3 tips to...", "How to..." |

### 3.4 Silent vs. Sound-On Optimization

**85% of Facebook videos are watched without sound.** This means:

- Text overlays must carry the full hook weight on Facebook
- Visual hooks must be self-explanatory without audio
- Captions increase watch time by 12% (Facebook internal study)
- TikTok is predominantly sound-ON, so audio hooks carry more weight there
- YouTube pre-roll is sound-ON by default

### 3.5 The Modular Creative Testing System

The modern performance creative workflow treats hooks as interchangeable modules:

```
[HOOK] + [BODY] + [CTA] = Complete Ad

3 hooks x 2 bodies x 2 CTAs = 12 ad variations from minimal assets
```

**Testing protocol:**
- Keep body and CTA identical
- Swap only the hook (first 3 seconds)
- 10-20 hook variations per winning angle
- Refresh every 7-10 days when hook rate declines
- Kill underperformers fast, push budget to top 2 winners

## 4. Vertical-Specific Hook Patterns

### 4.1 Health & Supplement Hooks

**Dominant hook archetypes in this vertical:**

| Pattern | Template | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| The "Weird Food" | "[Strange food] [surprising health benefit]" | "This purple berry peel tricks your mitochondria into burning fat" |
| The "Doctor Reveals" | "[Authority] reveals [hidden truth]" | "Harvard researchers found anthocyanin-rich foods reduce heart attack risk by 34%" |

## 6. The Hook-Angle Relationship

### Definitions

- **Angle** = The strategic idea. The pain point, desire, enemy, or transformation you're attacking. WHAT the ad is about.
- **Hook** = One of many ways to express that angle. HOW you open the ad.

### The Multiplication Principle

**One winning angle = 20+ hook variations.**

You don't need a new video for every ad. Change the first 3 seconds of the same video to test different psychological triggers.

Example -- Angle: "You're overpaying for skincare"

| Hook Type | Hook Expression |
|-----------|----------------|
| Question | "How much do you spend on skincare each month?" |
| Statistic | "The average woman spends $8,000 on skincare in her lifetime." |
| Warning | "Stop wasting money on expensive serums that don't work." |
| Social Proof | "5,000 women have already switched to this $12 alternative." |
| Reverse Psychology | "Don't buy this unless you actually want great skin for less." |
| Pain Point | "Tired of spending $200/month and still dealing with breakouts?" |
| Secret | "The one ingredient luxury brands don't want you to know about." |
| Demonstration | (Side-by-side texture comparison, $200 cream vs. $12 cream) |

### The Seven Core Angles

From performance creative agency frameworks:

1. **Pain** -- The specific suffering the audience wants to escape
2. **Enemy** -- The external force causing the suffering
3. **Curiosity** -- The unknown information that could change everything
4. **Cost Framing** -- The price of inaction or the wrong solution
5. **Before/After** -- The transformation delta
6. **Switch Trigger** -- The event that causes someone to finally act
7. **Identity** -- Who the person becomes after the transformation

Each angle can be expressed through ANY of the 32 hook types.

## Appendix B: The Russell Brunson "Hook, Story, Offer" Framework

The meta-structure of all direct response advertising:

1. **Hook** -- Grabs attention (the first 1-3 seconds / first line of copy)
2. **Story** -- Builds connection, changes beliefs, creates desire (the body)
3. **Offer** -- Makes the sale irresistible (the close / CTA)

If there's an issue in your funnel, it's always related to either the hook, the story, or the offer. This framework applies to ads, emails, landing pages, VSLs, and sales letters.

## Appendix C: Native Advertising / Advertorial Hook Patterns

The native ad funnel (Taboola, Outbrain, Facebook native) uses a distinct hook structure:

**Ad Hook** (curiosity-driven, editorial tone) -> **Advertorial** (education + social proof, styled as article) -> **Landing Page** (offer + close)

Common native ad hook formulas:
- "The One Thing [experts] Won't Tell You About [topic]"
- "This Is What Happens When You [unusual action]"
- "[City Name] [Professional] Discovers [surprising finding]"
- "Little-Known [solution] Could [dramatic benefit]"
- "[Celebrity/Expert] Finally Reveals [secret]"

The key differentiator: native ad hooks must look like editorial headlines, not advertising. The click leads to an advertorial that does the heavy selling, not the ad itself.
