## 1. The 8 Major Script Frameworks

Every winning video ad script maps to one of these eight archetypal frameworks:

### Framework 1: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- **Structure:** Name pain → amplify consequences of inaction → present product as hero
- **Best for:** Products solving acute, felt problems
- **Length sweet spot:** 15-60 seconds
- **Template:** "Still dealing with [problem]? Here's what happens when you ignore it... [agitation]. That's why [product] was built to [solution]."

### Framework 2: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
- **Structure:** Pattern interrupt → connect to benefit → build emotional want → clear next step
- **Best for:** Products requiring desire-building (not just problem-solving)
- **Length sweet spot:** 30-90 seconds
- **Maps directly to the awareness ladder**

### Framework 3: BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
- **Structure:** Show current painful state → show transformed state → explain how product bridges the gap
- **Best for:** Transformation products (health, fitness, personal development, golf improvement)
- **Length sweet spot:** 15-60 seconds
- **The visual version:** split-screen before/after format

### Framework 4: Hook-Body-CTA (The Social-Native Compression)
- **Structure:** Scroll-stopping opener (3-5s) → value delivery (5-20s) → action prompt (3-5s)
- **Best for:** TikTok, Reels, Shorts — any platform where the algorithm rewards retention
- **Length sweet spot:** 15-30 seconds
- **Baseline format** most UGC creators and performance agencies default to

### Framework 5: Story-Driven Narrative (Mini Hero's Journey)
- **Structure:** Protagonist + conflict (0-15s) → journey through struggle (16-75s) → resolution + brand reveal (76-90+s)
- **Best for:** Emotional products, brand-building campaigns, premium positioning
- **Length sweet spot:** 60-120 seconds
- **Key principle:** The brand/product appears as the MENTOR (not the hero) — the customer is always the hero

### Framework 6: Edutainment (Teach-Then-Sell)
- **Structure:** Promise a valuable outcome (0-15s) → deliver actionable lesson (16s-4min) → soft CTA to next step (final 15-30s)
- **Best for:** Authority positioning, high-ticket products, sophisticated audiences (Schwartz Stage 4-5)
- **Length sweet spot:** 2-5 minutes
- **Philosophy:** "Teach, don't sell" — genuine education that naturally leads to the product

### Framework 7: UGC Direct Response (The Authentic Peer Recommendation)
- **Structure:** Relatable problem → authentic discovery moment → transformation + recommendation
- **Best for:** eCommerce, DTC, social-first brands
- **Length sweet spot:** 15-45 seconds
- **Key:** Embrace imperfection. Vertical video. Natural lighting. Unpolished framing. The "realness" IS the hook.

### Framework 8: Fast-Paced Viral/TikTok-Native
- **Structure:** Pattern interrupt first frame (0-1s) → rapid-fire value with quick cuts 2-3 per second (2-12s) → curiosity gap resolution + CTA (13-15+s)
- **Best for:** Young demographics, trend-riding, awareness campaigns
- **Length sweet spot:** 6-15 seconds
- **The first FRAME matters** — obsess over it

## 2. Script Structures by Ad Length

### 6-Second Bumper Ads
- **Constraints:** Non-skippable. Must be 6 seconds or shorter.
- **Structure:** ONE idea. ONE message. No narrative arc.

| Beat | Timing | Content |
|------|--------|---------|
| Visual hook | 0-2s | Bold, brand-led visual or motion |
| Core message | 2-5s | Single benefit or brand statement |
| Logo/CTA | 5-6s | Brand mark + one action word |

- **Word count:** 10-15 words maximum
- **Performance:** Google found 70% of bumper campaigns drove significant brand awareness lift (average 9%), and 9 out of 10 drove ad recall with average lift over 30%
- **Rule:** Do NOT attempt to tell a story. A single striking idea works best.

### 15-Second Ads

| Beat | Timing | Word Count |
|------|--------|------------|
| Hook | 0-3s | 5-10 words |
| Core value/benefit | 3-10s | 15-20 words |
| CTA | 10-15s | 5-10 words |

- **Total word count:** 30-40 words
- **Best framework:** PAS compressed, or single-benefit demo

### 30-Second Ads

| Beat | Timing | Word Count |
|------|--------|------------|
| Hook + audience ID | 0-5s | ~10 words |
| Problem statement | 5-15s | ~20 words |
| Solution/benefit | 15-25s | ~20 words |
| CTA | 25-30s | ~15 words |

- **Total word count:** 60-75 words maximum
- **Proportional framework:** Problem (30%), Solution (40%), Proof (20%), Action (10%)

### 60-Second Ads

| Beat | Timing | Word Count |
|------|--------|------------|
| Hook | 0-5s | 10-15 words |
| Problem/agitation | 5-20s | 30-40 words |
| Mechanism/solution | 20-40s | 40-50 words |
| Proof element | 40-50s | 20-30 words |
| CTA | 50-60s | 15-20 words |

- **Total word count:** 140-160 words
- **First length where you can meaningfully introduce a "unique mechanism"**

### 2-3 Minute Long-Form Video Ads

| Beat | Timing | Function |
|------|--------|----------|
| Hook + big promise | 0-10s | Stop scroll, establish stakes |
| Problem deep-dive | 10-40s | Empathize, agitate, validate |
| Failed solutions | 40-60s | "You've tried X, Y, Z and they didn't work because..." |
| Unique mechanism | 60-90s | New explanation for why this approach is different |
| Proof/demo | 90-120s | Show it working: testimonials, data, demo |
| Offer + CTA | 120-180s | What they get, what it costs, why now |

- **Total word count:** 350-450 words
- **Best frameworks:** Story-driven, Edutainment, Harmon Brothers, or full DR structure

## 3. Script Structures by Vertical

### Health/Supplement Video Ad Scripts

**Format A: "Doctor Reveals" / Authority Mechanism Explainer**
- Hook: "Harvard researcher discovers..." or "Your doctor won't tell you this..."
- Structure: Authority hook → problem that seems unsolvable → hidden root cause reveal (the mechanism) → specific ingredient/compound → proof (studies, testimonials) → offer
- Length: 2-5 minutes (or 15-45 minute VSL)
- Visual: Talking head with green screen showing diagrams, cell animations, before/after
- This is the Craig Clemens / Gundry model

**Format B: Testimonial Compilation**
- Hook: Most dramatic before/after result
- Structure: Quick-cut montage of 3-5 real customer testimonials, each following problem → discovery → result arc
- Length: 60-120 seconds
- Visual: Authentic user-shot video, often with text overlay stats

**Format C: "Did You Know?" Educational**
- Hook: Surprising health statistic or counterintuitive claim
- Structure: Educate about biology/science → reveal most solutions miss this → introduce product as correct approach
- Length: 60-180 seconds
- Visual: Text-heavy overlays, infographics, B-roll of ingredients/labs

**Data from 500-ad analysis (Evolut Agency):**
- UGC formats: 32.6%
- Single image: 35.8%
- Video: 21%
- Product demonstrations: 44.2% of all creative types
- 76.9% of top performers ran for 90+ days

### Finance/Fintech Video Ad Scripts

- Hook: Quantified pain point ("Still paying $X in fees?" or "Your savings lost X% to inflation")
- Problem: Make the abstract concrete (show actual dollar cost)
- Solution: Simplify the complex (animation/motion graphics preferred)
- Trust: Regulatory compliance markers, security features
- CTA: Low-commitment first step ("See your rate" NOT "Buy now")
- **Key difference:** CTA must be low-friction because money decisions carry high anxiety
- **Length sweet spot:** 60-90 seconds for paid social

### Golf/Sports Video Ad Scripts

**Format A: The Demo/Results Ad**
- Hook: Dramatic result shot (ball flight, distance number, tight approach)
- Structure: Show result → reveal product/method → demonstrate use → more results → CTA
- Visual: Course/range footage, swing analysis overlays, launch monitor data

**Format B: The "Secret" or "Hack" Format**
- Hook: "The one thing tour pros do that amateurs don't..."
- Structure: Education first (mini-lesson) → reveal product makes it easy → demo → CTA
- This maps to Performance Golf model

**Format C: The Comparison/Before-After**
- Hook: Side-by-side swing or stat comparison
- Structure: Before stats → what changed → after stats → how to get same change

### Personal Development Video Ad Scripts

**Format A: Transformation Story**
- Hook: "One year ago I was [miserable state]. Today I [transformed state]."
- Structure: Authentic origin → turning point → discovery → results → invitation
- Length: 60-180 seconds

**Format B: Mini-Lesson (Expert Teaching)**
- Hook: Promise a specific, valuable insight
- Structure: Deliver genuine teaching → reveal it comes from larger system → CTA
- Uses Why-What-How framework
- Length: 2-5 minutes

**Format C: PASTOR Framework**
- Problem → Amplify → Story/Solution → Transformation → Offer → Response

### Technology/SaaS Video Ad Scripts

**Format A: Workflow Problem-Solution**
- Hook: Show painful workflow (cluttered spreadsheets, manual processes)
- Structure: Painful current → introduce tool → screen recording demo → quantified savings → free trial CTA
- Visual: Screen recording with cursor highlights, text overlays, PiP talking head

**Format B: "The Old Way vs. The New Way"**
- Hook: Split screen showing manual process vs. automated
- Key principle: Lead with benefits, not features ("Save 4 hours/week" > "Automated dashboard")

## 4. The Modular Script Architecture

### The Core Insight

Top performance marketers think of every video ad as a stack of interchangeable modules:

```
[HOOK MODULE] + [SETUP MODULE] + [MECHANISM MODULE] + [PROOF MODULE] + [CTA MODULE]
```

Each module can be swapped independently, creating combinatorial variant explosion from minimal assets.

### The Four Modular Testing Patterns

**Pattern 1: Same Body, Different Hooks**
- Keep everything from second 3 onward identical
- Swap only the opening hook
- **Highest-ROI test** — hooks account for largest single performance variable
- The Performance Golf model: same core lesson/demo video, 3-5 different opening hooks

**Pattern 2: Same Hook, Different Proof Sections**
- Hook and setup stay constant
- Swap social proof: testimonial A vs. B vs. data stat vs. demo clip
- Tests what type of evidence resonates most

**Pattern 3: Same Script, Different Visual Treatments**
- Identical script/voiceover
- Different visual execution: talking head vs. B-roll+VO vs. text-on-screen vs. screen recording
- Tests format preference

**Pattern 4: Same Angle, Different Formats**
- Same core message/angle
- Completely different creative execution: UGC vs. professional vs. animation
- Tests which "container" the audience trusts most

### Testing Volume: What Top Advertisers Actually Do

| Metric | Number | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Hooks to test per core video concept | 3-5 different hooks | Industry standard |
| Hooks to test before finding a winner | 10-20 variations per winning angle | VideoAI testing data |
| Variants per concept (small changes) | 20-50 variations per concept | Anchour Meta playbook |
| Core concepts per week (at scale) | 4-6 new concepts | UltraByRich formula |
| Win rate across all tested creatives | 6.6-30% (1-3 out of 10 win) | AdManage data |
| Creative lifespan before fatigue | 2-8 weeks depending on spend | Multiple sources |
| Hook refresh cycle | Every 7-10 days for high-spend | Motionapp |

### Creative Testing Formula

```
Minimum creatives per week = Weekly ad spend / (3 x Average Order Value)
```
Example: $7,000/week spend, $100 AOV = ~23 creatives per week.

### The Concept-to-Variant Workflow

1. **Concept Development:** Identify 3-5 strategic angles (from research)
2. **Script Writing:** One "master script" per concept
3. **Hook Bank:** 5-10 hook variants per master script
4. **Visual Treatment:** Produce each in 2-3 visual formats
5. **Assembly:** Combine hooks × bodies × CTAs into test matrix
6. **Launch:** Deploy 3-5 variants simultaneously
7. **Kill:** Cut losers at 3× AOV spend threshold
8. **Scale:** Increase winners by 20-30% incremental spend
9. **Iterate:** New hooks for winners; new concepts for exhausted angles

### The Combinatorial Output Example

```
Concept: "Hidden Enzyme Deficiency" (health supplement)

Hook variants: 5
  - Industry Secret hook
  - Skeptic Conversion hook
  - Doctor Reveals hook
  - POV Pain Point hook
  - Bold Claim + Proof hook

Body versions: 2
  - 60-second PAS
  - 30-second Hook-Body-CTA

CTA variants: 3
  - Urgency ("Limited supply")
  - Risk reversal ("Full refund guarantee")
  - Low-friction ("Take the free quiz")

Visual treatments: 2
  - Talking head + green screen diagrams
  - B-roll + voiceover + text overlays

Total testable combinations: 5 x 2 x 3 x 2 = 60 variants
```

## 5. Script-to-Visual Relationship

### The AV Script Format (Industry Standard)

| VISUAL (Left Column) | AUDIO (Right Column) |
|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Shot description, camera angle, B-roll notes, graphics, text overlays | Dialogue, voiceover, music cues, SFX, timing notes |

### Five Visual Treatment Types

**Treatment 1: Talking Head**
- Single person speaking directly to camera
- Script is a monologue — must sound natural spoken aloud
- Highest trust signal for UGC; lowest production cost
- Green screen variant: speaker in front of relevant imagery

**Treatment 2: B-Roll + Voiceover**
- No on-camera talent; visuals carry the story
- Audio and visual columns are fully independent
- Allows faster visual pacing

**Treatment 3: Text-on-Screen (Kinetic Typography)**
- Words appear on screen, synced to music
- No voiceover (sound-off optimized)
- 3-6 words per frame. Every word must earn its place.
- Key stat: Most social video is viewed without sound

**Treatment 4: Screen Recording / Product Demo**
- Screen capture showing product in use
- Often with PiP talking head in corner
- Script must include precise UI navigation in visual column

**Treatment 5: Mixed/Hybrid**
- Combines talking head + B-roll + text overlays + demo
- Most professional ads use this treatment
- Visual column must specify transition types

## 6. UGC Script Templates

### 5 Types × 3 Templates Each = 15 Proven Templates

**Type 1: Testimonial Scripts (trust-building)**
- "I didn't expect this to work..."
- "3 things I noticed after using this"
- "If you're on the fence..."

**Type 2: Problem/Solution Scripts (action-driven)**
- "If you struggle with ___, try this"
- "I finally fixed ___"
- "Here's what no one tells you about ___"

**Type 3: Demo Scripts (visual proof)**
- "Let me show you how I use this"
- "Watch this before you buy ___"
- "This is the fastest way to ___"

**Type 4: Comparison Scripts (decision drivers)**
- "I tried both... here's the difference"
- "The old way vs. the new way"
- "Don't buy ___ until you see this"

**Type 5: Founder-Style Scripts (trust + mission)**
- "I made this because I was tired of ___"
- "If you're new here, this is what we do"
- "This is what we fixed that others don't"

All follow universal structure: **Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → Clear action step.**

## 7. The Harmon Brothers Formula

The Harmon Brothers (Squatty Potty, Purple Mattress, Dr. Squatch, Poo-Pourri, Chatbooks) created a distinct genre: 2-5 minute "infomercial + branded entertainment" hybrid.

**H-P-S-G-CTA-SP (wrapped in story)**

| Element | Function | Execution |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **H** - Headline | Attention grabber | Visual hook in first 3-5 seconds |
| **P** - Problem | Pain articulation | Make them FEEL it; humor as vehicle |
| **S** - Solution | Product as answer | Show how it works; demo is critical |
| **G** - Guarantee | Risk reversal | Remove purchase anxiety |
| **CTA** - Call to Action | Direct response | Clear, specific, urgent |
| **SP** - Social Proof | Credibility | Reviews, endorsements, data |

**Key insight:** All infomercial elements are wrapped in a story with character and narrative arc. This makes them shareable, not skippable.

## 8. The VSL Structure

For long-form DR video (5-30+ minutes):

**Five-Phase Architecture:**

| Phase | Function | Timing |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 1. Hook + Big Idea | Establish the big promise | First 10-15 seconds |
| 2. Problem Validation | Articulate pain better than they can | 2-5 minutes |
| 3. Unique Mechanism | Why everything else failed + why YOUR approach differs | 5-15 minutes |
| 4. Proof + Credibility | Testimonials, data, authority, risk reversal | 5-10 minutes |
| 5. Offer Stack + CTA | Bundle + price anchor + reveal + urgency/scarcity | 3-5 minutes |

**Critical rules:**
- Drop first CTA immediately after introducing offer — most people won't make it to end
- Repeat CTAs multiple times through back half
- Length matches price: 5-10min (free/low), 15-20min (mid), 30+min (high-ticket)
- For health VSLs, unique mechanism section is linchpin
