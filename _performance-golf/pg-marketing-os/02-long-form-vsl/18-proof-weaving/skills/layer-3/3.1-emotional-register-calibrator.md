# Microskill 3.1: Emotional Register Calibrator

**Layer:** 3 (Refinement)
**Purpose:** Ensure proof emotional tone matches campaign brief direction

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

All proof has emotional register — the TONE in which it's delivered. A testimonial can be clinical, emotional, urgent, conversational, dramatic, or measured. The wrong register creates tonal whiplash. Elite copy maintains consistent emotional register throughout, with intentional shifts only at strategic moments.

---

## EMOTIONAL REGISTERS

### Register 1: Clinical/Professional

**Characteristics:**
- Measured language
- Precise terminology
- Emphasis on data
- Understated emotion

**Example:**
```
"After 8 weeks on the protocol, participants demonstrated a 47% improvement in fasting glucose levels, with 73% achieving normoglycemic status without additional intervention."
```

**Best for:**
- Medical/professional audiences
- Stage 4-5 sophistication
- B2B contexts
- Authority-driven proof

---

### Register 2: Emotional/Personal

**Characteristics:**
- Feeling words
- Personal details
- Vulnerability
- Story-driven

**Example:**
```
"I remember crying in my bathroom because I couldn't fit into any of my clothes. My kids were embarrassed to be seen with me. I felt like I'd lost myself completely..."
```

**Best for:**
- Personal transformation products
- Stage 2-3 sophistication
- Health/relationship markets
- Testimonial-heavy proof

---

### Register 3: Urgent/Warning

**Characteristics:**
- Time pressure
- Consequence emphasis
- Direct language
- Bold statements

**Example:**
```
"The window is closing. Every day you wait is another day of cellular damage. Another day closer to the point of no return. The time to act is NOW."
```

**Best for:**
- Financial/survival products
- Limited time offers
- Stage 4-5 markets
- Data-driven proof

---

### Register 4: Conversational/Friendly

**Characteristics:**
- Casual language
- "You and me" tone
- Relatable details
- Light touch

**Example:**
```
"Look, I get it. You've probably tried a dozen things that didn't work. I was the same way. My wife calls it my 'health experiment phase.' But this one? This one actually stuck."
```

**Best for:**
- Mass market products
- Stage 1-3 sophistication
- Trust-building phases
- Testimonials

---

### Register 5: Dramatic/Epic

**Characteristics:**
- Bold declarations
- Vivid imagery
- High contrast
- Stakes emphasis

**Example:**
```
"He was supposed to be dead. The doctors had written him off. His family was making funeral arrangements. And then — against all medical expectation — he walked out of that hospital on his own two feet."
```

**Best for:**
- Transformation products
- Proof section openings
- Scale proof
- Before/after proof

---

## CALIBRATION PROCESS

### Step 1: Identify Brief Direction

From campaign-brief-package:
```
Tone: [clinical | emotional | urgent | conversational | dramatic]
Avatar emotional state: [description]
Market sophistication: [1-5]
Brand voice: [description]
```

### Step 2: Audit Each Proof Block

For each drafted proof block:
```
Block ID: [id]
Current register: [identified register]
Target register: [from brief]
Match: YES | NO | PARTIAL
```

### Step 3: Flag Mismatches

```
IF current_register ≠ target_register:
  FLAG for rewrite
  SPECIFY: "Shift from [current] to [target]"
  EXAMPLE: How target register sounds
```

### Step 4: Rewrite Mismatched Blocks

**Example Transformation:**

From CLINICAL (wrong for emotional brief):
```
"Subjects demonstrated 47% improvement in metabolic markers."
```

To EMOTIONAL (matching brief):
```
"Imagine waking up with energy you haven't felt in years. That's what 47% improvement actually feels like — like someone gave you your life back."
```

---

## STRATEGIC REGISTER SHIFTS

Some register shifts are INTENTIONAL and CORRECT:

### Allowed Shifts

| From | To | When |
|------|-----|------|
| Emotional | Data | Validating emotional claim with numbers |
| Conversational | Dramatic | Building to transformation reveal |
| Clinical | Urgent | Transitioning to close |
| Any | Emotional | Story/testimonial moments |

### Forbidden Shifts

| From | To | Why Problematic |
|------|-----|-----------------|
| Urgent | Casual | Undermines urgency built |
| Dramatic | Clinical | Kills momentum |
| Emotional | Clinical (abrupt) | Creates whiplash |
