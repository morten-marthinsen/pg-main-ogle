# Microskill 2.2: Before/After Drafter

**Layer:** 2 (Drafting)
**Purpose:** Write maximum-contrast transformation narratives

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Before/after proof is CONTRAST ENGINEERING. The goal is to create the MAXIMUM POSSIBLE GAP between the before state and the after state. This gap creates believability (if change was this dramatic, the solution must be powerful) and hope (if they could change this much, so can I).

---

## THE MAXIMUM CONTRAST FORMULA

### Before State Elements

| Element | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Physical Suffering** | Tangible pain | "Couldn't walk more than 20 feet" |
| **Emotional Toll** | Internal devastation | "Had given up hope completely" |
| **Life Limitation** | What they couldn't do | "Missed my daughter's wedding" |
| **Medical Verdict** | Authority dismissal | "My doctor said I'd never..." |
| **Time Duration** | How long suffered | "For 15 years, I'd tried everything" |

### After State Elements

| Element | Purpose | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Specific Outcome** | Measurable change | "Lost 47 pounds" |
| **Timeframe** | Speed of change | "In just 8 weeks" |
| **Function Restored** | What they can now do | "Walking 3 miles every morning" |
| **Medical Reversal** | Authority surprise | "My doctor couldn't believe the numbers" |
| **Emotional Liberation** | Internal transformation | "For the first time in years, I have hope" |

---

## BEFORE/AFTER FORMATS

### Format 1: Side-by-Side Contrast

```
BEFORE: [Before state in 2-3 sentences]
AFTER: [After state in 2-3 sentences]
TIMEFRAME: [How quickly it happened]
```

**Example:**
```
BEFORE: Ken couldn't walk. Blind in one eye. Raging neuropathy keeping him up all night. His doctors said he had months to live.

AFTER: Walking two miles every day. Vision restored. Sleeping through the night. Working out at the gym.

In just 72 hours.
```

---

### Format 2: Narrative Contrast (Whitaker Style)

```
[Detailed before state with emotional weight]
[Transformation trigger]
[Detailed after state with specific outcomes]
[Emotional close]
```

**Example (Gold Standard):**
```
Ken was dying. His diabetes had ravaged his body for years. Raging neuropathy. Constant pain. Blind in one eye. He couldn't walk more than a few steps. His doctors had given up. His family was preparing for the worst.

Then his wife saw something on TV that changed everything.

72 hours later, Ken walked out of the Whitaker Wellness Institute. Diabetes-free. Walking without assistance. Vision restored. Pain gone.

Today, Ken works out at the gym regularly. He drives again. He plays with his grandchildren. The man who was supposed to be dead is more alive than he's been in decades.
```

---

### Format 3: Rapid-Fire Contrast List

```
BEFORE → AFTER
• [Before point 1] → [After point 1]
• [Before point 2] → [After point 2]
• [Before point 3] → [After point 3]
• [Before point 4] → [After point 4]
```

**Example:**
```
• Couldn't sleep → 8 hours of deep sleep nightly
• Constant joint pain → Pain-free movement
• Exhausted by noon → Energy all day long
• Size 18 → Size 10
• Pre-diabetic → Normal blood sugar
```

---

### Format 4: The Medical Reversal Format

```
[What doctors said]
[What actually happened]
[Medical professional's reaction]
```

**Example:**
```
"Your ejection fraction is 25%. You need surgery immediately." That's what my cardiologist told me.

6 weeks later, I went back for a follow-up. My ejection fraction had jumped to 40%.

My doctor literally dropped his clipboard. "I've never seen numbers move this fast," he said. "What are you doing?"
```

## CONTRAST INTENSIFICATION TECHNIQUES

### Technique 1: The "Death's Door" Anchor

**Start as close to worst-case as truthfully possible:**
- "Scheduled for surgery"
- "Given months to live"
- "Doctors had given up"
- "Was planning my funeral"

**Why:** Creates maximum contrast ceiling

---

### Technique 2: The Specific Function Restoration

**Before:** Can't do [specific activity]
**After:** Now doing [same activity] regularly

**Examples:**
- "Couldn't tie my shoes → Now running 5Ks"
- "Couldn't play with grandkids → Now coaches their soccer team"
- "Couldn't climb stairs → Now hiking mountains"

**Why:** Function restoration is more powerful than symptom relief

---

### Technique 3: The Medical Number Turnaround

**Before:** [Bad number]
**After:** [Good number]
**Context:** [What this means]

**Examples:**
- "A1C was 8.7 → Now 5.2 (completely normal range)"
- "Blood pressure 180/110 → Now 120/80"
- "Ejection fraction 25% → Now 45%"

**Why:** Numbers are undeniable and precise

---

### Technique 4: The Timeline Compression

**Emphasize SPEED of transformation:**
- "In just 72 hours"
- "Within 3 weeks"
- "By the end of the first month"

**Why:** Fast results imply powerful solution

---

### Technique 5: The Third-Party Witness

**Include someone else who witnessed the change:**
- "My wife couldn't believe it"
- "My coworkers asked what happened"
- "My doctor was speechless"

**Why:** Third-party validation makes transformation more believable

---

## BEFORE/AFTER PITFALLS TO AVOID

### Pitfall 1: Vague Before State

**WRONG:** "I wasn't feeling well"
**RIGHT:** "I couldn't get out of bed before 10am. Even brushing my teeth left me exhausted."

### Pitfall 2: Unspecific After State

**WRONG:** "I feel so much better now"
**RIGHT:** "I've lost 34 pounds, my energy is through the roof, and I'm off my blood pressure medication"

### Pitfall 3: Missing Timeframe

**WRONG:** "Eventually, things improved"
**RIGHT:** "Within 6 weeks, everything changed"

### Pitfall 4: Impossible Contrast

**WRONG:** "I was dying → Now I'm immortal"
**RIGHT:** "Doctors gave me months → Three years later, I'm still thriving"

```
[ ] Before state is specific (not vague suffering)
[ ] After state has measurable outcomes (numbers)
[ ] Timeframe is specified
[ ] Contrast is maximized (death's door → thriving)
[ ] Function restoration shown (not just symptom relief)
[ ] Third-party witness included (if available)
[ ] Sounds believable (not too good to be true)
```
