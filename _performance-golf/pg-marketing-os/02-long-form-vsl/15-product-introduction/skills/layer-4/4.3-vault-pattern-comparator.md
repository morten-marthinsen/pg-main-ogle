## PURPOSE

Compares the complete product introduction draft against elite product introductions from TIER1 controls. This microskill assesses whether the draft operates at the same structural and qualitative level as proven controls — not by copying them, but by demonstrating the same principles, patterns, and sophistication. The comparison also ensures DIFFERENTIATION — the draft must be clearly distinct from any single control while operating at the same level.

This is a calibration tool, not a copying tool. It answers: "Would this product introduction hold its own alongside Montezuma's Secret, Gundry Total Restore, and Billionaire Brain Wave?"

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A vault comparison engine that benchmarks draft against TIER1 elite controls
- A multi-dimensional quality assessor across 8 comparison dimensions
- A differentiation verifier ensuring the draft is original, not derivative
- A calibration tool that identifies where the draft falls short of elite standards

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A copy-paste detector (this is not plagiarism checking)
- A style matcher (the draft should NOT sound like any single control)
- A principle validator (that is 4.1)
- An anti-slop scanner (that is 4.2)
- A creative editor — it compares and scores, it does not rewrite

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Select Comparison Controls

1. Select 3-5 relevant TIER1 controls for comparison based on:
   - Product format match (supplement vs. supplement, digital vs. digital)
   - Niche proximity (health vs. health, golf vs. golf)
   - Campaign format match (VSL vs. VSL, advertorial vs. advertorial)
   - Introduction type match (similar primary type)

2. Selection matrix:

| Draft Product Format | Primary Comparison Controls |
|---|---|
| Supplement (health/weight) | Gundry Total Restore, Resurge, Montezuma's Secret |
| Supplement (men's health) | Montezuma's Secret, Testo Rise, Uri Varx |
| Digital (manifestation/self-help) | Billionaire Brain Wave, Superbrain |
| Physical (golf equipment) | 357 Fairway Hybrid |
| Info product (golf instruction) | Pendulum Swing Sequence, Simple Strike Sequence |
| Beauty/personal care | no!no! PRO, Australian Dream |
| Advertorial format | Uri Varx, Australian Dream, no!no! PRO |
| Magalog format | Testo Rise |

3. Always include at least 1 control from a DIFFERENT niche for cross-niche calibration

### Step 2: Compare Across 8 Dimensions

4. For each of the 8 comparison dimensions, score the draft on a 1-10 scale and compare to the selected controls:

**Dimension 1: BRIDGE SMOOTHNESS**
```
What to assess:
  - Does the bridge transition feel invisible? (like TIER1 controls)
  - Is there zero "now I'm selling" feeling?
  - Does the tonal register maintain continuity?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - BBW (9/10): Story continuation — no seam visible
  - Gundry (9/10): Accessibility bridge — practical, not commercial
  - 357 (8/10): Competitive elimination — emotional resolution
  - Montezuma (8/10): Deferred reveal + manufacturing quest

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 2: PRODUCT REVEAL IMPACT**
```
What to assess:
  - Is the product name revealed with appropriate drama?
  - Is the positioning statement clear and differentiated?
  - Is a uniqueness claim present ("first" / "only" / "never before")?
  - Is credibility established?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - Resurge (10/10): "I'm honored to introduce... world's first clinically proven..."
  - Total Restore (9/10): "First and only formula combining 16..."
  - 357 (9/10): "World's first Fairway Hybrid — a new category"

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 3: COMPONENT PROOF DENSITY**
```
What to assess:
  - Does every component carry proof? (not features)
  - Is proof diverse? (clinical, authority, statistic, comparison)
  - Is there a "parade of proof" feeling?
  - Are components connected to the mechanism?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - Gundry (10/10): Three-prong + Pepsin GI clinical trial (258 people, 92%)
  - Montezuma (9/10): Each ingredient with authority (Harvard, Examine.com, patent)
  - PSS (8/10): Each step with biomechanical proof + student results

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 4: VALUE STACK EFFECTIVENESS**
```
What to assess:
  - Is the value dramatically above price? (5-15x ratio)
  - Are bonuses specific and named?
  - Is there a "wow" bonus?
  - Does the value feel genuine, not padded?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - PSS (10/10): $1,744+ in bonuses, including $1,000 coupons + VIP credit
  - 357 (9/10): 6 bonuses across 5 categories + continuity trojan horse
  - Montezuma (7/10): 5 e-books ($136.92) — lower but authentic
  - Gundry (7/10): Authority play — no bonuses, Dr. Gundry carries it

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 5: PRICE PSYCHOLOGY EFFECTIVENESS**
```
What to assess:
  - Is anchoring present and effective?
  - Does the actual price feel dramatically low?
  - Is per-day reframe included (if applicable)?
  - Does the reader feel "this is a steal"?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - BBW (10/10): $10K → $5K → $1K → $100 → $49 → $39 (6 anchors)
  - Montezuma (9/10): $400 → $300 → $200 → $149.99 → $99 → $69 + "<$2/day"
  - Resurge (8/10): $599 → $297 → $49
  - Gundry (8/10): "$1.27/day — less than a cup of coffee"

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 6: GUARANTEE STRENGTH**
```
What to assess:
  - Is the guarantee branded and named?
  - Does it read as a feature, not a policy?
  - Is the strength appropriate for the promise level?
  - Is confidence amplification present?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - Montezuma (10/10): "Triple-Back-Guarantee" — triple refund, 365 days, extreme
  - Australian Dream (9/10): "Empty Jar Guarantee" — use entire product, still refund
  - SSS (9/10): "Flush It Or It's Free" — bold, memorable, outcome-based
  - BBW (8/10): "90-Day 100% Ironclad Money Back Guarantee" — strength + duration

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

**Dimension 7: SCARCITY CREDIBILITY**
```
What to assess:
  - Is scarcity justified with a real reason?
  - Is the justification believable?
  - Does the scarcity feel natural, not forced?
  - Is urgency proportional to the claim?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - 357 (9/10): Tariffs + manufacturing disruption (real-world events)
  - Montezuma (9/10): Rare ingredients + "first 500 orders" + "limit 6 per household"
  - BBW (8/10): Suppression threat (fits the narrative of powerful interests)
  - Gundry (7/10): Time-limited window for first-time customers

Score the draft: [1-10]
Note: If no scarcity present, score based on whether absence is appropriate for the format
```

**Dimension 8: EMOTIONAL ARCHITECTURE ADHERENCE**
```
What to assess:
  - Does the 13-step emotional sequence flow naturally?
  - Are the 3 critical shifts present?
  - Are there emotional dips or plateaus in wrong places?
  - Does the trajectory peak at the right moments?

Comparison benchmarks:
  - Montezuma (10/10): Perfect emotional arc from manufacturing quest through triple-back close
  - Resurge (9/10): Clear shifts from "I want" → "I can have" → "this is a steal"
  - PSS (9/10): Strong future pacing with specific golf scenarios

Score the draft: [1-10]
```

### Step 3: Calculate Composite Score

5. Calculate weighted average:

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Bridge smoothness | 15% | Most dangerous point (Principle 3) |
| Product reveal impact | 10% | Foundation of product section |
| Component proof density | 15% | Core of Principle 4 |
| Value stack effectiveness | 10% | Commercial architecture |
| Price psychology | 10% | Critical shift 2 |
| Guarantee strength | 10% | Principle 6 |
| Scarcity credibility | 10% | Principle 7 |
| Emotional architecture | 20% | Governs entire flow |

6. Composite score = weighted average of all 8 dimensions
7. Minimum passing score: 6.0 / 10.0

### Step 4: Assess Differentiation

8. Verify the draft is DIFFERENTIATED from any single comparison control:
   - Does it use a DIFFERENT bridge architecture than the closest niche match?
   - Does it use a DIFFERENT component reveal pattern? (or different execution within same pattern)
   - Does it use a DIFFERENT price cascade structure?
   - Does the guarantee have a UNIQUE branded name?
   - Is the future pacing SPECIFIC to this product (not echoing a control)?

9. Differentiation score: count unique elements vs. elements matching a single control
   - If >70% of structural choices match one control → WARNING: too derivative
   - If <70% match any single control → differentiation confirmed
   - Differentiation is about STRUCTURAL choices, not surface language

### Step 5: Document Matches and Differentiations

10. For each dimension:
    - Note which control the draft most closely resembles
    - Note where the draft DIFFERS from that control
    - Note where the draft FALLS SHORT of elite standard
    - Note where the draft EXCEEDS or INNOVATES beyond controls

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Controls selected | 3-5 relevant controls chosen by format, niche, and type |
| All 8 dimensions scored | Each dimension has score, benchmark, and notes |
| Composite score >= 6.0 | Weighted average meets minimum threshold |
| Differentiation confirmed | <70% structural match to any single control |
| Strengths documented | At least 2 specific strengths identified |
| Weaknesses documented | Any dimension <7.0 noted as weakness |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: All Controls From Same Niche
- **Symptom:** Comparison is too narrow — missing cross-niche quality signals
- **Cause:** Only format/niche-matched controls selected
- **Prevention:** Step 1 requires at least 1 cross-niche control
- **Recovery:** Add cross-niche comparison

### Failure Mode 2: Scores Inflated Without Evidence
- **Symptom:** All dimensions score 8+ but draft quality is mediocre
- **Cause:** Scoring not tied to specific text evidence
- **Prevention:** Each score requires comparison_notes with specific text references
- **Recovery:** Re-score with required evidence for each dimension

### Failure Mode 3: Derivative Draft Not Flagged
- **Symptom:** Draft closely copies Montezuma's Secret structure and language
- **Cause:** Differentiation check not performed
- **Prevention:** Step 4 explicit differentiation assessment
- **Recovery:** Identify derivative elements; recommend structural variations

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Strong Comparison — Supplement VSL

```
Controls Selected:
  1. Gundry Total Restore (supplement, gut health, VSL) — primary niche match
  2. Resurge (supplement, weight loss, VSL) — format match
  3. Montezuma's Secret (supplement, men's health, VSL) — pattern match
  4. BBW (digital, manifestation, VSL) — cross-niche calibration

Dimension Scores:
  Bridge smoothness: 8.0 (vs. Gundry 9.0) — accessibility bridge well-executed, slight tonal shift detected
  Product reveal: 8.5 (vs. Resurge 10.0) — strong positioning, uniqueness claimed, credibility present
  Component proof: 9.0 (vs. Gundry 10.0) — all components have proof, one weaker than others
  Value stack: 7.0 (vs. PSS 10.0) — bonuses present but ratio only 4:1
  Price psychology: 8.0 (vs. BBW 10.0) — 4-point cascade + per-day, effective but not as dramatic
  Guarantee strength: 9.0 (vs. Montezuma 10.0) — branded name, extreme terms, feature framing
  Scarcity credibility: 7.5 (vs. 357 9.0) — justified but justification could be more specific
  Emotional architecture: 8.0 (vs. Montezuma 10.0) — all shifts present, slight plateau in components

Composite Score: 8.1 (weighted) — PASS (>= 6.0)

Differentiation: CONFIRMED
  Most similar: Gundry (62% structural match)
  Unique elements: Different guarantee naming, different cascade structure, unique component proof
  Derivative warnings: None

Strengths: Component proof density (9.0), Guarantee strength (9.0)
Weaknesses: Value stack (7.0 — ratio below ideal), Scarcity specificity (7.5)
Recommendations: Strengthen bonus values, add more specific scarcity justification
```

### Example 2: Below Threshold — Needs Improvement

```
Composite Score: 5.4 (weighted) — FAIL (< 6.0)

Weaknesses:
  Bridge smoothness: 5.0 — commercial language detected in bridge
  Component proof: 4.0 — 3 of 8 components lack proof packages
  Price psychology: 5.0 — only 2 anchor points, no per-day

Recommendations:
  1. Rewrite bridge to remove all commercial language
  2. Add proof packages to 5 remaining components
  3. Add 2-3 more anchor points and per-day reframe
```
