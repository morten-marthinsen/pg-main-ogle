---

## PURPOSE

Writes the product name reveal with positioning statement, uniqueness claim, and credibility signals. This is the moment the reader learns WHAT the product is called and WHY it is different from everything else. In story-driven VSLs where the product name has been withheld (60-75% of the way through), this is a peak emotional moment — the culmination of desire built through mechanism education and personal results.

The product reveal follows directly from the bridge moment (2.1). It must deliver the product name with appropriate drama, establish the product's market position with a clear positioning statement, and claim at least one uniqueness dimension ("first," "only," "never before"). It must also establish credibility appropriate to the product format.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The product name reveal writer — the moment the reader learns what "it" is called
- A positioning statement constructor — establishing market position in one sentence
- A uniqueness claim writer — establishing "first" / "only" / "never before" dimensions
- A credibility signal arranger — manufacturing, clinical, authority, engineering, sourcing
- The emotional peak-delivery system for the "I want this" → "I can have this" shift

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A bridge writer (that is 2.1 — this follows directly from the bridge)
- A component reveal writer (that is 2.3 — this reveals the product itself, not its ingredients)
- A product namer (product name was confirmed in 0.4)
- An offer architect (that is Skill 11)
- A value stack builder (that is 2.4)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Determine Reveal Intensity

1. Based on naming_timing, determine how much DRAMA the reveal carries:

**IF LATE NAMING (60-75% of copy):**
- The product name is being revealed for the FIRST TIME
- This is a HIGH-DRAMA moment — the reader has been wanting to know "what is this thing?" for thousands of words
- Reveal requires ceremonial language, a positioning statement, and uniqueness claim
- The name drop IS an event

**IF IMMEDIATE NAMING (0-10% of copy):**
- The product name was already revealed in the headline or opening
- This is a LOW-DRAMA moment — the reader already knows the name
- This section focuses on POSITIONING and CREDIBILITY, not naming
- The name is reiterated with new context, not "revealed"

2. Set reveal_intensity: HIGH or LOW

### Step 2: Construct the Name Reveal

3. For HIGH reveal_intensity, write the name reveal using one of these TIER1 patterns:

**Pattern A: The Honored Reveal**
"I'm honored to introduce you to [Product Name] — [positioning statement]."
- TIER1: "I'm honored to introduce you to Resurge — the world's first clinically proven anti-aging nutritional protocol."
- Best for: Personal authority VSLs, supplement/health products

**Pattern B: The Creation Reveal**
"That's why I/we created [Product Name]."
- TIER1: "This is why we decided to partner with Jim Kwik to create Superbrain."
- Best for: Partnership narratives, development stories

**Pattern C: The Earned Reveal**
"After [journey/investment/time], I'm finally able to share [Product Name] with you."
- TIER1: Montezuma — after $120K investment, 6-month search, GMP certification
- Best for: Manufacturing quest narratives

**Pattern D: The Discovery Reveal**
"It's called [Product Name], and it's the first [uniqueness claim]."
- TIER1: "Total Restore — first and only formula combining 16 gut hero ingredients"
- Best for: Formula development stories, clinical products

**Pattern E: The News Reveal**
"Sold under the brand name [Product Name]..."
- TIER1: "Sold under the brand name Uri Varx..."
- Best for: Advertorial/news formats (LOW reveal intensity)

**Pattern F: The Survivor Reveal**
"It's called [Product Name], and it's unlike anything else available."
- TIER1: 357 — "the world's first Fairway Hybrid — a new category of club"
- Best for: Competitive elimination / alternative pivot

4. Select the pattern that matches:
   - Introduction type (from 1.1)
   - Bridge architecture (from 1.2 / 2.1)
   - Campaign format (VSL, magalog, advertorial)

### Step 3: Write the Positioning Statement

5. The positioning statement is a SINGLE SENTENCE that establishes market position:

**Pattern: "The [superlative/first/only] [category] [that/to/for] [key differentiator]"**

TIER1 examples:
- "The world's first clinically proven anti-aging nutritional protocol" (Resurge)
- "First and only formula combining 16 powerful gut hero ingredients" (Total Restore)
- "The world's first and ONLY Fairway Hybrid — a new category of club" (357)
- "The first Theta-based sound frequency to target the hippocampus" (BBW)
- "The ONLY all-natural supplement proven in clinical studies in a Western country to naturally increase testosterone" (Testo Rise)

6. Construct positioning statement using:
   - Confirmed positioning from 0.4 (if provided)
   - OR generate from: product_format + mechanism_name + uniqueness dimension

7. Every positioning statement MUST include at least ONE of:
   - "first" (temporal uniqueness — first to do X)
   - "only" (exclusivity — only product to do X)
   - "never before" (novelty — never before seen/done)

### Step 4: Write the Uniqueness Claim

8. The uniqueness claim EXPANDS on the positioning statement with a specific, provable assertion:

TIER1 examples:
- "Only product in the world containing the eight special nutrients in exact scientifically proven amounts" (Resurge)
- "First and ONLY formula to combine 16 powerful gut hero ingredients — backed by clinical research" (Total Restore)
- "A new category of golf club that doesn't exist anywhere else" (357)
- "Contains 9 decimal points hitting 3 frequencies scientifically proven to activate your hippocampus" (BBW)

9. Construct uniqueness claim using:
   - Confirmed uniqueness from 0.4 (if provided)
   - OR generate from: component count + mechanism + proof elements

10. The uniqueness claim should include at least ONE of:
    - Specific number (16 ingredients, 8 nutrients, 9 decimal points)
    - Clinical/scientific backing reference
    - Category creation claim ("a new category")
    - Patent or proprietary technology reference

### Step 5: Add Credibility Signals

11. Based on product_format, add appropriate credibility signals:

**For SUPPLEMENTS:**
```
Manufacturing credibility:
  - GMP certified facility
  - Third-party lab tested
  - Made in USA
  - Investment amount (if available): "$120K invested in manufacturing"
  - Named manufacturing partner (if available)
  - Non-GMO, vegetarian, all-natural (as applicable)
Clinical credibility:
  - "Backed by [study type] study with [N] participants"
  - "[X]% improvement documented"
  - "Published on clinicaltrials.gov" (if applicable)
```

**For DIGITAL PRODUCTS:**
```
Authority credibility:
  - Creator credentials: "[Name], who has worked with [celebrities/experts]"
  - Development story: "Developed over [X] years of research"
  - Technology credibility: "Using [specific technology/science] developed by [institution]"
Usage credibility:
  - User count: "Already used by [N] people"
  - Simplicity: "Just [X minutes] per day"
```

**For PHYSICAL PRODUCTS:**
```
Engineering credibility:
  - Named engineer or designer with credentials
  - Patent claims or proprietary technology
  - Technical specifications
  - Professional endorsement (PGA Tour, medical professionals)
Testing credibility:
  - Performance testing results
  - Comparison data vs. competitors
  - Professional use validation
```

**For INFO PRODUCTS:**
```
Authority credibility:
  - Creator credentials and experience
  - Student/client results
  - Professional standing (PGA professional, certified coach)
Method credibility:
  - "Based on [X years] of teaching/coaching [N] students"
  - "The same method used by [notable client/pro]"
```

12. Select 2-4 credibility signals most relevant to this product
13. Write credibility signals as natural sentences (not bullet points in copy)

### Step 6: Assemble the Complete Product Reveal

14. Assemble the full reveal section in this order:
    1. Name reveal (from Step 2)
    2. Positioning statement (from Step 3)
    3. Uniqueness claim (from Step 4)
    4. Credibility signals (from Step 5)

15. Target word count: 75-200 words (tight, impactful — not a wall of text)

16. The reveal should feel like an EVENT, not a product description:
    - HIGH reveal: Emotional peak — the reader has been WAITING for this
    - LOW reveal: Credibility reinforcement — the reader already knows the name, now they learn WHY it matters

### Step 7: Verify Reveal Quality

17. Quality checks:

**NAME MEMORABILITY CHECK:**
- Is the product name clear, pronounceable, and memorable?
- Does it appear with appropriate emphasis (bold, standalone sentence, etc.)?

**POSITIONING CLARITY CHECK:**
- Can a reader understand what the product IS in one sentence?
- Does the positioning differentiate from generic category? ("gut health supplement" → NO; "first formula combining 16 gut hero ingredients" → YES)

**UNIQUENESS CLAIMED CHECK:**
- Is there at least one "first" / "only" / "never before" claim?
- Is the claim SPECIFIC (includes numbers, categories, or comparisons)?

**CREDIBILITY ESTABLISHED CHECK:**
- Does the reveal include at least 2 credibility signals appropriate to format?
- Are credibility signals woven into prose (not listed as a spec sheet)?

**MECHANISM CONNECTION CHECK:**
- Does the product clearly connect to the mechanism? (product delivers the mechanism)
- Is Principle 1 maintained? (mechanism is hero, product is delivery vehicle)

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Name revealed | Product name appears clearly and prominently |
| Positioning statement present | One sentence establishing market position |
| Uniqueness claimed | At least one "first" / "only" / "never before" dimension |
| Credibility signals included | 2-4 signals appropriate to product format |
| Mechanism connection maintained | Product positioned as delivery vehicle for mechanism (Principle 1) |
| Reveal intensity appropriate | HIGH for late-naming, LOW for immediate-naming |
| Word count | 75-200 words |
| No generic descriptions | Product described with specifics, not generic category language |
| Continues from bridge | First sentence follows naturally from bridge moment ending |
| Leads to components | Ending sets up component reveal or value stack |

---

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Product Becomes the Hero
- **Symptom:** Product described as the "solution" with no mechanism reference
- **Cause:** Reveal focuses on product features instead of mechanism delivery
- **Prevention:** Mechanism connection check (Step 7) — product is VEHICLE for mechanism
- **Recovery:** Rewrite to frame product as "how you access [mechanism]"

### Failure Mode 2: Generic Positioning
- **Symptom:** "A powerful new supplement for gut health" — indistinguishable from any competitor
- **Cause:** Positioning statement lacks superlative, number, or specific differentiator
- **Prevention:** Step 3 requires "first" / "only" / "never before" in every statement
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with specific differentiator from confirmed product data

### Failure Mode 3: No Uniqueness Claimed
- **Symptom:** Product sounds like every other product in the category
- **Cause:** Uniqueness claim missing or too vague
- **Prevention:** Step 4 requires specific uniqueness with number or category creation
- **Recovery:** Add uniqueness claim with specific proof point

### Failure Mode 4: Spec Sheet Instead of Reveal
- **Symptom:** Reads like a product label or Amazon listing, not a copy reveal
- **Cause:** Credibility signals listed as bullets instead of woven into narrative prose
- **Prevention:** Step 5 requires natural sentence integration
- **Recovery:** Rewrite credibility as narrative elements within the reveal

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: High-Drama Honored Reveal (Resurge Pattern)

**Context:** Late naming (72%), supplement VSL, mechanism = Shallow Sleep Syndrome / HGH Suppression. Bridge ends with manufacturing story.

**Product Reveal Draft:**
"I'm honored to introduce you to Resurge.

Resurge is the world's first clinically proven anti-aging nutritional protocol — the only product in the world containing the eight special nutrients in the exact scientifically proven amounts shown to dramatically improve deep sleep quality and metabolic regeneration.

Each capsule is manufactured right here in the United States in an FDA-approved, GMP-certified facility. Every batch is third-party tested for purity and potency. And the formula is 100% natural, vegetarian, and non-GMO.

This isn't another sleep supplement. This is the first formula ever created specifically to address the shallow sleep crisis that accelerates aging and blocks the body's natural overnight fat-burning process."

**Word count:** 121
**Reveal intensity:** HIGH (late naming, first time reader hears "Resurge")
**Pattern:** Honored Reveal + Manufacturing Credibility
**Uniqueness:** "only product in the world" + "eight special nutrients in exact amounts"

### Example 2: News Format Reveal (Uri Varx Pattern)

**Context:** Immediate naming (5%), supplement advertorial, mechanism = Bladder control compound.

**Product Reveal Draft:**
"Sold under the brand name Uri Varx, this new bladder control pill combines a patented compound that clinical researchers say could fundamentally change how millions of adults manage urinary urgency.

In a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial involving 142 participants, Uri Varx demonstrated a 56% reduction in urinary frequency within the first 30 days of use. The results were published earlier this year, and the clinical data has been submitted to clinicaltrials.gov for public verification.

What makes Uri Varx different from over-the-counter bladder medications is its mechanism of action — it doesn't mask symptoms. It targets the underlying smooth muscle dysfunction that causes urgency in the first place."

**Word count:** 115
**Reveal intensity:** LOW (immediate naming, already known)
**Pattern:** News Reveal (journalistic frame)
**Uniqueness:** "patented compound" + clinical trial specifics + "doesn't mask symptoms"

### Example 3: Earned Reveal After Manufacturing Quest (Montezuma Pattern)

**Context:** Late naming (65%), supplement VSL, mechanism = Firming Flavonoids / Libido Override.

**Product Reveal Draft:**
"After six months of searching, a hundred-and-twenty-thousand-dollar investment, and partnerships with the most respected nutraceutical manufacturers in the country... I'm finally able to share this with you.

It's called Montezuma's Secret.

Montezuma's Secret is the first all-natural male vitality formula to combine all eight firming flavonoid compounds in a single capsule — the same compounds that powered the legendary stamina of the Aztec Emperor and his court.

Every bottle is manufactured in a GMP-certified facility right here in the United States. Third-party lab tested. And every ingredient is sourced from the highest-potency suppliers we could find — including some that had to be imported from regions most supplement companies have never heard of."

**Word count:** 132
**Reveal intensity:** HIGH (late naming, climax of manufacturing quest)
**Pattern:** Earned Reveal (journey justifies the reveal)
**Uniqueness:** "first" + "all eight firming flavonoid compounds in a single capsule"
