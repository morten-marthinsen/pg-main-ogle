## PURPOSE

Writes the component/ingredient/feature reveal section using the pattern selected in Layer 1 (microskill 1.3). This is the section where the product's inner workings are exposed — and the CRITICAL PRINCIPLE is that components are PROOF, not features (Principle 4). Every ingredient, step, feature, or module must carry its own mini-proof package that demonstrates why the mechanism works.

This is NOT a spec sheet or ingredient label. It is a parade of evidence where each component adds another layer of proof that the mechanism is real and the product is the legitimate vehicle for it. After reading this section, the reader should think "this is incredibly well-researched" — not "here are the ingredients."

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A component-as-proof writer that transforms features into evidence
- A mini-proof-package constructor for each individual component
- An executor of the component reveal pattern selected by 1.3
- A mechanism-connection writer that ties each component back to WHY it works

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A component selector (that is 1.3 — this executes 1.3's selection)
- A product reveal writer (that is 2.2 — the product name is already known at this point)
- A value stack builder (that is 2.4 — components are about PROOF, not VALUE)
- A proof inventor — it uses proof from the proof inventory and confirmed package
- An ingredient label formatter — this is narrative copy, not a supplement facts panel

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Component Map and Proof Packages

1. Load the component_proof_map from 1.3
2. Load the sequence_order (which component is revealed first, second, etc.)
3. Load the framework details (framework name, prong names, step names)
4. For each component, confirm:
   - Component name
   - Proof package (type + content + source)
   - Proof strength (strong / moderate / weak)
   - Sequence position

### Step 2: Write Framework Introduction (If Applicable)

5. If selected_pattern is three_prong_defense:
   - Write the framework introduction before individual components:
   ```
   "To make [Product Name] as effective as possible, I built it around a [Framework Name] — three distinct layers of protection that work together to [mechanism outcome]."
   ```
   - TIER1 example (Gundry): "Total Restore works through what I call the Three-Prong Defense — three distinct mechanisms that repair, absorb, and rebalance your gut..."
   - TIER1 example (357): "The 357 is built on Tri-Fusion Technology — three engineering breakthroughs that no other club combines..."
   - The framework name should sound PROPRIETARY — like it belongs to this product alone

6. If selected_pattern is step_by_step_system:
   - Write the system introduction:
   ```
   "The [System Name] is built on [N] simple steps. Each one builds on the last, and together they create a complete [outcome]."
   ```
   - TIER1 example (PSS): "The Pendulum Swing Sequence is built on three drills — each one taking less than 5 minutes to learn..."

7. If selected_pattern is ingredient_deep_dive, usage_demonstration, or nutrient_protocol:
   - No framework introduction needed — proceed directly to individual components

### Step 3: Write Each Component Section

8. For EACH component in sequence_order, write a mini-section following the selected pattern:

**IF THREE-PRONG DEFENSE:**
```
For each prong:
  - Prong name and function (1 sentence)
  - Key ingredients/features within this prong (1-2 sentences)
  - Proof package for this prong (2-4 sentences)
  - Connection to mechanism (1 sentence)

TIER1 example — Gundry Prong 1 (REPAIR):
  "The first prong REPAIRS your damaged gut walls. The key ingredient here is L-glutamine — at a specific dosage of 213 milligrams — combined with licorice root and marshmallow root. Together, these three ingredients create a protective barrier that patches the holes lectins have punched in your intestinal lining.

  But the real breakthrough in this prong is something called Pepsin GI — a unique compound of zinc and L-carnosine. I discovered Pepsin GI after reviewing a double-blind, placebo-controlled study of 258 people. 92% showed significant improvement in gut barrier integrity within just eight weeks."
```

**IF INGREDIENT DEEP DIVE:**
```
For each ingredient:
  - Ingredient name with any traditional/cultural context (1 sentence)
  - What it does for the mechanism (1-2 sentences)
  - Its UNIQUE proof element — authority endorsement, study, or comparison (2-3 sentences)
  - Transition to next ingredient (1 sentence)

TIER1 example — Montezuma's Secret, Ingredient 1 (Cacao):
  "The first ingredient is Cacao Flavanols — the same compound that made Montezuma's court legendary for their stamina. Harvard Medical School has published extensive research on cacao's vasodilatory effects — its ability to relax blood vessels and improve blood flow throughout the body.

  In fact, Examine.com — one of the world's most respected independent nutrition databases — ranks cacao as the #1 natural compound for cardiovascular flow support. This isn't a folk remedy. This is mainstream science."
```

**IF STEP-BY-STEP SYSTEM:**
```
For each step:
  - Step name and what it does (1-2 sentences)
  - How to perform it (2-3 sentences — simple, actionable)
  - Why it works (mechanism connection) (1-2 sentences)
  - Proof: student result, demonstration, or biomechanical explanation (1-2 sentences)

TIER1 example — PSS, Step 1 (Towel Turn):
  "Step one is something I call the Towel Turn. It's a simple drill that retrains your body to start the backswing with your core — not your arms.

  Here's how it works: You take a standard bath towel, fold it, and place it under your lead arm. Then you make a backswing. If the towel drops, your arms are doing the work. If it stays, your body is rotating correctly.

  This one drill eliminates the number-one swing fault I see in amateur golfers — the arm-dominant backswing that destroys consistency and costs you 20-30 yards off the tee."
```

**IF USAGE DEMONSTRATION:**
```
For each usage step:
  - Usage step name (1 sentence)
  - What to do — specific, visual, actionable (2-3 sentences)
  - Why this step matters (1 sentence)
  - Simplicity emphasis (1 sentence)

TIER1 example — BBW (Single usage demonstration):
  "Using the Billionaire Brain Wave is as simple as putting on a pair of headphones.

  Every morning, after your coffee, you press play on a 7-minute audio track. That's it. You sit comfortably and listen. The theta frequencies begin working from your very first session — stimulating your hippocampus and activating the neural pathways associated with intuition, creativity, and opportunity recognition.

  No meditation training required. No mantras. No special equipment beyond your everyday headphones."
```

**IF NUTRIENT PROTOCOL:**
```
For each nutrient:
  - Nutrient name at specific dosage (1 sentence)
  - What this nutrient does in the protocol (1-2 sentences)
  - Clinical backing for this specific amount (1-2 sentences)
  - How it interacts with other nutrients in the formula (1 sentence)

TIER1 example — Resurge (Key nutrient):
  "The breakthrough combination at the heart of Resurge is arginine and lysine — two amino acids that, when combined at specific dosages, have been clinically shown to boost Human Growth Hormone production by 695%.

  That's not a typo. A 695% increase in the hormone your body uses to burn fat, repair tissue, and reverse aging — activated naturally while you sleep. This combination has been validated in peer-reviewed research and is the reason Resurge works differently from every other sleep supplement on the market."
```

### Step 4: Ensure Every Component Carries Proof

9. After writing each component section, verify the proof requirement:

**PROOF TYPES (at least one per component):**
- Clinical trial reference (study, participant count, result percentage)
- Authority endorsement (named expert, institution, publication)
- Specific statistic (exact number, measurement, ranking)
- Discovery story (how the ingredient/feature was found)
- Comparison data (vs. competitor, vs. alternative, vs. placebo)
- Dosage specificity (exact mg, exact formulation — specificity IS proof)

10. If any component lacks proof:
    - Connect it to the mechanism instead: "This ingredient activates [mechanism element] by..."
    - Or connect it to another component: "Working together with [proven component], this creates..."
    - Flag: "Component [X] has mechanism-connection proof only — no independent evidence"

### Step 5: Write Component-to-Component Transitions

11. Between each component section, write a transition that:
    - Acknowledges what was just proven
    - Creates anticipation for the next component
    - Maintains the "parade of proof" feeling

12. Transition patterns:
    - "But that's just the first [prong/ingredient/step]..."
    - "And it gets even better..."
    - "The next [ingredient/step] is where things get really interesting..."
    - "Now here's where [Product Name] truly separates itself from everything else..."

13. Do NOT use the same transition pattern twice in a row

### Step 6: Write Component Section Summary

14. After all components are revealed, write a brief summary (2-4 sentences) that:
    - Recaps the total scope ("16 ingredients," "3 precision drills," "8 nutrients")
    - Connects ALL components back to the mechanism
    - Creates a sense of completeness ("every angle covered")
    - Transitions to the value stack or price section

15. TIER1 example summary:
    - Gundry: "Together, these 16 ingredients create the most comprehensive gut barrier formula ever developed — addressing repair, absorption, AND rebalancing in a single capsule."
    - 357: "Tri-Fusion Technology. Three breakthroughs. One club. Nothing else on the market combines all three."

### Step 7: Verify Component Reveal Quality

16. Quality checks:

**PROOF-NOT-FEATURES CHECK:**
- Read each component section. Does it PROVE something, or just DESCRIBE something?
- "Contains L-glutamine" = FEATURE (FAIL)
- "Contains L-glutamine at 213mg — the exact dosage shown in clinical research to repair intestinal permeability" = PROOF (PASS)

**PARADE-OF-PROOF CHECK:**
- After reading all components in sequence, does the reader feel a CUMULATIVE WEIGHT of evidence?
- Each component should make the NEXT component more believable
- The section should feel like overwhelming proof, not a product description

**MECHANISM CONNECTION CHECK:**
- Does every component connect back to the mechanism?
- Components should explain WHY they work (mechanism), not just WHAT they do (feature)

**WORD COUNT CHECK:**
- Each component: 50-150 words (tight, impactful — not a research paper)
- Total section: Proportional to component count
  - 3 components: 200-400 words
  - 8 components: 500-900 words
  - 16 components (grouped into 3-4 prongs): 400-700 words

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Every component has proof | Each component carries at least one proof element |
| Components are proof, not features | Descriptions explain WHY (mechanism) not just WHAT (feature) |
| Parade of proof feeling | Cumulative evidence weight builds across components |
| Pattern executed | Selected pattern (three-prong, deep dive, etc.) faithfully followed |
| Framework named | If three-prong, framework and prong names present |
| Steps named | If step-by-step, each step has proprietary name |

| Summary present | 2-4 sentence summary connects all components to mechanism |
| Leads to value stack | Section ending transitions to value or price section |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Ingredient Label Copy
- **Symptom:** "Contains vitamin D, magnesium, zinc, and ashwagandha" — sounds like a label
- **Cause:** Components listed without proof packages
- **Prevention:** Every component MUST have proof section (Step 4)
- **Recovery:** Rewrite each component with its proof package

### Failure Mode 2: All Proof is Same Type
- **Symptom:** Every component has "a study showed..." — monotonous
- **Cause:** Proof diversity not considered
- **Prevention:** Step 4 provides multiple proof types; vary across components
- **Recovery:** Replace repeated proof types with alternatives (authority, stat, story, comparison)

### Failure Mode 3: Components Disconnected from Mechanism
- **Symptom:** Reader knows WHAT is in the product but not WHY it matters
- **Cause:** Mechanism connection omitted from component sections
- **Prevention:** Step 3 requires mechanism connection in each component
- **Recovery:** Add 1 sentence per component connecting to mechanism

### Failure Mode 4: Section Too Long — Reader Fatigues
- **Symptom:** Component section exceeds 1000 words — reader skims or leaves
- **Cause:** Too many components with too much detail per component
- **Prevention:** Word count check (50-150 per component, total proportional)
- **Recovery:** Trim to strongest proof per component; group minor components

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Three-Prong Defense (Gundry Total Restore Pattern)

**Framework Introduction:**
"Total Restore works through what I call the Three-Prong Defense — three distinct mechanisms that work together to repair, protect, and rebalance your gut from the inside out."

**Prong 1 — REPAIR:**
"The first prong repairs your damaged gut walls. At the core of this prong is L-glutamine at 213 milligrams — the amino acid your gut lining uses as its primary fuel source. Alongside it, licorice root and marshmallow root create a protective coating over damaged areas while healing occurs.

But the real star of this prong is Pepsin GI — a proprietary zinc-carnosine compound I discovered after reviewing hundreds of gut health studies. In a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial with 258 participants, Pepsin GI produced a 92% improvement in gut barrier integrity in just eight weeks. Nothing else I've tested comes close."

**Prong 2 — ABSORB:**
"The second prong neutralizes lectins before they can do more damage. The key here is N-acetyl-D-glucosamine — what I call the 'decoy agent.' It mimics the sugar molecules on your gut wall, so lectins bind to the decoy instead of punching new holes in your intestinal lining. It's like sending a decoy convoy while the real supply line is protected."

**Prong 3 — REBALANCE:**
"The third prong rebalances your gut bacteria. A blend of mushroom extracts combined with cloves — which have an antioxidant score of 314,000, one of the highest of any food on earth — works to restore the bacterial balance that lectins have disrupted."

**Summary:**
"Together, these sixteen ingredients create the most comprehensive gut barrier formula ever developed — addressing repair, absorption, AND rebalancing in a single capsule. No other formula on the market combines all three mechanisms of defense."

**Word count:** 271 (across 3 prongs + introduction + summary)

### Example 2: Step-by-Step System (Pendulum Swing Sequence)

**Step 1 — The Towel Turn:**
"The first drill is called the Towel Turn. Take a standard bath towel, fold it, and tuck it under your lead arm. Now make a backswing. If the towel drops, your arms are taking over. **Step 2 — Tee to Toe:**
"Step two is the Tee to Toe Drill. This automates your weight shift — the move that generates 70% of your clubhead speed. Place a tee under the ball of your trail foot. During the downswing, your goal is to crush that tee with your foot. When you feel that tee flatten, you know your weight has shifted correctly. My students gain an average of 15-20 yards within the first session."

**Step 3 — The Reverse Release Move:**
"The final step is the Reverse Release Move — a simple guard against the most common overcorrection. After learning the Towel Turn and Tee to Toe, many students temporarily overrotate. This drill installs an automatic stop at the exact point where rotation becomes overrotation. Think of it as a governor on an engine — it lets you generate maximum power without going past the redline."

**Summary:**
"Three drills. Less than fifteen minutes total to learn. And together they create the most natural, repeatable swing sequence I've taught in thirty years of coaching."

**Word count:** 234 (across 3 steps + summary)
