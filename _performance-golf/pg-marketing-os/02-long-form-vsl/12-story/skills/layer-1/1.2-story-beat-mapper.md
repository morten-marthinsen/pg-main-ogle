# Layer 1.2 — Story Beat Mapper

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Story Architecture)
**Type:** Structural Mapping
**Dependencies:** upstream_data (from 0.1), vault_story_intelligence (from 0.2), teaching_index (from 0.3), story_type_classification (from 1.1)
**Output:** story_beat_map.json

---

## Purpose

Map the complete story beat structure for the classified story type. Each beat is a narrative unit with a specific sequence position, content function, emotional state, tension level, and open loop management. The beat map is the architectural blueprint for the entire story — it determines what happens, in what order, at what emotional intensity, and which curiosity loops open and close at each point.

The beat map must accomplish three things:
1. **Structural completeness** — every required beat for the classified type is present in the correct sequence
2. **Tension engineering** — the tension curve escalates appropriately with strategic peaks and valleys
3. **Open loop management** — curiosity loops are opened strategically and closed at optimal moments to maintain forward pull

Per Carlton: the story must create "breathless prose that makes your prospect afraid to exhale, for fear of missing a delicious detail." The beat map is HOW that breathlessness is structurally engineered — not through flowery language, but through strategic narrative sequencing and tension control.

**Primary Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter — Master Copy Composite
Brings decades of direct response storytelling mastery. Understands beat-level pacing — when to accelerate, when to slow for emotional impact, when to insert a cliffhanger, when to resolve a loop. Designs beat sequences that create the "can't stop reading" compulsion.

### Step 2: Map Content Summaries to Each Beat

```
FOR each beat in the selected template:
  MAP specific campaign content:
    content_summary: 2-4 sentence description of what happens in this beat
    mechanism_connection: How this beat connects to the mechanism (if applicable)
    proof_elements: Any proof/credibility woven into this beat
    upstream_asset_used: Specific data from upstream packages

  RULES:
    - Every content_summary must reference specific campaign assets (not generic placeholders)
    - Mechanism revelation beat must NAME the mechanism (mechanism.name)
    - Root cause beat must reference root_cause.expression
    - Verification beat must reference proof.knockout_proof or proof.top_credibility_elements
    - Segue beat must use Carlton's "And here's what that means for YOU" principle
    - NO beat should contain content that belongs to a different beat
      (e.g., don't put verification content in the discovery beat)
```

### Step 3: Assign Emotional States to Each Beat

```
FOR each beat in the sequence:
  ASSIGN emotional_state from this taxonomy:

  EMOTIONAL STATE TAXONOMY:
    - curiosity: "What happens next?" / "Tell me more"
    - empathy: "I understand this person" / "I feel for them"
    - identification: "That's EXACTLY how I feel" / "This is me"
    - vulnerability: "This person is being honest about pain"
    - hope: "Maybe there IS a solution" / "This could work"
    - surprise: "I didn't expect that" / "That's shocking"
    - fascination: "This is genuinely interesting" / "I need to understand"
    - alarm: "This is worse than I thought" / "I need to pay attention"
    - anger: "Someone is responsible for this" / "That's not right"
    - relief: "Finally, an answer" / "This makes sense"
    - excitement: "This could change everything" / "I want this"
    - conviction: "I believe this is real" / "This mechanism works"
    - greed: "I want those results" / "I need to get in on this"
    - urgency: "I need to act now" / "I can't miss this"

  RULES:
    - Emotional states must PROGRESS (no repeating the same state across consecutive beats)
    - The final beat before segue must be at conviction, excitement, or greed
    - The segue beat must transition to "this is relevant to MY life"
    - Vulnerability/shame states must come BEFORE hope states (not after)
    - Curiosity is typically the starting state (following the lead's tease)
```

### Step 4: Assign Tension Levels and Build Tension Curve

```
FOR each beat in the sequence:
  ASSIGN tension_level: enum[low, medium, high, peak]

  TENSION LEVEL DEFINITIONS:
    low (1-3): Establishing context, providing background, settling the reader
    medium (4-6): Building interest, introducing complications, raising stakes
    high (7-8): Approaching critical moments, obstacles intensifying, data escalating
    peak (9-10): Maximum tension — the cliffhanger, the revelation, the climactic result

  TENSION CURVE REQUIREMENTS:
    - Minimum 2 HIGH or PEAK tension points in the story
    - Tension must generally ESCALATE across the story (not flat or declining)
    - At least one tension DIP for contrast (high -> medium -> higher creates more impact)
    - Peak tension should occur at or near the mechanism revelation or climactic result
    - Final beats should resolve tension toward conviction/excitement (not leave reader tense)

  TENSION CURVE BY STORY TYPE:
    standard_discovery: low -> medium -> high (cry for help) -> medium (yoda speaks) -> high (verification) -> peak (root cause) -> medium (segue)
    accidental_discovery: medium (accident) -> high -> medium -> high -> peak -> medium
    straight_to_expert: medium -> high (paradigm shift) -> peak (revelation) -> high -> medium
    proof_data_cascade: medium (setup) -> high (first win) -> high (escalation) -> peak (full results) -> medium (transparency) -> medium (simplicity) -> high (compounding) -> medium (segue)
    vulnerability_arc: high (shame) -> peak (rock bottom) -> low (discovery) -> medium (try) -> high (signs) -> peak (triumph) -> medium (segue)

  VALIDATE: Plot tension levels sequentially and verify the curve has:
    - At least one clear peak
    - At least one valley-to-peak ascent
    - No more than 2 consecutive beats at the same tension level
    - Resolution before the segue (reader should feel relief/excitement, not anxiety)
```

### Step 5: Map Open Loops Created and Closed

```
FOR each beat in the sequence:
  IDENTIFY open_loops_created: Curiosity gaps OPENED at this beat
  IDENTIFY open_loops_closed: Curiosity gaps RESOLVED at this beat

  OPEN LOOP TYPES:
    - identity_loop: "Who is this person?" / "What happened to them?"
    - mechanism_loop: "What is the secret?" / "What did Yoda reveal?"
    - outcome_loop: "Did it work?" / "What were the results?"
    - explanation_loop: "How does it work?" / "Why does this happen?"
    - threat_loop: "What is the danger?" / "How bad is it?"
    - resolution_loop: "How does this end?" / "What's the solution?"

  LOOP MANAGEMENT RULES:
    - NEVER close all loops at once (maintain at least 1 open loop at all times until segue)
    - Open at least 1 new loop before closing the previous one
    - The mechanism_loop should be opened early and closed at the revelation beat
    - The outcome_loop should be opened at setup and closed at transformation/results beat
    - Lead-inherited loops (from lead_package.open_loops) must be acknowledged and resolved
    - Maximum 3 open loops at any single beat (too many creates confusion, not curiosity)

  FOR EACH BEAT, DOCUMENT:
    loops_open_entering: [list of all loops currently open]
    loops_created: [new loops opened at this beat]
    loops_closed: [loops resolved at this beat]
    loops_open_exiting: [list of all loops still open after this beat]

  VALIDATE:
    - Total loops created across story >= 4 (minimum curiosity engineering)
    - All loops created are eventually closed before or at the segue
    - No beat has 0 open loops (except possibly the very last resolution beat)
    - Lead-inherited loops are resolved within the story (not left dangling)
```

### Step 6: Validate Beat-to-Beat Continuity

```
FOR each consecutive beat pair (beat N, beat N+1):
  VALIDATE:
    - Content continuity: Beat N+1 logically follows from beat N
      (no unexplained jumps in time, location, or topic)
    - Emotional continuity: The emotional transition from N to N+1 is believable
      (no whiplash from "shame" to "excitement" without an intermediate state)
    - Tension continuity: Tension changes by no more than 2 levels between consecutive beats
      (no jump from "low" to "peak" without passing through "medium" and "high")
    - Loop continuity: At least 1 loop carries forward from N to N+1
      (maintaining the forward pull between beats)

  FLAG any discontinuity with specific location and recommendation for repair.
```

### Step 7: Cross-Reference with Vault Beat Structures

```
LOAD vault_story_intelligence.beat_structures
LOAD story_type_classification.vault_exemplar_beat_structure

COMPARE designed beat map against vault exemplar:
  - Beat count comparison (same number of beats? more? fewer?)
  - Sequence alignment (beats in same order?)
  - Tension curve comparison (similar shape?)
  - Loop management comparison (similar strategy?)

DOCUMENT:
  vault_structural_match: percentage similarity to vault exemplar
  intentional_deviations: where and why this beat map differs from vault
  risk_assessment: how risky are the deviations (low/medium/high)

VALIDATE: If vault_structural_match < 60%, FLAG for review.
  Low structural match is acceptable if intentional and justified,
  but unintentional deviation from proven patterns is risky.
```

### Step 8: Compile Complete Beat Map

```
BUILD complete beat_map object with:
  - All beats in sequence with position numbers
  - Content summaries for each beat
  - Emotional states for each beat
  - Tension levels for each beat
  - Open loops created and closed for each beat
  - Tension curve summary (shape description)
  - Loop management summary (total loops, resolution strategy)
  - Vault comparison results
  - Carlton compliance notes (three-line structure, breathless prose planning)

VERIFY CARLTON THREE-LINE STRUCTURE:
  The overall story maps to Carlton's three-line framework:
    SETUP = beats 1-2 (the tease of the payoff to come)
    ACTION = beats 3 through N-2 (fulfillment of the tease)
    MORAL = beats N-1 through N (punchline + segue to reader)

  If the story cannot be summarized in three lines (setup/action/moral),
  the beat map is too complex. Simplify.
```

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Missing Beats** | Standard discovery story skips "Cry for Help" beat | Every beat serves a structural function; skipping the vulnerability moment removes the emotional permission for the mechanism reveal |
| **Flat Tension Curve** | All beats scored at "medium" tension | No peaks or valleys means no drama, no breathless prose, no compulsion to keep reading |
| **Loop Flooding** | 5+ open loops at a single beat | Too many open loops creates confusion, not curiosity; the reader loses track of what they are waiting to learn |
| **Premature Loop Closure** | Mechanism loop closed in beat 2 (mechanism named immediately) | The mechanism loop is the primary curiosity driver; closing it early eliminates the forward pull through the middle beats |
| **Emotional Whiplash** | Beat jumps from "shame" directly to "greed" with no intermediate states | Emotional transitions must be believable; each state should logically follow the previous one |
| **Proof Bolting** | Verification beat contains 80% of the story's word count with dense research | Proof should be woven throughout, not dumped in one beat; Carlton's conciseness principle applies |
| **No Segue** | Story ends at the transformation/payoff without connecting to the reader | Per Carlton: "Don't try any clever transitions... just say 'And here's what that means for YOU'" — the segue is NON-NEGOTIABLE |
| **Template Rigidity** | Following the vault exemplar beat-for-beat with zero adaptation | The beat template is a starting point; it must be adapted to THIS campaign's specific assets, mechanism, and proof |
| **Data Cascade Without Transparency** | Proof story shows only wins with no losses acknowledged | Per Makepeace pattern: "winners AND losers" — transparency builds credibility; cherry-picked wins destroy it |
