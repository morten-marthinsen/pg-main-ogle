# Layer 4.2 — Mechanism-Story Alignment Checker

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Type:** Mechanism Integration Gate
**Dependencies:** full draft story text (from Layer 3 output), story_classification (from 1.1), story_beats (from 1.2), mechanism_revelation (from 2.3), transformation_payoff (from 2.4), mechanism_package (from 0.1 upstream), root_cause_package (from 0.1 upstream), teaching_index (from 0.3)
**Output:** mechanism_story_alignment.json

---

## Purpose

Verify that the story properly supports, proves, and emotionally validates the mechanism. The story is not a standalone narrative -- it is the emotional proof vehicle for the mechanism. A story that could work equally well with ANY mechanism swapped in has WEAK alignment. A story where the mechanism is woven into every beat, where the narrative DEPENDS on this specific mechanism, where removing the mechanism would collapse the story -- that story has STRONG alignment. This checker evaluates mechanism-story integration across 9 alignment dimensions and applies the definitive swap test: if you replaced this mechanism with a different one, would the story still work? If yes, the alignment is insufficient.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Verify Mechanism is NAMED in the Story

```
SCAN full_draft_story.full_text for the mechanism name:

  SEARCH FOR: mechanism_package.mechanism.name (exact match)
  SEARCH FOR: mechanism_package.mechanism.name (close variants -- abbreviated, colloquial)
  SEARCH FOR: descriptive references that clearly refer to the mechanism

  RECORD:
    - mechanism_named_explicitly: boolean (exact name or close variant found)
    - mechanism_name_occurrences: integer (how many times the name appears)
    - mechanism_name_positions: array (where in the story the name appears)
    - first_mention_position: string (beginning / first_quarter / middle / third_quarter / end)

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Mechanism named explicitly, appears at critical narrative beats (revelation, payoff),
        and the name itself carries meaning within the story
    8-9: Mechanism named explicitly at key moments, name is memorable
    6-7: Mechanism named but only once or twice, or only in the revelation section
    4-5: Mechanism referenced descriptively but never named explicitly
    2-3: Mechanism vaguely alluded to but could be any mechanism
    1: Mechanism not identifiable in the story

  CRITICAL CHECK:
    IF mechanism_named_explicitly = false → FLAG as ALIGNMENT_GAP
    The mechanism MUST be named in the story. A story that never names
    the mechanism cannot serve as the mechanism's proof vehicle.

  RECORD: mechanism_naming_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: mechanism_naming_evidence: string
```

### Step 2: Verify Story Demonstrates HOW the Mechanism Was Discovered or Proven

```
EVALUATE: Does the story show the mechanism's discovery/proof PROCESS?

  FOR discovery stories (standard, accidental, straight-to-expert):
    SCAN FOR evidence the story shows:
      - The CIRCUMSTANCES that led to the discovery
      - The MOMENT of discovery (not just "it was discovered")
      - The PROCESS of validation/verification
      - WHY this specific mechanism was the answer (not just that it was found)

  FOR proof stories (live experiment):
    SCAN FOR evidence the story shows:
      - The DESIGN of the experiment/test
      - The PARTICIPANTS and their characteristics
      - The RESULTS unfolding over time (data cascade)
      - The VERIFICATION process

  FOR transformation stories:
    SCAN FOR evidence the story shows:
      - WHEN the protagonist encountered the mechanism
      - HOW they applied it
      - WHAT changed and in what sequence
      - WHY the mechanism caused those changes

  FOR all other types:
    SCAN FOR evidence the story shows the mechanism IN ACTION
    (not just mentioned but DEMONSTRATED through narrative events)

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: The story IS the mechanism's proof -- you cannot separate the narrative
        from the mechanism's demonstration. Every beat advances the mechanism's case.
    8-9: Strong demonstration -- the discovery/proof/application process is vivid and specific
    6-7: Moderate demonstration -- the mechanism's process is shown but could be more specific
    4-5: Weak demonstration -- the story mentions the mechanism's process but doesn't SHOW it
    2-3: Minimal demonstration -- the mechanism is present but its process is skipped over
    1: No demonstration -- the story tells other events and the mechanism is just named

  RECORD: mechanism_demonstration_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: demonstration_evidence: string
```

### Step 3: Verify Mechanism Revelation Connects to Root Cause

```
EVALUATE: Does the mechanism's reveal in the story connect to the root cause?

  EXTRACT the root cause from root_cause_package.root_cause.expression

  SCAN the mechanism revelation section of the story:
    - Does the story explain WHY the mechanism addresses the ROOT CAUSE?
    - Does the revelation make the connection between mechanism → root cause → results?
    - Does the protagonist (or Yoda/authority character) articulate the root cause?
    - Does the story show the root cause being ADDRESSED by the mechanism?

  THE CRITICAL CHAIN:
    Root Cause → Mechanism Targets Root Cause → Results Follow

    The story must make this chain FEEL inevitable.
    If the root cause is absent, the mechanism feels arbitrary.
    If the mechanism doesn't target the root cause, the results feel unearned.

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Root cause is powerfully articulated in the story, mechanism directly addresses it,
        and the prospect understands WHY this mechanism produces results (because it targets
        the REAL problem)
    8-9: Strong root cause connection -- the link between mechanism and root cause is clear
    6-7: Moderate connection -- root cause mentioned but the mechanism-to-root-cause link
         could be stronger
    4-5: Weak connection -- root cause alluded to but mechanism's targeting of it is unclear
    2-3: Minimal connection -- root cause barely present, mechanism seems disconnected from
         the real problem
    1: No connection -- root cause absent from the story entirely

  RECORD: root_cause_connection_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: root_cause_evidence: string
```

### Step 4: Evaluate E5 Element 1 — Does the Story Make the Mechanism Feel NEW and DIFFERENT?

```
EVALUATE: Does the story make the mechanism feel genuinely novel?

  SCAN for narrative elements that communicate newness/differentiation:
    - Discovery context that positions the mechanism as previously unknown
    - Contrast with "what everyone else was doing" or "conventional wisdom"
    - Character reactions that communicate surprise or disbelief at the mechanism
    - Language that frames the mechanism as a departure from the expected
    - Specific reasons WHY this mechanism is different from alternatives

  THE KEY DISTINCTION:
    WEAK: The story SAYS the mechanism is new ("this was unlike anything I'd seen")
    STRONG: The story SHOWS the mechanism is new (protagonist tried everything else,
            all failed, then THIS specific thing worked because it approached the
            problem from an entirely different angle)

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: The mechanism's newness IS the story -- the narrative cannot exist without
        the mechanism being fundamentally different from everything else
    8-9: Strong differentiation shown through narrative events
    6-7: Newness communicated but through telling more than showing
    4-5: Mechanism is positioned as new but no narrative evidence supports the claim
    2-3: Mechanism feels like a variant of existing approaches within the story
    1: The story does nothing to differentiate the mechanism

  RECORD: e5_new_different_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: e5_new_different_evidence: string
```

### Step 5: Evaluate E5 Element 2 — Does the Story Make the Mechanism Feel SIMPLE?

```
EVALUATE: Does the story demonstrate the mechanism's simplicity?

  SCAN for narrative elements that show simplicity:
    - Protagonist's experience of using/applying the mechanism being straightforward
    - A specific simple step or action highlighted in the narrative
    - Absence of complexity barriers (no special equipment, rare ingredients, advanced skill)
    - For proof stories: "ordinary people" successfully using the method
    - Character expressions of surprise at how simple it is
    - "Step 1, Step 2, Step 3" type simplicity demonstration

  THE KEY DISTINCTION:
    WEAK: The story SAYS it's simple ("it was surprisingly easy")
    STRONG: The story SHOWS it's simple (protagonist did ONE specific thing
            and it started working -- reader can see themselves doing it)

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: The mechanism's simplicity is demonstrated through specific narrative action --
        the reader thinks "I could do that right now"
    8-9: Strong simplicity shown through protagonist's experience
    6-7: Simplicity mentioned with some narrative support
    4-5: Claimed but not demonstrated -- "it was easy" without showing easiness
    2-3: The story makes the mechanism sound complicated
    1: Simplicity absent -- mechanism sounds difficult or inaccessible

  RECORD: e5_simple_easy_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: e5_simple_easy_evidence: string
```

### Step 6: Evaluate E5 Element 3 — Does the Story Show QUICK Results?

```
EVALUATE: Does the story show the mechanism producing results quickly?

  SCAN for narrative elements that communicate speed:
    - Time markers showing when results began appearing
    - Speed of initial change (not full transformation -- INITIAL signals)
    - Protagonist's surprise at the speed of response
    - For proof stories: early data points showing quick results
    - Contrast with how long other approaches took

  THE KEY DISTINCTION:
    WEAK: "Results came quickly" (told)
    STRONG: "By the third morning, I noticed [specific change]" (shown with timeline)

  CRITICAL CHECK:
    Does the story show INITIAL results fast (correct) or FULL results fast (overclaim)?
    - CORRECT: "Within the first week, the pain started to fade"
    - OVERCLAIM: "Within the first week, I was completely healed"

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Specific timeline showing rapid initial results, demonstrated through narrative
        events with sensory detail
    8-9: Clear speed signals with credible timeframe shown in the story
    6-7: Speed mentioned with some narrative support
    4-5: Speed implied but no specific timeline
    2-3: Story suggests a long process
    1: Speed absent -- no timeline or pace indicators

  RECORD: e5_works_quickly_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: e5_works_quickly_evidence: string
  RECORD: initial_vs_full_results: enum[initial_correctly, overclaims_full, ambiguous]
```

### Step 7: Evaluate E5 Element 4 — Does the Story Show RELIABLE/PREDICTABLE Results?

```
EVALUATE: Does the story communicate that results are consistent and safe?

  SCAN for narrative elements that communicate reliability:
    - Multiple people getting results (for proof/experiment stories)
    - Consistency language ("every time," "without fail," "reliably")
    - Safety signals (no side effects, no risks mentioned or risks mitigated)
    - For proof stories: the breadth of success across participants
    - Transparency elements (acknowledging variability while showing pattern)
    - The Makepeace "ordinariness proof" -- "ordinary people just like you and me"

  THE KEY DISTINCTION:
    WEAK: "It works reliably" (told)
    STRONG: Multiple examples in the narrative showing consistent results across
            different people/situations (shown)

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Story demonstrates consistent results across multiple data points or characters --
        the reader feels this is a reliable, safe path (brain categorizes as "safe")
    8-9: Strong reliability signals woven into the narrative
    6-7: Some reliability mentioned with moderate narrative support
    4-5: One example of success but no pattern of reliability
    2-3: Story makes the mechanism feel experimental or risky
    1: Reliability absent -- mechanism feels unreliable or dangerous

  RECORD: e5_predictable_reliable_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: e5_predictable_reliable_evidence: string
```

### Step 8: Verify Proof-Claims Match

```
EVALUATE: Do the story's proof elements match the mechanism's claims?

  EXTRACT mechanism claims from mechanism_package:
    - mechanism.uniqueness_claim
    - E5 elements (what is claimed about newness, simplicity, speed, reliability)

  EXTRACT story proof from the narrative:
    - What results does the story show?
    - What claims does the story make (through events, dialogue, or narration)?
    - What evidence does the story present?

  CHECK ALIGNMENT:
    FOR each mechanism claim:
      - Is this claim supported by story evidence?
      - Does the story SHOW this claim being true (not just assert it)?
      - Is the proof proportional to the claim?
        (extraordinary claims require extraordinary evidence)
      - Is there any CONTRADICTION between mechanism claims and story events?

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Every mechanism claim is supported by specific story evidence --
        the story IS the proof of the mechanism's claims
    8-9: Strong alignment -- major claims supported with narrative evidence
    6-7: Moderate alignment -- most claims supported but some gaps
    4-5: Partial alignment -- some claims supported, others unsupported or contradicted
    2-3: Weak alignment -- story evidence doesn't match mechanism claims
    1: Misalignment -- story actually contradicts or undermines mechanism claims

  FLAG any contradictions:
    - Mechanism claims speed, story shows slow process
    - Mechanism claims simplicity, story shows complexity
    - Mechanism claims novelty, story shows conventional approach
    - Mechanism claims reliability, story shows inconsistent results

  RECORD: proof_claims_match_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: proof_claims_evidence: string
  RECORD: contradictions: array (if any)
```

### Step 9: Verify Logical Thread from Mechanism to Results

```
EVALUATE: Is there a clear, logical narrative thread connecting the mechanism to the results?

  TRACE the logical chain through the story:
    1. Problem/situation established → [CLEAR?]
    2. Root cause identified or revealed → [CLEAR?]
    3. Mechanism introduced/discovered → [CLEAR?]
    4. Mechanism addresses root cause → [CLEAR?]
    5. Results begin appearing → [CLEAR?]
    6. Results build/compound → [CLEAR?]
    7. Transformation/payoff achieved → [CLEAR?]

  FOR each link in the chain:
    - Is the transition EXPLICIT or ASSUMED?
    - Does the reader understand WHY each step leads to the next?
    - Are there any logical gaps where the reader must "fill in" missing connections?
    - Does the narrative EARN each progression (no magical leaps)?

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: The logical thread is unbroken and inevitable -- each step leads naturally
        to the next, and the results feel like the only possible outcome of the mechanism
    8-9: Strong thread with minor gaps that don't break the chain
    6-7: Adequate thread but some leaps require the reader to assume connections
    4-5: Thread present but fragmented -- the reader can follow it but must work to connect dots
    2-3: Weak thread -- the connection between mechanism and results is unclear
    1: No thread -- mechanism and results exist in the story but aren't connected

  RECORD: logical_thread_score: integer[1-10]
  RECORD: thread_evidence: string
  RECORD: gap_locations: array (where the thread breaks, if any)
```

### Step 10: Apply the Mechanism Swap Test

```
THE DEFINITIVE ALIGNMENT TEST:

  Mentally replace the mechanism in the story with a DIFFERENT mechanism.
  For example:
    - If the mechanism is an enzyme, replace it with a different supplement
    - If the mechanism is a trading algorithm, replace it with a different strategy
    - If the mechanism is a golf technique, replace it with a different method

  ASK: Does the story still work?

  IF YES — the story still works with a different mechanism:
    → ALIGNMENT IS WEAK
    → The story is generic and could serve as a vehicle for any mechanism
    → The mechanism is superficially attached, not structurally embedded
    → This is a FAIL condition

  IF NO — the story collapses without this specific mechanism:
    → ALIGNMENT IS STRONG
    → The narrative DEPENDS on this mechanism's specific properties
    → The discovery/proof/transformation is inseparable from the mechanism
    → This is a PASS condition

  IF PARTIALLY — some parts would work, some wouldn't:
    → ALIGNMENT IS MODERATE
    → Identify which parts are mechanism-specific and which are generic
    → The generic parts need strengthening

  SCORING CRITERIA:
    10: Story completely collapses without this mechanism -- every beat depends on it
    8-9: Story would lose most of its power without this mechanism
    6-7: Story would lose some power but could partially function with a different mechanism
    4-5: Story would work at 50%+ capacity with a different mechanism swapped in
    2-3: Story would work almost as well with any mechanism
    1: Story is entirely mechanism-agnostic -- a template with a mechanism label

RECORD: mechanism_swap_test: enum[STORY_COLLAPSES, STORY_WEAKENED, STORY_SURVIVES]
RECORD: swap_test_score: integer[1-10]
RECORD: mechanism_specific_elements: array (parts of the story that are mechanism-specific)
RECORD: mechanism_generic_elements: array (parts that could work with any mechanism)
```

### Step 11: Calculate Overall Alignment Score

```
overall_alignment_score = WEIGHTED AVERAGE:
  mechanism_naming × 0.10
  mechanism_demonstration × 0.15
  root_cause_connection × 0.15
  e5_new_different × 0.10
  e5_simple_easy × 0.08
  e5_works_quickly × 0.08
  e5_predictable_reliable × 0.08
  proof_claims_match × 0.10
  logical_thread × 0.06
  mechanism_swap_test × 0.10

GATE DECISION:
  IF mechanism_swap_test = STORY_SURVIVES → FAIL (regardless of other scores)
  IF mechanism_named_explicitly = false → FAIL (mechanism must be named)
  IF overall_alignment_score >= 7.0 AND mechanism_swap_test != STORY_SURVIVES → PASS
  IF overall_alignment_score >= 5.0 AND < 7.0 → PASS_WITH_WARNINGS
  IF overall_alignment_score < 5.0 → FAIL

NOTE: The mechanism swap test is an ABSOLUTE gate. If the story survives
a mechanism swap, the alignment is structurally insufficient regardless of
individual dimension scores.
```

### Step 12: Generate Remediation Notes (If Not PASS)

```
IF gate_result != PASS:

  FOR each dimension scoring below 7.0:
    GENERATE specific remediation:
      - Which alignment dimension is weak
      - What the story SHOULD demonstrate about the mechanism at that point
      - What specific narrative element needs strengthening
      - Which upstream microskill should re-execute
      - Suggested direction (without writing the copy)

  IF mechanism_swap_test = STORY_SURVIVES:
    GENERATE structural remediation:
      - List the mechanism_generic_elements that need to become mechanism-specific
      - For each generic element, suggest HOW to tie it to this specific mechanism
      - Identify which Layer 2 sections need the most mechanism-specific revision
      - NOTE: This is often a 2.1 (setup), 2.2 (discovery), or 2.3 (revelation) problem

  IF mechanism_named_explicitly = false:
    GENERATE naming remediation:
      - Where in the story should the mechanism be named
      - How the name should be introduced (through dialogue, narration, or discovery)
      - The name should feel EARNED by the narrative, not dropped in

  FOR E5 ELEMENT FAILURES (any element scoring below 6.0):
    GENERATE element-specific remediation:
      - Which E5 element is weak in the story
      - How the story should SHOW (not tell) this element
      - Reference to the E5 framework's guidance for this element
      - Which story beat should carry this element
```

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Mechanism name searched for in story text -- explicit naming verified
- [ ] All 9 alignment dimensions scored independently with specific text evidence
- [ ] No dimension score assigned without a quoted text reference
- [ ] Root cause from root_cause_package compared against story content
- [ ] All four E5 elements evaluated for SHOWN vs. TOLD distinction
- [ ] Works Quickly element checked for initial vs. full results overclaim
- [ ] Proof-claims match checked for contradictions between mechanism claims and story evidence
- [ ] Logical thread traced from problem through mechanism to results -- gaps identified
- [ ] Mechanism swap test applied as definitive alignment check
- [ ] STORY_SURVIVES swap test triggers automatic FAIL regardless of other scores
- [ ] Overall alignment score calculated with correct weighting
- [ ] Remediation notes generated for every dimension scoring below 7.0
- [ ] Mechanism-generic elements identified for strengthening if swap test is not STORY_COLLAPSES

## Anti-Patterns

- **Name-presence conflation:** Rating mechanism_naming high because the mechanism name appears once in passing -- naming must be MEANINGFUL within the narrative, not just present
- **Told-is-shown confusion:** Accepting "it was an amazing new discovery" as demonstrating the New and Different element -- the story must SHOW newness through narrative events, not just TELL the reader it's new
- **Generous root cause scoring:** Rating root_cause_connection high because the problem is mentioned -- the ROOT CAUSE (specific, three-part structure from root_cause_package) must be articulated, not just the surface problem
- **Swap test leniency:** Passing a story that would "mostly" work with a different mechanism -- the standard is structural dependence, not partial association. If 50%+ of the story survives a swap, alignment is weak.
- **E5 checkbox syndrome:** Marking E5 elements as present because related words appear -- each element must be demonstrated through narrative action, not just mentioned
- **Contradiction blindness:** Overlooking cases where the story's events contradict the mechanism's claims (e.g., mechanism claims simplicity but the story shows a complex process)
- **Template excuse:** Accepting generic alignment because "all discovery stories follow the same format" -- the FORMAT may be shared, but the mechanism-specific DETAILS must be unique to this mechanism

## Handoff

Pass `mechanism_story_alignment` to:
- **4.5-final-story-assembler.md** (required -- feeds mechanism_alignment score and e5_elements_in_story into story-package.json)
- **STORY-AGENT.md** (Gate 4 evaluation -- mechanism_swap_test must not be STORY_SURVIVES)
- If FAIL → remediation routes based on weakest dimensions:
  - Naming failure → back to **2.3-mechanism-revelation-constructor.md** (mechanism must be named in revelation)
  - Demonstration failure → back to **2.2-discovery-sequence-builder.md** (discovery must show mechanism in action)
  - Root cause failure → back to **2.3-mechanism-revelation-constructor.md** (revelation must connect to root cause)
  - E5 element failures → back to **2.1-2.4** depending on which section carries the weak element
  - Swap test failure → systemic revision of **Layer 2** (mechanism must be embedded throughout)
