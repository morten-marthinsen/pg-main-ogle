# Layer 4.1 — Story Function Validator

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Type:** Persuasion Function Gate
**Dependencies:** full draft story text (from Layer 3 output), story_classification (from 1.1), story_beats (from 1.2), emotional_arc (from 1.4), carlton_compliance (from 3.4), mechanism_package (from 0.1 upstream), promise_package (from 0.1 upstream), teaching_index (from 0.3)
**Output:** story_function_validation.json

---

## Purpose

Validate that the engineered story achieves its core persuasion function. Every story in copy exists to SELL -- not to entertain. A story that entertains but does not persuade is a failure. A story that persuades but does not entertain is merely suboptimal. The story's persuasion function varies by type, but the terminal outcome is always the same: the prospect must arrive at "This mechanism is REAL and it WORKS. I need this." If the prospect finishes the story thinking "That was a nice story," the function has NOT been achieved. This validator evaluates persuasion function across 10 dimensions using a type-specific scorecard, then applies the definitive binary test: did the story SELL?

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Identify the Story's Assigned Persuasion Function

```
DETERMINE the persuasion function based on story_classification.story_type:

  IF story_type = "standard_discovery" OR "accidental_discovery" OR "straight_to_expert":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Make the mechanism feel like a real discovery --
                        not a manufactured product pitch. The prospect must believe
                        the mechanism was genuinely FOUND, not INVENTED for marketing."
    FUNCTION_ID: "discovery_authenticity"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "This was really discovered. This isn't made up."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "This sounds like a marketing story."

  IF story_type = "proof_story_live_experiment":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Make the mechanism's results feel documented and undeniable.
                        The prospect must feel they are reviewing EVIDENCE, not hearing
                        a pitch. The data cascade must feel like a lab report, not a sales script."
    FUNCTION_ID: "proof_documentation"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "The results are right there. You can't argue with data."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "These numbers sound made up."

  IF story_type = "origin_story":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Make the product feel like it has authentic roots. The prospect
                        must feel the product/method emerged from genuine passion, genuine
                        need, or genuine expertise -- not from a desire to sell."
    FUNCTION_ID: "origin_authenticity"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "I understand WHY this exists. It's real."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "This is a manufactured backstory."

  IF story_type = "transformation_story":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Make the prospect feel the results are achievable for THEM.
                        The transformation must feel so vivid and so relatable that the
                        prospect projects themselves into the protagonist's outcome."
    FUNCTION_ID: "transformation_projection"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "If they did it, I can do it. I want this."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "Good for them. But that's them, not me."

  IF story_type = "revelation_story":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Make the prospect feel they are receiving insider knowledge
                        that most people do not have access to. The revelation must feel
                        privileged, almost forbidden."
    FUNCTION_ID: "insider_revelation"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "I'm learning something most people never find out."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "I've heard this kind of thing before."

  IF story_type = "warning_story":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Create urgency through consequence. The prospect must feel
                        the danger of NOT acting -- the cost of inaction must feel real
                        and imminent."
    FUNCTION_ID: "consequence_urgency"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "I can't afford to ignore this."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "That's fearmongering."

  IF story_type = "underdog_vulnerability":
    ASSIGNED_FUNCTION: "Create identification through shared vulnerability. The prospect
                        must feel 'that was ME' -- the protagonist's shame, struggle, and
                        eventual triumph must mirror the prospect's internal experience."
    FUNCTION_ID: "vulnerability_identification"
    SUCCESS_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "They understand exactly what I'm going through."
    FAILURE_SIGNAL: Prospect thinks "That's a sob story to manipulate me."

  IF story_type = "hybrid":
    IDENTIFY the primary and secondary functions from the hybrid components
    EVALUATE both functions (primary weighted 0.70, secondary weighted 0.30)

RECORD:
  - assigned_function: string
  - function_id: string
  - success_signal: string
  - failure_signal: string
```

### Step 2: Read the Story as a Skeptical Prospect

```
ASSUME the perspective of a real prospect:
  - They have been reading the campaign from the lead onward
  - The lead created emotional interest and teased the story
  - They are now reading the story to VALIDATE what the lead promised
  - They are NOT pre-sold -- they are emotionally interested but logically unconvinced
  - They have been burned by similar promises before
  - They will detect manipulation and artificiality instantly
  - They are HOPING this is real but EXPECTING it to be fake

READ the full_draft_story.full_text from beginning to end
in one continuous pass -- do NOT analyze sentence by sentence yet.

AFTER the full read, record FIRST IMPRESSION:
  - What is the prospect's emotional state at the END of the story?
  - Do they believe the mechanism is REAL?
  - Do they believe the results are ACHIEVABLE for them?
  - Do they want to keep reading the campaign?
  - Critical: did they think "nice story" or "I need this"?

RECORD: prospect_state_at_end: string (free-form emotional assessment)
RECORD: first_impression_function_achieved: boolean (gut check)
```

### Step 3: Score Dimension 1 -- Mechanism Believability

```
EVALUATE: Does the story make the mechanism feel REAL?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The mechanism feels as real as gravity -- the prospect cannot imagine it NOT being real
  8-9: Strong believability -- the discovery/proof/origin feels genuine and authentic
  6-7: Moderate believability -- the prospect is willing to consider it but has reservations
  4-5: Weak believability -- the mechanism feels like a marketing construct with a story wrapped around it
  2-3: Poor believability -- the story actually undermines the mechanism's credibility
  1: Zero believability -- the story reads as fiction and the prospect dismisses the mechanism

KEY SIGNALS:
  - Specific details that could not be fabricated (names, places, dates, circumstances)
  - Discovery/proof process that follows a logical chain
  - Obstacles and setbacks that make the journey feel real (not conveniently smooth)
  - The mechanism emerges NATURALLY from the narrative, not inserted as a product pitch
  - No "and then, miraculously..." moments that strain credulity

RECORD: mechanism_believability: integer[1-10]
RECORD: believability_evidence: string (quote specific text that supports or undermines)
```

### Step 4: Score Dimension 2 -- Emotional Transportation

```
EVALUATE: Does the story TRANSPORT the prospect into the experience?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect forgets they are reading a campaign -- they are IN the story
  8-9: Strong transportation -- vivid, immersive, emotionally gripping
  6-7: Moderate transportation -- engaged but aware they are reading marketing
  4-5: Weak transportation -- reading facts about a story rather than experiencing it
  2-3: Poor transportation -- the story is a sequence of events with no immersion
  1: Zero transportation -- clinical, flat, could be a product manual

KEY SIGNALS (Carlton "transporting" principle):
  - Sensory details that place the reader IN the scene (not just told about it)
  - Emotional states shown through action and detail, not told through labels
  - Pacing that creates breathlessness -- the reader is afraid to miss a detail
  - Dialogue that sounds real, not scripted
  - The reader can SEE, HEAR, FEEL the story's world

RECORD: emotional_transportation: integer[1-10]
RECORD: transportation_evidence: string
```

### Step 5: Score Dimension 3 -- Protagonist Identification

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect see THEMSELVES in the protagonist?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect IS the protagonist -- their situation, fears, and hopes are identical
  8-9: Strong identification -- the prospect sees deep parallels with their own life
  6-7: Moderate identification -- the prospect relates generally but not specifically
  4-5: Weak identification -- the protagonist feels like a different kind of person
  2-3: Poor identification -- the protagonist is so different that the story feels irrelevant
  1: Zero identification -- the prospect cannot connect to the protagonist at all

KEY SIGNALS:
  - Protagonist's starting situation mirrors the prospect's current reality
  - Protagonist's fears match the prospect's fears
  - Protagonist's failed attempts match what the prospect has tried
  - Protagonist is NOT too perfect, too special, or too lucky
  - The "ordinariness" element is present (for proof stories: "ordinary people just like you")
  - Prospect thinks "That could be me" not "That's a character in a story"

RECORD: protagonist_identification: integer[1-10]
RECORD: identification_evidence: string
```

### Step 6: Score Dimension 4 -- Result Achievability

```
EVALUATE: Does the prospect believe THEY could get similar results?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect is certain they can achieve similar results -- the path feels clear and accessible
  8-9: Strong achievability -- results feel realistic and within reach
  6-7: Moderate achievability -- results sound possible but the prospect has doubts about their own ability
  4-5: Weak achievability -- results seem real for the protagonist but unlikely for the prospect
  2-3: Poor achievability -- results seem exceptional and not replicable
  1: Zero achievability -- results feel impossible or limited to special circumstances

KEY SIGNALS:
  - Results are specific enough to be credible but not so extreme they strain belief
  - The mechanism's simplicity is demonstrated in the story (E5 element: Simple and Easy)
  - The speed of results matches what the lead promised (E5 element: Works Quickly)
  - Multiple data points or examples suggest consistency (E5 element: Predictable and Reliable)
  - The "and here's what that means for YOU" segue explicitly connects the story to the prospect
  - No barriers to entry visible (special equipment, rare access, extreme skill)

RECORD: result_achievability: integer[1-10]
RECORD: achievability_evidence: string
```

### Step 7: Score Dimension 5 -- Emotional Arc Completion

```
EVALUATE: Does the emotional arc reach its designed endpoint?

COMPARE designed arc (from 1.4) against the actual emotional journey in the story:

  DESIGNED ARC:
    - Starting state: FROM emotional_arc.starting_state
    - Ending state: FROM emotional_arc.ending_state (must include "believes mechanism is real")
    - Peak moment: FROM emotional_arc.peak_moment
    - Arc shape: FROM emotional_arc.arc_shape

  ACTUAL ARC (assessed from reading):
    - What is the actual starting emotional state?
    - What is the actual ending emotional state?
    - Where does the actual peak occur?
    - Does the actual arc match the designed shape?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: Arc perfectly executed -- prospect travels the entire emotional journey as designed
  8-9: Strong arc -- all major emotional beats hit, ending state achieved
  6-7: Moderate arc -- emotional movement present but some designed beats missed
  4-5: Weak arc -- some emotional movement but ending state not fully achieved
  2-3: Poor arc -- emotional journey is flat or goes in the wrong direction
  1: No arc -- the story has no discernible emotional progression

CRITICAL CHECK:
  Does the ending state include "believes mechanism is real"?
  IF NO -- the arc has failed its fundamental purpose regardless of score.

RECORD: emotional_arc_completion: integer[1-10]
RECORD: arc_alignment: enum[aligned, partially_aligned, misaligned]
RECORD: arc_evidence: string
```

### Step 8: Score Dimension 6 -- Proof Integration Naturalness

```
EVALUATE: Are proof elements woven naturally into the story?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: Proof is invisible -- the reader absorbs evidence through the narrative without
      realizing they are being "proved to"
  8-9: Strong integration -- proof emerges from the story's events naturally
  6-7: Moderate integration -- proof is present and mostly flows with the narrative
  4-5: Weak integration -- proof feels like it was inserted into the story after the fact
  2-3: Poor integration -- proof elements break the narrative flow (reader feels they
       left the story and entered a sales pitch)
  1: Bolted on -- proof is entirely disconnected from the narrative

KEY SIGNALS:
  - Data points emerge from the protagonist's experience (not from an omniscient narrator)
  - Credibility elements are part of the character (doctor's credentials, researcher's lab)
  - Results are SHOWN through the story's events, not TOLD through exposition
  - For proof stories: the data cascade feels like a natural progression of the experiment
  - No jarring shift from "story mode" to "proof mode"

RECORD: proof_integration: integer[1-10]
RECORD: proof_evidence: string
```

### Step 9: Score Dimension 7 -- Discovery/Revelation Power

```
EVALUATE: How powerful is the discovery/revelation moment?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The discovery moment is an emotional thunderclap -- the reader's worldview shifts
  8-9: Powerful revelation -- the reader feels genuine surprise or awe
  6-7: Adequate revelation -- the moment works but doesn't create a visceral response
  4-5: Weak revelation -- the discovery/revelation feels anticlimactic
  2-3: Poor revelation -- the moment is flat and fails to create any emotional response
  1: Missing -- there is no clear discovery/revelation moment

TYPE-SPECIFIC EVALUATION:
  Discovery stories: Does the mechanism reveal feel like a genuine eureka moment?
  Proof stories: Does the data cascade create escalating amazement?
  Origin stories: Does the origin feel like it reveals the product's true purpose?
  Transformation stories: Does the transformation feel like a genuine before/after shift?
  Revelation stories: Does the insider knowledge feel genuinely secret?
  Warning stories: Does the consequence feel genuinely alarming?

RECORD: discovery_revelation_power: integer[1-10]
RECORD: revelation_evidence: string
```

### Step 10: Score Dimension 8 -- Urgency Generation

```
EVALUATE: Does the story create urgency to continue reading and eventually ACT?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect feels they MUST act now -- delay feels dangerous or costly
  8-9: Strong urgency -- the prospect is compelled to continue and eager to find the solution
  6-7: Moderate urgency -- interested in continuing but no sense of time pressure
  4-5: Mild urgency -- would continue reading but could also stop
  2-3: Weak urgency -- the prospect has no reason to prioritize this
  1: Zero urgency -- the prospect could take it or leave it

KEY SIGNALS:
  - The story creates consequences for inaction (explicit or implicit)
  - Open loops from the story demand closure (what happened next?)
  - The transformation/results create desire strong enough to drive action
  - For warning stories: the danger feels imminent and personal
  - The "what that means for YOU" segue converts story urgency into personal urgency

RECORD: urgency_generation: integer[1-10]
RECORD: urgency_evidence: string
```

### Step 11: Score Dimension 9 -- Anti-Manipulation Radar Evasion

```
EVALUATE: Does the story avoid triggering the prospect's "I'm being manipulated" detector?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect has zero awareness they are in a sales funnel -- the story feels
      like genuine sharing of a genuine experience
  8-9: Very low manipulation radar -- the story feels authentic with perhaps one moment
       of slight awareness
  6-7: Moderate radar -- the prospect occasionally notices the persuasion architecture
  4-5: Elevated radar -- the prospect can feel the machinery behind the story
  2-3: High radar -- the story reads as a transparent sales device
  1: Full alarm -- the prospect feels insulted by the manipulation attempt

KEY SIGNALS OF EVASION:
  - No "stock character" archetypes (the Yoda character has specific personality, not generic wisdom)
  - No "convenient coincidence" chains (events feel earned, not orchestrated)
  - Transparency elements present (admitting limitations, acknowledging skepticism)
  - Dialogue sounds like real people, not sales scripts
  - The narrative has genuine texture and specificity (not template fill-in)
  - Story-to-sales transitions are smooth (Carlton's "and here's what that means for YOU")

KEY SIGNALS OF RADAR TRIGGER:
  - Template-obvious structure ("Let me tell you about John. John was just like you...")
  - Artificial emotion ("tears streamed down his face as he realized...")
  - Perfect protagonist journey with no genuine obstacles
  - Fortune-cookie wisdom from authority characters
  - Results that feel inflated or too perfect

RECORD: anti_manipulation_evasion: integer[1-10]
RECORD: evasion_evidence: string
```

### Step 12: Score Dimension 10 -- Downstream Momentum

```
EVALUATE: Does the story create momentum for the campaign argument and close?

SCORING CRITERIA:
  10: The prospect is leaning forward, hungry for the next section -- the story has
      created belief AND desire that demands logical proof and offer details
  8-9: Strong momentum -- the prospect actively wants to continue the campaign
  6-7: Adequate momentum -- the prospect will continue but without urgency
  4-5: Weak momentum -- the story resolves itself too completely (no forward pull)
  2-3: Poor momentum -- the prospect feels the story was self-contained and doesn't
       need to read further
  1: Negative momentum -- the story pushed the prospect away from the campaign

KEY SIGNALS:
  - Open loops from the story that demand closure downstream
  - Belief gaps created (emotional conviction without logical proof yet)
  - "And here's what that means for YOU" segue launches into the next section
  - The prospect's emotional state is "I believe this works -- now show me how to get it"
  - Story callbacks are available for body copy and close to reference

RECORD: downstream_momentum: integer[1-10]
RECORD: momentum_evidence: string
```

### Step 13: Apply the Definitive Binary Test

```
THE DEFINITIVE TEST:

Read the full story one final time.

At the end, ask THE question:
  "After reading this story, does the prospect FEEL:

   'This mechanism is REAL and it WORKS. I need this.'

   OR do they think:

   'That was a nice story.'"

This is a YES/NO answer. There is no middle ground.

IF "This mechanism is REAL and it WORKS. I need this.":
  - function_achieved = true
  - The story has done its job
  - The campaign argument can now provide logical proof
  - The close can now present the offer

IF "That was a nice story.":
  - function_achieved = false
  - DIAGNOSE the failure:
    a. Entertainment without persuasion (story was enjoyable but didn't SELL)
    b. Mechanism disconnection (story was good but mechanism felt irrelevant)
    c. Protagonist distance (story was good but prospect didn't see themselves)
    d. Result abstraction (results were mentioned but not FELT)
    e. Proof insufficiency (story didn't provide enough evidence to create belief)
    f. Manipulation detection (prospect noticed the sales architecture and disengaged)

RECORD: definitive_test: enum[FUNCTION_ACHIEVED, FUNCTION_NOT_ACHIEVED]
RECORD: failure_diagnosis: string (if not achieved -- which failure mode)
```

### Step 14: Calculate Overall Function Score

```
overall_function_score = WEIGHTED AVERAGE:
  mechanism_believability × 0.15
  emotional_transportation × 0.10
  protagonist_identification × 0.10
  result_achievability × 0.15
  emotional_arc_completion × 0.10
  proof_integration × 0.10
  discovery_revelation_power × 0.10
  urgency_generation × 0.05
  anti_manipulation_evasion × 0.10
  downstream_momentum × 0.05

GATE DECISION:
  IF definitive_test = FUNCTION_NOT_ACHIEVED → FAIL (regardless of scores)
  IF overall_function_score >= 7.0 AND definitive_test = FUNCTION_ACHIEVED → PASS
  IF overall_function_score >= 5.0 AND < 7.0 AND definitive_test = FUNCTION_ACHIEVED → PASS_WITH_WARNINGS
  IF overall_function_score < 5.0 → FAIL (story is below persuasion threshold)

NOTE: The definitive test is an ABSOLUTE gate. A story can score 9.0 on dimensions
but if the gestalt effect is "nice story" rather than "I need this," the function
has not been achieved.
```

### Step 15: Generate Remediation Notes (If Not PASS)

```
IF gate_result != PASS:

  FOR each dimension scoring below 7.0:
    GENERATE specific remediation:
      - Which persuasion dimension is weak
      - What the prospect SHOULD feel at that point
      - What specific story element is missing or underperforming
      - Which upstream microskill should re-execute (1.x, 2.x, or 3.x)
      - Suggested direction for the fix (without writing the copy)

  IF definitive_test = FUNCTION_NOT_ACHIEVED:
    DIAGNOSE using the failure mode categories (a-f from Step 13)
    GENERATE specific diagnosis:
      - Primary failure mode identified
      - Secondary failure modes contributing
      - Root cause analysis (is this a Layer 1 architecture problem,
        Layer 2 construction problem, or Layer 3 refinement problem?)
      - Recommended remediation path (which layer to revisit)

  FOR TYPE-SPECIFIC FAILURES:
    IF discovery_authenticity failed → revisit 2.1, 2.2 (setup and discovery sequence)
    IF proof_documentation failed → revisit 2.2, 2.4 (data cascade and results)
    IF origin_authenticity failed → revisit 2.1 (setup and context)
    IF transformation_projection failed → revisit 2.4, 1.3 (payoff and character design)
    IF insider_revelation failed → revisit 2.3 (mechanism revelation)
    IF consequence_urgency failed → revisit 2.4 (payoff and consequences)
    IF vulnerability_identification failed → revisit 1.3, 2.1 (character and setup)
```

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Story type identified and correct persuasion function assigned
- [ ] Full story read as a skeptical prospect in one continuous pass before dimension scoring
- [ ] All 10 persuasion dimensions scored independently with specific text evidence
- [ ] No dimension score assigned without a quoted text reference as evidence
- [ ] Emotional arc compared against designed arc from 1.4 -- alignment assessed
- [ ] Definitive binary test applied AFTER all dimension scoring -- not derived from scores
- [ ] FUNCTION_NOT_ACHIEVED triggers automatic FAIL regardless of dimension scores
- [ ] Overall function score calculated with correct weighting (0.15/0.10/0.10/0.15/0.10/0.10/0.10/0.05/0.10/0.05)
- [ ] Type-specific persuasion function evaluated (not generic "does it work")
- [ ] Remediation notes generated for every dimension scoring below 7.0
- [ ] Failure diagnosis identifies primary failure mode when definitive test fails
- [ ] Root cause layer identified for remediation routing

## Anti-Patterns

- **Entertainment scoring:** Rating the story highly because it is enjoyable to read -- entertainment is necessary but not sufficient. The question is not "is this a good story?" but "does this story SELL?"
- **Function confusion:** Evaluating a discovery story on transformation metrics, or a proof story on vulnerability metrics -- each story type has its own assigned function and must be scored against THAT function
- **Generous believability:** Rating mechanism believability high because the story mentions real-seeming details -- believability requires the ENTIRE narrative chain to feel authentic, not just individual details
- **Identification inflation:** Rating protagonist identification high because the protagonist is in the same demographic as the prospect -- identification requires EMOTIONAL mirroring, not just demographic matching
- **Definitive test derivation:** Computing the definitive test result from the dimension scores rather than applying it as an independent gestalt assessment -- the definitive test is a separate, holistic evaluation
- **Downstream blindness:** Ignoring downstream momentum because "the story's job is done" -- the story exists within a campaign and must propel the prospect forward, not resolve everything internally
- **Manipulation dismissal:** Ignoring anti-manipulation radar signals because "all sales copy is persuasion" -- the prospect's manipulation detector is real and must be evaded, not justified
