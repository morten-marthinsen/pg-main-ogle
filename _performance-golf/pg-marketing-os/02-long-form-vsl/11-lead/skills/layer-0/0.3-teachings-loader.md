## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Index E5 Campaign Lead Framework

From M4D, extract:
- **Definition:** First 350-800 words turning attention into engagement
- **Purpose:** Make the EMOTIONAL sale, not the logical/proof sale
- **Elevator pitch concept:** 45 seconds to pitch the unique mechanism
- **Don't bury the lead:** Most exciting content in first 350-400 words
- **Target response:** "This sounds amazing. This sounds perfect. Now prove it works for me."

**Four Core E5 Elements:**

| Element | What to Communicate | Key Principle |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| New and Different | HOW and WHY it's new and different | "New and different is better than better" |
| Simple and Easy | HOW and WHY it's simple — even one aspect | May showcase just one simple step |
| Works Quickly | Results begin fast (not full results fast) | Speed of initial results, not complete delivery |
| Predictable and Reliable | Consistency and safety | Brain categorizes safe vs. dangerous — reliability = safety |

**Elevator Pitch INCLUDES:** Benefits, primary promise, additional benefits, speed, ease, reliability, predictable results
**Elevator Pitch EXCLUDES:** Features, science, logical proof, discovery story

### Step 2: Index Stefan Georgi Lead Formula

From Week 14, extract:

**DO List (7 items):**
1. Call out problem(s)
2. Promise solution to problem(s)
3. Hint at unique mechanism behind solution
4. Hint at surprising counter-intuitive information
5. Briefly insert credibility/authority elements
6. Briefly tease discovery story
7. Briefly call out and address skepticism

**DON'T List (4 items):**
1. Explain how the unique mechanism works in detail
2. Explain in detail why old solutions don't work
3. Go deeply into the discovery story
4. Explain what the solution/product is

**Core principle:** The ONLY purpose of a lead is to call out prospects, catch attention, draw them in to keep reading. Don't start explaining before you've hooked them (fishing bait trap).

### Step 3: Index Craig Clemens Vagueness Principle

From Week 14, extract:
- **Problem callouts:** Keep vague enough for reader to fill in their OWN experience
- **Bad example:** Too many specifics ("all the right curves," "store in the mall," "several good opening lines")
- **Good example:** Vague enough to relate ("ever WUSSED OUT when approaching someone")
- **Rule:** Save ultra-descriptive specific copy for RESULTS, not problems
- **Principle:** Reader relates through their own memory, not your prescribed scenario

### Step 4: Index Open Loop Psychology

From Week 14, extract:
- **Definition:** A tease at a compelling thing you're going to tell someone later
- **Psychology:** Brain is hardwired to HATE missing key information — powerful urge to "close" the loop
- **TV analogy:** Cliffhangers work because incomplete information creates irresistible pull
- **Primary loop target:** The "tangible tease" (trick, secret, 5 fruits, specific ingredient, etc.)

**Open Loop Types:**
1. **Tangible tease:** "a simple 5-second breakfast ritual..."
2. **Discovery tease:** "what the doctor explained next changed everything..."
3. **Result tease:** "within weeks, decades of fat buildup virtually erased..."
4. **Counter-intuitive tease:** "he told them to abandon their diets and eat as many carbs as they want..."
5. **Mechanism tease:** "this 'fat release switch' takes advantage of a biological loophole..."

**Forward Signaling:** Explicitly tell reader important information is coming ("This is vital," "Pay attention")
**Suspense:** Deliberately leaving reader unsure of vital elements

### Step 5: Index Real-World Lead Templates

From Week 14, catalog the annotated lead structures:
- **Montezuma's Secret** — Erectile dysfunction (problem → discovery tease → credibility → promise → counter-intuitive → mechanism hint → intrigue → newness → attention lock)
- **Veda Soothe** — Joint pain (credibility first → problem → discovery tease → counter-intuitive → mechanism hint → promise → newness → attention lock)
- **Pure-FX** — Weight loss (problem → promise → discovery tease → credibility → counter-intuitive → mechanism hint → root cause → attention lock)
