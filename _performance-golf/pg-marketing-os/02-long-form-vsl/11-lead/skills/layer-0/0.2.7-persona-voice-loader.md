### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `generative_full_draft` arena mode, prioritize **actual selling copy specimens** that show each writer's voice, vocabulary, rhythm, and persuasion techniques in the specific type of copy this skill generates.

For leads, prioritize the opening sections of each writer's controls and ads. The lead is the first 350-800 words — it must hook, tease, and create the emotional sale. Every specimen should be evaluated for how it OPENS: what device does it use to capture attention? How quickly does it establish the problem? How does it tease the mechanism without explaining it? Makepeace's controls have powerful, aggressive leads that establish stakes immediately. Schwartz's ads show sophisticated lead structures calibrated to market awareness levels. Ogilvy's leads are research-dense and credibility-first — they earn attention through specificity and authority. Halbert's newsletters have attention-grabbing openings that feel like a friend pulling you aside to share something urgent. Bencivenga's promo letters demonstrate the educational-approach lead — teaching something valuable that naturally opens the door to the sale.
