## Purpose

Verify complete compliance with Stefan Georgi's Lead Formula — all 7 DO items present, all 4 DON'T items absent. Zero tolerance for DON'T violations. The Georgi Formula defines the exact boundary of what a lead MUST contain and what a lead MUST NEVER contain. A single DON'T violation means the lead has crossed the line from teasing into telling, from hooking into explaining — and the prospect's urgency to keep reading collapses.

## The Georgi Principle

Stefan Georgi's Lead Formula defines the lead's ONLY purpose: **call out prospects, catch attention, draw them in.** The lead is BAIT — not the meal. The moment you start explaining, proving, or storytelling in detail, you have moved from bait to meal and the prospect has no reason to keep consuming.

**The Fishing Analogy:** You do not put the entire fish dinner on the hook. You put a bright, tantalizing lure. The lead is the lure. The campaign argument is the dinner.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: DO Checklist Verification (All 7 Must Be Present)

```
FOR each DO item, search the full_lead_text_draft for evidence of presence:

DO #1: Call Out Problem(s)
  SEARCH FOR: Sentences that identify, name, or describe the prospect's
              problem, pain, frustration, or undesirable current state.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one clear problem identification sentence.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "Yes, that's MY problem" upon reading?
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead does not call out the prospect's problem.
             The prospect has no reason to identify with the message."

DO #2: Promise Solution to Problem(s)
  SEARCH FOR: Sentences that promise the problem CAN be solved, that a
              solution EXISTS, that relief/transformation IS available.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one clear promise of solution.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "There IS a solution?" upon reading?
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead identifies a problem but never promises it's
             solvable. The prospect feels called out but has no hope."

DO #3: Hint at Unique Mechanism Behind Solution
  SEARCH FOR: References to the mechanism — by NAME, by type, by
              distinction from other approaches. HINT, not explain.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: The mechanism is NAMED or clearly referenced.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "There's something specific
                and different about this solution"?
  CRITICAL DISTINCTION: HINT = naming, teasing, distinguishing.
                        NOT hint = explaining HOW it works.
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead promises a solution but gives no indication
             of a unique mechanism. The prospect has no reason to believe
             this is different from everything else they've tried."

DO #4: Hint at Surprising Counter-Intuitive Information
  SEARCH FOR: Teases of surprising facts, counter-intuitive claims,
              "what you thought was wrong" signals, "shocking truth" references.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one counter-intuitive tease.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "Wait, really? That contradicts
                what I believed..."?
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead has no counter-intuitive element. The prospect
             is not surprised and has no curiosity gap to close."

DO #5: Briefly Insert Credibility/Authority Elements
  SEARCH FOR: Credential mentions, authority signals, social proof,
              institutional affiliations, track record references.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one credibility signal.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "OK, this person/source has
                some authority to make this claim"?
  CRITICAL DISTINCTION: BRIEFLY = 1-2 sentences. NOT brief = a paragraph
                        of credentials, detailed track record, expanded proof.
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  BREVITY CHECK: Is the credibility element ≤ 2 sentences?
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead makes claims with zero credibility backing.
             The prospect's skepticism is unaddressed."

DO #6: Briefly Tease Discovery Story
  SEARCH FOR: References to how the mechanism/solution was discovered,
              origin story hints, "stumbled upon" / "discovered" / "found" language.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one story tease.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "How did they find this?"
  CRITICAL DISTINCTION: TEASE = a sentence or fragment hinting at the story.
                        NOT tease = actually telling the discovery story.
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  BREVITY CHECK: Is the story reference ≤ 2 sentences?
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead has no discovery story tease. The human
             interest element is missing."

DO #7: Briefly Call Out and Address Skepticism
  SEARCH FOR: Acknowledgment of doubt, "I know this sounds..." language,
              pre-emptive objection handling, "you might be thinking" references.
  EVIDENCE REQUIRED: At least one skepticism acknowledgment.
  VERIFICATION: Does the prospect think "OK, they understand that this
                sounds too good to be true — they're not trying to con me"?
  STATUS: present / absent
  EVIDENCE_TEXT: [quote from lead proving presence]
  IF ABSENT: Flag — "Lead makes bold claims without acknowledging the
             prospect's natural skepticism. Trust barrier unaddressed."
```

### Step 2: DON'T Checklist Verification (All 4 Must Be Absent — ZERO TOLERANCE)

```
FOR each DON'T item, search the full_lead_text_draft for evidence of violation:

DON'T #1: Explain How the Unique Mechanism Works in Detail
  SEARCH FOR: Step-by-step mechanism explanations, "how it works" language,
              process descriptions, scientific pathway explanations,
              cause-and-effect chains describing the mechanism's operation.
  THE LINE: Naming the mechanism = OK. Saying it's "new" or "different" = OK.
            Saying WHAT it does (the result) = OK.
            Explaining HOW it achieves that result = VIOLATION.
  EXAMPLES:
    OK: "It's called the Cortisol Reset Protocol — and it's unlike anything
         your doctor has ever told you about."
    VIOLATION: "The Cortisol Reset Protocol works by targeting the HPA axis,
               downregulating the stress response through a 3-phase process
               that first calms the amygdala, then..."
  STATUS: absent (compliant) / present (VIOLATION)
  IF VIOLATION:
    VIOLATION_TEXT: [exact text that violates]
    LOCATION: [section and sentence position]
    SEVERITY: critical
    REMEDIATION: "Remove mechanism explanation. Replace with tease language:
                 'And when you discover how [mechanism name] works,
                  you'll understand why nothing else has worked until now.'"

DON'T #2: Explain in Detail Why Old Solutions Don't Work
  SEARCH FOR: Detailed breakdowns of why diets fail, why previous approaches
              are flawed, extended "old way vs. new way" comparisons,
              multi-sentence explanations of competitor/conventional failures.
  THE LINE: Briefly acknowledging "nothing else has worked" = OK.
            Spending 3+ sentences explaining WHY nothing else worked = VIOLATION.
  EXAMPLES:
    OK: "If you've tried everything — the diets, the pills, the programs —
         and nothing has worked..."
    VIOLATION: "The reason diets don't work is because they restrict calories,
               which slows your metabolism by up to 30%, causing your thyroid
               to downregulate T3 production, which then triggers a cortisol
               spike that forces your body to store fat around the midsection..."
  STATUS: absent (compliant) / present (VIOLATION)
  IF VIOLATION:
    VIOLATION_TEXT: [exact text that violates]
    LOCATION: [section and sentence position]
    SEVERITY: critical
    REMEDIATION: "Compress old-solution criticism to 1 sentence of acknowledgment.
                 Move detailed failure analysis to the campaign argument."

DON'T #3: Go Deeply into the Discovery Story
  SEARCH FOR: Extended narrative sequences about how the mechanism/solution
              was discovered, multi-paragraph origin stories, detailed timelines
              of the discovery journey, character development of the discoverer.
  THE LINE: "I stumbled onto this after years of research" = OK (1 sentence tease).
            A 3-paragraph story about the trip to Okinawa = VIOLATION.
  EXAMPLES:
    OK: "What I'm about to share with you was discovered almost by accident
         by a rogue MIT researcher..."
    VIOLATION: "It all started in 2003, when Dr. Sarah Mitchell was working
               late in her MIT laboratory. She'd been studying cortisol
               for 7 years and was about to give up. Then one night,
               she noticed something strange in her data — a pattern
               that no one had ever seen before. She called her colleague
               Dr. James Park, and together they spent the next 3 months..."
  STATUS: absent (compliant) / present (VIOLATION)
  IF VIOLATION:
    VIOLATION_TEXT: [exact text that violates]
    LOCATION: [section and sentence position]
    SEVERITY: critical
    REMEDIATION: "Compress discovery story to 1-2 sentence tease.
                 Move the full story to the body copy / credibility section."

DON'T #4: Explain What the Solution/Product Is
  SEARCH FOR: Product descriptions, feature lists, ingredient lists,
              "what you get" language, offer structure details,
              pricing, format descriptions, delivery method explanations.
  THE LINE: The lead is about the MECHANISM and PROMISE — not the product.
            Naming the mechanism = OK. Describing the product = VIOLATION.
  EXAMPLES:
    OK: "There's a breakthrough called the Cortisol Reset Protocol that
         could change everything."
    VIOLATION: "The Cortisol Reset Protocol is a 6-week digital program
               that includes 42 video lessons, a downloadable recipe book,
               a supplement guide, and access to a private community..."
  STATUS: absent (compliant) / present (VIOLATION)
  IF VIOLATION:
    VIOLATION_TEXT: [exact text that violates]
    LOCATION: [section and sentence position]
    SEVERITY: critical
    REMEDIATION: "Remove all product/offer details. The lead sells the
                 MECHANISM and PROMISE, not the product. Product details
                 belong in the close / offer section."
```

### Step 3: Assess "Hint vs. Explain" Boundary for Ambiguous Cases

```
FOR any element where the DO "hint/briefly" and DON'T "detail/deeply" overlap:

  APPLY the Georgi Boundary Test:

  SENTENCE COUNT TEST:
    1-2 sentences on the topic → likely a HINT (compliant)
    3+ sentences on the topic → likely EXPLAINING (potential violation)

  DETAIL DEPTH TEST:
    Names/labels only → HINT
    Process/steps/reasons → EXPLAINING

  INFORMATION COMPLETENESS TEST:
    After reading, does the prospect STILL NEED to keep reading to understand?
    YES → HINT (compliant — curiosity preserved)
    NO → EXPLAINING (violation — curiosity resolved)

  QUESTION TEST:
    Does the passage raise MORE questions than it answers?
    YES → HINT (compliant — open loops created)
    NO → EXPLAINING (violation — loops closed prematurely)

  FOR ambiguous cases:
    LOG: text_in_question, boundary_analysis, determination, confidence_level
    IF confidence_level < 80%:
      FLAG for human review with both interpretations presented
```

### Step 4: Compile Compliance Report

```
ASSEMBLE the full Georgi compliance report:

DO CHECKLIST SUMMARY:
  Total present: integer (out of 7)
  Total absent: integer
  Missing items: [list of absent DO items with flags]

DON'T CHECKLIST SUMMARY:
  Total absent (compliant): integer (out of 4)
  Total present (VIOLATIONS): integer
  Violations: [list with full violation details]

COMPLIANCE STATUS:
  IF all 7 DO items present AND all 4 DON'T items absent:
    compliance_status = PASS
  ELSE IF any DON'T item present:
    compliance_status = FAIL (zero tolerance — DON'T violations are blocking)
  ELSE IF any DO item absent:
    compliance_status = FAIL (incomplete lead formula)

  IF FAIL:
    blocking_issues: [list of all failures]
    remediation_priority: ordered list of fixes (DON'T violations first)
```

## Anti-Patterns

**The Enthusiastic Explainer:**
The copywriter gets excited about the mechanism and starts explaining how it works — "The Cortisol Reset Protocol works by first targeting the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis..." This is the most common DON'T violation. The lead must TEASE, not teach. If the prospect understands the mechanism after reading the lead, the lead has failed.

**The Old Solution Dissertation:**
Spending 5+ sentences explaining why diets/pills/conventional approaches fail before introducing the new mechanism. This belongs in the campaign argument, not the lead. The lead acknowledges failure in 1 sentence and moves to the promise.

**The Novelist:**
Writing a multi-paragraph discovery story in the lead — the trip to the remote village, the chance encounter, the lab results. The lead teases the story in 1-2 sentences. The full story is told in the body copy / credibility section.

**The Brochure Writer:**
Including product details, features, formats, pricing, or "what you get" language in the lead. The lead sells the MECHANISM and PROMISE. The product is revealed much later. Any product language in the lead is a critical violation.

**The Hint-That-Isn't:**
Claiming something is a "hint" when it is actually a compressed explanation. "It works by resetting your cortisol using a 3-phase process" is NOT a hint — it is a compressed explanation. A hint would be: "It targets something called the 'cortisol switch' — and once you understand what that is, everything changes." The hint creates a question; the explanation answers it.

**The Missing Skepticism:**
Making bold claims without ever acknowledging that the prospect is thinking "this sounds too good to be true." Failing to briefly address skepticism leaves the trust barrier intact and the prospect disengages.
