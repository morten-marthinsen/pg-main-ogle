# Layer 1.3 — Four-Element Mapper

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Lead Architecture)
**Type:** Strategic Mapping
**Dependencies:** lead_context (from 0.1), teaching_index (from 0.3), lead_type_classification (from 1.1)
**Output:** four_element_mapping.json

---

## Purpose

Map each of the 4 E5 lead elements (New/Different, Simple/Easy, Works Quickly, Predictable/Reliable) to specific mechanism assets and determine HOW each will be communicated in the lead. This is the bridge between the mechanism package (what the mechanism IS) and the lead copy (how the mechanism is PITCHED). Every element must connect to a real upstream asset — no fabrication, no abstraction, no filler.

---

## Persona Deployment

**Primary Persona:** Alex Rivera — Strategic Integration Director
Ensures every element maps to a real upstream asset. Identifies the strongest mechanism feature for each E5 element. Prevents element inflation (claiming simplicity when the mechanism is complex) and ensures strategic coherence across all four elements.

**Secondary Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter — Master Copy Composite
Translates mapped assets into emotionally resonant communication approaches. Ensures each element is expressed as a BENEFIT the prospect feels, not a feature the mechanism has. Tests each communication approach for elevator-pitch compatibility.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Map NEW AND DIFFERENT Element

```
REFERENCE E5 TEACHING:
  Element: "New and Different"
  Communicate: "HOW and WHY it's new and different"
  Principle: "New and different is better than better"
  KEY INSIGHT: Do NOT just say it's "better" — explain what makes it DIFFERENT
              from everything the prospect has tried or heard of before.

MAP TO UPSTREAM ASSETS:
  PRIMARY SOURCE: mechanism.uniqueness_claim
  SECONDARY SOURCE: mechanism.e5_type (E1-E5 classification)
  TERTIARY SOURCE: root_cause.what_it_really_is (counter-intuitive element)

DETERMINE COMMUNICATION APPROACH:
  ANSWER THIS QUESTION: "How is this different from everything else the prospect
                          has tried, been told about, or encountered?"

  APPROACH OPTIONS:
    - Category differentiation: "This isn't a [category they know] — it's a [new category]"
    - Mechanism differentiation: "Unlike [old approach], this [specific new action]"
    - Discovery differentiation: "Until [timeframe/event], this [mechanism] was unknown to..."
    - Counter-intuitive differentiation: "While everyone says [common belief], this works because [opposite]"

  SELECT the approach that leverages the STRONGEST upstream asset.

DRAFT communication_approach (1-2 sentences):
  Must be expressible in the lead's elevator pitch
  Must NOT explain HOW the mechanism works (just that it IS different)
  Must create curiosity about WHY it's different

ASSESS strength_score (1-10):
  10: Genuinely novel — nothing like it exists in the market
  8-9: Clearly differentiated with specific, verifiable uniqueness
  6-7: Somewhat different but the differentiation is subtle
  4-5: Claims "new" but feels like a repackage
  1-3: Not genuinely new or different — forced claim
```

### Step 2: Map SIMPLE AND EASY Element

```
REFERENCE E5 TEACHING:
  Element: "Simple and Easy"
  Communicate: "HOW and WHY it's simple — even one aspect"
  Principle: "May showcase just one simple step of many"
  KEY INSIGHT: You do NOT need to claim the entire process is simple.
              Per E5: If there are 12 steps, show how step 3 is just
              "filling out a form." Showcase ONE genuinely simple aspect.

MAP TO UPSTREAM ASSETS:
  SCAN mechanism features and process steps for genuinely simple aspects:
    - Is there a single step that is trivially easy?
    - Is there a component that requires zero expertise?
    - Is there a time commitment that is surprisingly short?
    - Is there a "just do this one thing" entry point?

  PRIMARY SOURCE: The simplest genuine aspect of the mechanism
  SECONDARY SOURCE: promise.supporting_promises (any ease-related promises)

DETERMINE COMMUNICATION APPROACH:
  ANSWER THIS QUESTION: "What is the ONE thing about this that the prospect
                          will immediately think 'I can do that'?"

  APPROACH OPTIONS:
    - Single step showcase: "All you do is [one simple action]..."
    - Time simplicity: "It takes just [short timeframe] per [period]..."
    - Expertise inversion: "You don't need to know anything about [complex topic]..."
    - Effort comparison: "It's easier than [something they already do]..."

  CRITICAL RULE: Do NOT claim the entire process is simple if it isn't.
                 Showcase the genuinely simple aspect honestly.

DRAFT communication_approach (1-2 sentences):
  Must reference ONE specific simple element (not generic "it's easy")
  Must feel honest — not over-promising simplicity
  Must be elevator-pitch compatible

ASSESS strength_score (1-10):
  10: Genuinely effortless entry point — anyone could do this in seconds
  8-9: One clearly simple step that prospects will immediately believe
  6-7: Simple relative to alternatives, but requires some explanation
  4-5: Claims simplicity but the simple step isn't obvious
  1-3: Nothing genuinely simple — forced claim
```

### Step 3: Map WORKS QUICKLY Element

```
REFERENCE E5 TEACHING:
  Element: "Works Quickly"
  Communicate: "Results begin fast — speed of INITIAL results"
  Principle: "Not full results fast — just that it STARTS working fast"
  KEY INSIGHT: Per E5: "You don't promise someone they'll lose 100 pounds
              fast. You show them how the fat-burning process STARTS quickly."
              This is about speed of INITIAL results, not speed of complete
              transformation.

MAP TO UPSTREAM ASSETS:
  DISTINGUISH CAREFULLY:
    - speed_of_initial_results: When does the prospect FIRST notice something?
      (First measurable change, first feeling, first indicator)
    - speed_of_full_results: When is the complete transformation achieved?
      (DO NOT USE THIS — this is NOT what "works quickly" means in E5)

  PRIMARY SOURCE: The earliest measurable or noticeable result
  SECONDARY SOURCE: promise.supporting_promises (any speed-related promises)
  TERTIARY SOURCE: proof.top_credibility_elements (any speed-related evidence)

DETERMINE COMMUNICATION APPROACH:
  ANSWER THIS QUESTION: "How soon will the prospect notice something is
                          STARTING to work — even before full results?"

  APPROACH OPTIONS:
    - First indicator: "Within [short timeframe], you'll notice [first sign]..."
    - Process initiation: "The [mechanism] begins working the moment you [action]..."
    - Comparative speed: "Most people feel the first [indicator] within [timeframe]..."
    - Immediate experience: "Right away, you'll feel [immediate sensation/change]..."

  CRITICAL RULE: NEVER promise fast COMPLETE results.
                 ONLY promise fast INITIAL results / first indicators.

DRAFT communication_approach (1-2 sentences):
  Must reference speed of INITIAL results (not complete transformation)
  Must include a specific timeframe or indicator if possible
  Must feel credible — not "overnight miracle" territory

ASSESS strength_score (1-10):
  10: Initial results noticeable within hours/day — genuinely fast-acting
  8-9: Initial indicators within days — credible and impressive
  6-7: Initial signs within 1-2 weeks — reasonable but not remarkable
  4-5: Initial results unclear or timeframe vague
  1-3: No genuine speed claim — mechanism is inherently slow-acting
```

### Step 4: Map PREDICTABLE AND RELIABLE Element

```
REFERENCE E5 TEACHING:
  Element: "Predictable and Reliable"
  Communicate: "Consistency and safety signaling"
  Principle: "Brain categorizes safe vs. dangerous — reliability = safety"
  KEY INSIGHT: Per E5, this element communicates SAFETY to the brain.
              The brain's deepest assessment is: "Is this safe or dangerous?"
              Predictability and reliability signal SAFETY — which lowers the
              brain's defense mechanisms against the sales message.

MAP TO UPSTREAM ASSETS:
  LOOK FOR evidence of consistency across:
    - Different demographics (works for men AND women, young AND old)
    - Different conditions (works regardless of [variable])
    - Different situations (works whether you're [situation A] or [situation B])
    - Repeated results (works every time, not just once)

  PRIMARY SOURCE: Broadest evidence of consistency from proof inventory
  SECONDARY SOURCE: proof.top_credibility_elements (institutional validation)
  TERTIARY SOURCE: mechanism.proof_elements (mechanism-level reliability)

DETERMINE COMMUNICATION APPROACH:
  ANSWER THIS QUESTION: "Why should the prospect's brain feel SAFE believing
                          this will work for THEM specifically?"

  APPROACH OPTIONS:
    - Demographic breadth: "It works whether you're [A], [B], or [C]..."
    - Condition independence: "Regardless of [common variable], this [mechanism]..."
    - Track record: "[Number] people have already [result] using this..."
    - Consistency signal: "Every single time [mechanism is applied], [result] follows..."

  CRITICAL RULE: This is NOT about proof (proof comes later).
                 This is about SAFETY SIGNALING — making the brain feel
                 secure enough to keep reading and stay open to the message.

DRAFT communication_approach (1-2 sentences):
  Must communicate consistency/reliability without diving into proof
  Must signal "safe" to the brain — lowers defensive reading posture
  Must be brief — a signal, not a proof section

ASSESS strength_score (1-10):
  10: Overwhelming evidence of consistency — institutional validation + demographic breadth
  8-9: Strong reliability signals — multiple data points supporting consistency
  6-7: Reasonable reliability — some evidence of consistent results
  4-5: Weak reliability — one or two data points, limited demographics
  1-3: No genuine reliability claim — mechanism is unproven or inconsistent
```

```
DETERMINE element weighting based on lead_type_classification.selected_type:

  statistical:
    emphasis_order: [predictable_reliable, new_different, works_quickly, simple_easy]
    rationale: "Stats prove reliability; novelty of the data is the differentiator"

  story_vulnerability:

    rationale: "Story reveals something new; speed of change sustains hope"

  authority_flex:

    rationale: "Authority signals reliability first; credential reveals novelty"

  compressed_discovery:
    emphasis_order: [new_different, works_quickly, simple_easy, predictable_reliable]
    rationale: "Discovery IS the novelty; speed validates the breakthrough"

  conspiracy_expose:
    emphasis_order: [new_different, predictable_reliable, works_quickly, simple_easy]
    rationale: "Expose reveals hidden truth (novelty); evidence shows it's real (reliability)"

  prophecy_warning:
    emphasis_order: [predictable_reliable, new_different, works_quickly, simple_easy]
    rationale: "Warning must feel reliable; the data reframing is the novelty"

  doctor_discovery:
    emphasis_order: [new_different, predictable_reliable, works_quickly, simple_easy]
    rationale: "Medical discovery IS the novelty; clinical data signals reliability"

  problem_solution:
    emphasis_order: [simple_easy, works_quickly, new_different, predictable_reliable]

FLAG any element with strength_score < 5:
  "WARNING: [element] scores below viable threshold. Consider de-emphasizing
   in lead and compensating with stronger elements."
```

### Step 6: Compile Four-Element Map

```
BUILD complete mapping object with all four elements:
  - mechanism_connection (upstream asset referenced)
  - communication_approach (1-2 sentence draft)
  - supporting_evidence (proof elements supporting this claim)
  - strength_score (1-10)
  - emphasis_rank (1-4 based on lead type weighting)

VERIFY: All four elements have a genuine mechanism_connection
VERIFY: No element fabricates a claim unsupported by upstream data
VERIFY: Communication approaches are elevator-pitch compatible
VERIFY: At least 2 of 4 elements score >= 7
```

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | What It Looks Like | Why It Fails |
|---|---|---|
| **Element Fabrication** | Claiming "simple and easy" when nothing in the mechanism is genuinely simple | Prospects will sense the dishonesty; undermines credibility when the mechanism is later explained |
| **Full Results Speed** | "Works Quickly" promises complete transformation in days | Per E5: promise INITIAL results fast, not complete results fast; overpromising triggers skepticism |
| **Better vs. Different** | "New and Different" says "our version is the BEST" instead of explaining HOW it's different | Per E5: "New and different is better than better" — the brain responds to novelty, not superiority claims |
| **Proof Dumping for Reliability** | "Predictable and Reliable" turns into a 3-paragraph evidence section | Reliability is a SIGNAL in the lead, not a proof section; proof comes in the campaign argument |
| **Generic Communication** | "This is unlike anything you've seen" without specificity | Must connect to specific mechanism asset; generic "different" claims create no curiosity |
| **All Elements Equal** | Giving each element identical weight regardless of lead type | Lead type determines emphasis order; a statistical lead emphasizes reliability differently than a story lead |
| **Explaining the Mechanism** | Communication approach describes HOW the mechanism creates the result | The lead TEASES; HOW it works is revealed in the campaign argument body |
| **Ignoring Weak Elements** | Skipping an element because the score is low instead of flagging it | All four must be present per E5; weak elements get de-emphasized, not deleted |
