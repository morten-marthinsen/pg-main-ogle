# Skill 13.3.1: Evidence Integration Calibrator

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** 10 — Root Cause Narrative
**Layer:** 3 (Refinement & Polish)
**Position:** First refinement microskill — weaves evidence naturally into the narrative
**Type:** Copy Refinement
**Dependencies:** All Layer 2 outputs (phases_1_2_draft, phases_3_4_draft, phases_5_6_draft, countersells_and_phase_7_draft), upstream_data.json (from 0.1), tier1_intelligence_index.json (from 0.2)
**Output:** evidence_integration_report.json

---

## PURPOSE

Weave evidence patterns naturally into the drafted narrative prose so that proof elements feel like organic parts of the story rather than bolted-on citations or parenthetical interruptions. The 7 evidence patterns from TIER1 analysis are integrated at specific narrative moments where they create maximum persuasive impact. Evidence must SUPPORT the narrative arc — never interrupt it. A reader should absorb evidence without consciously noticing they are being "proved to."

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- An evidence weaver that integrates proof elements into existing narrative prose
- A naturalness calibrator that ensures evidence feels organic, not academic
- A pattern matcher that maps the 7 TIER1 evidence patterns to optimal narrative locations
- A proof inventory consumer that draws from available evidence and places it strategically

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A proof generator — it uses evidence from the proof inventory, not inventing new claims
- A citation formatter — evidence is woven into prose, never formatted as references or footnotes
- A draft rewriter — it refines and augments the existing draft, not replaces it
- A fact-checker — the proof inventory is assumed validated; this skill focuses on integration

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Inventory Available Evidence

```
CATALOG all available evidence by pattern type:

PATTERN 1: villain's_own_data
  Definition: Using the villain's own statistics, documents, or admissions against them
  SEARCH proof inventory for: villain-sourced data, institutional reports, admissions
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Present as ironic revelation — "According to the FDA's
    OWN data..." or "The food industry's own research showed..."
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 6 (Villain Deep Dive)
  TIER1 example: Stansberry uses Federal Reserve's own published monetary data
    to prove currency devaluation

PATTERN 2: paradox_proof
  Definition: Someone did everything "right" and still failed — proving the false belief wrong
  SEARCH proof inventory for: authority's personal story, counter-examples
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Present as narrative paradox — "He was running 30 miles
    a week, eating perfectly, and STILL developing heart disease"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 3 (Authority Bridge) or Phase 4 (Reveal)
  TIER1 example: Gundry was a marathon runner eating "healthy" and developing
    heart disease — the paradox that led to his discovery

PATTERN 3: specific_number
  Definition: Precise quantities that feel verifiable and authoritative
  SEARCH proof inventory for: statistics, measurements, quantities, percentages
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Drop specific numbers into prose as authority signals —
    "10-12 pounds of impacted fecal matter" or "$4.7 trillion in hidden debt"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 4 (Reveal) or Phase 6 (Villain)
  TIER1 example: Montezuma — "10-12 pounds of impacted fecal matter lining
    the average American's colon"

PATTERN 4: institutional_name_drop
  Definition: Major institutions cited to borrow credibility
  SEARCH proof inventory for: university names, research institutions, publications
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Weave institution names into narrative naturally —
    "Researchers at Yale discovered..." not "(Yale University, 2019)"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 3 (Authority) or Phase 4 (Reveal)
  TIER1 example: Gundry — Yale, Medical College of Georgia, Loma Linda University

PATTERN 5: geographic_historical_proof
  Definition: Populations or historical periods where the problem doesn't exist
  SEARCH proof inventory for: geographic comparisons, historical references
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Present as contrast narrative — "In [place/time],
    where [root cause] doesn't exist, [problem] is virtually unknown"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 4 (Reveal) or Phase 5 (Failure Explanation)
  TIER1 example: Gundry — "Tomatoes weren't introduced to Europe until 1492.
    Before that, the lectin-related diseases we see today were virtually nonexistent."

PATTERN 6: visual_demonstration
  Definition: Before/after imagery or visual evidence that makes the invisible visible
  SEARCH proof inventory for: images, charts, diagrams, before/after references
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Describe what the reader would SEE — "If you could see
    your gut lining under a microscope, you'd see holes where there should be
    a solid wall"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 4 (Reveal) or Phase 7 (Bridge, as teaser for mechanism results)
  TIER1 example: Stansberry — Federal Reserve chart showing dollar purchasing power
    decline; Gundry — endocardiograph images

PATTERN 7: person_as_proof
  Definition: Authority figure's own experience as evidence the approach works
  SEARCH proof inventory for: authority personal results, testimonials, transformations
  INTEGRATION TECHNIQUE: Use authority's personal story as proof — "Dr. Gundry
    himself lost 70 pounds using this approach"
  OPTIMAL PLACEMENT: Phase 3 (Authority), Phase 7 (Bridge)
  TIER1 example: Gundry lost 70 pounds; Sears has 12% body fat at age 60+

FOR each pattern:
  SET status = "available" (evidence exists) or "unavailable" (no evidence in inventory)
  IF available: LIST specific evidence elements
  IF unavailable: LOG as "evidence_gap"
```

### Step 2: Map Evidence to Narrative Phases

```
CREATE evidence_placement_map:

Phase 1 (Problem Agitation):
  Best patterns: specific_number (symptom statistics), person_as_proof (authority experienced it)
  Density: LOW — Phase 1 is about naming symptoms, not proving them
  Maximum: 1 evidence point

Phase 2 (False Belief):
  Best patterns: specific_number (how many people believe this)
  Density: VERY LOW — Phase 2 is about articulating the belief, not proving anything
  Maximum: 0-1 evidence points

Phase 3 (Authority Bridge):
  Best patterns: institutional_name_drop, person_as_proof, paradox_proof
  Density: HIGH — credibility requires evidence
  Maximum: 3-5 evidence points (credential stack IS evidence)

Phase 4 (Root Cause Reveal):
  Best patterns: specific_number, geographic_historical_proof, paradox_proof, institutional_name_drop
  Density: HIGH — counter-intuitive claims require evidence
  Maximum: 3-4 evidence points

Phase 5 (Failure Explanation):
  Best patterns: specific_number (failure rates), geographic_historical_proof (where failures don't happen)
  Density: MEDIUM — failures need some evidence but are mostly logical
  Maximum: 1-2 evidence points

Phase 6 (Villain Deep Dive):
  Best patterns: villain's_own_data, specific_number (profit figures), institutional_name_drop
  Density: HIGH — villain claims require evidence
  Maximum: 2-4 evidence points

Phase 7 (Mechanism Bridge):
  Best patterns: person_as_proof (authority's results), visual_demonstration (teaser)
  Density: LOW — bridge is about transition, not proof
  Maximum: 1 evidence point

Countersells:
  Best patterns: specific_number (failure rates of competitors), villain's_own_data
  Density: LOW-MEDIUM — each countersell may have 0-1 evidence points
```

### Step 3: Integrate Evidence Into Prose

```
FOR each evidence point to integrate:

  RULE: Evidence must feel ORGANIC to the narrative

  BAD integration (bolted-on):
    "Lectins damage the gut wall. (Source: Yale University study, 2015,
     published in the Journal of Clinical Investigation.)"

  GOOD integration (organic):
    "What Dr. Gundry's team at Yale discovered is that lectins don't just
     irritate the gut — they punch microscopic holes in the intestinal
     lining, allowing undigested food particles and bacteria to leak
     directly into the bloodstream."

  BAD integration (list format):
    "Evidence supports this: 1. Dr. Gundry lost 70 lbs. 2. 300+ studies
     confirm lectin damage. 3. Yale researchers validated the findings."

  GOOD integration (woven narrative):
    "Dr. Gundry put his own discovery to the test. He eliminated lectins
     from his diet. In months, he lost 70 pounds — without changing his
     exercise routine. His blood markers improved dramatically. The same
     man who'd been developing heart disease while running 30 miles a week
     reversed course by addressing a single root cause."

  INTEGRATION PROCESS for each evidence point:
    1. IDENTIFY the exact sentence or paragraph where evidence belongs
    2. DETERMINE the narrative voice at that point (scientific, confrontational, etc.)
    3. WRITE the evidence AS PART OF the story — never as a citation
    4. READ the paragraph aloud — does the evidence feel like a natural part?
    5. IF it feels inserted: rewrite so the evidence IS the story, not a footnote

  NATURALNESS TEST:
    Remove the evidence sentence. Does the paragraph feel like something is missing?
    IF YES: Good integration — the evidence carries narrative weight
    IF NO: Bad integration — the evidence is decorative, not structural
```

### Step 4: Check Evidence Density

```
CALCULATE evidence_density per phase:

  COUNT evidence points integrated per phase
  COMPARE against recommended maximums from Step 2

  IF density > maximum:
    TRIM: Remove weakest evidence points (lowest strength from proof inventory)
    PRIORITIZE: Keep evidence that serves the emotional arc

  IF density = 0 for Phase 3 or Phase 4:
    FLAG: "Authority bridge or root cause reveal has NO evidence — persuasion
           will be significantly weakened"
    ATTEMPT: Find any available evidence to add

  OVERALL DENSITY CHECK:
    Total evidence points across all phases: [count]
    TIER1 benchmark: 8-15 evidence points across the full narrative
    IF total < 5: FLAG "Evidence-thin narrative — may lack credibility"
    IF total > 20: FLAG "Evidence-heavy — may feel academic; consider trimming"
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **All evidence organic** — proof elements must feel like natural parts of the narrative, never bolted-on citations, footnotes, or parenthetical references
2. **Phase 3 and Phase 4 have evidence** — authority bridge and root cause reveal are the phases that MOST need proof support
3. **No evidence lists** — evidence never appears in bullet-point or numbered-list format within the narrative prose
4. **Evidence density within bounds** — 8-15 total evidence points; no phase exceeds its maximum density
5. **Naturalness test passes** — removing any evidence sentence should make the paragraph feel incomplete
6. **Evidence matches communication type voice** — scientific evidence in scientific_explanation type; data evidence in data_presentation type
7. **Evidence serves the emotional arc** — proof points should amplify the emotional target of their phase, not flatten it

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Evidence feels bolted on | HIGH | Naturalness score < 6 for any point | Rewrite integration; make evidence part of the story |
| Phase 3 has zero evidence | HIGH | No credentials or proof in authority bridge | Add institutional name-drops or person-as-proof from credential stack |
| Phase 4 has zero evidence | HIGH | Root cause reveal unsupported | Add specific_number or geographic_historical_proof |
| Evidence density too high | MODERATE | More than 20 total points | Trim weakest evidence; keep highest-impact points |
| Evidence contradicts narrative | CRITICAL | Evidence undermines the root cause claim | Remove contradictory evidence; verify proof inventory alignment |
| All evidence same pattern type | LOW | Only specific_numbers used, no variety | Diversify; use at least 3 of the 7 patterns |
| No villain's_own_data in Phase 6 | MODERATE | Villain deep dive lacks villain-sourced evidence | Search for any villain-attributable data; if unavailable, use institutional_name_drop |

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Gundry — Multi-Pattern Evidence Integration

```
EVIDENCE INTEGRATION MAP:

Phase 3 (Authority Bridge):
  - institutional_name_drop: "Yale University" — woven into credential narrative
  - institutional_name_drop: "Loma Linda University" — woven into career narrative
  - person_as_proof: "Tony Robbins' personal physician" — woven as pinnacle credential
  - paradox_proof: "Running 30 miles/week yet developing heart disease" — woven as discovery catalyst
  Naturalness: 9.0 — credentials ARE the narrative here; evidence is invisible

Phase 4 (Root Cause Reveal):
  - specific_number: "Lectins found in over 50% of the standard American diet"
  - geographic_historical_proof: "Tomatoes weren't introduced to Europe until 1492"
  - institutional_name_drop: "Published in peer-reviewed journals"
  Naturalness: 8.5 — evidence supports the counter-intuitive claim

Phase 6 (Villain Deep Dive):
  - villain's_own_data: "Food industry's own 1950 conference research showed lectin harm"
  - specific_number: "USDA recommends 6-11 servings of lectin-heavy grains daily"
  Naturalness: 8.0 — villain's own data used against them

Phase 7 (Bridge):
  - person_as_proof: "Dr. Gundry lost 70 pounds himself"
  Naturalness: 9.0 — personal transformation is part of the story

Total evidence points: 10
Overall naturalness: 8.6
Patterns used: 5 of 7 (villain's_own_data, paradox_proof, specific_number,
               institutional_name_drop, person_as_proof)
```

### Example 2: Stansberry — Data-Heavy Evidence Integration

```

Phase 6: institutional_name_drop (Congressional testimony reference)

Total evidence points: 12

```

### Example 3: Haney — Evidence-Light (Direct Confrontation)

```

Phase 4: paradox_proof ("Perfect grip, perfect stance — still slicing")
Phase 6: specific_number ("$2,700/year average spent on instruction")

Total evidence points: 5

NOTE: Lower evidence density is APPROPRIATE for this communication type
```
