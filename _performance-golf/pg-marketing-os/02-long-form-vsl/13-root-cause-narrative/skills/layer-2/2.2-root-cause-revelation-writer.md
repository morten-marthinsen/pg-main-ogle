## PURPOSE

Write Phase 3 (Authority Bridge) and Phase 4 (Root Cause Reveal) — the heart of the root cause narrative. Phase 3 builds the credibility necessary to deliver a counter-intuitive truth that the reader would otherwise dismiss. Phase 4 delivers the three-part structure (what_they_think / what_it_really_is / why_nothing_worked) with sufficient force and specificity to shatter the false belief installed in Phase 2. This is where the worldview shifts. This is the emotional peak. Everything before this builds to it; everything after flows from it.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The climax writer — Phase 4 contains the single most important persuasive moment in the root cause narrative
- An authority architect that stacks credibility BEFORE the counter-intuitive claim (Rule 9)
- A three-part structure deliverer that translates the strategic root cause into narrative prose
- A specificity enforcer that ensures the reveal is MORE SPECIFIC than the false belief (Rule 3)

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A credibility inventor — it uses the credential stack from the proof inventory
- A root cause strategist — the root cause was decided in Skill 03 and confirmed at checkpoint
- A failure explainer (that is Phase 5 in 2.3) — Phase 4 reveals the cause; Phase 5 explains failures
- A villain narrator (that is Phase 6 in 2.3) — Phase 4 may introduce the villain but does not deep-dive

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Select Entry-Into-Authority Transition

```
FROM transition_map.inter_phase.phase_2_to_3:
  LOAD the transition type selected by the sequence mapper

FROM the anticipation_hook of Phase 2:
  CONNECT the authority bridge opening to the hook

SELECT authority entry pattern from 6 transition-into-root-cause patterns:

  1. "But here's the real problem" — Direct challenge transition
     USE WHEN: Phase 2 ends with accumulated false beliefs
     EXAMPLE: "But here's what nobody told you..."

  2. credential_to_revelation — Authority credentials lead to discovery
     USE WHEN: Strong credential stack available
     EXAMPLE: "Dr. Steven Gundry didn't start as a nutrition guru..."

  3. data_cascade — Statistics build case for authority
     USE WHEN: data_presentation communication type
     EXAMPLE: "In 2008, a study at [institution] found something disturbing..."

  4. personal_paradox — Authority's own failure leads to discovery
     USE WHEN: Authority has a personal story of the same problem
     EXAMPLE: "He was running 30 miles a week, eating a 'perfect' diet, and
               developing heart disease anyway..."

  5. "What they don't want you to know" — Conspiracy framing
     USE WHEN: conspiracy_reveal or conspiracy_expose type
     EXAMPLE: "There's a reason you've never heard what I'm about to tell you..."

  6. question_sequence — Questions build anticipation
     USE WHEN: confusion_to_clarity arc; intellectual engagement needed
     EXAMPLE: "What if the real cause of your [problem] had nothing to do
               with [false belief]? What if it was something you'd never suspect?"

VALIDATE: Selected pattern matches communication type and emotional arc
```

### Step 2: Write Phase 3 — Authority Bridge

```
BUILD authority stack from credential_stack:

  TIER1 PATTERN — Gundry authority stack:
    "Dr. Steven Gundry graduated from Yale University with honors.
     He earned his Medical Doctorate from the Medical College of Georgia.
     For 16 years, he served as Chairman of Cardiothoracic Surgery
     at one of America's top medical centers.
     He's Tony Robbins' personal physician.
     He has published over 300 peer-reviewed articles."

  AUTHORITY STACK RULES:
    1. Lead with the most prestigious credential
    2. Stack 3-5 credentials in ascending emotional impact
    3. Include at least one "relatable" element (personal story, transformation)
    4. End with the credential most relevant to the root cause claim
    5. NEVER list credentials in a boring resume format — weave into narrative

  COMMUNICATION TYPE ADAPTATIONS:
    IF scientific_explanation:
      Emphasize research credentials, publications, institutional affiliations
      "After 16 years studying [field] at [institution]..."

    IF direct_confrontation:
      Emphasize practical authority, results, fame
      "He coached Tiger Woods. He's won [X] championships as an instructor."

    IF conspiracy_reveal:
      Emphasize insider status, access to hidden information
      "As a former [position], he had access to research most people never see."

    IF data_presentation:
      Emphasize analytical track record, predictive accuracy
      "He predicted [event] in [year]. He warned about [event] before it happened."

    IF not_your_fault:
      Emphasize personal experience with the same problem
      "He struggled with the exact same problem you're facing..."

DRAFT Phase 3 prose:
  OPEN with the selected entry pattern
  STACK credentials narratively (not as a list)
  BUILD credibility sufficient to make the counter-intuitive claim BELIEVABLE
  HIT emotional target from arc design (e.g., "cautious trust")
  END with a transition into the reveal

  CRITICAL: Authority must be ESTABLISHED before the reveal (Rule 9)
  The reader must think "this person is credible" BEFORE hearing the claim

  WORD COUNT: Match phase_3.word_count_target (tolerance: +/- 15%)
```

### Step 3: Write Phase 4 — Root Cause Reveal (THE PEAK)

```
THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT PROSE IN THE ENTIRE ROOT CAUSE NARRATIVE.

DELIVER the three-part structure:

  PART 1 — What They Think (callback to Phase 2):
    "You've been told the problem is [what_they_think]."
    Brief — one sentence restating the false belief

  PART 2 — What It Really Is (the reveal):
    "[what_it_really_is]"
    THIS is the counter-intuitive truth
    Must be MORE SPECIFIC than what_they_think (Rule 3)
    Must be COUNTER-INTUITIVE (Rule 6) — if the reader already believes it, no power
    Must be EXTERNAL (Rule 1) — blame on villain/biology/misinformation, never reader

  PART 3 — Why Nothing Worked (recontextualization):
    "[why_nothing_worked]"
    Explains why every past attempt was doomed
    Must connect back to the false belief AND forward to the villain

DELIVERY STYLES by communication type:

  IF scientific_explanation:
    DELIVERY: Present as a scientific finding
    "After decades of research, [authority] identified the real culprit:
     [specific root cause]. It's not [what_they_think]. It's [what_it_really_is].
     Specifically, [detailed scientific explanation in accessible language]."

    SPECIFICITY PATTERN (Gundry):
    False belief: "Bad diet" → Root cause: "Citrate Lyase enzyme"
    General → Very specific. This is Rule 3 in action.

  IF direct_confrontation:
    DELIVERY: State bluntly, almost aggressively
    "Here's the truth nobody has the guts to tell you:
     Your [problem] has NOTHING to do with [what_they_think].
     The real cause is [what_it_really_is]. Period."

    SPECIFICITY PATTERN (Haney):
    False belief: "Poor grip/stance" → Root cause: "Clubface-to-path relationship at impact"
    Vague symptoms → Precise geometric relationship

  IF conspiracy_reveal:
    DELIVERY: Reveal as suppressed information
    "The information I'm about to share with you has been deliberately kept
     from the public. The real cause of your [problem] isn't [what_they_think].
     It's [what_it_really_is]. And [villain] has known about it for [duration]."

  IF data_presentation:
    DELIVERY: Let data make the case
    "The data is irrefutable. [Specific statistic]. [Another statistic].
     [What_they_think] accounts for less than [X]% of the problem.
     The real driver — the one that explains [Y]% of the variance —
     is [what_it_really_is]."

  IF not_your_fault:
    DELIVERY: Liberation first, explanation second
    "It's not your fault. It was never your fault. The real reason nothing worked
     is [what_it_really_is]. You didn't fail — you were set up to fail because
     [why_nothing_worked]."

  IF opposite_of_truth:
    DELIVERY: Inversion shock
    "The very thing you've been doing to fix the problem... is CAUSING it.
     [What_they_think] isn't the solution. It's the disease.
     The actual cause is [what_it_really_is]."

EMOTIONAL PEAK EXECUTION:
  This phase should contain the PEAK emotional moment from the arc design
  The reader's worldview must SHIFT here — old belief → new understanding
  The specificity of the reveal creates the "aha" moment
  The externalization creates relief (not my fault)
  The counter-intuitive element creates engagement (I didn't know this)

  WORD COUNT: Match phase_4.word_count_target (tolerance: +/- 15%)
  This phase typically gets the highest word count — USE it
```

### Step 4: Validate Three-Part Delivery

```
CHECK Phase 4 text against three-part requirements:

  □ PART 1 (what_they_think) — False belief restated? PRESENT/ABSENT
  □ PART 2 (what_it_really_is) — Root cause revealed? PRESENT/ABSENT
  □ PART 3 (why_nothing_worked) — Past failures explained? PRESENT/ABSENT

  □ Specificity check (Rule 3):
    IS what_it_really_is MORE SPECIFIC than what_they_think?
    BAD: "Bad diet" → "The wrong foods" (equally vague)
    GOOD: "Bad diet" → "Lectins — plant proteins in wheat, tomatoes, and beans
           that destroy the intestinal lining" (much more specific)
    IF not more specific: FLAG for revision

  □ Counter-intuitive check (Rule 6):
    WOULD the reader be surprised by this reveal?
    BAD: "You need to eat better" (obvious, no persuasive power)
    GOOD: "The 'healthy' foods you eat are the actual cause" (counter-intuitive)
    IF not counter-intuitive: FLAG for revision

  □ Externalization check (Rule 1):
    IS blame placed on an external agent (villain, biology, misinformation)?
    BAD: "You haven't tried hard enough" (blame on reader)
    GOOD: "The food industry has been feeding you lectin-bombs for profit" (external)
    IF blame internalized: FLAG for critical revision

  □ Authority-first check (Rule 9):
    DID the authority stack come BEFORE the counter-intuitive claim?
    IF reveal appears before credentials: RESTRUCTURE
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Authority precedes reveal** (Rule 9) — credentials must be established BEFORE the counter-intuitive claim; violation of this order destroys credibility
2. **Three-part structure fully delivered** — all three parts (what_they_think, what_it_really_is, why_nothing_worked) must appear in Phase 4
3. **Root cause is more specific than false belief** (Rule 3) — "Bad diet" to "Lectins" not "Bad diet" to "Wrong foods"
4. **Root cause is counter-intuitive** (Rule 6) — must surprise the reader; obvious causes have no persuasive power
5. **Blame externalized** (Rule 1) — root cause is NEVER the reader's fault; blame goes to villain, biology, or misinformation
6. **Emotional peak achieved** — Phase 4 must be the emotional climax (or near-climax) of the entire root cause section
7. **Continuity with Phase 2** — the false belief shattered in Phase 4 must be the SAME belief installed in Phase 2
8. **Credential stack narrative, not resume** — authority must be woven into prose, not listed in bullet points

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Reveal before authority | CRITICAL | Rule 9 violation — counter-intuitive claim precedes credentials | Restructure: move entire credential stack before the reveal |
| Three-part structure incomplete | CRITICAL | Missing one or more of the three parts | Add the missing part(s); all three must appear |
| Root cause vague (Rule 3) | HIGH | what_it_really_is not more specific than what_they_think | Rewrite with greater specificity — name the enzyme, the process, the actor |
| Root cause not counter-intuitive (Rule 6) | HIGH | Reader would not be surprised | Sharpen the counter-intuitive element; increase contrast with false belief |
| Blame internalized (Rule 1) | CRITICAL | Any language suggesting the problem is the reader's fault | Remove ALL internalized blame; redirect to external cause |
| Credential stack too thin | MODERATE | Fewer than 2 credentials or credentials are weak | Seek additional credentials from proof inventory; pad with narrative authority |
| Emotional peak flat | MODERATE | Phase 4 doesn't feel like a revelation | Increase contrast, add sentence fragments for emphasis, slow down the reveal |
| Phase 2 false belief and Phase 4 reveal misaligned | HIGH | Different belief addressed | Realign so the exact belief from Phase 2 is the one shattered in Phase 4 |

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Gundry — Scientific Explanation, Credential-to-Revelation

```
PHASE 3 — AUTHORITY BRIDGE (225 words):

"Dr. Steven Gundry didn't start out trying to change the way America eats.

He was a heart surgeon. One of the most respected in the country. He graduated
from Yale University with honors. Earned his Medical Doctorate from the Medical
College of Georgia. For 16 years, he served as Chairman and Head of Cardiothoracic
Surgery at Loma Linda University — one of the top medical centers in America.

He published over 300 articles in peer-reviewed medical journals. He performed
more heart surgeries than most doctors see in a lifetime.

He was also Tony Robbins' personal physician.

But here's what changed everything: Despite doing everything 'right' — running
30 miles a week, eating a 'healthy' diet rich in whole grains and vegetables —
Dr. Gundry was developing heart disease himself.

Something was fundamentally wrong with what we'd all been taught about food.
And when he investigated, what he found shocked even him..."

Credentials cited: [Yale honors, Medical College of Georgia MD, 16 years Chairman,
  300+ peer-reviewed publications, Tony Robbins' physician]
Bridge type: "personal_paradox" → "credential_to_revelation"


PHASE 4 — ROOT CAUSE REVEAL (375 words):

"The problem isn't your willpower. It isn't how much you eat or how little
you exercise. It isn't your metabolism slowing down with age.

The real problem — the one Dr. Gundry discovered after decades of research
and thousands of patients — is a protein found in the very foods you've been
told are the healthiest things you can eat.

They're called LECTINS.

Lectins are plant proteins found in whole wheat, tomatoes, beans, soy, and
dozens of other 'health foods' that appear on every diet plan ever created.

These proteins don't nourish your body. They ATTACK it.

Lectins bind to the lining of your gut wall — the barrier that's supposed
to protect your bloodstream from toxins, undigested food particles, and
bacteria. When lectins punch holes in that lining, everything leaks through.

Doctors call it 'intestinal permeability.' Dr. Gundry calls it the root
cause of the obesity epidemic, chronic fatigue, brain fog, joint pain,
and nearly every other symptom plaguing Americans over 40.

That's why diets never worked for you.

Every diet you've ever tried — low-fat, low-carb, Mediterranean, keto —
includes lectin-heavy foods. You were following the rules perfectly while
unknowingly feeding the real enemy three meals a day.

You weren't eating too much. You were eating the wrong proteins.

And the food industry has known about this since 1950. At a landmark
conference, researchers presented evidence that lectins in modern wheat
were damaging human health. The industry's response? They didn't care.
They kept pushing whole grains, tomato sauces, and bean-based 'health foods'
because those products were cheap to produce and profitable to sell.

Your health was the acceptable cost of their bottom line."

Three-part delivered: TRUE
  What they think: "willpower, diet, exercise, metabolism"
  What it really is: "Lectins — plant proteins that destroy gut lining"
  Why nothing worked: "Every diet includes lectin-heavy foods"
Counter-intuitive element: "The 'healthiest' foods are the actual cause"
Blame externalized: TRUE — food industry knew and didn't care
```

### Example 2: Haney — Direct Confrontation, Authority-to-Reveal

```

"Hank Haney fixed Tiger Woods' swing. Let that sink in. The greatest golfer
who ever lived trusted ONE man to rebuild his entire game from the ground up.

Clubface-to-path.

Every grip change, every stance adjustment, every backswing drill you've
ever practiced? They're overcompensations. Band-aids on a misdiagnosed wound.
They don't address the root geometry of what's happening when club meets ball..."
```
