# Skill 13.4.2: Anti-Slop Validator

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** 10 — Root Cause Narrative
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Position:** Second validation microskill — zero-tolerance check for generic, cliched, or AI-default language
**Type:** Validation
**Dependencies:** All Layer 3 outputs (updated narrative text)
**Output:** anti_slop_report.json

---

## PURPOSE

Execute a zero-tolerance scan of the complete root cause narrative for generic, cliched, AI-default, or lazy copywriting language. Every word in the narrative must be specific, concrete, and grounded in the root cause data. "Slop" is any language that could appear in ANY copy about ANY product — language that carries no specificity, no persuasive weight, and no connection to the actual root cause, niche, or audience. A single violation fails this check. Zero violations required for pass.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A language purity enforcer that scans for banned phrases, banned patterns, and generic writing
- A zero-tolerance gate — any violation is a failure; there is no "acceptable slop level"
- A specificity guardian that ensures every claim is concrete and grounded
- An AI-default detector that catches language patterns common to AI-generated copy

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A style guide — it does not enforce a specific writing style, only prohibits generic language
- A grammar checker — it does not check grammar, spelling, or syntax
- A content validator (that is 4.1) — it validates LANGUAGE QUALITY, not narrative structure
- A creative constraint — it prohibits lazy writing, not creative writing; vivid specific language passes

  - "you won't believe"
  - "in today's world"
  - "in today's fast-paced"
  - "are you tired of"
  - "what if I told you"
  - "here's the thing"
  - "let's dive in"
  - "without further ado"
  - "at the end of the day"
  - "it goes without saying"
  - "needless to say"
  - "take it to the next level"
  - "empower"
  - "leverage"
  - "synergy"
  - "paradigm shift" (unless used ironically)
  - "holistic approach"
  - "comprehensive solution"
  - "it's time to"

  - "amazing"
  - "incredible"

  - "mind-blowing"

RESULT: violation_count_phrases = violations.length

### Step 2: Scan for BANNED PATTERNS

```
BANNED PATTERN LIST — zero tolerance:

PATTERN 1: Vague benefit claims without specifics
  Detection: Benefit statement with no specific mechanism, number, or outcome
  BAD: "This will help improve your health"
  BAD: "You'll feel better than ever"
  BAD: "Experience the difference"
  GOOD: "Eliminating lectin exposure reduces gut inflammation within 14 days"
  GOOD: "Correcting clubface-to-path eliminates the slice at its source"

  SCAN: Look for benefit-claiming language that lacks specificity
  MARKERS: "improve," "better," "enhance," "boost," "optimize" without
           a specific what, how much, or mechanism

PATTERN 2: Passive voice in action sections
  Detection: Action is described passively when it should be direct
  BAD: "The problem was found to be caused by..."
  BAD: "It has been discovered that..."
  BAD: "Results were achieved through..."
  GOOD: "Dr. Gundry identified lectins as the cause"
  GOOD: "Eliminating lectins restores the gut wall"

  SCAN: Look for passive constructions ("was found," "has been," "were achieved")
  NOTE: Passive voice is acceptable in some contexts (villain actions described
        passively for emphasis). Flag ALL instances; context review determines violation.

PATTERN 3: Excessive adjectives without substance
  Detection: Stacking adjectives that don't add specific information
  BAD: "An amazing, revolutionary, breakthrough discovery"
  BAD: "This incredible, powerful, life-changing approach"
  GOOD: "A specific, lectin-targeting protocol developed over 16 years"

  SCAN: Look for 3+ consecutive adjectives before a noun
  EXEMPT: When all adjectives are specific and informative (rare)

PATTERN 4: Unexplained jargon
  Detection: Technical terms used without accessible explanation
  BAD: "Citrate Lyase enzyme" (if never explained)
  GOOD: "Citrate Lyase — the enzyme that converts unused carbohydrates to fat"

  SCAN: Look for technical terms that appear without definition within 2 sentences
  NOTE: Only applies if communication_type is NOT scientific_explanation
        targeting educated audiences

PATTERN 5: Motivational platitudes
  Detection: Encouragement that lacks specificity and could apply to anything
  BAD: "You deserve to live your best life"
  BAD: "It's time to take control of your health"
  BAD: "Your best days are ahead of you"
  BAD: "You owe it to yourself"
  GOOD: "Now that you know lectins are the cause, you can eliminate them"

  SCAN: Look for encouragement language without specific root cause connection

PATTERN 6: Filler sentences
  Detection: Sentences that add no information and could be removed without loss
  BAD: "But wait, there's more." / "And that's not all."
  BAD: "Let me explain why this matters."
  BAD: "This is where things get interesting."
  GOOD: [Just proceed to the point — no meta-commentary needed]

  SCAN: Look for sentences that describe the writing rather than making the argument

FOR each pattern detected:
  ADD to violations:
    pattern: string
    location: string
    offending_text: string  # exact text that violates
    category: string
    suggested_replacement: string  # specific alternative

RESULT: violation_count_patterns = violations.length
```

### Step 3: Scan for GENERIC LANGUAGE

```
GENERIC LANGUAGE TEST:
  For each sentence in the narrative, apply this test:
  "Could this sentence appear in copy for a DIFFERENT product in a DIFFERENT niche?"

  IF YES: The sentence is generic and likely slop
  IF NO: The sentence is specific to this root cause, niche, and audience

  EXAMPLES:

  GENERIC (fails test — could appear anywhere):
    "This breakthrough approach will change the way you think about [topic]."
    "Thousands of people have already experienced the benefits."
    "Don't miss this opportunity to improve your life."

  SPECIFIC (passes test — cannot appear elsewhere):
    "Lectins in whole wheat punch holes in your intestinal lining."
    "Your clubface angle is 3.7 degrees open at impact — that's the slice."
    "The Federal Reserve has devalued the dollar by 97% since 1913."

  SCAN: Read each paragraph and flag sentences that feel like they
        could appear in ANY copy about ANY product

  FOR each generic sentence found:
    ADD to violations:
      text: string
      location: string
      reason: "Generic — could appear in any promotion"
      suggested_replacement: string  # specific to THIS root cause

RESULT: violation_count_generic = violations.length
```

### Step 4: Scan for AI-DEFAULT Writing Patterns

```
AI-DEFAULT PATTERN DETECTION:
  These patterns are characteristic of AI-generated copy and must be eliminated:

  PATTERN A: Triple/quadruple comma lists
    BAD: "Your health, your energy, your confidence, and your quality of life"
    Better: Name specific symptoms or outcomes, not abstract categories

  PATTERN B: "Not only... but also" overuse
    BAD: "Not only does this address the root cause, but it also provides..."
    Once is fine. Repeated use = AI default.

  PATTERN C: Em-dash overuse for dramatic effect
    BAD: "And the truth — the real, unvarnished truth — is that..."
    Once or twice per section is fine. More = AI default pattern.

  PATTERN D: "Here's the thing" / "Here's what most people don't realize"
    Detection: Overuse of this specific lead-in
    Once may be acceptable. Repeated = AI filler.

  PATTERN E: Parallel structure overuse
    BAD: "It's not about diet. It's not about exercise. It's not about willpower.
          It's about lectins."
    NOTE: Parallel structure IS used in TIER1 controls (e.g., three-part delivery).
          Flag when used more than once per section or when it adds no new information.

  PATTERN F: Rhetorical question clusters
    BAD: "What if? What if there was a way? What if you could finally...?"
    One rhetorical question is a tool. Three in a row is AI filler.

  FOR each AI-default pattern detected:
    ADD to violations:
      pattern: string
      text: string
      location: string
      reason: "AI-default writing pattern"
      suggested_fix: string

RESULT: violation_count_ai_patterns = violations.length
```

### Step 5: Compile Anti-Slop Report

```
COMPILE anti_slop_report:
  total_violations: integer  # sum of all violation categories
  pass: boolean  # true ONLY if total_violations == 0

  violations_by_category:
    banned_phrases:
      count: integer
      items:
        - phrase: string
          location: string
          context: string
    banned_patterns:
      count: integer
      items:
        - pattern: string
          text: string
          suggested_replacement: string
    generic_language:
      count: integer
      items:
        - text: string
          reason: string
          suggested_replacement: string
    ai_default_patterns:
      count: integer
      items:
        - pattern: string
          text: string
          suggested_fix: string

  summary:
    total_violations: integer
    pass: boolean
    worst_category: string  # which category has most violations
    remediation_needed: boolean

IF total_violations > 0:
  LOG: "ANTI-SLOP CHECK FAILED. [total_violations] violations found.
        Breakdown: [count] banned phrases, [count] banned patterns,
        [count] generic language, [count] AI patterns.
        ALL must be fixed before assembly."

  LOG: "ANTI-SLOP CHECK PASSED. Zero violations. Language is specific,
        concrete, and grounded in root cause data."
  SET pass = true

WRITE to: layer-4-outputs/anti_slop_report.json
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Zero violations for pass** — any violation, no matter how small, fails this check
2. **All 4 categories scanned** — banned phrases, banned patterns, generic language, and AI-default patterns all checked
3. **Every violation has a specific replacement** — not just "fix this" but "change to [specific alternative]"
4. **Context preserved** — each violation includes surrounding text so the fix can be made in context
5. **No false positives on TIER1 patterns** — parallel structure and rhetorical questions ARE used in TIER1 controls; only excessive use is flagged
6. **Generic language test applied honestly** — the "could this appear in any promotion?" test must be applied rigorously
7. **AI-default detection distinguishes tool from crutch** — one rhetorical question is a tool; three in a row is a crutch

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| Banned phrase found | HIGH | Direct text match | Replace with specific, root-cause-grounded alternative |
| Vague benefit claims | HIGH | Benefit without specifics | Add specific mechanism, number, or outcome |
| Generic sentences detected | HIGH | Fails "could appear anywhere" test | Rewrite with root-cause-specific language |
| AI-default pattern overuse | MODERATE | Pattern appears 3+ times | Reduce to 0-1 uses; replace with varied construction |
| Passive voice in action section | LOW | Passive construction in active context | Rewrite in active voice with named subject |
| False positive on TIER1 technique | LOW | Legitimate parallel structure flagged | Review context; exempt if it's a deliberate TIER1 technique used once |
| Too many violations to remediate efficiently | HIGH | 20+ violations | Recommend section rewrite rather than individual fixes |

```

total_violations: 7
pass: false

violations_by_category:
  banned_phrases: 2

    - "revolutionary" in Phase 7: "...a revolutionary approach to gut health"
      REPLACEMENT: "...a lectin-elimination protocol developed over 16 years
                     of clinical research"

  banned_patterns: 2
    - Vague benefit in Phase 7: "You'll feel better than ever"
      REPLACEMENT: "Patients report reduced bloating within 14 days and
                    sustained energy without afternoon crashes"
    - Motivational platitude in Phase 7: "It's time to take control of your health"
      REPLACEMENT: "Now that you know lectins are the cause, here's how to
                    eliminate them from your diet"

  generic_language: 2
    - Phase 5: "Thousands of people have already experienced the benefits"
      REASON: Could appear in ANY promotion
      REPLACEMENT: "Over 2,000 of Dr. Gundry's patients have eliminated
                    lectin-driven gut damage using this protocol"
    - Phase 7: "Don't miss this opportunity"
      REASON: Could appear in ANY promotion
      REPLACEMENT: "Every day you eat lectin-heavy foods is another day
                    of gut wall damage"

  ai_default_patterns: 1
    - Rhetorical question cluster in Phase 1:
      "What if? What if there was an answer? What if you could finally...?"
      FIX: Replace with single rhetorical question or direct statement

```

### Example 3: Edge Case — Legitimate TIER1 Technique Flagged

```
FLAGGED TEXT: "It's not your diet. It's not your exercise. It's not your
              willpower. It's lectins."

INITIAL FLAG: "Parallel structure overuse (Pattern E)"

CONTEXT REVIEW:
  This IS the three-part structure delivery — a core TIER1 technique.
  Used ONCE in Phase 4, at the peak emotional moment.
  This is the tool, not the crutch.

RULING: NOT A VIOLATION — exempt as deliberate TIER1 technique

This demonstrates why context review matters: the pattern itself is
not the problem; OVERUSE of the pattern is the problem.
```
