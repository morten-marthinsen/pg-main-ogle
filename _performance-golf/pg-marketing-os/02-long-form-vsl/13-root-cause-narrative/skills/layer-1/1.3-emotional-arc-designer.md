# Skill 13.1.3: Emotional Arc Designer

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** 10 — Root Cause Narrative
**Layer:** 1 (Strategic Classification)
**Position:** Third classification microskill — designs the emotional journey through the root cause narrative
**Type:** Emotional Architecture Design
**Dependencies:** communication_type_classification.json (from 1.1), framing_pattern_selection.json (from 1.2), checkpoint_response.json (from 0.4), tier1_intelligence_index.json (from 0.2)
**Output:** emotional_arc_design.json

---

## PURPOSE

Design the emotional progression that the reader experiences as they move through the seven phases of the root cause narrative. The emotional arc determines WHAT the reader feels at each phase, HOW INTENSELY they feel it, and WHERE the peak emotional moment occurs — the moment where their worldview shifts and they become receptive to the mechanism. This is the invisible architecture that makes the difference between informing and persuading.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- An emotional architect that maps specific feelings to specific narrative phases
- A peak moment locator that identifies exactly where the worldview shift must occur
- An intensity calibrator that prevents emotional flatness AND emotional overload
- A TIER1-pattern applier that uses proven arc shapes from elite controls

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A writer — it designs the emotional blueprint; Layer 2 writers build to this blueprint
- A framing selector (that is 1.2) — framing is structural; emotional arc is experiential
- A generic mood board — every phase has a specific, named emotional target with defined intensity
- A manipulation toolkit — it maps genuine emotional responses to truthful narrative progression

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load the 5 Emotional Arc Types

```
FROM tier1_intelligence_index.emotional_arcs, LOAD all 5 arc types:

ARC 1: frustration_to_vindication (Most Common)
  Start state: Frustration — "I've tried everything and nothing works"
  End state: Vindication — "It was NEVER my fault. Now I know the truth."
  Peak moment: The root cause reveal (Phase 4) — "THIS is why nothing worked"
  Phase progression:
    Phase 1 (Problem Agitation): frustration, recognition → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 2 (False Belief): deeper frustration, feeling misled → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 3 (Authority Bridge): relief, someone credible understands → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 4 (Root Cause Reveal): shock, vindication → intensity: PEAK
    Phase 5 (Failure Explanation): anger at wasted effort → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 6 (Villain Deep Dive): righteous anger → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 7 (Mechanism Bridge): hope, determination → intensity: MEDIUM-HIGH
  Best for: Markets with repeated personal failure (health, golf, finance)
  TIER1 prototype: Gundry — reader frustrated by failed diets → vindicated that lectins were the cause

ARC 2: fear_to_hope
  Start state: Fear — "Something terrible is happening/coming and I can't stop it"
  End state: Hope — "Now I know the danger AND I can protect myself"
  Peak moment: Phase 4-5 — the danger fully revealed, fear at maximum
  Phase progression:
    Phase 1: concern, growing worry → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 2: false security challenged → intensity: MEDIUM-HIGH
    Phase 3: authority confirms the danger is real → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 4: full threat revealed → intensity: PEAK
    Phase 5: scope of danger expanded → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 6: who caused this danger → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 7: but here's how to protect yourself → intensity: MEDIUM (relief)
  Best for: Financial markets, survival, health threats, macro-level dangers
  TIER1 prototype: Stansberry — fear of currency collapse → hope through alternative investment strategy

ARC 3: confusion_to_clarity
  Start state: Confusion — "I don't understand why this keeps happening"
  End state: Clarity — "Now I understand. The path forward is simple."
  Peak moment: Phase 4 — the moment of understanding, the "aha"
  Phase progression:
    Phase 1: bewilderment, naming the confusion → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 2: false explanations named → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 3: credible guide appears → intensity: LOW-MEDIUM
    Phase 4: clarity arrives — the real explanation → intensity: PEAK
    Phase 5: everything makes sense now → intensity: HIGH (satisfying)
    Phase 6: who perpetuated the confusion → intensity: MEDIUM-HIGH
    Phase 7: clear next step → intensity: MEDIUM (calm confidence)
  Best for: Complex root causes, educated audiences, lower-drama niches
  TIER1 prototype: Sears — confusion about aging → clarity about telomere biology

ARC 4: outrage_to_righteous_action
  Start state: Outrage — "They've been LYING to me and profiting from it"
  End state: Righteous action — "Time to fight back. I know what to do."
  Peak moment: Phase 6 — villain deep dive, outrage at maximum
  Phase progression:
    Phase 1: growing suspicion → intensity: MEDIUM
    Phase 2: "I was told wrong things" → intensity: MEDIUM-HIGH
    Phase 3: credible insider reveals the truth → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 4: the deception named → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 5: full scope of deception → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 6: who did this and WHY → intensity: PEAK
    Phase 7: take action, fight back → intensity: HIGH (channeled)
  Best for: Conspiracy-framed root causes, political/financial markets, Stage 4-5
  TIER1 prototype: Stansberry — outrage at Federal Reserve → righteous financial protection

ARC 5: shame_to_dignity
  Start state: Shame — "There must be something wrong with ME"
  End state: Dignity — "The problem has a real cause and I deserve the real solution"
  Peak moment: Phase 4-5 — blame shifts from self to external cause
  Phase progression:
    Phase 1: private shame named, acknowledged → intensity: HIGH (vulnerability)
    Phase 2: false belief that it's "your fault" → intensity: HIGH
    Phase 3: someone who understands appears → intensity: MEDIUM (relief begins)
    Phase 4: "It's not you. It's THIS." → intensity: PEAK (liberation)
    Phase 5: every failed attempt reframed → intensity: HIGH (burden lifting)
    Phase 6: who/what actually caused this → intensity: MEDIUM-HIGH
    Phase 7: dignified path forward → intensity: MEDIUM (quiet confidence)
  Best for: Embarrassing health conditions, ED, weight loss, self-improvement failures
  TIER1 prototype: Montezuma — shame about digestive issues → dignity through root cause understanding
```

### Step 2: Score Each Arc Against Campaign Data

```
FOR each emotional_arc:

  CALCULATE niche_fit (0-10):
    QUERY: cross_vertical_patterns.by_niche[niche]
    CHECK: which arcs are dominant for this niche
    IF arc is dominant: 8-10
    IF arc is secondary: 5-7
    IF arc is uncommon: 2-4

  CALCULATE reframe_alignment (0-10):
    not_your_fault → shame_to_dignity (10), frustration_to_vindication (8)
    conspiracy → outrage_to_righteous_action (10), fear_to_hope (7)
    hidden_truth → confusion_to_clarity (10), frustration_to_vindication (6)
    opposite_of_truth → frustration_to_vindication (9), confusion_to_clarity (7)
    suppressed_science → outrage_to_righteous_action (8), confusion_to_clarity (7)
    conspiracy_expose → outrage_to_righteous_action (10), fear_to_hope (8)

  CALCULATE communication_type_fit (0-10):
    conspiracy_reveal → outrage_to_righteous_action (9), fear_to_hope (8)
    scientific_explanation → confusion_to_clarity (9), frustration_to_vindication (7)
    direct_confrontation → frustration_to_vindication (9), outrage_to_righteous_action (7)
    metaphor_driven → confusion_to_clarity (8), frustration_to_vindication (7)
    data_presentation → fear_to_hope (8), confusion_to_clarity (7)
    conspiracy_expose → outrage_to_righteous_action (10)
    opposite_of_truth → frustration_to_vindication (9)
    hidden_truth → confusion_to_clarity (9)
    not_your_fault → shame_to_dignity (10), frustration_to_vindication (8)
    suppressed_science → outrage_to_righteous_action (8), confusion_to_clarity (7)

  CALCULATE frame_compatibility (0-10):
    villain_driven → frustration_to_vindication (8), outrage_to_righteous_action (9)
    science_driven → confusion_to_clarity (9), frustration_to_vindication (7)
    conspiracy_driven → outrage_to_righteous_action (10), fear_to_hope (8)
    ignorance_driven → confusion_to_clarity (10)
    inversion_driven → frustration_to_vindication (9), outrage_to_righteous_action (7)
    historical_driven → confusion_to_clarity (8)

  CALCULATE villain_emotional_fit (0-10):
    IF villain is strong (specific name, clear motive):
      frustration_to_vindication: 8
      outrage_to_righteous_action: 10
      fear_to_hope: 8
      shame_to_dignity: 6
      confusion_to_clarity: 5
    IF villain is weak or absent:
      confusion_to_clarity: 9
      shame_to_dignity: 8
      frustration_to_vindication: 6
      outrage_to_righteous_action: 3

  ARC_SCORE = (niche_fit * 0.25) + (reframe_alignment * 0.25) +
              (communication_type_fit * 0.20) + (frame_compatibility * 0.15) +
              (villain_emotional_fit * 0.15)
```

### Step 3: Select Arc Type

```
RANK all 5 arcs by ARC_SCORE descending

SELECT arc_type:
  - Highest scoring arc
  - Must score >= 6.0
  - IF frustration_to_vindication scores within 1.0 of top scorer:
    PREFER frustration_to_vindication (most common in TIER1, proven across verticals)

DOCUMENT arc_rationale:
  - Why this arc was selected
  - Which TIER1 control uses this arc in a similar niche
  - What the reader's emotional journey will be
```

### Step 4: Map Emotional Targets to All 7 Phases

```
USING the selected arc's phase progression template:

FOR each of 7 phases:
  DEFINE:
    phase_number: integer
    phase_name: string
    target_emotion: string  # specific named emotion
    intensity: enum[low, medium, medium_high, high, peak]
    emotional_purpose: string  # what this emotion accomplishes narratively
    reader_inner_monologue: string  # what the reader is thinking/feeling
    transition_emotion: string  # the emotion that bridges to the next phase

EXAMPLE for frustration_to_vindication:
  Phase 1:
    target_emotion: "frustrated recognition"
    intensity: medium
    emotional_purpose: "Reader sees their own symptoms named — feels understood"
    reader_inner_monologue: "Yes, that's exactly what I'm experiencing"
    transition_emotion: "deepening frustration, wanting answers"

  Phase 2:
    target_emotion: "misled frustration"
    intensity: high
    emotional_purpose: "Reader realizes they've been operating on false beliefs"
    reader_inner_monologue: "I've been told the wrong thing this whole time?"
    transition_emotion: "openness to a new explanation"

  Phase 3:
    target_emotion: "cautious trust"
    intensity: medium
    emotional_purpose: "Reader encounters a credible authority who 'gets it'"
    reader_inner_monologue: "This person has the credentials AND understands my problem"
    transition_emotion: "readiness to receive counter-intuitive truth"

  Phase 4:
    target_emotion: "shock and vindication"
    intensity: PEAK
    emotional_purpose: "The worldview shift — false belief shattered, real cause revealed"
    reader_inner_monologue: "THAT'S why nothing worked! It was never my fault!"
    transition_emotion: "anger at wasted time and effort"

  Phase 5:
    target_emotion: "righteous frustration"
    intensity: high
    emotional_purpose: "Every past failure now explained and externalized"
    reader_inner_monologue: "Of course that didn't work — it was never addressing the real problem"
    transition_emotion: "desire to know who's responsible"

  Phase 6:
    target_emotion: "directed anger"
    intensity: high
    emotional_purpose: "Anger channeled toward the villain, not self"
    reader_inner_monologue: "THEY knew about this and profited from my suffering"
    transition_emotion: "determination to break free"

  Phase 7:
    target_emotion: "determined hope"
    intensity: medium_high
    emotional_purpose: "Emotional state ready to receive the mechanism/solution"
    reader_inner_monologue: "Now I know the real problem. Show me the real solution."
    transition_emotion: "eager anticipation for mechanism"
```

### Step 5: Identify Peak Emotional Moment

```
LOCATE peak_moment:
  - The phase where intensity = PEAK
  - This is WHERE the worldview shifts
  - This is the single most important emotional beat in the root cause narrative

FOR the selected arc:
  IF frustration_to_vindication: peak at Phase 4 (root cause reveal)
  IF fear_to_hope: peak at Phase 4-5 (full threat revealed)
  IF confusion_to_clarity: peak at Phase 4 (the "aha" moment)
  IF outrage_to_righteous_action: peak at Phase 6 (villain exposed)
  IF shame_to_dignity: peak at Phase 4-5 (blame externalized)

DEFINE peak_moment:
  phase: integer
  emotion_at_peak: string
  what_triggers_peak: string  # the narrative event that creates the peak
  duration: string  # how long the peak sustains (brief flash vs. extended)
  what_follows_peak: string  # how the narrative handles the descent from peak

VALIDATE: Peak moment aligns with three-part structure delivery
  The peak SHOULD coincide with the delivery of "what_it_really_is"
  IF peak is at Phase 6 (villain), ensure Phase 4 still delivers strong revelation
```

### Step 6: Define Ending Emotional State

```
DEFINE ending_emotional_state:
  The emotion the reader EXITS the root cause narrative section with
  This emotion is the ENTRY STATE for Skill 11 (Mechanism Narrative)

  IF frustration_to_vindication: "determined hope — vindicated and ready for solution"
  IF fear_to_hope: "cautious hope — danger understood, protection desired"
  IF confusion_to_clarity: "calm confidence — problem understood, path clear"
  IF outrage_to_righteous_action: "channeled determination — ready to fight back"
  IF shame_to_dignity: "quiet dignity — freed from blame, ready for real answer"

VALIDATE:
  Ending state must be RECEPTIVE to mechanism presentation
  Reader cannot exit in despair, overwhelm, or apathy
  Reader must exit WANTING the solution — that desire drives engagement with Skill 11
```

---

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **All 7 phases have emotional targets** — no phase can be emotionally undefined; every phase serves an emotional purpose
2. **Exactly one PEAK moment** — the peak must occur at the worldview shift point; having zero or multiple peaks weakens the narrative
3. **Intensity curve is shaped, not flat** — intensity must rise, peak, and resolve; flat emotional lines fail to engage
4. **Ending state is receptive** — reader must exit the root cause section WANTING the solution; any other exit state breaks the mechanism handoff
5. **Peak aligns with three-part delivery** — the peak emotion should coincide with or immediately follow the revelation of "what_it_really_is"
6. **Arc type matches niche patterns** — TIER1 cross-vertical patterns validate that this arc works for this market
7. **Reader inner monologue is specific** — not "reader feels good" but "reader thinks 'THAT'S why nothing worked!'"

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Severity | Detection | Handling |
|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| No arc scores above 6.0 | HIGH | All ARC_SCORES < 6.0 | Default to frustration_to_vindication (most universal); flag for review |
| Peak moment at wrong phase | MODERATE | Peak does not align with root cause reveal | Adjust phase targets to move peak to Phase 4; or adjust if arc type places peak at Phase 6 |
| Ending state is negative | HIGH | Reader exits in fear, anger, or despair without hope | Adjust Phase 7 targets to include hope/determination element |
| Flat intensity curve | MODERATE | Multiple consecutive phases at same intensity | Adjust intensities to create rise-peak-resolve shape |
| Arc conflicts with communication type | MODERATE | shame_to_dignity selected for direct_confrontation type | Re-evaluate; direct confrontation rarely works with shame arc |
| Phase transitions emotionally jarring | LOW | Adjacent phases have incompatible emotions | Smooth transitions; ensure transition_emotion bridges naturally |
