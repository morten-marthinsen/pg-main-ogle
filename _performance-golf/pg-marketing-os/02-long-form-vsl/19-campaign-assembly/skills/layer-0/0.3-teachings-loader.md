## MAKEPEACE: FLOW PRINCIPLES

### The "Greased Slide" Doctrine
> "Every element of your copy should be so compelling that the reader has no choice but to read the next element."

**Application to Assembly:**
- No section should feel like a "stop" point
- Transitions must create momentum
- Each section ending should pull into next section beginning

### Section Sequencing
> "Story before mechanism, mechanism before proof, proof before offer, offer before close."

**Assembly Sequence (Default):**
1. Headline/Pre-head
2. Lead
3. Story (optional depending on format)
4. Root Cause Narrative
5. Mechanism Narrative
6. Proof Section
7. Product Introduction
8. Offer
9. Close

## SCHWARTZ: SOPHISTICATION-APPROPRIATE ASSEMBLY

### Stage-Based Assembly Adjustments

| Stage | Lead Length | Proof Placement | Mechanism Depth |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Short (direct) | Minimal | Light |
| 2 | Medium | After claim | Moderate |
| 3 | Longer (story) | Throughout | Full |
| 4 | Extended | Heavy, early | Deep + novel |
| 5 | Maximum | Before mechanism | Innovation focus |

## CARLTON: ENGAGEMENT MAINTENANCE

### The "Where We're Going" Principle
> "Your reader will abandon you at the first hint you don't know where we're going."

**Assembly Application:**
- Every section must feel purposeful
- Signpost transitions ("Now let me show you...")
- No wandering or tangents
- Clear momentum toward offer

### The "First Draft Is Too Long" Rule
> "Cut ruthlessly. Every word must earn its place."

**Assembly Application:**
- Target proportions, not maximums
- Trim sections that exceed benchmarks
- Eliminate redundancy between sections

## E5 METHOD: COHERENCE PRINCIPLES

### 5-Element Coherence

| Element | Must Thread Throughout |
|---------|----------------------|
| Enemy (Villain) | Root Cause → Mechanism → Close |
| Explanation (Root Cause) | Story → Root Cause → Mechanism |
| Epiphany (Mechanism) | Mechanism → Product → Close |
| Evidence (Proof) | All sections per density targets |
| Escape (Solution) | Product → Offer → Close |

### Threading Test
> "If you can remove a section without the reader noticing, that section wasn't properly threaded."

---

## GEORGI: RMBC ASSEMBLY

### Root Cause → Mechanism → Belief → Close Flow

```
R: Root Cause establishes WHY reader has problem
   └─ Threads to: Mechanism (explains solution)
   └─ Threads to: Close (callbacks)

M: Mechanism explains HOW solution works
   └─ Threads to: Product (mechanism → formula)
   └─ Threads to: Close (mechanism callbacks)

B: Belief (Story + Proof) builds conviction
   └─ Threads to: Offer (value proof)
   └─ Threads to: Close (transformation reminders)

C: Close drives action
   └─ Callbacks to: Lead (promise echo)
   └─ Callbacks to: Proof (transformation reminder)
   └─ Callbacks to: Story (protagonist reference)
```

## HALBERT: SEAMLESS TRANSITIONS

### The "Bucket Brigade" Technique
> "Use transitional phrases that force the reader forward."

**Transition Phrases for Assembly:**
- "But here's the thing..."
- "Which brings me to..."
- "Now, you might be wondering..."
- "And that's exactly why..."
- "But don't take my word for it..."
- "The question is..."

### Section Handoff Rule
> "The last sentence of every section should COMPEL reading of the first sentence of the next."
