# Microskill 2.5: Attention Maintenance

**Layer:** 2 (Assembly)
**Purpose:** Insert attention-maintenance techniques based on pacing plan

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE: THE ENGAGEMENT ENGINE

Long-form copy competes against distraction. The reader's finger hovers over the back button. Their mind drifts to notifications, tasks, doubts. Attention Maintenance builds in deliberate "pulls" that keep the reader moving forward.

**TIER1 Observation:** Elite controls insert engagement triggers approximately every 300-500 words, with increasing density toward the close.

---

## ATTENTION MAINTENANCE TECHNIQUES

### Re-Engagement Hooks

Pull readers back when attention wanes:

| Technique | Example | Word Count |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Direct Address | "Now, here's where it gets interesting for YOU specifically..." | 10-20 |
| Pattern Interrupt | "But wait. There's something I haven't told you yet." | 8-15 |
| Curiosity Loop | "In just a moment, I'll reveal the third factor—but first..." | 15-25 |
| Stakes Escalation | "And if you think that's bad, consider this..." | 8-15 |
| Insider Reveal | "What I'm about to share isn't something you'll find anywhere else..." | 15-20 |

### Forward Momentum Builders

Create the sense that something valuable is coming:

| Technique | Example | Word Count |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Preview Promise | "Keep reading, because what you'll discover next changes everything." | 12-20 |
| Numbered Progression | "That was the first secret. Now here's the second—and it's even more important." | 15-25 |
| Milestone Markers | "Now that you understand WHY, let me show you HOW." | 10-15 |
| Exclusivity Signal | "This is the part most people miss—but not you." | 10-15 |
| Benefit Foreshadow | "By the end of this, you'll have the exact formula I used to..." | 15-25 |

### Engagement Validators

Validate the reader's decision to keep reading:

| Technique | Example | Word Count |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Smart Reader | "If you've read this far, you're clearly serious about..." | 10-15 |
| Investment Payoff | "Everything you've read up to this point has been building to this..." | 12-18 |
| Community Signal | "Thousands of others who reached this point went on to..." | 12-18 |
| Progress Marker | "You now know more about [topic] than 95% of people ever will." | 15-20 |

### Objection Anticipators

Address objections before they derail attention:

| Technique | Example | Word Count |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| Skeptic Acknowledgment | "Now, you might be wondering if this really works for someone like you..." | 15-25 |
| Common Question | "The question I get most often at this point is..." | 10-15 |
| Doubt Preemption | "I know what you might be thinking—but here's the thing..." | 15-20 |

## PLACEMENT STRATEGY

### Based on Pacing Plan (from 1.4)

Insert attention techniques at:

| Location | Technique Type | Density |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| After REST sections | Re-engagement hook | Required |
| Before PEAK sections | Forward momentum | Required |
| At 500-word intervals | Any technique | Minimum |
| Danger zones | Re-engagement + validation | Required |
| Section transitions | Forward momentum | If transition alone insufficient |

### Danger Zone Protocol

From pacing plan danger zones:

```yaml
danger_zone_intervention:
  zone: "After first story section"
  risk: "Reader feels story complete"
  intervention:
    technique: "Curiosity Loop"
    copy: "That was just the beginning. What happened next shocked even me..."
    placement: "Last paragraph of story, before root cause transition"
```

## INSERTION PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Pacing Plan

From 1.4-pacing-planner output:

```yaml
pacing_reference:
  re_engagement_points: [list from pacing plan]
  danger_zones: [list with interventions]
  section_patterns: [HOOK, BUILD, PEAK, REST, BRIDGE for each section]
```

### Step 2: Calculate Insertion Points

For entire assembled copy:
- Total word count
- Word count per section
- Required insertion points (every 300-500 words)
- Danger zone locations

### Step 3: Select Techniques

For each insertion point:

```yaml
technique_selection:
  location: "[section + approximate word position]"
  pacing_context: "[what precedes and follows]"
  selected_technique: "[name]"
  rationale: "[why this technique here]"
```

### Step 4: Draft Insertions

```yaml
attention_insert:
  id: "ATT_001"
  section: "[name]"
  position: "[word count or paragraph reference]"
  technique: "Pattern Interrupt"
  copy: "But here's what nobody tells you about [topic]..."
  word_count: "[count]"
  integration: "[how it connects to surrounding copy]"
```

### Step 5: Validate Insertion

```
[ ] Copy flows naturally before and after
[ ] Technique matches pacing context
[ ] No redundancy with nearby insertions
[ ] Adds momentum without disrupting flow
```

## ATTENTION DENSITY TARGETS

### By Section

| Section | Target Density | Rationale |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| Lead | 1 per 250 words | Establish engagement momentum |
| Story | 1 per 400 words | Story carries its own momentum |
| Root Cause | 1 per 300 words | Prevent problem fatigue |
| Mechanism | 1 per 350 words | Balance explanation with engagement |
| Proof | 1 per 400 words | Proof is inherently engaging |
| Product | 1 per 300 words | Prevent "sales mode" dropout |
| Offer | 1 per 250 words | Maintain attention through details |
| Close | 1 per 200 words | Maximum density for urgency |

### Progressive Density

Attention techniques should increase in density toward the close:

```
Sections 1-2: Baseline density (1 per 350-400 words)
Sections 3-5: Medium density (1 per 300-350 words)
Sections 6-8: High density (1 per 200-250 words)
```
