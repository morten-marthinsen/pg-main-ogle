**Output:** phase-5-draft.json

---

## PURPOSE

Writes Phase 5 of the mechanism narrative: Proof Integration. After the mechanism has been named (Phase 3) and explained (Phase 4), the reader UNDERSTANDS the mechanism but does not yet BELIEVE it with enough conviction to act. Phase 5 weaves proof elements INTO the narrative — not as an appended list of citations, but as organically embedded evidence that makes the mechanism feel inevitable, proven, and trustworthy. Each proof element gets a mini-narrative wrapper that connects it emotionally and logically to the mechanism.

---

## IDENTITY

**What this IS:**
- A proof-weaving engine that integrates evidence into narrative prose
- A strategy executor that deploys one of 6 proof integration strategies
- The belief-building phase that converts understanding into conviction

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a citation appendix — proof is WOVEN, not listed
- Not a new explanation — the mechanism was explained in Phase 4
- Not a product pitch — proof validates the MECHANISM, not the product
- Not a standalone evidence section — must flow from Phase 4 naturally

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Select Proof Integration Strategy

```
LOAD 6 strategies from tier1_index.proof_strategies:

STRATEGY 1: institutional_stacking
  Description: 3-5 prestigious institutions cited in rapid succession
  Pattern: "[Institution 1] confirmed... [Institution 2] validated...
    [Institution 3] found..."
  Best For: scientific_revelation, proprietary_technology
  Effect: Overwhelming credibility through authority volume
  Minimum Requirements: 3+ institutional proof elements
  TIER1 Example (LA-3):
    "Researchers at Yale confirmed AMPK's fat-burning effects.
    The Salk Institute validated its anti-inflammatory properties.
    Studies published in the journal Nature showed its role in
    cellular repair. And a team at the National Institutes of
    Health called it 'one of the most promising targets for
    age-related disease.'"
    — 4 institutions in ~60 words = credibility avalanche

STRATEGY 2: escalating_proof_ladder
  Description: Starts small, escalates to increasingly impressive evidence
  Pattern: Small observation → preliminary finding → larger study →
    major institution → landmark result
  Best For: accidental_discovery, historical_ancient_secret
  Effect: Builds credibility progressively, each rung more convincing
  Minimum Requirements: 3+ proof elements of varying strength
  TIER1 Example (Montezuma):
    "First, the village observations (small). Then a local doctor
    documented the pattern (medium). Then a University study
    confirmed the mechanism (large). Then a double-blind clinical
    trial proved it conclusively (landmark)."
    — Each step more credible than the last

STRATEGY 3: self_proving_mechanism
  Description: The explanation itself is so logical it FUNCTIONS as proof
  Pattern: "Think about it — [logical argument that proves itself]"
  Best For: paradigm_shift, data_driven_debunking
  Effect: Reader convinces themselves through logic, not external authority
  Minimum Requirements: Strong core_logic, logical argument chain
  TIER1 Example (BBW):
    "Think about it. If 98% of people are stuck in Beta... and
    billionaires produce 6x more Theta... and the hippocampus
    controls Theta production... then isn't it obvious? The
    difference between wealth and poverty isn't effort or
    intelligence. It's a measurable brain frequency."
    — The argument structure IS the proof

STRATEGY 4: personal_transformation_anchor
  Description: One person's dramatic transformation IS the proof
  Pattern: "[Person] was [suffering]. After [mechanism], they [transformation]."
  Best For: identity_driven, personal_transformation frame
  Effect: Emotional proof — reader believes because they see it work for someone
  Minimum Requirements: 1+ compelling testimonial proof element
  TIER1 Example (Superbrain):
    "Jim Kwik went from 'The Boy With The Broken Brain' who
    couldn't read until age 10... to training the executives at
    Google, Nike, and SpaceX on memory and cognitive performance.
    The same brain that doctors said was permanently damaged now
    teaches billionaires how to think."
    — One transformation story carries more weight than 10 studies

STRATEGY 5: competitor_debunking_proof
  Description: Proving competitors DON'T work provides indirect proof mechanism DOES
  Pattern: "Studies show [competitor] fails because [reason]. [Mechanism]
    addresses the ACTUAL cause."
  Best For: data_driven_debunking
  Effect: Eliminates alternatives, mechanism is last option standing
  Minimum Requirements: Clinical data on competitor failure
  TIER1 Example (i-flex):
    "A meta-analysis of 10 clinical trials involving over 3,800
    patients found that glucosamine produced results
    indistinguishable from placebo. After billions of dollars
    and millions of users, the data is clear: glucosamine
    doesn't work. The Danish rosehip compound, by contrast,
    showed a 65% improvement in joint mobility in the same
    type of double-blind, placebo-controlled trial."
    — Proving the competitor fails IS proof the mechanism works

STRATEGY 6: visual_demonstration
  Description: Mechanism SHOWN working through demonstrations, tests, scans
  Pattern: "Watch what happens when [mechanism is applied]... [visual result]"
  Best For: proprietary_technology, scientific_revelation
  Effect: Seeing is believing — visual proof bypasses skepticism
  Minimum Requirements: Demonstrable proof element (video, image, scan)
  TIER1 Example (SF1):
    "We put Square Face Technology to the ultimate test. Our
    robot arm hit 1,000 drives with a traditional round-face
    driver and 1,000 with Square Face. The results? Square Face
    averaged 17 yards longer. But the real difference was
    consistency — 84% of Square Face drives landed in the
    fairway versus just 52% with the traditional design."
    — Robot test provides visual, quantifiable proof

SELECT strategy based on:
  1. Narrative type from 1.1 → strategy affinity mapping
  2. Available proof inventory → which strategies are feasible
  3. E-level → proof density requirements (E2 heavy, E5 lighter)

SELECTION LOGIC:
  IF primary_type == "scientific_revelation" AND institutional.count >= 3:
    primary_strategy = institutional_stacking
  IF primary_type == "paradigm_shift":
    primary_strategy = self_proving_mechanism
    secondary_strategy = institutional_stacking (if available)
  IF primary_type == "data_driven_debunking":
    primary_strategy = competitor_debunking_proof
  IF primary_type == "identity_driven":
    primary_strategy = personal_transformation_anchor
  IF primary_type == "proprietary_technology":
    primary_strategy = visual_demonstration (if available) OR institutional_stacking
  IF primary_type == "accidental_discovery" OR "historical_ancient_secret":
    primary_strategy = escalating_proof_ladder
  DEFAULT:
    primary_strategy = institutional_stacking (most versatile)
```

### Step 2: Select and Sequence Proof Elements

```
FROM proof_inventory, SELECT proof elements for Phase 5:

MINIMUM: 3 proof elements (across all categories)
OPTIMAL: 5-7 proof elements
MAXIMUM: 10 (beyond this, proof becomes repetitive)

SELECTION CRITERIA:
  - Relevance to mechanism (directly supports mechanism claim): REQUIRED
  - Strength score (if available): prefer highest scores
  - Category diversity: include at least 2 categories
  - Source credibility: institutional > clinical > expert > statistical > testimonial
  - Freshness: recent studies preferred when available
  - Specificity: specific numbers preferred over vague claims

SEQUENCE based on strategy:

  IF institutional_stacking:
    Order: Most prestigious institution first → cascade through others
    Pacing: Rapid — 1-2 sentences per institution
    Pattern: "Institution found... confirmed by Institution 2... validated by..."

  IF escalating_proof_ladder:
    Order: Weakest (but relatable) → increasingly impressive → landmark
    Pacing: Moderate — 2-4 sentences per rung
    Pattern: "First... then... but the real proof came when..."

  IF self_proving_mechanism:
    Order: Logical argument first → selective institutional support
    Pacing: Slow build of logical argument, quick institutional confirmation
    Pattern: "Think about it... [logic]. And the research confirms..."

  IF personal_transformation_anchor:
    Order: Transformation story → supporting institutional evidence
    Pacing: Story first (3-8 sentences) → brief institutional backup
    Pattern: "[Person's story]. And the science backs this up..."

  IF competitor_debunking_proof:
    Order: Competitor failure data → mechanism success data
    Pacing: Moderate — equal weight to failure and success
    Pattern: "Studies show [competitor] fails... while [mechanism] succeeds..."

  IF visual_demonstration:
    Order: Setup the demonstration → reveal the results → context
    Pacing: Build anticipation → dramatic result reveal
    Pattern: "We tested it... the results? [dramatic data]"
```

### Step 3: Write Proof Element Mini-Narratives

```
FOR EACH selected proof element, WRITE a mini-narrative wrapper:

MINI-NARRATIVE STRUCTURE (3-6 sentences per proof element):

  SENTENCE 1 — Context Setter:
    "Researchers at [institution] set out to test whether [mechanism claim]..."
    "In [year], a team of scientists published a study that..."
    "[Person name], a [credential], had been skeptical until..."
    Purpose: Set the scene, establish credibility of the source

  SENTENCE 2-3 — The Finding:
    "[Specific finding with numbers, percentages, measurements]"
    Purpose: Deliver the proof content with specificity
    RULE: Must include at least one specific number or measurement
    Examples:
      "67% of participants saw measurable improvement within 8 weeks"
      "AMPK activity increased by 52% in the treatment group"
      "Brain scans showed a 3.2x increase in Theta wave production"

  SENTENCE 4 — Connection to Mechanism:
    "This confirmed what the [mechanism] research predicted..."
    "In other words, [mechanism name] was doing exactly what the
    science said it would..."
    Purpose: Explicitly link this proof back to the mechanism

  SENTENCE 5-6 (optional) — Emotional Implication:
    "For the men in the study, this meant [personal impact]..."
    "What this means for you is [reader-relevant implication]..."
    Purpose: Make the proof FEEL relevant, not just factual

CRITICAL RULES FOR MINI-NARRATIVES:
  - Every proof claim MUST be traceable to proof-inventory
  - No fabricated institutions, studies, or statistics
  - Each element must name its source (institution, journal, researcher)
  - Numbers must be specific (not "studies show" or "research proves")
  - Mini-narratives must flow into each other (not feel like a list)
  - Transition between proof elements: "And that's not all..."
    / "But the most impressive evidence comes from..."
    / "Perhaps most convincingly..."
```

### Step 4: Weave Proof Into Narrative Flow

```
ASSEMBLE proof element mini-narratives into continuous prose:

OPENING TRANSITION (from Phase 4):
  "And the proof is even more impressive than the science behind it."
  "But don't take the theory alone. The evidence speaks for itself."
  "Now, here's where it gets exciting. Because [mechanism name]
  isn't just a theory. It's been tested."
  Purpose: Signal shift from explanation to proof

PROOF BODY:
  Deploy proof elements in sequenced order per strategy
  Connect each element to the next with transition language
  Maintain emotional arc progression (Phase 5 target from 1.3)
  Reference mechanism name and metaphor from Phase 4

  WEAVING TECHNIQUES:
    - Interleave proof categories (don't cluster all institutional together)
    - Alternate between data and story (number, then implication)
    - Build momentum — each proof element should feel MORE convincing
    - Use mechanism name as connective tissue ("And AMPK was at the center
      of every result...")

CLOSING TRANSITION (toward product bridge):
  Do NOT pitch the product yet
  DO create readiness for the product bridge (3.3)
  Pattern: "With this much evidence, the question became: how do you
    [activate/access/deliver] [mechanism name] reliably?"
  Pattern: "The science was clear. The proof was overwhelming.
    Now the challenge was making [mechanism] available to everyone."
  Purpose: Natural handoff toward product without pitching

TRACEABILITY:
  FOR EACH proof claim in the draft:
    CREATE trace_entry:
      claim_text: "exact text from draft"
      source_proof_element_id: "ID from proof inventory"
      institution: string
      data_point: string
    APPEND to traceability_log
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Minimum 3 proof elements** — each with specific source and data
2. **Strategy followed** — proof sequence matches selected integration strategy
3. **All organic** — proof woven into narrative prose, not listed
4. **All traceable** — every claim maps to proof-inventory element
5. **Specific numbers present** — percentages, measurements, timeframes
6. **Emotional connection maintained** — proof carries feeling, not just facts
7. **Forward momentum** — Phase 5 ending naturally leads toward product
8. **Mechanism name referenced** — proof connects back to named mechanism

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Symptom | Resolution |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Proof list format | Bullet points or numbered studies | Rewrite as continuous narrative with transitions |
| Untraceable claim | Statistic not in proof inventory | Remove or replace with traceable element |
| All same category | Only institutional, no variety | Add testimonial or statistical element for diversity |
| Vague proof | "Studies show..." without specifics | Replace with named institution + specific number |
| Proof disconnected from mechanism | Evidence doesn't reference mechanism name | Add explicit connection sentence after each proof element |
| Too much proof | >10 elements making section bloated | Select strongest 5-7, cut weakest |
| No emotional weight | Reads like academic abstract | Add reader-relevant implication after each data point |
| Product pitched in Phase 5 | "That's why we created [product]" | Remove product reference — Phase 5 is mechanism proof only |
