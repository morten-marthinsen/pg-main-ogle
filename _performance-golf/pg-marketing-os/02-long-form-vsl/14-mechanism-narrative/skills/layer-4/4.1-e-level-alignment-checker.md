# Skill 14 — Microskill 4.1: E-Level Alignment Checker

**Skill:** 11 — Mechanism Narrative
**Microskill:** 4.1 — E-Level Alignment Checker
**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Position:** First microskill in Layer 4
**Type:** Strategic Validation
**Dependencies:** 3.4-narrative-flow-polisher (polished narrative), 0.2-tier1-intelligence-loader (e_level_matrix), 0.4-checkpoint (confirmed e_level)
**Output:** e-level-alignment-report.json

---

## PURPOSE

Validates that the polished mechanism narrative fully matches the mechanism's E-level strategy. Each E-level (E2 through E5) demands specific narrative characteristics — explanation depth, claim amplification, countersell density, naming sophistication, proof density, and section length. This checker ensures the narrative is not under-delivering for higher E-levels (E4-E5 need maximum depth) or over-reaching for lower E-levels (E2-E3 should be more direct). A misaligned narrative undermines the entire mechanism strategy.

---

## IDENTITY

**What this IS:**
- A strategic alignment validator that checks narrative against E-level requirements
- A depth and sophistication calibrator that ensures appropriate complexity
- A guardrail that prevents under-reaching (lazy) or over-reaching (pompous) narratives

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a content generator — validates, does not write
- Not a grammar checker — validates strategy, not prose quality
- Not a final assembler — that is 4.4's job
- Not a creative evaluator — checks structural alignment, not subjective quality

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load E-Level Requirements

```
LOAD e_level_matrix[confirmed_e_level]:

E2 — New Application:
  narrative_approach: "Straightforward explanation, heavy proof stacking"
  claim_style: "AMPLIFIED — biggest claims, most direct"
  explanation_depth: "Moderate — mechanism is clear, emphasis on RESULTS"
  countersell_depth: "3-4 competing solutions debunked"
  proof_density: "Heavy — E2 needs MOST proof (>= 5 elements)"
  naming_sophistication: "Direct — scientific or branded, not metaphorical"
  typical_section_length: "Shorter — 600-1200 words total mechanism narrative"
  paradigm_shift_content: "NONE — E2 does not attempt worldview change"
  metaphor_requirement: "1-2 simple metaphors sufficient"
  binary_naming: "Not required"

E3 — Reframe:
  narrative_approach: "Strongest metaphor chains, heavy root cause revelation"
  claim_style: "Reframed — familiar problem, new cause, same solution category"
  explanation_depth: "Significant — the 'aha' comes from seeing familiar differently"
  countersell_depth: "4-7 competing explanations reframed or debunked"
  proof_density: "Moderate-heavy — 4-6 proof elements"
  naming_sophistication: "Moderate — metaphorical rename or historical origin"
  typical_section_length: "Medium — 1000-1800 words total mechanism narrative"
  paradigm_shift_content: "Minimal — reframes existing paradigm, doesn't replace it"
  metaphor_requirement: "2-4 metaphors with at least one strong analogy chain"
  binary_naming: "Optional but effective"

E4 — Proprietary:
  narrative_approach: "Maximum explanation depth, most sophisticated naming"
  claim_style: "Proprietary — unique to this product/system/formula"
  explanation_depth: "Deep — cross-domain analogies, visual demonstrations,
    multiple detail layers"
  countersell_depth: "5-7 competing approaches shown inadequate"
  proof_density: "Moderate — 4-6 proof elements, quality over quantity"
  naming_sophistication: "High — proprietary branded or metaphorical rename
    with scientific label backup"
  typical_section_length: "Long — 1400-2400 words total mechanism narrative"
  paradigm_shift_content: "Moderate — may challenge assumptions but within
    existing framework"
  metaphor_requirement: "3+ metaphors, at least one analogy chain,
    cross-domain analogies valued"
  binary_naming: "Effective but not mandatory"

E5 — Paradigm Shift:
  narrative_approach: "Entirely new worldview installation"
  claim_style: "Paradigm — completely new framework of understanding"
  explanation_depth: "Maximum — must dismantle old framework before building new"
  countersell_depth: "7+ — must invalidate ENTIRE previous worldview"
  proof_density: "Lower — mechanism explanation itself functions as proof.
    2-4 institutional elements sufficient"
  naming_sophistication: "Maximum — binary naming ESSENTIAL (good vs evil,
    rich vs poor, master vs slave)"
  typical_section_length: "Longest — 2000-3500 words total mechanism narrative"
  paradigm_shift_content: "DOMINANT — the entire narrative IS a paradigm shift"
  metaphor_requirement: "Multiple binary metaphors, framework metaphor mandatory"
  binary_naming: "MANDATORY — villain name vs mechanism name"

SET requirements = e_level_matrix[confirmed_e_level]
```

### Step 2: Check Explanation Depth

```
MEASURE explanation depth in polished narrative:

DEPTH INDICATORS:
  1. Detail layer count in Phase 4:
     Count distinct detail layers (each new fact/concept = 1 layer)
     E2 target: 2-3 layers
     E3 target: 3-5 layers
     E4 target: 5-8 layers
     E5 target: 6-10 layers

  2. Scientific terminology density:
     Count unique scientific terms introduced and defined
     E2 target: 1-3 terms
     E3 target: 3-5 terms
     E4 target: 5-8 terms
     E5 target: 4-7 terms (fewer than E4 — paradigm needs clarity over complexity)

  3. Cross-domain analogy presence:
     Count analogies that reference domains outside the mechanism's native domain
     E2 target: 0-1 (not needed)
     E3 target: 1-2
     E4 target: 2-4 (valued)
     E5 target: 1-3 (framework analogies, not cross-domain)

  4. Mechanism explanation word count:
     Phase 4 word count as percentage of total
     E2: 25-35% (results-focused, not explanation-focused)
     E3: 30-40% (moderate explanation)
     E4: 35-45% (deep explanation)
     E5: 30-40% (framework installation, not pure explanation)

SCORE explanation_depth_alignment: 1-10
  10: All indicators match E-level targets
  8-9: Most indicators match, minor deviations
  6-7: Some indicators off, but overall direction correct
  < 6: Significant misalignment — narrative too shallow or too deep for E-level
```

### Step 3: Check Claim Amplification

```
MEASURE claim amplification level:

SCAN for claim intensity markers:
  AMPLIFIED (E2 markers):
    "The most powerful..." / "Scientifically proven to..." /
    "Works for everyone..." / Direct result claims
  REFRAMED (E3 markers):
    "The real reason..." / "What actually causes..." /
    "Not what you think..." / Perspective shift language
  PROPRIETARY (E4 markers):
    "The only [product] with..." / "Proprietary..." / "Engineered..." /
    "Unlike anything else..." / Uniqueness claims
  PARADIGM (E5 markers):
    "Everything you knew was wrong..." / "A completely new..." /
    "Forget everything you learned..." / Total framework replacement

VERIFY claim style matches E-level:
  IF E2 narrative uses paradigm claims: OVER-REACHING — FAIL
  IF E5 narrative uses E2-style direct claims: UNDER-DELIVERING — FAIL
  IF claim style matches E-level: PASS

SPECIFIC CHECKS:
  E2: Are claims bold and direct? Are results emphasized over mechanism?
  E3: Are claims framed as reframes (new perspective on known problem)?
  E4: Are claims proprietary (only available through this mechanism/product)?
  E5: Do claims challenge the reader's entire worldview?

SCORE claim_alignment: 1-10
```

### Step 4: Check Countersell Depth

```
COUNT competing solutions, approaches, or beliefs debunked in narrative:

SCAN for countersell instances:
  - Explicit competitor mentions (products, approaches, beliefs named)
  - Implicit countersells ("Unlike conventional approaches...")
  - Framework dismissals ("Everything you were taught about [X]...")
  - Villain-based countersells (villain IS the competing approach)

COUNT total countersell instances

COMPARE to E-level target:
  E2: 3-4 countersells expected
  E3: 4-7 countersells expected
  E4: 5-7 countersells expected
  E5: 7+ countersells expected (must invalidate entire previous worldview)

VERIFY:
  IF count < E-level minimum: UNDER-SELLING — need more countersells
  IF count > E-level maximum + 3: OVER-SELLING — may feel aggressive
  IF count within range: PASS

SCORE countersell_alignment: 1-10
```

### Step 5: Check Naming Sophistication

```
EVALUATE mechanism naming against E-level requirements:

E2 CHECK:
  - Name should be direct and clear (scientific or branded)
  - Avoid over-sophisticated naming (no binary frameworks needed)
  - Name communicates function clearly
  PASS: Simple, clear, functional name

E3 CHECK:
  - Name should use metaphorical rename or historical origin
  - Moderate sophistication — memorable and reframing
  - Binary naming optional but effective
  PASS: Metaphorical or historical name with moderate sophistication

E4 CHECK:
  - Name should be proprietary branded or scientific with metaphorical backup
  - High sophistication — signals innovation and uniqueness
  - May combine scientific label with metaphorical rename
  PASS: Proprietary/sophisticated name that signals innovation

E5 CHECK:
  - Binary naming MANDATORY (mechanism name vs villain name)
  - Maximum sophistication — names create framework
  - Names should be emotionally loaded (not neutral)
  - Names create identity contrast (aspiration vs fear)
  PASS: Binary naming present, emotionally loaded, framework-creating

SCORE naming_alignment: 1-10
  10: Naming matches E-level perfectly
  5: Naming exists but doesn't match E-level sophistication
  < 5: Naming misaligned — too simple or too complex for E-level
```

### Step 6: Check Section Length

```
COMPARE total mechanism narrative word count to E-level range:

  E2 range: 600-1200 words
  E3 range: 1000-1800 words
  E4 range: 1400-2400 words
  E5 range: 2000-3500 words

  actual_word_count = total from polished narrative

  IF actual within range: PASS
  IF actual < range minimum:
    UNDER-DELIVERING: "Narrative is [N] words short of E[X] minimum.
      May feel rushed or underdeveloped for this sophistication level."
    Recommendation: Expand Phase 4 explanation or Phase 5 proof
  IF actual > range maximum:
    OVER-DELIVERING: "Narrative is [N] words over E[X] maximum.
      May lose reader attention for this sophistication level."
    Recommendation: Trim Phase 1 or Phase 4 detail layers

SCORE length_alignment: 1-10
```

### Step 7: Check Paradigm Shift Content (E5 Specific)

```
IF e_level == "E5":
  MANDATORY CHECKS:
    1. Old framework explicitly dismantled?
       Scan for: "Everything you believed about [X]..." /
       "The model you were taught..." / "Conventional wisdom says..."
       followed by: "...is wrong" / "...is incomplete" / "...was designed to..."
       PASS: Old framework dismantled

    2. New framework explicitly installed?
       Scan for: Binary naming (X vs Y), new worldview statement,
       replacement framework
       PASS: New framework clear and distinct from old

    3. Transition from old to new is complete?
       Reader should not be able to hold BOTH frameworks
       Old framework must feel impossible to return to
       PASS: Binary choice — old framework feels obsolete

    4. Countersell eliminates ENTIRE previous worldview?
       Not just 1-2 alternatives — the WHOLE paradigm
       PASS: Every element of old worldview challenged

  IF any E5 check FAILS:
    CRITICAL: "E5 narrative must fully replace reader's worldview.
      Missing elements undermine the entire paradigm shift."

ELSE IF e_level IN [E2, E3, E4]:
  CHECK: Narrative does NOT attempt paradigm shift
    Scan for E5-style framework replacement language
    IF found: OVER-REACHING — "Paradigm shift language found in E[X]
      narrative. This risks seeming grandiose for this sophistication level."
    Recommendation: Replace paradigm language with E-level-appropriate framing
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **All dimensions checked** — explanation depth, claims, countersells, naming, length, paradigm content
2. **Scoring accurate** — reflects actual narrative content, not assumed
3. **E-level requirements sourced from TIER1** — not generic assumptions
4. **Over-reaching caught** — lower E-levels not using paradigm shift language
5. **Under-delivering caught** — higher E-levels not skimping on depth
6. **Recommendations actionable** — each adjustment specifies what to change and where
7. **Overall alignment >= 7.0 to pass** — narrative must be strategically aligned

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Symptom | Resolution |
|---------|---------|------------|
| E5 without binary naming | Paradigm shift narrative lacks X vs Y contrast | Add binary naming — mandatory for E5 |
| E2 with paradigm language | "Everything you knew was wrong" in simple mechanism | Remove paradigm language, use direct claim style |
| Explanation too shallow for E4 | Only 2 detail layers for proprietary mechanism | Expand Phase 4 with additional specificity layers |
| Countersell count too low for E5 | Only 3 countersells in paradigm shift | Add countersells — E5 needs 7+ to dismantle worldview |
| Narrative too long for E2 | 2500 words for a simple new application | Trim to E2 range (600-1200 words) |
| Naming too simple for E4 | Generic name for proprietary mechanism | Strengthen naming to proprietary branded architecture |
