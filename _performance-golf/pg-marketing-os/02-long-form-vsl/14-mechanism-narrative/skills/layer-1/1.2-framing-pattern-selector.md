# Skill 14 — Microskill 1.2: Framing Pattern Selector

**Skill:** 11 — Mechanism Narrative
**Microskill:** 1.2 — Framing Pattern Selector
**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Strategic Classification)
**Position:** Second microskill in Layer 1
**Type:** Strategic Selection
**Dependencies:** 0.2-tier1-intelligence-loader (framing_patterns index), 1.1-narrative-type-classifier (primary_type)
**Output:** framing-selection.json

---

## PURPOSE

Selects the primary and secondary framing patterns that will govern HOW the mechanism narrative is positioned to the reader. While the narrative type (from 1.1) determines WHAT kind of story is told, the framing pattern determines the LENS through which the story is perceived. The frame controls signal words, emotional coloring, authority positioning, and the implicit promise of the narrative. Every mechanism narrative uses a primary frame as its dominant lens and a secondary frame for depth and variety.

---

## IDENTITY

**What this IS:**
- A strategic selector that pairs framing patterns with the classified narrative type
- A signal-word and emotional-effect mapper that downstream writers use for tone calibration
- The positioning layer that determines how the mechanism FEELS to the reader

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a writer — does not produce prose
- Not a narrative type re-classifier — accepts 1.1's classification as given
- Not a single-frame enforcer — always selects primary AND secondary

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load the 8 Framing Patterns

```
LOAD from tier1_index.framing_patterns:

FRAME 1: hidden_truth
  Signal: "This was always true, but no one told you"
  Emotional Effect: Righteous indignation + curiosity
  Signal Words: ["hidden", "concealed", "buried", "overlooked", "right under
    your nose", "been there all along", "nobody told you"]
  Authority Position: Revealer — the narrator exposes what was always available
  Implicit Promise: "Now that you know, you can act"
  Compatible Types: [scientific_revelation, historical_ancient_secret, paradigm_shift]
  TIER1 Example: LA-3 — "This enzyme has been inside you your whole life,
    but modern life has been SUPPRESSING it"

FRAME 2: suppressed_secret
  Signal: "Powerful people actively kept this from you"
  Emotional Effect: Anger + vindication + urgency
  Signal Words: ["suppressed", "silenced", "banned", "censored", "they don't
    want you to know", "kept secret", "demanded", "powerful interests"]
  Authority Position: Whistleblower — the narrator risks something to tell you
  Implicit Promise: "You're about to get what they tried to keep from you"
  Compatible Types: [paradigm_shift, scientific_revelation]
  TIER1 Example: BBW — "Billionaire investors DEMANDED this technology
    be kept from the public"

FRAME 3: scientific_breakthrough
  Signal: "Cutting-edge research has cracked the code"
  Emotional Effect: Awe + trust + excitement
  Signal Words: ["researchers discovered", "study reveals", "scientists found",
    "peer-reviewed", "published in", "clinical trial", "Nobel Prize"]
  Authority Position: Science Messenger — narrator brings frontier knowledge
  Implicit Promise: "Science has solved this. Here's the solution."
  Compatible Types: [scientific_revelation, data_driven_debunking, proprietary_technology]
  TIER1 Example: LA-3 — "Yale researchers discovered an enzyme that..." /
    DIM — "A metabolite that Harvard researchers identified..."

FRAME 4: expert_revelation
  Signal: "A world-class authority shares insider knowledge"
  Emotional Effect: Privileged access + trust + deference
  Signal Words: ["world-renowned", "leading expert", "insider", "exclusive",
    "30 years of research", "top of their field", "pioneered"]
  Authority Position: Expert Authority — narrator IS the authority or channels one
  Implicit Promise: "You're getting access to elite-level knowledge"
  Compatible Types: [scientific_revelation, identity_driven, proprietary_technology]
  TIER1 Example: Gundry — "Dr. Steven Gundry, former head of cardiothoracic
    surgery at Loma Linda University Medical Center"

FRAME 5: engineering_innovation
  Signal: "We built something that never existed before"
  Emotional Effect: Excitement + novelty + trust in engineering
  Signal Words: ["engineered", "designed", "developed", "patented", "built",
    "calibrated", "precision", "technology", "innovation"]
  Authority Position: Creator/Engineer — narrator built the solution
  Implicit Promise: "This is new, designed specifically for this problem"
  Compatible Types: [proprietary_technology]
  TIER1 Example: SF1 — "Square Face Technology — a precision-engineered
    club face geometry that..."

FRAME 6: personal_transformation
  Signal: "I was broken. This fixed me. Now I teach it."
  Emotional Effect: Empathy + hope + connection
  Signal Words: ["I struggled", "I was diagnosed", "I discovered",
    "changed my life", "I want to share", "my journey", "my story"]
  Authority Position: Fellow Sufferer — narrator has walked this path
  Implicit Promise: "If it worked for me, it can work for you"
  Compatible Types: [identity_driven, accidental_discovery]
  TIER1 Example: Superbrain — "I was called 'The Boy With The Broken Brain.'
    A childhood head injury left me struggling to learn..."

FRAME 7: ancient_wisdom_validated
  Signal: "The ancients knew this. Science confirms it."
  Emotional Effect: Wonder + dual credibility + timelessness
  Signal Words: ["ancient", "centuries-old", "traditional", "passed down",
    "now confirmed", "modern science validates", "time-tested"]
  Authority Position: Bridge — narrator connects past wisdom to present science
  Implicit Promise: "This has always worked. Now we know WHY."
  Compatible Types: [historical_ancient_secret, accidental_discovery]
  TIER1 Example: Montezuma — "In a remote village... men in their 70s and 80s
    were performing like men half their age" / i-flex — "Danish rosehips,
    used for centuries in Scandinavian folk medicine"

FRAME 8: competitor_debunking
  Signal: "Everything else is proven wrong. Here's what works."
  Emotional Effect: Frustration validated + relief + new hope
  Signal Words: ["doesn't work", "proven wrong", "failed", "outdated",
    "dangerous", "instead", "the truth about", "what they got wrong"]
  Authority Position: Truth-Teller — narrator exposes failed approaches
  Implicit Promise: "Stop wasting time on what fails. This actually works."
  Compatible Types: [data_driven_debunking, paradigm_shift]
  TIER1 Example: i-flex — "Glucosamine — the $2 billion supplement that
    clinical trials prove does NOTHING for your joints"
```

### Step 2: Retrieve Compatible Frames for Narrative Type

```
LOOKUP cross_references.narrative_type_to_frame[primary_type]:

  scientific_revelation → [hidden_truth, scientific_breakthrough, expert_revelation]
  accidental_discovery → [personal_transformation, ancient_wisdom_validated, hidden_truth]
  historical_ancient_secret → [ancient_wisdom_validated, hidden_truth, scientific_breakthrough]
  paradigm_shift → [suppressed_secret, hidden_truth, competitor_debunking]
  proprietary_technology → [engineering_innovation, scientific_breakthrough, expert_revelation]
  identity_driven → [personal_transformation, expert_revelation]
  data_driven_debunking → [competitor_debunking, scientific_breakthrough, hidden_truth]

SET compatible_frames = retrieved list for primary_type
LOG: "[N] frames compatible with [primary_type]"
```

### Step 3: Score Compatible Frames

```
FOR EACH frame IN compatible_frames:

  SCORE 1 — E-Level Alignment (0-10):
    IF e_level == "E5" AND frame IN [suppressed_secret, hidden_truth]:
      score = 10 (conspiracy/paradigm frames essential for E5)
    IF e_level == "E4" AND frame IN [scientific_breakthrough, engineering_innovation, expert_revelation]:
      score = 9 (authority and innovation frames match proprietary)
    IF e_level == "E3" AND frame IN [hidden_truth, ancient_wisdom_validated, personal_transformation]:
      score = 9 (reframe works through discovery/revelation)
    IF e_level == "E2" AND frame IN [scientific_breakthrough, competitor_debunking]:
      score = 9 (direct proof and contrast for new application)
    ELSE:
      score = 5 (neutral — neither boosted nor penalized)

  SCORE 2 — Evidence Fit (0-10):
    IF frame == "scientific_breakthrough" AND "institutional" IN available_evidence:
      score = 9
    IF frame == "competitor_debunking" AND "clinical" IN available_evidence:
      score = 9
    IF frame == "personal_transformation" AND "testimonial" IN available_evidence:
      score = 9
    IF frame == "expert_revelation" AND guru_authority_present:
      score = 10
    IF frame == "ancient_wisdom_validated" AND mechanism has historical origin:
      score = 9
    ELSE:
      score = 4

  SCORE 3 — Villain Compatibility (0-10):
    IF villain is systemic force AND frame IN [hidden_truth, suppressed_secret]:
      score = 9 (system hid the truth)
    IF villain is competing product AND frame IN [competitor_debunking]:
      score = 10 (direct debunk of villain)
    IF villain is false belief AND frame IN [hidden_truth, scientific_breakthrough]:
      score = 8 (science reveals what belief concealed)
    IF villain is industry AND frame IN [suppressed_secret]:
      score = 10 (industry suppression narrative)
    ELSE:
      score = 5

  SCORE 4 — Emotional Continuity (0-10):
    MAP emotional_state to compatible frame emotions:
      "fear" → [hidden_truth, scientific_breakthrough] (fear → discovery → relief)
      "shame" → [personal_transformation, suppressed_secret] (shame → it wasn't your fault)
      "frustration" → [competitor_debunking, engineering_innovation] (frustration → better exists)
      "curiosity" → [hidden_truth, ancient_wisdom_validated] (curiosity → revelation)
      "anger" → [suppressed_secret, competitor_debunking] (anger → vindication)
    IF frame matches emotional_state mapping: score = 9
    ELSE: score = 4

  TOTAL_SCORE = (SCORE_1 * 0.3) + (SCORE_2 * 0.25) + (SCORE_3 * 0.25) + (SCORE_4 * 0.2)
```

### Step 4: Select Primary and Secondary Frames

```
RANK compatible_frames by TOTAL_SCORE descending

SET primary_frame = highest scoring frame
SET secondary_frame = second highest scoring frame

VALIDATE:
  - primary_frame differs from secondary_frame: PASS/FAIL
  - primary_frame is in compatible list for narrative type: PASS/FAIL
  - primary and secondary create complementary (not contradictory) positioning:
    CONFLICTING PAIRS (cannot be primary + secondary together):
      - suppressed_secret + scientific_breakthrough
        (conspiracy vs. establishment science — contradicts)
      - personal_transformation + competitor_debunking
        (empathy vs. aggression — tonal clash)
    IF conflict detected: DROP secondary, promote third-ranked frame

LOG: "Primary frame: [name] (score: [X]). Secondary: [name] (score: [Y])."
```

### Step 5: Map Frame Signal Words and Effects

```
FOR primary_frame AND secondary_frame:

  COMPILE signal_word_library:
    primary_signals: [frame-specific signal words]
    secondary_signals: [frame-specific signal words]
    combined_available: [merged, deduplicated list]

  COMPILE emotional_effects:
    primary_effect: [frame-specific emotional effect]
    secondary_effect: [frame-specific emotional effect]
    arc_contribution: "How these effects feed into the emotional arc"

  COMPILE authority_positioning:
    primary_authority: [frame-specific authority position]
    secondary_authority: [frame-specific authority position]
    combined_stance: "How the narrator presents themselves"

  COMPILE implicit_promises:
    primary_promise: [frame-specific implicit promise]
    secondary_promise: [frame-specific implicit promise]
    combined_promise: "The combined implication of both frames"
```

### Step 6: Define Frame Application Rules

```
SET application_rules:

  primary_frame_usage:
    - Opens the mechanism narrative section
    - Dominates Phase 3 (naming reveal) and Phase 4 (explanation)
    - Provides the dominant signal words throughout
    - Sets the authority positioning for the narrator
    - Frequency: 60-70% of framing signals use primary frame

  secondary_frame_usage:
    - Supports transitions between phases
    - Adds depth to Phase 5 (proof integration)
    - Provides variety to prevent single-note positioning
    - May dominate Phase 1 (problem amplification) if more emotionally acute
    - Frequency: 30-40% of framing signals use secondary frame

  blending_rules:
    - Never use both frames in the same sentence
    - Primary opens paragraphs, secondary can close them
    - Transition between frames at phase boundaries (natural shift points)
    - If frames share signal words, those words carry double weight
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Two frames selected** — always a primary AND secondary, never just one
2. **Frames compatible with narrative type** — both appear in the compatible list from cross-references
3. **No conflicting pair** — primary and secondary do not create contradictory positioning
4. **Signal words populated** — each frame contributes its full signal word library
5. **Phase assignments clear** — downstream writers know which frame dominates which phase
6. **Emotional effects map to arc** — frame emotions align with the emotional state handed off from Skill 16
7. **Rationale is specific** — explains why these frames, not just which frames

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Symptom | Resolution |
|---------|---------|------------|
| No compatible frames found | Cross-reference returns empty list | Use default frames for e_level (E2: scientific_breakthrough, E3: hidden_truth, E4: engineering_innovation, E5: suppressed_secret) |
| All frames score identically | No differentiation in scoring | Use positional priority from compatible list (first = primary) |
| Conflicting pair selected | Primary and secondary contradict | Drop secondary, promote third-ranked frame |
| Signal words overlap completely | Primary and secondary have identical words | Acceptable — means strong thematic alignment. Add differentiation through emotional effects |
| Frame doesn't match villain | Villain type incompatible with selected frame | Adjust villain_compatibility score, may not change selection but flag for 3.2 |
| Secondary frame scores zero | Terrible fit | Select from adjacent narrative type's compatible frames |
