**Skill:** 17-close

**Position:** Fourth microskill in Layer 1
**Persona:** Legendary Copywriter (Primary), Alex Rivera (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

Selects the P.S. strategy from Makepeace's 6 P.S. techniques and assigns specific content to each P.S. entry. The P.S. is the second-most-read section of any promotion — more people read the P.S. than read the body copy. This makes it PRIME selling real estate. Makepeace's methodology defines six distinct techniques for P.S. sections, each serving a different persuasive function. The wrong P.S. strategy wastes the highest-impact copy position in the entire promotion.

This microskill analyzes available assets (testimonials, unrevealed bonuses, urgency justifications, guarantee boldness) and matches them to the 6 techniques, then selects 1-3 P.S. entries that maximize the close's final impact. The human checkpoint (0.4) has already confirmed the P.S. strategy direction — this microskill executes that confirmation with detailed content assignment for each P.S. entry.

A good P.S. section can take almost a full page. It is NOT an afterthought or administrative footnote. It is the last piece of selling real estate, and the best closers save their BEST ammunition for this position.

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The P.S. strategy selector from 6 Makepeace techniques

- A multi-P.S. architect — planning 1-3 P.S. entries with distinct functions
- A content assigner — specifying what goes in each P.S. entry
- A "save best for last" enforcer — ensuring strongest ammo reaches the P.S.
- The strategic blueprint provider for 3.3-ps-section-writer

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A P.S. writer (that is 3.3 — this selects and assigns, 3.3 writes)
- A CTA planner (that is 1.3 — though P.S. entries contain CTAs)
- A guarantee writer (that is 2.2 — though P.S. may restate the guarantee)
- A testimonial selector (proof comes from 0.1 — this assigns testimonials to P.S.)
- A bonus creator — it works with existing bonus inventory

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Confirm P.S. Strategy from Human Checkpoint

1. Read confirmed P.S. techniques and count from checkpoint response
2. Validate each technique is one of the 6 Makepeace P.S. techniques:
   - **(a) Fast-reply bonus** — urgency-driven bonus for immediate action
   - **(b) Call-in bonus** — phone-order exclusive bonus (requires phone number)
   - **(c) Additional FREE bonus revealed** — surprise bonus not mentioned earlier
   - **(d) Most powerful testimonials** — strongest social proof saved for P.S.
   - **(e) Additional reasons to act NOW** — urgency justification with guarantee tie-in
   - **(f) Restate guarantee** — bold guarantee restatement as final reassurance
3. Validate P.S. count: 1-3 entries (1 for focused impact, 2-3 for cascade effect)

### Step 2: Audit Available Assets for Each Technique

4. For each confirmed technique, verify assets are available:

   **Technique (a) — Fast-Reply Bonus:**
   - Requires: A bonus that can be credibly tied to fast action
   - Check: Is there a bonus in inventory NOT yet assigned to benefit summary?
   - Check: Can urgency be justified (supply, time, price)?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if bonus is unrevealed + urgency is credible
   - Asset quality: MEDIUM if bonus is already mentioned but urgency is fresh
   - Asset quality: LOW if no credible urgency exists (reconsider technique)

   **Technique (b) — Call-In Bonus:**
   - Requires: Phone number confirmed + phone-exclusive bonus
   - Check: Did human provide phone number at checkpoint?
   - Check: Is there a bonus that can be phone-exclusive?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if phone number + exclusive bonus available
   - Asset quality: NOT AVAILABLE if no phone number (skip technique)

   **Technique (c) — Additional FREE Bonus Revealed:**
   - Requires: At least 1 bonus NOT mentioned in the offer or benefit summary
   - Check: Are there unrevealed bonuses in the inventory?
   - Check: Is the bonus genuinely valuable (not filler)?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if unrevealed bonus has strong fascination potential
   - Asset quality: MEDIUM if bonus needs to be carved from existing content
   - Asset quality: LOW if no unrevealed bonus available

   **Technique (d) — Most Powerful Testimonials:**
   - Requires: At least 2 strong testimonials with specific results
   - Check: How many testimonials in proof_assets.top_testimonials?
   - Check: Are these testimonials specific (names, numbers, timeframes)?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if 3+ specific testimonials available
   - Asset quality: MEDIUM if 2 testimonials with moderate specificity
   - Asset quality: LOW if <2 testimonials or all generic

   **Technique (e) — Additional Reasons to Act NOW:**
   - Requires: Credible urgency justifications not already used in urgency section
   - Check: Supply scarcity? Demand data? Price increase? Deadline?
   - Check: Can reasons tie into guarantee ("you have nothing to lose AND...")?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if 2+ fresh urgency reasons + guarantee tie-in
   - Asset quality: MEDIUM if 1 urgency reason available
   - Asset quality: LOW if all urgency already deployed in main close

   **Technique (f) — Restate Guarantee:**
   - Always available — guarantee details always exist
   - Check: Is guarantee particularly bold (long duration, branded name, generous terms)?
   - Check: Can guarantee be presented in a NEW way (company character, risk quantification)?
   - Asset quality: HIGH if guarantee is bold + can be freshly framed
   - Asset quality: MEDIUM if guarantee is standard but can be restated with conviction
   - Asset quality: HIGH DEFAULT for Week 13 patterns — branded naming makes any guarantee P.S.-worthy

5. Flag any technique where asset quality is LOW — recommend substitution

### Step 3: Assign Content to Each P.S. Entry

6. For each P.S. entry (1-3), assign:

   **P.S. Entry Structure:**
   ```
   ps_entry:
     number: integer  # P.S. = 1, P.P.S. = 2, P.P.P.S. = 3
     technique: string  # One of (a)-(f)
     primary_content: string  # What the P.S. is about
     supporting_detail: string  # Specifics, numbers, names
     emotional_hook: string  # What emotion drives this P.S.
     cta_embedded: boolean  # Does this P.S. contain a CTA?
     word_count_target: string  # Per-entry target
   ```

7. Content assignment rules:
   - **P.S. (first entry):** Deploy STRONGEST ammo. This is the most-read P.S.
   - **P.P.S. (second entry):** Deploy second-strongest or complementary technique
   - **P.P.P.S. (third entry):** Deploy urgency escalation or guarantee final word
   - Every P.S. entry should contain an embedded CTA (ask for the sale continues through P.S.)

8. Word count allocation per entry:
   - Single P.S.: 100-300 words (concentrated power)
   - P.S. in triple cascade: 80-200 words each (building sequence)
   - Total P.S. section: match word_count_target from theme selection

### Step 4: Design P.S. Emotional Sequence

9. If multiple P.S. entries, design the emotional sequence:

   **Common sequences:**
   - **Fear → Hope → Urgency:** P.S. = testimonials (hope from social proof), P.P.S. = urgency reasons (fear of missing), P.P.P.S. = guarantee (safety net)
   - **Surprise → Proof → Safety:** P.S. = surprise bonus (greed), P.P.S. = testimonials (validation), P.P.P.S. = guarantee restatement (confidence)
   - **Urgency → Greed → Confidence:** P.S. = fast-reply bonus (urgency + greed), P.P.S. = additional reasons (logical urgency), P.P.P.S. = guarantee (final confidence)

10. Ensure P.S. sequence DOES NOT duplicate the emotional flow of the main close sections
11. The P.S. section should feel like a "BONUS close" — additional persuasion, not repetition

### Step 5: Check P.S. Against Closing Theme

12. Verify P.S. strategy complements (not conflicts with) the primary closing theme:
    - **Crossroads + Testimonials P.S.:** Good — testimonials show others who chose the right path
    - **Logical Restatement + Guarantee P.S.:** Good — logical close capped with safety net
    - **USP Close + Surprise Bonus P.S.:** Good — exclusivity enhanced with exclusive bonus
    - **But WAIT There's More + Bonus P.S.:** Caution — bonus fatigue if close already stacked bonuses. Use testimonials instead.
    - **Don't Go Alone + Fast-Reply P.S.:** Good — urgency of needing expert help NOW

13. If conflict detected, recommend adjustment to human or substitute technique

### Step 6: Package P.S. Strategy Output

14. Compile the complete P.S. strategy:
    - Number of P.S. entries
    - Each entry with technique, content assignment, emotional hook, CTA presence, word count
    - Emotional sequence across entries
    - Theme compatibility check
    - Asset quality assessment
    - Instructions for 3.3-ps-section-writer

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: P.S. as Afterthought
- **Symptom:** P.S. contains weak content — a generic "remember, your order is protected..."
- **Cause:** Best ammo deployed in main close; nothing strong left for P.S.
- **Prevention:** Step 3 rule — deploy STRONGEST ammo first in P.S. Reserve best for here.
- **Recovery:** Reassign content — move strongest testimonial or boldest guarantee statement to P.S.

### Failure Mode 2: Technique Without Assets
- **Symptom:** "Most powerful testimonials" selected but only 1 weak testimonial exists
- **Cause:** Asset audit not performed before technique selection
- **Prevention:** Step 2 asset audit with quality ratings; LOW assets trigger substitution
- **Recovery:** Substitute with technique that matches available assets (e.g., switch to guarantee restatement)

### Failure Mode 3: P.S. Duplicates Main Close
- **Symptom:** P.S. restates the exact same urgency argument from the urgency section
- **Cause:** P.S. content not differentiated from main close sections
- **Prevention:** Step 4 ensures P.S. emotional sequence differs from main close flow
- **Recovery:** Reframe P.S. content with a FRESH angle — same urgency, different framing

### Failure Mode 4: Too Many P.S. Entries for Format
- **Symptom:** P.P.P.S. in a 300-word email close
- **Cause:** P.S. count not adjusted for campaign format
- **Prevention:** Format-based limits: email = 1 P.S., VSL/magalog = 1-3 P.S., advertorial = 1-2 P.S.
- **Recovery:** Reduce to format-appropriate count; consolidate content into fewer entries

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Health Supplement — Dual P.S. (Testimonials + Guarantee)

**Context:** Male vitality supplement, VSL, Crossroads theme, strong testimonials available.

**P.S. Strategy:**
```yaml
ps_strategy:
  ps_count: 2
  total_word_count_target: "200-350 words"

  ps_entries:
    - number: 1
      technique: "d"
      technique_label: "Most powerful testimonials"
      primary_content: "Feature Robert M. and James T. testimonials — specific results that validate the crossroads decision"
      supporting_detail: "Robert: 'felt 20 years younger in 3 weeks.' James: 'Wife couldn't believe the difference.'"
      emotional_hook: "Social proof resolves final doubt — others chose the right path and succeeded"
      cta_embedded: true
      cta_direction: "Join Robert, James, and thousands of men who already..."
      word_count_target: "120-200 words"
      asset_quality: "high"

    - number: 2
      technique: "f"
      technique_label: "Restate guarantee"
      primary_content: "Bold restatement of Triple-Back-Guarantee with company character statement"
      supporting_detail: "180 days, keep the supply, full refund, plus gift bottle. 'If we don't get you results, we don't deserve your money.'"
      emotional_hook: "Final safety net — dissolve remaining risk perception"
      cta_embedded: true
      cta_direction: "You have absolutely nothing to lose. Click below now."
      word_count_target: "80-150 words"
      asset_quality: "high"

  emotional_sequence: "Hope (testimonials prove it works) → Safety (guarantee removes all risk)"
  theme_compatibility: "compatible"
  theme_compatibility_notes: "Crossroads theme resolved through social proof (others chose wisely), then sealed with safety (guarantee)"
```
