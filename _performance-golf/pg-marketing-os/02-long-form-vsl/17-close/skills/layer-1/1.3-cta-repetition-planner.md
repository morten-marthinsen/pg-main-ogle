---

## PURPOSE

Plans the 6-10+ CTA repetitions that form the backbone of the Makepeace close. This is Makepeace Element 3: Ask for the Sale — the element Makepeace considers the most important of all six. The master closer asks for the sale REPEATEDLY, not once, not twice, but six to ten or more times throughout the close section. Each ask uses a different emotional appeal, different phrasing, different supporting reason, and appears at a different placement point in the close.

This microskill is the strategic planner — it assigns the emotional appeal (greed, fear, urgency, logic, exclusivity, social proof, compassion, hope), the specific phrase variation, the supporting reason, and the placement in the close (benefit summary, guarantee, theme section, urgency, P.S., checkout). It also plans sidebar CTAs if the format supports them. The planner ensures no two CTAs use the same emotion AND phrase combination, preventing the patronizing repetition that kills close effectiveness.

The CTA planner draws from the theme-specific emotional map (1.1), the CTA exclusion list (phrases already used upstream in Skill 13), and TIER1 CTA phrase variations for niche-appropriate language. The plan feeds directly into 2.4-cta-action-sequence-writer, which writes the actual prose.

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The CTA repetition architect — planning 6-10+ asks for the sale
- An emotional variety enforcer — no two CTAs share the same emotion + phrase combination
- A placement mapper — distributing CTAs across all close sections
- A phrase variation library — drawing from TIER1 patterns and Makepeace methodology
- A supporting-reason assigner — each CTA has a WHY behind the ask
- The upstream exclusion filter — avoiding CTA phrases already used in Skill 13

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A CTA writer (that is 2.4 — this plans, 2.4 writes)
- A closing theme selector (that is 1.1)
- A benefit summary planner (that is 1.2)
- A P.S. strategist (that is 1.4)
- An action instruction writer — action steps are part of 2.4's scope

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Set CTA Count Target

1. Read confirmed CTA target count from human checkpoint
2. Validate minimum: 6 CTAs (Makepeace minimum)
3. Set target range:
   - VSL: 8-10+ CTAs
   - Magalog: 6-8 CTAs
   - Email: 6-7 CTAs
   - Advertorial: 6-8 CTAs
4. If human specified a count, use it (as long as >= 6)

### Step 2: Define Emotional Appeal Categories

5. Draw from the theme-specific emotional map (1.1 output):
   - Each CTA is assigned ONE primary emotional appeal
   - Available emotions: greed, fear, urgency, logic, exclusivity, social_proof, compassion, hope, authority, excitement

6. Allocate emotions across the planned CTAs. For example, with 9 CTAs for a Crossroads-themed health VSL:
   - CTA 1: Greed (desire for the benefit)
   - CTA 2: Logic (makes sense to act)
   - CTA 3: Fear (consequences of inaction)
   - CTA 4: Hope (positive future visualization)
   - CTA 5: Urgency (limited supply/time)
   - CTA 6: Compassion (health concern for loved ones)
   - CTA 7: Social proof (others are already benefiting)
   - CTA 8: Exclusivity (only available here)
   - CTA 9: Fear + Urgency (final push combining two emotions)

7. Rule: No more than 2 CTAs share the same primary emotion
8. Rule: The final CTA should combine 2 emotions for maximum impact

### Step 3: Assign Phrase Variations

9. Build a phrase library from three sources:
   - **Makepeace core phrases:** "Go ahead and..." / "Don't hesitate. Not even for a second." / "Join me now and..." / "Please — if your [concern] means anything to you — let me hear from you right away!"
   - **TIER1 niche phrases:** Pull from vault patterns for this niche
   - **Week 13 phrases:** "Click the button below right now" / "Select your package below"

10. Assign one unique phrase ROOT to each CTA:
    - **Direct command:** "Go ahead and click the button below"
    - **Reason-attached question:** "Doesn't it make sense to..."
    - **Urgency imperative:** "Don't hesitate. Not even for a second."
    - **Emotional appeal:** "What are you waiting for?"
    - **Permission invite:** "Join me now and..."
    - **Fear plea:** "Please — if your [health/wealth] means anything to you..."
    - **Social validation:** "Join the thousands who have already..."
    - **Logical conclusion:** "Given everything you've seen today..."
    - **Opportunity frame:** "This is your chance to..."
    - **Decision prompt:** "The choice is yours — but I'd hate to see you..."

11. Filter against CTA exclusion list — remove any phrases already used in Skill 13
12. Rule: No two CTAs use the same phrase root

### Step 4: Assign Supporting Reasons

13. Each CTA needs a SUPPORTING REASON — the "why" behind the ask:

    **Reason categories:**
    - **Benefit-driven:** "...and start experiencing [specific benefit]"
    - **Risk-driven:** "...before [negative consequence] gets any worse"
    - **Value-driven:** "...and save [savings amount] off the regular price"
    - **Guarantee-driven:** "...you're completely protected by the [branded guarantee]"
    - **Scarcity-driven:** "...while supplies last" / "...before this offer expires"
    - **Social-driven:** "...just like [testimonial name] who experienced [result]"
    - **Time-driven:** "...and see results in as little as [timeframe]"
    - **Authority-driven:** "...recommended by [expert/authority]"
    - **Exclusivity-driven:** "...this is the ONLY place you can get [unique feature]"

14. Assign one unique supporting reason to each CTA
15. Rule: No two consecutive CTAs use the same reason category
16. Rule: The guarantee-driven reason should appear within the first 3 CTAs (builds safety early)

### Step 5: Map Placement Across Close Sections

17. Distribute CTAs across the close sections. Each section should contain at least one CTA:

    **Placement map (for 9-CTA VSL example):**
    - Benefit summary section: 1-2 CTAs (after listing benefits)
    - Guarantee close section: 1 CTA (after presenting guarantee confidence)
    - Closing theme section: 2 CTAs (woven into theme argument)
    - Urgency/scarcity section: 1 CTA (attached to urgency justification)
    - Checkout/action instructions: 1 CTA (the step-by-step "here's what to do" CTA)
    - P.S. section: 1-2 CTAs (embedded in P.S. selling content)

18. For sidebar-supporting formats (magalog), plan 2-3 additional sidebar CTAs:
    - Value sidebar CTA: "Order now and save $[X]"
    - Guarantee sidebar CTA: "Risk-free — [branded guarantee]"
    - Urgency sidebar CTA: "[Scarcity element] — act now"

19. Note: Sidebar CTAs are IN ADDITION to the 6-10+ minimum text CTAs

### Step 6: Build CTA Escalation Curve

20. Design the emotional escalation curve across the CTA sequence:

    **Escalation pattern:**
    - CTAs 1-3: Building warmth — greed, logic, hope (inviting, reasonable)
    - CTAs 4-6: Increasing pressure — fear, urgency, social proof (consequences emerge)
    - CTAs 7-9: Peak intensity — exclusivity, compassion, combined fear + urgency (final push)
    - Final CTA: Maximum intensity — combine the two strongest emotional appeals

21. The escalation curve mirrors the close section's emotional trajectory:
    - Benefit summary (warm) → Guarantee (safe) → Theme (building) → Urgency (intense) → P.S. (peak)

22. Apply staccato tempo instruction for CTAs 7+ (Week 13 pattern):
    - Short sentences. Punchy rhythm. No wasted words.
    - "Click below. Do it now. Your future self will thank you."

### Step 7: Compile CTA Repetition Plan

23. Assemble the complete plan:
    - CTA count
    - Ordered CTA list with: number, emotional appeal, phrase root, supporting reason, placement, escalation position
    - Sidebar CTA plan (if applicable)
    - Exclusion list applied
    - Escalation curve documented
    - Staccato tempo points marked

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Minimum CTA count | At least 6 CTAs planned (target met) |
| Emotional variety | No more than 2 CTAs share the same primary emotion |
| Phrase variety | No two CTAs use the same phrase root |
| Reason variety | No two consecutive CTAs use the same supporting reason category |
| Section coverage | Every close section has at least 1 CTA assigned |
| Guarantee reason early | Guarantee-driven reason appears in first 3 CTAs |
| Escalation curve present | CTAs escalate from warm to peak intensity |
| Exclusion list applied | No phrases from Skill 16 CTA exclusion list used |
| Final CTA combines emotions | Last CTA uses 2 emotional appeals for maximum impact |
| Staccato marked | CTAs in positions 7+ marked for staccato tempo |
| Sidebar CTAs planned | If format supports sidebars, 2-3 sidebar CTAs planned |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Patronizing Repetition
- **Symptom:** Multiple CTAs feel like the same ask repeated mechanically
- **Cause:** Same emotional appeal and similar phrasing used across multiple CTAs
- **Prevention:** Variety verification — unique emotions, unique phrase roots, unique reasons
- **Recovery:** Reassign emotions and phrase roots to ensure each CTA feels like a fresh persuasive push

### Failure Mode 2: CTA Clustering
- **Symptom:** All CTAs bunched in one section (e.g., all at the end)
- **Cause:** Placement mapping not enforced across sections
- **Prevention:** Step 5 placement map requires at least 1 CTA per section
- **Recovery:** Redistribute CTAs across all close sections

### Failure Mode 3: Weak Final CTA
- **Symptom:** The last CTA is a generic "order now" without emotional power
- **Cause:** Strongest emotional appeals used early, leaving the final CTA weak
- **Prevention:** Step 6 reserves combined-emotion peak for the final CTA
- **Recovery:** Reassign the strongest combined emotional appeal to the final position

### Failure Mode 4: Exclusion List Ignored
- **Symptom:** CTA phrase identical to one used in Skill 13 offer section
- **Cause:** Exclusion list not applied during phrase assignment
- **Prevention:** Step 3 filters all phrases against the exclusion list before assignment
- **Recovery:** Replace the duplicate phrase with an alternative from the phrase library

### Failure Mode 5: Too Many Fear CTAs
- **Symptom:** Close feels threatening and prospect feels bullied
- **Cause:** Fear emotion over-allocated, especially in health niche
- **Prevention:** Step 2 limits any single emotion to maximum 2 CTAs; Step 6 balances fear with hope
- **Recovery:** Redistribute fear CTAs to hope or compassion — maintain urgency without threatening
