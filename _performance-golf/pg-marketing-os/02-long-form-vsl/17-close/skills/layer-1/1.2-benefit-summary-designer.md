## PURPOSE

Designs the structural blueprint for the "You get" benefit summary — the section of the close where every major benefit is restated in a bullet format that "tickles the greed gland" (Makepeace). This is Makepeace Element 1: Repeat Main Benefits. The benefit summary is NOT a simple list — it is strategically designed to re-ignite desire after the price presentation by reminding the prospect of everything they receive and WHY each benefit transforms their life.

This microskill selects 5-10+ benefits from the master benefit list (compiled by 0.1 from promise-package and proof-inventory), structures them in the "You get [benefit] — [reason why it changes your life]" format, determines ordering (strongest to weakest), and assigns categories to each bullet (product features, bonuses, guarantee, special access). The design also specifies which benefits have proof backing (for enhanced credibility in the summary) and which connect most powerfully to the primary promise.

The designer does NOT write the prose — that is Layer 2's job (2.1-benefit-summary-writer). This microskill provides the architectural plan that 2.1 executes against.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Compile Candidate Benefits

1. Gather all benefits from the master benefit list (0.1 output)
2. Add bonus items as benefits: each bonus becomes "You get [bonus name] — [what it does for you]"
3. Add guarantee as a benefit: "You get [branded guarantee name] — [duration] to try everything risk-free"
4. Add special access items if present: "You get exclusive access to [resource/community]"
5. Total candidate pool should be 8-15+ items
6. Tag each candidate with its source:
   - `promise` — from Skill 04 primary/secondary promise
   - `proof` — derived from testimonial results in Skill 01
   - `bonus` — from offer bonus inventory in Skill 13
   - `guarantee` — from guarantee details
   - `access` — from special features (community, hotline, etc.)

### Step 2: Score Benefits for Inclusion

7. Score each candidate benefit on three dimensions (1-10 each):

   **Dimension A: Emotional Impact**
   - How viscerally does this benefit hit the prospect?
   - 10 = life-changing (e.g., "Rock-solid confidence in the bedroom")
   - 5 = meaningful but not transformative (e.g., "Better sleep quality")
   - 1 = technical feature (e.g., "Contains 8 nutrients")

   **Dimension B: Proof Backing**
   - Does this benefit have testimonial, clinical, or statistical support?
   - 10 = multiple proof types (clinical + testimonial)
   - 5 = single proof type (one testimonial)
   - 1 = unproven (but still a credible claim)

   **Dimension C: Uniqueness**
   - Is this benefit unique to this product, or generic to the category?
   - 10 = completely unique (only this product delivers this)
   - 5 = differentiated (common benefit with unique framing)
   - 1 = generic (every competitor claims this)

8. Calculate composite score: (Emotional * 0.5) + (Proof * 0.3) + (Uniqueness * 0.2)
9. Rank all candidates by composite score

### Step 3: Select Final Benefits (5-10+)

10. Select the top 5-10+ benefits based on composite score
11. Apply selection rules:
    - MUST include at least 3 benefits from the primary promise
    - MUST include the guarantee as a benefit (always in final position or near-final)
    - MUST include at least 1 bonus item
    - SHOULD include at least 1 benefit with strong proof backing
    - SHOULD aim for variety across benefit types (health, wealth, relationship, access, peace of mind)
12. If fewer than 5 candidates score well, expand from supporting benefits
13. Target: 7-10 bullets for VSL/magalog, 5-7 for email/advertorial

### Step 4: Pair Each Benefit with "Reason Why"

14. For each selected benefit, assign a "reason why" that explains HOW this benefit transforms the prospect's situation:

    **Pattern: "You get [BENEFIT] — [REASON WHY IT CHANGES YOUR LIFE]"**

    The "reason why" answers: "Why does this matter to ME?"

    **Types of "reason why":**
    - **Outcome-based:** "...so you can finally [desired outcome]"
    - **Relief-based:** "...which means you'll never have to worry about [problem] again"
    - **Status-based:** "...the kind of [status result] that turns heads"
    - **Proof-based:** "...backed by [specific proof] showing [result]"
    - **Comparison-based:** "...worth more than [expensive alternative] at a fraction of the cost"
    - **Feeling-based:** "...imagine the feeling of [emotional payoff]"

15. Ensure no two "reasons why" use the same type — variety creates cumulative impact
16. The reason-why for the guarantee should focus on CONFIDENCE: "...giving you total peace of mind to try everything without a single penny of risk"

### Step 5: Determine Benefit Order

17. Order benefits for maximum psychological impact:

    **Ordering Strategy: STRONGEST-FIRST WITH STRONG CLOSE**
    - Position 1: STRONGEST benefit (highest emotional impact — hooks the reader into reading the list)
    - Positions 2-3: Second and third strongest benefits (maintain momentum)
    - Positions 4-6: Supporting benefits (build breadth of value)
    - Position 7-8: Bonuses (add perceived value)
    - Penultimate position: Most unique/surprising benefit (re-captures attention)
    - Final position: Guarantee (closes the summary with risk reversal — transition to guarantee section)

18. Exception: If the closing theme is "But WAIT There's More," reverse the bonus positioning — save bonuses for AFTER the initial summary to reveal incrementally

### Step 6: Assign Benefit Categories

19. Tag each benefit with its display category:
    - `core_feature` — primary product features and deliverables
    - `bonus` — bonus items with stated value
    - `guarantee` — the guarantee itself as a benefit
    - `access` — exclusive access, community, support
    - `outcome` — life transformation results

20. These categories help 2.1 vary the writing style:
    - Core features: factual with emotional reason-why
    - Bonuses: value emphasis ("a $97 value — yours FREE")
    - Guarantee: confidence emphasis ("total peace of mind")
    - Access: exclusivity emphasis ("normally reserved for...")
    - Outcome: emotional emphasis ("imagine finally...")

### Step 7: Generate Benefit Summary Blueprint

21. Compile the complete blueprint for 2.1:
    - Ordered list of selected benefits with scores
    - Each benefit paired with its reason-why type
    - Each benefit tagged with category and proof backing
    - Word count target from theme selection
    - Format-specific instructions (bullet style, spacing, emphasis)
    - Theme-specific considerations from 1.1

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| Minimum benefit count | At least 5 benefits selected (7-10 preferred for VSL/magalog) |
| Primary promise represented | At least 3 benefits trace to primary promise |
| Guarantee included | Guarantee appears as a benefit in the summary |
| Bonus included | At least 1 bonus item appears in the summary |
| Reason-why paired | Every benefit has a reason-why direction assigned |
| Reason-why variety | No two consecutive benefits use the same reason-why type |

| Category variety | At least 3 different categories represented |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Generic Benefit Selection
- **Symptom:** Benefits are vague and interchangeable ("better health," "more energy," "improved wellness")
- **Cause:** Selecting from abstract benefit descriptions rather than specific outcome claims
- **Prevention:** Score Dimension A (Emotional Impact) penalizes generic language; minimum score threshold
- **Recovery:** Rewrite generic benefits with specific outcomes from testimonials and proof inventory

### Failure Mode 2: Reason-Why Monotony
- **Symptom:** Every reason-why uses the same pattern ("...so you can finally...")
- **Cause:** Reason-why type assignment not enforced for variety
- **Prevention:** Step 4 variety check — no two consecutive bullets use same reason-why type
- **Recovery:** Reassign reason-why types to alternate patterns across the list

### Failure Mode 3: Wrong Ordering — Weak Start
- **Symptom:** First benefit is a minor bonus instead of the strongest emotional claim
- **Cause:** Composite scores not properly weighted or ordering strategy not followed
- **Prevention:** Step 5 ordering strategy mandates highest emotional impact first
- **Recovery:** Re-order by emotional impact score for position 1, then follow strategy

### Failure Mode 4: Missing Guarantee in Summary
- **Symptom:** Guarantee not included as a benefit bullet
- **Cause:** Guarantee treated as a separate section only, not also as a summary item
- **Prevention:** Step 1 explicitly adds guarantee as a candidate; Step 3 mandates its inclusion
- **Recovery:** Add guarantee bullet to final or penultimate position
