## PURPOSE

Validates that all 6 Makepeace foundational close elements are present and properly executed in the assembled close draft. Clayton Makepeace identifies these 6 elements as non-negotiable for every major sales piece close. This microskill provides pass/fail evidence for each element with specific textual citations.

Beyond the 6 foundational elements, this checker also validates critical close metrics: CTA count >= 6, guarantee framing as contract/promise (not refund policy), P.S. effectiveness, and overall close architecture integrity.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The structural compliance checker for Makepeace's 6 foundational close elements
- A pass/fail validator with textual evidence citations
- The close-specific quality gate (distinct from anti-slop or vault comparison)
- A CTA count and variety auditor
- A guarantee framing verifier
- A P.S. real estate effectiveness assessor

**This microskill is NOT:**
- An anti-slop validator (that is 4.2)
- A vault pattern comparator (that is 4.3)
- A copy rewriter (this validates, doesn't fix)
- A close theme selector (that was 1.1)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Makepeace 6 Elements Checklist

1. Index the 6 foundational elements:

| # | Element | What Makepeace Requires |
|---|---------|------------------------|
| 1 | Repeat Main Benefits | "You get" format bullets, summarizing everything prospect receives |
| 2 | Present Guarantee | As contract/personal promise, NOT "if not satisfied return for refund" |
| 3 | Ask for the Sale | REPEATEDLY — 6-10+ times with variety in phrasing, emotion, reasons |
| 4 | Tell What to Do | Step-by-step action instructions with main themes woven in |
| 5 | Powerful P.S. | Using at least 1 of 6 techniques, prime selling real estate |
| 6 | Sidebars to Enhance | Value sidebars, risk-relief sidebars, proof sidebars (or sidebar notes for non-print formats) |

### Step 2: Validate Element 1 — Benefit Summary

2. Locate the benefit summary section
3. Check for "You get" format (each bullet starts with "You get" or similar)
4. Count distinct benefits listed (minimum 5)
5. Verify each benefit connects to a reason why it changes the prospect's life
6. Verify the summary covers: product features, bonuses, guarantee, special access
7. Record: pass (5+ "You get" bullets with reasons) / fail (missing format, under 5, or no reasons)

### Step 3: Validate Element 2 — Guarantee

8. Locate the guarantee section in the close
9. Check for BRANDED guarantee name (not generic "money-back guarantee")
10. Check framing:
    - PASS: "You must be absolutely delighted" / "Contract" / "Personal Promise"
    - FAIL: "If not satisfied, return for refund" / "Satisfaction guaranteed"
11. Check for confidence expression (guarantee sells confidence, not exit)
12. Check for "rush" language for refund speed
13. Record: pass (branded, positive framing, confidence) / fail (generic, negative, refund-focused)

### Step 4: Validate Element 3 — Ask for Sale (REPETITION)

14. Count ALL distinct asks for the sale across the entire close
    - In benefit summary: implicit asks ("Send for your FREE report")
    - In guarantee: "give it a try"
    - In closing theme: direct asks
    - In CTA sequence: explicit asks
    - In urgency: "act now" asks
    - In P.S.: P.S.-specific asks
    - In sidebars/checkout: action asks

15. Verify minimum 6 distinct asks
16. Verify each uses different phrasing
17. Verify variety of emotions attached (greed, fear, urgency, logic, exclusivity)
18. Verify no two asks are identical in language
19. Record: pass (6+ varied asks) / fail (under 6 or identical language)

### Step 5: Validate Element 4 — Tell What to Do

20. Locate action instructions section
21. Verify step-by-step action process described ("Here's what to do: 1... 2... 3...")
22. Verify main themes/benefits woven INTO the action process
23. Verify specific action mechanism stated (phone number, button, URL, form)
24. Record: pass (step-by-step with themes and specifics) / fail (vague or missing)

### Step 6: Validate Element 5 — P.S.

25. Locate P.S. section
26. Verify at least 1 P.S. present
27. Identify which of 6 Makepeace techniques used:
    - (a) Fast-reply bonus, (b) Call-in bonus, (c) Additional bonus, (d) Testimonials, (e) Additional reasons, (f) Guarantee restatement
28. Verify P.S. advances the close (not just administrative text)
29. Verify P.S. contains its own CTA
30. Assess: could headline + P.S. standalone as compelling sell?
31. Record: pass (technique used, advances close, CTA present) / fail (missing, administrative, or no CTA)

### Step 7: Validate Element 6 — Sidebars

32. For PRINT formats (magalog, self-mailer):
    - Verify sidebar presence in close section
    - Check for value sidebars, risk-relief sidebars, proof sidebars
33. For DIGITAL formats (VSL, email, advertorial):
    - Verify sidebar-equivalent notes are provided for future formatting
    - OR verify sidebar content is integrated into the running text

34. Record: pass (sidebars present or sidebar notes for non-print) / fail (missing without format justification)

### Step 8: Additional Close Metrics

35. Verify CTA count >= 6 (reinforcing Element 3)
36. Verify checkout process described clearly (Element 4 extension)
37. Verify Cialdini commitment/consistency present (Layer 3.4 integration)
38. Verify future pacing / crossroads present
39. Verify staccato tempo used in action sections

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Guarantee Uses "If Not Satisfied"
- **Symptom:** Guarantee says "If you're not satisfied, simply return for a full refund"
- **Cause:** Writer defaulted to industry-standard guarantee language
- **Prevention:** Element 2 check specifically flags "if not satisfied" language
- **Recovery:** Rewrite: "You must be absolutely delighted... but in the very unlikely event..."

### Failure Mode 2: CTA Count Under 6
- **Symptom:** Only 3-4 asks for the sale in the entire close
- **Cause:** Writer felt aggressive asking more, or didn't distribute CTAs across sections
- **Prevention:** Element 3 counts ALL asks including sidebar, P.S., and implicit
- **Recovery:** Add CTAs to underrepresented sections (benefit summary, P.S., checkout)

### Failure Mode 3: P.S. as Logistics
- **Symptom:** "P.S. — To order, visit www.example.com or call 1-800-XXX-XXXX"
- **Cause:** P.S. treated as administrative footer, not selling real estate
- **Prevention:** Element 5 checks that P.S. uses a Makepeace technique and advances the close
- **Recovery:** Rewrite P.S. with strongest available ammo using selected technique

### Failure Mode 4: Missing Action Instructions
- **Symptom:** Close asks for the sale but never tells the prospect HOW to order
- **Cause:** Writer assumed prospect knows the process
- **Prevention:** Element 4 requires explicit step-by-step
- **Recovery:** Add: "Here's exactly what to do: Click the button below, fill in your information..."

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Full Pass — Financial Newsletter Close

- Element 1: PASS — 8 "You get" bullets covering service, reports, ratings, hotline
- Element 2: PASS — "Personal Promise" framing, "You must make at least $5,000 or everything is FREE"
- Element 3: PASS — 9 asks across sections with emotions: greed, fear, logic, urgency, exclusivity, social proof, scarcity, FOMO, confidence
- Element 4: PASS — "Dial toll-free 1-800-236-0407 RIGHT NOW. Or complete and return the enclosed RSVP form."
- Element 5: PASS — P.P.S. introduces fast-reply bonus, romances it, summarizes both-year deals
- Element 6: PASS — Two testimonial sidebars in close spread
- **Summary: 6/6 PASS**

### Example 2: Partial Fail — Supplement VSL Close

- Element 1: PASS — 6 "You get" bullets
- Element 2: FAIL — "If you're not happy, just email us for a refund" (generic)
- Element 3: PASS — 7 asks with variety
- Element 4: PASS — Step-by-step with security reassurance
- Element 5: FAIL — P.S. is just "P.S. — Don't forget to order today!" (no technique, no advancement)
- Element 6: PASS (N/A — VSL format, sidebar notes provided)
- **Summary: 4/6 PASS — Remediation needed for guarantee and P.S.**
