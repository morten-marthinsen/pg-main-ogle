## PURPOSE

Enforces zero tolerance for generic, clichéd, or AI-generated-sounding language in the close section. The close is the FINAL copy section the prospect reads before deciding — every word must feel written by a master closer, not assembled by a template engine. This microskill scans every sentence of the close draft for slop patterns specific to close copy: generic guarantee language, passive CTAs, patronizing repetition, fabricated urgency, hollow future pacing, and formulaic P.S. sections.

Close-specific slop is particularly dangerous because it appears at the moment of highest commitment tension. A single generic phrase ("Act now before it's too late!") can shatter the trust painstakingly built across 12 prior skills. This validator catches what the Makepeace elements checker (4.1) cannot — 4.1 checks structural presence, this checks language quality.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A zero-tolerance language quality enforcer for close copy
- A close-specific slop pattern scanner (distinct from general anti-slop)
- A sentence-level validator that checks every line of close text
- A violator of generic guarantee, CTA, urgency, and P.S. language
- The language-quality complement to 4.1's structural check
- A binary pass/fail gate — any violation = fail

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A structural elements checker (that is 4.1 — Makepeace elements)
- A vault pattern comparator (that is 4.3 — competitive benchmarking)
- A copy rewriter (this detects, doesn't fix)
- A general anti-slop tool (this is close-section-specific)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Close-Specific Slop Patterns

1. Load the master anti-slop lexicon (engine-wide banned phrases)
2. Load close-specific slop patterns:

| Category | Slop Patterns | Why They're Banned |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| Generic guarantee | "If you're not satisfied, simply return for a refund" / "Satisfaction guaranteed" / "Money-back guarantee" | Makepeace requires CONTRACT/PROMISE framing, not refund policy |
| Passive CTAs | "Consider trying" / "Why not give it a shot" / "Feel free to order" | CTAs must be direct, confident commands — not suggestions |
| Patronizing repetition | "As I mentioned before" / "Like I said" / "Remember what I told you" | Repetition must feel natural, not condescending |
| Fabricated urgency | "Act now before it's too late!" / "This won't last forever!" / "Hurry!" (without justification) | Every urgency claim must have a specific, believable reason |
| Hollow future pacing | "Imagine how great life will be" / "Think about the possibilities" / "Picture yourself succeeding" | Future pacing must be vivid and specific, not vague platitudes |
| Formulaic P.S. | "P.S. — Don't forget to order!" / "P.S. — Remember, you're protected by our guarantee" | P.S. is prime selling real estate — not administrative reminder space |
| Generic crossroads | "The choice is yours" / "You can keep doing what you've been doing, or..." (without specific contrast) | Crossroads must paint vivid specific futures, not generic either/or |
| AI tell phrases | "In conclusion" / "To summarize" / "In summary" / "As we've discussed" | Close copy must flow naturally, not signal section transitions |
| Weak modifiers | "Somewhat" / "Fairly" / "Quite" / "Rather" / "A little bit" | Close demands confidence — no hedge words |
| Filler transitions | "But wait, there's more!" (used generically) / "And that's not all!" (without substance) | Transitions must add value, not just bridge sections |

### Step 2: Scan Benefit Summary Section

3. Extract every sentence from benefit_summary_text
4. Check each sentence against:
   - Master anti-slop lexicon
   - Close-specific slop patterns
   - "You get" format quality (not "You will receive" / "Included is")
5. Flag any violations with exact text and violation category

### Step 3: Scan Guarantee Section

6. Extract every sentence from guarantee_close_text
7. Check specifically for:
   - "If not satisfied" language (CRITICAL violation)
   - "Money-back guarantee" without branded name (CRITICAL violation)
   - "Simply return" / "Just email us" / "No questions asked" without confidence framing
   - Refund-focused language instead of confidence-focused language
8. Flag any violations

### Step 4: Scan Closing Theme Section

9. Extract every sentence from closing_theme_text
10. Check for:
    - Generic sincerity language ("I truly care about you" without specificity)
    - Fabricated personal connection (claims not grounded in established persona)
    - "In conclusion" / "To wrap up" / "Finally" transition signals
    - Hollow emotional appeals without concrete anchoring
11. Flag any violations

### Step 5: Scan CTA/Action Sequence

12. Extract every sentence from cta_action_sequence_text
13. Check for:
    - Passive CTAs ("Consider" / "Feel free" / "Why not")
    - Identical CTA language repeated (exact same phrase twice)
    - Missing action specifics ("Click below" without saying what happens next)
    - Generic urgency attached to CTAs without justification
14. Flag any violations

### Step 6: Scan Urgency/Scarcity Section

15. Extract every sentence from urgency_scarcity_text
16. Check for:
    - Unjustified urgency ("Act now!" without reason)
    - "Limited time offer" without specifying what's limited or why
    - "Won't last forever" / "Running out" without production or inventory justification
    - Generic countdown language not tied to a real event or constraint
17. Flag any violations

### Step 7: Scan Future Pacing Section

18. Extract every sentence from future_pacing_text
19. Check for:
    - Vague future statements ("Imagine how great things could be")
    - Missing sensory or emotional specificity in dream scenario
    - Missing specific pain details in nightmare scenario
    - Generic crossroads without vivid contrast between two paths
20. Flag any violations

### Step 8: Scan P.S. Section

21. Extract every sentence from ps_section_text
22. Check for:
    - Administrative P.S. ("Don't forget to order" / "Remember our guarantee")
    - P.S. that merely repeats earlier content without adding new persuasive value
    - Missing CTA within P.S.
    - Generic bonus mention without romancing
23. Flag any violations

### Step 9: Scan Checkout Section

24. Extract every sentence from checkout_text
25. Check for:
    - Security theater language ("Your information is 100% safe and secure")
    - Generic trust signals without specificity
    - Overly technical process descriptions
    - Missing theme integration in checkout steps
26. Flag any violations

### Step 10: Compile Anti-Slop Report

27. Assemble:

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Generic Guarantee Language Survives
- **Symptom:** Guarantee says "If you're not satisfied, return for a full refund"
- **Cause:** Writer used industry-standard guarantee template
- **Prevention:** Step 3 specifically flags "if not satisfied" as CRITICAL violation
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with Makepeace contract/promise framing: "You must be absolutely delighted"

### Failure Mode 2: Passive CTAs Throughout
- **Symptom:** CTAs say "Consider giving it a try" / "Feel free to order when you're ready"
- **Cause:** Writer felt aggressive direct commands would alienate
- **Prevention:** Step 5 flags all passive/suggestive CTA language
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with confident, direct action commands: "Go ahead — click the button below right now"

### Failure Mode 3: Fabricated Urgency
- **Symptom:** "Act now — this offer won't last!" with no reason why it won't last
- **Cause:** Writer added urgency as afterthought without grounding it
- **Prevention:** Step 6 requires every urgency claim to have a specific justification
- **Recovery:** Either provide real justification or remove the urgency claim entirely

### Failure Mode 4: Hollow Future Pacing
- **Symptom:** "Imagine how great your life will be" — vague, no specific details
- **Cause:** Writer used generic future pacing instead of niche-specific vivid imagery
- **Prevention:** Step 7 checks for sensory and emotional specificity
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with concrete scenario: specific morning routine, specific feeling, specific outcome
