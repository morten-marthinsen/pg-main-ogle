---

## PURPOSE

Writes the full CTA sequence — Makepeace Elements 3 and 4 combined: Ask for the Sale (6-10+ times) and Tell Specifically What to Do. This is the SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT element of the close according to Makepeace. The difference between a promotion that generates orders and one that generates admiration is the relentless, varied, emotionally charged asking for the sale. Every ask must use different phrasing, attach a different reason, and charge a different emotion — creating a cumulative persuasive wave that makes NOT ordering feel like the irrational choice.

This microskill executes the CTA repetition plan from 1.3, transforming the planned emotional appeals, phrase roots, supporting reasons, and placement assignments into fully written prose. It also writes the step-by-step action instructions — "Here's exactly what's going to happen when you click the button below..." — which reduce checkout anxiety and associate the ACTION with the BENEFITS. Week 13 patterns are heavily integrated: checkout process clarity, security reassurance (256-bit, military grade), and staccato tempo for peak-intensity CTAs.

The CTAs are NOT bunched at the end. They are WOVEN throughout the close — appearing after benefit summary, within guarantee, throughout the theme section, during urgency, and in the P.S. Each CTA is a standalone persuasive moment that could, by itself, trigger the order.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Write the Direct Ask CTAs (Types A-B)

1. Write 2-3 DIRECT ASK CTAs that appear early in the close (warm escalation):

   **Type A — Direct Command CTA:**
   "Go ahead and click the button below to get started with [Product Name]. Your order is processed instantly, and you'll have access within minutes."

   **Type B — Reason-Attached Question CTA:**
   "Doesn't it make sense to at least give [Product Name] a try — especially when you're completely protected by the [Branded Guarantee]? Click the button below now."

2. Direct asks are CONFIDENT but not pushy — they assume the prospect is ready
3. Attach the supporting reason from the plan (benefit-driven or guarantee-driven)
4. Include the action instruction: "click the button below" / "call toll-free" / "mail the enclosed form"

### Step 2: Write the Emotional Appeal CTAs (Types C-E)

5. Write 2-3 EMOTIONAL CTAs that appear in the middle of the close (building escalation):

   **Type C — Urgency CTA:**
   "Don't hesitate. Not even for a second. Every moment you wait is another moment of [problem] you don't need to endure. Click below and let's get you started today."

   **Type D — Emotional Appeal CTA:**
   "What are you waiting for? You've seen the proof. You've read the testimonials. You know this works. The only thing standing between you and [promise] is one simple click."

   **Type E — Permission Invite CTA:**
   "Join me now and let me show you what [Product Name] can do for you. I promise you won't be disappointed — and if you are, you don't pay a thing."

6. Emotional CTAs tap into FEELINGS rather than logic
7. Each uses a different emotion from the plan (urgency, hope, permission)
8. Support with the planned reason (scarcity-driven, social-driven, guarantee-driven)

### Step 3: Write the Fear and Compassion CTAs (Types F-G)

9. Write 1-2 FEAR or COMPASSION CTAs that appear later in the close (intense escalation):

   **Type F — Fear CTA (Makepeace signature):**
   "Please — if your [health/wealth/game] means anything to you — let me hear from you right away. Don't put this off. Don't tell yourself you'll do it later. Later never comes."

   **Type G — Compassion CTA:**
   "You owe it to yourself. You owe it to [your family/partner/future self]. After everything you've been through, you deserve to finally experience [promise]. Click below."

10. Fear CTAs must be GENUINE concern, not scare tactics
11. Compassion CTAs frame ordering as SELF-CARE, not self-indulgence
12. These CTAs carry the heaviest emotional weight — use sparingly (1-2 max)

### Step 4: Write the Final Combined CTA (Type H)

13. Write 1 COMBINED CTA for the peak of the escalation curve:

   **Type H — Combined Emotion Peak CTA:**
   "This is it. Right here. Right now. The [Product Name] is in front of you. The [Branded Guarantee] protects every penny. The price is the lowest it's ever been. And the only person standing between you and [promise] is you. Click the button below. Do it now. Your future self will thank you."

14. The final CTA combines 2-3 emotions (fear + urgency + hope)
15. Use staccato tempo — short sentences, punchy rhythm
16. This CTA should feel like the CULMINATION of everything before it
17. It should be impossible to read this CTA and not feel compelled to act

### Step 5: Write the Step-by-Step Action Instructions

18. Write the "Here's what to do" section — Makepeace Element 4:

   **Week 13 checkout process clarity pattern:**
   ```
   Here's exactly what's going to happen when you click the button below:

   Step 1: You'll be taken to our secure order page. It's protected by 256-bit
   encryption — the same military-grade security used by Amazon and Apple.

   Step 2: You'll select your preferred package. [I recommend the 3-bottle option
   — it's the best value and gives you a full 90-day supply.]

   Step 3: Enter your shipping and payment information. That's it.

   Step 4: Within minutes, your order will be confirmed and your [product] will be
   on its way. [For digital: you'll have instant access to everything.]

   And remember — you're protected by the [Branded Guarantee] every step of the way.
   ```

19. Action instructions serve three functions:
    - DEMYSTIFY the checkout process (reduce anxiety)
    - PUSH the strongest option (Makepeace: "I recommend the 3-bottle option")
    - ASSOCIATE the action with benefits (weave benefits into the steps)

20. Apply security reassurance (Week 13 pattern):
    - "256-bit security software"
    - "Military-grade encryption"
    - "Verified by the same companies that protect Amazon.com and Apple"
    - Specific badge references if provided (Norton, McAfee, BBB)

### Step 6: Push the Strongest Option

21. Following Makepeace's "push strongest option" principle:
    - Always mention the PREMIUM option first (6-bottle, 2-year, platinum)
    - Explain WHY it's the best value: "Most customers choose the 3-bottle option because..."
    - Make the lower option seem like a fine choice too (don't shame the prospect)
    - "I recommend the [premium option] because it gives you the best value and the longest supply. But any option you choose is a step in the right direction."

22. The push should feel like ADVICE from a trusted friend, not a hard upsell

### Step 7: Write Sidebar CTAs (If Format Supports)

23. If magalog or format supports sidebars, write sidebar CTA boxes:

   **Value Sidebar CTA:**
   ```
   TOTAL VALUE: $397
   YOUR PRICE: Just $49
   YOU SAVE: $348!
   ORDER NOW — Call 1-800-XXX-XXXX
   ```

   **Guarantee Sidebar CTA:**
   ```
   RISK-FREE
   Protected by the Triple-Back-Guarantee
   180 days to try everything — or your money back
   ```

   **Urgency Sidebar CTA:**
   ```
   Only [X] bottles remaining
   This batch produced: [date]
   Next batch: [timeline]
   Don't miss out — order now
   ```

24. Sidebar CTAs are concise — 3-5 lines maximum
25. They COMPLEMENT the text CTAs, not replace them

### Step 8: Verify CTA Sequence Quality

26. Quality verification:
    - Total CTA count >= 6 (minimum)
    - No two CTAs use the same emotional appeal AND phrase combination
    - Each CTA has a supporting reason attached
    - CTAs are distributed across all close sections (not bunched)
    - Escalation curve present (warm → building → intense → peak)
    - Staccato tempo applied to final 2-3 CTAs
    - Action instructions present with step-by-step clarity
    - Security reassurance included
    - Strongest option pushed with advisory language
    - No CTA matches any phrase on the exclusion list
    - CTAs feel varied and FRESH, not patronizing or mechanical

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| CTA count minimum | 6 or more CTAs written |
| Emotional variety | No more than 2 CTAs share the same primary emotion |
| Phrase variety | No two CTAs use the same opening phrase |
| Reason variety | No two consecutive CTAs use the same supporting reason |
| Section distribution | CTAs appear in benefit summary, guarantee, theme, urgency, checkout, and P.S. |
| Escalation curve | CTAs escalate from warm to building to intense to peak |
| Staccato tempo | Final 2-3 CTAs use short, punchy sentence rhythm |
| Action instructions present | Step-by-step "here's what to do" section written |

| Exclusion list clean | No CTA matches upstream Skill 13 CTA phrases |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: All CTAs Sound the Same
- **Symptom:** "Order now" / "Buy now" / "Act now" — same structure, same words
- **Cause:** Writer used similar phrase roots instead of true variety
- **Prevention:** Each CTA assigned unique phrase root from 1.3 plan; variety check enforced
- **Recovery:** Rewrite each CTA using distinct opening phrases from the phrase library

### Failure Mode 2: Missing Action Instructions
- **Symptom:** Close asks for the sale but never tells the prospect HOW to order
- **Cause:** Writer focused on persuasion and forgot practical checkout instructions
- **Prevention:** Step 5 mandates "Here's what to do" section with step-by-step process
- **Recovery:** Add action instruction section with checkout process, security, and strongest option push

### Failure Mode 3: CTAs Bunched at End
- **Symptom:** First 80% of close has zero CTAs; last 20% has all 8
- **Cause:** Writer treated CTAs as a finale rather than a rhythm throughout
- **Prevention:** Placement mapping from 1.3 distributes CTAs across all sections
- **Recovery:** Redistribute CTAs to their assigned placement points throughout the close

### Failure Mode 4: Security Reassurance Missing
- **Symptom:** Checkout instructions describe the process but don't address security fears
- **Cause:** Writer focused on process clarity but forgot trust signals
- **Prevention:** Step 5 explicitly requires security reassurance language
- **Recovery:** Add security language: encryption, badges, trusted platform comparisons

### Failure Mode 5: Strongest Option Not Pushed
- **Symptom:** All options presented equally without recommendation
- **Cause:** Writer avoided appearing sales-y by not recommending premium option
- **Prevention:** Step 6 Makepeace principle — always push strongest option with advisory tone
- **Recovery:** Add recommendation: "I recommend the [premium option] because..." with value justification

### Example 1: Health Supplement VSL — 9 CTAs Written

**Context:** Male vitality supplement, VSL, 9 CTAs from plan.

**CTA Sequence (selected CTAs shown):**

**CTA 1 (Greed — after benefit summary):**
"Go ahead and select your package below. Your order ships within 24 hours, and you'll have Montezuma's Secret in your hands within days. This is the beginning of everything I've promised you today."

**CTA 4 (Fear — within closing theme):**
