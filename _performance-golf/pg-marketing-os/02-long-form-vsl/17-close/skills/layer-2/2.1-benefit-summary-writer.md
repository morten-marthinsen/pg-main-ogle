## PURPOSE

Writes the "You get" benefit summary — Makepeace Element 1: Repeat Main Benefits. This is the section of the close where every major benefit is restated in bullet format using the phrase "You get" to start each line. Makepeace teaches that "You" is the most powerful word in advertising and "get" may be the second. The repetition of "You get... You get... You get..." builds subconscious desire — it "tickles the greed gland" — creating a cumulative wave of wanting that primes the prospect for the sale ask.

This section comes AFTER the price presentation (which happened in Skill 13) and serves a critical psychological function: it re-ignites desire. The prospect has just been told the price — some will hesitate. The benefit summary reminds them of EVERYTHING they receive and WHY each benefit transforms their situation. It is a value restatement that makes the price feel small compared to the mountain of benefits.

The writer follows the blueprint from 1.2-benefit-summary-designer, which specifies the ordered benefits, reason-why types, categories, and word count targets. This microskill transforms that blueprint into persuasive prose — each bullet connecting the benefit to a powerful reason why the product changes the prospect's life.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Set Up the Benefit Summary Frame

1. Write the transition INTO the benefit summary. This comes after the price presentation and before the individual bullets:

   **Transition patterns:**
   - "So let me quickly recap everything you're getting today..."
   - "Here's everything that's included when you order [Product Name] today..."
   - "When you say YES to [Product Name], here's exactly what you'll receive..."
   - "Let me lay it all out for you — everything you get with your order today..."

2. The transition should feel like a generous review, not a hard sell
3. The word "today" should appear — it creates immediacy
4. Do NOT use: "Let me remind you of all the benefits..." (patronizing)

### Step 2: Write Each "You Get" Bullet

5. For each benefit in the ordered list from the blueprint, write one "You get" bullet:

   **Core Pattern:**
   "You get [BENEFIT] — [REASON WHY IT CHANGES YOUR LIFE]."

   **Execution rules:**
   - EVERY bullet starts with "You get" — this is non-negotiable (Makepeace methodology)
   - The benefit portion is SPECIFIC and OUTCOME-ORIENTED
   - The reason-why portion answers "why does this matter to ME?"
   - Each bullet is 1-3 sentences maximum
   - The em dash (—) separates benefit from reason-why

6. Write bullets according to the reason-why type assigned in the blueprint:

   **Outcome-based reason-why:**
   "You get rock-solid confidence in the bedroom — so you never have to worry about performance again. You'll feel like the man you were in your twenties."

   **Relief-based reason-why:**
   "You get natural testosterone support without synthetic hormones — which means you'll never have to deal with dangerous side effects or awkward doctor visits."

   **Status-based reason-why:**
   "You get the kind of all-day stamina that has your wife asking 'What changed?' — the kind of vitality that turns heads at the gym and the boardroom."

   **Proof-based reason-why:**
   "You get the same formula that helped Robert M. feel 20 years younger in just 3 weeks — backed by clinical research showing measurable improvement."

   **Comparison-based reason-why:**
   "You get The Aztec Emperor Diet Guide (a $97 value) — yours absolutely FREE. That's more actionable nutrition guidance than most dieticians charge hundreds for."

   **Feeling-based reason-why:**
   "You get the Bedroom Confidence Masterclass — imagine the feeling of walking into any situation knowing you have complete control."

7. For BONUS items, add the value reference:
   "You get [Bonus Name] (a $[value] value) — [reason why]."

8. For the GUARANTEE bullet (typically last or near-last):
   "You get the [Branded Guarantee Name] — a full [duration] to try everything completely risk-free. If you're not absolutely delighted, you get every penny back. No questions asked."

### Step 3: Apply "You Get" Rhythm and Cadence

9. Read through all bullets and verify the RHYTHM:
   - "You get" should create a drumbeat — each repetition building desire
   - Vary bullet LENGTH — mix short punchy bullets with longer descriptive ones
   - Short bullet example: "You get priority VIP reorder access — because you deserve first-in-line service."
   - Long bullet example: "You get rock-solid confidence in the bedroom — the kind of unwavering certainty that lets you perform at your peak, every single time, without a moment's hesitation. Imagine what that feels like."

10. Check cadence across the full sequence:
    - Bullets 1-2: Medium length (establish the pattern)
    - Bullets 3-4: Vary — one short, one longer
    - Bullets 5-6: Building — add more emotional language
    - Final bullets: Bonus values + guarantee (tangible + safe)

11. The "You get" repetition should feel GENEROUS, not mechanical
    - Generous: "You also get..." / "And you get..." / "Plus, you get..."
    - Mechanical: "You get. You get. You get." (same structure, same length, monotone)

### Step 4: Add Emphasis and Formatting

12. Apply formatting for impact:
    - BOLD the benefit name in each bullet (for scanners)
    - Use ALL CAPS sparingly for emphasis on key words: "FREE," "ONLY," "$97 value"
    - Italicize emotional phrases for inner-voice effect: "*imagine the feeling...*"

13. Apply the "value pile" technique:
    - After each bonus bullet, include the value: "(a $97 value — yours FREE)"
    - This creates a running mental tally of value received

14. For VSL format: Bullets read as natural speech (no bold, less formatting)
    For magalog format: Bullets can use visual formatting (bold, checkmarks, indentation)
    For email format: Shorter bullets, less formatting
    For advertorial format: Editorial style, no promotional formatting

### Step 5: Write the Summary Close

15. After all bullets, write a transition sentence that connects to the next section (guarantee or closing theme):

   **Transition patterns:**
   - "All of this — a total value of $[X] — is yours today for just $[price]."
   - "That's everything. And it's all protected by the [Branded Guarantee Name]."
   - "Everything listed above is yours the moment you click the button below."
   - "And the best part? Every single thing I just listed is completely risk-free, thanks to the [Branded Guarantee Name]."

16. The transition should create a value-to-price contrast that makes the deal feel irresistible
17. The transition should connect emotionally to the next close section

### Step 6: Verify Benefit Summary Quality

18. Quality verification checklist:
    - Every bullet starts with "You get" (or "You also get" / "Plus, you get")
    - Every benefit has a reason-why connected by em dash
    - Reason-why types are varied (no two consecutive same type)
    - At least 1 bonus with value reference included
    - Guarantee bullet present (typically last)
    - Total word count within target range
    - Rhythm feels generous, not mechanical
    - Transition into section is natural
    - Transition out connects to guarantee or next section

### Step 7: Package Benefit Summary Output

19. Compile the complete benefit summary draft:
    - Full text (transition in + bullets + transition out)
    - Word count
    - Bullet count
    - Benefit-to-reason-why mapping
    - Quality check results
    - Connection points (continues from price, leads to guarantee/theme)

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Mechanical "You Get" Repetition
- **Symptom:** Every bullet same length, same structure, reads like a spec sheet
- **Cause:** Writer treated "You get" as a template rather than a rhythm
- **Prevention:** Step 3 cadence rules — vary length, add "Also," "Plus," vary sentence structure
- **Recovery:** Rewrite alternating short and long bullets; add emotional variation within reason-whys

### Failure Mode 2: Missing Reason-Why
- **Symptom:** Bullets list benefits without explaining WHY they matter
- **Cause:** Writer stopped at the benefit without the em-dash extension
- **Prevention:** Step 2 core pattern enforces [BENEFIT] — [REASON WHY]
- **Recovery:** Append reason-why to each bare benefit using the assigned type from blueprint

### Failure Mode 3: Generic Benefit Language
- **Symptom:** "You get improved health and wellness" — indistinguishable from any product
- **Cause:** Using category-level language instead of specific outcome language
- **Prevention:** Blueprint benefits are scored for specificity; generic benefits should have been caught in 1.2
- **Recovery:** Rewrite with specific outcomes: "improved health" → "morning energy you haven't felt since your 20s"

### Failure Mode 4: Value Pile Absent
- **Symptom:** Bonuses listed without dollar value — no running value tally
- **Cause:** Writer omitted "(a $X value)" from bonus bullets
- **Prevention:** Step 4 specifically requires value references for bonus items
- **Recovery:** Add value reference to each bonus bullet; add total value in transition-out

### Failure Mode 5: Guarantee Not Included as Benefit
- **Symptom:** Guarantee treated as separate section only — not also listed in benefit summary
- **Cause:** Writer viewed guarantee as risk reversal, not a benefit
- **Prevention:** Blueprint mandates guarantee as final or near-final bullet
- **Recovery:** Add guarantee bullet: "You get the [Branded Name] — [duration] to try everything risk-free"
