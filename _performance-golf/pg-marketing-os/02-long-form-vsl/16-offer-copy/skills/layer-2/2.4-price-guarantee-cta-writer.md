## PURPOSE

The Price, Guarantee & CTA Writer produces the prose for the final and most commercially critical portion of the offer section: the price reveal, stack review, savings statement, branded guarantee, and the three-CTA sequence with consequence amplification and urgency. This section converts desire into action. Everything before it — deliverables, bonuses, value demonstration — has built the prospect's desire to own the product. This section shows them the price, removes their risk, and tells them exactly what to do.

This writer handles SIN Chunks 8 through 18: Price Presentation, Stack Review, Savings Statement, Risk Reversal Segue, Guarantee Details, CTA 1, Consequence Amplification, CTA 2, Urgency Section, CTA 3, and Optional Contrast Enhancement. Each chunk serves a specific purpose in the conversion sequence. The price anchor cascade creates a perception of extraordinary value. The guarantee eliminates risk. The CTAs provide clear action instructions with different emotional appeals — confidence, consequence avoidance, and urgency — ensuring the prospect is compelled to act regardless of their dominant emotional trigger.

This is the section where all 10 Offer Copy Principles are most densely tested. D-F-W-B-P must be complete (preceding sections). Value must precede price. Guarantee must be branded. Three CTAs minimum with different emotional appeals. Consequence amplification between CTAs. Urgency must be justified. Every element must work together in an ascending emotional arc that culminates in irresistible action.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The price anchor cascade writer (SIN Chunk 8) — retail to middle to actual price reveal
- The stack review writer (SIN Chunk 9) — re-listing everything with individual values
- The savings statement writer (SIN Chunk 10) — amount and percentage presentation
- The risk reversal segue writer (SIN Chunk 11) — transition to guarantee
- The branded guarantee writer (SIN Chunk 12) — named guarantee with specific terms
- The triple-CTA writer (SIN Chunks 13, 15, 17) — three calls to action with different emotions
- The consequence amplification writer (SIN Chunk 14) — pain reminder between CTAs
- The urgency section writer (SIN Chunk 16) — justified scarcity or time pressure
- The optional contrast enhancement writer (SIN Chunk 18) — before/after comparison

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A price setter — pricing comes from the price presentation plan (1.4)
- A guarantee designer — guarantee terms come from confirmed offer data (0.4)
- A value demonstration writer — that was 2.3
- A deliverable or bonus writer — those were 2.1 and 2.2
- A strategic planner — all planning was done in Layer 1

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Write the Price Presentation (SIN Chunk 8)

1. The price section follows immediately from the value demonstration conclusion
2. Write the anchor cascade using the planned structure from 1.4:

**Standard Anchor Cascade:**
```
But today, when you grab [Product], it isn't gonna cost you $[retail_value].

[If middle anchor used:] It's not even gonna cost you $[middle_anchor].

Instead, today, you get [Product] for just $[actual_price].

[If payment plan:] Or, if you prefer, just [N] easy payments of $[payment_amount].
```

TIER1 price reveal variations:
- "But you're not going to pay anywhere near $[retail]. Not even close."
- "Now, if I charged $[retail] for all of this, I think you'd agree that's more than fair. But I'm not going to charge you $[retail]."
- "And here's the best part. When you grab [Product] today, you won't pay $[retail]. You won't even pay $[middle]. Today, [Product] is yours for just $[actual]."

3. The price reveal must:
   - Reference the retail value anchor (already established in value totaling)
   - Create a clear DESCENT from high to low
   - Make the actual price feel shockingly low by contrast
   - State the actual price clearly and confidently

### Step 2: Write the Stack Review (SIN Chunk 9)

4. Re-list every deliverable and bonus with individual values:

**Stack Review Format:**
```
Just to recap everything you're getting:

[checkmark] [Deliverable 1] .............. ($[value] value)
[checkmark] [Deliverable 2] .............. ($[value] value)
[checkmark] [Bonus 1] .................... ($[value] value)
[checkmark] [Bonus 2] .................... ($[value] value)
[checkmark] [Bonus 3] .................... ($[value] value)

Total Value: $[retail_value]
Your Price Today: Just $[actual_price]
```

5. Use the exact item names and values from the stack_review plan (1.4)
6. Verify all values sum to the stated total value

### Step 3: Write the Savings Statement (SIN Chunk 10)

7. Write the savings statement:

**Standard Pattern:**
"That's [savings_percentage]% off. That's a savings of over $[savings_amount]."

**TIER1 variations:**
- "You're saving $[savings_amount] — that's [savings_percentage]% off the retail value."
- "That means you save over $[savings_amount] when you order today."
- "That's a [savings_percentage]% discount — saving you $[savings_amount]."

8. If per-day breakdown is available:
   - "That works out to less than $[daily_cost] per day."
   - "That's less than a cup of coffee per day for [transformation description]."

### Step 4: Write the Risk Reversal Segue (SIN Chunk 11)

9. Write the transition from price/savings to the guarantee:

**Standard Pattern:**
"And here's the thing..."

**TIER1 variations:**
- "And here's the best part..."
- "Now, I know what you might be thinking..."
- "But I want to make this even easier for you..."
- "And because I'm so confident in [Product]..."

10. This segue must:
    - Acknowledge the prospect may still have hesitation
    - Introduce the guarantee as the answer to that hesitation
    - Transition smoothly — not feel like a new section

### Step 5: Write the Branded Guarantee (SIN Chunk 12)

11. Write the full guarantee section. The guarantee MUST be branded — never generic.

**Branded Guarantee Pattern:**
```
[Product] comes with our [Branded Guarantee Name].

Here's how it works: [specific terms explained in plain language].

[Duration] days. That's how long you have to try everything — the [Product], the [bonuses], the complete system. And if for any reason — or no reason at all — you're not [specific satisfaction standard], just [action to take], and I'll [specific refund terms]. No questions. No hassle. No hoops to jump through.

That's how confident I am that [Product] is going to [promise reference].
```

TIER1 guarantee patterns:
- "I call it the [Product Name] Promise. Try everything for a full [duration] days. If you don't [specific measurable result], just send me an email and I'll refund every penny. I'll even let you keep the bonuses."
- "Your order is backed by our [Branded Name] — a full [duration]-day, no-questions-asked, money-back guarantee. I'm putting my money where my mouth is."
- "I'm so confident you're going to love [Product] that I'm giving you a full [duration] days to try it risk-free. If you don't [result], you pay nothing."

12. The guarantee MUST NOT use:
    - "If not satisfied, return for refund" (generic)
    - "Money-back guarantee" without a branded name
    - "Satisfaction guaranteed" without specifics
    - Any language that sounds like a policy rather than a FEATURE

13. The guarantee MUST include:
    - A branded name (e.g., "The Resurge Promise," "The 90-Day Transformation Guarantee")
    - Specific duration
    - Clear terms in plain language
    - A confidence statement linking back to the promise

### Step 6: Write CTA 1 — Confidence (SIN Chunk 13)

14. Write the first call to action with a CONFIDENCE emotional appeal:

**CTA 1 Pattern:**
"Go ahead, click the button below, and grab [Product] right now. You'll get instant access to [key deliverable] plus all [N] bonuses — everything you need to [promise restated]."

TIER1 CTA 1 patterns:
- "Click the button below right now to get your supply of [Product] today."
- "Go ahead — click the button, grab your copy, and start [transformation] today."
- "Hit the button below to secure your order of [Product] right now."

15. CTA 1 characteristics:
    - Emotional appeal: CONFIDENCE and EXCITEMENT
    - Tone: positive, forward-looking, action-oriented
    - Includes: specific action ("click the button"), product name, brief benefit reference
    - This is the CTA for prospects who are READY to buy

### Step 7: Write Consequence Amplification (SIN Chunk 14)

16. Write the consequence amplification section — a pain reminder between CTAs:

**Consequence Amplification Pattern:**
"Look, I understand if you need a moment to think about it. But I want you to consider something. Right now, you're dealing with [specific pain/frustration]. And every day you wait is another day of [continued pain]. Another day of [specific negative consequence]. Another [timeframe] of [what they're missing]."

TIER1 consequence amplification patterns:
- "Think about where you'll be in 30 days if nothing changes. Still [pain]. Still [frustration]. Still [limitation]."
- "You've been dealing with [problem] for how long now? Another month? Another year? At what point do you say 'enough'?"
- "There's a cost to doing nothing. Every day you wait is another day of [pain], another day of [loss], another day you're not [desired state]."

17. Consequence amplification must:
    - Reference SPECIFIC pains from the promise package transformation (before state)
    - Make the prospect feel the cost of inaction
    - Be empathetic, not aggressive — "I understand" before "but consider"
    - Lead naturally into CTA 2

### Step 8: Write CTA 2 — Consequence Avoidance (SIN Chunk 15)

18. Write the second call to action with a CONSEQUENCE AVOIDANCE emotional appeal:

**CTA 2 Pattern:**
"There's no reason to continue dealing with [pain]. You don't have to keep [negative situation]. You can change all of that today. Just click the button below and grab [Product] right now."

TIER1 CTA 2 patterns:
- "You don't have to spend one more night [pain]. Click the button below and start your transformation tonight."
- "Enough is enough. You deserve better than [current situation]. Grab [Product] now and take the first step."
- "You've waited long enough. Click below and let today be the day everything changes."

19. CTA 2 characteristics:
    - Emotional appeal: FEAR/PAIN AVOIDANCE — escape from current suffering
    - Tone: empathetic but urgent, problem-to-solution transition
    - Includes: specific pain reference, action instruction, product name
    - This is the CTA for prospects who need to be reminded WHY they need to act

### Step 9: Write Urgency Section (SIN Chunk 16)

20. Write the urgency/scarcity section with JUSTIFIED reasons:

**Urgency Pattern:**
"Please understand — this is a limited-time offer. [Specific justification for urgency]. [Specific detail about what changes]. So if you want to get [Product] at this price, with all the bonuses included, I'd encourage you to act now."

TIER1 urgency patterns:
- Supply-based: "We can only produce [X] bottles per batch, and this batch is already [percentage] sold."
- Time-based: "This special pricing is only available during this [event/launch]. After [date], the price goes back to $[higher price]."
- Demand-based: "We've already shipped [X] units this week. At this rate, this batch will be gone by [date]."
- Bonus-based: "The bonuses alone are worth $[value], and I can't guarantee I'll include them in future orders."

21. Urgency MUST be justified — never use:
    - "Limited time offer" without specifying WHY it's limited
    - "Act now" without a credible reason
    - "Don't miss out" without specifics
    - Generic scarcity language with no backing

### Step 10: Write CTA 3 — Urgency (SIN Chunk 17)

22. Write the third call to action with an URGENCY emotional appeal:

**CTA 3 Pattern:**
"If you want [Product] at this price — with all the bonuses, with the [Guarantee Name] protecting every penny — now is the time to act. Click the button below and get started right now."

TIER1 CTA 3 patterns:
- "This offer won't last. Click the button below now — before this page comes down — and secure your order."
- "Don't wait on this. Click below right now, grab [Product], and start [transformation] today. You won't find this price again."
- "The button is right below. Click it now. Grab [Product]. Your [guarantee] means you risk nothing. But waiting means you lose everything you've seen today."

23. CTA 3 characteristics:
    - Emotional appeal: URGENCY and FINALITY
    - Tone: direct, no-nonsense, time-sensitive
    - Includes: urgency reference, guarantee reminder, action instruction
    - This is the CTA for prospects who procrastinate — the "now or never" push

### Step 11: Write Optional Contrast Enhancement (SIN Chunk 18)

24. If appropriate, write a contrast enhancement before CTA 3:

**Contrast Enhancement Pattern:**
"Think about this. A year from now, you could still be [current pain state]. Or... you could be [transformed state]. The difference is one click."

TIER1 contrast patterns:
- "You have two choices right now. Choice one: close this page, go back to [current situation], and nothing changes. Choice two: click the button, grab [Product], and [start transformation]."
- "Imagine looking back a year from now. In one version of your future, you took action today and [positive outcome]. In the other version, you didn't — and you're still [negative situation]. Which version do you want?"

25. The contrast enhancement is optional but recommended when:
    - The transformation is dramatic (before/after states are very different)
    - The consequence amplification was strong (double down on the contrast)
    - The product addresses chronic, long-standing pain

### Step 12: Verify Complete Section Quality

26. Run all quality checks:
    - Price anchored correctly (retail > middle > actual)
    - Stack review values sum to retail value
    - Savings math correct
    - Guarantee is branded (has a NAME, not just "money-back guarantee")
    - Guarantee has specific terms (duration, conditions, action to take)
    - 3 CTAs present with DIFFERENT emotional appeals
    - CTA 1 = confidence, CTA 2 = consequence avoidance, CTA 3 = urgency
    - Consequence amplification present between CTA 1 and CTA 2
    - Urgency section present between CTA 2 and CTA 3
    - Urgency is justified (specific reason given)
    - Each CTA uses different action language (not all "click the button")
27. Calculate total word count for the complete section

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Generic Guarantee
- **Symptom:** "Backed by our 60-day money-back guarantee. If not satisfied, return for a full refund."
- **Cause:** Writer used policy language instead of feature language; no branded name
- **Prevention:** Guarantee MUST have a branded name and sound like an expression of confidence, not a return policy
- **Recovery:** Rewrite: "Your order is protected by our [Branded Name]. You have [duration] days to try everything. If you don't [specific result], I'll refund every penny."

### Failure Mode 2: Identical CTAs
- **Symptom:** All three CTAs say "Click the button below to order now" with slight wording variations
- **Cause:** Writer did not differentiate emotional appeals across CTAs
- **Prevention:** Each CTA MUST have a different emotional trigger: confidence, consequence avoidance, urgency
- **Recovery:** Rewrite each CTA with distinct emotional framing and different supporting reasons

### Failure Mode 3: Unjustified Urgency
- **Symptom:** "This is a limited time offer. Don't miss out." — no reason WHY it's limited
- **Cause:** Writer used generic urgency language without providing justification
- **Prevention:** Every urgency claim MUST have a specific reason (supply, time, demand, bonus expiration)
- **Recovery:** Add justification: "We can only produce [X] units per batch..." or "This pricing is only available during this launch..."

### Failure Mode 4: Stack Review Math Error
- **Symptom:** Individual values add up to $523 but total stated as $597
- **Cause:** Values not reconciled with the price presentation plan
- **Prevention:** Pull exact values from 1.4 stack_review plan; verify sum before writing
- **Recovery:** Adjust individual values to sum correctly to retail_value

### Failure Mode 5: Missing Consequence Amplification
- **Symptom:** CTA 1 immediately followed by CTA 2 with no pain reminder between them
- **Cause:** Writer treated CTAs as a list instead of a sequence with emotional content between them
- **Prevention:** Consequence amplification is MANDATORY between CTA 1 and CTA 2
- **Recovery:** Insert consequence amplification section referencing specific prospect pains from the promise transformation

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Supplement Price/Guarantee/CTA Section

**Product:** Resurge | **Retail:** $597 | **Price:** $49 | **Guarantee:** 60-day

**Draft (key sections):**

**Price Presentation:**
"But today, when you grab Resurge, it isn't gonna cost you $597. It's not even gonna cost you $297. Instead, today, you get Resurge — the complete system, all eight clinically studied ingredients, plus every bonus — for just $49."

**Guarantee:**
"Your order is backed by The Resurge Promise — our personal 60-day, empty-every-bottle guarantee. Here's how it works. I want you to open your bottle tonight. Take Resurge for a full 60 days. Experience the deep sleep. Feel the energy. See the difference in the mirror. And if for ANY reason — or no reason at all — you're not absolutely thrilled with the results, just send me an email and I'll refund every single penny. No questions asked. No hassle. No hoops. I'll even let you keep the bonuses as my gift. That's how confident I am that Resurge is going to change the way you sleep, the way you look, and the way you feel."

**CTA 1 (Confidence):**
"Go ahead — click the button below and grab your supply of Resurge right now. You'll get instant access to the complete system plus all three free bonuses. Your order ships today."

**Consequence Amplification:**
"Look, I understand if you're taking a moment to think about it. But consider this. Tonight, when you go to bed, you're either going to fall into the same shallow, restless sleep you've been getting for years — tossing, turning, waking up exhausted — or you can start doing something about it. Every night of bad sleep ages your body. Drains your energy. Steals the moments that matter most. And that's not going to change on its own."

**CTA 2 (Consequence Avoidance):**
"You don't have to spend one more night tossing and turning. You don't have to wake up tomorrow feeling ten years older than you are. You can change all of that — starting tonight. Click the button below and get Resurge now."

**CTA 3 (Urgency):**
"Please understand — this special pricing won't last. We can only produce a limited number of bottles per batch, and this current batch is already more than half gone. If you want Resurge at this price, with all the bonuses, backed by The Resurge Promise — now is the time. Click below and secure your order before this batch sells out."
