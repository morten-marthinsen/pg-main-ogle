## PURPOSE

The Price Presentation Planner designs the complete price reveal structure: how the retail value anchor is established, whether a middle anchor is used, how the actual price is revealed, what the payment terms look like, how savings are calculated and presented, and how the stack review is formatted. This is the mathematical and structural backbone of the price section — every number must be accurate, every anchor ratio must be effective, and every savings calculation must be verifiable.

Price presentation in the SIN methodology follows a strict anchor cascade: the prospect first learns the TOTAL VALUE of everything they are getting (retail value), then potentially sees a middle anchor ("it's not even gonna cost you this much"), and finally sees the actual price. The contrast between the high anchor and the low actual price creates the perception of extraordinary value. Without careful planning, the price section can undermine everything the offer has built — if the math is wrong, the anchors are weak, or the savings statement is unclear, the prospect's desire collapses.

This planner also designs the stack review format — the section where every deliverable and bonus is re-listed with its individual value before the total is revealed. The stack review is the final value reinforcement before the price drop. It must be complete, accurate, and formatted for maximum impact.

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The price anchor cascade architect — designing the full retail-to-actual price reveal
- A savings calculator — computing exact savings amounts and percentages
- A stack review designer — formatting the re-listing of all deliverables and bonuses with values
- A middle anchor strategist — determining whether an intermediate price point strengthens the reveal
- A payment terms formatter — structuring single-pay, multi-pay, and per-day breakdowns
- A math verifier — ensuring all price calculations are correct and consistent
- A price psychology implementer — using TIER1 anchor ratio patterns

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A price setter — pricing strategy comes from Skill 06 or human checkpoint
- A value demonstration designer — that is 1.3 (this handles price math, not value rhetoric)
- A draft writer — it plans the numbers and structure, writer 2.4 produces the prose
- A guarantee planner — guarantee is a separate section (though it follows price closely)
- An offer architect — this executes the pricing plan, not designs it

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Validate All Pricing Inputs

1. Confirm retail_value is present and > 0
2. Confirm actual_price is present and > 0
3. Confirm retail_value > actual_price (anchor must be HIGHER)
4. Confirm payment terms are specified
5. Validate individual bonus values:
   - Each bonus has a stated dollar value
   - Sum of all bonus values is calculated
6. Calculate total value composition:
   - Product/deliverable value = retail_value - total_bonus_value
   - Verify this is positive (product value should be most of the retail value)
7. Flag any pricing inconsistencies for resolution

### Step 2: Calculate Anchor Ratio and Assess Strength

8. Calculate primary anchor ratio: retail_value / actual_price
   - Ratio >= 10x: EXCELLENT — very strong price contrast
   - Ratio 5x-9x: GOOD — solid price contrast
   - Ratio 3x-4x: ADEQUATE — workable but not dramatic
   - Ratio < 3x: WEAK — price contrast may not be compelling
9. Record anchor_ratio and anchor_strength assessment
10. If anchor_ratio < 3x: recommend reviewing retail_value or adding bonus values to increase total perceived value

### Step 3: Determine Middle Anchor Strategy

11. Decide whether to include a middle anchor between retail and actual price:

**USE MIDDLE ANCHOR when:**
- Anchor ratio >= 5x (enough room for a meaningful middle step)
- Actual price >= $97 (lower prices don't need middle anchor — the final price is already perceived as low)
- Product format is supplement, physical, or hybrid (info products sometimes skip middle anchor)

**SKIP MIDDLE ANCHOR when:**
- Anchor ratio < 5x (not enough room for a meaningful step)
- Actual price < $50 (direct drop to price is more dramatic)
- The price cascade would feel forced or artificial

12. If using middle anchor, calculate:
    - Middle anchor = approximately halfway between retail and actual, rounded to a clean number
    - Example: Retail $597, Actual $49 → Middle anchor $297 or $197
    - The middle anchor should be a number the prospect can imagine paying ("not even $297")
    - Round to nearest $97, $95, or $99 ending for psychological pricing

### Step 4: Calculate Savings

13. Calculate savings amount: retail_value - actual_price
14. Calculate savings percentage: ((retail_value - actual_price) / retail_value) * 100
    - Round to nearest whole number for clean presentation
    - If percentage >= 90%: present as "over 90% off"
    - If percentage 70-89%: present as "[X]% off"
    - If percentage 50-69%: present as "more than half off"
15. Calculate per-day breakdown (optional but recommended):
    - Daily cost: actual_price / 30 (for 30-day supply products)
    - Daily cost: actual_price / 365 (for annual programs)
    - Daily cost: actual_price / 90 (for 90-day programs)
    - Round to nearest cent
    - Frame with comparison: "less than a cup of coffee per day," "less than your daily [common expense]"
16. If payment plan is available:
    - Calculate per-payment amount
    - Calculate total with payments vs. single pay
    - Frame as: "just [N] easy payments of $[amount]"

### Step 5: Design the Stack Review Format

17. The stack review re-lists every deliverable and bonus with values. Design the format:

**Standard Stack Review Format:**
```
Here's everything you get when you grab [Product Name] today:

✓ [Deliverable 1] ...................... ($[value] value)
✓ [Deliverable 2] ...................... ($[value] value)
✓ [Deliverable 3] ...................... ($[value] value)
✓ [Bonus 1] ........................... ($[value] value)
✓ [Bonus 2] ........................... ($[value] value)
✓ [Bonus 3] ........................... ($[value] value)

Total Value: $[retail_value]
Your Price Today: $[actual_price]
You Save: $[savings_amount] ([savings_percentage]%)
```

18. Assign individual values to each item:
    - Bonuses: use stated values from confirmed data
    - Primary deliverable: use (retail_value - total_bonus_value) or the product's standalone price
    - Supporting deliverables: allocate remaining value proportionally
19. Verify: sum of all individual values = retail_value (MANDATORY — math must be exact)

### Step 6: Plan Price Reveal Language Structure

20. Map the price reveal to the SIN template structure:

**Value Totaling (Chunk 6):**
- "With everything you get today... [product] has a total value of $[retail_value]"

**Price Presentation (Chunk 8):**
- "But today, when you grab [product], it isn't gonna cost you $[retail_value]."
- (If middle anchor): "It's not even gonna cost you $[middle_anchor]."
- "Instead, today, you get [product] for just $[actual_price]."
- (If payment plan): "Or, if you prefer, just [N] easy payments of $[payment_amount]."

**Stack Review (Chunk 9):**
- Re-listing format from Step 5

**Savings Statement (Chunk 10):**
- "That's [savings_percentage]% off. That's a savings of over $[savings_amount]."
- (If per-day): "That's less than $[daily_cost] per day."

21. Note the emotional arc of the price section:
    - Value totaling: ANTICIPATION — "wow, this is worth a lot"
    - Retail anchor: EXPECTATION — "I bet this costs [retail]"
    - Middle anchor (if used): HOPE — "okay, not as much as I feared"
    - Actual price: EXCITEMENT — "that's ALL? I have to get this"
    - Savings statement: VALIDATION — "I'm making a smart decision"

### Step 7: Validate Price Presentation Plan

22. Run all validation checks:
    - retail_value > actual_price: PASS/FAIL
    - anchor_ratio >= 3: PASS/FAIL (WARNING if below 3)
    - savings_amount calculated correctly: PASS/FAIL
    - savings_percentage calculated correctly: PASS/FAIL
    - stack review values sum to retail_value: PASS/FAIL
    - payment terms are clear: PASS/FAIL
    - middle anchor decision documented: PASS/FAIL
    - per-day breakdown calculated (if applicable): PASS/FAIL
23. Record all numbers in a price_math_verification table:

```yaml

  daily_cost: float | null
  payment_amount: float | null
  payment_count: integer | null
  stack_values_sum: float  # must equal retail_value
  all_math_correct: boolean
```

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Stack Review Values Do Not Sum to Retail
- **Symptom:** Individual item values add up to $523 but retail value is $597
- **Cause:** Values assigned to deliverables and bonuses were not reconciled with total retail value
- **Prevention:** Step 5, item 19 — mandatory verification that sum equals retail_value
- **Recovery:** Adjust the primary deliverable value to absorb the difference. The primary deliverable value = retail_value - sum(bonus_values) - sum(secondary_deliverable_values).

### Failure Mode 2: Middle Anchor Too Close to Actual Price
- **Symptom:** Middle anchor $79, actual price $49 — not enough contrast
- **Cause:** Middle anchor calculated as halfway but actual price is already very low
- **Prevention:** Middle anchor must be at least 2x actual_price to create meaningful contrast
- **Recovery:** Either raise middle anchor higher or skip middle anchor entirely (direct drop is more dramatic for low-priced products)

### Failure Mode 3: Savings Percentage Calculation Error
- **Symptom:** Claimed "85% off" but actual calculation shows 78%
- **Cause:** Rounding or formula error in percentage calculation
- **Prevention:** Step 4, item 14 — explicit formula: ((retail - actual) / retail) * 100
- **Recovery:** Recalculate with exact numbers, verify with manual math, fix any rounding

### Failure Mode 4: Per-Day Breakdown Unrealistic
- **Symptom:** "Less than $0.003 per day" — too small a number to be meaningful
- **Cause:** High days_basis (365) with low actual_price produces trivially small daily cost
- **Prevention:** Check if daily_cost < $0.25 — if so, use a different frame (per-week, per-month)
- **Recovery:** Switch from daily to weekly or monthly breakdown: "less than $[weekly] per week"

### Failure Mode 5: Anchor Ratio Below Minimum
- **Symptom:** Retail $197, Actual $97 — ratio of 2.03x — price contrast is weak
- **Cause:** Retail value was not built up enough through deliverable and bonus values
- **Prevention:** Flag weak anchor ratio in validation and recommend adding bonus values or increasing perceived retail value
- **Recovery:** Two options: (1) Add bonus items with values to increase total retail, or (2) reframe the price presentation to emphasize absolute savings rather than ratio

### Example 1: Supplement — Strong Anchor Ratio with Middle Anchor

**Input:** Retail $597, Actual $49, 3 bonuses ($97, $47, $29)

**Price Presentation Plan:**
```yaml

  middle_anchor_template: "It's not even gonna cost you $297."
  actual_price_template: "Instead, today, you get Resurge for just $49."
```
