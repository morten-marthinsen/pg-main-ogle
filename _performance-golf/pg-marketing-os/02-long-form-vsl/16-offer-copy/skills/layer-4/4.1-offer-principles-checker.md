---

## PURPOSE

Validates all 10 offer copy principles against the assembled draft from Layers 2-3. Each principle represents a non-negotiable quality standard derived from the SIN Offer Presentation methodology and TIER1 elite offer patterns. This is the first validation gate — it checks structural and strategic compliance before anti-slop and vault comparison.

The 10 principles are the guardrails that separate elite offer copy from generic sales pages. Violating even one principle can collapse the persuasive architecture of the entire offer section. This microskill provides pass/fail evidence for each principle with specific textual citations from the draft.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The structural compliance checker for all 10 offer copy principles
- A pass/fail validator with textual evidence for each principle
- The first quality gate in Layer 4 — structural before stylistic
- A principle-by-principle audit with specific citations from the draft
- The enforcer of SIN methodology structural requirements
- A remediation direction provider when principles fail

**This microskill is NOT:**
- An anti-slop validator (that is 4.2 — language-level checking)
- A vault pattern comparator (that is 4.3 — competitive benchmarking)
- A copy editor or rewriter (this validates, doesn't fix)
- A subjective quality scorer (this is binary pass/fail per principle)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load All 10 Principles

1. Index the 10 offer copy principles from the master agent:

| # | Principle | What to Check |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | D-F-W-B-P IS SACRED | Every deliverable and bonus has all 5 elements |
| 2 | PROMISE RESTATED, NEVER REPEATED | Promise appears multiple times, never identical |
| 3 | VALUE BEFORE PRICE, ALWAYS | Total value and stack review precede actual price |
| 4 | "IF ALL IT DID WAS..." IS NOT OPTIONAL | Value demonstration has 3+ iterations |
| 5 | GUARANTEE IS A FEATURE, NOT A POLICY | Guarantee is branded, no "if not satisfied" |
| 6 | THREE CTAS MINIMUM | At least 3 calls to action with different appeals |
| 7 | CONSEQUENCE AMPLIFICATION BETWEEN CTAS | Pain/frustration reminder between CTAs |
| 8 | URGENCY MUST BE JUSTIFIED | Every urgency claim has specific reason |
| 9 | STACK REVIEW BEFORE PRICE | Full stack re-listed before actual price |
| 10 | SEAMLESS ENTRY FROM PRODUCT INTRODUCTION | First line flows from Skill 15 handoff |

### Step 2: Validate Principle 1 — D-F-W-B-P Sacred

2. For each deliverable in the stack:
   - Verify Deliverable name present (the "D")
   - Verify Feature described (the "F") — what it IS
   - Verify Why it matters stated (the "W") — why prospect should care
   - Verify Benefit articulated (the "B") — what prospect GAINS
   - Verify Proof element included (the "P") — evidence it works

3. For each bonus:
   - Same D-F-W-B-P check
   - Verify stated dollar value present

4. Record: pass/fail per item, with specific missing elements noted.

### Step 3: Validate Principle 2 — Promise Restated, Never Repeated

5. Scan full draft for all instances of the primary promise or primary transformation
6. Extract each instance verbatim
7. Compare pairwise — are any two instances using identical language?
8. Verify minimum 3 distinct restatements exist
9. Record: pass (3+ unique restatements) / fail (identical copy-paste or fewer than 3)

### Step 4: Validate Principle 3 — Value Before Price

10. Locate the total value statement (e.g., "a total value of $X")
11. Locate the actual price statement (e.g., "for just $X")
12. Verify the total value appears BEFORE the actual price in the text
13. Record: pass (value before price) / fail (price before value or no value stated)

### Step 5: Validate Principle 4 — "If All It Did Was..." Present

14. Count instances of the "if all it did was..." pattern (or semantic equivalents)
15. Verify minimum 3 iterations
16. Verify each iteration uses a different benefit
17. Verify each iteration includes a "why it would be worth it" reason
18. Record: pass (3+ iterations with reasons) / fail (fewer than 3 or missing reasons)

### Step 6: Validate Principle 5 — Guarantee as Feature

19. Check guarantee section for branded name (a specific name, not just "guarantee")
20. Check for "if not satisfied" language — this is a FAIL trigger
21. Check for positive framing ("You must be absolutely delighted")
22. Check that guarantee is presented as confidence expression, not refund policy
23. Record: pass (branded, positive framing) / fail (generic or negative framing)

### Step 7: Validate Principle 6 — Three CTAs Minimum

24. Count distinct calls to action (explicit requests for the prospect to take action)
25. Verify each CTA uses a different emotional appeal
26. Verify each CTA uses different action language
27. Record: pass (3+ varied CTAs) / fail (fewer than 3 or identical CTAs)

### Step 8: Validate Principle 7 — Consequence Amplification

28. Locate text between CTAs that references current pain, frustration, or negative status quo
29. Verify consequence amplification appears between at least CTA 1 and CTA 2
30. Verify it references specific pains (not generic "don't miss out")
31. Record: pass (specific consequence amplification between CTAs) / fail (none or generic)

### Step 9: Validate Principle 8 — Urgency Justified

32. Locate all urgency claims (time-limited, quantity-limited, price-increasing, etc.)
33. For each urgency claim, verify a specific justification exists
34. Flag any "act now" or "limited time" language without a backing reason
35. Record: pass (all urgency justified) / fail (unjustified urgency found)

### Step 10: Validate Principle 9 — Stack Review Before Price

36. Locate the stack review section (re-listing of deliverables + bonuses)
37. Verify it appears BEFORE the actual price is stated
38. Verify the review includes both deliverables AND bonuses
39. Record: pass (complete stack review before price) / fail (missing or misplaced)

### Step 11: Validate Principle 10 — Seamless Entry

40. Compare the first sentence of the offer section with the ending of Skill 12's handoff
41. Verify narrative continuity — no jarring "now here's the offer" shift
42. Verify emotional state continuity from product introduction
43. Record: pass (seamless flow) / fail (jarring transition)

### Step 12: Compile Principle Audit Report

44. Assemble results:

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Missed D-F-W-B-P Element
- **Symptom:** Deliverable has Feature but no Why or Benefit — reads as feature dump
- **Cause:** Writer focused on what the deliverable IS, not why it MATTERS
- **Prevention:** Step 2 checks each element independently for every item
- **Recovery:** Flag specific deliverable/bonus and missing element for Layer 2 rewrite

### Failure Mode 2: Copy-Pasted Promise
- **Symptom:** Promise appears identically in two or more locations
- **Cause:** Writer used same phrase for efficiency
- **Prevention:** Step 3 pairwise comparison catches identical instances
- **Recovery:** Flag identical instances and request rephrasing with preserved meaning

### Failure Mode 3: Generic Guarantee
- **Symptom:** "Money-back guarantee" or "If not satisfied, return for refund"
- **Cause:** Writer defaulted to industry-standard guarantee language
- **Prevention:** Step 6 checks for branded name AND positive framing AND absence of "if not satisfied"
- **Recovery:** Rewrite guarantee with branded name and "You must be absolutely delighted" framing

### Failure Mode 4: Identical CTAs
- **Symptom:** All CTAs say "Click the button below and order now"
- **Cause:** Writer treated CTAs as repetition rather than escalation
- **Prevention:** Step 7 requires different emotional appeal for each CTA
- **Recovery:** Rewrite each CTA with assigned emotion: confidence, consequence avoidance, urgency

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Full Pass — Supplement Offer (Montezuma Pattern)

**Context:** Male vitality supplement, 3 deliverables, 2 bonuses, $197 retail, $49.95 actual

**Audit Results:**
- Principle 1: PASS — All 3 deliverables have D-F-W-B-P, both bonuses have D-F-W-B-P with values
- Principle 2: PASS — Promise restated 4 times: "reclaim your sexual prime" / "experience the vigor of your twenties" / "unlock your body's natural vitality" / "transform your intimate life"
- Principle 3: PASS — "$197 total value" at word 842, "$49.95" at word 923
- Principle 4: PASS — 3 iterations: bedroom confidence, relationship satisfaction, overall energy
- Principle 5: PASS — "Triple-Back Guarantee" branded, "You must be blown away by your results"
- Principle 6: PASS — CTA 1 (confidence), CTA 2 (fear of continued decline), CTA 3 (urgency/supply)
- Principle 7: PASS — Between CTA 1 and 2: "There's no need to continue feeling the shame of..."
- Principle 8: PASS — "Today, this video is being released to hundreds of thousands of men..."
- Principle 9: PASS — Stack review at word 910, price at word 923
- Principle 10: PASS — First line: "And that's exactly why I created this for you" flows from product reveal

**Summary:** 10/10 pass. All principles validated.

### Example 2: Partial Fail — Digital Product (Info Course)

**Context:** Marketing course, 5 deliverables, 3 bonuses, $997 retail, $297 actual

**Audit Results:**
- Principle 1: FAIL — Deliverable 4 missing Proof element, Bonus 2 missing Why element
- Principle 2: PASS — 3 unique restatements found
- Principle 5: FAIL — Guarantee says "If you're not happy with the course, just email us for a refund"
- All others: PASS

**Remediation:** Deliverable 4 needs clinical or testimonial proof added. Bonus 2 needs "Why this matters" sentence. Guarantee needs branded name and positive framing rewrite.
