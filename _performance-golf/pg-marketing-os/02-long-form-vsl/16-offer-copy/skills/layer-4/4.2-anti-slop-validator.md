## PURPOSE

Performs zero-tolerance language quality validation on the assembled offer copy. "Slop" is any generic, cliched, AI-default, or lazy commercial language that undermines the persuasive specificity of the offer section. The offer section is particularly vulnerable to slop because it deals with commercial elements (pricing, guarantees, CTAs) that have been written millions of times in generic form.

This microskill scans every sentence of the offer copy against a comprehensive slop dictionary organized by category. Any violation is logged. The pass threshold is ZERO violations — even one instance of generic offer language requires remediation before the package can be assembled.

The offer section must read as if it was written specifically for THIS product, THIS audience, in THIS niche — never as if a template was filled in.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- A zero-tolerance language quality scanner for offer copy
- A slop dictionary matcher organized by offer section type
- A violation logger with specific line/phrase citations
- The language-level quality gate (vs. 4.1's structural gate)
- An AI-default language detector — catches patterns that LLMs frequently produce
- A specificity enforcer — every commercial claim must be concrete, not generic

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A structural principle checker (that is 4.1)
- A vault pattern comparator (that is 4.3)
- A copy editor or rewriter (this flags, doesn't fix)
- A tone or style guide (this catches specific forbidden phrases)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Load Slop Dictionary by Category

1. Index the following slop categories and their forbidden patterns:

**CATEGORY A: Generic Offer Language**
- "amazing bonuses" / "incredible bonuses" / "valuable bonuses"
- "incredible value" / "amazing value" / "unbelievable deal"
- "this is a no-brainer" (without specific justification)
- "what are you waiting for?" (as standalone — acceptable with context)
- "you won't believe..." / "you'll be amazed..."
- "this changes everything" (without specifying what changes)
- "revolutionary" / "groundbreaking" / "game-changing" (without specifics)

**CATEGORY B: Generic Guarantee Language**
- "money-back guarantee" (without branded name)
- "satisfaction guaranteed"
- "if not satisfied, return for refund"
- "risk-free trial" (without specific guarantee terms)
- "nothing to lose" (without specific guarantee backing)
- "100% guaranteed" (without specifying what is guaranteed)

**CATEGORY C: Generic CTA Language**
- "don't miss out" (without specific consequence)
- "act now" (without specific reason)
- "limited time offer" (without specific time/reason)
- "order now" (as the entire CTA — too thin)
- "buy now" / "purchase today" (without supporting reason)
- "hurry" / "rush" (without specific urgency justification)
- "don't wait" / "don't delay" (without consequence)

**CATEGORY D: Generic Urgency/Scarcity**
- "supplies are limited" (without specific quantity or reason)
- "this offer won't last" (without specific end date or trigger)
- "prices will go up" (without specific increase or date)
- "exclusive offer" (without specifying what makes it exclusive)
- "once in a lifetime" / "opportunity of a lifetime"
- "you'll never see this price again" (without justification)

**CATEGORY E: Feature Dumps**
- Any deliverable or bonus described ONLY with features, no Why/Benefit
- Bulleted specs without transformation language
- "includes..." lists without "so you can..." bridges
- Technical descriptions without prospect relevance

**CATEGORY F: AI-Default Patterns**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "Are you tired of..." (overused AI opening)
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Look, I'm going to be honest with you..."
- "Here's the thing..." (overused, though acceptable in SIN risk reversal segue)
- "Let me ask you something..."
- "The truth is..." (when used as filler, not revelation)
- "But that's not all..." (acceptable per SIN methodology IF used sparingly)
- Excessive exclamation marks (more than 2 per 100 words)
- ALL CAPS for more than 3 words in sequence (except branded guarantee names)

### Step 2: Scan Full Draft

2. Process the full offer narrative text sentence by sentence
3. For each sentence, check against ALL categories
4. Flag any match with:
   - Category (A-F)
   - Exact phrase matched
   - Location in draft (section + approximate position)
   - Severity: CRITICAL (guaranteed fail) or WARNING (contextual — may be acceptable)

### Step 3: Evaluate Contextual Warnings

5. For WARNING-level matches, evaluate context:
   - "But that's not all..." — acceptable in SIN bonus transition (1 use)
   - "Here's the thing..." — acceptable in SIN risk reversal segue (1 use)
   - "this is a no-brainer" — acceptable if preceded by specific justification
   - "what are you waiting for?" — acceptable if preceded by specific consequence

6. Upgrade to CRITICAL if context doesn't justify, or downgrade to ACCEPTABLE if it does

### Step 4: Check for Specificity

7. Beyond dictionary matching, check that every commercial claim has specificity:
   - Price claims reference specific dollar amounts
   - Guarantee claims reference specific branded name and terms
   - Urgency claims reference specific reason or deadline
   - Value claims reference specific dollar values
   - Benefit claims reference specific outcomes, not vague improvements

8. Flag any commercial claim that relies on vague language rather than specifics

### Step 5: Compile Violation Report

9. Assemble final report:

```yaml
anti_slop_report:
  total_violations: integer
  critical_violations: integer
  warnings_evaluated: integer
  warnings_accepted: integer
  violations:
    - category: string  # A through F
      phrase: string
      location: string
      severity: enum[critical, warning]
      context_evaluation: string  # if warning
      resolution: enum[critical, accepted]
  specificity_gaps:
    - claim_type: string
      location: string
      issue: string
  pass: boolean  # true only if total_violations (critical) = 0
  summary: string
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

| Criterion | Pass Condition |
|-----------|---------------|
| All categories scanned | Categories A through F all checked |
| Zero critical violations | No CRITICAL-level slop detected |
| Contextual warnings evaluated | Every WARNING assessed for context |
| Specificity gaps identified | All vague commercial claims flagged |
| Locations precise | Each violation cites section and approximate position |
| Resolutions clear | Each violation suggests specific replacement direction |
| SIN methodology exceptions honored | "But that's not all" and "Here's the thing" allowed in SIN context |
| AI-default patterns caught | LLM-typical phrases flagged even if not in standard slop lists |

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Over-Flagging SIN Methodology
- **Symptom:** SIN template language flagged as slop (e.g., "But that's not all...")
- **Cause:** Dictionary doesn't account for methodology-approved usage
- **Prevention:** Step 3 evaluates context — SIN-approved usage in correct position = ACCEPTABLE
- **Recovery:** Downgrade to ACCEPTABLE with methodology citation

### Failure Mode 2: Missing AI-Default Patterns
- **Symptom:** Draft reads like AI-generated copy but no violations flagged
- **Cause:** Category F dictionary incomplete for current AI patterns
- **Prevention:** Category F is living — updated as new AI patterns emerge
- **Recovery:** Add newly identified patterns to dictionary, re-scan

### Failure Mode 3: False Specificity
- **Symptom:** Claims appear specific but use made-up numbers
- **Cause:** Writer added specifics to pass anti-slop but invented data
- **Prevention:** Step 4 cross-references specifics against confirmed offer data from 0.4
- **Recovery:** Replace invented specifics with confirmed data

### Failure Mode 4: Slop Hiding in Long Sentences
- **Symptom:** Generic phrases buried in otherwise good sentences
- **Cause:** Scanner checks full sentences but generic phrases are sub-sentence fragments
- **Prevention:** Step 2 scans at both sentence level AND phrase level
- **Recovery:** Re-scan with phrase-level granularity

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Clean Pass — Supplement Offer

**Scan Result:**
- Category A: 0 violations
- Category B: 0 violations — guarantee uses "Triple-Back Guarantee" branded name
- Category C: 0 violations — all CTAs have supporting reasons
- Category D: 0 violations — urgency justified by "released to hundreds of thousands today"
- Category E: 0 violations — all deliverables have full D-F-W-B-P
- Category F: 0 violations — no AI-default patterns detected
- Warnings: 1 — "But that's not all" used once in bonus transition → ACCEPTABLE (SIN methodology)
- **PASS: Zero critical violations**

### Example 2: Fail — Digital Course Offer

**Scan Result:**
- Category A: 2 violations — "incredible value" (line 34), "amazing bonuses" (line 67)
- Category B: 1 violation — "satisfaction guaranteed" without branded name (line 89)
- Category C: 1 violation — "Don't miss out!" standalone CTA without consequence (line 102)
- Category D: 1 violation — "This offer won't last!" without end date or trigger (line 108)
- Category E: 1 violation — Bonus 3 is feature-only, no benefit or proof
- Category F: 1 violation — "In today's fast-paced digital world..." opening (line 1)
- **FAIL: 7 critical violations. Remediation required.**

**Remediation Directions:**
- Replace "incredible value" with specific: "$2,347 total value"
- Replace "amazing bonuses" with: "three strategy guides valued at $597"
- Brand the guarantee: "The ROI-or-Free Guarantee"
- Add consequence to CTA: "Don't let another quarter pass without..."
- Add end date to urgency: "This founder's pricing expires Friday at midnight"
- Add Why/Benefit to Bonus 3
- Rewrite opening with niche-specific hook
