# 3.4-lead-connection-mapper.md

> **Layer:** 3 (Refinement)
> **Skill:** 10-headlines
> **Purpose:** Map how each headline unit connects to potential lead architectures
> **Inputs:** Complete headline units from 3.3
> **Outputs:** Lead connection mapping for each candidate

---

## FUNCTION

Analyze each headline unit and map how it would connect to the LEAD (the copy that immediately follows). The headline makes a promise; the lead must begin fulfilling it. This skill ensures the headline-to-lead handoff is clean and compelling.

**Key Principle:** The headline and lead are a continuous experience. If the headline opens a loop, the lead must acknowledge that loop before opening new ones. Disconnected headlines and leads lose readers.

---

## LEAD TYPE TAXONOMY

### Lead Type L1: STORY LEAD
Opens with a narrative, character, or scene.

```
BEST PAIRED WITH:
  - Story hook headlines ("They laughed when I...")
  - Discovery narratives ("A doctor was shocked...")
  - First-person headlines ("I haven't had water in 20 years")

CONNECTION METHOD:
  Headline creates "what happened?" curiosity
  Lead delivers the story that answers it
```

### Lead Type L2: QUESTION LEAD
Opens by asking the reader questions.

```
BEST PAIRED WITH:
  - Benefit headlines (lead asks if they want the benefit)
  - Problem headlines (lead asks if they have the problem)
  - Curiosity headlines (lead expands the curiosity)

CONNECTION METHOD:
  Headline makes a claim or promise
  Lead asks if reader wants that outcome
```

### Lead Type L3: REVELATION LEAD
Opens by revealing something unexpected.

```
BEST PAIRED WITH:
  - Contrarian headlines ("Why everything you know is wrong")
  - Secret headlines ("The hidden reason...")
  - Warning headlines ("What doctors don't tell you...")

CONNECTION METHOD:
  Headline teases the revelation
  Lead delivers the "aha" moment
```

### Lead Type L4: PROOF LEAD
Opens by stacking evidence and credibility.

```
BEST PAIRED WITH:
  - Bold claim headlines (need immediate proof)
  - Authority headlines (leverage the authority)
  - Specificity headlines (validate the numbers)

CONNECTION METHOD:
  Headline makes bold promise
  Lead immediately backs it up with proof
```

### Lead Type L5: IDENTIFICATION LEAD
Opens by describing the reader's situation.

```
BEST PAIRED WITH:
  - Question headlines ("Do you make these mistakes?")
  - Emotional headlines ("Had enough?")
  - Problem-focused headlines

CONNECTION METHOD:
  Headline triggers "that's me" response
  Lead deepens identification before solution
```

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Analyze Headline Promise Type

```
FOR each headline unit:

  CLASSIFY the promise/hook:
    - STORY: Implies a narrative to be told
    - CURIOSITY: Creates an information gap
    - PROOF: Makes a bold claim needing backup
    - EMOTION: Triggers a feeling needing resolution
    - IDENTITY: Calls out a specific type of person

  NOTE what the headline PROMISES to deliver
```

### Step 2: Map to Lead Types

```
FOR each headline classification:

  DETERMINE compatible lead types:

    IF promise_type = STORY:
      PRIMARY: L1 (Story Lead)
      SECONDARY: L5 (Identification Lead)

    IF promise_type = CURIOSITY:
      PRIMARY: L3 (Revelation Lead)
      SECONDARY: L1 (Story Lead)

    IF promise_type = PROOF:
      PRIMARY: L4 (Proof Lead)
      SECONDARY: L3 (Revelation Lead)

    IF promise_type = EMOTION:
      PRIMARY: L5 (Identification Lead)
      SECONDARY: L1 (Story Lead)

    IF promise_type = IDENTITY:
      PRIMARY: L5 (Identification Lead)
      SECONDARY: L2 (Question Lead)
```

### Step 3: Design Transition Moment

```
FOR each headline-lead pairing:

  DEFINE the transition:
    - What QUESTION does the headline create?
    - What is the FIRST THING the lead must do?
    - How quickly must the headline promise be addressed?

  TIMING OPTIONS:
    - IMMEDIATE: Lead directly fulfills headline promise
    - BRIDGED: Lead acknowledges headline, then bridges
    - DELAYED: Lead opens new loop, returns to headline promise

  MOST COMMON: BRIDGED (acknowledge, expand, deliver)
```

### Step 4: Identify Callback Opportunities

```
FOR each headline unit:

  FIND elements that can CALLBACK in body copy:
    - Specific phrases that can be repeated
    - Numbers that can be referenced
    - Promises that can be restated

  EXAMPLE:
    Headline: "The 17-Cent Circulation Miracle"
    Callbacks possible:
      - "17 cents" repeated in offer
      - "Miracle" referenced in testimonials
      - "Circulation" as recurring theme
```

## CONNECTION EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Story Headline → Story Lead

**HEADLINE:** "How a 69-Year-Old Doctor Has The Body Of a 30-Year-Old"

**LEAD CONNECTION:**
```
Lead Type: L1 (Story Lead)
Transition: Immediate

FIRST 50 WORDS OF LEAD:
"At 69 years old, Dr. Jeffry Life had every reason to give up on his body.
Three decades of sitting behind a desk. A metabolism that had all but
quit. Friends his age accepting that decline was inevitable..."
```

**WHY IT WORKS:** Headline shows the result; lead shows the starting point. Creates "how did he do it?" momentum.

---

### Example 2: Curiosity Headline → Revelation Lead

**HEADLINE:** "The WEIRD Reason Your Diet Keeps Failing"

**LEAD CONNECTION:**
```
Lead Type: L3 (Revelation Lead)
Transition: Bridged

FIRST 50 WORDS OF LEAD:
"You've tried everything. Keto. Paleo. Counting calories until you
wanted to scream. And nothing worked — at least not for long.
Here's why: There's an enzyme in your gut called lipoprotein lipase
that your body uses as a 'fat storage switch'..."
```

**WHY IT WORKS:** Lead validates frustration (bridge), then delivers the revelation headline promised.

---

### Example 3: Emotional Headline → Identification Lead

**HEADLINE:** "Had Enough?"

**LEAD CONNECTION:**
```
Lead Type: L5 (Identification Lead)
Transition: Immediate

FIRST 50 WORDS OF LEAD:
"If you're like me, you're sick of it. Sick of being told eggs will
kill you one week and save you the next. Sick of 'experts' who change
their minds every time a new study comes out. Sick of feeling guilty
for enjoying a damn steak..."
```

**WHY IT WORKS:** Headline triggers emotion; lead deepens identification by articulating EXACTLY what they're "enough" of.

## ANTI-PATTERNS (FORBIDDEN)

1. **Headline-Lead Disconnect**
   - Headline about weight loss, lead about energy
   - Reader thinks they clicked the wrong thing

2. **Promise Abandonment**
   - Headline: "The 3 Foods That Cause Heart Attacks"
   - Lead talks about something else for 500 words
   - Reader came for the 3 foods

3. **Loop Stacking**
   - Headline opens loop A
   - Lead opens loops B, C, D without addressing A
   - Reader forgets why they started reading

4. **Tone Whiplash**
   - Headline: Urgent, fear-based warning
   - Lead: Casual, friendly story
   - Emotional trajectory broken
