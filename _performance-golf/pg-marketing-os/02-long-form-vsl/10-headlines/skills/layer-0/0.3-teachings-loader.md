## TEACHING SOURCES

### 1. David Ogilvy — Headline Fundamentals

**Core Principles:**
- On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy
- When you have written your headline, you have spent eighty cents out of your dollar
- Headlines that promise a benefit outsell those that don't by 4:1
- The headline is the "ticket on the meat" — it must flag down the right prospects
- Never use tricky or irrelevant headlines

**Application to Generation:**
- Every headline must contain a benefit (explicit or implicit)
- Specificity increases response
- The headline is the ad for the ad
- News headlines work because they promise new information

### 2. Gary Bencivenga — Big Idea in Headlines

**Core Principles:**
- The headline is where the Big Idea first makes contact
- If you can't express your Big Idea in a headline, you don't have a Big Idea
- The best headlines combine benefit + unique mechanism in one statement
- Headlines should create a "that's interesting" response, not just attention

**Application to Generation:**
- Big Idea must be expressible in headline form
- Mechanism should be hinted (if not named) in headline
- Headline tests whether Big Idea is truly compelling

### 3. Stefan Georgi — Curiosity Gap Engineering

**Core Principles:**
- The headline creates a gap between what reader knows and needs to know
- The gap must be relevant to their desires/fears
- The gap must feel resolvable within the content
- Too much gap = confusion; too little = no engagement

**Curiosity Gap Types:**
- **Mechanism Gap:** "What IS this thing?"
- **Result Gap:** "What results did they get?"
- **Identity Gap:** "Who is this person?"
- **Enemy Gap:** "What's been holding me back?"
- **Secret Gap:** "What information am I missing?"

**Application to Generation:**
- Every headline should create at least one gap
- Gap resolution timing affects lead architecture
- Multiple gaps can compound curiosity

### 4. RSF Framework — Schema Distance

**Core Principles:**
- Headlines that violate expectations capture attention
- Too much violation creates confusion before engagement
- Optimal schema distance: 6-7 (strong but comprehensible)
- Resolution must feel achievable

**Schema Distance Scale:**
- 1-3: Conventional — sounds like everything else
- 4-5: Moderate — "That's interesting..."
- 6-7: Strong — "Wait, what? Tell me more!"
- 8-9: Significant — "That's surprising..."
- 10: Extreme — may confuse

**Application to Generation:**
- Calibrate schema distance to 6-7 range
- Identify which expectation to violate
- Ensure resolution is visible

### 5. Headline Formula Library

**Proven Formulas:**

| Formula | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| How To + Benefit | How to [achieve desired outcome] | How to Win Friends and Influence People |
| Number + Noun | [Number] [Nouns] that [Benefit] | 7 Foods That Fight Cancer |
| Question | [Question about problem/desire]? | Do You Make These Mistakes in English? |
| Better Than | [Solution] Better Than [Known Alternative] | Better Than Botox? |
| Secret Of | The Secret of [Desired Outcome] | The Secret of Making People Like You |
| Warning | Warning: [Threat to avoid] | Warning: Your Retirement Is at Risk |
| They Laughed | They Laughed When [Situation], But Then... | They Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano |
| Confessions | Confessions of a [Relatable Identity] | Confessions of an Acid Reflux Victim |
| News Hijack | [News Event] + [Pivot to Offer] | Obama's Brain Research Reveals... |
| Metaphor | [Unexpected Comparison] | A cream developed for stretch marks... |

## LOADING PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 0.3-teachings-loader:

  1. LOAD Ogilvy principles
     INDEX: by application type (benefit, specificity, news, avoid)

  2. LOAD Bencivenga principles
     INDEX: by application type (big_idea, mechanism, test)

  3. LOAD Georgi principles
     INDEX: by gap type (mechanism, result, identity, enemy, secret)

  4. LOAD RSF schema principles
     INDEX: by distance level (1-3, 4-5, 6-7, 8-9, 10)

  5. LOAD formula library
     INDEX: by formula name
     CROSS-INDEX: by niche effectiveness

  6. ASSEMBLE teachings object
  7. RETURN teachings
```
