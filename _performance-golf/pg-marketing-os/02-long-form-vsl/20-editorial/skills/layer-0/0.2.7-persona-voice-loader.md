### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses editorial_revision arena mode, load a balanced mix of teaching and selling specimens. Editorial uses specimens as REFERENCE for what good copy looks like — calibrating voice, flow, clarity, and proof standards for revision.

For editorial, load a MIX of teaching and selling specimens for comprehensive voice reference. Schwartz's process essay (`schwartz-i-write-with-my-ears.md`) is essential — it reveals how a legendary copywriter THINKS about copy quality, what he listens for, how he judges whether writing is working. This is directly applicable to editorial evaluation. Makepeace controls serve as flow and architecture reference — when evaluating whether assembled copy flows, these specimens define the standard. Halbert's Boron Letters calibrate personality and entertainment — they show where copy should feel alive versus where it feels flat. Ogilvy's ads establish the credibility and clarity standard — every sentence earns its place, every claim has specificity. Bencivenga's maxims provide the proof-first revision lens — when evaluating whether proof is woven effectively, his framework defines the standard. Clemens VSLs establish the mechanism clarity standard — when evaluating whether the mechanism explanation is accessible and compelling, these specimens are the reference. Editorial uses specimens as calibration instruments, not as generation templates.
