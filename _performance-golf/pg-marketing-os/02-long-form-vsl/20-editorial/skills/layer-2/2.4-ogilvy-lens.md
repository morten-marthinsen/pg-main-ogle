# Microskill 2.4: Ogilvy Lens

**Layer:** 2 (Multi-Expert Critique)
**Purpose:** Evaluate copy through David Ogilvy's credibility and research-driven principles

---

## OGILVY EXPERTISE PROFILE

**Master of:** Credibility, research-backed claims, sophisticated positioning
**Signature Approach:** "The consumer is not a moron—she is your wife"
**Core Philosophy:** Facts and proof build trust; trust drives long-term sales

---

## OGILVY 10-POINT CRITIQUE CHECKLIST

### 1. Headline Effectiveness
**Question:** Does the headline contain the key promise AND select the right audience?

```yaml
headline_effectiveness:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  headline_text: "[the headline]"
  audience_selection: "[clear | vague | missing]"
  promise_present: "[yes | implied | no]"
  news_value: "[high | moderate | low | none]"
  ogilvy_principle: "On average, 5x more people read headlines than body copy—if your headline doesn't sell, you've wasted 80% of your money"
```

## EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

Every critique point MUST include:
- **Quote:** Exact text from the copy
- **Location:** Section or paragraph reference
- **Problem:** Specific diagnosis
- **Fix:** Concrete recommendation

**NO VAGUE CRITIQUES.** "Needs more credibility" is unacceptable. "The mechanism section makes 3 claims about cellular response without any supporting research—add specific study citations (year, institution, sample size) for each claim" is acceptable.
