# Microskill 2.2: Halbert Lens

**Layer:** 2 (Multi-Expert Critique)
**Purpose:** Evaluate copy through Gary Halbert's entertainment and personality principles

---

## HALBERT EXPERTISE PROFILE

**Master of:** Personality, entertainment value, memorable voice
**Signature Approach:** Make them ENJOY reading—even when you're selling
**Core Philosophy:** "The best copy doesn't feel like copy"

---

## HALBERT 10-POINT CRITIQUE CHECKLIST

### 1. Voice Distinctiveness
**Question:** Does this copy have a memorable, unique voice?

```yaml
voice_distinctiveness:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  voice_description: "[How would you describe this voice?]"
  memorable_phrases:
    count: "[number of standout phrases]"
    examples: ["[phrase 1]", "[phrase 2]"]
  halbert_principle: "Your copy should sound like ONE specific person wrote it—not a committee"
```

---

### 2. Entertainment Value
**Question:** Would readers enjoy this even if they weren't buyers?

```yaml
entertainment_value:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  would_share_test: "[Would someone share this? yes | maybe | no]"
  entertainment_elements:
    - type: "[story | humor | surprise | intrigue | controversy]"
      location: "[where]"
      quote: "[example]"
  entertainment_gaps:
    - location: "[section lacking entertainment]"
      current_feel: "[dry | corporate | generic]"
  halbert_principle: "If it's not interesting to read, it won't be profitable to write"
```

---

### 3. Story Quality
**Question:** Are stories engaging, specific, and relatable?

```yaml
story_quality:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  stories_found:
    count: "[number]"
    types: "[origin | customer | discovery | failure | success]"
  story_analysis:
    - story_location: "[section]"
      engagement_level: "[gripping | decent | flat]"
      specificity: "[vivid details | some details | generic]"
      relatability: "[universal | niche | disconnected]"
  halbert_principle: "A good story makes people forget they're reading an ad"
```

---

### 4. Personality Injection
**Question:** Does the copy reveal personality, opinions, quirks?

```yaml
personality_injection:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  personality_markers:
    opinions_expressed: "[count]"
    personal_revelations: "[count]"
    humor_attempts: "[count]"
    quirks_shown: "[count]"
  examples:
    - quote: "[personality moment]"
      type: "[opinion | revelation | humor | quirk]"
  missing_personality_zones:
    - location: "[section that's too sterile]"
  halbert_principle: "Readers connect with humans, not corporations"
```

---

### 5. Curiosity Creation
**Question:** Does the copy make readers genuinely curious?

```yaml
curiosity_creation:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  curiosity_devices:
    open_loops: "[count]"
    teasers: "[count]"
    mysteries: "[count]"
    contrarian_claims: "[count]"
  effective_examples:
    - quote: "[curiosity device]"
      location: "[where]"
      why_effective: "[explanation]"
  missed_opportunities:
    - location: "[where curiosity could be added]"
      suggestion: "[what would create curiosity]"
  halbert_principle: "Curiosity killed the cat—and sold a billion dollars of products"
```

---

### 6. Readable Formatting
**Question:** Does formatting invite reading or discourage it?

```yaml
readable_formatting:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  formatting_elements:
    short_paragraphs: "[yes | mostly | no]"
    subheads_frequency: "[sufficient | sparse | none]"
    bullet_usage: "[appropriate | overused | underused]"
    white_space: "[good | moderate | dense]"
  readability_barriers:
    - location: "[where]"
      problem: "[wall of text | too dense | confusing layout]"
  halbert_principle: "Make it look easy to read, and they'll read it"
```

---

### 7. Conversational Tone
**Question:** Does it read like a letter from a friend or a corporate memo?

```yaml
conversational_tone:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  tone_markers:
    contractions_used: "[freely | some | avoided]"
    first_person: "[natural | occasional | absent]"
    second_person: "[frequent | moderate | rare]"
    rhetorical_questions: "[count]"
  corporate_speak_violations:
    - quote: "[stiff/formal language]"
      location: "[where]"
      conversational_alternative: "[how to rewrite]"
  halbert_principle: "Write like you talk—to your best friend, after a few drinks"
```

---

### 8. Surprise Elements
**Question:** Are there moments that defy reader expectations?

```yaml
surprise_elements:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  surprises_found:
    - type: "[twist | revelation | contrarian | admission | prediction]"
      location: "[where]"
      quote: "[the surprise]"
      impact: "[how it affects reading]"
  predictability_zones:
    - location: "[section that's too predictable]"
      problem: "[reader can guess what's coming]"
  halbert_principle: "Predictable copy is forgettable copy"
```

---

### 9. Reader Enjoyment
**Question:** Would reading this be a PLEASURE or a chore?

```yaml
reader_enjoyment:
  assessment: "[PLEASURE | NEUTRAL | CHORE]"
  enjoyment_factors:
    - factor: "[what makes it enjoyable]"
      evidence: "[quote or example]"
  enjoyment_killers:
    - factor: "[what makes it tedious]"
      location: "[where]"
      evidence: "[quote or example]"
  halbert_principle: "Nobody has to read your copy—make them WANT to"
```

---

### 10. Memorability
**Question:** Will readers remember this tomorrow?

```yaml
memorability:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  memorable_elements:
    phrases: ["[sticky phrase 1]", "[sticky phrase 2]"]
    stories: ["[memorable story]"]
    images: ["[vivid mental image]"]
    ideas: ["[big idea that sticks]"]
  forgettable_zones:
    - location: "[section that's generic/forgettable]"
      problem: "[why it won't stick]"
  halbert_principle: "If they can't remember it, they can't buy it later"
```

---

## EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

Every critique point MUST include:
- **Quote:** Exact text from the copy
- **Location:** Section or paragraph reference
- **Problem:** Specific diagnosis
- **Fix:** Concrete recommendation

**NO VAGUE CRITIQUES.** "Needs more personality" is unacceptable. "The mechanism section reads like a medical textbook—inject the writer's voice with a personal anecdote about discovering this, add 2 opinion statements, use contractions throughout" is acceptable.
