## Headline

"Teazles, entasis and other tremendous trifles make the Rolls-Royce a bargain at $15,655"

## Context & Background

The perfect example of how to sell luxury goods. In this ad, Ogilvy focuses almost exclusively on the amount of tedious work and expert craftsmanship that goes into creating a Rolls-Royce. One of the most effective ways to sell luxury is to describe in detail the amount of effort made to make the product (the more personal effort and attention, the higher the perceived value). Ogilvy nails this approach, getting super specific and quantifying everything (e.g. "doors are tested for 100,000 slammings").

## Key Specimen Value

- Demonstrates the "justify the price through craftsmanship details" approach
- Using obscure, fascinating words ("teazles," "entasis") to create curiosity
- Framing a $15,655 car as a "bargain" through accumulated proof of effort
- Quantification as persuasion: specific numbers > vague claims
