## Headline

"How three trunks of India Madras started Yale University"

## Context & Background

Two ads selling India Madras shirts. The one from 1961 uses a fascinating story tying India Madras fabric to Yale University's founding. The 1954 version (7 years earlier) is a more straightforward approach.

The first ad is particularly fascinating because of the story used, which ties in perfectly with the shirts they're selling. It's also a great ad to reference if you're selling something that's traditionally and authentically made. Multiple phrases and examples of copy emphasize tradition and authenticity. The originality and rarity helps drive up the perceived value of the product.

## Key Specimen Value

- Story-driven headline that creates irresistible curiosity
- Selling authenticity and tradition: "India Madras" as genuine, not mass-produced
- Historical narrative elevates perceived value of a commodity product (shirts)
- Shows evolution of same campaign over 7 years (1954 vs 1961 versions)
