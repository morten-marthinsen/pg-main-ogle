## OCR Extracted Body Copy (2026-02-15)

Ogilvy & Mather has created over $1,500,000,000 in advertising for clients in many financial fields — banking, insurance, and investment. Here are some of the things we have learned.

**1. The savings-institution problem.** How do you attract deposits? First, it is important to know that most savings come from people who already have savings accounts. The best prospects are families with relatively high incomes.

In most cases, interest rate has become a commodity. Everybody offers the same rate, so there is nothing to advertise. What you can do is what you say in advertising.

Research shows that three things motivate depositors: safety, convenience, and yield. American Express Travellers Cheques are the world's best selling travelers cheques. They are sold on safety.

**2. Positioning for financial companies.** Financial ad men are beginning to learn the lesson long known in the packaged-goods business. That before a product can be sold, it has to be positioned. It follows that positioning must be decided before any advertising is created.

The most successful financial company positioning is one that separates you from your competitors in the mind of the prospect.

**3. Build a brand of trust.** An advertisement of a financial institution should express a character that evokes trust. Advertising — and the package — should be instantly recognizable. There should be a "family resemblance" linking all your communications. You don't change the character of your advertising every year. You shouldn't even change your agency unless your advertising has not been working for two years.

There are four ways to build trust:
(a) Show the top man. Even an ordinary-looking man gets enormous authority from the aura of his office.
(b) Testimonials. From professionals, if possible, or failures. Perhaps the greatest efforts in all advertising were the Merrill Lynch ads called "What Merrill Lynch thinks." Real editorial discussion of what one major brokerage house said could be done to reverse adverse market trends. Prestigious advertising that can help build confidence in your stability.
(c) Seminar advertising. American Express has acquired extraordinary loyalty among a certain type of customer by regularly advertising specialized financial information in the business press. Key published any anyway, and offered it in their ads.
(d) Be a good citizen. Dreyfus featured a lion walking in blue. It paid dividends in goodwill and investment.

**4. Tell the success story.** When J.P. Morgan & Co. retained Ogilvy & Mather, they were looking for a way to communicate how they differ from other banks. The agency researched the story behind the bank, and found its record interesting enough to publish. The original "J.P. Morgan" ad was a black and white full page of text in the Economist.

In two years, the J.P. Morgan campaign has helped the bank gain in share-of-mind measured by recall and "favorable comments." In its first three months, awareness of the company among target audience from 4% to 11%, a remarkable achievement.

Once you have decided on your image, use it in all communications — annual reports, car cards, and on your stationery. It should permeate every aspect of your contact with the public. Citibank's "Citi Never Sleeps," Cal Investing Corp., British-American Tobacco-the best campaigns have a clear-cut identity.

**Notes on corporate advertising**

Few security analysts or sophisticated investors would take your corporate advertising literally if it were just corporate puffery. But if you are considering advertising to the financial community, consider these guidelines:

1. Be ready to make a long-term commitment. Six months won't do.

2. Define your target audience. You can't give to savings or savings per share. The performance of your stock will determine your image faster than any advertising.

3. Coordinate your advertising with the rest of your communications. Research shows that your annual report — and your advertising — and not one is understood by 70% of the people who read it.

4. If you know enough about your advertising situation to decide to advertise, be frank with them about your advertising situation or you probably know more than most of them from other observations.

**5. Appeal to self-interest.** There is no lack of advertising which is designed to appeal to the self-interest of the individual investor, the personal saver. This type of financial advertising has been subject to those who advertise whimsical and those who advertise plain-speaking.

**6. Offer a unique benefit — and advertise it.** Our advertising for the Dreyfus Fund, one of the earliest ads for an open-end investment fund, was what might be called a "pioneer ad." It was one of the first times any fund company used consumer advertising. This may seem commonplace [...]  Their advertisements never fail to get high readership.

One of our most successful campaigns for a national bank is FirstBank. The FirstBank campaign, which ran for over a year, offered a uniquely simple financial product — combination checking, or interest or savings account. The idea was so simple and so appealing to ordinary people that customers could open a new account with as little as $100 in a savings account.

In the same way, the credit card from American Express was built on the back of a "unique benefit" — a checking program — and enjoyed a healthy in price.

**7. Use testimonials.** Advertising for financial services provides one with "warm circulation" — the basis of advertising for products where there is a distinction between one product and the next.

**8. Educate the consumer effectively for small ones as well.**

**9. Find a unique symbol.** A distinctive and consistent visual symbol can be the best way of

tying a look that will catch on in your prospect's mind. We are helping the Morgan Guaranty Trust Company accumulate its new image strength of effectiveness over a period of time. In another case, the First National City Bank symbol has proved to be one of the most popular devices ever used by a financial firm.

**10. Copy testing can govern the success of your financial advertising.** Before you change an already profitable approach, before you drop or adopt, prospects will assume that your service is slipping.

Starch scores show that bank and financial advertiser offers different inclina in different positions to other advertisers. Insurance companies, rather than from one-large-size, First National City Bank ran their famous "country by country" tax advertisement.

**11. Talk your customers' language.** Part of the secret of Merrill Lynch's success lies in the way they explain what everybody else mystifies. They build confidence and destroy mystique around financial services.

There is an example of research as illustration. Bowne's chart of the growth in savings bank deposits over the past several years shows, among other things, why this market is so important.

**Seek headlines.** Most people are afraid of financial advertising — that makes it even easier for you to be noticed, because you offer something unusual. "Fidelity Bank lost $41 million to other banks this year, and we think that's great." Does a headline like that surprise you? Then it would surprise even more people in the ad. This kind of financial advertising gets more readership.

This type of advertising was originated by our advertisements on Marre Three councils was written by an ex-reporter and an ex-stockbroker, both of whom were writers in its advertising department.

Their success was noticeable. Both worked writers were unable to exchange their function in developing additional campaigns.

Witness these two advertisements of the London and Paris Direct Investment with Fidelity.

The first advertisement got 54 per cent higher readership than the second one.
