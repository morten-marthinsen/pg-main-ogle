## SPECIMEN A — Conversational Bridge + Colloquial Register (H-09, Gold)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Notice the conversational bridge line that frames evidence from the audience's perspective before the evidence lands. Email copy lives or dies on conversational register — this specimen shows what "spoken on paper" looks like.

> Because here's what was making me lose my mind:
>
> It's not like I'd cheaped out on anything.
>
> I'd hired coaches who used to work for the MOST elite academies on the planet.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** Conversational bridge lines between emotional setup and evidence. Colloquial language ("cheaped out") that sounds like a person talking, not a brand writing. Vocal stress (CAPS) on the word that carries the most weight in spoken delivery.

## SPECIMEN B — Reframe Over Repetition (H-08, Candidate)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Email subject lines and hooks need to make claims land hard in minimal space. This specimen shows how to make a statistic hit harder by REFRAMING it into relatable terms instead of just repeating it.

> He said 70% of golfers don't improve with traditional instruction.
>
> Yes, that's nearly 3 out of every 4 golfers seeing no real change to their game.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** Reframing is emphasis, not repetition. "Yes," as a conversational confirmation that invites the reader to process the claim. Translating abstract numbers into concrete, visualizable terms ("3 out of every 4").

## SPECIMEN C — Punchline Discipline (H-07, Gold — highest impact)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Email copy must be ruthlessly concise. This specimen shows the most important humanization lesson: trust the reader. If something was established earlier, reference it in a phrase — never re-teach it.

> The moment you stop thinking about it — the moment you're on the course, under pressure, playing for real — this is where your deeper, longer-term memory must be active.
>
> Encoding that requires a VERY specific process that we've now perfected in our 3-phase system.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** Two sentences replacing six paragraphs of re-teaching. Forward-pointing statement that creates curiosity without over-explaining. Trust the reader to remember what was already established.
