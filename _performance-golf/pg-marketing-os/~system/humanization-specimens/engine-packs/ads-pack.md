## SPECIMEN A — Visceral Language at Scale (H-05, Gold)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Ad copy has zero tolerance for sanitized language. Every word must earn its place. This specimen shows how verb intensity must match narrative stakes — and how a single dismissive sentence replaces multi-beat negation.

> That's when I hatched what industry insiders considered to be a "diabolical" scheme.
>
> Because I looked at the entire golf instruction landscape — an industry I've been in for years, that I built a 1,400,000-golfer community inside of... and I decided to burn almost everything to the ground.
>
> No minor cosmetic changes or so-called upgrades.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** "Burn almost everything to the ground" — visceral verbs, not corporate-safe verbs. "So-called" adds attitude in two words. Third-party validation ("industry insiders") is more credible than self-characterization. One-sentence dismissals replace multi-beat negations.

## SPECIMEN B — Emotional Climax Delivery (H-06, Gold)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Ad hooks need emotional peaks that land HARD in minimal space. This specimen shows how to treat a turning point as a CHARACTER MOMENT, not a transition.

> So I finally said: "ENOUGH!"
>
> I'm going to build the experience that should have existed all along.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** "ENOUGH!" gets its own quoted, capitalized, exclamation-marked moment. The declaration and the resolve live in separate paragraphs — the peak hangs alone before the next thought arrives. Ultra-short rhythm for maximum impact.

---

## SPECIMEN C — Reframe for Impact (H-08, Candidate)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Ad copy needs statistics that HIT, not just inform. This specimen shows how to make a number visceral by translating it into terms the audience can picture.

> He said 70% of golfers don't improve with traditional instruction.
>
> Yes, that's nearly 3 out of every 4 golfers seeing no real change to their game.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** Reframing > repeating. "3 out of every 4" is a foursome with three stuck players — the audience can SEE it. "Yes," opens with conversational confirmation. "Seeing no real change to their game" adds the emotional consequence.

## USAGE INSTRUCTIONS

These 3 specimens demonstrate the structural qualities that matter most for ad copy:

1. **Visceral register** — Verbs that match stakes, attitude in every line (Specimen A)
2. **Impact rhythm** — Ultra-short beats for emotional peaks (Specimen B)
3. **Concrete reframing** — Numbers the audience can visualize (Specimen C)

Hold these passages in active context. Let their STRUCTURE influence your output's structure. Do NOT copy their content, metaphors, or vertical-specific language.
