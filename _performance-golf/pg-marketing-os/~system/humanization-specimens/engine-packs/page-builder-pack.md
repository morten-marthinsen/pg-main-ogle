## SPECIMEN A — Escalating Clause Over Parallel Lists (H-03, Candidate)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Landing page sections (benefits, FAQs, trust elements) degrade into parallel structures fast. This specimen shows how to collapse a list into an escalating clause that builds naturally.

> Contrary to popular belief, it's not lack of talent, age or even discipline causing this problem.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** Four parallel sentences collapsed into one. "Or even discipline" creates escalation — each item builds on the last. "Contrary to popular belief" opens with a frame-break that earns attention. One sentence does the work of four.

## SPECIMEN B — Single Declarative Impact (H-02, Gold)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Landing page hero sections and CTAs need confident, singular statements — not three-beat build-ups. This specimen shows how one word with vocal stress replaces an entire tricolon.

> And the results were PERMANENT.
>
> That's right — when we checked back months later — Harry's improvement had held.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** PERMANENT — one word, capitalized, does what three fragments tried to do. "That's right" as conversational confirmation. No structural scaffolding around a word strong enough to stand alone. Proof follows immediately after the claim.

## SPECIMEN C — Visceral Action Language (H-05, Gold)

Study the STRUCTURE of this passage. Landing page copy often sounds corporate-safe where it should sound bold. This specimen shows how verb intensity must match the magnitude of the claim.

> That's when I hatched what industry insiders considered to be a "diabolical" scheme.
>
> Because I looked at the entire golf instruction landscape — an industry I've been in for years, that I built a 1,400,000-golfer community inside of... and I decided to burn almost everything to the ground.
>
> No minor cosmetic changes or so-called upgrades.

**Structural qualities to absorb:** "Burn almost everything to the ground" > "rethink the whole approach." Third-party validation ("industry insiders") over self-characterization. "So-called upgrades" — two words of attitude replacing a tricolon negation. Match verb intensity to claim magnitude.
