# Skill 0.3: Teachings Loader

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 0 (Foundation)
**Type:** Framework Ingestion
**Dependencies:** source-teachings/ folder contents
**Output:** teaching_frameworks.json

---

## Purpose

Load, parse, and structure the two primary teaching frameworks that govern offer architecture: Alex Hormozi's Grand Slam Offer framework ($100M Offers) and Todd Brown's SIN Offer Presentation methodology (E5 Accelerator). These frameworks provide the theoretical scaffolding — the "how to think about" and "how to present" offer design.

---

## The Principle

**"Frameworks without evidence produce generic offers. Evidence without frameworks produces incoherent offers. Both are required."**

This skill loads the frameworks. Skill 0.2 loads the evidence. Together they provide the complete foundation.

## Loading Logic

### Step 1: Load Hormozi Framework

```
LOAD hormozi-grand-slam-offer-framework.md

EXTRACT and STRUCTURE:

hormozi_framework:

  value_equation:
    formula: "Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)"
    levers:
      - name: "Dream Outcome"
        direction: INCREASE
        definition: "The ultimate desire or goal of the prospect"
        key_principle: "Must be specific, vivid, and emotionally resonant"

      - name: "Perceived Likelihood of Achievement"
        direction: INCREASE
        definition: "The probability the prospect believes they can achieve the outcome"
        drivers: [proof, specificity, testimonials, authority, guarantee]

      - name: "Perceived Time Delay"
        direction: DECREASE
        definition: "Time between purchase and realization of dream outcome"
        key_principle: "Shorter perceived time = higher perceived value"

      - name: "Perceived Effort & Sacrifice"
        direction: DECREASE
        definition: "The work and sacrifices required to achieve the outcome"
        hierarchy: ["Done for you", "Done with you", "Do it yourself", "Figure it out"]

  grand_slam_construction:
    steps:
      - step: 1
        name: "Identify Dream Outcome"
        instruction: "What does the prospect ACTUALLY want? Not your product — their transformation."

      - step: 2
        name: "List ALL Problems"
        instruction: "Problems BEFORE, DURING, and AFTER using your product"
        problem_categories:
          - "Problems with Dream Outcome (they don't believe it's possible)"
          - "Problems with Likelihood (they think they'll fail)"
          - "Problems with Time (they think it'll take too long)"
          - "Problems with Effort (they think it'll be too hard)"

      - step: 3
        name: "Create Solutions for Every Problem"
        instruction: "For each problem, create a solution"

      - step: 4
        name: "Design Delivery Vehicles"
        dimensions:
          - "Attention Level: 1-on-1, small group, large group"
          - "Client Effort: DIY, done-with-you, done-for-you"
          - "Delivery Method: in-person, virtual, recorded"
          - "Communication Frequency: real-time, daily, weekly"

      - step: 5
        name: "Trim & Stack"
        instruction: "Remove low-value/high-cost. Keep high-value/low-cost. Stack into compelling bundle."
        goal: "I get ALL this for ONLY that?"

  enhancement_stack:
    scarcity:
      types: ["Limited Supply of Seats/Slots", "Limited Supply of Bonuses", "Never Available Again"]
      principle: "Use honest scarcity (transparency about capacity)"

    urgency:
      types: ["Cohort-Based Rolling", "Rolling Seasonal", "Pricing/Bonus-Based", "Exploding Opportunity"]

    bonuses:
      checklist:
        - "Always offer bonuses"
        - "Name bonuses with a benefit"
        - "Explain how each bonus addresses customer issues"
        - "Provide proof of value"
        - "Use vivid imagery for potential experiences"
        - "Assign and justify a price tag"
        - "Prefer tools and checklists over extensive training"
        - "Counter specific objections"
        - "Anticipate future customer needs"
        - "Ensure bonus value exceeds core offer value"
        - "Add scarcity and urgency to bonuses"
        - "Leverage guarantees as bonuses"

    guarantees:
      types:
        - type: "Unconditional"
          description: "Full refund, no questions asked"
        - type: "Conditional"
          description: "Refund if specific conditions met (increases commitment)"
        - type: "Anti-Guarantee"
          description: "All sales final (exclusivity signal)"
        - type: "Implied"
          description: "Performance-based (revenue share, pay-on-results)"
      stacking: "Combine multiple guarantee types for maximum risk reversal"

    naming:
      formula:
        - "Magnetic Reason Why (Free / 88% Off / Exclusive)"
        - "Announce Your Avatar (Hinsdale Moms / SaaS Founders)"
        - "Give Them A Goal (Celebrity Smile / Pain Free)"
        - "Indicate Time Interval (6 Weeks / 30 Days)"
        - "Complete With Container Word (Challenge / Blueprint / System)"

  pricing_principles:
    - "Charge premium — higher price = higher perceived value, better clients, better results"
    - "Virtuous cycle: Higher price → more investment → better engagement → better results → more proof"
    - "Never compete on price. Differentiate on value."
    - "The gap between cost and price IS the value. Maximize the gap."
    - "Those who pay the most, pay the most attention."
```

### Step 2: Load Todd Brown Framework

```
LOAD todd-brown-sin-offer-presentation.md

EXTRACT and STRUCTURE:

todd_brown_framework:

  dfwbp_formula:
    name: "D-F-W-B-P"
    components:
      - D: "Deliverable — what they get"
      - F: "Feature — what it IS"
      - W: "Why — why it matters"
      - B: "Benefits — what it DOES for them"
      - P: "Proof — evidence it works"
    application: "Used for EVERY deliverable AND every bonus"

  presentation_sequence:
    chunks:
      - order: 1
        name: "Core Product Introduction"
        purpose: "Introduce product by name"
      - order: 2
        name: "Deliverable Presentations"
        purpose: "D-F-W-B-P for each deliverable"
      - order: 3
        name: "Product Summary"
        purpose: "Restate primary promise"
      - order: 4
        name: "Bonus Transition"
        purpose: "Bridge to bonus section"
      - order: 5
        name: "Bonus Presentations"
        purpose: "D-F-W-B-P for each bonus"
      - order: 6
        name: "Total Value Statement"
        purpose: "Aggregate stated value"
      - order: 7
        name: "Value Demonstration"
        purpose: "'If all it did was...' technique × 3"
      - order: 8
        name: "Price Presentation"
        purpose: "Anchor → discount → actual → terms → savings"
      - order: 9
        name: "Risk Reversal"
        purpose: "Guarantee presentation"
      - order: 10
        name: "CTA 1"
        purpose: "Direct call to action"
      - order: 11
        name: "Consequence Amplification"
        purpose: "Remind of current pain"
      - order: 12
        name: "After-State Contrast"
        purpose: "Paint the desired future (optional)"
      - order: 13
        name: "CTA 2"
        purpose: "Urgency-enhanced call to action"
      - order: 14
        name: "CTA 3"
        purpose: "Final call to action"

  value_demonstration:
    technique: "'If all it did was...'"
    structure: "At [retail value], if all [product] did was [single benefit], would it be worth it?"
    repetitions: 3
    principle: "Each individual benefit alone justifies the full price"

  price_presentation:
    sequence:
      - "Anchor at retail value"
      - "Discount reveal ('it isn't going to cost you [retail]')"
      - "Intermediate anchor (optional)"
      - "Actual price reveal"
      - "Terms presentation"
      - "Savings statement (percentage + dollar amount)"

  risk_reversal:
    key_phrase: "You don't have to say yes. Just maybe."
    structure: "Guarantee statement → explanation → promise restatement → or money back"

  three_cta_structure:
    cta_1: "Direct action + promise restatement"
    between_1_2: "Consequence amplification (current pain) + after-state contrast (optional)"
    cta_2: "Urgency-enhanced action + scarcity/deadline"
    cta_3: "Final direct action"

  key_principles:
    - "D-F-W-B-P is universal for all components"
    - "Restate promise multiple times with varied language"
    - "Value before price — always"
    - "Each benefit alone justifies the price"
    - "3 CTAs, not 1"
    - "Consequence amplification between CTAs"
    - "Current pain vs. after state creates natural urgency"
```

### Step 3: Map Framework Intersections

```
IDENTIFY where Hormozi and Todd Brown frameworks OVERLAP and COMPLEMENT:

INTERSECTION MAP:

  offer_construction:
    primary: Hormozi (what to build)
    secondary: Todd Brown (how to present what's built)

  value_equation:
    hormozi: Defines the 4 levers of perceived value
    todd_brown: "If all it did was..." demonstrates value in presentation
    intersection: Value equation drives WHAT to emphasize; presentation technique drives HOW

  bonuses:
    hormozi: 12-point checklist for designing bonuses
    todd_brown: D-F-W-B-P formula for presenting each bonus
    intersection: Design with Hormozi, present with Todd Brown

  guarantees:
    hormozi: 4 guarantee types + stacking concept
    todd_brown: "Just say maybe" presentation + guarantee explanation flow
    intersection: Choose type with Hormozi, present with Todd Brown

  price:
    hormozi: Premium pricing principles (charge more, never compete on price)
    todd_brown: Anchor → discount → actual → savings presentation sequence
    intersection: Set price with Hormozi principles, reveal with Todd Brown sequence

  scarcity_urgency:
    hormozi: Types and mechanisms (supply-based, time-based)
    todd_brown: CTA 2 urgency integration in presentation flow
    intersection: Design mechanism with Hormozi, integrate into CTA sequence per Todd Brown

COMPILE framework_intersection_map for downstream skills
```
