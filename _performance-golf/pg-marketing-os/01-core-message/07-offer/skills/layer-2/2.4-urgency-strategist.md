## Purpose

Design the urgency mechanism that creates time-based motivation to act NOW. While scarcity asks "Will there be enough?", urgency asks "When does this end?" It answers the universal buyer delay: "I'll think about it and come back later."

Urgency and scarcity are complementary but independent. Scarcity = supply limitation. Urgency = time limitation. Together they create the two forces that prevent the deadliest threat to any offer: inaction.

---

## The Principle

**"The prospect who leaves to 'think about it' almost never comes back. Urgency isn't pressure — it's the honest acknowledgment that delay has a real cost."**

---

## Urgency Types (Hormozi Framework)

```
TYPE 1: COHORT-BASED ROLLING URGENCY
  "The next group starts [date]. After that, the next one isn't until [later date]."
  Mechanism: Real enrollment periods with gaps between
  Best for: Courses with live components, coaching programs, group experiences
  Strength: Very believable — obvious why you can't start anytime

TYPE 2: ROLLING SEASONAL URGENCY
  "This offer is only available during [event/season]."
  Mechanism: Real calendar-based promotion windows
  Best for: Product launches, seasonal relevance, annual events
  Strength: Believable — markets understand promotional periods

TYPE 3: PRICING OR BONUS-BASED URGENCY
  "This price/these bonuses are only available until [date/time]."
  Mechanism: Price increases or bonuses removed after deadline
  Best for: Any offer — pricing changes are always justifiable
  Strength: Medium-high — must explain WHY price changes

TYPE 4: EXPLODING OPPORTUNITY
  "The opportunity itself is diminishing — every day you wait, [consequence]."
  Mechanism: External reality creates natural urgency
  Best for: Markets with timing sensitivity (health, investing, seasonal)
  Strength: Very high when genuine — natural urgency is most powerful
```

## Design Process

### Step 1: Identify Natural Urgency Anchors

```
EVALUATE the offer and market for genuine time pressure:

INTERNAL ANCHORS (within the offer):
  - Is there a real start date? (cohort, program, event)
  - Is the price genuinely going to increase? (launch pricing, inflation, demand)
  - Are bonuses genuinely time-limited? (seller's time, seasonal relevance)
  - Is this truly a limited-time promotion? (campaign window, partnership expiring)

EXTERNAL ANCHORS (in the prospect's world):
  - Is the problem getting worse over time?
    "Every month without [solution], [consequence] compounds"
  - Is there a life event creating a deadline?
    "Wedding season," "Tax season," "Back to school," "New Year"
  - Is the market opportunity shrinking?
    "Regulation changes in Q3," "Competition is catching up"
  - Is there a biological/health urgency?
    "Your metabolism slows 2% each year after 40"

LIST all genuine urgency anchors (internal + external)
```

### Step 2: Select Urgency Type

```
MATCH genuine anchors to urgency types:

IF real cohort/start dates exist:
  → TYPE 1 (Cohort-Based) — strongest structural urgency
  Communicate: "Next group starts [date]. After that, [wait time]."

IF promotional campaign has a real window:
  → TYPE 2 (Seasonal) — natural calendar urgency
  Communicate: "[Event/Season] special — offer ends [date]."

IF price will genuinely increase or bonuses will be removed:
  → TYPE 3 (Pricing/Bonus-Based) — actionable deadline
  Communicate: "This price is locked until [date]. After that, it goes to [$higher]."

IF the prospect's problem compounds with time:
  → TYPE 4 (Exploding Opportunity) — the strongest emotional urgency
  Communicate: "Every [time unit] you wait costs you [specific consequence]."

BEST PRACTICE: Layer multiple urgency types:
  External + Internal = Maximum urgency
  "Your [problem] gets worse every day (external), AND this offer
   closes Friday at midnight (internal)."

NOTE: Urgency COMPLEMENTS scarcity from 2.3 — they are different levers.
  Scarcity: "Limited spots" (quantity)
  Urgency: "Offer ends Friday" (time)
  Together: "Only 25 spots left AND the price goes up Friday."
```

### Step 3: Design the Deadline

```
IF urgency involves a specific deadline:

  CALIBRATE the deadline:

  TOO SHORT: "Offer ends in 2 hours"
    Risk: Prospect hasn't had time to evaluate
    Works for: Flash sales, impulse purchases only

  TOO LONG: "Offer ends in 90 days"
    Risk: No urgency at all — "I'll get to it eventually"
    Doesn't create decision pressure

  OPTIMAL RANGE by context:
    Email promotion: 3-7 days
    Sales page (cold traffic): 48-72 hours from first visit
    Evergreen funnel: 24-72 hours from opt-in (automated)
    Launch campaign: 5-7 day launch window
    Webinar follow-up: 24-48 hours post-event

  SELECT deadline with reasoning

  WHAT HAPPENS AT DEADLINE:
    Be specific about what changes:
    - Price increases by $[X]
    - Specific bonuses removed
    - Enrollment closes
    - Offer reverts to standard terms

  DO NOT:
    - Set a deadline and then extend it repeatedly (destroys credibility)
    - Set a deadline without stating consequences
    - Create fake countdown timers that reset
```

### Step 4: Design the Consequence Narrative

```
URGENCY IS INCOMPLETE WITHOUT CONSEQUENCES:

"What happens if the prospect does NOTHING?"

CONSEQUENCE TYPE 1: OFFER CONSEQUENCE
  What changes about the OFFER if they wait?
  "After Friday, the price goes to $497. You'll pay $200 more for
   the same thing."

CONSEQUENCE TYPE 2: PROBLEM CONSEQUENCE
  What happens to their PROBLEM if they wait?
  "Every week you continue [current approach], you're [specific
   negative outcome]. That's [quantified loss] you can't get back."

CONSEQUENCE TYPE 3: OPPORTUNITY CONSEQUENCE
  What do they MISS by waiting?
  "The members who started in January are already [specific achievement].
   You could be there too — but only if you start now."

WRITE consequence narrative that includes at least 2 types:
  - What happens to the offer (internal consequence)
  - What happens to them (external consequence)
```

### Step 5: Craft Urgency Communication

```
WRITE urgency messaging for multiple contexts:

URGENCY STATEMENT (for offer presentation):
  Full statement including type, deadline, and consequence
  Example: "Please understand — this is a limited-time offer. [Product Name]
           and all the bonuses at this price are only available until this
           Friday at midnight. After that, the price goes to $497 and the
           Personal Strategy Audit bonus is permanently removed. I'm not
           saying this to pressure you — I'm saying it because I don't want
           you to come back Monday and find a different offer."

URGENCY REMINDER (for follow-up):
  Shorter, more direct
  Example: "Just a reminder — the [Product Name] special closes in 48 hours.
           After Friday, the price increases by $200 and the bonus is gone."

URGENCY + SCARCITY COMBINATION (if both apply):
  Weave both signals together
  Example: "Only 12 spots remain at this price — and the offer closes Friday.
           When those spots fill OR the deadline hits (whichever comes first),
           this opportunity is gone."

EXPLODING OPPORTUNITY LANGUAGE (if Type 4):
  Frame the prospect's inaction as accumulating cost
  Example: "Your metabolism isn't waiting for you to decide. Every week
           without the Reset Protocol is another week your hormones
           work against you instead of for you."
```

### Step 6: Validate Authenticity

```
Dr. Stern SKEPTIC TEST:

  TEST 1: "Is this deadline real?"
    Would I believe this expires when they say it does?
    Or does every company say this and then extend?

  TEST 2: "Do I believe the consequences?"
    Will the price actually increase?
    Will the bonuses actually be removed?

  TEST 3: "Is this manipulative or informative?"
    Am I feeling informed about a real situation?
    Or am I feeling pressured by a manufactured emergency?

  TEST 4: "Does this align with the rest of the offer?"
    If the offer feels evergreen, does a hard deadline make sense?
    If the offer is truly limited, does the urgency match?

  RATE authenticity: 1-10
  MINIMUM: 7/10 to proceed
  IF < 7: Redesign with more genuine urgency anchors
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Urgency anchored to genuine time pressure (internal or external)
2. Deadline is specific and consequences are clear
3. Consequence narrative includes both offer and problem consequences
4. Messaging written for multiple contexts (presentation, follow-up, combined)
5. Urgency complements scarcity from 2.3 (not redundant)
6. Skeptic authenticity score ≥ 7/10
7. No fake countdown timers or manufactured deadlines
