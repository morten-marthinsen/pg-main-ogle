## Purpose

Generate compelling offer names using Hormozi's 5-Part Naming Formula. The offer name is the first thing prospects encounter and the last thing they remember. It must communicate the transformation, target the right audience, and create desire — all in a few words.

Jake Torres brings creative energy and naming instinct. The Legendary Copywriter ensures the names sell.

---

## The Principle

**"The name isn't a label. It's a micro-promise. The right name makes someone want to know more before they've read a single word of copy."**

---

## Hormozi 5-Part Naming Formula

```
PART 1: MAGNETIC REASON WHY
  What's the hook that makes them stop scrolling?
  Examples: "Free" / "88% Off" / "Exclusive" / "New" / "Secret" / "Limited"

PART 2: ANNOUNCE YOUR AVATAR
  Who is this for? (The more specific, the better)
  Examples: "Hinsdale Moms" / "SaaS Founders" / "Golfers Over 50"

PART 3: GIVE THEM A GOAL
  What outcome are they chasing?
  Examples: "Celebrity Smile" / "Pain Free" / "6-Figure Launch" / "Single-Digit Handicap"

PART 4: INDICATE TIME INTERVAL
  How fast will they get there?
  Examples: "6 Weeks" / "30 Days" / "90-Day" / "Weekend"

PART 5: COMPLETE WITH CONTAINER WORD
  What type of thing IS this?
  Examples: "Challenge" / "Blueprint" / "System" / "Protocol" / "Accelerator" /
            "Masterclass" / "Method" / "Formula" / "Playbook" / "Bootcamp"
```

**Combined Example:**
"The Exclusive [Golfers Over 50] [Single-Digit Handicap] [6-Week] [Accelerator]"

**NOTE:** Not every name uses all 5 parts. Some work with 3-4. The formula is a tool, not a prison.

## Generation Process

### Step 1: Harvest Raw Material

```
FROM core_offer_definition:
  product_name: existing name (if any)
  positioning: system/secret/method/tool/experience
  primary_avatar: who it's for
  dream_outcome: what they get
  mechanism_name: the proprietary method
  timeframe: how fast

FROM big_idea:
  big_idea_statement: overarching concept
  campaign_angle: messaging direction

FROM upstream_data:
  customer_language: how THEY describe what they want
  market_psychology: what emotionally resonates

FROM vault_offer_patterns:
  naming_patterns: how successful offers in this space are named
  power_words: frequently used high-impact words

COMPILE naming_inputs:
  avatar_descriptors: [specific ways to describe the target audience]
  goal_descriptors: [specific ways to describe the desired outcome]
  timeframe_options: [specific timeframes that are accurate]
  mechanism_terms: [words from the mechanism that could be in the name]
  emotional_hooks: [words that trigger desire or curiosity]
  container_options: [system, protocol, method, blueprint, etc.]
```

### Step 2: Generate Name Candidates

```
GENERATE minimum 10 name candidates using different approaches:

APPROACH A: Full 5-Part Formula
  Use all 5 components of Hormozi's formula
  Generate 3 candidates
  Example: "The [Magnetic Reason] [Avatar] [Goal] [Time] [Container]"

APPROACH B: Mechanism-Led Naming
  Lead with the proprietary mechanism name
  Generate 2 candidates
  Example: "The [Mechanism Name] [Container]"
  Example: "The [Mechanism Name] [Goal] [Container]"

APPROACH C: Outcome-Led Naming
  Lead with the desired outcome
  Generate 2 candidates
  Example: "[Outcome] [Container]" — "The Pain-Free Golf System"
  Example: "[Time] to [Outcome]" — "30 Days to Your Best Round"

APPROACH D: Identity-Led Naming
  Lead with who the prospect becomes
  Generate 2 candidates
  Example: "The [Identity] [Container]" — "The Confident Golfer Protocol"
  Example: "Become [Identity] in [Time]" — "Become a Scratch Player in 90 Days"

APPROACH E: Contrarian/Curiosity Naming
  Use the big idea's contrarian angle
  Generate 1-2 candidates
  Example: "The [Unexpected Claim] [Container]" — "The No-Practice Putting System"
  Example: "[Challenge Belief]" — "Why Everything You Know About Putting Is Wrong"

FOR each candidate:
  DOCUMENT which formula/approach was used
  DOCUMENT which naming inputs were combined
```

### Step 3: Evaluate Candidates

```
FOR each name candidate:

  SCORE on 6 criteria (1-10 each):

  CLARITY (1-10):
    Can someone understand what this is from the name alone?
    10 = Crystal clear what it does and who it's for
    1 = Completely opaque

  DESIRE (1-10):
    Does the name make someone WANT it?
    10 = "I need that" reaction
    1 = "So what?" reaction

  SPECIFICITY (1-10):
    Is it specific or generic?
    10 = Obviously about ONE thing for ONE audience
    1 = Could be about anything

  MEMORABILITY (1-10):
    Will someone remember the name?
    10 = Sticks in the mind, easy to repeat to a friend
    1 = Forgettable, blends with everything else

  DIFFERENTIATION (1-10):
    Does it stand out from competitors?
    10 = Nothing else sounds like this
    1 = Identical to 5 competitors

  CREDIBILITY (1-10):
    Does the name sound legitimate and trustworthy?
    10 = Sounds like a real, professional product
    1 = Sounds like a scam or hype

  COMPOSITE = average of all 6

  RANK all candidates by composite score
```

### Step 4: Test Against Market

```
FOR the top 3 candidates:

  TEST 1: Competitor Name Comparison
    FROM upstream_data → competitor_landscape
    List top 5 competitor product names
    Does our name stand out or blend in?

  TEST 2: Prospect Language Match
    Would the PROSPECT use these words?
    Or are these marketer words the prospect wouldn't say?

  TEST 3: Google/Search Test
    Would someone searching for this topic find this name?
    Is the name too obscure to discover?

  TEST 4: "Tell A Friend" Test
    "Hey, have you heard of [name]? It's a [one sentence]."
    Does it sound natural spoken aloud?
    Is it easy to spell and type?

  TEST 5: Vault Pattern Test
    FROM vault_offer_patterns → product_name_structures
    Does our name follow patterns that work in this market?
    Or does it break conventions in a confusing way?

  DOCUMENT test results for top 3
```

### Step 5: Select Primary and Alternates

```
SELECT:
  primary_name: The strongest overall candidate
  alternate_1: Second choice (different approach for A/B testing)
  alternate_2: Third choice (most different from primary for variety)

FOR the primary name:
  WRITE the full name as it would appear:
    - On the sales page header
    - In the subject line of an email
    - In an ad headline
    - Spoken aloud in a VSL

  VERIFY it works across all contexts
  IF it's awkward in any context: Consider alternate
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Minimum 10 name candidates generated across 5 approaches
2. All candidates scored on 6 criteria with composite ranking
3. Top 3 tested against market (competitor, prospect, search, spoken, vault)
4. Primary name has composite score ≥ 7.0/10
5. Primary name is differentiated from top 5 competitor names
6. Name works across all presentation formats (page, email, ad, spoken)
7. At least one alternate name available for A/B testing

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT use names that require explanation ("The X7 Protocol" — what does X7 mean?)
- Do NOT make names so long they don't fit in an email subject line
- Do NOT use insider jargon the prospect doesn't know
- Do NOT prioritize cleverness over clarity — clear beats clever every time
- Do NOT ignore how the name SOUNDS spoken aloud (VSL and word-of-mouth matter)
- Do NOT use names that sound identical to a well-known competitor product
