# Skill 3.3: Competitor Differentiation Checker

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 3 (Validation & Selection)
**Type:** Competitive Validation
**Dependencies:** core_offer_definition.json (1.6), offer_names.json (2.5), upstream_data.json (0.1)
**Output:** differentiation_scores.json
**Persona:** Dr. Alena Vasquez (Evidence Validation) + Sarah Chen (Competitive Intelligence)

---

## Purpose

Validate that the complete offer is clearly differentiated from competitor offers in the market. An offer that looks, sounds, and feels like every other offer in the space will be treated as a commodity regardless of how good its architecture is. This skill ensures the prospect can immediately answer: "Why THIS offer instead of the others?"

---

## The Principle

**"Differentiation isn't a section of the offer. It's a property of the entire offer. If you removed the brand name, could a prospect tell this apart from competitors? If not, you have a commodity."**

---

## Differentiation Process

### Step 1: Compile Competitor Offer Profiles

```
FROM upstream_data → competitor_landscape:

FOR the top 5 competitors:
  COMPILE offer profile (from research data):
    competitor_name: string
    product_name: string
    core_promise: string
    mechanism_claim: string
    price_point: string (if known)
    guarantee_type: string (if known)
    key_differentiator: string
    bonus_strategy: string (if known)
    positioning: string

IF competitor data is incomplete:
  USE available information
  FLAG gaps — differentiation check is only as good as competitive intelligence
```

### Step 2: Dimension-by-Dimension Differentiation Check

```
FOR each of 8 differentiation dimensions:

DIMENSION A: MECHANISM DIFFERENTIATION
  Our mechanism: [from mechanism_package]
  Competitor mechanisms: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Is our mechanism name, type, and explanation distinct?
  SCORING:
    10 = Completely unique mechanism with no market parallel
    7-9 = Distinct mechanism with clear differentiation from competitors
    4-6 = Somewhat different but could be confused with competitors
    1-3 = Identical or nearly identical to competitor mechanisms

DIMENSION B: PROMISE DIFFERENTIATION
  Our promise: [from core_offer_definition]
  Competitor promises: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Is our transformation claim distinct in specificity or scope?
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION C: OFFER STRUCTURE DIFFERENTIATION
  Our stack: [from value_stack]
  Competitor stacks: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Is our offer structured differently? (components, delivery, bonuses)
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION D: PRICE/VALUE DIFFERENTIATION
  Our price architecture: [from price_architecture]
  Competitor pricing: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Is our price positioned distinctly? (not just different price — different framing)
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION E: GUARANTEE DIFFERENTIATION
  Our guarantee: [from guarantee_architecture]
  Competitor guarantees: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Is our guarantee stronger, different, or more specific?
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION F: NAMING DIFFERENTIATION
  Our offer name: [from offer_names]
  Competitor product names: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Does our name stand out in a list of competitor names?
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION G: TARGETING DIFFERENTIATION
  Our avatar: [from core_offer_definition]
  Competitor targets: [from competitor profiles]

  TEST: Are we targeting a more specific or different segment?
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]

DIMENSION H: LANGUAGE DIFFERENTIATION
  Our copy style: [from value_demonstration_script]
  Competitor copy style: [from competitor ads/funnels if available]

  TEST: Does our language sound different from the market?
  SCORING: [same 1-10 scale]
```

### Step 3: The "Blind Test"

```
PERFORM the blind test:

EXERCISE:
  Remove brand names from our offer and top 3 competitor offers.
  Present just: Promise + Mechanism + Stack + Price + Guarantee

  ASK: Can a prospect tell these apart?

  IF YES:
    IDENTIFY what makes ours distinct
    DOCUMENT the differentiators

  IF NO:
    FLAG as critical differentiation failure
    IDENTIFY specifically what's identical
    RECOMMEND changes to create distinction
```

### Step 4: The "Why This One?" Test

```
SIMULATE a prospect comparing options:

SCENARIO:
  A prospect has our offer and 2 competitor offers side by side.
  They can only buy one.

  WHY would they choose ours?

  COMPLETE this sentence:
  "I chose [Our Offer] instead of [Competitor A] because ___________"
  "I chose [Our Offer] instead of [Competitor B] because ___________"

  IF the sentences are weak or rely on price:
    The offer needs stronger differentiation

  STRONG answers reference:
    - Unique mechanism ("their method addresses the real root cause")
    - Specific results ("they promise [specific] in [specific time]")
    - Better proof ("they have actual data, not just testimonials")
    - Stronger guarantee ("I literally risk nothing")
    - Better fit ("this is made specifically for people like me")
```

### Step 5: Calculate Differentiation Score

```
COMPOSITE DIFFERENTIATION:

  Average of 8 dimension scores = raw_differentiation_score

  BONUSES:
    +0.5 if blind test passes
    +0.5 if "why this one" test produces strong answers
    +0.5 if mechanism is proprietary and named

  PENALTIES:
    -1.0 if any dimension scores < 4 (commodity zone)
    -0.5 if blind test fails
    -0.5 if "why this one" relies primarily on price

  differentiation_composite = raw + bonuses - penalties

THRESHOLD: ≥ 7.0/10

IF < 7.0:
  Identify the 2-3 lowest-scoring dimensions
  Recommend specific changes to create differentiation
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. Top 5 competitors profiled with available data
2. All 8 dimensions scored with evidence
3. Blind test performed and documented
4. "Why This One?" test completed for 2 competitors
5. Composite score calculated with bonuses/penalties
6. Commodity risks identified (any dimension < 4)
7. Recommendations are actionable (not "be more different")
