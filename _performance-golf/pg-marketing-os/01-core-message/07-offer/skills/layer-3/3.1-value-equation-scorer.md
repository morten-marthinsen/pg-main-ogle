## Purpose

Score the complete offer on all 4 dimensions of the Hormozi Value Equation. This is not optimization (that was 1.3) — this is EVALUATION of the finished product. Does the fully constructed, enhanced offer maximize the numerator and minimize the denominator?

Dr. Vasquez ensures scores are evidence-based. Dr. Stern ensures scores aren't inflated by wishful thinking.

---

## The Value Equation

```
            Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
            Perceived Time Delay × Perceived Effort & Sacrifice
```

Each dimension is scored 1-10.
**Numerator dimensions:** Higher = better (INCREASE)
**Denominator dimensions:** Scored INVERSELY — 10 means the delay/effort is MINIMAL (DECREASE achieved)

---

## Scoring Process

### Dimension 1: Dream Outcome (1-10, INCREASE)

```
EVALUATE: How compelling, specific, and desirable is the dream outcome?

SCORING RUBRIC:

1-3 (WEAK):
  - Vague outcome ("feel better", "improve your life")
  - Generic language indistinguishable from competitors
  - Only functional layer addressed (no emotional, social, status)
  - No specificity (no numbers, timeframes, or vivid details)

4-6 (MODERATE):
  - Somewhat specific outcome with some measurables
  - 2-3 layers addressed but not deeply
  - Some prospect language used but mixed with marketer-speak
  - Differentiated from some competitors but not all

7-8 (STRONG):
  - Specific, measurable, vivid outcome
  - 4-5 layers addressed (functional through status)
  - Prospect language dominant
  - Clear differentiation from all major competitors
  - Movie scene moment is visualizable

9-10 (EXCEPTIONAL):
  - Cinematic specificity — prospect sees their future self
  - All 5 layers addressed with emotional depth
  - Entirely in prospect language — sounds like their inner monologue
  - Unique to this mechanism — no competitor could make this exact claim
  - Creates genuine desire (reader WANTS this)

EVIDENCE REQUIRED:
  Cite specific elements from dream_outcome.json
  Reference specific language from upstream customer_language
  Note any competitive overlap from upstream competitor data

SCORE: [1-10]
JUSTIFICATION: [specific evidence for the score]
```

### Dimension 2: Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (1-10, INCREASE)

```
EVALUATE: How much does the prospect believe they will actually achieve the outcome?

SCORING RUBRIC:

1-3 (LOW LIKELIHOOD):
  - No proof or only vague proof ("studies show")
  - No testimonials from people like the prospect
  - Mechanism isn't explained (seems like magic)
  - No guarantee or weak guarantee
  - Major objections unaddressed

4-6 (MODERATE LIKELIHOOD):
  - Some proof but not specific or not from similar prospects
  - Mechanism explained but not fully convincing
  - Guarantee present but standard
  - Most major objections addressed
  - Some credibility anchors present

7-8 (HIGH LIKELIHOOD):
  - Multiple proof types (testimonials + data + expert)
  - Specific proof from people like the prospect
  - Mechanism is credible and well-explained
  - Strong guarantee that reverses risk
  - All major objections addressed with specific responses
  - Credibility anchors are strong

9-10 (NEAR-CERTAIN):
  - Overwhelming proof from multiple independent sources
  - Proof from prospects nearly identical to the reader
  - Mechanism is undeniable (logic + evidence + demonstration)
  - Guarantee makes it risk-free to try
  - Every conceivable objection preempted
  - Authority/credibility is beyond question

EVIDENCE REQUIRED:
  Cite specific proof assets from proof_inventory
  Reference guarantee type and strength from guarantee_architecture
  Note objection coverage from problem_solution_map
  Cite mechanism credibility from mechanism_package

SCORE: [1-10]
JUSTIFICATION: [specific evidence]
```

### Dimension 3: Perceived Time Delay (1-10, INVERTED — 10 = minimal delay)

```
EVALUATE: How fast does the prospect believe they'll see results?

SCORING RUBRIC (INVERTED):

1-3 (LONG PERCEIVED DELAY):
  - No quick wins or early milestones
  - Vague timeframes ("eventually", "over time")
  - Full transformation timeline dominates with no intermediate wins
  - No milestone framework
  - Delivery isn't instant (shipped, waitlisted, scheduled)

4-6 (MODERATE PERCEIVED DELAY):
  - Some quick wins mentioned but not prominent
  - Timeframe stated but not emphasized
  - 1-2 milestones between start and final outcome
  - Digital access but not instant

7-8 (SHORT PERCEIVED DELAY):
  - Quick wins within first week prominently featured
  - Specific milestone framework (Day 1, Week 1, Week 4)
  - "First result" timeframe is short (hours or days)
  - Instant digital access
  - Time-specific language throughout

9-10 (MINIMAL PERCEIVED DELAY):
  - First result promised within 24-48 hours
  - Vivid quick wins that are genuinely achievable fast
  - Complete milestone framework from Day 1 to Final Outcome
  - Instant access + immediate first action step
  - Prospect believes "I could start seeing change TODAY"

EVIDENCE REQUIRED:
  Cite quick wins from value_equation_optimization
  Reference milestone framework
  Note delivery speed and access timing
  Cite time-specific language from scripts

SCORE: [1-10]
JUSTIFICATION: [specific evidence]
```

### Dimension 4: Perceived Effort & Sacrifice (1-10, INVERTED — 10 = minimal effort)

```
EVALUATE: How easy does the prospect believe this will be?

SCORING RUBRIC (INVERTED):

1-3 (HIGH PERCEIVED EFFORT):
  - All DIY or "figure it out" components
  - Complex multi-step processes with no simplification
  - Significant sacrifices required (time, comfort, money, habits)
  - No tools, checklists, or templates
  - Overwhelm likely (too many components with no guidance)

4-6 (MODERATE PERCEIVED EFFORT):
  - Mix of DIY and DWY components
  - Some simplification tools (checklists, templates)
  - Some sacrifices required but reframed
  - Quick-start guide prevents initial overwhelm
  - Prospect thinks "I can do this, but it'll take work"

7-8 (LOW PERCEIVED EFFORT):
  - DWY or DFY components dominate
  - Comprehensive tools/templates/calculators provided
  - Sacrifices minimized or eliminated
  - Clear "do this next" path at every stage
  - Prospect thinks "this is easier than I expected"

9-10 (MINIMAL PERCEIVED EFFORT):
  - Mostly DFY — the system does the heavy lifting
  - "Plug and play" tools that require minimal input
  - No significant sacrifices ("works with your current lifestyle")
  - Step 1 is effortless (reduces activation energy to near-zero)
  - Prospect thinks "I just need to follow the system"

EVIDENCE REQUIRED:
  Cite effort hierarchy from value_equation_optimization
  Reference tools/checklists from delivery_vehicles
  Note sacrifice reductions from effort optimization
  Cite overwhelm prevention measures

SCORE: [1-10]
JUSTIFICATION: [specific evidence]
```

### Step 5: Calculate Composite

```
COMPOSITE CALCULATION:

  Numerator = Dream Outcome × Likelihood
  Denominator = (11 - Time Delay Score) × (11 - Effort Score)

  NOTE: Time and Effort are inverted (10 = minimal),
  so to calculate the equation properly:
  11 - inverted_score = actual delay/effort burden

  Raw Value = Numerator / Denominator

  NORMALIZE to 1-10 scale:
    Composite = (Dream_Outcome + Likelihood + Time_Inverted + Effort_Inverted) / 4

  This simplified composite is used for gate thresholds.

THRESHOLDS:
  Composite ≥ 8.0 — Exceptional offer (proceed with high confidence)
  Composite 7.0-7.9 — Strong offer (proceed with confidence)
  Composite 6.0-6.9 — Acceptable offer (proceed with warnings)
  Composite < 6.0 — Weak offer (remediation required)

DIMENSION THRESHOLDS:
  No individual dimension below 6.0
  If any dimension < 6.0: Flag for remediation regardless of composite
```

## Quality Criteria

1. All 4 dimensions scored with specific evidence (not subjective impressions)
2. Each score includes justification citing specific outputs from earlier skills
3. Improvement opportunities identified for every dimension (even high-scoring ones)
4. Composite calculated correctly
5. Weakest and strongest dimensions identified
6. Scores are honest — Dr. Stern prevents inflated scoring
7. No dimension scored without referencing the rubric criteria

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT inflate scores to pass the gate — honest scoring leads to better offers
- Do NOT score based on intention ("we plan to add proof") — score based on what EXISTS now
- Do NOT skip the evidence requirement — a score without evidence is an opinion
- Do NOT use composite alone — individual dimension scores matter for targeted improvement
