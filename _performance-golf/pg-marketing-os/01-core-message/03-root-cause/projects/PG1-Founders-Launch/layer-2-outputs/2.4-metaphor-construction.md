## Metaphor Variants

### MET-1: The Doctor Who Never Ran Tests (Medical Diagnosis Metaphor)

**Metaphor:** Golf improvement has been like visiting doctors who prescribe medicine based on how you look walking in the door — without ever running blood work, imaging, or any diagnostic test. Each doctor sees something different. Each prescribes something different. None of them ran the test.

**Three-part structure:**
- **What they think:** "I need to find the right doctor (instructor/app/method)."
- **What's real:** "The problem isn't the doctor. The problem is that nobody ran the test. You wouldn't accept a medical treatment without a diagnosis. But that's exactly what the golf industry has been doing to you — prescribing treatment after treatment without ever identifying what's actually wrong."
- **Why nothing worked:** "When you skip the diagnosis, every prescription is a guess. Some guesses are educated. Some even help for a while. But without knowing THE root issue, you're treating symptoms. And symptoms keep coming back."

**Anchor phrase:** "Nobody ever ran the test."
**Metaphor strength:** 9/10 — Medical analogy is ORGANIC to golfer language (MECH-03 score 8.15, "Civilians don't own X-ray machines" — MonteScheinblum). Instantly intuitive. Creates visceral "of course" reaction.
**Soul.md compliance:** PASS — Not clinical (avoids tech-speak). Conversational, revelatory tone.
**Mechanism setup:** 10/10 — PG1's SwingScan AI IS the diagnostic test. "We finally built the test."

### MET-2: The Combination Lock (Keynote Validated)

**Metaphor:** Golf isn't like fitness where getting 70% right still produces results. Golf is a combination lock — get 4 of 5 numbers right and the lock stays shut. You don't need to try more combinations. You need to find which ONE number is wrong.

**Three-part structure:**
- **What they think:** "If I keep working on all the pieces, eventually they'll come together."
- **What's real:** "Golf is a combination lock. You could have 4 of 5 numbers right — and the lock stays shut. The problem isn't that you need ALL the numbers. It's that nobody has identified which ONE number is wrong. That's your root flaw."
- **Why nothing worked:** "Every tip, every lesson, every drill was trying to give you another number. But if you already have 4 right and you keep spinning ALL the dials, you might actually LOSE a number you already had. You needed someone to identify the ONE wrong number. Nobody did."

**Anchor phrase:** "Which one number is wrong?"
**Metaphor strength:** 8/10 — Already validated in the keynote. Golfers understand the "all or nothing" nature of golf. The "one wrong number" reframe is powerful. Slightly less organic than the medical metaphor (the keynote INTRODUCES it; golfers don't use it spontaneously).
**Soul.md compliance:** PASS — Brixton already uses this in the keynote. Conversational, builds logically.
**Mechanism setup:** 9/10 — SwingScan AI identifies "which number is wrong."

### MET-3: The X-Ray Machine (Technology Reframe)

**Metaphor:** For decades, golf instructors have been diagnosing swings with their eyes — like a doctor diagnosing a broken bone by looking at your arm from across the room. What they needed was an X-ray machine. What every golfer needed was someone to look INSIDE the swing, not just at the surface. That machine now exists.

**Three-part structure:**
- **What they think:** "A good instructor can see what's wrong with my swing."
- **What's real:** "A good instructor can see SYMPTOMS of what's wrong. But the root flaw — the one underlying movement pattern causing everything else — is invisible to the human eye at full speed. You needed an X-ray, not just eyes. Instructors were trying to diagnose with the wrong tool."
- **Why nothing worked:** "Every instructor saw your swing with their eyes. Each one caught a different symptom. None of them could see the ROOT flaw — because you can't see it without the right diagnostic tool. Now there's a system trained on 2 million swings that can."

**Anchor phrase:** "You needed an X-ray, not just eyes."
**Metaphor strength:** 8/10 — "Civilians don't own X-ray machines" is already in golfer vocabulary (MonteScheinblum). The technology reframe positions PG1 naturally without being clinical. Slightly risks sounding like AI-hype if overextended.
**Soul.md compliance:** PASS — Describes what the tech DOES (sees what eyes can't), not HOW it works (no neural networks, no algorithms).
**Mechanism setup:** 10/10 — SwingScan AI IS the X-ray machine. Direct product connection.

### MET-4: The Mechanic Who Never Runs Diagnostics

**Metaphor:** Imagine taking your car to a mechanic who pops the hood, hears the engine for 30 seconds, and says "Probably your timing belt. Let's replace it." No diagnostic computer. No error codes. Just a guess. Then when the problem persists, you go to another mechanic who says "Probably your alternator." You're not picking bad mechanics — you're going to mechanics who don't run diagnostics.

**Three-part structure:**
- **What they think:** "I've had bad luck with instructors."
- **What's real:** "Your instructors weren't bad. They just didn't have a diagnostic system. They looked at your swing, saw something, and addressed it. But without a diagnostic tool that can analyze the FULL picture — across 77+ potential flaw patterns — they were guessing. Educated guessing, but guessing."
- **Why nothing worked:** "Each mechanic replaced a different part. None ran the diagnostic to find the ACTUAL problem. That's what happened with your swing. Different instructors, different fixes, same undiagnosed root issue underneath."

**Anchor phrase:** "Nobody ran the diagnostic."
**Metaphor strength:** 7/10 — Clear and relatable (everyone understands car mechanics). Slightly less elevated than the medical metaphor — cars are objects, bodies/swings are personal. May resonate more with practical-minded golfers.
**Soul.md compliance:** PASS — Conversational, relatable.
**Mechanism setup:** 8/10 — PG1 as the diagnostic computer. Slightly less elegant than medical or X-ray framing.
