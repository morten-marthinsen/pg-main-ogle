# 3.1 Truth Validation

## Purpose
Validate that the expressed root cause is ACTUALLY TRUE—grounded in research, not fabricated for copy convenience. This is the ethical foundation of the root cause skill. False root causes lead to broken promises and failed copy.

---

## The Truth Principle

**Root cause must be TRUE, not just persuasive.**

A root cause can be brilliantly expressed but fundamentally false. Truth validation ensures we're not inventing problems to sell solutions.

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Trace Back to Research Source

Document where the root cause originated:

```
RESEARCH TRACEABILITY:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

Source Origin:
├── Deep Research Finding: "[What research revealed]"
├── Research Type: [audience_interviews | competitor_analysis | market_data | expert_sources | scientific_literature]
├── Confidence Level: [high | medium | low]
└── Direct Quote/Data: "[Actual evidence]"
```

**Red Flag**: If root cause cannot be traced to specific research, it may be fabricated.

---

### Step 2: Verify Against Audience Reality

Check if the root cause matches actual audience experience:

```
AUDIENCE REALITY CHECK:
Root Cause: "[Statement]"

1. Would audience recognize this as their experience?
   └── Evidence: "[Specific audience complaints/behaviors that confirm]"

2. Have they expressed this themselves (in their words)?
   └── Quote: "[Actual audience language]"

3. Does this explain problems they've actually articulated?
   └── Problems explained: "[List]"

4. Would this surprise them or just confirm what they know?
   └── Revelation level: [New insight | Articulated | Confirms suspicion | They already knew]
```

---

### Step 3: Check Scientific/Factual Validity

For root causes with factual claims:

```
FACTUAL VALIDATION:
Claim: "[The factual element of root cause]"

1. Is this scientifically/factually accurate?
   └── [YES—cite source | PARTIALLY—with caveat | NO—needs adjustment]

2. Is this a mainstream view or fringe theory?
   └── [Mainstream | Emerging | Controversial | Fringe]

3. Could this claim be challenged by experts?
   └── [Low risk | Medium risk | High risk]

4. Are we oversimplifying for effect?
   └── [No | Acceptable simplification | Misleading simplification]
```

**Examples**:
- "Low point shifts during swing" → Factually accurate (golf biomechanics)
- "Lectins damage gut lining" → Partially accurate (debated, needs nuance)
- "Toxins cause all disease" → Too broad, not factually defensible

---

### Step 4: Test for Manufactured Root Cause

Check if we invented a problem to fit our mechanism:

```
MANUFACTURE CHECK:
□ Did this root cause emerge FROM research (truth-driven)?
  OR
□ Did we work BACKWARD from mechanism (solution-driven)?

If solution-driven, verify:
├── Does research actually support this root cause? YES/NO
├── Would audience recognize this problem WITHOUT our framing? YES/NO
└── Are we creating a problem that only our product solves? YES/NO
```

**Warning Signs of Manufactured Root Cause**:
- Root cause perfectly matches mechanism capabilities (suspiciously convenient)
- No competitor acknowledges this root cause
- Audience doesn't recognize it without heavy persuasion
- Scientific evidence is thin or cherry-picked

---

### Step 5: Assign Truth Score

```
TRUTH SCORE CALCULATION:

Research Grounding (0-10):
├── Strong research support: 8-10
├── Moderate support with gaps: 5-7
├── Weak or anecdotal support: 2-4
└── No research support: 0-1
Score: [X]

Audience Reality Match (0-10):
├── Audience articulates this themselves: 8-10
├── Audience recognizes when stated: 5-7
├── Requires persuasion to accept: 2-4
└── Audience doesn't relate: 0-1
Score: [X]

Factual Accuracy (0-10):
├── Factually uncontroversial: 8-10
├── Generally accurate with simplification: 5-7
├── Contested/debatable claims: 2-4
└── Factually questionable: 0-1
Score: [X]

Origin Integrity (0-10):
├── Emerged from research: 8-10
├── Research-supported but mechanism-influenced: 5-7
├── Primarily mechanism-driven: 2-4
└── Manufactured: 0-1
Score: [X]

TOTAL TRUTH SCORE: [Average of 4 scores] / 10
```

---

## Quality Gates

Before proceeding:

- [ ] Total truth score is 6.0+ (minimum for ethical copy)
- [ ] No "manufactured" warning signs present
- [ ] Factual claims are defensible
- [ ] Research source is documented
- [ ] Audience would recognize the root cause

---

## Threshold Guidelines

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 8.0+ | Strong | Proceed with confidence |
| 6.0-7.9 | Acceptable | Proceed with minor adjustments |
| 4.0-5.9 | Marginal | Revisit derivation, may need softening |
| Below 4.0 | Failed | Do not proceed—root cause unreliable |

---

## Handoff

Pass Truth Validation Report to:
- **3.2 Mechanism Alignment Score** (to continue validation)
- **If failed**: Return to **Layer 1 Derivation** for revision
