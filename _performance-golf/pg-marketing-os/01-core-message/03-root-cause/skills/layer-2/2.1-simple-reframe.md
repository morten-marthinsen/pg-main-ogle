# 2.1 Simple Reframe Expression

## Purpose
Express the derived root cause as a simple, direct reframe without syndrome naming or villain creation. This expression method works best for technical niches (golf, finance, skills) where the power comes from the INSIGHT, not the naming.

---

## When to Use

**Ideal for**:
- Golf and sports instruction
- Technical skill development
- Finance and investing
- Any niche where "the truth is simple once you see it"

**Not ideal for**:
- Health (typically needs syndrome/villain framing)
- Weight loss (emotional, needs more dramatic expression)
- Supplement-based products (need physiological villain)

---

## The Simple Reframe Pattern

```
STRUCTURE:
1. Acknowledge what they THINK the problem is
2. Reveal what the problem ACTUALLY is (the reframe)
3. Show why this explains their failures

FORMAT:
"You've been told [conventional wisdom]. But here's what's actually happening:
[simple truth]. That's why [everything makes sense now]."
```

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: State the Conventional Wisdom

Take the "what they think" from derivation and express it as common knowledge:

```
CONVENTIONAL WISDOM FORMATS:
├── "Everyone says you need to [solution]"
├── "You've probably tried [approaches]"
├── "Traditional advice tells you to [method]"
├── "Most [audience] believe [belief]"
└── "You've been told [instruction]"
```

**Vault Examples**:

| Swipe | Conventional Wisdom Statement |
|-------|------------------------------|
| pg_ssts_vsl_001 | "You've probably tried to focus on hitting ball first, then turf" |
| pg_fixd_vsl_001 | "You've been told to work on your grip, stance, backswing positions, downswing mechanics" |
| pg_sf1_vsl_001 | "Every slice lesson, almost every YouTube video... all 'attempt' to do the same thing" |

---

### Step 2: The "But Here's What's Actually True" Turn

Introduce the reframe with a turn phrase:

```
TURN PHRASES:
├── "But here's what's actually happening..."
├── "The truth is..."
├── "But there's only ONE reason..."
├── "What almost no one realizes..."
├── "Here's the fundamental truth..."
└── "But the real issue is..."
```

---

### Step 3: Express the Root Cause Simply

State the derived root cause in plain language:

```
SIMPLE REFRAME FORMULA:
The [real root cause] in [plain terms].

NOT: "Metabolic Dysfunction Syndrome caused by insulin resistance cascades"
YES: "Your body is hardwired to store fat—and no diet can override that"

NOT: "Biomechanical swing arc misalignment"
YES: "The ball isn't at the bottom of your swing"
```

**Vault Examples**:

| Swipe | Simple Reframe |
|-------|---------------|
| pg_ssts_vsl_001 | "The biggest misconception in golf: the ball is NOT supposed to be at the bottom of your swing" |
| pg_fixd_vsl_001 | "There's ONE root cause: the off-track first move in the backswing—a 'forgotten fundamental'" |
| pg_sf1_vsl_001 | "The ONLY way to not slice is to square the clubface relative to the path of your swing at impact" |

---

### Step 4: Connect to Failure Pattern

Show how the simple truth explains everything:

```
CONNECTION FORMAT:
"That's why [specific failure pattern] happens."
"This is why [nothing has worked]."
"This explains why [symptom] keeps appearing."
```

**Full Pattern**:
```
"You've been [conventional wisdom approach].
But here's what's actually happening: [simple root cause].
That's why [explanation of failures]."
```

---

### Step 5: Write the Complete Simple Reframe

Combine all elements:

**SSTS Example (Vault)**:
```
"You've probably tried to focus on hitting ball first, then turf.
You've worked on swing mechanics, positions, timing.

But here's what almost NO golfer knows—the biggest misconception in golf:
The ball is NOT supposed to be at the bottom of your swing.

Your low point shifts during the swing due to body rotation and weight shift,
so you must hit the ball BEFORE reaching the low point.

That's why you keep slamming the club into ground before the ball,
thinning it, or whiffing completely—you're trying to hit the wrong point
in your swing arc."
```

## Quality Gates

Before considering expression complete:

- [ ] Root cause stated in 25 words or less
- [ ] No medical/scientific jargon (unless unavoidable)
- [ ] Audience would immediately understand
- [ ] Connects clearly to their failure pattern
- [ ] Doesn't require naming or syndrome creation

---

## Simple Reframe Patterns from Vault

**High-performing simple reframes:**

| Pattern | Example | Effect |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **The ONE thing** | "There's only ONE reason..." | Focus, simplicity |
| **The misconception** | "The biggest misconception in golf..." | Reframe without blame |
| **The forgotten** | "A forgotten fundamental..." | Discovery, revelation |
| **The real/only way** | "The ONLY way to not slice..." | Authority, finality |

---

## Anti-Patterns

**Weak Simple Reframe** (Avoid):
- "Your technique is wrong" (too vague)
- "You need to understand swing dynamics better" (doesn't reframe)
- Technical jargon that requires explanation

**Strong Simple Reframe** (Target):
- Specific and concrete
- Creates "oh, THAT'S why" moment
- Audience can repeat it to a friend
