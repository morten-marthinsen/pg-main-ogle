# Layer 1.4 — Argument Strategy Selector

## Purpose

Select the overall argument strategy and emphasis distribution based on the campaign thesis type, prospect gap landscape, Schwartz sophistication stage, and vault intelligence patterns. This determines HOW the argument is prosecuted — the prosecutorial stance, proof weighting, and tonal approach.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Assess the Four Strategy Types

Each strategy type represents a different prosecutorial stance:

**1. Prosecution Strategy**
- **Stance:** "Here is the evidence. The case is airtight."
- **Best for:** Strong proof inventory (strength ≥ 70), clear villain/enemy, mechanism with uniqueness claim
- **Schwartz alignment:** Stage 3-4 (market needs evidence, not just claims)
- **Proof weighting:** Heavy on studies, statistics, authoritative proof
- **Tone:** Authoritative, evidence-forward, methodical
- **Risk:** Can feel cold/clinical if benefits aren't dimensionalized

**2. Revelation Strategy**
- **Stance:** "Here is what you don't know. Let me show you the truth."
- **Best for:** Strong root cause reframe, discovery narrative, paradigm shift thesis
- **Schwartz alignment:** Stage 4-5 (market needs reframe, not just new claims)
- **Proof weighting:** Heavy on reason why, logic flow, demonstrations
- **Tone:** Insider knowledge, revelation, "here's what they won't tell you"
- **Risk:** Can feel conspiratorial if overplayed

**3. Education Strategy**
- **Stance:** "Let me teach you how this works. Once you understand, the choice is obvious."
- **Best for:** Dense proof inventory, step-by-step mechanism, clear results evidence
- **Schwartz alignment:** Stage 2-3 (market open to learning, not yet jaded)
- **Proof weighting:** Balanced across all proof types, especially reason why and examples
- **Tone:** Expert teacher, conversational authority, patient explanation
- **Risk:** Can be boring if claims aren't compelling or benefits aren't vivid

**4. Demonstration Strategy**
- **Stance:** "Watch this. See for yourself."
- **Best for:** Demonstration proof available, before/after evidence, tangible results
- **Schwartz alignment:** Stage 1-3 (market responds to visible proof)
- **Proof weighting:** Heavy on demonstration, before/after, strong examples
- **Tone:** Show-don't-tell, results-forward, "the proof speaks for itself"
- **Risk:** Limited if demonstration proof is weak or unavailable

### Step 2: Score Strategy Fit

For each strategy, calculate fit score:

```yaml
strategy_scoring:
  prosecution:
    proof_strength_fit: integer[1-10]  # does inventory support this stance?
    thesis_alignment: integer[1-10]  # does thesis type match?
    schwartz_alignment: integer[1-10]  # does stage match?
    gap_coverage_fit: integer[1-10]  # can gaps be addressed this way?
    weighted_average: float
  revelation:
    proof_strength_fit: integer[1-10]
    thesis_alignment: integer[1-10]
    schwartz_alignment: integer[1-10]
    gap_coverage_fit: integer[1-10]
    weighted_average: float
  education:
    proof_strength_fit: integer[1-10]
    thesis_alignment: integer[1-10]
    schwartz_alignment: integer[1-10]
    gap_coverage_fit: integer[1-10]
    weighted_average: float
  demonstration:
    proof_strength_fit: integer[1-10]
    thesis_alignment: integer[1-10]
    schwartz_alignment: integer[1-10]
    gap_coverage_fit: integer[1-10]
    weighted_average: float
```

**Weights:** proof_strength_fit (0.30), thesis_alignment (0.25), schwartz_alignment (0.25), gap_coverage_fit (0.20)

### Step 3: Determine Emphasis Distribution

Based on the selected strategy, allocate proof emphasis across chunk categories:

```yaml
emphasis_distribution:
  mechanism_proof_pct: integer  # % of argument proving mechanism works
  result_proof_pct: integer  # % proving results are real
  ease_proof_pct: integer  # % proving it's easy/accessible
  speed_proof_pct: integer  # % proving it works fast
  safety_proof_pct: integer  # % proving it's safe/reliable
  # Must sum to 100
```

**Distribution guidelines by strategy:**

| Strategy | Mechanism | Results | Ease | Speed | Safety |
|----------|-----------|---------|------|-------|--------|
| Prosecution | 25% | 30% | 10% | 15% | 20% |
| Revelation | 35% | 25% | 10% | 10% | 20% |
| Education | 30% | 20% | 20% | 15% | 15% |
| Demonstration | 20% | 35% | 15% | 20% | 10% |

These are starting points — adjust based on gap priority distribution.

### Step 4: Determine Argument Density

```yaml
argument_density:
  level: enum[dense, moderate, lean]
  rationale: string
```

- **Dense:** High proof count (>25 elements), Stage 4-5, complex mechanism, skeptical audience → use more CPB chunks, more proof per chunk
- **Moderate:** Medium proof count (15-25), Stage 2-3, moderate complexity → balanced approach
- **Lean:** Lower proof count (<15), Stage 1-2, simple mechanism, receptive audience → fewer chunks, cleaner flow

### Step 5: Cross-Reference Vault Patterns

Compare the selected strategy against vault intelligence structural patterns. Identify:
- Which elite controls used a similar strategy?
- What proof density distribution did they use?
- What pacing rhythm worked for this strategy type?
- What differentiation opportunities exist?

## Anti-Patterns

- **Strategy without evidence:** Selecting "demonstration" when no demonstration proof exists
- **Ignoring Schwartz:** Using "education" at Stage 5 when the market is saturated with educational content
- **Uniform emphasis:** Equal distribution (20/20/20/20/20) — every argument should lean into its strengths
- **Strategy mimicry:** Copying a vault pattern without differentiation
- **Overthinking:** Spending too long deliberating between strategies that score within 0.5 points — just pick one and optimize
