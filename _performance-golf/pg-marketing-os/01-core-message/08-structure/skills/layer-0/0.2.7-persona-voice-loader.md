### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `strategic` arena mode, prioritize **teaching and philosophy specimens** that show each writer's THINKING process, not just their selling voice.

For structure specifically, prioritize specimens that demonstrate how each writer **architects full campaigns and sequences persuasion arguments**. The structure skill needs to design the logical backbone of the campaign — what sections go where, how the argument builds, where proof is concentrated, and how the transition from education to selling happens. The best specimens for this skill show:

1. **Full campaign architecture** — How does the writer structure an entire persuasion sequence from headline to close?
2. **Argument building** — How does the writer sequence claims, proof, and benefits to build belief progressively?
3. **Section flow and transitions** — How does the writer move between sections without losing the reader?
4. **Content proportion planning** — How much of the copy is education vs. selling, and where does each occur?

Makepeace's controls are strongest here for complete persuasion flow — each control demonstrates full campaign architecture from headline hook through mechanism explanation to close. Bencivenga's 100-Seminar Letter shows long-form structure at its most sophisticated (46 pages of sequential argument building). Halbert's Boron Letters show sequential argument building across chapters where each letter builds on the last, demonstrating how extended persuasion sequences maintain momentum.
