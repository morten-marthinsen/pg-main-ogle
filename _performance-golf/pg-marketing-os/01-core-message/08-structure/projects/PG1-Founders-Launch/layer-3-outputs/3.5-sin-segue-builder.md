# 3.5 SIN Segue Builder — PG1 Founders Launch

## SIN Segue (Argument → Offer Transition)

The SIN segue transitions from the last education chunk (CPB-006, Personalization Proof) through the value case (CPB-005) to the offer (CPB-007). It must include mechanism callback, result callback, and offer bridge.

### SIN Segue Text

"So let me bring this all together.

You have spent years looking for the right tip, the right instructor, the right app. And your handicap has not moved. Not because you were not trying. Because nobody ever diagnosed your root swing flaw — the single movement underneath everything else.

SwingScan Diagnosis changes that. For the first time in golf, there is a way to identify YOUR specific root flaw — trained on 2M+ swings, with 96% accuracy, across 77+ documented flaw patterns. And then build a correction plan, match you with a coach, and adapt as your flaw resolves.

One root flaw. One plan. One clear path.

And for the first 1,000 golfers who want to be part of this — we built the Founders Club."

### Components

**Mechanism callback:** "SwingScan Diagnosis changes that" + "identify YOUR specific root flaw — trained on 2M+ swings, with 96% accuracy, across 77+ documented flaw patterns" — directly names the mechanism and its key proof points.

**Result callback:** "One root flaw. One plan. One clear path." — echoes the primary promise. Does not overclaim results — stays in CLARITY territory, not IMPROVEMENT territory.

**Offer bridge:** "And for the first 1,000 golfers who want to be part of this — we built the Founders Club." — transitions naturally from the mechanism/result summary to the offer presentation. "Want to be part of this" frames the purchase as membership. "First 1,000" introduces scarcity matter-of-factly.

### Emotional Arc at SIN Point

The golfer arrives at the SIN segue having built through:
1. Frustration validated (CPB-001)
2. Root cause understood (CPB-002)
3. Mechanism seen (CPB-003)
4. Evidence reviewed (CPB-004)
5. Personalization felt (CPB-006)
6. Value reframed (CPB-005)

At this point, the golfer believes:
- The current approach has failed (data proves it)
- The failure was systemic, not personal (root cause)
- A diagnostic solution now exists (SwingScan Diagnosis)
- The approach is validated by research (proof stack)
- It works for their specific swing (personalization)
- It costs less than what they have already wasted (value)

The SIN segue lands on all of this with a summary that does NOT re-argue — it reminds. Then it opens the door to the offer.
