# 1.4 Argument Strategy Selector — PG1 Founders Launch

## Strategy Evaluation

### Strategy Options

| Strategy | Description | Schwartz Fit | PG1 Fit |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Prosecution | Build airtight case with evidence chain | Stage 3-4 | STRONG — zero competitors claim diagnosis, data-rich proof |
| Revelation | Reveal hidden truth that reframes everything | Stage 4-5 | STRONG — "you've never been diagnosed" is a genuine revelation |
| Education | Teach prospect about the mechanism | Stage 2-3 | MODERATE — works for mechanism explanation but market is skeptical of education |
| Demonstration | Show mechanism working in real-world | Stage 3 | MODERATE — limited by no customer testimonials (GAP-01) |

### Selected Strategy: PROSECUTION (with revelation opening)

**Rationale:** At Late Stage 3 / Early Stage 4, the market is skeptical of new mechanism claims. A prosecution strategy builds belief through accumulated evidence — each CPB chunk adds another piece of proof until the case is overwhelming. The revelation opening ("You've never been diagnosed") creates the initial reframe, then the prosecution proves it.

This aligns with:
- Todd Brown E5: "Think like a prosecutor building an airtight case"
- Clayton Makepeace: "An argument requires premises/claims + evidence/proof = conclusion"
- Schwartz Stage 4: root cause discovery thesis leads, mechanism proof follows

### Emphasis Distribution

```yaml
emphasis_distribution:
  mechanism_proof_pct: 35
  result_proof_pct: 15
  ease_proof_pct: 15
  speed_proof_pct: 5
  safety_proof_pct: 30
```

**Rationale:**
- **Mechanism proof (35%):** Highest emphasis because the mechanism IS the differentiator. Zero competitors claim diagnosis. The mechanism proof IS the competitive advantage.
- **Safety proof (30%):** Second highest because this is a $299 commitment to a new, unproven product. 60-day guarantee, 7-day trial, lifetime pricing, financial waste comparison — all reduce perceived risk.
- **Ease proof (15%):** Film your swing with your phone → get diagnosis. Low friction entry. Matched coach handles the "what do I do next" question.
- **Result proof (15%):** Limited by MODERATE proof ceiling (62/100). No customer testimonials, no handicap data. Lead with Brixton's story and analogous proof (AI in medicine). Do NOT overclaim.
- **Speed proof (5%):** Minimal emphasis. No timeframe data exists. Do not promise speed of results.

### Argument Density: MODERATE

**Rationale:** The Founders page is a post-keynote landing page, not a cold-traffic VSL. The audience has watched the docu-series and keynote. They arrive warm. The argument needs to be complete but not dense — it recaps, proves, handles objections, and converts. It does not need to re-persuade from scratch.

### Primary Gap Sequence

```yaml
primary_gap_sequence:
  1: "GAP-001 — Why has nothing worked? (validation + frustration acknowledgment)"
  2: "GAP-002 — What is really going on? (root cause revelation)"
  3: "GAP-003 — Is there a way to identify my root flaw? (mechanism reveal)"
  4: "GAP-004/005 — Can AI do this? How is this different? (proof stack)"
  5: "GAP-007 — Will this work for MY swing? (personalization)"
  6: "GAP-006 — Is this worth $299? (value case)"
  7: "GAP-008 — How can I get started? (access/offer)"
```

**Sequence logic:** Problem validation → Root cause → Mechanism → Proof → Personalization → Value → Access. This follows the natural belief-building arc: first acknowledge where they are, then reveal why, then show the solution, then prove it works, then make it personal, then make it affordable, then make it accessible.

**NOTE:** GAP-006 (value/price) comes AFTER personalization (GAP-007) because the golfer must believe the system works for THEM before price becomes relevant. Presenting price before establishing personal relevance triggers "too expensive" before the value is felt.
