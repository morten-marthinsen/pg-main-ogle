## E5 Campaign Argument Framework (Todd Brown) — Indexed

### Core Principles
1. **Campaign argument proves campaign thesis — not the product.** The argument educates on WHY the mechanism works, building belief through evidence. It does not sell features.
2. **Think like a prosecutor building an airtight case.** Every claim has evidence. Every assertion has proof. The argument is a logical chain that leads to an inevitable conclusion.
3. **Education-based marketing = 75% of the argument.** The majority of the campaign argument is educational content demonstrating the mechanism's value through CPB chunks. Only 25% is the selling transition.
4. **CPB chunks are the fundamental building blocks.** Claim-Proof-Benefit. Each chunk makes a specific claim, proves it with evidence, and connects it to a benefit the prospect cares about.
5. **Prospect gap questions determine what chunks are needed.** Start with the prospect's current state. What must they believe to buy? The gaps between current beliefs and purchase-ready beliefs = the chunks needed.
6. **"How can I get started?" always comes last.** The access question is the final gap — it's only relevant after all other gaps are closed.
7. **Read aloud — the argument must sound conversational.** If it sounds written, it fails. The argument must flow as natural speech.

### Clayton Makepeace Logical Argument Framework — Indexed
1. Logical argument = series of claims forming the backbone of persuasion
2. An argument requires premises/claims + evidence/proof = conclusion
3. A string of claims alone is NOT an argument — reasons must follow each claim
4. People buy on emotion BUT rationalize with logic — BOTH required
5. "Reason why" is the most powerful proof method
6. Interrogate the mechanism deeply to uncover all claims and supporting reasons
7. Coherence markers connect sentences into continuation of thought

### Key Teaching Applications for PG1

**Thesis crystallization:** The thesis must connect treatment-without-diagnosis (root cause) + SwingScan Diagnosis (mechanism) + know your ONE root flaw (promise) in a single prosecutable statement.

**Gap mapping:** The prospect starts at "problem-aware / solution-aware" (they've tried lessons, apps, tips — they know solutions exist but none have worked). They must arrive at "I need a DIAGNOSIS, not more treatment" (the reframe). Gaps include: Why hasn't anything worked? What's different about this? Can AI really do this? Is this worth $299? How do I get started?

**Chunk construction:** Each CPB chunk must have a factual, defensible claim (not opinion), proof traced to the inventory, and benefits dimensionalized across functional/dimensionalized/emotional layers.

**Flow architecture:** Simple segue introduces mechanism + promise. SIN segue transitions from argument to offer. Connectors and coherence markers create continuation of thought throughout.
