# 0.2.5 Specimen Decomposer — PG1 Founders Launch

## Specimen Analysis

### Most Relevant TIER1 Specimen: Simple Strike Sequence VSL

**Structural Decomposition:**
- Opens with problem amplification (golfer frustration, nothing working)
- Root cause revelation (the real reason your swing fails)
- Mechanism introduction with branded name
- Step-by-step mechanism explanation
- Proof stack (data, authority, demonstration)
- Benefit dimensionalization (functional → emotional)
- Objection handling woven through body
- SIN segue to offer
- Value stacking with anchoring
- Guarantee + CTA

**Key Structural Elements for PG1:**
- Problem section stays in the problem — does not rush to solution
- Mechanism is introduced through WHAT IT DOES, not what it IS
- Proof is distributed across multiple chunks, not clumped
- Benefits are layered: functional first, then dimensionalized, then emotional
- The "access" question (how do I get started) is always final

### Structural Differentiation from Specimen
- SSS: behavioral mechanism (do this movement) → PG1: diagnostic mechanism (discover your flaw)
- SSS: one-size-fits-all fix → PG1: personalized diagnosis per golfer
- SSS: Stage 3 (new mechanism claim) → PG1: Stage 3-4 (mechanism must prove uniqueness)
- SSS: single instructor authority → PG1: AI + human coaching hybrid authority
- SSS: immediate result promise → PG1: clarity promise (know your flaw, have a plan)

### Patterns to Adopt
1. Problem amplification before mechanism reveal (minimum 2 chunks in problem territory)
2. Proof distributed across argument, not front-loaded or back-loaded
3. Conversational tone throughout — read-aloud test
4. Coherence markers creating continuation of thought
5. Single clear thesis proven through accumulated evidence

### Patterns to Avoid
1. Do not copy SSS's immediate-result promise framing (PG1's proof ceiling is MODERATE)
2. Do not use SSS's single-authority positioning (PG1 uses AI + coaches)
3. Do not adopt SSS's one-fix-for-everyone structure (PG1's entire argument is personalized diagnosis)
