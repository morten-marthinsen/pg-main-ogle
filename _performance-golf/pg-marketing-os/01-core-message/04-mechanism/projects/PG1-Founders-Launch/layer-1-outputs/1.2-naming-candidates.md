## Naming Candidates — 4-Pass Verbalized Sampling

### Pass 1: Medical/Diagnostic Domain (concept-down)

1. **SwingScan** — The brand name of the diagnostic AI itself. Already exists in product architecture. Clear, functional, proprietary. "Scan" implies medical imaging without being clinical. Mouth feel: clean, two syllables, strong.

2. **The Root Flaw Scan** — Descriptive. Names both the target (root flaw) and the action (scan). Embedded benefit: you get your root flaw identified. Risk: slightly long (4 words).

3. **The Diagnostic** — Stark, borrowed from medical. "I got my diagnostic." Simple, authoritative. No embellishment needed. Risk: could sound clinical (Soul.md anti-voice #3).

4. **The First Test** — Positions as the test nobody ever gave you. "The step that should have come first." Evokes medical testing without the clinical feel. Embedded benefit: implies everything else was treatment without testing.

5. **Root Flaw Diagnosis** — Direct, descriptive, names the exact output. "Your Root Flaw Diagnosis" is what you receive. Uses audience's own language ("root cause" appears 240+ times in research). Risk: "diagnosis" may sound too medical for some.

### Pass 2: Insider/Origin-Story Naming (concept-down)

6. **The Missing Step** — Origin-story name: the step golf has always skipped. Matches root cause anchor "the step that should have come first." Embedded benefit: explains why nothing else worked. Mouth feel: conversational, easy to say.

7. **The First Read** — "Nobody has ever read your swing before." A "read" is both a golf term (reading the green) and a diagnostic term (reading test results). Dual resonance. Mouth feel: clean, two words.

8. **The One Thing** — Radical simplification. "We find the ONE thing." Matches the anti-overload whitespace (WZ-02). Mouth feel: conversational, quotable. Risk: generic without context (Gary Keller book association).

9. **Step Zero** — The step that should come before everything else. "You skipped Step Zero your whole life." Positions the diagnosis as foundational — before lesson one, before tip one. Mouth feel: strong, technical-feeling without being technical.

10. **The Swing Verdict** — "Verdict" is final, authoritative, decisive. "Your Swing Verdict is in." Medical-judicial crossover — a verdict based on evidence. Mouth feel: strong, memorable.

### Pass 3: Experiential/Metaphor Naming (concept-down)

11. **The X-Ray** — Borrowed directly from audience language ("Civilians don't own X-ray machines" — MonteScheinblum, GolfWRX). An X-ray shows what's invisible to the naked eye. Simple, vivid, one image. "PG1 gives your swing an X-ray." Risk: overused in some marketing contexts.

12. **The Root Read** — Combines "root flaw" + "read." A "root read" sounds like a proprietary assessment. Embedded benefit: reading the root, not the symptoms. Mouth feel: strong alliteration, two syllables each.

13. **The Swing Blueprint** — "Blueprint" implies the structural plan underneath. "Your swing has a blueprint — and one line is wrong." Visual, architectural. Risk: "blueprint" is moderately common in marketing.

14. **The Flaw Chain** — Visualizes the cascade: one root flaw → chain of compensations. "We map your Flaw Chain back to the root." Concept D's explanatory power embedded in the name. Mouth feel: strong, visual.

15. **Pulse Diagnosis** — Ties to the product name (PG1 Pulse) and the medical metaphor. "Your Pulse Diagnosis" — feels like the output of the system. Mouth feel: proprietary, clean. Risk: "Pulse" is already the app name, so this may conflate product and mechanism.

### Pass 4: E5 Strategies + Pattern-Breaking (concept-down)

16. **The Domino Flaw** — "One domino falls, and everything else goes with it." The root flaw is the first domino. Find it, stop it, and the cascade stops. Simple image, physical metaphor. Mouth feel: memorable, visual. Risk: "domino effect" is a known phrase, which helps recognition but limits novelty.

17. **The Swing Diagnosis** — Plain, authoritative, exactly what it is. "Get your Swing Diagnosis." No cleverness, no metaphor — just the truth. In a market full of cleverness, plain truth is surprising. Mouth feel: professional, trustworthy.

18. **Root Lock** — From the combination lock analogy in the keynote: you've had 4 of 5 numbers right, but the lock stayed locked. The "Root Lock" is the one number you've been missing. Finding it "unlocks" everything. Mouth feel: strong, short, proprietary.

19. **The Swing Audit** — "Audit" implies thorough, independent, objective review. Not a lesson, not a tip — an audit. Professional, serious. "We audit your swing for the root flaw." Mouth feel: clean, authoritative. Risk: "audit" has negative associations (tax audit).

20. **SwingScan Diagnosis** — Full proprietary name: the AI tool (SwingScan) performing the action (Diagnosis). "Your SwingScan Diagnosis revealed..." Mouth feel: proprietary, branded, complete.

### Quote-First Names (derived from audience vocabulary)

21. **"The Root Cause"** — Golfers use this exact phrase 240+ times organically. "PG1 finds The Root Cause." Zero competitors own this phrase in golf. It IS the audience's language. Risk: generic outside of golf context.

22. **"What's Actually Wrong"** — From golfer language: "What is actually causing the inconsistent ball striking? What's the root cause?" (GGTTH). "PG1 tells you What's Actually Wrong." Conversational, real. Risk: negative framing may conflict with hope-forward Soul.md.

23. **"The Real Problem"** — From golfer frustration language: "nobody identified the real problem." "PG1 finds The Real Problem." Direct, conversational. Same risk as above.

24. **"The One Flaw"** — From root cause copy blocks: "one flaw, a dozen compensations." "PG1 identifies The One Flaw." Simple, specific, embedded benefit. Mouth feel: clean, quotable.

25. **"Your Root Flaw"** — The exact language from the brief, keynote, and root cause package. "PG1 identifies Your Root Flaw." Already embedded in the entire campaign architecture. Not creative — but precisely audience-aligned.
