# Mechanism Naming Arena — PG1 Founders Launch (Whole System)

**Date:** 2026-03-17
**Purpose:** Name the PG1 mechanism — the WHOLE SYSTEM, not just SwingScan diagnosis

**Status:** COMPLETE — 3 rounds, final ranking produced

---

## Context

The previous naming arena (arena-layer-2.5.md) named the diagnostic feature: **SwingScan Diagnosis**. That name is locked.

This arena names the **entire PG1 mechanism** — the ongoing system that:
1. Discovers everything about the golfer (root flaw, time, body, handicap, goals)
2. Diagnoses the root swing flaw
3. Gives the ONE thing to do right now (the "smart step")
4. Follows up with a matched coach
5. Adapts as the flaw resolves, moves to the next area
6. Loops: discover > diagnose > recommend > coach > adapt > repeat

The mechanism name must describe this systematic loop — not just diagnosis, not just coaching, not just AI. The whole thing working together.

**Product context:**
- "PG1 Pulse" is the in-app feature that keeps a pulse on your game
- Pulse has full vision: root flaw, time available, body type, handicap
- Root swing flaw varies by area (driver vs. irons vs. short game) — ongoing, not one-time
- The binary argument: nothing works without fixing the root swing flaw / everything works once you do

**Rejected candidates (will not re-propose):**
- SwingScan Diagnosis (over-indexes on diagnosis — that's the feature name, not the system name)
- PG1 Root System
- PG1 Core Loop
- PG1 Precision System
- PG1 Pulse System

## ROUND 2: Critique, Revise, Add Wild Cards

### Hard Critique of Top 4

#### PG1 Signal System — What's weak?

**The problem:** "Signal" is abstract. When a golfer tells his buddy what he's using, "signal system" requires explanation. "What do you mean, signal?" It doesn't immediately evoke coaching, golf, or improvement. It's a metaphor that works in copy but may not stick in conversation. Also — "Signal" is already the name of a major messaging app. That's a brand association that could cause confusion or dilute ownability.

**The fix:** The signal/noise framework is strong for COPY, but the mechanism name itself needs to be more immediately graspable. Consider whether "signal" works better as supporting language than as the name itself.

#### PG1 Smart System — What's weak?

**The problem:** "Smart" is everywhere. Smart TV, smart home, smartphone. It's become so common that it's lost some of its premium weight. The word does a lot of heavy lifting (implies intelligence, diagnosis, adaptiveness) but precisely because it's so versatile, it's not distinctive. A golf competitor could launch "Smart Golf" tomorrow and the differentiation erodes. Also: "system" was flagged on the anti-slop ban list from the naming guidelines. The human overrides this — and has good reasons — but the word itself is generic.

**The fix:** The conversational ease is the real strength here. "My Smart System" rolls off the tongue. The weakness isn't fatal — it's about distinctiveness. If the supporting language (smart step, smart path, smart coaching) builds a vocabulary ecosystem around "smart," the genericness of the word becomes a feature (easy to understand) rather than a bug.

#### PG1 Game Intelligence — What's weak?

**The problem:** It's stiff in conversation. Golfers don't say "my game intelligence." The word "intelligence" is two syllables too long for casual use. There's no natural shorthand — you can't shorten "Game Intelligence" without losing meaning. "GI" doesn't work. And "intelligence" has military/corporate associations that may feel cold to a golfer audience looking for warmth and hope.

**The fix:** Need to find a conversational shorthand. "My GI" doesn't work. "My Intel" is slightly better but borrows from a chip manufacturer. The premium feel is the strength — but it has to work when a 55-year-old golfer tells his foursome about it.

#### PG1 Clarity Engine — What's weak?

**The problem:** "Engine" is robotic. Engines are mechanical. PG1 is supposed to feel like an insider coaching system, not a machine. "My Clarity Engine" sounds like something a Silicon Valley product manager would name. It's the anti-voice pattern #2 (generic app marketing) and #3 (clinical/corporate tech-speak) lurking just beneath the surface. Also: "Clarity" is used by a major supplement brand (Clarity by Neuriva, etc.) — crowded word.

**The fix:** The concept of clarity is strong (the opposite of the symptom spiral is clarity). But "engine" as the second word needs to go.

The Symptom Spiral is golfers working on the wrong thing, at the wrong time, in the wrong order. "Smart" is the antidote. You don't have to say "you've been dumb" — "smart" implies it. The binary is embedded in the word itself.

No other word captures the full loop. "Coach" is too narrow. "Path" is too linear. "Engine" is too mechanical. "System" is what it is.
