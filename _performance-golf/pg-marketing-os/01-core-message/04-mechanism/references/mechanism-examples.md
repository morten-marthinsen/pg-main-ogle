## THE SCHLITZ STORY (Full Reference)

**The Setup:**
In the early 1900s, Schlitz was struggling to differentiate in a crowded beer market. They hired legendary copywriter Claude Hopkins.

**The Discovery:**
Hopkins toured the Schlitz brewery and was fascinated by their purity process:
- Water came from artesian wells 4,000 feet deep
- Bottles were sterilized with live steam
- Air in brewing rooms was filtered
- Each bottle was cleaned four times
- Beer was aged for months in glass-lined tanks

**The Insight:**
Hopkins asked Schlitz executives why they didn't advertise this. They said: "Every brewery does this."

Hopkins's response: "But no one has TOLD the story."

**The Result:**
Schlitz ran campaigns showcasing their purity process. Sales exploded. They went from 5th place to tied for 1st.

**The Lesson:**
When competitors tried to respond with "We do that too," they looked like copycats. Schlitz owned the mechanism because they CLAIMED IT FIRST.

**Takeaway:** The unspoken mechanism belongs to whoever claims it first. Once claimed, competitors cannot use it without appearing derivative.

## MECHANISM EVOLUTION EXAMPLES

### Example: P90X Journey

**Before mechanism:** "Extreme home workout program that gets results"
(Generic - any competitor could say this)

**After mechanism:** "The only home workout built on Muscle Confusion—the principle that keeps your body from adapting so you keep burning fat and building muscle"
(Proprietary - competitors cannot claim without copying)

**Result:** $400M+ in sales, cultural phenomenon

### Example: Ask Method Journey

**Before mechanism:** "Survey your audience to understand them better"
(Generic advice anyone could give)

**After mechanism:** "The Ask Method—a systematic approach to uncovering your prospect's dominant buying motivation through strategic micro-commitments"
(Proprietary framework)

**Result:** Best-selling book, multi-million dollar business

## MECHANISM TESTING FRAMEWORK

When you have a mechanism candidate, test against these proven mechanisms:

### The "Better Than Schlitz" Test
Could this mechanism be as ownable as Schlitz's purity process?
- Is it first-to-claim in your space?
- Would competitors look like copycats if they tried to use it?

### The "Muscle Confusion" Test
Is the name as memorable and visual as Muscle Confusion?
- Does it create an instant mental image?
- Could someone remember it after hearing it once?

### The "Ask Method" Test
Is it as simple and proprietary as Ask Method?
- Two words that own a methodology?
- Impossible to explain without using the name?

## ANTI-PATTERNS: WHAT DOESN'T WORK

| Bad Mechanism | Why It Fails | Better Alternative |
|---------------|--------------|-------------------|
| "Our proprietary system" | Generic, no image | Name the system specifically |
| "Advanced technology" | Vague, meaningless | Name what the technology does |
| "Revolutionary method" | Claim without substance | Show WHY it's revolutionary |
| "Cutting-edge formula" | Overused, skepticism trigger | Name the specific innovation |
| "Our unique approach" | Every company says this | Define what's actually unique |
| "Secret ingredient" | Sounds unbelievable | Name the ingredient, explain why it works |
