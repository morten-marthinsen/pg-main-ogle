### Selection Priority for This Skill

Since this skill uses `strategic` arena mode, prioritize **teaching and philosophy specimens** that show each writer's THINKING process, not just their selling voice.

For mechanism specifically, prioritize specimens that demonstrate how each writer **explains and names mechanisms**. The mechanism skill needs to develop the HOW behind the solution — making complex science feel simple, creating memorable names, and building scientific credibility without losing the audience. The best specimens for this skill show:

1. **Mechanism explanation and naming** — How does the writer introduce and name a proprietary mechanism?
2. **Scientific credibility building** — How does the writer make science feel authoritative without being boring?
3. **Simplification techniques** — How does the writer make complex processes graspable for a general audience?
4. **Binary reframes** — How does the writer reduce complex mechanisms to simple on/off, works/doesn't-work frames?

Clemens' VSLs are the strongest specimens here — his Gundry scripts demonstrate mechanism explanation and naming at the highest level (binary reframes, 12-year-old language, dramatic mechanism reveals). Schwartz's ads show sophisticated mechanism framing where scientific concepts are made accessible through layered analogies. Bencivenga's Persuasion Equation framework from Maxim 12 structures how mechanism proof paths connect to the broader persuasion architecture.
