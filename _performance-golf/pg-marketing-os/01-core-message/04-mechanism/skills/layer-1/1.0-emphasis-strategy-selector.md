# Skill 1.0: Emphasis Strategy Selector

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Mechanism Ideation — runs FIRST)
**Type:** Strategic Decision
**Dependencies:** upstream_context.json, vault_patterns.json, teachings_framework.json
**Output:** emphasis_strategy.json

---

## Purpose

Determine where the mechanism's persuasion bandwidth should be concentrated. Every mechanism has three components — Root Cause, Science/Conceptual, Delivery/Tangible — but the prospect's psychological bandwidth is limited. Going deep on all three overwhelms. This skill decides which element gets 50%+ of the copy's mechanism energy, and which get supporting roles.

This decision shapes EVERYTHING downstream: what the explanation-architect emphasizes, what scorecard dimensions get priority, how the naming lands, and what proof gets marshaled.

---

## The 3-Part Mechanism Framework

```
FROM SOURCE TEACHINGS (Steal Our Winners):

Every mechanism consists of three elements:

1. ROOT CAUSE ELEMENT — "The Real Problem"
   What's actually causing the prospect's pain.
   The reframe. The blame shift. The "nobody told you this" reveal.

2. SCIENCE/CONCEPTUAL ELEMENT — "How the Solution Works"
   The logical or scientific explanation of WHY this mechanism produces results.
   The "here's what happens inside your body/system/business" walkthrough.

3. DELIVERY/TANGIBLE ELEMENT — "The Action That Delivers the Benefit"
   The concrete, demonstrable thing the product DOES differently.
   The tangible innovation. The "this is what makes it work in the real world."

KEY INSIGHT: "The prospect only has so much psychological bandwidth for your
mechanism — and if you go deep into all three areas, it can be too much."

THEREFORE: One element gets the lion's share. The other two support.
```

## Schwartz Stage → Emphasis Decision

```
THE CORE MAPPING:

STAGE 3 (Solution Aware):
  → SCIENCE/CONCEPTUAL emphasis (50-60% of mechanism bandwidth)
  Rationale: Prospect knows they want a solution but hasn't committed
  to any particular approach. They need to understand WHY your mechanism
  works differently at the conceptual level. The "how it works" explanation
  is what creates the "aha, THIS is the one" moment.
  Root Cause: Brief setup (10-20%) — they already know the problem
  Delivery: Supporting (20-30%) — mentioned but not the star

STAGE 4 (Product Aware):
  → DELIVERY/TANGIBLE emphasis (50-60% of mechanism bandwidth)
  Rationale: Prospect knows solutions exist, may have tried several.
  They're evaluating YOUR specific product against others. What they
  need is the tangible, demonstrable difference — what your product
  DOES that others don't. The innovation. The "show me" proof.
  Root Cause: Brief (10-20%) — they've heard the problem before
  Science: Supporting (20-30%) — credibility backdrop, not the star

STAGE 5 (Most Aware):
  → ROOT CAUSE emphasis (50-60% of mechanism bandwidth)
  Rationale: Prospect has tried everything. They're skeptical.
  What they need is the REFRAME — "here's why nothing worked before,
  and here's what's ACTUALLY causing your problem." The revelation
  that makes them say "THAT'S why I've been failing." Only THEN
  does the mechanism land as the solution to the REAL problem.
  Science: Supporting (20-30%) — credibility for the reframe
  Delivery: Brief (10-20%) — mentioned, not dwelt upon

STAGE 2 (Problem Aware):
  → BALANCED with Root Cause lead (35-40% RC, 30% Science, 30% Delivery)
  Rationale: Prospect knows the problem but hasn't searched for solutions.
  Need to name/validate the problem (root cause) while introducing
  the mechanism concept as the answer. More balanced because they
  have bandwidth — they haven't been saturated yet.
```

## Selection Logic

### Step 1: Determine Schwartz Stage

```
FROM upstream_context.audience_sophistication:

VALIDATE stage assignment:
  Stage 2 indicators:
    - Prospect knows the problem but hasn't searched for solutions
    - No competitor exposure, fresh market
    - "I know I have this problem but don't know what to do"

  Stage 3 indicators:
    - Prospect is actively looking for solutions
    - Has researched but not committed
    - "I'm comparing options, show me something new"

  Stage 4 indicators:
    - Prospect has seen similar products
    - May have tried competitors, comparing features
    - "Why should I choose THIS over what I already know?"

  Stage 5 indicators:
    - Prospect has tried multiple solutions
    - Skeptical, jaded, "nothing works for me"
    - "I've heard it all — prove this is different"

IF stage is ambiguous:
  LEAN toward the HIGHER stage (more sophisticated)
  Rationale: Overestimating sophistication produces more substantive copy.
  Underestimating produces copy they dismiss as too basic.
```

### Step 2: Apply Stage → Emphasis Mapping

```
APPLY the core mapping from the framework above.

THEN CHECK for overrides:

OVERRIDE CONDITIONS:

1. Exceptionally strong root cause reframe available:
   IF root_cause_strength > 0.8 AND reframe is novel:
   → Increase root_cause_pct by 10-15%, even at Stage 3-4
   → The reframe IS the competitive advantage

2. Breakthrough product innovation:
   IF product_innovation is genuinely novel (not just incremental):
   → Increase delivery_pct by 10-15%, even at Stage 5
   → The tangible difference IS the story

3. Heavy proof available:
   IF proof_availability includes strong clinical/scientific data:
   → Increase science_pct by 10-15%
   → Let the evidence carry the argument

4. Saturated niche with jaded audience:
   IF competitive_landscape shows heavy mechanism fatigue:
   → Increase root_cause_pct regardless of stage
   → They've heard every science/delivery pitch, need fresh reframe

APPLY maximum 1-2 overrides. Don't let overrides flatten to balanced.
```

### Step 3: Calculate Bandwidth Allocation

```
START with stage-default percentages
APPLY any override adjustments
VERIFY sum = 100%
VERIFY primary emphasis element ≥ 45%
VERIFY no element < 10% (even light mentions need minimum substance)
```

### Step 4: Generate Dimensionality Budget

```
FROM primary_emphasis → dimension priority map (see above)

SELECT 4-5 primary dimensions
ASSIGN remaining 7-8 as supporting (threshold = ≥ 6)

GENERATE budget_rationale explaining:
  - Why these dimensions matter most for this emphasis
  - What "optimize hard" means (target 8-10 vs. ≥ 6 threshold)
  - How this prevents flat, generic mechanism development
```

### Step 5: Generate Downstream Instructions

```
FOR each downstream Layer 1 skill, produce a concise instruction:

for_type_selector:
  "Given [emphasis] emphasis, bias toward mechanism types that excel at
   [primary element]. Types with strong [X] characteristics preferred."

for_naming:
  "Name should evoke [primary element]. If ROOT_CAUSE emphasis, name
   implies the problem/reframe. If SCIENCE emphasis, name implies the
   process. If DELIVERY emphasis, name implies the tangible action."

for_explanation:
  "Explanation architect should allocate [X]% of Level 3 walkthrough
   to [primary element]. This element gets the most depth, specificity,
   and sensory language."

for_proof_mapper:
  "Proof mapping should prioritize evidence that supports [primary
   element]. [Type of proof] matters most here."
```

## Vault Pattern Integration

```
FROM vault_patterns.emphasis_calibrations:

CROSS-REFERENCE:
  - What emphasis patterns score highest in this niche?
  - Do vault exemplars confirm or contradict the stage mapping?
  - Are there niche-specific overrides the vault reveals?
    (e.g., golf mechanisms may favor delivery emphasis regardless of stage)

USE vault data to VALIDATE the selected emphasis, not to override
the teaching framework. If vault contradicts framework, note the
discrepancy but follow the framework — vault data may reflect
market norms rather than optimal strategy.
```

## Market Skepticism Assessment (v1.1 Addition)

```
CRITICAL: Assess market skepticism BEFORE generating downstream instructions.
High skepticism markets require different naming and explanation strategies.

SKEPTICISM ASSESSMENT:

  skepticism_level: enum[high, medium, low]

  INDICATORS OF HIGH SKEPTICISM:
    - Market has history of pseudoscience products (EMF, crystals, energy healing)
    - Category has FTC warnings or regulatory scrutiny
    - Mechanism physics cannot be easily explained to laypeople
    - Audience has been burned by similar products ("nothing works")
    - Competitors make unverifiable claims
    - Medical establishment dismisses the category

  skepticism_type: enum[scientific_validity, price, category, brand, none]
    - scientific_validity: "Does this even work? Prove it with real science."
    - price: "Is this worth what they're charging?"
    - category: "This whole category is snake oil."
    - brand: "I don't trust THIS company."

NAMING MODIFIER OUTPUT:

  IF skepticism_level = high AND skepticism_type = scientific_validity:

    naming_strategy_modifier:
      priority: "scientific_credibility"
      instruction: >
        Mechanism name MUST sound like existing biology or established
        science. Avoid benefit-embedded names that sound like marketing
        ("The 700% Response"). Avoid emotional/mysterious names that
        sound invented ("Stress Switch-Off"). Prefer clinical terminology,
        biological processes, physiological mechanisms.

      weight_adjustments:
        - Pass 1 (Scientific/Precise): +0.3 boost to composite scores
        - Pass 2 (Visceral/Emotional): -0.2 penalty to composite scores
        - Pass 3 (Mysterious/Intriguing): -0.3 penalty to composite scores
        - Pass 4 (E5 Strategy): Favor TRANSSUBSTANTIATED with clinical elevation

      example_good: "Cellular Resilience Switch", "Autonomic Regulation Response"
      example_bad: "The 700% Response", "Stress Switch-Off", "EMF Freedom Method"

  IF skepticism_level = medium:
    naming_strategy_modifier:
      priority: "balanced_credibility"
      instruction: "Moderate weight on scientific terminology. Some flexibility."

  IF skepticism_level = low:
    naming_strategy_modifier:
      priority: "standard"
      instruction: "Follow standard composite scoring. No credibility penalty."

ADD TO OUTPUT SCHEMA:
  market_skepticism:
    level: enum[high, medium, low]
    type: enum[scientific_validity, price, category, brand, none]
    indicators: [string]
    naming_strategy_modifier: object
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Schwartz stage assigned with specific rationale (not just "Stage 4")
2. Primary emphasis matches stage mapping (or override is explicitly justified)
3. Bandwidth percentages sum to 100%, primary ≥ 45%, none < 10%
4. Dimensionality budget selects exactly 4-5 primary dimensions
5. Downstream instructions are specific enough to constrain (not generic advice)
6. At most 2 overrides applied, each with clear justification
7. Vault pattern cross-reference completed
