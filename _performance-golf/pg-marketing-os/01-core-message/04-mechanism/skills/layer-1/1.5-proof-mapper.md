# Skill 1.5: Proof Mapper

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Mechanism Ideation)
**Type:** Evidence Analysis
**Dependencies:** explanation_structures.json, upstream_context.json, vault_patterns.json
**Output:** proof_maps.json

---

## Purpose

Map available proof elements to each mechanism candidate's claims. Every mechanism makes implicit claims — this skill identifies those claims and matches them with the strongest available evidence. A mechanism without a proof map is just a story.

---

## Proof Hierarchy

```
PROOF STRENGTH (strongest → weakest):

1. PEER-REVIEWED STUDIES — Published research directly supporting the mechanism
2. INSTITUTIONAL DATA — Government, university, or hospital data
3. EXPERT ENDORSEMENT — Named authorities vouching for the mechanism
4. CLINICAL RESULTS — Before/after measurements from practitioners
5. CASE STUDIES — Documented individual transformations
6. DEMONSTRATIONS — Visual/experiential proof it works
7. TESTIMONIALS — User-reported results
8. LOGICAL PROOF — First-principles reasoning that's hard to argue
9. HISTORICAL PRECEDENT — Long-standing use/results
10. SOCIAL PROOF — Volume of adoption/usage

RULE: Every mechanism needs proof from at least 2 levels.
Top mechanisms have proof from 3+ levels.
```

## Mapping Logic

### Step 1: Identify All Claims

```
FOR each mechanism candidate:

  FROM explanation_structures.core_logic:

  EXTRACT every CLAIM the mechanism makes:

  CAUSAL CLAIMS (mechanism causes the result):
    - "This works because..."
    - "When [mechanism] activates, [result] happens"
    - "The reason this produces [result] is..."

  EFFICACY CLAIMS (it actually works):
    - "This has been shown to..."
    - "Users experience..."
    - "The result is..."

  UNIQUENESS CLAIMS (nothing else does this):
    - "Unlike other approaches..."
    - "This is the only..."
    - "No other method..."

  SAFETY CLAIMS (it won't hurt you):
    - "Without side effects..."
    - "Completely natural..."
    - "No risk of..."

  SPEED CLAIMS (how fast it works):
    - "In just [timeframe]..."
    - "Within [period]..."
    - "Immediately..."

  CLASSIFY each claim by importance:
    CRITICAL: The mechanism falls apart without this proof
    SUPPORTING: Strengthens the case but not essential
    BONUS: Nice to have, adds credibility
```

### Step 2: Inventory Available Proof

```
FROM upstream_context + general knowledge:

CATALOG what proof exists or could exist:

  STUDIES:
    - Published research in the mechanism's domain
    - Related studies that support the underlying science
    - Meta-analyses or review papers

  DATA:
    - Statistics from relevant institutions
    - Market data, health data, performance data
    - Survey results or polling data

  EXPERTS:
    - Named authorities in the field
    - Credential holders (MD, PhD, etc.)
    - Practitioners with track records

  RESULTS:
    - Clinical outcomes
    - Before/after measurements
    - Tracked improvements

  TESTIMONIALS:
    - Customer language from research (direct quotes)
    - Case studies from the product/service
    - Reviews and feedback

  DEMONSTRATIONS:
    - Visible/experiential proof
    - Live demonstrations possible
    - Comparison tests
```

### Step 3: Match Claims to Proof

```
FOR each claim:

  FIND the strongest available proof:
    - Does a study directly support this?
    - Does an expert validate this?
    - Do results demonstrate this?
    - Can this be logically proven?
    - Does historical precedent support this?

  ASSIGN proof with:
    - proof_type: what kind of evidence
    - proof_strength: how strong (1-10)
    - evidence_description: specific details
    - source: where it comes from
    - availability:
        available = we have it now
        findable = it exists, we can locate it
        constructible = we can create it (demonstration, case study)
        unavailable = doesn't exist or can't be found

  IF no proof available for a critical claim:
    FLAG as proof_gap
    SUGGEST resolution options
```

### Step 4: Design Proof Architecture

```
FOR each candidate:

  DETERMINE proof sequence:

  LEAD PROOF (first shown):
    - Most surprising or compelling evidence
    - Often a specific number or study
    - Must hook attention ("According to a Harvard study...")

  SUPPORTING PROOF (builds case):
    - Expert endorsements
    - Logical reasoning chains
    - Additional data points

  CLOSING PROOF (seals the deal):
    - Testimonials and case studies
    - Before/after demonstrations
    - Social proof / volume of adoption

  RATIONALE: Why this sequence?
    - Skepticism level of audience
    - What they need to believe FIRST
    - How trust builds progressively
```

### Step 5: Assess Coverage

```
FOR each candidate:

  CALCULATE proof_coverage:
    coverage_ratio = critical_claims_proved / total_critical_claims

  IDENTIFY:
    strongest_proof_element = highest strength score
    weakest_link = lowest-proved critical claim

  COMPARE to vault patterns:
    - How does this proof density compare to top-25 exemplars?
    - Are we above or below the vault average?
    - What proof types do high-scorers use that we're missing?

  FLAG candidates with coverage_ratio < 0.60 as RISKY
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. All critical claims have assigned proof (coverage_ratio ≥ 0.80)
2. Proof from at least 2 hierarchy levels per candidate
3. Lead proof is specific (includes numbers, names, or sources)
4. No vague proof ("studies show..." without citation specifics)
5. Proof gaps have resolution options listed
6. Proof architecture has clear sequence rationale
