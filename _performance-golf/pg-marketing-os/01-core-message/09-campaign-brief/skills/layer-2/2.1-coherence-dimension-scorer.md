## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Score Each of the 10 Coherence Dimensions

For each dimension, produce:

```yaml
dimension_score:
  dimension_number: integer
  dimension_name: string
  score: integer  # 1-10
  assessment: string  # 2-3 sentences explaining the score
  weakness_flag: boolean  # true if score < 6
  improvement_suggestion: string  # if weakness_flag, specific actionable recommendation
```

**DIMENSION 1: Root Cause -> Mechanism**
- Question: Does the mechanism directly address the root cause?
- Perfect (10): The mechanism IS the solution to the root cause — hearing one makes the other inevitable
- Weak (1-4): The mechanism addresses a symptom, not the root cause
- Failure mode: Mechanism feels like a non-sequitur to the problem
- Reference: element_relationships pair 1 strength score

**DIMENSION 2: Mechanism -> Promise**
- Question: Does the mechanism credibly deliver the promise?
- Perfect (10): The promise is the natural, inevitable outcome of the mechanism working
- Weak (1-4): The promise is aspirational but the mechanism doesn't logically produce it
- Failure mode: "That sounds nice but how does THIS method lead to THAT result?"
- Reference: element_relationships pair 2 strength score

**DIMENSION 3: Promise -> Proof**
- Question: Is the promise supported by available proof?
- Perfect (10): Multiple proof types directly support the specific promise
- Weak (1-4): Promise exceeds what proof can credibly support — "proof ceiling" violation
- Failure mode: Promise sounds like hype because the proof doesn't match
- Reference: element_relationships pair 3 strength score

**DIMENSION 4: Big Idea -> Mechanism**
- Question: Does the Big Idea frame the mechanism in a compelling, coherent way?
- Perfect (10): The Big Idea IS the mechanism seen from the audience's perspective
- Weak (1-4): The Big Idea frames something different than what the mechanism does
- Failure mode: Big Idea captures attention but the mechanism reveal is a bait-and-switch
- Reference: element_relationships pair 4 strength score

**DIMENSION 5: Big Idea -> Audience Awareness**
- Question: Is the Big Idea appropriate for the audience's awareness stage?
- Perfect (10): The Big Idea meets the audience exactly where they are
- Weak (1-4): The Big Idea assumes awareness the audience doesn't have
- Failure mode: Audience feels "this isn't for me"

**DIMENSION 6: Villain -> Root Cause**
- Question: Does the villain explain why the root cause persists?
- Perfect (10): The villain IS the reason the root cause hasn't been solved
- Weak (1-4): The villain is emotionally satisfying but logically disconnected
- Failure mode: Villain framing feels manipulative rather than illuminating
- Reference: element_relationships pair 6 strength score

**DIMENSION 7: Offer -> Promise**
- Question: Does the offer deliver what the promise claims?
- Perfect (10): Every offer component maps to a specific promise dimension
- Weak (1-4): Offer includes unconnected components or promise implies undelivered items
- Failure mode: Prospect confused about what they're getting
- Reference: element_relationships pair 7 strength score

**DIMENSION 8: Structure -> Big Idea**
- Question: Does the argument structure serve the Big Idea's narrative?
- Perfect (10): The CPB sequence builds the case inevitably
- Weak (1-4): The argument follows a generic pattern
- Failure mode: Argument feels like a template rather than a custom case
- Reference: element_relationships pair 8 strength score

**DIMENSION 9: Lead Direction -> Market Psychology**
- Question: Does the optimal lead type match the market's psychological state?
- Perfect (10): The lead enters the conversation already in the prospect's mind
- Weak (1-4): The lead assumes an emotional state the market isn't in
- Failure mode: Lead falls flat — wrong emotional entry point

**DIMENSION 10: Story Direction -> Proof Assets**
- Question: Does the optimal story type leverage the strongest proof assets?
- Perfect (10): The story type naturally showcases the most compelling proof
- Weak (1-4): The story type doesn't align with available proof
- Failure mode: Story feels unsupported or strongest proof sits unused

### Step 2: Calculate Overall Coherence Score

Apply dimension weighting:
- **Dimensions 1-4** (strategy chain): weight 1.5x — these are load-bearing
- **Dimensions 5-8** (framing alignment): weight 1.0x
- **Dimensions 9-10** (creative direction): weight 0.75x

```
overall_coherence = (
  (D1 + D2 + D3 + D4) * 1.5 +
  (D5 + D6 + D7 + D8) * 1.0 +
  (D9 + D10) * 0.75
) / (4 * 1.5 + 4 * 1.0 + 2 * 0.75)
```

### Step 3: Flag Critical Conditions

```
IF D1 < 6 OR D2 < 6:
  FLAG: "STRUCTURAL FAILURE — load-bearing connection broken"
  structural_failure = true

IF count(dimensions_below_6) >= 3:
  FLAG: "CRITICAL COHERENCE FAILURE — 3+ dimensions weak"
  critical_failure = true

IF overall_coherence < 5.0:
  FLAG: "FUNDAMENTAL STRATEGIC MISALIGNMENT — return to upstream"
```

### Step 4: Cross-Reference with Vault Intelligence

Compare dimension scores against vault patterns:
- How do these scores compare to patterns from elite controls?
- Are the weak dimensions typically weak in the same niche?
- Do vault patterns suggest specific remediation approaches?

## Quality Gate

- [ ] All 10 dimensions scored with score, assessment, weakness_flag, and improvement_suggestion
- [ ] Each assessment is 2-3 specific sentences (not generic filler)
- [ ] Overall coherence score calculated with correct weighting
- [ ] Structural failure flagged if dimensions 1-2 below 6
- [ ] Critical coherence failure flagged if 3+ dimensions below 6
- [ ] Vault intelligence cross-referenced for empirical grounding
- [ ] No inflated scores — at least one dimension should be notably lower than others
- [ ] Overall coherence score >= 7.0 required (or flagged for human override)

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **All-high scoring:** Every dimension 8+ with no weaknesses — SUSPECT. Challenge: "What's the weakest connection? Even a 7 has a reason it's not a 9."
- **Generic assessments:** "This dimension is strong" without citing specific upstream evidence — REJECT
- **Ignoring vault patterns:** Scoring without comparing to empirical reference points — incomplete
