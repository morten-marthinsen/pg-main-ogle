## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Load Creative Brief Philosophy

Extract Ogilvy's creative brief framework:
- "Give me the freedom of a tight brief" — the brief as focusing lens, not limitation
- Every downstream writing decision is sharper when the brief answers: "What is THIS campaign trying to do, and how does MY section serve that?"
- The brief provides specific, actionable constraints — not generic instructions

### Step 2: Load E5 Campaign Architecture (Todd Brown)

Extract the 6 core principles:
1. The campaign thesis is the single overarching claim everything proves
2. Every section serves the thesis — nothing exists for decoration
3. The lead makes the emotional sale; the argument makes the logical sale; the close makes the financial sale
4. Lead type must match mechanism type and awareness level
5. Story type must match the proof assets and narrative needs
6. The entire campaign is one continuous conversation, not separate sections bolted together

### Step 3: Load Schwartz Awareness Level Integration

Extract lead type decision logic by awareness stage:
- **Most Aware:** Direct offer lead (rare in long-form)
- **Product Aware:** Differentiation lead, mechanism-focused
- **Solution Aware:** Story lead, discovery lead, scientific lead
- **Problem Aware:** Problem-agitation lead, empathy lead
- **Unaware:** Story lead, curiosity lead, prediction lead

The awareness level from Skill 01 research directly informs lead type recommendation in Layer 3.

### Step 4: Load Coherence Principles (Makepeace)

Extract the coherence-as-competitive-advantage teaching:
- "A promotion falls apart when any link in the logical chain breaks"
- Incoherence is the silent killer of conversion — prospects don't consciously detect it, they just stop believing
- Root cause that doesn't connect to mechanism = credibility leak
- Promise that proof can't support = credibility leak
- Big Idea that frames the mechanism differently than the argument presents it = credibility leak
- Highest-converting promotions have tight coherence across ALL elements

Assemble all teaching references into a structured index:
