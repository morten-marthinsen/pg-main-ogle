## PATTERN LIBRARY

### PATTERN 1: Tightness Test

**Rule:** If a contrarian hook requires explanation to land, it's not tight enough.

**Test:** Can someone grasp the hook in ONE read without context?
- YES → Proceed
- NO → Tighten or discard

**Examples:**
| Not Tight | Tight |
|-----------|-------|
| "The equipment lie that's costing you distance" | "The worse your slice, the more this works" |
| "What they don't want you to know about slicing" | "Your slice is gone the moment you switch drivers" |
| "The yards you're already owed" | "Regain the 30 yards your slice stole" |

---

### PATTERN 2: Specificity Filter

**Rule:** Replace every abstract term with a number, moment, or action.

**Common Swaps:**
| Abstract | Specific |
|----------|----------|
| "Distance" | "30 yards" |
| "Immediately" | "On your first shot" |
| "Improvement" | "8 strokes off your handicap" |
| "Results" | "70% hit it straighter" |
| "Equipment" | [Name the specific item] |
| "Benefits" | [Name the specific benefit] |

**Test:** Read the copy aloud. Every vague noun should trigger revision.

---

### PATTERN 3: Tongue-in-Cheek Filter

**Rule:** Clever wordplay signals marketing. Direct statements signal truth.

**Signs of tongue-in-cheek (AVOID):**
- Puns or wordplay
- "You're owed" or "collect" (metaphoric ownership)
- Anything that sounds like a tagline
- Anything that would make a skeptic roll their eyes

**Signs of direct (USE):**
- Active verbs: stole, regain, eliminate, fix
- Specific numbers: 30 yards, first shot, 70%
- Simple declarative sentences
- Sounds like something a trusted friend would say

**Test:** Would a skeptical expert roll their eyes reading this? If yes, revise.

---

### PATTERN 4: Niche Cultural Fit

**Rule:** Every niche has messaging that works and messaging that fails. Map these BEFORE generating.

**Example — Golf:**
| Works | Fails |
|-------|-------|
| Physics explanations | Conspiracy framing |
| Equipment innovation | "Industry lies" |
| Specific results | Vague transformation |
| Tour validation | Anti-establishment |
| Vindication without villains | Villain personification |

**Process:** Before generating Big Ideas, explicitly list:
1. What cultural messaging works in this niche
2. What cultural messaging fails in this niche
3. Why (the underlying psychology)

---

### PATTERN 5: Recovery vs Improvement Framing

**Rule:** Loss aversion is more powerful than gain-seeking. But expression must be DIRECT, not clever.

**Works:**
- "Regain the 30 yards your slice stole from you"
- "Stop losing 30 yards to your slice"
- "Get back what your driver has been taking"

**Fails:**
- "The 20 yards you're already owed" (too clever)
- "Time to collect" (metaphoric, not direct)
- "Recovery is yours" (abstract)

**Test:** Does the recovery framing use active verbs and specific losses? If not, revise.

---

### PATTERN 6: Equipment-As-Fix Positioning

**Rule:** For equipment products, the equipment change CAN be the climactic moment.

**Standard framing:** Equipment enables → User does thing → Result
**Advanced framing:** Equipment change IS the fix → Result is automatic

**Examples:**
- "Your slice is gone the moment you switch drivers"
- "Eliminate your slice before you even swing"
- "No swing change required — because the driver IS the change"

**When to use:** When the mechanism truly makes the fix automatic. Don't use if user effort is required.

---

### PATTERN 7: Counterintuitive Inversion

**Rule:** Counterintuitive hooks work when:
1. The inversion is immediately graspable
2. The mechanism logically backs it up
3. It resolves a common objection

**Template:** "The [negative attribute] the [subject], the [positive outcome]"

**Examples:**
- "The worse your slice, the more this works"
- "The older you are, the faster this works" (if mechanism supports)
- "The less time you have, the better this fits" (if mechanism supports)

**Test:** Can you explain WHY the inversion is true in one sentence? If yes, proceed.

## APPLICATION PROTOCOL

### During Layer 2 (Candidate Generation)

1. **Before generating:** Review this file's patterns
2. **After generating each candidate:** Run through filters:
   - [ ] Tightness test passed?
   - [ ] Specificity filter applied?
   - [ ] Tongue-in-cheek filter passed?
   - [ ] Niche cultural fit verified?
   - [ ] If recovery framing, is expression direct?
   - [ ] If counterintuitive, is it immediately graspable?

3. **Revise or discard** candidates that fail filters

### During Layer 3 (Validation)

Add to scoring dimensions:
- **Expression Quality:** Is language direct, specific, tight? (1-10)
- **Cultural Fit:** Does it match niche messaging patterns? (1-10)
