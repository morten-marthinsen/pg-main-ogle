# 1.2-lead-structure-planner.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Architecture)
> **Skill:** ADV-01-hook-lead-writer
> **Purpose:** Plan lead structure for editorial voice establishment
> **Outputs:** `lead_structure` object with paragraph plan and voice markers

---

## FUNCTION

Design the lead structure that follows the hook and establishes the editorial voice. The lead (typically 3-5 paragraphs, 100-250 words) must accomplish three things: (1) sustain the momentum from the hook, (2) establish the editorial voice register that continues through body copy, and (3) begin teasing the mechanism without naming the product.

---

## LEAD STRUCTURE PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Define Lead Architecture

```
BASED ON advertorial_type + hook_formula:

  native_article + discovery_frame:
    P1: Expand on the research context (who, where, what was found)
    P2: Why this matters to the reader (connect finding to their problem)
    P3: The scope of the issue (how many people affected, how long overlooked)
    P4: Tease the mechanism ("What researchers found was...")
    voice: journalistic third-person

  blog_style + narrative_scene:
    P1: Continue the story (what happened next)
    P2: Reader identification moment ("If you've ever experienced...")
    P3: The turning point hint
    P4: Why this story matters beyond one person
    voice: conversational first-person

  listicle + curiosity_statistic:
    P1: Context for the statistic (why this number matters)
    P2: What most people do about the problem (conventional approach)
    P3: Why conventional approach falls short
    P4: Setup for the list ("Here are the [N] things...")
    voice: authoritative second-person

  editorial_feature + contrarian_statement:
    P1: The conventional wisdom being challenged
    P2: Who is challenging it and their credentials
    P3: The evidence that sparked the challenge
    P4: What this means for the reader
    voice: investigative journalistic
```

### Step 2: Plan Voice Markers

```
FOR selected voice_register:
  PLAN specific voice markers for each paragraph:

  journalistic:
    - Third-person attribution ("Dr. [Name] explains that...")
    - Institutional references ("Research published in [Journal]...")
    - Hedging language ("appears to", "may", "suggests")
    - Source diversity (at least 2 different attribution types)

  conversational:
    - First-person experience ("I was skeptical when I first heard...")
    - Reader direct address ("You know that feeling when...")
    - Colloquial phrasing (reads like talking, not writing)
    - Personal credibility markers ("After 3 months of research...")

  expert:
    - Technical-but-accessible language
    - Process explanation without jargon
    - Evidence-first ordering
    - Authority without condescension

  peer:
    - Shared experience framing ("Like most of us...")
    - Discovery language ("What I found surprised me...")
    - Collaborative tone ("Here's what we need to know...")
    - Authentic uncertainty markers
```

### Step 3: Plan Mechanism Teasing

```
PLAN how lead teases mechanism WITHOUT naming product:

  TEASE LEVELS:
    Level 1 (Light): "A compound" / "A specific approach" / "One factor"
    Level 2 (Moderate): "A [category] compound" / "A technique involving [general area]"
    Level 3 (Heavy): Describe mechanism function without brand name

  FOR lead structure:
    Paragraphs 1-2: No mechanism mention (context only)
    Paragraph 3: Level 1 tease maximum
    Paragraph 4: Level 2 tease maximum
    Product name: NEVER in lead (reserved for body copy mid-section)
```

### Step 4: Define Pacing

```
PACING PLAN:
  - Sentence length variation: short-medium-long-short pattern
  - Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences each (no walls of text)
  - Information density: increase gradually from hook to end of lead
  - Emotional trajectory: hook_emotion -> sustained_interest -> growing_curiosity
  - Transition to body: lead's last sentence must create pull into body
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Voice register undefined | Strategy lacks voice specification | Default to journalistic, flag |
| Mechanism tease too early | Plan reveals too much too soon | Push first tease to paragraph 3+ |
| Lead too long | Word count > 300 | Compress or split into lead + body transition |
| Lead too short | Word count < 80 | Expand with context, not padding |
| Voice drift planned | Different registers in different paragraphs | Lock to single register throughout |
