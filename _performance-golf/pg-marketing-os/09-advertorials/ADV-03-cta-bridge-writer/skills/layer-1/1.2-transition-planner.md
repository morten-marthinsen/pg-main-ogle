# 1.2-transition-planner.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Architecture)
> **Skill:** ADV-03-cta-bridge-writer
> **Purpose:** Plan natural editorial-to-offer transition
> **Outputs:** `transition_plan` object with paragraph-level transition blueprint

---

## FUNCTION

Design the paragraph-by-paragraph transition from editorial body copy to product recommendation. The transition must be gradual enough that the reader doesn't feel the shift. This microskill creates the architectural blueprint for how the bridge unfolds from body's endpoint to CTA.

---

## TRANSITION PLANNING PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Analyze Body Endpoint

```
FROM body_handoff:
  ASSESS:
    - last_section_theme: What the body ended discussing
    - reader_state: Where the reader's mind is now
    - mechanism_status: How much has been explained
    - emotional_state: What the reader feels (informed, curious, convinced)

  IDENTIFY transition opportunity:
    - Natural next thought from body's conclusion
    - What question would the reader ask themselves
    - What editorial next step makes sense
```

### Step 2: Design Transition Gradient

```
PLAN 3-4 paragraph transition:

  Bridge Paragraph 1: EDITORIAL EXTENSION
    Purpose: Continue the editorial thread naturally
    Tone: 100% editorial (no shift yet)
    Content: Broader implications, "What this means for you" editorial analysis
    Sales signal: 0%

  Bridge Paragraph 2: SOFT PIVOT
    Purpose: Shift from analysis to practical application
    Tone: 90% editorial, 10% recommendation
    Content: "For those looking to apply this..." or "The practical question is..."
    Sales signal: 10%

  Bridge Paragraph 3: RECOMMENDATION
    Purpose: Make the recommendation in selected frame
    Tone: 70% editorial, 30% recommendation
    Content: Frame-specific recommendation language
    Sales signal: 30% (but framed as advice, not selling)

  Bridge Paragraph 4: CTA
    Purpose: Provide the single text link
    Tone: 80% editorial recommendation, 20% CTA
    Content: Natural sentence containing the text link
    Sales signal: 20% (minimized by natural integration)

  GRADIENT RULE:
    NO paragraph should have sales signal > 30%
    Total transition: 4+ sentences minimum
    Transition gradient score target: >= 8 (where 10 = invisible)
```

### Step 3: Plan CTA Integration

```
CTA PLACEMENT within bridge:
  PREFERRED: Last sentence of final bridge paragraph
  ACCEPTABLE: Standalone final paragraph (1-2 sentences)
  FORBIDDEN: Mid-bridge (disrupts flow), multiple positions

CTA LANGUAGE plan:
  Use bridge_frame.application.cta_integration guidance
  Must read as natural part of recommendation, not separate element
  Must be text link format (no buttons, no styled elements)

EXAMPLE CTA integrations:
  Discovery: "I found more details about [mechanism] at [link text]."
  Recommendation: "You can learn more about this approach [link text]."
  Expert: "[Expert name] explains the full protocol at [link text]."
  Community: "Others have shared their experiences at [link text]."
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Transition too abrupt | Gradient < 6 | Add intermediary paragraph |
| Sales signal too high | Any paragraph > 30% | Reduce promotional elements |
| CTA too prominent | CTA reads as separate element | Integrate into natural sentence |
| Voice break at pivot | Voice changes at transition | Smooth voice continuity |
