# 1.2-voice-consistency-checker.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Assembly)
> **Skill:** ADV-04-advertorial-assembly
> **Purpose:** Validate consistent voice across all assembled sections
> **Outputs:** `voice_consistency_result` with seam scores and overall rating

---

## FUNCTION

Validate that the assembled advertorial maintains a consistent voice register from first word to last. Check each seam between sections for voice drift. The reader must experience one unified voice throughout, not three different writers.

---

## VOICE CHECK PROTOCOL

### Step 1: Overall Register Verification

```
VERIFY voice register across full piece:
  EXPECTED: strategy_reference.voice_register (e.g., "journalistic")

  SCAN full assembled text:
    - Identify register markers (attribution style, pronoun use, sentence structure)
    - Flag any paragraphs that deviate from expected register
    - Count register-consistent vs register-deviant paragraphs

  IF > 80% paragraphs register-consistent: PASS
  IF 60-80%: CONDITIONAL -- specific deviations flagged
  IF < 60%: FAIL -- significant voice drift
```

### Step 2: Seam-by-Seam Analysis

```
FOR each of 4 seams:

  SEAM 1: Hook -> Lead
    READ: last 2 sentences of hook, first 2 sentences of lead
    SCORE voice_consistency (1-10)
    SCORE tone_continuity (1-10)

  SEAM 2: Lead -> Body
    READ: last 2 sentences of lead, first 2 sentences of body
    SCORE voice_consistency (1-10)
    SCORE tone_continuity (1-10)

  SEAM 3: Body -> Bridge
    READ: last 2 sentences of body, first 2 sentences of bridge
    SCORE voice_consistency (1-10)
    SCORE tone_continuity (1-10)
    NOTE: This seam is highest risk for voice drift (editorial -> recommendation)

  SEAM 4: Bridge -> CTA
    READ: last sentence before CTA, CTA sentence
    SCORE voice_consistency (1-10)
    SCORE tone_continuity (1-10)

  MINIMUM per seam: 7/10 for both metrics
```

### Step 3: Identify Voice Drift Patterns

```
COMMON drift patterns to detect:
  - Lead is conversational but body shifts to formal
  - Body is journalistic but bridge shifts to promotional
  - Hook is punchy but lead becomes academic
  - Bridge starts editorial but CTA becomes salesy

FOR each detected drift:
  LOG: seam location, drift direction, severity
  RECOMMEND: specific fix (word choice, sentence structure, register markers)
```

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Seam score < 7 | Voice drift at section boundary | Add/modify transitional sentence |
| Overall consistency < 60% | Major voice register issues | Flag for ADV-05 editorial revision |
| Body-bridge drift | Most common failure point | Smooth bridge opening to match body voice |
