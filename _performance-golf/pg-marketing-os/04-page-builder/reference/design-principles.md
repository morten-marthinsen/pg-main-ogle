# Landing Page Engine — Design Principles

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Source:** Cross-analysis of 11 landing page specimens from high-impression Facebook ads

---

### Principle 1: ONE OFFER PER PAGE
Every specimen confirms this. Even pages with bundles (LMNT, BIOptimizers, Native) have a single conversion goal — add to cart. Graziosi's page with 5-6 order bumps still has ONE primary offer ($1 trial membership). Closers.io's page has ONE action: submit application. The only exception is Sunday Red (brand homepage) which is not a conversion page.

### Principle 2: PROOF DENSITY IS BIMODAL
Pages either go all-in on proof (Hyros 80%, LMNT 83K reviews, BIOptimizers 23 sections of stacked credibility) or rely on authority alone (Closers.io, Graziosi, Sunday Red — zero customer testimonials). Middle-ground proof strategies were rare. Implication: Don't sprinkle proof. Either make proof the dominant conversion mechanism OR make authority/guarantee the dominant mechanism. Commit to one.

### Principle 3: CONSISTENT CTA TEXT CONVERTS
7 of 11 pages use the exact same CTA text everywhere on the page. MAMG: always "start my free trial." LMNT: always "Add to cart." Closers.io: always "Schedule Demo." Graziosi is the exception — varies CTA text with emotional hooks at each placement — but even Graziosi's CTAs all point to the same action. Consistency reduces cognitive load.

### Principle 4: RISK REVERSAL PROXIMITY
The most effective pages attach risk reversal directly to the CTA button. MAMG: "Try Risk-Free • Cancel Anytime" on every CTA. LMNT: "No Questions Asked Refund Policy" adjacent to Add to Cart. BIOptimizers: "365 Day Guarantee" in trust badge strip. The closer the risk reversal is to the action, the more it reduces friction.

### Principle 5: TEMPLATE REUSE IS A STRATEGY
Me and My Golf runs the EXACT same page template for Fix Your Slice and Pure Your Irons — same testimonials, same pricing, same structure. Only the curriculum content changes. Closers.io runs the same template for STA and DFY services. Implication: Build one high-converting template per page type, then swap content modules.

### Principle 6: PROOF GRADUATION
When proof is present, the best pages graduate it: Social proof (review count, star rating) → Celebrity/Expert endorsements → Customer testimonials → Case studies with metrics → Scientific citations. BIOptimizers is the gold standard: 1M+ customers → 4 expert endorsements → 3 customer reviews → 38 scientific studies. Each layer adds credibility on top of the last.

### Principle 7: SUBSCRIPTION NEEDS ITS OWN SECTION
BIOptimizers and MAMG both dedicate a full section to subscription benefits. BIOptimizers: "Cancel Anytime" + "Monthly Peace of Mind" + "Stay Subscribed—Save 12%" + "Risk-Free for 365 Days." This isn't about the product — it's about overcoming subscription-specific objections. Every subscription page should have a dedicated subscription benefits module.

### Principle 8: TRANSPARENCY BUILDS TRUST
LMNT publishes their DIY recipe in the FAQ. BIOptimizers lists every ingredient with exact amounts. LMNT uses "No Dodgy Ingredients." BIOptimizers shows a comparison table where they win on every metric. Radical transparency is a conversion mechanism, not a risk.

### Principle 9: EDUCATION-BEFORE-SALE CORRELATES WITH SECTION COUNT
The more a product requires education, the more sections the page needs. BIOptimizers: 23 sections (complex supplement, 7-form mechanism). Graziosi: 13 sections (AI membership, feature-by-feature). MAMG: 9 sections (golf instruction, 5-step curriculum). LMNT: 6 sections (simple product, reviews do the work). Closers.io: 3 sections (qualified leads, no education needed). Match section count to buyer education needs.

### Principle 10: QUALIFIER GATES INCREASE QUALITY
Closers.io uses "If not — STOP AND LEAVE THIS PAGE IMMEDIATELY" as the first question. Richmond Dinh uses a 6-point qualification checklist. Both pages are for high-touch offers where lead quality matters more than lead volume. Use qualifier gates when downstream conversion (phone call, onboarding) is expensive.
