# Landing Page Engine — Visual Hierarchy Reference

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Source:** Cross-analysis of 11 landing page specimens

---

## Above-Fold Patterns

### Pattern A: VSL-First (Info Products / B2B)
- **Structure:** Urgency Banner → Headline → VSL Video → CTA
- **Specimens:** Graziosi, Closers.io STA
- **When to use:** When the video IS the selling mechanism. Minimal copy on page; video does the heavy lifting.
- **Key detail:** CTA appears BELOW the video (viewer must watch before clicking)

### Pattern B: Product Hero (DTC / Ecomm)
- **Structure:** Product Image Gallery → Review Count Badge → Product Title → Price/Savings → Subscribe Toggle → Add to Cart → Trust Badges
- **Specimens:** LMNT, BIOptimizers
- **When to use:** Physical product where the packaging, label, and lifestyle imagery matters.
- **Key detail:** Review count and star rating always visible above fold. Price always visible.

### Pattern C: Headline + CTA + Trust (Trial / Opt-in)
- **Structure:** Headline → Subheadline → CTA Button → Trust Badge (Trustpilot)
- **Specimens:** MAMG x2, Richmond Dinh
- **When to use:** Free trial or free event registration where the headline and risk reversal are the primary hooks.
- **Key detail:** Trustpilot/trust badge ALWAYS adjacent to CTA. Risk reversal text ("Try Risk-Free") is part of the CTA cluster.

### Pattern D: Proof-First (SaaS / High Social Proof)
- **Structure:** Headline → Celebrity Endorsement Cards → CTA
- **Specimens:** Hyros
- **When to use:** When proof is the primary conversion mechanism. Skip product explanation; lead with who uses it.
- **Key detail:** Celebrity cards with photos, names, and pull quotes above the fold — before any product explanation.

### Pattern E: Brand Hero (Premium / No Conversion)
- **Structure:** Full-bleed Product Image → Product Name → Subhead → Dual CTAs
- **Specimens:** Sunday Red
- **When to use:** Luxury/premium brand where aesthetics > conversion optimization.
- **Key detail:** Zero urgency, zero proof, zero pricing above fold. Pure visual.

## Section Flow Patterns

### Long-Form (Type A) Universal Flow:
1. HERO / ABOVE FOLD (attention)
2. TRUST SIGNAL (credibility — logos, badge, endorsement)
3. ABOUT / CURRICULUM / FEATURES (education)
4. SOCIAL PROOF (testimonials, reviews)
5. VALUE STACK / MEMBERSHIP (desire)
6. PRICING / CHECKOUT (conversion)
7. FAQ (objection handling)
8. FINAL CTA (close)

### PDP (Type B) Universal Flow:
1. PRODUCT HERO + PURCHASE OPTIONS (attention + conversion)
2. BENEFITS / HOW IT WORKS (education)
3. SUBSCRIPTION BENEFITS (for subscription products)
4. PROOF (reviews, endorsements, citations)
5. COMPARISON / DIFFERENTIATION (competitive positioning)
6. FAQ (objection handling)
7. COMPANY / FOUNDER STORY (brand trust)

## CTA Placement Rules (From Specimen Data)

- **After the hero** — Always (11/11)
- **After the proof section** — Almost always (8/11)
- **After the value stack** — When present (3/3)
- **After FAQ** — Common (5/11)
- **Final CTA (dedicated section)** — Type A pages (6/6)
- **Inline checkout** — ClickFunnels only (1/11)
