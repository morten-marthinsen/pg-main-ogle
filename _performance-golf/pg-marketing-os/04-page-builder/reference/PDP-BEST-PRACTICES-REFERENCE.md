## 1. FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

The "perfect" PDP is founded on four pillars:

1. **DR Principles Remixed** — Traditional direct response persuasion (agitation, mechanism, proof, offer, close) restructured for hunt-and-peck browsing behavior seen on PDPs
2. **Semi-Qualified Traffic** — Most traffic is somewhat warm (product-first ads, organic searches with intent). But the page must also speak to category searchers who don't know the specific product.
3. **UX/UI Best Practices** — Baymard Institute and NN/g usability/conversion testing research governs specific interaction patterns (thumbnails vs dots, stepper vs dropdown, accordion vs scroll)
4. **Scannability** — The page is easily scannable so visitors can pick and choose which sections to focus on to answer their questions and alleviate their concerns

**Key Selling Points (KSPs):** The most salient, outcome-driven reasons to buy the product, expressed as fast-scannable claims that answer why this product is worth choosing right now versus doing nothing or buying an alternative.

## 2. 10-THUMBNAIL STORY ARCHITECTURE

The hero carousel tells the product story visually in 10 slides, following a deliberate persuasion arc. Show first 5 thumbnails beneath the main image, large enough to tease content. Include arrows on both sides.

### Thumbnail 1 — Outcome (Why Am I Here Again?)
- **Shows:** Clean product shot + primary outcome promise + play button overlay → 30-second overview video
- **Purpose:** Reconfirms the exact promise the user was pre-sold on; fastest path to re-belief
- **Copy:** "Calm Focus. All Day." / "Steady Energy. No Crash."
- **Visual:** Bottle/pouch centered, neutral or lifestyle background, bold outcome headline, play icon
- **Specimen:** kachava.com/products/shakes (thumbnail 1, no video overlay)

### Thumbnail 2 — Problem Signs (Is This for Me?)
- **Shows:** Visual checklist or ring of symptoms/frustrations signaling need for product
- **Purpose:** Instant self-identification and relevance before evaluating ingredients or mechanisms
- **Copy:** "Low Energy" | "Brain Fog" | "Jitters" | "Crash Later" | "Gut Issues"
- **Visual:** Lifestyle image with labeled callouts/badges around subject highlighting problem signs
- **Specimen:** thepetlabco.com/products/probiotic-chews-dogs (thumbnail 5)

### Thumbnail 3 — Ingredients (What's in It?)
- **Shows:** Product centered, key ingredient visuals orbiting in circular layout, each with 1-2 word caveman benefit
- **Purpose:** Answers "what's in this" in human terms; makes ingredients feel real and intentional
- **Copy:** "Lion's Mane – Focus" | "Cordyceps – Stamina" | "Reishi – Calm"
- **Visual:** Product center with ingredient photography/illustrations orbiting, subtle connectors, benefit words under each
- **Specimen:** ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee (thumbnail 2)

### Thumbnail 4 — Solution Enjoyment (What Do I Enjoy?)
- **Shows:** Real-life usage moments communicating enjoyment, ease, lifestyle integration
- **Purpose:** Builds desire by showing the lived experience of using the product
- **Copy:** "Feels Good Every Day." / "Easy Morning Ritual."
- **Visual:** Lifestyle imagery, real person enjoying product in natural setting, optional play overlay
- **Specimen:** kachava.com/products/shakes (thumbnail 3)

### Thumbnail 5 — How It Works (Why It Makes Sense)
- **Shows:** Clear 4-step flow: relatable problem → specific ingredient → biological mechanism → felt benefit
- **Purpose:** Makes solution feel logical and earned via straight-line reasoning
- **Copy:** "Stress Spike → L-theanine → Boosts calming alpha waves → Calm Focus."
- **Visual:** Four panels or arrows in sequence, each with short phrase + minimal icon
- **Specimen:** trycreate.co/products/sour-peach-core-create-creatine-monohydrate-gummies (thumbnail 6)

### Thumbnail 6 — What to Expect (When Will I Notice?)
- **Shows:** Simple 3-phase timeline showing results unfolding over time
- **Purpose:** Sets expectations, reframes subtle early signals as progress, prevents premature doubt
- **Copy:** "Days 1–3: Feel Subtle." → "Week 1–2: Feel Steady." → "Week 3+: Feel Change."
- **Visual:** Timeline with large time markers, short caveman labels, calm non-hype styling
- **Specimen:** primalqueen.com (in body — simplified version ideal)

### Thumbnail 7 — How to Use (Is This Easy?)
- **Shows:** Simple 3-step instruction: dose, timing, method
- **Purpose:** Removes friction by making the habit feel easy and obvious
- **Copy:** "2 Pills." → "Morning." → "With Water."
- **Visual:** Numbered steps with large numerals, relevant icons
- **Specimen:** kachava.com/products/shakes (thumb 7), ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee (thumb 4)

### Thumbnail 8 — Believability/Credibility (Where's the Proof?)
- **Shows:** One strong credibility anchor — study reference, expert endorsement, or substantiated claim
- **Purpose:** Signals seriousness and legitimacy
- **Copy:** "Clinically Studied Ingredients" / "Doctor Formulated"
- **Visual:** Medical professional, study citation graphic, or authoritative badge
- **Specimen:** im8health.com/products/essentials (thumb 3), drinkag1.com/products/greens-powder-pouch (thumb 7)

### Thumbnail 9 — Alternative Comparison (Why Is This Better?)
- **Shows:** Simple comparison table: our product vs typical alternative, capped at 5 rows
- **Purpose:** Collapses choice into obvious verdict, reduces comparison shopping
- **Copy:** Rows: "No Jitters" | "Better Mood" | "Gut Support" | "Clean Ingredients" | "No Added Sugar"
- **Visual:** Three-column table, feature / our product / alternative, checks and Xs, our column emphasized
- **Specimen:** foursigmatic.com/products/think-ground-coffee-bag (in body)

### Thumbnail 10 — Testimonials (Emotional Close)
- **Shows:** Real user mid-use with play overlay → fast-cut testimonial montage (20-30 sec)
- **Purpose:** Transfers belief through social proof, closes emotional loop with FOMO
- **Copy:** "This Changed My Life." / "I Finally Feel Normal Again."
- **Visual:** Authentic lifestyle shot, happy customer, caption + play icon → video testimonial montage
- **Specimen:** ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee (thumbnail 6)

### Carousel Best Practices
- High fidelity visuals to bring things to life
- Product in action, lifestyle imagery
- Show taste/beauty/delight where possible
- 10 thumbnails following the story architecture above
- Show first 5 beneath the main image, large enough to tease
- Easily visible arrows on both sides of hero and/or mini thumbs
- At least one product video in carousel (ideally first thumbnail)

## 3. FACTS PANEL

**Position:** Below the thumbnail carousel, above buy box
- **Shows:** Full Nutrition/Supplement Facts panel (left 2/3) + vertical stack of quality/diet icons (right 1/3)
- **Purpose:** Gives analytical buyers immediate verification while reassuring casual shoppers with scannable quality cues
- **Why Here:** Nutrition/supplement facts are extremely important to a specific buyer segment; placing here gives prominent visibility as a carousel caption
- **Copy:** "Full Supplement Facts" paired with icon labels: "Vegan," "Gluten Free," "No Added Sugar," "Dairy Free," "Soy Free"
- **Visual:** Clean facts panel left, right column of minimal quality icons, no extra badges or callouts

## 4. BUY BOX ARCHITECTURE

### Product Title Rules
- **ALWAYS display the full product title** — never truncate. If it takes 3 lines, let it take 3 lines.
- **Baymard finding:** Users rely on the title to confirm they landed on the correct variation (e.g., "Men's Vitamin" vs "Men's Vitamin 50+"). Truncating creates anxiety.

### Rating Strip
- Stars + "(X,XXX Reviews)" must be a **page anchor link** → smooth-scrolls to Reviews section
- **Baymard finding:** Users see the rating and immediately want to verify why it's not 5 stars

### Price Display
- **Always show original price struck through** — establishes value anchor
- **Always show savings as BOTH % and dollar amount** — different buyers prefer different formats
- Explicit savings messaging: "You save $X with the 3-pack"
- **Baymard finding:** Users determine "value" by combining Price + Shipping. If shipping cost isn't visible near price, they assume it's expensive.

### Variant Selector — Exposed Chips
- Use **exposed buttons (chips)**, NOT dropdowns
- **Baymard finding:** Dropdowns require 3+ taps (open, scroll, close) and hide available options → 60% more friction
- Each chip shows: variant name, price if different, availability status
- Currently-selected chip visually distinct (filled vs outlined)
- Touch targets: minimum 44x44px

### Short Description
- Use a distinct "Product Subtitle" or "Key Benefits" list (3-5 bullets)
- Placed between title/rating and price
- **Baymard finding:** Users need to quickly assess "what makes this different" before evaluating price

### Subscription vs One-Time Purchase
- Use **selectable tiles** (radio buttons), NOT pre-selected subscription
- OTP tile: "One-Time Purchase — $XX.XX"
- S&S tile: "Subscribe & Save XX% — $XX.XX/month" + "Cancel anytime" + frequency selector
- **Baymard finding:** "Sneaking" a subscription causes cart abandonment and chargebacks. Honest tile design works.

### Quantity Selector
- Use a **stepper (+ / -)**, NOT dropdown
- Default to 1. Touch targets minimum 44x44px.
- **Baymard finding:** Dropdowns are high-interaction cost (tap, scroll, done). For 90% of users, desired quantity is "1."

### Add to Cart Button
- Full-width, standard "Add to Cart" label (not creative alternatives)
- State change on tap (brief animation/color shift confirming action)
- **Post-click:** Slide-out mini-cart (stay on PDP), NOT redirect to cart page
- **Baymard finding:** Cart redirect interrupts shopping flow. If user wanted to add a second item or read reviews, forced navigation is frustrating.

### Express Payments
- Below ATC with "OR" divider
- One row of logos (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Shop Pay, etc.)
- **Baymard finding:** Too many colorful buttons compete with main ATC. Prioritize primary action.

### Micro-Trust Signals
- Small gray text immediately below ATC button
- 3-4 signals max: "Secure Checkout • Ships Tomorrow • 30-Day Returns • Free Shipping"
- **Baymard finding:** Users have last-second anxieties right before tapping ATC — "Is this secure? When will it ship? Can I return it?"

### Shipping Proximity
- Concise shipping summary directly below price or ATC
- Format: "Free Shipping" or "Ships in 1-2 business days" or "Free shipping on orders over $X"
- **Baymard finding:** Users combine Price + Shipping to determine value

## 5. BTF SECTION TEMPLATES

Each BTF section follows a consistent template: What It Shows / Why It Exists / Best Practices / Specimens.

### 5.1 Product Highlights (TLDR)
- **Shows:** Digestible caveman bits of copy addressing top 3-5 questions/issues upfront
- **Purpose:** Resolve major objections fast for those who scroll past CTA to re-confirm or discover what the product is about
- **Best Practices:**
  - Choose top 3-5 questions as mental blockers: What's in It, How it Helps, Why it Works, Shipping & Returns, Our Guarantee
  - "What's in It" → show supplement/nutrition facts panel
  - "How it Helps" → 5-7 short benefit bullets with microscripts
  - "Why it Works" → 1 sentence mechanism + simple graphic
  - "Shipping & Returns" → free S&H emphasis + expected speed
  - "Our Guarantee" → key benefit to experience within guarantee period
  - Mix hard data (nutrition panel) with good copy (personality, not boring facts)
- **Good specimen:** kachava.com/products/shakes — 5 key points, mixes KSPs with objections, personality
- **Bad specimen:** blueprint.bryanjohnson.com/products/longevity-blend — boring labels, missing shipping/returns

### 5.2 UGC Video Carousel (Social Proof)
- **Shows:** 5-7 energetic UGC video testimonials
- **Purpose:** Social proof + FOMO that the product works
- **Best Practices:**
  - Curate best-of-best testimonials with engaging, enthusiastic people
  - Select variety of avatars/personas for max coverage
  - Re-use UGC ads that performed well (strip the selling)
  - Best: real customers filming with phone, up close
  - Keep weirdness, ums, uhs — keep it real
  - Can mix in expert videos if authentic
  - Arrows on both sides of carousel
  - Video muted autoplay with clear signal to click for sound
  - Navigating to next video stops previous
- **Specimens:**
  - stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-brain — best integrated example, authentic, influencer mix, visible arrows
  - shop.upwellness.com/pages/golden-revive-plus — 100% UGC, diverse, clear play button
  - trycreate.co/products/sour-peach-core-create-creatine-monohydrate-gummies — video-only, great UGC vibe
  - simplynootropics.com/products/nmn-powder — combo video + written

### 5.3 The Expert (Credibility)
- **Shows:** Credible image/video of expert who created or endorses the product
- **Purpose:** Overcome skepticism about safety and effectiveness
- **Best Practices:**
  - Ideally video with thumbnail of them speaking
  - White coat where possible
  - Full title + school (e.g., "XX School of Medicine")
  - Natural speaking, no canned scripts; splice takes or use prompting questions
  - Captions like UGC ad
  - Video stops when user scrolls away
- **Specimens:**
  - humann.com/products/superbeets-soft-chew — excellent video execution, two experts
  - trycreate.co/products/sour-peach-core-create-creatine-monohydrate-gummies — images, great credentials, missing quotes

### 5.4 What's in It (Ingredients)
- **Shows:** Visual-first ingredient deep-dive
- **Best Practices:**
  - Compelling visual that brings ingredient to life (flower, molecular structure, powder textures)
  - Ingredient Name + Microscript Benefit: "L-Theanine for Tranquility" (X for Y naming)
  - 1-2 sentence description connecting benefit to mechanism
  - **List vertically and expanded** — NOT accordion-rolled unless many ingredients
  - If accordion: show teaser with first ingredient expanded; collapsing one expands the other
  - Baymard: vertical stacking > horizontal carousels for ingredients
- **Specimens:**
  - stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint — horizontal slider but well-executed, beautiful imagery
  - healthybonesco.com/products/collagen-peptides — vertical accordion, first expanded, plus/X signs

### 5.5 What to Expect (Benefits Timeline)
- **Shows:** Step-by-step timeline of when/how ingredients affect them
- **Purpose:** Future-pacing with tangible benefits, visualizing biochemistry changes, building believability
- **Best Practices:**
  - 3-5 key timeframes: short, medium, long term (ideally: 7 days, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months)
  - Vertical line on left with big circles at each milestone (appears gradually on scroll)
  - Benefit Title = powerful benefit microscript
  - Short description connecting ingredient mechanism to felt benefit at that stage
  - Optional lifestyle imagery per milestone
- **Specimens:**
  - seed.com/daily-synbiotic — nice labels, great headlines, line should be animated
  - stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint — great circle styling, progressive feeling descriptions
  - superfoodscompany.com/products/superfood-tabs — creative auto-playing transformation images

### 5.6 The Problem (Agitation)
- May consolidate with Solution and Logic into unified Problem→Solution→Logic section
- **Specimens:** mengotomars.com, nutreance.com/products/redimove

### 5.7 The Solution (USP)
- **Pattern:** Paradigm shift → Real cause → New solution (3 sections)
- May consolidate with Problem and Logic
- **Specimens:** mengotomars.com, nutreance.com/products/redimove, nutreance.com/products/glucoven

### 5.8 Why It Works (Logic)
- **Shows:** Biochemical/mechanistic diagram showing how formula works
- **Purpose:** Logical answer to "how does it work" — a simple biochem tutorial
- **Specimen:** pendulumlife.com/pages/shop-pendulum-glucose-control-probiotic-blood-sugar-support (biochem diagram)

### 5.9 Comparison Chart (Advantages)
- **Shows:** Product vs primary alternative comparison
- **Best Practices:**
  - 3 columns: Feature | Our Product | Comparison (don't do 3-4 comparison columns — too noisy, unless highly commoditized market)
  - Each row = critical feature/advantage/benefit (grass-fed, free-from-X, etc.)
  - Use primacy/recency: most important comparisons first AND last, least important in middle
  - Checkmarks for product, X for competition
  - 5-7 rows for digestibility
  - Optional icon to left of each feature
  - Product column highlighted with colored background
- **Specimens:**
  - foursigmatic.com/products/think-ground-coffee-bag — near-ideal, one comparison, mixed attributes, good primacy
  - instanthydration.com/products/premium-electrolyte-drink-mix — 2 comparisons (appropriate for competitive market)

### 5.10 Cost Savings Chart (Affordability)
- **Shows:** Side-by-side cost of buying each ingredient individually vs all-in-one product
- **Purpose:** Dramatize value and cost savings
- **Best Practices:**
  - 3 columns: Ingredient | Product (checkboxes + price at bottom) | Standalone Price (prices + total)
  - Centered "You Save $X" punchline at bottom in styled starburst/rectangle
  - Always do the math for people — reduce cognitive load
- **Specimen:** mudwtr.com/products/coffee-starter-kit — shows benefits instead of ingredients (smart variation), missing the "You Save" punchline

## 6. BAYMARD MOBILE UX FINDINGS

All findings from Baymard Institute and NN/g research, organized by PDP component.

### 6.1 Image Gallery
| Finding | Stat/Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| Dots vs Thumbnails | **50-80% of users** overlook additional images when only dots are used | Display thumbnails below main image — even on mobile, visible thumbnails encourage interaction |
| Pinch-to-Zoom | **40% of mobile sites** fail to support pinch-to-zoom | Support BOTH double-tap AND pinch. Dynamically swap for high-res on zoom (no pixelation). |
| In-Scale Images | Users lose sense of scale on 6-inch screen | Include at least one "in scale" image showing product relative to everyday object |
| Gallery Overlay | Carousel is for browsing; overlay is for inspecting | Tapping main image → full-screen dark overlay with left/right swiping + 2x-4x deep zoom |
| Back Button Trap | Users hit browser back to close overlay → leaves PDP entirely | Hijack back button while overlay is open so it only closes the image |
| Video in Carousel | Users expect to see product in motion | Mix video directly into carousel (slide 1 or 2). Prominent play icon overlay on thumbnail. |

### 6.2 Product Title & Rating
| Finding | Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Title Truncation | Users confirm they landed on correct variation via title | ALWAYS display full product title — 3 lines is fine |
| Rating as Anchor Link | Users want to verify why not 5 stars | Stars + "(X Reviews)" must smooth-scroll to Reviews section |

### 6.3 Price & Savings
| Finding | Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Explicit Savings | Users don't do mental math on mobile | Show savings as BOTH percentage AND dollar amount |
| Shipping Proximity | Users combine Price + Shipping for value | Place shipping summary directly below price or ATC |

### 6.4 Variant Selection
| Finding | Stat | Best Practice |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Dropdowns vs Chips | Dropdowns = 3+ taps, hide options → 60% more friction | Use exposed buttons (chips). Currently-selected visually distinct. |
| Touch Targets | Mobile tap precision is low | Minimum 44x44px touch targets |

### 6.5 Subscription UX
| Finding | Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Sneaky Subscriptions | Top cause of cart abandonment and chargebacks | Selectable tiles with EXPLICIT savings. "Cancel anytime" visible. |

### 6.6 Quantity & ATC
| Finding | Stat | Best Practice |
|---------|------|---------------|
| Quantity Dropdown | High interaction cost, 90% want "1" | Stepper (+ / -) interface, minimum 44x44px |
| Post-ATC Redirect | Interrupts shopping flow | Slide-out mini-cart. Stay on PDP. |
| Express Payments | Compete with main ATC | Below ATC with "OR" divider. ONE row of logos. |
| Last-Second Anxiety | Right before tapping ATC | Micro-trust signals (3-4 max) in small gray text below button |

### 6.7 BTF Content Layout
| Finding | Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Long Scroll Problem | Users lose context, can't skip to section they care about | Vertically stacked accordions (expandable sections) |
| Dense Paragraphs | Mobile: scanner's fatigue → users skip entirely | "Snackable" chunks: short paragraphs, bullets, headers |
| Ingredient Lists | Need truncation, not full hiding | Show first 3-5 ingredients visible + "Show All" — NOT accordion-hidden |
| Images of Text | Unreadable on mobile without pinching, bad for SEO | HTML text + "label" image. Never image-only text. |
| Horizontal Carousels | Users miss content in primary decision areas | Vertical stacking or vertical accordions for ingredients. Horizontal OK for supplementary content. |
| Cross-Sells Placement | Interrupts reading about Product A | Place below product details, ABOVE reviews |

### 6.8 Sticky Elements
| Finding | Detail | Best Practice |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| Sticky ATC | Mobile pages are 4-10x longer than desktop | Sticky footer bar appears when main ATC scrolls out of view: product title + price + ATC button |

## 7. REVIEW & RATING SYSTEM

### 7.1 Review Histogram
- **Mandatory.** Display bar chart at top of review section.
- Bars must be tappable → filter by star rating
- **Baymard finding:** A single number is insufficient. Users need distribution — "safe" 4.5 (mostly 5s/4s) vs "controversial" 4.5 (many 5s and worrying 1s).

### 7.2 UGC Photo Strip
- Customer photo strip or grid immediately above text reviews
- Tapping one photo → **full-screen gallery overlay** with swipe through ALL customer photos across ALL reviews (not restricted to single reviewer's photos)
- **Baymard finding:** Customer photos = "unfiltered truth" to verify color, texture, size

### 7.3 Keyword Filter Chips
- Expose 5-8 keyword chips above reviews: "Taste," "Shipping," "Texture," "Result," "Smell"
- Tapping a chip filters reviews containing that keyword
- **Baymard finding:** Scrolling through 500 reviews is impossible on mobile. Users need "reviews from people like me."

### 7.4 Review Loading
- Use "Load More" button (15-30 reviews per batch)
- **NOT** pagination (forces new page load) or infinite scroll (position loss)
- **Baymard finding:** Load More preserves reading context while giving access to depth

### 7.5 Review Card Template
- **Row 1:** Stars + Bold Headline (review title)
- **Row 2:** Reviewer Attributes (name, location, verified buyer badge, "Time Owned")
- **Row 3:** Pros/Cons Bullets (if structured)
- **Row 4:** Body text
- **Verified Buyer badge mandatory** — users are skeptical of fake reviews
- "Time Owned" for long-term credibility products

### 7.6 Merchant Response
- Respond to 3-star and below reviews
- Shows brand cares and addresses concerns publicly

### 7.7 "Write a Review" Placement
- De-emphasize. Small link, not prominent button.
- **Baymard finding:** You sell to buyers, not recruit writers. Giant "Write a Review" distracts purchasers.

### 7.8 Specimen
- stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint — Amazon-style breakdown, image carousel with popover, formatted seamlessly

## 8. FAQ BEST PRACTICES

### 8.1 Curation
- **Top 5-7 product-specific questions ONLY**
- Curate from actual customer service data, reviews, support tickets
- **Baymard finding:** Users will not sift through 20 questions on mobile. Information overload.

### 8.2 No Marketing
- Keep questions strictly functional and defensive
- ❌ "Why is [product] the best choice?" → This is marketing, not FAQ
- ✅ "Does this contain caffeine?" / "Is the bottle glass or plastic?" / "Will this break my fast?"
- **Baymard finding:** Users identify marketing-disguised-as-FAQ immediately. Lowers credibility of entire section.

### 8.3 Accordion Format
- Collapsed accordions by default
- One open = others collapse (exclusive accordion)
- Smooth animated expand/collapse

### 8.4 Contextual Linking
- For complex answers (return policy, shipping details): give "headline answer" (1 sentence) + link to full policy page
- **Do NOT** write 3 paragraphs inside the accordion

## 9. Q&A SECTION

### 9.1 Search-First
- Prominent keyword search field at top: "Search questions..."
- Instant filtering as user types
- **Baymard finding:** Users look for their specific question, won't read 5 pages of unrelated queries

### 9.2 Brand vs Community Answers
- Clearly distinguish brand answers (official badge) from community answers (user badge)
- Brand answers for objective facts (safety, expiration)
- Community answers for subjective experience (taste, routine)
- **Baymard finding:** Confusion between brand/community answers damages trust

### 9.3 Zero-State Handling
- If 0 questions: **hide the section entirely** or replace with direct "Ask a Question" link to support
- **Do NOT** show empty Q&A board — creates negative social proof
- **Baymard finding:** Empty public board signals "nobody is interested in this product"

## 10. STICKY CTA & NAVIGATION

### 10.1 Sticky ATC Bar
- Appears when main ATC button scrolls out of view
- Compact: product title (truncated OK here) + price + "Add to Cart" button
- Slides up from bottom
- **Baymard stat:** Mobile pages are 4-10x longer than desktop. Don't make users scroll back up 10 screens to buy.

### 10.2 Promo Bar
- Feature key promotions: sale, special promotion, free shipping, etc.
- Persistent at top of page

### 10.3 Navigation
- Hamburger menu, logo, search, cart icon
- Standard e-commerce header pattern
