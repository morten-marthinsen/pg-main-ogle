# 1.2 — Testimonial Selection Planner

**Skill:** LP-10-social-proof-writer
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Map available testimonials from the proof inventory to assigned proof slots. Plan formatting decisions: which testimonials feature in which slots, what gets bolded, how attributions are structured. This is the selection and formatting strategy — NOT the actual formatting execution (that is 2.2).
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `proof-slot-analysis.md` (1.1), `proof-slots-loaded.md` (0.2), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `testimonial-plan.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  slots_needing_testimonials: "[from proof-slot-analysis.md — all testimonial_format slots]"
  proof_inventory: "[from proof-slots-loaded.md — available testimonials with content source data]"
  audience_profile: "[from brief-loaded.md — demographics, pain, desire]"
  page_type: "[from brief-loaded.md]"
  quality_requirements: "[per-slot quality specs from proof-architecture.json via proof-slots-loaded.md]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

1. **Inventory available testimonials.** For each testimonial in the proof inventory:
   - Does it contain a SPECIFIC outcome? (number, timeframe, measurable result)
   - What is the person's demographic/profile? (age, gender, location, title)
   - What problem did they solve?
   - How long/detailed is the testimonial?
   - Is there a photo available?
   - Is there video available?

2. **Score each testimonial for specificity** (1-10):
   - 1-3: Vague ("Great product!", "I love it", "Recommend to everyone")
   - 4-5: Partially specific ("It helped with my sleep", "I noticed a difference")
   - 6-7: Specific ("I sleep 2 hours more per night", "Lost 15 pounds")
   - 8-10: Highly specific ("I went from sleeping 4 hours to 7.5 hours in 3 weeks", "Lost 23 lbs in 8 weeks — down from 187 to 164")

3. **Map testimonials to slots** using these priority rules:
   - **Match audience segment:** Testimonials from people similar to the target audience go in early proof blocks (higher impact)
   - **Match problem/outcome:** Testimonials addressing the PRIMARY pain from page-brief.json go in primary proof blocks
   - **Match quality requirement:** LP-05's quality spec for each slot determines minimum testimonial quality
   - **Distribute variety:** Don't cluster similar testimonials. Spread diverse demographics, outcomes, and story angles across proof blocks
   - **Ascending specificity:** Save the most specific, most compelling testimonial for the final proof block (near close)

4. **Plan formatting for each testimonial:**
   - Attribution format: Name + [which qualifier(s): location, age, title, verified buyer, photo indicator]
   - Bold strategy: Which sentence/phrase to bold (the specific outcome statement)
   - Length treatment: Full text or pull-quote excerpt?
   - Photo/video indicator: Will the formatted testimonial include a visual?

5. **Handle `requires_sourcing` slots:**
   - Plan placeholder copy format
   - Write sourcing specification (what kind of testimonial is needed)
   - Flag with severity based on slot importance

6. **Handle video testimonials specifically:**
   - Plan pull-quote selection (most compelling sentence for text below video)
   - Plan CTA copy direction ("Watch [Name]'s Story")
   - Duration display format

7. **Write `testimonial-plan.md`.**

## Quality Criteria
- Every testimonial-type slot has an assigned testimonial (or `requires_sourcing` flag)
- No testimonial scoring < 6 on specificity is assigned to a slot without improvement notes
- Distribution includes variety — demographics, outcome types, and story angles
- Bold strategy identifies the single most conversion-relevant phrase per testimonial
- Video testimonials have pull-quote selections and CTA directions
- Sourcing specs are specific enough that someone could source the missing testimonial

## Failure Modes
- Fewer available testimonials than slots -> Assign best testimonials to highest-priority slots (early proof blocks, near-close blocks). Remaining slots get `requires_sourcing` with detailed specs.
- All testimonials are vague (score < 6) -> Flag as CRITICAL gap. Write improvement notes for each. Proceed with best available but note quality limitation in output.
- Testimonials all from same demographic -> Flag distribution gap. If no alternatives available, proceed but note the monotony risk.
- No video testimonials available for video_testimonial slots -> Convert to photo_testimonial or text_testimonial format. Update slot type in plan. Document the format change.
