# 3.2 — Specificity Audit

**Skill:** LP-10-social-proof-writer
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Audit every testimonial, before/after caption, expert endorsement, and volume signal for specificity. This is the single most important quality check in LP-10 — vague proof is worse than no proof because it signals fakeness. Every proof element must contain specific, measurable, verifiable claims.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `formatted-testimonials.md` (2.2), `before-after-captions.md` (2.4), `expert-endorsements.md` (2.5), `volume-signals.md` (2.7)
**Output:** `specificity-audit.md`

## Specificity Scoring Framework

### Testimonial Specificity (1-10 scale)

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1-2 | Completely vague — no outcome at all | "Great product!" / "I love it!" |
| 3-4 | Vague outcome — states direction but no measurement | "I feel better" / "It helped my sleep" |
| 5 | Partial specificity — general metric without detail | "I lost weight" / "My energy improved" |
| 6-7 | Specific — includes a number OR a timeframe | "I lost 15 pounds" / "Results in 3 weeks" |
| 8-9 | Highly specific — includes number AND timeframe | "I lost 23 lbs in 8 weeks" / "Handicap dropped 6 strokes in 4 months" |
| 10 | Maximum specificity — number, timeframe, and context | "I went from 187 to 164 lbs in 8 weeks after 4 years of trying every diet — Dr. verified" |

**Minimum threshold: 6/10 per testimonial.** Testimonials scoring below 6 must be flagged for improvement or replacement.

### Before/After Caption Specificity

Each caption MUST contain:
- A specific measurable result (number/metric) = required
- A timeframe = required
- A before state reference = required

**Missing any one of these three = FAIL for that caption.**

### Expert Endorsement Specificity

Each endorsement MUST contain:
- Full credential chain (degree + institution + role) = required
- Relevance statement (why THIS expert for THIS product) = required
- Specific endorsement claim (not just "recommends it") = required

**Missing any one of these three = FAIL for that endorsement.**

### Volume Signal Specificity

Each signal MUST contain:
- Exact number or properly flagged approximate (with "+") = required
- Audience-specific descriptor (not just "customers") = required

**Rounded numbers without "+" flag = FAIL. Generic descriptor = FAIL.**

---

## Execution Steps

1. **Audit every formatted testimonial:**
   - Read the bolded outcome sentence
   - Score specificity 1-10
   - Flag any scoring below 6

2. **Audit every before/after caption:**
   - Check for measurable result, timeframe, before state reference
   - Pass/Fail each caption

3. **Audit every expert endorsement:**
   - Check for credential chain, relevance statement, specific claim
   - Pass/Fail each endorsement

4. **Audit every volume signal:**
   - Check for exact numbers and audience-specific descriptors
   - Pass/Fail each signal

5. **Calculate audit results:**
   - Overall PASS requires: ALL testimonials >= 6, ALL captions pass, ALL endorsements pass, ALL signals pass
   - Any single failure = overall FAIL with revision requirements

## Quality Criteria
- Every proof element audited — zero skipped (including `requires_sourcing` placeholders, which should be noted as such, not scored)
- Specificity scores justified with the actual outcome text
- Failing elements include specific revision requirements (not just "make it more specific")
- Audit is exhaustive — not a sample check but a full audit of every element

## Failure Modes
- Auditing only a sample of testimonials ("checked 5 of 15") -> EVERY testimonial must be audited individually. No sampling.
- Scoring vague testimonials at 6 to pass them -> Score honestly. "I feel better" is 3-4, not 6. The scoring framework is explicit.
- Skipping `requires_sourcing` elements -> Note them as "placeholder — not scored" but still list them in the audit. They don't count against the score but must be visible.
- Passing endorsements without complete credential chains -> Credential chain is required. Missing institution = FAIL, even if name and degree are present.
- Accepting rounded numbers in volume signals -> Exact or "+" notation only. "Over 1,500" = FAIL.
