# 2.4 — Threading Validator

> **Skill:** LP-15-page-assembly
> **Layer:** 2
> **Position:** 4 of 4
> **Purpose:** Validate the threading chain from headline promise through mechanism to close. Confirm that the page tells ONE coherent story. Identify any breaks in the promise chain where the narrative thread is lost.
> **Effort Level:** opus
> **Dependencies:** `above-fold-assembly.md`, `body-section-assembly.md`, `conversion-section-assembly.md`
> **Output:** `threading-validation.md`

---

## INPUT

```yaml
input:
  above_fold: "above-fold-assembly.md"
  body_sections: "body-section-assembly.md"
  conversion_sections: "conversion-section-assembly.md"
  transition_strategy: "transition-strategy.md"
  page_type: "[type_a | type_b | hybrid]"
```

---

## EXECUTION

**POSITIONAL REINFORCEMENT:** You are validating, not fixing. You trace the promise chain from headline to close. You identify breaks. You score the threading. You do NOT rewrite sections to fix threading — that would require returning to upstream skills.

### Step 1: Identify the Headline Promise

From `above-fold-assembly.md`, extract the Arena-selected headline. State the promise in one sentence:
- What does this headline commit to delivering?
- To whom?
- How specifically?

Example: "The headline promises golfers they will find more fairways and lower their scores."

### Step 2: Trace the Promise Through the Page

Read through every section in order (above-fold -> body -> conversion) and track where the promise is:
- **Explicitly referenced** — the promise is restated or directly mentioned
- **Implicitly supported** — the section builds toward the promise without naming it
- **Absent** — the section does not reference the promise at all

Create a threading trace:

| # | Section | Promise Status | Evidence |
|---|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Above Fold | EXPLICIT | Headline states the promise directly |
| 2 | Lead/Hook | [EXPLICIT/IMPLICIT/ABSENT] | [quote or description] |
| 3 | Story | [EXPLICIT/IMPLICIT/ABSENT] | [quote or description] |
[...every section...]
| N | Close/CTA | [EXPLICIT/IMPLICIT/ABSENT] | [quote or description] |

### Step 3: Trace the Mechanism Connection

Identify the mechanism (from the mechanism/root cause sections in the body):
- What is the mechanism name?
- How does it explain WHY the promise can be kept?

Then trace whether the mechanism connects to the headline promise:
- Does the mechanism section reference the headline promise?
- Does the transition from mechanism to product/offer maintain the connection?
- Does the close reference the mechanism?

### Step 4: Trace the Proof Chain

Identify the proof blocks and their connection to the promise and mechanism:
- Do testimonials reference the specific outcome promised in the headline?
- Do scientific citations support the mechanism?
- Does the proof build toward the close?

### Step 5: Identify Threading Breaks

A threading break occurs when:
1. **Promise gap:** 3+ consecutive sections where the promise is ABSENT — the reader forgets what was promised
2. **Mechanism disconnect:** The mechanism section does not connect to the headline promise — the "why" and the "what" feel like separate stories
3. **Close orphan:** The close references a promise or mechanism not established in the page — the ending does not match the beginning
4. **New promise injection:** A section introduces a new promise that was not in the headline — the page suddenly talks about something different
5. **Transition thread loss:** A transition between sections drops the thread — the connective tissue changes the subject

For each break found:
- Identify the exact boundary where the break occurs
- Describe what the thread was before the break
- Describe what it became after the break
- Rate severity: HIGH (fundamentally broken) / MEDIUM (noticeable gap) / LOW (minor drift)

### Step 6: Score Threading

Score the overall threading on a 10-point scale:

| Score | Label | Criteria |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 9-10 | Excellent | Promise explicitly referenced in 80%+ of sections. Mechanism clearly connects to promise. Close directly callbacks to headline promise AND mechanism. Zero breaks. |
| 7-8 | Good | Promise referenced in 60%+ of sections. Mechanism connects to promise. Close references headline. 0-1 low-severity breaks. |
| 5-6 | Adequate | Promise referenced in 40%+ of sections. Some mechanism connection. Close has some callback. 1-2 medium-severity breaks. |
| 3-4 | Weak | Promise referenced in <40% of sections. Mechanism loosely connected. Close is generic. 2+ medium or 1+ high-severity breaks. |
| 1-2 | Broken | Promise disappears after headline. Mechanism unrelated to promise. Close is generic or introduces new promise. Multiple high-severity breaks. |

### Step 7: Write threading-validation.md

## QUALITY CRITERIA

- Every section traced for promise presence (no skipped sections)
- Promise statement clearly distilled from headline
- Mechanism identified and connection to promise evaluated
- All threading breaks identified with severity rating
- Score justified with evidence
- File exists after execution

---

## FAILURE MODES

- Scoring threading as "Excellent" on a page where the promise disappears for 3+ sections -> Re-read the trace. If the promise is ABSENT in 3+ consecutive sections, the score cannot be 9-10. Recalibrate.
- Confusing implicit and explicit promise references -> EXPLICIT = the promise or its specific language is directly stated. IMPLICIT = the section builds toward the promise conceptually. ABSENT = no connection. Do not inflate implicit to explicit.
- Ignoring the close -> The close is the most important threading checkpoint. If the close does not reference the headline promise, that is at least a MEDIUM-severity break regardless of how well-threaded the body is.
- Threading validation becomes a rewrite session -> You VALIDATE. You do not rewrite. If threading is broken, flag it. LP-17 will score it. Upstream skills fix it.
