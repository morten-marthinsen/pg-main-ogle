# 1.2 — Section Ordering

**Skill:** PDP-04-btf-section-sequencer
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Take the included sections from taxonomy selection (1.1) and order them according to the NLS Recommended PDP Flow. Apply the 6 ordering principles. The output is the sequenced section list that all downstream proportioning and density planning operates on. Critical: this ordering optimizes for hunt-and-peck behavior, NOT linear reading.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `taxonomy-selection.md` (1.1), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `section-ordering.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  included_sections: "[list of section_ids with INCLUDE decision from taxonomy-selection.md]"
  consolidation_decisions: "[any consolidation from taxonomy-selection.md]"
  ordering_reference: "[NLS Recommended PDP Flow from pdp-reference-loaded.md]"
  above_fold_proof: "[proof elements already in above-fold from above-fold-loaded.md]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Load the NLS Recommended PDP Flow from the reference data:
```
1. Product Highlights (TLDR) — immediate answers for scanners
2. UGC Video Carousel — authentic social proof early
3. The Expert — authority establishment
4. What's in It (Ingredients) — education zone entry
5. What to Expect (Benefits Timeline) — expectation setting
6. The Problem / The Solution / Why It Works — education consolidation
7. Comparison Chart — competitive positioning
8. Cost Savings — price objection handling
9. How It's Made — quality proof
10. How to Use — friction reduction
11. Reviews & Ratings — volume social proof
12. The Proof (Data) — evidence for skeptics
13. Identity Matching — self-selection
14. FAQ — final objection removal
```

**Step 2:** Filter the reference flow to only included sections. Remove any section not in the INCLUDE list from taxonomy-selection.md. Maintain relative ordering of remaining sections.

**Step 3:** Apply the 6 ordering principles as a validation check:
1. **Scannable sections FIRST** — Positions 1-3 must be from {product_highlights, ugc_video_carousel, the_expert, reviews_ratings}
2. **Authority sections EARLY** — At least one authority signal (Expert, UGC, review count) before first education section
3. **Education MIDDLE** — Problem/Solution/Why, Ingredients, Timeline in the middle zone
4. **Comparison + Savings AFTER education** — Only meaningful after product value is established
5. **Reviews + FAQ LATE** — Final decision support before checkout return
6. **FAQ ALWAYS in last 3 positions** — The closer for undecided visitors

**Step 4:** Handle consolidation decisions:
- If Problem/Solution/Why consolidation was decided in 1.1, place them as adjacent positions in the education zone (positions 5-8 typically)
- Maintain their internal order: Problem -> Solution -> Why It Works
- Document them as a "consolidation group" in the output

**Step 5:** Number the final sequence. Assign position numbers 1 through N.

**Step 6:** Validate the sequence against the 6 ordering principles. Flag any violations.

## Quality Criteria
- Every included section has exactly one position — no duplicates, no omissions
- Positions are sequential (1 through N) with no gaps
- All 6 ordering principles evaluated — not assumed
- FAQ is in last 3 positions
- Scannable sections occupy top 2-3 positions
- At least one authority section appears before first education section
- Consolidation groups documented if applicable

## Failure Modes
- Included section missing from ordered list -> HALT. Every INCLUDE section from 1.1 must appear in the sequence.
- FAQ not in last 3 positions -> Reorder. Move FAQ to position N, N-1, or N-2.
- No scannable section in top 3 positions -> Reorder. Move Product Highlights or UGC to position 1-2.
- No authority section before education -> Reorder. Move Expert, UGC, or Reviews earlier.
- Section appears twice -> HALT. Remove duplicate and verify taxonomy-selection.md.
- Ordering principle violations detected but not resolved -> Do NOT proceed to 1.3 with unresolved violations.
