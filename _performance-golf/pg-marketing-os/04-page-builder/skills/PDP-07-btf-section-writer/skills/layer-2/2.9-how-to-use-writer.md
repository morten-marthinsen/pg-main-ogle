# 2.9 — How to Use Writer

**Skill:** PDP-07-btf-section-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 9
**Purpose:** Write the How to Use (Instructions) section — a simple 3-step numbered format with icons that removes usage friction and makes the product feel easy. This section answers "How do I take this?" in the simplest possible way: dose, timing, method.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `per-section-briefs.md` (1.1 — how_to_use brief), `data-validation.md` (1.2 — GREEN or YELLOW), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3 — Section 5.12 template)
**Output:** `section-how-to-use.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  section_brief: "[how_to_use brief from per-section-briefs.md]"
  word_count_target: "[from PDP-04]"
  proof_density: "[from PDP-04 — none: instructional only]"
  pdp_reference: "[Section 5.12 template from pdp-reference-loaded.md]"
  dosage: "[from brief — amount per serving]"
  timing: "[from brief — when to take]"
  method: "[from brief — how to take (with water, mix, etc.)]"
  special_instructions: "[from brief — with food, contraindications, etc.]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read the How to Use template from PDP-BEST-PRACTICES-REFERENCE.md Section 5.12. Key pattern: 3-step numbered instructions with icons, dose + timing + method.

**Step 2:** Structure the 3 steps:

```
STANDARD 3-STEP FRAMEWORK:
  Step 1: DOSE — How much
    "Take 2 capsules" / "Mix 1 scoop" / "Apply 2 pumps"

  Step 2: TIMING — When
    "Every morning" / "30 minutes before bed" / "With breakfast"

  Step 3: METHOD — How
    "With 8oz of water" / "Blend into your smoothie" / "Apply to clean skin"
```

**Step 3:** Write each step:
- **Step Number:** Large numeral (1, 2, 3)
- **Step Label:** 2-4 word action label (e.g., "Take Two Capsules")
- **Step Detail:** 1 sentence of additional context if needed
  - Only if the step requires clarification
  - Keep under 15 words
- **Icon Direction:** Suggested icon for each step (pill icon, clock icon, water glass icon)

**Step 4:** Add special instructions (if any):
- "Best taken with food" / "Can be taken on an empty stomach"
- "For best results, use consistently for 30+ days"
- Place below the 3 steps as a note/tip, not as a 4th step

**Step 5:** Write section header:
- Not: "Usage Instructions" (clinical)
- Better: "How to Take [Product Name]" or "Simple as 1-2-3"

**Step 6:** Ensure simplicity:
- The entire section should be comprehensible in 3-5 seconds
- No paragraphs — only numbered steps with short labels
- Icons are visual-first; text is secondary

**Step 7:** Verify word count against target (+/-10%). This is typically one of the shortest sections.

**Step 8:** Write `section-how-to-use.md`.

```markdown
## Visual Direction
- Layout: 3 large numbered circles with icons, horizontal or vertical
- Step labels: large, bold, readable at a glance
- Icons: simple line icons, monochrome or brand color
- Optional: product-in-use photo below or beside steps
- Good specimen: kachava.com/products/shakes (thumb 7)

## Simplicity Check
- Entire section comprehensible in 3-5 seconds: [Y/N]
- Each step is 2-4 words (label only): [Y/N]
- No paragraphs: [Y/N]
- Word count within +/-10% of target: [Y/N]
```

---

## Quality Criteria
- Exactly 3 steps: dose, timing, method
- Each step has a 2-4 word action label
- Icon direction provided per step
- Special instructions separated from main steps (not a 4th step)
- Self-contained (no references to ingredient section or mechanism)
- Section comprehensible in 3-5 seconds
- Word count within +/-10% of PDP-04 target

## Failure Modes
- More than 3 steps -> Consolidate. If the product truly has 4+ steps, group related steps.
- Step labels are sentences instead of short labels -> Rewrite as 2-4 word actions.
- Missing dosage in brief -> Use "follow label directions" and flag as critical data gap.
- Section includes marketing copy ("Experience the difference...") -> Remove. This is purely instructional.
- Word count significantly under target -> Add optional tips section or product-in-use context. But do not pad.
