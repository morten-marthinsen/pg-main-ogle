# 1.1 — Per-Section Brief Extractor

**Skill:** PDP-07-btf-section-writer
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 1
**Purpose:** For each section in PDP-07's execution queue, extract the relevant product data, proof elements, and audience quotes from the brief and research that the section writer will need. This creates a focused "mini-brief" per section so Layer 2 writers have targeted input rather than the full brief.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `input-verification.md` (0.1), `section-sequence-loaded.md` (0.2), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `per-section-briefs.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  input_verification: "[path to input-verification.md]"
  section_sequence: "[path to section-sequence-loaded.md — PDP-07 execution queue]"
  pdp_reference: "[path to pdp-reference-loaded.md — section templates]"
  page_brief: "[path to original page-brief.json]"
  research_quotes: "[path to research quotes, or 'not available']"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Load the PDP-07 execution queue from `section-sequence-loaded.md`. This is the list of sections PDP-07 will write.

**Step 2:** Load the full brief data from `input-verification.md` and `page-brief.json`.

**Step 3:** For EACH section in the execution queue, extract the data it needs:

**Product Highlights (product_highlights):**
- Top 3-5 product questions/concerns from brief
- Key selling points (KSPs)
- Shipping and returns information
- Guarantee details
- Quick mechanism summary

**The Expert (the_expert):**
- Expert name, credentials, institution
- Expert photo/video availability
- Expert's role (formulator, endorser, advisor)
- Key credential details (degree, school, publications)
- Expert quote (if available from brief)

**What's in It (whats_in_it):**
- Complete ingredient list with dosages
- Per-ingredient benefits (for "X for Y" naming)
- Per-ingredient mechanism of action
- Per-ingredient study references (if available)
- Ingredient photography/visual references

**What to Expect (what_to_expect):**
- Timeline data from brief (when results appear)
- Per-phase mechanisms (what happens biologically)
- Per-phase felt benefits (what the user notices)
- Any clinical timeline data
- Any customer experience timeline quotes

**Problem/Solution/Logic (the_problem, the_solution, why_it_works):**
- Root cause of the problem
- Conventional approach and its failings
- Paradigm shift / new understanding
- Product's mechanism of action
- 4-step logical flow (problem -> ingredient -> mechanism -> benefit)
- Audience pain quotes (for agitation)

**Comparison Chart (comparison_chart):**
- Product's key differentiators (5-7 most important)
- Competitor category weaknesses (what most competitors lack)
- Product's strongest advantages (for primacy/recency positioning)

**Cost Savings (cost_savings):**
- Individual ingredient/component pricing
- Bundle pricing
- Savings calculation
- Price-per-serving or price-per-day math

**How It's Made (how_its_made):**
- Sourcing details (where ingredients come from)
- Manufacturing process
- Certifications (GMP, NSF, USDA, etc.)
- Testing protocols (third-party, purity, potency)
- "Made in [country]" claims

**How to Use (how_to_use):**
- Dosage instructions
- Timing recommendations
- Method of consumption
- Special instructions (with food, empty stomach, etc.)

**Identity Matching (identity_matching):**
- 3 primary customer personas from brief
- Per-persona demographics
- Per-persona psychographics
- Per-persona key benefit

**The Proof (the_proof):**
- Clinical study citations (journal, year, sample size, results)
- Survey data
- Before/after data
- Ingredient-level study references

**Step 4:** For each section, pull relevant audience quotes from research (if available):
- Pain quotes for Problem section
- Desire quotes for Timeline and Identity Matching
- Objection quotes for Product Highlights and Comparison
- Ingredient-specific interest quotes for What's in It

**Step 5:** Write `per-section-briefs.md` with one brief per section.

## Quality Criteria
- One brief per section in the execution queue — no sections missing
- Each brief contains the data specific to that section type
- Audience quotes mapped to relevant sections (not dumped generically)
- Data gaps explicitly flagged per section
- Ingredient list for What's in It includes individual dosages
- Expert credentials for The Expert include institution name
- Study citations for The Proof include journal, year, and sample size

## Failure Modes
- Execution queue section has zero applicable data from brief -> Flag as critical gap. Section cannot be written without at least minimal product data.
- Research quotes not available -> Proceed with brief data only. Note "No audience voice available" per section.
- Ingredient list missing for What's in It section -> Critical gap. Halt this section or escalate to human.
- No expert data for The Expert section -> Critical gap. Section cannot be written without credentials. Escalate.
- No study data for The Proof section -> Critical gap. This section should have been excluded by PDP-04. Flag mismatch.
