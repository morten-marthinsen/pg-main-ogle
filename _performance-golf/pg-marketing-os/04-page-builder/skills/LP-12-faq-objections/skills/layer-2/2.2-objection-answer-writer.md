# 2.2 — Objection Answer Writer (Core Objections)

**Skill:** LP-12-faq-objections
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Write the Q&A pairs for the core purchase objections — price/value, efficacy/results, trust/guarantee, timing, and comparison. These are the highest-stakes answers in the FAQ section. Each answer must directly address the surface question, resolve the hidden objection, and end with a confidence builder. This is the single most important microskill in LP-12.
**Effort Level:** max
**Dependencies:** `answer-strategies.md` (1.3), `sequence-plan.md` (1.2), `objection-classification.md` (1.1), `packages-loaded.md` (0.1), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `core-objection-answers.md`

## Pre-Writing Protocol (Execute Before Writing Any Answer)

**Step 1: Load voice calibration**
Hold the voice direction from answer-strategies.md in active context. Every answer must match this voice. Before writing the first answer, state: "The voice for these FAQ answers is: [voice description]."

**Step 2: Review anti-slop word list**
Before writing, review the 5-category forbidden word list from LP-12-AGENT.md. Commit to zero instances.

**Step 3: Review the 3-part answer structure**
Every answer follows: Direct Answer (sentence 1) -> Supporting Detail (sentences 2-3) -> Confidence Builder (sentence 4). This is non-negotiable.

**Step 4: State what you will NOT do**
Before writing the first answer, state:
- "I will NOT open any answer with a sycophantic opener."
- "I will NOT write generic template answers."
- "I will NOT use hedge language as the primary answer."
- "I will address the hidden objection, not just the surface question."

## Writing Execution

For each core objection (price/value, efficacy, trust, timing, comparison), write the Q&A pair using the assigned strategy:

### Price/Value Objections

**Common questions:** "Why is this more expensive than [alternative]?" "Is it worth the money?" "Are there any discounts?" "Why should I pay $X for this?"

**Answer approach:**
- NEVER open with the price. Open with what makes the value proposition unique.
- Reframe from absolute cost to relative value (per serving, per day, vs alternative, vs cost of the problem)
- Include specific math where possible ($X/day, $X/serving, saves $Y vs buying Z separately)
- End with a guarantee reference to remove residual risk

**Example template (adapt to specific product):**
```
Q: "[specific price/value question for this product]"

A: "[Product Name] delivers [specific differentiator] that [cheaper alternatives] simply don't include.
[Specific comparison: per-serving math, ingredient quality, or measurable superiority data].
[Reframe: cost of NOT solving the problem, or cost of continuing with the inferior alternative].
[Confidence close: guarantee reference or value-per-result statement]."
```

### Efficacy/Results Objections

**Common questions:** "Does this actually work?" "How fast will I see results?" "What kind of results can I expect?" "How do I know this isn't just placebo?"

**Answer approach:**
- ALWAYS include proof. Review data, clinical data, specific customer results, named authority endorsements.
- Never answer "Does it work?" with "Results may vary" as the first sentence. The first sentence must be a direct, confident statement.
- Timeframe questions should include realistic ranges, not just "results may vary"
- If clinical data exists, cite it specifically (study size, results, publication)

**Example template:**
```
Q: "[specific efficacy question for this product]"

A: "[Direct answer — yes, with a specific proof anchor: X% of users reported Y, or: Z study found...]
[Supporting detail: mechanism of action, why it works, what makes it different from what they've tried before].
[Realistic timeframe or expectation-setting with specifics, not vague hedging].
[Confidence close: review count, named expert endorsement, or 'and if it doesn't work for you, [guarantee]']."
```

### Trust/Guarantee Objections

**Common questions:** "What if I don't like it?" "What's your return policy?" "Is this a legitimate company?" "How do I know I won't get scammed?"

**Answer approach:**
- State the SPECIFIC guarantee terms. Duration, conditions, process. No vague "we stand behind our products."
- If the guarantee is strong (365-day, no questions asked), lead with it — it IS the answer.
- If the company has credibility markers (years in business, customer count, certifications), include them.
- The hidden objection is always "I need an exit before I can enter." Make the exit clear and easy.

**Example template:**
```
Q: "[specific trust/guarantee question for this product]"

A: "[Specific guarantee statement: You have X days to try it — if you're not satisfied, [exact refund process]].
[Supporting detail: customer count, years in business, certifications, or review count].
[Process clarity: exactly what they need to do to get a refund — email X, click Y, etc.].
[Confidence close: 'We've had X customers and [positive stat about satisfaction/returns]']."
```

### Timing Objections

**Common questions:** "Do I need this now?" "Can I wait and buy later?" "Is there a better time to start?"

**Answer approach:**
- Reframe "waiting" as "cost of not acting." What does the reader lose by delaying?
- If there is a legitimate time-sensitive element (price increase, limited supply, seasonal relevance), state it specifically.
- If there is no urgency, be honest — but reframe: "There is no deadline, but [cost of continuing to have the problem]."
- Do NOT manufacture false urgency.

### Comparison Objections

**Common questions:** "How is this different from [competitor]?" "Why not just use [alternative]?" "What makes this better than what I'm already doing?"

**Answer approach:**
- Name the comparison directly (or the category of comparison: "other magnesium supplements," "DIY approaches," "doing nothing")
- State the specific differentiator. Not "we're better" but "we include X which they don't, and here's why that matters"
- If possible, include comparison data (more forms, higher dose, better absorption, more reviews)
- End with a frame that makes the comparison favor this product without attacking the alternative

## Quality Criteria
- Every answer follows the 3-part structure: Direct Answer -> Supporting Detail -> Confidence Builder
- Zero sycophantic openers (no "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "That's a common concern!")
- Every answer is product-specific (product name mentioned, specific data used)
- At least 2 core objection answers include proof/data (ideally all 3 mandatory-category answers)
- Hidden objection is addressed, not just the surface question
- 2-4 sentences per answer (strict)
- Voice matches page voice direction

## Failure Modes
- Opening with "Great question!" or "That's a common concern!" -> Delete the opener. Start with the direct answer.
- Answering "Does it work?" with "Results may vary" -> This is a hedge, not an answer. Lead with proof ("X% of users reported Y" or "In a study of Z participants..."), then add the disclaimer.
- Price answer that only states the price -> "$97" is information, not persuasion. Reframe the value. Why is it WORTH $97? Cost per day, comparison to alternatives, cost of the problem.
- Guarantee answer that says "We stand behind our products" -> Specifics: "365-day money-back guarantee. Email support@company.com. Full refund, no questions asked." That is a real answer.
- Writing 6+ sentences per answer -> Cut. If the answer is that complex, the question should be split into two questions.
