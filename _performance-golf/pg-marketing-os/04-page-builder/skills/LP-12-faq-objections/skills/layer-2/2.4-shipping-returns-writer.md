# 2.4 — Shipping/Returns Writer (Logistics FAQ)

**Skill:** LP-12-faq-objections
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Write the Q&A pairs for logistics objections: shipping, returns, refund process, subscription management, delivery, and access. These are the "last mile" questions — a prospect who asks "How fast does it ship?" is close to buying. The hidden objection is almost never actually about shipping speed — it is about the risk and hassle of the transaction.
**Effort Level:** low
**Dependencies:** `answer-strategies.md` (1.3), `sequence-plan.md` (1.2), `packages-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `logistics-faq-answers.md`

## Execution Logic

1. **Identify the relevant logistics question set** based on product type:

   **Physical Product / Supplement:**
   - "How fast does it ship?" (Hidden: "Will I have to wait a long time?")
   - "Is shipping free?" (Hidden: "Are there hidden costs?")
   - "Do you ship internationally?" (Hidden: "Can I even get this?")
   - "What's your return policy?" (Hidden: "What if I'm stuck with something I don't want?")
   - "How do I cancel my subscription?" (Hidden: "Am I going to get trapped?")
   - "Can I pause/skip a delivery?" (Hidden: "Do I have control over this?")

   **Info Product / Course / Service:**
   - "How do I access the content?" (Hidden: "Is this going to be complicated?")
   - "How long do I have access?" (Hidden: "Will it disappear before I finish?")
   - "Can I download the materials?" (Hidden: "Do I actually own this?")
   - "What if I need a refund?" (Hidden: "What's my exit if this isn't for me?")
   - "Is there a community or support?" (Hidden: "Will I be alone trying to figure this out?")

2. **Write each logistics Q&A pair:**

   **Answer structure for logistics:**
   - Sentence 1: Direct factual answer (the specific fact they need)
   - Sentence 2: Process clarity (exactly what happens / what they need to do)
   - Sentence 3: Hidden objection resolution (address the real concern underneath)

   Logistics answers are the most concise in the FAQ — 2-3 sentences maximum. The reader wants quick, clear facts.

3. **Always address the hidden objection:**

   | Surface Question | Wrong Answer | Right Answer |
   |-----------------|-------------|--------------|
   | "How fast does it ship?" | "3-5 business days" | "Orders ship within 24 hours and arrive in 3-5 business days. You'll receive a tracking number the moment it leaves our facility." |
   | "Can I cancel anytime?" | "Yes" | "Yes — cancel with one click in your account dashboard, or email support@company.com. No hoops, no phone calls, no retention team." |
   | "What's the return policy?" | "30-day return policy" | "You have 365 days to try it risk-free. If you're not satisfied, email us and we'll issue a full refund — even on opened bottles. No interrogation." |

   The difference: the right answer addresses the hidden concern (speed anxiety, trapped feeling, hassle fear) — not just the surface fact.

4. **Handle unknown logistics data:**
   - If shipping speed, return policy, or other logistics data is not available from upstream packages, write the answer structure with a clear NEEDS_HUMAN_INPUT marker
   - Do NOT invent logistics policies (these are factual commitments)
   - Common fallback: "Contact support@[company].com for shipping details" is acceptable only as a last resort

5. **Subscription-specific FAQ (if applicable):**
   - If the product has a subscription model, at least 1 logistics FAQ must address cancellation
   - The cancellation FAQ must state the EXACT process (not "contact us")
   - If pause/skip is available, mention it (this reduces cancel anxiety)

6. **Per-answer quality gate:**
   - Direct factual answer in first sentence
   - Hidden objection addressed (not just the fact)
   - 2-3 sentences (logistics answers are the shortest)
   - No corporate deflection ("Please contact customer service for details")
   - Zero AI telltales

## Quality Criteria
- Every answer addresses the hidden objection, not just the surface fact
- Cancellation/refund processes are specific (exact steps, not "contact us")
- Shipping answers include timeline AND tracking mention (reduces waiting anxiety)
- Return/refund answers state duration, conditions, AND ease of process
- No corporate deflection ("Please contact customer service" as the entire answer)
- 2-3 sentences per answer (logistics answers are concise)

## Failure Modes
- Answering "Can I cancel?" with just "Yes" -> State the process. "Yes — one click in your account, or email support. No retention team, no questions." The hidden objection is about being TRAPPED, not about policy existence.
- Answering shipping with just a timeframe -> "3-5 days" answers the surface question. Add tracking info and ship-by speed to address the hidden concern about waiting and uncertainty.
- Using "Please contact support" as the primary answer -> This is a deflection. If the information is not available, mark as NEEDS_HUMAN_INPUT and write the answer structure. Do not pass the reader to another channel as your answer.
- Inventing shipping/return policies -> These are factual commitments. If the data is not in upstream packages, mark as NEEDS_HUMAN_INPUT. Do not make up a "365-day guarantee" that does not exist.
- Writing 5+ sentence logistics answers -> Logistics answers should be the shortest in the FAQ. 2-3 sentences. If the answer needs more, the logistics are too complex for FAQ format (point to a dedicated shipping/returns page).
