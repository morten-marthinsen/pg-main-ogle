# 2.3 — Price Deadline Writer

**Skill:** LP-13-urgency-scarcity
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 3
**Purpose:** Write price-based urgency copy — introductory pricing explanations, scheduled price increase announcements, early-bird discount framing, and price change justifications. Every price claim must reference a real pricing schedule with specific current price, future price, and change date. This microskill ONLY executes when Type 3 (Price-Based) is selected in Layer 1.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `justification-plan.md` (1.2), `placement-strategy.md` (1.3), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `urgency-types-selected.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `price-deadline-copy.md`

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Read authorized claims.** Verify the specific price claims Layer 2 is authorized to make.

**Step 2: Verify price data completeness.** Before writing, confirm ALL of:
- Current price (specific dollar amount)
- Future price (specific dollar amount)
- Price change date (specific date)
- Reason for price increase

If ANY of these are missing, write a placeholder framework with [AMOUNT] or [DATE] markers and flag for client input. Do not fabricate pricing data.

**Step 3: Calculate savings.** Future price minus current price = savings amount. This is the most concrete urgency number available — use it.

**Step 4: Read specimens.** Extract technique: How do effective price deadline pages present the price change? Do they lead with the savings or the deadline?

---

## Execution Logic

### For Type A Pages

1. **Write the price urgency section copy** (50-200 words):

   Structure:
   - Sentence 1: State the current price clearly
   - Sentence 2: State when and how much the price increases
   - Sentence 3: Explain WHY the price is increasing (real reason)
   - Sentence 4: Frame the current price as the smart decision
   - Sentence 5 (optional): Anti-manipulation disclaimer if appropriate for voice

   Example:
   > "Right now, you can get [Product] for $67. Starting Monday, March 10th, the price goes to $97 — permanently. This isn't a fake deadline. We priced the launch at $67 to get the first 1,000 customers in the door and generate proof. Once we hit that number, the price reflects the actual value of what's inside. At $67, you're saving $30 — and you're getting the exact same product that will cost $97 next week."

2. **Write the price comparison element** (for value stacking):
   ```
   Regular Price: $[future_price]
   Your Price Today: $[current_price]
   You Save: $[savings_amount]
   ```

3. **Write the P.S. price callback** (1-2 sentences):
   - Restate savings amount and deadline in compressed form
   - Example: "P.S. The $67 launch price ends Monday. After that, it's $97. Same product, $30 more."

4. **Write the close section price line** (1 sentence):
   - Example: "Lock in the $67 launch price before it increases to $97 on Monday."

### For Type B Pages

5. **Write the price deadline badge** (3-8 words):
   - For product card or price display area
   - Patterns:
     - "Launch Price -- Ends [Day]"
     - "Save $[amount] -- Offer Ends [Date]"
     - "Introductory Rate -- $[current_price]"
     - "$[savings_amount] Off -- [Timeframe] Only"

6. **Write the price context line** (1 sentence):
   - Appears near the price display
   - States the future price and when the change happens
   - Example: "After [Date], this price returns to $[future_price]."

7. **Write the strikethrough price context** (if applicable):
   - For visual price anchoring
   - Original price: ~~$[future_price]~~ -> Your price: $[current_price]
   - Supporting line: "Launch week savings of $[amount]"

## Quality Criteria
- Both current AND future price stated explicitly (not just "price going up")
- Specific date for price change named (not "soon" or "shortly")
- Reason for price increase explained (real business reason, not "because we feel like it")
- Savings amount calculated and presented as concrete dollar value
- All copy within authorized claims
- Voice direction matched -- price urgency is factual, not panicked
- Zero forbidden words

## Failure Modes
- "Price going up soon" without specific date and amount -> Forbidden. Must state exact date, exact current price, exact future price.
- Writing price urgency for a product with no planned price increase -> This is fabricated urgency. If no price change is planned, Type 3 is the wrong urgency type. Should have been caught in Layer 1.
- Savings amount not stated -> The savings is the most tangible urgency element. Always calculate and state it.
- Reason for price increase sounds fabricated ("prices always go up") -> Must be a specific business reason: launch period ending, demand-based pricing, cost increase, introductory offer window closing.
- Using "was $X, now $Y" format when there was never a "was" price -> Strikethrough pricing requires that the higher price is or will be the actual price. Inflated "was" prices are deceptive.
