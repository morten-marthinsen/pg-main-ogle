# 2.4 — Availability Window Writer

**Skill:** LP-13-urgency-scarcity
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Write availability/capacity urgency copy — limited spots, cohort-based enrollment, waitlist framing, and capacity justifications. Every capacity claim must explain WHY capacity is limited (coaching ratio, platform limits, personalization requirements). This microskill ONLY executes when Type 4 (Availability Window) is selected in Layer 1.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `justification-plan.md` (1.2), `placement-strategy.md` (1.3), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `urgency-types-selected.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `availability-copy.md`

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Read authorized claims.** Verify the specific capacity/availability claims Layer 2 is authorized to make.

**Step 2: Verify capacity data.** Before writing, confirm you have:
- Total capacity number (specific)
- Reason for capacity limit (real constraint)
- Next availability window (specific date/period)

If total capacity or reason is missing, write a placeholder framework and flag for client input. "Limited spots" without a number or reason is not acceptable.

**Step 3: Read specimens.** Extract technique from enrollment/capacity urgency specimens: How did they frame the capacity limit as a BENEFIT (quality guarantee) rather than an exclusion tactic?

---

## Execution Logic

### For Type A Pages

1. **Write the availability justification paragraph** (50-150 words):

   Structure:
   - Sentence 1: State the capacity limit and what it applies to (cohort, membership, program)
   - Sentence 2: Explain WHY capacity is limited (the real operational constraint)
   - Sentence 3: Frame the constraint as a quality commitment
   - Sentence 4: State what happens when capacity is reached (waitlist, next cohort date)

   Example:
   > "We accept a maximum of 200 students per cohort. This isn't an artificial limit — every participant gets direct feedback from our 4-person coaching team, and we've found that going beyond 200 degrades the quality of that feedback significantly. When this cohort fills, we close enrollment. The next cohort begins in March. If you want in now, the spots are filling."

2. **Write the enrollment status statement** (1-2 sentences):
   - State current enrollment or spots remaining (if data available)
   - Example: "As of today, 127 of 200 spots are filled."
   - If specific enrollment number is not available, write a general capacity statement instead

3. **Write the waitlist framing** (if applicable, 1-3 sentences):
   - Explain the waitlist process
   - State the expected wait time
   - Example: "If you're reading this and the cohort is full, you can join the waitlist. We'll notify you as soon as the next cohort opens — typically 8-12 weeks."

4. **Write the P.S. availability callback** (1-2 sentences):
   - Compressed restatement of capacity and next opening
   - Example: "This cohort has 200 spots. Once they're filled, the next opening is in March."

5. **Write the close section availability line** (1 sentence):
   - For integration into final CTA area
   - Example: "Secure your spot in this cohort before enrollment closes."

### For Type B Pages (rare for this type, but included for completeness)

6. **Write the availability indicator** (5-15 words):
   - For product card placement
   - Example: "Limited to 200 per cohort — [X] spots remaining"
   - Example: "Enrollment open — closes [Date] or when 200 spots fill"

7. **Write the availability context sentence** (1 sentence):
   - Explains the capacity limit briefly
   - Example: "Class size limited to 200 students for direct coaching access."

## Quality Criteria
- Specific capacity number stated (not "limited spots" or "filling fast")
- Capacity reason explained (operational constraint, not marketing tactic)
- Capacity framed as quality commitment, not exclusion
- Next availability window stated (what happens when this cohort/window closes)
- All copy within authorized claims from justification-plan.md
- Voice direction matched
- Zero forbidden words

## Failure Modes
- "Limited spots available" without stating how many -> Forbidden. Must state specific capacity number.
- Capacity limit with no explanation of why -> "200 spots" alone is not compelling. "200 spots because each student gets direct coaching feedback" is compelling and justified.
- Using availability urgency for a product with no real capacity constraint -> If capacity is unlimited, Type 4 is the wrong urgency type. Digital products with no coaching/feedback component rarely have genuine capacity limits.
- Fabricating enrollment numbers -> If current enrollment data is not available, state the capacity without claiming specific enrollment. "200 spots per cohort" is honest. "Only 12 spots left!" without data is fabricated.
- Framing the capacity limit as exclusionary rather than quality-focused -> "We only let the best in" feels elitist and dishonest. "We limit each cohort to ensure everyone gets direct feedback" feels like a quality commitment.
