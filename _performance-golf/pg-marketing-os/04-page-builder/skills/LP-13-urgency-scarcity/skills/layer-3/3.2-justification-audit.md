# 3.2 — Justification Audit

**Skill:** LP-13-urgency-scarcity
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Trace every urgency claim in the final copy back to a real constraint documented in the brief or upstream packages. This is the fabrication detection gate. Zero fabricated claims is the threshold — a single fabricated claim means the audit FAILS and the claim must be removed entirely (not justified after the fact).
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** All Layer 2 output files, `justification-plan.md` (1.2), `urgency-data.md` (0.2), `packages-loaded.md` (0.1), `urgency-audit.md` (3.1)
**Output:** `justification-audit.md`

---

## Execution Logic

1. **Extract every urgency claim from the copy.** Read through every urgency copy element and identify each distinct claim. A "claim" is any statement that creates urgency or scarcity:
   - Deadline claims: "Price increases on [date]"
   - Stock claims: "Only [number] left"
   - Capacity claims: "[Number] spots per cohort"
   - Price claims: "Current price $[X], future price $[Y]"
   - Consequence claims: "Every day of [problem] costs you [specific amount]"
   - Bonus claims: "Bonuses expire on [date]"

   List every claim with its exact text and location (which copy element it appears in).

2. **For each claim, trace to source:**

   **Level 1 -- Justification Plan Check:**
   - Is this claim in justification-plan.md's authorized claims list?
   - If YES: Note the authorized claim it matches
   - If NO: Flag as POTENTIALLY UNAUTHORIZED

   **Level 2 -- Data Source Check:**
   - For authorized claims: verify the justification-plan.md source is real
   - Trace to: page-brief.json field, offer-cta-architecture.json field, or client input
   - Document the specific field/source

   **Level 3 -- "Why?" x3 Test (for any claim not already tested in 3.1):**
   - Why is this urgent/scarce? [Specific answer]
   - Why does that constraint exist? [Specific answer]
   - Why can't the constraint be removed? [Specific answer]
   - If the third "Why?" has no answer: flag as UNVERIFIABLE

3. **Classify each claim:**

   | Classification | Definition | Action |
   |---------------|-----------|--------|
   | **JUSTIFIED** | Claim traced to documented data source, survives "Why?" x3 | Proceed |
   | **UNVERIFIABLE** | Claim stated in brief but not independently confirmable | Flag for client confirmation. Do not remove. |
   | **FABRICATED** | Claim has no source in any upstream document | REMOVE immediately. Do not attempt to justify. |
   | **UNAUTHORIZED** | Claim exists but was not in justification-plan.md authorized list | Remove OR add to justification plan with proper documentation |

4. **Check for implicit urgency** — urgency that is implied through word choice even if not an explicit claim:
   - "before they're gone" (implies scarcity without stating a number)
   - "you don't want to wait" (implies urgency without stating a deadline)
   - "spots are filling" (implies capacity without stating a number)
   - These implicit urgency phrases need the same justification as explicit claims

5. **Produce the audit result:**
   - PASS: Zero FABRICATED claims. All claims are JUSTIFIED or UNVERIFIABLE (flagged for client).
   - FAIL: One or more FABRICATED claims exist.

6. **If FAIL:** List every fabricated claim with specific instruction: REMOVE (not revise — fabricated claims cannot be rescued by rewriting).

## Quality Criteria
- EVERY urgency claim in the copy is audited (explicit AND implicit)
- Each claim traced through all 3 levels (plan check, data source, "Why?" x3)
- Classification is honest -- FABRICATED is not downgraded to UNVERIFIABLE to avoid removing it
- Implicit urgency phrases caught and audited (not just explicit claims)
- PASS requires ZERO fabricated claims (not "mostly justified")
- Fabricated claims are marked for REMOVAL, not revision

## Failure Modes
- Classifying a fabricated claim as UNVERIFIABLE to pass the audit -> FABRICATED = no source anywhere. UNVERIFIABLE = source exists (brief/client statement) but cannot be independently confirmed. These are different. Do not conflate them.
- Missing implicit urgency phrases -> "Spots are filling" is an implicit capacity claim that requires justification just as "127 of 200 spots filled" does. Scan for implied urgency, not just explicit numbers.
- Attempting to justify a fabricated claim after finding it ("well, it COULD be true because...") -> If the data does not exist in upstream documents, the claim is fabricated. Post-hoc rationalization is exactly what this audit prevents.
- Treating UNAUTHORIZED claims as acceptable ("the copy is better with this claim") -> If a claim was not pre-authorized in justification-plan.md, it was not vetted. Either add it to the plan with proper documentation OR remove it. Do not leave unvetted claims in the copy.
- Passing the audit with FABRICATED claims because the urgency score from 3.1 was high -> The justification audit is INDEPENDENT of the urgency audit score. A score of 10.0 with one fabricated claim = justification audit FAIL. Both must pass.
