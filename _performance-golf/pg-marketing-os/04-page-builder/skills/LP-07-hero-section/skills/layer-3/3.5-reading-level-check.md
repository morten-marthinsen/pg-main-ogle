# 3.5 — Reading Level Check

**Skill:** LP-07-hero-section
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 5
**Purpose:** Verify that the lead copy (Type A) or above-fold copy (Type B) meets the grade 5-7 target reading level and does not exceed grade 8. High reading levels cause bounce — especially in DR markets where the audience skims first, reads second. This is a mechanical check, not a creative judgment.
**Effort Level:** low
**Dependencies:** `lead-final.md` (3.1) OR `typeb-abovefold.md` (2.5)
**Output:** `reading-level.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  copy_to_check: "[full text of lead-final.md or typeb-abovefold.md]"
  page_type: "[type_a | type_b | hybrid]"
  copy_element: "[lead | above-fold-copy]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

1. **Identify all sentences** in the copy. A sentence ends at a period, question mark, exclamation point, or ellipsis followed by a capital letter.

2. **Calculate average sentence length** (words per sentence):
   - Target: 12-18 words per sentence average
   - Flag if average > 20 words per sentence

3. **Count multi-syllable words** (3+ syllables):
   - Calculate percentage of total words that are 3+ syllables
   - Target: < 15% multi-syllable words
   - Flag if > 20% multi-syllable words

4. **Check for passive voice constructions:**
   - Identify all passive constructions (was/were/been/being + past participle)
   - Target: < 10% of sentences use passive voice
   - Flag if > 15% passive

5. **Estimate Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level:**
   ```
   Grade = 0.39 × (total words / total sentences) + 11.8 × (total syllables / total words) - 15.59
   ```
   - Target: Grade 5-7
   - Acceptable: Grade 8 or below
   - FAIL: Grade 9 or above

6. **If grade > 8:** Identify the specific sentences causing the elevation:
   - List every sentence with > 25 words
   - List every sentence with 2+ words of 4+ syllables
   - List every passive voice construction
   - For each flagged sentence: provide a simplified alternative

7. **Check headline and deck separately** (these should be even simpler):
   - Headline: Should be grade 4-6 equivalent
   - Deck: Should be grade 5-7 equivalent
   - Flag any headline above grade 7

---

## Quality Criteria
- All three metrics checked (sentence length, syllable count, passive voice)
- Grade level estimated with formula, not guessed
- If FAIL: every problematic sentence identified with a specific simplified alternative
- Headline and deck checked separately from the lead body
- Result is binary PASS/FAIL with the grade level stated

## Failure Modes
- Grade estimated as "probably around 7" without calculation → Run the formula. Estimation is not checking.
- Grade 9 reported but no specific sentences flagged → Every FAIL must identify which sentences push the grade above 8 and provide alternatives.
- Simplified alternatives change the meaning or close a loop → Simplification must preserve meaning and open loops. Shorter sentences, simpler words, same information architecture.
- Passive voice counted but not located → Quote each passive construction so revision knows exactly what to fix.
