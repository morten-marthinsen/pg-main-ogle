## TEACHING SOURCES

### 1. David Ogilvy — Headline Fundamentals

**Core Principles:**
- On average, five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy
- When you have written your headline, you have spent eighty cents out of your dollar
- Headlines that promise a benefit outsell those that don't by 4:1
- The headline is the "ticket on the meat" — it must flag down the RIGHT prospects
- Never use tricky or irrelevant headlines — cleverness without clarity is waste
- Include the brand or product name in the headline when possible
- News headlines outperform all others when news is genuine

**Application to Hero Section:**
- The hero headline is the ecom equivalent of Ogilvy's "ticket on the meat"
- Every hero headline must contain a benefit (explicit or implied)
- Specificity in the headline (numbers, measurements, timeframes) increases scroll-down rate
- On ecom, the headline must flag down the RIGHT shoppers — not everyone
- Clever-but-unclear headlines are the #1 ecom hero failure mode

**Cross-References:** `2.1-headline-subhead-generator.md` (generation), `4.1-scroll-stop-validator.md` (validation)

### 2. Eugene Schwartz — Sophistication Matching

**Core Principles:**
- Market sophistication determines headline approach — there is no "best" headline formula
- **Level 1 (Unaware):** Lead with the problem or story, not the product
- **Level 2 (Problem-Aware):** Lead with the problem stated in their words, then pivot to solution
- **Level 3 (Solution-Aware):** Lead with the mechanism or unique approach — they already know solutions exist
- **Level 4 (Product-Aware):** Lead with differentiators, proof, or a new angle on the known product
- **Level 5 (Most Aware):** Lead with the deal — price, offer, availability
- The wrong sophistication level in the headline means the RIGHT audience scrolls past

**Application to Hero Section:**
- PDP-01 strategy identifies market sophistication level — hero headline MUST match
- Most ecom products are Level 3-4 (shoppers know solutions exist, they're comparing)
- At Level 3: "The [Named Mechanism] that [Specific Result]" — show HOW it's different
- At Level 4: "[Specific Proof Point] + [Differentiator]" — show WHY it's better
- At Level 5: "[Offer] + [Urgency]" — get out of the way and sell

**Cross-References:** `1.1-headline-formula-selector.md` (formula matching), `2.1-headline-subhead-generator.md`

### 3. Scroll-Stop Psychology (E-Comm Specific)

**Core Principles:**
- **The 3-Second Rule:** Ecom visitors decide to scroll or leave in ~3 seconds. The hero must work at scan speed.
- **Pattern Interrupt:** The headline must violate the visitor's expectation — something they haven't seen on every other product page
- **Visual-Copy Integration:** On ecom, the hero headline competes with the product image for attention. Copy must complement, not compete with, the visual.
- **Mobile-First Scan:** 60-80% of ecom traffic is mobile. Hero copy must work at phone viewport width.
- **Above-Fold Economy:** Every word ATF costs screen real estate. Word budget is not a guideline — it's a physical constraint.

**Application to Hero Section:**
- Test every headline against the 3-second rule: can a stranger understand the promise in 3 seconds?
- Pattern interrupt for ecom = saying something the CATEGORY doesn't usually say
- If the product image is strong (e.g., golf club), headline should focus on RESULT not PRODUCT
- If the product image is weak (e.g., supplement bottle), headline should focus on MECHANISM or BENEFIT
- Mobile viewport = headline is 2-4 lines on phone. More than 4 lines = invisible subhead

**Cross-References:** `4.1-scroll-stop-validator.md`, `2.2-hero-section-generator.md`

### 4. Value Proposition Frameworks

**Core Principles:**
- A value proposition answers one question: "Why should I buy THIS from YOU instead of THAT from THEM?"
- **The One-Sentence Test:** If you can't state the value prop in one sentence, you don't have one
- **Unique Value = [Desired Outcome] + [Unique Mechanism] + [Proof Element]**
- Value prop is NOT a feature list — it's a PROMISE backed by a REASON
- On ecom, the value prop appears as subhead or first line below the headline

**Value Proposition Structures:**

| Structure | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Outcome + Mechanism | "[Result] through [Named Process]" | "23 more yards through Dynamic Loft Control" |
| Problem + Solution | "Stop [pain]. Start [benefit]." | "Stop slicing. Start splitting fairways." |
| Proof + Promise | "[Evidence] proves [claim]" | "87% of golfers gained distance in 30 days" |
| Comparison + Edge | "Unlike [alternative], [product] does [unique thing]" | "Unlike fixed-loft drivers, the SF2 adjusts mid-swing" |

**Application to Hero Section:**
- Value prop goes in the subhead or the first line below the headline
- Match value prop structure to market sophistication:
  - Level 3: Outcome + Mechanism (show the HOW)
  - Level 4: Comparison + Edge (show the DIFFERENCE)
  - Level 5: Proof + Promise (show the EVIDENCE)
- Word budget: 15-30 words. Every word must earn its place.

**Cross-References:** `2.2-hero-section-generator.md`, `1.3-highlights-selector.md`

### 5. Hero Section Formula Library

**Proven Hero Architectures:**

| Architecture | Headline Focus | Subhead Focus | Best For |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|----------|
| Benefit-First | [Primary benefit promise] | [How it works / mechanism] | Level 1-2 markets |
| Mechanism-First | [Named mechanism / technology] | [What it does for you] | Level 3 markets |
| Proof-First | [Specific result / statistic] | [How you get this result] | Level 4 markets |
| Curiosity-First | [Unexpected question / statement] | [Partial answer → scroll to learn more] | Saturated markets |
| Deal-First | [Offer / price / availability] | [Why this deal matters now] | Level 5 / promotions |
