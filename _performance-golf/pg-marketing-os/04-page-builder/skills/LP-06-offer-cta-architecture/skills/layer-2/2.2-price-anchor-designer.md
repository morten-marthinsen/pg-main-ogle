# 2.2 — Price Anchor Designer

**Skill:** LP-06-offer-cta-architecture
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Design the specific price anchoring mechanisms — comparison anchors, per-day minimizers, savings displays, and bundle tier presentations. This microskill turns raw pricing data into a structured anchoring strategy that LP-11 executes. The goal: make the actual price feel like an obvious bargain relative to the anchor frame.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `value-stack-architecture.md` (2.1), `pricing-display-strategy.md` (1.2), `offer-data-loaded.md` (0.2)
**Output:** `price-anchor-design.md`

## Anchoring Method Library

| Method | How It Works | Best For | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|---------|
| **Value Stack Anchor** | Total value sum dwarfs actual price | Type A, bonus-heavy offers | "Total value: $397 — Today: $69" |
| **Strikethrough Anchor** | Crossed-out original price next to sale price | Type B, ecomm, subscription | "~~$79.99~~ $59.99 (Save 25%)" |
| **Comparison Anchor** | Compare to more expensive alternative | All types, especially services | "A nutritionist costs $200/session" |
| **Per-Day Minimizer** | Divide price by days of use | Subscription, supplements | "Less than $2.33/day" |
| **Per-Unit Minimizer** | Divide by number of items/servings | Bundle offers, consumables | "$1.13 per serving" |
| **Savings Stack** | Show multiple savings at once | Bundle + subscription | "Save $60 + Free Shipping + Bonus" |
| **Category Comparison** | Compare to category alternatives | Competitive markets | "Others charge $89+. Ours: $49" |

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Select primary and secondary anchoring methods based on page type and offer type:

```
IF price_after_value (Type A):
  Primary: Value Stack Anchor
  Secondary: Comparison Anchor + Per-Day Minimizer
  Tertiary: Category Comparison (if competitive data available)

IF price_above_fold (Type B):
  Primary: Strikethrough Anchor
  Secondary: Per-Unit Minimizer + Savings Stack
  Tertiary: Bundle incentive ("Save more with 3-pack")

IF hybrid_both:
  Above-fold: Strikethrough Anchor
  Below-fold: Value Stack Anchor + Comparison Anchor
```

**Step 2:** Design the strikethrough anchor (if applicable):
- Original price: $[crossed out]
- Sale price: $[highlighted, accent color]
- Savings: [percentage and/or dollar amount]
- Visual treatment: [strikethrough style, color contrast]

**Step 3:** Design the comparison anchor:
- What comparable service/product costs more
- How to frame the comparison (e.g., "A single month with a dietitian costs $300+")
- Ensure the comparison is BELIEVABLE and RELEVANT

**Step 4:** Design the per-day/per-unit minimizer:
- Calculate: price / days of supply (or servings, or sessions)
- Select relatable comparison: cup of coffee ($5), parking meter ($2), streaming subscription ($0.50/day)
- Frame: "That's less than $[amount] per [day/serving] — less than [relatable item]"

**Step 5:** Design bundle tier display (if multi-pack/subscription exists):
- Identify the HIGHLIGHTED tier (the one the business wants to sell most)
- Calculate savings per tier
- Design the visual hierarchy: highlighted tier gets "Best Value" badge, larger display, pre-selected state

**Step 6:** Design savings communication:
- Percentage savings: "Save 25%"
- Dollar savings: "Save $20"
- Combined: "Save $20 (25% off)"
- Additional savings: free shipping, bonus items, etc.

## Quality Criteria
- Primary and secondary anchoring methods selected and designed
- Comparison anchor is believable and relevant (not inflated)
- Per-day/per-unit minimizer calculated correctly with relatable comparison
- Bundle tiers (if applicable) have clear highlighted tier with savings
- Savings communication combines all savings into a compelling summary
- All designs are specific enough for LP-11 to execute against

## Failure Modes
- No original price for strikethrough -> Skip strikethrough. Use comparison anchor and per-day minimizer instead.
- No bundle options -> Skip tier design. Focus on single-price anchoring methods.
- Comparison anchor feels unreliable -> Find a different comparison. The anchor must be something the audience would actually consider paying for.
- Per-day cost > $10 -> Consider per-week or per-month framing instead. The goal is a number that feels small.
