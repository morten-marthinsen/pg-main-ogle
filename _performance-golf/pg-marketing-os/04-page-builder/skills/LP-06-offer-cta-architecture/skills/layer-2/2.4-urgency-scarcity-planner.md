# 2.4 — Urgency/Scarcity Planner

**Skill:** LP-06-offer-cta-architecture
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Plan the urgency and scarcity approach for the page — using ONLY justified, credible urgency types with documented real-world reasons. If no legitimate urgency exists, this microskill explicitly EXCLUDES urgency and documents the reason. Fabricated urgency (fake timers, fake stock counts, perpetual "sales") is forbidden.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `offer-data-loaded.md` (0.2 — urgency levers), `offer-type-classification.md` (1.1), `cta-strategy.md` (1.3), `brief-loaded.md` (0.1 — awareness stage, skepticism level)
**Output:** `urgency-scarcity-plan.md`

## Justified Urgency Types

| Type | Real-World Basis | Credibility Requirement | Example |
|------|-----------------|------------------------|---------|
| **Limited Batch** | Ingredient sourcing, production constraints | Must name the constraint | "Only 2,000 bottles per batch — high-altitude ashwagandha sourced from Himalayan farms" |
| **Promotional Period** | Launch pricing, seasonal offer, partnership deal | Must have specific end date | "Introductory pricing ends March 15, 2026 — after that, retail price applies" |
| **Limited Stock** | Actual inventory count | Must be real and change | "47 remaining from current batch — next batch ships in 6 weeks" |
| **Subscription Lock** | Price increases after promotional period | Must specify reversion terms | "Lock in $49/mo — regular price reverts to $79/mo after March 31" |
| **Pre-Sale** | Product not yet generally available | Must have launch date | "Pre-order pricing available until launch date — save 30%" |
| **Seasonal** | Real seasonal event or relevance | Must be tied to actual season | "New Year supply — order before January 15 for delivery by February 1" |

---

## Fabricated Urgency (FORBIDDEN)

| Pattern | Why Forbidden | Risk |
|---------|--------------|------|
| Countdown timer that resets on refresh | Sophisticated buyers test this immediately | Instant trust destruction |
| "Only 3 left!" that never changes | Obvious manipulation upon return visit | FTC deceptive practices risk |
| "Sale ends tonight!" running indefinitely | Screenshot evidence for complaints/reviews | Legal liability + brand damage |
| Generic "Act now!" without reason | No conversion lift — just noise | Wastes page space |
| Inflated "regular price" never actually charged | FTC deceptive pricing regulations | Legal liability |

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Review urgency levers from `offer-data-loaded.md`. For each lever:
- Is it REAL? (documented evidence of actual limitation)
- Is it CREDIBLE? (would a sophisticated buyer believe it?)
- Is it SPECIFIC? (names dates, quantities, or reasons — not vague claims)

**Step 2:** Check offer type from 1.1:
- **Limited Offer:** Urgency is inherent. Document the quantity limit and depletion rate.
- **Limited-Time Offer:** Urgency is inherent. Document the deadline and reason.
- **OTO (One-Time Offer):** Urgency is inherent. The page itself is the urgency mechanism.
- **All other types:** Urgency is OPTIONAL. Only include if a legitimate lever exists.

**Step 3:** Cross-check urgency against skepticism level:
- **High/Very High skepticism:** Urgency must be EXTREMELY well-justified. Vague urgency will backfire.
- **Low/Medium skepticism:** Standard justified urgency is effective.

**Step 4:** For each justified urgency type being used:
- Write the specific claim (not "limited supply" but "Only 2,000 bottles per batch")
- Document the real-world justification
- Assign to a page section (urgency section or inline with CTA)
- Note visual treatment direction (banner, countdown, stock indicator, badge)

**Step 5:** If NO legitimate urgency exists:
- Explicitly document the exclusion
- Write the reason: "This is an evergreen product with unlimited digital delivery. No legitimate scarcity or deadline exists. Including fabricated urgency would damage credibility with the [vertical] audience."
- DO NOT fabricate urgency to fill the section

## Quality Criteria
- Decision is explicit: INCLUDED or EXCLUDED
- If included: every claim has documented real-world justification
- If included: every claim passes specificity check (names dates, quantities, or constraints)
- If included: credibility verified against audience skepticism level
- If excluded: reason is documented and alternative approach provided
- Zero fabricated urgency patterns (countdown resets, fake stock, perpetual sales)

## Failure Modes
- Offer data shows urgency levers but all are vague ("has_stock_scarcity: Y" with no details) -> Flag as `needs_validation`. Cannot include urgency without specific details. Request from client: "What is the actual production limitation? What is the real stock count? What is the real deadline?"
- Client insists on fake countdown timer -> Document the risk explicitly: "Fabricated urgency destroys trust, damages brand long-term, and creates FTC compliance risk. Recommend exclusion or replacement with legitimate urgency." Flag for human review.
- Offer type is Limited or Limited-Time but no specific limit is documented -> Flag as contradiction: "Offer classified as Limited but no quantity/date specified. Either provide the real limit or reclassify the offer type."
