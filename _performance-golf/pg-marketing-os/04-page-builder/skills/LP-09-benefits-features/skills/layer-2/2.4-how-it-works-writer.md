# 2.4 — How-It-Works Writer

**Skill:** LP-09-benefits-features
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Write step-by-step "how it works" sections that make the product's process or method feel simple, clear, and achievable. Each step includes an action, explanation, and the benefit the customer gains at that step. Maximum 5 steps — cognitive load kills conversion.
**Effort Level:** max
**Dependencies:** `benefit-section-classification.md` (1.1), `dfwbp-structure-plan.md` (1.2), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `packages-loaded.md` (0.1)
**Output:** `how-it-works.md`

## Pre-Execution Gate

**This microskill runs ONLY when:**
- At least one section is classified as `how_it_works` in 1.1
- Product has a describable process, method, or usage sequence

**If conditions not met:** Write `how-it-works.md` with content: "N/A — no sections classified as how_it_works."

---

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Load how-it-works specimens.**
Study LP-SPEC-01/02 (MAMG — 5-step curriculum preview with named lessons) and LP-SPEC-06 (Richmond Dinh — 4-lesson challenge structure). Extract: naming convention, step-to-benefit pairing, final outcome framing.

**Step 2: Identify the process.** What is the customer's journey from purchase to benefit? For supplements: order -> receive -> take daily -> feel result. For SaaS: sign up -> connect data -> configure -> see results. For info products: enroll -> watch module -> implement -> achieve outcome.

**Step 3: Determine step count.** 3 steps for simple products. 4-5 steps for multi-stage processes. NEVER more than 5. If the actual process has more than 5 steps, combine related steps.

**Step 4: Read hero threading anchor.** The final step's benefit must connect to the hero promise.

## Generation Execution

### How-It-Works Structure

#### Type A Format (Long-Form):

```markdown
## How [Product Name] Works

[1-2 sentence intro: Frame the simplicity. "Getting started takes [time estimate]. Here's exactly what happens."]

### Step 1: [Action Verb] + [What You Do]
[2-3 sentences explaining the step — what happens, what the customer does or receives]

**After this step:** [1 sentence — the immediate benefit or change the customer notices]

### Step 2: [Action Verb] + [What You Do]
[Same structure]

**After this step:** [Benefit at this stage]

### Step 3: [Action Verb] + [What You Do]
[Same structure]

**After this step:** [Benefit at this stage]

[Optional Steps 4-5 if needed]

### The Result
[2-3 sentences: Tie all steps together. State the cumulative outcome. Connect directly to the hero promise. Include a proof point if available — "On average, customers who complete all [X] steps see [specific result] within [timeframe]."]
```

#### Type B Format (Scan-Optimized):

```markdown
## How It Works

**1. [Action] — [What happens]**
[1 sentence benefit]

**2. [Action] — [What happens]**
[1 sentence benefit]

**3. [Action] — [What happens]**
[1 sentence benefit]
```

#### Process Types

| Product Type | Typical Steps | Step Focus |
|-------------|--------------|------------|
| Supplement | Order -> Receive -> Take daily -> Feel results (4 steps) | Simplicity, dosing ease, timeline to results |
| SaaS | Sign up -> Connect -> Configure -> Monitor -> Optimize (3-5) | Speed of setup, ease of use, time to first value |
| Info product | Enroll -> Access -> Follow program -> Implement -> See results (3-5) | What they learn, what they do, what changes |
| Service | Apply -> Onboard -> Receive deliverable -> Review -> Scale (3-5) | What happens for them, timeline, milestones |
| Physical product | Order -> Unbox -> Use -> Experience (3-4) | Arrival experience, ease of use, first benefit |

### Step Naming Rules

1. **Start each step with an action verb:** Order, Connect, Watch, Take, Open, Apply, Review
2. **No passive voice:** "Your data is analyzed" -> "We analyze your data"
3. **Include time estimate where credible:** "Step 1: Connect Your Ad Accounts (2 minutes)"
4. **Number steps explicitly:** Always use "Step 1, Step 2..." numbering
5. **Keep step names to 5-8 words:** "Connect Your Ad Accounts" not "Connect your existing advertising accounts from multiple platforms"

## Quality Criteria
- 3-5 steps (never more than 5)
- Each step starts with an action verb
- Each step has its own benefit callout
- Steps are sequential and logically connected (Step 2 could not happen before Step 1)
- Final outcome statement connects to the hero promise
- Time estimates included where credible
- Tone matches hero threading anchor
- Zero forbidden words from anti-slop list

## Failure Modes
- More than 5 steps -> Combine related steps. "Sign up and connect" can be one step. "Configure settings and customize" can be one step.
- Steps without benefits -> Every step must answer "what changes after this step?" If the step has no benefit, it either needs one or should be combined with another step.
- Final outcome disconnected from hero -> The how-it-works section is a bridge from "what you get" to "what happens." It must land on the hero promise.
- Steps are too vague ("Step 2: Use the product") -> Specific. What exactly do they do? For how long? When?
- All benefits at each step are the same -> Differentiate. Step 1 benefit might be "confidence that you're set up." Step 2 might be "first insights within an hour." Step 3 might be "full optimization in 30 days."
