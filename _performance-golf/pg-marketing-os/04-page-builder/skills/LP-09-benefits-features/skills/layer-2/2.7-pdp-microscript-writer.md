# 2.7 — PDP Microscript Writer

**Skill:** LP-09-benefits-features
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 7
**Purpose:** Generate 2-5 word benefit microscripts — tightly compressed benefit phrases that map 1:1 to specific product ingredients or features. Microscripts appear throughout the PDP (carousel slides, ingredient cards, benefit bullet sections, comparison tables) and must be consistent everywhere the same ingredient/feature is referenced. This is the master list that all other PDP skills consume for benefit language consistency.
**Effort Level:** max
**Dependencies:** `packages-loaded.md` (0.1), `dfwbp-structure-plan.md` (1.2), `ingredient-strategy.md` (1.3 — if supplement), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `pdp-microscripts.md`

---

## Pre-Execution Gate

**This microskill runs ONLY when:**
- Page type is `type_b` or `hybrid`

**If page type is `type_a`:** Do NOT run. Type A pages use full benefit language, not microscripts.

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Inventory all ingredients/features.** Build a complete list from the brief. Every ingredient or feature gets exactly ONE microscript.

**Step 2: Extract the primary benefit from each D-F-W-B-P chain.** The Benefit component (B) in the 1.2 plan is the source material. The microscript compresses this into 2-5 words.

**Step 3: Load PDP specimens** and study their benefit language patterns. Look for scan-optimized phrases: "Supports Deep Sleep," "Promotes Gut Health," "Naturally Boosts Energy."

**Step 4: Read hero threading anchor.** Microscripts must reinforce — not contradict — the hero promise.

**Step 5: Review anti-slop word list.** Microscripts are especially vulnerable to AI telltales because the short format tempts vague language. "Supports overall wellness" is forbidden. "Promotes deep, restorative sleep" is specific.

---

## Execution Logic

### Microscript Format

Every microscript follows this pattern:

```
[Benefit Verb] [Specific Outcome]
```

**Word count:** 2-5 words. Never more than 5. Never less than 2.

**Examples (supplement):**
- "Supports Deep Sleep"
- "Promotes Gut Health"
- "Naturally Boosts Energy"
- "Strengthens Immune Defense"
- "Reduces Stress Response"
- "Enhances Mental Clarity"
- "Restores Joint Mobility"

**Examples (SaaS/consumer):**
- "Saves Hours Weekly"
- "Automates Lead Follow-Up"
- "Simplifies Tax Filing"
- "Tracks Every Conversion"
- "Eliminates Manual Reporting"

### Writing Rules

1. **Benefit-forward, NOT feature-forward.** "Supports Deep Sleep" not "Contains Magnesium Glycinate." The ingredient/feature is the mapping key, not the microscript content.
2. **Present tense, active voice.** "Supports" not "Supported" or "Will support."
3. **One microscript per ingredient/feature.** 1:1 relationship. No ingredient gets two microscripts. No microscript maps to two ingredients.
4. **Consistency is non-negotiable.** The microscript for Magnesium Glycinate is "Supports Deep Sleep" EVERYWHERE on the page — in the ingredient card, on the carousel slide, in the benefit bullets section, in the comparison table. If it appears in two places with different language, the page feels incoherent.
5. **No AI telltales.** "Revolutionary" "Transformative" "Unlock" "Harness" "Synergy" — all forbidden. Use plain, specific benefit language.
6. **Each microscript must be DISTINCT.** No two microscripts should describe the same outcome. If two ingredients both support sleep, differentiate: "Supports Deep Sleep" vs. "Reduces Time to Fall Asleep."
7. **Benefit must be specific and picturable.** "Supports Health" fails. "Supports Deep Sleep" passes — the reader can picture it.

### Verb Selection Guide

**Strong benefit verbs (preferred):**
- Supports, Promotes, Strengthens, Enhances, Reduces, Restores, Protects, Boosts, Delivers, Calms, Sharpens, Fuels, Repairs, Stabilizes

**Weak/forbidden verbs (never use):**
- Revolutionizes, Transforms, Unlocks, Supercharges, Turbocharges, Optimizes (too vague), Empowers, Elevates, Harmonizes

### Downstream Consumers

This microscript master list is consumed by:
- **PDP-03** (Carousel Copy Directions) — microscripts appear on carousel slides
- **PDP-07** (BTF Section Copy) — microscripts appear in below-the-fold benefit sections
- **LP-15** (Page Assembly) — microscripts are placed wherever the ingredient/feature is referenced
- **2.6** (PDP Ingredient Card Writer) — microscripts align with the "X for Y" card naming

### Microscript-to-Card Alignment

If 2.6 (PDP Ingredient Card Writer) is also running, the microscript for each ingredient must be CONSISTENT with the "X for Y" headline:
- Card headline: "Magnesium Glycinate for Deep Sleep"
- Microscript: "Supports Deep Sleep"
- The benefit dimension ("Deep Sleep") is the same in both. The microscript adds the verb.

## Quality Criteria
- Every ingredient/feature has exactly ONE microscript (1:1 mapping)
- All microscripts are 2-5 words
- All microscripts start with a benefit verb in present tense, active voice
- No two microscripts describe the same benefit dimension
- Zero AI telltales or forbidden words
- Every microscript is specific and picturable (not "Supports Health")
- Microscripts align with "X for Y" card naming (if 2.6 is running)
- Consistency map shows where each microscript appears across the page
- Hero promise alignment documented

## Failure Modes
- Microscript exceeds 5 words -> Compress. "Naturally Helps Support Deep Restorative Sleep" is 6 words. "Supports Deep Sleep" is 3.
- Microscript is feature-forward ("Contains 500mg Magnesium") -> Rewrite as benefit-forward. The mapping table links microscript to ingredient — the microscript itself is pure benefit.
- Two microscripts overlap ("Boosts Energy" and "Increases Natural Energy") -> Differentiate or combine. Each microscript must own a distinct benefit lane.
- Microscript uses passive voice ("Is Known to Support") -> Active voice only. "Supports Deep Sleep" not "Is Known to Support Sleep."
- Same ingredient has different language in different page locations -> CRITICAL FAILURE. The entire purpose of this file is consistency. If the microscript is "Supports Deep Sleep," it is "Supports Deep Sleep" in the carousel, the ingredient card, and the benefit bullets. No variation.
- Running this microskill for Type A pages -> BLOCKED. Type A uses full benefit language, not microscripts.
- Microscript uses a forbidden verb ("Revolutionizes Recovery") -> Replace with strong, plain verb. "Accelerates Recovery" or "Speeds Recovery."
