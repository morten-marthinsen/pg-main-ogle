# 2.7 — PDP Expert Section Writer

**Skill:** LP-08-trust-elements
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 7
**Purpose:** Write expert endorsement section copy specifically formatted for PDP pages. PDP expert sections prefer VIDEO over text, use "white coat" authority framing (literal or metaphorical), and are scan-optimized (1-2 paragraphs maximum). This microskill produces both the text endorsement copy AND a video direction brief when video is the primary format.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `trust-classification.md` (1.1), `trust-placement-map.md` (1.3), `trust-inventory.md` (0.2 — named expert data), `volume-signal-copy.md` (2.5 — authority signals if already written, to avoid duplication)
**Output:** `pdp-expert-section-copy.md`

**Execution Condition:** Type B or Hybrid pages ONLY. Skip entirely for Type A — write `pdp-expert-section-copy.md` with `status: skipped_type_a` and terminate.

---

## Execution Logic

### If Type A page:
Write `pdp-expert-section-copy.md` with:
```
Status: skipped_type_a
Reason: PDP expert sections are Type B/Hybrid only. Type A uses authority signals from 2.5 directly. This microskill does not apply.
```
Terminate this microskill.

### If authority `not_applicable` (no named experts in trust inventory):
Write `pdp-expert-section-copy.md` with:
```
Status: not_applicable
Reason: No named experts with credentials found in trust inventory. PDP expert sections require a real, named expert — anonymous authority is zero authority.
```
Terminate this microskill.

### If Type B or Hybrid AND authority `included`:

**Step 1: Extract expert data from trust inventory.**
For each named expert, confirm availability of:
- Full name (required)
- Specific credential relevant to product category (required)
- Institution or practice (required)
- Endorsement quote or endorsement substance (required)
- Photo (preferred — check `expert_photo_available`)
- Video (preferred — check `expert_video_available`)

If any of the 4 required fields are missing for an expert, that expert CANNOT be used. Do not fabricate credentials or institutions.

**Step 2: Determine primary format — Video or Text.**
PDP expert sections prefer video. Apply this decision tree:

```
IF expert_video_available = Y:
  -> Primary format: VIDEO
  -> Also write text fallback (for no-video browsers, email, etc.)
ELSE IF expert_photo_available = Y:
  -> Primary format: TEXT WITH PHOTO
  -> Photo with name/credential overlay
ELSE:
  -> Primary format: TEXT ONLY
  -> Credential-lead formatting
```

**Step 3: Write credential-lead introduction.**
PDP expert sections use credential-lead framing — the credential comes BEFORE the endorsement. The reader registers authority BEFORE processing the claim.

Format: `Dr. [Full Name], [Credential] — [Institution]`

Examples:
- "Dr. Sarah Chen, Board-Certified Dermatologist — Stanford Dermatology Associates"
- "Dr. James DiNicolantonio, PharmD, Cardiovascular Research Scientist — Saint Luke's Mid America Heart Institute"
- "Coach Mike Robertson, CSCS, MS — Robertson Training Systems"

Rules:
- Credential MUST be relevant to the product claim it supports
- Use the expert's actual credential abbreviation (MD, PharmD, PhD, CSCS, RD, etc.)
- Include institution or practice name
- No inflated titles ("World-Renowned" is marketing, not a credential)

**Step 4: Write endorsement copy — 1-2 paragraphs maximum.**
PDP pages are scan-optimized. The expert endorsement must be BRIEF:

- Maximum 2 paragraphs (3-5 sentences total)
- Natural speaking style — not scripted marketing copy
- Must tie the expert's credibility to a SPECIFIC product claim (not generic "this is a great product")
- Use first person if quoting ("I've been recommending..." not "Dr. Chen recommends...")
- Lead with the most compelling specific claim

Format:
```
"[Specific endorsement tying expertise to product claim. 1-2 sentences maximum for the lead claim.]

[Optional second paragraph: supporting detail, personal usage, or specific patient/client outcome. 1-3 sentences.]"

— Dr. [Full Name], [Credential], [Institution]
```

Bad examples (FORBIDDEN):
- "This is a great product and I recommend it to all my patients." (Generic — could apply to anything)
- "After extensive research, I can say this is one of the best supplements on the market." (Vague — no specific claim)
- "I've been in practice for 30 years and this is unlike anything I've seen." (Hyperbolic — no specific claim)

Good examples:
- "The magnesium bisglycinate in [Product] has the highest absorption rate of any form I've tested. Most magnesium supplements use oxide, which has roughly 4% bioavailability. This formula delivers 80%+ — and my patients feel the difference within the first week."
- "I formulated [Product] with the specific carotenoid ratio shown in the AREDS2 study to support macular health. Every batch is third-party tested to confirm the exact dosages match what the clinical research validated."

**Step 5: Write photo/visual direction.**
If expert photo is available, specify the visual treatment:
- Photo placement: left-aligned or right-aligned next to endorsement text
- Name/credential overlay on or below the photo
- Recommended photo style: professional headshot, "white coat" (literal or metaphorical authority visual), or in-practice/in-lab context
- Photo size: 120-180px width for desktop PDP, 80-120px for mobile

**Step 6: Write video direction brief (if video format).**
If video is the primary format, produce a video direction brief:

```
VIDEO DIRECTION BRIEF — Expert Endorsement
Duration: 30-60 seconds
Format: Talking head (expert speaking directly to camera)
Setting: Professional context (office, lab, clinic — NOT a studio)
Style: Natural, conversational — NOT scripted read
Key claim callout: [The 1 specific claim the expert should address]
Credential display: Name + credential lower-third at 0:03
Structure:
  0:00-0:05 — Credential introduction (name, title, quick context)
  0:05-0:25 — Primary claim: [specific claim tied to product]
  0:25-0:45 — Supporting evidence or personal recommendation
  0:45-0:60 — Direct recommendation + product name
Overlay text: [Key claim as text overlay at most compelling moment]
Thumbnail: Expert in professional setting with product visible
```

**Step 7: Specify section placement.**
PDP expert sections belong BTF (below the fold), typically placed:
- After the ingredients/how-it-works section
- Before or within the social proof section
- NOT above fold (competes with buy box)
- NOT at very bottom (too late for credibility impact)

Confirm placement from `trust-placement-map.md` or recommend default placement.

**Step 8: Cross-reference against 2.5 authority signals.**
If 2.5 already wrote authority signal copy for the same expert, the PDP expert section SUPERSEDES it for Type B pages (the PDP format is more detailed with photo/video direction). Document what 2.5 wrote and how the PDP version extends it.

**Step 9: Write `pdp-expert-section-copy.md`.**

## Quality Criteria
- Expert has all 4 required fields: Name, Credential, Institution, Endorsement substance
- Credential is relevant to the specific product claim (not just any credential)
- Endorsement copy is 1-2 paragraphs maximum (PDP is scan-optimized — no essays)
- Endorsement ties to a SPECIFIC product claim (not "this is a great product")
- Natural speaking style (first person if quoting, conversational tone)
- Credential-lead formatting (authority registers before claim)
- Photo/visual direction provided when photo is available
- Video direction brief provided when video is primary format (30-60s, talking head, key claim callout)
- Section placed BTF, after ingredients/mechanism, before or within social proof
- Zero duplication with 2.5 authority signals (PDP version supersedes for Type B)
- No fabricated credentials, institutions, or endorsement quotes

## Failure Modes
- Running this microskill for Type A pages -> Type A authority signals are handled by 2.5. Skip entirely.
- Generic endorsement ("I recommend this product to everyone") -> Must tie to a SPECIFIC product claim. Rewrite with specific mechanism, ingredient, or outcome.
- Endorsement longer than 2 paragraphs -> PDP visitors scan. Cut to the 1-2 most compelling paragraphs. Save longer-form endorsements for Type A.
- Credential not relevant to product -> A PhD in Literature endorsing a supplement has zero credibility. Only use experts with category-relevant credentials.
- Missing video direction brief when video is primary format -> If expert video is the primary format, the video direction brief is REQUIRED, not optional.
- Fabricating expert credentials or quotes -> Every expert detail must come from trust inventory. If the inventory says "Dr. Chen, Dermatologist" do not upgrade to "Dr. Chen, Board-Certified Dermatologist" unless that specific credential is in the inventory.
- Placing expert section above fold -> Expert sections belong BTF. Above-fold real estate is for buy box, hero, and ATC. Expert section above fold competes with the conversion action.
- Writing scripted marketing copy as endorsement -> "After extensive testing, I can confidently say..." is marketing language. Natural: "I started recommending this to my patients about six months ago when I saw the third-party testing results..."
