# 3.1 — Research Intelligence Extractor

**Skill:** LP-00-brief-classifier
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 1
**Purpose:** Pull the top 10 pain quotes, top 5 hope quotes, and villain identification from CopywritingEngine research packages. This microskill ONLY executes in Downstream Mode. All extracted quotes must be verbatim — no paraphrasing, no summarizing.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `package-inventory.md` (0.3 — confirms research packages exist), CopywritingEngine research output files
**Output:** `research-extract.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  operating_mode: "downstream"  # This microskill ONLY runs in Downstream Mode
  research_package_paths:
    - "[path to 01-research/outputs/FINAL_HANDOFF.md]"
    - "[path to 01-research/outputs/research-quotes.md]"
    - "[any other research output files]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1: Confirm Downstream Mode.**
If operating mode is `standalone`, skip this microskill entirely. Log: "3.1 skipped — Standalone Mode. No research packages to extract."

**Step 2: Load research output files.**
Load in priority order:
1. `FINAL_HANDOFF.md` — the master research output (richest source)
2. `research-quotes.md` — curated quote collection
3. Any additional `.md` files in the research outputs folder

**Step 3: Extract top 10 pain quotes.**
Scan all loaded research files for audience pain statements. Select the 10 most vivid, specific, and emotionally charged quotes. Priority criteria:
- First-person voice ("I can't sleep because...")
- Specific details (names conditions, timeframes, failed solutions)
- Emotional intensity (frustration, fear, desperation, embarrassment)
- Relevance to the product's problem category

**All quotes must be VERBATIM** — copied exactly as they appear in the research files. Include the source attribution if available (forum, review, survey response).

**Step 4: Extract top 5 hope quotes.**
Scan for desire/aspiration statements. Select the 5 most vivid expressions of what the audience wants. Priority criteria:
- Specific desired outcomes (not vague "feel better")
- Identity-level desires ("I want to be the kind of person who...")
- Emotional resonance (hope, longing, determination)

**Step 5: Identify the villain.**
Extract the villain framework from research. The villain is who/what the audience blames for their problem:
- **External villain:** A specific entity (Big Pharma, Wall Street, the medical establishment)
- **Systemic villain:** A broken system or hidden cause (toxins in food supply, outdated advice)
- **Internal villain:** Self-blame pattern (my lack of willpower, my genetics)

If research identifies multiple villains, rank by prominence and emotional charge.

**Step 6: Extract supplementary intelligence.**
Pull any additional research insights that inform landing page architecture:
- Objections inventory (what prevents buying)
- Trigger events (what makes them search for a solution NOW)
- Language patterns (words and phrases they use repeatedly)
- Competitor mentions (products they've tried and abandoned)

## Quality Criteria
- All quotes are VERBATIM — zero paraphrasing
- Minimum 10 pain quotes and 5 hope quotes extracted (or all available if fewer exist in source)
- Quotes are specific and emotionally charged — not generic category statements
- Villain identified with evidence — not assumed from product category
- Source attribution included for every quote
- Extraction completeness section honestly reports what was vs. wasn't found

## Failure Modes
- Research files exist but contain fewer than 10 pain quotes → Extract all available, note shortfall in completeness section
- Research files are malformed or unreadable → Log error, extract what's possible, flag gaps
- No villain framework in research → Mark as `not_identified`, note that LP-07 (Hero Section) will need to infer villain framing from pain quotes
- Research files are stale (>30 days per package-inventory.md) → Extract normally but add staleness warning
