# 2.4 — Traffic Temperature Mapper

**Skill:** LP-00-brief-classifier
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 4
**Purpose:** Classify expected traffic temperature (Cold / Warm / Hot) based on traffic source, audience awareness, and campaign context. Map the structural implications of the temperature classification for downstream architecture and writing skills.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `brief-load.md` (Layer 0), `classification-signals.md` (Layer 1 — S5 traffic source signal), `audience-profile.md` (2.1 — sophistication dimension)
**Output:** `traffic-temp.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  brief_content: "[from brief-load.md — full brief text]"
  s5_traffic_signal: "[from classification-signals.md — Signal 5 evidence]"
  awareness_stage: "[from audience-profile.md — sophistication dimension]"
  page_type: "[from type-declaration.md]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1: Traffic source identification.**
Extract the primary traffic source from the brief. If no traffic source is explicitly stated, infer from campaign context clues (ad platform mentioned, funnel position described, audience type referenced).

Common sources by temperature:
```
COLD: Facebook/Instagram interest targeting, YouTube pre-roll, native ads,
      cold email, affiliate cold traffic, programmatic display
WARM: Retargeting ads, email list (subscribers), organic search (category terms),
      influencer referral, podcast listener traffic, webinar registrants
HOT:  Branded search, direct URL, existing customer, cart abandoner retarget,
      product-name search, affiliate warm-list
```

**Step 2: Temperature classification.**
Assign one of three temperatures based on the dominant traffic source:
- **Cold:** Visitor has no prior awareness of this product or brand
- **Warm:** Visitor is aware of the problem or category; may have encountered the brand
- **Hot:** Visitor is actively searching for this product or ready to buy

If multiple traffic sources are indicated, classify based on the PRIMARY source (the one driving the most volume). Note secondary sources separately.

**Step 3: Cross-validate against awareness stage.**
The traffic temperature should be consistent with the audience sophistication profile:
- Cold traffic → typically Stages 1-3 (Unaware through Solution-Aware)
- Warm traffic → typically Stages 3-4 (Solution-Aware through Product-Aware)
- Hot traffic → typically Stages 4-5 (Product-Aware through Most-Aware)

If there's a mismatch (e.g., cold traffic but Product-Aware audience), note the conflict and explain.

**Step 4: Map structural implications.**
Based on temperature, document what this means for page architecture:

```
COLD → Above-fold must earn attention before stating offer
     → Headline leans curiosity or problem-agitation
     → More educational content before CTA
     → Social proof required earlier
     → Type A or Hybrid almost always preferred

WARM → Can lead with benefit/solution sooner
     → Mechanism still needs explaining but faster
     → Testimonials and reviews highly effective
     → CTA can appear earlier in page

HOT  → Type B often sufficient
     → Above-fold CTA can be primary conversion element
     → Proof density more important than education
     → Price and value comparison critical
     → Shorter pages can work
```

**Step 5: Page structure recommendation.**
Based on temperature + page type, note any structural tensions or confirmations. Example: Cold traffic + Type B declaration = structural tension that should be flagged.

## Output Schema

`traffic-temp.md`:

```markdown
# Traffic Temperature Map — [Product Name]

## Temperature: [COLD | WARM | HOT]

## Traffic Source Analysis
- Primary source: [e.g., Facebook cold traffic — interest targeting]
- Secondary sources: [list or "none identified"]
- Source confidence: [explicit — stated in brief | inferred — derived from context clues]

## Evidence
- Brief says: "[direct quote or observation from brief about traffic/campaign]"
- Classification signal S5: [type_a | type_b | neutral] — "[evidence from 1.1]"

## Cross-Validation
- Traffic temperature: [COLD | WARM | HOT]
- Audience awareness stage: [Stage X — name]
- Alignment: [consistent | tension — explanation]

## Structural Implications
- Above-fold approach: [earn attention | lead with benefit | direct CTA]
- Headline direction: [curiosity/problem | benefit/solution | offer/deal]
- Education requirement: [heavy — multiple sections | moderate | light — proof-focused]
- CTA placement: [deep — after education | mid-page | above-fold primary]
- Social proof timing: [early — trust building | mid-page | near CTA — reassurance]

## Page Type Alignment
- Declared page type: [type_a | type_b | hybrid]
- Temperature alignment: [confirmed | tension]
- [If tension: "Flag: [Cold/Warm] traffic classified as Type B — verify this is intentional. Cold traffic typically requires Type A or Hybrid architecture."]
```

---

## Quality Criteria
- Temperature classified as exactly one of three values: COLD, WARM, HOT
- Primary traffic source identified with evidence from brief
- Cross-validation against awareness stage performed — conflicts noted
- Structural implications are specific to the declared temperature (not generic)
- Page type alignment check performed

## Failure Modes
- Brief provides no traffic source information → Infer from campaign type and audience description, flag as `inferred`, set confidence to low
- Multiple traffic sources at different temperatures → Classify based on primary (highest volume), list all sources
- Temperature conflicts with declared page type → Flag tension explicitly, do NOT silently resolve
