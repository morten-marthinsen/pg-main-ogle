# 2.1 — Audience Profile Builder

**Skill:** LP-00-brief-classifier
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 1
**Purpose:** Build a complete 5-dimension audience profile (demographics, pain, desire, sophistication, language) from available inputs. In Downstream Mode, extract from CopywritingEngine research packages. In Standalone Mode, infer from brief and flag every dimension as `brief_inferred`. All 5 dimensions are mandatory — no dimension may be left empty.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `brief-load.md` (Layer 0), `mode-declaration.md` (Layer 0), `type-declaration.md` (Layer 1)
**Output:** `audience-profile.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  brief_content: "[from brief-load.md — full brief text]"
  operating_mode: "[downstream | standalone]"
  page_type: "[type_a | type_b | hybrid — from type-declaration.md]"
  research_packages: "[paths from package-inventory.md, if Downstream Mode]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1: Source determination.**
- If Downstream Mode AND research packages available → Extract dimensions from research outputs (FINAL_HANDOFF.md, research-quotes.md). Mark source as `research_validated`.
- If Standalone Mode OR no research packages → Infer dimensions from brief content. Mark every dimension source as `brief_inferred`.

**Step 2: Dimension 1 — Demographics.**
Extract or infer: age range, gender skew, income level, education, geography. If brief provides explicit demographic data, use it. If not, infer from product category, price point, and market norms. Flag confidence level.

**Step 3: Dimension 2 — Pain Profile.**
Extract or infer: primary pain (single most acute), secondary pains (2-3), pain intensity (1-10 with rationale), duration, failed solutions, self-blame pattern. In Downstream Mode, pull verbatim quotes (minimum 3) from research. In Standalone Mode, construct from brief language and flag.

**Step 4: Dimension 3 — Desire Profile.**
Extract or infer: primary desire, dream outcome (vivid language), identity desire (who they want to become), timeline expectation. In Downstream Mode, pull verbatim quotes (minimum 2). In Standalone Mode, construct from brief promises.

**Step 5: Dimension 4 — Sophistication Profile.**
Map: awareness stage (from Layer 1 S4 signal), market sophistication (Schwartz 1-5), tried-before status, skepticism level, mechanism familiarity. This dimension connects to 2.5 (Awareness Stage Mapper) — use consistent staging.

**Step 6: Dimension 5 — Language Profile.**
Extract or infer: vocabulary level (reading grade), idioms they use, vocabulary to avoid, emotional tone, sample quotes. In Downstream Mode, pull actual audience language from research quotes. In Standalone Mode, estimate from brief tone and market category norms.

**Step 7: Validation sweep.**
- Confirm all 5 dimensions populated (zero empty dimensions)
- Flag any dimension with low confidence as `needs_validation`
- In Standalone Mode, add global note: "All dimensions inferred from brief — consider running CopywritingEngine Skill 01 research for validated audience data"

---

## Quality Criteria
- All 5 dimensions populated — zero empty dimensions
- Every dimension has a `source` tag (research_validated or brief_inferred)
- Downstream Mode profiles include verbatim quotes (minimum 3 pain, 2 desire)
- Standalone Mode profiles flag every dimension as `brief_inferred`
- Pain profile has specific primary pain statement — not generic category
- Desire profile has vivid dream outcome — not abstract benefit
- Sophistication maps awareness stage consistently with Layer 1 S4 signal

## Failure Modes
- Brief provides zero audience information → Build minimal profile from product category norms, flag ALL dimensions as `needs_validation`
- Research packages found but contain no audience quotes → Extract what's available, flag gaps, note in validation flags
- Conflicting data between brief and research packages → Prioritize research data, note conflict in validation flags
