# 2.3 — Reviewer Persona Builder

**Skill:** PDP-05-social-proof-review-system
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 3
**Purpose:** Build 5-8 distinct reviewer archetypes with demographic profiles, purchase motivations, voice patterns, and skepticism levels. These personas seed the featured reviews (2.1), general review generation, and ensure the review section feels like real humans — not a single AI voice writing every review.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `input-verification.md` (0.1), `research-load.md` (0.2), `review-system-architecture.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `reviewer-personas.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  input_verification: "[from 0.1 — product data, target demographics, product category]"
  research_load: "[from 0.2 — audience demographics, verbatim quotes, pain points, purchase motivations]"
  review_architecture: "[from 1.1 — card template fields including reviewer attributes]"
  scope: "[from 0.1 — full | standard | minimal | generation_only]"
  product_category: "[from 0.1]"
  target_demographic: "[from 0.1 — primary age range, gender split, etc.]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Determine persona count:
- `scope = full`: 7-8 personas
- `scope = standard`: 5-6 personas
- `scope = minimal`: 4-5 personas
- `scope = generation_only`: 5-6 personas (generation needs diversity)

**Step 2:** Extract demographic signals from research data (0.2):
- Age ranges mentioned in quotes
- Gender indicators
- Geographic signals
- Experience levels with product category
- Common purchase motivations
- Common prior solutions tried
- Skepticism language patterns

**Step 3:** Build persona archetypes. Each persona must include:

```
PERSONA TEMPLATE:
- Name: [First name + Last initial — realistic, diverse names]
- Age Range: [e.g., 35-44]
- Gender: [M/F/NB]
- Location: [City, State — realistic geography]
- Product-Specific Attributes: [skin type, fitness level, tech savviness, etc.]
- Purchase Motivation: [WHY they bought — specific trigger]
- Prior Solutions Tried: [what they used before — competitor products, DIY, etc.]
- Skepticism Level: [low | medium | high]
- Voice Pattern: [how they write — sentence length, vocabulary level, formality]
- Emotional Register: [matter-of-fact | enthusiastic | analytical | casual | story-telling]
- Star Rating Tendency: [which rating this persona would give: 3, 4, or 5]
- Time Owned: [realistic duration for this persona's review]
- Likely Con to Mention: [what minor issue this persona would flag]
```

**Step 4:** Mandatory persona archetypes (must be represented in the set):

| Archetype | Description | Star Tendency | Skepticism |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| The Enthusiast | Loves the product, detailed positive review, BUT still has a con | 5 stars | Low |
| The Analytical Buyer | Data-driven, compares features, values metrics | 4-5 stars | Medium |
| The Skeptic Convert | Was doubtful, bought anyway, pleasantly surprised | 4-5 stars | High |
| The Practical User | Focused on utility, no-nonsense, value-conscious | 4 stars | Medium |
| The Long-Term User | Has owned for 6+ months, can speak to durability/sustained results | 4-5 stars | Low-Medium |

Additional archetypes (select based on product category):
| Archetype | When to Include |
|-----------|-----------------|
| The Gift Buyer | Products commonly gifted |
| The Switcher | Products with strong competitors (bought X, switched to this) |
| The Repeat Buyer | Consumables, subscriptions |
| The Professional User | Products with professional applications |
| The Budget-Conscious | Higher-priced products where value is a concern |

**Step 5:** Voice differentiation rules:
- No two personas share the same sentence length pattern (one writes short punchy sentences, another writes flowing paragraphs)
- No two personas share the same vocabulary level (one uses technical terms, another uses everyday language)
- No two personas share the same emotional register
- At least one persona uses casual/colloquial language ("honestly," "tbh," "not gonna lie")
- At least one persona is more formal/detailed
- At least one persona tells a brief story (before/after narrative)

**Step 6:** Demographic diversity requirements:
- Age: spread across at least 3 decades (e.g., 20s, 30s-40s, 50s+)
- Gender: realistic mix for product category (not all same gender unless product is gender-specific)
- Geography: at least 3 different US regions (or appropriate regions for target market)
- No two personas with identical product-specific attributes (e.g., not all "dry skin" for skincare)

**Step 7:** Assign each persona to a review function:
- Which personas will be used for featured reviews (2.1) — typically The Enthusiast, The Skeptic Convert, The Long-Term User
- Which personas will be used for general review pool
- Which persona is best for the "initially skeptical" featured review
- Which persona is best for the "4-star honest" featured review

**Step 8:** Anti-uniformity check:
- Read all persona voice patterns together — do they sound like different people?
- If any two personas could be confused for the same person: rewrite one
- If all personas are excessively positive: add more nuance to at least 2
- If no persona mentions a realistic con naturally: revise

## Quality Criteria
- 5-8 distinct personas generated with all template fields populated
- All 5 mandatory archetypes represented (Enthusiast, Analytical, Skeptic Convert, Practical, Long-Term)
- Voice patterns differ between all personas (sentence length, vocabulary, register)
- Demographic diversity verified (3+ age decades, gender mix, 3+ regions)
- Every persona has a "Likely Con" (no persona is 100% positive)
- At least 1 persona with high skepticism level
- Sample sentences sound like different people
- Review assignments mapped (featured vs. general pool)

## Failure Modes
- All personas same age range -> HALT. Spread across 3+ decades.
- All personas same voice pattern -> HALT. Differentiate sentence length, vocabulary, and register.
- No high-skepticism persona -> HALT. At least 1 persona must be initially skeptical.
- Missing mandatory archetype -> HALT. All 5 mandatory archetypes must be represented.
- Two personas with identical product attributes -> HALT. Vary product-specific attributes.
- No persona assigned to "Likely Con" -> HALT. Every persona must have a natural con.
- All star tendencies at 5 -> HALT. Include at least 1 persona with 4-star tendency and optionally 1 with 3-star.
