---

## PURPOSE

Write the human-readable MOBILE-AUDIT-SUMMARY.md from the compiled `mobile-audit.json`. This is the file the human reviewer will read first. It must be clear, scannable, and actionable — no jargon, no buried information, no ambiguity about what to do next.

---

## PROCEDURE

1. **Read** `mobile-audit.json`.
2. **Write** `MOBILE-AUDIT-SUMMARY.md` following the template in LP-16-AGENT.md.
3. **Ensure** the summary is scannable:
   - Verdict and score are the first thing visible
   - Score table shows all 7 dimensions at a glance
   - Speed estimate is called out separately (it is the most actionable metric)
   - Top 3 fixes are immediately visible
   - Full fix list is available but secondary

## CRITICAL RULES

1. **Human-readable first.** This is not a technical document — it is a decision document. The reviewer needs to know: how did we score, what is broken, and what do we do about it.
2. **Verdict and score are the header.** They are the first words the reviewer sees. No preamble, no methodology explanation first.
3. **Top 3 fixes are separate from the full list.** Busy reviewers will read the top 3 and skip the rest. Make sure the top 3 are the highest-impact, most actionable fixes.
4. **Speed gets its own section.** Speed is the most concrete, most actionable metric in the audit. It deserves a dedicated callout with specific numbers.
5. **Next steps are prescriptive.** Do not say "consider" or "you might want to." Say "proceed to LP-17" or "return to LP-15." The verdict determines the next step — it is not optional.
