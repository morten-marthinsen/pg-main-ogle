## NLS Type 4: Product Highlights (TLDR)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Digestible caveman bits of copy that address the top 3-5 questions/issues upfront, at a quick glance
- **Why It Exists:** To resolve major objections as fast as possible for those who scroll beneath the CTA to re-confirm, or discover in the first place, what the product is about
- **Best Practices:**
  - Choose top 3-5 questions or issues people have — think of them as mental blockers that, until resolved, will block purchase
  - Customizable framework:
    - **What's in It** — Show supplement/nutrition facts panel
    - **How it Helps** — 5-7 short benefit bullets with microscripts
    - **Why it Works** — 1 sentence crystal clear delivery of the problem mechanism + simple graphic dramatizing how it works in the body
    - **Shipping & Returns** — Emphasize free S&H if available and expected shipping speed
    - **Our Guarantee** — Simple summary emphasizing key X benefit within Y days

### NLS Live References
- **GOOD:** [Kachava](https://www.kachava.com/products/shakes)
  - Shows 5 key points their specific buyers want to know about
  - Mixes KSPs with typical buyer objections (quality, shipping, etc.)
  - Mixes hard data (nutrition facts panel) with good copy (e.g., "Unbeatable Taste" section)
  - Brings personality to it — not just boring facts
- **BAD:** [Blueprint Bryan Johnson](https://blueprint.bryanjohnson.com/products/longevity-blend-multinutrient-drink-mix-blood-orange-flavor)
  - Boring labels
  - Missing shipping/returns

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Product Highlights TLDR (Q&A Format)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| What is it? | A forward-weighted putter engineered to eliminate face drift — the hidden physics flaw in 95% of putters. |
| Who is it for? | Any golfer who's tired of blaming their hands for missed putts. |
| What makes it different? | Traditional putters fight you. LAB putters sit neutral. The RS1 actively helps. |
| What if it doesn't work? | 365-Day "Love Your Game" Guarantee. |

**Why This Works:**
- Questions match the prospect's actual mental model
- Each answer is one sentence
- "What makes it different?" is a competitive positioning micro-story in one sentence
- Scannable in under 10 seconds

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- Use QUESTIONS as headers (not feature names) — mirrors how the prospect thinks
- Mix KSPs with objection-busting (shipping, guarantee)
- Hard data + personality copy > boring facts
- 5-7 items max for scannability
- Each answer: 1-2 sentences, never more

## NLS Type 5: UGC Video Carousel (Social Proof)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** 5-7 energetic UGC video testimonials
- **Why It Exists:** Provide social proof and FOMO that the product actually works
- **Best Practices:**
  - Curate the best of the best — engaging people who deliver energy and enthusiasm through the screen
  - Select a variety of avatars/personas for max coverage
  - Great to re-use UGC ads that did well, just strip out the selling
  - Best to get real customers to shoot with phone, up close
  - Keep all weirdness, ums and uhs — keep it real
  - Can mix in expert videos, just keep them authentic
  - Arrows on either side of carousel
  - Video should play on mute with clear signal to click for audio
  - If user navigates to next video, stop previous video

## NLS Type 6: Expert Credibility

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Credible image and/or video of whatever expert created or endorses the product
- **Why It Exists:** To provide credibility needed to overcome skepticism and anxiety about product safety and effectiveness
- **Best Practices:**
  - Ideally use a video, with thumbnail of them in act of speaking
  - Always put them in white coat where possible
  - Full title and ideally where they went to school (e.g., XX School of Medicine)
  - Natural speaking — no canned scripts; splice different takes if necessary, or ask prompting questions for natural answers
  - Show captions like it was a UGC ad
  - When user scrolls away, video should stop playing

### NLS Live References
- **BEST:** [HumanN — SuperBeets Soft Chew](https://humann.com/products/superbeets-soft-chew)
  - Excellent execution of video
  - Uses two experts for different perspectives
- **GOOD:** [TryCreate — Creatine Gummies](https://trycreate.co/products/sour-peach-core-create-creatine-monohydrate-gummies)
  - Uses images instead of video
  - Great credentials
  - Misses opportunity to bring alive what they say about the product

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Section 9 (Chris McGinley Story)

**Section Pre-Head:** "MEET THE DESIGNER"
**Section Header:** "The Wizard of Wedges" (ONE.1 variant)

**Copy Pattern:**
1. Authority credential (30+ years, named tour players)
2. What he noticed (the problem from his perspective)
3. The question he asked ("What if we could engineer a wedge that manages ground contact FOR you?")
4. Designer quote (long-form, conversational, technical but accessible)

**Key Quote:**
> "Forward weighting is our special magical sauce. Why hasn't anyone else done this? It makes too much sense."

**Why This Works:**
- PERSON introduces the product, not the brand
- Designer quote is conversational, not polished — builds authenticity
- "Why hasn't anyone else done this?" rhetorical question positions innovation as obvious
- Named tour players (Tiger, Rory) provide borrowed credibility without endorsement claims

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- PERSON > brand — expert section should feel like meeting someone, not reading a brand bio
- Video > image (but image + credentials + quote still works)
- Conversational > scripted — authenticity is the whole point
- Include credentials but make them serve the story, not just list them
- NLS: white coat where possible; PG: workshop/design studio context (golf equivalent of credibility attire)

## NLS Type 7: What's In It (Ingredients / Features)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Individual ingredients/features with compelling visuals and benefit explanations
- **Why It Exists:** To answer "what's in this" in human terms and make ingredients feel real and intentional
- **Best Practices:**
  - **Compelling Visual** — Begin with a visual that brings the ingredient to life (e.g., beautiful flower the ingredient is sourced from; molecular structures; different colored powders with different textures)
  - **Ingredient Name + Microscript Benefit** — Combine in format: "L-Theanine for Tranquility"
  - **Description** — Succinct 1-2 sentence explanation: what the ingredient IS and how it delivers the benefit (tie benefit to mechanism)
  - **List Vertically and Expanded** — Show vertically, do NOT roll up accordion-style unless many ingredients; if accordion, show first ingredient expanded as signal + collapse when another opens

### NLS Live References
- **GOOD:** [Stonehenge Health — Dynamic Joint](https://stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint)
  - Horizontal slider (imagine vertical instead)
  - Beautiful imagery bringing each ingredient to life with variety
  - Good ingredient descriptions — succinct but powerful
  - Missing benefit in ingredient name
  - Body font too small
- **GOOD:** [Healthy Bones Co — Collagen Peptides](https://healthybonesco.com/products/collagen-peptides)
  - Vertical accordion style
  - First entry expanded already (good signal)
  - Clean plus/X signs for collapse
  - Labels are benefit-first (good), but should add ingredient in "X for Y" format (e.g., "Fortibone for Bone Density Support")

### PG Implementation: ONE.1 — Section 5 (Features & Benefits)

Each feature block:
```
FEATURE NAME: [Named feature from PDP-02]
HEADLINE: [Benefit-first micro-headline, 3-7 words]
COPY: [Mechanism → benefit → dimensionalization, 40-60 words]
VISUAL: [Feature animation or diagram]
```

**Example — Auto-Glide Sole:**
- **Headline:** "Your Fat Shot Fix"
- **Copy:** "The 'Auto-Glide Sole' gives you Four-Way Forgiveness because instead of digging, its four-way cambered shape glides through the ground automatically — regardless of your attack angle. This means you make consistently clean contact and finish closer to the hole — even when you're in a bad lie or you hit way behind the ball."
- **Visual:** Hero Animation

**Why This Works:**
- Benefit headline (not feature headline) — "Your Fat Shot Fix" not "Auto-Glide Sole Technology"
- The "This means..." bridge from mechanism to felt benefit
- Ends with a scenario ("even when you're in a bad lie") that dimensionalizes
- Animation reinforces the copy — visual + verbal working together

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- NLS format: "Ingredient Name for Benefit" (compound naming)
- PG format: Benefit headline + mechanism → "This means..." → dimensionalization
- Both formats: ALWAYS lead with benefit, not feature/ingredient name
- Visuals are critical — this section is visual-first, copy-second
- 1-2 sentences per feature description, never more
- Vertical layout always; accordion only if 7+ features

## NLS Type 8: What to Expect (Benefits Timeline)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Step-by-step timeline of when and how the product will affect them
- **Why It Exists:** To future-pace them, showing real tangible benefits of continuing to use it, helping them visualize how their underlying experience is changing, increasing believability
- **Best Practices:**
  - **Cover Key Time Frames** — 3-5 timeframes: short, medium, and long-term minimum; ideally 7 days, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months
  - **Vertical Line on Left** — Gives it a timeline feel; ideally line appears gradually as user scrolls, with big circle to left of each milestone
  - **Benefit Title** — Title for each milestone should be a powerful benefit microscript
  - **Description** — Short explanation connecting dots to what the ingredient/mechanism is doing and what benefits they should enjoy
  - **Optional Image** — Can add image dimensionalizing benefits (lifestyle imagery, exciting activities) without being cheesy
  - Note: More than 3 milestones isn't necessary for thumbnail version; focus on immediate, medium, and long-term (1 week, 1 month, 1 year or 3-6 months)

### NLS Live References
- **GOOD:** [Seed — Daily Synbiotic](https://seed.com/daily-synbiotic)
  - Nice milestone labels that pop
  - Great benefit headlines for each milestone
  - Bullets somewhat generic (probably for compliance)
  - Make circles bigger + line unfold as user scrolls
  - Optionally add lifestyle imagery per milestone
- **GOOD:** [Stonehenge Health — Dynamic Joint](https://stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint)
  - Great circle execution (dark border, lighter inside matches label)
  - Descriptions nicely progress through feelings
  - Missing mechanism detail; timeline doesn't unfold
- **CREATIVE:** [Superfood Tabs](https://superfoodscompany.com/products/superfood-tabs)
  - Auto-playing simple images dramatize the transformation
  - Missing many best practices, but nice transformation visualization feature

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Section 16

| Phase | Milestone | What Happens |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| First Putt | Feel the weight | "The RS1 sits differently in your hands... you'll notice the face wants to hold square." |
| First Round | Stop thinking | "Somewhere around the 4th or 5th hole, you'll realize you haven't been thinking about your putting stroke." |
| First Month | Confidence builds | "Not because of one dramatic putt — because of a pattern. Fewer pushed putts. Fewer three-putts." |
| Three Months | Identity shift | "The RS1 is your putter now. You stop thinking about it. You just putt." |

**Why This Works:**
- 4 phases with clear time markers
- Progression: physical sensation → behavioral change → emotional confidence → identity shift
- Each phase is a 3-5 sentence paragraph, not a bullet
- The final phase answers the question prospects won't ask: "When does it become part of me?"

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- NLS milestones: 7 days, 2 weeks, 1 month, 3 months, 6 months (supplement cadence)
- PG milestones: First Putt, First Round, First Month, Three Months (usage cadence)
- Key: milestones must match PRODUCT USAGE PATTERN, not arbitrary timeframes
- Progression is always: physical → behavioral → emotional → identity
- Benefit title at each milestone should be a microscript (not a generic description)
- Vertical line + circles on left is the NLS gold standard layout
- Each milestone description: mechanism + felt benefit (what's happening + what they notice)

## NLS Type 9: Problem / Solution (Pain → Product)

### NLS Framework (Problem)
- **What It Shows:** The problem the product solves, dramatized
- **Why It Exists:** To agitate the pain and create urgency for a solution
- **Best Practices:**
  - NLS notes Problem and Solution may be consolidated into one section
  - 3-section rhythm (from Nutreance specimens):
    1. **Paradigm Shift** — "It's NOT what you think"
    2. **Reveal Real Cause** — "It's actually Y"
    3. **Introduce New Solution** — Product as the answer

### NLS Framework (Solution / USP)
- The Solution section often consolidates with the Problem section
- Follows the 3-section rhythm above

### NLS Live References
- [MenGoToMars](https://mengotomars.com/) — Problem/solution combined
- [Nutreance — Redimove](https://www.nutreance.com/products/redimove) — 3-section rhythm: paradigm shift → real cause → new solution
- [Nutreance — Glucoven](https://www.nutreance.com/products/glucoven) — Same 3-section rhythm (pattern confirmed across products)
- Note: NLS observes these are shopping links with limited pre-sold context — shopping ads may want customized landers with more problem/solution/rationale

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Section 4 (Face Drift Villain Reveal)

**Copy Pattern:**
1. Open with a SCENE the prospect has lived ("Standing over a 4-footer for par...")
2. Describe the FAILURE ("And you push it right.")
3. Rule out their self-blame ("Your read was fine. Your speed was fine.")
4. Reveal the REAL cause ("It's not your hands. It's not your nerves. It's the physics of your putter.")
5. Name the villain ("Face Drift")
6. Dimensionalize with a stat ("90% of your putt's starting direction comes from face angle")
7. Show they've been fighting it forever ("You've tried new putters. New grips. YouTube drills.")
8. Redirect blame ("The problem was never your stroke. The problem was your putter's center of gravity.")

**Word Count:** ~350 words (largest section in any PG product)

**Why This Works:**
- Carlton empathy technique: opens IN the prospect's experience, not describing it from outside
- Clemens vindication: "It's not your fault" — the prospect is absolved
- The villain has a NAME ("Face Drift") — turns abstract frustration into diagnosable condition
- Statistical proof ("90%") gives vindication scientific backing
- Ends by redirecting blame from self → equipment, setting up mechanism reveal

### Runner-Up: ONE.1 — Section 3

**Headline:** "The Consistency Crisis"

**Copy:**
> 84% of golfers use cavity-backed irons because they help you hit straighter, more consistent shots without perfect technique. But those same golfers use bladed wedges that punish anything but perfection.

**Why It Also Works:**
- Named problem: "The Consistency Crisis"
- Stat-first ("84%") to establish legitimacy
- The contradiction IS the problem — cavity-back irons vs. bladed wedges
- Only 2 sentences — extreme compression for a problem section

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- NLS 3-section rhythm (paradigm shift → real cause → solution) works for products with unsophisticated audiences
- PG 8-beat pattern (scene → failure → absolution → villain → proof → history → redirect) works for sophisticated audiences who need vindication
- Both patterns: the problem MUST have a NAME (Face Drift, Consistency Crisis, etc.)
- Stats make dismissal harder — always include one proof element
- Word count range: 2 sentences (ONE.1 extreme compression) to 350 words (RS1 full villain reveal)
- Adapt length to: market sophistication level + how far the product departs from conventional solutions

## NLS Type 10: Why It Works (Mechanism / Logic)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Dramatizes how the formula/product works from a mechanistic perspective — like a simple tutorial that conveys the underlying reason it works
- **Why It Exists:** Provides the logical answer to "how does it work?" so people can check that off the list
- **Best Practices:** (NLS notes this section as TBD but provides the framework)
  - Biochem or physics diagram/animation
  - Simple enough for non-experts to understand
  - Connects problem → mechanism → solution in a visual flow

### NLS Live References
- [Pendulum Life — Glucose Control](https://pendulumlife.com/pages/shop-pendulum-glucose-control-probiotic-blood-sugar-support) — Has biochem diagram; "not the best but it'll do"

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Section 5 (Roll Straight Technology)

**Copy Pattern:**
1. Name the TWO causes of the problem (equipment + human)
2. Acknowledge predecessor innovation (LAB Golf — "They deserve credit")
3. Name what predecessor LEFT unsolved ("But LAB solved one problem and left the other on the table")
4. Introduce the evolution ("The RS1 is the next step")
5. Explain the physics with an analogy ("Think of towing a trailer forward versus backing one up")
6. Position the category ("It's not neutral. It's helpful.")

**Key Analogy:**
> "Instead of pushing the face through impact (where it rotates open), the forward weight creates a pulling force — and pulled objects track straight. Think of towing a trailer forward versus backing one up. One tracks perfectly. The other jackknifes."

**Why This Works:**
- The trailer analogy converts complex physics into a universal experience
- Acknowledging the competitor (LAB) builds credibility — then positioning beyond them
- "Not neutral. Helpful." is a 3-word category creation
- The mechanism section POSITIONS the product, not just explains it

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- Must include a NAMED ANALOGY that converts complexity into universal experience
- Acknowledge predecessors where genuine — builds credibility AND sets up the "next step" frame
- The mechanism section does double duty: explains HOW and positions WHERE (category creation)
- Visual/diagram is critical — NLS emphasizes this is a visual-first section
- Category positioning statement at the end ("Not neutral. Helpful.") is the payoff

## NLS Type 11: Comparison Chart (Advantages)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Simple comparison of the product vs. the primary competitor (usually "Other Products" but can be specific)
- **Why It Exists:** Visually show superiority with key features/elements listed
- **Best Practices:**
  - **3 columns:** Feature label | Product | Comparison (don't do 3-4 comparison columns unless super competitive commoditized market with well-known brand names)
  - **Rows:** Critical features/advantages/benefits covering all major things people care about
  - **Primacy/Recency Effect:** Most important comparisons first AND last, least important in between
  - **Values:** Ideally simple checkboxes for product / X for comparison; can mix quantitative measurements or descriptions minimally
  - **Keep to 5-7 rows** for digestibility
  - **Optional icon** to left of feature that dramatizes it
  - **Product column** can have colored background to make it pop

### NLS Live References
- **NEAR IDEAL:** [FourSigmatic — Think Ground Coffee](https://us.foursigmatic.com/products/think-ground-coffee-bag)
  - Limits to one comparison (generic category: "Regular Coffee")
  - Mixes positive presence + negative absence attributes — everything becomes a checkbox
  - Mixes in words where needed (coffee quality, "100% Real Mushrooms"), leveraging primacy/recency
  - Primary USP ("Fruiting Body") positioned prominently
  - Only issue: reverse text (white on color) can be hard to read — use lighter background with black text
- **GOOD:** [Instant Hydration](https://instanthydration.com/products/premium-electrolyte-drink-mix)
  - Super competitive market → 2 comparisons (appropriate)
  - Nice highlighting of product column
  - Weakness: first four rows don't differentiate; should lead with truly unique feature ("Full Spectrum Electrolytes" since it has calcium)

### PG Implementation: ONE.1 — Competitive Comparison

| Feature | ONE.1 | Vokey SM10 | Cleveland CBX4 | PING ChipR | Smart Sole 4 | Square Strike |
|---------|-------|------------|----------------|------------|--------------|---------------|
| Sole Design | 4-way cambered | Standard grind | V/S/C grinds | Cambered | 3-tiered wide | Massive anti-chunk |
| Appearance | Traditional | Traditional blade | Traditional cavity | Chipper-like | Non-traditional | Non-traditional |
| Counterweight | 40g in butt | None | None | None | Feel Balancing | Toe-weighted |

**Why This Works:**
- 6 competitors shown — comprehensive market coverage
- Rows chosen strategically: ONE.1's differentiators appear first
- "Appearance: Traditional" is a stealth differentiator (competitors like ChipR look embarrassing)
- Factual, not promotional — facts do the positioning work

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- NLS standard: 3 columns (yours vs. 1 competitor). PG variant: 7 columns (yours vs. 6). Use NLS 3-column for most products; PG 7-column only when market is highly fragmented with known alternatives
- Lead with DIFFERENTIATING rows, not commodity features (Instant Hydration mistake)
- Primacy/recency: best differentiators first AND last
- Keep factual — let the checkboxes do the positioning work
- Product column visually emphasized (colored background or bold)
- 5-7 rows maximum

## NLS Type 12: Cost Savings Chart (Affordability)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Side-by-side comparison of cost of buying each individual ingredient/component vs. the all-in-one product price
- **Why It Exists:** To dramatize the value and cost savings easily
- **Best Practices:**
  - **3 columns:**
    - Column 1: Name of ingredient/component
    - Column 2: Product with checkboxes as values + price at bottom
    - Column 3: "Standalone Price" with individual prices + total at bottom
  - **Bottom punchline:** Centered "You Save $49" in styled starburst or rectangle

## NLS Type 14: Reviews & Ratings (Social Proof)

### NLS Framework
- **What It Shows:** Amazon-like user ratings with individual user reviews beneath
- **Why It Exists:** People mostly look for "how bad is it" vs. "how good is it" — they know negative reviews exist and want to confirm the downside is limited
- **Best Practices:**
  - **Amazon-style ratings breakdown** with horizontal bars (not just absolute rating — people want distribution)
  - **Curated UGC photo strip** that when clicked triggers a popover with full review + left/right navigation
  - **Individual user ratings** beneath the photo strip
  - **Formatting:** Must match page style precisely — no shrunken plug-in fonts; seamless with rest of page

### NLS Live References
- **BEST:** [Stonehenge Health — Dynamic Joint](https://stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint)
  - Has Amazon-style breakdown with horizontal bars
  - Image carousel: when clicked, triggers browsable popover (image + written review)
  - Formatted seamlessly with rest of page

### PG Implementation: 357 — Customer Reviews Section

**Format:** Named customer quotes with specific claims:
> "I hit two inches behind the ball and it still launched high and landed on the green." — Brad Armstrong
> "Feels like cheating. Missed a mile off the toe and still ended up almost pin high." — Mike Matzen
> "I'm 73 and hitting greens from 170 yards again." — Nick Leone

**Why This Works:**
- Each quote contains a SPECIFIC scenario (not "great product!")
- Quotes reference FEATURES by name ("That Bulldozer face")
- Age/distance specifics create reader identification
- Mix of emotional ("game-changer") and mechanical ("hits clean from Bermuda grass") quotes

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- NLS review section = design specification (histogram, photo strip, popover, formatting)
- PG review section = curated quote selection + formatting
- For copy generation: focus on SELECTING the best quotes and structuring the section
- Best quotes: specific scenario + feature reference + reader identification details (age, distance, context)

## NLS Type 17: Guarantee

### NLS Framework
(Guarantee is referenced throughout NLS but doesn't have a dedicated section — it appears in Product Highlights, CTA Box micro-trust signals, and thumbnail 10)
- **In Highlights:** Simple summary emphasizing key X benefit to be experienced within Y days
- **In CTA Box:** Include guarantee beneath CTA as micro-trust signal
- **As Thumbnail:** Part of the trust-building story arc

### PG Implementation: RS1 — Section 13 ("Love Your Game" Guarantee)

**Opening:**
> "Here's what we're not going to do. We're not going to ask you to trust a marketing page."

**Structure:**
1. Anti-marketing opening (what we won't do)
2. Named guarantee with time frame (365 days = full season)
3. Usage scenarios ("Take it to your Saturday game. Use it in your league.")
4. Specific failure conditions ("If you don't feel more confidence...")
5. Zero-friction return process ("No interrogation. No hoops. No guilt.")
6. Reframe as their PROOF opportunity ("You can prove it to yourself with zero risk")

**Why This Works:**
- Opens by REJECTING marketing language — builds trust through meta-honesty
- 365 days = "a full season" — frames guarantee in golf terms, not business terms
- Three specific failure conditions give the guarantee teeth
- Ends by reframing: guarantee is not a safety net, it's an invitation to prove it yourself

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
- Named guarantees that echo product language ("No Dig, No Doubt," "Love Your Game") convert better than generic
- Frame guarantee duration in PRODUCT USAGE terms (season, not days)
- Anti-marketing opening is a pattern — works because the guarantee section is where skepticism peaks
- Specific failure conditions > generic "if you don't love it"
- Reframe: guarantee = their experiment, not your risk transfer

- **What It Shows:** 3-column section showing different use cases/avatars
- **Why It Exists:** Broadens the market — tells the "warm" visitor "yes, this is for you, even if you're not the obvious user"
- **Example:**
  - Image 1 (Gym Rat): "For Strength & Reps."
  - Image 2 (Professional): "For Focus & Cognition."
  - Image 3 (Wellness/Longevity): "For Recovery & Vitality."

### NLS Live References

### PDP-07 Calibration Notes
