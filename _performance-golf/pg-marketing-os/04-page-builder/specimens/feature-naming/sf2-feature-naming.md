## Umbrella Architecture

```
SliceFix Technology (umbrella — 6 features under one roof)
├── Speed Geometry Toe Slot (hero — aerodynamic face closure)
├── Counter-Slice Weighting (CG manipulation)
├── Aero Crown Channels (aerodynamic guidance)
├── Draw-Calibrated Face Angle (pre-set geometry)
├── Draw Spin Face Bulge (mishit correction)
├── 445cc Speed Geometry Head (head shape)
└── Fast Face Construction (material advantage — resolves the OR/AND binary)
```

**Naming Architecture Note:** "Speed Geometry" appears in both the toe slot AND the head shape — creates a naming sub-family within the umbrella. "Draw" appears in Face Angle and Face Bulge — another sub-family. This is deliberate: naming sub-families group related features while the umbrella ("SliceFix Technology") holds them all.

## PDP-02 Calibration Notes

### What's Distinct About SF2 Naming
1. **Binary frame is the anchor** — Every feature name connects back to "AND not OR" (distance AND accuracy)
2. **"Speed" and "Draw" sub-families** within the umbrella create naming cohesion without repetition
3. **Dual naming exists** (Aero Crown Channels vs. Directional Control Channels) — shows a naming decision that hasn't been finalized; PDP-02 should force a single name
4. **7 features** is the max for this category — RS1 also has 7; 357 has 5; ONE.1 has 5
5. **Material claim in the umbrella name's modifier** — "3-Piece Forged Titanium" precedes "Slice-Fix Driver" in the full product name, establishing premium construction BEFORE the benefit category
6. **Counter-[Problem] naming pattern** — "Counter-Slice" is a pattern PG uses: name the thing you're fighting against. See also RS1's counter-positioning against "Face Drift"
