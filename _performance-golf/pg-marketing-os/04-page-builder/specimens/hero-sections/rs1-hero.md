## ATF Components

### Product Title
**"RS1 Putter with Roll Straight Technology"**
- Format: Product Name + "with" + Named Technology
- The "with" construction positions Roll Straight Technology as a distinguishing feature, not the product name

### Headline
**"Stop Fighting Your Putter. Start Flowing With It."**
- Word count: 9
- Formula: Stop [Pain] + Start [Desire] — binary opposition in two sentences
- Scroll-stop mechanism: "Fighting Your Putter" names the invisible frustration; "Flowing With It" names the desired state
- No mechanism name in headline — leads with FEELING, not technology
- Schwartz level: Stage 2-3 — names the feeling rather than the benefit or mechanism

### Subtitle
**"The first putter with Forward Axis Balance — designed to eliminate face drift and work with your natural stroke. Not neutral. Helpful."**
- Word count: 22
- Formula: First Claim + Named Mechanism + Problem Eliminated + Benefit + Category Positioning
- "Not neutral. Helpful." — 3-word competitive counter-positioning against LAB Golf
- Packs enormous information density: first claim, mechanism name, problem name, benefit, and competitive frame

### Price
**RS1 ($399) | RS1+ ($429)**
- Dual pricing in hero — shows the decision is WHICH one, not WHETHER to buy
- No strikethrough/discount (unlike 357) — premium positioning

### CTA
**"Add to Bag — Roll It Straight"**
- Format: E-commerce Standard + Product Tagline
- "Add to Bag" is e-commerce convention; "Roll It Straight" echoes the technology name
- 6 words total

## Analysis

### What Works
- **Headline leads with feeling** ("Stop Fighting. Start Flowing.") — doesn't require product knowledge
- **Subtitle does ALL the heavy lifting** — mechanism, problem, benefit, and competitive positioning in one sentence
- **10-thumbnail carousel** follows NLS framework almost exactly — definitive specimen
- **TLDR uses questions** — mirrors how the prospect actually thinks
- **"Not neutral. Helpful."** — 3-word competitive kill shot positions against LAB Golf without naming them

### What's Unique to RS1
- **Dual-tier pricing** ATF (RS1 vs. RS1+) — the decision frame is "which" not "whether"
- **Rules compliance badge** — unique to putters (regulatory concern)
- **Table Test** — a named proof mechanism IN the hero carousel (Thumbnail 1 and 8)

### Model for PDP-03
- The RS1 hero is the **gold standard NLS specimen** — use it to calibrate PDP-03's carousel planning
- The **question-based TLDR** is a template for highlights that match how prospects think
- The **"Stop [Pain]. Start [Desire]."** headline formula is a universally applicable template
- The **subtitle information density** shows how much positioning can be compressed into one sentence
