## ALGORITHM MECHANICS: HOW SAVES DRIVE DISTRIBUTION

### The Signal Hierarchy (2026)

1. **Shares/DMs** -- top discovery signal
2. **Saves** -- "super like"; signals future intent and high-value content
3. **Comments** -- signal discussion quality
4. **Swipes/Dwell Time** -- primary session duration currency
5. **Completion Rate** -- how many people reach the final slide
6. **Likes** -- passive; lowest weight

### The Re-Show Mechanic

Unseen carousel slides get re-shown to the same user in their feed. This creates multiple exposure opportunities per post -- a mechanic unique to carousels. Each re-impression is another chance for saves.

### The Music/Audio Distribution Hack

Adding music to a carousel pushes it into the Reels algorithm:
- Appears in the Reels tab with substantially greater reach
- Content becomes discoverable on dedicated audio pages
- Extended lifespan (weeks vs. days)
- Target trending tracks with under 5,000 uses

## OPTIMAL CAROUSEL ARCHITECTURE

### Slide Count Sweet Spots

| Use Case | Optimal Slides | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| Educational/Tutorial | 8-10 | Enough depth for value, not overwhelming |
| Deep Guides/Case Studies | 12-20 | Maximizes dwell time and save-worthy density |
| Quick Tips/Lists | 6-8 | Scannable, high completion rate |
| Quote/Wisdom | 3-5 | Brevity is the weapon |

### The 5-Pillar Slide Framework

| Position | Purpose | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Slide 1 | HOOK -- stop scroll, communicate value | Bold contrarian claim + visual incompleteness |
| Slide 2 | CREDIBILITY -- address skepticism | "Why this matters" / proof / "What you'll learn" |
| Slides 3-8 | VALUE DELIVERY -- the core content | One idea per slide, 15-30 words max |
| Slide 9-10 | PROOF/ENGAGEMENT | Data, testimonials, interactive elements |
| Final Slide | CTA -- explicit behavioral direction | "Save this for later" + specific next step |

### Mid-Carousel CTA Strategy

Place a soft CTA at Slide 5-6 ("Save this if it's useful so far") and the primary CTA on the final slide. Catches people who don't complete.

## COVER SLIDE HOOK PATTERNS (THE 6 PROVEN CATEGORIES)

### 1. Question Hooks (Brain Completion Instinct)
- "Do you really need a college degree in 2026?"
- "Are you making this common marketing mistake?"

### 2. Shock/Statistic Hooks (Attention Grabbing Numbers)
- "87% of side hustles fail in the first year. Here's the 13% that don't."
- "I made $48k in 30 days with this faceless Instagram strategy."

### 3. Promise Hooks (Specific Outcomes)
- "Save this post: 30 days of outfit ideas for work"
- "Steal my Notion template that runs my entire business"

### 4. Step-by-Step/List Hooks (Digestible Formats)
- "How to plan a 7-day Italy trip under $1500 →"
- "From broke to 6-figure freelancer in 9 slides"

### 5. Mistake/Myth Hooks (Calls Out Incorrect Practices)
- "The #1 reason your reels get zero views in 2026"
- "You've been doing X wrong your whole life."

### 6. Curiosity/Cliffhanger Hooks (Tease Reveals)
- "I got fired last week... here's what happened next →"
- "Wait until slide 7. You won't believe it."

### Cover Slide Design Rules

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Text Size | Minimum 70-100pt for main hook |
| Word Count | Maximum 12 words (ideal: 8-10) |
| Read Time | Under 0.7 seconds |
| Safe Zone | Top 60% of image; avoid bottom third |
| Contrast | Dark on light OR light on dark |
| Swipe Indicator | +15-30% swipe-through rate |
| Visual Incompleteness | Make cover feel incomplete without swiping |

## SAVE-DRIVING CONTENT FORMATS (Ranked)

| Rank | Format | Save Driver | Structure |
|------|--------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | Checklist/Audit | "I need this later" | 6-10 points with scoring key |
| 2 | Step-by-Step Tutorial | Reference material | Sequential instruction guide |
| 3 | Template/Script | Copy-paste utility | Ready-to-use frameworks |
| 4 | Tool Listicle | Discovery + utility | Tool per slide with description |
| 5 | Myth vs. Fact | Cognitive dissonance | Alternating myth/fact structure |
| 6 | Before/After | Proof of transformation | 3 levers that changed outcome |
| 7 | Framework/Mental Model | Reusable thinking tool | Named system with steps |
| 8 | Decision Tree/Flowchart | "Which path am I?" | Interactive visual decision aid |
| 9 | Comparison (X vs Y) | Identity alignment | Side-by-side with verdict |
| 10 | Data Deep Dive | Reference material | Multi-stat visualization sequence |

### The Fundamental Principle
**Saves happen when content has future utility.** Not inspiration. Not entertainment. Utility they will need again. Templates, checklists, scripts, decision trees, pricing ranges, scoring rubrics, screenshot-able slides.

## CTA STRATEGIES THAT DRIVE SAVES

### Direct Save Prompts
- "Save this for the next time you ___"
- "Bookmark this -- you'll need it when ___"
- "Save this post for your reference library"

### Send/Share Prompts (Also Drive Saves)
- "Send this to the person who handles ___"
- "Share with someone building [skill]"
- "Tag someone who needs this"

### DM Automation Prompts
- "DM 'CHECKLIST' and I'll send the template"
- "Comment 'GUIDE' and I'll DM the full version"

### Engagement Amplifiers
- "Which surprised you most?" (drives comments + revisits)
- "What did I miss? Drop it below" (drives comments)

### CTA Placement
- Soft CTA at Slide 5-6 (catches early dropoffs)
- Primary CTA on Final Slide (captures completers)
- Caption CTA (doubles the ask)

## VISUAL DESIGN PATTERNS

### Typography Rules
- Headlines: Bold + condensed sans-serif (Montserrat, Inter, Open Sans)
- Body text: Clean sans-serif at regular weight
- Limit: Maximum 3 fonts per carousel
- Minimum size: 24pt body, 70-100pt headlines
- Max characters per slide: 125

### Color and Contrast
- High contrast mandatory
- Brand color consistency across all carousels
- 60% empty space rule
- 4:5 aspect ratio (1080 x 1350px)

### Design Patterns by Visual Cluster

| Cluster | Key Elements | Example Creators |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|

### Seamless/Panoramic Designs
Create visual flow across slides = 2x higher swipe-through rates vs. disjointed decks.

### The Carousel Loop Trick
Design the last slide to transition visually back to the first slide for infinite loop effect.

## COMMON MISTAKES THAT KILL SAVE RATES

1. **No explicit save CTA** -- biggest miss
2. **Content that entertains but has no future utility** -- entertainment gets likes, utility gets saves
3. **Weak first slide** -- if hook doesn't stop scroll in 0.7 seconds, nothing else matters
4. **Text walls** -- exceeding 125 characters per slide
5. **No narrative arc** -- random tips without structure
6. **Inconsistent visual branding** -- signals low quality
7. **Passive final slide** -- ending with content instead of behavioral directive
8. **Padding slides** -- engagement drops beyond 13 slides if value isn't sustained
9. **Text in UI dead zone** -- bottom 30-40% covered by Instagram UI
10. **Not adding music** -- missing the Reels distribution hack
