## TRAJECTORY #001 — Sahil Bloom (@SahilBloom)

### Profile
- **Name:** Sahil Bloom
- **Niche:** Mental models, personal development, investing, frameworks
- **Starting Point:** ~1,000 followers (2020), former VC at Altamont Capital Partners
- **Current Size:** 1,500,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years
- **Notable:** One of the fastest-growing creator accounts in Twitter history. Cross-platform to LinkedIn (300K+)

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Thread Discovery (0-10K) — Months 1-6**
- Began tweeting frameworks and mental models from his VC background
- Discovered the "thread" format was dramatically outperforming single tweets
- First viral thread: "The Feynman Technique" (~50K likes)
- Content style: educational, framework-heavy, visual diagrams
- Growth rate: ~1,500 followers/month

**Phase 2: Thread Machine (10K-100K) — Months 6-14**
- Published 2-3 threads per week consistently
- Developed a recognizable formula: Named framework + visual diagram + step-by-step
- Each thread became a "micro-course" — 10-15 tweets of dense value
- Newsletter "The Curiosity Chronicle" launched, converting followers to subscribers
- Growth rate: ~10,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Cross-Platform Explosion (100K-500K) — Months 14-30**
- Content system became multi-platform: Twitter + LinkedIn + Newsletter
- Same frameworks, adapted for each platform's native format
- Brand partnerships began (6-figure deals)
- Speaking engagements at major conferences
- Growth rate: ~20,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Authority + Business Building (500K-1.5M) — Months 30-60**
- Launched SRB Ventures (VC fund) — audience became deal flow
- Content diversified: fatherhood, personal growth, not just business frameworks
- Revenue streams: newsletter sponsorships, speaking, VC fund, brand deals
- Growth rate: ~15,000 followers/month (plateauing at scale)

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 3-5 tweets/day + 2-3 threads/week |
| Primary format | Threads (60%), single tweets (30%), visual frameworks (10%) |
| Content pillars | Mental models, personal development, investing, fatherhood |
| Hook style | Named frameworks + "Here's how it works:" |
| Thread length | 10-15 tweets average |
| Signature move | Custom visual diagrams for each framework — proprietary and uncopiable |

### Engagement Patterns
- **Visual frameworks** are the growth engine — each diagram gets saved, screenshotted, and shared beyond Twitter
- **Newsletter as conversion hub** — Twitter drives traffic, newsletter captures the audience
- **Cross-platform synergy** — Same idea, native format per platform
- **Generous attribution** — Credits sources, which builds goodwill and reciprocal engagement

### Monetization Approach
1. "The Curiosity Chronicle" newsletter — large subscriber base, $5K-25K/sponsorship
2. Brand partnerships — 6-figure deals with major brands
3. Speaking engagements — $25K-50K+ per appearance
4. SRB Ventures (VC fund) — audience as deal flow and brand for founders
5. Courses and digital products (periodic launches)

### Key Lessons
- **Named frameworks are intellectual property.** When you name a concept with a visual, you own it in people's minds forever.
- **Threads are the long-game play.** Single tweets drive impressions; threads drive followers and newsletter signups.
- **Visual content is a moat.** Custom diagrams can't be easily copied — they require design skill that most creators lack.
- **Platform-native adaptation > cross-posting.** The same idea performs differently on each platform and must be reformatted.
- **Audience is an asset class.** Bloom literally built a VC fund on the back of his Twitter audience.

## TRAJECTORY #002 — Dan Koe (@thedankoe)

### Profile
- **Name:** Dan Koe
- **Niche:** One-person business, content creation, personal development, philosophy
- **Starting Point:** ~500 followers (2020), freelance web designer
- **Current Size:** 900,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years
- **Notable:** Built a $4M+/year one-person business entirely from organic Twitter content

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Freelancer to Creator (0-5K) — Months 1-10**
- Started tweeting about freelancing and building an online business
- Content was scattered — too many topics, no clear identity
- Growth was slow and painful
- The breakthrough: niching into "the one-person business" concept
- Growth rate: ~500 followers/month

**Phase 2: Identity Lock (5K-50K) — Months 10-24**
- "One-person business" became his identity
- Content formula: philosophical + practical (a unique blend on Business Twitter)
- Threads about building leverage, digital products, and modern work
- Newsletter "The Koe Letter" became a conversion engine
- Growth rate: ~3,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Product Launch Acceleration (50K-300K) — Months 24-40**
- Launched "Modern Mastery" course ($150) — massive revenue spike
- Each product launch generated weeks of content and engagement
- Expanded to YouTube (grew to 400K+ simultaneously)
- Content became more philosophical — "The Art of Focus" themes
- Growth rate: ~8,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Platform Domination (300K-900K) — Months 40-60**
- Multiple product lines: courses, coaching, community
- YouTube and Twitter feeding each other
- Content matured: less tactical, more philosophical/strategic
- Book writing ("The Art of Focus") creating new content vectors
- Growth rate: ~12,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 5-8 tweets/day + 1-2 threads/week |
| Primary format | Single tweets (70%), threads (20%), images (10%) |
| Content pillars | One-person business, focus/attention, philosophy of work, digital leverage |
| Hook style | Philosophical statements + contrarian takes on modern work |
| Signature move | Blending philosophy with practical business advice — "Nietzsche meets solopreneurship" |

### Monetization Approach
1. "Modern Mastery" course — $150-500 (primary revenue driver)
2. "The Koe Letter" newsletter — sponsorships
3. YouTube ad revenue (400K+ subscribers)
4. Coaching/mentorship programs (premium tier)
5. Book sales and speaking

### Key Lessons
- **Philosophy differentiates in a tactical sea.** Most Business Twitter is "do this to get that." Koe's philosophical lens makes him distinct.
- **Product launches are growth events.** Each launch creates weeks of anticipation content, testimonials, and social proof that drive organic growth.
- **YouTube + Twitter is the most powerful combination.** Short-form ideas on Twitter, long-form depth on YouTube. Each validates the other.
- **Single tweets can build a massive following.** You don't need threads — Koe's growth is primarily from standalone tweets. Volume + quality > format.

## TRAJECTORY #003 — Alex Hormozi (@AlexHormozi)

### Profile
- **Name:** Alex Hormozi
- **Niche:** Business, offers, entrepreneurship, fitness industry
- **Starting Point:** ~10,000 followers (2021), already successful entrepreneur ($100M+ portfolio)
- **Current Size:** 2,500,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~4 years on Twitter
- **Notable:** Multi-platform content machine. Grew from 10K to 2.5M in under 4 years

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Book Launch Catalyst (10K-100K) — Months 1-8**
- "$100M Offers" book created massive word-of-mouth on Twitter
- The book became the single most recommended business book on the platform
- Every reader became a promotional agent — sharing quotes, tagging him
- Content: short, punchy business truths (no threads)
- Growth rate: ~12,000 followers/month

**Phase 2: Content Blitz (100K-500K) — Months 8-20**
- Hired a content team to produce volume across all platforms
- Twitter style: 1-3 line punchy business insights, no fluff
- Quote tweets of his own content with commentary
- Second book "$100M Leads" further accelerated growth
- Growth rate: ~25,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Platform Celebrity (500K-2.5M) — Months 20-48**
- Became the most recognizable face of "Business Twitter"
- Content team producing 10-20 tweets per day across his accounts
- Podcast appearances driving massive cross-platform discovery
- YouTube shorts and Instagram reels creating discovery loops back to Twitter
- Growth rate: ~40,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 10-20 tweets/day (content team) |
| Primary format | Single tweets (90%), occasional images (10%) |
| Content pillars | Business growth, offers/pricing, entrepreneurship mindset |
| Hook style | Ultra-blunt, no-BS business truths in 1-3 sentences |
| Signature move | Extreme compression — entire business lessons in single tweets |

### Key Lessons
- **Books are the ultimate authority asset on Twitter.** "$100M Offers" did more for his Twitter growth than any content strategy.
- **Blunt truth-telling cuts through noise.** No hedging, no nuance — just direct business wisdom.
- **Content volume matters at scale.** 10-20 tweets/day from a content team ensures constant feed presence.
- **Cross-platform discovery loops are unstoppable.** YouTube → Twitter → Instagram → Podcast creates a flywheel where each platform feeds the others.
- **You don't need threads.** Hormozi's growth is 90%+ from single tweets. Compression > elaboration for his audience.

## TRAJECTORY #004 — Dickie Bush (@dickiebush)

### Profile
- **Name:** Dickie Bush
- **Niche:** Online writing, content creation, digital writing
- **Starting Point:** ~200 followers (2020), finance professional
- **Current Size:** 450,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years
- **Notable:** Co-founder of Ship 30 for 30. Built audience by teaching the platform ON the platform

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: The Ship 30 for 30 Experiment (0-5K) — Months 1-6**
- Committed to publishing an "atomic essay" (250-word piece) every day for 30 days
- Tweeted every essay, building discipline and testing topics
- Discovered that "meta-content" about writing online performed best
- Partnered with Nicolas Cole to formalize "Ship 30 for 30" as a course
- Growth rate: ~800 followers/month

**Phase 2: Course as Growth Engine (5K-50K) — Months 6-18**
- Each Ship 30 for 30 cohort (hundreds of students) created content that tagged and referenced him
- Students became an organic promotional army
- Content formula: "How to write online" threads and tips
- Newsletter "The Saturday Solopreneur" (with Justin Welsh) added conversion
- Growth rate: ~4,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Authority + Product Expansion (50K-200K) — Months 18-36**
- Recognized as THE "online writing" guy alongside Nicolas Cole
- Expanded to teach broader creator economy topics
- Typeshare (SaaS product for writers) launched
- Content became less frequent but higher quality
- Growth rate: ~5,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Established Creator (200K-450K) — Months 36-60**
- Revenue diversified: course, SaaS, newsletter, consulting
- Content pillars broadened beyond writing
- Growth rate: ~4,000 followers/month (steady, compounding)

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 3-5 tweets/day + 1-2 threads/week |
| Primary format | Threads (50%), single tweets (40%), images (10%) |
| Content pillars | Online writing, content strategy, solopreneurship, digital leverage |
| Hook style | "I [did X thing]. Here's what I learned:" |
| Signature move | Teaching online writing techniques via viral threads about writing |

### Key Lessons
- **Teaching the platform ON the platform creates a self-reinforcing loop.** People learning to write online share HIS writing tips — infinite organic distribution.
- **Cohort-based courses are growth accelerators.** Each cohort is a wave of new content creators who reference you.
- **Partnerships multiply reach.** Bush + Cole + Welsh = a network effect greater than any individual.
- **The "Ship 30" constraint was the breakthrough.** Forcing daily publication created the volume needed to find what works.

## TRAJECTORY #005 — Jack Butcher (@jackbutcher / @visualizevalue)

### Profile
- **Name:** Jack Butcher
- **Niche:** Visual communication, design, internet business philosophy
- **Starting Point:** ~500 followers (2019), failed freelance designer
- **Current Size:** 600,000+ followers (2025, combined personal + VV accounts)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~6 years
- **Notable:** Pioneered visual content on Twitter. Made millions from NFTs of his visual designs

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Visual Discovery (0-10K) — Months 1-8**
- Created "Visualize Value" — minimalist black-and-white visuals expressing business concepts
- Each visual was a single tweet: image + 1-line caption
- The visual style was radically different from anything else on Twitter
- First viral visual: "Productize yourself" — simple graphic, millions of impressions
- Growth rate: ~1,200 followers/month

**Phase 2: Visual Empire (10K-100K) — Months 8-20**
- Published 1-2 visuals per day, every day
- Each visual was independently shareable — people saved and reposted them constantly
- The style became instantly recognizable (black/white, minimal, conceptual)
- Launched "Build Once, Sell Twice" course
- Growth rate: ~7,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: NFT Phenomenon (100K-400K) — Months 20-34**
- Turned Visualize Value designs into NFTs — made $1M+ in first month
- NFT buyers became evangelists, sharing the visuals across crypto Twitter
- Cross-pollination between design Twitter and crypto Twitter created explosive growth
- The controversy around NFTs generated massive attention (both positive and negative)
- Growth rate: ~12,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Post-NFT Maturation (400K-600K) — Months 34-72**
- Pivoted from NFTs to broader internet business philosophy
- Content matured: more text-based, less purely visual
- Community (Check) became a revenue stream
- Growth rate: ~3,500 followers/month (post-hype normalization)

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 1-3 times/day |
| Primary format | Visual images (70%), single tweets (25%), threads (5%) |
| Content pillars | Business philosophy, leverage, visual thinking, internet economics |
| Hook style | Minimal text + powerful visual metaphor |
| Signature move | Black-and-white minimalist visuals expressing complex business concepts in one image |

### Key Lessons
- **A unique visual style IS the moat.** Butcher's visuals are instantly recognizable. Nobody else does this, so nobody else can compete in his lane.
- **Images get saved and reshared in ways text doesn't.** His visuals show up in pitch decks, blog posts, and presentations worldwide — each one driving discovery.
- **Ride waves but don't depend on them.** NFTs were a massive accelerator, but the underlying content quality survived the hype cycle.
- **Constraint breeds creativity.** Black-and-white, minimal, conceptual — the constraints defined the brand.

## TRAJECTORY #006 — George Mack (@george__mack)

### Profile
- **Name:** George Mack
- **Niche:** Mental models, psychology, creative thinking, cultural analysis
- **Starting Point:** ~2,000 followers (2020), marketing professional
- **Current Size:** 400,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years
- **Notable:** Became the "mental models" guy on Twitter through consistently brilliant thread craftsmanship

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Thread Experimentation (0-10K) — Months 1-10**
- Tested various content types: marketing tips, business analysis, random observations
- First major hit: "10 Psychological Razors" thread (~50K likes)
- Realized that mental model / psychology threads dramatically outperformed everything else
- Growth rate: ~800 followers/month

**Phase 2: Thread Mastery (10K-100K) — Months 10-28**
- Published 1 mega-thread every 2-4 weeks (quality over quantity)
- Each thread was meticulously researched and crafted (10+ hours per thread)
- Titles followed a pattern: "X Psychological/Mental [Concept] That Will Change How You Think"
- Threads were so good they got shared by massive accounts (Elon Musk retweeted one)
- Growth rate: ~5,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Cross-Platform Recognition (100K-400K) — Months 28-60**
- Threads became reference material — cited in newsletters, podcasts, YouTube videos
- Invited to major podcasts and speaking events
- Content frequency stayed low (1-2 threads/month) but engagement stayed high
- Growth rate: ~6,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 1-2 mega-threads/month + 3-5 tweets/day |
| Primary format | Long threads (80% of growth), single tweets (20%) |
| Content pillars | Mental models, psychology, contrarian thinking, cultural analysis |
| Hook style | "I spent X hours studying..." + numbered promise |
| Thread length | 15-25 tweets average |
| Signature move | Exhaustively researched, beautifully crafted threads that become reference material |

### Key Lessons
- **Quality trumps quantity when the quality is truly exceptional.** Mack posts 1-2 threads/month but each one is a masterpiece.
- **A single viral thread from a massive account can change your trajectory.** Elon Musk retweeting one thread accelerated growth by months.
- **Threads become evergreen reference material.** His "Psychological Razors" thread from 2021 still gets shared weekly in 2025.
- **The "I spent X hours" hook works because it's TRUE.** He actually spends 10+ hours per thread, and the quality proves it.

- "My First Million" podcast was growing rapidly
- Twitter became the distribution channel for podcast clips and ideas
- Content: business ideas, market analysis, startup stories

**Phase 2: Hot Take Machine (50K-200K) — Months 12-30**
- Became known for spicy, contrarian business takes
- Tweets were short, punchy, and often intentionally provocative

| Element | Detail |

### Growth Rate Benchmarks on X/Twitter

| Phase | Monthly Follower Growth | What's Happening |
|-------|------------------------|------------------|
| 0-5K | 300-1,000/month | Finding voice, testing content types |
| 5K-25K | 1,000-5,000/month | Content system established, first viral moments |
| 25K-100K | 5,000-12,000/month | Breakout phase, recognized in niche |
| 100K-500K | 8,000-25,000/month | Platform authority, content team often needed |
| 500K-1M | 10,000-40,000/month | Cross-platform celebrity, multiple revenue streams |

### X/Twitter vs. LinkedIn Growth Comparison

| Dimension | X/Twitter | LinkedIn |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Content velocity required | Higher (5-10 tweets/day) | Lower (1 post/day max) |
| Thread importance | Critical | Less important (single posts dominate) |
| Visual content impact | High (for some niches) | Medium (carousels dominate) |
| Controversy tolerance | High (rewarded by algorithm) | Medium (professional norms limit) |
| Monetization timeline | Faster (viral potential is higher) | Slower but more consistent |
| Audience quality for B2B | Mixed | Higher (professional context) |
| Newsletter conversion | Strong | Very strong |
| Cross-platform synergy | Essential (YouTube, podcast) | Optional (self-contained platform) |
