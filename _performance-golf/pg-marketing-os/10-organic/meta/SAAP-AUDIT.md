## STEP 2: AUTHORITY MAPPING (Per Sub-Skill)

### SUB-DOMAIN 1: AUDIENCE PSYCHOLOGY & BEHAVIOR

#### 1.1 Influence & Persuasion
```
CANONICAL AUTHORITY:
  → Name: Robert Cialdini
  → Work: "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" (1984, revised 2021)
  → Why canonical: Coined the 6 (now 7) principles of persuasion used by every
    marketing practitioner worldwide. Unity added in 2021 revision. The most-cited
    work in influence science applied to marketing.
  → What we extract: 7 principles (reciprocity, commitment/consistency, social proof,
    authority, liking, scarcity, unity), defense mechanisms, ethical application

SECONDARY AUTHORITIES:
  → Daniel Kahneman: "Thinking, Fast and Slow" — System 1/System 2 decision framework
  → Dan Ariely: "Predictably Irrational" — Specific irrational behaviors in consumer decisions
```

#### 1.2 Consumer Psychology / Decision Making
```
CANONICAL AUTHORITY:
  → Name: Eugene Schwartz
  → Work: "Breakthrough Advertising" (1966, Ch. 1-3 specifically)
  → Why canonical: The 5 levels of market awareness is THE framework for calibrating
    ALL marketing messages to where the prospect actually is. Every direct response
    marketer and content creator references this. Nothing has replaced it in 60 years.
  → What we extract: 5 awareness levels (unaware → most aware), sophistication stages,
    headline formulas per awareness level, mass desire channeling

SECONDARY AUTHORITIES:
  → Robert Cialdini: "Pre-Suasion" — Priming and attention channeling before the message
  → Rory Sutherland: "Alchemy" — Psychological framing and perception shifts in marketing
```

#### 1.3 Habit Formation & Behavior Design
```
CANONICAL AUTHORITY:
  → Name: Nir Eyal
  → Work: "Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products" (2014)
  → Why canonical: The Hook Model (Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment)
    is the standard framework for designing content loops that create return behavior.
    Directly applicable to recurring content consumption.
  → What we extract: Hook model 4-phase cycle, trigger types (internal vs external),
    variable reward types, investment mechanics, habit zone mapping

SECONDARY AUTHORITIES:
  → BJ Fogg: "Tiny Habits" — Behavior design fundamentals (B=MAP formula)
  → James Clear: "Atomic Habits" — Habit stacking, identity-based habit design
```

#### 1.4 Social Proof & Group Dynamics
```
CANONICAL AUTHORITY:
  → Name: Jonah Berger
  → Work: "Invisible Influence" (2016)
  → Why canonical: Goes beyond Cialdini's social proof principle to map the full
    mechanics of how others influence our choices — imitation, differentiation,
    and social signaling. Essential for understanding why audiences follow, share,
    and engage (or don't).
  → What we extract: Imitation vs differentiation drivers, social signaling framework,
    familiarity dynamics, peer influence mechanics, optimal distinctiveness

SECONDARY AUTHORITIES:
  → Robert Cialdini: "Influence" — Social proof as one of 7 principles
  → Malcolm Gladwell: "The Tipping Point" — Connectors, mavens, salesmen
```

#### 1.5 Market Awareness Levels (Prospect Sophistication)

  → Name: Eugene Schwartz
