# 1.3 Series Concepts

## Purpose
Design recurring content series CONCEPTS (WITHOUT naming) that build anticipation and consistency.

---

## Input Requirements
- Pillar concepts from 1.1
- Function mapping from 1.2
- Specimen series formats from 0.3

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Define Series Architecture Principles
**A strong series has:**
- Clear, repeatable structure
- Builds anticipation (people wait for next episode)
- Ownable format (only you could make this)
- Maps to business objective
- Appropriate frequency (weekly, biweekly, monthly)

---

### Step 2: Develop Series Concepts (1-3 series)
For each series, define the CONCEPT without naming:

```yaml
series_concepts:
  series_1:
    concept_id: "series_1"
    concept_description: |
      [What makes this series distinctive — the CONCEPT, not the name]

    format: "[Video/carousel/thread/etc]"
    platform: "[Primary platform]"
    pillar: "[Which pillar from 1.1]"
    function: "[Primary function: awareness/engagement/conversion/community]"

    structure:
      hook_pattern: |
        [How each episode hooks — the pattern, not specific hooks]
      body_pattern: |
        [What the middle looks like — the structure]
      close_pattern: |
        [How each episode ends — the pattern]

    frequency: "[How often: weekly/biweekly/monthly]"
    total_episodes: "[If limited] or 'Ongoing'"

    example_episodes:
      - episode_concept: "[Episode 1 concept]"
        hook_approach: "[Specific hook approach]"
      - episode_concept: "[Episode 2 concept]"
        hook_approach: "[Specific hook approach]"
      - episode_concept: "[Episode 3 concept]"
        hook_approach: "[Specific hook approach]"

    success_criteria:
      views_target: "[Per episode]"
      engagement_target: "[%]"
      series_growth: "[Episode-over-episode expectation]"

    business_objective: |
      [How this series supports revenue/goals]
```

---

### Step 3: Verify Series Design
Check each series concept against principles:

- [ ] Has clear, repeatable structure (hook/body/close patterns defined)
- [ ] Builds anticipation (example episodes show progression)
- [ ] Ownable (distinctive enough that only this creator could make it)
- [ ] Maps to business objective (revenue connection or strategic goal)
- [ ] Frequency is realistic (creator can sustain it)
- [ ] Aligns with pillar and function assignments

---

### Step 4: Example Episode Generation
For each series, generate 3 example episode concepts to prove structure works:

**Each example must show:**
- How the hook pattern applies
- How the body pattern applies
- How the close pattern applies
- Variation within consistency
