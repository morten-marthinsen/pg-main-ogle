# 1.2 Psychographic Depth

## Purpose
Go beyond demographics to understand psychology — values, interests, lifestyle, worldview. This is where audience intelligence becomes ACTIONABLE for content creation.

---

## Input Requirements
- demographic-foundation.yaml (from 1.1)
- specimens-loaded (from 0.3) for pattern reference

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Core Values Identification
```
IDENTIFY 3-5 values that drive decisions:

SOURCES:
  - What they celebrate in comments/posts
  - What they criticize/complain about (inverse values)
  - Purchase decisions they make
  - Brands/creators they follow

DOCUMENT:
  - Core value name
  - How it's expressed in behavior (observable evidence)

EXAMPLE:
  value: "Freedom"
  how_expressed: "Frequent complaints about 9-5 jobs, celebrate remote work, value flexibility over stability"
```

### Step 2: Interest Mapping
```
CATEGORIZE interests into 3 layers:

PRIMARY INTERESTS:
  - What they actively engage with most
  - Content they consume regularly
  - Communities they participate in

ADJACENT INTERESTS:
  - Peripheral topics they explore
  - Related but not core to identity

ANTI-INTERESTS:
  - What they explicitly reject
  - What they distance themselves from
  - (This is CRITICAL for voice/positioning)

EVIDENCE from:
  - Social media follows
  - Content engagement patterns
  - Reddit/forum participation
  - Comment section activity
```

### Step 3: Lifestyle Analysis
```
DETERMINE:

DAILY ROUTINE:
  - How they spend time
  - Work/life balance patterns
  - Time constraints

CONSUMPTION HABITS:
  - When they consume content
  - How long (micro-moments vs deep dives)
  - Device preferences (phone/desktop)

ASPIRATIONAL LIFESTYLE:
  - What they want their life to look like
  - Gap between current and desired state
  - What's blocking the aspiration
```

### Step 4: Worldview Mapping
```
EXTRACT:

BELIEFS:
  - What they believe about the world
  - Optimistic vs cynical tendencies
  - Trust in institutions/authority

ATTITUDES:
  - Stance on relevant topics to brand category
  - Political/social leanings (if relevant to niche)
  - Risk tolerance

TRIBE IDENTITY:
  - What group do they see themselves as part of?
  - Who is "us" vs "them"?
  - Identity markers they value
```

---

## Output
- **File:** `psychographic-depth.yaml`
- **Format:** YAML

```yaml
psychographics:
  values:
    core_values:
      - value: "[Value name]"
        how_expressed: "[Observable behavior]"
        evidence: "[Source]"
      # 3-5 values

  interests:
    primary_interests: []
    adjacent_interests: []
    anti_interests: []
    evidence_sources: []

  lifestyle:
    daily_routine: "[Description]"
    consumption_habits: "[Patterns]"
    aspirational_lifestyle: "[What they want]"
    current_gap: "[What's missing]"

  worldview:
    beliefs: []
    attitudes: []
    tribe_identity: "[Group they identify with]"
    us_vs_them: "[Identity boundaries]"

pattern_observations:
  strongest_psychological_driver: "[Primary motivation]"
  decision_framework: "[How they make choices]"
  emotional_baseline: "[Default emotional state]"
```

- [ ] Aspirational lifestyle vs current gap documented
- [ ] Tribe identity clearly defined
- [ ] All observations have evidence sources
