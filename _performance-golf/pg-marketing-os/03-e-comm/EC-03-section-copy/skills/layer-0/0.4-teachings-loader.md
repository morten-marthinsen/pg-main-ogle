## FUNCTION

Load and index source teachings from direct-response masters, organized by NLS PDP section type. Unlike skill-level teachings loaders, this loader is SECTION-TYPE-INDEXED — the generator looks up principles based on which section type (problem, mechanism, offer, proof, etc.) it is currently writing. This enables section-specific principle application across all 19 NLS section types.

## TEACHING SOURCES BY SECTION TYPE

### Problem/Agitation Sections (NLS Types: 2-Copy Block [problem variant], 9-Problem/Solution)

**John Carlton — Empathy Architecture:**
- Enter the conversation already happening in the prospect's head — use THEIR words, not yours
- The problem must feel RECOGNIZED, not explained — "You've tried everything..." beats "Many people suffer from..."
- Pain hierarchy: inconvenience < frustration < fear < shame < hopelessness
- The best problem sections make the reader feel SEEN, not lectured
- Pain must be SPECIFIC — "Your back aches every morning when you reach for your shoes" beats "Back pain affects millions"

**Craig Clemens — Vindication Framing:**
- "It's not your fault" — reframe the problem as external, not personal failure
- The villain is a SYSTEM, INDUSTRY, or HIDDEN CAUSE — never the prospect
- Vindication creates psychological permission to try again
- Structure: [You've tried] → [It didn't work] → [Here's the real reason why] → [It wasn't you]

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Problem sections use Carlton empathy first (enter their conversation), then Clemens vindication (it's not their fault)
- Pull VOC quotes from context reservoir for "their words" (when available)
- Every problem section needs a specific, sensory pain description — not abstract suffering
- Proof element for problem sections: customer story that mirrors the pain

#### Winning Specimens — Problem/Agitation

**SPECIMEN 1: RS1 Putter — Face Drift Villain Reveal (8-Beat Pattern, ~350 words)**

> Standing over a 4-footer for par. You've read the line. You know the speed. You make your stroke — and you push it right.
>
> Your read was fine. Your speed was fine. So what happened?
>
> It's not your hands. It's not your nerves. It's the physics of your putter.
>
> It's called **Face Drift** — and 90% of your putt's starting direction comes from face angle at impact. Most putters have their center of gravity behind the face. That means through impact, the face is free to rotate open. Not a lot. Just 1-2 degrees. But at 10 feet, 1 degree of face rotation sends your ball 3 inches offline.
>
> You've tried new putters. New grips. YouTube drills. But the problem was never your stroke. The problem was your putter's center of gravity.

**Why this wins:** Opens IN the prospect's experience (Carlton empathy). Absolves them ("It's not your hands" — Clemens vindication). Names the villain ("Face Drift"). Backs vindication with a stat ("90%"). Redirects blame from self → equipment.

**SPECIMEN 2: ONE.1 Wedge — "The Consistency Crisis" (2-sentence extreme compression)**

> 84% of golfers use cavity-backed irons because they help you hit straighter, more consistent shots without perfect technique. But those same golfers use bladed wedges that punish anything but perfection.

**Why this wins:** Named problem ("Consistency Crisis"). Stat-first ("84%"). The contradiction IS the argument — two sentences, zero waste. Proves you don't need 350 words if the insight is sharp enough.

**SPECIMEN 3: NLS Pattern — Nutreance 3-Section Rhythm (Problem → Real Cause → Solution)**

1. **Paradigm Shift:** "It's NOT what you think — the real cause of [problem] isn't [obvious culprit]"
2. **Reveal Real Cause:** "It's actually [hidden mechanism/villain]"
3. **Introduce New Solution:** Product as the answer to the REAL cause

**When to use which:** RS1 8-beat for sophisticated audiences who need vindication; ONE.1 2-sentence for products with a single sharp insight; Nutreance 3-beat for unsophisticated audiences who need the paradigm shift.

### Mechanism/Technology Sections (NLS Types: 10-Why It Works, 7-Ingredients/Materials)

**Eugene Schwartz — Mechanization:**
- Name the process — a named mechanism is 10x more persuasive than an unnamed benefit
- Show the mechanism WORKING — the prospect should SEE the steps in their mind
- Match mechanism depth to sophistication: Level 3+ needs HOW, not just WHAT
- The mechanism must feel INEVITABLE — "Of course this works, because..."

**Todd Brown — E5 Mechanism Criteria:**
- Named: the mechanism has a proprietary label (from EC-01 feature names)
- Unique: the name signals differentiation from alternatives
- Credible: grounded in real science/engineering/ingredients
- Demonstrable: you can show/prove it works
- Ownable: competitors can't use the same name

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Mechanism sections MUST reference the EC-01 feature name (exact match)
- Show the mechanism in 3 steps or fewer — ecom is scanned, not studied
- Use "because" connectors: "[Result] because [mechanism step]"
- Proof element for mechanism sections: study, patent, ingredient source, or expert validation
- Design note: mechanism sections benefit from diagrams, icons, or process visuals

#### Winning Specimens — Mechanism/Technology

**SPECIMEN 1: RS1 Putter — Roll Straight Technology (Trailer Analogy)**

> There are two causes behind missed putts. One is human: you don't make a perfect stroke every time. The other is equipment: most putters fight your stroke instead of helping it.
>
> LAB Golf solved the first problem — they proved that a face-balanced, lie-angle-balanced putter eliminates natural face rotation. They deserve credit for that.
>
> But LAB solved one problem and left the other on the table. Their putters sit neutral. They don't fight you — but they don't HELP you either.
>
> The RS1 is the next step. Instead of pushing the face through impact (where it rotates open), the forward weight creates a pulling force — and pulled objects track straight. **Think of towing a trailer forward versus backing one up. One tracks perfectly. The other jackknifes.**
>
> It's not neutral. It's helpful.

**Why this wins:** Names TWO causes (positions comprehensively). Acknowledges competitor (credibility). The trailer analogy converts complex physics into universal experience. "Not neutral. Helpful." — 3-word category creation. The mechanism section does double duty: explains HOW and positions WHERE.

**SPECIMEN 2: ONE.1 Wedge — Auto-Glide Sole (Feature Block)**

> **Your Fat Shot Fix**
>
> The "Auto-Glide Sole" gives you Four-Way Forgiveness because instead of digging, its four-way cambered shape glides through the ground automatically — regardless of your attack angle. This means you make consistently clean contact and finish closer to the hole — even when you're in a bad lie or you hit way behind the ball.

**Why this wins:** Benefit headline ("Your Fat Shot Fix," not "Auto-Glide Sole Technology"). The "because" connector links mechanism to result. "This means..." bridge from mechanism to felt benefit. Ends with worst-case scenario ("even when you hit way behind the ball") that dimensionalizes.

**SPECIMEN 3: NLS Pattern — Pendulum Glucose Control (Biochem Mechanism)**

Structure: Problem → Ingredient → Mechanism → Felt Benefit in a simple visual flow
- "Stress Spike → L-theanine → Alpha waves → Calm Focus"
- "Blood Sugar Spike → Akkermansia → Gut Lining Repair → Stable Energy"

**When to use which:** RS1 analogy pattern for products with novel physics/engineering; ONE.1 feature block for products with multiple named features; NLS 4-step flow for supplements with biological mechanisms.

### Offer/Pricing Sections (NLS Types: 3-CTA Box, 12-Cost Savings, 16-Offer/Pricing, 17-Guarantee)

**Dan Kennedy — Risk Reversal:**
- The guarantee should be MORE prominent than the price — "Your confidence > your wallet"
- Risk reversal isn't just money-back — it's "what do you lose by trying?" The answer must be NOTHING
- Stack guarantees: 30-day money back + free returns + keep-the-bonus = irresistible
- Name the guarantee — "The No-Questions 60-Day Promise" beats "Money-back guarantee"

**Joe Sugarman — Price Anchoring:**
- Always establish the VALUE before revealing the PRICE
- Price anchoring works through comparison: "A single lesson costs $200. This gives you 100 lessons for $49."
- Break the price down: "That's less than $1 per day" or "Less than your morning coffee"
- Urgency must be REAL — fake scarcity is detected instantly and destroys trust

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Offer sections lead with VALUE, not PRICE — what they get, then what it costs
- Guarantee sections are a FEATURE, not legal fine print — give them copy weight
- Name the guarantee using EC-01 naming principles (caveman-benefit)
- Cost savings sections use specific comparisons (not "save money" — "save $47/month vs. brand X")
- Proof element for offer sections: value comparison chart, customer savings quote, or guarantee redemption stats

#### Winning Specimens — Offer/Pricing

**SPECIMEN 1: RS1 Putter — "Love Your Game" Guarantee (Anti-Marketing Opening)**

> Here's what we're not going to do. We're not going to ask you to trust a marketing page.
>
> Instead, we're going to ask you to trust YOUR experience.
>
> Take the RS1 out for a full season — 365 days. Take it to your Saturday game. Use it in your league. Bring it to your member-guest.
>
> If you don't feel more confidence standing over 4-footers... if you don't notice fewer pushed putts... if your playing partners don't ask what putter you switched to... send it back.
>
> No interrogation. No hoops. No guilt.
>
> We'll refund every penny.
>
> You can prove it to yourself with zero risk.

**Why this wins:** Opens by REJECTING marketing language — trust peaks in the guarantee section, so meta-honesty works hardest here. Named guarantee ("Love Your Game"). 365 days framed in golf terms ("a full season"), not business terms. Three specific failure conditions give the guarantee teeth. "No interrogation. No hoops. No guilt." — staccato rhythm. Final reframe: guarantee = their experiment, not a safety net.

**SPECIMEN 2: 357 Fairway Hybrid — Bonus Stack + Value Anchor**

> **Pin High Approach Shots Training** — $197+ value
> **Green Stopping Power Protocol** — $197+ value
> **Contact Control Masterclass** — $197+ value
> **PG App (Smart Scoring System)** — $97+ value
> **Equipment Coupons** — $197+ value
>
> **That's over $885 of bonuses** — yours FREE when you claim your 357 today.

**Why this wins:** Every bonus is NAMED (not "Bonus 1, Bonus 2"). Each has a dollar value. Each addresses a different benefit. "That's over $885" — always do the math for people. "Yours FREE when you claim" — possessive + urgency.

**SPECIMEN 3: NLS Cost Savings Pattern (MUD\WTR style)**

|---------|-----------|-----------------|

**When to use which:** RS1 anti-marketing guarantee for premium products where price anxiety is the barrier; 357 bonus stack for products with educational/digital bonuses; NLS cost savings for supplements/consumables where ongoing cost matters.

### Proof/Social Proof Sections (NLS Types: 5-UGC Carousel, 6-Expert Credibility, 14-Reviews, 19-Awards/Press)

**David Ogilvy — Specificity in Proof:**
- "At 60 miles an hour, the loudest noise in a Rolls-Royce comes from the electric clock" — specificity IS the proof
- Vague proof is worse than no proof — "Thousands of happy customers" convinces no one
- Expert credibility requires NAMED experts with SPECIFIC credentials
- Awards and press are only proof if the SOURCE is recognizable to the audience

**Gary Bencivenga — Proof Cascade:**
- Layer proof types: testimonial + statistic + expert endorsement + comparison = irresistible
- Each proof element should answer a DIFFERENT objection
- Proof should appear in the ORDER of objection priority (biggest doubt first)
- The best proof sections feel like a VERDICT, not a sales pitch

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Every proof element must be SPECIFIC — names, numbers, dates, credentials
- UGC carousel sections: real customer quotes with names and contexts
- Expert credibility sections: named expert + specific credential + specific claim
- Review sections: diverse reviews answering different objections (not 5 reviews all saying "great product")
- Awards/press sections: recognizable source names + specific achievement + date
- Design note: proof sections benefit from visual separation (cards, stars, badges, logos)

#### Winning Specimens — Proof/Social Proof

**SPECIMEN 1: 357 Fairway Hybrid — Customer Reviews (Specific Scenario Quotes)**

> "I hit two inches behind the ball and it still launched high and landed on the green." — Brad Armstrong
>
> "Feels like cheating. Missed a mile off the toe and still ended up almost pin high." — Mike Matzen
>
> "I'm 73 and hitting greens from 170 yards again." — Nick Leone

**Why these win:** Each quote contains a SPECIFIC SCENARIO (not "great product!"). Quotes reference FEATURES by experience ("That Bulldozer face"). Age/distance specifics create reader identification ("I'm 73"). Mix of emotional ("feels like cheating") and mechanical ("hit two inches behind the ball") quotes.

**SPECIMEN 2: RS1 Putter — Expert Endorsement (Chris McGinley, Designer)**

> "Forward weighting is our special magical sauce. Why hasn't anyone else done this? It makes too much sense."
>
> **Chris McGinley** — 25+ years designing wedges for the world's best golfers, including Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy

**Why this wins:** PERSON introduces the product, not the brand. Conversational, not polished — builds authenticity. "Why hasn't anyone else done this?" rhetorical question positions innovation as obvious. Named tour players provide borrowed credibility without endorsement claims.

**SPECIMEN 3: Stonehenge Health — Amazon-Style Reviews Section (NLS Gold Standard)**

Structure:
1. Amazon-style ratings breakdown with horizontal bars (not just "4.8 stars" — show the distribution)
2. Curated UGC photo strip with popover interaction (click → full review + left/right navigation)
3. Individual written reviews beneath

**Why this wins:** People look for "how bad is it" not "how good is it" — the histogram answers that. Photo strip creates FOMO. Formatted seamlessly with page (not a shrunken plug-in).

**SPECIMEN 4: HumanN SuperBeets — Expert Credibility Video (NLS Best)**

Structure:
1. Video thumbnail of expert in act of speaking (white coat where possible)
2. Full title + credentials (e.g., "XX School of Medicine")
3. Natural speaking, no canned scripts — splice takes if needed
4. Captions like UGC ad
5. Video stops when user scrolls away

**When to use which:** Specific scenario quotes for products with strong customer results; expert endorsement for products with named designers/formulators; Amazon-style for products with 500+ reviews; expert video for products with credentialed experts.

### Product Introduction Sections (NLS Types: 4-Product Highlights, 8-What to Expect, 13-How to Use, 18-Use Cases)

**Specificity + Benefit Pairing:**
- Every product detail must pair with a customer benefit — "[Feature] so you can [benefit]"
- "What to Expect" timelines must be SPECIFIC — "Day 1: ..., Week 2: ..., Month 1: ..."
- How-to-use sections reduce perceived complexity — make the product feel EASY
- Use case sections segment the audience — "Perfect for [persona A] who [specific situation]"

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Product highlights: use EC-01 feature names paired with one-line benefits (from micro-scripts)
- What-to-Expect: specific timeline with milestones — vague timelines are worse than none
- How-to-Use: 3-5 steps maximum, each step is one action + one sentence
- Use Cases: 3-4 distinct audience segments with specific scenarios
- Design note: product sections benefit from icons, step numbers, and visual timelines

#### Winning Specimens — Product Introduction

**SPECIMEN 1: RS1 Putter — Product Highlights TLDR (Q&A Format)**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| What is it? | A forward-weighted putter engineered to eliminate face drift — the hidden physics flaw in 95% of putters. |
| Who is it for? | Any golfer who's tired of blaming their hands for missed putts. |
| What makes it different? | Traditional putters fight you. LAB putters sit neutral. The RS1 actively helps. |
| What if it doesn't work? | 365-Day "Love Your Game" Guarantee. |

**Why this wins:** Questions match the prospect's actual mental model. Each answer is one sentence. "What makes it different?" is a competitive positioning micro-story in one sentence (3-way comparison). Scannable in under 10 seconds.

**SPECIMEN 2: ONE.1 Wedge — Feature Showcase (Ingredients/Features Block)**

> **FEATURE NAME:** Auto-Glide Sole
> **HEADLINE:** "Your Fat Shot Fix"
> **COPY:** The "Auto-Glide Sole" gives you Four-Way Forgiveness because instead of digging, its four-way cambered shape glides through the ground automatically — regardless of your attack angle. This means you make consistently clean contact and finish closer to the hole — even when you're in a bad lie or you hit way behind the ball.
> **VISUAL:** Hero Animation

**Why this wins:** Benefit headline leads ("Your Fat Shot Fix"), not feature name. "because" connector links mechanism to result. "This means..." bridge from mechanism to felt benefit. Ends with worst-case scenario dimensionalization. Animation reinforces the copy — visual + verbal working together.

**SPECIMEN 3: RS1 Putter — What to Expect Timeline (4-Phase Progression)**

| Phase | Milestone | What Happens |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| First Putt | Feel the weight | "The RS1 sits differently in your hands... you'll notice the face wants to hold square." |
| First Round | Stop thinking | "Somewhere around the 4th or 5th hole, you'll realize you haven't been thinking about your putting stroke." |
| First Month | Confidence builds | "Not because of one dramatic putt — because of a pattern. Fewer pushed putts. Fewer three-putts." |
| Three Months | Identity shift | "The RS1 is your putter now. You stop thinking about it. You just putt." |

**Why this wins:** 4 phases with time markers tied to PRODUCT USAGE (not arbitrary days). Progression: physical sensation → behavioral change → emotional confidence → identity shift. Each milestone is 3-5 sentences. Final phase answers the question prospects won't ask: "When does it become part of me?"

**SPECIMEN 4: NLS "Ingredient for Benefit" Naming Pattern (Supplements)**

Format: `"L-Theanine for Tranquility"` — ingredient name + microscript benefit
- Vertical layout, NOT accordion (unless 7+ ingredients)
- First item expanded as signal if accordion
- Each entry: 1-2 sentence description connecting ingredient → mechanism → felt benefit
- Visual: bring ingredient to life (flower it's sourced from, molecular structure, colored powders)
- Best: [Stonehenge Health — Dynamic Joint](https://stonehengehealth.com/products/dynamic-joint), [Healthy Bones Co](https://healthybonesco.com/products/collagen-peptides)

**When to use which:** Q&A format for TLDR/highlights; feature showcase for products with 3-5 named features; timeline for products with progressive benefits; ingredient format for supplements.

### Comparison Sections (NLS Type: 11-Comparison Chart)

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Compare against the ALTERNATIVE the prospect is currently using, not just competitors
- Always be truthful — false comparisons destroy trust faster than any other ecom tactic
- Comparison dimensions should match the prospect's actual decision criteria (not your strongest features)
- "Why this, not that" framing: lead with the dimension where you win biggest

**Application to Section Copy:**
- Comparison charts: 4-6 dimensions, always include a dimension where you DON'T win (credibility)
- Use check/X visual format — ecom comparison is SCANNED, not read
- Include one surprise dimension the prospect hadn't considered (differentiation)
- Proof element: objective third-party source for at least one comparison dimension

#### Winning Specimens — Comparison

**SPECIMEN 1: ONE.1 Wedge — Full Competitive Comparison (6 Competitors)**

| Feature | ONE.1 | Vokey SM10 | Cleveland CBX4 | PING ChipR | Smart Sole 4 | Square Strike |
|---------|-------|------------|----------------|------------|--------------|---------------|
| Sole Design | 4-way cambered | Standard grind | V/S/C grinds | Cambered | 3-tiered wide | Massive anti-chunk |
| Appearance | Traditional | Traditional blade | Traditional cavity | Chipper-like | Non-traditional | Non-traditional |
| Counterweight | 40g in butt | None | None | None | Feel Balancing | Toe-weighted |

**Why this wins:** Factual, not promotional — facts do the positioning work. "Appearance: Traditional" is a stealth differentiator (ChipR looks embarrassing, Smart Sole looks non-traditional). Rows chosen to lead with ONE.1's differentiators. 6 competitors = comprehensive market coverage.

**SPECIMEN 2: FourSigmatic — NLS Near-Ideal 3-Column Comparison (Coffee vs. Regular)**

| Feature | Think Coffee | Regular Coffee |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Organic | ✓ | ✗ |
| 100% Real Mushrooms | ✓ | ✗ |
| Jitter-Free | ✓ | ✗ |
| Adaptogens | ✓ | ✗ |
| Fruiting Body Mushrooms | ✓ | ✗ |
| Fair Trade | ✓ | ✓ |

**Why this wins:** Limits to ONE comparison column (generic category, not named competitor). Mixes positive presence + negative absence — everything becomes a checkbox. Primary USP ("Fruiting Body") positioned prominently. Includes a row where BOTH have a check (Fair Trade) — builds credibility by being truthful.

**When to use which:** ONE.1 multi-competitor table for highly fragmented markets with 3+ known alternatives; FourSigmatic 3-column for products competing against a generic category ("regular coffee," "other supplements," "traditional putters"). NLS standard: 3 columns. PG variant: 7 columns — use sparingly.
