## NLS 10-Thumbnail Hero Carousel Architecture

The NLS framework defines a 10-thumbnail carousel that tells a complete sales story visually. Each thumbnail answers a specific buyer question. This is the PRIMARY template for EC-02.

### Foundational Strategy
- Tell the story visually, quickly — like an Amazon PDP
- Select top 10 KSPs once PDP has been developed, turn into engaging visuals
- Include at least one product video (ideally thumbnail 1), overlaid with play button triggering a 30-second highlight reel
- Show first 5 thumbnails beneath the hero, large enough to act as teasers
- Easily visible arrows on either side of hero or mini-thumbs

### Best Practices (General)
- High fidelity to bring things to life
- Product in action
- Lifestyle imagery
- Delicious/taste/beauty to bring it alive where possible

### The 10-Thumbnail Story Architecture

**Thumbnail 1 — Outcome ("Why Am I Here Again?")**
- **What It Shows:** Clean product shot + primary outcome promise + play button overlay → 30-second "best of" overview video
- **Why It Exists:** Reconfirms the exact promise the user was pre-sold on and offers fastest path to re-belief
- **Example Copy:** "Calm Focus. All Day." / "Steady Energy. No Crash."
- **Example Visual:** Bottle or pouch centered on neutral/lifestyle background, bold outcome headline, subtle play icon (Amazon PDP behavior)
- **Live Reference:** [Kachava](https://www.kachava.com/products/shakes) (thumb 1) — no video overlay though

**Thumbnail 2 — Problem Signs ("Is This For Me?")**
- **What It Shows:** Visual checklist or ring of common symptoms, behaviors, or frustrations signaling the user may need this product
- **Why It Exists:** Creates instant self-identification and relevance BEFORE asking user to evaluate ingredients or mechanisms
- **Example Copy:** "Low Energy" | "Brain Fog" | "Jitters" | "Crash Later" | "Gut Issues"
- **Example Visual:** Lifestyle image with labeled callouts or badges around subject highlighting recognizable problem signs
- **Live Reference:** [PetLab](https://thepetlabco.com/products/probiotic-chews-dogs/optexp/whrfnd2) (thumb 5)

**Thumbnail 3 — Ingredients ("What's In It?")**
- **What It Shows:** Product image centered, surrounded by key ingredient visuals in circular layout, each paired with 1-2 word caveman-style benefit
- **Why It Exists:** Answers "what's in this" in human terms; makes ingredients feel real and intentional
- **Example Copy:** "Lion's Mane – Focus" | "Cordyceps – Stamina" | "Reishi – Calm" | "Turkey Tail – Gut" | "Shiitake – Immune"
- **Example Visual:** Product centered with ingredient photography/illustrations orbiting around it, subtle connectors pointing inward, benefit words under each
- **Live Reference:** [Ryze](https://www.ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee) (thumb 2)

**Thumbnail 4 — Solution Enjoyment ("What Do I Enjoy?")**
- **What It Shows:** Real-life usage moments communicating enjoyment, ease, and lifestyle integration
- **Why It Exists:** Builds desire by showing the lived experience of using the product, not just outcome or claim
- **Example Copy:** "Feels Good Every Day." / "Easy Morning Ritual."
- **Example Visual:** Lifestyle imagery of real person enjoying product in natural setting, optionally with subtle play overlay
- **Live Reference:** [Kachava](https://www.kachava.com/products/shakes) (thumb 3)

**Thumbnail 5 — How It Works ("Why It Makes Sense")**
- **What It Shows:** Clear 4-step flow linking relatable problem → specific ingredient → biological mechanism → felt benefit
- **Why It Exists:** Makes the solution feel logical and earned by showing a straight line from problem to benefit
- **Example Copy:** "Stress Spike → L-theanine → Boosts calming alpha waves → Calm Focus"
- **Example Visual:** Four simple panels or arrows in sequence, each labeled with one short phrase + minimal icon
- **Live Reference:** [TryCreate](https://trycreate.co/products/sour-peach-core-create-creatine-monohydrate-gummies) (thumb 6)

**Thumbnail 6 — What to Expect ("When Will I Notice?")**
- **What It Shows:** Simple 3-phase timeline showing how results typically unfold over time
- **Why It Exists:** Sets expectations, reframes subtle early signals as progress, prevents premature doubt
- **Example Copy:** "Days 1-3: Feel Subtle." → "Week 1-2: Feel Steady." → "Week 3+: Feel Change."
- **Example Visual:** Timeline with large time markers, short caveman labels, calm non-hype styling
- **Live Reference:** [Primal Queen](https://primalqueen.com) (in body) — decent framework but needs simplification; line to left with checkmarks gives timeline feel; anchor to specific milestones (1 week, 1 month, 1 year)
- **NLS Note:** More than 3 milestones isn't necessary for thumbnail. Focus on immediate, medium, long-term.

**Thumbnail 7 — How to Use ("Is This Easy?")**
- **What It Shows:** Simple 3-step instruction overview: dose, timing, method
- **Why It Exists:** Removes friction by making the habit feel easy and obvious on mobile
- **Example Copy:** "2 Pills." → "Morning." → "With Water."
- **Example Visual:** Numbered steps with large numerals: capsules next to bottle, sun/moon icon, glass-of-water icon
- **Live References:** [Kachava](https://www.kachava.com/products/shakes) (thumb 7), [Ryze](https://www.ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee) (thumb 4)

**Thumbnail 8 — Believability / Credibility ("Where's the Proof?")**
- **What It Shows:** One strong credibility anchor: study reference, expert endorsement, or substantiated claim
- **Why It Exists:** Signals seriousness and legitimacy — answers "why should I believe this works"
- **Example Copy:** "Clinically Studied Ingredients" / "Doctor Formulated"
- **Example Visual:** Medical professional in neutral setting, study citation graphic, or authoritative badge
- **Live References:** [IM8 Health](https://im8health.com/products/essentials) (thumb 3), [AG1](https://drinkag1.com/products/greens-powder-pouch) (thumb 7)

**Thumbnail 9 — Alternative Comparison ("Why Is This Better?")**
- **What It Shows:** Simple comparison table with product highlighted vs. typical alternatives, capped at 5 rows
- **Why It Exists:** Collapses the choice into an obvious verdict; reduces comparison shopping
- **Example Copy:** Rows like "No Jitters" | "Better Mood" | "Gut Support" | "Clean Ingredients" | "No Added Sugar"
- **Example Visual:** Three-column table: Feature | Our Product | Typical Alternative — checks and Xs, product column emphasized
- **Live Reference:** [FourSigmatic](https://us.foursigmatic.com/products/think-ground-coffee-bag) (in body)

**Thumbnail 10 — Testimonials ("Emotional Close")**
- **What It Shows:** Real user mid-use with play overlay → fast-cut testimonial montage
- **Why It Exists:** Transfers belief through social proof; closes the emotional loop with FOMO
- **Example Copy:** "This Changed My Life." / "I Finally Feel Normal Again."
- **Example Visual:** Authentic lifestyle shot of happy customer using product, captioned + play icon → 20-30 second testimonial video
- **Live Reference:** [Ryze](https://www.ryzesuperfoods.com/products/mushroom-coffee) (thumb 6) — NLS note: ideally single person close-up with power quote, video play overlay; when clicked brings testimonial to life then quick cuts into additional high-energy testimonials. That higher energy OMG factor creates FOMO.

## Below-the-Thumbnails ATF Components

### Facts Panel ("What Exactly Is In It?")
- **What It Shows:** Full Nutrition/Supplement Facts panel (left) + vertical stack of quality/diet icons (right)
- **Why It Exists:** Gives analytical buyers immediate verification; reassures casual shoppers with scannable quality cues
- **Why Here:** Nutrition/supplement facts are extremely important to a specific user set — placing it here gives prominent visibility as a "caption" to the carousel
- **Example:** Facts panel occupying left two-thirds, right-hand column of minimal quality icons (Vegan, Gluten Free, No Added Sugar, etc.)

### CTA / Offer Box
- Include free shipping, guarantee, fast shipping, etc. bar beneath the CTA
- For subscription products: [PetLab](https://thepetlabco.com/products/probiotic-chews-dogs/optexp/whrfnd2) — clean CTA box, nice toggle

## PG Headline Formula Inventory

| Product | Headline | Formula | Words |
|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| 357 | "THE FIRST-EVER SUPER 7-WOOD WITH TRI-FUSION TECHNOLOGY!" | Category Claim + Named Tech | 9 |
| ONE.1 (PDP) | Product title serves as headline | Product-As-Headline | 4 |
| ONE.1 (Sales) | "New: 'Auto-Correct' Wedge Glides Through Any Lie" | Novelty + Product Name + Core Benefit | 8 |
| RS1 | "Stop Fighting Your Putter. Start Flowing With It." | Stop [Pain] + Start [Desire] | 9 |

### Formula 1: Category Claim (357)
- Uses "FIRST-EVER" to create a new category
- Named technology in headline establishes mechanism immediately
- Best for: Products that genuinely create a new category
- Risk: If category claim is weak, headline falls flat

### Formula 2: Product-As-Headline (ONE.1)
- The product name ("Auto-Correct Wedge") IS the value proposition
- Supported by staccato pre-head ("NEVER. CHUNK. AGAIN.")
- Best for: Products where the name alone communicates the benefit
- Requires: Exceptional naming in EC-01

### Formula 3: Novelty + Benefit (ONE.1 Sales Page)
- "New:" signals freshness (Schwartz Stage 1)
- Combines product identity + core promise in one line
- Best for: Product launches where novelty is the scroll-stopper

### Formula 4: Binary Opposition (RS1)
- "Stop [X]. Start [Y]." — names the pain and desire in parallel
- No mechanism or product name in headline — pure feeling
- Best for: Products solving an emotional/frustration problem
- Requires: Strong subtitle to deliver mechanism and positioning

## Scroll-Stop Techniques

### Technique 1: Staccato Pre-Head
**"NEVER. CHUNK. AGAIN."** (ONE.1)
- Three words, separated by periods, all caps
- Names #1 pain and resolves it in 3 words
- Template: `[NEVER/STOP/END]. [PAIN WORD]. [AGAIN/NOW/EVER].`

### Technique 2: Named New Category
**"THE FIRST-EVER SUPER 7-WOOD"** (357)
- "First-ever" + new category name
- Creates curiosity: "What is a Super 7-Wood?"
- Template: `THE FIRST [SUPERLATIVE] [NEW CATEGORY NAME]`

### Technique 3: Binary Opposition
**"Stop Fighting Your Putter. Start Flowing With It."** (RS1)
- Names negative state + positive state in parallel
- Template: `STOP [CURRENT PAIN]. START [DESIRED STATE].`

### Technique 4: Product Name as Scroll-Stopper
**"ONE.1 Auto-Correct Wedge"** (ONE.1 PDP)
- The name itself is so benefit-loaded it stops the scroll
- Only works when EC-01 naming is exceptional

## Subhead Strategies

| Product | Subhead Strategy | Information Delivered |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 357 | Benefit stack + safety valve | What it does + "without changing your swing" |
| ONE.1 | Only claim + mechanism + safety valve | Differentiation + how it works + "even on bad swings" |
| RS1 | First claim + mechanism + problem name + competitive position | Everything the headline didn't say |

**Rule:** The subhead delivers everything the headline DIDN'T. Emotional headline → informational subhead. Informational headline → benefit-focused subhead.

## CTA Patterns

| Product | CTA | Type |
|---------|-----|------|
| 357 | "Hurry! Claim your 38% Discount + Get 5 Exclusive Bonuses" | Urgency + Offer Summary |
| ONE.1 | "GET MY ONE.1 WEDGE NOW" | Possessive + Product + Urgency |
| RS1 | "Add to Bag — Roll It Straight" | E-comm Standard + Tagline |

**Insight:** CTA urgency correlates inversely with price confidence — discount = urgency; premium = calm confidence.

**NLS CTA Standard:** "Add to Cart" or "Add to Bag" — standard terminology reduces cognitive load (Baymard). Full width. Visual state change on tap. Micro-trust signals beneath.

## EC-02 Calibration Summary

1. **NLS 10-thumbnail carousel** is the gold standard — each thumbnail answers a specific buyer question; the carousel IS a sales conversation compressed into 10 images
2. **Headline formula selection** depends on product type and market sophistication — no single formula wins
3. **Pre-heads** (staccato format) are optional but powerful scroll-stoppers — use for products with a single dominant pain
4. **Subhead must deliver what the headline doesn't** — emotional headline → informational subhead, and vice versa
5. **10-thumbnail carousel** follows strict NLS story architecture with specific buyer questions at each position
6. **TLDR highlights** in Q&A format (RS1) is the most scannable pattern
7. **Price ATF** when there's a discount or when dual-tier creates "which one" frame
8. **Named guarantees** echo product language and convert better than generic
9. **CTA urgency** should match pricing confidence — discount = urgency; premium = calm confidence
10. **NLS CTA rules:** Full width, standard terminology, visual state change, micro-trust signals below
11. **Facts panel** below thumbnails for analytical buyers (supplements/ingredient products)
12. **Video overlay** on thumbnail 1 is the fastest path to re-belief after ad pre-sell
