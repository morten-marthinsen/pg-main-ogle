**Sources:** ONE.1 micro-scripts, RS1 micro-scripts, 357 copy
**Purpose:** Pattern extraction for EC-04 calibration and EC-01 micro-script generation
**Extracted:** 2026-03-13

---

## Micro-Script Anatomy

A micro-script is a 1-3 sentence copy unit that:
1. Names a feature, benefit, or concept
2. Can stand alone (works outside its section context)
3. Can be used across multiple page locations (hero, section, thumbnail, ad)
4. Has rhythmic or rhetorical structure that makes it memorable

---

## The PG Micro-Script Taxonomy

Across ONE.1 (30+ scripts) and RS1 (55+ scripts), micro-scripts fall into 10 distinct categories:

### Category 1: A/B Equations
**Format:** "If A, then B" or "X, not Y" or "X AND Y"

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Weight forward, ball straight forward." | RS1 |
| "Fix the physics, fix the putting." | RS1 |
| "Square face AND natural stroke — not one or the other." | RS1 |
| "Less Grind, More Glide." | ONE.1 |
| "Glide Beats Grind." | ONE.1 |
| "Bad Swing, Good Contact." | ONE.1 |

**Craft Principle:** A/B equations create a false binary (or resolve one). The audience remembers the contrast because the brain naturally seeks patterns and oppositions.

### Category 2: Rule of Three
**Format:** Three parallel elements, escalating

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Aim it. Stroke it. Trust it." | RS1 |
| "See the line. Trust the weight. Roll it straight." | RS1 |
| "Pull, don't push. Flow, don't fight. Putt, don't pray." | RS1 |
| "Square It, Swing It, Stick It." | ONE.1 |
| "Stop, Drop, And Roll It Close." | ONE.1 |
| "Launch, Reach, And Stick More Greens." | 357 |

**Craft Principle:** Three beats is the minimum for a pattern. The third element often resolves or surprises. Verbs create action and urgency.

### Category 3: Staccato Fragments
**Format:** Short, punchy fragments separated by periods or line breaks

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "NEVER. CHUNK. AGAIN." | ONE.1 |
| "No Dig. No Doubt." | ONE.1 |
| "Not neutral. Helpful." | RS1 |
| "Less drift. Less doubt. Less three-putts." | RS1 |

**Craft Principle:** Staccato fragments work at scan speed. Each word earns its place. Periods create visual and rhythmic pauses that force the reader to absorb each word separately.

### Category 4: Named Compounds
**Format:** Two concepts fused into a branded name

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Fat Shot Fix" | ONE.1 |
| "Un-Chunk-Able" | ONE.1 |
| "Four-Way Forgiveness" | ONE.1 |
| "Face-Down Focus" | RS1 |
| "Forward-Weighted Freedom" | RS1 |
| "Physics-Powered Putting" | RS1 |

**Craft Principle:** Named compounds create proprietary-sounding concepts from common words. They're immediately comprehensible but feel engineered. Alliteration increases memorability.

### Category 5: Wordplay Clusters
**Format:** Variations on a single word or concept

| Specimen | Concept | Product |
|----------|---------|---------|
| "Sole Control", "Sole Mate", "Good For The Sole", "Heart And Sole", "Trust Your Sole" | "Sole" (double meaning: club sole + soul) | ONE.1 |
| "The putter that pulls its own weight. Forward." | "Pull" (physics + idiom) | RS1 |
| "Half your shots happen on the green. Make the other half of your bag jealous." | "Half" (stat + personification) | RS1 |

**Craft Principle:** Find the word in your product that has a double meaning. Then exhaust its variations. ONE.1 generated 6+ "sole" scripts — not all will be used, but the VARIETY gives creative options.

### Category 6: Stark Reminders
**Format:** Direct challenge to the prospect's current behavior/belief

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Your hands didn't miss that putt. Your putter's physics did." | RS1 |
| "You can't fix face drift with a grip change." | RS1 |
| "95% of putters have the same flaw built in. Yours probably does too." | RS1 |
| "84% of golfers use forgiving irons but punishing wedges." | ONE.1 |

**Craft Principle:** Stark reminders create cognitive dissonance. The prospect must resolve the tension — either dismiss the claim or investigate. When backed by a stat, dismissal is harder.

### Category 7: Whole Micro-Stories
**Format:** Complete narrative arc in 2-3 sentences

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "For 100 years, the weight sat in the back and the face flopped open. One small company finally asked: what if we moved it forward? That's the RS1." | RS1 |
| "Chris McGinley spent 25 years engineering putters. When he showed tour players the RS1, they said: 'Why hasn't anyone else done this? It makes too much sense.'" | RS1 |
| "You don't have a putting problem. You have a face drift problem. And every putter you've ever owned has had it built in." | RS1 |

**Craft Principle:** Micro-stories compress beginning → middle → end into 2-3 sentences. They work as standalone ad copy, email subject lines, or thumbnail overlay text. The RS1 has the richest micro-story library.

### Category 8: Rhyme
**Format:** End rhyme or internal rhyme

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Face down, birdie bound." | RS1 |
| "Weight in front, end the hunt." | RS1 |
| "Spin It To Win It." | ONE.1 |
| "Grip It And Chip It." | ONE.1 |

**Craft Principle:** Rhyme creates phonetic memory. These work as social media captions, ad hooks, and carousel overlay text. Keep to 4-8 words — longer rhymes feel forced.

### Category 9: Alliteration
**Format:** Repeated initial consonant sounds

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Physics-Powered Putting." | RS1 |
| "Square-to-Square Simplicity." | RS1 |
| "Counteracting Confidence." | RS1 |
| "Fat Shot Forgiveness." | ONE.1 |
| "Delicate Distance Control." | ONE.1 |

**Craft Principle:** Alliteration makes compounds sticky. Best used for 2-3 word feature or benefit names. Beyond 3 alliterative words, it sounds gimmicky.

### Category 10: Category Evolution
**Format:** "Old → Better → Best" progression

| Specimen | Product |
|----------|---------|
| "Old putters fought you. LAB stopped fighting. RS1 fights for you." | RS1 |
| "Traditional. Neutral. Evolution." | RS1 |
| "Not neutral. Not passive. Active." | RS1 |

**Craft Principle:** Category evolution positions your product as the next chapter in a story. It respects predecessors while claiming the next step. Works best when there IS a genuine predecessor to acknowledge.

**Rule of Thumb:** Generate 5-8 micro-scripts per named feature. For the hero feature, generate 15-20. Not all will be used — the volume gives creative options for hero, sections, thumbnails, ads, and emails.

## EC-04 Calibration Summary

1. **Micro-scripts are NOT section-bound** — they float across page locations and channels
2. **Generate by CATEGORY** (A/B equations, Rule of Three, etc.) not by feature
3. **Minimum 30 micro-scripts per product** for a robust library
4. **The hero feature gets 3-5x more micro-scripts** than supporting features
5. **Find the double-meaning word** in your product and exhaust its variations
6. **Stat-backed reminders** are the highest-converting format for ads
7. **Micro-stories** compress full narratives into 2-3 sentences — ideal for video scripts and email hooks
8. **Rule of Three** is the most versatile format — works in headlines, CTAs, thumbnails, and body copy
