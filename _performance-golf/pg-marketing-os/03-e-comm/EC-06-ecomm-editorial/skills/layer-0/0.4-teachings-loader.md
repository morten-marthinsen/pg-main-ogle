## FUNCTION

Load the 6 editorial lenses that define how e-commerce copy quality is evaluated. Unlike long-form editorial (which uses 6 copywriting master perspectives), e-comm editorial uses 6 FUNCTIONAL lenses because ecom quality is about scannability, structural correctness, and conversion mechanics — not prose mastery.

These lenses feed Layer 1 audit microskills (1.1-1.6) and Layer 2 revision with the principles and criteria for evaluation.

---

## THE 6 E-COMM EDITORIAL LENSES

### Lens 1: Scan Optimization

**Purpose:** Does the page work when scanned in 3-5 seconds?

**Principles:**
- **3-5 Second Comprehension Test:** A first-time visitor should understand WHAT the product does and WHY they should care within 3-5 seconds of landing on any section
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Information must have clear priority — what to read first, second, third
- **Bold Key Phrases:** The 3-5 most important phrases per section should be bolded so a scanner catches them
- **Short Paragraphs:** Maximum 2-3 sentences per paragraph on ecom. Anything longer is a wall of text on mobile
- **Bullet Points:** Whenever 3+ items are listed, use bullets — not embedded in prose
- **Whitespace:** Sections need breathing room. Cramming = losing

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: Every section passes 3-second test; clear visual hierarchy; key phrases bolded; no wall-of-text
- 7-8: Most sections optimized; 1-2 could be tighter; no major scan failures
- 5-6: Half the page scans well; half requires reading (not scanning)
- 3-4: Most sections read as paragraphs; minimal scan optimization
- 1-2: Page reads like a letter, not an ecom page

**Audit Microskill:** `1.1-scan-optimization-auditor.md`

### Lens 2: Proof Density

**Purpose:** Does every section have at least 1 specific proof element?

**Principles:**
- **1+ Proof Per Section:** No section should make claims without backing. "Improves performance" needs a stat, testimonial, or study
- **Specific Not Generic:** "Thousands of happy customers" is not proof. "87% of testers gained 15+ yards in 30 days" is proof
- **Visually Distinct:** Proof elements should be visually separated from copy — call-out boxes, star ratings, badges, quote blocks
- **Proof Type Diversity:** A page with 10 testimonials and no stats is under-proofed. Mix types: testimonials, statistics, expert endorsements, awards, comparisons, before/after
- **Source Attribution:** Every proof element needs a source — name, credential, publication, date

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: Every section has 1+ specific proof; proof types are diverse; sources attributed; visually distinct
- 7-8: Most sections have proof; 1-2 gaps; good diversity
- 5-6: Half the sections have proof; some generic; limited diversity
- 3-4: Most sections lack proof; claims unsupported
- 1-2: Page makes claims with no proof elements

**Audit Microskill:** `1.2-proof-density-reviewer.md`

### Lens 3: Feature Naming Consistency

**Purpose:** Are EC-01 feature names used exactly throughout the entire page?

**Principles:**
- **Exact Match:** Feature names from EC-01 must appear EXACTLY as defined — no paraphrasing, shortening, or synonym substitution
- **No Invention:** Editorial cannot introduce new feature names not in the EC-01 registry
- **No Degradation:** Common degradation: "HyperSpeed Face Technology" becomes "HyperSpeed face" (lowercase) or "the HyperSpeed technology" (paraphrased) or "advanced face technology" (genericized)
- **Consistent Frequency:** Hero features should appear in hero section + at least 2 BTF sections. Supporting features in their primary section + at least 1 cross-reference
- **Micro-Script Alignment:** When a feature is described in section copy, the description should align with EC-01's micro-script (not contradict it)

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: Every feature name exact match; consistent frequency; micro-script alignment; zero invention
- 7-8: Most names exact; 1-2 minor deviations (capitalization, article); no invention
- 5-6: Some paraphrasing detected; hero feature consistent but supporting features drift
- 3-4: Multiple names paraphrased or shortened; consistency broken
- 1-2: Feature names genericized or replaced; EC-01 registry ignored

**Audit Microskill:** `1.3-feature-naming-consistency-auditor.md`

### Lens 4: DR Principles (Direct Response)

**Purpose:** Does the page follow core direct-response conversion principles?

**Principles:**
- **Offer Clarity:** The visitor must understand EXACTLY what they're getting. No ambiguity about the product, quantity, or deliverables
- **Risk Reversal:** The guarantee must be prominent and specific — not buried in footer. Named guarantees outperform "money-back guarantee"
- **CTA Quality:** Every CTA is action-specific ("Get Yours Today", "Try Risk-Free") — never "Learn More" or "Submit" on an ecom page
- **Urgency Credibility:** If urgency is used, it must be REAL — limited stock, seasonal, limited-edition. Fake urgency ("Order in the next 10 minutes!") destroys trust
- **Value Stack:** Before showing price, stack the value — what they get, what it's worth individually, what alternatives cost, then the actual price
- **Objection Handling:** Every major buying objection should be addressed somewhere on the page (FAQ, guarantee, comparison, or inline)

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: Offer crystal clear; strong risk reversal; action-specific CTAs; real urgency if used; value stacked; objections addressed
- 7-8: Most DR principles present; 1-2 could be stronger; no major violations
- 5-6: Some DR principles applied; CTA or guarantee weak; urgency missing or generic
- 3-4: Page reads informational, not direct-response; weak CTAs; no risk reversal
- 1-2: No DR principles applied; page is a product sheet, not a sales page

**Audit Microskill:** `1.4-dr-principles-auditor.md`

### Lens 5: Design-Copy Integration

**Purpose:** Are design notes present, specific, and integrated with the copy?

**Principles:**
- **Layout Specs Present:** Every section must specify layout direction — single column, two-column, card grid, carousel, etc.
- **Mobile Behavior Defined:** Every section must specify how it behaves on mobile — stack, carousel, accordion, hide, etc.
- **Image/Video Placement:** Every section must specify what visual media appears and where — product shots, lifestyle images, icons, video embeds
- **Copy-Design Relationship:** Copy must be written WITH the layout in mind — a 3-column feature grid needs 3 equal-length feature descriptions, not 2 short and 1 long
- **Handoff Readiness:** A page builder (designer/developer) should be able to implement the section from the copy + design notes alone, without asking questions

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: Every section has layout + mobile + visual specs; copy fits the specified layout; ready for page builder
- 7-8: Most sections complete; 1-2 need minor detail; copy generally fits layout
- 5-6: Half the sections have design notes; others vague or missing; some copy-layout mismatch
- 3-4: Most design notes are vague ("nice layout"); copy written without layout consideration
- 1-2: Design notes missing or placeholder; copy-only output

**Audit Microskill:** `1.5-design-copy-integration-auditor.md`

### Lens 6: Mobile-First

**Purpose:** Does the page work on a phone viewport?

**Principles:**
- **Phone Viewport Readability:** All copy must be readable at phone width (375-414px) — no sentences that become 5+ lines on mobile
- **Touch Targets:** CTAs and interactive elements must be large enough to tap — minimum 44x44px implied by copy placement
- **Carousel Function:** If a section uses a carousel, the mobile carousel must show enough of the next slide to indicate swipability
- **Scroll Depth Economy:** Mobile users scroll more but read less per screen — front-load the most important sections
- **Text Size Hierarchy:** Headlines, subheads, body, and micro-copy must have clear size differentiation that survives mobile viewport
- **Image-to-Copy Ratio:** On mobile, images take full width — if the section is 80% image and 20% copy, that copy may be invisible on scroll

**Scoring Criteria:**
- 9-10: All copy phone-readable; touch targets adequate; carousels functional; scroll economy optimized
- 7-8: Most sections mobile-friendly; 1-2 need line length adjustment; no major failures
- 5-6: Some sections break on mobile; long paragraphs, tiny touch targets, or unclear carousels
- 3-4: Page designed desktop-first; mobile is an afterthought
- 1-2: Page would be unusable on mobile

**Audit Microskill:** `1.6-mobile-first-auditor.md`

## LENS INTERACTION MAP

The 6 lenses are not independent — they interact:

```
Scan Optimization ←→ Mobile-First
  (what scans well on desktop may not on mobile)

Proof Density ←→ Design-Copy Integration
  (proof elements need visual treatment to stand out)

Feature Naming ←→ Scan Optimization
  (feature names should be in bolded key phrases)

DR Principles ←→ Mobile-First
  (CTAs must be thumb-friendly on mobile)

DR Principles ←→ Proof Density
  (risk reversal IS a proof element)
```
