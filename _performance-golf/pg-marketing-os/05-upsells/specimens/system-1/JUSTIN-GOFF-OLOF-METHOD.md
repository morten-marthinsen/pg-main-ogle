## SOURCE 1: Justin Goff — The OLOF Method (Slide Deck)

### Core Framework: OLOF
- **O** — Offer (most important by far)
- **L** — Length
- **O** — Opening
- **F** — Format

### Key Claim
"GREAT upsells will convert at 30-50% on upsell #1"

### Biggest Mistake
Not planning the first upsell BEFORE creating the product. Upsell must be planned simultaneously with the front-end — not "what do we have laying around."

---

### O — OFFER (Most Important)

**Core Rule:** If your offer is NOT congruent with what they just bought, no amount of copy/video changes will get it to 30-50%.

**Top 3 Congruent Upsell Types:**

#### 1. Done-For-You Version
- **Credit Secrets:** $39 book on credit repair → Upsell: "The Accelerator" software ($97) that fills out 90% of the forms for you. "There's no other offer they could put in place of this one that would beat this."
- **Capital Syndicate:** $97 real estate syndicate course → Upsell: Done-for-you website template ($297, converts at 30%)
- **Blake Tinder Offer:** $37 Tinder dating course → Upsell: 200+ proven messages/opening lines ($97, converts at 40-50%). "The guys buying this course are NOT good at Tinder. They have no faith in themselves when it comes to messaging girls."

**Principle:** "Anytime you can do done-for-you on an info product I would do it cause it works like crazy"

#### 2. More of the Same
- **Supplement:** Bought 1 bottle → sell 3 more. Bought 3 → sell 6 more. Bought 6 → sell 6 more. "This seems very counterintuitive... But it works."
- **4Patriots Solar Charger:** Solar phone charger on FE → Full solar generator for $1,997 on upsell (premium version)
- **Taylor Welch/Chris Evans:** $7/month newsletter subscription → Get all past issues for $77 (more of the same)
- **Agora Newsletters:** $49 yearly subscription → Lifetime subscription ($350) + special videos, live event tickets, extra trades
- **Extension variants:** Monthly → yearly, yearly → lifetime

#### 3. Get Results Faster
- **Info → Supplement:** $49 blood pressure info product → Supplement that helps fix blood pressure faster (1-3-6 bottle option)
- **Keto Slow Cooker:** $9 recipe book → "Keto Fat Loss Accelerator Pack" ($37) — 30 days meal plans, non-slow-cooker recipes, keto snacks, IF guide

**Congruence Test Data (Anti-Aging Supplement):**
| Upsell Product | Conversion Rate |
|---------------|----------------|
| Fish oil supplement (inflammation angle) | 9% |
| Probiotic (feel better/energy angle) | 11% |
| Ebook (look 10-15 years younger) | 14% |
| Double-size bottle of what they bought | **44%** |

"The double-size bottle won not because of better copy, or video or anything like that… It won because it was the most congruent offer."

### L — LENGTH

**Key Insight:** "The best upsells are almost always 15-20 minutes long (if its a video)"

**Common Mistake:** Trying to get away with short copy because customer is "already bought in." FALSE. "You have to go through the full selling process with them."

**Split Test Data (Video Length on Upsell #1):**
| Video Length | Conversion Rate |
|-------------|----------------|
| 2:45 minutes | 12.3% |
| 7 minutes | 13.9% |
| 14:50 minutes | **17.3%** |

"We got a 39% boost in conversions simply by making the upsell VSL longer."

**Upsell Copy Structure (15-25 minute video):**
1. Opening — congratulate on purchase
2. Future Pace — talk about all benefits coming their way (3-5 minutes)
3. Present a new problem — "fixing your credit is hard, you might not do it"
4. Issues if they don't fix the new problem
5. Hit on benefits of what you're about to present
6. Present upsell offer
7. Price justification
8. Close — final pitch, reminder, restate benefits, one-time offer, special price

### O — OPENING ("Sticking the Sale")

**Core Principle:** Before pitching the next product, alleviate the anxiety they're feeling and make them FEEL they made a great purchase. "You have to stick the sale!"

**Bad Example (Credit Secrets):**
> "Did you know there are undercover forces in our government that don't want you to get credit? It's true. They want you to rely on them..."

"This opening jumps right into pitching the next product and does NOTHING to stick the sale. Result? The customer is confused."

**Good Example (Jay Deiboldt's Credit Secrets Upsell Opening):**
> "Bingo. You did it. You just made the smartest move of your life and you don't even know it, aren't you lucky. This video is short. Please watch it till the very end.
>
> Yes, you're lucky, as well as smart. Look at what just happened here. You came in a prisoner and your ball and chain are about to be permanently removed. You came in scared and now you're a hero to yourself and your family. You were getting beat up by creditors and now you're about to be the one beating creditors up.
>
> You knew it was bad. You were willing to admit it. You knew it was unfair and you decided to do something about it. You looked at the situation and you said, 'I'm mad as hell and I'm not going to take it anymore.' And from that moment on, your nightmare came to an end.
>
> So you didn't just buy a product, you saved your life. And, I mean it. There isn't a single aspect of your life that won't get better, when your credit is fixed. Your anxiety levels will normalize and a sense of calm will wash over you. The stress in your shoulders, back and neck will melt away as a result of all this.
>
> Your relationship with your spouse will flourish like it's your honeymoon all over again. After all, you'll have an unlimited line of credit. So instead of fighting over scraps, you'll both be bathing in the sun of financial freedom. Everything she asks for you, will be a yes. And when all that happens, your happiness will burst from your heart like a starburst between your teeth.
>
> And, those are just the personal benefits, that's not to mention the financial benefits. That's not to mention, how you soon will be able to get access to cash anytime you please, which can allow you to finally start living the life you truly deserve."

**Key:** "Getting the opening right is like getting the lead right on your VSL — it can lead to HUGE conversion boosts."

### F — FORMAT

**3 Options:** Text Only, Text & Video, Video Only

**Winner:** "Video only — where you force them to watch the video usually outpulls the other two by a significant margin"

**Mechanical Details:**
- Add-to-cart button pops under the video about 3/4 of the way through
- Super basic pages
- Headline does NOT make them think they're going to be sold something

## SOURCE 2: Halbert/Kennedy/Goff Upsell Formula (Podcast)

### Origin
Gary Halbert's "stick letter" formula → Dan Kennedy used it → Justin Goff taught a version → podcast host (Roy Furr) tested it.

### The Formula (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Congratulate + Flatter**
"Hey congratulations, you just made a brilliant decision. You are a smart, attractive, wonderful, underappreciated human being."

**Step 2: Reinforce Results**
"Because you've made this decision, here are all the results you're about to get" — reinforce the same results promised in the sales letter.

**Step 3: Make Them Feel Good (Extended)**
"You're awesome and incredible and the world is about to see your brilliance... you're about to be appreciated."

**Step 4: PS / Transition**
Throw in the upsell as a PS, or if longer format: first 1-2 minutes is all step 1-3, then transition.

**Step 5: Logical Sales Pitch**
"Since you're so smart, since you make such good decisions, here's something else..." — presented as the next logical step (more of same, or next logical problem to solve).

### Key Insight
"If you just go straight to the next offer, you can actually make them feel bad about the decision they just made — like 'oh shoot, that wasn't enough for this person, they're just scamming me.'"

### Where to Use
- 1-click upsell pages (immediately post-purchase)
- Confirmation emails
- Package inserts (Gary Halbert's original "stick letter")
- Any post-purchase touchpoint

## SOURCE 3: JD Vault — Credit Secrets Case Study

### Scale
- **Front-end:** $39 book
- **Total revenue:** ~$50 million
- **Total sales:** 500,000+
- **Traffic:** National television (coldest traffic — no targeting beyond channels)
- **Spokesperson:** Larry King

### Key Principles

#### "The Upsell Starts on the Front End"
"The more emotionally involved you get them on the front, the higher your take rates are gonna be."
- Longer VSL = higher AOV (guaranteed)
- Short sales processes = shitty buyers
- 35-minute VSL vs 12-minute Larry King page = $20 higher AOV just from process length
- Free + shipping creates worse buyers than $9.95 + free shipping ($12 AOV increase)

#### Novelty vs Anxiety Battle
"There is a battle going on in the customer's mind... Humans crave novelty. We want to get novelty to beat anxiety."

Anxiety on checkout page includes:
- Am I getting scammed?
- Is this gonna work for me?
- Am I gonna use it?
- Am I gonna get what I paid for?
- Is my data safe?
- What will my wife say?

**Secret Weapon:** "Imagine the possibilities" — override anxiety with opportunity/dopamine.

#### If They Don't Consume, They Cannot Convert
"Rule number one: if they don't consume, they cannot convert."

**Page Load Speed:**
- Originally 11-12 second delay on upsell load
- Reduced to <3 seconds → instant conversion increase
- 1-second slowdown = up to 22% conversion rate impact

**First 15 Seconds Are Everything:**
"The most important part of your sales process is the first 15 seconds they're on your upsell because you finally have their undivided attention AND there is money involved now."

**Consumption Directive:**
"Do this first: please watch this important new member update in full right now. Your access link will appear here at the end of your video." → **17% increase in conversion**

**DON'T give them a reason to leave:** Remove "your email is on its way" type messaging — "you should be giving them reasons to stay."

#### Engagement Data
- **Before optimization:** 40% avg engagement, 78% play rate, 59% still watching at 30 sec, 31% at price reveal
- **After optimization:** 80% avg engagement, 94% play rate, 92% still watching at 1 min, 75% at price reveal

### JD Vault's 5-Step Copy Formula: C-A-I-R-O (Congratulate-Amplify-Intrigue-Reason-Offer)

#### 1. Congratulate
"Most people have this down" but don't stop at one line.
- "You just made the second most important decision in your life. This one's going to be the first."
- "By being brave enough to take action, take that leap of faith, and become our newest member today, you're about to discover all the amazing benefits..."
- "Congratulations, you just took the first step toward freedom from [pain point], a step toward [main benefit], and a step that has already changed your life for the better in a way you don't even realize yet."
- **Open a loop** in the congratulations — don't just say "congrats, now buy this."

#### 2. Amplify (Future Pace Benefits of FE Purchase)
"They're still not sure they made the right decision. Oh did I do the right thing?"
- Use future pacing with all five senses
- Summarized version of benefits (not full FE treatment) but still dimensionalized
- "Paint a vivid picture of how life is going to be better now that they've bought your front end product"

#### 3. Intrigue (Insider Feeling)
"Make them feel like they found a page they shouldn't have."
- "Because you just landed on a page that was never part of the plan, never even supposed to exist, but by becoming the newest member of [blank] today, you now qualify for something less than 1% of 1% will ever get a chance to access."
- "Before you get in, I have something so important to share with you I literally stayed up all night making this video..."
- **Showmanship is essential** — "it's not just facts, it's not just benefits, you have to make this an experience"

#### 4. Reason Why (The Turn)
Short story-based reason why the upsell offer exists.
- "We get a lot of emails from our happy members but not long ago our team received one message we never expected, and it was this one single message that wound up becoming the very reason you're seeing this page right now."
- "When we first released [FE product]... they started calling our office asking us to do [the thing] for them... we did something we thought would stop them from asking... [created the done-for-you version]."
- **Cialdini "because" principle:** "It doesn't have to be a good reason, it just has to be a reason, because it has to have a reason to exist."

#### 5. Offer (Pitch Hard)
"This may be the very last time you will ever have their attention like you do now."
- "This offer is only available for new members... you will only see it once... this is only for members who are truly serious about [desired result]... those who understand the value of dramatically accelerating their results."
- Aggressive selling is OK if the product works and delivers results.

### Additional Upsell Tactics

**JD Vault's 4th Congruent Upsell Type:**
"Can you introduce a new problem that the thing they just ordered will now cause them?" → Upsell = solution to that new problem.
- FB ads course → You can run ads but you can't WRITE ads → Done-for-you ad templates

**Flexible Upsell Path as Asset:**
"A winning upsell path is an asset" — create it so it can be stapled onto any new front-end offer in the same market. Use developer scripts to skip upsells customers already bought.

### Split Test Data

**Bump Offer:**
- 14-day free trial into $29/month continuity vs $12.95 audiobook
- Day 1: Audiobook won (+$3.60/sale)
- But LTV: Free trial won (+$14/sale total)
- **"The real important number... is LTV"**

**Screen Cap Testing:**
- Tested 5+ variations of video thumbnail
- Removing headline, adding social proof, animated testimonial GIF, "click play now", bigger play button, "Step 1" framing
- Key finding: "The less it looks like another sales message, the higher it's going to convert"

**Price Point Testing (Upsell 1):**
| Price | Result |
|-------|--------|
| $97 | Baseline |
| $149 | Middle |
| $198 (2 payments of $97) | **Winner — highest total cash collected** |

"Five words added $38 to the upsell one average order value" (adding "two easy payments of")

Additional: "Regular price $197, yours today only $97" → +$7/order

**Downsell Price Testing:**
| Price | Result |
|-------|--------|
| $49 | Lower total |
| $99 (3 payments of $33) | **Winner — $6-7 more revenue per order** |

**Exit Intent on No-Click:**
- "Wait! Due to high television demand and limited availability, you must confirm that you're giving up your reserved upgrade" → Lift from urgency/scarcity

**Hardship Discount Test (Downsell):**
- Interactive: "Wait, you may qualify for a hardship discount" with text input field
- vs. Standard: "Wait, we don't want you to miss out, please accept this hardship discount"
- Standard won on conversion
- BUT: Collected 12,593 open-ended responses → word cloud → fed customer language back into downsell copy → **12% lift in downsell conversion**
- Top words: unemployed, no money, divorce, not working, disabled, can't afford, no job
