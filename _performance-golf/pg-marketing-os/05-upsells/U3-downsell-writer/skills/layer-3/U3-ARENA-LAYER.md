# U3-ARENA-LAYER.md — Downsell Writer (U3)

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Layer Position:** 2.5 (between Layer 2: ARO Draft Generation and Layer 4: Validation)
> **Type:** Multi-Perspective Generation + Judgment + Human Selection
> **Dependency:** Requires GATE_2 PASS (ARO draft complete, all 3 sections present, word count valid)
> **Output:** Selected downsell page candidate for validation
> **Arena Mode:** `generative_full_draft` — Competitors write COMPLETE downsell pages from scratch using upstream packages. Layer 2 draft = reference material, not template. See `~system/protocols/ARENA-CORE-PROTOCOL.md` for 3-round execution protocol.

---

## PURPOSE

Generate **downsell page candidates** through 7 competitors, each bringing a differentiated approach to reframing the declined upsell. The downsell must acknowledge the buyer's "no," reframe the offer from a genuinely different angle, and present a simpler alternative — all while maintaining congruence with the front-end mechanism.

**Why Arena for Downsells:**
- The reframe is the CREATIVE CHALLENGE — there are multiple valid ways to reframe any offer
- Different competitors naturally emphasize different reframe strategies
- The Acknowledge section can be calibrated differently (minimal vs empathetic vs matter-of-fact)
- Post-decline tone is delicate — multiple approaches ensure the right balance of warmth and directness
- Human selection ensures the downsell matches brand voice and emotional continuity

---

## ARENA COMPETITORS FOR DOWNSELL GENERATION

### 1. The Empathy Expert

**Approach:** Deep acknowledgment, genuinely validates hesitation. The Acknowledge section is the centerpiece — more empathetic, more validating than any other competitor. Creates emotional safety before reframing.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge section: 75-100w (longer than minimum), deeply empathetic, names the specific reason they might have hesitated
- Reframe section: gentle pivot, "actually, there's a version that might be even better for where you are right now"
- Tone throughout: warm, understanding, almost conversational — like a trusted friend suggesting an alternative
- Zero selling energy — pure relationship

**Signature Moves:**
- Opens with "I completely understand, and honestly..." (personal, not corporate)
- Names likely hesitation reason: "Maybe it felt like too much right now" or "Maybe the timing isn't right"
- Reframe positioned as BETTER for them, not a consolation: "Actually, for most people starting out, this is the smarter choice"

**Weakness to Watch:** Can be TOO soft — so much validation that the reframe feels like an afterthought. The Offer section must still be clear and decisive despite the gentle tone.

---

### 2. The Core Extractor

**Approach:** Strip to essential. "You don't need the whole thing — here's the one piece that matters most." Positions the downsell as the concentrated core of the upsell, not a lesser version.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Brief Acknowledge (50-60w) — efficient validation
- Reframe: identifies the SINGLE most valuable component of the declined upsell
- Positions downsell as "the essence" — concentrated value, not diluted product
- Language: "Just the quick-start" / "Just the core protocol" / "The one piece our fastest responders use"

**Signature Moves:**
- "Here's what we've found: 80% of the results come from just this one component"
- Names the core component specifically
- Makes the full upsell sound NICE-TO-HAVE while the core sounds ESSENTIAL

**Weakness to Watch:** Risk of making the upsell sound overpriced/bloated in retrospect (congruence damage). Must frame core as "the right starting point," not "the only thing that works."

---

### 3. The Angle Shifter

**Approach:** Completely different value angle. Doesn't extract a component — reframes the ENTIRE offer from a new perspective. "Actually, there's a better way to think about this for you."

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge: brief and matter-of-fact (50-60w)
- Reframe: the star of the page — new angle that makes the downsell feel like a DIFFERENT product, not a lesser one
- May use a different benefit emphasis, different use case, or different audience segment angle
- The downsell should feel like "huh, that actually makes more sense for me" — not "fine, I'll settle for this"

**Signature Moves:**
- "Let me suggest something different..."
- Reframe around a SPECIFIC use case: "If your main goal is [specific thing], this is actually the better choice"
- Positions downsell as specialized, not stripped down

**Weakness to Watch:** Highest congruence risk — the "different angle" can drift into a genuinely different product/mechanism. Must maintain same mechanism name and root cause.

---

### 4. The Value Calculator

**Approach:** Mathematical breakdown of downsell value. Per-day costs, savings vs upsell, value-per-dollar comparison. Makes the MATH of the downsell compelling.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge: brief (50-60w), transitions quickly to numbers
- Reframe: built around value comparison — "$X per day" / "$Y saved vs full version" / "Z% of the value at W% of the price"
- The decision becomes a math problem with an obvious answer
- Works especially well for Payment Plan reframe type

**Signature Moves:**
- Side-by-side: "Full program: $X. Core essentials: $Y. Same [mechanism], $Z less."
- Per-day breakdown: "That's less than $0.XX per day for the next 30 days"
- Value ratio: "Get 80% of the results for 30% of the investment"

**Weakness to Watch:** Can feel transactional/cold after the buyer just made an emotional decision. Needs warmth in Acknowledge to balance.

---

### 5. The Payment Architect

**Approach:** Same product, different commitment structure. Split payments, trial period, money-back-if-not-satisfied-in-X-days. Reduces the commitment barrier rather than changing the product.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge: focuses on commitment being the barrier, not the product
- Reframe: "What if you could try it with zero risk?" / "What if you could spread the cost?"
- Makes the barrier disappear rather than changing the offer
- Works when price (not product) was clearly the objection

**Signature Moves:**
- "What if the commitment felt smaller?"
- "3 easy payments of $X" / "Try it for 7 days, pay only if you love it"
- Repositions from "$X investment" to "$Y per month — less than [daily comparison]"

**Weakness to Watch:** Payment Plan reframe is the LEAST creative of the 4 types. Must add genuine reframe beyond just splitting payments — perhaps shorter commitment period, trial window, or money-back after first milestone.

---

### 6. The Format Flipper

**Approach:** Same content, different delivery. Audio instead of video. PDF instead of course. Quick-reference instead of comprehensive guide. Matches content to the buyer's preferred consumption style.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge: suggests the format might have been the barrier
- Reframe: "Same [mechanism] insights, delivered the way YOU prefer to learn"
- Positions format change as a FEATURE, not a limitation
- Works when the upsell was video-heavy, time-intensive, or complex

**Signature Moves:**
- "Not everyone learns the same way"
- "Get the same [mechanism] protocol in a format you can use anywhere"
- "15-minute audio version" / "One-page quick-reference" / "Done-in-a-weekend workbook"

**Weakness to Watch:** Format change alone may not feel like sufficient value. Must also adjust scope or emphasis to make the format change meaningful, not just a file-type conversion.

---

### 7. The Gentle Closer

**Approach:** Soft time sensitivity without pressure. "Before this page closes" framing, but with genuine warmth. The closest to a sales approach of all 7 competitors, but still firmly in post-decline territory.

**Downsell Generation Focus:**
- Acknowledge: brief, respectful, then transitions to "one more option before you go"
- Reframe: combines the best of other approaches with soft page-only positioning
- Includes legitimate scarcity element (page-only pricing, limited batch, session-only availability)
- Tone: warm urgency, not fear urgency — "I want to make sure you have every option before you finalize"

**Signature Moves:**
- "Before this page closes, I want you to have one more option"
- Page-only or session-only pricing
- Combines reframe with time-bounded availability
- Still clean binary with zero guilt in NO

**Weakness to Watch:** Highest risk of tone violation. "Soft urgency" can slide into regular urgency very quickly. The Critic should closely monitor this competitor for pressure language.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

**See `~system/protocols/ARENA-CORE-PROTOCOL.md` for the complete 3-round execution protocol.**

> **Effort Level:** All generation phases use `effort: max`. Critique uses `effort: high`.

This skill uses `arena_mode: generative_full_draft`:
- **Competitors write COMPLETE downsell pages from scratch**
- Layer 2 ARO draft = reference material, NOT a template
- 7 competitors generating independently
- Adversarial critique before scoring
- Targeted revision
- 3 rounds with learning briefs
- Post-arena synthesis: 2-3 phrase-level hybrids
- Human selection from 9-10 candidates

### Input Requirements from Upstream Layers

Each competitor receives:
- U0 handoff spec (position, pricing, reframe_type recommendation)
- U2 downstream handoff (declined upsell context: product, price, angle, bonuses, reframe_suggestions)
- Mechanism package (mechanism name, explanation)
- Layer 1.1 reframe selection (selected type + rationale + angle statement)
- Layer 1.2 congruence map (exact FE language)
- Layer 2 ARO draft (reference only)
- Soul.md (if available)

### Position-Aware Instructions

**Position 1 (Downsell after Upsell 1 decline):**
- Full ARO with balanced proportions
- 500-1000w target range
- Reframe has room to develop (buyer has more patience here than after Upsell 2 decline)
- Standard Acknowledge opening

**Position 2 (Downsell after Upsell 2 decline):**
- Compressed ARO — shorter everything
- 300-600w target range
- Buyer patience is VERY LOW — get to the point fast
- Acknowledge can be minimal (50w), Reframe must be CRISP, Offer immediate

## DOWNSELL JUDGING CRITERIA

| Criterion | Weight | Scoring Focus |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Reframe Quality | 40% | Is this genuinely a different angle? NOT just "cheaper." Does it feel like a thoughtful alternative, not a consolation prize? |
| Congruence | 25% | Front-end mechanism referenced by name, root cause language preserved, promise maintained (not replaced). |
| Tone | 20% | Acknowledging and warm, NOT selling. Zero guilt, zero pressure. Buyer feels respected, not manipulated. |
| Simplification | 15% | Is this SIMPLER than the upsell? Lower decision weight? Easier to understand? Cleaner offer? |

### Scoring Rubric (Per Criterion)

#### Reframe Quality (40%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Genuinely brilliant reframe — the buyer thinks "that's actually better for me." Different angle feels like a FEATURE, not a downgrade. Reframe type (Core Extract/Payment Plan/Lite Version/Different Format) is creatively executed. |
| 7-8 | Strong reframe — clearly different angle, well-executed. Feels like a genuine alternative, not a lesser version. |
| 5-6 | Adequate reframe — different angle identifiable but execution is generic. Feels somewhat like "same thing, less." |
| 3-4 | Weak reframe — mostly just a cheaper version of the upsell. Same angle, lower price. |
| 1-2 | No reframe — literally the upsell copy shortened with a lower price. Undifferentiated discount. |

#### Congruence (25%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Mechanism named. Root cause verbatim. Promise maintained. Language register consistent. Feels like the same brand. |
| 7-8 | Mechanism named. Root cause present with minor variation. Promise maintained. Consistent feel. |
| 5-6 | Mechanism named once. Root cause paraphrased. Promise present but loosely connected. |
| 3-4 | Mechanism generic reference. Root cause reframed. Promise feels different. |
| 1-2 | No mechanism reference. New language. Feels like different company. |

#### Tone (20%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Perfect acknowledgment — buyer feels respected and understood. Zero guilt. Zero pressure. The "no" was honored. Warm and human. |
| 7-8 | Strong acknowledgment. Mostly warm. Minor moments that could be read as pressure but don't dominate. |
| 5-6 | Mixed tone. Acknowledge is clean but Reframe drifts into selling. Some pressure in transitions. |
| 3-4 | Pressure present. Acknowledge is perfunctory. Reframe feels like a second pitch. Guilt implied if not stated. |
| 1-2 | Full sales pitch after a thin "I understand." Guilt-trip CTA. Buyer feels ambushed. Trust destroyed. |

#### Simplification (15%)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 9-10 | Dramatically simpler than upsell. Lower word count, clearer offer, easier decision. Feels like relief after complexity. |
| 7-8 | Noticeably simpler. Offer is clear and direct. Decision weight is lower. |
| 5-6 | Somewhat simpler but still feels like work to evaluate. Offer complexity not sufficiently reduced. |
| 3-4 | About as complex as the upsell. Doesn't feel simpler, just smaller. |
| 1-2 | MORE complex than upsell (e.g., confusing payment plan, unclear product scope). |

## ANTI-SLOP ENFORCEMENT (U3-SPECIFIC)

**Auto-Reject Phrases in Downsell Copy:**
- "Wait! Before you go..." / "Hold on!" / "Don't leave yet!" (pressure openers)
- "You're about to miss..." / "Most people regret not..." (guilt/FOMO)
- "revolutionary breakthrough" / "game-changing" / "cutting-edge" (slop)
- "imagine struggling with" / "picture your frustration" (pre-purchase PAS)
- "this changes everything" / "you'll never look back" (hyperbole)
- "limited time only" / "act now" / "don't miss this" (fear-based urgency)
- Generic mechanism references: "this system," "this breakthrough," "this method"
- "No, I don't want to [desirable outcome]" (guilt-trip CTA)
- "Are you sure?" / "Really?" / "Think about it..." (pressure in decline path)

## CRITIQUE-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

**What The Critic should particularly target in U3 Arena:**

1. **Guilt Leaking In:** Even subtle guilt ("But before you finalize your decision..." with implied pressure). The Acknowledge section is sacred — zero pressure means ZERO.
2. **Undifferentiated Reframe:** The downsell that's really just "same thing, less money." If the Critic can describe the reframe as "cheaper version," the reframe has failed.
3. **CAIRO Structure Appearing:** Any Congratulate, Amplify, or Intrigue sections. This is ARO, not CAIRO. Post-decline, not post-purchase.
4. **Congruence Breaks:** Especially watch for mechanism name disappearing in the Reframe section (where new angles can drift from FE language).
5. **Over-Proving:** Any more than 1 proof element. Downsells are about simplicity, not evidence stacking.
6. **Tone Shift in Reframe:** Acknowledge is warm, but Reframe slides into selling. The transition must maintain the acknowledging tone.

## CRITICAL REMINDERS

1. **ARO, not CAIRO.** Downsells Acknowledge, they don't Congratulate.
2. **Reframe is 40% of the score.** A beautifully written downsell that's just "cheaper upsell" LOSES.
3. **Zero guilt in Acknowledge.** This is non-negotiable. The most trust-sensitive copy in the funnel.
4. **Mechanism named.** Generic references = auto-reject.
5. **Max 1 proof element.** Simpler than upsell.
6. **300-1000w hard limits.** Position-aware: 500-1000 for Downsell 1, 300-600 for Downsell 2.
7. **Human selection is BLOCKING.**
8. **Price MUST be lower than declined upsell.**
9. **Clean binary choice.** Zero guilt in NO option.
10. **3 rounds mandatory.** No "good enough after Round 1."
