# 2.2 — Price Presentation Builder

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U3 — Downsell Writer
**Microskill:** 2.2 — Price Presentation Builder
**Layer:** 2
**Model:** opus
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (FE price, declined upsell price, downsell price, guarantee terms), `layer-1/1.1-reframe-selection.md` (reframe type — affects framing language), `layer-1/1.2-congruence-map.md` (promise maintenance for value framing), `layer-2/2.1-aro-draft.md` (for integration context)
**Output:** `layer-2/2.2-price-presentation.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Build the complete downsell price presentation for integration into the Offer + Choice section of the ARO draft. The price presentation anchors against the DECLINED UPSELL price (not the FE price — that is U2's anchor), presents the downsell price with contrast, includes per-day breakdown where applicable, frames savings vs the declined upsell, specifies the guarantee (mirror FE), and provides the final binary CTA with clean YES/NO options.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Extract pricing data** from `0.1-input-context.md`:
   - FE price: $[X]
   - Declined upsell price: $[Y] — this is the PRIMARY anchor for downsell pricing
   - Downsell price: $[Z]
   - Pricing cascade: FE → Upsell → Downsell
   - Guarantee terms from FE

2. **Validate the pricing cascade:**
   - Downsell price MUST be lower than declined upsell price. If $Z >= $Y, HALT. Pricing cascade is broken. Escalate to U0.
   - Calculate: savings = upsell price - downsell price
   - Calculate: savings percentage = (savings / upsell price) x 100
   - Calculate: downsell as % of upsell

3. **Build the 4-element price presentation:**

   **(a) Declined Upsell Anchor:**
   - Reference what they passed on and its price. This establishes the comparison frame.
   - Pattern: "You passed on [Upsell Product] at $[upsell price]..."
   - Alternative: "The [Upsell Product] — the full [description] — was $[upsell price]."
   - Keep it brief and factual. No guilt about passing. Just establish the price point for contrast.

   **(b) Downsell Price with Contrast:**
   - Present the downsell price in direct contrast to the declined upsell.
   - Adapt contrast language to reframe type:
     - **Core Extract:** "This gives you the essential [component] — the single most impactful piece — for just $[downsell price]."
     - **Payment Plan:** "Same [product], but spread across [X] easy payments of $[payment amount]."
     - **Lite Version:** "The [lite version] — everything you need to get started — is just $[downsell price]."
     - **Different Format:** "The [format B] version is available for just $[downsell price]."
   - The word "just" is earned here — the downsell price IS lower. Use it.

   **(c) Per-Day Breakdown (if applicable):**
   - Divide downsell price by a relevant time period (30 days, 60 days, 90 days — based on product usage period).
   - Pattern: "That's less than $[per-day] per day."
   - Compare to a familiar daily expense: "Less than a cup of coffee." / "About the cost of a parking meter."
   - Skip this element if per-day amount is not meaningfully small (e.g., $97 / 30 = $3.23 — worth including. $197 / 30 = $6.57 — borderline. $47 / 7 = $6.71 — skip).
   - Rule of thumb: include if per-day is under $3. Optional if $3-5. Skip if over $5.

   **(d) Savings vs Declined Upsell:**
   - Frame the downsell as a savings relative to what they declined.
   - Pattern: "That's $[savings] less than the [Upsell Product]" or "You save $[savings] — [X]% less than the full [upsell]."
   - Keep this factual, not manipulative. The savings are real — state them cleanly.

4. **Build the guarantee:**
   - Default: Mirror the FE guarantee. "Same [X]-day money-back guarantee applies."
   - If FE guarantee terms are available, match them exactly (duration, conditions, refund method).
   - If FE guarantee terms are NOT available, use: "Backed by our full money-back guarantee."
   - Do NOT invent guarantee terms. If unsure, use the generic version and flag for human confirmation.
   - Keep guarantee statement to 1-2 sentences. This is not the FE — no guarantee stacking.

5. **Build the binary CTA:**

   **YES option:**
   - Format: "Yes, I'll Take [Downsell Product Name] — Just $[Price]"
   - Must include: product name + price + action verb
   - The product name in the button reinforces what they are getting
   - The price in the button reduces surprise / friction at checkout

   **NO option:**
   - Format: "No thanks, I'll continue with just my original purchase"
   - Alternative: "No thanks, I'm happy with just the [FE Product Name]"
   - MUST be clean. Zero guilt. Zero shame. Zero manipulation.
   - The NO option affirms they still have their FE purchase — they are not losing anything.
   - **FORBIDDEN NO patterns:**
     - "No, I don't want to [desirable outcome]" — guilt-trips the decline
     - "No, I'm fine being [undesirable state]" — shames the decliner
     - "No, I'll pay full price later" — creates false urgency/scarcity
     - "No, I'd rather struggle with [problem]" — manipulates via problem awareness
     - Any version that makes clicking NO feel like a mistake

6. **Assemble the complete price presentation block** for integration into the ARO draft's Offer + Choice section.

7. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

- Downsell price MUST be lower than declined upsell price. This is non-negotiable. If pricing cascade is broken, HALT and escalate to U0. Do not proceed with inverted pricing.
- The price anchor is the DECLINED UPSELL price, not the FE price. U2 anchors against FE price. U3 anchors against upsell price. Different anchor, same technique.
- Per-day breakdown is OPTIONAL. Only include when the per-day amount is meaningfully small (under $3 recommended, $3-5 optional, over $5 skip). A per-day of $6.57 undermines the "it's nothing" framing.
- Guarantee mirrors the FE guarantee. Do not invent guarantee terms. If FE terms are unknown, use generic language and flag for human.
- Binary CTA is the CLEANEST in the entire funnel. The NO option must be cleaner than the upsell's NO option. Zero guilt, zero manipulation, zero shame.
- Do not over-engineer the price presentation. This is 100-200 words within a 300-1000 word page. Brevity is the point. One sentence per element, not a paragraph.
- Contrast language must match the reframe type. Core Extract contrast is different from Payment Plan contrast. Use the reframe-specific patterns in step 3(b).

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Pricing cascade broken (downsell >= upsell) | Price comparison in step 2 | HALT. Escalate to U0. Cannot fix pricing locally. |
| Anchor references FE price instead of upsell price | Anchor sentence mentions FE price as the comparison | Rewrite anchor to reference declined upsell price. FE price is U2's anchor, not U3's. |
| Per-day included when amount is too high | Per-day > $5 and included in presentation | Remove per-day breakdown. It undermines the "it's nothing" frame at high daily amounts. |
| Guilt-trip in NO option | NO text uses "don't want," "fine being," "pay full price," or shame language | Replace with: "No thanks, I'll continue with just my original purchase." |
| Guarantee invented | Terms stated that are not from FE or confirmed by human | Replace with generic: "Backed by our full money-back guarantee." Flag for human confirmation. |
| Price presentation too long | Block exceeds 200 words | Trim to one sentence per element. No paragraphs. Brevity is the point. |
