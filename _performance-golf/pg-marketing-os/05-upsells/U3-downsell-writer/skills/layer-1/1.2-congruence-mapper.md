# 1.2 — Congruence Mapper

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U3 — Downsell Writer
**Microskill:** 1.2 — Congruence Mapper
**Layer:** 1
**Model:** sonnet
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (FE mechanism, root cause, promise, product context), `layer-1/1.1-reframe-selection.md` (selected reframe type and angle)
**Output:** `layer-1/1.2-congruence-map.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Map the congruence thread from the front-end through to the downsell, ensuring the exact mechanism name, exact root cause language, and exact promise phrasing are preserved while the OFFER is reframed through the selected angle. The downsell reframes the OFFER, not the mechanism or promise. This is the critical distinction: the buyer already bought the mechanism. They already accepted the root cause. They said NO to the upsell's specific product packaging — not to the underlying logic.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Extract the congruence anchors** from `0.1-input-context.md`:
   - **Mechanism name** — the EXACT string, including articles and capitalization. If the mechanism is "The Metabolic Switch Protocol," record "The Metabolic Switch Protocol" — not "metabolic switching," not "the Metabolic Switch," not "MSP."
   - **Root cause language** — the VERBATIM phrasing from the front-end. If the FE says "hormonal disruption caused by environmental toxins," record that exact phrase. Not "hormone problems." Not "toxin-based hormonal issues."
   - **Promise statement** — the exact promise as stated in the FE. Not paraphrased, not elevated, not softened.
   - **FE product name** — exact, for reference in Acknowledge section callbacks

2. **Extract the declined upsell context** from `0.1-input-context.md`:
   - Upsell product name
   - Upsell angle (what value proposition was the upsell framed on)
   - Upsell promise extension (how the upsell extended the FE promise)
   - Understanding what was declined helps define what the downsell must NOT repeat

3. **Load the selected reframe type** from `1.1-reframe-selection.md`:
   - Reframe type (Core Extract / Payment Plan / Lite Version / Different Format)
   - Reframe angle statement (the specific creative brief)

4. **Map how congruence is MAINTAINED through the reframe:**
   - The mechanism name MUST appear in the downsell. It appeared in the FE, it appeared in the upsell. The downsell extends the same thread. Specify WHERE it should appear (Acknowledge section — "The [Mechanism Name] you just secured..." or Reframe section — "This gives you [Mechanism Name] in a [reframed format]").
   - The root cause language MUST appear verbatim at least once. Specify WHERE (typically in the Reframe section when bridging from what they have to what the downsell adds).
   - The promise is MAINTAINED, not replaced. The downsell offers a different PATH to the same promise, or a PARTIAL fulfillment of the same promise. Specify the relationship: "The FE promises [X]. The upsell extended that to [X+Y]. The downsell offers [X + core of Y] via [reframe type]."

5. **Define the congruence maintenance strategy:**

   For each reframe type, congruence works differently:

   **Core Extract:**
   - Congruence logic: "You already have [Mechanism Name] working on [root cause]. The [upsell] would have given you the complete system. This gives you just the [core component] — the single most impactful piece of that system."
   - The mechanism anchors to the core component. The root cause stays identical. The promise narrows to the component's specific contribution.

   **Payment Plan:**
   - Congruence logic: "You already have [Mechanism Name]. The [upsell] extends it with [extension]. Same product, just spread across [X] payments."
   - The mechanism, root cause, and promise are IDENTICAL to the upsell. Only the commitment structure changes. This is the highest congruence reframe.

   **Lite Version:**
   - Congruence logic: "You already have [Mechanism Name] working on [root cause]. The [upsell] was the full [X]-day program. This is the [Y]-day version — same mechanism, smaller scope, faster to start."
   - The mechanism stays identical. The root cause stays identical. The promise scales with scope: "results in [shorter timeframe]" or "the first [X]% of the transformation."

   **Different Format:**
   - Congruence logic: "You already have [Mechanism Name]. The [upsell] delivered [content] via [format A]. This gives you the same [content] as [format B]."
   - Mechanism identical. Root cause identical. Promise identical. Only delivery vehicle changes.

6. **Define what changes vs what stays:**

   | Element | FE | Upsell (declined) | Downsell |
   |---------|----|--------------------|----------|
   | Mechanism name | [exact] | [exact — same] | [exact — SAME. Never changes.] |
   | Root cause language | [verbatim] | [verbatim — same] | [verbatim — SAME. Never changes.] |
   | Promise | [exact] | [extended version] | [maintained or narrowed — never replaced] |
   | Product | [FE product] | [upsell product] | [DIFFERENT — this is what changes] |
   | Angle | [FE angle] | [upsell angle] | [DIFFERENT — per reframe type] |
   | Price | [FE price] | [upsell price] | [LOWER — always] |

7. **Flag any congruence risks:**
   - If the downsell product introduces concepts not present in FE or upsell → FLAG
   - If the reframe angle requires new language not in the congruence thread → FLAG
   - If the promise changes rather than narrows → FLAG

8. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

- Mechanism name must be the EXACT string from the front-end. Case-sensitive, full name, including articles. "The Metabolic Switch Protocol" is not matched by "metabolic switch" or "the metabolic method."
- Root cause language must be VERBATIM. Not "close enough." Not "similar phrasing." The exact words.
- The downsell reframes the OFFER, not the mechanism or promise. If the congruence map shows the mechanism name changing or the root cause being paraphrased, the map is wrong. Re-extract from canonical sources.
- Promise can be NARROWED (Core Extract, Lite Version) or MAINTAINED (Payment Plan, Different Format). It can NEVER be REPLACED with a different promise. "Lose 30 pounds in 90 days" can narrow to "lose your first 10 pounds in 30 days." It cannot change to "boost your energy levels."
- The congruence map is a BINDING reference for Layer 2 generation. Every string marked as "exact" or "verbatim" must appear in the generated ARO draft exactly as recorded here.
- Congruence is measured against the FRONT-END, not the declined upsell. The FE established the mechanism, root cause, and promise. The upsell extended them. The downsell maintains them. The FE is the canonical anchor.

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Mechanism name paraphrased | Congruence anchor does not match FE source exactly | Re-extract from `0.1-input-context.md`. Use the mechanism-package.json as canonical if sources conflict. |
| Root cause language reworded | Verbatim check fails against FE source | Re-extract exact phrasing. If multiple phrasings exist across sources, use the earliest canonical version. |
| Promise replaced instead of maintained/narrowed | Downsell promise introduces new outcome not present in FE | Revise downsell promise to maintain or narrow FE promise. Escalate to U0 if product does not support this. |
| Congruence map shows mechanism or root cause changing | LOCKED elements in the thread table show differences | These elements NEVER change across the funnel. If they differ, one source is wrong. Find canonical source. |
| New concepts introduced in downsell | Downsell product requires language not in congruence thread | FLAG as risk. Layer 2 must introduce new concepts through the lens of existing congruence anchors, not independently. |
