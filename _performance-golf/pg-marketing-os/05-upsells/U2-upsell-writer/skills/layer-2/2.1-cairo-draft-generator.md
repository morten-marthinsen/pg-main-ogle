# 2.1 — CAIRO Draft Generator

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U2 — 1-Click Upsell Writer
**Layer:** 2
**Microskill:** 2.1 — CAIRO Draft Generator
**Purpose:** Generate the full 500-2000 word upsell page following the CAIRO structure, using the generation blueprint from Layer 1 as the structural guide and the 5 Laws of Upsell as the tonal governor.

- `layer-1/1.4-structure-selection.md` (the generation blueprint — PRIMARY input, contains everything needed)
- `layer-0/0.3-format-classification.md` (format-specific structural requirements)

## PROCESS

1. **Read the generation blueprint** (`1.4-structure-selection.md`) completely. This file contains: CAIRO variant, word count allocation, opening pattern, transition mechanics, proof integration, price presentation template, congruence directives, and format directives. It is the single authoritative guide for this draft.

2. **Internalize the 5 Laws of Upsell** before writing a single word:
   - Law 1: This is NOT a sales page. The buyer already purchased. Post-purchase psychology.
   - Law 2: Congruence is everything. Every word connects to the front-end mechanism and promise.
   - Law 3: Speed kills. 500-2000 words total. Every extra word is friction.
   - Law 4: Yes-or-No architecture. This is a decision page, not a persuasion page.
   - Law 5: Descending commitment, ascending value. The ask is smaller than the FE; the perceived value is higher.

3. **Generate the CONGRATULATE section (target: per blueprint):**
   - Open with the selected opening pattern (congratulate, warning/confession, or insider reveal)
   - Name the FE product by name within the first 2 sentences
   - Name the mechanism by its EXACT name within the first 3 sentences
   - Create an emotional high — validate their intelligence, their decision, their timing
   - Open a loop at the end: "But before you go to your confirmation page..." / "But there's one thing that could make this work even better..." / "And I want to share one more thing with you..."
   - Word count: stay within the blueprint's C allocation (typically 50-100w)
   - DO NOT mention the upsell product yet. This section is about THEIR purchase, not your next offer.

4. **Generate the AMPLIFY section (target: per blueprint):**
   - Future pace the benefits of what they ALREADY BOUGHT (the FE product, not the upsell)
   - Use five senses: what will they SEE (scale number, mirror reflection, bank statement), HEAR (compliments, congratulations), FEEL (energy, confidence, relief), DO (activities they can resume or start), EXPERIENCE (moments with family, at work, in social settings)
   - Dimensionalize: not "you'll feel better" but "you'll wake up on a Saturday morning with the kind of energy you haven't felt since your twenties — the kind where you're out of bed before the alarm and actually looking forward to the day"
   - If proof element 1 is placed here (from blueprint), weave it in naturally: "And you'll be joining over 14,000 [audience] who have already experienced this exact transformation."
   - Build to a peak of excitement about the FE product's benefits
   - Transition to Intrigue using the blueprint's A → I transition mechanic
   - DO NOT sell the upsell here. This section sells the PURCHASE THEY ALREADY MADE.

5. **Generate the INTRIGUE section (target: per blueprint):**
   - Create the insider feeling. The buyer should feel they've been given access to something exclusive.
   - Use one of these proven patterns:
     - Insider access: "There's one additional element that our most successful [audience] have in common..."
     - Almost didn't share: "We almost didn't include this in the offer because the [FE product] works great on its own. But after seeing the data..."
     - Private knowledge: "This is something we've only shared with our [premium tier / coaching clients / founding members] — people who paid $[high amount] for exactly this guidance."
   - Build curiosity without revealing the upsell product yet. Name the CATEGORY, not the product: "a specific morning routine" / "one additional protocol" / "a complementary system"
   - Transition to Reason using the blueprint's I → R transition mechanic
   - Keep this section TIGHT. It is a bridge, not a destination. The Intrigue section is the shortest non-Congratulate section.

6. **Generate the REASON section (target: per blueprint):**
   - Tell a SHORT story explaining WHY the upsell exists. This is Cialdini's "because" principle — people comply at dramatically higher rates when given a reason, even a seemingly small one.
   - Story structure options:
     - Discovery story: "When we first developed [mechanism name], we noticed something unexpected. About [X]% of our users were getting results significantly faster than everyone else..."
     - Creation story: "Here's why we created this: After working with [X] people using [mechanism name], we kept hearing the same question..."
     - Personal revelation: "I wasn't going to offer this. But after seeing what happened when [X] of our users accidentally discovered..."
   - The reason must connect the FE mechanism to the upsell product with SPECIFIC, logical causation. Not "it's a great complement" but "when your [mechanism process] is primed BEFORE [usage context], the [mechanism name] activates [X]% faster and stays active [X] longer."
   - If proof element 2 is placed here (from blueprint), weave it into the story naturally.
   - NOW name the upsell product. It earns its introduction here, after the reason is established.
   - Transition to Offer using the blueprint's R → O transition mechanic

7. **Generate the OFFER section (target: per blueprint):**
   - Product name and one-sentence description
   - Bonus stack (from 2.2 — will be integrated after 2.2 runs; leave a marked placeholder if 2.2 has not run yet): `[BONUS STACK — from 2.2]`
   - Price presentation (from 2.3 — will be integrated after 2.3 runs; leave a marked placeholder if 2.3 has not run yet): `[PRICE PRESENTATION — from 2.3]`
   - Guarantee: mirror FE guarantee ("Same [X]-day money-back guarantee applies") or introduce upsell-specific guarantee (per blueprint)
   - Binary CTA:
     - YES: "Yes, Add [Product Name] To My Order — Just $[Price]"
     - NO: "No thanks, I'll pass on this special offer" OR "No thanks, I'll continue with just the [FE Product Name]"
   - NO guilt trips. NO manipulation. The NO option is a clean, respectful decline. Test: would you be comfortable clicking the NO option yourself without feeling shamed? If not, rewrite it.

8. **Apply format-specific treatment:**
   - If video_script → add stage directions, speaker attribution, visual cues, text overlays, timing marks per 0.3 spec
   - If text_page → add headline hierarchy (H1/H2/H3), CTA button formatting, paragraph breaks, bold emphasis per 0.3 spec

9. **Run word count check.** Total must be 500-2000. If outside range:
   - Over 2000: trim Reason (usually the longest section) and Amplify. Cut adjectives, compress stories, remove redundancies. Do NOT trim Congratulate (sets tone) or Offer (decision architecture).
   - Under 500: expand Amplify (more five-senses detail) and Reason (more story specificity). Do NOT pad with filler.

10. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

1. **Generate section-by-section, NOT freeform-then-label.** Each CAIRO section is written to its specific purpose and word count target. Do not write a continuous piece and then divide it into CAIRO sections after the fact.
2. **The buyer already purchased.** Do not write as if they haven't. No PAS structure. No problem agitation. No fear-based urgency. No "imagine your problem." The opening line assumes a completed purchase.
3. **Mechanism name must appear in Congratulate AND at least one other section.** "This system" / "this breakthrough" / "this discovery" are not acceptable substitutes. The EXACT mechanism name from the congruence map.
4. **Root cause language must appear verbatim** from the congruence map. Not paraphrased. Not summarized. The exact phrasing.
5. **Amplify sells the PURCHASE, not the upsell.** The Amplify section future paces what the FE product will deliver. It does NOT sell the upsell product. This is the most commonly violated constraint.
6. **Intrigue does NOT name the upsell product.** It names the category or describes the function. The upsell product earns its name in the Reason section.
7. **Binary CTA is mandatory.** Both YES and NO options. NO option has zero guilt, zero manipulation, zero shame. If the NO option makes the reader feel bad about declining, rewrite it.
8. **Tone is celebration + logic + extension.** Not conviction + urgency + persuasion. Scan the draft for any of: "limited time," "act now," "don't miss out," "you can't afford to," "imagine your problem." Flag and rewrite any instances.
9. **Word count 500-2000 is a HARD GATE.** No "just slightly over 2000." No "it's only 480 words but the content is strong." If outside range, fix it before proceeding.
10. **This is the reference draft for Arena, not the final output.** Arena competitors will generate their own CAIRO pages. This draft provides the structural foundation and content direction, not a template to be varied.
