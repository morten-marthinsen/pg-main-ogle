# 4.2 — Congruence Score

**Version:** 1.0
**Skill:** U2 — 1-Click Upsell Writer
**Layer:** 4
**Microskill:** 4.2 — Congruence Score
**Purpose:** Score the congruence between the upsell page and the front-end offer on a 1-10 scale across 5 sub-dimensions, determining whether the upsell reads as a natural extension of the purchase or a disconnected new pitch.

---

## INPUT

- Arena-selected upsell page (validated by 4.1 — all 7 gates PASS)
- `layer-4/4.1-validation-results.md` (confirmation that validation passed)
- `layer-1/1.1-congruence-map.md` (canonical congruence anchors: mechanism name, root cause language, promise, extension logic)
- `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md` (FE product details, voice register from soul.md)
- Front-end sales page or brief (if available — for language register comparison)

**PREREQUISITE:** 4.1 validation must be PASS on all 7 gates before running this microskill. If 4.1 has any failures, resolve those first. Do not score congruence on a structurally invalid page.

## PROCESS

1. **Score Sub-dimension (a): Mechanism Name Exact Match (2 points)**
   - Check: does the EXACT mechanism name from the front-end appear in the upsell?
   - **2.0 points:** Mechanism name appears 3+ times by exact name, including in Congratulate and at least 2 other sections. Fully integrated into the copy's logic, not just mentioned.
   - **1.5 points:** Mechanism name appears 2 times by exact name (meets minimum). Present but not deeply integrated.
   - **1.0 point:** Mechanism name appears once by exact name. Technically present but not structural.
   - **0.5 points:** Mechanism name appears but is slightly modified (e.g., "Metabolic Switch" without "The" or "Protocol").
   - **0.0 points:** Mechanism name is absent, replaced by a generic term, or a new mechanism is introduced.
   - Evidence: list every appearance with section location and surrounding context.

2. **Score Sub-dimension (b): Root Cause Language Preservation (2 points)**
   - Check: does the VERBATIM root cause language from the front-end appear in the upsell?
   - **2.0 points:** Root cause language appears verbatim at least once, AND the root cause is referenced contextually in at least one additional place (even if not the exact phrase).
   - **1.5 points:** Root cause language appears verbatim exactly once. No additional contextual references.
   - **1.0 point:** Root cause is referenced but paraphrased — close but not verbatim. The MEANING is preserved even if the WORDS are different.
   - **0.5 points:** Root cause is vaguely alluded to but not recognizably the same root cause from the FE.
   - **0.0 points:** Root cause is absent. A new/different root cause is introduced. The upsell addresses a different problem than the FE.
   - Evidence: quote the root cause instances and compare against the canonical version from 1.1.

3. **Score Sub-dimension (c): Promise Extension Logic (2 points)**
   - Check: does the upsell logically EXTEND the front-end promise (make it bigger, faster, more complete), or does it introduce a NEW promise?
   - **2.0 points:** Clear, specific extension logic. The upsell promise is explicitly framed as making the FE promise work better/faster/more completely. The buyer can see the logical chain: "I bought X to get Y. This makes Y happen better."
   - **1.5 points:** Extension logic is present but somewhat generic. "This makes everything work better" rather than a specific extension mechanism.
   - **1.0 point:** Parallel promise — the upsell has its own promise that doesn't contradict the FE but also doesn't explicitly extend it. Two separate value propositions that happen to coexist.
   - **0.5 points:** Weak connection. The upsell's promise is tangentially related to the FE but requires a stretch to see the extension.
   - **0.0 points:** New promise. The upsell introduces a completely different outcome unrelated to the FE promise. Congruence break.
   - Evidence: quote the congruence bridge sentence from the upsell and the extension logic from 1.1. Assess alignment.

4. **Score Sub-dimension (d): Language Register Consistency (2 points)**
   - Check: does the upsell "sound like" the same brand/writer as the front-end?
   - **2.0 points:** Indistinguishable register. If you read the FE and the upsell back-to-back, they feel like one continuous piece by the same voice. Sentence length patterns match. Vocabulary level matches. Formality level matches.
   - **1.5 points:** Consistent register with minor variations. Recognizably the same voice but with occasional tonal shifts (e.g., slightly more casual or slightly more formal than the FE).
   - **1.0 point:** Noticeable register shift. The voice is different enough that a reader might notice the change — but it's not jarring. Like a different author writing in a similar style.
   - **0.5 points:** Significant register mismatch. The FE is conversational and the upsell is formal, or vice versa. The reader would feel a clear tonal break.
   - **0.0 points:** Complete register mismatch. The upsell reads like it was written by a different brand for a different audience.
   - Evidence: quote 2-3 representative sentences from the upsell and compare with the FE voice register (from soul.md or front-end reference).

5. **Score Sub-dimension (e): Emotional Continuity (2 points)**
   - Check: does the upsell feel like "the next page" in a continuous experience, or does it feel like a new pitch?
   - **2.0 points:** Seamless continuity. The buyer's emotional state (excitement, trust, momentum) from the purchase carries directly into the upsell. The Congratulate section picks up where checkout left off. The overall experience feels like one transaction, not two separate encounters.
   - **1.5 points:** Good continuity with minor breaks. The emotional thread is mostly maintained but there's a moment where the upsell "resets" — briefly feels like a new pitch before reconnecting.
   - **1.0 point:** Partial continuity. The opening connects to the purchase but the middle sections drift into independent selling mode before the offer reconnects.
   - **0.5 points:** Weak continuity. The upsell references the purchase in the opening but otherwise reads as a standalone offer. The buyer's post-purchase emotional state is not leveraged.
   - **0.0 points:** No continuity. The upsell could appear on any page, at any time, to any buyer. No connection to the purchase experience.
   - Evidence: describe the emotional journey of the upsell page and identify where it connects to/disconnects from the purchase experience.

6. **Calculate total congruence score:**
   - Sum all 5 sub-dimension scores (max 10.0)
   - Apply thresholds:
     - **Score >= 7.0:** PASS — proceed to 4.3 (Output Packager)
     - **Score 5.0 - 6.9:** FLAG — congruence is weak. Return to Layer 2 with specific congruence constraints derived from the lowest-scoring sub-dimensions.
     - **Score < 5.0:** HALT — fundamental congruence mismatch. Escalate to U0 for strategy review. The upsell may be mispositioned or the wrong product for this front-end.

7. **Generate congruence improvement directives** (if score < 7.0):
   - For each sub-dimension scoring below 1.5, write a specific directive for Layer 2 revision
   - Example: "Sub-dimension (b) scored 0.5 — root cause language 'hormonal disruption caused by environmental toxins' does not appear. Directive: insert verbatim root cause phrase in Amplify section, sentence 3, replacing 'hormone problems.'"

8. **Write output file.**

## CONSTRAINTS

1. **4.1 validation must be ALL PASS before running this microskill.** Do not score congruence on a page that failed structural validation. Fix structure first, then assess congruence.
2. **Sub-dimension scores use the rubric exactly.** Do not award 1.7 or 0.3 — use the defined score levels (2.0, 1.5, 1.0, 0.5, 0.0). This ensures consistent, reproducible scoring.
3. **Score < 7.0 = FLAG, not ignore.** A score of 6.5 is not "close enough." It means the congruence has a measurable gap that the buyer may perceive. Return to Layer 2 with specific revision directives.
4. **Score < 5.0 = HALT.** Do not attempt to fix a fundamentally incongruent upsell through copywriting. The problem is strategic — the wrong product, wrong positioning, or wrong mechanism extension. Escalate to U0.
5. **Evidence is mandatory for every sub-dimension.** A score without evidence is an assertion, not a measurement. Quote the specific text that supports the score level.
6. **Improvement directives must be specific enough for Layer 2 to act on immediately.** "Improve congruence" is not a directive. "Insert exact mechanism name 'The Metabolic Switch Protocol' in Reason section paragraph 2, replacing 'this system'" IS a directive.
7. **This microskill runs AFTER 4.1 and BEFORE 4.3.** The execution order in Layer 4 is strictly sequential.
