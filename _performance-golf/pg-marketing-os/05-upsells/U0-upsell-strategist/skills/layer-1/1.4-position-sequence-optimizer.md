# 1.4 — Position Sequence Optimizer

**Layer:** 1
**Model:** opus
**Depends on:** 1.2 + 1.3
**Produces:** `layer-1/1.4-sequence-order.md`

---

## Purpose

Determine the optimal ordering of upsell positions. The sequence order affects conversion rates, buyer psychology, and total cart value.

## Sequencing Principles

### 1. Highest congruence first
The first upsell after purchase should have the HIGHEST congruence with the front-end. Buyer momentum is highest immediately after purchase — congruent offers convert at the highest rate.

### 2. Natural logical flow
Each step should feel like a natural continuation: "You got X... now here's what makes X work better... and here's the core of X if the full version isn't right for you right now."

### 3. Descending complexity
The first upsell can be the most complex (longer copy, bigger decision). Each subsequent offer should be simpler and faster to decide on.

### 4. Paired downsells follow their upsells
If buyer says NO to Upsell 1, they see Downsell 1 (a reframe of Upsell 1). They do NOT see Upsell 2 first — that breaks the narrative thread.

## Execution Steps

1. Read product mappings (1.2) and pricing cascade (1.3)
2. Score each potential sequence order:

### Sequence Scoring

| Criterion | Weight | How to Assess |
|-----------|--------|---------------|
| Congruence gradient | 35% | Does congruence naturally decrease through sequence? |
| Logical flow | 30% | Does each step naturally follow the previous? |
| Conversion likelihood | 20% | Does the sequence maximize expected revenue? |
| Narrative coherence | 15% | Can a story thread connect all positions? |

3. Test 2-3 sequence orderings if there are multiple upsells
4. Select optimal ordering with rationale
5. Document the final flow diagram
