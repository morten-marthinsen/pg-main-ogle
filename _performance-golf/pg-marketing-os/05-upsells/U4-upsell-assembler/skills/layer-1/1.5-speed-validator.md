# 1.5 — Speed Validator

**Skill:** U4 — Upsell Sequence Assembler
**Microskill:** 1.5 — Speed Validator
**Layer:** 1
**Model:** sonnet
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`, `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`, all loaded copy pieces (U1, U2, U3)
**Output:** `layer-1/1.5-speed-validation.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Calculate total reading time across all upsell sequence pages and verify compliance with the 4-minute maximum threshold. Speed is a core constraint of upsell sequences — Law 3 says "Speed kills" and every extra second of reading time reduces conversion.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Read `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`** for loaded pieces and their word counts (as reported by 0.1).

2. **Read `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`** for the ordered sequence and per-piece word counts (as calculated by 1.1).

3. **Independently verify word counts.** Do NOT rely solely on upstream-reported counts. For each loaded copy piece:
   - Read the actual copy file
   - Count words in the COPY BODY only — exclude:
     - YAML metadata headers
     - Section labels (## CONGRATULATE, ## OFFER, etc.)
     - Implementation notes
     - Downstream handoff data
     - Stage directions in video scripts (count only the spoken words)
   - Record the verified word count

4. **Calculate per-page reading time.**
   - Formula: word count / 200 wpm = reading time in minutes
   - 200 wpm is the CONSERVATIVE reading speed for sales copy (accounts for emphasis, re-reading key claims, scanning pricing)
   - Round to one decimal place

5. **Check per-page thresholds:**

   | Page | Word Limit | Time Limit | Rationale |
   |------|-----------|------------|-----------|
   | Order Bump | 200 words | < 1 minute | Appears during checkout — buyer is in purchase mode, not reading mode |
   | Upsell 1 | 400 words | < 2 minutes | Post-purchase — buyer has brief attention window. CAIRO structure must be tight. |
   | Downsell 1 | 200 words | < 1 minute | Buyer already declined once — shorter = better. Reframe must be fast. |

   **Exception:** If U2 format is `video_script`, the upsell word limit extends to accommodate spoken delivery (video scripts run longer than text pages because spoken pacing is different). For video scripts:
   - Upsell video script: up to 600 words (< 3 minutes at ~200 wpm spoken pace)
   - Check for EXTENDED_VIDEO flag from U0/U2 — if present, limit is 2000 words (per US-18 pattern: extended video scripts >2000w show +39% conversion in specimen data, but this is an exception, not the default)

6. **Calculate total sequence reading time.**
   - Sum all per-page reading times
   - Total threshold: < 4 minutes
   - If video_script format: total threshold adjusts to < 5 minutes (video pacing is different from text reading)

7. **If any threshold is exceeded:**
   - Identify which page(s) exceed their limit
   - Calculate the OVERAGE: how many words over the limit
   - Calculate trimming target: how many words need to be cut to reach compliance
   - Recommend WHERE to trim: which section of the page is most expendable
     - Bump: trim proof or explanation (keep the What + Why + Price core)
     - Upsell: trim Reason section first (often over-explained), then Amplify (can be tightened)
     - Downsell: trim any repeated proof from the upsell (buyer already saw it)

8. **Write output file** with all counts, calculations, and recommendations.

## CONSTRAINTS

- 200 wpm is NON-NEGOTIABLE as the reading speed assumption. Do not use 250 wpm or "average adult reading speed" — sales copy is read slower than prose because buyers re-read claims, scan pricing, and evaluate offers.
- Word counts must be VERIFIED against actual copy, not just reported from upstream. Upstream word counts may include metadata or exclude elements. Count the copy body independently.
- The 4-minute total threshold applies to TEXT-BASED sequences. If the upsell is a video script, total threshold adjusts to 5 minutes (video pacing differs from text reading, and video scripts legitimately run longer).
- EXTENDED_VIDEO flag (from U0/U2) is the ONLY exception to per-page limits for upsell pages. It must be explicitly flagged in U0 strategy — do not infer it.
- Trimming recommendations are for U5 (editorial), not for U4. U4 validates and recommends — it does not edit copy.
- When recommending trims, never suggest cutting: the mechanism name, the core offer details (product, price, guarantee), the CTA, or the buyer validation opening. These are structural elements that cannot be sacrificed for speed.

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Single page exceeds its word limit | Per-page calculation shows overage | Flag the page with exact overage count and trimming target. Identify which CAIRO section (if upsell/downsell) or which element (if bump) is most expendable. |
| Total sequence exceeds 4-minute threshold | Sum of per-page reading times > 4.0 minutes | Flag with total overage. Identify which page contributes the most reading time — that's the primary trimming candidate. If multiple pages are over, prioritize the one with the largest overage. |
| Word count discrepancy between upstream report and actual count | Verified count differs from 0.1 or 1.1 reported count by >10% | Use the VERIFIED count. Note the discrepancy — upstream may have included/excluded different elements. |
| Video script counted at text reading speed | Upsell format is video_script but reading time calculated at text rate | Use video-appropriate thresholds: 600w limit (or 2000w with EXTENDED_VIDEO), 3 min per-page limit, 5 min total threshold. Video pacing is different from text scanning. |
| Page exactly at threshold (e.g., 200w bump, 4.0 min total) | Reading time equals but does not exceed threshold | PASS. The threshold is "less than 4 minutes" — 4.0 minutes rounds to the threshold boundary. Strictly, < 4 min means 3.99 or less = PASS, 4.0 = on the line. Flag as "AT LIMIT — no margin for editorial additions" to warn U5. |
