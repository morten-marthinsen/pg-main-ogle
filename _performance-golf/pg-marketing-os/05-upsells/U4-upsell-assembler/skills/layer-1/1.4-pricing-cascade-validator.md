# 1.4 — Pricing Cascade Validator

**Skill:** U4 — Upsell Sequence Assembler
**Microskill:** 1.4 — Pricing Cascade Validator
**Layer:** 1
**Model:** sonnet
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`, `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`
**Output:** `layer-1/1.4-pricing-cascade.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Validate that pricing ratios across the upsell sequence fall within standard ranges (or documented LOSS_LEADER_MODE exceptions), and verify that price anchoring language on each qualifying page is accurate and present.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Read `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`** to extract all prices:
   - FE price
   - Bump price
   - Upsell price
   - Downsell price

2. **Read `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`** to confirm the price assignments match the sequence positions.

3. **Calculate actual pricing ratios:**
   - **Bump / FE ratio:** (Bump price / FE price) x 100 = X%
   - **Upsell / FE ratio:** (Upsell price / FE price) x 100 = X%
   - **Downsell / Upsell ratio:** (Downsell price / Upsell price) x 100 = X%

4. **Compare against standard ranges:**

   | Ratio | Standard Range | Source |
   |-------|---------------|--------|
   | Bump / FE | 10-30% | UPSELL-ENGINE.md |
   | Upsell / FE | 50-150% | UPSELL-ENGINE.md |
   | Downsell / Upsell | 30-50% | UPSELL-ENGINE.md |

   Per ratio:
   - Within range → PASS
   - Outside range → check for LOSS_LEADER_MODE before failing

5. **Check for LOSS_LEADER_MODE.**
   - Read U0 strategy for any LOSS_LEADER_MODE flag or low-FE-price indicator
   - LOSS_LEADER_MODE applies when the FE is priced extremely low (e.g., $7-$14.95) specifically to acquire customers, with the real revenue coming from the upsell sequence
   - Example: US-28 pattern — $497 upsell on $14.95 FE = 33x ratio (3,324% of FE). This PASSES under LOSS_LEADER_MODE but would massively fail standard ranges.
   - If LOSS_LEADER_MODE is active:
     - Bump / FE: standard range still applies (bump should still be proportional to FE)
     - Upsell / FE: ratio can exceed 150% — up to 50x (5,000%) documented in specimen vault. Check that the upsell price is reasonable for the VALUE delivered, not just the FE ratio.
     - Downsell / Upsell: standard range still applies (downsell should still be proportional to the declined upsell)

6. **Check U0 strategy pricing targets.**
   - Compare actual prices against U0's planned prices
   - If actual prices differ from U0 targets, flag the deviation (this feeds into the drift report in 4.1)

7. **Verify price anchoring on each qualifying page.**
   - Read the upsell page copy: search for price anchoring language (comparisons to FE value, "for less than," "compared to," value stacking, price-per-day breakdowns)
   - Read the downsell page copy: same search
   - For each page, verify the anchor is ACCURATE:
     - If the upsell says "for less than you paid for [FE]" — is the upsell price actually less than the FE price? If not, the anchor is inaccurate.
     - If the downsell says "a fraction of the [upsell price]" — is the downsell actually a fraction? Verify the math.
   - Order bump does NOT require price anchoring (too short, appears during checkout)

8. **Write output file** with all ratio calculations, range compliance, and anchoring verification.

## CONSTRAINTS

- Ratios are calculated to one decimal place (e.g., 24.7%, not 25%).
- LOSS_LEADER_MODE must be documented in U0 strategy to be active. Do not infer it from prices alone — a $9.95 FE without a LOSS_LEADER_MODE flag in U0 should still be evaluated against standard ranges (and will likely fail the upsell/FE ratio, which is the correct outcome — U0 should have set the flag).
- Price anchoring accuracy verification requires MATH, not just presence. "For less than the price of a daily coffee" must be checked: is $X/day actually less than a coffee (~$5-7)? If the product is $197 one-time, the daily comparison is $197/365 = $0.54/day — the anchor is accurate. Document the math.
- Do not recommend price changes. U4 validates and reports. If pricing is wrong, that's a U0 strategic issue. U4 flags the deviation; the human decides whether to adjust prices or accept the non-standard ratios.
- Downsell / Upsell ratio uses the DECLINED upsell price, not the FE price. The downsell is a reframe of the upsell, so its ratio is measured against what was declined.

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Bump / FE ratio outside 10-30% | Calculation shows ratio outside range | Flag with exact ratio. If bump is >30% of FE, buyer may resist (too expensive for an add-on). If <10%, bump may be undervalued. |
| Upsell / FE ratio outside 50-150% (non-LOSS_LEADER) | Calculation shows ratio outside range and no LOSS_LEADER_MODE flag | Flag with exact ratio. If >150%, the jump may cause sticker shock. If <50%, upsell may be underpriced relative to FE (value perception issue). |
| Downsell / Upsell ratio outside 30-50% | Calculation shows ratio outside range | Flag with exact ratio. If >50%, downsell isn't enough of a price reduction to feel like a genuine concession. If <30%, downsell may be too cheap to generate meaningful revenue. |
| Price anchor claims inaccurate math | Anchor says "less than X" but actual math shows otherwise | Flag as INACCURATE ANCHOR with the correct math. Recommend U5 fix the specific claim. Inaccurate price anchoring damages credibility. |
| U0 target prices don't match actual prices | Comparison shows deviation | Document the deviation. This feeds into 4.1 drift report. Not necessarily a failure — prices may have been adjusted during U1/U2/U3 development — but must be tracked. |
