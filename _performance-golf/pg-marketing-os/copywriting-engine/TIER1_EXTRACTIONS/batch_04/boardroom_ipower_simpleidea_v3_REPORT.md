## Root Cause Architecture

### Three-Part Structure

**What They Think:**
"Business improvement requires expensive consultants ($200,000-a-year), complex MBA strategies, or executive-level thinking. Innovation comes from the top down. Only highly educated experts can solve business problems."

**What It Really Is:**
"The best business ideas come from everyone at every level - especially from frontline workers who see daily inefficiencies that executives miss. Simple questions from ordinary employees contain more ROI than consultant reports. Shared idea power creates a perpetual improvement machine."

**Why Nothing Worked:**
"Traditional top-down management suppresses the ideas at every level. Business schools don't teach how to unlock collective intelligence. Companies spend fortunes on consultants who don't have the ground-level insights that clerks and frontline workers possess daily."

### Villain Profile

**Villain Type:** Corporate Establishment
**Villain Name:** Business Schools and Management Consultants
**Villain Role:** Gatekeeper

**Villain Motivation:**
"Business schools and $200,000-a-year consultants perpetuate the myth that only educated experts can solve business problems because it protects their status, fees, and institutional power. They have no incentive to teach that ordinary workers can out-think them."

**Villain Evidence:**
- "No business school in the world teaches this!" (page 213)
- Clerk saved more money than any consultant
- Robert Half endorsement validates this is suppressed knowledge
- Columbia professor endorsement adds ironic validation

**Reframe Technique:** David vs. Goliath

**Key Insight:** The villain creates powerful emotional appeal - rage at gatekeepers, vindication that "ordinary" people have value, hope that you don't need expensive credentials to succeed.
