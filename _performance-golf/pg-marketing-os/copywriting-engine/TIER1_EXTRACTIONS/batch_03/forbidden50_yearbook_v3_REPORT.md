### Swipe-able Patterns

**Pattern 1: Villain Validation Hook**
```
Template: "[Villain Quote Expressing Fury], [Villain Identity] are [Emotion] as [Your Name/Brand] [Action That Threatens Villain]"

Example: "Curse you, Marty Edelston! America's vicious swindlers are in an uproar as tattletale consumer advocate gives away the deepest, darkest secrets..."

Application: Any offer where establishment/industry opposes you. The stronger their opposition, the better this works.
```

**Pattern 2: Reason-Why Extreme Offer**
```
Template: "They say I'm [Negative Label] to [Unprecedented Offer]. After all, that's [Comparison to Previous]. But I have a very, very good reason... [New Product/Discovery] is [Multiplier] better than [Previous Best]."

Example: "They say I'm NUTS to give away 50 reports - 5 times more than we've ever given before! But I have a good reason... our new Yearbook is 5 times better than any we've ever published."

Application: When your offer seems "too good to be true." Acknowledge it, then justify it with product quality logic.
```

```
Template: "Exposing [Specific Enemy] from [Impressive Number] [Categories/Industries/Sources]"

Example: "Deepest, darkest secrets from almost 500 industries"

Application: When you can aggregate comprehensive information/solutions. The breadth itself becomes proof of depth.
```

```
Template: "Get [5x more of X] because [Y is 5x better]"

Example: "Get 50 FREE reports (5x more than ever) because the new Yearbook is 5x better than any previous edition"

Application: When introducing improved product version. Use same multiplier for both quantity of bonuses and quality of core product.
```

**Pattern 5: Suppression Narrative**
```
Template: "[Villain] don't want you to know [Secret] because [Threat to Their Profit/Power]"

Example: "Vicious swindlers don't want you to know their deepest, darkest secrets because exposure threatens their profit models"

Application: Information products, expose offers, anything positioned against establishment interest.
```
