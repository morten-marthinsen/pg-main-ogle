## ROOT CAUSE ARCHITECTURE

### Three-Part Reframe Structure

#### What They Think
"Health requires sacrifice, self-denial, and extreme measures. You must give up coffee, alcohol, meat, eggs, fatty foods, sunshine. You must exercise intensely, drink gallons of water, eat turkey burgers and sprouts. Health is hard, unpleasant work."

#### What It Really Is
"Mainstream health advice is 'junk medicine' - false dogma that makes people suffer needlessly while missing the real path to health. The conventional wisdom is backwards: the things you're told to avoid are actually fine, and the extreme measures you're told to follow are unnecessary."

#### Why Nothing Worked
"Previous health attempts failed because they were based on junk medicine myths pushed by the medical establishment, fitness industry, and food industry. You were fighting against human nature and common sense, trying to sustain unsustainable behaviors based on false premises. No amount of willpower can overcome fundamentally flawed advice."

### Villain Profile: Medical Establishment

**Villain Type:** Medical Establishment
**Villain Name:** "Mainstream Medicine" / "The Medical Establishment" / "Junk Medicine Pushers"
**Villain Role:** Deceiver (spreading false information)

**Villain Motivation:**
Perpetuating false health dogma either through:
- Ignorance (don't know better)
- Groupthink (everyone believes it)
- Financial incentives (diet/fitness/pharmaceutical industries profit from junk medicine)

**Villain Evidence:**
Observable reality proves the villain exists:
- People following mainstream advice are still sick, overweight, exhausted
- Extreme nature of recommendations (turkey burgers, gallons of water, exercising until you can't breathe) is evidence of failed thinking
- If the advice worked, people wouldn't be suffering

### Reframe Technique: Opposite of Truth

This employs the **"opposite_of_truth"** reframe - claiming that conventional wisdom is not just wrong but completely inverted:

- Mainstream says: Avoid coffee → Dr. Douglass says: Coffee is fine
- Mainstream says: Avoid alcohol → Dr. Douglass says: Alcohol is fine
- Mainstream says: Avoid fatty foods → Dr. Douglass says: Fatty foods are fine
- Mainstream says: Exercise intensely → Dr. Douglass says: Extreme exercise unnecessary
- Mainstream says: Avoid sunshine → Dr. Douglass says: Sunshine is fine

This is more aggressive than "conventional wisdom is incomplete" - it's "conventional wisdom is backwards."

### Countersells (5 Major)

1. **Vegetarian/low-fat diets** → Don't work because based on junk medicine myths about fat
2. **Extreme exercise** → Unnecessary and unsustainable, creates suffering without health
3. **Forced hydration** → Gallons of water is uncomfortable junk medicine, not beneficial
4. **Giving up enjoyable foods** → Coffee, alcohol, meat, eggs are fine, deprivation helps nothing
5. **Avoiding sunshine** → Dangerous junk medicine (inverse of conventional sun exposure warnings)

### Emphasis Level: Foundation

The root cause reframe is the FOUNDATION of the entire promotion. Every section builds on "junk medicine vs real health" framework. This isn't a side element - it's the organizing principle.
