### 2026 Compliance Issues:

**Would Not Pass Modern Compliance:**

1. **"100% success rate" / "every single person"**
   - Modern FTC: Needs "Results not typical" qualifier
   - Would require hedging: "In Dr. Null's study, participants who completed the program..."

2. **"Month to live" testimonial**
   - Modern compliance: Needs verified medical documentation
   - Health claims this dramatic require substantiation

3. **"Reverse aging"**
   - Anti-aging claims heavily scrutinized
   - Would need specific biomarker claims (e.g., "improved cardiovascular markers")

4. **Harry B. marathon training at 90**
   - Individual testimonial without typical results disclaimer
   - Modern version needs: "Harry's results are not typical. Individual results vary."

5. **"Unheard of in mainstream medicine"**
   - Comparative claim without substantiation
   - Would need clinical trial comparisons

### Pre-2010 vs. 2026 Compliance:

**Pre-2010 (when this ran):**
- Testimonials allowed without disclaimers
- "Results may vary" not yet standard
- Anti-aging claims less scrutinized
- PBS validation considered sufficient backing

**2026:**
- Every testimonial needs "Results not typical"
- Specific health claims require clinical substantiation
- Structure/function claims heavily regulated
- Third-party validation helps but doesn't exempt from substantiation

### How to Update for 2026:

This maintains:
- Scale (11,214)

- Proof (measurable improvements)
- Harry B. case (dramatic example)

While adding:
- Hedging ("Individual results varied")
- Qualifier ("who completed the full protocol")
- Spectrum language ("dramatic" to "moderate")
