# Microskill 2.3: Schwartz Lens

**Layer:** 2 (Multi-Expert Critique)
**Purpose:** Evaluate copy through Eugene Schwartz's sophistication and desire principles

---

## SCHWARTZ EXPERTISE PROFILE

**Master of:** Market sophistication, desire channeling, awareness levels
**Signature Approach:** Meet the market where it IS, not where you want it to be
**Core Philosophy:** "Copy cannot create desire—it can only channel existing desire"

---

## SCHWARTZ 10-POINT CRITIQUE CHECKLIST

### 1. Sophistication Calibration
**Question:** Is the copy calibrated to the market's sophistication level?

```yaml
sophistication_calibration:
  assessment: "[CALIBRATED | MISCALIBRATED]"
  detected_market_stage: "[1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5]"
  copy_approach_stage: "[1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5]"
  calibration_evidence:
    - quote: "[text showing calibration or miscalibration]"
      diagnosis: "[how it matches or misses the market]"
  schwartz_principle: "Stage 1 needs direct promises; Stage 5 needs new mechanisms or markets"
```

**Sophistication Stages:**
- **Stage 1:** Direct claim (market hasn't heard it before)
- **Stage 2:** Enlarged claim (bigger, better, faster)
- **Stage 3:** Mechanism (HOW it works)
- **Stage 4:** Enhanced mechanism (better mechanism)
- **Stage 5:** Identification/experience (new market or paradigm)

---

### 2. Desire Identification
**Question:** Does the copy tap into existing mass desire?

```yaml
desire_identification:
  assessment: "[STRONG | ADEQUATE | WEAK]"
  primary_desire_targeted: "[specific desire]"
  desire_evidence_in_copy:
    - quote: "[where desire is touched]"
      desire_type: "[health | wealth | relationships | status | fear reduction]"
  desire_strength: "[primal | strong | moderate | weak]"
  schwartz_principle: "Find the existing desire and channel it—never try to create new desire"
```

---

### 4. Awareness Level Match
**Question:** Does the copy match the prospect's awareness level?

```yaml
awareness_level_match:
  assessment: "[MATCHED | MISMATCHED]"
  assumed_awareness: "[unaware | problem-aware | solution-aware | product-aware | most-aware]"
  copy_opening_approach: "[matches | too advanced | too basic]"
  evidence:
    - quote: "[opening text]"
      diagnosis: "[how it matches or misses awareness]"
  schwartz_principle: "Unaware prospects need education; most-aware need deals"
```

**Awareness Levels:**
- **Unaware:** Don't know they have a problem
- **Problem-Aware:** Know the problem, not the solution
- **Solution-Aware:** Know solutions exist, not your product
- **Product-Aware:** Know your product, undecided
- **Most-Aware:** Ready to buy, need the right offer

**Question:** Is the core promise fresh for this market?


## EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

Every critique point MUST include:
- **Quote:** Exact text from the copy
- **Location:** Section or paragraph reference
- **Problem:** Specific diagnosis
- **Fix:** Concrete recommendation

**NO VAGUE CRITIQUES.** "Market calibration seems off" is unacceptable. "This is a Stage 4 market but copy opens with Stage 2 enlarged claims—needs mechanism reveal in first 200 words to establish differentiation" is acceptable.
