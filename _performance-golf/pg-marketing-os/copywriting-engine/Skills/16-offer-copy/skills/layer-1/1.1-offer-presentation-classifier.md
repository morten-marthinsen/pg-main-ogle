**Position:** First microskill in Layer 1
**Persona:** Sarah Chen — Competitive Intelligence (Primary), Marcus Webster — Pattern Synthesis (Secondary)

---

## PURPOSE

The Offer Presentation Classifier determines HOW the offer should be presented based on the product type, niche, audience sophistication, and deliverable complexity. Not all offers are presented the same way. A supplement stack with a single bottle and three digital bonuses requires a different D-F-W-B-P depth than a digital bundle with eight modules. A physical product like a golf club needs engineering-focused features, while an info product needs authority-focused proof. A service package needs outcome-focused benefits, while a hybrid product needs to manage both physical and digital deliverable types.

This classifier outputs the offer format classification that governs all downstream writing decisions: how deep each D-F-W-B-P treatment goes, how many words per deliverable, what proof types to emphasize, and what transition language patterns to use. Without this classification, the writer microskills (Layer 2) would apply a one-size-fits-all approach that produces flat, generic offer presentations.

The classifier also determines the total offer presentation scope: expected word count, section proportions (what percentage of the offer goes to deliverables vs. bonuses vs. value demo vs. price), and the emotional progression appropriate for the product type and niche.

---

## IDENTITY

**This microskill IS:**
- The offer format classifier that determines the presentation approach for the entire offer section
- A D-F-W-B-P depth calibrator — setting how detailed each deliverable and bonus treatment should be
- A niche-specific presentation adapter — adjusting approach for health, business, golf, self-help, etc.
- A product type analyzer — distinguishing supplement, digital, physical, information, hybrid, and service formats
- A scope planner that sets word count targets and section proportions
- A proof type recommender — determining which proof categories are most effective per product type
- A TIER1 pattern matcher — connecting product characteristics to elite offer presentation patterns

**This microskill is NOT:**
- A deliverable mapper — that is 1.2 (this classifies the format, 1.2 maps the sequence)
- A value demonstration designer — that is 1.3 (this sets the framework, 1.3 designs the specific demos)
- A draft writer — this produces classification data, not prose
- An offer architect — the offer structure comes from Skill 06 / human checkpoint
- A price planner — that is 1.4 (this provides format context, 1.4 plans the price presentation)

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Classify Primary Offer Format

1. Determine the primary offer format from confirmed product_format and deliverable analysis:

**SUPPLEMENT STACK:**
- Product is a physical supplement (capsules, powder, liquid)
- Primary deliverable is the supplement itself (30-day, 60-day, 90-day supply)
- Bonuses are typically digital (guides, cookbooks, protocols)
- D-F-W-B-P emphasis: Feature = ingredients/formula, Why = scientific reason, Benefit = health outcome, Proof = clinical data
- Typical deliverable count: 1-3 physical + 2-4 digital bonuses

**DIGITAL BUNDLE:**
- Product is a collection of digital assets (courses, templates, software)
- Multiple deliverables of roughly equal importance
- D-F-W-B-P emphasis: Feature = what's included, Why = skill/knowledge gap it fills, Benefit = practical outcome, Proof = user results
- Typical deliverable count: 4-8 modules/components + 2-5 bonuses

**INFO PRODUCT:**
- Product is a single information product (book, course, membership)
- Primary deliverable is the core training/content
- D-F-W-B-P emphasis: Feature = content depth, Why = expertise gap, Benefit = transformation, Proof = authority/testimonials
- Typical deliverable count: 1 core + 3-6 bonuses

**PHYSICAL PRODUCT:**
- Product is a tangible item (golf club, device, equipment)
- Primary deliverable is the physical product itself
- D-F-W-B-P emphasis: Feature = engineering/design, Why = performance advantage, Benefit = measurable improvement, Proof = testing data
- Typical deliverable count: 1-2 physical + 1-3 digital bonuses

**SERVICE PACKAGE:**
- Product is a service (coaching, consulting, done-for-you)
- Primary deliverable is the service engagement
- D-F-W-B-P emphasis: Feature = service scope, Why = expertise/access, Benefit = outcome without effort, Proof = case studies
- Typical deliverable count: 1 core service + 2-4 support elements

**HYBRID:**
- Product combines physical and digital elements
- Multiple deliverable types requiring different D-F-W-B-P approaches
- Must manage transitions between physical and digital item descriptions
- Typical deliverable count: 2-3 physical + 3-5 digital

2. Record: `primary_format`, `format_confidence`, `format_characteristics`

### Step 2: Determine D-F-W-B-P Depth Per Deliverable

3. For each deliverable, assign a depth level:

**FULL DEPTH (all 5 elements, 100-200 words per deliverable):**
- Assign to: Primary deliverables, high-value items, items that need explanation
- All 5 elements fully developed: Deliverable name, Feature description, Why it matters, Benefit to prospect, Proof element
- Use when: The deliverable IS the core product or a major component

**MEDIUM DEPTH (D-F-W-B, abbreviated proof, 60-100 words per deliverable):**
- Assign to: Secondary deliverables, supporting components
- Deliverable named, Feature stated, Why briefly noted, Benefit stated, Proof light or implied
- Use when: The deliverable supports the core product but is not the main attraction

**BRIEF DEPTH (D-F-B only, 30-60 words per deliverable):**
- Assign to: Minor bonuses, supplementary materials, quick-reference items
- Deliverable named, Feature stated, Benefit stated
- Use when: The item adds value but does not need extensive explanation

4. Apply format-specific depth rules:
   - Supplement: Primary = FULL (the supplement itself), Bonuses = MEDIUM or BRIEF
   - Digital bundle: Top 2-3 modules = FULL, remaining = MEDIUM, bonuses = BRIEF to MEDIUM
   - Info product: Core training = FULL, bonuses = MEDIUM
   - Physical: Product = FULL, digital bonuses = MEDIUM to BRIEF
   - Service: Core service = FULL, support elements = MEDIUM, bonuses = BRIEF
   - Hybrid: Each type's primary = FULL, secondaries = MEDIUM

### Step 3: Determine Proof Type Emphasis

5. Based on product format and niche, rank proof types by effectiveness:

| Format | Primary Proof | Secondary Proof | Tertiary Proof |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Supplement | Clinical data, ingredient research | Manufacturing credibility | Testimonials |
| Digital bundle | User results, case studies | Authority credentials | Method validation |
| Info product | Authority credentials, student results | Method uniqueness | Testimonials |
| Physical | Testing/performance data, engineering specs | Professional endorsement | User experience |
| Service | Case studies, client outcomes | Authority credentials | Process proof |
| Hybrid | Varies by deliverable type | Varies | Varies |

6. Map available proof assets (from proof inventory) to the ranked proof types
7. For each deliverable, assign the most effective available proof type for its D-F-W-B-P proof element

### Step 4: Set Section Proportions

8. Determine the word count allocation for each offer section:

**Supplement Stack Proportions:**
- Deliverable Stack: 25-30% (fewer deliverables, deeper treatment)
- Bonus Section: 20-25% (digital bonuses need explanation)
- Value Demonstration: 15-20% (health benefits for value demo)
- Price/Guarantee/CTA: 25-35% (strong close structure)

**Digital Bundle Proportions:**
- Deliverable Stack: 30-40% (many modules to present)
- Bonus Section: 15-20% (bonuses are similar to deliverables)
- Value Demonstration: 10-15% (outcome-based value demo)
- Price/Guarantee/CTA: 25-30%

**Info Product Proportions:**
- Deliverable Stack: 20-25% (one core product, depth over breadth)
- Bonus Section: 25-30% (bonuses add perceived completeness)
- Value Demonstration: 15-20%
- Price/Guarantee/CTA: 25-35%

**Physical Product Proportions:**
- Deliverable Stack: 20-25% (physical item needs engineering focus)
- Bonus Section: 15-20% (fewer bonuses typical)
- Value Demonstration: 15-20%
- Price/Guarantee/CTA: 30-40% (physical products need strong guarantee)

9. Calculate target word counts per section using total word_count_target and proportions

### Step 5: Determine Emotional Progression Approach

10. Based on niche and product type, set the emotional progression:

**Health/Wellness niche:**
- Deliverables: relief and hope ("finally, a solution")
- Bonuses: completeness ("everything you need")
- Value demo: health-is-priceless framing
- Price: investment in health, not expense
- Guarantee: confidence in science and ingredients

**Business/Finance niche:**
- Deliverables: capability and confidence ("the exact system")
- Bonuses: unfair advantage ("tools the competition does not have")
- Value demo: ROI framing ("if it just added one client")
- Price: investment vs. revenue generated
- Guarantee: confidence in results

**Golf/Sports niche:**
- Deliverables: performance improvement ("add yards, cut strokes")
- Bonuses: insider access ("what the pros use")
- Value demo: enjoyment framing ("if it just took 5 strokes off")
- Price: less than a round of golf / lesson
- Guarantee: confidence in technology/method

**Self-Help/Personal Development niche:**
- Deliverables: transformation and empowerment
- Bonuses: accelerated results
- Value demo: life-quality framing
- Price: investment in yourself
- Guarantee: confidence in the transformation

### Step 6: Match TIER1 Presentation Patterns

11. From the TIER1 index, identify elite controls with similar:
    - Product format (supplement, digital, etc.)
    - Niche (health, business, golf, etc.)
    - Deliverable count (similar quantity)
    - Price range (similar actual price)
12. Extract the presentation approach from matched TIER1 patterns:
    - How did elite controls structure their deliverable stack?
    - What depth did they use for primary vs. secondary items?
    - What transition patterns did they employ?
    - What value demonstration approach was most effective?
13. Record matched patterns as reference for Layer 2 writers

### Step 7: Compile Classification Output

14. Assemble the complete offer presentation classification:
    - Primary format with confidence level
    - D-F-W-B-P depth assignment per deliverable and bonus
    - Proof type ranking and assignment
    - Section proportions with word count targets
    - Emotional progression blueprint
    - Matched TIER1 patterns for reference
15. Validate classification completeness:
    - Every deliverable has a depth assignment
    - Every section has a word count target
    - Proof types are ranked
    - Emotional progression is defined
    - At least 1 TIER1 pattern matched

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Misclassification of Hybrid Product
- **Symptom:** Hybrid product classified as single format, missing one deliverable type
- **Cause:** Classifier defaulted to the dominant deliverable type and ignored the secondary type
- **Prevention:** Check for mixed deliverable types (physical + digital) and force hybrid classification when both present
- **Recovery:** Reclassify as hybrid; assign format-appropriate depth to each deliverable type separately

### Failure Mode 2: All Deliverables Assigned Same Depth
- **Symptom:** Every deliverable gets FULL depth, producing an excessively long offer section
- **Cause:** Classifier did not differentiate between primary and secondary deliverables
- **Prevention:** Step 2 requires ranking deliverables by importance and assigning varied depths
- **Recovery:** Re-rank deliverables; assign FULL to top 1-2, MEDIUM to middle items, BRIEF to minor items

### Failure Mode 3: Proof Type Mismatch
- **Symptom:** Clinical proof assigned to info product where no clinical data exists
- **Cause:** Proof ranking used format default without checking available proof assets
- **Prevention:** Step 3 item 6 — cross-reference ranked proof types against actual proof inventory
- **Recovery:** Re-rank proof types based on what is actually available, not what would be ideal

### Failure Mode 4: Section Proportions Misaligned for Deliverable Count
- **Symptom:** 8-deliverable digital bundle has only 25% allocated to deliverable stack — not enough words
- **Cause:** Default proportions applied without adjusting for actual deliverable count
- **Prevention:** Adjust proportions based on actual deliverable + bonus count
- **Recovery:** Recalculate proportions with minimum words-per-deliverable constraint (FULL = 100 min, MEDIUM = 60 min, BRIEF = 30 min)
