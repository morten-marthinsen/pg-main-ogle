# Skill 2.2-B: Belief Excavator (E5 Tool)

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** E5 Method - Belief Analysis
**Dependencies:** scored_quotes.json, theme_synthesis.json, web_analysis.json
**Output:** belief_analysis.json

---

## Purpose

Excavate the beliefs held by the target WEB. Surface explicit beliefs, hidden assumptions, limiting beliefs, and the belief structure that must be addressed for a sale to occur.

---

## Belief Framework

```
BELIEF LAYERS:

1. SURFACE BELIEFS
   - What they openly say they believe
   - Stated positions
   - Conscious thoughts

2. IMPLICIT BELIEFS
   - What their behavior reveals they believe
   - Unspoken assumptions
   - Taken-for-granted truths

3. LIMITING BELIEFS
   - What's holding them back
   - False limitations
   - Self-imposed barriers

4. CONVERSION BELIEFS
   - What they must believe to buy
   - Preconditions for action
   - The belief bridge needed
```

## Excavation Logic

### Step 1: Surface Belief Extraction

```
FROM scored_quotes:

IDENTIFY explicit beliefs:

BELIEF INDICATORS:
- "I believe..."
- "I think..."
- "The problem is..."
- "The truth is..."
- "What works is..."
- "What doesn't work is..."
- "You have to..."
- "You can't..."

FOR each belief statement:
  CAPTURE:
  - Exact belief
  - Frequency (similar beliefs expressed)
  - Source quotes
  - Marketing impact

CATEGORIZE by type:
- Beliefs about self
- Beliefs about market/problem
- Beliefs about solutions
- Beliefs about success/failure
```

### Step 2: Implicit Belief Detection

```
IDENTIFY beliefs revealed by behavior:

FROM behavioral_patterns (theme_synthesis):
  INFER underlying belief

EXAMPLES:
Behavior: "I keep changing strategies"
Implicit belief: "There exists a perfect strategy I haven't found yet"

Behavior: "I move my stops"
Implicit belief: "Price will come back / My analysis is right"

Behavior: "I revenge trade"
Implicit belief: "I need to 'make back' losses immediately"

FOR each behavioral pattern:
  EXTRACT implicit_belief
  NOTE behavioral_indicator
  ASSESS marketing_impact
```

### Step 3: Limiting Belief Identification

```
IDENTIFY beliefs that limit action:

LIMITING BELIEF PATTERNS:
- "I'm not [type of person]"
- "I can't [action]"
- "I don't have [resource]"
- "It's impossible to [outcome]"
- "People like me don't [outcome]"

FROM quotes:
  EXTRACT limiting statements
  ANALYZE what they limit
  Identify counter_belief_needed

CATEGORIZE:
- Identity-based limits ("I'm just not disciplined")
- Capability limits ("I can't control my emotions")
- Resource limits ("I don't have enough capital")
- External limits ("The market is rigged")
```

### Step 4: Belief Clustering

```
GROUP related beliefs:

IDENTIFY clusters around:
- Core problem beliefs
- Self-concept beliefs
- Solution beliefs
- Success beliefs
- Failure beliefs

FOR each cluster:
  NAME the cluster
  IDENTIFY central belief
  LIST related beliefs
  COUNT quotes supporting
  ASSESS importance
```

### Step 5: Belief Hierarchy Analysis

```
RANK beliefs by importance:

CORE BELIEFS:
- Most frequently expressed
- Most emotionally charged
- Most behavior-driving
- Most relevant to sale

SECONDARY BELIEFS:
- Support core beliefs
- Moderately frequent
- Less emotional charge

PERIPHERAL BELIEFS:
- Mentioned occasionally
- Context-dependent
- Nice to address but not critical
```

### Step 6: Conversion Requirement Mapping

```
IDENTIFY beliefs required for sale:

MUST BELIEVE:
- What they need to accept before buying
- Bridge from current belief to target belief

EXAMPLES:
Current: "I just need a better strategy"
Target: "My strategy isn't the real problem"
Bridge: "Show them pattern of strategy failure"

Current: "I'm an emotional trader, that's just who I am"
Target: "Emotional trading is a skill gap, not identity"
Bridge: "Neurological explanation + examples of change"

MUST NOT BELIEVE:
- Beliefs that would prevent sale
- How to address/dissolve them
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. **Depth** - Surface through limiting beliefs captured
2. **Evidence** - Each belief has quote support
3. **Actionability** - Clear conversion requirements
4. **Completeness** - Belief landscape fully mapped
