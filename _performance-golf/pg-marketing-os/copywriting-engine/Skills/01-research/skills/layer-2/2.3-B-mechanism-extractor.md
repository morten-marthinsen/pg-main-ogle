# Skill 2.3-B: Mechanism Extractor

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** Mechanism Intelligence
**Dependencies:** competitor_analysis.json, instructional_content, scored_quotes.json
**Output:** mechanism_inventory.json

---

## Purpose

Extract and catalog all mechanisms found in the market - competitor mechanisms, implied mechanisms from customer language, and potential new mechanisms. Build the raw material for mechanism development.

---

## Mechanism Framework

```
WHAT IS A MECHANISM?

A mechanism is the WHY and HOW behind a result.
It's the believable explanation for why something works.

MECHANISM TYPES:

1. ROOT CAUSE MECHANISMS
   - Explain why the problem exists
   - "The real reason X happens..."

2. SOLUTION MECHANISMS
   - Explain how the solution works
   - "This works because..."

3. DIFFERENTIATING MECHANISMS
   - Explain why this is different
   - "Unlike other approaches..."

4. CREDIBILITY MECHANISMS
   - Explain why to believe
   - "Backed by research..."
```

## Extraction Logic

### Step 1: Extract Competitor Mechanisms

```
FROM competitor_analysis and funnel_content:

FOR each competitor:

  IDENTIFY their mechanism claims:
  - What do they say is "the reason"?
  - What do they name their method?
  - How do they explain it works?

  CAPTURE:
  - mechanism_name (their proprietary name if any)
  - mechanism_type (root_cause/solution/differentiating)
  - description (how they explain it)
  - language_used (specific phrases)
  - supporting_claims (what they say backs it up)

  ASSESS:
  - strength (how compelling is it?)
  - vulnerability (what's the weakness?)
```

### Step 2: Extract Implied Mechanisms (Customer Language)

```
FROM scored_quotes:

IDENTIFY mechanism-adjacent language:

PHRASES INDICATING ROOT CAUSE BELIEFS:
- "The problem is..."
- "What's really going on is..."
- "I realized that..."
- "It's because..."

PHRASES INDICATING SOLUTION BELIEFS:
- "What works is..."
- "The key is..."
- "Once I understood..."
- "What changed was..."

FOR each pattern:
  EXTRACT the mechanism concept
  NOTE frequency
  CAPTURE example quotes

INTERPRET: What mechanisms do customers believe in?
ASSESS: Development potential
```

### Step 3: Extract Implied Mechanisms (Instructional)

```
FROM instructional_content:

IDENTIFY teaching frameworks:
- How do experts explain problems?
- What causes do they identify?
- What solutions do they recommend?
- What terminology do they use?

CAPTURE:
- Framework names
- Explanatory models
- Technical language
- Causal chains

These represent validated mechanisms used by educators
```

### Step 4: Categorize Mechanisms

```
ORGANIZE all mechanisms:

ROOT CAUSE MECHANISMS:
- What explains why the problem exists?
- What is "the real reason"?

SOLUTION MECHANISMS:
- What explains how solutions work?
- What is "the key" to fixing it?

DIFFERENTIATING MECHANISMS:
- What explains why something is different?
- What makes one approach unique?

FOR each category:
  LIST mechanisms
  NOTE prevalence
  ASSESS effectiveness indicators
```

### Step 5: Analyze Language Patterns

```
ACROSS all mechanisms:

MECHANISM INTROS:
- How do people introduce mechanisms?
- "The real reason is..."
- "What most people don't know..."
- "The truth is..."

CAUSE-EFFECT LANGUAGE:
- "Because of X, Y happens..."
- "When you X, then Y..."
- "The result of X is Y..."

TECHNICAL TERMS:
- Domain-specific vocabulary
- Scientific-sounding language
- Proprietary terminology

METAPHORS:
- How mechanisms are explained
- Analogies used
- Simplifying comparisons
```

### Step 6: Saturation Analysis

```
ASSESS mechanism landscape:

OVERUSED:
- Used by multiple competitors
- Customers have heard it before
- Diminishing effectiveness

EMERGING:
- Used by 1-2 innovators
- Gaining traction
- Still has novelty

UNTAPPED:
- Not explicitly used anywhere
- Implied by customer language
- Opportunity for development
```

### Step 7: Development Opportunity Identification

```
SYNTHESIZE opportunities:

FOR each untapped or emerging space:

IDENTIFY opportunity:
- What mechanism could be developed?
- What customer belief supports it?
- What makes it credible?

SUGGEST development_angle:
- How to frame it?
- What to name it?
- How to explain it?

GENERATE naming_suggestions:
- Proprietary name options
- Memorable phrases
- Technical-sounding terms
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. **Comprehensiveness** - All market mechanisms cataloged
2. **Organization** - Clear categorization
3. **Analysis** - Saturation and opportunity identified
4. **Actionability** - Development opportunities clear
