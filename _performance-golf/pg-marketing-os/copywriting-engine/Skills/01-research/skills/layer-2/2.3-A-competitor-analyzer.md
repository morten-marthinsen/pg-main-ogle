# Skill 2.3-A: Competitor Analyzer

**Version:** 2.0
**Layer:** 2 (Intelligence)
**Type:** Competitive Intelligence
**Dependencies:** ads_content, funnel_content, review_content, market_config.yaml
**Output:** competitor_analysis.json

---

## Purpose

Analyze competitor positioning, mechanisms, messaging, and market reception. Identify what's working, what's failing, what's saturated, and where differentiation opportunities exist.

---

## Analysis Dimensions

```
COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE:

1. MECHANISM ANALYSIS
   - What do they claim works?
   - How do they explain it?
   - What language do they use?

2. POSITIONING ANALYSIS
   - How do they differentiate?
   - Who do they target?
   - What claims do they make?

3. PROOF ANALYSIS
   - What evidence do they show?
   - What testimonials do they use?
   - What results do they claim?

4. RECEPTION ANALYSIS
   - What do customers say?
   - What complaints are common?
   - What praise is common?

5. SATURATION ANALYSIS
   - What's overused?
   - What angles are crowded?
   - What's still fresh?
```

## Analysis Logic

### Step 1: Compile Competitor List

```
FROM market_config.competitors:
  EXTRACT known competitors

FROM ads_content and funnel_content:
  IDENTIFY additional competitors

DEDUPLICATE and CATEGORIZE:
- Direct competitors (same solution type)
- Indirect competitors (different approach, same problem)

BUILD comprehensive competitor list
```

### Step 2: Mechanism Analysis

```
FOR each competitor:

FROM ads and funnels:
  EXTRACT mechanism claims:
  - What do they say works?
  - What's their "secret" or "method"?
  - What proprietary names do they use?

ANALYZE mechanism:
  primary = main mechanism claim
  supporting = additional mechanisms
  proprietary_terms = named methods/systems

RATE mechanism_strength:
  - Unique and compelling = 8-10
  - Differentiated = 5-7
  - Generic/commodity = 1-4
```

### Step 3: Positioning Analysis

```
FOR each competitor:

FROM funnels and landing pages:
  EXTRACT positioning:
  - Who are they targeting?
  - What's their main promise?
  - How do they differentiate?

CATEGORIZE positioning_angle:
  - Speed/efficiency ("fast results")
  - Ease/simplicity ("easy method")
  - Completeness ("all-in-one")
  - Expertise/authority ("from the experts")
  - Uniqueness ("unlike anything else")
  - Community ("join others")
  - Results ("proven results")
```

### Step 4: Messaging Analysis

```
FOR each competitor:

FROM ads:
  EXTRACT headline_themes:
  - What topics dominate headlines?
  - What hooks are used?

  EXTRACT hook_patterns:
  - Opening approaches
  - Attention mechanisms
  - Pattern interrupts

FROM funnels:
  EXTRACT proof_types:
  - Testimonials
  - Statistics
  - Demonstrations
  - Credentials

  EXTRACT cta_approaches:
  - Free vs paid entry
  - Urgency tactics
  - Risk reversal
```

### Step 5: Reception Analysis

```
FOR each competitor:

FROM reviews:
  CALCULATE overall_rating
  COUNT review_count

  EXTRACT common_praise:
  - What do happy customers mention?
  - What benefits resonate?
  - What surprises positively?

  EXTRACT common_complaints:
  - What do unhappy customers mention?
  - What expectations unmet?
  - What frustrations arise?

SYNTHESIZE sentiment_summary
```

### Step 6: Landscape Analysis

```
ACROSS all competitors:

MECHANISM LANDSCAPE:
  saturated = mechanisms used by 3+ competitors
  emerging = mechanisms used by 1-2, gaining traction
  white_space = mechanisms not addressed

POSITIONING LANDSCAPE:
  common_positions = angles used by many
  differentiation_opportunities = underserved angles
  crowded_angles = overdone positions

PROOF LANDSCAPE:
  common_proof = what everyone uses
  effective_proof = what reviews suggest works
  weak_proof = what reviews suggest doesn't convince

COMPLAINT PATTERNS:
  universal = complaints appearing across competitors
  addressable_gaps = problems competitors don't solve
```

### Step 7: Opportunity Synthesis

```
SYNTHESIZE opportunities:

MECHANISM OPPORTUNITIES:
- What mechanisms could differentiate?
- What white space exists?
- What could be positioned as "the real answer"?

POSITIONING OPPORTUNITIES:
- What underserved segments exist?
- What angles aren't crowded?
- What fresh claims are possible?

PROOF OPPORTUNITIES:
- What proof would stand out?
- What would address skepticism?
- What's missing from current proof?

GENERATE overall_recommendation
```

---

## Quality Criteria

1. **Comprehensiveness** - All major competitors analyzed
2. **Depth** - Mechanisms, positioning, reception covered
3. **Actionability** - Clear opportunities identified
4. **Evidence** - Claims backed by source material
