## PURPOSE

Writes the guarantee as a "Contract" or "Personal Promise" — Makepeace Element 2: Present Guarantee. This is NOT a refund policy statement. The guarantee in a Makepeace close is an expression of CONFIDENCE in the product. It sells the relationship, not the exit door. The guarantee section should make the prospect think "This company is SO confident in their product that they're willing to take on ALL the risk" — not "Oh good, I can return it if I don't like it."

Makepeace's critical distinction: Write "You must be absolutely delighted... you must agree this is without a doubt the best [product category] you've ever tried... you must experience ALL [the benefits]... but in the very unlikely event that you don't experience these things — for any reason or no reason at all — I'll rush you a full refund, no questions asked." NEVER write: "If not satisfied, return for refund." The former builds confidence. The latter plants doubt.

This microskill also integrates Week 13 patterns: branded guarantee naming (the guarantee gets a NAME, not just terms), company character statements ("If we don't get you results, we don't deserve your money"), numbered guarantee terms, and risk quantification ("I'm taking on 300% of the risk"). The result is a guarantee section that functions as a persuasive SELLING tool, not a legal obligation.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Determine Guarantee Presentation Style

1. Read the guarantee type from upstream: "contract" or "personal_promise"
2. Select the presentation approach:

   **CONTRACT style:**
   - Third-person or company voice
   - Formal but confident tone
   - Uses language: "Here is our binding commitment to you..."
   - Numbered terms (1, 2, 3...)
   - Emphasizes the COMPANY's confidence and integrity
   - Best for: supplements, physical products, institutional brands

   **PERSONAL PROMISE style:**
   - First-person voice (from the spokesperson)
   - Intimate and personal tone
   - Uses language: "I personally guarantee..." / "This is my promise to you..."
   - Narrative format (paragraphs, not numbered terms)
   - Emphasizes the INDIVIDUAL's reputation and conviction
   - Best for: info products, coaching, newsletters, personality-driven brands

3. The style determines the voice, format, and language patterns throughout

### Step 2: Write the "You Must Be Absolutely Delighted" Core

4. This is the Makepeace gold standard — the guarantee opens by setting EXPECTATIONS, not exit conditions:

   **Makepeace pattern:**
   "You must be absolutely delighted with [Product Name].
   You must agree this is without a doubt the best [product category] you've ever tried.
   You must experience ALL of these benefits:
   [List 3-5 key benefits from the main benefit list]
   ...but in the very unlikely event that you don't experience these things — for any reason or no reason at all — I'll rush you a full refund. No questions asked."

5. Adapt the pattern to this specific product:
   - Replace [Product Name] with actual product name
   - Replace [product category] with specific category language
   - Select 3-5 benefits that represent the STRONGEST outcomes
   - Use "rush" for the refund — implies speed, not bureaucracy
   - "No questions asked" — removes friction entirely

6. CRITICAL: The benefit list within the guarantee is SELLING copy. The prospect reads these benefits again and thinks "I want ALL of these things." The guarantee paradoxically SELLS while appearing to offer an exit.

### Step 3: Integrate Branded Guarantee Name

7. The branded guarantee name must appear prominently — it is a PRODUCT within the product:

   **Naming integration patterns:**
   - Opening: "That's why every order is protected by our [Branded Guarantee Name]."
   - Midway: "Here's how the [Branded Guarantee Name] works..."
   - Closing: "That's the [Branded Guarantee Name] — and I stand behind every word of it."

8. The branded name creates MEMORABILITY — the prospect should remember the guarantee name even if they forget specific terms.

9. Week 13 examples to reference:
   - "Triple-Back-Guarantee" (Montezuma's Secret)
   - "Double Your Money Back" (Elixir of Eros)
   - "The Editor's Honor Guarantee" (financial)
   - "Double-Eagle Guarantee" (golf)

### Step 4: Add Company Character Statement

10. Week 13 pattern: Humanize the guarantee by revealing company values:

    **Company character templates:**
    - "[Company] is a legitimate, family-operated business. If we don't get you results, we don't deserve your money."
    - "I've built my reputation on honesty. If [Product Name] doesn't deliver everything I've promised, I don't want a single dollar from you."
    - "We believe in earning your trust, not just taking your money. That's why we back every single order with..."
    - "I'd rather refund every penny than have even one customer feel disappointed. That's the kind of company we are."

11. The company character statement adds a HUMAN dimension to the guarantee:
    - It positions the company as ethical and principled
    - It reframes the guarantee as a value statement, not a policy
    - It builds trust by revealing the WHY behind the guarantee

12. Place the company character statement either before or after the guarantee terms — it frames the terms with warmth.

### Step 5: Write Numbered Guarantee Terms (If Contract Style)

13. For CONTRACT style, add numbered terms that make the guarantee feel ironclad:

    **Week 13 numbered terms pattern:**
    ```
    Here's exactly what the [Branded Guarantee Name] covers:

    1. You keep your entire supply — even if you request a refund. It's yours. Consider it a gift.
    2. You receive a complete, 100% refund of every penny you paid. No questions. No hassles. No fine print.
    3. [Bonus element] — as a thank-you for giving us a fair chance, we'll [bonus gesture].
    ```

14. The numbered format creates CLARITY and TRANSPARENCY:
    - Each numbered item is a specific commitment
    - The prospect can see exactly what they're protected by
    - The bonus element (term 3) is SURPRISING — it makes the guarantee feel GENEROUS, not grudging

15. For PERSONAL PROMISE style, integrate these elements as narrative paragraphs instead of numbered lists.

### Step 6: Add Risk Quantification

16. Week 13 pattern: Make the company's risk-taking VISIBLE with specific language:

    **Risk quantification templates:**
    - "I'm taking on 100% of the risk — and you're taking on exactly zero."
    - "Think about that: I'm putting up 300% of the risk because that's how confident I am."
    - "You risk nothing. Not a single penny. The ONLY person with anything to lose here is me."
    - "If this doesn't work, I lose your payment AND the cost of the product I shipped you. You lose nothing."

17. Risk quantification works because it flips the traditional buyer-seller dynamic:
    - Normal: buyer risks money, seller has money
    - Quantified: seller risks MORE than the buyer — paying for product, shipping, processing
    - This makes the prospect feel like the seller has MORE to lose than they do

### Step 7: Write the Guarantee Close Transition

18. End the guarantee section with a confidence-building transition that leads to the closing theme or CTA:

    **Transition patterns:**
    - "So you literally have nothing to lose — and a lifetime of [promise] to gain."
    - "That's how confident I am. The only question left is: are you ready?"
    - "With that kind of protection, the only risk is NOT trying [Product Name]."
    - "There's zero risk on your end. All the risk is on mine. So what are you waiting for?"

19. The transition should make NOT ordering feel like the irrational choice
20. Include a CTA embedded naturally: "Go ahead and click the button below..."

### Step 8: Verify Guarantee Quality

21. Quality verification against Makepeace standards:
    - Does it open with expectations ("You must be absolutely delighted"), NOT exit conditions?
    - Does it list specific benefits within the guarantee (selling while guaranteeing)?
    - Does the branded guarantee name appear at least once?
    - Does it include "rush" or equivalent speed language for the refund?
    - Does it include "no questions asked" or equivalent frictionless language?
    - Does it include a company character statement (humanization)?
    - Does it avoid ALL prohibited language?
    - Does it feel like CONFIDENCE, not a refund policy?

22. PROHIBITED language check (zero tolerance):
    - "If not satisfied, return for refund" — BANNED
    - "Satisfaction guaranteed" — BANNED (generic)
    - "Money-back guarantee" without branding — BANNED (generic)
    - "Return within [X] days" — BANNED (emphasizes returning, not keeping)
    - "If you're unhappy..." — BANNED (plants the idea of unhappiness)

## FAILURE MODES

### Failure Mode 1: Doubt-Planting Guarantee
- **Symptom:** "If you're not happy with the product, simply return it for a full refund."
- **Cause:** Writer defaulted to standard refund policy language
- **Prevention:** Prohibited language list in Step 8; quality check specifically tests for doubt-planting
- **Recovery:** Rewrite entirely using Makepeace "You must be absolutely delighted" pattern

### Failure Mode 2: Generic Guarantee Name
- **Symptom:** "Our money-back guarantee" instead of "The Triple-Back-Guarantee"
- **Cause:** Branded guarantee name not integrated or not available
- **Prevention:** Step 3 mandates branded name integration; 0.4 checkpoint should have provided name
- **Recovery:** Use the branded name from upstream data; if truly missing, create a placeholder and flag for human

### Failure Mode 3: No Benefits Within Guarantee
- **Symptom:** Guarantee just states terms without listing the benefits the prospect "must experience"
- **Cause:** Writer treated guarantee as pure terms, not as selling copy
- **Prevention:** Step 2 Makepeace pattern requires listing 3-5 benefits within the guarantee
- **Recovery:** Insert benefit list: "You must experience [benefit 1]... [benefit 2]... [benefit 3]..."

### Failure Mode 4: Missing Company Character
- **Symptom:** Guarantee feels cold and corporate — no human values statement
- **Cause:** Company character pattern from Week 13 not integrated
- **Prevention:** Step 4 requires company character statement
- **Recovery:** Add 1-2 sentences of company/spokesperson values before or after the guarantee terms

### Failure Mode 5: Over-Legalistic Tone
- **Symptom:** Guarantee reads like legal fine print — terms and conditions
- **Cause:** Writer prioritized legal accuracy over persuasive impact
- **Prevention:** This is COPY, not legal text. Persuasion is the goal; legal review is separate.
- **Recovery:** Rewrite in conversational, confident voice. Legal disclaimers belong elsewhere.

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Health Supplement VSL — Personal Promise Style

**Context:** Male vitality supplement, VSL, "Triple-Back-Guarantee," 180 days, personal promise style.

**Guarantee Close Draft:**
```
Now, I want to make this as easy as possible for you. That's why every single order of Montezuma's Secret is protected by what I call the Triple-Back-Guarantee.

Here's what that means:

You must be absolutely delighted with your results.

You must feel the rock-solid confidence surging through your body. You must experience the morning vitality — the kind you haven't felt in years. You must see the look on your wife's face when she notices the difference. And you must agree that Montezuma's Secret is without a doubt the most powerful natural vitality formula you have ever tried.

But in the very unlikely event that you don't experience every single one of these things — for any reason at all, or no reason whatsoever — here's exactly what happens:

1. You keep your entire supply. Every bottle. Consider it my gift to you.
2. I'll rush you a complete refund of every penny you paid. No questions asked. No forms. No runaround.
3. And as a thank-you for giving us a fair chance, we'll send you a complimentary bottle of our premium B-vitamin complex — on the house.

That's the Triple-Back-Guarantee. And I stand behind every word of it.

Here's the truth: Second Prime is a family-operated business. We built this company on one simple principle — if we don't get you the results I've promised today, we don't deserve a single dollar of your money. Period.

I'm taking on 100% of the risk here. You're taking on zero. The only person with anything to lose is me.

So you literally have nothing to lose — and everything to gain. Go ahead and click the button below to get started.
```

**Word count:** 261
**Presentation style:** Personal promise
**Prohibited language violations:** 0

You're risking nothing. Not a penny. The only risk is missing the opportunities I'll uncover for you this year.
