# Skill 2.8: Doomsday Developer

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 2 (Scorecard Optimization)
**Type:** Dimension Evaluation + Optimization
**Dimension:** 8 of 12 — DOOMSDAY SCENARIO
**Dependencies:** gate_1_report.json, upstream_context.json, explanation_structures.json
**Output:** doomsday_scores.json

---

## Purpose

Evaluate and optimize each mechanism candidate on DOOMSDAY SCENARIO: What TERRIBLE thing happens if they DON'T use this mechanism? The doomsday scenario creates urgency and loss aversion — it's not just about what they gain, but what they LOSE by not acting.

---

## The Question

**"What TERRIBLE thing keeps happening if they DON'T activate this mechanism?"**

---

## Scoring Rubric

```
1-3 (NO CONSEQUENCE): No clear downside to NOT using mechanism.
     - "You'll miss out on improvements" — so what?
     - No specific negative outcome articulated
     - Reader thinks "I can live without this"

4-6 (MILD CONSEQUENCE): Some downside but not urgent.
     - "You'll continue struggling" — vague continuation
     - Consequence is gradual, not immediate
     - Reader thinks "I'll get to this eventually"

7-8 (SERIOUS CONSEQUENCE): Clear, specific, escalating negative outcome.
     - "Every day without this, the damage compounds"
     - Specific timeline of deterioration
     - Reader thinks "I need to handle this soon"

9-10 (EXISTENTIAL): The cost of inaction is devastating and irreversible.
     - Specific, quantifiable loss per day/week/month
     - Irreversibility element (window closing, damage accumulating)
     - Reader thinks "I can't afford to wait another day"
```

---

## Evaluation Logic

### Step 1: Identify Natural Consequences

```
FOR each candidate:

  FROM root_cause_context + mechanism explanation:

  IF the mechanism addresses a root cause, then NOT addressing it means:
    - The root cause CONTINUES operating
    - The damage ACCUMULATES over time
    - Previous failed solutions REMAIN failed
    - The gap between where they are and where they want to be WIDENS

  IDENTIFY the natural progression of inaction:
    NOW: [current state with root cause active]
    +30 DAYS: [what gets worse?]
    +90 DAYS: [what compounds?]
    +1 YEAR: [what's the full doomsday?]
```

### Step 2: Quantify the Cost

```
FOR each candidate:

  MAKE THE COST SPECIFIC:

  HEALTH MECHANISMS:
    - How much more damage occurs per day?
    - What symptoms worsen?
    - What becomes irreversible?

  FINANCIAL MECHANISMS:
    - How much money lost per week/month?
    - What opportunities pass by?
    - What compounding effect is missed?

  PERFORMANCE MECHANISMS:
    - How many more failures?
    - What skill gap widens?
    - What competitors gain while you don't?

  RELATIONSHIP MECHANISMS:
    - What connections deteriorate?
    - What trust erodes?
    - What opportunities for connection pass?

  THE BEST DOOMSDAY has a NUMBER:
    "Every day costs you $47 in lost opportunity"
    "You're losing 6 hours of deep sleep per week"
    "Your body adds 2mg of inflammation per day"
```

### Step 3: Add Irreversibility Element

```
FOR each candidate:

  ASSESS: Is there a point of no return?

  TYPES OF IRREVERSIBILITY:
    - Age-based: "After 50, this becomes 10x harder to reverse"
    - Cumulative: "The damage compounds daily — each day costs more"
    - Opportunity-based: "This window closes on [date/trigger]"
    - Competitive: "Your competitors are already doing this"
    - Biological: "Once [process] shuts down, restarting is..."

  IF natural irreversibility exists: leverage it
  IF NOT: focus on compounding cost instead
```

### Step 4: Build Doomsday Narrative

```
FOR each candidate:

  CONSTRUCT the full doomsday statement:

  STRUCTURE:
    "Without [mechanism name], [root cause] continues to [specific action].
     Every [time period], you're [losing/damaging/missing] [specific thing].
     After [timeline], this becomes [irreversible/exponentially worse/permanent].
     The cost of waiting isn't zero — it's [specific quantified cost]."

  REQUIREMENTS:
    - Specific to this mechanism (not generic fear)
    - Connected to the root cause (logical, not manipulative)
    - Quantified where possible
    - Escalating (gets worse, not stays same)
```

### Step 5: Optimize If Below Threshold

```
IF current_score < 7:

  TECHNIQUE 1: Quantify the Daily Cost
    ADD specific numbers to vague consequences
    "You're losing energy" → "You're losing 3-4 productive hours per day"

  TECHNIQUE 2: Add Compound Effect
    SHOW how cost multiplies over time
    "That's not just $47/day — it's $17,000 this year alone"

  TECHNIQUE 3: Add Irreversibility Warning
    IDENTIFY a point where reversal becomes harder
    "After age X, this becomes permanent..."
    "After Y months, the window closes..."

  TECHNIQUE 4: Connect to What They Value Most
    LINK doomsday to their CORE identity/values:
    "If you value [thing they care about], this is stealing it
     from you one day at a time"

  RE-SCORE after optimization
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Every candidate has a timeline progression (now → 30d → 90d → 1yr)
2. Doomsday is connected to ROOT CAUSE (not generic fear)
3. Cost is quantified where possible (numbers, not vague statements)
4. Irreversibility element present in candidates scoring ≥ 7
5. Narrative is specific to THIS mechanism, not generic scare copy
