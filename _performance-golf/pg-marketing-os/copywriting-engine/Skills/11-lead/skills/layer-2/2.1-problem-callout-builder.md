# Layer 2.1 — Problem Callout Builder

## Purpose

Craft the problem identification section that calls out the prospect — making them feel "this is about ME." The problem callout is the first emotional handshake between the lead and the prospect. It must use language the prospect uses about their own pain, NOT clinical or marketing language. Apply the Craig Clemens Vagueness Principle: problems should be vague enough for the reader to fill in their OWN experience, while results and solutions should be vivid and specific. The reader relates through their own memory, not your prescribed scenario.

**Primary Persona:** The Legendary Copywriter — Master Copy Composite

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Extract Prospect Pain Points from Upstream Context

From the root cause, promise, and emotional arc, identify the 2-4 pain points the prospect would recognize as their daily experience.

**Sources for pain points:**
- `root_cause.what_they_think` — what the prospect believes is causing their problem
- `root_cause.villain_profile` — external factors the prospect blames
- `promise.emotional_frame` — the emotional pain the promise addresses
- `headline.implied_promise` — what the headline already flagged as the prospect's desire

**Pain point requirements:**
- Must be observable symptoms, NOT root causes (the root cause comes later)
- Must be things the prospect would SAY about their own situation
- Must be frustrations, not clinical descriptions
- Must be vague enough to cover a RANGE of prospect experiences

### Step 2: Apply the Clemens Vagueness Calibration

For EVERY pain point drafted, run the Clemens test:

**Too Specific (REJECT):**
- Describes a particular scene with too many details
- Names specific places, physical descriptions, times
- Reader thinks "that's not exactly how it happened to me"
- Example: "Have you been at the gym on a Monday morning, looked at the weights, and felt overwhelmed by the 20-year-old guys lifting twice your weight?"

**Appropriately Vague (ACCEPT):**
- Describes the emotional experience without locking in specifics
- Reader fills in their OWN scene, their OWN version of the pain
- Reader thinks "yes, that's EXACTLY what happens to me"
- Example: "If you've ever walked into a gym and felt like you didn't belong..."

**Too Vague (REJECT):**
- So generic it could mean anything
- No emotional specificity — just broad categories
- Reader thinks "I guess, sort of?"
- Example: "If you've ever felt bad about your health..."

| Test | REJECT | ACCEPT |
|------|--------|--------|
| Clemens Specificity | "Every morning at 6am you drag yourself out of bed, look in the mirror at your midsection, and feel disgusted" | "If you wake up feeling tired no matter how much sleep you get..." |
| Clemens Specificity | "You've tried Weight Watchers, Jenny Craig, keto, and paleo — and nothing stuck past 2 weeks" | "If you've tried dieting or exercising yet nothing works permanently..." |
| Clemens Specificity | "Your wife gave you a disappointed look last night when you couldn't perform" | "If you've felt the debilitating humiliation and shame of being unable to perform..." |

### Step 3: Draft the Problem Callout Using the "If You..." Structure

Use the proven callout structure from Georgi's formula and vault patterns:

```
Structure:
  [Optional: Brief emotional frame from hook momentum]

  If you [pain point 1 — most universal, broadest]...

  If you [pain point 2 — more specific emotional pain]...

  If you [pain point 3 — the consequence pain, what it costs them]...

  [Optional: If you [pain point 4 — the hidden fear]]...

  Then pay close attention to what I'm about to [share/tell you/reveal] in the next [X] minutes.
```

**Sequencing logic:**
1. First pain point: Cast the widest net — the symptom almost every prospect has
2. Second pain point: Narrow to the emotional experience — how it FEELS
3. Third pain point: The consequence — what it's costing them (time, money, relationships, health)
4. Fourth pain point (optional): The hidden fear they haven't admitted — the thing they worry about at 2am

### Step 4: Validate Prospect Language

Every phrase in the problem callout must pass the Prospect Language Test:

> "Would the prospect say this exact phrase when describing their problem to a trusted friend?"

| Clinical/Marketing (REJECT) | Prospect Language (ACCEPT) |
|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| "Suboptimal metabolic function" | "No matter what you eat, the weight keeps piling on" |
| "Erectile dysfunction symptoms" | "Your erections seem weaker, softer, less full" |
| "Declining golf performance metrics" | "You keep losing distance off the tee" |
| "Financial underperformance" | "Your savings aren't growing the way they should" |
| "Decreased energy output" | "You feel tired to the bone" |

### Step 5: Verify Callout Does NOT Over-Explain

The problem callout CALLS OUT problems — it does NOT:
- Explain WHY the prospect has these problems (that is the root cause section)
- Identify what the prospect has been doing wrong (that is the argument's job)
- Detail why previous solutions failed (Georgi DON'T)
- Hint at or name the root cause (too early — that comes after credibility)
- Offer any solution language (the elevator pitch handles this)

**Over-explanation test:** If the callout contains the word "because" followed by an explanation, it has crossed into explanation territory. Remove it.

### Step 6: Score the Problem Callout

| Dimension | Weight | Scale | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| Clemens vagueness compliance | 0.30 | 1-10 | Are problems vague enough for self-identification? |
| Prospect language authenticity | 0.25 | 1-10 | Would the prospect say these exact words? |
| Emotional resonance | 0.20 | 1-10 | Does the prospect feel "this is about ME"? |
| Pain point coverage | 0.15 | 1-10 | Are 2-4 distinct pain points addressed? |
| Structural flow | 0.10 | 1-10 | Does the callout flow naturally from hook to attention lock? |

**Minimum threshold:** Weighted average >= 7.0/10

## Anti-Patterns

- **The Clemens Violation:** "Have you ever been sitting at your desk at 2pm on a Tuesday, drinking your third cup of coffee, staring at a spreadsheet, feeling your eyelids getting heavy..." — too many specific details. Reader thinks "I don't drink coffee at my desk." Fix: "If you feel exhausted no matter how much sleep you get..."
- **Clinical language:** "Are you experiencing symptoms of adrenal fatigue?" — no prospect says "experiencing symptoms of." Fix: "If you're tired ALL the time, even after a full night's sleep..."
- **Marketing voice:** "Are you SICK AND TIRED of struggling with your weight?" — sounds like an infomercial, not a conversation. Fix: "If the scale keeps creeping up no matter what you try..."
- **Root cause leakage:** "If your gut microbiome is out of balance and that's why you feel bloated..." — the callout just named the root cause. Save that for after credibility insertion.
- **Solution contamination:** "If you're looking for a natural way to boost your energy..." — this is solution language. The callout describes PROBLEMS, not solution-seeking.
- **Too vague to connect:** "If you're not happy with your life..." — so broad it means nothing. The prospect doesn't feel called out, they feel generalized.
