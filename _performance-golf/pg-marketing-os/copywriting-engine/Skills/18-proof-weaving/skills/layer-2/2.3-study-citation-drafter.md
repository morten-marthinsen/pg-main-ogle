# Microskill 2.3: Study Citation Drafter

**Layer:** 2 (Drafting)
**Purpose:** Write persuasive (not academic) study citations

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Study citations in copy are NOT academic references. They are PERSUASION TOOLS that borrow institutional credibility to support claims. The goal is to make science feel accessible, relevant, and emotionally compelling — not to pass peer review.

---

## ACADEMIC VS. PERSUASIVE CITATIONS

### Academic Style (WRONG for copy)

```
"A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study published in the
Journal of Clinical Nutrition (Smith et al., 2019) demonstrated that
supplementation with CoQ10 resulted in a statistically significant
improvement in cardiovascular function (p<0.05)."
```

**Problems:**
- Jargon-heavy
- Emotionally dead
- Reader doesn't know what to feel
- "So what?" not answered

### Persuasive Style (RIGHT for copy)

```
"When researchers at Harvard Medical School tested this exact compound
on heart patients, something remarkable happened: blood flow improved
by 62% in just 60 minutes. Not over weeks or months — in one hour.
That's the power of what you're about to discover."
```

**Strengths:**
- Institution named (credibility)
- Specific outcome (62%)
- Timeframe emphasized (60 minutes)
- Emotional bridge (remarkable)
- Reader relevance (what YOU'RE about to discover)

---

## THE 6-STEP PERSUASIVE CITATION STRUCTURE

### Step 1: Institution Name-Drop

**Lead with the credibility source:**
- "Harvard researchers discovered..."
- "Scientists at the Mayo Clinic found..."
- "A landmark study from Johns Hopkins revealed..."

**Institution Hierarchy:**
1. Harvard, Mayo Clinic, Johns Hopkins (household names)
2. Major universities (UCLA, Stanford, Oxford)
3. Government institutions (NIH, CDC)
4. International bodies (WHO, medical associations)
5. Specialized research institutes

---

### Step 2: Study Context (Optional)

**Make it feel recent, relevant, important:**
- "In a groundbreaking 2023 study..."
- "For the first time ever, researchers..."
- "What they found shocked the medical community..."

---

### Step 3: Specific Finding with Numbers

**The heart of the citation — specific, measurable:**
- "Blood flow improved by 62%"
- "Participants lost an average of 14.7 pounds"
- "Inflammation markers dropped by 40%"

**Number Rules:**
- Use exact numbers (62%, not "about 60%")
- Include timeframe when impressive
- Compare to placebo/control when dramatic

---

### Step 4: Timeframe Emphasis

**Speed creates impact:**
- "In just 60 minutes"
- "After only 8 weeks"
- "Within the first month"

---

### Step 5: Emotional Bridge

**Connect science to feeling:**
- "That's the power of..."
- "Which explains why..."
- "Think about what that means..."
- "Now imagine..."

---

### Step 6: Reader Relevance

**Make it about THEM:**
- "And you're about to have access to the same..."
- "This is exactly what's in the formula I'm sharing with you"
- "The same compound that produced these results..."

---

## STUDY CITATION FORMATS

### Format 1: The Authority Sandwich

```
[Institution] + [Finding] + [What It Means For You]
```

**Example:**
```
Harvard Medical School researchers tested this compound on 87 heart patients with severely compromised cardiac function.

The results? Blood flow improved by 62% in just 60 minutes.

Now, you're about to have access to the exact same formula.
```

---

### Format 2: The Mechanism Validator

```
[Mechanism Claim] + [Study Proof] + [Simplification]
```

**Example:**
```
When CoQ10 levels drop, your heart literally runs out of fuel.

Johns Hopkins researchers proved this when they measured CoQ10 levels in heart failure patients — they were 50% lower than healthy individuals.

Think of it like trying to run a car on half a tank of gas. It might still move, but it's struggling.
```

---

### Format 3: The Comparison Crusher

```
[Our Approach] vs [Standard Approach] + [Study Results]
```

**Example:**
```
Most calcium supplements barely absorb — studies show only 30% gets into your bloodstream.

But this form? Stanford researchers found it delivers 250% more usable calcium to your bones.

That's not a small difference. That's the difference between a supplement that works and one that's just expensive urine.
```

---

### Format 4: The Data Cascade (Stansberry Style)

```
[Setup Question] + [Data Point 1] + [Data Point 2] + [Data Point 3] + [Conclusion]
```

**Example:**
```
How do we know this is a real crisis? Look at the government's own data:

• Bureau of Labor Statistics: Lowest workforce participation since 1970
• Federal Reserve: Money supply expanded 400% in just 6 years
• Census Bureau: 49% of Americans now receive government benefits

The patterns don't lie. Something unprecedented is happening.
```

## STUDY CITATION INTENSIFICATION

### Intensifier 1: The Counter-Narrative Reveal

**Study contradicts conventional wisdom:**
```
"For years, doctors told you to avoid dietary fat.

Then researchers at the University of Connecticut discovered something that turned that advice on its head: people who ate MORE fat actually lost MORE weight — and kept it off.

Everything you've been told was wrong."
```

---

### Intensifier 2: The "Even The Skeptics" Frame

**Study convinced doubters:**
```
"Even the researchers were skeptical at first. How could a simple nutrient have this much impact?

But after 12 weeks, the evidence was undeniable: participants showed a 47% improvement in cognitive function.

The lead researcher said, 'We've never seen results like this.'"
```

---

### Intensifier 3: The Scale Proof

**Study backs up massive adoption:**
```
"There's a reason 16 million bottles have been sold.

It's not marketing. It's results — backed by dozens of published studies showing exactly why this formula works."
```

---

## COMMON CITATION MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Over-Qualifying

**WRONG:** "A study suggested that participants may have experienced possible improvements..."
**RIGHT:** "Participants improved by 47%."

### Mistake 2: Missing Emotional Bridge

**WRONG:** "The study showed a 62% improvement in blood flow markers."
**RIGHT:** "Blood flow improved by 62% — meaning more oxygen, more nutrients, more life reaching every cell in your body."

### Mistake 3: Burying the Number

**WRONG:** "In the study, various cardiovascular markers improved, including blood flow which increased by approximately 62%."
**RIGHT:** "62% improvement in blood flow. In just 60 minutes."

### Mistake 4: Generic Institution

**WRONG:** "Studies show..."
**RIGHT:** "Harvard researchers proved..."
