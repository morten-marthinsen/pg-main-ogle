**Version:** 1.0

**Persona:** Dr. James Liu (Research Director) + Marcus Webster (Pattern Synthesis)

---

## Purpose

Map EVERY problem the prospect faces — before, during, and after engaging with the product — and create a specific solution for each. This is Hormozi Grand Slam Offer Construction Step 2+3: the exhaustive problem-solution mapping that becomes the raw material for the value stack.

Most offers fail because they solve the OBVIOUS problem and ignore the 20 surrounding problems that prevent the prospect from buying, starting, continuing, or succeeding. This skill maps the entire problem landscape.

---

## The Principle

**"Every unsolved problem is either an objection that prevents purchase or a failure that prevents results. Both kill your offer."**

## Mapping Process

### Step 1: Enumerate Problems by Phase

```
MAP problems across the COMPLETE prospect journey:

PHASE 1 — PROBLEMS BEFORE PURCHASE:
  "What stops them from BUYING?"

  Category A: Awareness Problems
    - They don't know the root cause of their problem
    - They don't know this type of solution exists
    - They've never heard of the mechanism

  Category B: Belief Problems
    - They don't believe this can work (for them specifically)
    - They've tried similar things and failed
    - They think their situation is unique/different

  Category C: Trust Problems
    - They don't trust the seller
    - They don't trust the claims
    - They think it's too good to be true

  Category D: Cost Problems
    - They think the price is too high
    - They can't afford it right now
    - They don't see the ROI

  Category E: Timing Problems
    - "Now isn't the right time"
    - "I need to do X first"
    - "I'll start after [event]"

  FOR each category:
    EXTRACT specific problems from upstream research data
    USE customer_language where available
    MINIMUM 2 problems per category
```

```
PHASE 2 — PROBLEMS DURING USAGE:
  "What stops them from STARTING and CONTINUING?"

  Category F: Getting Started Problems
    - They don't know where to begin
    - The product feels overwhelming
    - They lack prerequisite knowledge/tools

  Category G: Implementation Problems
    - They get stuck at specific steps
    - They make mistakes that derail progress
    - They can't do it consistently

  Category H: Motivation Problems
    - They lose momentum after initial excitement
    - They don't see results fast enough
    - They compare themselves to others and feel behind

  Category I: Environment Problems
    - Their spouse/family doesn't support them
    - Their schedule doesn't accommodate it
    - External circumstances interfere

  FOR each category:
    EXTRACT specific problems from upstream research
    REFERENCE conventional_failures for known sticking points
    MINIMUM 2 problems per category
```

```
PHASE 3 — PROBLEMS AFTER INITIAL USE:
  "What stops them from ACHIEVING and MAINTAINING the result?"

  Category J: Plateau Problems
    - Initial results stall
    - They hit a ceiling they can't break through
    - The approach stops working

  Category K: Sustainability Problems
    - They can't maintain the changes long-term
    - They revert to old habits
    - Life events cause regression

  Category L: Next-Level Problems
    - They achieved the initial goal but want more
    - New problems emerge from the transformation
    - They need ongoing support/updates

  FOR each category:
    EXTRACT specific problems from research and conventional_failures
    MINIMUM 1 problem per category
```

### Step 2: Classify by Value Equation Dimension

```
FOR every problem enumerated above:

  CLASSIFY which Value Equation dimension it threatens:

  DREAM OUTCOME THREAT:
    Problem makes the prospect doubt the outcome is POSSIBLE
    "I don't think I can really achieve [dream outcome]"
    Example: "I've tried 12 diets and none worked for me"

  LIKELIHOOD THREAT:
    Problem makes the prospect doubt THEY can achieve it
    "Maybe it works for others but not for someone like me"
    Example: "I don't have the discipline"

  TIME DELAY THREAT:
    Problem makes the prospect think it'll take too LONG
    "This sounds like it'll take forever"
    Example: "I need results before my wedding in 8 weeks"

  EFFORT THREAT:
    Problem makes the prospect think it'll be too HARD
    "This sounds like too much work"
    Example: "I don't have 2 hours a day to dedicate to this"

  TAG each problem with its primary dimension
  NOTE: Some problems threaten multiple dimensions — tag ALL that apply
```

### Step 3: Create Solutions for Every Problem

```
FOR each problem:

  GENERATE a specific solution:

  SOLUTION DESIGN QUESTIONS:
    1. "What would I need to SHOW someone to eliminate this concern?"
    2. "What would I need to GIVE someone to remove this obstacle?"
    3. "What would I need to DO for someone to solve this?"

  SOLUTION TYPES:
    - EDUCATION: Teach them something that removes the concern
      Example: Problem "I've tried diets before" →
              Solution "Here's why those diets failed (root cause) and why this is different"

    - TOOL: Give them a resource that handles the problem
      Example: Problem "I don't know where to start" →
              Solution "Quick Start Checklist — your first 48 hours mapped step-by-step"

    - SYSTEM: Provide a process that prevents the problem
      Example: Problem "I lose motivation after week 2" →
              Solution "Accountability check-in system with daily progress tracking"

    - DONE-FOR-YOU: Remove the burden entirely
      Example: Problem "I don't have time to plan meals" →
              Solution "Pre-built meal plans for every week"

    - SOCIAL PROOF: Show evidence that people like them succeeded
      Example: Problem "I don't think this works for people my age" →
              Solution "Case studies of 50+ members who achieved [outcome]"

    - GUARANTEE: Eliminate the financial risk
      Example: Problem "What if it doesn't work for me?" →
              Solution "60-day money-back guarantee — try everything risk-free"

  FOR each solution:
    DOCUMENT:
      problem_id: unique identifier
      problem_statement: in prospect language
      problem_phase: before / during / after
      value_equation_dimension: dream_outcome / likelihood / time_delay / effort
      solution_type: education / tool / system / done_for_you / social_proof / guarantee
      solution_description: what the solution IS
      solution_delivery: how it would be delivered
      mechanism_connection: how this relates to the core mechanism
```

### Step 4: Prioritize by Impact

```
FOR each problem-solution pair:

  SCORE on two axes:

  PROSPECT IMPACT (1-10):
    How much does solving this problem increase perceived offer value?
    10 = Solving this is a dealmaker
    1 = Solving this barely matters

  DELIVERY COST (1-10):
    How expensive/complex is it to deliver this solution?
    10 = Very expensive to deliver
    1 = Nearly free to deliver

  CALCULATE priority_score = Impact / Cost
    HIGH PRIORITY: Score ≥ 3.0 (high impact, low cost)
    MEDIUM PRIORITY: Score 1.0-2.9
    LOW PRIORITY: Score < 1.0 (low impact, high cost)

  RANK all problem-solution pairs by priority_score
```

### Step 5: Validate Completeness

```
COMPLETENESS CHECKS:

CHECK 1: All 3 phases covered (before, during, after)
  COUNT problems per phase
  MINIMUM: 5 before, 4 during, 3 after = 12 total minimum

CHECK 2: All 4 value equation dimensions represented
  COUNT problems per dimension
  MINIMUM: 2 per dimension

CHECK 3: All solution types used
  At least 3 different solution types present

CHECK 4: High-priority solutions exist
  At least 5 problem-solution pairs with priority_score ≥ 3.0

CHECK 5: Objections covered
  Cross-reference objections_inventory from research
  Every documented objection should map to at least one problem-solution pair

IF any check fails:
  RETURN to relevant step and expand
  DO NOT proceed to 1.3 with gaps
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Minimum 15 unique problems identified across all phases
2. Minimum 10 problems at HIGH priority tier
3. All 4 value equation dimensions represented with minimum 2 problems each
4. Every documented objection from research has a corresponding solution
5. Solutions are specific (not "provide support" but "weekly live Q&A call every Tuesday at 7pm EST")
6. Every solution connects back to the mechanism or product capability
7. Problem statements use prospect language, not marketer language

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT list only obvious problems — dig into the non-obvious (Phase 2 and 3 problems are often missed)
- Do NOT create solutions that the product can't actually deliver
- Do NOT ignore the "before purchase" phase — these are the objections that kill sales
- Do NOT use vague solution descriptions ("ongoing support" — support HOW? WHEN? FROM WHOM?)
- Do NOT skip the prioritization — not every problem-solution pair belongs in the offer
