# Skill 1.6: Core Offer Definer

**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 1 (Offer Construction)
**Type:** Strategic Synthesis
**Dependencies:** dream_outcome.json (1.1), value_stack.json (1.5), value_equation_optimization.json (1.3), upstream_data.json (0.1)
**Output:** core_offer_definition.json
**Persona:** Legendary Copywriter + Alex Rivera (Strategic Integration)

---

## Purpose

Synthesize all Layer 1 outputs into a single, clear, complete definition of the core offer — what the prospect is buying, what it does, who it's for, and why it matters. This is the crystallized offer identity before enhancement (Layer 2) and validation (Layer 3).

Think of this as the offer's "pitch in one breath." If someone asked "what is this offer?" — this skill produces the answer.

---

## The Principle

**"If you can't explain the offer in 30 seconds and make someone lean forward, it's not defined yet."**

---

## Definition Process

### Step 1: Synthesize the Offer Identity

```
FROM all Layer 1 outputs, ANSWER these questions:

QUESTION 1: "WHAT is this?"
  Combine mechanism_name + product positioning + value stack
  → Single sentence: "[Product Name] is a [mechanism_type] that [core function]"
  Example: "The Metabolic Reset Protocol is a 6-week system that reprograms
           your body's fat-burning signals so you lose weight without dieting."

QUESTION 2: "WHO is this for?"
  FROM upstream_data → demographic_profile + market_psychology
  → Single sentence: "[Specific avatar] who [specific situation/pain]"
  Example: "Women over 40 who've tried every diet and still can't lose
           the stubborn weight around their midsection."

QUESTION 3: "WHAT do they get?"
  FROM value_stack → core_product.components + bonuses
  → Bullet list of tangible deliverables
  Example: "You get the 6-week protocol, the meal planning system,
           the progress tracker, plus 3 bonuses..."

QUESTION 4: "WHAT happens when they use it?"
  FROM dream_outcome → primary_statement + supporting_outcomes
  → Transformation narrative
  Example: "In 6 weeks, you'll be 20 pounds lighter, sleeping better,
           and actually enjoying what you see in the mirror."

QUESTION 5: "WHY does this work?"
  FROM mechanism → mechanism_explanation + differentiation_claim
  → Mechanism justification
  Example: "Unlike calorie counting, this targets the hormonal root cause
           of weight resistance — the metabolic signals that have been
           working against you."

QUESTION 6: "WHY should they believe it?"
  FROM proof_inventory → proof_assets (top 3)
  → Proof summary
  Example: "Backed by a Stanford metabolic study, used by 1,400+ members,
           with a 91% success rate in the first 6 weeks."

QUESTION 7: "WHY NOW?"
  FROM value_equation_optimization → time optimization
  → Urgency justification
  Example: "Your metabolism slows 2% every year you wait. Every month
           without this protocol is a month harder to reverse."
```

### Step 2: Determine Offer Positioning

```
HOW is the offer FRAMED in the market?

POSITIONING OPTIONS:

OPTION A: THE SYSTEM
  "A complete system for [outcome]"
  Best when: Multiple components work together, prospect needs structure
  Example: "The Complete Golf Transformation System"

OPTION B: THE SECRET/DISCOVERY
  "The [discovery/secret] behind [outcome]"
  Best when: Mechanism is novel, market is sophisticated, curiosity-driven
  Example: "The Neural Putting Secret That Tour Pros Don't Talk About"

OPTION C: THE METHOD/PROTOCOL
  "The [name] method/protocol for [outcome]"
  Best when: Step-by-step process, credibility matters, professional audience
  Example: "The Metabolic Reset Protocol"

OPTION D: THE TOOL
  "The [name] tool that [action]"
  Best when: Single-use focus, simplicity matters, specific function
  Example: "The TradeGuard Alert System"

OPTION E: THE EXPERIENCE
  "The [name] [experience type]"
  Best when: Transformation is personal, community matters, premium positioning
  Example: "The 90-Day Breakthrough Challenge"

SELECT positioning based on:
  - mechanism_type from Skill 03
  - market_psychology from Skill 01 (what resonates with this audience?)
  - big_idea_type from Skill 05 (what angle does the campaign use?)
  - vault_offer_patterns (what positioning dominates successful offers in this space?)

JUSTIFY the selection with specific upstream data
```

### Step 3: Construct the Offer Thesis

```
THE OFFER THESIS = One paragraph that captures the entire offer.

FORMULA:
"[Product Name] is a [positioning type] for [specific avatar] who [specific problem].
Using the [mechanism name] — [one-sentence mechanism explanation] — you'll [primary
dream outcome] in [timeframe]. You get [core components summary], plus [bonuses summary].
[Proof snippet]. [Guarantee snippet]."

EXAMPLE:
"The Metabolic Reset Protocol is a 6-week system for women over 40 who've tried every
diet and still can't lose the stubborn weight. Using the Hormonal Signal Reset Method
— which targets the metabolic root cause that diets ignore — you'll drop up to 20 pounds
in 6 weeks without giving up the foods you love. You get the complete protocol, custom
meal plans, and daily tracking system, plus 3 fast-action bonuses. Tested by 1,400+
members with a 91% success rate. Backed by a full 60-day money-back guarantee."

CONSTRAINTS:
  - Maximum 4 sentences
  - Must include: product name, avatar, mechanism, outcome, timeframe, proof hint, guarantee hint
  - Must sound like a human speaking, not a template
  - Must differentiate from competitors (not generic)
```

### Step 4: Define the Offer Anatomy

```
COMPILE the complete structural definition:

OFFER ANATOMY:

  identity:
    product_name: "[Name]"
    positioning: "[system/secret/method/tool/experience]"
    tagline: "[One-line hook that captures the core value proposition]"

  target:
    primary_avatar: "[Who specifically]"
    avatar_situation: "[Their current painful reality]"
    avatar_desire: "[What they want instead]"

  transformation:
    primary_dream_outcome: "[From dream_outcome.json]"
    mechanism: "[Name + type from mechanism-package]"
    mechanism_one_liner: "[One sentence: how it works]"
    timeframe: "[Specific expected timeframe]"

  components:
    core_product:
      name: "[Core product name]"
      total_components: integer
      total_stated_value: "$[amount]"
      components_list: [from value_stack.json]

    bonuses:
      total_bonuses: integer
      total_bonus_value: "$[amount]"
      bonuses_list: [from value_stack.json]

  proof_summary:
    strongest_proof_elements: [top 3 proof assets]
    credibility_anchor: "[strongest authority signal]"

  risk_reversal_preview:
    guarantee_type_anticipated: "[from proof gaps and market analysis]"
    risk_frame: "[How risk is positioned]"

  grand_total_stated_value: "$[amount]"

  offer_thesis: "[The one-paragraph synthesis from Step 3]"
```

### Step 5: Internal Coherence Validation

```
BEFORE passing to the construction gate:

COHERENCE CHECK 1: Avatar-Outcome Alignment
  Does the dream outcome match what the avatar actually wants?
  Does the language match how THEY would describe it?

COHERENCE CHECK 2: Mechanism-Outcome Alignment
  Can the mechanism actually deliver the claimed outcome?
  Is the mechanism credible for this type of transformation?

COHERENCE CHECK 3: Stack-Outcome Alignment
  Does the value stack contain everything needed to achieve the outcome?
  Are there gaps between what's promised and what's delivered?

COHERENCE CHECK 4: Proof-Claim Alignment
  Do the proof assets support the specific claims being made?
  Are there major claims with zero proof?

COHERENCE CHECK 5: Positioning-Market Alignment
  Does the positioning match the market's sophistication level?
  Does it differentiate from competitors identified in upstream research?

FOR each check:
  RATE: ALIGNED / PARTIALLY_ALIGNED / MISALIGNED
  IF MISALIGNED: Flag with specific description of the gap

OVERALL COHERENCE: PASS if zero MISALIGNED, WARNING if any PARTIALLY_ALIGNED
```

## Quality Criteria

1. All 7 identity questions answered with specific, non-generic content
2. Offer positioning selected with upstream data justification (not arbitrary)
3. Offer thesis is ≤ 4 sentences and includes all required elements
4. Offer anatomy is complete — no empty or placeholder fields
5. All 5 coherence checks pass (zero MISALIGNED)
6. Offer is distinguishable from any competitor offer (positioning test)
7. A non-expert could read the offer thesis and understand what's being sold

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT define the offer as a list of features — define it as a transformation with components
- Do NOT use jargon the prospect wouldn't use
- Do NOT leave the mechanism out of the definition — it IS the differentiator
- Do NOT write the thesis as a template fill-in — it should read as natural speech
- Do NOT skip coherence validation — misaligned offers collapse under scrutiny
