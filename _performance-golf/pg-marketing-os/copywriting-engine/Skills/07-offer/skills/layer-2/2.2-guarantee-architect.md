## Purpose

Design the guarantee architecture that eliminates perceived purchase risk. The guarantee is not a refund policy — it's a psychological instrument that transfers risk from buyer to seller, increasing both perceived likelihood of achievement (Lever 2) and reducing perceived effort/sacrifice (Lever 4: "even if it doesn't work, I lose nothing").

Sarah A. Conco ensures the guarantee is ethically sound and legally defensible. Dr. Vasquez ensures the guarantee claims are evidence-based.

---

## The Principle

**"The guarantee's job isn't to handle refunds. Its job is to make the purchase decision feel risk-free BEFORE the prospect buys. The best guarantees are almost never used because the offer delivers."**

---

## Guarantee Types (Hormozi Framework)

```
TYPE 1: UNCONDITIONAL
  "Full refund, no questions asked, within [X] days."
  - Highest trust signal
  - Lowest friction for buyer
  - Risk: attracts tire-kickers and refund abuse
  - Best for: Low-to-mid ticket offers, new market entry, high confidence in product

TYPE 2: CONDITIONAL
  "Full refund IF you [do X, Y, Z] and don't get [result]."
  - Increases perceived likelihood (conditions = steps to success)
  - Filters out non-serious buyers
  - Conditions should be achievable and reasonable
  - Best for: Mid-to-high ticket, performance-based products

TYPE 3: ANTI-GUARANTEE
  "All sales final. No refunds."
  - Exclusivity signal ("only serious people")
  - Creates scarcity psychology (can't just "try it out")
  - Risk: reduces conversion; requires extreme proof/authority
  - Best for: Ultra-premium offers, established authority, exclusive access

TYPE 4: IMPLIED / PERFORMANCE-BASED
  "You don't pay unless/until [result achieved]."
  - Revenue share, pay-on-results, trial-then-pay
  - Maximum risk reversal
  - Risk: cash flow implications; only works for certain business models
  - Best for: Services, consulting, high-ticket coaching

TYPE 5: STACKED GUARANTEE
  Combine 2+ types for maximum impact:
  "If you don't [result] in [time], you get a full refund AND you keep the bonuses."
  "If you do [conditions] and don't get [result], I'll refund you AND pay you $500."
  - Creates extreme confidence
  - Signals total belief in the product
  - Best for: Premium offers where conversion matters more than refund cost
```

## Design Process

### Step 1: Assess Risk Landscape

```
FROM upstream_data + core_offer_definition:

MAP the prospect's risk perception:

FINANCIAL RISK:
  "What if I waste my money?"
  SEVERITY: Proportional to price
  Price < $50: Low financial risk
  Price $50-$500: Moderate financial risk
  Price $500-$2000: High financial risk
  Price > $2000: Very high financial risk
  → Determines guarantee STRENGTH needed

OUTCOME RISK:
  "What if it doesn't work?"
  FROM objections_inventory + proof_gaps
  What specifically are they afraid won't happen?
  → Determines what the guarantee should PROMISE

TIME RISK:
  "What if I waste my time?"
  FROM value_equation_optimization → time perception
  How long until they know if it works?
  → Determines guarantee DURATION

EFFORT RISK:
  "What if I put in the work and still fail?"
  FROM problem_solution_map → during_usage problems
  What would make them feel the effort was wasted?
  → Determines guarantee CONDITIONS (what they must do)

SOCIAL RISK:
  "What if people judge me for buying this?"
  FROM market_psychology → social dynamics
  Is there embarrassment risk in buying this type of product?
  → Determines guarantee DISCRETION (ease of refund process)

COMPILE: risk_profile with severity rating for each dimension
```

### Step 2: Select Guarantee Type(s)

```
BASED ON risk_profile:

DECISION MATRIX:

IF financial risk HIGH + proof strong + product confidence high:
  → CONDITIONAL GUARANTEE (filters serious buyers, demonstrates confidence)

IF financial risk HIGH + proof moderate + new market:
  → UNCONDITIONAL GUARANTEE (removes all barriers to first purchase)

IF financial risk LOW + product established:
  → CONDITIONAL with generous terms (adds perceived value without high risk)

IF ultra-premium positioning + exclusive access:
  → ANTI-GUARANTEE or IMPLIED (exclusivity signal)

IF conversion maximization is priority:
  → STACKED GUARANTEE (conditional + "keep bonuses" + refund)

REFERENCE vault_offer_patterns → guarantee_patterns:
  What guarantee types dominate successful offers in this niche?
  What's the most common duration?
  What conditions are typical?

SELECT primary guarantee type with justification
CONSIDER secondary guarantee type for stacking
```

### Step 3: Design Guarantee Components

```
FOR the selected guarantee type(s):

DURATION:
  How long does the guarantee last?
  PRINCIPLE: Long enough to genuinely try the product + see results
  COMMON: 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, 365 days
  NOTE: Counterintuitively, LONGER guarantees often produce FEWER refunds
        (less urgency to request refund + more time to see results)

  SELECT duration with reasoning

CONDITIONS (if conditional):
  What must the buyer DO before claiming the guarantee?
  PRINCIPLE: Conditions should be:
    - Achievable by any serious buyer
    - Documented (verifiable)
    - Tied to the product's usage (not arbitrary)
    - Reasonable (not "attend 100% of sessions AND complete all homework AND...")

  Examples:
    "Complete the first 3 modules and submit your results worksheet"
    "Follow the protocol for 30 consecutive days"
    "Attend at least 4 of the 6 coaching calls"

  NOTE: Conditions INCREASE perceived likelihood (they feel like steps to success)

  LIST conditions with achievability assessment

OUTCOME PROMISE:
  What specific result does the guarantee promise?
  ALIGN with primary_dream_outcome (or a subset):
    Full dream outcome: "If you don't [achieve primary dream outcome]..."
    Partial outcome: "If you don't [achieve measurable milestone]..."
    Experience outcome: "If you don't feel [specific improvement]..."

  SELECT outcome promise — should be specific but achievable

REFUND MECHANISM:
  How does the refund process work?
  PRINCIPLE: The easier the refund, the more trust it builds
    "Send one email to [address] and get a full refund within 48 hours"
    NOT: "Fill out a 3-page form, schedule a call, wait 30 business days"

BONUS RETENTION:
  Does the buyer keep anything if they refund?
  Hormozi: "Keep the bonuses" = powerful trust signal
  DECIDE: Keep everything / Keep bonuses only / Return everything

PENALTY REVERSAL (for stacked):
  Does the seller PAY the buyer if the guarantee triggers?
  "I'll refund your purchase AND pay you $100 for your trouble"
  NOTE: Only use if extreme confidence in product delivery
```

### Step 4: Craft Guarantee Language

```
WRITE the actual guarantee statement:

STRUCTURE:
  1. Acknowledge the risk ("I know you might be wondering...")
  2. State the guarantee type ("That's why...")
  3. State the duration ("You have a full [X] days")
  4. State the conditions (if conditional)
  5. State the outcome promise
  6. State the refund mechanism
  7. State bonus retention (if applicable)
  8. Close with confidence statement

EXAMPLE (Stacked Conditional):
  "I know you might be wondering, 'What if this doesn't work for me?'
   That's why I'm putting all the risk on my shoulders.
   Try the entire [Product Name] system for a full 60 days.
   Complete the first 3 modules and follow the Quick Start Protocol.
   If you don't [specific outcome] — or if you don't feel it's the
   single best investment you've made in your [domain] this year —
   just send one email to support@[domain].com. You'll get every
   penny back within 48 hours. AND you keep all three bonuses as
   my thank you for giving it a real shot. That's how confident I am
   this will work for you."

WRITE 2-3 versions of guarantee language:
  - Version 1: Full formal statement (for sales page)
  - Version 2: Conversational version (for VSL script)
  - Version 3: Short version (for email/ads)
```

### Step 5: Business Model Viability Check

```
CRITICAL: Evaluate guarantee sustainability

ESTIMATE refund exposure:
  Expected refund rate for this guarantee type:
    Unconditional 30-day: 5-15% typical
    Unconditional 60-day: 3-10% typical (longer = fewer)
    Conditional: 2-8% typical
    Anti-guarantee: 0%
    Stacked: 3-12% typical

  AT projected price and volume:
    Monthly revenue × refund_rate = monthly_refund_cost
    Is this sustainable?

FLAG if:
  - Refund rate × price creates unsustainable cash exposure
  - Conditions are so loose that abuse is likely
  - Guarantee promises outcomes the product can't reliably deliver
  - Industry regulations require specific guarantee language

COMPLIANCE CHECK (Sarah A. Conco):
  - Does the guarantee language comply with FTC guidelines?
  - Are there implied claims that need substantiation?
  - Is "typical results" language needed?
  - Are there industry-specific refund requirements?
```

## Quality Criteria

1. Risk profile covers all 5 dimensions with evidence from upstream data
2. Guarantee type selection justified with decision matrix reasoning
3. If conditional, conditions are achievable and tied to product usage
4. Guarantee language written in 3 versions (formal, conversational, short)
5. Business viability check completed — refund exposure is sustainable
6. Compliance check completed — no FTC red flags
7. Guarantee clearly increases perceived likelihood (Lever 2)
8. Guarantee language makes purchase feel risk-free, not defensive

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Do NOT design guarantees the business can't sustain
- Do NOT make conditions so difficult they feel like escape clauses
- Do NOT write guarantee language that sounds like a legal document — it should sound like a confident promise
- Do NOT guarantee specific numerical outcomes without proof to support them
- Do NOT ignore compliance — FTC enforcement is real and penalties are severe
- Do NOT copy competitor guarantees verbatim — design for THIS offer's specific risk profile
