# Skill 14 — Microskill 4.2: Anti-Slop Validator

**Skill:** 11 — Mechanism Narrative
**Microskill:** 4.2 — Anti-Slop Validator
**Version:** 1.0
**Layer:** 4 (Validation & Assembly)
**Position:** Second microskill in Layer 4
**Type:** Zero-Tolerance Quality Gate
**Dependencies:** 3.4-narrative-flow-polisher (polished narrative)
**Output:** anti-slop-report.json
**BLOCKING:** Zero violations required to pass

---

## PURPOSE

Performs a zero-tolerance check for generic, cliched, AI-default, or vague language in the mechanism narrative. Elite copy has a specific quality that generic AI output lacks — it is precise, concrete, surprising, and grounded in real detail. This validator catches every instance of banned phrases, banned patterns, and lazy writing that would make the narrative feel machine-generated rather than human-crafted. A single violation triggers a FAIL, and every violation must be resolved before assembly.

---

## IDENTITY

**What this IS:**
- A zero-tolerance quality gate that catches generic and AI-default language
- A specificity enforcer that demands concrete, surprising, grounded prose
- The final defense against "slop" — vague, cliched, or motivational filler

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a style guide — it catches BAD patterns, not subjective style preferences
- Not a grammar checker — checks for slop, not syntax
- Not a content validator — checks language quality, not factual accuracy
- Not lenient — zero violations means zero. Not "a few is fine." Zero.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Scan for Banned Phrases

```
BANNED PHRASE LIST (case-insensitive scan):

CATEGORY A — AI Copywriting Defaults:
  "cutting-edge"
  "revolutionary"
  "game-changing"
  "groundbreaking" (unless quoting a specific institution's exact words)
  "breakthrough" (unless quoting a specific institution or study title)
  "transform your"
  "unlock the power"
  "the secret to"
  "discover the"
  "harness the"
  "unleash"
  "supercharge"
  "turbocharge"
  "level up"
  "next level"
  "paradigm-shifting" (the adjective — "paradigm shift" as noun is acceptable for E5)
  "life-changing"
  "mind-blowing"
  "world-class" (unless describing a specific verified credential)
  "state-of-the-art"
  "best-in-class"

CATEGORY B — Vague Intensifiers:
  "incredibly"
  "amazingly"
  "unbelievably"
  "absolutely"
  "truly remarkable"
  "extraordinary"
  "phenomenal"
  "stunning"
  "jaw-dropping"
  "astonishing"
  "mind-boggling"

CATEGORY C — Empty Connectors:
  "in today's fast-paced world"
  "in this day and age"
  "now more than ever"
  "it's no secret that"
  "the fact of the matter is"
  "at the end of the day"
  "when all is said and done"
  "needless to say"
  "it goes without saying"
  "as we all know"

CATEGORY D — AI Structural Tells:
  "But here's the thing:" (overused AI transition)
  "Here's the kicker:" (AI cliche)
  "The truth is:" (acceptable once; flagged if used 3+ times)
  "Let me explain:" (AI instructional tone)
  "What if I told you" (AI hook cliche)
  "Imagine a world where" (AI future-casting)
  "Picture this:" (acceptable once; flagged if used 3+ times)

FOR EACH phrase IN banned_list:
  SCAN full_narrative for exact match (case-insensitive)
  IF found:
    LOG violation:
      phrase: string
      category: string
      location: { phase, paragraph, sentence }
      surrounding_context: string (10 words before and after)
      severity: "BANNED"

COUNT total_violations
```

### Step 2: Scan for Banned Patterns

```
BANNED PATTERN LIST:

PATTERN 1 — Vague Mechanism Description:
  Indicators: Mechanism described without concrete detail
  Examples:
    "This powerful compound works by addressing the root cause"
    — WHAT compound? HOW does it address? WHAT root cause specifically?
    "The technology uses advanced methods to improve results"
    — WHAT methods? HOW does it improve? WHAT results?
  Test: Can you draw a picture of what's described? If NO → violation
  Severity: BANNED

PATTERN 2 — Hand-Waving Explanation:
  Indicators: Explanation skips the "how" entirely
  Examples:
    "The mechanism naturally restores balance to your body"
    — HOW does it restore? WHAT balance? Through what process?
    "By leveraging the body's natural healing processes"
    — WHICH processes? How are they leveraged?
  Test: Could a skeptic say "but HOW exactly?" If YES → violation
  Severity: BANNED

PATTERN 3 — Unexplained Jargon:
  Indicators: Scientific term used without definition within 1 sentence
  Examples:
    "AMPK activates autophagy and reduces mTOR signaling"
    — "autophagy" and "mTOR" used without explanation
  Test: Would a layperson understand every term? If NO → violation
  Exception: Mechanism name itself (defined in Phase 3)
  Severity: BANNED

PATTERN 4 — Motivational Language in Place of Specifics:
  Indicators: Emotional language substituting for concrete explanation
  Examples:
    "You deserve to feel amazing and this is your chance"
    — No specific mechanism information, just motivation
    "It's time to take control of your health and finally
    experience the energy you've been missing"
    — Motivation, not mechanism explanation
  Test: Remove the sentence — does the mechanism explanation lose anything?
    If NO → the sentence was filler → violation
  Severity: BANNED

PATTERN 5 — Passive Voice in Action Sections:
  Indicators: Mechanism described in passive voice where active is clearer
  Examples:
    "Fat is burned by the activation of the enzyme"
    → Should be: "The enzyme activates and burns fat"
    "Inflammation is reduced through the compound's action"
    → Should be: "The compound reduces inflammation by [specific action]"
  Test: Is the actor (mechanism/compound/enzyme) the subject?
    If NO in action descriptions → violation
  Severity: WARNING (not full ban, but should be corrected)
  Threshold: Passive voice > 20% of sentences = FAIL

PATTERN 6 — Unsupported Superlatives:
  Indicators: "Most," "best," "first," "only" without source
  Examples:
    "The most powerful enzyme ever discovered"
    — Says who? Based on what measurement?
    "The only solution that actually works"
    — Proven by what? Compared to what?
  Test: Is the superlative backed by a named source or specific data?
    If NO → violation
  Exception: Quoting a specific institution ("Yale called it 'the most
    promising target'")
  Severity: BANNED

PATTERN 7 — Hedge Language:
  Indicators: Unnecessary hedging that weakens claims
  Examples:
    "This might help with..."
    "Some researchers believe..."
    "It's possible that..."
    "Evidence suggests that perhaps..."
  Test: Does the hedge add accuracy or just weaken the copy?
    If weakening without accuracy benefit → violation
  Severity: WARNING
  Note: Some hedging is legally required — distinguish legal hedges from
    weak writing

PATTERN 8 — Generic Benefit Lists:
  Indicators: List of benefits without mechanism connection
  Examples:
    "More energy, better sleep, clearer skin, improved mood"
    — Listed without explaining HOW mechanism produces each
  Test: Is each benefit traced to a specific mechanism action?
    If NO → violation (benefits should be EXPLAINED, not listed)
  Severity: BANNED

FOR EACH pattern, SCAN full_narrative
IF found: LOG violation with location, context, and severity
```

### Step 3: Scan for Repetitive Structures

```
REPETITION CHECK:

CHECK 1 — Sentence Opener Repetition:
  Scan first 3 words of every sentence
  FLAG if same opener used 3+ times in same phase:
    "And that's..." (3x)
    "But here's..." (3x)
    "The truth is..." (3x)
    "In fact..." (3x)
  Severity: WARNING

CHECK 2 — Paragraph Structure Repetition:
  Scan paragraph structures within each phase
  FLAG if 3+ consecutive paragraphs follow same structure:
    [Statement]. [Evidence]. [Implication].
    [Statement]. [Evidence]. [Implication].
    [Statement]. [Evidence]. [Implication].
  Severity: WARNING
  Fix: Vary paragraph structure — use questions, short punchy paragraphs,
    direct address

CHECK 3 — Transition Word Repetition:
  Scan transition words between paragraphs
  FLAG if same transition used 3+ times in narrative:
    "Furthermore..." / "Moreover..." / "Additionally..."
    (These are essay words, not copy words)
  Severity: WARNING
  Fix: Use varied, natural transitions or eliminate transition words entirely

CHECK 4 — Adjective Clustering:
  Scan for 3+ adjectives modifying same noun
  FLAG: "The powerful, scientifically-proven, all-natural compound"
  Severity: WARNING
  Fix: Choose the strongest adjective; let specifics do the work
```

### Step 4: Generate Violation Report

```
FOR EACH violation found:

  COMPILE violation_entry:
    id: sequential integer
    type: enum[BANNED_PHRASE, BANNED_PATTERN, REPETITION]
    category: string
    severity: enum[BANNED, WARNING]
    location:
      phase: string
      paragraph: integer
      sentence: integer
      character_offset: integer
    exact_text: string                 # The violating text
    surrounding_context: string        # 15 words before and after
    reason: string                     # Why this is a violation
    suggested_replacement: string      # Concrete alternative

GENERATE suggested replacements for each violation:

  FOR banned phrases:
    "cutting-edge" → Use specific: "published in 2023" or "identified
      by [institution]"
    "revolutionary" → Use specific: "the first [thing] to [specific action]"
    "game-changing" → Use specific: "[specific result] in [timeframe]"
    "breakthrough" → Use specific: "a discovery that [specific finding]"
    "transform your" → Use specific: "restore [specific function]" or
      "activate [specific process]"
    "unlock the power" → Use specific: "activate [mechanism name]"
    "the secret to" → Use specific: "[mechanism name] — the [specific
      function] that controls [specific outcome]"
    "supercharge" → Use specific: "increase [function] by [amount]"
    "world-class" → Use specific: "former head of [department] at
      [institution]"

  FOR banned patterns:
    Vague description → Add concrete mechanism detail
    Hand-waving → Add specific "how" step
    Unexplained jargon → Add inline definition
    Motivational filler → Replace with mechanism-relevant information or delete
    Passive voice → Rewrite in active voice
    Unsupported superlative → Add source or remove
    Generic benefit list → Connect each benefit to mechanism step
```

### Step 5: Determine Pass/Fail Status

```
COUNT violations by severity:

  banned_violations = count of BANNED severity violations
  warning_violations = count of WARNING severity violations

PASS/FAIL LOGIC:

  IF banned_violations == 0 AND warning_violations <= 3:
    STATUS: PASS
    MESSAGE: "Narrative passes anti-slop validation."

  IF banned_violations == 0 AND warning_violations > 3:
    STATUS: CONDITIONAL PASS
    MESSAGE: "No banned violations, but [N] warnings should be addressed
      before assembly. May proceed if warnings are acknowledged."

  IF banned_violations > 0:
    STATUS: FAIL
    MESSAGE: "[N] banned violations found. ALL must be resolved before
      assembly. Zero tolerance for banned phrases and patterns."

  ZERO BANNED VIOLATIONS REQUIRED TO PASS.
  THIS IS NON-NEGOTIABLE.
```

### Step 6: Compile Anti-Slop Report

```
CONSTRUCT anti_slop_report:
  metadata:
    validated_at: timestamp
    microskill: "4.2-anti-slop-validator"
    version: "1.0"

  scan_summary:
    total_words_scanned: number
    banned_phrases_checked: integer     # Size of banned phrase list
    banned_patterns_checked: integer    # Number of pattern types
    repetition_checks_run: integer

  violations:
    total: integer
    banned: integer
    warnings: integer
    by_phase:
      phase_1: integer
      phase_3: integer
      phase_4: integer
      phase_5: integer
      bridge: integer
    by_category: object

  violation_details: [
    {
      id: integer
      type: string
      severity: string
      location: object
      exact_text: string
      reason: string
      suggested_replacement: string
    }
  ]

  pass_status:
    status: enum[PASS, CONDITIONAL_PASS, FAIL]
    banned_count: integer
    warning_count: integer
    message: string

  resolution_required:
    must_fix: [violation_ids]          # BANNED violations (mandatory)
    should_fix: [violation_ids]        # WARNING violations (recommended)

WRITE anti_slop_report to working memory
IF FAIL: Return to Layer 3 for revision with violation details
IF PASS or CONDITIONAL_PASS: PASS to 4.3 and 4.4
```

## QUALITY CRITERIA

1. **Zero banned violations** — not one instance of banned phrases or patterns
2. **Warnings <= 3** — limited warning-level issues
3. **Every violation has replacement** — not just flagged, but alternative provided
4. **Full narrative scanned** — all phases and bridge checked
5. **Repetition patterns caught** — sentence openers, structures, transitions
6. **No generic descriptions** — every mechanism description is concrete and specific
7. **No motivational filler** — every sentence earns its place with mechanism-relevant content

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Symptom | Resolution |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Banned phrase found | "cutting-edge research" in Phase 4 | Replace with specific: "research published in Nature in [year]" |
| Vague mechanism description | "The compound works naturally" | Add specifics: "The compound inhibits [enzyme] which reduces [process] by [amount]" |
| Hand-waving explanation | "The mechanism restores balance" | Add HOW: "The mechanism increases [specific activity] which restores [specific measurement] to [specific range]" |
| Motivational filler | "You deserve better health" | Delete or replace with mechanism content |
| Excessive passive voice | >20% passive sentences | Rewrite action descriptions in active voice |
| Repetitive structure | 5 paragraphs all start "And that's why..." | Vary openers: question, statement, direct address, statistic |
| Generic benefit list | "Energy, focus, weight loss, mood" | Connect each to mechanism: "AMPK activation burns fat (weight loss), reduces inflammation (energy), triggers autophagy (cellular focus)" |

## EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Clean Pass — LA-3 Narrative

```
Scan Results:
  Banned phrases found: 0
  Banned patterns found: 0
  Warnings: 1 (passive voice in Phase 5, sentence 4)

Pass Status: PASS
Message: "Narrative passes anti-slop validation. 1 minor warning
  (passive voice) — recommended but not required to fix."

Warning Detail:
  "Inflammation markers were reduced by 47% in the Yale study"
  → Suggested: "The Yale study showed AMPK activation reduced
    inflammation markers by 47%"
```

### Example 2: Fail — Generic AI Output Detected

```
Scan Results:
  Banned phrases found: 4
    1. "cutting-edge research" (Phase 4, paragraph 2) — BANNED
    2. "revolutionary discovery" (Phase 3, Beat 1) — BANNED
    3. "unlock the power" (Phase 4, Part C) — BANNED
    4. "transform your health" (Product Bridge) — BANNED

  Banned patterns found: 2
    1. Vague description: "This powerful compound works by addressing
       the core issues" (Phase 4) — no specifics given — BANNED
    2. Motivational filler: "You deserve to feel your best and this
       is finally possible" (Phase 5) — no mechanism content — BANNED

Pass Status: FAIL
Message: "6 banned violations found. ALL must be resolved. Zero tolerance."

Resolution Required:
  Must Fix:
    1. "cutting-edge research" → "research published by Yale's Department
       of Cellular Biology"
    2. "revolutionary discovery" → "a discovery that identified the
       enzyme controlling cellular aging"
    3. "unlock the power" → "reactivate AMPK"
    4. "transform your health" → "reactivate the metabolic switch that
       controls fat burning, inflammation, and cellular repair"
    5. Vague description → Add specific compound name, specific action,
       specific biological pathway
    6. Motivational filler → Replace with: "Clinical evidence shows
       AMPK activation produces measurable changes within [timeframe]"
```

### Example 3: Conditional Pass — BBW Narrative

```
Scan Results:
  Banned phrases found: 0
  Banned patterns found: 0
  Warnings: 4
    1. Sentence opener repetition: "And that's" used 4x in Phase 4
    2. Paragraph structure repetition: 3 consecutive [claim].[evidence].[implication]
    3. Transition repetition: "But here's the thing" used 3x
    4. Adjective clustering: "the powerful, ancient, scientifically-validated frequency"

Pass Status: CONDITIONAL PASS
Message: "No banned violations. 4 warnings should be addressed for
  optimal quality. May proceed if acknowledged."

Should Fix:
  1. Vary sentence openers in Phase 4
  2. Break paragraph pattern with a question or direct address
  3. Replace 2 of 3 "But here's the thing" with varied transitions
  4. Reduce to strongest adjective: "the scientifically-validated frequency"
```
