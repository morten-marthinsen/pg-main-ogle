## FEEDBACK RECEIVED

### Learning #60: Don't Lead with Cerebral/Educational Hooks — Lead with Emotion

**What Happened:**
The VSL structure outline proposed the lead hook: "How many phases of liver detoxification are there?" Client rejected this as "horrible" — too cerebral, not emotional.

**Why It Was Wrong:**
- "How many phases of liver detoxification are there?" is an educational question, not an emotional hook
- Nobody CARES about phases of liver detoxification at the top of funnel
- The Phase 3 mechanism works IN CONTEXT — after the prospect understands why their supplements failed — not as the opening hook
- The lead must be EMOTIONAL first, then bridge to education
- Prospects need to feel understood before they'll accept a new framework

**The Distinction:**
- Phase 3 as MECHANISM (middle of copy): Works well — explains why nothing worked
- Phase 3 as LEAD HOOK (opening): Too cerebral — nobody wakes up wondering about detox phases

**Rule Established:**
> **MANDATORY:** Lead hooks must be EMOTIONAL, not educational. The mechanism framework (Phase 3, three phases, etc.) belongs in the mechanism reveal section AFTER the prospect is emotionally engaged. Never open with a cerebral/educational question that assumes the prospect already cares about your framework. They don't. Lead with pain, frustration, or a disruption to their existing beliefs — not with category education.

**Implementation:**
- Structure skill should flag cerebral/educational lead hooks during validation
- Lead skill (09-Lead) will generate proper emotional hooks via Arena process
- VSL structure outline lead section should note: "Lead hook TBD — generated by 09-Lead skill"
