# Core Wounds Framework

**Version:** 1.0

**Source:** Parris Lampropoulos — "The Emotional Trauma of Schemas"
**Purpose:** Deep psychological driver intelligence for copy that resonates at the core identity level

## CRITICAL CONTEXT

> "Everything we sell is about defectiveness... The answer to every 'so what' is: 'So I can feel better about myself.' That's why people buy."
> — Parris Lampropoulos

Core Wounds are childhood emotional injuries that drive adult purchasing behavior. They operate beneath surface emotions and logical justifications. Every purchase ultimately traces back to these wounds.

**Implementation Philosophy (from Parris):**
- Learn it deeply, internalize it completely
- Then **forget about it** and write from emotional connection
- Never force it mechanically during generation
- Check afterward if needed
- Write to a real person, not an avatar

---

## THE 11 CORE WOUNDS

### 1. Abandonment Wound
**Core Belief:** "Please don't leave me."

**Psychology:** The feeling that people you love will leave you and you will end up emotionally isolated forever. Often stems from parent death, divorce, or emotionally absent parents.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Needy, clingy behavior that paradoxically drives people away, reinforcing the wound.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Loss prevention copy
- Relationship products
- "I'll be with you every step" guru bonding
- Loyalty/continuity programs

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Mistakes parents make that push children away"
- "Do's and don'ts for a closer relationship"
- "Coping strategies to help you get over a divorce or the death of a loved one"
- "Best way to keep your luggage from getting lost on an airline" (loss = abandonment trigger)
- "Three places you should never hide your valuables—burglars look there first"

**Copy Strategy:** At pitch end, create "Here's What You Get" bonding section: "I'll tell you when to buy, when to sell. You'll never have to wonder. Step by step, I'll be with you, hold you by the hand."

---

### 2. Mistrust Wound
**Core Belief:** "People cannot be trusted."

**Psychology:** Expectation that others will hurt, abuse, cheat, lie, manipulate, or take advantage. Often stems from parents who lied or betrayed trust.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Constant suspicion, assumption of ulterior motives, difficulty accepting help.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Exposé angles ("12 Smiling Swindlers")
- Protection/watchdog positioning
- Transparency copy (tell them your motive upfront)
- Prepper markets

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Insurance everyone buys but no-one needs"
- "Two famous cold remedies that make you sicker if taken together"
- "Insurance policy scam clauses at the back that cancel coverage promised on the front"
- "How to spot when someone is lying to you—it has nothing to do with eye contact"
- "What hospitals don't want you to know: shocking facts about tainted equipment, staff cons, doctor misconduct"
- "Shortcuts doctors take when giving you a checkup"

**Copy Strategy:** Head off mistrust by being transparent: "Why am I sending you this for free? The answer is simple—it's a shameless bribe to get you to take a trial subscription."

**Incompatible With:** Can combine with most wounds, but copy tone must remain skeptical/protective.

---

### 3. Dependence Wound
**Core Belief:** "I need you to tell me what to do."

**Psychology:** Feels unable to handle everyday life competently without considerable help from others. Seeks strong figures to depend on. Often stems from overprotective parenting or being made to feel incompetent.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Seeks authority figures, needs constant reassurance, avoids independent decisions.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Step-by-step systems
- Done-for-you offers
- Strong guru/authority positioning
- "I'll tell you exactly what to do"

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Follow this simple 3-step system"
- "I'll tell you exactly when to buy and when to sell"
- "Just do what I say and watch what happens"

**Copy Strategy:** Position yourself as the benevolent authority who removes all guesswork. Make decisions FOR them.

**Note:** Parris says he rarely leads with this wound because he sells the opposite (independence/mastery), but understanding it helps channel prospects who have it.

---

### 4. Vulnerability Wound
**Core Belief:** "The world is an unsafe place."

**Psychology:** Exaggerated fear that catastrophe will strike at any moment. Often stems from early loss, trauma, or living through dangerous times.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Hypervigilance, worst-case thinking, excessive preparation.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Financial doom ("Plague of the Black Debt," "End of America")
- Health scares
- Prepper/survival markets
- Protection products
- Home security

**Bullet Examples:**
- "What to do if you have a heart attack while on an airplane"
- "Colonoscopy shocker: Two out of seven instruments are not properly sterilized"
- "Early warning signs of stroke—if you have even ONE of these symptoms, call 911 immediately"
- "Top mistakes that make homeowners prime targets for burglars"
- "Early warning sign of a deadly aneurysm—get to an emergency room immediately if you experience this"
- "When memory lapses are a sign of something more serious"

**Copy Strategy:** Agitate the danger, then provide the protection/solution. Fear of loss is more powerful than promise of gain.

---

### 5. Deprivation Wound
**Core Belief:** "I never get what I need."

**Psychology:** Belief that needs for love, attention, or material things will never be adequately met. Feels cheated by life. Often stems from poverty or emotional neglect.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Scarcity mindset, hoarding, intense response to "limited time" offers, attracted to luxury/abundance imagery.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Scarcity/urgency triggers
- Luxury lifestyle copy
- "Finally yours" positioning
- Abundance fantasy sequences

**Bullet Examples (Clayton Makepeace lifestyle sequence):**
- "Have you ever walked into a Porsche dealership, plonked down in the driver's seat of the hottest 911 on the floor and simply told the salesman, 'I'll take it'?"
- "Have you ever had the pleasure of shocking your spouse with a shiny new Harley Davidson?"
- "Front row concert seats within 15 feet of Paul McCartney?"
- "Do you know how it feels to hand your horse-crazy daughter the reins of the flashiest Palomino she's ever seen?"

**Copy Strategy:** Paint vivid pictures of abundance. Use countdown timers and scarcity. "Only X left" works powerfully on this wound.

---

### 6. Failure Wound
**Core Belief:** "I am inadequate."

**Psychology:** Belief that one is fundamentally inadequate and will inevitably fail. Even highly successful people can carry this wound (Spielberg was certain every film would bomb for 20 years).

**Behavioral Pattern:** Self-sabotage, fear of trying, or compensatory overachievement.

**Marketing Applications:**
- "When all else fails" positioning
- Products for repeated failures (dieting, trading, etc.)
- Success stories from "losers"
- "The mistake that's been holding you back"

**Bullet Examples:**
- "The diet mistake that can destroy 80% of your immune cells in just two weeks"
- "Canola oil and sunflower oil are good for you, right? Wrong."
- "Popular moisturizers that actually dry out your skin"
- "Why you should never take Aspirin or Tylenol when you have a cold"
- "How to get rid of warts when all else fails"
- "Why combining cold medicine and antibiotics can kill you"

**Copy Strategy:** Acknowledge repeated failures, then explain why THIS time is different (new mechanism, previously unknown cause, etc.). One study reference that "worked for every single person" is powerful.

---

### 7. Defectiveness Wound (THE MASTER WOUND)
**Core Belief:** "I'm not lovable. I'm fundamentally flawed."

**Psychology:** Feeling inwardly flawed and defective. Believes anyone who truly knew them wouldn't love them. Fear of exposure. This is the CORE wound—everything ultimately traces here.

> "Everything we sell is about defectiveness." — Parris

**Behavioral Pattern:** Impostor syndrome, hiding true self, compensatory behaviors to prove worth.

**Marketing Applications:**
- ALL marketing ultimately addresses this
- Transformation promises
- "They laughed when I sat down at the piano"
- Before/after narratives
- Social proof (others like me succeeded)

**Bullet Examples:**
- "She was always ashamed of her poor complexion—she locked herself in the bedroom"
- "They laughed when I sat down at the piano. But when I started to play..."
- "Clumsy mistakes men make in bed"

**Copy Strategy:** Every benefit chain ends here. Keep asking "So what?" until you reach "So I can feel better about myself." That's where the real sale happens.

---

### 8. Exclusion Wound
**Core Belief:** "I don't belong."

**Psychology:** Feeling different, like an outsider who doesn't fit in. Often stems from being different (immigrant, poor, awkward) during formative years.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Seeks belonging, attracted to exclusive groups, sensitive to social rejection.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Velvet rope positioning
- Exclusive clubs/memberships
- "Finally be one of the cool kids"
- Insider access

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Off-limits information"
- "How thousands of Americans are curing themselves by doing what you AREN'T"
- "Dear Fellow Member: You have been chosen from a select group..."
- "What to do if you have to introduce someone whose name you forgot"
- "Five fashion mistakes that make you look much older than your age"
- "The secret to throwing dinner parties people will talk about for months"
- "The biggest mistake you can make when e-mailing your resume—employers won't even read it"

**Copy Strategy:** Position offer as entry to an exclusive club. Credentialing the guru (Lord William Rees-Mogg, House of Lords, advisor to Margaret Thatcher) makes reader feel "I'm with HIM now. I'm not a peon anymore."

---

### 9. Subjugation Wound
**Core Belief:** "I'm a people pleaser."

**Psychology:** Sacrifices own needs for others. Allows others to control them. Does this from guilt or fear of punishment/abandonment.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Difficulty saying no, martyrdom, resentment beneath surface compliance.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Guilt-based copy (especially parenting)
- Empathy copy
- "You've done so much for everyone else"
- Self-care positioning for caregivers

**Bullet Example (Susan Lark Women's Health empathy copy):**
> "You've done it all—juggled the demands of your career while being a loving wife and mother. Being the family nurse, bookkeeper, chef, and social activities director. Lost the same 10 pounds 10 times and stayed awake countless nights worrying about everyone around you. Sometimes you feel like all the cooking, cleaning, working, and family time are sucking you dry. Sometimes you wake up so drained and exhausted that you can barely get yourself out of bed. With all these demands on your time and energy, how are you supposed to find time to care for your health? I have good news for you..."

**Copy Strategy:** Acknowledge their sacrifice. Give them permission to care for themselves. Frame self-care as necessary for them to keep caring for others.

**Incompatible With:** Entitlement (opposite wound)

---

### 10. Perfectionism Wound (Unrelenting Standards)
**Core Belief:** "No matter what I do, it's never enough."

**Psychology:** Strives relentlessly to meet impossibly high standards. Places excessive emphasis on achievement at expense of happiness. Often stems from parents who only acknowledged exceptional performance.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Workaholic tendencies, harsh self-criticism, difficulty enjoying success.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Premium/luxury positioning
- "Not just good—GREAT"
- Upgrade/upsell backend
- "The best" claims

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Be a 60-year-old who looks like a 40-year-old"
- "Be a 40-year-old who looks like a 27-year-old"
- Chinese Emperor story: "Required to have sex with nine women and satisfy each one—here's how he did it"

**Copy Strategy:** Don't just promise adequate results—promise exceptional, elite-level outcomes. This wound is the SECRET TO BACKEND SALES: they won't settle for the basic newsletter, they want the options course, the advanced system, the threesome course.

---

### 11. Entitlement Wound
**Core Belief:** "The rules don't apply to me."

**Psychology:** Believes they should get what they want regardless of rules that bind others. Often stems from overindulgent parenting or compensatory development from other wounds.

**Behavioral Pattern:** Cuts corners, seeks "angles," resents limitations, demands special treatment.

**Marketing Applications:**
- Insider secrets
- VIP treatment
- "How to game the system"
- Tax loopholes, special access

**Bullet Examples:**
- "Why not the best?"
- "How to get VIP treatment wherever you go"
- "How to snag a window or aisle seat when they're all taken"
- "How to skip the line in the emergency room"
- "How to get tables at trendy restaurants that are all booked up"
- "Feels it's okay to pay late? Here's how"
- "How to deduct the family vacation as a business expense"
- "How to get your home for free, courtesy of the US government"
- "Auto insurance discount for people with high IQs"

**Copy Strategy:** Promise insider knowledge that gives them an edge. "You'll know what others don't." Appeal to their sense of deserving more.

**Incompatible With:** Subjugation (opposite wound)

## MARKET-TO-WOUND CORRELATIONS

Different markets have different dominant wounds. Know your market's wound profile before writing.

### Financial/Investment Markets
**Primary:** Mistrust, Vulnerability, Entitlement
**Secondary:** Failure, Deprivation

*"They think everybody's out to screw them, they always want an angle, and they love telling people all the shit they know."* — Parris on Boardroom readers

### Health Markets
**Primary:** Vulnerability, Failure, Defectiveness
**Secondary:** Deprivation, Mistrust

### Relationship Markets
**Primary:** Abandonment, Defectiveness, Exclusion
**Secondary:** Deprivation, Subjugation

### Business/Make Money Markets
**Primary:** Failure, Deprivation, Perfectionism
**Secondary:** Exclusion, Entitlement

### Self-Improvement/Personal Development
**Primary:** Defectiveness, Failure, Perfectionism
**Secondary:** Dependence, Exclusion

### Prepper/Survival Markets
**Primary:** Vulnerability, Mistrust
**Secondary:** Abandonment, Deprivation

### Parenting Markets
**Primary:** Subjugation, Abandonment, Failure
**Secondary:** Perfectionism

### Weight Loss/Fitness
**Primary:** Failure, Defectiveness, Exclusion
**Secondary:** Perfectionism, Deprivation

## THE "SO WHAT" CHAIN

To find the core wound driving a purchase, keep asking "So what?" until you reach identity.

**Example: Air Purifier**

```
FEATURE: HEPA filter
    ↓ So what?
BENEFIT: Filters out particles
    ↓ So what?
EMOTIONAL BENEFIT: Clean air to breathe
    ↓ So what?
DEEPER BENEFIT: Won't be embarrassed when people come over
    ↓ So what?
CORE WOUND: Defectiveness ("I'm flawed, my home reveals it")
            + Exclusion ("People will judge me")
```

**Example: Trading Course**

```
FEATURE: Video lessons on risk management
    ↓ So what?
BENEFIT: Learn to manage position size
    ↓ So what?
EMOTIONAL BENEFIT: Stop blowing up accounts
    ↓ So what?
DEEPER BENEFIT: Finally prove I can do this
    ↓ So what?
CORE WOUND: Failure ("I am inadequate")
            + Defectiveness ("Maybe I'm just not built for this")
```

## WOUND INCOMPATIBILITY MATRIX

Some wounds cannot be combined in the same copy without creating dissonance:

| Wound A | Incompatible With | Reason |
|---------|-------------------|--------|
| Entitlement | Subjugation | Opposite orientations—"rules don't apply to me" vs "I sacrifice for others" |
| Dependence | Entitlement | "Tell me what to do" vs "I make my own rules" |

**Compatible Wound Stacks:**
- Vulnerability + Mistrust (prepper stack)
- Failure + Defectiveness + Deprivation (weight loss/money stack)
- Exclusion + Deprivation + Perfectionism (luxury/aspiration stack)
- Abandonment + Subjugation (relationship/caregiver stack)

## IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

### How to Use This Framework

**During Research (01-research):**
- Emotional Mapper (1.4) identifies dominant wounds from VoC quotes
- Map wound patterns across forums, reviews, testimonials
- Output 2-3 dominant market wounds in layer-1 handoff

**During Big Idea Development (06-big-idea):**
- Emotional mapper correlates quotes to wounds
- Big Idea should tap dominant wound
- Arena judges resonance with identified wounds

**During Generation (All Narrative Skills):**
- Load wound context, then write naturally
- Don't force wound language into copy
- Let personas channel wound awareness organically

**During Editorial (20-editorial):**
- Post-generation check: Does copy tap identified wounds?
- Non-blocking flag if wounds are missed
- Suggest enhancement opportunities

### What NOT To Do

- Do NOT mechanically insert wound references
- Do NOT create wound-specific templates
- Do NOT gate completion on wound coverage
- Do NOT use clinical language in copy ("your abandonment wound")
- Do NOT combine incompatible wounds

### The Parris Method

> "Learn this stuff, then hand copy the most successful controls ever. As you're hand copying, you'll notice: 'Oh look, he's using this wound here.'"

> "All the techniques you've learned—Cialdini stuff, authority, scarcity—if you think about it, you'll see there's an associated wound. Authority = Dependence wound. Scarcity = Deprivation wound."

> "I don't have wounds on my checklist because I don't want to force it. Learn it, internalize it, then forget about it and just write."
