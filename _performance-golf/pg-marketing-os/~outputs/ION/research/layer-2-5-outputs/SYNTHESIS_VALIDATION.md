## Hope vs. Pain Market Assessment

**This market is solution-aware and actively searching — not stuck in pain.**

With strict keyword matching across 1,080 quotes:
- 56% neutral/informational — golfers comparing, researching, sharing data
- 22% frustration — real pain, but channeled into search behavior
- 11% hope — active satisfaction with products they've found
- 11% curiosity — experimenting, testing, open to new options

**Strategic interpretation (per WORKSPACE.md copywriting principles):**

This is NOT a pain-dominant market where the reader needs to be convinced something is wrong. These golfers KNOW they want more distance and better value. They're in comparison mode. The copy approach should be:

1. **Solution leads.** Don't spend 3 pages on the problem — they already feel it. Get to the mechanism fast.
2. **Problem section is shorter.** Use pain as a mirror (recognition, not education) — one tight section that says "you already know this."
3. **Proof is the conversion point.** In a solution-aware, skeptical market, the Golf Laboratories robot data is what moves them. Not emotional storytelling — data.
4. **Distance and price are the two dominant drivers.** Everything else (feel, alignment, brand) is supporting.

**Copy pacing implication:** Lead with what iON+ does (distance at YOUR swing speed, proven by independent testing). Use pain sparingly for recognition ("you've noticed your drives getting shorter"). Spend the bulk of the page on mechanism, proof, and offer.
