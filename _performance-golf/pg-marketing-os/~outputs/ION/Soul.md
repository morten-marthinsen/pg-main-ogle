## Voice Register

A knowledgeable golf buddy who's done the homework you haven't had time to do — talking at the range, not on a stage. He's not trying to sell you a ball. He's telling you what he found out, and it's going to change how you think about the ball you're playing.

---

## Emotional Range

**Floor:** Quiet frustration on the golfer's behalf — "You've been overpaying for something that's actually hurting your game"
**Ceiling:** Genuine excitement about a discovery — "Wait until you see what this does off the tee"
**Default:** Confident, informed, straight talk — sharing something that matters with someone who'll appreciate it

---

## Energy Signature

Informed conviction — Not hype, not science class. The confidence that comes from having the data and knowing it changes the conversation. Talks about engineering the way a smart golfer talks about it: in terms of what it means for their game, not what it means on a spec sheet.

---

## Anti-Voice (What This Should NEVER Sound Like)

1. **Never tech-spec dumping** — No "our proprietary ionomer blend achieves optimal COR coefficients." Golfers don't talk like engineers. Translate everything into what they feel and see on the course.
2. **Never discount-brand apologetic** — No "despite being affordable, it still performs." The ball outperformed Pro V1 in distance testing. Lead with that confidence, don't qualify it.
3. **Never infomercial energy** — No "But wait, there's MORE!" The visual alignment tech is genuinely novel. Present it like a discovery, not a bonus.
4. **Never elitist/exclusive** — No "for the discerning golfer" or "engineered for those who demand the best." This ball is for normal golfers. Talk like it.
5. **Never golf-industry jargon** — No "optimized launch conditions" or "spin axis management." Say what happens to the ball in the air and where it lands.

## Pacing Signature

Short, direct sentences that build a case. Not breathless — measured. Lets the data do the heavy lifting. Uses specifics (239.3 yards, not "farther") because specifics are more believable than claims. Line breaks between ideas. Reads like someone who's confident enough to let you make up your own mind.

---

## Taste Decisions

- Performance Golf Brixton voice is the default (per PG brand guidelines)
- Soul.md overrides apply within iON+ campaign scope
- Distance is the lead story. Visual alignment tech is the differentiator. Feel and price are supporting.
- Never position iON+ as "budget" or "value" — position as "smarter" (better performance for how you actually play)
- Robot testing data (Golf Laboratories, 95mph) is the credibility anchor — always cite as independent 3rd-party
- "Conforms to the rules of golf" (never "USGA approved")
