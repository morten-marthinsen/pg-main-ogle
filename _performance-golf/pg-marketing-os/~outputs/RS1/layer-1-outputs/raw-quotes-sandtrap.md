## ROOT CAUSE — Explanations of why putts are missed

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "the conclusion my fitter pretty quickly came to, is that I have created some interesting swing compensations to fit my swing to my putter."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "Toe hang is my enemy. the more toe hang your putter has, the more the club promotes head rotation in your swing."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "my hands have a tendency to move independently from my body, rather than all as a unit. This is created, or at least exaggerated, by the fact that I am tall (6'2\") and I have to lean over quite a bit to putt"
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "reviewing those results, it became evidently clear that while I was producing consistent results, these was nothing consistent about my swing."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "You need to figure out whether your putting sucks because you're not starting the ball on the intended line, or you're not putting to the correct pace, or you're not reading the greens correctly. Find out where the inconsistency lies and you can address the problem."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "What you think you're aiming and lined up oftentimes is quite different than the actual line. I think someone had done a study on this issue and proven that many of us have vision distorted when we looked down and sideways to the putting line."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "The best equipment and the best putting stroke will not improve your putting if your aim is off."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "I suspected I was opening the clubface on impact. Knowing I was doing this consistently between 0.5 and 1.5 degrees while maintaining a very straight path has given me tons of confidence out on the course."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "They could hit it in the middle of the face all day but if it's pointing wide open that isn't going to do them any good."
---

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "I think a poor short game is the last refuge of a poor long game. They want their short game to save them, and it can't most of the time, and as a result, they blame their short game."

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "Recency bias. The putt or bad chip happens at the end of the hole."

**Bucket:** Root Cause / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "I have a tendency sometimes to maintain that triangle for the backswing and then on the actual stroke the shoulder line blocks. This causes me to hit too high on the ball for one thing, but I also found this had a dramatic impact on not closing the putter face at impact."

## SOLUTIONS TRIED — Putters tried, techniques, fittings

**Bucket:** Solutions Tried / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "I got a putter fitting over at Club Champion, with the Sam putt lab."
---

**Bucket:** Solutions Tried / **Source:** The Sand Trap / **Quote:** "We moved to a 38\" counter balanced shaft. Scotty Cameron and Ping both make a putters that is weighted to match a shaft like this. Additionally, he put one of these shafts in a Taylor Made Spider as I was swinging that very consistently. The 38\" shaft is meant to be gripped a few inches down, so your hands are in the middle of the grip rather than on the end. This, as I had mentioned, was a game changer. This drastically quieted my hands."
