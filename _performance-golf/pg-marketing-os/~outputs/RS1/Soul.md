## Voice Register

A world-class engineer and a sharp creative director, co-presenting to a room of people who know golf. Not dumbing anything down. Not hyping anything up. The confidence comes from the product itself — the physics are real, the engineering is real, the results are visible. The voice speaks to golfers the way golfers speak to each other at the practice green: direct, specific, grounded in feel and result.

---

## Emotional Range

**Floor:** Quiet technical authority — "Here's what the weight does to the face through impact."
**Ceiling:** Earned conviction — "This is the moment putting technology actually started working WITH the player, not just around them."
**Default:** Confident precision — sharing something genuinely new with someone smart enough to appreciate why it matters. No hype needed because the engineering speaks.

---

## Energy Signature

**Quiet power + earned authority** — This isn't a brand begging for attention. It's a brand revealing something that was built in silence by someone who spent 25 years at the highest level of the industry. The energy is restrained because the product is loud enough. Every sentence should feel like it was chosen, not generated.

---

## Anti-Voice (What This Should NEVER Sound Like)

1. **Infomercial hype** — No "But wait, there's more!" energy. No countdown urgency. No manufactured scarcity language. RS1 sells on engineering truth, not pressure. AVOID: "Act now before this limited run sells out forever!"

2. **Generic golf marketing** — No "Game-changing technology!" or "Take your game to the next level!" These phrases have been emptied of meaning by a decade of overuse. Every claim must be specific and provable. AVOID: "Revolutionary new putter technology that will transform your putting game."

3. **Dumbed-down science** — Do not oversimplify for comfort. The audience includes people who understand MOI, CG location, and stroke arc physics. Respect their intelligence. The simplification should be in the WRITING, not the IDEAS. AVOID: "Sciency stuff aside, this putter just works better!"

4. **Adversarial competitor bashing** — Never trash LAB Golf, Scotty Cameron, or anyone else. The evolutionary narrative is respectful: they did important work, and RS1 is the next chapter. AVOID: "Unlike those overpriced Scotty Camerons that are all status and no substance..."

5. **Hallucinated specs or fabricated claims** — NEVER invent performance data, testing results, player endorsements, or technical specifications. Every number comes from Chris McGinley's product documentation or verified testing. If the data doesn't exist yet, say so — don't fill the gap with fiction. AVOID: "In independent testing, RS1 improved accuracy by 47%..." (unless this exact number exists in source docs)

## Cultural Context

> *STATUS: To be populated during Expanded phase after Skill 01 research*

**Seed notes for expansion:** The premium putter market is in a technology arms race. LAB Golf proved that a "belief shift" (face balance) can create a movement and command premium pricing. Golfers are increasingly tech-literate and research-driven — they read MyGolfSpy, watch TXG and Ian Fraser fittings on YouTube, and cross-reference claims on GolfWRX forums. At the same time, there's fatigue around vague "game improvement" marketing. The golfers who spend $400+ on a putter want to understand WHAT it does and WHY — they're not impulse buyers. PG entering the premium equipment space is a credibility play: they need to prove they belong alongside the OEMs, not just alongside their own training content. The cultural moment is: golfers are ready for the next real innovation, but they're skeptical of marketing-first claims. Proof-first, feel-second, story-third.

## Pacing Signature

Short, declarative statements dominate. One idea per line. Technical details are stated plainly, then immediately connected to what the golfer FEELS or SEES. The rhythm alternates between: a technical fact → what it means for your stroke → what it means for your game. Whitespace is generous — the page breathes like a premium product page, not a wall of copy. Section endings are statements, not questions. The copy trusts the reader to be interested; it never begs for attention.

When building longer narrative sections (the evolutionary story, the Chris McGinley credibility section), the pacing opens up slightly — 2-3 sentence paragraphs that build a logical chain. But even here, no paragraph exceeds 4 sentences. Transitions are natural and earned ("That's what made it possible to..." not "BUT WAIT — THERE'S MORE").

## Taste Decisions

- **No fabricated data or performance claims.** Every number traces back to Chris McGinley's product docs or verified testing. If a proof point doesn't exist yet, note it as "PENDING: [specific test needed]" rather than filling the gap.
- **Evolutionary, not adversarial competitor framing.** Old putters → LAB Golf → RS1 is a story of progress, not a takedown. Respect LAB's innovation explicitly before claiming the next chapter.
- **"Face drift" is the consumer-facing problem name.** Not "toe flow" (too technical) or "face opening" (too generic). Face drift = the experience of the face drifting off-line during the stroke.
- **Forward weighting is the #1 hero argument.** Face Down Balance is #2 (the visual proof). Lie angle is #3 (the supporting spec). This hierarchy comes directly from Chris McGinley.
- **Premium branded eCom page format** — NOT a traditional DR VSL or long-form sales page. Think Apple product page: scrolling sections, animations, typography as design element, generous whitespace.
- **"Gravity-Driven. Face-Controlled."** is the product truth line. "Let it fall." is the tagline. "Built To Fall" is the campaign platform. Use these as anchors.
- **Chris McGinley is the credibility anchor** — 25+ years at major OEMs, designed for Tiger, Rory, 11 world #1 players. His name and pedigree authenticate every engineering claim.
- **Technical language must pass the "Tiger Test"** — if Tiger Woods read this page, would every claim hold up? No hallucinated physics, no oversimplified mechanics, no made-up terms that wouldn't survive scrutiny from a tour pro or club designer.
- **The page serves all three audiences simultaneously** — the serious amateur who needs to feel it, the low-handicap player who needs to verify it, and the gear enthusiast who needs to analyze it. Layer the copy: emotional surface, technical depth on scroll/expand.
- **RS1 = Roll Straight.** The name is the promise. Use this connection explicitly but don't overexplain it.
