## Step 4: Blame Removal Test

```
1. Who/what was blamed BEFORE?
   SELF -- Their technique, their mental game, their practice habits, their nerves.
   Golf culture's default: "it's the archer, not the arrow."

2. Who/what is blamed NOW?
   EQUIPMENT DESIGN -- Rearward CG as a physics flaw shared by virtually every putter.
   Not a specific brand. Not an individual. A design paradigm.

3. Is blame fully removed from audience?
   YES, COMPLETELY -- "Your technique was never the problem." "That miss wasn't
   mental. It was mechanical." "The arrow has been bent this whole time."
   The expression explicitly states that the golfer was not at fault.

4. Does this create relief or additional burden?
   RELIEF -- The emotional payload is liberation from years of self-blame.
   No new burden is created. The solution path (move the weight forward) is
   simple and does not require the golfer to change behavior.

BLAME REMOVAL SCORE: 10/10
```

```
1. Does knowing the root cause make them WANT a solution?
   URGENTLY -- Once a golfer understands that their putter has been working

   Q0424: The golfer who felt "regular putters try to twist" would urgently
   want a putter where the face doesn't twist.

2. Does the root cause point TOWARD the mechanism naturally?
   OBVIOUS NEXT STEP -- "The weight is in the wrong place" -> "Where's the
   putter with the weight in the right place?" The mechanism path is the
   most natural in the PG portfolio.

3. Is the audience in a state to receive the solution?
   READY AND OPEN -- After the blame removal creates relief, the audience
   is in an open, receptive state. They are not defensive (blame was removed)
   and not overwhelmed (the explanation is simple).

4. Is the root cause overwhelming or actionable?
   ACTIONABLE -- The golfer doesn't need to change their swing, their routine,
   or their mental approach. They need a different putter. The action is simple:
   try a putter with the weight in front of the shaft.

RECEPTIVITY SCORE: 10/10
```
