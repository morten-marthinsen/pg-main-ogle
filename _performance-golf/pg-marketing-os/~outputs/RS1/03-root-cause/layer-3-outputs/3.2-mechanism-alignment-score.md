## Root Cause -> Mechanism Pair

```
Root Cause: "Rearward CG in virtually every putter creates a gravitational
force that drifts the face open. The golfer's hands compensate but that
compensation is imperfect and degrades under pressure."

Mechanism: "Forward Weighting" (75%+ of 360g head weight in front 25%
of head, placing CG ahead of the shaft axis)

Mechanism Description: Forward Weighting moves the center of gravity
in front of the shaft axis so that gravity pulls the face TOWARD square
(face-down) instead of away from it (face-open). Result: Face Down Balance.
The putter's face falls down when released at lie angle, eliminating
the drift force the golfer's hands had to fight.
```

```
Root Cause: "Rearward CG creates face drift."

1. Could a generic solution solve this root cause?
   PARTIALLY -- Zero-torque (LAB Golf) partially addresses face drift by
   placing CG at the shaft axis (zero torque, neutral face hang). But zero
   torque is PASSIVE (prevents drift) while Forward Weighting is ACTIVE
   (gravity drives face toward square). Generic face-balanced putters
   partially address it but through hosel offset, not CG relocation.

2. What makes THIS mechanism the specific answer to THIS root cause?
   Forward Weighting is the ONLY mechanism that moves CG AHEAD of the shaft
   axis, creating a positive face-closing force. Zero torque neutralizes the
   force (to zero). Forward Weighting reverses it (to positive). This is
   architecturally unique in the market.

3. Would another mechanism solve it better?
   NO -- No other mechanism produces Face Down Balance (the visual proof
   that CG is forward of shaft). LAB's zero torque eliminates the face-open
   force but doesn't create a face-closing force. Forward Weighting is
   the definitive solution to the specific root cause as stated.
```
