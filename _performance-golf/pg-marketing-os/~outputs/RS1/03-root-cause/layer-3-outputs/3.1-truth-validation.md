## Step 3: Factual Validation

```
Claim: "Rearward CG causes the face to drift open during the stroke."

1. Scientifically/factually accurate?
   YES -- This is basic physics. Center of gravity behind the shaft axis creates
   a gravitational moment that tends to rotate the putter head face-open when
   held at lie angle. This is the same principle that creates "toe hang" --
   which is already an accepted concept in putter fitting.

2. Mainstream or fringe?
   MAINSTREAM -- Toe hang and face balance are established putter fitting concepts.
   The reframe is not that the physics are novel -- it's that the physics represent
   a PROBLEM rather than a feature to be matched. The "forward CG" application
   is emerging (Axis1 exists, LAB popularized face stability) but the underlying
   physics are uncontroversial.

3. Could this claim be challenged by experts?
   LOW RISK -- The physics are sound. The main challenge would come from fitters
   who argue that toe hang should be matched to stroke arc (i.e., some face
   drift is desirable). Our claim uses "virtually every putter" (per human
   direction) and positions the issue as degree, not binary. Soul.md supports
   this nuance.

4. Are we oversimplifying for effect?
   ACCEPTABLE SIMPLIFICATION -- The full physics involves CG position, shaft axis
   location, hosel offset, head weight, and stroke dynamics. The expression
   simplifies to "weight behind shaft = face drifts open." This is accurate
   for the directional claim and the audience's experience. It is not misleading.
```
