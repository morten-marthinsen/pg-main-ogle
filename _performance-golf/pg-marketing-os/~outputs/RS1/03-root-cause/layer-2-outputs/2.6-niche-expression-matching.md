## Step 5: Language Calibration

| Parameter | Calibration |
|-----------|------------|
| **Tone** | Conversational, direct, engineer-to-golfer. Quiet power. |
| **Vocabulary** | Swing terms golfers know (face angle, stroke, impact, toe hang, MOI) + new terms they'll learn (face drift, forward weighting, face-down balance). No medical language. |
| **Intensity** | Medium. Not urgent/alarming. Not soft/philosophical. The energy of someone showing you something important that you've never seen -- "hold your putter by the shaft and let go. See that? That's the problem." |
| **Sentence length** | Short. One idea per line. 8-15 words average. |
| **Paragraph length** | 2-4 sentences max (per Soul.md pacing signature). |
| **Proof style** | Visual demonstration first ("Let It Fall"), then credentials (McGinley), then logic (every putter has the same flaw). |

**Niche-specific language to USE:**
- "Face drift" (not "toe flow" -- Soul.md taste decision)
- "Forward weighting" (the hero mechanism)
- "Face-down balance" (the visual proof)
- "The weight" / "where the weight sits" (simple, physical)
- "Your hands" / "your hands compensate" (connects to golfer's experience)

**Niche-specific language to AVOID:**
- "Syndrome," "condition," "disorder"
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "next level"
- "Conspiracy," "they don't want you to know"
- "But wait," "act now," countdown language
- "Scientifically proven" (without specific data)
