## Step 2: Villain Crime Sheet

### Primary Villain: The Self-Blame Trap

```
Villain: Golf's "It's the archer, not the arrow" belief system
Crime: Prevented golfers from recognizing equipment as a root cause of putting failure
Method: Cultural conditioning -- golf forums, instructors, and golfers themselves
        enforce the norm that blaming equipment is a character flaw
Duration: Generations. The belief predates modern putter design.
Cover-up: The belief is self-policing. Golfers who suggest equipment might be
          the problem are dismissed, mocked, or corrected by other golfers.
          (Q0139: "That means it can't be the stick, it's you.")
```

### Supporting Villain: Rearward CG Design Paradigm

```
Villain: A century of putter design with weight behind the shaft
Crime: Created an invisible opposing force that golfers must fight on every stroke
Method: Placed center of gravity behind the shaft axis, which gravity pulls
        open during the stroke. Called this "toe flow" and marketed it as a feature.
Duration: 100+ years. Every major OEM has built putters this way.
Cover-up: The resulting face drift was normalized as "toe flow" and "toe hang" --
          neutral-sounding terms that disguised a design limitation as a design choice.
```

## Step 3: Villain Reveal Expressions

### Variant A: Soft Villain -- The Self-Blame (Soul.md Compliant)

```
You've blamed yourself for years.

Your stroke isn't consistent enough. Your mental game isn't strong enough.
You don't practice enough. If you were better, you'd make those putts.

Here's the truth: golf has trained you to blame yourself for a problem
that was never yours.

Virtually every putter ever made has its center of gravity behind the shaft.
That's a gravitational force pulling the face open on every stroke.
Your hands have to fight that force in real time.

You didn't fail. You were fighting invisible physics with your bare hands.
And no one told you, because golf's deepest belief is: "It's the archer,
not the arrow."

But what if the arrow has been bent this whole time?

Every lesson you took, every drill you practiced, every putter you tried --
they all assumed the putter was neutral. It wasn't.

Once you understand that, you stop fixing the wrong thing.
And you start looking for a putter where the weight is in the right place.
```

**Villain intensity:** Soft
**Soul.md compliance:** YES -- evolutionary, not adversarial. No companies named. Blame goes to a belief system and a physics reality, not to people.

### Variant B: Medium Villain -- The Design Flaw They Named a Feature

```
For over a century, putter designers have placed the weight behind the shaft.

The face drifts open during the stroke. That's physics.
The industry called it "toe flow." That's marketing.

They told you to match your toe hang to your stroke arc --
as if the right AMOUNT of face drift was the answer,
rather than eliminating face drift entirely.

Think about that.

You've been fitted for the optimal amount of a design flaw.

Meanwhile, you blamed your hands, your nerves, your commitment to practice.
But your hands were doing exactly what they should --
fighting a force the putter was creating.

Your technique was never the problem.
The design was the problem.
And calling it "toe flow" kept anyone from questioning it.
```

**Word count:** 127
**Villain intensity:** Medium
**Soul.md compliance:** YES -- the villain is a design paradigm and a naming convention, not competitors. Matches the evolutionary narrative.

### Variant C: The Evolutionary Narrative (Softest, Most Soul.md-Aligned)

```
Putters haven't changed in a fundamental way for a long time.

Head shapes, materials, alignment aids -- those evolved.
But the weight? The weight stayed behind the shaft.
Every brand. Every model. Every generation.

The face drifts open because of that weight.
Your hands compensate. Sometimes well. Sometimes not.
Under pressure, almost never well enough.

This is not a criticism of the companies that built those putters.
It's the way putters were built. Period.

What changed is that someone with 25 years inside the major OEMs
asked a different question:

What if the weight went forward instead of back?
What if gravity worked WITH the face instead of against it?

That question led to the RS1.
And the answer changes what a putter can do for the golfer holding it.
```

**Word count:** 141
**Villain intensity:** Soft (almost neutral -- the villain is simply "the way it's always been done")
**Soul.md compliance:** STRONG -- this is the evolutionary narrative in its purest form. No blame, no anger, no conspiracy. Just a paradigm that persisted until someone questioned it.
