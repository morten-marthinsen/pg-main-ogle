## Step 3: Syndrome Reveal (Adapted for Golf)

### Variant A: Face Drift Syndrome

```
If you've been pushing putts that feel solid...
missing short ones you know you should make...
going through putter after putter and getting the same results...

These aren't separate problems. They're symptoms of the same thing.

It's called Face Drift -- the gravitational tendency of virtually every
putter to pull the face open during the stroke because the weight
sits behind the shaft.

Here's what happens: On every stroke, the rearward center of gravity
creates a rotational force. Your hands must compensate for that force
in real time. That compensation varies from putt to putt, degrades
under pressure, and creates the inconsistency you've been blaming
on your technique.

It's not three problems. It's one.
And it's not yours -- it's the putter's.
```

**Word count:** 127
**Assessment:** This actually works better WITHOUT the word "Syndrome." The named-problem approach ("Face Drift") is effective, but the medical framing adds nothing. This confirms that **Simple Reframe + Named Problem** is the correct expression for golf, not Named Syndrome.
