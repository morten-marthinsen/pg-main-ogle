When putts miss, the golfer is the variable -- the putter is a neutral tool that performs according to how it's used. If you're missing, practice more. Get a lesson. Work on your mental game. Try a different brand. But the answer is always in your hands, your stroke, your head.

## Step 5: Complete Simple Reframe Expressions

### COMPLETE EXPRESSION A: Physics-First

```
You've worked on your stroke. You've worked on your mental game.
You've probably gone through three, five, maybe ten putters looking for the answer.

But here's what's actually happening:

Virtually every putter ever made has its center of gravity behind the shaft.
That rearward weight creates a gravitational force that drifts the face open
during the stroke.

Your hands have to compensate for that force on every single putt.
That compensation varies from stroke to stroke.
Under pressure, it gets worse.

That's why you push putts that felt solid.
That's why the practice green doesn't transfer to the course.
That's why every new putter works for two weeks -- then stops.

You've been solving for technique when the real variable was physics.
```

### COMPLETE EXPRESSION B: Liberation-First

```
You've blamed your stroke. You've blamed your nerves.
You've spent years believing the problem was somewhere in your hands or your head.

But here's what's actually happening:

Your putter has been actively working against you.

Its weight sits behind the shaft, which means gravity is pulling the face open
on every stroke. Your body has been making involuntary micro-corrections to
fight that force -- and you've been calling those corrections "bad technique."

Your technique was never the problem.
The equipment was the problem.

That's why nothing worked. Practice, lessons, mental coaching, new putters --
they all treated you as the variable.
But the putter was the variable the whole time.
```

### COMPLETE EXPRESSION C: Combined (Recommended)

```
You've tried working on your stroke. Your grip. Your mental game.
You've been through more putters than you want to admit.

But here's what virtually no golfer knows:

Every putter you've ever owned has its weight in the wrong place.

Behind the shaft. Creating a gravitational pull that drifts the face open
during the stroke. Your hands compensate. But that compensation is imperfect,
changes from putt to putt, and falls apart under pressure.

Your technique was never the problem.
The weight was in the wrong place. That's it.

That's why you push putts that feel pure.
That's why the practice green lies to you.
That's why every putter on the carousel misses the same way.

Every solution you've tried addressed your stroke, your head, your confidence.
None of them moved the weight.
```
