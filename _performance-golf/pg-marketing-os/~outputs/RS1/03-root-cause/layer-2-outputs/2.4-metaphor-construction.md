## Concept Being Explained

**Root cause element:** Rearward center of gravity creates a gravitational force that drifts the face open. The golfer's hands must compensate for this invisible force on every stroke.

**Why metaphor helps:** The physics concept (CG position relative to shaft axis creating rotational torque) is simple in principle but abstract until demonstrated. A metaphor bridges the gap between "hearing about it" and "understanding it" for golfers who haven't yet seen the face-down balance demo.

## Step 3: Metaphor Expressions

### Metaphor A: The Car That Pulls Right (Automotive -- Primary)

**Metaphor mapping:**
| Literal | Parallel |
|---------|----------|
| Rearward CG creating face drift | Car with alignment pulling right |
| Hand compensation every stroke | Constantly correcting the steering wheel |
| Worse under pressure (tight grip) | Worse on a tight highway curve at speed |
| Buying new putters (carousel) | Getting the car repainted / new tires but never fixing alignment |
| Moving CG forward (RS1) | Actually fixing the alignment |

**Complete expression:**

```
Imagine driving a car that pulls to the right.

You can still drive it. You learn to hold the wheel a little left.
On a straight, empty road, you barely notice.

But on a tight curve, at speed, with traffic --
you notice.

That's what virtually every putter does.

The weight sits behind the shaft, so the face drifts open.
You've learned to compensate. On the practice green, with no pressure,
your hands manage it fine.

But on a four-footer for par? With your buddies watching?
Your grip tightens. Your compensation gets erratic.
And the face drifts right off line.

You don't need a better driving technique.
You need a car that tracks straight.

That's what moving the weight forward does.
It's not a different brand of misalignment.
It's alignment.
```

**Quality test:**
- Familiarity: PASS -- everyone has driven a car that pulls
- Accuracy: PASS -- the parallel holds at every level
- Clarity: PASS -- simplifies, doesn't complicate
- Memorability: PASS -- "You don't need better technique. You need a car that tracks straight."
- Completeness: PASS -- covers mechanism, compensation, pressure, and solution
- Originality: PASS -- not a standard golf metaphor

### Metaphor B: The Bent Arrow (Sports/Universal -- Supporting)

**Metaphor mapping:**
| Literal | Parallel |
|---------|----------|
| Rearward CG | Arrow that curves in flight because of a manufacturing flaw |
| Golfer blamed for misses | Archer blamed for missing the target |
| Compensation | Archer learns to aim left to compensate for the curve |
| Pressure failure | Under competition, the aim adjustment falls apart |
| RS1 | A straight arrow |

**Complete expression:**

```
Golf has always said: "It's the archer, not the arrow."

But what if the arrow has been bent this whole time?

Not visibly bent. Not broken. Just built with the weight
in a position that makes it curve slightly on every flight.

The archer compensates. Adjusts. On a good day, makes it work.
On a pressure day, the compensation fails
and the arrow misses the target.

After enough misses, the archer starts to believe they're the problem.

They practice more. Change their form. Try different bows.
But the arrow was bent the whole time.

Your putter's weight sits behind the shaft.
That's the bend.
Moving it forward straightens the arrow.
```

**Quality test:**
- Familiarity: PASS -- "it's the archer, not the arrow" is a known golf phrase
- Accuracy: PASS -- the parallel holds
- Clarity: PASS -- immediate understanding
- Memorability: PASS -- "What if the arrow has been bent this whole time?"
- Completeness: MODERATE -- doesn't fully explain the mechanism
- Originality: STRONG -- subverts a known cliche

### Metaphor C: The Leaky Faucet (Household -- Quick Reference)

**Simplified expression:**

```
It's like trying to fill a bucket under a leaky faucet.

You can hold the bucket perfectly.
You can adjust your position.
You can buy a better bucket.

But if the faucet drips to the left, you're losing water.

The fix isn't a better bucket. It's fixing the faucet.

That's what forward weighting does.
It fixes the leak in the putter's physics --
so your stroke actually goes where you aim it.
```

**Quality test:**
- Familiarity: PASS
- Accuracy: MODERATE -- parallel is looser
- Clarity: PASS -- very simple
- Memorability: MODERATE
- Completeness: LOW -- doesn't explain pressure element
- Originality: MODERATE -- bucket metaphor is somewhat common
