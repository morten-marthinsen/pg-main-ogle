## Step 3: Identifying the Core False Belief

### False Belief Hierarchy

**Surface False Beliefs (symptoms of the core belief):**
- "I need a different grip"
- "I need more practice"
- "I need mental game coaching"
- "I need a fitting"
- "I need a different putter (within the conventional design paradigm)"

**CORE FALSE BELIEF (root):**

> **"Putting performance is determined primarily by the golfer's technique, mental state, and equipment preference -- the putter itself is essentially a neutral instrument that performs according to how the golfer uses it."**

This core false belief creates all surface false beliefs because it directs every solution toward the GOLFER (technique, confidence, practice, fitting) and away from the EQUIPMENT ARCHITECTURE (where the center of gravity sits relative to the face and shaft).

**Test:** If the core false belief were corrected -- if golfers understood that the putter's CG position creates an active gravitational force that the golfer must fight -- would the surface false beliefs dissolve?

**YES.** If they understood that:
- Practice can't solve a physics problem → "practice more" dissolves
- Mental toughness can't override gravity → "it's all mental" dissolves
- Fitting optimizes the wrong variables → "I need a fitting" dissolves
- Every conventional putter shares the same flaw → "I need a different putter" (within the paradigm) dissolves
- Their stroke was never the problem → "I can't putt" (identity) dissolves

## Step 5: The Truth Reframe

**FALSE:** "Putting is determined by the golfer. The putter is neutral."

**TRUE:** "Every putter you have ever used has its center of gravity behind the shaft, creating a gravitational force that drifts the face open during your stroke. You have been compensating for this force with your hands your entire golfing life. Your technique was never the problem. The physics of your putter were working against you the whole time."

**IMPLICATION:** "You do not need a new technique, a new mental approach, a new fitting, or a new version of the same design. You need a putter where the center of gravity is in a fundamentally different position -- in front of the shaft, so gravity works WITH your stroke instead of against it."
