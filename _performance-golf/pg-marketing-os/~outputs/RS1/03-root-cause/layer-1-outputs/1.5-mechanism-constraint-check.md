## Step 2: Mechanism Capability Map

**Mechanism Name:** RS1 Roll Straight Technology — Heavy Forward Weighting + Face Down Balance + 74-Degree Lie Angle

**Mechanism Type:** Product (physical technology)

### Core Capabilities:
1. **Forward Weighting (CG ahead of shaft axis)** — 75%+ of 360g head weight in front 25% of head. Moves center of gravity IN FRONT of the shaft, creating a gravitational force that drives the face TOWARD square (not away from square). (Chris McGinley: "Forward weighting is the special magical sauce of the RS1")
2. **Face Down Balance (FDB)** — When released at lie angle, the RS1 face falls DOWN, not open. This is the visual proof that the gravitational force is actively squaring the face.
3. **74-Degree Upright Lie Angle** — 4 degrees more upright than industry standard. Promotes straighter stroke path with less arc, reducing the opportunity for face rotation.
4. **Steel Face** — CNC machined steel provides solid, traditional feel (vs. LAB's aluminum). Feel parity with premium conventional putters.
5. **Heel-Shaft Option** — Conventional visual at address (vs. LAB's mandatory center-shaft).
6. **No Adaptation Period** — Works with existing stroke from day one. No "torque hangover."
7. **Dual Pistol Grip** — Patented design that locks square into the lifeline, reducing hand manipulation.

### Limitations:
1. **Cannot change the golfer's stroke mechanics** — RS1 does not teach putting; it removes the CG variable that was corrupting the stroke.
2. **Cannot fix green reading or speed judgment** — RS1 addresses face angle (direction), not green-reading skill (line selection) or distance calibration.
3. **Cannot guarantee elimination of yips** — Yips have neurological components beyond face drift. RS1 addresses the mechanical trigger but cannot fully resolve clinical yips.
4. **No tour validation at launch** — No tour professionals using RS1 yet. Relies on McGinley's engineering credentials as tour-adjacent authority.
5. **No independent testing data at launch** — No SAM PuttLab data, no MyGolfSpy review yet.
