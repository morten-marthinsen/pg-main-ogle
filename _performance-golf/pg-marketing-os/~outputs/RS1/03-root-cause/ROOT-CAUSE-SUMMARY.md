## The Root Cause (One Sentence)

**Every putter you've ever owned has its weight in the wrong place -- behind the shaft -- creating a gravitational force that drifts the face open. That miss wasn't mental. It was mechanical.**

---

## Three-Part Structure

### What They Think
"My putting problems come from my technique, my mental game, or not having found the right putter yet. The putter is a neutral tool -- if I'm missing, it's my fault."

### What's Actually Happening
Virtually every putter has its center of gravity behind the shaft, creating a gravitational pull that drifts the face open during the stroke. The golfer must compensate with their hands on every putt -- and that compensation is imperfect, varies putt to putt, and degrades under pressure. The putter is not neutral. It has been actively working against the golfer.

### Why Nothing Has Worked
Every solution tried -- practice, lessons, mental coaching, fittings, new putters -- addressed variables downstream of the root cause. All solutions flow from the false belief that the golfer is the variable and the putter is the constant. When the premise is wrong, every solution derived from it fails.
