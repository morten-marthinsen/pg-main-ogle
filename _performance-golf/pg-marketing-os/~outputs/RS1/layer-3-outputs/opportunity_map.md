## Executive Summary

Deep research across 1,442 voice-of-customer quotes, 8 competitors, 47 beliefs, 12 villains, 67 mechanisms, and 137 proof elements reveals a once-in-a-cycle market opening for the RS1 Putter. The premium putter market (Stage 4, 88% confidence) has been educated by LAB Golf to believe that face stability matters -- but the only available implementation forces tradeoffs in aesthetics, feel, adaptation time, and price that exclude 55-65% of interested buyers. Zero-torque copycats from TaylorMade, Odyssey, Bettinardi, and Evnroll are now commoditizing the very mechanism LAB pioneered, accelerating the moment when "zero torque" becomes as undifferentiated as "high MOI." The RS1 enters at the exact inflection point where the market is ready for the next chapter.

Seven Tier 1 opportunities emerged from the analysis, converging on a unified strategy: own the "Active Face Control" category through Forward Weighting, coin "face drift" as the named root cause of missed putts, and deploy the "Let It Fall" face-down balance demonstration as visual proof that bypasses the Stage 4 skepticism barrier. The recommended positioning is evolutionary -- Traditional putters (face drifts open) to LAB Golf/Zero Torque (face hangs neutral) to RS1/Forward Weighting (face falls square) -- respecting LAB's innovation while transcending it. Chris McGinley's 25-year OEM pedigree and 11 world #1 players provide the engineering authority that a new brand from Performance Golf cannot earn on its own.

The biggest strategic risk is the comprehensive proof gap inherent to a pre-launch product: no customer testimonials, no independent testing, no tour adoption. The entire strategy depends on closing this gap within 90 days of launch through a beta tester program (50-100 golfers with Arccos tracking), SAM PuttLab submission, MyGolfSpy Most Wanted entry, and YouTube creator seeding. The product's mechanism is genuinely differentiated -- no competitor falls face-down when released. The brand's credibility is genuinely deficient -- no hardware track record. Speed of proof acquisition determines whether this launch captures the category or gets absorbed into the ZT noise.

**RS1's structural advantage:** It is the only putter in the market whose face falls DOWN when released at lie angle. Traditional putters twist open. ZT putters hang neutral. RS1 falls face-down. This visible, demonstrable physics difference is the foundation of every Tier 1 opportunity.

### OPP-006: Name the Honeymoon Effect -- Score: 8.22

- **Why it matters:** The Putter Carousel is the highest-importance behavioral pattern in the entire 51-pattern dataset (importance 92, frequency 74). Every golfer recognizes the pattern. No brand has ever explained WHY it happens.

- **The opportunity:** Proactively name and explain the honeymoon effect: confidence boost from novelty reduces grip pressure, which temporarily reduces face manipulation, which temporarily improves putting. Once novelty fades and grip pressure normalizes, rearward CG reasserts, and the same miss pattern returns. Forward Weighting changes the physics permanently. "Gravity does not have a honeymoon period."

- **Key evidence:**

  - *"Since then I've picked up at least 20 different putters."* (Q0874) -- The number 20 reveals compulsion, not rational evaluation.

- **Risks:** Naming the honeymoon too aggressively could make buyers question whether RS1 itself will honeymoon; must be paired with a physics-based explanation for why RS1 is different; carousel riders are the most skeptical segment.

- **Objections to preempt:** "How do I know RS1 won't honeymoon too?" (counter: the honeymoon happens because confidence fades -- but RS1's face stability comes from gravity, not confidence. The physics are permanent. Test it at 30 days, 60 days, 90 days -- the face still falls down).

- **Actions:** Create "Why Every Putter Works for Two Weeks (And What Finally Changed)" content (ACT-I-006, HIGH). Deploy as email sequence opening and social ad concept. Launch beta tester longitudinal tracking to generate anti-honeymoon proof data (ACT-I-007, CRITICAL).

**The alternative (OPP-002):** Everything you wanted from LAB Golf -- face stability that works -- without the tradeoffs. Steel face feel, premium aesthetics, heel shaft, no adaptation period, and $399 instead of $449-$649.
