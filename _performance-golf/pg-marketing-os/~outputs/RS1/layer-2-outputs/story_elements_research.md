### The Discovery Moment

#### How Forward Weighting Was Identified as the Key Innovation

- McGinley's insight: Every conventional putter puts its CG behind the shaft axis. This is the universal flaw that creates toe flow / face drift across all traditional designs [FACTUAL]
- LAB Golf identified half the problem (mechanical torque) but solved it passively — their zero-torque design prevents the putter from twisting on its own, but the face still opens and closes relative to the target line [FACTUAL]
- McGinley's breakthrough: What if you moved the CG dramatically FORWARD of the shaft axis? Instead of eliminating twist (passive), you create an **opposing force** that actively fights face opening (active) [FACTUAL]
- The result: 75%+ of the 360g head weight concentrated in the front 25% of the head — 265g in the face alone, which is the weight of a typical #7 iron head [FACTUAL]
- This forward weighting simultaneously creates: (1) Face Down Balance, (2) Higher MOI in the face, (3) A natural pendulum stroke, (4) Gravity-driven forward motion [FACTUAL]

#### The "Special Magical Sauce" Insight

- McGinley's own words — the forward weighting, specifically the weight forward of the shaft, is what he calls the "special magical sauce" [FACTUAL — (00:50:55)]
- This is a hierarchy call from the designer himself: forward weighting is #1, face-down balance is #2 (the visual proof of the physics), lie angle is #3 (the supporting spec) [FACTUAL]
- The forward weighting GENERATES everything else — FDB, the counteracting force, the higher face MOI [FACTUAL]

#### What Makes This Different from Existing Putter Physics

| Concept | Conventional Putters | LAB Golf (Zero Torque) | RS1 (Forward Weighting) |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| CG Location | Behind shaft axis | At shaft axis (at lie angle) | In front of shaft axis |
| Face Behavior | Opens during stroke (toe flow) | Neutral — no twist, no assist | Falls face-down — active counteracting force |
| Player's Job | Control face rotation throughout stroke | Still must control face relative to target line | Putter HELPS player keep face square |
| Release Type | Rotational timing-based | Rotational timing-based | Vertical, face-square release |
| Problem Solved | None (IS the problem) | Eliminates mechanical twist | Eliminates drift AND assists human stroke |

## 3. Competitive Context Story

### LAB Golf Origin Story

#### The Founding

- **Bill Presse**, a former mini-tour player and industrial engineer, invented the original technology in 2014. He created the Reno 2.1 prototype (later renamed Directed Force 2.1). [RESEARCHED]
- Presse's insight: Traditional putters have an off-axis center of gravity that causes the face to flare open. By aligning the shaft with the putter's CG at lie angle, he could eliminate this mechanical torque. [RESEARCHED]
- He used an angled hosel geometry and up to eight high-density weights to achieve what he called "Lie Angle Balance." [RESEARCHED]

#### Sam Hahn Takes Over

- **Sam Hahn** was a golf-obsessed bar and music venue owner in Eugene, Oregon, who tinkered with equipment and cycled through putters without success. [RESEARCHED]
- Hahn discovered Presse's Directed Force putter through a friend. After a warranty issue, he connected with Presse directly. [RESEARCHED]
- Hahn bought out partners with his family's resources and founded **L.A.B. Golf in spring 2018** to relaunch the technology. [RESEARCHED]
- Key reframe: Hahn renamed the technology "Lie Angle Balanced" and emphasized "zero torque" as the consumer-facing concept. [RESEARCHED]

#### How LAB Popularized "Zero Torque" — The Playbook PG Must Study

1. **Grassroots online evangelism**: Hahn rejected big sponsorships and traditional marketing. Instead, he engaged directly in golf forums (GolfWRX), podcasts, and social media 24/7, personally explaining the physics. [RESEARCHED]
2. **YouTube creator ecosystem**: Dozens then hundreds of review videos from golf creators comparing LAB to traditional putters. The "no thumbs drill" (demonstrating zero torque by putting with thumbs off the grip) became a viral proof-of-concept. [RESEARCHED]
3. **DTC during COVID**: Captive audiences during the pandemic discovered LAB through online content. DTC model meant no retail gatekeepers. [RESEARCHED]
4. **Organic Tour adoption**: Players discovered LAB through club members, not paid endorsements. Lucas Glover, Adam Scott, Will Zalatoris, Wyndham Clark, J.J. Spaun all adopted organically. [RESEARCHED]
5. **Made the invisible visible**: LAB's genius was making "torque" — a physics concept recreational golfers didn't think about — into a mainstream conversation. They gave golfers a NEW WORD for a problem they'd always felt but couldn't name. [INFERRED from research]
6. **The demo is the pitch**: The "hang test" (holding the putter at lie angle and watching it not twist) became the signature proof moment. Simple, visual, undeniable. Equivalent to RS1's "let it fall" face-down demo. [RESEARCHED + INFERRED]

They made it a MOVEMENT by giving it a name ("zero torque"), a visible demo (the hang test), a community (DTC customers who became evangelists), and organic tour validation. PG must execute the same playbook with "forward weighting" and "face drift." [INFERRED]

### Where RS1 Enters the Timeline

RS1 arrives at a specific inflection point: **after zero torque has been validated but before the next category has been named.**

The evolutionary narrative:
1. **Old putters** (pre-2014): CG behind shaft. Face drifts open. The golfer IS the face-stabilization mechanism.
2. **LAB Golf / Zero Torque** (2014-2025): CG at shaft axis. Face doesn't twist on its own. But the golfer STILL must control face rotation relative to the target line.
3. **RS1 / Forward Weighting** (2026): CG in front of shaft. Face falls DOWN — an active opposing force that helps the human player counteract their own tendency to open the face.

This is the progression from PROBLEM → HALF-SOLUTION → FULL SOLUTION. [FACTUAL — from Chris McGinley call + product spec]

## 6. Key Story Tensions to Exploit

### Tension 1: Insider Knowledge vs. Outsider Brand
McGinley is the ultimate insider (21 years at Titleist, worked with Tiger). PG is the outsider brand. This tension IS the story — the insider who went outside because the industry wouldn't build what he knew needed building.

### Tension 2: Simple Physics vs. Complex Engineering
The concept is simple (put the weight in the front, not the back). The execution is complex (10-piece multi-material construction, CNC machining, carbon composite crown, precision-milled interchangeable shaft spuds). The demo is undeniable (face falls down in 2 seconds). This tension creates the "why hasn't anyone done this before?" question.

### Tension 3: Respected Competition vs. Claimed Superiority
The evolutionary narrative must RESPECT LAB Golf while CLAIMING the next chapter. This is not a takedown — it's a succession story. LAB did important work. RS1 does more.

### Tension 4: Training Company Credibility vs. Equipment Ambition
PG has taught millions of golfers to improve. That means they've seen millions of swings and heard from hundreds of thousands of frustrated putters. They didn't enter equipment because they wanted to sell another product — they entered because they saw that the best instruction couldn't fix a problem built into the equipment.

### Tension 5: Premium Price vs. Non-Premium Brand Perception
$399 from Performance Golf vs $449 from Scotty Cameron. The price says "we belong here." The engineering says "we outperform here." The brand says "wait, who are you?" Resolving this tension IS the marketing challenge. Chris McGinley is the resolution.
