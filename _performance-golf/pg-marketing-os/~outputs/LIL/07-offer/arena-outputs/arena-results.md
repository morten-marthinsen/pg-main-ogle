## CANDIDATE GENERATION (6 Perspectives)

### Candidate A: "The Halbert" — Direct Response Fundamentalist (Adapted)

**Core Offer Framing:** Lead with the serial failure story. Position the Start Set as "the last set you'll ever buy." Value stack through failure-cycle math.

```yaml
candidate_a:
  offer_name: "The Lil' Legends Start Set"
  price: $149
  headline_frame: "Stop buying sets that break. Start buying the game."
  value_anchor: "Serial failure math — $60-120 wasted → one system that works"
  bonus_emphasis: "First Swings Card as 'the manual you wish came with your first set'"
  guarantee_recommendation: "Option A — First Swings Promise (unconditional 60-day)"
  differentiation_play: "Category of one — 'the first toddler golf SYSTEM'"
  strengths:
    - Direct, clear value proposition
    - Serial failure math is the strongest price justification in research
    - "Last set you'll ever buy" is a powerful promise for frustrated parents
  weaknesses:
    - "Stop buying sets that break" leans negative — tension with hope market
    - Failure-forward framing could feel like problem agitation, not hope
    - Too DR for branded initiative
```

  headline_frame: "The first toddler golf system where the equipment teaches."
  value_anchor: "Mechanism uniqueness — zero competitors have visual-tactile teaching"

  differentiation_play: "Mechanism differentiation — 4 mechanisms no competitor has"

  weaknesses:
    - Mechanism-forward may feel cold for emotional product
    - "See & Swing System" in the offer name may overcomplicate
    - Parents buy emotions, not systems
