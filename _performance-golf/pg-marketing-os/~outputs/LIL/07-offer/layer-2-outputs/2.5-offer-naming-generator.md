```

```

**Adaptation notes for branded physical product:**
- "Magnetic Reason Why" = not "free" or "88% off" — instead, positioning/exclusivity for branded launch
- "Avatar" = the buyer identity, not a demographic label
- "Goal" = the dream outcome in buyer language
- "Time Interval" = not applicable for many physical products — adapted to developmental window or omitted
- "Container Word" = "System" / "Set" / "Kit" — not "Challenge" or "Blueprint"

**Strategic rationale:** The product name should be simple and accessible. The mechanism (See & Swing System), the promise (seven words), and the identity (golf buddy) are communicated through copy, not the product name. Trying to stuff everything into the name makes it unwieldy. "The Lil' Legends Start Set" is clean, memorable, established across 6 upstream skill packages, and does its job: name the product.

**Mechanism name usage:** "See & Swing System" is the mechanism name used IN copy to describe how the system works. It sits ABOVE the product name (same architecture as "Roll Straight Technology" for the RS1 Putter). The product is the "Start Set." The system inside it is "See & Swing."
