### Round 1: "If all it did was replace the cycle of broken sets..."

"Think about what you've already spent. The $25 plastic set that lasted a week. The $35 one that broke in the backyard. The $40 set that was too heavy to swing. Most parents in our research went through 2-4 sets before finding something that worked. That's $60-120 gone — plus a kid who started to think golf was frustrating.

If the Lil' Legends Start Set did nothing else but end that cycle — one set that works, that lasts, that your kid actually wants to use — would $149 be worth it?

Three clubs. One purchase. Years of use. The math works before you even see what the See & Swing System does."

**Benefit isolated:** Ends the serial failure cycle
**Price justification:** $149 < cumulative failure cost ($60-120) + frustrated kid
**Proof source:** DAT-11 (serial purchase pattern, 8.53), DAT-18 (cost-per-broken-set, 8.03)

---

### Round 2: "If all it did was teach alignment without a single word..."

"Every kids' golf club on the market assumes someone will be standing there coaching. 'Hold it like this.' 'Stand here.' 'Keep your head down.' But your toddler's brain doesn't work that way. They learn through what they can see and touch — like a shape sorter.

If the Lil' Legends Start Set did nothing else but teach your toddler to align the club, set up their stance, and position the ball — all through shapes and colors, without you saying a word — would $149 be worth it?

'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.' Seven words. That's the whole golf lesson. Your kid thinks it's a game."

**Benefit isolated:** Self-teaching mechanism replaces verbal coaching
**Price justification:** $149 < private lessons ($50-75 each) and this works better for toddlers
**Proof source:** MEC-13 knockout (10.00), AUT-11 Piaget (8.42), shape sorter analogy (8.90)

---

### Round 3: "If all it did was turn you into their golf buddy..."

"Here's what most parents don't expect: the hardest part of getting your toddler into golf isn't the golf. It's not becoming the pushy parent. It's not turning into the coach who corrects every swing, repositions them every 30 seconds, and watches their kid walk away after three frustrated attempts.

If the Lil' Legends Start Set did nothing else but take the coaching off your plate — so your only job was to stand next to your kid, play alongside them, and watch them figure it out on their own — would $149 be worth it?

Golf buddy. Not golf coach. That's the job you signed up for."

**Benefit isolated:** Identity transformation — parent role redefined
**Price justification:** $149 for a completely different parenting experience around golf
**Proof source:** Root cause (9.19), SP-3 emotional close (9.00), research: "frustrated coach" quotes
