## Alignment Gap Analysis

### Gaps Identified

1. **GAP-05 (Demo Video):** The strongest promise-offer alignment would show the product working. "Equipment teaches" and "seven words" are self-demonstrating — but only AFTER seeing the product. Pre-purchase, the demo video is the bridge between promise and perceived delivery. **Impact on alignment: -0.5 from potential ceiling.** Target: May 1, 2026.

2. **GAP-02 (Testimonials):** "Your little legend won't stop swinging" is part of the promise. Without testimonials from real families, this remains aspirational. **Impact on alignment: -0.3 from potential ceiling.** Target: April 15 (beta testing).

3. **GAP-01 (Guarantee):** Guarantee is the risk reversal that says "we believe this enough to back it." Without a finalized guarantee, the offer's perceived likelihood takes a hit. The 3 options are architected but Leadership hasn't selected. **Impact on alignment: -0.2 from potential ceiling.** Target: Before launch.

### What's Excellent

1. **Mechanism-to-offer perfect match:** The See & Swing System IS the product. The mechanism and the deliverable are the same thing. This is the strongest possible alignment — no "the product helps you do the thing that the mechanism describes." The product IS the mechanism.

2. **Identity transformation embedded in offer structure:** "Golf buddy, not golf coach" isn't a claim bolted onto the offer — it's a structural consequence of the offer design. If the equipment teaches, the parent doesn't coach. The offer architecture makes the identity transformation automatic.

3. **Narrative flow preserves Big Idea arc:** Hybrid-1's 10-step narrative flow mirrors the Big Idea v3 beat structure exactly: personal story → science earns claim → mechanism reveal → knockout proof → product → value → price → guarantee → identity close.
