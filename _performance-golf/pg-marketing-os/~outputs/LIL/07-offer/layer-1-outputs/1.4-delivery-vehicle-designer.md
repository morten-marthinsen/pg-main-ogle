## Component-Level Delivery Vehicles

### Component 1: The Driver (Club 1 of 3)
- **Solution Delivered:** Long-game introduction — "Drive"
- **Problems Addressed:** #2 (too heavy), #11 (repositioning), #12 (can't aim)
- **Teaching Mechanism:** Walk-In Frame (self-setup) + Focal Sphere (visual alignment)
- **Unique Delivery:** Club stands upright on mat — child walks in and grabs it. No parent setup required.
- **Value Contribution:** Highest "wow factor" component. The Walk-In Frame is the most visually distinctive mechanism.

### Component 2: The Iron/Wedge (Club 2 of 3)
- **Solution Delivered:** Mid/short game — "Hit"
- **Problems Addressed:** #2 (too heavy), #12 (can't aim), #13 (where to stand)
- **Teaching Mechanism:** Focal Sphere (visual alignment) + Skills Mat guides (stance)
- **Unique Delivery:** Focal Sphere on clubhead matches ball position on mat. Child sees the color match and aligns naturally.
- **Value Contribution:** Core hitting experience. Where most "first contact" moments happen.

### Component 3: The Putter (Club 3 of 3)
- **Solution Delivered:** Putting / short game — "Putt"
- **Problems Addressed:** #2 (too heavy), #12 (can't aim), #15 (boredom)
- **Teaching Mechanism:** Face Forward (visual face angle) + Focal Sphere
- **Unique Delivery:** Putting is the easiest entry point for toddlers. Lowest barrier to "first success" moment.
- **Value Contribution:** Highest frequency of use. Most accessible skill level. Best for indoor/anywhere practice.

### Component 4: Skills Mat
- **Solution Delivered:** Practice station + stance teaching + ball positioning
- **Problems Addressed:** #13 (where to stand), #14 (need a range), #15 (boredom)
- **Teaching Mechanism:** 8 visual guides — stance markers, ball position, alignment aids
- **Unique Delivery:** Portable training surface. Rolls up, fits in carry bag. Creates a practice station anywhere.
- **Value Contribution:** The "system" differentiator. No competitor has an integrated teaching surface. Transforms "just clubs" into "a system."

### Component 5: Carry Bag
- **Solution Delivered:** Portability + ownership + "real golfer" identity
- **Problems Addressed:** #14 (need a range — bag enables mobile practice)
- **Teaching Mechanism:** None — functional accessory
- **Unique Delivery:** Child-sized bag the toddler can carry. Makes them feel like "a real golfer."
- **Value Contribution:** Emotional value exceeds functional value. "My kid carries their own bag" = proud parent moment. Also practical — keeps system together, easy transport.
