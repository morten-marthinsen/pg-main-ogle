## Playbook 1: Guarantee Definition

```yaml
playbook: "Guarantee Definition"
gap: GAP-01
category: risk_reversal
owner: Business Leadership
timeline: "This week — decision only"
effort: low
```

### Decision Framework

| Factor | Consideration for LIL |
|--------|----------------------|
| Product type | Physical product — child must use it over time |
| Price point | Premium toddler golf set — higher risk perception |
| Competition | Charlie Golf offers standard returns; no competitor has a named guarantee |
| Child engagement timeline | Parents need 2-4 weeks to see if child engages |
| Return logistics | Physical product requires shipping back |

### Recommended Option: 60-Day "Little Legend" Guarantee

**Why 60 days:** Gives families 8+ weeks to observe their child's engagement pattern. Toddlers may take 1-2 weeks to warm up to new toys. 60 days provides genuine evaluation time while remaining operationally manageable.

**Copy template:**
> **The 60-Day "Little Legend" Guarantee**
>
> We're so confident your little one will love their Lil' Legends set that we're giving you a full 60 days to put it to the test.
>
> If your child doesn't light up when they see their clubs... if the Skills Mat doesn't become their favorite play station... if you don't see them swinging with more confidence week after week...
>
> Just send it back for a complete refund. No questions. No hassle. No risk.

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Leadership approves guarantee terms and duration
- [ ] Legal reviews guarantee language
- [ ] CS team briefed on refund process
- [ ] Guarantee copy added to e-comm page, checkout, order confirmation
- [ ] Refund tracking system configured

## Playbook 2: Beta Testing Program

```yaml
playbook: "Beta Testing Program"
gap: GAP-02
category: social
owner: Product / Creative Team
timeline: "Launch by April 15, 2026 (6 weeks before May 27)"
effort: medium
```

### Phase 1: Family Selection (Week 1)

**Target: 15-25 families**

| Segment | Count | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Golf-playing parents with toddlers (2-5) | 8-10 | Core target audience |
| Non-golf parents with toddlers | 4-5 | Tests "gateway to golf" positioning |
| Grant Horvat's family | 1 | Built-in content opportunity |
| PG-affiliated families (employees, partners) | 3-5 | Accessible, cooperative |
| Youth golf instructors with young children | 2-3 | Expert + parent dual perspective |

**Selection criteria:**
- Child age 2-5 (mix across range)
- Willing to sign media release
- Willing to share photos/videos
- Geographic diversity (outdoor settings variety)
- Mix of boys and girls

### Phase 2: Kit Shipping (Week 2)

**Each beta kit includes:**
- Complete Lil' Legends set (Driver, Iron, Putter, Skills Mat, Carry Bag)
- Welcome card with QR code to feedback form
- Photo/video guidelines card (simple, one-page)
- Pre-paid return label (if needed)
- Small thank-you gift (e.g., LIL-branded item)

**Media release template (simplified):**
> I grant Performance Golf permission to use photos and videos of my child using Lil' Legends products in marketing materials. Only first names will be used. I confirm my child is between ages 2-5. I may withdraw permission at any time by emailing [address].

### Phase 3: Feedback Collection (Weeks 2-5)

**Touchpoint schedule:**
- **Day 1:** "Did the set arrive? What was the unboxing reaction?"
- **Week 1:** "How many times has your child played with it this week?"
- **Week 2:** "Have you noticed any changes in their swing or engagement?"
- **Week 4:** "Overall impressions — would you recommend to another parent?"

**Guided questions (each touchpoint):**
1. What's your child's favorite club? Why?
2. How long does a typical play session last?
3. Does your child use the Skills Mat independently?
4. What words does YOUR CHILD use to describe the set? (authentic language gold)
5. Would you buy this as a gift for another family?
6. Any photos or videos to share?

### Phase 4: Content Harvest (Weeks 4-6)

**Minimum viable output:**
- 10+ written testimonials with specific observations
- 5+ usable photos of children playing
- 3+ video clips (even phone quality)
- 2+ "unboxing reaction" moments
- 1+ progression comparison (week 1 vs. week 4)

**Ideal output:**
- 20+ written testimonials
- 10+ high-quality photos across diverse settings
- 5+ video clips showing genuine engagement
- Before/after skill progression documentation
- Authentic child language quotes ("I hit it with the red ball!")

**Testimonial scoring criteria:**
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Specific observation (time, frequency, behavior) | +3 |
| Named attribution willing | +2 |
| Photo/video included | +2 |
| Emotional moment described | +2 |
| Mentions specific mechanism (Focal Sphere, Skills Mat) | +1 |

## Playbook 3: Grant Horvat Demo Video

```yaml
playbook: "Spokesperson Demo Video"
gap: [GAP-02, GAP-05]
category: [social, demonstration]
owner: Creative Team + Grant Horvat
timeline: "Film by April 15, 2026; edit by May 1"
effort: medium
```

### Pre-Production

**Scheduling:**
- Coordinate with Grant's content calendar
- Allow 2-3 hours for filming (toddler attention span is variable)
- Outdoor location preferred (backyard, park, or course)
- Morning light best for video quality + child energy

**Shot list (priority order):**

| Shot | Purpose | Target Duration |
|------|---------|----------------|
| Child's first reaction to the set | Authentic unboxing moment | 30-60 sec |
| Walk-In Frame demo | Child walks up and grips independently | 15-30 sec |
| Focal Sphere close-up + child using | Show mechanism in action | 15-30 sec |
| Skills Mat play session | Father-child activity together | 60-120 sec |
| Grant explaining design involvement | Authority + mechanism explanation | 60-90 sec |
| Candid play moments | Unscripted authentic joy | Ongoing capture |
| Grant's endorsement statement | Direct-to-camera recommendation | 30-60 sec |

**Key capture moments (don't miss these):**
- The moment the child picks up a club without being told how
- Any spontaneous laughter or excitement
- Grant's genuine reaction to his child's swing
- The child returning to play unprompted

### Production Notes

**DO:**
- Capture candid, unscripted moments (most powerful for social proof)
- Let the child lead — follow their interest
- Film multiple takes of Walk-In Frame (the "no instruction needed" demo)
- Get Grant's authentic reactions, not scripted reads
- Film in natural light, real settings

**DON'T:**
- Over-direct the child (forced smiles read as fake)
- Script Grant's child interactions (authenticity is everything)
- Use studio settings (this is a backyard product)
- Rush — toddler filming requires patience

### Post-Production Deliverables

| Deliverable | Format | Use |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Long-form YouTube video (3-5 min) | 16:9 HD | Grant's channel (1.6M subs) |
| 60-sec product overview | 16:9 + 9:16 | E-comm hero video |
| 15-sec mechanism clips (×4) | 9:16 | Social ads (one per mechanism) |
| Still frames (×10-15) | High-res | E-comm product page, email |
| Grant endorsement quote card | Static image | Social, email, page |
| Behind-the-scenes reel (30 sec) | 9:16 | Organic social content |

## Playbook 4: Engineering Performance Data

```yaml
playbook: "Engineering Data Collection"
gap: GAP-04
category: data
owner: Chris McGinley / Product Development
timeline: "2-3 weeks"
effort: low
```

### Data Collection Checklist

| Metric | How to Measure | Copy Application |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Total set weight | Digital scale (grams) | "The complete set weighs just X grams — lighter than a bag of groceries" |
| Individual club weights | Digital scale each | "Each club weighs less than X" |
| Competitor weight comparison | Weigh 3-5 competitor sets | "X% lighter than [competitor]" table |
| Grip circumference | Calipers (mm) | "Sized for hands as small as X inches around" |
| Club length range | Tape measure | "Adjustable from X to X inches — grows with your child" |
| Face Forward launch angle | Launch monitor testing | "Launches X° higher than standard loft" |
| Skills Mat dimensions | Tape measure | "X' × X' — fits in any living room or backyard" |
| Skills Mat activity count | Count distinct stations | "X different training activities" |
| Material durability | Impact cycle testing | "Tested to withstand X,000+ impacts" |
| Carry bag weight (loaded) | Scale with full set | "Everything packs into X pounds" |

**Documentation format:**
Each data point should include the measurement, methodology, and date tested. This becomes citable engineering data for the e-comm product page.

## Playbook 5: Product Photography

```yaml
playbook: "Product Photography"
gap: GAP-05
category: demonstration
owner: Creative Team
timeline: "3-4 weeks before launch (by May 1)"
effort: medium
```

### Shot Categories

**Category A: Hero Product Shots (MUST HAVE)**
- Full set laid out (all 3 clubs + Mat + Bag)
- Individual club hero shots (white background)
- Skills Mat fully deployed, top-down view
- Carry bag packed and standing

**Category B: Mechanism Detail Shots (MUST HAVE)**
- Focal Sphere close-up (color visible, sunk into clubhead)
- Walk-In Frame — club standing upright, child-height perspective
- Face Forward Design — angle showing offset
- Skills Mat — close-up of stance markers and path guides
- Grip detail — showing size relative to child's hand

**Category C: In-Use Photography (HIGH PRIORITY)**
- Child swinging driver (outdoor setting)
- Child putting on Skills Mat
- Child carrying bag independently
- Parent and child playing together
- Multiple children using set (social play)

**Category D: Lifestyle Shots (NICE TO HAVE)**
- Backyard setting (suburban family)
- Park/open space setting
- Golf course (child-friendly area)
- Indoor use (living room/playroom)
- Set stored in home (showing how it fits into life)
