## Usage Guidance

### Best Positions for Knockout (MEC-13)

| Position | How to Use | When |
|----------|-----------|------|
| **Lead** | Open with it: "Imagine teaching your toddler golf with just one sentence..." | Cold traffic, e-comm hero, ad hooks |
| **Mechanism** | After explaining Focal Sphere: "And here's the beautiful part — one sentence is all it takes." | Stage 3 deep-dive content |
| **Close** | Bring back before CTA: "Remember: 'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.' That's it. That's all they need." | Always — reinforce before ask |

### Copy Integration (Mechanism-Led, Stage 3)

```
MECHANISM INTEGRATION:
1. Setup: Establish the problem (toddlers can't process verbal instruction)
2. Introduce Focal Sphere mechanism (visual alignment)
3. KNOCKOUT: "All the parent says is: 'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.'"
4. Let the simplicity land (pause beat — ellipsis or line break)
5. Dimensionalize: "No 'keep your head down.' No 'square the face.'
   No frustrated tears. Just match the yellow ball... to the white ball."
6. Connect to parent emotion: "That's a golf lesson. That's a bonding moment."
```

### Formatting Recommendations
- Present the sentence in quotes, italics, or as a standalone line
- Use the exact wording: "Match the yellow ball to the white ball"
- Never paraphrase — the specificity IS the proof
- In video: show parent saying it, child responding
- On e-comm: use as a callout/highlight element
