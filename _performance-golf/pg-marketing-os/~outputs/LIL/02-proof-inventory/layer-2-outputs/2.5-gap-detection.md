### GAP-03: AUTHORITY — 7 Uncited Scientific Claims

```yaml
gap_id: GAP-03
category: authority
gap_type: subtype_gap
severity: medium
element_count: 14
strong_count: 8
medium_count: 6
weak_count: 0
missing_subtype: study_peer_reviewed
```

**Issue:** The proof inventory contains 7 scientific claims that are stated as fact but lack cited sources:

1. "90% of brain development occurs by age 5" — cited widely but needs primary source
2. "Children are visual learners at ages 2-5" — developmental psychology claim
3. "Piaget preoperational stage (2-7)" — well-established but should cite Piaget
4. "Color focus drives attention in early childhood" — needs study citation
5. "Play-based motor skill development" — needs developmental research citation
6. "Screen time affecting child development" — needs specific study/data
7. "Youth golf participation statistics" — needs source attribution (NGA, GFF, etc.)

**Impact:**
- Using uncited claims risks credibility if challenged
- At Stage 3 (mechanism-hungry market), authority backing strengthens mechanism claims
- Cannot use "research shows" or "studies confirm" language without citations
- All 7 claims are Layer 3 DISCOVERY targets

**Resolution type:** LAYER 3 DISCOVERY — web search for peer-reviewed studies, institutional reports, and expert citations. All 7 claims are well-established enough that supporting research should be findable.
