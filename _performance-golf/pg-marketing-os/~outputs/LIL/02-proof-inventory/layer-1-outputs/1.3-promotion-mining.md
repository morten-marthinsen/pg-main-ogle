### Competitor Promotional Claims Extracted

#### 1. Tot Clubs — Promotional Claims
- **Mechanism claim:** "Golf clubs designed to make learning the game easy and fun...from the age they start walking, until they're ready for metal clubs"
- **Weight claim:** "140-180 grams total club weight" (specific, verifiable)
- **Limitation admission:** "Those clubs don't really simulate a shot very well. It's just a stick to hit a ball rather than actually a lofted iron." — Max Franklin, founder [EXP2-CM-032]
- **Proof function:** MECHANISM (competitor_admission) — founder admits his own product's limitation. This is usable proof that "lightweight alone doesn't solve the problem."

#### 2. The Littlest Golfer (TLG) — Promotional Claims
- **Mechanism claim:** "We didn't simply shrink down an adult set to kid size. Our durable, lightweight clubs promote proper hinge and swing, while Littlest Grip Technology ensures the right grip every time."
- **Patented feature:** "Littlest Grip Technology (LGT)" — forced grip guides hands into proper position
- **Warranty limitation:** "Not designed or intended for use with real golf balls. Using these clubs with standard golf balls will void any warranty claims." [EXP2-CM-015]
- **Proof function:** MECHANISM (competitor_admission) — TLG solved grip but NOT visual alignment. Their foam-ball-only limitation is a structural weakness LIL doesn't have.

#### 3. Charlie Golf Co. — Promotional Claims
- **Social proof claim:** "Trusted by over 13,000 golfing families" [YF-119]
- **Weight philosophy:** "Weight is going to be the most important factor when finding the perfect kids golf club set" [EXP2-CM-002]
- **Design philosophy:** "We wanted this set to look like mom's and dad's clubs" / "Simple is best. Especially for a toddler."
- **Growth data:** Founded November 2023, grew to 13,000 families by research date (~2 years)
- **Proof function:** DATA (market_validation) — Charlie's growth proves DEMAND for premium toddler golf. Also MECHANISM (competitor_admission) — "simple" positioning lacks mechanism innovation.

#### 4. Liteyear Golf — Promotional Claims
- **Category reframe claim:** "Golf adapted for kids, not golf designed for kids. Zero innovation, and maybe more importantly, no excitement."
- **Ambition claim:** "We want to be every kid's introduction to golf. Everything we build, design, and produce is through the lens of the child first."
- **Social proof claim:** "Multiple top-10 golfers in the world have bought Liteyear sets for their children" [YF-109]
- **Proof function:** MECHANISM (competitor_admission) — Liteyear names the problem ("zero innovation") but their solution is "better design" not a specific mechanism. LIL has the mechanism they're describing the need for.

#### 5. Wilson Ultra BLK — Promotional Claims
- **Heritage claim:** "100+ years of constant research creates a formula so special, it simply can't be mimicked."
- **Sizing claim:** "8 sizing options" (most in market)
- **Mechanism claim:** "Super Game Improvement technology" (vague, undefined)
- **Proof function:** DATA (competitor_comparison) — Wilson relies on brand heritage, not innovation. "Super Game Improvement" is meaningless jargon vs. LIL's concrete Focal Sphere mechanism.

#### 6. Shorties Tot Golf — Promotional Claims
- **Social proof:** "67 reviews on Scheels with 5.0 average (66 five-stars, 1 four-star)" [EXP2-CM-060]
- **Positioning claim:** "Best clubs to get a toddler started loving the game! No more plastic clubs — they are the real deal."
- **Proof function:** SOCIAL (review_rating) — competitor has achieved strong ratings, validating market demand. However, even satisfied parents note grow-out: "2nd set for our growing golfer!" [EXP2-CM-059]
