# **PG | Product Concept Doc – Lil’ Legends**

**Purpose:** Lil’ Legends is a set of toddler golf clubs (ages 2-5 mainly). This product is meant to be part of a movement to get kids into golf at a very young age. Lil’ Legends is meant to be fun for kids and is built to simplify instructions while increasing engagement. We’re giving parents a place to start for getting their kids into golf. Grant Horvat is the promoter, who is widely viewed and respected, to be the trusted source spearheading this movement. This fits with the PG “love your game” and is meant to start kids into golf early and by association gain adult customers as well.

## **1\. Product Overview**

**Product Name / Working Title:** Lil’ Legends  
**Category / Line:** Junior Golf / Toddler Development  
**Launch Target:** May 27, 2026  
**Product Type:** New innovation (not seasonal)

**Summary Statement:**  
Lil’ Legends is the first golf system engineered specifically for the cognitive and physical developmental stage of toddlers (ages 2-5), solving the "attention span" and "coordination" gap that makes traditional instruction impossible for this age group. Unlike plastic toys that offer no feel, or cut-down junior clubs that are too heavy and abstract, Lil’ Legends utilizes a proprietary Focal Sphere visual mechanism and a Stand-Up, Walk-In design to bypass complex verbal instructions. By turning the golf swing into a simple task of "matching shapes and colors," Lil’ Legends creates instant fun and tangible success, building golfing motor skills through play rather than frustration

## **2\. Strategic Rationale**

**Business Objective:**

* **Primary:** Expand into a new category (toddlers/junior). Create brand loyalty at the earliest possible entry point (LTV expansion), capturing the next generation of golfers AND their parents.  
* **Secondary:** Solve the "barrier to entry" for golfing parents who want to introduce their kids to the game but find current options (toys vs. heavy clubs) ineffective.  
* **Additionally:** Establish Performance Golf as an expert in golf education across *more* age and skill levels, proving our "simple is better" philosophy application to 2-5 year olds as it does adults.

**Consumer Insight:**  
Parents want to share their love of golf with their children, but the reality is often frustrating. Toddlers have limited attention spans, cannot process abstract verbal commands like "keep your head down" or "square the face," and are fidgety in motion. Current market options fail them: plastic toys are just toys, and "real" junior clubs require motor skills a 3-year-old hasn't developed yet. Parents need a way to create *immediate* success so the child associates golf with fun rather than failure.

Research supports the importance of brain development by age 5 (90% of brain growth by then), both for motor skills as well as the related areas of the brain linked to learning and socializing.

**Brand Fit:**  
Just as our adult products (like the ONE.1) simplify the game by engineering forgiveness into the equipment, Lil’ Legends simplifies the *learning process* by engineering the instruction into the visual design of the club. This aligns perfectly with the "Love Your Game" ethos–ensuring even the youngest players love the game from their very first swing.

**Market Opportunity:**  
The junior market is polarized between "cheap plastic" and "scaled-down pro gear." There is a "Blue Ocean" opportunity for developmental equipment. Additionally, there is an influx of junior golfers, these Lil’ Legends clubs promote golf at its earliest stages. Also, presenting toddler clubs opens up channels for parents and grandparents who may be open to purchasing other adult PG equipment.

**Cognitive Alignment:** Kids are visual learners; they process shapes and colors better than words.

**Unmet Need:** There is no product currently designed to handle the "fidget factor" of a toddler trying to align themselves to a ball.

**Premium Potential:** Golfing parents are willing to invest in a "system" (Mat \+ Clubs \+ Bag) that actually works, rather than disposable toys.

## **3\. Product Details**

**Product Description:**  
The Lil’ Legends set is a comprehensive 3-club system (Driver, Iron, Putter) paired with an integrated Skills Mat and carry bag. The core innovation is the visual interface: oversized heads featuring a visually differentiated **Focal Sphere** sunk into the clubhead.

The system features include (among other features):

1\. **Visually Differentiated Focal Sphere**: A hi-vis, golf-ball-sized sphere sunk into the crown/back of the clubs to guide focus and contact.

2\. **Stand-Up, Walk-In Driver Frame**: A slotted sole allowing the driver to stand upright on the mat independently, letting the child "walk in" to the grip.

3\. **Skills Mat & Bag**: A mat with visual guides for stance and swing path that rolls up to become the carry bag for the kit.

4\. **Face Forward Design**: Oversized heads with easy-up loft specs and impact guidance marks.

**Key Benefits / Claims:**

***Functional Benefits:***

* Stand-Up, Walk-In Driver Frame: Allows the driver to stand up independently for easier walk up and hit.  
* Focal Sphere Design: Replaces abstract instruction with visual matching. The Focal Sphere bypasses complex mechanics. The child simply focuses on swinging the colored sphere on the club into the ball on the ground.  
  * Simplifies Eye-Hand Coordination. The hi-vis sphere sunk into the head draws the child’s eye, making it easier to track the clubhead path into the ball.  
  * Instant Alignment. The round shape of the Sphere mirrors the ball, creating a "lock and key" visual that tells the child exactly where to make contact.  
* Skills Mat & Bag Package: Provides an easier storage system as well as a variety of instructive and game features.

***Emotional Benefits:***

* Tangible Success: The higher loft and oversized heads make contact and getting the ball in the air easier with the driver and iron, leading to instant positive feedback (ball in air), which is critical for a toddler's engagement.  
* Parental Relief: No need to nag or over-coach. The parent can simply say, "Match the yellow ball to the white ball," creating a fun, low-stress bonding experience.  
* Confidence of "I Did It\!": The oversize head combined with the focal point allows the club to do the work, giving the child the feeling of being a "real golfer."  
* Independence: Need for less instruction, stand-up walk-in frame, and skills mat accessories allow the toddler to operate with more independence during a time when more independence is desirable developmentally.  
* Challenges & Achievement: Golf in general is a challenging game and is rewarding when success is achieved. This helps teach toddlers important lessons during a young and influential age.  

**Unique Selling Proposition (USP):**  
Lil’ Legends is the only toddler golf system centered on the Focal Sphere Mechanism, a visually differentiated guide that transforms the abstract difficulty of hitting a golf ball into a simple, intuitive game of matching shapes and colors–toddlers are set up to make consistent contact without verbal instruction.

**Product Story / Hero Message:**  
Golf is not just a sport it is a lifestyle, one that trains the body athletically and the mind given the challenges of the sport. Early engagement often sets the stage for the rest of a golfer’s life. This product’s purpose is to get junior golfers on the right path from a very early stage.

Regarding the product design itself \- "Toddlers don't learn easily with words; they learn with their eyes.” Lil’ Legends replaces complicated swing thoughts with one simple visual cue: The Focal Sphere. By sinking a hi-vis sphere into the clubhead, we’ve turned the golf swing into a simple game of 'connect the dots.' Swing the sphere. Hit the ball. Love your game. The first set designed for how your child actually learns.

## **4\. Target Consumer**

**Primary Audience:**  
Boys and girls, ages 2-5 for the product itself. In order to sell this product, the audience being spoken to are usually the parents and grandparents. At this age range, the parents are most likely to be in their 30s.

Potential “Psychographic Profile”:

* Avid golfers who dream of playing with their kids.  
* Have tried plastic sets and found them flimsy/boring.  
* Are currently frustrated that their toddler loses interest after 2 minutes on the range.  
* Value "developmental" toys that build skills, not just distract.

**Consumer Mindset:**  
*Internal Dialogue:* "I want my kid to like golf, but it's too hard for them right now. I try to tell them how to stand or hold the club, but they just get frustrated and run away. I don't want to be the pushy sports parent, but I don't want them to quit before they start. How do I get started?"

Key Barriers / Overcome:

* Cost ←Value proposition, that it’s worth the cost given the fun, developmental benefits, and future this sets the child up with  
* Growth Trajectory ←Parents will worry that they only get a couple years out of this product. Overcoming this may include options such as: Having enough benefit that they want the product despite this; Offering another line of junior clubs for the next age range; Offering a trade-in value or used set.

**Usage Occasion:**

* Year round (not seasonal) for kids to learn golf.  
* Backyard / Living Room (?): The "Stand-Up, Walk-In" feature and foam balls may allow for safe indoor/outdoor play in some house environments.  
* Course / Driving Range: The Skills Mat provides a defined "station" for the child while the parent practices.

**Competitive Analysis:**

* **The "Stand-Up, Walk-In" Frame:** This is a massive functional innovation unique to this product. While even premium sets like "TaylorMade" require the child to lift the club and organize their body, the Lil' Legends driver stands vertically on its own. This solves the "fidgety kid" problem by allowing them to simply walk into the setup. Even if not utilized, this feature builds intrigue.  
    
* **Concrete vs. Abstract Alignment:** Competitors use abstract lines or logos. Lil' Legends literally sinks a 3D yellow ball into the club head. This changes the instruction from "hit the center" (abstract) to "match the one ball to the other ball" (concrete), which is cognitively superior for most 2-5 year olds.  
    
* **Training Ecosystem vs. Equipment Bag:** Most competitors (Callaway, Wilson) sell tools in a bag. Lil' Legends sells a *station*. The bag unfurls into a "Skills Mat" with markings for stance, low point, and path. It turns any floor into a lesson tee; competitors just hold clubs.  
    
* **Face Forward Design:** The Iron and Putter feature a distinct "Face Forward" offset. While "U.S. Kids" focuses on lightness, this design specifically combats the toddler tendency to deloft the club, making it mechanically easier to launch the ball than standard "mini-adult" irons found in "Tour X" sets.

## **7\. Risks & Open Questions**

**Risks:**

1\. **Price Sensitivity:** Will parents pay a premium (vs. $25 plastic sets) for a set the child might outgrow in 2 years? Potential response: Emphasize the "Skills Mat" and the foundational motor skills that last a lifetime.

2\. **Durability:** Toddlers bang clubs on concrete/cart paths. The Focal Sphere and Stand-Up frame need to be sturdy.

3\. **Size Gapping:** Ensuring the "One Size" fits the variance between a small 2-year-old and a tall 4-year-old.

**Open Questions:**

1. **Surfaces:** Will the Skills Mat work on wet and uneven surfaces if outside?  
2. **Left & Right**: Is this offered for left-handed players as well as right?
