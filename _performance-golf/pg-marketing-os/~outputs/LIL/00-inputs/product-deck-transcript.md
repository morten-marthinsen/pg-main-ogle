## Slide 2: Problem/Solution
**How do we create fun while helping kids develop golfing motor skills?**

PROBLEM:
- Toddlers have a limited attention span, fun is the key to learning
- Abstract concepts are too complicated, even the simple task of setting up to hit a golf ball can be a challenge
- Hand-eye coordination is developing, making it easy to set-up, swing and make contact is the goal
- Kids are visual learners first and foremost, they process shapes and colors better than verbal instructions
- Color helps focus, holds engagement longer, makes for tangible success

SOLUTION:
Little Legends develops Drive, Hit and Putt motor skills in Toddler golfers ages 2-5 through fun, focused, play based activities

## Slide 4: Hero Features — Focal Sphere + Simplified Process
**Visually Differentiated Focal Sphere:**
- Round, golf ball sized sphere sunk into the club head
- Visually differentiated from the club head via hi-vis contrasting color
- Shape and color make the sphere easy for the parent to point out
- Shape and color make the sphere easy for the child to focus on
- Sunk area in crown mimics a smaller driver profile

**Simplified Process:**
- Swing the sphere on the club into the ball to be hit
- Sphere reduces too much ball on the ground focus (hit mentality)
- Simple ball to ball contact, swing and let the oversize club heads do the work
- The stand up, walk in feature helps makes it simple for parent and child

*Photo: Stand Up, Walk In feature on the skills mat — driver standing upright with ball teed up*

## Slide 5: Hero Features — Stand Up, Walk In
- Kids are fidgety, always in motion
- The simple act of getting them to stay still to hit a golf ball is challenging
- Getting them to hold the club and align at the target is a bigger challenge
- The stand up, walk in feature lets kids be kids, goof off between shots
  - Parent stands up the Driver using the mat frame and sole slot
  - Child walks in when ready, grips the club, swings
- It is easier to call the child over, versus having to get them in the right place
- Child does not have to figure out how to set up, just walk into the shot
- The club is waiting aligned and ready to be gripped
- This creates good process & habits that carry over to the iron and putter
