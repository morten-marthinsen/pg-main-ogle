## 2. LANGUAGE DON'Ts — Never Use These

### Words to Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Why | Say Instead |
|------------|-----|-------------|
| "Proprietary technology" | Corporate jargon | "It works" |
| "Engineered" | Product-manager voice | "Designed for" / "Built for" |
| "Motor skills development" | Clinical / academic | "He can actually hit it" |
| "Hand-eye coordination" | Clinical | "She connects every time" |
| "Cognitive development" | Academic | "He's learning" |
| "Executive function" | Academic | (Don't reference at all) |
| "Optimized" / "Maximized" | Absent from ALL parent data | (Don't use) |
| "Studies show" / "Research indicates" | Parents share stories, not studies | Tell a story instead |
| "Investment in their future" | Sounds like pressure | "Something we can do together" |
| "Performance" | For kids = pressure | "Progress" / "Getting better" |
| "Curriculum" / "Program" | School language | "Games" / "Practice" |
| "Tournament-ready" | Pressure language | "Having fun" |
| "System" (in parent-facing copy) | Too technical | "Clubs" / "A set" |
| "FOMO" | Marketing jargon | "I don't want him to fall behind" |

### Tone to Avoid

- **Urgency/scarcity** — Zero countdown-timer energy
- **Guilt** — "Most parents don't know this" triggers defensiveness
- **Techspeak** — Specs discussed in plain language only
- **Competitive framing** — "Get ahead" is absent; "together" dominates
- **Gender-specific** — Moms AND dads post, about sons AND daughters
- **Preachy** — Screen time concern is real but do NOT lecture
