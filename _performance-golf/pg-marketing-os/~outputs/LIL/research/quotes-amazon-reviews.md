## KEY THEMES IDENTIFIED

### Pain Themes (for ad copy)
1. **Clubs break within hours/days** -- the #1 complaint across ALL brands
2. **Plastic too brittle** -- cannot withstand even normal toddler play
3. **Missing pieces** -- arrives incomplete or with broken parts
4. **Adjustable mechanisms fail** -- locking systems unreliable
5. **Too heavy for toddlers** -- metal clubs designed for older kids
6. **Bags/carts are cheap** -- wheels break, storage flimsy
7. **Assembly frustration** -- overcomplicated for simple toys

### Hope Themes (for ad copy)
1. **"Golf family" identity** -- parents/grandparents desperate to share the game
2. **Indoor-safe play** -- plastic won't damage furniture
3. **Developmental benefits** -- hand-eye coordination, motor skills
4. **Bonding across generations** -- grandparents buying for grandkids
5. **Year-round engagement** -- kids play with them constantly
6. **"Introduction to golf"** -- low-cost trial before real clubs

### Villain Themes (for positioning)
1. **Cheap plastic = planned obsolescence** -- designed to break
2. **Misleading "adjustable" claims** -- mechanisms that don't work
3. **Missing what's advertised** -- fewer pieces than promised
4. **Sharp edges after breakage** -- safety concern
5. **No middle ground** -- either cheap toy or expensive real clubs
