## D. Language Parents NEVER Use (Anti-Voice Fuel)

### Words/Tone Parents Avoid:
- **"Proprietary technology"** -- No parent says this. They say "it works" or "it's the right weight."
- **"Engineered"** -- Parents say "designed for" or "made for" or "built for."
- **"Motor skills development"** / **"hand-eye coordination"** -- Used by editorial/expert content, never by actual parents in forums or reviews. Parents say "he can actually hit it" or "she's getting better."
- **"Cognitive development"** / **"executive function"** -- Academic language that never appears in parent voice. Parents say "he's learning" or "it teaches them patience."
- **"Optimized"** / **"maximized"** -- Engineering language absent from all parent data.
- **"Studies show"** / **"Research indicates"** -- Parents don't cite studies. They share stories.
- **"Investment in their future"** -- Parents don't frame toddler golf this way. They say "something we can do together."
- **"Performance"** -- In parent context, this word is about adult golf. For kids, they say "progress" or "getting better."
- **"Curriculum"** / **"Program"** -- Parents say "games" and "practice."
- **"Tournament-ready"** -- Used sarcastically or as a red flag. Parents want "having fun."
- **"System"** -- Parents don't buy "systems." They buy "clubs" or "a set."
- **"FOMO"** -- Parents feel it but never name it this way. They say "I don't want him to fall behind" or "I wish I started earlier."

### Tone Parents Avoid:
- **Urgency/scarcity** -- Zero countdown-timer energy in how parents talk about kids' golf.
- **Guilt** -- "Most parents don't know this" would trigger defensiveness. Parents are ALREADY worried about pushing too hard.
- **Techspeak** -- Specs are evaluated (weight, grip size, shaft material) but discussed in plain language.
- **Competitive framing** -- "Get ahead of other kids" is absent. "Something we do together" dominates.
- **Gender-specific** -- Moms and dads both post, about sons and daughters equally. No gendered voice.

## E. Feature Description Language (RAW MATERIAL FOR RENAMING)

This section maps how parents describe what they want in product features, providing the linguistic raw material for renaming LIL's engineering features (Focal Sphere, Stand-Up Walk-In, Face Forward, etc.).

### What Parents Say About WEIGHT:

> **"Light enough to swing"** -- The universal desire. Not "ultralight" or "featherweight." Just: can my kid actually swing this?

Key phrases:
- "light enough to swing freely" [EXP2-ST-003]
- "too heavy to swing properly" [EXP2-P-012]
- "if the club is too heavy they'll compensate with bad habits" [EXP2-P-014]
- "lightweight and kid-friendly" [EXP2-P-069]
- "as lightweight as a club can get" [R3-RC-049]

**Parent language for weight: "light enough to swing" / "not too heavy" / "they can actually swing it"**

### What Parents Say About SIZE / FIT:

> **"Right size for my kid"** -- Not "ergonomically optimized." Just: does it fit my kid?

Key phrases:
- "right size" / "right height" [multiple]
- "properly sized" [EXP2-P-044]
- "fit for their age" / "sized for a toddler" [multiple]
- "the grip is too big for small hands" [R3-RC-053]
- "junior grips allow the kid to be able to wrap their hands around the club easily" [R3-RC-053]

**Parent language for size: "fits their hands" / "right size for a [age]" / "small enough for little hands"**

### What Parents Say About MAKING CONTACT WITH THE BALL:

> **"Actually hit the ball"** -- The single most common phrasing for the desired outcome.

Key phrases:
- "can't hit real balls" [R-001]
- "he can actually hit it" [multiple]
- "making contact with the ball was too hard" [R3-P-016]
- "the balls were traveling only a few feet" [EXP2-P-004]
- "a few shots up in the air" [EXP2-H-069]
- "the feeling of hitting a ball with a stick -- and making the ball go in the air -- is an addictive one" [R3-RC-051]

**Parent language for contact: "actually hit the ball" / "make contact" / "get it in the air" / "she can hit it"**

### What Parents Say About DURABILITY:

> **"Survive a toddler"** -- The durability standard is not "engineered for impact." It's: will a toddler destroy this?

Key phrases:
- "broke pretty easily" [EXP2-P-017]
- "fell apart" [multiple]
- "withstand the pounding they will inevitably take" [EXP2-P-061]
- "can withstand the full extent of a toddler's fury" [R3-RC-048]
- "constantly break" [EXP2-P-029]

**Parent language for durability: "won't break" / "can take a beating" / "survives a toddler" / "lasts"**

### What Parents Say About DESIGN / LOOKS:

Key phrases:
- "real" (vs. toy) [massive recurring theme]
- "cute" [occasional, primarily from moms]
- "looks like real clubs" [multiple]
- "quality" [the visual/tactile proxy for "this isn't junk"]

**Parent language for design: "looks like a real club" / "not a toy" / "actual clubs"**
