## SECTION 4: HYPOTHESES TO VALIDATE

```yaml
hypotheses:
  - statement: "Parents' primary emotional driver is the desire to share something they love with their child — not skill development"
    rationale: >-
      The concept doc frames this as 'giving back to golf through kids' and the
      consumer insight centers on parents wanting to share their love of the game.
      We believe the emotional hook is legacy/bonding, not athletic training.

  - statement: "The biggest purchase barrier is not price — it's the fear of forcing something on their kid and creating a negative association with golf"
    rationale: >-
      The concept doc's internal dialogue includes 'I don't want to be the pushy
      sports parent.' Parents may hesitate not because of cost but because they
      worry about killing the joy before it starts.

  - statement: "Visual/tactile product design (the sphere, the colors) will be a stronger conversion driver than feature specs or engineering claims"
    rationale: >-
      This is fundamentally a visual product — the renders are striking and the
      sphere concept is immediately intuitive. Parents will 'get it' from seeing
      it, not from reading about it. The product sells itself visually.

  - statement: "Grant Horvat's authentic personal connection to this product will outperform traditional influencer endorsement patterns"
    rationale: >-
      Grant's father was a pro golfer who shaped his love of the game. This isn't
      a paid endorsement — it's autobiographical. His audience will sense the
      difference. The 'give your kids the greatest gift' narrative is HIS story.

  - statement: "The 'not a toy, not a scaled-down adult club' positioning creates a blue ocean category that commands premium pricing"
    rationale: >-
      The competitive landscape is polarized between $20-40 plastic toys and
      $130-300 junior sets that are essentially miniaturized adult equipment.
      A 'developmental golf system' category doesn't exist yet. The Skills Mat
      ecosystem reinforces this — it's a station, not just clubs.
```

## STRATEGIC NOTES

Research should capture the LANGUAGE parents use when describing what they want — this feeds directly into naming during Core Message (Skills 03-09).

4. **Pipeline routing: Research → Core Message → E-Comm + Organic.** No VSL. No long-form sales copy. This goes from research and positioning directly into e-commerce product pages and organic social content. The campaign architecture is fundamentally different from standard PG flows.

5. **The product renders are a major asset.** The visual design is striking — PG orange with hi-vis yellow sphere. The product practically sells itself visually. The e-comm and organic phases should be visually led, not copy-led. Research should note any patterns around visual-first purchase decisions for children's products.
