## Market Skepticism Assessment

| Skepticism Vector | Level | Handling |
|-------------------|-------|----------|
| "Can a toddler really learn golf?" | LOW — parents already see toddlers imitate | Leverage existing belief, don't argue for it |
| "Does equipment matter at this age?" | MEDIUM — many think "any club will do" | This is where mechanism does its work — explain WHY design matters |
| "Is this just overpriced toy clubs?" | MEDIUM — price skepticism for toddler gear | Piaget science + "match yellow to white" demo dissolves this |
| "Can I teach without being a coach?" | LOW — parents WANT this to be true | Belief-compatible — mechanism fulfills existing hope |

**Overall skepticism: MODERATE-LOW** — This is a hope market. Prospects want the mechanism to work. Our job is to show them HOW it works, not convince them it's necessary.

## Dimensionality Budget

Based on SCIENCE_CONCEPTUAL emphasis at Stage 2→3:

### Primary Dimensions (optimize first — these carry the mechanism)
1. **Proof (D3)** — The mechanism must be DEMONSTRABLE. MEC-13 knockout ("match yellow to white") is the anchor.
2. **Image Strength (D1)** — Prospect must PICTURE the mechanism working. Shape sorter analogy drives this.
3. **Simplicity (D2)** — 12-year-old test. The learning principle must be immediately graspable.
4. **Thesis Cohesion (D10)** — Mechanism must be the natural answer to the root cause.

### Secondary Dimensions (score well, but don't over-optimize)
5. **Belief Compatibility (D9)** — Parents already believe toddlers learn through play. Lean into this.
6. **Differentiation (D6)** — Zero competitors have this approach. Easy to score high.
7. **Delivery Tangibility (D13)** — Physical product advantage. Show it working.

### Tertiary Dimensions (let them emerge naturally)
8. **Ease of Use (D5)** — Inherent in the mechanism (equipment teaches, parent plays)
9. **Super Power (D11)** — "Golf buddy, not golf coach" — the new capability
10. **Embedded Benefits (D7)** — Benefit in the mechanism name
11. **Virality (D4)** — "Cocktail party" / "at the range" repeatability

### Adapted Dimensions (hope market treatment)
12. **Doomsday (D8)** — NOT fear-based. Adapted: "missing the window" — developmental window (ages 2-5) closes. Gentle urgency, not doom.
13. **Visceral Response (D12)** — Joy/excitement, not discomfort/relief. Parent's eyes light up imagining their toddler "getting it."
