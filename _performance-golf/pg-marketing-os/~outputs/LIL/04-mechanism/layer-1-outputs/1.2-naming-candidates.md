## Naming Principles Applied

1. **Name the PRINCIPLE** — not the product, not the components
2. **Sound real, not manufactured** — like a developmental scientist would call it, not a product manager (per Soul.md + E5)
3. **Embedded benefits** — benefit implied or baked into the name
4. **Memorable** — quotable at the range, repeatable parent-to-parent
5. **Mental image** — the name creates a picture
6. **12-year-old test** — understandable, not jargon
7. **Golf-anchored** — must tie to golf (user directive)

## Anti-Patterns Avoided
- ❌ "The System" / "The Method" / "The Technique" — too generic
- ❌ Hyperbolic benefit names — feels commercial
- ❌ Engineering names — Soul.md anti-voice
- ❌ >4 words — loses memorability
- ❌ Names requiring explanation — should be self-evident or intriguing

## Top 5 Anchored Names (Proceed to Layer 2)

Ranked by composite score, with strategic fit assessment:

### 1. See & Swing — Composite: 7.9
**Why it leads:** Visual learning + golf action in two words. Parents immediately understand: the child SEES the cue, then SWINGS. Rhythmic, memorable, passes all 5 naming tests. Grant would say "the See & Swing system" naturally. Action pair matches the strongest TIER1 naming pattern for golf (Simple Strike, First Move Fix). Captures both the shapes/visual element AND the golf connection.

### 2. Just Play — Composite: 8.0
**Why it's #2 despite highest score:** Perfect audience language and Soul.md fit. Directly from root cause copy: "You don't coach. You just play." But it functions more as a tagline than a mechanism name per E5 framework. "Just Play" describes the EXPERIENCE, not the HOW. If repositioned as the mechanism name (the Just Play system), it works — but it needs the explanation architecture to carry the "how" that the name doesn't.

### 3. Match Play — Composite: 7.6
**Why it's strong:** Transsubstantiated golf term — every golf parent knows "match play" but here it means "match the colors, play the game." Dual meaning is sticky and clever. Very Grant. The concern is ownability (match play is universal golf language) but the reframing is powerful. If this can be positioned distinctly from the golf format meaning, it's the most golf-native option.

### 4. First Swing — Composite: 7.7
**Why it's here:** Captures the parental dream moment. Emotional, golf-native, milestone-driven. But like Just Play, it names the RESULT (the first swing) not the MECHANISM (how the equipment teaches). Strongest as a campaign/brand name that sits alongside the mechanism name.

### 5. Grip & Go — Composite: 7.5
**Why it's here:** Most energetic. Very Grant — "grip it and go, man." Captures the ease-of-use dimension perfectly. Strong golf tie ("grip" is fundamental). But it foregrounds setup ease over the visual/shape learning principle. Best if the "shapes teach" story is carried by the explanation architecture rather than the name.

### Runner-Up: Shape Play — Composite: 7.4
**Why it's close:** Cleanest embodiment of the approved concept (shapes + play = self-learning golf). Directly maps to shape sorter principle. Ownable. But slightly plainer than the top 5 — less rhythmic, less emotional. Could benefit from pairing with a tagline.
