## Scorecard Dimensions (13 Total — 12 Original + Delivery Tangibility)

| # | Dimension | Question | Optimization Principle |
|---|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Image Strength** | Can the prospect PICTURE this mechanism working? | Use strong visual metaphors. Name should evoke a mental image. Personification strengthens images. |
| 2 | **Simplicity** | Can a 12-year-old understand the core concept? | Obvious in hindsight. Explainable in 15 seconds. Minimal logical connections. |
| 3 | **Proof** | Is there believable evidence this is real? | Demonstrative proof > institutional proof > testimonial. Prove the CORE conclusion, not every piece. |
| 4 | **Virality** | Would someone share this at a cocktail party? | One impossible-to-forget concept. Easy to retell without reference. Taps cultural zeitgeist. |
| 5 | **Ease of Use** | Does the product seem EASY to use through this mechanism? | Hard work elimination is INHERENT, not just claimed. Speed relative to alternatives. |
| 6 | **Differentiation** | Is this clearly DIFFERENT from everything else? | Explains why past solutions failed. Alleviates blame. Creates "aha" about real reason for pain. |
| 7 | **Embedded Benefits** | Are BENEFITS baked into the mechanism name? | Benefit in name ("firming flavonoids"). Mechanism solves primary problem through secondary benefit. |
| 8 | **Doomsday** | What TERRIBLE thing happens if they DON'T act? | Amplify existing fears. Future doom countdown. Problem is scarier than they assumed. |
| 9 | **Belief Compatibility** | Does this FIT what they already believe? | Reaffirm their "known" problem. Leverage hope. Reinforce worldview. Easier if they WANT it to be true. |
| 10 | **Thesis Cohesion** | Does this SUPPORT the central thesis / Big Idea? | Binary, black-and-white argument. 4 words or less. Mechanism is perfect payoff to thesis. |
| 11 | **Super Power** | Does this give the prospect a NEW capability? | Tangible super power speaking to hopes and dreams. Most effective in solution-focused/hope markets. |
| 12 | **Visceral Response** | Does this trigger a GUT-LEVEL emotional response? | Primal discomfort from problem. Primal relief from solution. Eyes light up on first read. |
| 13 | **Delivery Tangibility** | Can they VISUALIZE using the product? | Can they see themselves with it? Physical product advantage — show it in action. |

## E5 Framework (from E5AA Unique Mechanism Teaching)

### Three Types of Unique Mechanisms

| Type | Definition | LIL Assessment |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **Actual Mechanism** | A genuine, unique piece/component/system within the product that delivers the result | ✅ PRIMARY — LIL has an ACTUAL mechanism: visual-tactile learning through equipment design. 4 real components (Focal Sphere, Walk-In, Skills Mat, Face Forward) that no competitor has. |
| **Unspoken Mechanism** | A mechanism competitors also have but don't talk about | ❌ Not applicable — competitors don't have this approach |
| **Transsubstantiated Mechanism** | Taking something ordinary and reframing it as extraordinary | ⚠️ PARTIAL — "learning through play" is ordinary, but "equipment that teaches through its design" is the transsubstantiation |

### Key E5 Principles for LIL

1. **Mechanism ≠ Product** — The mechanism is CONTAINED WITHIN the product. For LIL: the mechanism is the visual-tactile learning PRINCIPLE. The product is the 3-club set + Skills Mat. The mechanism is WHY the product works.

2. **Name the principle, not the product** — Glucerna vs. CarbSteady. P90X vs. Muscle Confusion. LIL (product) vs. [mechanism name TBD].

3. **Name should sound official/scientific, not hyperbolic** — Per E5: "you're better off having it sound more official, more scientific, more credible, more legit." BUT Soul.md anti-voice prohibits clinical/corporate tone. The name should sound REAL, not manufactured — like something a developmental scientist would call it, not something a product manager would trademark.

4. **Educate on the mechanism, not the product** — "By educating on the unique mechanism and showing prospects what makes it great, you lead them to want the mechanism. The only place they can get it is from you." For LIL: educate parents on WHY visual-tactile learning works for toddlers. The only product that uses this principle = Lil' Legends.

## 3-Part Emphasis Strategy (from "Steal Our Winners")

### The Three Parts of Every Mechanism

| Part | What It Covers | LIL Content |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Root Cause Element** | "The Real Problem" — why nothing else worked | "Every kids' club was designed for coaching. Toddlers can't learn from words." |
| **Science/Conceptual Element** | "How Solution Works" — the mechanism itself | Visual-tactile learning through equipment design. Shape sorter principle applied to golf. |
| **Delivery/Tangible Element** | "Action That Delivers Benefit" — product in action | Focal Sphere, Walk-In Frame, Skills Mat, Face Forward — the 4 components in use |

### Emphasis Selection by Schwartz Stage

| Stage | Emphasis | Rationale |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Stage 3 | **Science/Conceptual** ← LIL | "How does it work?" Apple Keynote style — focus on the breakthrough and how it enables results |
| Stage 4 | Delivery/Tangible | Pure demonstration — product in action, what it does |
| Stage 5 | Root Cause | Identification — reframe the problem to create demand |

## Naming Rules

### Principles (from E5 + Scorecard)
1. Name the PRINCIPLE, not the product or its components
2. Sound official/credible, not hyperbolic (but NOT clinical per Soul.md)
3. Benefit should be embedded or implied in the name
4. Must be memorable — quotable at a "cocktail party" (or in LIL's case, at the range)
5. Must create a mental image
6. Must pass the "12-year-old test" — understandable, not jargon

### Anti-Patterns (from teachings + anti-degradation)
- ❌ "The System" / "The Method" / "The Technique" — too generic
- ❌ Hyperbolic benefit names ("Explosive Results System") — feels commercial
- ❌ Engineering names ("Visually Differentiated Focal Sphere") — Soul.md anti-voice
- ❌ Overlong names (>4 words) — loses memorability
- ❌ Names requiring explanation — should be self-evident or intriguing

### Naming Tests
1. **Cocktail Party Test:** Would a parent repeat this name to another parent?
2. **Google Slap Test:** Can they only get this from LIL? (vs. generic term)
3. **Image Test:** Does the name create a mental picture?
4. **12-Year-Old Test:** Would a teenager understand what it means?
5. **Soul.md Test:** Does it match Grant's voice — conversational, not corporate?
