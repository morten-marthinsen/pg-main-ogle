## Final Sequence

### Position 1: CPB-001 — THE DREAM AND THE GAP
**Page section:** Hero + Problem/Empathy
**Emotional beat:** Relief
**Why this position:** Every great argument begins with the dream and the obstacle. Grant's personal story ("My dad didn't just give me a golf lesson. He gave me the game.") creates the emotional entry. The empathy beat ("You've tried the plastic clubs. You've crouched down and said 'hold it like this, buddy.' Three swings later — chasing the dog.") builds relatability. The root cause reframe ("It's not your kid. It's not you. The equipment was speaking the wrong language.") provides relief and sets up the mechanism.

**Dependencies satisfied:** None required — this is the opening chunk.
**What it establishes for later chunks:** The root cause (equipment designed for coaching, not discovery), the emotional stakes (sharing golf with your kid), and the prospect's current state (frustrated, has tried 2-4 sets, thinks maybe the kid is too young).

### Position 2: CPB-002 — HOW SEE & SWING WORKS
**Page section:** Mechanism Education
**Emotional beat:** Wonder
**Why this position:** Once the root cause is established (equipment speaks the wrong language), the prospect's natural question is "so what speaks the RIGHT language?" This chunk answers that directly with the shape sorter analogy and the See & Swing System explanation. The mechanism must be understood BEFORE the knockout proof can land — you need to know what "match the yellow ball to the white ball" means before seven words can feel like a revelation.

**Dependencies satisfied:** CPB-001 (root cause established — verbal coaching fails toddlers)
**What it establishes for later chunks:** The See & Swing principle (visual-tactile learning), the shape sorter analogy, the four component teaching methods. All subsequent chunks reference this mechanism understanding.

### Position 3: CPB-003 — SEVEN WORDS — THE SCIENCE BEHIND THE SIMPLICITY
**Page section:** Knockout Proof + Scientific Authority
**Emotional beat:** Confidence
**Why this position:** The mechanism has been explained (CPB-002). Now the knockout proof DEMONSTRATES it in one sentence: "Match the yellow ball to the white ball." Seven words. The prospect FEELS the simplicity after understanding the mechanism. The science layer (Piaget, AAP) then EARNS the FSSIT claim: "The most fun way for a toddler to learn golf IS the most effective way." Science precedes the claim — this is the v3 structure locked in Big Idea.

**Dependencies satisfied:** CPB-002 (mechanism understood — knockout proof now has context)
**What it establishes for later chunks:** Scientific credibility, the "seven words" reference point, and the ease claim. All subsequent chunks can reference "seven words" as shorthand.

### Position 4: CPB-004 — WHY NOTHING ELSE WORKED
**Page section:** Competitive Differentiation
**Emotional beat:** Resolution
**Why this position:** After the mechanism is understood (CPB-002) and proven (CPB-003), the prospect's natural question shifts to "why hasn't anyone done this before?" This chunk answers that: zero competitors have visual-tactile teaching mechanisms. 16 brands profiled, none teach through design. This resolves the "too good to be true" skepticism by explaining the competitive gap — it is not that the idea was obvious and everyone missed it, it is that the entire market was built on a wrong assumption (verbal coaching).

**Dependencies satisfied:** CPB-002 (mechanism understood), CPB-003 (proof established)
**What it establishes for later chunks:** The "only" claim is now credible. The "new category" (developmental golf system) is established. Value comparison with competitors is now possible.

### Position 5: CPB-006 — THE COMPLETE SYSTEM
**Page section:** Product Presentation
**Emotional beat:** Pride
**Why this position:** The mechanism is understood, proven, and differentiated. Now the prospect needs to SEE the actual product — what they are buying. This is where the See & Swing principle becomes tangible: three clubs, Skills Mat, carry bag. Each component demonstrated through D-F-W-B-P format. The prospect can now PICTURE their kid using this system. Product before value — you must show what $149 buys before asking whether $149 is worth it.

**Dependencies satisfied:** CPB-002 (mechanism understood), CPB-003 (simplicity proven), CPB-004 (differentiation established)
**What it establishes for later chunks:** The tangible product system. The prospect now knows exactly what they would receive. Value assessment is now possible.

### Position 6: CPB-005 — THIS WORKS FOR YOUR KID
**Page section:** Personalization + Value + Guarantee
**Emotional beat:** Security
**Why this position:** The prospect has seen the product (CPB-006). Their remaining questions are personal: "Will this work for MY kid?" and "Is $149 worth it?" This chunk resolves both with universality proof (Piaget applies across ages 2-5), value math (less than cycling through 2-4 broken sets), and the 365-day guarantee (zero risk). Placed AFTER the product because the guarantee is most powerful when the prospect already wants the product — it removes the last barrier.

**Dependencies satisfied:** CPB-003 (science credibility), CPB-004 (differentiation), CPB-006 (product seen)
**What it establishes for later chunks:** All belief barriers resolved. Risk eliminated. Prospect is ready for the access/offer chunk.

### Position 7: CPB-007 — GET THE SET — GOLF BUDDY CLOSE
**Page section:** Offer + CTA + Identity Close
**Emotional beat:** Identity Transformation
**Why this position:** ALWAYS LAST (per E5 framework — access chunk is final). All belief barriers are resolved. The prospect understands the mechanism, trusts the science, sees the product, knows it works for their kid, and faces zero risk. Now: the offer. $149. Everything included. Three CTAs with escalating emotional resonance. The identity close lands the thesis: "Golf buddy. Not golf coach. That's what my dad was for me. That's what you get to be."

**Dependencies satisfied:** ALL previous chunks (CPB-001 through CPB-006)
**What it establishes:** Purchase decision. Identity transformation. "A lifetime of golf together starts with seven words."
