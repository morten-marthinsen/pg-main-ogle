## SIN Segue Context

The SIN (Sell It Now) segue bridges from the last content chunk before the offer (CPB-005: This Works for Your Kid) into the access/offer chunk (CPB-007: Get the Set — Golf Buddy Close). This is where 75% education ends and 25% selling begins.

**Last content chunk (CPB-005):** Resolves personalization ("works for your kid"), value ($149 vs. 2-4 broken sets), and risk (365-day guarantee). Emotional state: Security. The prospect believes the mechanism works, knows it works for their age range, and faces zero financial risk.

**Access chunk (CPB-007):** The complete offer — $149, everything included, identity close ("Golf buddy. Not golf coach.").

**The SIN segue must:**
1. Call back to the mechanism (See & Swing System)
2. Echo the primary result/promise (give your kid golf fundamentals through play)
3. Bridge naturally to the product as the access vehicle
4. Shift from education to opportunity without feeling like a pitch

**Adaptation note:** This is a branded e-comm product page, not a DR sales letter. The SIN segue should feel like the natural culmination of Grant's conversation — "and here's how you get this" — not a hard pivot to selling. The education-to-selling shift must be nearly invisible because the entire page is already a product experience.

## Placement Note: SIN Segue Position in E-Comm Context

In a traditional DR sales letter, the SIN segue sits between the last argument chunk and the offer section. On a branded e-comm product page, the SIN segue functions slightly differently:

1. **CPB-005 (Trust/Value)** resolves the last belief barriers
2. **SIN segue** summarizes and transitions
3. **CPB-007 (Offer/Close)** presents the complete offer with identity close

Because this is an e-comm page (not a linear letter), the SIN segue may appear as a transitional text block between the trust section and the offer section, or it may be incorporated into the opening of the offer section itself. The copy execution skill (EC-02/EC-03) will determine exact placement on the page.
