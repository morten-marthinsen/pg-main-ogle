### Teaching 1: Engineering Your Campaign Argument (E5AA M5A)
**Core principles extracted:**
- The campaign argument is NOT a sales pitch — it's an education-based case that proves the campaign thesis
- 75% education / 25% selling transition
- The argument proves the THESIS, not the product
- Think like a prosecutor presenting evidence to a jury, not a salesperson pitching
- Every piece of the argument must serve the thesis — anything that doesn't serve it gets cut

**Application to LIL:**
- Campaign thesis must connect root cause (equipment designed for coaching, not discovery) + mechanism (See & Swing System) + promise (equipment teaches, you just play)
- 75% of the e-comm page should educate about WHY this works and HOW it's different
- 25% transitions to the offer (price, guarantee, CTA)
- Prosecutorial stance: "Here's the science, here's the mechanism, here's the proof — the case is clear"

### Teaching 2: Structure of the Campaign Argument (E5AA M5C)
**Core principles extracted:**
- Campaign argument = sequence of CPB (Claim-Proof-Benefit) chunks
- Each chunk answers one prospect gap question
- Claims must be factual, specific, defensible — NEVER opinion
- Proof must be mapped from inventory — NEVER fabricated
- Benefits must be dimensionalized: functional → dimensionalized → emotional
- Chunks must flow logically with planned segues/transitions

**Application to LIL:**
- Each e-comm section corresponds to one or more CPB chunks
- Claims are grounded in mechanism specs + developmental science + competitive data
- Proof is mapped from 68-element inventory (MEC-13 knockout, AUT-11 Piaget, DAT-19 competitive gap)
- Benefits dimensionalized for parents: functional (teaches alignment) → dimensionalized (no coaching needed, anywhere practice) → emotional (golf buddy, sharing the game)

### Teaching 3: Determining and Ordering CPB Chunks (E5AA M5D)
**Core principles extracted:**
- Start with the 9 core gap questions — which ones does YOUR thesis trigger?
- Critical gaps get dedicated chunks; supporting gaps can share chunks
- "Access" (How can I get started?) is ALWAYS the final chunk
- Order chunks to build belief progressively — each chunk should make the next more believable
- Dependency mapping: mechanism explanation must precede mechanism proof

**Application to LIL:**
- Primary gaps for LIL: mechanism (how does See & Swing work?), results (what will my kid do?), differentiation (how is this different from other kids' clubs?), ease (is it really that simple?), safety/trust (is this backed by real science?), personalization (will this work for MY kid's age?), access (how do I get it?)
- Access chunk = offer presentation (price, guarantee, CTA) — always last
- Belief progression: story → science → mechanism → proof → product → offer

### Teaching 4: Filling Your CPB Chunks (E5AA M5E)
**Core principles extracted:**
- Claims: Use the interrogation method (direct answer → why? → how? → says who?)
- Proof: Map from inventory — never fabricate. Include proof_type and proof_id
- Benefits: Three layers — functional (what it does), dimensionalized (what that means in practice), emotional (how it feels)
- Every chunk must have all three elements (C + P + B) — incomplete chunks fail the gate

**Application to LIL:**
- Claims grounded in mechanism specs, developmental science, and competitive data
- Proof mapped from 68-element inventory with proof_ids
- Benefits layered: functional (teaches alignment through colors) → dimensionalized (practice anywhere, no range needed, no verbal coaching) → emotional (pride, bonding, "golf buddy" identity)
