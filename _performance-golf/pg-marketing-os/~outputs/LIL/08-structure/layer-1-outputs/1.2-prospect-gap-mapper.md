## Skeptical Academic Filter

Reading this thesis as a skeptical, tired parent who has already tried 2-4 sets of kids' clubs and been disappointed:

> "Yeah, sure. Every kids' club brand says they're different. I've heard 'lightweight' and 'designed for kids' before. My kid lost interest in ten minutes. What makes THIS so special? How does 'equipment teaching' even work — that sounds like marketing talk. Is there real science behind this or just buzzwords? Will this work for MY kid (she's only 2 and barely coordinates)? $149 is real money for something a toddler might destroy or ignore. And what's the catch with that 365-day guarantee? Also — I'm not a golfer myself, can I still make this work?"
