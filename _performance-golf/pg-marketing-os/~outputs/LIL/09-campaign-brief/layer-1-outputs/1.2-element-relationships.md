## Core Relationship Pairs

### Pair 1: Root Cause → Mechanism
**Strength: 10/10 — EXCEPTIONAL (direct solve)**

The root cause states that every toddler golf product was designed for verbal coaching, but toddlers cannot convert words into movement. The See & Swing System directly addresses this by replacing verbal instruction with visual-tactile design cues. This is a textbook "direct solve" — the mechanism IS the answer to the root cause. The shape sorter analogy bridges the gap with zero cognitive load: "Nobody teaches a toddler a shape sorter. The design teaches. Same thing with golf."

**Connection type:** Direct solve
**Friction points:** None — the mechanism was literally designed to solve this specific root cause.

### Pair 6: Villain → Root Cause
**Strength: 8/10 — STRONG (adapted for hope market — obstacle, not villain)**

LIL operates in a hope market with zero-enemy architecture. There is no named villain — the "enemy" is an incorrect assumption embedded in every existing product: equipment designed for coaching, not discovery. This is effective because it externalizes blame without attacking anyone. The root cause ("toddlers can't learn from words") is caused by the obstacle ("every product assumed verbal coaching"). The two-point deduction: traditional DR campaigns gain significant energy from a named villain. LIL deliberately sacrifices this energy for brand alignment and hope market positioning. This is the correct strategic choice but it means the argument has less "punch" than a villain-driven campaign.

**Connection type:** Obstacle framing (not villain framing)
**Friction points:** No named villain means less emotional charge in the problem section. Mitigated by empathy and relief framing.

The LIL campaign exhibits exceptional upstream coherence. The Big Idea wraps this chain in Grant's personal story and scientific validation, creating both emotional resonance and intellectual credibility.

1. **Grant's father story verification** — speculative element needs confirmation before copy lock

Neither threatens the strategic coherence of the campaign. Both have clear resolution paths and timelines.
