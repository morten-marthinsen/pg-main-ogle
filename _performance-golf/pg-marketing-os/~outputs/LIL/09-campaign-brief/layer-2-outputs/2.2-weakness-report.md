### Creative Weaknesses (Optimization Opportunities)

#### CW-1: Secondary Audience Entry Points
**Dimensions affected:** D5 (Big Idea → Audience Awareness)
**Severity:** LOW — optimization, not deficiency
**Description:** The Big Idea is optimized for the primary avatar (golf dad, 28-40, Grant's YouTube audience). Secondary audiences (golf grandparents, non-golf parents, golf moms) may need different entry points. The grandparent enters through legacy and gift-giving. The non-golf parent enters through developmental benefits and screen-time alternative. The golf mom enters through shared activity and growing the game.
**Resolution:** Develop 2-3 adapted hooks per secondary audience in the organic content strategy. The core mechanism and promise remain the same — only the emotional entry point changes.
**Impact if unresolved:** Campaign still works for 70%+ of the buyer base (primary avatar). Secondary audiences may need additional touches before converting.

#### CW-2: E-Comm Section Fragmentation
**Dimensions affected:** D8 (Structure → Big Idea)
**Severity:** LOW — format constraint, not strategic issue
**Description:** The Big Idea's narrative arc is linear (personal story → science → mechanism → proof → identity). E-comm product pages are sectional (scrollable blocks with visual breaks). Some narrative momentum may be lost in translation from linear story to section-based layout. The connectors and sin segue mitigate this.
**Resolution:** EC-03 (Section Copy) execution must lean heavily on connectors and ensure each section's opening line bridges from the previous section's close. Consider a "read the full story" linear page option for engaged visitors.
**Impact if unresolved:** Minor — most e-comm visitors scan rather than read linearly. The section-based format is standard. The connectors maintain narrative thread for those who read in order.
