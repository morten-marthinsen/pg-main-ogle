You don't have to teach them. At all.

They learn the same way they learn a shape sorter.

Nobody sits a 2-year-old down and explains geometry. The toy
teaches through its design — circles go in circle holes, squares
go in square holes — and the kid figures it out by playing.

We took that exact principle and built golf equipment around it.

Yellow ball on the clubhead matches white ball on the ground.
That's alignment — no words needed.

Stance markers on the mat show where to stand.
That's setup — no words needed.

Club stands upright so they walk in and grab it.
That's the starting position — no words needed.

See the pattern? The equipment does ALL the teaching. Through
shapes. Through colors. Through things a toddler's brain is
wired to understand.

Every other kids' club on the market was built with one
assumption: someone would be there coaching. Telling the kid
"do this" and "don't do that." But a 3-year-old can't convert
those instructions into a golf swing. That's developmental
science, not opinion.

That's why your kid walked away after three swings. Not because
they didn't want to play. Because nobody designed equipment
that speaks their language.

Until now.

"Match the yellow ball to the white ball." That's the whole
lesson. Seven words a 2-year-old follows.

And your job? Your ONLY job?

Just play alongside them.

Golf buddy. Not golf coach. That's what this was always about.
