## Step 3: Check Scientific/Factual Validity

```
FACTUAL VALIDATION:
Claim: "A toddler's brain (ages 2-5) cannot convert verbal instruction
into physical movement. They learn through visual and tactile discovery."

1. Is this scientifically/factually accurate?
   └── YES — Piaget's preoperational stage (ages 2-7) is one of the
       most well-established frameworks in developmental psychology.
       Children in this stage learn through sensorimotor experience
       and symbolic play, not through processing verbal instructions
       into body movement. The claim is a defensible simplification
       of mainstream developmental science.

2. Is this a mainstream view or fringe theory?
   └── MAINSTREAM — Piaget's stages are taught in every developmental
       psychology course. CDC motor development milestones corroborate.
       18 independent sources verified (GAP-03 RESOLVED).

3. Could this claim be challenged by experts?
   └── LOW RISK — The core claim is uncontroversial. An expert might
       note that some 4-5 year olds CAN process simple verbal
       instructions, but the general principle that toddlers (esp.
       2-3) learn primarily through visual/tactile means is not
       disputed. The expression uses "ages 2-5" which includes the
       full LIL target range.

4. Are we oversimplifying for effect?
   └── ACCEPTABLE SIMPLIFICATION — The full developmental science is
       more nuanced (there's a spectrum, not a binary switch). But
       "toddlers learn from what they can see and touch, not from
       words" is a fair, accessible simplification that doesn't
       mislead. No parent will act harmfully based on this framing.
```
