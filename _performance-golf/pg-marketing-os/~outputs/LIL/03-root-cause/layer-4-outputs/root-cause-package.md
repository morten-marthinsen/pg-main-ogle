## 2. COPY BLOCKS

### 2A. Full Expression — Primary (Hybrid A Revised, ~260 words)

**Use for:** E-comm product page, long-form landing page, core message foundation

```
Every golf parent has the same picture in their head — out
on the course with their kid. Sharing the game you love with
the person you love most.

So you get the little clubs. You get out in the backyard.
You crouch down behind your 3-year-old and say "hold it
like this, buddy." Three swings later — chasing the dog.

And you think: wrong clubs. Wrong timing. Maybe next year.

But the problem was never your kid. And it wasn't you.

Every toddler golf club on the market — plastic, metal,
cheap, expensive — was designed around one assumption: that
someone would be standing there giving verbal instructions.

Designed for coaching. Not for discovery.

And that's the gap. A toddler's brain cannot convert "keep
your head down" into body movement. Not won't — can't. The
wiring isn't there yet. That's developmental science confirmed
seventy years ago.

But hand that same kid a shape sorter? Thirty seconds. Circle
goes in the circle hole. Nobody taught them. The toy taught
them — through its design.

That's what we built. A shape sorter for golf.

Yellow ball on the clubhead matches white ball on the ground
— that's alignment. Stance markers on the mat — that's setup.
Club stands upright so they walk in and grab it — that's the
starting position.

No coaching. No corrections. No words needed.

"Match the yellow ball to the white ball."

Seven words. That's the whole golf lesson.

The equipment teaches. Your kid discovers. You just play.

Not their swing coach. Their golf buddy.

And when your 3-year-old connects — when the ball goes up
and their face lights up — you'll know:

It started with seven words.
```

Here's the thing about getting your toddler into golf...

They learn the same way they learn a shape sorter.

go in square holes — and the kid figures it out by playing.

We took that exact principle and built golf equipment around it.

Yellow ball on the clubhead matches white ball on the ground.
That's alignment — no words needed.

Stance markers on the mat show where to stand.
That's setup — no words needed.

Club stands upright so they walk in and grab it.
That's the starting position — no words needed.

See the pattern? The equipment does ALL the teaching. Through
shapes. Through colors. Through things a toddler's brain is
wired to understand.

Every other kids' club on the market was built with one
assumption: someone would be there coaching. Telling the kid
"do this" and "don't do that." But a 3-year-old can't convert
those instructions into a golf swing. That's developmental
science, not opinion.

That's why your kid walked away after three swings. Not because
they didn't want to play. Because nobody designed equipment
that speaks their language.

Until now.

"Match the yellow ball to the white ball." That's the whole
lesson. Seven words a 2-year-old follows.

And your job? Your ONLY job?

Just play alongside them.

Golf buddy. Not golf coach. That's what this was always about.
