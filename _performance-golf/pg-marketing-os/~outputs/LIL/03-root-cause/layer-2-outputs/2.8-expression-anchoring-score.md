Playing golf with your kid is the dream.

Built from: P4 ("they quit because it feels impossible"), P3 ("walk away after frustrating swings"), P8 ("must be discovered")

```
Your kid didn't quit golf. They quit the experience.

When a toddler swings three times, misses every one, and walks
away — that's not a kid who doesn't like golf. That's a kid whose
equipment made golf feel impossible.

Here's what nobody tells you: a 3-year-old literally cannot process
"keep your head down" or "follow through." Their brain isn't wired

toy teaches through its design. That's exactly what golf equipment
should do.

Match the yellow ball to the white ball. That's the whole instruction.
The equipment does the teaching. The child does the discovering.

And you? You just play alongside them. That's the whole job.
```

You don't have to teach your kid golf.

stance or when to say "follow through." You don't have to worry
about building bad habits. You don't have to be a coach.

The equipment teaches through its design. Your kid learns by playing.
And you just stand next to them and enjoy it.

Every Sunday morning. Every backyard session. Every time they grab
the club and say "let's go."

That's the whole point. Giving them the game.
