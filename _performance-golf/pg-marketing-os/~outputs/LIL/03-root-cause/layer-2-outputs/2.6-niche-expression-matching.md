## Step 5: Niche-Calibrate the Language

| Dimension | Standard Golf | LIL Calibration |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Tone** | Conversational, direct | Conversational, warm, excited — "dad at the range" not "instructor at a seminar" |
| **Vocabulary** | Swing terms, golf lingo | MINIMAL golf lingo — must work for non-golfer parents too. "Hit the ball" not "make solid contact with proper face angle." Use parent language: "play together," "give them the game," "golf buddy" |
| **Intensity** | Medium | Medium-low — excitement, not urgency. "Wait till you see this" energy, not "you need to know this NOW" energy |
| **Pronoun** | "You" (direct address) | "We/you" mix — Grant includes himself as a parent, not above the audience. "Here's what we figured out" not "here's what you need to understand" |
| **Emotional target** | "Aha" recognition | "Aha" + hope + excitement. Standard golf reframe creates recognition ("oh, that's why I slice"). LIL reframe creates recognition + possibility ("oh, THAT'S why it hasn't clicked — and there's a way to fix it that's actually easier") |

### Language Calibration Examples

**Standard golf niche (SSTS):**
> "The biggest misconception in golf: the ball is NOT supposed to be at the bottom of your swing."

**LIL calibration (same insight pattern, hope-framed, parent register):**
> "Here's what we figured out: toddlers don't learn golf from words. They learn from what they can see and touch."

**Key differences:**
- SSTS leads with "misconception" (you're wrong) → LIL leads with "what we figured out" (we discovered something)
- SSTS uses "NOT" emphasis (correction energy) → LIL uses positive framing (discovery energy)
- SSTS addresses golfer as individual → LIL addresses parent as fellow parent
- SSTS goal: fix your understanding → LIL goal: open a new possibility
