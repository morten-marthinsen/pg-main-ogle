# 1.4 Emotional Mapper — Lil' Legends Big Idea

## Emotional Landscape for Big Idea

### Primary Emotion: HOPE
The dominant emotional register for this campaign. Parents buying into a dream — sharing golf with their child for a lifetime. Not escaping a problem. Not fixing a failure. Reaching toward something beautiful.

**Hope triggers in component pool:**
- "Nothing beats golfing with your family" (EM-3)
- "When your 3-year-old connects — when the ball goes up and their face lights up" (EM-2)
- "A lifetime of golf together" (PM-4)
- "Proud dad" (EM-5)

### Secondary Emotion: RELIEF
The "it's not your fault" moment. Parents who've tried and failed feel validated — the problem was the equipment, not the kid, not the parent. Relief opens the door for hope.

**Relief triggers:**
- "But the problem was never your kid. And it wasn't you." (RC-5)
- "You don't have to teach them. At all." (FSSIT-1)
- Explains why "three swings and they're done" happened — developmental science, not bad parenting

### Tertiary Emotion: PRIDE
The "golf buddy" identity. Pride in being the parent who gave their child the game. Connects to Grant's personal story — his father gave him golf, and that changed his life.

**Pride triggers:**
- "My dad gave me this game" (EM-1)
- "Golf buddy, not golf coach" (EM-4)
- "Proud dad" (EM-5)
- Grant's father as PGA Tour pro (AU-2)

### Emotional Sequence for Big Idea

The optimal emotional arc for the Big Idea is:

```
CURIOSITY → RELIEF → HOPE → PRIDE
```

1. **Curiosity:** "Seven words?" / "You don't have to teach them?" / "A shape sorter for golf?"
2. **Relief:** "It's not your fault — the equipment was designed wrong"
3. **Hope:** "Imagine your little legend matching colors, swinging on their own"
4. **Pride:** "Golf buddy. Not golf coach. My dad gave me this game."

### Forbidden Emotional Territory

Per Soul.md anti-voice and hope market rules:

| Emotion | Status | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Fear | FORBIDDEN | No "your kid will fall behind" |
| Guilt | FORBIDDEN | No "you're wasting their developmental window" |
| Shame | FORBIDDEN | No "most parents don't know this" |
| Urgency | FORBIDDEN | No "before it's too late" |
| Competitive anxiety | FORBIDDEN | No "other parents are already doing this" |

### Emotional Calibration for Big Idea Concepts

Each Big Idea concept must be tested against:
1. Does it open with curiosity or hope? (NOT fear or guilt)
2. Does it offer relief without implying failure?
3. Does it close on pride/identity? (NOT logistics or urgency)
4. Would Grant genuinely feel this emotion while saying it?
5. Would a parent hearing this feel seen, not sold to?
