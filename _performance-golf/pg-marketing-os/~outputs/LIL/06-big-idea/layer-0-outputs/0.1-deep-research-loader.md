## Market Context (from Skill 01 Research via Soul.md)

### Schwartz Stage
- **Stage:** 2->3 (Promise + Mechanism)
- **Implication:** Market needs mechanism differentiation. Promises alone insufficient. "New" still resonates but mechanism must be visible.

### Market Psychology
- **Dominant emotion:** Hope (not frustration — this is a hope market)
- **Hope level:** High — parents want to share golf with their kids
- **Skepticism level:** Moderate — serial purchase pattern (2-4 failed products) creates "is this different?" mindset
- **Guilt dimension:** Screen time guilt (use sparingly, never preach)

### Competitive Landscape
- **Saturated claims:** "Real clubs for real kids," "not a toy," durability claims, "lightweight"
- **Whitespace:** Self-teaching mechanism, design-based instruction, visual-tactile learning system
- **Key competitor:** Charlie Golf Co (0->13K families, quality positioning but no teaching system)

### Customer Language
- **Problem descriptions:** "broken a LOT of plastic clubs," "too heavy," "three swings and done," "walked away"
- **Success descriptions:** "loves it," "obsessed," "won't stop swinging," "asking to go back"
- **Dream language:** "nothing beats golfing with your family," "game for life," "proud dad"
- **The universal parent mantra:** "keep it fun" / "make it fun"
