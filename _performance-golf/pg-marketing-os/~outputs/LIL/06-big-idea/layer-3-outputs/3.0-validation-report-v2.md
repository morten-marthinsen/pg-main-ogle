## 3.7 SCHEMA DISTANCE CALCULATOR

### Three-Index Composite for Hybrid-A v2 Big Idea

**Big Idea:** "He Didn't Give Me a Golf Lesson. He Gave Me a Game."
**FSSIT Moment:** "The most fun way for your toddler to learn golf is also the most effective way. Fun IS the learning mechanism."

#### Index 1: Claim Novelty Index (CNI) = 7.5

**Reasoning:** The claim that fun and effective are the same thing for toddler golf has low precedent in the category. No competitor positions fun as a learning mechanism — they either sell "real clubs for kids" or "fun toys." The synthesis of fun + fundamentals through developmental science is novel. Slightly lower than v1 (8.0) because "fun way to learn" is a more familiar frame than "you were never supposed to teach."

- Competitor frequency: ~5% use "fun" in marketing, but none frame fun as THE mechanism
- Score: 7.5

#### Index 2: Belief Contradiction Index (BCI) = 6.5

**Reasoning:** The claim softly contradicts the belief that "fun and serious learning are different things." Parents assume fun = toy, serious = real learning. Saying fun IS the learning mechanism challenges that binary. But this is a gentler contradiction than v1's "you were never supposed to teach them" (which contradicted the belief that teaching = parenting).

- Core belief contradicted: "Fun and effective learning are different things"
- Contradiction strength: Moderate — most parents intuitively agree that play-based learning works
- Score: 6.5

#### Index 3: Linguistic Novelty Index (LNI) = 6.0

**Reasoning:** "He gave me a game, not a lesson" uses familiar words in a novel construction. "Fun IS the learning mechanism" creates linguistic novelty through the IS emphasis. Supporting phrases ("shape sorter for golf," "golf buddy, not golf coach") add novelty. No jargon.

- Score: 6.0

#### Information Gap Calibration (IGC) = 1.0

The headline creates an intermediate gap: parents want to know HOW he gave him a game (not a lesson) and what that means for their kid.

#### Composite Schema Distance Score

```
SDS = (CNI x 0.4) + (BCI x 0.35) + (LNI x 0.25) x IGC
SDS = (7.5 x 0.4) + (6.5 x 0.35) + (6.0 x 0.25) x 1.0
SDS = 3.0 + 2.275 + 1.5
SDS = 6.78
```

**Schema Distance Score: 6.78 (within optimal 4-8 range)**

**v1 vs v2 comparison:** v1 SDS was 7.15. v2 is 6.78 — slightly lower because the fun/fundamentals center is warmer and more accessible (lower belief contradiction), which is exactly what the human directed. The trade is intentional: less surprising, more inviting. Still well within optimal range.
