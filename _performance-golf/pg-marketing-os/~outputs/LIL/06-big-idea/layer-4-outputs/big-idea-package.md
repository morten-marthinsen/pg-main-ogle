## THE BIG IDEA

### Headline

**You Were Never Supposed to Teach Your Toddler Golf.**

### Big Idea Statement (Hybrid-A — Arena Winner, 8.50)

You were never supposed to teach your toddler golf.

Not because it doesn't matter — giving your kid the game might be the most meaningful thing you ever do. But because a toddler's brain can't learn movement from words. "Hold it like this, buddy" — that instruction goes in the ears and stops there. They can't turn words into a golf swing. Not yet. That's developmental science, confirmed for nearly eighty years.

My dad was a PGA Tour pro. He never coached me. He just played alongside me. Made it fun. Let me discover the game on my own terms. And for twenty years, I thought that was just his style. Turns out, he was the only one doing it the right way — because he was using the channel that actually works at that age. Shapes. Colors. Things I could see and touch.

Think about a shape sorter. Nobody teaches a toddler how to use one. The design does the teaching. Thirty seconds. No words. We took that same principle and built golf equipment around it.

"Match the yellow ball to the white ball." Seven words. That's the whole golf lesson.

Every kids' club on the market was designed for coaching — for someone standing there giving verbal instructions. The See & Swing System is the first one designed for discovery. The equipment teaches through shapes and colors. And your job — the best job — is just to play alongside them. Golf buddy. Not golf coach. That's what my dad was for me. That's what you get to be.

### Opening Hook (Halbert)

Here's what nobody tells you about teaching your toddler golf: you were never supposed to. Not because it doesn't matter. Because their brain — at this age — literally cannot turn your words into a golf swing. That's not a guess. That's eighty years of developmental science. And it changes everything.
