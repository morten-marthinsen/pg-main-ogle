# 2.1 Candidate Strategy Selector — Lil' Legends Big Idea

## Three Emphasis Strategies for Concept Generation

### Strategy A: RESONANCE-HEAVY (FSSIT-Dominant)

**Lead FSSIT:** FSSIT-1 — "You don't have to teach them. At all."
**Big Idea Type:** MECHANISM
**Focus:** Maximize emotional connection through the radical simplicity of the mechanism
**Schema violation:** Moderate (SD 5-6) — violates "teaching requires instruction" assumption
**NR potential:** 9 — explains why past coaching attempts failed

**Strategic rationale:** This strategy leads with the most powerful FSSIT candidate — the statement that directly violates every parent's assumption about teaching golf to toddlers. The mechanism (See & Swing System) is the ANSWER to why you don't have to teach them. The schema violation is natural and grounded — "seven words" is the proof that resolves the surprise instantly.

**Concept generation direction:**
- Concepts should center on the RELIEF of not coaching
- The mechanism explains WHY you don't have to teach
- The proof ("seven words") demonstrates it instantly
- The close lands on identity ("golf buddy, not golf coach")

---

### Strategy B: SURPRISE-HEAVY (Schema-Dominant)

**Lead FSSIT:** FSSIT-3 — "The same principle that teaches your kid a shape sorter can teach them golf."
**Big Idea Type:** MECHANISM + DISCOVERY
**Focus:** Maximize unexpectedness through category bridge (toy learning principle applied to sport)
**Schema violation:** Strong (SD 6-7) — toy design principle applied to sports equipment
**NR potential:** 7 — connects known learning success to new domain

**Strategic rationale:** This strategy leads with the most SURPRISING element — the category bridge between shape sorters and golf equipment. Parents have never heard anyone connect these two domains. The surprise creates the curiosity gap. The resolution (See & Swing System uses the same visual-tactile principle) closes the gap with an "of course!" moment.

**Concept generation direction:**
- Concepts should center on the BRIDGE between toy learning and golf learning
- The shape sorter is the anchor — familiar, trusted, proven
- Golf is the surprise domain — unexpected application
- The mechanism IS the bridge — See & Swing applies shape sorter principle to golf

---

### Strategy C: BALANCED (Equal Weight)

**Lead FSSIT:** FSSIT-5 — "What if your only job was to play alongside them?"
**Big Idea Type:** IDENTITY + MECHANISM
**Focus:** Optimize both resonance (identity transformation) and surprise (role inversion)
**Schema violation:** Moderate-strong (SD 5-6) — parent role inverted from teacher to companion
**NR potential:** 8 — reframes the parent's entire approach to youth golf

**Strategic rationale:** This strategy leads with the IDENTITY transformation — the parent becoming a golf buddy instead of a golf coach. The mechanism is the enabler (See & Swing teaches so you can play). The schema violation is the role inversion (parents expect to teach; this says you don't). Both resonance and surprise are strong but neither dominates.

**Concept generation direction:**
- Concepts should center on the IDENTITY shift for the parent
- The mechanism enables the shift (equipment teaches, you play)
- Grant's legacy story ("my dad gave me this game") provides emotional depth
- The close is aspirational — "golf buddy" is who you become

## Concept Generation Plan

Each strategy will produce 2+ concepts in plain language. Total target: 6+ concepts (minimum 5 per anti-degradation rules). Concepts will be presented WITHOUT headlines, leads, creative wrappers, or any creative packaging — plain language strategic synthesis only.

Each concept will include:
- Plain language statement (1-2 sentences)
- FSSIT anchor
- Root cause angle
- Mechanism angle
- Promise angle
- Schema violation
- NR potential
- Soul.md alignment
- Strategic rationale (2-3 sentences)
