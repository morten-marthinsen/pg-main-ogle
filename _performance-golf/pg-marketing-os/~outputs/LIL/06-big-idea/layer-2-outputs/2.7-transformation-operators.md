**Applied to:** The parent's role in toddler golf
**Base claim:** "Parents should teach their toddler golf fundamentals"

**Mechanism justification:** Verbal instruction creates cognitive overload in preoperational-stage children. Disengagement (walking away after 3 swings) is the brain's natural response to input it cannot process. Presence without instruction allows discovery-based learning.
**Evidence sources:** Piaget preoperational stage research; motor learning literature on instruction-overload in early childhood

**Predicted SDS impact:**

- **Rationale:** Parents believe more instruction = more learning. Inversion says instruction itself is the problem — not the quality of instruction, but the act of verbal teaching at this age.
