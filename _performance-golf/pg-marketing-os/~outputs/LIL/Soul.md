## Voice Register

Grant talking to a fellow golf-loving parent at the range while their kids mess around behind them. Not selling — sharing something he's genuinely fired up about. The energy of "dude, you have to see what we built" mixed with the tenderness of a guy who fell in love with golf because his dad put a club in his hands.

---

## Emotional Range

**Floor:** Quiet gratitude — "My dad gave me this game. That changed everything."
**Ceiling:** Infectious excitement — "Wait till you see your kid's face when they actually hit it."
**Default:** Warm, proud enthusiasm — a parent who found the thing they've been looking for and can't wait to tell you about it.

---

## Energy Signature

**Authentic joy + proud purpose** — This isn't a pitch, it's a mission. Grant lights up talking about this because it connects his past (dad teaching him golf) to his future (giving that same gift to the next generation). The energy is contagious without being performative. There's genuine weight behind the excitement — this matters to him.

---

## Anti-Voice (What This Should NEVER Sound Like)

1. **Never pushy-parent** — No "your kid NEEDS to start golf NOW or they'll fall behind." No developmental pressure, no FOMO parenting. This is about joy, not optimization. AVOID: "Studies show children who start sports before age 3 have a 47% higher chance of..."

2. **Never corporate-product** — No "engineered with proprietary technology" energy. No spec-sheet copy dressed up as enthusiasm. The product is brilliant but the voice should sound like a dad, not a product manager. AVOID: "The Visually Differentiated Focal Sphere utilizes hi-vis contrasting color to facilitate..."

3. **Never condescending about parenting** — No "most parents don't know this" or "you're probably making this mistake." Every parent is doing their best. We're offering something that makes it easier, not implying they were doing it wrong. AVOID: "Stop wasting money on plastic toys that teach your kid nothing."

4. **Never generic influencer** — No "link in bio" energy. No hollow enthusiasm. Grant's audience follows him because he's real. If the copy could come from any influencer selling any product, it's wrong. AVOID: "I'm SO excited to partner with Performance Golf to bring you guys..."

5. **Never hard-sell DR** — No countdown timers in the voice. No "But that's not all..." stacking. No artificial urgency. This is a branded initiative. The product and the mission create the pull. AVOID: "Order in the next 24 hours and we'll include a BONUS..."

## Pacing Signature

Social-native conversational flow. Short sentences that land like they're being spoken, not read. Mixes genuine emotion with practical clarity. Paragraphs breathe — 1-3 sentences max. Uses natural transitions ("Here's the thing..." / "And the best part..." / "Look...") rather than headers or section breaks. Reads like Grant talking to camera, not a copywriter writing for Grant.

---

## Taste Decisions

- Grant Horvat IS the voice — first person, his perspective, his story
- Branded initiative, not direct response — no urgency mechanics, no stacking, no countdown energy
- "Give your kids the game" is the emotional spine — everything connects back to this
- Parent-to-parent register — never talk down, never lecture, never guilt
- The product is visually self-evident — copy supports what the eye already understands, doesn't over-explain
- Feature names will be reworked — do NOT use engineering names (Focal Sphere, Face Forward, etc.) in final copy until new names are locked
- Both golfing AND non-golfing parents are in the audience — copy can't assume golf knowledge
- Screen time / outdoor play tension is a cultural lever — use it sparingly, never preachy
- May 27, 2026 launch — seasonal timing (summer, Father's Day proximity) is a natural asset
- PG brand ("Love Your Game") extends naturally to "Love Your Game... from the very beginning"
