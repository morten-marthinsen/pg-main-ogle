### Promise Archetypes for LIL (Derived from Patterns)

Based on upstream analysis, 5 promise archetypes emerge:

**Archetype A: The Golf Buddy Promise (Identity)**
"You don't have to teach them. You just play alongside them."
- Strongest emotional resonance per research (bonding > performance 2x)
- Connected to Grant's personal story (dad gave him the game)
- Risk: Could feel vague without mechanism anchor

**Archetype B: The Seven Words Promise (Ease/Speed)**
"Seven words replace every golf lesson a 2-year-old will ever need."
- Knockout proof (MEC-13) IS the promise demonstration
- Extremely specific — passes the specificity test
- Risk: Could feel like a gimmick without context

**Archetype C: The Equipment Teaches Promise (Capability)**
"The first golf set where the equipment teaches and the parent just plays."
- Directly connected to mechanism
- Differentiates from every competitor
- Risk: Could sound like a product claim rather than an emotional promise

**Archetype D: The No Coaching Promise (Relief)**
"Your kid learns golf without a single lesson, correction, or coaching session."
- Addresses the #1 frustration (teaching difficulty)
- Strong relief frame
- Risk: Could sound too good to be true — needs mechanism backing

**Archetype E: The Lifetime Together Promise (Emotional)**
"Give your kid the game. Share it together for decades."
- Connected to Grant's mission and father story
- Longest emotional arc — goes beyond the product
- Risk: Could feel generic without product/mechanism grounding

These archetypes will seed Layer 1 blue sky generation. Each will be explored across multiple promise types and emotional frames.
