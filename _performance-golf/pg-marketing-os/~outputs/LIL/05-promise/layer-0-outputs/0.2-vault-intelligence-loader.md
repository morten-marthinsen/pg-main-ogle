### E5 Promise Formula (from Source Teachings)

> "A big, bold, audacious, true and believable promise of specific result"

Each word matters:
- **Big, Bold, Audacious** — Exciting enough to get prospect's attention
- **True** — Actually achievable, not fabricated
- **Believable** — Market-calibrated for skepticism level
- **Specific** — Concrete, measurable, visual

### LIL-Specific Promise Considerations

**Hope market adaptations (from Soul.md anti-voice):**
- No fear-based promises ("your kid will fall behind")
- No guilt triggers ("most parents don't know this")
- No competitive performance promises ("your kid will beat their friends")
- Promise should feel like Grant sharing good news, not a pitch

**Proof ceiling constraints (from proof inventory handoff):**
- CAN promise: mechanism effectiveness, ease, developmental appropriateness, uniqueness
- CANNOT promise: specific results, timelines, risk-free, testimonial-backed outcomes
- STRONGEST proof: demonstrative (MEC-13 knockout) + authority (Piaget) + competitive (DAT-19 zero competitors)

**Unique challenge:**
- This is a PHYSICAL PRODUCT promise, not a training/supplement promise
- The promise is partially EXPERIENTIAL — "you'll experience this with your kid"
- The mechanism IS visible in the product — promise can be demonstrated, not just claimed
- The emotional promise (golf buddy dream) may be stronger than the functional promise

**Schwartz Stage 2→3 implication:**
- Promise alone won't sell (that's Stage 1-2)
- Promise + mechanism credibility will sell (that's Stage 3)
- Promise must be explicitly connected to See & Swing System
- The mechanism MAKES the promise believable — they're inseparable
