# 3.6 Mechanism Story Coherence — Lil' Legends

## Purpose

Verify that the primary promise fits coherently within the mechanism narrative arc. The mechanism tells a story — root cause → mechanism → transformation. The promise must fit logically without contradicting, skipping steps, or exceeding scope.

---

## Primary Promise — Coherence Analysis

> "Seven words: 'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.' The See & Swing System handles the rest — alignment, stance, setup — all through shapes and colors. No lesson. Just a little legend who won't stop swinging. I'm his golf buddy now."

### Check 1: Logical Sequence (Score: 10/10)

**Does the promise flow logically from the mechanism?**

YES — perfectly:

1. "Match the yellow ball to the white ball" → This IS the mechanism in action (MEC-01, MEC-13). The promise doesn't describe the mechanism abstractly — it demonstrates it.
2. "The See & Swing System handles the rest" → Names the mechanism. Establishes causation: system teaches, parent doesn't.
3. "Alignment, stance, setup" → Names the specific outcomes the mechanism delivers. These are the exact three fundamentals from the mechanism package.
4. "Through shapes and colors" → Names the mechanism's operating principle (visual-tactile learning).
5. "No lesson" → Logical consequence of equipment-as-teacher.
6. "Won't stop swinging" → Emotional description of engagement (not a mechanism claim).
7. "Golf buddy now" → The transformation — parent freed from coach role.

**Sequence integrity:** Root cause insight (toddlers learn visually) → mechanism action (color matching) → mechanism outcomes (alignment, stance, setup) → transformation (golf buddy). No steps skipped. No leaps. Perfect narrative flow.

### Check 2: Contradictions (Score: 10/10)

**Does the promise contradict any mechanism claims?**

NO — zero contradictions:

| Promise Element | Mechanism Claim | Alignment |
|---|---|---|
| "Seven words... that's the whole lesson" | MEC-13: Mechanism can be communicated in 7 words | ALIGNED — promise mirrors mechanism proof |
| "The See & Swing System handles the rest" | System is self-teaching through design | ALIGNED — system does the work |
| "No lesson" | Equipment teaches, parent plays | ALIGNED — "no lesson" means parent doesn't need to instruct |
| "Won't stop swinging" | Not a mechanism claim | N/A — emotional framing |
| "Golf buddy now" | Root cause: shift parent from coach to buddy | ALIGNED — mechanism enables role shift |

**Cross-promise contradiction check:**
- Primary promise says "No lesson" → Supporting promise says "Seven words. That's the whole lesson." → NOT a contradiction. "No lesson" means no coaching session. "Seven words" is the parent's only verbal input. Consistent.
- Primary says "handles the rest" → Supporting says "shape sorter for golf" → ALIGNED. Both describe the same principle: equipment teaches through design.

### Check 3: Root Cause Connection (Score: 10/10)

**Does the promise connect to the root cause?**

This campaign uses hope market framing — no villain, no enemy. The "obstacle" is equipment designed for coaching. The promise connects to root cause by demonstrating the solution to that obstacle:

- Root cause: "Toddlers don't learn from words — they learn from what they can see and touch."
- Promise: "Match the yellow ball to the white ball... through shapes and colors."
- Connection: The promise IS the root cause solution in action. Color matching (visual) replaces verbal instruction. The promise doesn't need to name the obstacle because it demonstrates the alternative directly.

**Connection type:** DIRECT_DEMONSTRATION — the promise proves the root cause insight by showing the mechanism working.
