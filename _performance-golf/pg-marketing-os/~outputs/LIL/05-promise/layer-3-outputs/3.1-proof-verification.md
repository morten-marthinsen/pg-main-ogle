## Claim Detail Analysis

### Claim 9: "won't stop swinging" — Special Handling

**Status:** EMOTIONAL_FRAMING (not a factual claim requiring proof)

This phrase functions as an emotional description of engagement, not a measurable product claim. It's the equivalent of "you'll love it" — aspirational language, not a guarantee.

**Why it's safe:**
- Not positioned as a product guarantee
- No specific duration, frequency, or outcome promised
- Preceded by "Just a little legend who..." — framing as a parental observation, not a spec
- Within Level 4 proof ceiling (significant_improvement, not transformation)
- Hope market framing — positive aspiration, not measurable claim

**Risk level:** LOW — no reasonable consumer would interpret "won't stop swinging" as a warranty of engagement

### Claim 8: "No lesson" — Defensibility

**Status:** VERIFIED_STRONG

"No lesson" means the parent doesn't need to provide verbal golf instruction. Defensible via:
- DAT-19: Zero competitors offer visual-tactile self-learning
- Mechanism package: all 4 components teach through design, not words
- Root cause: developmental science supports visual-tactile learning for ages 2-5
- Softened by context: "No lesson" followed by emotional/identity language, not a guarantee

**Important note:** "No lesson" does NOT claim "the child learns without ANY input." The parent still hands the child the club, says "match yellow to white," and plays alongside. The promise accurately reflects the mechanism: the EQUIPMENT teaches, the parent PLAYS.
