## Risk Level Categorization

**Overall Risk Level: LOW**

This is a physical product (golf equipment for toddlers), not a supplement, health product, income opportunity, or financial product. The promise makes no health claims, no weight claims, no income claims, no disease claims. The risk profile is fundamentally different from the compliance framework's high-risk categories.

Risk factors identified:
- "only" claim in thesis → EXCLUSIVITY_CLAIM (moderate, but defensible via DAT-19)
- Grant Horvat endorsement → MATERIAL_CONNECTION (requires FTC disclosure)
- Developmental science reference ("shapes and colors") → LOW risk (describes learning approach, not health/medical claim)

## FTC Compliance Check

### Primary Promise

> "Seven words: 'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.' The See & Swing System handles the rest — alignment, stance, setup — all through shapes and colors. No lesson. Just a little legend who won't stop swinging. I'm his golf buddy now."

| Check | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Substantiation | PASS | All factual claims backed by mechanism proof (MEC-01, MEC-03, MEC-06, MEC-13). Product physically does what's described. |
| Typical results | N/A | No quantified result claimed. "Won't stop swinging" is emotional framing, not a measurable outcome. |
| Guarantee language | PASS | No guarantee language present. |
| Testimonial rules | N/A | No testimonials cited in promise. |
| Misleading implications | PASS | Promise doesn't imply the child becomes a golfer — claims equipment teaches alignment, stance, setup. Scoped. |

**FTC Status: COMPLIANT**

### Campaign Thesis

> "The only toddler golf system where the equipment teaches and your little legend can just play — the See & Swing System makes you their golf buddy, not their golf coach."

| Check | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| "Only" claim | DEFENSIBLE | DAT-19: Zero of 16 competitors have visual-tactile self-learning mechanisms. Factually accurate as of research date. Monitor for competitive launches. |
| Substantiation | PASS | "Equipment teaches" is literal — product design teaches through visual-tactile cues. Demonstrable. |
| Misleading implications | PASS | "Can just play" doesn't promise specific outcomes — describes the experience. |

**FTC Status: COMPLIANT**

### Supporting Promises

| Supporting Promise | FTC Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| "'Match the yellow ball to the white ball.' Seven words. That's the whole lesson." | COMPLIANT | Literal description of the mechanism. "Whole lesson" refers to parent's verbal input — defensible per mechanism design. |
| "A shape sorter for golf" | COMPLIANT | Analogy, not factual claim. Describes learning principle. |
| "From frustrated coach to golf buddy" | COMPLIANT | Describes role shift, not measurable outcome. |

## Health/Supplement Compliance

**NOT APPLICABLE** — This is physical sports equipment, not a supplement, health product, or wellness product. No FDA claims, no disease claims, no structure/function claims. No required health disclaimers.

The developmental science reference ("shapes and colors" / "how toddlers learn") describes a learning methodology, not a health intervention. This does not trigger FDA or health compliance requirements.
