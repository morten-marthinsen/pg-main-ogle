# 4.6 Copy Integration Guide — Lil' Legends

## Purpose

Provide specific guidance for integrating promises into copy across all LIL channels. This bridges promise development with actual copywriting — ensuring every downstream skill knows exactly how, where, and when to deploy each promise element.

**Pipeline:** E-comm product page + Organic content (no VSL). Branded initiative.
**Voice:** Grant at the range, talking to a fellow golf parent. Soul.md governs.
**Engineering names:** INTERNAL ONLY. Do NOT use Focal Sphere, Stand-Up Walk-In Frame, Face Forward in any consumer copy. Feature names TBD in e-comm phase.

---

## Integration Principles (LIL-Specific)

```yaml
integration_principles:

  promise_density:
    rule: "At least one promise element per major section"
    variation: "Higher density in hero and close. Lower in mechanism explanation (let the mechanism breathe)."

  promise_progression:
    pattern:
      ecomm:
        hero: "Hook promise (SP-1 — seven words curiosity)"
        mechanism: "Mechanism bridge (SP-2 — shape sorter analogy)"
        proof: "Full promise (Primary — demonstrates mechanism working)"
        benefits: "All promises as bullets"
        close: "Emotional promise (SP-3 — golf buddy) + callback to hero"
      organic_video:
        hook: "SP-1 (seven words)"
        relief: "Primary hook (you don't have to teach them)"
        insight: "SP-2 (shape sorter)"
        proof: "Primary (seven words demonstration + product demo)"
        mechanism: "Primary condensed (See & Swing explanation)"
        close: "SP-3 (golf buddy) + callback"

  variation_rotation:
    rule: "Never repeat exact promise wording. Use a different variation each time."
    exception: "'Match the yellow ball to the white ball' can repeat — it's the knockout proof line and repetition reinforces memorability."
    exception_2: "'Golf buddy, not golf coach' can repeat as a refrain."

  proof_proximity:
    rule: "Proof within 50-100 words of any claim."
    note: "For LIL, most promises self-prove (proof-as-promise architecture). The primary exception is SP-3 (emotional close), which benefits from Grant's personal story as proof."

  hope_market_tone:
    rule: "Every promise deployment sounds like sharing good news, not making a sales pitch."
    check: "If a line could come from a countdown-timer page, rewrite it."
```

## E-Comm Product Page Integration

### Section-by-Section Instructions

#### Hero Section (ATF)

```yaml
hero_integration:
  headline: "Seven Words. That's the Whole Golf Lesson."
  subhead: "The Equipment Teaches. Your Little Legend Plays."
  promise_source: "SP-1 headline + Primary short-form thesis"

  proof_to_deploy: null  # Headline self-proves. No additional proof ATF.
  word_count_target: "Headline + subhead only. No body copy ATF per EC-02 Apple/VanMoof clean design."

  what_comes_before: "Nothing — this is the first thing the visitor sees"
  what_comes_after: "Product imagery/video (Carousel Position 1 if following RS1 pattern)"

  warnings:
    - "Do NOT add bullet highlights ATF — premium clean design"
    - "Do NOT add price ATF unless business decision changes"

  example: |
    [HEADLINE] Seven Words. That's the Whole Golf Lesson.
    [SUBHEAD] The Equipment Teaches. Your Little Legend Plays.
    [CTA] See How It Works
```

#### Mechanism Section

```yaml
mechanism_integration:
  section_header: "A Shape Sorter for Golf"
  promise_source: "SP-2"

  body_flow:
    1: "Root cause (quick): 'Every kids' club was designed for coaching — someone telling the kid what to do. But toddlers don't learn from words.'"
    2: "Bridge: 'They learn the same way they learn a shape sorter — through the design itself.'"
    3: "Mechanism reveal: 'That's why we built the See & Swing System.'"
    4: "Component demonstration: Yellow ball → alignment. Walk-in → setup. Mat markers → stance."
    5: "Knockout proof: 'Match the yellow ball to the white ball. Seven words. That's the whole lesson.'"

  proof_to_deploy:
    - "AUT-11 (Piaget) — translate to parent language: 'That's how the brain works at this age.'"
    - "MEC-01, MEC-03, MEC-06 — show each component teaching one skill"
    - "MEC-13 — seven words as the proof climax"

  word_count_target: "200-400 words"

  warnings:
    - "Do NOT use engineering names (Focal Sphere, Walk-In Frame, Face Forward)"
    - "Present components as 4 examples of ONE principle, not a feature list"
    - "Do NOT lecture on developmental psychology — keep it parent-friendly"
    - "Follow mechanism explanation directive from mechanism-package.md Section 7"

  what_comes_before: "Hero section + product imagery"
  what_comes_after: "Proof/demonstration section or product highlights"
```

#### Proof/Demonstration Section

```yaml
proof_integration:
  promise_source: "Primary full promise"

  body_flow:
    1: "Setup: 'Here's what it looks like in action.'"
    2: "Full primary promise deployed — mechanism in action narrative"
    3: "Visual proof: product demo images/video (when GAP-05 resolved)"
    4: "Developmental backing: 'Nearly 80 years of developmental science confirms it.'"
    5: "Competitive proof: 'The only toddler golf system designed this way.'"

  proof_to_deploy:
    - "MEC-13 knockout — live demonstration"
    - "DAT-19 — competitive differentiation"
    - "AUT-11 — developmental science (brief)"
    - "DEM-02, DEM-03 — product visuals (when available)"

  word_count_target: "200-300 words (plus visual assets)"

  what_comes_before: "Mechanism section"
  what_comes_after: "Benefit stack or component details"
```

#### Benefit Stack

```yaml
benefit_stack_integration:
  promise_source: "All promises — benefit bullet variations"

  bullets:
    - "'Match the yellow ball to the white ball' — seven words, that's the whole lesson"
    - "Teaches alignment, stance, and setup through shapes and colors — no verbal coaching needed"
    - "Designed for how toddlers actually learn — through what they can see and touch"
    - "You become their golf buddy, not their golf coach"
    - "Built for ages 2-5 — the See & Swing System"

  proof_to_deploy: "Each bullet is self-proving or backed by mechanism demonstration"
  word_count_target: "5 bullets, each 10-20 words"

  what_comes_before: "Proof section or component details"
  what_comes_after: "Offer details or Grant's story"
```

#### Close Section

```yaml
close_integration:
  promise_source: "SP-3 (emotional close) + Primary (callback)"

  body_flow:
    1: "Future pace: 'Picture this: your little legend matching colors, swinging on their own, asking to go back to the range.'"
    2: "Identity shift: 'And you're right there next to them. Not coaching. Just playing.'"
    3: "Grant's story (brief): 'My dad gave me this game. That changed everything.'"
    4: "Dream close: 'Golf buddy. Not golf coach.'"
    5: "Callback: 'It started with seven words.'"
    6: "CTA"

  proof_to_deploy:
    - "AUT-03 — Grant's father story as emotional proof"
    - "SP-1 callback — structural unity (page ends where it began)"

  word_count_target: "150-250 words"

  warnings:
    - "NO urgency mechanics (no countdown timers, no 'limited time')"
    - "NO stacking ('But that's not all...')"
    - "Close on feeling, not logistics (WORKSPACE.md Heuristic #6)"

  what_comes_before: "Offer/product details, guarantee (when GAP-01 resolved)"
  what_comes_after: "CTA button"

  example: |
    Picture this.

    Your little legend matching colors. Swinging on their own.
    Asking to go back to the range.

    And you're right there next to them.
    Not coaching. Just playing.

    My dad gave me this game. That changed everything.
    Now I get to give it to mine.

    Golf buddy. Not golf coach.

    It started with seven words.

    [CTA: Get the See & Swing System]
```

## Transitions Between Promise Mentions

```yaml
transitions:
  hero_to_mechanism:
    type: "curiosity_bridge"
    options:
      - "Here's how it works."
      - "Let me show you why."
      - "Here's the idea behind it."

  mechanism_to_proof:
    type: "demonstration"
    options:
      - "Here's what that looks like."
      - "Let me show you."
      - "Watch this."

  proof_to_benefits:
    type: "expansion"
    options:
      - "And that's just the start."
      - "Here's what else it does."

  benefits_to_close:
    type: "emotional_turn"
    options:
      - "But here's what really matters."
      - "And the best part?"
      - "That's when it hit me."

  note: "Use natural transitions, not headers or section breaks (Soul.md pacing signature). Grant's voice IS the structure."
```

## Integration Checklist

### Pre-Writing
- [ ] Confirm primary promise and all 3 supporting promises (locked, do not modify)
- [ ] Confirm all variations available (4.3)
- [ ] Confirm proof pairings for each position (4.5)
- [ ] Review Soul.md voice register
- [ ] Review mechanism-package.md explanation directive (Section 7)
- [ ] Confirm no engineering names in any consumer copy

### During Writing
- [ ] SP-1 headline deployed in hero
- [ ] SP-2 deployed in mechanism section
- [ ] Primary full promise deployed in proof/mechanism section
- [ ] All benefit bullets deployed in benefit stack
- [ ] SP-3 deployed in close
- [ ] Callback to hero in close
- [ ] No exact repetition (use variation rotation — exceptions: "seven words" and "golf buddy")
- [ ] Proof within 50-100 words of each claim
- [ ] No urgency mechanics anywhere

### Post-Writing
- [ ] Anti-slop check: zero AI tells, zero vague quantifiers, zero hype words
- [ ] Hope market check: zero fear, zero guilt, zero competitive attack
- [ ] Engineering names check: zero instances of Focal Sphere, Walk-In Frame, Face Forward
- [ ] Voice check: reads like Grant talking, not marketing copy
- [ ] Promise progression: hook (SP-1) → explain (SP-2) → demonstrate (Primary) → close (SP-3)
- [ ] Structural unity: close references opening
- [ ] Proof ceiling respected: no claims beyond "significant improvement" (Level 4)

### Before Publishing
- [ ] FTC disclosure: Grant discloses material connection with Performance Golf
- [ ] "Only" claim in thesis: verify still defensible per competitive landscape
- [ ] Demo video included (when GAP-05 resolved)
- [ ] Guarantee terms included (when GAP-01 resolved)
