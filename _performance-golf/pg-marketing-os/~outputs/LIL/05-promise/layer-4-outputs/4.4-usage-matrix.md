## Position-by-Position Guidelines

### E-Comm Hero
- **What to use:** SP-1 headline ("Seven Words. That's the Whole Golf Lesson.") as primary headline. Primary short-form thesis ("The Equipment Teaches. Your Little Legend Plays.") as subhead.
- **Why:** SP-1 headline creates immediate curiosity. Short-form thesis delivers the positioning. Together they communicate the full value proposition in 16 words.
- **What comes after:** Product imagery/video, then mechanism section.

### E-Comm Mechanism Section
- **What to use:** SP-2 full statement as section header. Primary full promise as section body. SP-1 as the proof moment.
- **Why:** SP-2 (shape sorter analogy) makes the mechanism instantly intuitive. Primary promise then demonstrates it. SP-1 crystallizes it.
- **What comes after:** Proof/demonstration section.

### E-Comm Close
- **What to use:** SP-3 future pace leading into callback to SP-1 ("seven words").
- **Why:** SP-3 lands the emotional identity shift (golf buddy). The callback to "seven words" creates structural unity — the page ends where it began.
- **What comes after:** CTA button.

### Organic Video (Grant on Camera)
- **Structure follows root cause video script block (2F in root-cause-package.md):**
  1. Hook: SP-1 ("Seven words...")
  2. Relief: Primary hook ("You don't have to teach them")
  3. Insight: SP-2 ("Think about a shape sorter...")
  4. Proof: SP-1 ("Match the yellow ball...") + product demo
  5. Mechanism: Primary condensed (See & Swing explanation)
  6. Dream close: SP-3 ("Golf buddy, not golf coach")
  7. Callback: Primary future pace ("A lifetime of golf together — seven words")

### Email Sequence
- **Email 1 (Tease):** SP-1 subject line. SP-1 hook in body. Don't reveal the seven words — drive to page.
- **Email 2 (Mechanism):** SP-2 subject line ("A shape sorter for golf"). Shape sorter explanation in body. Primary condensed.
- **Email 3 (Dream):** SP-3 subject line ("Golf buddy, not golf coach"). Emotional close. Grant's story. CTA.

### Social Ads
- **Test matrix:** Rotate SP-1 hook vs. Primary question hook as opening line. SP-1 ("Seven words...") is the bolder hook. Primary question ("What if you didn't have to teach your toddler golf?") is the broader curiosity play. Test both.
