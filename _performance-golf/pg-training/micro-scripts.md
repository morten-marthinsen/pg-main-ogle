Page 22-23
Big Rule 4: Give ‘Em A Micro-Script
The human mind craves simple
A micro-script is a short phrase - almost always a rule of thumb or key idea - that people like to repeat word for word to inform, persuade, or remind themselves and others. It uses vivid, descriptive metaphors and rhyme or rhythmic language to trigger a story in the mind. All in all, micro scripts are brain-speak
Just enough information in the least number of words to form an opinion, change a mind, or cause to act
They compress stories so people can remember and repeat

Page 23
They are the self-talk we use as the number-one way to control our primitive brain’s automatic fear center (the amygdala) so we can act quickly in the face of uncertainty or disaster

Page 24
Metaphors are the way humans think, persuade, remember, and organize thoughts
They are the building blocks of stories

Page 25
It causes the right brain to form hypnotic visualizations that go way beyond the metaphor itself, stimulating personal associations that cause our story-loving brain to fill in the imaginary blanks

Page 25
Big Rule 4 ½: If you want a brand that sells, build your micro-script on a dominant selling idea

Page 26
The one most important difference that sets you apart
Difference you stand for that noone else does
A superlative about a unique advantage others can’t claim (safest car, fastest human, the only shoe that can breathe, most durable make-up, longest-lasting lightbulb, most popular sitcom)
Implicit in every DSI is “buy this product get this benefit”

Page 27
Must know DSI first before microscript
Example: know you are the world’s toughest watch before “takes a lickin’ and keeps on tickin’”
Same as your “positioning” (own a distinctive piece of real estate in the prospect’s mind no one else owns)
Room for only one superlative (one safest car)

Page 28
If your message doesn’t work by word of mouth, it doesn’t work
If it works by word of mouth, it works everywhere

Page 109-110
