## Key Takeaways

### Frameworks Discussed

1. **Market/Segment/Pocket/Fragment Framework**
   - Market: Anyone who could buy your product
   - Segment: Larger audience sharing common pain point/desire/identity
   - Pocket: Smaller audiences responding to specific ad concepts
   - Fragment: Micro audiences responding to specific hooks (first 3 seconds)

2. **MINT Framework (Audience Scaling)**
   - Segment identification
   - Angle positioning
   - Big idea development
   - Hook creation

3. **Graduation Process**
   - Winning fragments can elevate to pockets
   - Winning pockets can elevate to segments
   - Winning segments can become entirely new products

### Key Insights

- **Pareto Principle in Ads**: 2% of ad creative drives 42% of ad spend; top 10% drives ~80%
- **Congruency = 20-25% lift**: Making headlines congruent throughout the funnel consistently boosts performance
- **Tease vs Taste**: Put best value-delivering content in first 15 seconds (not hidden)
- **Physical vs Digital**: Physical products do better with upfront product reveal; digital does better with mechanism-focused approach

### Action Items

1. Reactivate assets turned off due to old KPI (now use net loss per trial)
2. Track expansion stages for 10-50 winning assets retroactively
3. Build data-driven recommendation system based on what expansion elements work
4. Align on common nomenclature (stages vs elements, vertical vs horizontal)
5. Develop drilling/fracking metaphor for creative/audience expansion

### Vertical vs Horizontal Scaling

- **Vertical (Video Editor Level)**: Visual hooks, copy hooks, durations, mashups - can be done without copywriters
- **Horizontal (Creative Team Level)**: Different presenters, new environments, production - requires copywriters/production
- Both can run in parallel for faster creative acceleration
