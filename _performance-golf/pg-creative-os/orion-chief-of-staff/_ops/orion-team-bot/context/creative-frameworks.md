# PG Creative Frameworks

## Six-Axis Model of Influence

Only 3 of 6 axes need to be elevated for persuasion — but they must be elevated significantly.

| Axis | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Focus** | Degree to which a person maintains attention and excludes competing stimuli |
| **Suggestibility** | Degree to which a person will accept and act on a suggestion |
| **Openness** | Degree to which a person feels they can become vulnerable without fear of judgment |
| **Connection** | Degree to which a person feels social connection and desire for future contact |
| **Compliance** | Degree to which a person will comply with direction to perform an action |
| **Expectancy** | Degree to which a person can predict positive outcomes and enjoys anticipation |

### Priority by Format

| Format | Priority Order |
|--------|---------------|
| **Paid Ads (Cold)** | Focus → Suggestibility → Compliance |
| **Sales Page (Warm)** | Expectancy → Focus → Suggestibility → Compliance |
| **Email Nurture** | Connection → Openness → Expectancy → Suggestibility |
| **Retargeting** | Expectancy → Compliance → Focus |

### Diagnostic Table

| Symptom | Likely Axis Issue |
|---------|------------------|
| Low engagement/views | Focus |
| High bounce rate | Suggestibility or Focus |
| Low opt-in rate | Compliance or Expectancy |
| No response to CTA | Compliance |
| Comments but no action | Connection without Compliance |
| Objections in responses | Suggestibility |

## Golf Suggestibility Principle

> **For golfers, emotion IS in the education.**

Golfers are learners. New information creates the emotional response. The feeling of discovery — "I've never heard this before" — IS the emotion. The desire to learn more IS the compliance pathway.

### Conversion Chain: Focus → Education → Desire-to-Learn → Compliance

1. **Focus (The Grab)**: Contrarian statement that challenges an existing belief. Creates a belief gap the brain needs resolved.
2. **Suggestibility (The Teach)**: Genuine education — new information that makes the golfer think differently. UMP (reframes the problem), UMS (reveals the mechanism), or insider knowledge.
3. **Compliance (The Click)**: Desire to learn more becomes the action driver. CTA promises continuation of the lesson.

### The "I Didn't Know That" Test
If the teaching doesn't make the golfer think something they've never thought before, it's not creating suggestibility. Recycled information creates zero suggestibility.

### When Copy Feels Flat
1. Is the teaching genuinely new? (not reframed old info)
2. Does the contrarian claim set up the teaching?
3. Does the teaching build progressively?
4. Does the education create desire-to-learn-more?
5. Is the CTA a natural continuation of the teaching?

## 8 Laws of Ad Angle Ideation

1. **Primal wound or desire first, benefit second.** The angle names the wound (distress) or desire (aspiration), never the benefit. The product resolves/fulfills it.
2. **Research-grounded language only.** Every angle traceable to audience research or existing copy. Their exact words.
3. **Instantly understood by target.** "Would the target read these 2-4 words and instantly feel something without the description?"
4. **Complete emotional arc.** Description does both: names the wound/desire AND shows how the product resolves/fulfills it.
5. **Specific emotion, not category.** Never "fear" or "confidence" — name the exact physical sensation, exact moment, exact internal dialogue.
6. **Visceral resonance test.** Does it create a feeling in the body? Recognition ("that's me"), desire ("I want that"), or fear ("I need to fix this").
7. **Influencer usability.** Could they read the name, understand it in 3 seconds, and riff naturally?
8. **Product-mechanism connection.** Description connects to the SPECIFIC product mechanism, not generic benefit language.

## Buyer Personas (P1-P9)

| ID | Name | Core Wound/Drive | Key Trigger |
|----|------|-----------------|-------------|
| **P1** | Chronic Slicer | Exhaustion of a problem that defines his game. The slice IS his identity on the course. | Empathy ("you're not alone") + proof ("60,000 golfers found something") |
| **P2** | Equipment Skeptic | Betrayed trust from failed "miracle" products. Cynicism armoring guarded hope. | One piece of evidence too strong to dismiss (non-return data > purchase data) |
| **P3** | Peer-Influenced Golfer | Social itch — doesn't want to be left out when buddies upgrade. | Seeing someone he trusts use and endorse the product |
| **P4** | Smart Shopper | Fear of being "sold." Purchase decision IS an intelligence test. | The "I didn't know that" moment — one genuinely new piece of information |
| **P5** | Comeback Golfer | Gap between who he WAS and who he IS. Grief for a version of himself. Buying restoration, not renovation. | Reframe: "my equipment needs to catch up to me" (not "I've lost my skill") |
| **P6** | Gift Buyer | Anxiety of buying in a domain they don't understand. Fear of buying a "gimmick." | Confidence it will be appreciated (recipient testimonials > user reviews) |
| **P7** | Tech/Innovation Enthusiast | Hunger for innovation + early-adopter pride. Frustrated by surface-level marketing. | The "under the hood" reveal — engineering story |
| **P8** | Weekend Warrior | Golf has become something he endures, not enjoys. Low-grade anxiety on the tee. | Contrast between dread and relief. "This could be easy." |
| **P9** | Competitive Amateur | Plateau. Knows he's capable of more but equipment has taken him as far as it can. | Performance proof — specific numbers (yards, dispersion) at HIS level |

## Copy Quality Rules

1. **Never fabricate** product names, mechanisms, metrics, or claims. If unknown, say "UNKNOWN."
2. **Research-grounded language only** — trace every angle to audience research or existing copy.
3. **Specific emotions, not categories** — never "fear" or "confidence." Name the exact sensation and moment.
4. **Primal wound/desire first** — the angle names the wound or desire, never the benefit.
5. **Complete emotional arc** — every description names the wound/desire AND shows resolution/fulfillment.
6. **Product-mechanism connection** — connect to the SPECIFIC mechanism, not generic benefits.
7. **"I didn't know that" test** — if the teaching isn't genuinely new, suggestibility is zero.
