The Root Problem: Face Drift

In 95% of putters, most of the weight sits behind the shaft axis. This rear weighting pulls the face off the path of the intended target line. That is face drift — and it is arguably the biggest problem in putting.

Two causes:

  • Technology — The putter’s rear weighting opens and closes the face on an arc during the stroke
  • Technique — The player’s natural tendency to control the face, often leaving it open or closed before impact

The RS1 eliminates face drift at the source — by fixing both the technology and the technique simultaneously.

Messaging Architecture

Problem Framework

  • Root cause named: Face drift — putter face drifting open/closed due to rearward center of gravity
  • Reframe: “Your stroke was never the problem. Your putter’s center of gravity was.”
  • Dual cause: Technology (rear weighting opens the face) + Technique (player’s natural tendency to manipulate the clubface)
  • Villain: 100 years of rear-weighted putters. The industry normalized face drift as a “feature” (toe flow) instead of the problem it is.
  • Failed solutions: Technique changes, mental coaching, practice routines, and even zero-torque putters that solved the technology problem but left the player alone

Solution Framework

  • Core claim: Forward Axis Weighting eliminates face drift AND improves your stroke — both pillars of great putting, in one putter
  • Mechanism name: Forward Axis Weighting (within Roll Str8 Technology)
  • Key differentiator: Active face control (gravity drives face toward square) vs. passive (zero torque prevents twist)
  • Result frame: Technology AND technique — not a tradeoff. The first putter that helps both.

Gravity does the work. Show the natural pendulum stroke. The putter wants to stay square.

Face-Down Balance
Place it face-down on a flat surface. It stays. Every other putter flops open. Visual proof.

Chris McGinley’s Story
30+ years designing clubs for 11 world #1 players. RS1 is his life’s work.

“Science of the Stroke” + 125-Year Evolution
