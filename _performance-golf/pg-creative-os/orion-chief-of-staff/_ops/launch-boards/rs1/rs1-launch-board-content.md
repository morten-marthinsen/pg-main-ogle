### The Root Problem: Face Drift

In 95% of putters, most of the weight sits behind the shaft axis. This rear weighting pulls the face off the path of the intended target line. That is face drift — and it is arguably the biggest problem in putting.

**Two causes:**

1. **Technology** — The putter's rear weighting opens and closes the face on an arc during the stroke
2. **Technique** — The player's natural tendency to control the face, often leaving it open or closed before impact

The RS1 eliminates face drift at the source — by fixing both the technology and the technique simultaneously.

### Past, Present, Future — The Evolution of Putters

| Era | Technology | Face Behavior | Player's Job |
|-----|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **THE PAST** — Traditional rear-weighted putters | Weight behind shaft axis | Face drifts open. Player relies on timing to open and close the face on an arc. | Control face rotation throughout. Fight the physics. |
| **THE PRESENT** — LAB Golf / Zero-Torque | Weight at shaft axis (lie angle balanced) | Face hangs neutral. Passive stability — prevents twist but still requires player face control. | Still must control face relative to target line. |
| **THE FUTURE** — RS1 / Forward Axis Weighting | Weight in front of shaft axis | Face falls square. Active gravitational force drives face toward square. | Pull it back, let it fall. Gravity does the work. |

LAB Golf changed the game. They solved the technology problem with zero-torque putters — but left an element of face control up to the player. The RS1's Forward Axis Weighting gives you the technology that prevents face drift AND creates a counteracting force that wants to keep the face square to the target line.

### Research Executive Summary

Top insights from deep research (1,442 voice-of-customer quotes, 12 sources, 8 competitors analyzed, 137 proof elements inventoried):

1. **The premium putter market sits at Stage 4 sophistication (Mechanism Fatigue).** LAB Golf's zero-torque mechanism was the Stage 3 breakthrough, but 4 major OEMs have copied it in 18 months. Zero torque is rapidly commoditizing. Forward Axis Weighting enters at the exact inflection point.
2. **400,000-600,000 serious amateur golfers are stuck on the "Putter Carousel"** — buying and discarding putters every 6-18 months because every putter shares the same design flaw: rearward center of gravity causing face drift.
3. **LAB Golf's lost majority is the primary capture opportunity.** 55-65% of LAB-interested golfers are blocked by tradeoffs (center-shaft aesthetics, aluminum face feel, 2-8 week adaptation, $449-$649 pricing). RS1 is face stability without the compromises.
4. **The self-blame trap is the #1 belief barrier.** 32% of the addressable market blames their technique, not their equipment. The reframe ("your stroke was fine, your putter's CG was in the wrong place") is the most powerful belief shift available.
5. **No brand has named why putts miss.** "Face drift" as a consumer-friendly root cause term is completely unclaimed. Own the vocabulary, own the conversation.
6. **Forward Axis Weighting occupies completely unclaimed competitive white space.** Only Axis1 (niche, minimal presence) uses forward CG. No major brand has claimed the territory.
7. **The face-down balance test is the most powerful proof device.** In a Stage 4 market drowning in verbal claims, a 5-second visual demonstration bypasses all skepticism.
8. **Chris McGinley's credentials are the credibility bridge.** PG has zero hardware credibility on its own. McGinley's 30+ years and 11 world #1 players authenticate every engineering claim.

- **Root cause named**: Face drift — the putter face drifting open or closed during the stroke due to rearward center of gravity
