The Root Problem: Toe Lag

The slice isn't a swing problem — it's a physics problem. The toe is farther from your hands, has more distance to travel, and naturally lags behind the heel. SF2's answer: don't fight the physics — engineer around it.

8SR-71 titanium passes believability. Tangible, verifiable origin story.

Persona & Value Driver Matrix
Who are we targeting? 5 launch personas, 10 value drivers
Each batch 1 ad variation targets one persona. Variation 1 → Persona 1, etc. Remaining personas move to Phase 2 expansion.
P1 — The Chronic Slicer
Slices every round. Tried lessons, YouTube, new grips — nothing sticks. Largest segment: 96% of recreational golfers slice.
"I've tried everything. Nothing fixes my slice."
Primary Emotion: Frustration + Shame
P2 — The Equipment Skeptic
Burned by "miracle" golf products before. Needs overwhelming proof — credentialed authority + volume social proof.
"I've been burned by golf gadgets before. Why would this be different?"
Primary Emotion: Distrust + Guarded Hope
P3 — The Tech/Innovation Enthusiast
Loves engineering stories, materials science. Early adopter who tells his foursome what he bought and why.
"Aerospace-grade forged titanium? Tell me more about the engineering."
Primary Emotion: Wonder + Early-Adopter Pride
P4 — The Weekend Warrior
Plays once a week or less. Not trying to go pro — just wants to enjoy the round and not embarrass himself off the tee.
"I just want to have fun out there without dreading every tee shot."
Primary Emotion: Desire for Easy Enjoyment
P5 — The Comeback Golfer
Used to play regularly. Life got in the way. Coming back now and doesn't want to start over with lessons.
"I used to shoot in the 80s. I just need to get my drive back."
Primary Emotion: Nostalgia + Eagerness
