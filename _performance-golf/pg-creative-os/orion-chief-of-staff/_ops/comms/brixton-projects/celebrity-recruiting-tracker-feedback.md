## Response

A few things to add from the creative side:

### On Historical Performance

Our strongest-performing content type is authentic UGC. Conversational, 15-60 second vertical, shot in real settings — range, course, home. Scripted or polished production consistently underperforms. Pain-focused hooks outperform desire-focused across every product we've tested. And authority transfer — real golf credentials, expert associations — converts better than pure reach.

### On the Female Segment

Our female-focused quiz is one of the best-performing digital offers right now. Marisa Messana on our female-focused quiz and Averee Dovsek on Click Stick have both performed well, and the key insight across both is that feel-based messaging works significantly better than technical with women — sensation and emotion over data and mechanics. We are currently fleshing out our dedicated female personas because of the strong performance we're seeing, and I'd love to see the talent strategy include a dedicated female creator tier to match that momentum.

### On Product Roadmap

SF2 (Anti-Slice Driver) just launched March 25. We also have ONE.1 (wedge), SpeedTrac (speed training), and an upgraded version of Click Stick in the pipeline. Each has a different ideal talent profile, so aligning talent mapping to the full product calendar will be important.
