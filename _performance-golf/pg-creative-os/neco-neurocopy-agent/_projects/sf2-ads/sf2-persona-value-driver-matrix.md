# SF2 Anti-Slice Driver — Persona x Value Driver Matrix

> **Purpose**: Reusable strategic asset for all SF2 creative. Maps buyer personas against purchase motivations per Andromeda-era testing principles — each intersection = a potential ad variant that reaches a different person for a different reason.
> **Created**: S031 (2026-02-17)
> **Source framework**: Savannah Sanchez / Motion — Andromeda Creative Matrix

## SF2 Buyer Personas

| # | Persona | Description | Primary Emotion | They'd Say... |
|---|---------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| P1 | **The Chronic Slicer** | Slices every round, has tried everything. Knows it's a problem but can't fix it. Been told to change his swing, take lessons, buy a different driver. Nothing sticks. | Frustration + shame | "I've tried everything. Lessons, YouTube, new grips — nothing fixes my slice." |
| P2 | **The Equipment Skeptic** | Burned before by "miracle" golf products. Sees ads and thinks "yeah right." Needs overwhelming proof to believe anything works. | Distrust + guarded hope | "I've been burned by golf gadgets before. Why would this be any different?" |
| P3 | **The Peer-Influenced Golfer** | Doesn't research deeply. Buys what his buddies are talking about. If everyone in his foursome says something works, he's in. | FOMO + social belonging | "If everyone's hitting it, I don't want to be the only one still slicing." |
| P4 | **The Smart Shopper** | Researches before buying. Wants value for money. Compares specs, reads reviews. Not impulsive — needs to feel like this is the intelligent choice. | Analytical confidence | "I want the best driver for MY game, not what TaylorMade tells me to buy." |
| P5 | **The Comeback Golfer** | Used to play regularly, fell off. Getting back into it. Doesn't want to start over with lessons — wants gear that helps him pick up where he left off. | Nostalgia + eagerness | "I used to shoot in the 80s. I just need to get my drive back." |
| P6 | **The Gift Buyer** | Buying for a golfer in their life (husband, dad, friend). Doesn't know golf tech — needs to trust the product is genuinely special and appreciated. | Desire to impress + uncertainty | "I want to get him something he'll actually use, not another golf gimmick." |
| P7 | **The Tech/Innovation Enthusiast** | Loves new technology in all areas. Drawn to engineering stories, materials science, "how it works." The aerospace-grade titanium and SR-71 story would captivate him. | Wonder + early-adopter pride | "Aerospace-grade forged titanium? Tell me more about the engineering." |
| P8 | **The Weekend Warrior** | Plays once a week or less. Not trying to go pro — just wants to enjoy the round and not embarrass himself off the tee. Low practice time. | Desire for easy enjoyment | "I just want to have fun out there without dreading every tee shot." |
| P9 | **The Competitive Amateur** | Plays regularly, mid-handicap. Doesn't slice badly but wants more consistency + distance. Already decent — looking for an edge. | Ambition + optimization | "I'm already pretty good. Can this get me to single digits?" |

## Recommended 5 Hooks for sf2-0002 (Phase 2)

Selected to maximize Andromeda persona/value driver diversity while flowing into the "Most Popular Way" body. Each targets a different person with a different reason to stop scrolling.

| Hook # | Persona | Value Driver | Hook Concept | Visual Opening (3-5s) |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **H1** | P1 Chronic Slicer | V1 Pain + V3 Social Proof | "If your slice has survived every fix you've thrown at it — you're not alone. But 60,000 golfers found something that actually works." | ON CAMERA — Christopher, empathetic, direct |
| **H2** | P2 Equipment Skeptic | V9 Contrarian + V3 Social Proof | "I used to roll my eyes at every 'miracle driver' ad. Then I saw why 60,000 golfers weren't coming back for a refund." | ON CAMERA — Christopher, skeptical-then-convinced tone |
| **H3** | P8 Weekend Warrior | V5 Convenience + V3 Social Proof | "What if the fastest way to enjoy golf again had nothing to do with your swing?" | B-ROLL — golfer relaxed on tee box, easy swing, ball flying straight. No VO for first 3s, then Christopher VO enters. |
| **H4** | P5 Comeback Golfer | V6 Identity + V3 Social Proof | "Thousands of golfers are getting back to the game they remember — and it's starting from the tee box." | B-ROLL — montage of golfers at various ages/skill levels hitting straight drives, smiling. Warm tone. |
| **H5** | P4 Smart Shopper | V4 Innovation + V3 Social Proof | [VISUAL HOOK — no VO] | VISUAL — SF2 driver rotating with callout text: "6 FEATURES. 60,000 GOLFERS. ZERO SWING CHANGES." Stats/proof as visual pattern interrupt. |

**Design logic**: All 5 hooks thread back to social proof ("most popular way") because that's the body's root angle. But each hook *enters* through a different persona's worldview and a different purchase motivation. Meta can now match each variation to the viewer most likely to resonate with that specific entry point.
