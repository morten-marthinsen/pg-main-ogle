## Executive Summary

15 unique creative concepts reviewed across 3 root angles (A, B, C). Production quality and visual design are solid across all three series. The B and C series in particular have a strong premium aesthetic. However, there is **one critical error that affects the entire batch** and several per-ad issues that need to be corrected before these can go live.

**Overall status: REVISIONS REQUIRED — do not launch until corrected.**

---

## 🔴 CRITICAL — Batch-Level Issue (Affects All Ads)

### Wrong Mechanism Name: "Square-Speed Aero Head™"

**Affects:** A1, A2, A3, A4, A5, B3, B5, C2, C3, C5

Every ad that names the core technology uses **"Square-Speed Aero Head™"** — this is the wrong name.

**Correct name:** **Slice Fix Speed Technology™**

The "Square-Speed Aero Head™" name was tested and rejected. The confirmed, approved mechanism name for the SF2 is "Slice Fix Speed Technology™." Running ads with the wrong mechanism name creates brand inconsistency across channels and will need to be updated across every placement once live — which is significantly harder to fix post-launch.

**Required action:** Find/replace "Square-Speed Aero Head™" → "Slice Fix Speed Technology™" across all affected ads before launch.

### B4 — Absolute Claim ("Every swing")
**File:** `B4 - [all dimensions]`
**Issue:** Body copy reads: *"The SF2 fixes your timing automatically. Straight drives. Every swing."*
"Every swing" is an absolute 100% performance claim we cannot make. It creates consumer protection exposure.
**Fix:** Replace "Every swing." with something like "Swing after swing." or "Most swings." or simply remove it.

## 🟡 SHOULD FIX — Copy & Consistency Issues

### A3 — Weak Audience Line
**File:** `A3 - [all dimensions]`
**Issue:** Body copy includes "Designed for real golfers" — this is too vague. Every golf product is "for real golfers." It doesn't say anything specific.
**Brief said:** "Built for average golfers" or "No swing changes required"
**Fix:** Replace with "Designed for everyday golfers" or "No lesson required" or align with the brief language.

---

### A5 & B3 — Duplicate Headline Across Two Creative Treatments
**Files:** `A5 - [all dimensions]` and `B3 - [all dimensions]`
**Issue:** Both ads use the exact same headline: **"CHANGE YOUR DRIVER. NOT YOUR SWING."**
Running two different design treatments of the same headline splits performance data and makes it impossible to isolate whether results are driven by the headline or the design. You'll have muddy data from day one.
**Fix:** Either consolidate to one treatment, or differentiate the headline on one of them. Suggest keeping B3 as-is (strong visual) and updating A5 to a different angle.

---

### Series B — Inconsistent CTAs
**Issue:** CTAs within Series B mix curiosity-style and commerce-style calls to action with no clear logic:
- B1: "SEE HOW IT WORKS" (curiosity)
- B2: "SEE THE PROOF" (curiosity)
- B3: "SHOP THE SF2" (commerce)
- B4: "SEE THE PROOF" (curiosity)
- B5: "SHOP NOW" (commerce)

Curiosity CTAs (See How It Works, See The Proof) work best for cold traffic. Commerce CTAs (Shop Now, Shop the SF2) work for warm/retargeting audiences. If these are all running cold, the commerce CTAs will underperform. If mixed audiences, it needs to be intentional.
**Fix:** Align CTAs to the intended audience temperature. Recommend: all cold traffic B series uses curiosity CTAs. Commerce CTAs reserved for retargeting variants.

## Recurring Patterns for NLC to Address Systemically

1. **Mechanism name** — Needs to be corrected at the source document/template level, not just per ad. If your team has a style guide or master brief for SF2, update it there first so future rounds don't repeat this.

2. **Feature name accuracy** — When the brief specifies technology names (Speed Geometry Toe Slot, Counter-Slice Keel, 3-Piece Forged Titanium Face), use them exactly as written. Do not paraphrase or rename them. These are trademarked product features.

3. **Absolute claims** — Avoid "every," "always," "guaranteed," or "100%" language in body copy without explicit sign-off from PG legal/creative.

4. **Logo consistency** — All static ad series (A, B, C) should carry the product logo lock-up at a legible size. This is especially important for small display sizes (336x280, 300x250) where the club branding on the product itself becomes too small to read.
