## CRITICAL — Batch-Level Issue (Repeated from R1)

### Wrong Mechanism Name: "Square-Speed Aero Head™" — STILL PRESENT

**Affects:** B3, B5, C2, C3, C5 (5 of 10 ads)

This was the #1 critical error in the R1 review (2026-03-18). It was not corrected in the brief or template before producing B & C.

**"Square-Speed Aero Head™" is not a real PG product name.** It appears to be a mashup of two actual feature names ("AeroSquare Crown Channels" + "Speed Geometry Toe Slot"). Using a fabricated mechanism name in ads creates brand inconsistency with the sales page and every other customer touchpoint.

**Correct technology name:** **Slice-Fix SpeedTech™** (the proprietary system of 7 features)

**Individual feature names (when naming specific features):**
- Speed Geometry Toe Slot
- Counter-Slice Weighting
- AeroSquare Crown Channels
- Fast Face Construction
- Draw-Calibrated Face Angle
- Draw Spin Face Bulge
- 445cc Speed Geometry Head

**Required action:** Global find/replace "Square-Speed Aero Head™" → "Slice-Fix SpeedTech™" or use a specific feature name where appropriate. Update the agency's master template/style guide so this does not recur in future rounds.

## CRITICAL — Brief-Level Issue (PG Owns)

### C Series Copy Direction: Engineering Specs, Not Consumer Copy

**Affects:** C1, C2, C3, C5

The C series reads like internal product specs, not ads aimed at golfers who slice. Compare:

| C Series (current) | Sales Page (source of truth) |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| "Draw-bias CG. Calibrated face closure." | "Squares it and sends it — FOR YOU." |
| "Forged for distance." | "Fix your slice AND gain 20+ yards." |
| "Slice-fixing built into every piece." | "The club does the work for you." |

The sales page speaks to the golfer's problem and the feeling of the result. The C series speaks to the engineer who designed the club. These are two completely different audiences.

**This is a brief-level issue** — the agency built what the brief asked for. The brief (via Chris Fleeks) needs to be realigned with the sales page copy before the next round.

**Required action:** Rewrite C series body copy to match sales page language (see Copy Brief for exact rewrites).

## SHOULD FIX — Copy Congruence Issues

### B1 — "Square the Face" Is Internal Language

**Current:** "SQUARE THE FACE. WITHOUT THE EFFORT."

"Square the face" is golf-instruction language. The sales page uses benefit language: "Squares it and sends it — FOR YOU." The core promise is that THE CLUB does it, not that YOU square anything.

**Fix:** Reframe around the club doing the work (see Copy Brief).

---

### B2 — Decent Headline, Weak Body

**Current headline:** "FIX YOUR SLICE. KEEP YOUR SPEED." (Strong — keep)
**Current body:** "Straighter drives off the tee. More distance without the work."

"Straighter drives off the tee" was flagged in A1 (R1) and changed to "straighter drives in five shots." The same vague language is back.

**Fix:** Update body to match sales page specificity (see Copy Brief).

### C1 — Too Technical for Any Audience

**Current:** "Draw-bias CG. Calibrated face closure."

No golfer who slices knows what "CG" means or why they should care about "calibrated face closure." This is spec-sheet language. Even for retargeting (warm traffic), the language needs to connect the feature to the benefit.

**Fix:** Full body rewrite (see Copy Brief).

---

### C4 — Feature Names Correct, Headline Strong

**Current headline:** "TOE LAG. MEET YOUR MATCH."
**Current body:** "Counter-Slice Keel. Speed Geometry Toe Slot."

C4 is the only ad that uses correct feature names. The headline is also the most insight-driven in the batch — "toe lag" names a specific problem golfers experience. However, the body just lists two features without connecting them to a benefit.

**Fix:** Keep headline. Add benefit connection to body (see Copy Brief).

## Recurring Patterns for Agency

1. **Template still contains "Square-Speed Aero Head™"** — This must be corrected at the source before any future round. Update the master style guide/brief template with "Slice-Fix SpeedTech™" and the 7 correct feature names.

2. **Body copy defaults to spec language when the brief doesn't provide consumer copy.** The agency builds what the brief says. If the brief contains engineering language, the ads will too. PG must provide sales-page-aligned copy in the brief (this is a PG-side fix, not an agency fix).

3. **Absolute claims keep appearing.** Add a checklist item to the agency's QA process: scan all copy for "every," "always," "guaranteed," "100%," "all," "never fails." Replace with qualified language before delivery.
