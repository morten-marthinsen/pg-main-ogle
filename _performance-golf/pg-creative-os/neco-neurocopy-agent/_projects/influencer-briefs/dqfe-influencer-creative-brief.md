## **Product Overview**

**What it is**: A digital quiz-led experience that maps golfers into relevant instruction pathways/offers based on how they move and what they need next.

**Core Problem it Solves**: Golfers consume generic tips that don't match their body, swing pattern, or real constraint profile.

**Positioning From Transcript**:

* Quiz-focused ads have been recent winners and should stay in the rotation.
* Women-forward messaging was emphasized: "finally something for us."
* Authentic delivery matters more than rigid script compliance.
* Keep hooks short and let the quiz do heavy lifting.

**Known Winning Inputs To Carry Forward**:

* Lesson-with-Erica style framing performed strongly for female digital audiences.
* "Beat the guys" and women-specific fit framing showed promising response.

---

## **Target Audience Profile**

**Who We're Speaking To**: Golfers who want a personalized next step and are tired of generic one-size-fits-all instruction.

### **Psychographics**

* Consumes golf content but feels stuck
* Wants clarity fast, not another long theory session
* Responds to personalization and identity-based messaging
* Open to quizzes if payoff is specific and practical

### **Physical Experience (USE THIS LANGUAGE)**

* "I don't know what to fix first"
* "I can hit one good shot then lose it"
* "Tips online don't transfer to my body"
* "I need a plan, not more noise"

### **Emotional Experience (TAP INTO THESE)**

* **Overwhelm** from too much conflicting content
* **Frustration** from random trial-and-error
* **Hope** when guidance feels tailored
* **Empowerment** when they feel seen and understood

---

## **Creative Direction**

### **Tone & Style**

* Clear, concise, conversational
* Personal and specific, not lecture-like
* Authentic voice and practical examples
* Strong first 3 seconds; fast path to CTA

### **Visual Requirements**

* Show mobile quiz flow when possible
* Use simple b-roll + voiceover format if needed
* Include clear "take the quiz" moment
* 15-45 seconds ideal, 9:16 preferred

## **The 13 Ad Concepts**

Use these as prompts for your own delivery.

## **PERSONAS**

Use these personas to spark your own ideas for **who** you're speaking to before choosing an angle.

1. **The Female Golfer Seeking Better-Fit Guidance**: Wants coaching language and sequencing that feels designed for her.
2. **The Stuck Improver**: Works hard but doesn't know which fix actually matters first.
3. **The Busy Golfer**: Wants fast clarity and an immediate next step.
4. **The Skeptical Content Consumer**: Has seen too many generic promises and only responds to specific personalization.

# **PAIN-FOCUSED CONCEPTS**

## **Concept 1: Generic Advice Isn't Working** {#concept-1-generic-advice-isnt-working}

* **Angle**: Broad tips fail because they ignore individual context.
* **Hook Ideas**: "If generic tips worked, you'd already be better." / "You're not broken; your advice is too generic." / "Stop copying random drills."
* **Bridge**: Position quiz as fast personalization layer.
* **CTA**: "Take the quiz and get your next best step."

## **Concept 2: Most Golf Instruction Wasn't Built for You (Women's-Focused Angle)** {#concept-2-most-golf-instruction-wasnt-built-for-you-womens-focused-angle}

* **Angle**: Transcript-supported women-focused mismatch framing.
* **Hook Ideas**: "A lot of golf instruction wasn't built for women." / "Different body, different needs." / "You deserve a better-fit plan."
* **Bridge**: Keep respectful and empowering, not divisive.
* **CTA**: "Start with a quiz built to personalize your path."

## **Concept 3: Stop Guessing Your Swing Type** {#concept-3-stop-guessing-your-swing-type}

* **Angle**: Clarify swing circle and path-specific guidance.
* **Hook Ideas**: "Flat, incline, or vertical?" / "Most golfers don't actually know." / "Guessing costs progress."
* **Bridge**: Quiz identifies direction before prescribing drills.
* **CTA**: "Find your swing circle first."

## **Concept 4: Lessons But No Lasting Change** {#concept-4-lessons-but-no-lasting-change}

* **Angle**: Progress gap from non-tailored implementation.
* **Hook Ideas**: "You took lessons, but what stuck?" / "Information isn't personalization." / "Let's make it stick."
* **Bridge**: Quiz gives sequencing clarity and relevance.
* **CTA**: "Get a plan that matches how you move."

## **Concept 5: You Don't Need More Tips** {#concept-5-you-dont-need-more-tips}

* **Angle**: Content overload vs actionable path.
* **Hook Ideas**: "You need a path, not another tip." / "Too much content, not enough clarity." / "Start with diagnosis."
* **Bridge**: Position quiz as triage step.
* **CTA**: "Take the quiz, then train with direction."

## **Concept 6: The Average-Golfer Friction** {#concept-6-the-average-golfer-friction}

* **Angle**: Messaging mismatch from "pretend average golfer" scripts.
* **Hook Ideas**: "If ads feel fake, this is different." / "Real golfers, real fit." / "No forced persona acting."
* **Bridge**: Emphasize authentic creator voice.
* **CTA**: "Use the quiz to find your real path."

## **Concept 7: Why the Quiz Ads Are Winning** {#concept-7-why-the-quiz-ads-are-winning}

* **Angle**: Direct transcript signal that quiz creative has recent momentum.
* **Hook Ideas**: "These quiz ads are working for a reason." / "Simple hook, clear next step." / "Less pitch, more clarity."
* **Bridge**: Explain low-friction CTA model.
* **CTA**: "Click, take quiz, get matched."

## **Compliance Notes (DQFE)**

* Avoid promising specific score drops or guaranteed outcomes.
* Avoid absolute claims about gender biomechanics unless approved language is provided.
* Keep quiz claims to personalization and guidance, not guaranteed transformation.
