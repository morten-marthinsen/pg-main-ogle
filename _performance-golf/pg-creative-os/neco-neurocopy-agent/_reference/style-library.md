# Style Library — Winning Ad Styles from $100K+ Campaigns

This library documents the proven styles from Performance Golf's highest-performing ads. Use these styles in combination with Chase Hughes' frameworks for maximum impact.

**Source:** Reverse-engineered from $100K+ ad spend winners

---

## Intro/Hook Styles

### CURIOSITY
**What It Is:** Opens an information gap the brain demands to close.

**Pattern:**
- Unexpected statement or claim
- Incomplete information
- Question that can't be answered "no"
- Teases a secret/reveal

**Example Hooks:**
```
"There's ONE reason you slice the ball. And it has nothing to do with your grip."
"Most amateur golfers make this mistake on EVERY swing. Do you?"
"What if everything you learned about the golf swing was backwards?"
```

**When to Use:** Cold audiences, problem-unaware audiences, scroll-stopping needed

---

### PROCLAMATION
**What It Is:** Bold, authoritative claim that demands attention.

**Pattern:**
- Confident declarative statement
- No hedging or qualification
- Often contrarian or surprising
- Stakes a position

**Example Hooks:**
```
"Stop hitting balls. Yes, really."
"Your slice isn't a swing problem. It's a setup problem."
"The golf swing is easy. Instructors make it complicated."
```

**When to Use:** Establishing authority, contrarian positioning, cutting through noise

---

### CONTRARIAN
**What It Is:** Challenges conventional wisdom or common practice.

**Pattern:**
- "Everything you've been told about X is wrong"
- "Stop doing X" when X is common advice
- Positions against established beliefs

**Example Hooks:**
```
"Stop keeping your head down. Here's why."
"Forget everything your pro told you about grip."
"The MORE you practice, the WORSE you get. Here's why."
```

**When to Use:** Experienced audiences who've tried everything, differentiation from competitors

---

### OUTCOME
**What It Is:** Paints the desired end state vividly.

**Pattern:**
- Describes the transformation achieved
- Sensory details of success
- Before/after contrast
- Emotional payoff

**Example Hooks:**
```
"Imagine striping your driver 280 down the middle while your buddies watch in disbelief."
"Picture stepping up to the first tee with ZERO swing thoughts—just pure confidence."
"What would it feel like to finally shoot in the 70s?"
```

**When to Use:** Aspirational audiences, transformation-focused offers, emotional resonance

---

### EDUCATION
**What It Is:** Positions as teaching valuable information.

**Pattern:**
- "Here's what you need to know about..."
- "The truth about..."
- Framework or method reveal
- Insider knowledge sharing

**Example Hooks:**
```
"Here's what 70,000 lessons taught me about the amateur golf swing."
"The 3 fundamentals every low-handicapper has mastered."
"What Tiger's coach knows that your local pro doesn't."
```

**When to Use:** Building credibility, value-first positioning, solution-aware audiences

---

### DEMOGRAPHICS
**What It Is:** Calls out the specific audience directly.

**Pattern:**
- "If you're a [specific type]..."
- "For golfers who [specific situation]..."
- Age, skill level, or situation targeting

**Example Hooks:**
```
"If you're over 50 and losing distance, this is for you."
"For golfers who've tried everything and still slice..."
"Every amateur golfer shooting 90-100 needs to hear this."
```

**When to Use:** Audience targeting, relevance signaling, "this is for ME" feeling

## Body Styles

### CREDIBILITY
**What It Is:** Establishes authority and trust through proof.

**Pattern:**
- Credentials, titles, awards
- Volume proof (lessons, customers, years)
- Association proof (famous clients, endorsements)
- Media mentions

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"Martin Hall—Hall of Fame golf instructor with over 70,000 lessons to amateur golfers."
"Eric Cogorno—trusted by 1.2 million golfers on YouTube."
"The same method Tiger's former coach uses with tour players."
```

**When to Use:** Early in body to establish why they should listen

---

### UMP (Unique Mechanism Problem)
**What It Is:** Names the root cause of their problem.

**Pattern:**
- "The REAL reason you [problem]..."
- "It's not X, it's Y"
- Root cause identification
- Reframes the problem

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"The real reason you slice isn't your grip or your stance. It's this ONE move at the top of your backswing."
"You don't have a chipping problem. You have a SETUP problem."
"Every issue in your swing traces back to this single root flaw."
```

**When to Use:** After hook to reframe problem, prerequisite to solution

---

### UMS (Unique Mechanism Solution)
**What It Is:** Explains how the solution works.

**Pattern:**
- "Here's how it works..."
- Names the mechanism
- Explains why it's different
- Shows the logic

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"This 'Reverse Loop Trick' retrains your downswing path automatically—without thinking about 15 different positions."
"The ONE Connected System gives you the right drill, at the right time, in the right order."
"This simple setup adjustment fixes the root cause—and all the symptoms disappear."
```

**When to Use:** After UMP to show the path, builds belief in mechanism

---

### PAIN
**What It Is:** Agitates the problem, makes it feel urgent.

**Pattern:**
- Describes the frustration vividly
- Names specific emotions
- Physical sensations
- Specific moments of pain

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"You know that feeling—standing over the ball with 12 different swing thoughts, shoulders climbing, grip tightening..."
"Another Saturday morning making excuses to skip golf because you dread the embarrassment."
"Thousands spent on lessons that didn't stick. Training aids collecting dust in your garage."
```

**When to Use:** To create emotional urgency, validate their struggle, make change feel necessary

---

### OBJECTION
**What It Is:** Handles resistance and objections preemptively.

**Pattern:**
- "Even if you've tried X before..."
- "This works whether you're Y or Z..."
- Addresses specific doubts
- "What makes this different is..."

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"Even if you've tried every YouTube video and nothing worked—this is different because it addresses the ROOT cause."
"It doesn't matter if you're 25 or 75, athletic or not—this works because it's based on physics, not athleticism."
"Unlike lessons that treat symptoms, this fixes the ONE thing causing all your problems."
```

**When to Use:** Before CTA, after value presentation, to remove final barriers

---

### WHAT IT'S NOT
**What It Is:** Differentiates by naming what failed approaches look like.

**Pattern:**
- "This isn't [failed approach]"
- "Stop [ineffective method]"
- "Unlike [competitor approach]"
- Names the enemy/contrast

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"This isn't another random YouTube tip that contradicts what you learned last week."
"Stop watching hours of videos that give you 47 different things to think about."
"Unlike generic golf instruction, this is personalized to YOUR root flaw."
```

**When to Use:** Competitive differentiation, positioning against failed approaches, The Scattered Playbook contrast

---

### TIMEFRAME
**What It Is:** Anchors the speed or ease of transformation.

**Pattern:**
- Specific time ("5 minutes," "10 shots")
- Simplicity anchor ("just ONE thing")
- Effort framing ("no equipment needed")

**Example Body Elements:**
```
"In just 5 minutes, you'll understand why everything else failed."
"Fix your slice in 10 shots using this one simple drill."
"One practice session. That's all it takes to feel the difference."
```

**When to Use:** Making transformation feel attainable, reducing perceived effort

## Close/CTA Styles

### CTA (Call to Action)
**What It Is:** Clear direction for what to do next.

**Pattern:**
- Specific action verb
- Outcome reminder
- Platform-specific direction (if relevant)
- Urgency element (if true)

**Example CTAs:**
```
"Click through to the next page to see exactly how this works."
"Tap below and let's fix your slice together TODAY."
"If you're on desktop, click bottom left. Mobile, bottom right. I'll see you on the next page."
"Click now before we're out of stock—and grab your FREE bonus training."
```

**Required Elements:**
1. Clear action verb (click, tap, watch, grab)
2. What they get/where they're going
3. Outcome reminder (optional but recommended)
4. Urgency (only if genuine)

## Style Combinations by Ad Type

### Cold Traffic Awareness Ad
```
Hook: CURIOSITY or DEMOGRAPHICS
Body: UMP → CREDIBILITY → UMS → WHAT IT'S NOT
Close: CTA with outcome reminder
```

### Retargeting Ad
```
Hook: OUTCOME or PROCLAMATION
Body: TIMEFRAME → OBJECTION
Close: CTA with urgency
```

### Problem-Aware Ad
```
Hook: CONTRARIAN or PAIN (implied)
Body: UMP → CREDIBILITY → UMS
Close: CTA
```

### Authority-Building Ad
```
Hook: EDUCATION
Body: CREDIBILITY → UMP → UMS → OUTCOME
Close: CTA
```

## Style Selection by Audience

| Audience Segment | Best Hook Style | Best Body Progression |
|------------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Cold / Unaware | Curiosity, Demographics | UMP → Credibility → UMS |
| Problem-Aware | Contrarian, Pain | Credibility → UMP → UMS → Objection |
| Solution-Aware | Outcome, Proclamation | Credibility → UMS → Timeframe |
| Ready to Buy | Outcome, Timeframe | Objection → Timeframe → CTA |
| Skeptical | Contrarian, Education | Credibility → What It's Not → UMP → UMS |
| Frustrated | Pain, What It's Not | Credibility → UMP → UMS → Objection |

## Common Style Mistakes

❌ **All Pain, No Solution** — Agitating without showing the way out

❌ **Credibility Without Relevance** — Credentials that don't match the audience

❌ **Weak CTA** — "Learn more" instead of specific action + outcome

❌ **Missing UMP** — Solution without reframing the problem

❌ **Timeframe Without Proof** — Speed claims that aren't believable

❌ **Generic What It's Not** — Differentiation that doesn't land specifically
