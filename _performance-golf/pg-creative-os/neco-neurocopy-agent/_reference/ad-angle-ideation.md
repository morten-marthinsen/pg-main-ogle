# AD ANGLE IDEATION SUB-SKILL

## PURPOSE

Generate short-form ad angle names (2-4 words + one-sentence description) for influencer content. These angles must be instantly understood by influencers who will improvise 15-30 second content around them.

This is SEPARATE from full hook production. Ad Angle Ideation produces the seed idea; Hook Production expands it into full scripts.

---

## WHEN TO USE THIS SUB-SKILL

Use Ad Angle Ideation when:
- Creating content for influencer shoots
- Generating angle libraries for creative teams
- Brainstorming campaign directions before full copy production
- Producing short-form content briefs

Do NOT use for:
- Full ad scripts
- Long-form VSL hooks
- Complete campaign copy
- Anything requiring body copy or CTAs

---

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Each angle must follow this exact structure:

**[2-4 Word Name] — [One-sentence description that completes the emotional arc]**

Example:
> **The Yips Are Over** — That lightning bolt of fear before impact disappears when the sole glides instead of digs.

The name must:
- Be instantly understood by the target audience
- Create immediate emotional recognition or curiosity
- Be speakable (influencer can say it naturally)

The description must:
- Name the primal wound OR desire
- Show how the product resolves the wound or fulfills the desire
- Complete the emotional arc in one sentence

---

## THE 8 LAWS OF AD ANGLE IDEATION

### LAW 1: PRIMAL WOUND OR DESIRE FIRST, BENEFIT SECOND

Angles pull people in one of two directions:
- **Away from** a primal wound (unresolved distress, fear, pain, shame)
- **Toward** a primal desire (unfulfilled aspiration, status, recognition, freedom)

**Wrong:** "Chunk-Proof" (this is a feature statement)
**Wrong:** "Built To Glide" (this describes mechanism)
**Right (Wound):** "The Yips Are Over" (names the wound, implies resolution)
**Right (Desire):** "They'll Ask What Changed" (names the desire for recognition)

The angle must name the deep identity threat, tribal fear, or status anxiety (wound) OR the aspirational identity, tribal belonging, or status elevation (desire) — not the product benefit. The benefit resolves the wound or fulfills the desire, but the wound/desire is the hook.

### LAW 2: RESEARCH-GROUNDED LANGUAGE ONLY

**Wrong:** "The Flinch" (invented — sounds good but not their language)
**Wrong:** "Equipment Betrayal" (abstract concept, not how they talk)
**Right:** "It's Not Mental" (directly from PGA research framing)
**Right:** "The Yips Are Over" (yips = their exact word)

Every angle must be traceable to:
- Direct quotes from audience research
- Language in existing offer copy
- Terms the audience actually uses

If you cannot point to where the language came from, rewrite it.

### LAW 3: INSTANTLY UNDERSTOOD BY TARGET

**Wrong:** "Walk Up Like You Belong" (requires explanation)
**Wrong:** "The Tension Lifts" (vague, abstract)
**Right:** "They'll Ask What Changed" (golfer immediately understands)
**Right:** "Permission To Commit" (golfer knows exactly what this means)

Pressure-test every name: "Would the target read these 2-4 words and instantly feel something without reading the description?"

If the answer is no, rewrite.

### LAW 4: COMPLETE EMOTIONAL ARC

The description must do BOTH in one motion:
1. Name the primal wound OR desire
2. Show how this specific product resolves or fulfills it

**Wrong (pain only):** "That lightning bolt of fear before impact that makes you chunk."
**Wrong (desire only):** "Imagine your buddies respecting your short game."
**Wrong (solution only):** "The Auto-Glide Sole helps you make clean contact."

**Right (wound arc):** "That lightning bolt of fear before impact disappears when the sole glides instead of digs."
**Right (desire arc):** "Your buddies will notice when you start getting up and down; tell them it's the wedge."

The influencer needs to feel the arc: here's the wound/desire → here's how the product ends/fulfills it.

### LAW 5: SPECIFIC EMOTION, NOT CATEGORY

**Wrong:** "Overcome your fear" (fear is a category)
**Wrong:** "Build confidence" (confidence is a category)
**Right:** "That lightning bolt of fear before impact" (specific sensation, specific moment)
**Right:** "Your buddies will notice when you start getting up and down" (specific social outcome)

Name the:
- Exact physical sensation (tight chest, grip tightening, shoulders climbing)
- Exact moment (standing over a tight lie, walking to a bunker)
- Exact internal dialogue ("Please don't chunk it")

### LAW 6: VISCERAL RESONANCE TEST

Structural quality (pattern interrupt, curiosity gap, specificity) is necessary but not sufficient.

The ultimate test: **Does this create a feeling in the body when the target reads it?**

- Does it create recognition? ("That's me")
- Does it create desire? ("I want that")
- Does it create fear? ("I need to fix this")

If the angle scores high on craft but low on gut response, rewrite.

### LAW 7: INFLUENCER USABILITY

These angles are for influencers who will improvise 15-30 seconds of content. They need to:
- Read the name
- Instantly understand the emotional territory
- Riff on it authentically without a script

If the angle requires explanation beyond the one-sentence description, it fails.

**Test:** Could an influencer read this angle name, understand it in 3 seconds, and start talking about it naturally?

### LAW 8: PRODUCT-MECHANISM CONNECTION

The description must connect to the SPECIFIC product mechanism, not generic benefit language.

**Wrong:** "...because this wedge is more forgiving."
**Right:** "...when the sole glides instead of digs."
**Right:** "...the 40g counterweight stops the involuntary freeze."

The mechanism IS the resolution. Name it specifically.

## PROCESS: HOW TO GENERATE ANGLES

### STEP 1: DEEP AUDIENCE IMMERSION

Before generating any angles:
1. Read ALL audience research
2. Extract exact quotes describing pain (wounds)
3. Extract exact quotes describing aspirations (desires)
4. Note specific words they use
5. Identify physical sensations mentioned
6. Find specific moments they reference
7. Understand their internal dialogue

**Create a language bank** of their exact words before writing anything.

### STEP 2: IDENTIFY PRIMAL TERRITORIES

Using the NeuroCopy frameworks, identify the psychological territories available:

From FATE:
- What FOCUS triggers exist? (novelty, threat, value signals)
- What AUTHORITY angles exist? (credentials, proof, demonstrations)
- What TRIBE dynamics exist? (identity, belonging, exclusion fear, status elevation)
- What EMOTION triggers exist? (specific feelings, physical sensations)

From Behavior Compass:
- What NEEDS are unmet? (Significance, Acceptance, Reassurance, etc.)
- What DECISION styles dominate? (Investment, Necessity, Social, etc.)
- What VALUES do they seek? (Recognition, Freedom, Growth, etc.)

### STEP 3: MAP PRODUCT MECHANISMS TO WOUNDS OR DESIRES

For each primal wound or desire identified, map which product mechanism resolves or fulfills it:

| Wound/Desire | Type | Mechanism | Resolution/Fulfillment |
|--------------|------|-----------|------------------------|
| Fear of digging | Wound | Auto-Glide Sole | Sole glides instead of digs |
| Involuntary deceleration | Wound | 40g Counterweight | Forces swing to finish |
| Technique self-blame | Wound | Forgiveness design | Equipment was the problem |
| Earning respect from foursome | Desire | Consistent performance | Become the one they count on |
| Being asked "what changed?" | Desire | Visible improvement | Social recognition of transformation |
| Swinging freely without thought | Desire | Auto-Glide Sole | Mental freedom, just play |

### STEP 4: GENERATE ANGLES (Wound/Desire + Resolution/Fulfillment)

Write angles that fuse the wound or desire with the mechanism resolution or fulfillment:

**Formula (Wound):** [Wound named in their language] + [Mechanism resolution]
**Formula (Desire):** [Desire named in their language] + [Mechanism fulfillment]

Draft 30+ raw angles (mix of wound and desire), then filter.

### STEP 5: FILTER WITH THE 8 LAWS

Run each angle through all 8 laws:
1. Primal wound or desire first? ✓/✗
2. Research-grounded language? ✓/✗
3. Instantly understood? ✓/✗
4. Complete emotional arc? ✓/✗
5. Specific emotion? ✓/✗
6. Visceral resonance? ✓/✗
7. Influencer usable? ✓/✗
8. Product-mechanism connected? ✓/✗

Angles that fail any law get rewritten or cut.

### STEP 6: PRIORITIZE BY PSYCHOLOGICAL WEIGHT

Rank surviving angles by:
1. **Depth of wound/desire** — How primal is the pain or aspiration? (tribal exclusion > mild frustration; status elevation > minor convenience)
2. **Resonance breadth** — What % of audience feels this?
3. **Uniqueness** — Is this angle overused in market?
4. **Mechanism fit** — How directly does product resolve this wound or fulfill this desire?

Top 10 = Deepest wounds/desires + Broadest resonance + Unique positioning + Direct mechanism fit

## QUALITY CHECKLIST (Before Delivery)

For each angle, verify:

- [ ] Name is 2-4 words
- [ ] Name is instantly understood by target audience
- [ ] Name creates emotional response (recognition, desire, or fear)
- [ ] Description names the primal wound OR desire
- [ ] Description shows product resolution (wound) OR fulfillment (desire) via specific mechanism
- [ ] Description completes the emotional arc in one sentence
- [ ] All language is traceable to research (no invented emotional language)
- [ ] Influencer could riff on this without additional explanation
- [ ] Mix of wound and desire angles across the full set

---

## HARD RULES (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Never invent emotional language** — If you can't trace it to research, don't use it.
2. **Never deliver angles that require explanation** — If the name doesn't land instantly, rewrite.
3. **Never separate wound/desire from resolution/fulfillment** — Every description must complete the arc.
4. **Never use category emotions** — Specific sensations, moments, and dialogue only.
5. **Never skip the research immersion step** — Angles without research grounding will fail.
6. **Always include both wound and desire angles** — A complete angle set moves people away from pain AND toward aspiration.
7. **Always connect to specific product mechanism** — Generic benefits fail; named mechanisms succeed.
