# The Behavior Compass - Audience Profiling

The Behavior Compass reveals the hidden psychology driving purchase decisions through three interconnected maps: Needs, Decisions, and Values.

**Core Insight**: When you profile someone's needs, you're seeing what they were missing during their developmental years. You're looking into their past to influence their present.

## The Needs Map

**What It Reveals**: What someone craves from social interactions and what produces chemical cascades in their brain.

### The Six Core Needs

| Need | Definition | What They Seek | Trigger Language |
|------|------------|----------------|------------------|
| **Significance** | Feel important, exceptional | Recognition, status, being special | "You're not like everyone else", "Few people understand this" |
| **Intelligence** | Feel smart, competent | Validation of their knowledge | "Smart people know...", "You've probably figured out..." |
| **Acceptance** | Feel welcomed, belonging | Inclusion, non-judgment | "You belong here", "No matter where you're starting" |
| **Pity** | Feel understood in struggle | Sympathy, acknowledgment of difficulty | "I know it's been hard", "You've been through a lot" |
| **Approval** | Feel validated by others | External confirmation | "You're doing the right thing", "This is the smart choice" |
| **Reassurance** | Feel safe, certain | Comfort, reduced anxiety | "This is guaranteed", "You can't mess this up" |

### Identifying Needs in Copy Research

**Listen for these patterns**:

Significance: "I want to stand out", "I'm different from most people"
Intelligence: "I've done my research", "I understand the nuances"
Acceptance: "I just want to fit in", "Am I doing this right?"
Pity: "No one understands my situation", "It's harder for me because..."
Approval: "What do you think I should do?", "Is this the right choice?"
Reassurance: "What if it doesn't work?", "How do I know this is safe?"

### Needs-Based Messaging

**Significance-Driven Copy**:
```
"This isn't for everyone. In fact, most people will scroll right past this—and that's fine. But if you're the kind of person who refuses to settle for average results..."
```

**Intelligence-Driven Copy**:
```
"You've probably already noticed what most marketers miss: the metrics everyone celebrates are actually vanity metrics. Here's what actually matters..."
```

**Acceptance-Driven Copy**:
```
"Whether you're just getting started or you've been in the game for years—you belong here. No judgment. No gatekeeping. Just results."
```

**Reassurance-Driven Copy**:
```
"I know you've been burned before. That's exactly why we offer a 365-day guarantee, unlimited support, and a proven system that's worked for 12,847 people just like you."
```

## The Decision Map

**What It Reveals**: How someone makes decisions about what to do to meet their needs.

### The Six Decision Styles

| Style | Core Question | Decision Driver | Buying Signals |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Deviance** | "How is this different?" | Standing apart from the crowd | Tattoos, unconventional choices, counter-culture signals |
| **Novelty** | "Is this new/exciting?" | Fresh experiences, latest things | Early adopter behavior, interest in "new" and "first" |
| **Social** | "Will this help me connect?" | Relationships, social status | Focus on how others will react, community-oriented |
| **Conformity** | "Is this what others do?" | Fitting in, social proof | "What do most people choose?", follows trends |
| **Investment** | "What's the ROI?" | Long-term return, value | Quality over price, research-heavy, durability focus |
| **Necessity** | "Do I actually need this?" | Practical, functional utility | Skeptical of extras, "If it ain't broke" mentality |

### Identifying Decision Styles

**Phone Case Shopping Example**:

- **Deviance**: Seeks the most unusual, statement-making case
- **Novelty**: Wants the newest model, minimal design to show off phone
- **Social**: Chooses case with team logo or conversation-starter
- **Conformity**: Picks what looks like everyone else's case
- **Investment**: Researches protection ratings, warranty, durability
- **Necessity**: Already has a cracked phone, "it works fine"

### Decision-Style Copy Frameworks

**For Deviance Buyers**:
```
"While everyone else follows the same tired playbook, you're about to discover the strategy they hope stays underground..."
```

**For Novelty Buyers**:
```
"Just released: The first-ever [innovation]. Be among the first 100 to get access before the public launch."
```

**For Social Buyers**:
```
"Imagine showing up to your next mastermind and casually mentioning your [impressive result]. Watch how the conversation shifts."
```

**For Conformity Buyers**:
```
"47,000+ professionals have made this their go-to solution. Here's why it's become the industry standard..."
```

**For Investment Buyers**:
```
"This isn't a course—it's an asset. The skills you'll build pay dividends for the next decade. Here's the math..."
```

**For Necessity Buyers**:
```
"No fluff. No upsells. Just the essential toolkit you need to solve [specific problem]. Nothing more, nothing less."
```

## The Values Map

**What It Reveals**: What someone ultimately wants from life—the destination all their decisions are building toward.

### The Six Core Values

| Value | Core Desire | Life Vision | Trigger Language |
|-------|------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Connection** | Deep relationships | Being surrounded by loved ones | "Nothing matters more than who you share life with" |
| **Information** | Knowledge, access | Having insights others don't | "Access to information is the ultimate power" |
| **Recognition** | Being seen, celebrated | World acknowledging achievements | "When people finally see what you've built" |
| **Experience** | Rich memories | Looking back on an adventure | "A life well-lived is measured in moments" |
| **Freedom** | Choice, autonomy | Answering to no one | "Doing whatever you want, whenever you want" |
| **Growth** | Becoming better | Reaching full potential | "The only competition is who you were yesterday" |

### Identifying Values in Language

**Listen for phrases like**:

- Connection: "At the end of the day, what matters is family and friends"
- Information: "I'd finally have access to what no one else can see"
- Recognition: "People would finally see what I've accomplished"
- Experience: "I could travel anywhere, try anything"
- Freedom: "I could finally do whatever I want"
- Growth: "I'd finally reach my potential"

### Values-Based Vision Casting

**Connection-Focused Close**:
```
"Imagine sitting at dinner with the people you love most, present in the moment because the business runs without you. No phone buzzing. No Sunday dread. Just the faces that matter most."
```

**Recognition-Focused Close**:
```
"Picture the moment your name appears on that list. The emails from peers congratulating you. The screenshot you'll send to everyone who doubted this was possible."
```

**Freedom-Focused Close**:
```
"Wake up Tuesday. Check your phone. Decide you want to spend the day at the beach. Do it. No permission needed. No meetings to cancel. This is what freedom actually feels like."
```

**Growth-Focused Close**:
```
"A year from now, you'll look back at who you are today and barely recognize that person. Every ceiling you've hit will become a floor. That's not motivation—that's the path we're building together."
```

## Connecting the Maps

The three maps work together in a sequence:

**"If I [Decision], then people will [Needs], and I can finally [Values]."**

### Example: High-Ticket Coach Buyer

- **Needs**: Significance + Intelligence (wants to feel special and smart)
- **Decision Style**: Investment (ROI-focused, quality over price)
- **Values**: Recognition (wants world to see their success)

**Copy Approach**:
```
"Most entrepreneurs throw money at tactics. You're different—you invest in transformation. [INTELLIGENCE + INVESTMENT]

This program isn't for everyone. We cap enrollment at 50 because that's what allows for real results. [SIGNIFICANCE]

12 months from now, you won't just have a bigger business—you'll have a story people actually want to hear. The case study your industry talks about. [RECOGNITION]

The ROI calculation is simple: [detailed breakdown]. This pays for itself by month 3. [INVESTMENT]"
```

## Copy Applications

### Behavior Compass Diagnostic Questions

**Before writing, determine**:

1. What social validation does my audience crave? (Needs)
2. How do they typically evaluate purchases? (Decision Style)
3. What life outcome are they ultimately building toward? (Values)

### Matching Copy to Compass

| Funnel Stage | Primary Map | Why |
|--------------|-------------|-----|
| Ad Hook | Needs | Immediate emotional resonance |
| Landing Page Body | Decision Style | Match how they evaluate |
| Close/CTA | Values | Connect to ultimate desire |
| Email Nurture | Needs → Decision → Values | Build relationship through full sequence |
| Objection Handling | Decision Style | Address how they evaluate risk |

### Persona-Based Copy Matrix

Create copy variations based on Compass combinations:

| Audience Segment | Needs | Decision | Values | Lead Angle |
|-----------------|-------|----------|--------|------------|
| Aspiring CEO | Significance | Investment | Recognition | "The playbook elite leaders use" |
| Burnt-out Freelancer | Reassurance | Necessity | Freedom | "The simple system to escape client jail" |
| Status-Conscious Pro | Intelligence | Conformity | Information | "What top 1% performers know" |
| Creative Rebel | Acceptance | Deviance | Experience | "Do it your way and actually win" |
