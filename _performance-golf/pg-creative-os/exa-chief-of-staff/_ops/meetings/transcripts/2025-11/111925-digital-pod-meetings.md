### Overview

The meeting reviewed the weekly performance of various digital product offers, focusing on spend, conversion rates, and average order values for the period of November 11 through November 17. The team analyzed the impact of Black Friday sales, creative asset performance, and funnel optimization strategies to guide future improvements.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team decided to abandon the Black Friday sale for OSSF and revert to pre–Black Friday pricing to address the drop in average order value.
*   They agreed to convert the approved Black Friday creatives for OSSF to evergreen versions and implement a roll-out of six new creatives weekly for the Japanese market.
*   The team chose to maintain the Black Friday sale for the SSTS offer through the end of the month to capture higher buyer intent.
*   A plan was set to review and potentially pause underperforming PG1 campaigns while exploring a $1 trial option and an improved upsell flow.
*   Danielle implemented a hack to remove the problematic pricing banner for OSSF, addressing immediate technical issues.

### Key Topics
*   **Weekly Performance Overview** Update
    *   Jesus provided a comprehensive review of the week’s performance (November 11-17) across various digital product offers.
    *   Key metrics such as spend, net loss per trial, average order value (AOV), and conversion rates were discussed.
    *   The overall performance was identified as being in the red, though some offers showed signs of potential turnaround.
    
*   **OSSF Performance and Black Friday Sale Impact** Decision
    *   Jesus highlighted that OSSF performance deteriorated, particularly noting a drop in AOV from over $100 to $86.
    *   It was identified that the Black Friday sale was adversely affecting conversion rates and overall performance.
    *   A decision was made to abandon the Black Friday sale for OSSF and revert to pre–Black Friday pricing to help improve AOV and conversion metrics.
    
*   **Creative Updates and Japanese Market Strategy** Decision
    *   Cameron reported that some Black Friday creatives for OSSF, initially approved last week, had not been converted to evergreen and planned to address this promptly.
    *   A new creative strategy for the Japanese market was discussed, involving the rollout of six new creatives each week (five expansions and one native creative) to combat creative fatigue.
    *   The approach aims to stabilize and potentially improve performance in the Japanese market despite its smaller size.
    
*   **WPSS Offer Performance Update** Update
    *   Jesus noted significant improvement in WPSS performance, with an increase in spend and net ROI.
    *   AOV reverted back to the healthy range observed pre–Black Friday sale.
    *   The Black Friday creatives outperformed evergreen ads for WPSS, suggesting that the sale elements were effective for this offer.
    
*   **SSTS Offer Analysis and Sale Continuation** Decision
    *   Discussion focused on SSTS where spend increased and conversion rates improved slightly, but AOV remained a major challenge.
    *   It was observed that the Black Friday sale did not significantly affect AOV for SSTS, maintaining the low price point in the 60s.
    *   The team agreed to continue the Black Friday sale on SSTS through the end of the month to capitalize on upcoming high buyer intent days.
    
*   **Quiz and PG1 Funnel Optimization** Decision
    *   Christopher raised concerns about the ongoing performance of the PG1 campaigns, suggesting they be paused due to low conversion rates.
    *   The discussion included ideas to optimize the quiz funnel by integrating app sign-ups at various points (e.g., on exit or upon quiz completion) to drive higher conversions.
    *   There was consideration of introducing a $1 trial (instead of a free trial) to ensure commitment by capturing credit card details, followed by developing a robust upsell flow.
    *   Action items include syncing with Gabe regarding the PG1 campaigns and planning a meeting with Todd to discuss next steps for the PG1 funnel.
    
*   **Technical Updates** Update
    *   Danielle provided a technical update indicating that a hack was implemented to remove the problematic pricing banner on OSSF.
    *   The resolution is expected to improve performance by eliminating the pricing distraction until a permanent fix is deployed.
