### Overview

The meeting reviewed the weekly performance of digital products, focusing on key metrics and challenges across various channels including WPSS, OSSF, SSTs, and the quiz funnel. The team analyzed creative optimization needs, technical issues, and strategic decisions for scaling, particularly in the Japanese market and ahead of Black Friday.

*   Technical issues in the quiz funnel—including looping video delays and missing upsell conversions—were flagged for immediate investigation.
*   The team resolved to follow up on PG1 launch updates and Hay Flow account access to ensure smooth operations.

### Key Topics
*   **Overall Digital Product Performance Metrics** Update
    *   Jesus Rodriguez reported an 80% increase in overall performance metrics, highlighting improvements in net ROAS, new customer net ROAS, and a reduction in net loss per trial.
    *   He noted that while most days showed sustainability, one particularly poor day significantly impacted performance.
    *   Metrics across various offers were reviewed to set the context for deeper analysis.
    
*   **WPSS Offer Performance Analysis** Update
    *   Jesus detailed that WPSS started strong with four consecutive green days but ended the week in the red due to deteriorating creative performance and bidding issues.
    *   He mentioned that although new customer acquisition was rising, the overall performance was dragged down by underperforming creatives (fake pages and the PSS scale page).
    *   Geographic performance varied; UK, New Zealand, and Singapore performed well, whereas Australia and Canada missed targets.
    *   Jesus and the team recognized the need for deeper review to understand the conversion breakdown, especially given the rocky performance in recent days.
    
*   **OSSF and Japanese Funnel Scaling Strategy** Decision
    *   Jesus reported robust performance from OSSF with more than doubled spending, increased net ROAS, and significant improvements in new customer metrics.
    *   The Japanese funnel, part of OSSF, was specifically highlighted, with impressive metrics such as 12K spend at 135 ROAS.
    *   A strategic debate was held regarding how aggressively to scale the Japanese market given the absence of an established recurring revenue stream.
    *   Gabe suggested maintaining scaling efforts as long as the net ROAS target of 120% was achieved, with future plans including backend work to generate recurring revenue and potential physical product shipments from China starting February 2026.
    
*   **Creative Optimization and Coordination for Japanese Market** Decision
    *   The discussion focused on optimizing creative assets for the Japanese market by adapting top-performing ads.
    *   Questions were raised about the creative agency’s turnaround time, costs, and whether internal resources could prepare the necessary briefs for translation and adaptation.
    *   Christopher requested that Cameron initiate a conversation with Donnie and Michael to clarify these aspects, and to potentially negotiate a process that would allow quicker modifications, such as creating ad expansions from existing assets.
    
*   **Hay Flow Subscription Access Issue** Update
    *   Jesus raised a concern regarding access to the Hay Flow platform for updating the Quiz Funnel’s URL, as he could not log in.
    *   Gabe and Cameron clarified that the subscription had transitioned from free to paid membership, and mentioned previous credit card issues.
    *   Action was noted to follow up on the access issue to ensure smooth operation.
    
*   **SSTs Funnel Underperformance** Problem
    *   Jesus mentioned that the SSTs offer posted underwhelming performance with limited ad launches (6 ads) and subpar conversion metrics.
    *   There was debate about whether to pause the offer or analyze the conversion rate issues further.
    *   Christopher emphasized the need to investigate the breakdown in conversion rates, including potential issues with AOV and specific funnel steps, and scheduled a follow-up meeting with Danielle, Catherine, and himself.
    
*   **Quiz Funnel and Technical Issues** Problem
    *   Jesus reported that the Quiz Funnel had a poor start, with a net negative performance and issues like a 71-second average wait time on the results page due to a looping video error.
    *   It was observed that all sales were at the $47 front-end digital product level, and there were no bump or upsell sales, raising concerns about potential technical problems possibly identified during QA.
    *   Christopher suggested investigating the upsell conversion issue, and the team planned to examine if the breakdown occurred during the VSL or the checkout process.
    
*   **PG1 Launch and Appsflyer Delay** Problem
    *   Gabe updated the team on the PG1 launch, indicating that the rollout of swing circle content videos into the PG1 subscription was delayed due to an Appsflyer hold-up.
    *   He mentioned waiting for further updates from Peter and planned to follow up later in the day, with Christopher suggesting a timely follow-up to get clarity.
    
*   **Black Friday Creatives** Update
    *   Cameron and Renee discussed the pending status of Black Friday digital ad creatives, noting that many ads were approved but not yet live.
    *   Cameron emphasized the urgency of getting these creatives live in time for the Black Friday period, to which Jesus agreed and planned to coordinate follow-up actions.
