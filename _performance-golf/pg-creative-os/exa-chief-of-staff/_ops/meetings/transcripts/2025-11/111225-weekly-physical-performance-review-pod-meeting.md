### Overview

The meeting reviewed comprehensive performance metrics and creative strategies for the ongoing Black Friday and holiday campaigns. The discussion focused on analyzing spend figures, creative performance, data reporting issues, and market dynamics to prepare for both immediate and upcoming seasonal challenges.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team implemented a fixed return multiplier of 8% across campaigns to standardize net loss calculations.
*   The team addressed technical issues with COGS data ingestion in DOMO, ensuring that updated metrics reflect true performance.
*   The team optimized creative strategies by revising static ad overlays, testing varied video formats, and adjusting bid strategies to manage daily spend effectively.
*   The team acknowledged fluctuations in international markets and discussed adjusting creative rollout to combat campaign fatigue.
*   The team reviewed potential upsell opportunities for Click Stick and shared initial performance insights for the Drawforce launch.

### Key Topics
*   **Campaign Performance Overview** Update
    *   Samuel Mercado initiated the meeting with highlights on overall campaign performance including a notable 179% increase in spend and over 100 new scratch trials on the first day.
    *   The performance review covered both meta and physical product channels, with metrics indicating early success despite some tracking issues in the first three days.
    *   There was discussion about balancing spend and ensuring net loss per trial finishes consistently around the -40 to -50 range.
    
*   **Fixed Return Multiplier Implementation & COGS Data Correction** Decision
    *   Gabe Medeiros announced the approval to implement a fixed return multiplier of 8%, with the new 'net loss per trial' column reflecting a constant value.
    *   A past issue with the COGS data ingestion in DOMO was clarified; previously, missing data had changed net loss per trial from -39 to -60, though this has now been corrected.
    *   The corrected COGS data has been applied to November, and steps have been taken to prevent a recurrence of the tracking issue.
    
*   **Market Dynamics: US vs. International Performance** Update
    *   There was a discussion regarding market spend distribution, noting that while US campaigns received the majority of the spend, international performance (in Australia, UK, and others) fluctuated more due to smaller market sizes.
    *   Samuel and Omer provided hypotheses that international markets quickly tap out due to lower spend levels leading to volatility, and adjustments (like pausing and re-launching creatives) would be applied to manage fatigue.
    *   Morten Marthinsen and others suggested that brand awareness efforts might further stabilize performance internationally, though market size was identified as a primary factor.
    
*   **Creative Strategy and Testing** Idea
    *   In-depth analysis was provided on creative performance, particularly comparing static ads and video ads with Black Friday messaging.
    *   For the 357 product, static creatives with Black Friday overlays showed strong performance, while video advertising performance varied across products (e.g., SF1 and Click Stick benefitted from sale overlays).
    *   There were discussions on testing shorter video formats (ranging from 10 to 60 seconds) on both Google Shorts and Meta’s bumper ads to improve engagement and lower CPMs.
    *   Omer shared insights on using full real estate for desktops and the benefits observed during both image and video creative testing.
    
*   **Campaign Adjustments: SF1 and Click Stick** Update
    *   For SF1, initial performance was weak, leading to a strategy change from launching separate dedicated campaigns to grouping creatives under a cost cap strategy to maintain consistent performance.
    *   The meeting detailed adjustments like the tactic of lowering bids early in the day to manage spend and then increasing them later to optimize performance.
    *   Click Stick campaigns were noted for their slow start during the day, but later performance improved with a final net loss per trial of around -30, although cost per click remains higher than desired.
    *   Christopher Ogle raised the possibility of using an upsell (potentially the product 359) to complement the Click Stick funnel, suggesting a low-hanging fruit opportunity to boost revenue.
    
*   **Drawforce Launch Performance** Update
    *   An update was provided on Drawforce's launch where initial performance was disappointing with high CPMs and low conversions despite testing new creatives.
    *   After the initial overspend, budgets were pulled back resulting in a more controlled cost per action (~$152 CPA), but overall scalability remains a concern.
    *   The team planned to support Drawforce by leveraging the upcoming Collections page from Danielle and the CRO team after final quality assurance checks.
    
*   **Next Steps and Action Items**
    *   Gabe Medeiros planned to follow up with FARAS regarding out-of-stock messaging for Christmas to ensure proper customer communication.
    *   The team decided to continue experimenting with creative performance, including further testing of short-form videos on Google and Meta platforms.
    *   Samuel Mercado mentioned ongoing adjustments to campaign bidding strategies to control daily spend and improve performance consistency.
    *   There was an acknowledgment of the need to review upsell strategies for Click Stick (and possibly product 359) to enhance funnel performance.
