### Overview

The meeting focused on reviewing the performance of the digital quiz funnel and planning the launch of the new PG1 subscription funnel. The team discussed current metrics, technical blockers, and strategies to optimize ad spend and user engagement while preparing for an upcoming test campaign.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team decided to launch a controlled test campaign for the PG1 subscription funnel with a budget of $300 per day for one week to gather early performance data.
*   The team agreed to focus ad spend on top-performing quiz ads by having JROD review current ad performance.
*   The results page video update was approved to reduce the 71-second delay and improve click-through rates.
*   The PG1 subscription funnel was confirmed as a web-based checkout process that immediately captures revenue, while the Apps Flyer tracking integration has been delayed until November 17.
*   Data ingestion issues in DOMO were acknowledged as a factor in current performance metrics, prompting closer monitoring as the Black Friday campaign unfolds.

### Key Topics
*   **Monthly Optimization Update & Quiz Insights** Update
    *   Christopher informed the team that Maeve would deliver a detailed monthly optimization update every Friday, including insights from quiz interactions, ad performance (via Domo), and Mixpanel data.
    *   The update aims to integrate data from ad performance, Quiz metrics, and additional platforms to provide a comprehensive view of campaign performance.
    
*   **Quiz Funnel Performance Metrics** Update
    *   The quiz completion rate was reported to be high (around 89–90%), though the start rate of the quiz was low, prompting investigation into the drop-off.
    *   Discussion included insights on quiz engagement, with remarks on how well the current funnel captures user interest along the quiz-to-VSL sequence.
    
*   **Results Page Video Issue and Resolution** Update
    *   The team noted an issue with the results page video, where a 71-second delay was observed before users clicked the call-to-action.
    *   A new video implementation was planned, which now clearly transitions from 0 to 100% and prompts users with a text cue ('your results are ready, click the button below') to improve click-through rates.
    
*   **Discussion on PG1 Subscription Funnel & Apps Flyer Tracking** Decision
    *   The discussion shifted to launching a dedicated quiz funnel for the PG1 subscription, which would test whether users would subscribe for a $39 monthly option or the yearly plan.
    *   The main blocker identified was the delay in Apps Flyer tracking integration (now pushed to November 17th), limiting the ability to track revenue directly.
    *   Gabe and Christopher agreed to run a controlled, low-budget test campaign (proposed at $300 per day for a week) to gather qualitative data on app downloads and user engagement.
    *   It was clarified that the PG1 funnel was web-based and would capture revenue at the checkout before routing users to download the app, with full tracking data to be integrated later.
    
*   **Ad Strategy and Targeted Budget Allocation** Decision
    *   Christopher proposed focusing on top-performing quiz ads rather than launching all the available ads, to maximize ad spend efficiency.
    *   JROD was tasked with reviewing and identifying the best-performing ads to optimize further spending.
    *   Gabe agreed to align on this strategy with Peter and to update Faraz regarding budget considerations.
    
*   **Sales, Upsell Performance, and Funnel Congruence** Problem
    *   The current sales performance through existing funnels (SSTS and WPSS) was reviewed; sales were below KPIs and upsell uptake was minimal.
    *   There was discussion on the lack of continuity between the quiz and the subsequent funnel offers, with concerns that the current upsell may not align well with user expectations.
    *   Donnie and Christopher debated integrating a new upsell video (for the Click Stick as a swing circle trainer), with differing views on how it fits with the overall messaging.
    *   Jesus provided demographic spending data, clarifying that the majority of spending and sales came from the 55+ age group, contradicting earlier assumptions about younger users.
    
*   **Data Integrity and DOMO Ingestion Issues** Update
    *   Gabe informed the team that a connection issue had prevented certain COGS (codes 357 and 359) from being ingested into DOMO, skewing October performance metrics.
    *   The corrected data now shows a worse net loss per trial, affecting the perception of performance, though upcoming campaigns (like Black Friday) are performing well.
    
*   **Final Next Steps and Action Items** Decision
    *   The team agreed to launch the test campaign for the PG1 subscription funnel as soon as the funnel was fully ready.
    *   Christopher committed to sending the checkout page links and other funnel materials to Gabe (and Bill) to confirm proper DOMO tracking.
    *   Participants acknowledged that initial tests would provide critical signals, such as app downloads and conversion actions, to optimize the funnel before scaling.
    *   Team members, including Gabe, Todd, and Omer, expressed alignment on the approach, while cautioning that low signal volumes might limit granular optimization.
