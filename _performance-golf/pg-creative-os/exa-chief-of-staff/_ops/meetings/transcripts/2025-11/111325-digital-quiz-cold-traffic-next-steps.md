### Key Topics
*   **Introduction and Meeting Objectives**
    *   Christopher opened the meeting by addressing the need to review and optimize the new PG1 subscription funnel, particularly focusing on the quiz-to-VSL (Video Sales Letter) sequence.
    *   The team acknowledged the urgency of discussing early data insights and initiating rapid iterations for improving conversion rates.
    
*   **Review of Early Performance Data** Update
    *   The team reviewed initial data showing 261 quiz starts, 235 completed quizzes, and only 141 VSL plays, highlighting a significant drop-off between quiz completion and video engagement.
    *   It was noted that the current start rate (hovering around 25-38%) was far below the industry standard of 60%+, and the funnel had generated zero sales despite considerable ad spend.
    *   The group stressed the importance of dissecting the funnel steps to diagnose where user drop-offs were occurring.
    
*   **VSL Optimization Strategy** Decision
    *   Christopher detailed plans to optimize the VSL by submitting a ticket to the VSL team with Vlad as the point person.
    *   The proposed VSL improvements include adding high-ticket 'gold school' content, one-on-one lesson elements, and real-user testimonials to enhance engagement and credibility.
    *   The revised VSL is expected to address drop-offs and boost conversion rates by providing clearer proof of value.
    
*   **Technical and Data Reporting Challenges** Problem
    *   Issues regarding data access and consistency were discussed, with concerns about exporting CSV data from Metalytics, and discrepancies between Wistia and Vitalytics reporting.
    *   Katherine confirmed that most data was coming in through Mixpanel, while Maeve and Danielle noted the need for seamless tracking on quiz videos and VSL engagement.
    *   Maeve was tasked with securing access to Mixpanel and investigating the tracking differences on Wistia-hosted videos.
    
*   **Quiz Interface Enhancements and Progress Bar Adjustments** Idea
    *   The discussion included optimizing the quiz flow by updating the first question to 'Which swing circle do you have?' and incorporating a PNG image of the swing circles with differentiated colors (blue for men, pink for women) to build curiosity and engagement.
    *   There was a focus on fixing the progress bar issue, which was distorted by extra dummy or redirected pages in the current setup.
    *   Maeve mentioned plans to duplicate the quiz funnel for parallel testing, ensuring that proper data integration (with Domo, Mixpanel, etc.) is maintained.
    
*   **Ad Creative Insights and External Feedback** Idea
    *   Gabe shared insights from his discussions with Facebook and Meta, noting that successful funnels for app subscriptions typically sell through quizzes rather than VSLs.
    *   The team reviewed high-performing ad creatives, such as UGC ads featuring lessons from Martin, and considered integrating contextual imagery in the ads to match the landing page content.
    *   Donnie suggested testing variations with a radically altered first question or landing page to determine if it improves the quiz start rate.
    
*   **Testing, Budget Considerations, and Parallel Funnel Strategy** Decision
    *   The team agreed to run a three-day test block (with a budget of approximately $300-$400 per day) to gauge the impact of the planned changes on the quiz start rate and overall conversion funnel.
    *   Discussions included the possibility of running both quiz versions in parallel, allowing the team to compare performance and adjust tactics quickly.
    *   There was also conversation around potential price adjustments for the subscription given the high pricing ($39/month) and its effect on conversion rates.
    
*   **Next Steps and Action Items** Decision
    *   Christopher committed to sending a PNG version of the swing circle image and updating the first quiz question accordingly.
    *   Maeve was tasked with duplicating the quiz funnel for testing, ensuring proper data tracking integration, and resolving the progress bar calculation by removing redundant pages.
    *   Katherine confirmed that she would assist with data access issues in Mixpanel to support the analysis of these changes.
    *   The team decided to monitor the changes over a three-day period and reconvene on Monday to review the data and make further adjustments as necessary.
