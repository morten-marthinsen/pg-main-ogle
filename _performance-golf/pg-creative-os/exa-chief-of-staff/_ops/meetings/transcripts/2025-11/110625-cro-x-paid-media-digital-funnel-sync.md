### Overview

The meeting focused on diagnosing and optimizing the digital sales funnels, with particular attention given to the underperforming WPSS funnel compared to the strong performance of OSSF. The discussion addressed switching from a closed video sales letter (VSL) to a redirect that sends traffic to a sales page, and it finalized the promotional timeline for Black Friday, Christmas/holiday, and New Year campaigns.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team decided to update the WPSS funnel by redirecting traffic from the closed VSL to an open sales page that clearly displays discount details for Black Friday.
*   They finalized the promotional timeline: extended Black Friday from November 29th to December 5th, a Christmas/New Year sale from December 6th to December 25th with a reduced 15% discount, and a New Year resolution campaign running until January 15th.
*   The well-performing OSSF funnel was maintained without any changes.
*   Technical tests confirmed that pricing and cart functionalities were working properly, supporting the planned funnel update.
*   Performance metrics will be closely monitored post-implementation to determine if further adjustments are necessary.

### Key Topics
*   **Digital Funnel Performance Evaluation** Update
    *   Jesus Rodriguez presented performance metrics, noting that the WPSS funnel had a low new customer conversion rate (0.26) and net loss per trial issues, while the OSSF funnel performed strongly.
    *   Danielle Schwolow and Gabe Medeiros discussed concerns regarding the underperformance of the WPSS funnel and customer confusion in the digital flow.
    
*   **WPSS Funnel Optimization Strategy** Problem
    *   The team examined the WPSS funnel performance and identified that the current closed video sales letter (VSL) format might be contributing to poor conversion rates, especially during Black Friday promotions.
    *   Discussion highlighted the contrast between the closed VSL where the discount was not immediately visible, and the potential benefits of an open VSL that displays discount pricing.
    
*   **Black Friday Ad Testing and VSL Redirection Decision** Decision
    *   Gabe Medeiros pointed out the discrepancy between the Black Friday ads on Facebook and the landing page experience, suggesting testing the open VSL instead of the closed VSL.
    *   It was decided to update the WPSS funnel by redirecting the VSL to a direct sales page with Black Friday messaging, allowing the discount to be visible immediately.
    *   This test was limited to WPSS with the intention to monitor conversion rate and average order value changes before further adjustments are made.
    
*   **Promotional Messaging and Campaign Scheduling for Q4** Decision
    *   The team mapped out the promotional timeline to maintain momentum through the holiday season.
    *   It was decided to extend the Black Friday sale for an additional week (from November 29th to December 5th) to continue leveraging discount momentum.
    *   Following the extended Black Friday period, the promotion would transition to a Christmas/New Year sale phase (from December 6th to December 25th) with a reduced discount (15%) to preserve brand value.
    *   Post-Christmas, the plan included launching a New Year resolution campaign (tentatively until January 15th) that would remove discount messaging, focusing on uplifting, non-discounted promotional language.
    
*   **Technical Coordination and Creative Updates** Update
    *   Danielle Schwolow confirmed that technical tests showed proper functioning of pricing integration and cart behavior, ensuring a smooth transition when redirecting traffic.
    *   Jesus Rodriguez mentioned that the updated Black Friday creative and batch updates have been rolled out, and the new campaigns would utilize the sales page URL to keep track of performance changes.
    *   Gabe Medeiros emphasized aligning creative assets with messaging changes to avoid customer confusion, and Cameron Fair stressed the need for hard deadlines to transition between promotions.
    
*   **Regional Performance and Creative Variations** Update
    *   The discussion briefly touched on regional performance differences, noting that while the OSSF funnel performed strongly overall, part of its success was attributed to performance in Japan.
    *   The team acknowledged the need to utilize tailored creative variations for specific markets, with additional Japanese creative assets in the pipeline.
