### Key Topics
*   **Organic vs. Paid Ad Production Strategy** Decision
    *   The team discussed the integration of influencer organic content with traditional paid ads, addressing whether to continue producing scripted ads with local talent or shift focus to influencer-generated organic content.
    *   Tim Filler emphasized the value of maintaining high-quality copy writing, while Christopher Ogle highlighted that the copy team would continue to produce ad scripts regardless of filming method.
    *   The discussion included the potential use of organic performance as a testing ground for ad angles before scaling up with paid traffic, ensuring that quality insights drive spending decisions.
    *   The group noted that relying solely on influencers for ad content without the structured script approach could present risk to consistency and performance.
    
*   **Streamlining the Ideation and Creative Brief Document** Decision
    *   Christopher Ogle introduced the concept of an ideation document to capture high-level creative ideas before finalizing briefs, ensuring that input from creators is gathered efficiently.
    *   Will McIlroy demonstrated an initial two-page document which was discussed and refined into a more concise one-page version to simplify communication with influencers.
    *   The objective was to clearly state the background, idea, and required elements (such as props and locations) in a creator-friendly format, enabling a quick review and approval process.
    *   The team agreed that this streamlined document would help jumpstart discussions with creators while keeping the process agile and focused.
    
*   **Process and Communication Systems** Update
    *   The meeting reviewed current challenges with internal documentation in ClickUp and communication via Slack, noting ongoing restructuring within the Creative Ops team.
    *   Christopher Ogle explained that due to the temporary setback in ClickUp documentation, the team would rely on Slack for immediate communications and tracking creative requests.
    *   Issues such as being removed from Slack channels were mentioned, underscoring the need for clear, consistent internal communication until new processes are fully implemented.
    
*   **Coordination of the Christmas/Gift-Giving Influencer Campaign** Decision
    *   A detailed discussion focused on an upcoming Christmas/gift-giving campaign with influencer Jerry, centered around a Santa-themed concept on a golf course.
    *   Tim Filler planned to have a call with Jerry to finalize creative details, clarify logistics (e.g., timeline set around early to mid-December), and resolve physical prop issues such as removing unrealistic elements (e.g., a decorated cart).
    *   The conversation addressed how Jerry would manage giving away products (like an SF1) and the importance of collecting raw footage to allow for flexible post-production editing.
    *   It was agreed that clear communication and a simple template for creative guidelines would support the campaign's smooth execution.
    
*   **Creative Brief Process and Asset Collection** Update
    *   Discussions were held regarding the process for documenting requests and creative briefs, emphasizing the need to capture key details (such as shoot dates, required assets, and timelines) efficiently.
    *   The team iterated on document revisions, ensuring that if an influencer edits their own content, raw footage is still sent for backup and flexibility in editing across different versions.
    *   Different ad length guidelines were debited, with a consensus toward keeping raw footage under a few minutes to maximize editing flexibility.
    *   This approach aimed to maintain clarity and consistency in creative deliverables even amid evolving strategic directions.
    
*   **Task and Priority Alignment for the Week** Update
    *   Chris Hibbert updated the team on his priority to complete beginner ad scripts and later develop expansion tickets to refresh existing ads.
    *   Will McIlroy outlined his plan to write five new supplement ads by Friday, with a strong emphasis on integrating AI into the ad creation process to expedite workflow and maintain competitiveness.
    *   The team discussed the upcoming follow-up conversation on leveraging AI for ad copy creation to formalize guidelines and reassess resource allocation.
    *   Aligning tasks and confirming individual responsibilities ensured that both strategic initiatives and day-to-day creative production remained on track.
