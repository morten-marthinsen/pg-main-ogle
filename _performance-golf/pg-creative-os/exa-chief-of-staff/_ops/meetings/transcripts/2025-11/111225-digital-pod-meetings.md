### Overview

The meeting focused on reviewing performance metrics across multiple digital marketing funnels—such as OSSF, WPSS, SSTS, and the quiz funnel—with a strong emphasis on testing creative assets and optimizing VSL approaches. The team discussed adjustments for upcoming campaigns, including Black Friday initiatives and strategies to improve conversion rates across domestic, international, and digital channels.

*   The team decided to monitor WPSS open VSL performance and potentially revert to a closed format if conversion rates do not improve.
*   A decision was made to refresh creative assets by collaborating on new templates for upcoming campaigns, including Scratch Club and beginner funnels.
*   The group agreed to expand the quiz funnel targeting beyond the 55+ demographic to capture broader performance insights.
*   Emphasis was placed on testing Google and YouTube campaigns by leveraging YouTube shorts and optimizing desktop ad placements.
*   Participants confirmed that in the Japan market, the closed VSL outperformed the open version, reinforcing localized funnel strategies.

### Key Topics
*   **Japan Market Testing and Funnel Page Strategies** Idea
    *   Donnie inquired about targeting the Japan market and whether the discussion would cover the performance of campaigns in this region.
    *   Jesus confirmed that closed funnel pages were outperforming open ones significantly for Japan.
    *   Discussion included testing different funnel pages (VSLs vs. standard pages) for physical products to determine if a VSL approach might work similarly to US campaigns.
    *   Omer suggested that any tests should control for variables (same creative, audience, timing, and CPM) to yield reliable conversion data.
    
*   **Funnel Performance Metrics Overview** Update
    *   Jesus provided a detailed update on performance metrics for the period November 4th to 10th.
    *   Reported declines included spend, net ROAS, new customer ROAS, and overall conversion rates across multiple funnels.
    *   Metrics were evaluated on an offer-by-offer basis to ensure clarity despite the impact of early quiz tests that muddied some overall numbers.
    *   OSSF was highlighted as the bright spot, though adjustments in credit allocation (OSSF and WPSS metric switches) were being considered.
    
*   **VSL (Video Sales Letter) Testing – Open vs. Closed** Decision
    *   The team discussed testing an open VSL versus a closed VSL, particularly in the context of Black Friday promotions.
    *   Jesus acknowledged that the open VSL, despite being a new test initiated by Gabe's idea, had underperformed compared to historical closed VSL performance.
    *   Christopher argued that the closed VSL typically engaged users more effectively by drawing them deeper into the purchase journey.
    *   There was consensus to closely monitor the open VSL test, with the possibility of reverting to the closed format if performance metrics did not improve.
    *   Donnie proposed the potential for a radically different paid strategy if an open VSL is pursued further, especially for physical products.
    
*   **Creative Asset Refresh and Testing** Decision
    *   Donnie mentioned coordination with John to develop a process for refreshing creative assets, including assets for the upcoming Scratch Club campaign.
    *   The discussion extended to creating tailored creative templates for the beginner and women's funnels, with a focus on leveraging proven visual elements (e.g., images with directional cues).
    *   It was agreed that a more engaging creative format might help lift digital conversion rates, as evidenced by positive references to engaging content like Grant Horvat’s videos.
    
*   **SSTS Funnel Performance and Testing Adjustments** Update
    *   Jesus provided an update on SSTS metrics, noting that while spend had decreased slightly, there were improvements in net loss per trial due to targeting existing ecosystem customers.
    *   Despite a lower conversion rate and unchanged AOV, the gradual trend toward target metrics was acknowledged.
    *   Testing of the open VSL approach for SSTS using Black Friday creatives was also shared, with plans to continue monitoring performance.
    
*   **Quiz Funnel Demographics and Testing Strategy** Decision
    *   Jesus updated the team on increased spend and testing within the quiz funnel, noting various performance metrics such as click-through rate, CPC, and conversion rate.
    *   Christopher recommended broadening the age targeting beyond 55+ to attract a younger audience, as the initial funnel data was tied to existing demographics.
    *   It was agreed that further clarity on performance would be provided in upcoming reports (with Maeve’s report expected by Friday).
    
*   **Google and YouTube Campaign Performance Challenges** Idea
    *   Omer detailed issues with Google campaigns, including low spend, unstable performance metrics, and negative trends following platform changes (e.g., the discontinuation of a key 'video action' campaign).
    *   Three options were discussed to improve performance: focusing on YouTube Shorts to accelerate algorithm learning, optimizing desktop ad placements (leveraging full real estate for Black Friday sales), and testing proven winning images with contextual directional cues.
    *   These suggestions were aimed at stabilizing performance while accumulating sufficient data to guide future spending.
