### Overview

The team met to optimize the creative production and testing process for ad campaigns, focusing on balancing ad quality with increasing demand as spend ramps up. They reviewed current challenges with capacity, testing methodology, and data-driven decision making to align on a more structured approach.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team agreed to develop and implement a structured testing framework that clearly outlines testing hypotheses and adopts standardized tracking methods.
*   They decided to enhance daily communication between creative, copy, and media buying teams through dedicated channels such as Slack for rapid feedback.
*   Participants acknowledged the current capacity constraints and the need to re-prioritize ad asset production while considering outsourcing for routine editing tasks.
*   A consensus was reached on the importance of transforming the current 'spray and pray' approach into a more calculated, quality-focused strategy to support scaling efforts.

### Key Topics
*   **Diversification and Composition of Ad Sets**
    *   Russell initiated discussion on the ideal mix of ads within each ad set, querying whether to include static creatives as well as longer, narrative-driven pieces and shorter variations.
    *   The conversation examined if the ad requests should be based on historical ad types or evolve with the increasing spend and a new strategic approach.
    *   Participants considered how a balanced mix of digital and physical ads, and various creative lengths, could support scaling efforts.
    
*   **Testing Structures and Hypotheses Documentation** Decision
    *   The team discussed the need to move from an ad hoc approach in Asana to a structured testing framework with clear hypotheses embedded in each ticket.
    *   There was emphasis on tracking data properly (e.g., with improved naming conventions and integration with Domo) to allow quick, data-driven decision-making.
    *   Gabe, Omer, and Christopher highlighted that pairing media buyers with video editors and copywriters would help create a more robust feedback loop and clarify the intention behind each test.
    *   It was suggested that a designated lead (such as Chris Fleeks or another creative strategist) take ownership of developing this testing structure.
    
*   **Balancing Quantity and Quality in Creative Production** Idea
    *   Jessie and other participants noted that while current output has high quantity (30-40 ads per offer), the focus should shift towards higher quality, story-driven creatives.
    *   Russell and Christopher stressed the importance of not just creating more content but ensuring each ad has a clear narrative, quality production, and stronger performance hypothesis.
    *   The discussion pointed out that fewer, higher quality ads could deliver better engagement than a 'spray and pray' approach.
    
*   **Workload, Capacity, and Resource Allocation** Problem
    *   Multiple participants, including Sam and Morten, raised concerns about current resource capacity issues, particularly the manual workload placed on editors.
    *   It was acknowledged that current ticket volumes and turnaround times (e.g., a five-minute ad edit taking five days) are unsustainable given the volume of ad requests.
    *   The team considered outsourcing lower-skill tasks (such as repurposing content for YouTube) to free up highly skilled resources for core creative work.
    *   Discussion involved recalibrating expectations (e.g., 10 digital and 25 physical ads) based on actual editing capacity and available manpower.
    
*   **Communication and Feedback Loops between Teams** Update
    *   A strong emphasis was placed on the need for tighter real-time communication between media buyers, editors, and the copy team.
    *   Participants suggested daily check-ins via Slack and pairing media buyers with video editors to continuously share performance data and creative insights.
    *   This improved communication was viewed as key to quickly iterating on successful hypotheses and optimizing ad performance.
    
*   **Immediate Next Steps and Action Items** Decision
    *   The team agreed on the need for a short-term prioritization review to determine critical ad formats for the upcoming weeks, especially for currently scaling offers.
    *   It was decided to develop a more standardized template for creative testing that includes documented hypotheses, expected outcomes, and performance tracking.
    *   Consideration was given to potentially hiring additional support or outsourcing low-skill tasks to ensure core creative work can proceed efficiently.
    *   Next steps included establishing a clear process for feedback loops, likely through dedicated Slack channels, and setting milestones for evaluating new creative strategies.
