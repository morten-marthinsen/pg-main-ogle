### Key Topics
*   **International Naming Conventions for Ad Campaigns** Decision
    *   The team discussed the need to standardize naming conventions for non-US regions, especially for international markets such as Japan, UK, Australia, and Canada.
    *   Liv Galloway referenced a document from Mike that proposed appending the country code to the US parent ID, but the team expressed concerns about external naming interfering with internal data consistency.
    *   A consensus was reached that the naming should be handled internally to ensure proper attribution and continuity of parent IDs across ad variations.
    
*   **Control Over the Naming Process and Data Attribution** Decision
    *   Christopher Ogle emphasized the importance of keeping the parent ad ID connected with its variations to avoid tracking and attribution issues in Domo.
    *   The discussion highlighted that external naming (as suggested by Mike) could lead to confusion and misattribution, hence a need to manage naming conventions in-house.
    *   The team recognized that any mistakes in the naming codes could affect how performance data is filtered and reported.
    
*   **Spreadsheet Usage and Transition to ClickUp Tasks** Update
    *   Liv Galloway mentioned the current reliance on a spreadsheet for tracking ad IDs and variations, which contains existing and upcoming tasks.
    *   The discussion involved converting the spreadsheet into about 30 ClickUp tasks, providing a more structured workflow and clear assignment of renaming tasks.
    *   It was noted that this integration will help streamline processes and reduce errors, while also linking creative brief details with task management.
    
*   **Managing Ad Expansions and Variations** Decision
    *   The team reviewed how additional ad variations should be incorporated under existing campaigns to maintain a connection with the original parent ad.
    *   Christopher and Chris Hibbert discussed handling new variations, including the proposal to add five net new ads and one expansion ticket per week for top performers.
    *   Maintaining continuity in the ad IDs was emphasized so that tracking in Domo remains consistent even as new variations are introduced.
    
*   **Integration with Domo Reporting and Data Attribution** Update
    *   There was an in-depth discussion on how the ad naming conventions affect data filtering and the attribution process in Domo.
    *   Incorrect placement or formatting of country codes could lead to data misattribution, emphasizing the need for strict adherence to the new naming standard.
    *   The team agreed that the media buying team and Patrick (responsible for Domo data) need to be updated on the changes to ensure seamless reporting.
    
*   **Next Steps and Future Meetings** Update
    *   Liv Galloway will revise the spreadsheet to correct ad IDs according to the newly agreed naming conventions and create ClickUp tasks for these adjustments.
    *   The team planned to coordinate with Mike’s team regarding ticket assignments for net new ads and expansion work, aiming for five new ad tickets and one expansion ticket per week.
    *   A follow-up meeting was proposed to discuss further details on the scaling acquisition map and to organize a reporting meeting on January 5th, ensuring all strategic and operational aspects are aligned.
