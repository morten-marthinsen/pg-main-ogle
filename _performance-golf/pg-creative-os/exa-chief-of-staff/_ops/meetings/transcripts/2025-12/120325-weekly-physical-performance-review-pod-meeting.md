### Key Topics
*   **Influencer and Creator Communications Transition** Update
    *   Christopher updated the team on the current state of influencer communications, noting that Donnie has been managing these but will step back soon.
    *   Sean and JoJo were designated to manage creative shoots, with Sean focusing on the shoots and JoJo’s team handling filming based on provided briefs.
    *   The approach is moving from having influencers follow strict scripts to providing them with ideas and angles to foster creative freedom.
    *   It was highlighted that John Hardesty should be looped into influencer communications to handle permissions and whitelisting for influencer deals.
    
*   **Ad Performance and Campaign Analysis** Update
    *   Samuel Mercado shared detailed performance metrics for various campaigns, including campaign 357 and SF1, clarifying spend distribution and conversion outcomes.
    *   He noted that the collections page for physical products scaled up impressively to $40,000 and performed well against net ROAS targets.
    *   Campaign insights included observations that certain static creatives outperformed video ads in terms of quick conversion and auction performance.
    *   Concerns were raised regarding lower than usual conversion rates and average order value (AOV) on some days, prompting discussions on refining ad spend and targeting strategies.
    *   International campaign performance varied, with strong results in the US, mixed outcomes in Canada and the UK, and evolving performance in Australia.
    
*   **Landing Page and Offer Messaging Strategy** Decision
    *   Danielle confirmed that the out-of-stock messaging update on the checkout page would be completed today.
    *   The team decided to stick with a 25% offer instead of the initial 15% due to development constraints and resource limitations.
    *   There was agreement on pivoting the Black Friday landing page to a holiday-themed evergreen page by updating copy, banners, and messaging after the offer period.
    *   The successful quick launch of a streamlined landing page was cited as a significant win, with plans to further optimize the format in the future.
    
*   **Creative Asset Management and Coordination** Update
    *   Russell Der raised questions about how the release of Black Friday static creatives would be managed, asking whether assets should be directly shared with him or via another channel.
    *   Samuel advised that if there was confusion, Russell could reach out directly so the assets could be moved appropriately, and mentioned coordinating with Liv for a solution.
    *   The conversation underscored the importance of clear asset handoffs, especially with many creatives being tailored for Black Friday messaging.
    
*   **Holiday Messaging Focus for Digital Product Campaigns** Decision
    *   Morten Marthinsen and the team discussed holiday messaging for digital offers, debating whether to create new holiday overlays or adjust existing evergreen pages.
    *   Samuel suggested maintaining the evergreen page by simply updating the ad copy, as it was a low-lift approach that preserved successful creatives for future use.
    *   The team agreed that the method would allow for quick adjustments during the holiday period without significant creative resource expenditure.
    
*   **Australian Market Creative Opportunity (Lesson Video Repurposing)** Idea
    *   Christopher Ogle proposed repurposing lesson video footage from a previous shoot with Craig Hansen to create targeted ads for the Australian market.
    *   He suggested that the repurposed content could serve as a mini VSL or lesson clip on the landing page, leveraging the Australian accent and unique perspective.
    *   Samuel indicated that while the idea had potential, it would be lower in priority compared to the main app focus, and any further testing would be exploratory.
    *   Christopher planned to share the footage with Morten Marthinsen for review and possible ad testing.
