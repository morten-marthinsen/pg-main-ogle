*   **Team Onboarding and Introductions**
    *   Russell Der introduced Fatima Cantos as the new Associate Director of Creative Ops, highlighting her background and past experience with NOOM.
    *   Team members including Christopher Ogle, Chris Hibbert, Liv Galloway, and others provided brief introductions, outlining their roles and contributions.
    *   The purpose of the introduction was to integrate Fatima into the team and prepare her for upcoming process and workflow improvements.
    
*   **Ad Ticket Backlog and Workflow Management** Update
    *   The team reviewed current digital ad ticket requests from JROD, detailing ticket types including net new tickets, mashups, and expansions for various funnels (e.g., OSSF, DQFE, WPSS, SSTS).
    *   Liv Galloway presented the current ad script status and assignments, emphasizing the need to match JROD’s requests with the available backlog.
    *   Christopher Ogle stressed that ad requests should be fulfilled from the backlog rather than on a same-day basis, as creative production needs better planning due to limited editorial capacity.
    *   Chris Hibbert confirmed his understanding of the current workload and timelines, noting that tasks from last week extend through the end of the year.
    
*   **Clarification and Prioritization of Ad Funnels** Decision
    *   Discussion clarified the distinctions between various funnels such as OSSF, DQFE (quiz funnel), WPSS, and SSTS, including naming conventions and target demographics.
    *   The team recognized that the quiz funnel (DQFE) was currently performing well and decided to focus creative resources on scaling it, even if it meant delaying or reducing efforts on underperforming funnels.
    *   Liv Galloway and Christopher Ogle deliberated over whether to maintain separate tickets for SSTS and WPSS or to concentrate on the profitable DQFE funnel, with JROD’s requirements as a key factor.
    
*   **Process Improvements and Transition Challenges** Idea
    *   The meeting highlighted challenges transitioning from Asana to ClickUp, which contributed to the current workflow chaos.
    *   Christopher Ogle outlined the need to build a reliable backlog system to prevent last-minute scramble when JROD makes requests.
    *   Discussion included refining briefing formats, such as a potential move to a three-column ad brief (instructions, script ideas, visual notes) to improve clarity.
    *   Participants emphasized the necessity of aligning future strategy through follow-up calls (scheduled for Thursday and possibly before Christmas) once new creative leadership (e.g., Chris Fleeks) and processes are fully integrated.
    
*   **Physical Content Production and Organic-to-Ad Conversion** Update
    *   Christopher Ogle described the process for converting organic physical content into ad tickets, which involves filming content with an organic focus, cutting unnecessary parts, and using the best segments to build ads.
    *   Assistant editors are being trained to perform A-roll cuts to extract 'gold' content, reducing the editing workload when converting footage into ad tickets.
    *   Donnie French contributed by noting challenges with using raw files and ensuring there is no duplication of work between his team and the assistant editors.
    
*   **Use of AI and Legal Considerations in Creative Production** Idea
    *   The conversation briefly addressed the potential use of AI-generated content (such as voice synthesis) for creating ad variations including horizontal and vertical expansions.
    *   The team discussed the need to compile an approved list of voices and secure proper sign-offs to ensure legal compliance and brand consistency.
    *   Participants acknowledged that while AI can speed up production, careful boundaries must be maintained to avoid misuse or legal issues.
    
*   **Action Items and Next Steps** Decision
    *   Chris Hibbert was tasked with creating two net new OSSF tickets by the next day and additional tickets (including expansions) by the end of the week to address JROD's requests and build a backlog.
    *   Liv Galloway was assigned to confirm with JROD regarding whether his ad requests were for general OSSF or specific to Japan, and to coordinate file uploads and communication via designated channels.
    *   Christopher Ogle planned to reschedule a follow-up call with Chris Hibbert to further refine ticket creation processes, and a broader process review call was scheduled for Thursday.
    *   Team members also discussed the importance of tracking digital ad performance and adjusting creative resource allocation to overcome the current editorial capacity constraint.
