### Overview

The meeting reviewed the weekly performance of several digital campaigns from November 25th to December 1st, focusing on key performance metrics such as Net ROAS, new customer ROAS, net loss per trial, and AOV. Participants discussed account segmentation, pricing tests, creative updates, and strategic initiatives to enhance funnel performance.

### Key Topics
*   **Digital Product Performance Review** Update
    *   Jesus Rodriguez reviewed the week’s performance metrics for multiple digital campaigns, including OSSF, WPSS, SSTS, and SSDP.
    *   Metrics discussed included Net ROAS, new customer Net ROAS, net loss per trial, ad spend, and AOV.
    *   While some metrics were stable or improved in certain areas, issues such as a $30 increase in net loss per trial and declining AOV were identified, prompting deeper analysis.
    
*   **Account Segmentation – Japan Traffic** Decision
    *   A decision was made to segment Japan traffic into its own account to isolate its unique performance metrics, especially given its different focus on net loss per trial metrics.
    *   Jesus Rodriguez explained that separating Japan would provide clearer data and allow for tailored optimization without conflating it with US and other market data.
    *   The new account has been set up in Japan’s time zone and access will be provided to all relevant team members by the next day.
    
*   **Digital Pricing Strategy and Price Testing** Decision
    *   A detailed discussion ensued on whether to move away from the traditional $47 front-end price toward a higher price point (e.g., $67).
    *   Historical tests were reviewed, showing that increasing the price led to a significant AOV increase, though at the cost of lower conversion rates.
    *   The team debated the trade-off between boosting AOV and maintaining a high conversion rate, considering additional bump and upsell offers to mitigate the drop.
    *   A consensus was reached to test the higher price point, with further monitoring and discussion planned for the next call.
    
*   **Creative Strategy and Testing** Idea
    *   Jesus Rodriguez reported minimal new creative testing for SSTS and noted the reliance on retesting older creatives, with mixed results.
    *   For SSDP, performance issues were attributed to stale creatives; the need to update banners with Christmas and New Year themes was emphasized.
    *   Morton was tasked with updating SSDP banners to reflect the new holiday promotions.
    *   There was a brainstorming session about leveraging AI for creating fresh, innovative ads, including the idea of state-specific or geo-targeted messaging and generating AI avatars to avoid legal concerns with using real golfer images.
    
*   **Operational Adjustments on Holiday Campaigns** Decision
    *   The team confirmed that all Black Friday/Cyber Monday pricing and creative elements would be turned off to normalize performance post-sale.
    *   Danielle Schwolow and Christopher Ogle ensured that no new holiday-themed ads would be launched, and existing holiday ads were to be deactivated across all digital offers.
    
*   **Upsell and Bump Offer Enhancements** Update
    *   Discussion centered on the need to optimize bump and upsell offers to drive higher AOV as part of the pricing test strategy.
    *   Christopher Ogle highlighted that multiple bump offers will be critical in complementing the new price point, and emphasized speeding up Vanessa’s onboarding for creating these offers.
    *   The enhancement of these offers was recognized as a key lever for scaling overall funnel performance.
    
*   **Geo-targeted and State-Specific Creative Strategies** Idea
    *   The potential to develop region-specific creatives was discussed, focusing on tailoring messaging for audiences in colder northern states versus warmer regions such as Florida, California, and Arizona.
    *   Christopher Ogle and others suggested using AI to generate state-specific avatars and messages that resonate with local weather conditions and regional advantages.
    *   This approach aimed to enhance ad relevance and address the seasonal challenges affecting digital performance.
