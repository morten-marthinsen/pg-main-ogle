    *   Christopher Ogle provided clarifications on correctly using elements such as 'V' for variational numbering and 'I' for images to prevent confusion (e.g., mixing up video with variation).
    *   They noted that the formula currently in the document contained errors (e.g., an unnecessary 'V' in the script ID) and agreed to correct these to streamline the process.
    *   The discussion underscored the importance of clear documentation to support accurate ad tracking.
    
*   **Two-Phase Naming Process** Decision
    *   Christopher explained the two-phase approach: Phase one involves editors naming assets either using an automated formula or manually, while phase two involves media buying teams adding further details once assets are uploaded to Domo.
    *   The two-phase process was designed to reduce manual workloads for editors and allow media buyers to capture necessary information post-launch.
    *   This strategic division was intended to streamline operations while ensuring that critical data (such as actor names and asset roots) is maintained for fast, effective decision-making.
    
*   **Creative Performance Spreadsheets and Data Synchronization** Idea
    *   Russell Der and Christopher Ogle discussed building a streamlined strategic scaling system spreadsheet to replace redundant data sources like multiple Google Sheets, Airtable, and Asana tasks.
    *   The intended new system would integrate only the essential data points for tracking performance and optimizing future ad tests, with a plan to hide unnecessary columns for easier visibility.
    *   This effort was aimed at reducing duplication, increasing data accuracy, and ensuring that critical information—such as actor names—is readily accessible for rapid response actions.
