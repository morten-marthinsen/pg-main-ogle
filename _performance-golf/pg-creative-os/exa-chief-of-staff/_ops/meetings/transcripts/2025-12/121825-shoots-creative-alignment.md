### Overview

The meeting aimed to realign creative production and ad shoot processes during a new creative transition period as the team shifts from a pre‐booked influencer model to a more agile, scalable, idea‐driven workflow. The discussion focused on clarifying creative brief processes, establishing a scaling acquisition roadmap, and enhancing interdepartmental communication and accountability.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team agreed to adopt a proactive and scalable creative process with formal kickoffs and improved cross-department collaboration.
*   A dual-stage creative brief process was decided upon, beginning with an ideation brief for creator feedback followed by a finalized detailed brief.
*   A scaling acquisition map will be implemented to outline upcoming projects, deadlines, KPIs, and resource allocations.
*   The team committed to standardizing documentation by separating internal planning documents from the creator-facing versions and organizing footage by concept.
*   The ICE methodology will be used to prioritize creative ideas and projects based on business impact, creative lift, and confidence.

### Key Topics
*   **Introduction and Context Setting**
    *   Christopher Ogle opened the meeting by outlining the need to realign and adapt creative production processes in light of an upcoming transition period and holiday season pressures.
    *   He emphasized the importance of synchronizing teams (copy, creative, and production) to move from a pre-booked influencer model to a more flexible and scalable creative workflow.
    *   The discussion set the stage for addressing internal siloing, accountability, and ensuring the creative output aligns with business goals.
    
*   **New Shoot Workflow Transition** Update
    *   The team discussed moving away from scheduling fixed influencer shoots (previously managed monthly) toward a process where marketing and copy own the ideation for shoots.
    *   Christopher raised questions about how the new workflow will be operationalized, clarifying whether creative briefs come from the copy team or if the creative team will request support.
    *   JoJo Phillips and others acknowledged the need to balance proactive creative planning with reactive adjustments based on what’s working in real time.
    
*   **Creative Briefs & Collaboration with Creators** Idea
    *   The discussion detailed two types of briefs: the traditional shoot brief and the at-home shoot brief used by creators for recording ads in their own environment.
    *   A key debate revolved around initiating an ideation phase where brief ideas are sent to creators first to gather feedback before developing a full, detailed brief.
    *   JoJo advocated for leveraging creators’ expertise by asking for their input on concepts, pointing out that creators might improve on initial log lines with suggestions aligned to their strengths.
    
*   **Scaling Acquisition Map and Timeline Accountability** Decision
    *   Christopher introduced the concept of a scaling acquisition map to outline at least eight upcoming projects with defined KPIs, deadlines, and asset requirements.
    *   He discussed how campaign ideas (e.g., the 357 women’s page) will be tied directly to business performance metrics and how delays are to be managed with more structure.
    *   There was agreement that this proactive planning and clear timeline accountability would help mitigate last-minute rushes and improve overall workflow efficiency.
    
*   **Organization and Documentation Process** Update
    *   The team discussed the need for clear documentation of creative briefs, including having an internal version that contains detailed production notes and a stripped-down external version for creators.
    *   Questions were raised about the process for submitting briefs (e.g., via ClickUp) and how to document logistics such as footage organization (naming concepts like 'Easy Money concept one') and shot listing templates.
    *   Concerns about data sensitivity and reducing duplicate work were mentioned, suggesting potential workflow improvements or integration with existing tools like ClickUp.
    
*   **Prioritization and ICE Methodology for Creative Ideas** Idea
    *   Russell Der and Fatima Cantos proposed using an ICE (Impact, Creative lift, Confidence) methodology to prioritize creative ideas and projects more objectively.
    *   This numerical ranking system was suggested as a means to balance the creative input from various team members against business impact and resource requirements.
    *   Implementing this system would allow the team to make clear decisions on which projects to advance, ensuring that the highest impact ideas receive appropriate support.
    
*   **Workflow Integration Between Organic and Ad Content Production** Update
    *   The discussion addressed the need to clearly delineate the responsibilities between organic content editing and ad-specific editing to avoid duplication of effort.
    *   It was agreed that while both organic and ad content might use footage from the same shoots, the editing teams would have distinct roles, ensuring that the best performing organic output could be repurposed for ads.
    *   This separation would also help in efficiently managing workloads and specialized output requirements for different types of content.
    
*   **Post-Production Process and Next Steps** Decision
    *   Logistical details were discussed regarding post-shoot processes, including how footage should be organized by concept to facilitate A-roll cuts and subsequent editing.
    *   Action items were identified: Christopher Ogle committed to finalizing and sharing the scaling acquisition map by the next day, and a follow-up call was planned to discuss detailed next steps.
    *   Additional steps included creating quick-reference guides for organizing footage and refining shot listing templates, as well as clarifying access logistics for creative documents.
    *   There was also a conversation about ensuring proper editorial responsibility—such as tagging and file organization—so that neither organic nor ad editing teams duplicate efforts.
