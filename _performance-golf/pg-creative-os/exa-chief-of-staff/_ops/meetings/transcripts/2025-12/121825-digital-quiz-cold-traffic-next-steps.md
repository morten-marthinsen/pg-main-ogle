    *   Christopher proposed removing the swing circle image from the top of the quiz start page to reduce confusion and improve start rates.
    *   It was decided to update the introductory text to 'Let's find out your swing circle. Start one minute quiz now.'

*   **Price Test Implementation for AOV Improvement** Decision
    *   A price test was planned to adjust the funnel pricing from $47 to $67 as an interim measure until clearer data on the 26-minute VSL is available.
    *   Danielle confirmed that Jesse would implement the updates on the URLs and adjust the VSL and CTA pop accordingly by the end of day.

*   **WPSS VSL Conversion Optimization** Decision
    *   The team planned to enhance the WPSS VSL by implementing a Focus Full Screen feature, similar to the successful OSSF test.
    *   Updates included embedding a blurred banner to maintain 9x16 viewing and adding an animation in the first 10-13 seconds to reduce early drop-off.
    *   There was also discussion about testing an autoplay feature, with Danielle confirming readiness to activate the WPSS autoplay feature for further testing.
    
*   **SOP and Reporting Process Enhancements** Update
    *   The need to develop and maintain Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for report production was highlighted.
    *   The team discussed documenting changes such as Vitalytics embed code adjustments, URL modifications, and maintaining a static history tab in weekly reports.
    *   Nicole was assigned the task to update the SOP documentation to streamline future report preparations.
    
*   **Ad Creative and Campaign Optimization** Update
    *   Discussion focused on current CTR challenges, particularly for the 35-44 age group, and the limited creative resources available.
    *   The team noted the importance of developing new quiz-specific ads, with separate creatives highlighting the quiz in both male and female campaigns.
    *   There were considerations on how to retest different creative elements, including the possibility of a ‘loud and in your face’ autoplay setup for WPSS, replicating past successful tests.
    
*   **Scheduling Adjustments and Meeting Frequency** Decision
    *   It was confirmed that the next meeting call would be rescheduled to December 30th due to the upcoming holiday period.
    *   There was discussion about possibly extending future calls from 30 to 45 minutes to address the increasing volume of updates and discussions.
    *   The team agreed to manage interim communications via Slack to avoid unnecessary reports on minimal data.
    
*   **Quiz Question Wording Revision** Decision
    *   The team addressed friction noted with the quiz question wording 'how long have you taken golf seriously?'.
    *   It was decided to change the question to 'how long have you played golf? (ballpark)' for both male and female respondents to reduce user friction.
    *   This update was planned to be implemented immediately to improve quiz completion rates.
    
*   **Conversion Tracking and Test Purchase Procedures** Update
    *   Omer highlighted issues with conversion tracking due to problems with Google Tag Manager implementation.
    *   He mentioned that current campaigns had been paused and that he would perform a test purchase, ensuring it was flagged to avoid impacting overall sales data.
    *   The tracking issues were acknowledged as critical for ensuring that future campaign data remains accurate.
    
*   **Future Funnel and VSL Optimization Ideas** Idea
    *   There were discussions about potential future modifications, including creating a new VSL targeting a different age group (removing older demographics) to better engage younger audiences.
    *   The idea also included revisiting the PG1 funnel once the Quiz 2.0 version is live, as the current funnel is considered underoptimized.
    *   The team considered leveraging new female and quiz-specific ad creatives and discussed the forthcoming VSL from Vlad, expected by the end of the month.
