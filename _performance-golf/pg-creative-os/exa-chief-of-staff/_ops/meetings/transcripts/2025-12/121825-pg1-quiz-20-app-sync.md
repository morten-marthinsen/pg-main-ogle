### Key Topics
*   **Quiz Design and Personalization**
    *   Christopher explained that the new quiz is modeled on the Simply Piano approach, intended to deliver a personalized practice plan based on user goals (e.g., break 80, break 90, break 100).
    *   The design focuses on dynamic, time-sensitive messaging that informs users of quick wins, such as improvements perceived in a set number of days.
    *   The quiz aims to drive sign-ups by shifting from technical onboarding questions (e.g., swing path, divot data) to goal-based, motivational content.
    
*   **Asset Collection and Design Elements** Update
    *   The team discussed required assets including logos, awards, icons, stats, fun facts, testimonials, and app images to populate the quiz screens.
    *   Responsibilities were assigned: Corey was mentioned as the contact for logos, Peter or David for app images, and Aaron for fun facts and testimonials.
    *   It was agreed that a Figma file and a Meta Sandbox page would be used to centralize and share design elements efficiently with the meta design team.
    
*   **Pricing Strategy and Conversion Optimization** Decision
    *   Peter highlighted concerns that the current premium pricing of 299/300 is a deterrent and does not align with user willingness to pay.
    *   A plan was established to conduct price testing with variants (e.g., 99, 150, 200) and a control at 300, with the aim to optimize conversion rates.
    *   Price testing results from email campaigns (open and click-through rates) will inform decisions on whether to adjust pricing for the paid acquisition funnel.
    
*   **Onboarding Process Improvements** Update
    *   The new onboarding approach, centered on a personalized quiz, was compared favorably against the previous method that focused on technical aspects (such as swing or ball flight data).
    *   Peter and Christopher agreed that the simpler, more intuitive onboarding will resonate better with the target market—especially advanced and intermediate golfers—without alienating curious, new users.
    *   There was an emphasis on aligning messaging to promote a fast and clear path to improvement, positioning the app as offering rapid progress.
    
*   **Coordination with Meta Design Team and Timeline** Update
    *   Christopher informed the team that the meta design team (which includes the designer who developed the Simply Piano quiz) would begin design work on Sunday.
    *   The design team aims to complete the quiz screens by the end of next week, with a target launch by the end of this year or early January.
    *   Coordination efforts include ensuring access to the Figma files and the Meta Sandbox page, with Peter confirming asset delivery and sharing relevant links and contacts via Slack.
    
*   **Additional Design and Feature Discussions** Idea
    *   The team discussed incorporating additional visual slides to showcase the variety of offerings (e.g., one-on-one coaching, online videos, in-person lessons) via the premium dashboard card design.
    *   There was an agreement to explore using tiles with images and text to better communicate product features and enhance the onboarding message.
    *   Christopher mentioned the need to experiment with layout due to limited screen real estate, considering a manual design approach with the meta designers.
    
*   **Follow-ups and Action Items** Update
    *   Peter committed to sending a Figma file with all required design assets and tagging Corey for logos, as well as confirming sources for app images and testimonials.
    *   Christopher planned to update the Google Doc checklist and ensure the Meta Sandbox link was current for the design team’s reference.
    *   The team agreed to track completion and conversion metrics from the quiz, and to coordinate further adjustments based on asset readiness and price testing outcomes.
