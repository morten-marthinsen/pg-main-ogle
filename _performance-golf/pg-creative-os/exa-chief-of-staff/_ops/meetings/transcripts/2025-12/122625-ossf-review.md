    *   The team reviewed the current backlog, noting that while 25 assets had been launched (covering 5 ad IDs with 5 variations each), there remained additional assets and potential creative expansions.
    *   Discussion included the possibility of launching existing backlog assets next week if the media buyer (J Rod) required more live ad placements, given the high season and budget sensitivity.
    *   The need to balance timely launches with ensuring quality performance (meeting new KPI requirements) was emphasized.
    
*   **Establishing Process for Creative Expansion (Vertical Hook Stacking)** Decision
    *   Christopher Ogle led a detailed walkthrough of creating expansion tickets, demonstrating how to duplicate winning ads in the spreadsheet and overlay new hook variations (vertical expansion) in order to refresh proven assets.
    *   The new process involved stacking fresh hook segments (ranging from a few seconds to 20 seconds) onto the beginning of existing winning ads to capture attention early in the viewer’s feed.
    *   Instructions included guidance on linking asset URLs, duplicating spreadsheet tabs, and preparing clear instructions for the creative team to follow when routing assets to Iconic.
    *   The strategy was positioned as a systematic, low lift method to generate multiple variations quickly, thereby increasing opportunities to find new winners.
    
*   **Naming Conventions and Asset Tracking Challenges** Problem
    *   The team addressed confusion stemming from repeated changes to ad naming conventions across platforms (Iconic, Domo, and internal spreadsheets), notably after Liv’s updates.
    *   Issues were raised regarding difficulty in tracking asset performance and correlating ad IDs between platforms, with suggestions to standardize naming to maintain the connection to original parent IDs.
    *   Participants emphasized that clear and consistent naming conventions are vital for accurate performance analysis and ensuring that subsequent creative iterations are correctly aligned.
    
*   **Coordination with Media Buyers and Spending Strategy** Update
    *   The discussion touched on the cautious spending levels observed in current campaigns, which were attributed to media buying decisions made post-Black Friday to improve subscription recoupment.
    *   It was noted that campaigns not meeting the break-even net loss target were being minimized or paused, leading to overall lower spend.
    *   The need for further dialogue with media buyers (e.g., Omar, J Rod) was identified to clarify spending rationales and optimize campaign scaling.
    
*   **Roles, Responsibilities, and Next Steps for Creative Expansion** Decision
    *   Christopher Ogle and Michael Croisetiere agreed on using the detailed expansion ticket approach to refresh winning ad creatives and that the team would create separate ClickUp tasks supporting these tickets.
    *   It was decided that the new expansion process, along with the pending net new ad launches, would ideally be implemented by next week to provide 10-15 fresh assets.
    *   A tentative plan was established to have further review and coordination in the second week of January, ensuring that any naming or routing issues are resolved and subsequent performance is monitored.
    *   Team roles were clarified, with responsibilities allocated among the creative team (including the lead translator and designers), media buying team, and internal coordinators to maintain a streamlined workflow.
