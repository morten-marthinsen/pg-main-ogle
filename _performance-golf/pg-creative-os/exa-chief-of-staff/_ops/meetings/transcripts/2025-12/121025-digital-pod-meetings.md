### Overview

The meeting reviewed the performance of digital product advertising campaigns for early December, focusing on campaign metrics, creative strategies, and technical integration challenges. The group discussed optimizing the quiz funnel, managing creative assets, and planning next steps for the holiday season and Q5.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team decided to maintain current spending on the quiz funnel without a 10% increase due to underwhelming early-day performance.
*   Jesus was tasked with confirming international tracking for the quiz funnel by messaging Maeve in the digital quiz channel.
*   Danielle was assigned to follow up with Brandon on the international shipping integration ticket to address shipping rules for non-prime orders.
*   The group agreed to test an expanded (open) VSL page for the quiz funnel in collaboration with Danielle to reduce checkout drop-offs.
*   Jesus will share a Q5 creative list for OSSF and WPSS campaigns to review and adjust messaging for a smooth transition from Black Friday creatives.
*   Omer planned to test Bing ads with a small budget to leverage more targeted search and image campaigns.
*   The team opted to keep Black Friday creatives off, focusing instead on developing new, differentiated creative assets.

### Key Topics
*   **Initial Discussion & State-Level Ads Priority**
    *   Jesus Rodriguez mentioned that state-level ads were low priority and could be scheduled later, possibly in late December, due to resource constraints.
    *   Morten and Jesus briefly confirmed the idea of creating tickets/creative briefs on the pod channel for scheduling.
    
*   **Weekly Digital Product Campaign Performance Review** Update
    *   Jesus presented overall campaign performance for December 2nd to 8th, highlighting that overall spend was down while net ROAS and new customer rates showed improvements in some areas.
    *   He shared detailed metrics: WPSS showed down spend but increased net loss per trial, while OSSF showed a strong performance with US and Japan breakdowns.
    *   The quiz funnel was a standout performer with a notable increase in new customer conversion rate and a drop in net loss per trial from minus 45 to zero.
    
*   **Quiz Funnel Performance & Optimization** Idea
    *   The quiz funnel showed promising results with improved new customer conversion (up to 64-70%), high click-through rates, and low cost per quiz completion (around $6.50 per completion).
    *   Discussion included potential testing of an expanded/open VSL page to provide more information prior to checkout, aimed at reducing drop-off rates at the checkout stage.
    *   Jesus mentioned plans to introduce fake pages and bids to drive more spend and possibly test international performance for the quiz funnel.
    
*   **International Testing & Shipping Integration Challenges** Problem
    *   The team discussed testing the quiz funnel internationally; however, there were concerns due to international shipping issues with physical bump offers.
    *   Danielle Schwolow outlined three issues (hiding bump orders, adding shipping costs for prime countries, and needing CoC-written shipping options for upsells) that needed to be resolved post-New Year.
    *   Christopher Ogle suggested following up with Brandon and confirming tracking with Maeve for international traffic, especially from YouTube.
    
*   **Creative Strategy & Ad Asset Management** Update
    *   Jesus explained the rationale behind launching 57 ads (mostly older creative assets) in order to stimulate campaign performance given low spend levels.
    *   Concerns were raised by Christopher and Gabe regarding creative overlap and differences between US and Japan assets, emphasizing the need for strategic uploads to avoid algorithm confusion on Facebook.
    *   The discussion included balancing the use of old Black Friday creatives against new messaging for WPSS, and addressing creative backlog under limited editor capacity.
    
*   **Platform-Specific Strategies: Bing & Facebook** Idea
    *   Christopher and Omer discussed potential advantages of running Bing ads, especially for more targeted search and image campaigns.
    *   They deliberated that Bing’s targeting might help reach the core audience faster, despite current low expectations for digital Bing campaigns in the end of December.
    *   Plans were mentioned for testing Bing ads with a small budget towards the end of the month and possibly expanding to Google ads later.
    
*   **Checkout Flow Limitations & Technology Constraints** Problem
    *   There was discussion about testing alternative checkout flows such as Hay Flow to reduce drop-offs; however, Christopher noted that current funnels are limited by Checkout Champ’s integration.
    *   The team recognized that a migration to Shopify is planned for January/February, which may allow more flexibility in checkout optimization.
    
*   **WPSS and SSTS Campaign Performance & Transition Strategies** Update
    *   WPSS experienced a significant letdown post-Black Friday with reduced spend and conversion rates, prompting a discussion on whether to keep or reintroduce certain Black Friday creatives.
    *   Danielle highlighted potential learnings on transitioning creative slowly rather than a complete shutdown to retain campaign momentum.
    *   For SSTS, performance was poor with sub-0.3 conversion rates and the team discussed leaning on the quiz funnel as an alternative focus, while planning additional male-focused creative for DQFE ads.
    
*   **Next Steps & Action Items** Decision
    *   Jesus planned to message Maeve in the digital quiz channel for international tracking clarification and follow up with Danielle regarding international shipping integration ticket updates.
    *   A review of Q5 creative lists for OSSF and WPSS was planned with potential adjustments to ad messaging to better align with upcoming holiday strategies.
    *   Omer intended to test Bing ads with a small budget at the end of the month, and the team agreed to monitor and possibly re-launch paused 'winner' ads after evaluating performance data.
