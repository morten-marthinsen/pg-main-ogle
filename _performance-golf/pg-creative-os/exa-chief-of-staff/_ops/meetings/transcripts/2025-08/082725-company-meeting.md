### Key Topics
*   **Introduction and Vision Setting**
    *   Brixton Albert welcomed participants, emphasizing the importance of visual connection by asking everyone to turn on their cameras.
    *   He shared his personal background and motivation, including his childhood speech impediment, to inspire persistence and focus.
    *   Brixton introduced the two key themes: transforming the customer experience and improving internal operations to align toward a clear company goal.
    
*   **Customer Journey Transformation with PG1** Decision
    *   Brixton detailed the current issue: customers experience a confusing journey with too many options across the golf industry.
    *   He introduced the PG1 system—a step-by-step process guiding customers from an initial assessment through personalized video content, targeted challenges, live coaching, and iterative improvements.
    *   He used the metaphor of transitioning from a scattered 'East Coast' approach to a precise destination ('LAX, Terminal 1, Gate 2') to illustrate the shift to a clear, sequential customer journey.
    
*   **Internal Alignment and KPI Clarity** Decision
    *   Brixton emphasized that every team member must identify a clear KPI relevant to their role and track progress with the support of their manager.
    *   He stressed adopting the 'power of the spoken word' to commit to project timelines, meaning that teams should set concrete completion dates for initiatives.
    *   He also highlighted the need to organize innovative ideas in dedicated channels rather than losing them in Slack.
    
*   **Rebranding and Unified Brand Communication** Decision
    *   John Hardesty and Brixton introduced the rebranding initiative under the theme 'Love Your Game,' aiming to create a unified customer experience across all touchpoints.
    *   The rebrand involves aligning messaging on the website, email journeys, phone support, influencer kits, and overall visual and verbal identity.
    *   The target rollout for this comprehensive rebrand is set for January 1, 2026, to clearly communicate the company’s unique value proposition.
    
*   **App and Digital Experience Enhancements (PG1 v5.0)** Update
    *   Aaron Ungvarsky and Peter Chau provided an update on the upcoming PG1 version 5.0 release.
    *   They showcased new features designed to simplify the user experience, including dynamic score entry, a streamlined personal dashboard, fewer screens, and a practice scheduler.
    *   The enhancements aim to reduce friction, ensure users receive personalized guidance, and drive premium conversions by delivering the next best actions to improve their golf game.
    
*   **Physical Product Innovations** Update
    *   Chris McGinley presented a series of new and enhanced products aimed at disrupting traditional golf equipment.
    *   Updates included the SF2 titanium driver, a new wedge (with bold design features), innovative training aids (e.g., draw force training aid, Speed Spoon), and a concept putter targeted for tour-level performance.
    *   He also discussed the development of innovative consumables, such as new golf balls with advanced alignment and performance features.
    
*   **Sales Performance and Global Expansion** Update
    *   Asal Moradian detailed high ticket sales performance, noting that elite sales contributed significant additional revenue and setting a target of reaching a $4M month.
    *   He discussed strategies to expand the team and increase the number of elite sales, including a focus on VIP coaching and bundled academy events.
    *   He also introduced initiatives to expand into European and UK markets, with early steps already taken such as launching ads and onboarding new coaches.
    
*   **Retail Partnerships and Global Distribution** Update
    *   Stuart MacDonald provided an update on securing key retail partnerships, including Gulf Galaxy stores, PGA Tour Superstore, and Walmart, which adopted the entire product line.
    *   He mentioned ongoing discussions with global retailers in Japan and the UK to further enhance market penetration.
    *   The retail update reinforced the company’s strategic intent to extend its presence globally in tandem with its rebranding and product innovation efforts.
    
*   **Wrap-up and Next Steps** Decision
    *   Brixton and the leadership team reiterated the three core takeaways for all departments: establish clear KPIs, commit to specific project deadlines using verbal pledges, and capture innovative ideas in organized channels.
    *   Each department was encouraged to execute on the roadmap: finalizing the PG1 app release, rolling out the rebranding initiative, scaling sales teams, and aligning retail and global distribution strategies.
    *   The meeting concluded with a call to align all efforts toward the unified vision of transforming the customer journey and the overall golfing experience.
