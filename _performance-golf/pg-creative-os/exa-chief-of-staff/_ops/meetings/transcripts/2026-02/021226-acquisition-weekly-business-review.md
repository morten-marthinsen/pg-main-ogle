*   **Price Strategy and Trade-offs for Product 357** Decision
    *   Team members debated whether to maintain the current higher price or consider reducing it slightly, emphasizing an anticipated 'price shock' effect.
    *   Danielle and John contributed by noting that a modest negative KPI may be acceptable if it leads to improved long-term LTV, while Gabe stressed the need for further data.
    *   The discussion underscored a trade-off between immediate trial volume and sustaining a premium product image.
    
*   **Creative and App Promotion Strategies** Update
    *   Gabe outlined two new funnel strategies for promoting the app: one using a quiz that requires a subscription purchase to access the app, and a direct funnel prompting a trial download.
    *   The conversation emphasized testing of new creatives and placements, with a focus on scaling the digital campaign for product 357.
    *   The team expressed optimism about the quiz funnel's scalability, though acknowledged that both funnels needed further data for optimization.
    
*   **Tracking Issues and Data Integrity for PG1 App Sales** Problem
    *   Gabe raised concerns over missing UTM parameters on PG1 app sales, which impeded accurate attribution of revenue and CPA measurement.
    *   He detailed that the data team (Bill, Patrick, and Peter) had been directed to resolve tracking issues as a top priority, with plans to launch tracking updates promptly.
    *   The target CPA of 120 was set based on the subscription revenue model, with expected revenue data coming in seven days after the campaign launch.
    
*   **International Expansion Strategy** Update
    *   The discussion highlighted opportunities in key markets like Japan, Canada, the UK, and Australia, with particular emphasis on Japan due to its large base of golfers.
    *   Gabe noted that while international expansion is promising, the immediate focus remained on improving LTV, AOV, and digital funnel performance.
    *   There was mention of partnering with a new digital product promoter in Japan and ensuring that international efforts do not divert excessive resources from core priorities.
    
*   **Shopify Site Launch and Member vs. Non-Member Pricing** Update
    *   Updates were shared regarding the upcoming launch of the new Shopify site, set to improve navigation, SEO, and overall user experience.
    *   The team discussed a phased approach to implementing member pricing versus non-member pricing, initially using a toggle feature on select pages.
    *   Danielle and John questioned how pricing strategies would be aligned with paid media efforts, and it was agreed that phase one would focus on a better site experience without integrated member pricing for all products.
    
*   **GTM Calendar and Upcoming Product Launches** Update
    *   John provided updates on the GTM calendar, mentioning upcoming product launches including supplements, the SF2 launch in mid-March, and the 'Love Youe Game' campaign slated for April.
    *   Further discussion included improvements on app tracking technology and the planned migration to the new Shopify platform to enhance page creation speed.
    *   The discussion emphasized that these launches and calendar adjustments were aligned with broader strategic priorities, ensuring optimal rollout and performance.
    
*   **Reworking the Fitness Program Funnel** Update
    *   Juan Carlos and Gabe discussed the need to rework the fitness program funnel, which had been paused due to underperformance on paid media.
    *   The current funnel was noted to be overly branded and better suited for direct response, prompting plans for adjustments to improve its conversion performance.
    *   The team planned further optimization to align the funnel with proven elements from other successful campaigns.
