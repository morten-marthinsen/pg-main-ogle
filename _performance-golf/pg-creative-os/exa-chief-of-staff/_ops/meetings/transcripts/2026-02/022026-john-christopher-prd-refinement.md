## PRD Refinement Notes

### 1. Core Design Philosophy — "One Foundation, Many Outputs"

**Addition — Performance Data Feedback Loop:**
- Outputs adjust based on test data as it comes in
- As ad angle tests return data, the system recalibrates recommendations and scaling decisions daily
- For GTM launches (e.g., RS1), there will be an initial campaign based on research + Claude recommendations + human taste
- Post-launch, all outputs (email flows, UX pages, PDPs) retool based on what angles are working
- Multiple angles get tested at the ad set level → confidence builds → angles expand into broader campaigns → if a new angle outperforms the original campaign, the original gets replaced

### 2. Brand Threads — Three-Tier Model

**Correction:** "Smarter way to improve golf" → **"Smarter way to play better golf"**

**John's three-tier framework (bottom → top):**

| Tier | Thread | Function | Example |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| **1 — Closest to conversion** | **Innovation** | Sell the product, fix an immediate need | "The 357 uses face drift technology..." |
| **2 — Broader mission** | **Smarter way to play better golf** | Connected journey, app experience, VIP experiences, upsell/LTV | "This is part of a system that helps you play smarter" |
| **3 — Brand apex** | **Love Your Game** | Overall brand tagline — everything ladders up here | "A love of their game they haven't felt before" |

**How they feed each other:**
- Innovation → Smarter Way: First email post-purchase tells the innovation story, then introduces the bigger mission
- In DR angle testing: can lead with either thread, then bridge to the other
  - *Smarter Way first:* Trigger emotion (you're not taking the smarter path) → open a void → fill with Innovation (the solution)
  - *Innovation first:* Branded ad shows innovation → viewer asks "what does this help me do?" → bridge to Smarter Way → emotional payoff
- The two threads are always interconnected — they don't have to be used separately

### 3. Root Angle Principle

**Terminology correction — Campaign vs. Ad Set:**
- **Campaign** = 360-degree brand campaign (multi-channel: ads, email, social, organic, landing pages)
- **Ad Set** = individual angle tests within a platform (Meta, YouTube, etc.)
- Team must understand: campaigns are larger, broader, 360-degree efforts. Ad sets are the first step in testing new angles.
- "We elevate root angles into brand campaigns over time" — correct use of "campaign"
- **Action:** Update all documentation to align on this terminology

### 4. Script-Visual Congruence

**Edit needed:** "Quality means the ad communicates" → **"Quality means the ad communicates the one root angle effectively"**
- Visuals must align and magnify the script
- Incongruence = dissonance = wasted ad spend
- Reviewers must watch an ad and know it delivers the one root angle in a way that hooks, holds, and inspires action

### 5. Scale the Vision, Not the Meeting

- PRD captures strategic conversations once so they don't need repeating
- System + subagents with challenger functions push back and question assumptions
- Output should be 90-95% aligned before human review

**John's addition — Proactive recommendations:**
- As the system learns what angles are working, it should push ideas and recommendations to the team
- Example: An ad set angle works → the system identifies it should become a 360 campaign → notifies relevant people (Jojo for organic, influencer team for briefs) with draft ideas
- Christopher's vision: This maps to the influencer brief system — a living document showing angles by priority/spend, with tested vs. untested categories
- Long-term: Tess connects organic data + paid data under one dashboard (Domo) → angles escalate through stages → system prompts "Turn this into a campaign? Y/N" → auto-generates 360 campaign assets and distributes to relevant teams
