### Overview

The meeting focused on aligning and refining the new 14-position naming convention and asset generation process for digital ad creatives across multiple platforms. The team reviewed challenges related to consistency, automation, and data integration while discussing technical modifications to support the strategic scaling system.

### Key Takeaways

*   The team agreed that every ad submission must be tied to a single root angle with unified messaging to ensure consistent data aggregation.
*   They decided to incorporate new codes for ad category, expansion type (EXV for vertical and EXH for horizontal), and asset type, along with revisions to talent, editor, and copywriter initials.
*   It was resolved that for scaling campaigns, simply appending a "-SCA" suffix was acceptable for tracking duplicate or scaled ads.
*   A discrepancy between ClickUp labels and the new system requirements was noted, requiring a retrospective update of promo codes and naming fields.
*   The need for improved process automation was acknowledged, with plans to transition from manual spreadsheets to an integrated system connecting ClickUp, Iconic, and Domo.

### Key Topics
*   **Naming Convention Discrepancies in Iconic and ClickUp** Update
    *   Omer raised an issue with the current naming field in Iconic versus ClickUp, noting that downloaded names sometimes lacked proper dashes, which complicates copy-pasting for ad platform entries.
    *   Liv emphasized the need for a simpler method to extract names directly from Iconic, referring to previous processes managed by Renee and Alexa.
    *   It was agreed that while not urgent, a more accessible naming sheet (possibly cloud-based) should be explored to ease the manual process.
    
*   **Overview of the New 14-Position Asset Naming Convention and Strategic Scaling System** Update
    *   Christopher presented the new strategic scaling system based on a 14-position asset naming format, integrating elements like funnel script ID, variation ID, platform indicator (FB, YT, etc.), dimensions, and length tier.
    *   New additions include ad category, expansion type, and asset type codes which will enable automated data flow from Facebook to Domo.
    *   Detailed discussions covered how talent codes are structured (using first and last initials for talent, editor, and copywriter) and the use of 'XX' for missing data.
    *   The system also includes markers such as a creation date, promo name (which will be populated when in promo stages), and a '-SCA' suffix for scaling assets.
    
*   **Google Asset Naming Challenges and Single Message Requirement per Ad Batch** Decision
    *   Liv and Omer discussed complexities with Google ad assets due to multiple headlines and descriptions, which prevent embedding of a singular tracking code.
    *   Christopher emphasized that each ad batch should be focused on one clear message or 'root angle' to ensure proper aggregation and tracking in the system.
    *   It was noted that if ad submissions (e.g., by Mike) include multiple messages, adjustments must be made to align with the single-message per batch directive.
    
*   **Platform-Specific Considerations and Upcoming Changes for Snapchat** Update
    *   Jessie mentioned the potential upcoming use of Snapchat and the need to reuse or adapt asset codes from Facebook.
    *   Christopher agreed that a distinct code (e.g., including 'SC' for Snapchat) would be integrated once ad launches for Snapchat are finalized.
    *   Team members agreed to inform each other of ad platform updates to ensure consistent coding in the naming convention.
    
*   **Length Tier Codes, Net New vs. Expansion Assets, and Expansion Type Categorization** Decision
    *   Christopher detailed the implementation of length tier codes (e.g., 30s for under 30 seconds, 60s for 31-60 seconds) and introduced NN (Net New) and NNMU (Net New Mashup) codes for asset variations.
    *   A clear distinction was established: net new assets would default the expansion type to 'XX', while expansion assets would use specific codes.
    *   Liv proposed refining expansion type codes by using 'EXV' for vertical and 'EXH' for horizontal expansions to enhance clarity, which was agreed upon by the team.
    
*   **Asset Type Codes – Differentiating AIBO and Podcast Assets** Update
    *   The team discussed asset type codes, particularly distinguishing between AIBO (AI voiceover used with B roll) and podcast assets.
    *   Christopher clarified that when an asset contained elements of both, podcast would take precedence over AIBO due to its defined context.
    *   The conversation acknowledged that future hybrid asset types might arise, with definitions refined based on performance insights.
    
*   **Promo Codes, Talent Code Updates, and ClickUp Adjustments** Decision
    *   Christopher instructed team members to add and update promo codes for upcoming campaigns, citing the example of a Fourth of July promo.
    *   Liv highlighted the need to update ClickUp by removing outdated generic expansion labels and replacing them with specific vertical or horizontal codes.
    *   Fatima was designated to handle promo code integration in ClickUp, ensuring that ad submissions reflect the updated naming format.
    
*   **Post-Launch Naming Process, Data Aggregation, and Next Steps for Automation** Decision
    *   The meeting covered the two-step naming process: a pre-launch asset ID and a post-launch duplicate used in scaling campaigns.
    *   A discussion ensued regarding whether adding additional identifiers (such as SCA with possible further notation) would interfere with data aggregation; it was decided that simply appending '-SCA' was adequate.
    *   Christopher announced plans to work with Liv on updating the asset generator and documentation, moving towards greater automation by integrating ClickUp and Iconic to streamline data flow from tickets to the strategic scaling system.
