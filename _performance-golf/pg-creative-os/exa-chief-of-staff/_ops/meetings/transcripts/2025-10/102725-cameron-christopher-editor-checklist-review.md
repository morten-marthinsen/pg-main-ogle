### Key Topics
*   **Guidelines for Call Out Text and Scroll Stoppers** Update
    *   The team discussed the importance of incorporating engaging visual elements—such as red arrows, zoom effects, and call out text—to grab attention in the opening frames.
    *   It was agreed that these items should be part of a checklist, ensuring that at least one or two attention-grabbing elements are included in every ad edit.
    *   The guidelines were framed as common sense instructions rather than strict formulas, giving editors creative leeway while maintaining quality control.
    
*   **Script and Subtitle Management** Update
    *   Discussion highlighted issues with AI-generated captions including spelling mistakes, improper breaks, and lack of manual refinement.
    *   The team considered using a two-column script (copy on the left and creative direction on the right) to better align visual instructions with the teleprompter version.
    *   Emphasis was placed on ensuring subtitles are manually checked—correcting hanging words, pacing, punctuation, and emphasizing important text—to improve readability and message clarity.
    
*   **Training and Addressing the Talent Gap in Editing** Idea
    *   The conversation acknowledged a noticeable talent gap, particularly in advanced motion graphics and nuanced creative execution.
    *   Both participants agreed on the need for training sessions (e.g., Loom tutorials, detailed review calls) and view-sharing of best practices to uplift the overall editing quality.
    *   It was discussed that higher-value scripts should be directed to top-performing editors (like Morton) while providing development opportunities for others.
    
*   **Music and Sound Effects Curation** Idea
    *   The significance of music and sound effects in setting the emotional tone of the ads was emphasized, with specific examples on how the right audio choice can elevate an ad.
    *   The team discussed building a curated library of proven music tracks and sound effect assets.
    *   There was a focus on ensuring correct audio cues are used (for example, using the appropriate club hit sounds) and avoiding generic 'elevator music' that does not enhance the edit.
    
*   **Review Process and Ticketing Workflow** Decision
    *   A clear review process was established with a 72-hour review window for tickets, supported by automated notifications at 48 hours and again 24 hours before auto-approval.
    *   The participants agreed that if the copywriter does not review within this timeframe, the ad edit should automatically be approved, with any further feedback handled via separate expansion tickets.
    *   This approach aims to streamline the workflow and reduce delays in launching ads.
    
*   **Use of Templates and Resource Sharing** Decision
    *   Plans were discussed to introduce standardized templates incorporating new branding, expected to roll out around December.
    *   These templates are designed to set clear guidelines (e.g., proper call out text size) and reduce manual errors in the editing process.
    *   There was also an idea to create a resource/graphic sharing sheet where editors can share high-quality examples and reusable creative elements with the team.
    
*   **B Roll Selection and Visual Consistency** Update
    *   The discussion included the importance of matching B roll footage with the script content, ensuring that selected clips educate, demonstrate, or connect emotionally with the viewer.
    *   Editors were reminded to understand the nuances of golf shots (e.g., lay-ups versus mis-hits) to avoid mismatches between the narrative and visuals.
    *   It was suggested that additional training or resource references (such as a cheat sheet for common golf shot types) could further improve consistency.
    
*   **Finalization and Rollout of Guidelines** Decision
    *   It was agreed that the refined set of guidelines and checklist should be reviewed with senior editors (e.g., Morton, David, Gabriel) before being shared with the entire ad editing team.
    *   The finalized guidelines will be published publicly in the ad editing channel to ensure visibility and consistency across the team.
    *   A follow-up meeting is expected to confirm the final decisions and adjustments based on feedback from the senior editors.
    
*   **Automation and Ticket Review Notifications** Decision
    *   The team decided to explore setting up an automated notification system (via Asana/ClickUp) so that tickets in the review phase automatically ping copywriters at set intervals.
    *   This system aims to help maintain accountability and ensure timely reviews, reducing the need for manual tracking through Slack or email.
    *   The proposed automation sends a reminder at 48 hours, followed by a 24-hour warning before auto-approval at 72 hours.
