The meeting focused on reviewing and optimizing the customer life cycle management with a particular emphasis on the activation phase of the customer journey. ### Key Topics
*   **Overview of Customer Lifecycle and Activation Phase**
    *   The meeting began with an overview of the customer lifecycle, emphasizing the transition from the acquisition phase to activation.
    *   Kateryna explained the importance of the activation phase in building trust, driving first actions (such as app downloads and initial engagements), and reducing early attrition.
    *   The discussion set the stage for refining the customer journey and ensuring long-term engagement.
    
*   **Email Campaign Analysis and Performance** Update
    *   Kateryna presented a deep-dive analysis of various email campaigns, including the Prospect Welcome Flow, Abandoned Cart Journey, and New Customer Welcome Flow.
    *   Prospect Welcome Flow issues were highlighted, such as high bounce rates, elevated unsubscribe rates on multiple emails, and a low click-to-open rate (below industry benchmarks).
    *   The New Customer Welcome Flow showed strong initial performance with high open and click rates, although declines in later emails (especially Day 3) indicated a need for clearer CTAs and improved messaging.
    *   For the Abandoned Cart Journey, the session revealed that too many emails (six or more) led to customer fatigue and a drop in conversions after the first email touch.
    
*   **Design and User Experience (UX) Issues** Problem
    *   The team discussed various design challenges, including inconsistent branding due to rotating sender names and cluttered email layouts that hinder scanning, particularly on mobile devices.
    *   Issues such as unclear hooks, weak or conflicting CTAs, and excessive white space were cited as contributors to lower engagement.
    *   These design concerns were identified as blocking factors in moving users from awareness to action.
    
*   **Abandoned Cart Journey Critique and Redesign** Decision
    *   The Abandoned Cart flow was critiqued for sending an excessive number of emails, which could lead to customer fatigue and reduced urgency.
    *   Technical issues such as inconsistent trigger logic and re-entry problems were noted; some fixes have already been implemented.
    *   The team agreed on the need to streamline the number of touch points and refocus the messaging to maintain a transactional nature.
    
*   **Omnichannel Integration and Strategy** Idea
    *   The presentation emphasized moving towards an omnichannel framework that integrates email, push notifications, in-app messages, and eventually SMS.
    *   Push notifications were highlighted for their high ROI and immediacy. Peter Chau mentioned that the push implementation was expected to be live by Thanksgiving.
    *   The importance of tracking customer consent for push notifications and improving the opt-in rate during onboarding was underscored, with a target inspired by previous industry performance.
    
*   **Technical Improvements and Version Control** Problem
    *   Issues with multiple versions of email flows (particularly in the abandoned cart campaign) and inconsistent suppression logic were identified as technical blockers.
    *   The team noted that fixes around trigger logic and re-entry have already been applied, but a more robust version control mechanism is needed.
    *   Plans were discussed to design a new app activation flow from scratch, aligning with the upcoming SDK setup and a December launch timeline.
    
*   **Data Integration, Dashboarding, and Future Metrics** Decision
    *   Faraaz Jamal proposed developing a dashboard to track key performance metrics such as open rates, click-through rates, and revenue per email, consolidating data across channels.
    *   Discussion included integrating insights from both email and push channels into a unified customer profile to better segment customers and map Lifetime Value (LTV).
    *   John Hardesty reinforced that these data-driven insights would enhance cohort analysis and drive targeted marketing efforts.
    
*   **Next Steps and Action Items** Decision
    *   It was decided to redesign the New Customer and Prospect Welcome flows with clearer, action-oriented CTAs and consistent branding, including evergreen modules for app downloads and free assessments.
    *   The team agreed on streamlining the Abandoned Cart emails to avoid fatigue and maintain urgency.
    *   Plans to implement push notifications and track opt-in consent were confirmed, with a pilot push campaign expected by Thanksgiving.
    *   Faraaz will work with Patrick to develop a dashboard (MVP by early January) to integrate and monitor data across the merged omnichannel strategy.
