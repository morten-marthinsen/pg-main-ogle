### Key Topics
*   **Meeting Purpose & Overview**
    *   Christopher Ogle opened the meeting by explaining that the primary focus was to dissect the entire creative asset production process and identify potential blockers.
    *   The team aimed to determine specific bottlenecks and measure lead times for different asset types (e.g., net new ads vs. expansions) to improve workflow efficiency.
    *   The discussion emphasized a mathematical approach to understanding production capacity and matching it with media buying demands.
    
*   **Identifying Process Bottlenecks & Lead Times** Update
    *   The group discussed the need to systematically map out each step of the current production process to identify minor (1%) and significant (5–10%) blockers.
    *   They compared asset types, noting that net new ads might take 2-3 hours while expansions could take longer, highlighting the importance of understanding different turnaround times.
    *   The goal was to use this data to optimize production, improve scheduling, and balance workload across editors.
    
*   **Data Collection Using ClickUp Automation** Update
    *   The team discussed the implementation of ClickUp automation to record start dates when tasks transition to 'in progress', thus capturing baseline turnaround times.
    *   Fatima Cantos noted that while time tracking has historically not been used with contractors, the upcoming ClickUp automation would help gather relevant data for further planning.
    *   This system would initially use ballpark estimates from editors rather than precise hourly tracking, providing a preliminary measure of task durations.
    
*   **Granularity of Time Tracking and Its Impact** Idea
    *   There was an in-depth discussion on whether to track performance by precise hours or by weekly output.
    *   Christopher Ogle argued for a detailed, mathematically driven approach (tracking hours) to better understand capacity, assign tasks accordingly, and improve process efficiency.
    *   Morten Marthinsen and Fatima Cantos voiced concerns that overly granular tracking could create pressure and negatively impact creativity by making editors feel excessively monitored.
    *   The consensus was to start with ballpark estimates for lead times and later assess if more detailed tracking is needed, maintaining flexibility and avoiding punitive oversight.
    
*   **Utilizing AI & Automation to Enhance Workflow** Update
    *   The discussion covered the integration of AI tools to assist in the production workflow, especially for tasks such as generating creative ideas and selecting B-roll footage.
    *   Morten Marthinsen mentioned that he was working on providing AI access to all editors, and highlighted that some team members were already using AI to speed up parts of their process.
    *   Assistant editors had been trained to use AI for pre-selecting footage, enabling a more streamlined review process and faster completion of editing tasks.
    
*   **Alignment with Media Buying and Future Planning** Decision
    *   The team discussed the importance of aligning production capacity with the media buying team’s asset demand forecasts.
    *   Fatima Cantos proposed coordinating through Slack and during the Monday cross-functional call to share weekly or bi-weekly asset requirements and ensure production meets media demand.
    *   The conversation emphasized planning metrics, such as asset counts per week, and how to adjust production resources based on evolving media needs.
    
*   **Immediate Next Steps & Action Items** Decision
    *   Christopher Ogle indicated that this call was the beginning of a series of meetings to further refine the production process and address identified blockers.
    *   Tim was tasked with sending over the resource detailing different lead times via Slack to assist in data analysis for future planning.
    *   Fatima planned to communicate with the media buying team asynchronously and during the next cross-functional call to clarify asset demands.
    *   The team agreed to gather initial baseline data via ClickUp, reassess the effectiveness of the approach, and explore further process optimizations (including potential plugins and automation tools).
