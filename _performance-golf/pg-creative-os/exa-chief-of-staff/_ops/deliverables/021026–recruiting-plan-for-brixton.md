## 1. Executive Summary

The creative team has the foundation but is missing critical roles that block execution at the speed and quality John and Brixton expect. The plan prioritizes hires that:

1. Fill the **visual execution gap** left by Russ's departure — John's most emphatic need
2. Support the **organic content engine** (JoJo's operation needs a dedicated preditor)
3. Replace **known gaps** already in pipeline (social media, US editors)
4. Leverage AI to **reduce headcount pressure** in areas where TESS/Neco/VEDA can substitute or augment

This is not a wishlist. It's a sequenced plan where each hire unlocks the next stage of creative output and maps to a brand thread.

## 4. AI Leverage Impact on Headcount

This is the OPEX reallocation story. The AI Creative OS (TESS → Neco → VEDA) doesn't just improve quality and speed — it changes the headcount math.

### Where AI Reduces Headcount Pressure

| Function | Traditional Headcount Need | AI Offset | Impact |
|----------|--------------------------|-----------|--------|
| **Ad brief generation** | Copywriter(s) writing hooks, scripts, angles | Neco generates ad hooks, scripts, influencer briefs, static image briefs from shared TESS research | Reduces need for junior copywriters. Reallocate budget to senior creative talent. |
| **Video editing (derivatives)** | 2-3 editors producing cutdowns, hook stacks, scroll stoppers | VEDA automates derivative asset production from source videos | 2 US editors may become 1 + VEDA. Reassess at Day 45 after VEDA Phase 2B. |
| **Research & angle identification** | Analyst or senior strategist reviewing performance data | TESS analyzes ad performance, classifies winners, identifies expansion angles | No dedicated analyst needed. Research flows directly into Neco briefs. |
| **Campaign alignment** | Multiple meetings + coordination overhead to keep teams on same angles | Shared research pipeline ensures paid, organic, influencer, and landing page teams all work from the same data | Reduces coordination headcount. Fatima's ops role shifts from traffic cop to strategic partner. |

### Where AI Increases Need

| Function | Why AI Can't Replace This | Hire Impact |
|----------|--------------------------|-------------|
| **Visual design / brand direction** | AI can't set brand taste. It can execute, but someone must define the standard. | Art director is MORE important in an AI world — they direct the machine. |
| **Organic content / on-camera talent** | JoJo's authentic presence and community trust can't be replicated. | Preditor SUPPORT for JoJo increases leverage of her irreplaceable talent. |
| **Strategic brand judgment** | Knowing when to break the rules, when a campaign "feels" right, taste-level decisions. | This is Christopher's role + the senior design hire. |

### The OPEX Reallocation Pitch

> Every headcount dollar saved by AI is a dollar reinvested in brand amplification — celebrities, ACC activations, PR placements, experiential marketing. The things AI can't do. The creative team gets leaner on production and richer on brand.

### Evaluation Criteria (Non-Negotiables)

Per the Feb 6 meeting agenda and John's signals:

1. **Taste** — Can they demonstrate brand-level visual judgment? (Art director: Titleist/Apple caliber)
2. **Speed** — Can they operate at PG's pace without sacrificing quality?
3. **Ownership** — Do they take a brief and run, or need constant direction?
4. **AI fluency** — Are they comfortable working WITH AI tools (TESS research, Neco briefs, VEDA editing)? Not "can they build AI" — can they leverage it?
