# 1.6: Angle Deduplication & Ranking

## Purpose

Merge all angle candidates from 1.1-1.5, remove duplicates and near-duplicates, rank by strategic strength, research grounding, and competitive differentiation to produce 10-15 unique ranked angles.

## Inputs

- 1.1-pain-angles.md
- 1.2-mechanism-angles.md
- 1.3-identity-angles.md
- 1.4-proof-angles.md
- 1.5-competitive-gap-angles.md

## Process

1. **Collect all angle candidates**
   - Load all angles from microskills 1.1-1.5
   - Total angle count before deduplication

2. **Remove exact duplicates**
   - Same plain language statement
   - Different source but same strategic idea

3. **Identify near-duplicates**
   - Same strategic territory, slightly different phrasing
   - Example: "Foods you trust harm you" and "Healthy foods causing damage"
   - **Keep strongest version** (best sourced, clearest statement)

4. **Rank remaining angles**
   Score each angle on three dimensions (1-10 scale):

   - **Strategic Strength (1-10):**
     - How powerfully does this angle lead to the big idea?
     - Is the pathway natural and compelling?
     - Does it create genuine curiosity?

   - **Research Grounding (1-10):**
     - How many research quotes/data points support this?
     - Is the sourcing strong and specific?
     - Does prospect language validate this angle?

   - **Competitive Differentiation (1-10):**
     - How different is this from what competitors are running?
     - Does this give us unique positioning?
     - Does this exploit whitespace?

5. **Calculate composite score**
   ```
   Composite = (Strategic Strength × 0.4) + (Research Grounding × 0.3) + (Competitive Differentiation × 0.3)
   ```

6. **Rank by composite score (descending)**
   - Top angle = highest composite
   - All angles scored and ranked

7. **Verify minimum diversity**
   - At least 3 of 5 angle categories represented (Pain, Mechanism, Identity, Proof, Competitive)
   - No single category dominates (>60% of angles)
