## Cross-Specimen Pattern Analysis

### Performance Optimization Patterns Observed

1. **Variant Architecture:** AG1 runs 5+ simultaneous variants with different hooks (gift bundle, celebrity, immune, segment-specific, identity) targeting different audience segments. This maximizes testing surface area while maintaining brand consistency.

2. **Funnel-Position CTA Matching:**
   - Top-of-funnel (awareness): "Learn More" — lower commitment
   - Mid-funnel (consideration): "Learn More" with specific benefit claims
   - Bottom-of-funnel (retargeting): "Get Offer" / "Shop Now" — direct conversion

3. **Longevity as Performance Signal:** SoFi's student loan ads have been running 9+ months. Nike's "on and off the field" ad ran 8+ months. Long-running ads in the Meta system indicate strong ROAS — Meta's algorithm would stop delivering non-performing ads. Longevity IS performance data.

4. **Retargeting Differentiation:** Nutrisystem runs distinct copy for retargeting ("Come back") vs. acquisition. The retargeting variants test different value propositions (variety vs. price vs. social proof) against the same warm audience.

5. **Audience Segmentation Variants:** SoFi segments by profession (dental school) and AG1 segments by identity (first responders, athletes). Same core offer, audience-specific hooks — maximum conversion with minimal creative investment.

6. **Modular Testing Elements per Ad:**
   - Hook (emotion vs. feature vs. price vs. celebrity)
   - Proof type (testimonial vs. social proof vs. specificity)
   - CTA (Install Now vs. Learn More vs. Shop Now vs. Get Offer)
   - Offer structure (gift stack vs. discount vs. trial)
   - Audience targeting (broad vs. niche segment)

7. **Price Anchoring Patterns:** Nutrisystem leads with "$89" — specific, testable price points. AG1 leads with "FREE" (gift bundle value stack). SoFi leads with "save thousands" (quantified but non-specific). Each represents a different price psychology approach, all testable.

## Collection Notes

- **No actual performance metrics available:** Meta Ad Library does not expose CTR, conversion rates, or ROAS. Performance signals are inferred from: (1) ad longevity (longer = better performing), (2) variant density (more variants = active optimization), (3) CTA variation (testing different CTAs = performance optimization).
- **AG1 is the standout performer:** 5 distinct hook variants running simultaneously from the same brand demonstrates sophisticated testing infrastructure. AG1 is widely recognized as one of the top performance advertisers on Meta.
- **Noom ads were behind login wall:** Noom's Meta ads required age verification login, preventing copy extraction. Nutrisystem substituted as a health/supplement performance advertiser.
- **Format limitation:** All specimens captured as static images. Video ads (which are a major performance format) were not fully extractable through the Meta Ad Library scraper.
