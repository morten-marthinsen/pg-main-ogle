# 1.3-niche-calibrator.md

> **Layer:** 1 (Formula Selection)
> **Skill:** E2-subject-line-engine
> **Purpose:** Calibrate formula selection and generation guidance for the target audience/niche
> **Outputs:** `niche_calibration` object with niche-specific constraints, vocabulary, and warnings

---

## FUNCTION

Take the formula selection from 1.2 and calibrate it for the target audience. Different niches have different tolerance for edginess, different vocabulary expectations, different urgency thresholds, and different cultural reference pools. This calibration prevents cross-niche contamination and ensures SLs feel native to the audience.

---

## NICHE CALIBRATION MATRIX

| Niche | Edginess Tolerance | Formality Level | Urgency Sensitivity | Humor Threshold | Key Vocabulary |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------|----|
| **Health/Supplements** | Medium | Low-Medium | Medium (compliance) | Medium | Body parts, conditions, ingredients, "doctor," "study" |
| **Finance/Trading** | Medium-High | Medium | High | Low-Medium | Returns, markets, portfolio, "insider," "analyst" |
| **Golf/Sports** | High | Low | Low | High | Swing, course, handicap, "pro," game-specific |
| **Personal Development** | Medium | Low | Medium | Medium | Growth, habits, mindset, purpose, "coach" |
| **Technology** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium-High | Features, specs, innovation, "update," platform |
| **Info Products/Courses** | High | Low | High | High | Secrets, systems, blueprint, "method," results |
| **E-commerce/Physical** | Low-Medium | Medium | High | Low | Quality, materials, shipping, savings, "limited" |

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

```
EXECUTE 1.3-niche-calibrator:
  INPUT: formula_selection (from 1.2), email_context (from Layer 0)
  OUTPUT: niche_calibration object

  1. IDENTIFY target niche from email context
     Sources:
       a. E0 campaign blueprint (if Mode A)
       b. Project brief (if Mode B)
       c. Email content analysis (fallback)

  2. LOAD niche calibration parameters
     FROM: shared/niche-adaptation-guide.md (if available)
     OR: Use built-in matrix above

  3. CALIBRATE each selected formula:
     FOR each formula in formula_selection:
       a. CHECK edginess compatibility
          IF formula = SL-EDG and niche edginess_tolerance = Low:
            DOWNGRADE: "Reduce edginess or replace with milder formula"
          IF formula = SL-EDG and niche edginess_tolerance = High:
            UPGRADE: "Full edginess permitted"

       b. CHECK urgency compatibility
          IF formula = SL-URG and niche urgency_sensitivity = Low:
            WARN: "Urgency may not resonate. Use sparingly."
          IF formula = SL-URG and niche = Health:
            WARN: "Health compliance may restrict urgency language."

       c. CHECK humor compatibility
          IF formula = SL-POP or SL-BPM and niche humor_threshold = Low:
            WARN: "Humor may not land. Test carefully."

       d. ASSIGN niche-specific vocabulary guidance
          What words/phrases feel NATIVE to this audience?
          What words/phrases feel FOREIGN and should be avoided?

       e. ASSIGN niche-specific SL examples
          What do successful SLs in this niche look like?

  4. IDENTIFY niche-specific forbidden patterns:
     - Health: "cure," "treat," "guaranteed results" (compliance)
     - Finance: "guaranteed returns," "risk-free," "insider trading" (compliance)
     - Golf: Guru language, health buzzwords, finance jargon
     - Any niche: Cross-niche vocabulary contamination

  5. GENERATE niche-adapted generation constraints
     FOR each selected formula:
       a. Vocabulary whitelist (preferred words for this niche)
       b. Vocabulary blacklist (words to avoid in this niche)
       c. Tone adjustment (more/less formal, more/less edgy)
       d. Cultural reference pool (what references this audience knows)

  6. VALIDATE formula selection post-calibration
     IF any formula becomes incompatible after niche calibration:
       a. Flag for replacement
       b. Suggest alternative formula
       c. Ensure minimum 5 formulas remain after calibration

  7. ASSEMBLE niche_calibration object
  8. RETURN niche_calibration
```

## NICHE ANTI-CONTAMINATION RULES

These are HARD rules that cannot be overridden:

```
HEALTH/SUPPLEMENTS:
  NEVER: "cure," "treat," "diagnose," "prevent" (FDA compliance)
  NEVER: Financial jargon, golf jargon, tech jargon
  CAUTION: "Guaranteed" (requires specific legal framing)

FINANCE/TRADING:
  NEVER: "guaranteed returns," "risk-free," "can't lose" (SEC compliance)
  NEVER: Health jargon, golf jargon, personal dev jargon
  CAUTION: "Insider" (legal implications)

GOLF/SPORTS:
  NEVER: Health supplement language, financial advice language
  NEVER: Guru/spiritual language
  CAUTION: Overpromising scores or distances

ANY NICHE:
  NEVER: Cross-niche vocabulary (health words in finance SLs, etc.)
  NEVER: AI telltale language ("unlock," "harness," "leverage")
  NEVER: Generic clickbait regardless of niche
```

## FAILURE MODES

| Mode | Trigger | Remediation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CRITICAL | Niche not identifiable | HALT -- Request niche from user |
| CRITICAL | Post-calibration formula count < 5 | Add replacement formulas to reach minimum |
| DEGRADED | Niche adaptation guide not found | WARN -- Use built-in matrix (less precise) |
| DEGRADED | Cross-niche contamination detected | WARN -- Flag specific vocabulary issues |
