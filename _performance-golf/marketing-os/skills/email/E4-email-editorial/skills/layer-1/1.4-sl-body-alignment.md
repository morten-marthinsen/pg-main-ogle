# Layer 1.4 -- Subject Line / Body Alignment Check

## Purpose

Verify that each email's subject line accurately matches its body content. Subject line / body mismatch is a trust-destroying failure: the reader opens expecting one thing and gets another. This check ensures the subject line's promise, curiosity gap, or hook is DELIVERED by the email body. Every email must be checked -- no sampling, no skipping.

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Classify Each Subject Line's Promise Type

Before checking alignment, understand what the subject line promises:

```
SUBJECT LINE PROMISE TYPES:

  CURIOSITY GAP:
    SL creates a question the reader wants answered.
    Body MUST answer that question (or clearly lead to it).
    Example: "The #1 mistake golfers make on the range"
    Body must: Identify and explain the mistake.

  BENEFIT CLAIM:
    SL promises a specific outcome or benefit.
    Body MUST deliver content supporting that benefit.
    Example: "How to add 30 yards to your drive"
    Body must: Contain technique/advice for distance.

  STORY HOOK:
    SL teases a story or narrative.
    Body MUST tell that story (or a story that connects).
    Example: "What happened when I fired my coach"
    Body must: Contain the story about firing the coach.

  PROVOCATIVE/CONTRARIAN:
    SL makes a bold or controversial claim.
    Body MUST argue for that claim with evidence/reasoning.
    Example: "Why stretching is ruining your game"
    Body must: Argue against stretching with rationale.

  QUESTION:
    SL asks a direct question.
    Body MUST address/answer that question.
    Example: "Are you making this putting mistake?"
    Body must: Describe the putting mistake and its solution.

  NAME-DROP:
    SL references a specific person or authority.
    Body MUST involve that person (quote, story, lesson).
    Example: "What Tiger Woods taught me about pressure"
    Body must: Contain a Tiger Woods reference with a lesson.

  URGENCY/DEADLINE:
    SL signals time pressure.
    Body MUST contain deadline information and CTA.
    Example: "Last chance - enrollment closes tonight"
    Body must: Contain deadline details and enrollment link.

  LIST/NUMBER:
    SL promises a specific number of items.
    Body MUST contain at least that many items.
    Example: "5 things I wish I knew about iron play"
    Body must: Contain 5 (not 4, not 3) things about irons.
```

### Step 2: Check Alignment for Every Email

For EVERY email in the sequence:

```
FOR email IN loaded_sequence.emails:

  1. READ the subject line
  2. CLASSIFY the promise type (from Step 1)
  3. READ the email body
  4. CHECK: Does the body DELIVER on the subject line's promise?

  ALIGNMENT SCORING:

    ALIGNED (no issue):
      The body clearly delivers on the SL promise.
      A reader would not feel deceived or confused.
      The SL accurately represents what the email contains.

    PARTIALLY_ALIGNED (P3 - minor):
      The body touches on the SL topic but doesn't fully deliver.
      Examples:
        - SL promises "5 things" but body has only 4
        - SL teases a specific story but body only briefly mentions it
        - SL names a person but that person is a minor reference, not the focus
      Reader might notice but isn't likely to feel deceived.

    MISALIGNED (P2 - major):
      The body does NOT deliver on the SL promise.
      Examples:
        - SL promises a technique but body is about a testimonial
        - SL teases a story that never appears in the body
        - SL names Tiger Woods but Tiger is never mentioned in body
        - SL promises urgency/deadline but body is a regular content email
      Reader WILL notice and feel misled. Trust damaged.

    COMPLETELY_MISALIGNED (P1 - critical):
      The body is about a DIFFERENT TOPIC than the SL.
      The SL was clearly written for a different email.
      Examples:
        - SL about putting, body about driving
        - SL about a health topic, body about a financial topic
        - SL is a deadline warning, body has no mention of any deadline
      Reader will feel deceived. Unsubscribe risk.

  5. RECORD the alignment result with evidence:
     - What does the SL promise?
     - Does the body deliver?
     - Specific text evidence (quote from body that does/doesn't match)
```

### Step 3: Check SL-Body Tone Alignment

Beyond content alignment, check tonal alignment:

```
TONE ALIGNMENT CHECKS:

  1. SL TONE vs. BODY TONE:
     If SL is playful/humorous, body should have humor.
     If SL is serious/urgent, body should be serious.
     If SL is provocative, body should be bold.

     TONE MISMATCH:
       SL is playful but body is dry and formal → P3
       SL is urgent but body is casual with no deadline → P2
       SL is provocative but body is timid and hedging → P3

  2. ENERGY LEVEL MATCH:
     High-energy SL → Body should open with matching energy
     Low-key SL → Body should not be hyperactive
     Mismatch in energy = P4 (cosmetic but noticeable)
```

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Remediation |
|---------|-------------|
| Subject line missing from an email | Log as P1 issue (email has no SL). Alignment check impossible. |
| Subject line is extremely vague (e.g., "Hey") | Vague SLs align with almost any body. Score as ALIGNED but note "vague SL -- low engagement risk." |
| SL promise type is ambiguous (could be 2 types) | Classify as the stronger/more specific type. Check alignment against that interpretation. |
| SL uses a formula not in the promise type list | Classify as closest match. Note the novel formula. |
| Email body is extremely short (<50 words) | Short bodies make alignment hard to judge. Score conservatively. |
