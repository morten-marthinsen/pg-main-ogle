# 1.5 — Reader-Focus & Deliverability Check

**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** 2026-03-02
**Layer:** 1 (Issue Identification)
**Model:** opus
**Source:** Ann Handley (customer-centricity), Chad White (deliverability factors)

---

## PURPOSE

Two lightweight checks that catch issues other audit dimensions miss:
1. **Reader-Focus:** Flag emails where the brand/company/product is the grammatical subject too often (company-speak instead of reader-speak)
2. **Deliverability:** Flag copy patterns that risk spam filtering before the email reaches the inbox

---

## CHECK 1: READER-FOCUS (Customer-Centricity)

**The Rule:** In any email, the reader (you/your) should be the grammatical subject of sentences MORE often than the brand/company/product/we.

**How to Check:**
1. Count sentences where the brand/company/product is the subject ("We created...", "Our formula...", "[Brand] is...")
2. Count sentences where the reader is the subject ("You'll notice...", "Your results...", "If you...")
3. If brand-as-subject exceeds 30% of total sentences → flag as P2

**Transformation Pattern:**
| Company-Speak (Flag) | Reader-Speak (Pass) |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| "Our new formula contains 5 nutrients" | "You'll feel the difference in 3 days" |
| "We've been in business for 30 years" | "You're getting 30 years of expertise in one system" |
| "[Brand] is the #1 rated..." | "You deserve the #1 rated..." |
| "Our team developed..." | "What this means for you..." |

**Severity:**
- 30-50% brand-as-subject → P2 (major — email reads as company newsletter)
- 50%+ brand-as-subject → P1 (critical — complete voice failure for email format)

**Exclusions:** Testimonial (TM) body type is partially exempt — testimonials naturally reference the brand. Still flag if TM exceeds 50% brand-as-subject.

---

## CHECK 2: DELIVERABILITY RISK

**The Rule:** Scan each email for copy patterns that trigger spam filters. This is NOT a technical deliverability audit — it's a COPY check for language that hurts inbox placement.

**Spam Trigger Words/Patterns (Flag if present):**

| Category | Triggers | Severity |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Financial claims** | "make money", "earn $X", "cash bonus", "double your", "financial freedom" | P3 if 1-2 instances, P2 if 3+ |
| **Urgency pressure** | ALL CAPS urgency ("ACT NOW", "LIMITED TIME", "DON'T MISS"), excessive exclamation marks (3+) | P3 |
| **Guarantee language** | "100% guaranteed", "risk-free", "no obligation", "satisfaction guaranteed" | P3 |
| **Medical/health claims** | "cure", "miracle", "breakthrough treatment" (unqualified) | P2 |
| **Formatting red flags** | All-caps subject line, image-only email (no text), excessive links (5+), colored/oversized fonts | P3 |

**What This Check Does NOT Cover:**
- Technical authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) — that's ESP/platform responsibility
- Send volume/frequency — that's operational, not copy
- List hygiene — outside email content scope

**Severity:**
- 1-2 minor triggers → P3 (swap individual words)
- 3+ triggers OR any medical/financial claim → P2 (Arena revision)
