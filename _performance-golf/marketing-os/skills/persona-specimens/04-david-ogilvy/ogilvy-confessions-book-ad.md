## Headline

"Every secret but one is in this book."

## Context & Background

An ad promoting Ogilvy's own book, "Confessions of an Advertising Man." The headline is a masterclass in curiosity: by saying "every secret but one," Ogilvy simultaneously promises massive value (every secret) while creating an irresistible itch (which one is missing?).

## Key Specimen Value

- Curiosity gap headline: "every secret but one" -- promises completeness while withholding
- Ogilvy selling himself -- demonstrates how he applied his own principles to self-promotion
- Book-as-lead-generation: the book itself was a client acquisition tool for the agency
