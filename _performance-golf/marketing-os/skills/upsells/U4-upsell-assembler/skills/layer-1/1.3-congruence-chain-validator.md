# 1.3 — Congruence Chain Validator

**Skill:** U4 — Upsell Sequence Assembler
**Microskill:** 1.3 — Congruence Chain Validator
**Layer:** 1
**Model:** sonnet
**Input:** `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`, `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`, all loaded copy pieces (U1, U2, U3)
**Output:** `layer-1/1.3-congruence-chain.md`

---

## PURPOSE

Validate the congruence CHAIN across the full upsell sequence — not just individual page congruence (U2 and U3 already validated that internally) but the page-to-page logical connection that makes the sequence feel like a continuous journey rather than disconnected offers.

---

## EXECUTION STEPS

1. **Read `layer-0/0.1-input-context.md`** for FE context (product, price, mechanism) and the loaded pieces.

2. **Read `layer-1/1.1-sequence-order.md`** for the ordered sequence with positions.

3. **Read each copy piece in sequence order.** For each adjacent pair of pages, evaluate three dimensions of congruence:

4. **Evaluate Link 1: FE → Order Bump.**
   - **Logical extension:** Does the bump extend the FE mechanism? The bump should feel like "the missing piece" or "the accelerator" for what the buyer just purchased. It should NOT introduce an unrelated product.
   - **Evidence:** Cite specific language from the bump that references or extends the FE mechanism/promise.
   - **Break indicators:** Bump introduces a new mechanism, new root cause, or product with no clear connection to FE.
   - Score: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK / BROKEN

5. **Evaluate Link 2: Order Bump → Upsell 1.**
   - **Logical extension:** Does the upsell build on what the buyer now has (FE + bump)? The upsell should feel like the natural "next level" — not a sideways move to a different category.
   - **Evidence:** Cite specific language from the upsell opening (Congratulate/Amplify sections) that acknowledges both the FE and the bump.
   - **Break indicators:** Upsell ignores the bump entirely, addresses a different audience segment, or requires different prerequisite knowledge than FE + bump provided.
   - Score: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK / BROKEN

6. **Evaluate Link 3: Upsell 1 → Downsell 1.**
   - **Logical reframe:** Does the downsell genuinely reframe the declined upsell — different angle, NOT just a cheaper version? Law 2 of the 5 Laws: congruence is everything, and the downsell must maintain congruence while offering a legitimate alternative perspective.
   - **Reframe quality check:** What is the downsell's reframe type?
     - GOOD: Different emphasis (speed → simplicity), different scope (comprehensive → core only), different format (full course → quick-start guide)
     - BAD: Same exact pitch at a lower price, same product with fewer bonuses stripped but same angle, guilt-based "last chance" without reframe
   - **Evidence:** Cite the downsell's opening and angle. How does it reframe what was just declined?
   - **Break indicators:** Downsell is just "the same thing but cheaper" with no new angle. Or downsell contradicts the upsell's positioning.
   - Score: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK / BROKEN

7. **Check language register consistency across ALL pages.**
   - Read the voice/tone of each page. Are they all in the same register?
   - Consistent: all conversational, or all authoritative, or all urgent. The register set by the FE should carry through.
   - Inconsistent: bump is casual but upsell is formal. Or upsell is calm but downsell is panicky.
   - Cite specific phrases from each page that demonstrate the register.

8. **Check emotional tone progression.**
   - Expected progression: Celebration (bump) → Celebration + Aspiration (upsell Congratulate/Amplify) → Acknowledgment + Gentle Pivot (downsell)
   - The tone should never go BACKWARD — a downsell that shames the buyer for declining is a congruence break.
   - Cite the emotional tone of each page's opening.

9. **Check mechanism language consistency.**
   - Beyond just the mechanism NAME (Thread 1 in 1.2), check whether the mechanism is DESCRIBED consistently.
   - If the upsell says the mechanism "rebalances hormonal signals" and the downsell says it "detoxifies cellular pathways" — same name but different description = congruence break.
   - Cite the mechanism description from each page.

10. **Compile the congruence chain report** with per-link scores and overall chain assessment.

## CONSTRAINTS

- This validator checks BETWEEN pages, not within pages. U2's internal congruence score (from 4.2) already validated the upsell page against the FE. U4's job is to validate the CHAIN: does the full sequence hold together as a journey?
- "Just cheaper" is NOT a legitimate downsell reframe. If the downsell is the same product, same angle, same copy structure but with a lower price tag, score the Upsell→Downsell link as WEAK or BROKEN. A real reframe changes the emphasis, scope, or format.
- Emotional tone should never go backward. Shame, guilt, or "you're making a mistake" language in the downsell is a congruence break — it violates the buyer validation thread and creates a hostile experience.
- Language register must be consistent across ALL pages. The upsell sequence is one experience from the buyer's perspective, not separate documents. A shift from casual to formal (or vice versa) breaks immersion.
- Cite EXACT QUOTES from the copy as evidence. Do not summarize or paraphrase. The quote is the evidence.
- Do not confuse congruence with repetition. Pages should be congruent (logically connected, consistent mechanism/tone) but NOT repetitive (same copy, same angle, same structure). Repetition across pages is a different problem (staleness) but not a congruence break.

---

## FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Detection | Response |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Bump introduces unrelated product/mechanism | Link 1 evidence shows no connection between bump and FE mechanism | Score BROKEN. Recommend U5 add congruence bridge language to bump, or flag for U1 revision if the product itself is misaligned. |
| Upsell ignores the bump entirely | Link 2 evidence shows upsell acknowledges FE but not bump | Score WEAK (not necessarily BROKEN — many upsells reference only the FE). Note the gap for U5 consideration. |
| Downsell is "just cheaper" with no reframe | Link 3 reframe quality check fails — same angle, lower price | Score BROKEN. This violates Law 2. Recommend specific reframe angle for U5, or flag for U3 revision if structural. |
| Register shifts between pages | Cross-page register check shows inconsistency | Document the shift with quotes. Recommend U5 normalize to the FE's register. |
| Downsell uses shame/guilt language | Emotional tone check shows negative tone at downsell opening | Score BROKEN for tone progression. Recommend U5 replace with acknowledgment + pivot language. Cite the specific guilty language. |
