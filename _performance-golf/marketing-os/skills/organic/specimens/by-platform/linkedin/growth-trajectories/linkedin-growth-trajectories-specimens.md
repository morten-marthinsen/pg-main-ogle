## TRAJECTORY #001 — Justin Welsh

### Profile
- **Name:** Justin Welsh
- **Niche:** Solopreneur, one-person business, creator economy
- **Starting Point:** ~2,000 followers (2019), former SaaS executive
- **Current Size:** 500,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~6 years, but exponential after Year 2
- **Notable:** Generates $5M+/year as a solopreneur, entirely from content-driven products

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Foundation (0-5K) — Months 1-8**
- Posted daily about leaving corporate and building independently
- Content was raw and experimental — testing what resonated
- Focused on "lessons from leaving a $5M executive career"
- Engagement strategy: replied to every single comment, commented on 20+ posts daily
- Growth rate: ~500 followers/month

**Phase 2: System Building (5K-25K) — Months 9-18**
- Developed a repeatable content system (templates + batching)
- Identified 3 core content pillars: solopreneurship, LinkedIn growth, digital products
- Started the "Saturday Solopreneur" newsletter
- Introduced carousel documents as primary format
- Growth rate: ~1,500 followers/month

**Phase 3: Breakout (25K-100K) — Months 18-30**
- Multiple viral posts (10K+ likes) per month
- Newsletter crossed 100K subscribers
- Launched "The Operating System" (LinkedIn growth course, $150)
- Content became a funnel: post → newsletter → product
- Growth rate: ~5,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Authority (100K-500K) — Months 30-72**
- Recognized as THE solopreneur voice on LinkedIn
- Posts average 5,000-15,000 likes
- Revenue diversified: courses, consulting, sponsorships
- Content cadence: 5 posts/week (never misses)
- Growth rate: ~8,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 5x/week (M-F), never misses |
| Primary format | Text-only (80%), carousel (15%), image+text (5%) |
| Content pillars | Solopreneurship, LinkedIn growth, digital products |
| Hook style | Personal story + specific numbers |
| Post length | 25-40 lines (optimized for dwell time) |
| Signature move | "Saturday Solopreneur" newsletter (consistent branding) |

### Engagement Patterns
- **Comment strategy:** Replies to every comment within the first 2 hours
- **Community building:** Created a paid community (The Operating System) that engages with content
- **Cross-platform:** Repurposes LinkedIn content to Twitter/X and newsletter
- **Engagement pods:** Publicly against pods, relies on organic engagement

### Monetization Approach
1. Free content on LinkedIn (top of funnel)
2. Newsletter "Saturday Solopreneur" (middle of funnel)
3. "The Operating System" course — $150 (primary revenue)
4. "The LinkedIn Playbook" course — $97 (secondary revenue)
5. Sponsorships in newsletter — $5,000-10,000 per placement
6. Advisory/consulting (limited) — $1,000+/hour

### Key Lessons
- **Consistency beats virality.** Welsh posts every weekday without exception. The compound effect of 1,500+ posts is more powerful than any single viral hit.
- **Systemize everything.** He batches content weekly, uses templates, and has a repeatable process. This is sustainable creativity.
- **Niche into one identity.** "The solopreneur guy" is instantly recognizable. He owns that word on LinkedIn.
- **Newsletter is the conversion engine.** LinkedIn is rented land. The newsletter is owned audience.
- **Products should be $100-200.** High volume, low friction. He sells thousands per month at accessible price points.

## TRAJECTORY #002 — Sahil Bloom

### Profile
- **Name:** Sahil Bloom
- **Niche:** Personal development, investing, frameworks, mental models
- **Starting Point:** ~500 followers (2020), former VC at Altamont Capital
- **Current Size:** 300,000+ followers on LinkedIn (1.5M+ on Twitter/X)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years
- **Notable:** Cross-platform creator who built equally on LinkedIn and Twitter

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Twitter-First (0-3K LinkedIn) — Months 1-12**
- Primary growth was on Twitter (viral threads about frameworks)
- LinkedIn was a repurposing channel — Twitter threads reformatted as LinkedIn posts
- Content: mental models, investing frameworks, personal development
- Growth rate: ~200 followers/month on LinkedIn (secondary focus)

**Phase 2: LinkedIn Adaptation (3K-30K) — Months 12-24**
- Realized LinkedIn posts needed different formatting than Twitter
- Adopted broetry-style formatting and longer-form posts
- Visual frameworks (named models with diagrams) became signature
- "The Curiosity Chronicle" newsletter launched
- Growth rate: ~2,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Platform Synergy (30K-150K) — Months 24-42**
- Content became platform-native for both Twitter and LinkedIn
- LinkedIn posts focused more on career/professional development
- Twitter posts stayed broader (investing, frameworks, entrepreneurship)
- Newsletter became the hub connecting both audiences
- Growth rate: ~4,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Brand Scale (150K-300K+) — Months 42-60**
- Recognized as one of the top "framework creators" globally
- Launched paid products, courses, and brand partnerships
- Content pillars refined: frameworks, fatherhood, entrepreneurship
- Growth rate: ~3,500 followers/month (plateauing at scale)

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 4-5x/week on LinkedIn |
| Primary format | Text-only with visual frameworks (image+text) |
| Content pillars | Frameworks/mental models, personal growth, investing, fatherhood |
| Hook style | Specific numbers + named frameworks |
| Post length | 20-35 lines |
| Signature move | Named frameworks with visual diagrams (Regret Minimization, Feynman Technique, etc.) |

### Engagement Patterns
- **Cross-platform amplification:** Same idea, different format per platform
- **Newsletter as conversion hub:** LinkedIn and Twitter both funnel to "The Curiosity Chronicle"
- **Comment strategy:** Selective — engages with high-quality comments, not all
- **Visual content:** Custom-designed framework diagrams are his scroll-stoppers

### Monetization Approach
1. Newsletter "The Curiosity Chronicle" — large subscriber base, sponsorship revenue
2. Brand partnerships and sponsorships
3. Course/cohort launches (periodic)
4. Speaking engagements — $20,000-50,000+ per event
5. VC fund (SRB Ventures) — leverages audience for deal flow

### Key Lessons
- **Named frameworks are proprietary IP.** When you name a concept, you own it in people's minds.
- **Cross-platform ≠ cross-post.** The same idea must be reformatted for each platform's native style.
- **Visuals are moats.** Custom framework diagrams can't easily be copied in text — they require design effort, making his content defensible.
- **The "right" niche evolves.** He started as investing-focused, expanded to personal development, then fatherhood. Follow your curiosity, but keep the through-line.

## TRAJECTORY #003 — Lara Acosta

### Profile
- **Name:** Lara Acosta
- **Niche:** Personal branding, LinkedIn growth, authenticity
- **Starting Point:** ~1,000 followers (2022), personal branding consultant
- **Current Size:** 200,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~3 years (one of the fastest LinkedIn growth stories)
- **Notable:** Grew by breaking LinkedIn's professional norms — vulnerability and raw emotion

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Bold Entry (0-5K) — Months 1-4**
- Immediately differentiated by sharing deeply personal stories on a "professional" platform
- First viral post about crying at work crossed 20K likes
- Established voice: vulnerable, direct, anti-corporate-speak
- Growth rate: ~1,500 followers/month (fast start from viral hits)

**Phase 2: Voice Amplification (5K-50K) — Months 4-12**
- Doubled down on personal branding as her niche
- Content mix: 60% personal stories, 40% branding/LinkedIn tips
- Started offering personal branding services at premium prices
- Became known for "permission-giving" content — making it okay to be human on LinkedIn
- Growth rate: ~5,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Authority Lock (50K-200K) — Months 12-30**
- Launched courses on personal branding and LinkedIn growth
- Speaking engagements at major marketing conferences
- Content evolved: more strategic, still personal, added frameworks
- Built a team to support content creation and business
- Growth rate: ~6,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 5-6x/week |
| Primary format | Text-only (90%), heavy broetry style |
| Content pillars | Personal branding, vulnerability/authenticity, LinkedIn tips, career empowerment |
| Hook style | Shocking personal admission + emotional punch |
| Post length | 30-50 lines (longer than average, but high dwell) |
| Signature move | Starting posts with deeply vulnerable confessions that break LinkedIn norms |

### Engagement Patterns
- **Comment sections become support groups.** Her vulnerability triggers reciprocal sharing in comments
- **Massive comment reply investment.** Responds to nearly every comment with genuine engagement
- **DM strategy:** Personal messages to early commenters, building real relationships
- **Anti-pod stance:** Growth is purely organic — her content naturally triggers emotional responses

### Monetization Approach
1. Personal branding consulting/coaching (premium pricing)
2. LinkedIn growth course
3. Speaking engagements
4. Brand partnerships
5. 1:1 advisory for executives

### Key Lessons
- **Breaking platform norms gets attention.** LinkedIn was a sea of corporate speak. Vulnerability stood out precisely because it broke expectations.
- **Speed to audience depends on emotional resonance.** She grew faster than most because personal stories trigger deeper engagement than educational content.
- **The audience you attract reflects the content you create.** Her community is deeply supportive because she modeled that energy from Day 1.
- **Authenticity is a positioning strategy, not just a value.** Being "the authentic one" on a platform known for inauthenticity is a deliberate competitive advantage.

## TRAJECTORY #004 — Alex Hormozi

### Profile
- **Name:** Alex Hormozi
- **Niche:** Business, offers, entrepreneurship, fitness industry
- **Starting Point:** ~5,000 followers (2021), already successful entrepreneur
- **Current Size:** 400,000+ followers on LinkedIn (2M+ on YouTube, 3M+ on Instagram)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~4 years on LinkedIn (but leveraged existing cross-platform audience)
- **Notable:** Multi-platform content machine; LinkedIn is part of an omni-channel strategy

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Platform Entry (0-20K LinkedIn) — Months 1-6**
- Already had massive audiences on YouTube and Instagram
- LinkedIn content was adapted from video content — text versions of key ideas
- "$100M Offers" book created huge awareness
- Growth rate: ~3,000 followers/month (cross-platform lift)

**Phase 2: LinkedIn Optimization (20K-100K) — Months 6-18**
- Hired LinkedIn-specific ghostwriter to optimize for the platform
- Content shifted from pure business advice to contrarian takes + frameworks
- "Unpopular opinion" format became a staple
- Introduced his signature blunt, no-BS tone to LinkedIn
- Growth rate: ~6,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Authority Domination (100K-400K+) — Months 18-48**
- Every post averages 10,000+ likes
- Content team producing platform-native content across 7+ platforms simultaneously
- LinkedIn serves as "professional authority" channel while Instagram/YouTube drive consumer audience
- Book launches ($100M Leads) amplified across all platforms
- Growth rate: ~7,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | 5-7x/week (content team) |
| Primary format | Text-only (85%), some image+text (15%) |
| Content pillars | Business frameworks, contrarian takes, entrepreneurship, offers/pricing |
| Hook style | Bold contrarian statements, specific dollar amounts |
| Post length | 15-30 lines (punchier than average) |
| Signature move | Blunt, no-fluff delivery. Short sentences. High authority tone. |

### Engagement Patterns
- **Polarization drives engagement.** His contrarian takes split audiences into agree/disagree camps
- **Content team handles comment replies** for scale
- **Cross-platform arbitrage:** Same core idea, 7 different platform-native versions
- **Book launches as engagement spikes:** Each book launch creates weeks of content and engagement

### Monetization Approach
1. Acquisition.com portfolio (primary business — $200M+/year across portfolio)
2. Books ($100M Offers, $100M Leads) — sold millions of copies
3. Speaking and events
4. LinkedIn serves brand building, not direct monetization
5. Audience building → deal flow for business acquisitions

### Key Lessons
- **Cross-platform audiences accelerate growth.** Hormozi didn't start from zero on LinkedIn — existing audiences on other platforms provided a launchpad.
- **Contrarian takes are LinkedIn currency.** His blunt, no-BS style cuts through corporate speak.
- **Invest in a content team.** At scale, a single creator cannot maintain quality across 7+ platforms without a team.
- **LinkedIn can serve a different purpose than other platforms.** For Hormozi, LinkedIn builds professional credibility for business acquisition, not direct sales.
- **Books are the ultimate authority asset.** "$100M Offers" created instant credibility on LinkedIn that pure social content would take years to build.

## TRAJECTORY #005 — Jasmin Alic

### Profile
- **Name:** Jasmin Alic
- **Niche:** LinkedIn ghostwriting, personal branding, content strategy
- **Starting Point:** ~500 followers (2021), copywriter looking for clients
- **Current Size:** 200,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~4 years
- **Notable:** Built a 6-figure ghostwriting business entirely from LinkedIn content

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: The Grind (0-5K) — Months 1-10**
- Posted daily without exception, even when getting <50 likes
- Tested every format: stories, frameworks, polls, carousels
- Discovered that "meta-content" about LinkedIn itself performed best
- Commented on 30+ posts per day to build visibility
- Growth rate: ~500 followers/month

**Phase 2: Meta-Content Discovery (5K-30K) — Months 10-20**
- Niched into "how to write on LinkedIn" — teaching the platform ON the platform
- Posts about hooks, formatting, and writing techniques went viral repeatedly
- Launched LinkedIn ghostwriting services (quickly booked out)
- Growth rate: ~2,500 followers/month

**Phase 3: Platform Celebrity (30K-100K) — Months 20-32**
- Became THE LinkedIn writing expert on the platform
- Every post about "how to write a hook" or "LinkedIn mistakes" got massive engagement
- Launched premium ghostwriting packages ($3K-10K/month per client)
- Started training other ghostwriters
- Growth rate: ~5,000 followers/month

**Phase 4: Business Scaling (100K-200K+) — Months 32-48**
- Shifted from 1:1 ghostwriting to courses and training
- "LinkedIn Writing Masterclass" became primary revenue driver
- Agency model: trained ghostwriters handle client work
- Content evolved from tactical tips to strategic frameworks
- Growth rate: ~3,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Posting frequency | Daily (7x/week, including weekends) |
| Primary format | Text-only (95%) |
| Content pillars | LinkedIn writing tips, hook formulas, personal stories, ghostwriting insights |
| Hook style | "I've [done X massive thing]. Here's what I learned:" |
| Post length | 20-35 lines |
| Signature move | Teaching LinkedIn writing techniques using viral LinkedIn posts as examples |

### Monetization Approach
1. LinkedIn ghostwriting services ($3K-10K/month per client)
2. LinkedIn Writing Masterclass (course)
3. Ghostwriter training program
4. Brand consulting
5. Speaking engagements

### Key Lessons
- **Meta-content about the platform itself is a growth hack.** Teaching LinkedIn ON LinkedIn creates a self-reinforcing loop.
- **Volume precedes quality.** The first 300 posts were learning reps. The next 300 were hits.
- **Commenting is 50% of the strategy.** Jasmin attributes at least half his growth to strategic commenting, not just posting.
- **Service business funds the creator business.** Ghostwriting revenue funded the time and energy to create content and build courses.
- **Teach what you do, do what you teach.** The fact that his posts about writing ARE well-written is the ultimate proof of concept.

- **Starting Point:** ~3,000 followers (2020), entrepreneur
- **Current Size:** 500,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~5 years

**Phase 2: Format Mastery (10K-100K) — Months 8-24**
- Refined carousel templates into a repeatable system
- Every carousel followed the same visual identity (recognizable brand)
- Topics: entrepreneurship comparisons, success habits, productivity frameworks
- Growth rate: ~5,500 followers/month

**Phase 3: Scale (100K-500K) — Months 24-60**
- Carousels became content empire — each one takes 2-4 hours to produce
- Diversified into text posts and newsletters
- Built content team to maintain production cadence
- Brand partnerships with major companies
- Growth rate: ~7,000 followers/month

### Content Strategy Breakdown

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|

| Content pillars | Entrepreneurship, habits, productivity, comparisons |

## TRAJECTORY #007 — Dr. Julie Gurner

### Profile
- **Name:** Dr. Julie Gurner
- **Niche:** Executive performance, high-achiever psychology, leadership
- **Starting Point:** ~2,000 followers (2021), executive coach
- **Current Size:** 100,000+ followers (2025)
- **Growth Timeline:** ~4 years
- **Notable:** Premium positioning — charges $50,000+ per coaching engagement, uses LinkedIn purely for authority

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Authority Seeding (0-10K) — Months 1-12**
- Shared psychology-backed insights on performance and leadership
- Content tone: clinical expertise with warm accessibility
- Never used engagement bait — pure value delivery
- Growth rate: ~800 followers/month

**Phase 2: Newsletter Launch (10K-50K) — Months 12-30**
- Launched "Ultra Successful" newsletter (premium positioning)
- Posts became teasers for deeper newsletter content
- Audience attracted high-caliber executives and founders
- Growth rate: ~2,000 followers/month

**Phase 3: Authority Lock (50K-100K+) — Months 30-48**
- Recognized as go-to for executive performance on LinkedIn
- Content stays in lane — never chases trends
- Newsletter commands premium sponsorship rates
- Growth rate: ~1,500 followers/month (slower but higher quality)

### Key Lessons
- **Premium positioning requires patience.** She grew slower than mass-market creators but attracted exactly the right audience.
- **You don't need millions to monetize.** 100K highly targeted followers (executives, founders) is more valuable than 1M general followers.
- **Expertise-based content has a longer shelf life.** Psychology-backed posts get saved and referenced long after posting.
- **Never compromise positioning for engagement.** She never posted polls, engagement bait, or trend-jacking — and it protected her premium brand.

## TRAJECTORY #008 — Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole (Ship 30 for 30)

### Profile
- **Names:** Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole
- **Niche:** Writing, content creation, online writing education
- **Starting Points:** Dickie: ~500 followers (2021); Cole: ~30K (2020, already established writer)
- **Current Sizes:** Dickie: 100K+; Cole: 150K+ (LinkedIn); combined newsletter: 200K+
- **Growth Timeline:** ~4 years
- **Notable:** Partnership model — two creators amplifying each other

### Growth Phases

**Phase 1: Complementary Launch**
- Nicolas Cole (established writer) and Dickie Bush (emerging voice) partnered
- Cross-promotion created immediate audience sharing
- "Ship 30 for 30" cohort-based course gave students incentive to share content

**Phase 2: Student Amplification Network**
- Every Ship 30 for 30 cohort produced hundreds of new LinkedIn creators
- Students tagged them, shared their frameworks, attributed their teachings
- Created a network effect: 500+ students each creating content that referenced them

**Phase 3: Content Ecosystem**
- Newsletter, Twitter, LinkedIn all cross-feeding
- Each platform served a different purpose in the funnel
- LinkedIn: authority and professional credibility
- Twitter: reach and viral potential
- Newsletter: conversion and owned audience

### Key Lessons
- **Partnerships multiply reach.** Two creators with complementary audiences grow faster than one.
- **Students are the ultimate distribution network.** Teaching people to create content creates an army of people who reference you.
- **Cohort-based courses create engagement spikes.** Every new cohort brings a wave of content creation and engagement.
- **The meta-game works.** Teaching content creation ON content platforms creates a self-reinforcing growth loop.

## CROSS-TRAJECTORY PATTERNS

### Universal Growth Factors

| Factor | Present In | Weight |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Daily/near-daily posting | 8/8 trajectories | Critical |
| Strategic commenting | 7/8 trajectories | Critical |
| Newsletter as conversion mechanism | 7/8 trajectories | High |
| Cross-platform presence | 6/8 trajectories | High |
| Product/course launch | 7/8 trajectories | High |
| Niche specificity | 8/8 trajectories | Critical |
| Personal story content | 6/8 trajectories | Medium-High |
| Visual/carousel content | 4/8 trajectories | Medium |
| Team/ghostwriter support | 5/8 trajectories | Medium |

### Growth Rate Benchmarks

| Phase | Monthly Follower Growth | What's Happening |
|-------|------------------------|------------------|
| 0-5K | 300-1,500/month | Finding voice, testing content |
| 5K-25K | 1,500-3,000/month | Content system established, first viral posts |
| 25K-100K | 3,000-7,000/month | Breakout phase, authority recognized |
| 100K-500K | 5,000-10,000/month | Platform celebrity, content team needed |
| 500K+ | Varies widely | Diminishing growth rate, focus shifts to monetization |

### The Monetization Sequence

Most successful LinkedIn creators follow this sequence:
1. **Free content** builds audience (months 1-12)
2. **Newsletter** converts followers to subscribers (months 6-18)
3. **Service/consulting** monetizes expertise directly (months 8-24)
4. **Course/product** scales income beyond time-for-money (months 12-36)
5. **Speaking/partnerships** leverage authority for premium revenue (months 24+)
