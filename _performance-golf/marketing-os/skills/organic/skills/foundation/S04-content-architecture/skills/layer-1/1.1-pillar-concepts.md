# 1.1 Pillar Concepts

## Purpose
Define 3-5 content pillar CONCEPTS based on audience needs, WITHOUT naming them yet. This is Phase A of the concept/naming separation protocol.

---

## Input Requirements
- Strategic context from 0.4
- AIF pain points and desires
- Business context (core offerings, goals)

---

## Execution Protocol

### Step 1: Map Audience Needs to Pillar Opportunities
For each major pain point or desire from AIF, identify potential pillar themes:

| Audience Need (from AIF) | Content Pillar Opportunity | Business Connection |
|--------------------------|----------------------------|---------------------|
| [Pain/desire] | [What content pillar would address this] | [How this connects to revenue] |

**MUST:** Every pillar MUST map to a real audience need from AIF. No invented needs.

---

### Step 2: Develop Pillar Concepts (3-5 pillars)
For each pillar, define the CONCEPT without naming:

```yaml
pillar_concepts:
  pillar_1:
    concept_id: "pillar_1"
    description: |
      [What this pillar covers — the CONCEPT, not the name]

    audience_need: |
      [Which pain/desire from AIF this addresses — DIRECT QUOTE from AIF]

    topic_clusters:
      - cluster: "[Topic area]"
        subtopics:
          - "[Subtopic 1]"
          - "[Subtopic 2]"
          - "[Subtopic 3]"

    business_connection: |
      [How this pillar connects to revenue — specific offer/product]

    percentage_of_content: [15-40%]

    strategic_positioning: |
      [Why this pillar is important, what gap it fills]
```

**Design Principles:**
1. Each pillar MUST map to a real audience pain or desire from AIF
2. Pillars should be distinct but related
3. At least one pillar must directly connect to revenue
4. No pillar should be <15% or >40% of content
5. Total percentages across all pillars must sum to 100%

---

### Step 3: Verify Pillar Coverage
Check that pillar concepts collectively address:
- [ ] Top 3 pain points from AIF
- [ ] Top 3 desires from AIF
- [ ] At least 1 direct revenue connection
- [ ] Voice-appropriate topics (no conflicts with BVF anti-voice)

---

### Step 4: Calculate Percentages
Assign content percentage to each pillar:
- Sum must equal 100%
- Revenue-connected pillars typically 25-40%
- Pure value/education pillars typically 15-30%
- Community/engagement pillars typically 10-20%

**MATH CHECK: Percentages MUST sum to exactly 100%**

---

- **File:** `1.1-pillar-concepts.yaml`
- **Format:** YAML

```yaml

  pillar_1:
    concept_id: ""

    audience_need: |

    strategic_positioning: |

  pillar_2:
    # Same structure

  pillar_3:
    # Same structure

  # Optional pillar_4 and pillar_5

percentage_verification:
  total: 100
  breakdown: {}

coverage_check:
  pain_points_addressed: []
  desires_addressed: []
  revenue_connections: []
  voice_compatibility: true | false
```

---

## Quality Gates
- [ ] 3-5 pillar concepts defined
- [ ] Each pillar has audience_need from AIF (direct quote)
- [ ] Each pillar has business_connection
- [ ] Percentages sum to exactly 100%
- [ ] At least 1 pillar has direct revenue connection
- [ ] No pillar <15% or >40%
- [ ] All pillars compatible with BVF voice

---

## Handoff
**THIS IS PHASE A. DO NOT PROCEED TO PHASE B.**

Pass pillar concepts to: **HUMAN REVIEW (Concept Checkpoint)**

Human will review concepts and create `checkpoints/CONCEPT_APPROVED.yaml` if approved.

Only after CONCEPT_APPROVED.yaml exists can Phase B (naming) proceed.
