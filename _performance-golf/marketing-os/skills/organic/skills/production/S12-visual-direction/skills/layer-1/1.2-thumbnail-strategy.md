# Microskill 1.2: Thumbnail Strategy
Skill: S12 Visual Direction
Layer: 1
Model: claude-sonnet-4.5
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

Develop the overarching thumbnail strategy that will stop scrolls and drive clicks. The thumbnail is the #1 determinant of content consumption — great content with poor thumbnail gets ignored, mediocre content with great thumbnail gets consumed.

## Input

**Required files:**
- `s12-0.2-teaching-context.md` — scroll-stop mechanisms
- `s12-0.3-specimens.yaml` — high-performing thumbnail patterns
- `s12-1.1-platform-analysis.yaml` — platform-specific requirements
- Content draft from S08-S11 — to understand content promise
- `s12-0.4-brand-guidelines.yaml` — brand constraints

## Process

### MUST
1. **Extract content promise from draft**
   - Read content draft (video script, carousel copy, post copy)
   - Identify the core promise/value proposition:
     - What transformation does content deliver?
     - What curiosity gap does it create?
     - What pain does it address?
   - Document in one sentence: "This content helps [audience] achieve [outcome] by [method]"

2. **Identify scroll-stop mechanism**
   - Choose primary mechanism from teaching context:
     - **Pattern interrupt:** Unexpected visual that breaks feed pattern
     - **Emotional trigger:** Face expressing strong emotion (surprise, concern, joy)
     - **Curiosity gap:** Visual question that demands answer
     - **Social proof:** Recognizable authority or massive result
     - **High contrast:** Color combination that pops visually
   - Match mechanism to content promise and platform behavior

3. **Define thumbnail concept**
   - Develop 2-3 thumbnail concept directions:
     - Concept A: [mechanism] via [visual approach]
     - Concept B: [different mechanism] via [different approach]
     - Concept C: [hybrid or alternative]
   - For each concept, specify:
     - Primary visual element (face, product, text, graphic)
     - Secondary visual element (background, supporting imagery)
     - Text overlay approach (headline only, headline + subhead, no text)
     - Color strategy (brand colors, high contrast, mood-aligned)

4. **Evaluate concepts against platform + specimens**
   - Cross-reference each concept against platform analysis:
     - Will it read at mobile size (320px)?
     - Does it account for UI safe zones?
     - Does it match platform visual trends?
   - Cross-reference against specimens:
     - Does it align with proven patterns for this platform + vertical?
     - Does it differentiate enough to stand out?
   - Rank concepts by likelihood of scroll-stop performance

5. **Select primary concept**
   - Choose strongest concept based on:
     - Alignment with content promise
     - Platform/vertical performance data
     - Brand guideline compatibility
     - Differentiation from competitor thumbnails
   - Document rationale for selection

6. **Define supporting concepts (for Arena)**
   - Document 2 alternative concepts for Arena evaluation
   - Each alternative should test different scroll-stop mechanism
   - This gives Arena real options to evaluate (not just variations)

### NEVER
- Choose scroll-stop mechanism without content promise context
- Develop only 1 concept (Arena needs alternatives)
- Copy specimen thumbnails directly (reference patterns, don't duplicate)
- Ignore platform-specific behavior in strategy
- Select concept that violates brand guidelines

## Output

**File:** `s12-1.2-thumbnail-strategy.md`

```markdown
# S12 Thumbnail Strategy
Timestamp: 2026-03-05T15:15:00Z
Model: claude-sonnet-4.5

## Content Promise
**One-sentence promise:** This content helps financial professionals achieve retirement at 40 by implementing the "3-Bucket System" for aggressive wealth building.

**Core value:** Early retirement framework (transformational outcome)

**Curiosity gap:** What is the "3-Bucket System"? (mechanism curiosity)

## Scroll-Stop Mechanism Selection

### Evaluated Mechanisms
1. **Emotional trigger (face):** Strong — finance vertical benefits from authority/trust cues
2. **Curiosity gap (visual question):** Strong — "3 buckets" can be visualized
3. **Pattern interrupt:** Medium — less critical for YouTube (intentional viewing)
4. **Social proof:** Medium — unless using testimonial or massive result
5. **High contrast:** Strong — proven in platform analysis (78% of high-performers)

### Selected Primary Mechanism
**Emotional trigger + Curiosity gap** — Face expressing confident authority + visual representation of "3 Bucket System"

Rationale: Finance content benefits from trust (face) AND mechanism curiosity (buckets). Combination addresses both psychological triggers.

## Thumbnail Concepts

### Concept A: Authority + Mechanism Visualization
- **Scroll-stop mechanism:** Emotional trigger (confident face) + Curiosity gap (3 visible buckets)
- **Primary visual:** Professional (30s-40s) looking directly at camera, slight smile, confident posture
- **Secondary visual:** 3 stylized bucket graphics to right of face, labeled "Now / Soon / Later"
- **Text overlay:** "THE 3-BUCKET SYSTEM" (headline, 8 words max)
- **Color strategy:** Brand colors (Trust Blue #1E40AF + Energy Orange #F59E0B) on clean white background
- **Composition:** Face occupies left 60%, buckets + text occupy right 40%, rule of thirds alignment

**Strengths:** Clear mechanism preview, authority presence, brand-aligned
**Risks:** Buckets might feel literal/boring, text-heavy

### Concept B: Result-Focused + Lifestyle Aspiration
- **Scroll-stop mechanism:** Social proof (result) + Emotional trigger (aspiration)
- **Primary visual:** Lifestyle image suggesting early retirement (beach, relaxation, freedom)
- **Secondary visual:** Overlaid "AGE 40" text with checkmark/achievement graphic
- **Text overlay:** "RETIRED AT 40" (headline) + "Here's How" (subhead)
- **Color strategy:** Warm aspirational tones + brand accent color (Energy Orange) for text
- **Composition:** Full-bleed lifestyle image, text in upper third with contrast overlay for readability

**Strengths:** Emotionally compelling, clear outcome, aspirational
**Risks:** Less mechanism curiosity, lifestyle image might feel stock/generic

### Concept C: Data Visualization + Pattern Interrupt
- **Scroll-stop mechanism:** Pattern interrupt (unexpected graphic style) + Curiosity gap
- **Primary visual:** Clean infographic showing wealth accumulation curve with 3 distinct phases
- **Secondary visual:** Minimalist icons representing the 3 buckets
- **Text overlay:** "THE SYSTEM WALL STREET IGNORES" (curiosity-gap headline)
- **Color strategy:** High contrast (dark navy #111827 background + bright Growth Green #10B981 for growth curve)
- **Composition:** Centered infographic, text in top third, clean geometric layout

**Strengths:** Highly differentiated, data-driven (authority), clean/professional
**Risks:** No human element (less emotional connection), might not pop on mobile

## Concept Evaluation

| Concept | Content Promise | Platform Fit | Specimen Match | Brand Fit | Differentiation | Score |
|---------|----------------|--------------|----------------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| A: Authority + Mechanism | 9/10 | 8/10 | 9/10 | 10/10 | 6/10 | 8.4/10 |
| B: Result-Focused | 7/10 | 9/10 | 8/10 | 8/10 | 5/10 | 7.4/10 |
| C: Data Visualization | 8/10 | 7/10 | 6/10 | 9/10 | 9/10 | 7.8/10 |

## Primary Concept Selection

**Selected: Concept A (Authority + Mechanism Visualization)**

**Rationale:**
- Strongest content promise alignment (mechanism is the hook)
- Face provides trust (critical in finance vertical)
- Brand guidelines perfectly support this approach
- Specimens show face+text combo performs well on YouTube
- "3 buckets" visual satisfies curiosity gap while maintaining clarity

**Risk mitigation:**
- Ensure buckets are stylized/modern (not literal buckets — avoid cheesy)
- Test text-only variation in Arena (no buckets, just face + headline)

## Alternative Concepts for Arena

**Arena will evaluate:**
1. **Concept A** (selected above)
2. **Concept B** (result-focused variation)
3. **Concept C** (data visualization variation)

Each concept tests different scroll-stop mechanism. Arena evaluation will validate primary selection or surface better alternative.

## Implementation Notes

- Layer 2 (2.1 thumbnail-design-brief) will execute Concept A
- Layer 2.5 (Arena) will evaluate all 3 concepts
- Expect Arena to potentially generate hybrid (e.g., Concept A with Concept C's high-contrast palette)
```
