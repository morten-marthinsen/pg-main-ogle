# 2.8 — Quality/Manufacturing Writer

**Skill:** PDP-07-btf-section-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 8
**Purpose:** Write the How It's Made (Quality) section — covering sourcing, manufacturing processes, certifications, and testing protocols that give quality-conscious buyers confidence in the product's integrity. This section answers "Can I trust what's in this bottle?"
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `per-section-briefs.md` (1.1 — how_its_made brief), `data-validation.md` (1.2 — GREEN or YELLOW), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3 — Section 5.11 template)
**Output:** `section-how-its-made.md`

---

```yaml
input:
  section_brief: "[how_its_made brief from per-section-briefs.md]"
  word_count_target: "[from PDP-04]"
  proof_density: "[from PDP-04 — medium: certification badges, sourcing photos]"
  pdp_reference: "[Section 5.11 template from pdp-reference-loaded.md]"

  manufacturing_details: "[from brief — facility, process]"

```

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read the How It's Made template from PDP-BEST-PRACTICES-REFERENCE.md Section 5.11. This section covers sourcing, manufacturing, certifications.

**Step 2:** Organize quality claims into 3-4 proof categories:

**Category Framework:**
1. **Sourcing:** Where ingredients come from, quality of raw materials
   - Examples: "Organic turmeric sourced from farms in Kerala, India"
   - Examples: "Wild-caught Alaskan salmon collagen"

2. **Manufacturing:** How the product is made, facility standards
   - Examples: "Made in a GMP-certified facility in the USA"
   - Examples: "Small-batch manufacturing with temperature-controlled processing"

3. **Testing:** Third-party testing, purity verification
   - Examples: "Every batch third-party tested for purity and potency"
   - Examples: "NSF Certified for Sport — tested for banned substances"

4. **Certifications:** Formal certifications and badges
   - Examples: GMP, NSF, USDA Organic, Non-GMO Project, FDA Registered Facility
   - Each certification gets a badge visual

**Step 3:** For each category, write:
- **Category Header:** Clear, specific label
- **Quality Claim:** 1-2 sentences with specific details (not vague)
  - Good: "Manufactured in an FDA-registered, GMP-certified facility in Austin, Texas"
  - Bad: "Made with the highest quality standards"
- **Visual Direction:** Badge, photo, or icon for this category

**Step 4:** Write section header:
- Not: "Quality & Manufacturing" (boring/generic)
- Better: "How [Product Name] Is Made" or "From Source to Shelf"

**Step 5:** Add a brief framing line:
- Example: "We believe you should know exactly what goes into your body and how it gets there."

**Step 6:** Ensure claims are specific and defensible:
- No vague quality language ("premium ingredients," "highest standards")
- Every claim backed by a specific detail (facility location, certification name, testing protocol)

**Step 7:** Verify word count against target (+/-10%).

**Step 8:** Write `section-how-its-made.md`.

## Quality Criteria
- 3-4 quality categories with specific claims
- Every claim has a specific detail (not "premium" or "high quality")
- Certifications listed by name with badge visuals
- Manufacturing facility location specified (if in brief)
- Testing protocol named (third-party, which tests)
- Self-contained (no references to other sections)
- Word count within +/-10% of PDP-04 target
- Zero generic AI language

## Failure Modes
- All claims are vague ("highest quality," "premium sourcing") -> HALT. Rewrite with specifics from brief. If no specifics exist, flag as data gap.
- Fewer than 2 quality claims from brief -> HALT. Section cannot be written with minimal quality data. Recommend PDP-04 exclude this section.
- Certification names not available -> Use generic "Quality Certified" badge and flag as data gap.
- Section reads like marketing copy rather than transparency disclosure -> Rewrite in factual, straightforward language.
- "Made in USA" claimed without basis in brief -> Do not fabricate location claims. Use only verified information.
