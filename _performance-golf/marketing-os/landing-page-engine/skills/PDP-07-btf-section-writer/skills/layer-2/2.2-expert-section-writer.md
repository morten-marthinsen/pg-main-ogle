# 2.2 — Expert Section Writer

**Skill:** PDP-07-btf-section-writer
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Write The Expert section — the credibility anchor that overcomes skepticism about product safety and effectiveness. This section features the expert who created or endorses the product, with credentials front-loaded and a trust-building frame of "why this expert formulated this product."
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `per-section-briefs.md` (1.1 — the_expert brief), `data-validation.md` (1.2 — GREEN or YELLOW), `pdp-reference-loaded.md` (0.3 — Section 5.3 template)
**Output:** `section-the-expert.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  section_brief: "[the_expert brief from per-section-briefs.md]"
  word_count_target: "[from PDP-04]"
  proof_density: "[from PDP-04 — heavy: credential strip, institution logos, video]"
  pdp_reference: "[Section 5.3 template from pdp-reference-loaded.md]"
  expert_name: "[from brief]"
  expert_credentials: "[from brief — degree, title, institution]"
  expert_role: "[formulator | endorser | advisor]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

**Step 1:** Read The Expert template from PDP-BEST-PRACTICES-REFERENCE.md Section 5.3. Key pattern: credible image/video of expert, white coat where possible, full title + school, natural speaking.

**Step 2:** Build the credential strip:
- Full name with title (e.g., "Dr. Sarah Chen, MD, PhD")
- Institution (e.g., "Harvard Medical School")
- Role descriptor (e.g., "Board-Certified Neurologist" or "Formulator")
- Years of experience or practice (if available)
- Notable publications or achievements (if available)

**Step 3:** Write the trust framing narrative — the "Why Dr. X formulated this" story:
- What problem the expert observed in patients/practice
- Why existing solutions were inadequate
- What led them to create/endorse this specific product
- Their personal conviction about the formula

This should be 3-5 short sentences maximum. Not a biography — a trust-building narrative arc.

**Step 4:** Write the video direction (if expert video is available or planned):
- Video thumbnail description
- Suggested video length (30-60 seconds per NLS)
- Talking points for expert
- Caption/subtitle direction
- "Video stops when user scrolls away" behavior note

**Step 5:** Write credential badges/trust signals:
- Institution logo placement
- Certification badges relevant to expert
- Publication or media mention badges

**Step 6:** Ensure the section:
- Leads with credentials (not generic "meet our expert" framing)
- Uses the expert's actual name and real credentials
- Does NOT include a canned script or marketing copy in the expert's voice
- Keeps trust framing concise (3-5 sentences)
- Does NOT reference other PDP sections

**Step 7:** Verify word count against target (+/-10%).

**Step 8:** Write `section-the-expert.md`.

## Quality Criteria
- Expert name and real credentials prominently displayed
- Institution name included (not generic "leading university")
- Trust narrative is 3-5 sentences (not a biography)
- Expert's role is clear (formulator vs endorser vs advisor)
- Video direction provided if video is available/planned
- Section is self-contained (no "as you saw in the ingredients section")
- Word count within +/-10% of PDP-04 target
- Zero generic AI language

## Failure Modes
- No expert name in brief -> HALT. This section cannot be written without a real expert. Escalate to human or recommend PDP-04 exclude this section.
- No credentials in brief -> HALT. "Our expert" without credentials is worse than no expert section. Escalate.
- Trust narrative exceeds 5 sentences -> Condense. This is a credibility anchor, not an interview.
- Section reads like a bio page -> Rewrite with trust-building framing: problem -> inadequacy -> solution -> conviction.
- Generic framing ("Meet our amazing expert") -> Rewrite with credential-first framing: "Formulated by Dr. X, [Title] at [Institution]"
