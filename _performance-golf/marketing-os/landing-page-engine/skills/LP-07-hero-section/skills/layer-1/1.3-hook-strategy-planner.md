# 1.3 — Hook Strategy Planner

**Skill:** LP-07-hero-section
**Position:** Layer 1, Microskill 3
**Purpose:** Decide the first-sentence approach for headline and lead. The hook strategy determines the ENERGY and ENTRY POINT of the hero section — whether the reader encounters a question, a scene, a claim, a proof anchor, or a pain mirror in their first 10 words. This decision bridges headline type (1.1) and lead type (1.2) into a unified opening strategy.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `headline-type-confirmed.md` (1.1), `lead-type-selected.md` (1.2), `copy-assets.md` (0.2), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `hook-strategy.md`

## Execution Logic

1. **Determine hook type** from the 5 hook categories:

   | Hook Type | First-Sentence Function | Best When |
   |-----------|------------------------|-----------|
   | **Curiosity** | Opens an information gap immediately — reader must know the answer | Cold traffic, Stage 1-2, T2/T4/T6 headlines |
   | **Pain** | Mirrors the reader's worst moment or frustration in vivid specific language | High-pain markets, Stage 2-3, T3/T5 headlines, pain quotes available |
   | **Proof** | Leads with a specific number, study result, or named authority | High-skepticism audiences, Stage 3-4, T1/T7 headlines |
   | **Story** | Drops reader into a specific scene with a named/described person | Discovery-based offers, T6 headlines, strong origin story available |
   | **Question** | Asks a specific yes-question the target reader cannot help answering | Self-selection-first markets, T5 headlines, fragmented audience |

2. **Check headline-hook alignment:**

   | Headline Type | Natural Hook Types | Misaligned Hook Types |
   |--------------|-------------------|----------------------|
   | T1 Direct Benefit | Proof, Pain | Story (too indirect for a direct headline) |
   | T2 Curiosity/Revelation | Curiosity, Story | Proof (contradicts mystery frame) |
   | T3 Problem-First | Pain, Question | Proof (shifts frame away from problem) |
   | T4 Warning/Alert | Curiosity, Pain | Question (weakens warning urgency) |
   | T5 Question | Question, Pain | Story (question headline + story lead = tonal mismatch) |
   | T6 How-To/Discovery | Story, Curiosity | Question (discovery headlines need narrative) |
   | T7 Number-Specific | Proof, Pain | Curiosity (number already provides specificity) |

3. **Determine opening cadence:**
   - **High-energy:** Short first sentence (5-12 words). Declarative or imperative. Best for pain, proof, curiosity hooks.
   - **Medium-energy:** Standard first sentence (12-20 words). Descriptive or conditional. Best for question, story hooks with warm traffic.
   - **Slow-burn:** Longer first sentence (20-30 words) that builds scene or context. Best for story hooks with cold traffic and long-form pages.

4. **Determine first-sentence energy level** (1-10 scale):
   - 1-3: Quiet authority. Understated. "In 2019, a research team at Johns Hopkins..."
   - 4-6: Confident claim. Direct. "There are 3 reasons your joints hurt — and none of them are arthritis."
   - 7-8: Urgent. Pattern-interrupting. "Stop taking magnesium. Most forms are doing nothing."
   - 9-10: Maximum interrupt. Bold claim or vivid scene. "At 3 AM, Dr. [Name] stared at a lab result that shouldn't exist."

5. **Write the hook strategy** with enough specificity that Layer 2 microskills can execute without reinterpreting intent.

## Quality Criteria
- Hook type selected with alignment to both headline type and lead type
- Opening cadence and energy level specified numerically (not just "energetic")
- First-sentence direction is specific enough to guide generation but does not write the sentence
- Anti-patterns listed to prevent the most common failure for the selected hook type
- Pre-head coordination ensures the hero section reads as a unified sequence, not disconnected elements

## Failure Modes
- Hook type misaligned with headline type AND no rationale given → Select an aligned hook type
- Energy level mismatch with audience awareness (e.g., energy 9 for Stage 5 hot traffic) → Recalibrate. Stage 5 readers need less interrupt, more specificity.
- Hook strategy is too vague to guide generation ("open with something compelling") → Rewrite with the specific hook type, cadence, and energy level
