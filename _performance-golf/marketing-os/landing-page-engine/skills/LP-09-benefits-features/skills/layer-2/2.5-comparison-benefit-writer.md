**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 5

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Load comparison specimens.**
Study LP-SPEC-10 (BIOpt — competitor comparison table: Magnesium Breakthrough vs. competitors on 6 criteria). Extract: criteria selection, advantage display, fair framing.

**Step 2: Verify comparison claims.** Every claim about the product AND every claim about competitors must be factually verifiable from the brief data. If a claim cannot be verified, it cannot be made.

**Step 3: Determine comparison approach:**
- Named competitors -> direct comparison table or inline statements
- Category alternatives ("most supplements," "typical tools") -> category comparison
- Previous methods ("the old way vs. the new way") -> before/after style comparison

**Step 4: Read hero threading anchor.** Comparison sections must reinforce the hero promise by showing why THIS product delivers where alternatives fail.

## Generation Execution

### Format A: Inline Comparison Statements

Used for 2-4 specific differentiators. Best for Type A pages or embedded within other sections.

```markdown
**Unlike [alternative/competitor], [Product Name] [specific differentiator]** — which means [benefit for the customer].

[1-2 sentences of supporting evidence or explanation.]
```

**Rules for inline comparisons:**
- Lead with the differentiator, not the competitor name
- Every "unlike" claim must state the customer benefit (the "which means")
- Supporting evidence must be specific (not "we're better")
- Never attack competitors personally — state facts about their approach/product

### Format B: Comparison Table

Used for 4-6 criteria comparisons. Best for supplement pages (ingredient comparisons) and SaaS pages (feature comparisons).

```markdown
## How [Product Name] Compares

| Criteria | [Product Name] | [Competitor/Alternative] |
|----------|---------------|------------------------|
| [Criterion 1] | [Specific fact — e.g., "7 forms of magnesium"] | [Specific fact — e.g., "1-2 forms (typically oxide)"] |
| [Criterion 2] | [Specific fact] | [Specific fact] |
| [Criterion 3] | [Specific fact] | [Specific fact] |
| [Criterion 4] | [Specific fact] | [Specific fact] |
| [Criterion 5 (optional)] | [Specific fact] | [Specific fact] |
| [Criterion 6 (optional)] | [Specific fact] | [Specific fact] |

[1-2 sentences: Summary statement highlighting the key takeaway — not "we win on everything" but the single most important differentiator.]
```

**Rules for comparison tables:**
- 4-6 criteria maximum
- Criteria must be meaningful to the CUSTOMER (not just technical specs)
- Product column uses specific facts, not superlatives ("7 forms" not "the best formula")
- Competitor column uses specific facts, not attacks ("1-2 forms" not "inferior formula")
- It's acceptable if the competitor "wins" on 1-2 criteria (price, availability) — fairness builds trust
- Summary highlights the dimension that matters most to the target audience

### Format C: Category Comparison

When competitors cannot be named, compare against the category:

```markdown
**Most [product category] [common limitation].** [Product Name] [specific differentiator].

Here's the difference: [2-3 sentences explaining why the category norm fails and how this product's approach succeeds. Include proof.]
```

## Quality Criteria
- Every comparison claim is factually verifiable (claim verification table complete)
- Product advantages stated as specific facts, not superlatives
- Competitor/alternative descriptions are fair (no attacks, no misrepresentations)
- At least one customer benefit per comparison point (the "which means")
- Summary highlights the single most important differentiator for the target audience
- Comparison table has 4-6 criteria (not 10+ which dilutes focus)
- Tone is confident but fair — not aggressive or dismissive
- Zero forbidden words from anti-slop list

## Failure Modes
- Unverifiable claim about competitor -> Remove the claim. Comparison copy with false claims creates legal liability and destroys trust when discovered.
- Product "wins" on every single criterion -> Suspicious. Allow the competitor to have 1-2 genuine advantages (usually price or availability). This builds trust and makes the product's real advantages more credible.
- Comparison criteria are technical specs with no customer translation -> Each criterion must connect to something the customer cares about. "7 forms vs. 1 form" means nothing until you explain "which means your body actually absorbs it instead of flushing 90% of it."
- Competitor bashing -> State facts. "Most supplements use magnesium oxide because it's cheap to manufacture" = fact. "Other companies don't care about your health" = bashing. Facts are allowed. Attributions of motive are not.
- Comparison table exceeds 6 criteria -> Reduce. More criteria = less impact per criterion. Focus on the 4-6 that matter most to the buying decision.
