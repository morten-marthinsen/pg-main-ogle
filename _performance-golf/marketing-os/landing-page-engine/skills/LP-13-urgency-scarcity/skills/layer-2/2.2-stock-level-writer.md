# 2.2 — Stock Level Writer

**Skill:** LP-13-urgency-scarcity
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 2
**Purpose:** Write supply scarcity copy — stock level indicators, batch manufacturing explanations, ingredient sourcing constraints, and restock timelines. Every stock claim must be backed by real inventory or manufacturing data. This microskill ONLY executes when Type 1 (Supply Scarcity) is selected in Layer 1.
**Effort Level:** high
**Dependencies:** `justification-plan.md` (1.2), `placement-strategy.md` (1.3), `specimens-loaded.md` (0.3), `urgency-types-selected.md` (1.1)
**Output:** `stock-level-copy.md`

## Pre-Generation Protocol

**Step 1: Read authorized claims.** Open justification-plan.md. List the specific supply claims Layer 2 is authorized to make. Do NOT generate claims beyond this list.

**Step 2: Verify supply data specifics.** Before writing, confirm you have at least ONE of:
- Specific batch size number
- Specific current inventory number
- Specific restock timeline
- Specific ingredient sourcing constraint

If NONE of these are available, do NOT generate stock level copy. Write a placeholder framework and flag for client input.

**Step 3: Read specimens.** Extract technique from supply scarcity specimens: How did they make the supply constraint feel real? What manufacturing/sourcing details did they share?

## Execution Logic

### For Type A Pages

1. **Write the supply scarcity justification paragraph** (50-150 words):
   This is the copy that EXPLAINS the supply constraint. It is the most important output of this microskill because it is what makes the stock claim believable.

   Structure:
   - Sentence 1: State the manufacturing/sourcing approach (why small batches)
   - Sentence 2: State the specific constraint (batch size, ingredient, timeline)
   - Sentence 3: State the consequence (when this batch sells out, what happens)
   - Sentence 4 (optional): Frame the constraint as a quality commitment, not a limitation

   Example:
   > "We manufacture [Product] in small batches of 5,000 bottles. Each batch requires [Ingredient] sourced from [Specific Source], which has a 6-8 week lead time. Once this batch sells out, the next one won't be available until [Date/Timeframe]. We could mass-produce with cheaper alternatives, but the quality difference is why [Product] works."

2. **Write the stock status statement** (1-2 sentences):
   - Direct statement of current availability
   - Example: "As of today, 2,847 bottles remain from this batch."
   - Must use specific number from authorized claims

3. **Write the P.S. supply callback** (1 sentence):
   - Compressed restatement of the supply constraint for P.S. section
   - Example: "Remember — this batch is limited to 5,000 bottles, and once they're gone, the next batch is 6-8 weeks out."

4. **Write the close section supply line** (1 sentence):
   - For integration into the close/final CTA area
   - Example: "Current batch availability: [number] remaining."

### For Type B Pages

5. **Write the stock level indicator** (3-10 words):
   - For product card or above ATC placement
   - Must include a specific number (not "low stock" or "selling fast")
   - Patterns:
     - "Only [number] left in stock"
     - "[Number] remaining — [next batch date]"
     - "Batch #[X]: [number] of [total] remaining"

6. **Write the Limited Edition copy** (if applicable, 5-15 words):
   - For products with limited-edition variants (scents, colors, formulations)
   - Example: "Limited Edition — [Variant Name]. Available while this batch lasts."

7. **Write the cart stock reinforcement** (1 sentence):
   - For cart/checkout page
   - Example: "Items in your cart are from the current batch. Stock is not reserved until checkout is complete."

## Quality Criteria
- Every stock claim includes a specific number (no "low stock" or "selling fast")
- Justification paragraph explains WHY supply is limited (manufacturing, sourcing, quality)
- Restock timeline stated so the visitor understands the consequence of waiting
- All copy within authorized claims from justification-plan.md
- Voice direction matched -- supply scarcity copy is factual, not panic-inducing
- Zero forbidden words from anti-slop list

## Failure Modes
- Writing "almost sold out" or "selling fast" without inventory data -> Forbidden. Must state specific number or remove claim entirely.
- Justification paragraph that does not explain WHY batch size is small -> The justification is the believability engine. "Small batches" without reason = vague claim. "Small batches because [Ingredient] requires 6-week cold extraction" = credible.
- Using round numbers that feel fabricated ("Only 100 left!") -> Real inventory numbers are rarely round. If the real number is round, note it. If the number is fabricated, remove it.
- Writing stock level copy for a product with no genuine supply constraint -> If the product is mass-produced with unlimited inventory, Type 1 is the wrong urgency type. This should have been caught in Layer 1.
- Cart reinforcement that creates false urgency ("Your cart expires in 10 minutes!") -> Cart reservation timers are acceptable ONLY if the cart actually expires. If it does not, this is fabricated urgency.
