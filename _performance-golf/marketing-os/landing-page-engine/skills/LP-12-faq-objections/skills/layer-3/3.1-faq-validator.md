# 3.1 — FAQ Validator (10-Point Audit)

**Skill:** LP-12-faq-objections
**Position:** Layer 3, Microskill 1
**Purpose:** Run the 10-point FAQ audit against the complete set of FAQ items from Layer 2. This is the quality gate that determines whether the FAQ section meets the minimum standard for assembly. A score below 7.5/10 blocks Layer 4. Every point is scored 0 (fail) or 1 (pass) with explicit evidence for each score.
**Effort Level:** medium
**Dependencies:** `core-objection-answers.md` (2.2), `technical-faq-answers.md` (2.3), `logistics-faq-answers.md` (2.4), `faq-header.md` (2.1), `sequence-plan.md` (1.2)
**Output:** `faq-audit.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  all_faq_items: "[complete Q&A pairs from 2.2 + 2.3 + 2.4, in sequence order]"
  faq_header: "[from faq-header.md]"
  sequence_plan: "[from sequence-plan.md -- strategy, positions]"
  page_type: "[type_a | type_b | hybrid]"
  anti_slop_list: "[from LP-12-AGENT.md -- 5-category forbidden word list]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

1. **Assemble the complete FAQ** by combining items from 2.2 (core), 2.3 (technical), and 2.4 (logistics) in the locked sequence order from sequence-plan.md.

2. **Run the 10-point audit.** For each point, evaluate with explicit evidence:

### COUNT + COVERAGE (3 Points)

**Point 1: Total FAQ items = 8-12**
- Count all Q&A pairs across 2.2, 2.3, and 2.4
- PASS (1): 8-12 items total
- FAIL (0): Fewer than 8 or more than 15
- Evidence: "Total items: [count]"

**Point 2: Price/value objection addressed**
- Check: At least 1 FAQ item categorized as price_value
- PASS (1): At least 1 price/value question with a non-evasive answer
- FAIL (0): No price/value question present, OR the answer is evasive (only states price without reframe/proof)
- Evidence: "Price/value item: Q[X] — '[question text]'"

**Point 3: Efficacy/results objection addressed**
- Check: At least 1 FAQ item categorized as efficacy_results
- PASS (1): At least 1 efficacy question with proof or social proof in the answer
- FAIL (0): No efficacy question present, OR the answer is only a hedge ("results may vary")
- Evidence: "Efficacy item: Q[X] — '[question text]' — Proof: [what proof is in the answer]"

### ANSWER QUALITY (4 Points)

**Point 4: Every answer leads with direct answer (no sycophantic openers)**
- Scan the first word/phrase of every answer
- PASS (1): Zero answers open with "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "That's a common concern!", "Of course!", "We're glad you asked", or any other sycophantic opener
- FAIL (0): Any answer opens with a sycophantic phrase
- Evidence: "Checked [X] answers. Sycophantic openers found: [count]. Instances: [list]"

**Point 5: Every answer is product-specific**
- Check: Each answer mentions the product name OR includes product-specific data (ingredients, features, pricing, guarantee terms)
- PASS (1): Zero generic template answers (answers that could apply to any product without modification)
- FAIL (0): Any answer is generic
- Evidence: "Generic answers found: [count]. Instances: Q[X] — '[quoted phrase that is generic]'"

**Point 6: At least 3 answers include proof, data, or specific credentials**
- Check: Count answers that include: a specific number, a study reference, a named authority, review count, comparison data, specific guarantee terms, or measurable claim
- PASS (1): 3 or more answers include proof/data
- FAIL (0): Fewer than 3 answers include proof/data
- Evidence: "Proof count: [X]. Answers with proof: Q[X] ([data point]), Q[Y] ([data point]), Q[Z] ([data point])"

**Point 7: Every answer is 2-4 sentences (scan-friendly)**
- Count sentences in each answer
- PASS (1): All answers are 2-4 sentences (5 acceptable for 1-2 complex technical items)
- FAIL (0): Any answer is 1 sentence (too terse) or 6+ sentences (too long)
- Evidence: "Sentence counts: Q1=[X], Q2=[X], ... Violations: [list any over 4 or under 2]"

### STRUCTURAL QUALITY (3 Points)

**Point 8: Sequence matches page type strategy**
- Check: Type A = hardest-first (highest-strength objections in positions 1-3). Type B = hardest-last (highest-strength in final 3 positions)
- PASS (1): Hard objections are in the correct zone
- FAIL (0): Hard objections are buried in the middle or in the wrong zone
- Evidence: "Page type: [type]. Hardest objection '[question]' at position [X]. Expected zone: [first 3 | last 3]. Actual zone: [correct | incorrect]"

**Point 9: Trust/guarantee objection addressed with specific guarantee details**
- Check: At least 1 FAQ item addresses trust/guarantee AND includes specific guarantee terms (duration, conditions, process)
- PASS (1): Trust/guarantee item present with specific details
- FAIL (0): No trust/guarantee item, OR the answer is vague ("We stand behind our products")
- Evidence: "Trust item: Q[X]. Guarantee details in answer: [specific terms quoted]"

**Point 10: Zero AI telltales or sycophantic openers in any answer**
- Full scan of all FAQ copy (header, questions, answers) against the 5-category anti-slop list
- PASS (1): Zero forbidden words or phrases from any category
- FAIL (0): Any forbidden word/phrase found
- Evidence: "Scanned [X] total words. Forbidden words found: [count]. Instances: [list with location]"

3. **Calculate total score:**
   `Score = sum of all points (0 or 1 each) = X/10`

4. **Determine result:**
   - Score >= 7.5: PASS — proceed to Layer 4
   - Score 5.0-7.4: CONDITIONAL FAIL — specific items must be revised before proceeding
   - Score < 5.0: HARD FAIL — significant quality issues, return to Layer 2

5. **For any FAIL points, provide specific revision instructions:**
   - What exactly needs to change
   - Which Q&A items are affected
   - What the corrected version should look like

## Quality Criteria
- Every point scored with explicit evidence (not just Y/N — actual quoted text or counts)
- Anti-slop scan covers ALL copy (header + questions + answers, not just answers)
- Revision requirements are specific and actionable (not "improve quality")
- Score is honest — do not inflate scores to pass the gate

## Failure Modes
- Scoring a point as PASS without evidence -> Every PASS must include the evidence that proves it. "Checked — looks good" is not evidence.
- Ignoring header copy in the anti-slop scan -> The header is copy too. Scan it.
- Inflating scores to reach 7.5 -> The gate exists to catch quality issues. A FAQ section that scores 6/10 has real problems. Honest scoring + revision is better than false PASS.
- Revision instructions that are vague -> "Improve the price answer" is not actionable. "Q3 price answer: replace sentence 1 with a specific per-serving cost comparison instead of the current generic value statement" is actionable.
