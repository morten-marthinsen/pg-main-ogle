# 2.3 — Certification Writer

**Skill:** LP-08-trust-elements
**Position:** Layer 2, Microskill 3
**Purpose:** Write certification explanation copy that translates industry jargon into audience-meaningful benefits. Each certification gets two outputs: a short-form badge label (3–7 words) and a long-form benefit explanation (1–2 sentences). If no certifications exist in the trust inventory, this microskill produces a `not_applicable` output.
**Effort Level:** high (when applicable)
**Dependencies:** `trust-classification.md` (1.1), `trust-placement-map.md` (1.3), `trust-inventory.md` (0.2), `specimen-trust-patterns.md` (0.3)
**Output:** `certification-copy.md`

---

## Input Schema

```yaml
input:
  certification_status: "[from trust-classification.md — included | not_applicable]"
  certifications: "[from trust-inventory.md — list with issuing body, what it certifies]"
  placement_map: "[from trust-placement-map.md — where certifications appear]"
  page_type: "[type_a | type_b | hybrid]"
  vertical: "[from packages-loaded.md]"
  specimen_patterns: "[from specimen-trust-patterns.md — certification display patterns]"
```

---

## Execution Logic

### If `not_applicable`:
Write `certification-copy.md` with:
```
Status: not_applicable
Reason: No certifications in trust inventory.
```
Terminate this microskill.

### If `included`:

1. **For each certification, write two versions:**

   **Short-form badge label (3–7 words):**
   - Used in trust bar, badge icons, and scannable contexts
   - Format: `[Certification Abbreviation] + [1-2 clarifying words]`
   - Example: "GMP Certified Facility" (3 words), "NSF Certified for Sport" (4 words)

   **Long-form benefit explanation (1–2 sentences):**
   - Used in certification explanation blocks, tooltips, and ingredient sections
   - Format: `[What this certification means]: [What that means FOR YOU as a customer]`
   - The explanation must answer: "Why should I care that this product has this certification?"

2. **Apply certification-specific writing guidance:**

   | Certification | Badge Label Direction | Explanation Direction |
   |--------------|---------------------|---------------------|
   | **GMP** | "GMP Certified Facility" | Manufactured in a facility meeting pharmaceutical-grade purity and potency standards. Every batch independently verified. |
   | **FDA-Registered** | "FDA-Registered Facility" | Produced in a facility registered with the FDA and subject to federal inspection standards. (NOTE: Never write "FDA Approved" for supplements — they are not.) |
   | **Prop 65 Compliant** | "California Prop 65 Compliant" | Tested to meet California's strictest consumer safety standards for heavy metals, contaminants, and toxins. |
   | **NSF Certified** | "NSF Certified" | Independently tested by NSF International — the gold standard for supplement quality verification. |
   | **NSF Certified for Sport** | "NSF Certified for Sport" | Independently verified free of 270+ banned substances — the standard trusted by Olympic and professional athletes. |
   | **Informed Sport** | "Informed Sport Certified" | Every batch tested for banned substances by LGC, the world's leading anti-doping laboratory. |
   | **COA (Certificate of Analysis)** | "Third-Party Lab Tested" | Every batch tested by an independent laboratory with results available for full transparency. |
   | **ISO 9001** | "ISO 9001 Certified" | Quality management system independently audited to international standards. |
   | **SOC 2** | "SOC 2 Compliant" | Customer data protected by independently audited security and privacy controls. |
   | **BBB** | "BBB Accredited — A+ Rating" | Accredited by the Better Business Bureau with their highest customer trust rating. |
   | **Non-GMO** | "Non-GMO Verified" | Independently verified to contain no genetically modified ingredients. |
   | **Organic** | "USDA Certified Organic" | Grown and processed without synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or artificial fertilizers. |

3. **Customize each explanation to the specific product and audience:**
   - A golf supplement audience cares about "purity" and "what goes in my body"
   - A tech/SaaS audience cares about "data security" and "uptime guarantees"
   - A health audience cares about "tested for safety" and "verified potency"

4. **CRITICAL WARNING on FDA language:**
   - Supplements are NEVER "FDA Approved" — write "FDA-Registered Facility" or "Manufactured in an FDA-Registered Facility"
   - Using "FDA Approved" for a supplement is both inaccurate and potentially a regulatory violation
   - If the trust inventory says "FDA Approved" for a supplement, correct it to "FDA-Registered Facility" and flag the discrepancy

5. **Write `certification-copy.md`.**

## Quality Criteria
- Every certification has BOTH a badge label (3–7 words) AND a benefit explanation (1–2 sentences)
- Badge labels are within word count (3–7 words)
- Explanations answer "What does this mean for ME?" — not just restating the certification name
- FDA language is correct (NEVER "FDA Approved" for supplements)
- Explanations are customized to the vertical's audience (not generic boilerplate)
- All certifications traceable to trust inventory

## Failure Modes
- Listing certifications without explanations → The entire point of LP-08 is to TRANSLATE certifications into audience benefit. A list without explanation is what every competitor does.
- Writing "FDA Approved" for a supplement → Regulatory risk. Always use "FDA-Registered Facility."
- Generic explanations that could apply to any product → "This certification ensures high quality" is meaningless. Explain the SPECIFIC standard the certification verifies.
- Badge label over 7 words → Too long for badge format. Trim to constraint.
- Inventing certifications not in inventory → If the product does not have GMP certification, do not write GMP copy. Fabrication destroys the trust this element is supposed to build.
