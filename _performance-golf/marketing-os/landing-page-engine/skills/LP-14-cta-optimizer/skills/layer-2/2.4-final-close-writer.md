## Pre-Writing Protocol

**Step 1: Load threading anchors**
Read LP-07's threading anchors verbatim:
- `primary_hook_phrase`: "[phrase]"
- `promise_statement`: "[statement]"
These MUST appear (directly or as clear echoes) in the final close.

**Step 2: Identify the final hesitation**
Based on the audience profile and offer structure, determine the single most likely reason a convinced reader would NOT click:
- Risk aversion -> Address with guarantee
- Skepticism about claims -> Address with proof reference
- Price resistance -> Address with value reframe
- "I'll do it later" -> Address with consequence/urgency (only if LP-13 permits)

**Step 3: Load primary CTA text**
The final close paragraph must flow naturally into the terminal CTA. Load the top primary CTA button text so the close can set up the action.

---

## Execution Logic

**Write the final close paragraph following this structure:**

**Sentence 1: Mechanism/approach recap (1 sentence, brief)**
- Do NOT re-explain the mechanism. One sentence that names it and acknowledges what the reader has learned.
- Pattern: "Now you know [mechanism name / approach / what makes this different]."
- This sentence exists to ground the reader before the close.

**Sentence 2: Promise restatement (MUST reference LP-07 threading anchor)**
- Echo the primary hook phrase or promise statement from the hero section.
- This closes the loop opened in the headline. The reader should feel "this is what was promised, and now I see how it works."
- Pattern: "[Hook phrase echo] — and now you have everything you need to [promise restatement]."

**Sentence 3: Final hesitation address**
- Address the ONE hesitation identified in pre-writing.
- Guarantee: "And with [guarantee terms], you risk nothing by trying."
- Proof: "The [study/number/authority] showed it works. Your only question is how fast."
- Value: "For [price], you get [value summary] — less than [comparison]."
- Consequence: "Every day you wait is another day of [specific pain]." (ONLY with LP-13 permission)

**Sentence 4-5: CTA with emotional pull**
- Transition to the terminal CTA button.
- Pattern: "[Emotional bridge to action]. [CTA button text reference]."
- This is NOT a separate CTA — it is the text that wraps around the terminal CTA button.

**Generate 2 variants** of the final close for human selection.

## Quality Criteria
- 3-5 sentences exactly — not 2, not 6
- LP-07's threading anchor explicitly referenced (identifiable phrase echo)
- Final hesitation addressed with specific evidence (guarantee terms, proof stat, or value comparison — not vague reassurance)
- Flows naturally into the terminal CTA button
- 2 variants provided for human selection
- Zero AI telltales

## Failure Modes
- Final close does not reference LP-07 threading anchors -> The loop is not closed. Reader feels disconnected from the headline promise. Rewrite with explicit echo.
- More than 5 sentences -> The final close becomes a new section instead of a bridge. Cut to 5 max.
- Hesitation addressed with vague reassurance ("You have nothing to lose") -> Must be SPECIFIC: name the guarantee duration, cite the proof stat, state the price comparison.
- Close does not flow to CTA -> The last sentence must set up the button click. If there is a jarring transition, revise.
- New claims introduced in the close -> Like P.S., the close restates and synthesizes. It does not introduce new benefits, mechanisms, or proof not already on the page.
