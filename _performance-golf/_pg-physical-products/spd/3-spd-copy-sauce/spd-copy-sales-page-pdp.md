## SECTION 5: PROBLEM

**THE PROBLEM WITH TRADITIONAL SPEED TRAINING**

**YOU TRAIN SPEED, BUT NOT FACE CONTROL. (YOU NEED BOTH!)**

Here's the real problem with modern-day speed training they don't want you to know:

Speed Sticks are sticks with weights on the end.

That's good for gaining speed, BUT...

Speed Sticks do not have a clubhead.

**No clubhead means:**
❌ No air resistance
❌ No torque
❌ No feedback on what your clubface would actually be doing during your swing
❌ No Clubface Control.

**Visual:** MP4 video of golfer swinging a speed stick

---

**THE SCIENCE OF SPEED STICKS**

Speed Sticks have no clubhead, so **the center of mass is in the middle of the shaft...**

Like a baseball bat.

But golf clubs have a clubhead, so **the center of mass IS NOT in the middle of the shaft.**

That's why your clubface wants to rotate around the shaft.

**ONE FATAL FLAW. TWO BIG PROBLEMS.**

Since Speed Sticks don't have a clubhead, that creates two big problems for you:

**#1: Speed Sticks don't look, feel, or perform like a real golf club.**

**#2: Speed Sticks can give you speed, but NO face control.**

## SECTION 6: CONSEQUENCE

**3 REASONS TRAINING SPEED WITHOUT FACE CONTROL**
**MAKES YOUR BIG MISS BIGGER**

**#1: The faster you swing, the more your clubface wants to open or close in the downswing.**

Trackman data proves:

- Higher clubhead speed creates more angular momentum.

- More angular momentum means there's more force trying to open or close your clubface.

- The more your face is open or closed at impact, the less accurate you are.

**Visual:** [MP4 VIDEO (looping): guys makes a swing, show swing speed, distance, and how far right the ball goes with a tracer. Then show the same golfer with 7mph more swing speed making the SAME kind of swing (the two swings should look almost identical), makes swing, show swing speed, distance, and how much farther right the ball goes with a tracer.]

**#2: The faster you swing, the less time you have to square your clubface.**

If you can't square the face swinging 90 miles per hour...

You DEFINITELY can't square the face swinging 100 miles per hour.

**Visual:** [IMAGE: side by side freeze frame at impact, close up low angle DTL from behind the golfer, red arrow at impact, 90mph 4.5* open 100mph 6.5*open]

**#3: The faster you swing, the more stress you put on your mechanics.**

Speed training puts your body under a lot of stress.

When you're stressed, you default to bad habits.
(That's why your swing gets worse as you get tired.)

**Visual:** [MP4 LOOPING VIDEO: Sweaty golfer who has clearly been grinding on the range, looks exhausted, makes a bad swing with bad form (realistic) and hits a bad shot]

---

**WHAT'S THAT MEAN FOR YOU?**

**WHEN YOU STRUGGLE WITH A SLICE:**

You're leaving the clubface OPEN relative to your path at impact.

When you swing faster, you WILL open the clubface more...

So you launch your slice farther offline.
