## Micro-Pitch

**Problem/Root Cause**:

Most speed trainers on the market work the same way. You swing a weighted stick to build swing speed. And oftentimes, your speed *does* go up, but here's what nobody talks about:

**THE SCIENCE OF SPEED TRAINING**

The weight on the end of speed sticks is round, so **the center of mass IS in the middle of the shaft.** That's why, when you swing a speed stick it does NOT want to twist.

Golf clubs have a clubhead and clubface, so **the center of mass IS NOT in the middle of the shaft.** That's why, when you swing a golf club, it DOES want to twist.

**THE FEEDBACK FLAW**

That means speed sticks don't look, feel, or behave like a real club... because when you speed train with a weighted stick, you get no resistance, no torque, and most importantly, no feedback on what your clubface would actually be doing during your swing.

So what happens when an amateur golfer tries to swing faster, using a weighted stick that gives you no clubface feedback?

Your direction control gets measurably worse.

**TWO FACTS ABOUT FACE CONTROL**

#1: The faster you swing, the more the face wants to rotate.

#2: While you're speed training, your body is under a lot of stress, so it defaults to your typical motor pattern.

In other words, every single speed training rep you make without clubface feedback – engrains your root swing flaw that's causing your big miss – even deeper into your swing.

**Speed Without Control**
**Makes Your Big Miss BIGGER**

For slicers: As you're learning to swing faster, you're also engraining the feel of leaving your clubface wide open... launching your slice even farther offline.

For golfers who hook the ball: As you're learning to swing faster, you're also learning to flip your hands early... so now you duck-hook it 30 yards deeper into the woods.

That's why when you try to take your new speed back to your driver, your big miss even worse.

**Solution:**

That's why we created SpeedTrac, the first speed training system for amateurs with adjustable Y-Trac Weighting built into the clubhead that forces you to control the face while you gain speed.

And because the Y-Trac's off-axis weight is ADJUSTABLE, with three positions — heel, toe, and center... no matter what your big miss is, you gain speed, distance, and total face control.

If you slice, you start with the weight in the heel. This trains your hands to feel the toe passing the heel — the feel of a square face at impact. You're building speed with the correct release pattern for YOUR specific miss.

If you hook, you put the weight on the toe for maximum resistance against the clubhead closing. You build speed with a controlled, stable release.

And if you don't slice or hook but just hit it "straight but short" — use the center position for pure speed training with realistic clubface feedback.

THAT'S how you build speed you can take to the golf course AND train face control at the same time. Fast AND straight. Not fast OR straight.
