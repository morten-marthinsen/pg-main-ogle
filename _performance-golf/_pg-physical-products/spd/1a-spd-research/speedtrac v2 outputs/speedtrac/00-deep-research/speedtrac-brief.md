## WHAT WAS INTENTIONALLY EXCLUDED FROM THIS BRIEF

> **For transparency:** The following elements from the original product concept document were deliberately removed to prevent biasing the research process. These represent internal marketing hypotheses that should be validated or invalidated by research, not treated as inputs.

- **Pre-baked positioning** ("first speed trainer that...", "ONLY device that...")
- **Competitive conclusions** (competitors described as "fundamentally flawed," "same design flaw")
- **Named narrative frames** ("fast inconsistency," "Speed Training's Dirty Secret," "Transfer Gap")
- **Assumed emotional benefits** (confidence, pride, relief, empowerment, excitement)
- **USP and positioning statements** — these are outputs of the copy engine, not research inputs
- **Go-to-market strategy, channel fit, and storytelling frameworks**
- **Hero messages and product stories**
- **Revenue targets and success metrics**
- **Assumed "white space" and market readiness conclusions**
- **Instructor endorsements and credibility strategy**

These elements may prove valid — but the research should discover whether they're true, not start from the assumption that they are.
