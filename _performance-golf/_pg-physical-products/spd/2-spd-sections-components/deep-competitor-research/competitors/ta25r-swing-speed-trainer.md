## Marketing System Breakdown

### Message/Angle Clusters
- Speed-to-distance promise (`Boost Distance`, mph gain framing).
- Mechanism-led proof (`weighted` trainer + `ribbon` visual feedback).
- Ease-of-use positioning (`Swing Training, made easy.`).
- Social validation (`Best Seller`, `7600+ Golfers Who Love Us`).
- Risk reversal (`90 Day Risk-Free Trial`).

### Funnel Logic (Observed)
1. Utility value anchor (`FREE Shipping...`) + full navigation trust context.
2. ATF commerce clarity (title, price, variants, CTA, urgency, shipping ETA).
3. Mechanism + spec substantiation (description, dimensions, included items).
4. Objection handling (`Ask a question`, guarantee, shipping timing).
5. Long-form education + differentiation (`How it works`, reasons, comparison chart).
6. Social proof + reviews + capture + footer trust utilities.

### Offer Architecture
- Core offer is single-SKU with ATF price framing (`Regular price` and price display).
- Risk-reversal is integrated into ATF purchase module (`90 Day Risk-Free Trial`).
- Shipping threshold incentive appears at top (`Orders Over $100`).

### Proof Model
- Uses social proof density (customer count + many review snippets).
- Uses authority provenance (former JGTO pro + top Japanese coach).
- Uses structured product-vs-alternative comparison table.

### Risk Reversal Model
- Clear trial + return window + no-questions phrasing near checkout path.

### Conversion Strengths
- Strong above-the-fold purchase clarity and objection handling.
- High proof density via testimonials and user-count signaling.
- Good mechanism explanation with actionable training routine.
- Explicit comparison framing versus adjacent trainer categories.

### Conversion Weaknesses
- Dynamic timer placeholders (`[hours]`, `[mins]`) may reduce authenticity if unresolved.
- Price display shows `Regular price` and current price but no visible strike-through delta copy.
- Long page depth can dilute attention without sticky recap CTAs.
