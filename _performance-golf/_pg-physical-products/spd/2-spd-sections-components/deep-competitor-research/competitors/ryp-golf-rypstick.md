## Marketing System Breakdown

### Message/Angle Clusters
- Overspeed-to-distance equation: `MORE SPEED = MORE DISTANCE`
- Convenience angle: `BUILD SPEED FROM ANYWHERE`
- Structured training angle: app-guided progression + drill system
- Authority angle: tour usage + coach credentials + review scale
- Downside-protection angle: multiple guarantee/return frames

### Funnel Logic (Observed)
1. Promo strip + nav orientation (`FREE SHIPPING...`, product/training routes)
2. ATF commerce block (title/reviews/price/variant/cart)
3. Mechanism/spec proof and use-context copy
4. Long-form outcome + benefits/features narrative
5. Social proof depth (`Customer Reviews`, `All Reviews` ecosystem)
6. Policy/FAQ/contact utilities and email retention modules

### Offer Architecture
- Core hero SKU at `$199.00` with shaft-length variants.
- Repeated lower-page product blocks to recover intent late in scroll.
- Cross-sell stack on collection pages and bundles (`Rypstick Training Package`, `Power Bundle`).

### Proof Model
- Quant-style promise framing (`For every MPH... you'll gain 3 yards...`).
- Large review counts (`Based on 371 reviews` on PDP; `Based on 883 reviews` on all-reviews page).
- Authority references (tour athletes + Golf Digest/MyGolfSpy mentions + Dr. Luke credentialing).

### Risk Reversal Model
- On-product promise: `60 Day Money Back Guarantee`.
- Site trust row: `40-Day Money Back Guarantee on all products.`
- Policy layer: 14-day baseline, conditional 30-day extension for documented training, 1-year defect pathway.

### Conversion Strengths
- Strong ATF clarity (title/price/variant/cart all explicit).
- Deep mechanism-to-outcome chain with concrete specs.
- Multi-layer trust stack (reviews, authority, media, guarantees).
- Robust objection handling (FAQ + form + policy text).

### Conversion Weaknesses
