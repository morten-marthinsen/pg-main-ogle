## Marketing System Breakdown

### Message/Angle Clusters
- Distance + accuracy outcome framing (`hit it longer and straighter`, `Distance or Accuracy? Both Please!`).
- Mechanism framing (own shaft + adapter/weight + structured program).
- Authority framing (TPI/PGA references + review counts + testimonial snippets).

### Funnel Logic (Observed)
1. Promo/logistics context (top strips)
2. Product certainty + ATF purchase controls (gallery + product form)
3. Mechanism and deliverables (description/specs + FAQ)
4. Long-form education and proof (`Made for speed....`, `SpeedStix - The Program`, reviews)
5. Footer trust utilities (policies, social, account links)

### Offer Architecture
- Anchor price framing (`Original price was...` vs current price).
- Core single-SKU PDP offer with compatibility-led positioning.
- AOV expansion via `Frequently bought together` + PRGR bundle prompt.

### Proof Model
- Quantitative statements (`38 reviews`, `from 43 reviews`, outcome percentages).
- Qualitative testimonial headlines and body snippets.
- Authority references (`TPI certified instructors and PGA pros`).

### Risk Reversal Model
- Explicit 30-day guarantee language repeated in ATF and description sections.
- Return framing emphasizes low-friction reversal (`no quibble`).

### Conversion Strengths
- Strong compatibility clarity for TaylorMade owners.
- Repeated guarantee copy near key decision points.
- Program detail increases implementation confidence.

### Conversion Weaknesses
- No observed countdown/event timer for deadline urgency.
- No observed dedicated email capture to recover non-buyers.
- No observed currency selector or visible payment-icon reassurance block.
