## Marketing System Breakdown

### Message/Angle Clusters
- Own-shaft transfer angle: speed gains should carry into the player's real driver.
- Distance-to-score angle: speed is framed as the fastest path to lower scores.
- Science/protocol angle: app programming, recovery structure, and drill sequencing.
- Athlete-authority angle: Team Toad, world-record claims, and media references.

### Funnel Logic (Observed)
1. Header utility + brand trust framing.
2. ATF product form with mechanism + options + add-ons.
3. PDP explanation and media proof.
4. Related trust pages (Team Toad, blog PR, about/app pages).
5. FAQ/shipping&returns/contact objection handling.
6. Newsletter + footer utility retention layer.

### Offer Architecture
- Core hero SKU: `Speed Toad` at `$124.99`.
- Order-bump style add-ons: `PRGR Portable Launch Monitor (HS130A)` and `Hybrid Speed Toad Head Cover`.
- Catalog laddering: `Speed Toad App [Lifetime Access]`, screws, accessories, international checkout path (`ADD TO CART` Stripe link on UK page).

### Proof Model
- Quant claims: `5.4 mph` gain framing repeated across product/app/blog pages.
- Athlete proof: LPGA/long-drive performance claims and world-record PR posts.
- Third-party language: MyGolfSpy feature quote integrated into blog proof.

### Risk Reversal Model
- Explicit 30-day unused-item return window with refund process details.
- Contact-led support path for returns and pre-purchase questions.

### Conversion Strengths
- Clear ATF mechanism differentiation and product configuration clarity.
- Strong authority scaffolding via athletes, records, and content depth.
- International routing and app-lifetime framing increase perceived completeness.

### Conversion Weaknesses
- No extracted timer/countdown urgency module.
- No extracted payment icon stack on publicly captured pages.
- `Verified Reviews` page header is present, but review body content was not extractable in captured HTML.
