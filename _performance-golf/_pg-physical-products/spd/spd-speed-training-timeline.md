**Headline:** A BRIEF HISTORY OF SPEED TRAINING

**Sub-Headline:** The Overload/Underload Discovery

708 BC - Ancient Olympic Athletes in throwing events experiment with throwing varying weights to enhance performance.

1920 - Babe Ruth popularizes training with heavy bats en route to becoming "The Sultan of Swat"

1967 - 9-Time All-Star Elston Howard slides the first weighted donut onto a baseball bat to make the regular bat feel lighter & faster afterward.

1968 - Mickey Mantle, Roger Maris, and Willie Mays are all using The "Elston Howard Bat Weight" as it becomes ubiquitous across Major League Baseball.

1971 - Soviet Olympic coach Yuri Verhoshansky discovers Overload/Underload principle: Training with heavy AND light weights "tricks" your body to move faster.

1992 - Harvey Penick, the legendary instructor who taught Ben Crenshaw and Tom Kite, writes in his Little Red Book: "Every golfer should own a heavy club. Swinging a weighted club, with your regular grip and stance, is the best exercise I know to build the golf muscles".

1992 - Jim Sorenson releases The Momentus Weighted Golf Clubs, endorsed by Former World #1 David Duval. By 1998, it's the #1 selling training aid of all time.
​
2010 - Jim Hackenberg creates the Orange Whip after observing that PGA Tour pros' swings look "like a chain with a ball on the end of it."

2012 - Dr. Tom House brings overload/underload concepts to golf at the 2012 TPI Summit.

2013 - The Modern Speed Stick Revolution: SuperSpeed Golf begins applying Overload/Underload training to the golf swing using TrackMan, 3D motion capture systems, and physical screening equipment.

2014 - SuperSpeed Golf coins the term "Overspeed Training" as they dial in "The Three-Club System": Swinging clubs approximately 20% lighter than a driver, 10% lighter, and 5% heavier, swung in that order.

2015 - Speed Stik releases sliding magnet weight and speed gauge that gives you feedback while you're speed training
​
2020 - Rypstick: A single driver-length stick with adjustable, removable weights and counterweight for all-in-one overspeed training
​
2021 - The Stack System	uses a single shorter stick with changeable weight discs and app to deliver data-driven overspeed training
