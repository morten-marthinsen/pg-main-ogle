## Problem: Countersell

**TRADITIONAL PUTTERS:**

Traditional putters place the center of gravity behind the shaft. That geometry creates more face movement during the stroke and makes the golfer responsible for squaring everything up with feel and timing. Some golfers love that responsibility. A lot of everyday golfers do not.

**ZERO-TORQUE CATEGORY:**

Zero-torque putters deserve credit for pushing the category forward. They made more golfers aware that balance matters. But the neutral-balance story still leaves the golfer handling the face through the motion. It reduces one part of the problem without fully addressing the natural player tendency to let the face open.

**THE GAP:**

The whitespace for RS1 is simple: a putter built to help the human side of the problem too. Weight forward of the shaft is the opening move. Face-down balance, hammerhead stability, and the 74-degree stock lie are downstream results of that move.

## Features

### FEATURE 1: Weight Forward Of The Shaft (HERO)

**CLAIM:** RS1 is built around weight forward of the shaft so the putter is engineered to help counter face drift.

**PROOF:** The front section carries the meaningful mass, while lighter crown and tail materials support that geometry. The product deck and transcript repeatedly identify this as the clearest product difference and the root of the entire mechanism story.

**Functional:** The face is designed to stay squarer through the motion with less timing-dependent correction.

**Dimensionalized:** On the 3- to 5-footers that usually make the hands tighten up, the head feels more stable and less busy.

**Emotional:** More trust over the ball. Less sense that you have to rescue the putter.

---

### FEATURE 2: Face-Down Balance

**CLAIM:** Face-down balance gives golfers visible proof that RS1's geometry is different.

**PROOF:** Balance the putter under the shaft and the face tips down instead of hanging in a more traditional orientation. The prompt is explicit that this should be treated as a downstream result of the forward-of-shaft geometry, not the main story.

**Functional:** The orientation supports the face-control story and makes the geometry easier to demonstrate.

**Dimensionalized:** Golfers can see that this is not the same balance pattern they are used to.

**Emotional:** Belief gets easier when the proof is visual.

---

### FEATURE 3: Heavy Front Stability

**CLAIM:** The hammerhead-style front section is built to create a more stable face feel through the stroke and at impact.

**PROOF:** The source materials repeatedly describe a heavily weighted front section, a steel face structure, and a front-loaded stability story. If exact percentage language is used in live copy, confirm the final engineering number first.

**Functional:** The face resists twisting more than a typical rear-weighted layout.

**Dimensionalized:** Mishits feel less flimsy and lag putts are built to come off the face with more consistency.

**Emotional:** Speed control feels less fragile.
