## SECTION 4: THE RS1 MECHANISM

**Pre-Headline:** THE RS1 DIFFERENCE

**Headline:** The story starts with one geometry decision.

**Sub-Headline:** RS1 puts the weight forward of the shaft.

**Supporting Copy:**

That matters because traditional putters usually place the center of gravity behind the shaft.

That creates a pushing relationship.

And pushed objects like to rotate.

That is one reason the face wants to drift open and closed during the stroke.

RS1 was engineered around the opposite idea.

Put the mass forward of the shaft.

Create a pulling relationship instead of a pushing one.

Give the golfer a putter that is designed to help the face track squarer through the motion.

That single decision drives the rest of the product story.

It is what creates the face-down balance.

It is what gives the hammerhead front section its stable feel.

It is what makes the upright lie story matter more.

It is what lets Roll Straight Technology feel like one connected system instead of a pile of disconnected features.

**Mechanism Visual:** [Exploded product visual showing shaft axis, heavy front section, light crown, and light tail with callouts: "weight forward of the shaft"]

**Simple Analogy Block:**

Pull something and it tends to track straight.

Push it and it wants to wander.

RS1 is built around the first relationship, not the second.

### FEATURE 1: Weight Forward Of The Shaft

**Headline:** The putter does not wait for your hands to save it.

**Claim:** RS1 concentrates the meaningful mass in the front section of the head and places that weight forward of the shaft axis.

**Proof / Mechanism:** The design pairs a heavy steel front with lighter materials through the crown and tail so the geometry can do the work. This "hammerhead" front section is the clearest expression of the entire product.

**Benefit:** Functionally, that forward geometry is engineered to resist face drift and reduce unwanted rotation. On the course, that means putts are designed to start on line more consistently and off-center strikes feel less flimsy. Emotionally, it gives you a stroke that feels more stable when you need it most.

**Visual:** [Hero front-weighting animation with shaft-axis callout]

## SECTION 12: FAQ

### FAQ 1: Is RS1 only for great putters?

No.

RS1 is for everyday golfers who are tired of feeling streaky on the greens.

In fact, the whole page is built around golfers who do not want a putter that demands perfect timing every time.

### FAQ 2: Do I need to change my stroke to use it?

No.

RS1 is designed to help with your current motion.

Set it down.

Use the alignment.

Make your normal stroke.

### FAQ 3: What does "weight forward of the shaft" actually mean?

It means the meaningful mass is pushed in front of the shaft axis instead of living mostly behind it.

That forward geometry is what creates the face-down balance and the counteracting force that sits at the center of the RS1 story.

### FAQ 4: Is RS1 just another zero-torque putter?

No.

The framing is different.

Zero-torque designs focus on neutralizing twisting.

RS1 is built around forward-of-shaft weighting that is designed to help counter face drift too.

### FAQ 5: Why is the stock lie angle 74 degrees?

Because the launch story is about a more upright, square-to-square setup that supports a simpler motion for everyday golfers.

Custom or tour-side flatter options can exist, but 74-degree is the main stock build.

### FAQ 6: What is the difference between Standard and Upgraded?

Standard gives you the stepless steel shaft and Dual Pistol rubber grip.

Upgraded gives you the 15mm low-torque graphite shaft and upgraded PU grip.

Both keep the same core RS1 head geometry and stock launch specs.

### FAQ 7: Is RS1 available for right-handed and left-handed golfers?

Yes.

RS1 is available in both RH and LH.
