## Problem: Countersell

The current putter market gives golfers two options. Both are incomplete.

**TRADITIONAL PUTTERS (Scotty Cameron, Odyssey, TaylorMade Spider):** These are rear-weighted designs. The center of gravity sits behind the shaft. This creates "toe flow" — the face mechanically wants to rotate open during the stroke. Golfers are taught to "time" this rotation and use their hands to square the face at impact. It works for tour players with 10,000 reps and zero pressure. For the everyday golfer standing over a 4-footer with his buddies watching? The small muscles in the hands and wrists are the first to fail.

**LAB GOLF (DF3, Directed Force):** LAB made a genuine breakthrough with zero-torque, center-shafted putters. They eliminated the equipment's tendency to flop open. But they also eliminated any assistance. A LAB putter is geometrically neutral — 100% of face control is on the golfer. There's no counteracting force. If your natural stroke arc opens the face (and it does — that's human biomechanics), a LAB putter does nothing to help. LAB solved the technology problem. They left the human problem on the table.

**THE GAP:** No putter on the market creates a counteracting force that actively works WITH the golfer's natural tendency to open the face during the stroke. Traditional putters make it worse. LAB putters ignore it. The RS1 is the first putter designed to address it.

## Features

### FEATURE 1: Forward Axis Balance (HERO)

**CLAIM:** The core of Roll Straight Technology. Forward Axis Balance positions the center of gravity in front of the shaft axis — reversing the physics that causes face drift in 95% of putters on the market.

**PROOF:** In a traditional putter, the CG sits behind the shaft. This creates a pushing relationship — and physics dictates that pushed objects rotate. The face naturally drifts open during the stroke. Forward Axis Balance reverses this by loading weight in front of the shaft, creating a pulling relationship. Pulled objects track straight. This is the same principle that makes towing a trailer forward easy and backing one up nearly impossible. The RS1's forward CG creates face-down balance — place it on a table with the head hanging off the edge, and the face naturally points toward the ground instead of toward the ceiling. That's the visible, demonstrable proof that the physics have been reversed. Less than 5% of putters on the market use this approach.

**Functional:** The putter face is designed to resist drifting open during the stroke. Instead of fighting the head, you flow with it.

**Dimensionalized:** That tension in your hands on a 4-footer for par? That death grip on the putter when your buddies are watching? Forward Axis Balance is engineered to take the fight out of the equation — so you stop squeezing and start stroking.

**Emotional:** The feeling of standing over a putt and knowing the putter is working with you — not against you. Confidence you didn't have to manufacture.

### FEATURE 2: High Forward MOI

**CLAIM:** Massive face stability on off-center strikes, not from perimeter weighting — from a heavier face.

**PROOF:** 75% of the RS1's head weight is concentrated in the front 25% of the putter head. The front section alone weighs 265 grams — roughly the weight of a typical 7-iron head. A heavier face resists twisting at impact, both during the stroke and at the moment of contact. This is different from traditional MOI designs that spread weight around the perimeter. The RS1 puts the mass where it matters most: directly behind the ball.

**Functional:** Off-center hits maintain speed and line. Distance control stays consistent because the face doesn't twist at impact.

**Dimensionalized:** You catch one a little toward the toe on a 30-foot lag putt. With a traditional putter, that ball pulls up 5 feet short and 3 feet left. With the RS1, it finishes pin-high and close. The face held.

**Emotional:** The forgiveness to stop steering and start trusting your stroke.

### FEATURE 4: 74-Degree Square-to-Square Lie Angle

**CLAIM:** The most upright stock lie angle on the market — designed for a straighter back-and-through stroke with less face rotation.

**Functional:** Straighter stroke path with less face rotation. Simpler mechanics. Fewer variables.

**Dimensionalized:** Instead of timing a complex arc and hoping the face squares up at impact, you rock your shoulders and the ball goes where you're looking. That's what a 74-degree lie does — it takes the wrist timing out of the stroke.

**Emotional:** Simplicity. The feeling that putting just got less complicated.

**The Evolution of Putting: Why Neutral Was Never the Finish Line**

Here's a question most golfers have never been asked: what if the reason you miss putts has nothing to do with your stroke?

For over a hundred years, putters have been built the same way. Weight in the back. Shaft in the heel. And a face that mechanically wants to drift open the moment you start your stroke. The industry called it "toe flow" and taught golfers to time it. They built an entire instruction economy around fixing a problem that was engineered into the equipment.

Meanwhile, you blamed your hands. Your nerves. Your mental game. You bought new putters — all with the same flaw. You took lessons — all teaching you to compensate for the same physics. Nothing stuck because nothing addressed the root cause.

Then a small company called LAB Golf changed the conversation. They built putters with zero torque — perfectly neutral, center-shafted, with no tendency to rotate in either direction. It was a genuine breakthrough. They proved that the equipment itself was part of the problem. And they deserve credit for it.

But LAB solved half the equation.

A neutral putter doesn't fight you. That's real progress. But it also doesn't help you. Your natural stroke arc still wants to open the face. Your wrists still twitch under pressure. Your hands are still doing all the work. LAB fixed the technology. They left the human on their own.

Performance Golf asked the next question: what if the putter could actually help?

That's what the RS1 is. Designed by 25-year putter engineering veteran Chris McGinley and manufactured in Oceanside, California, the RS1 moves the weight forward of the shaft instead of behind it. This creates something no other putter offers — a counteracting force that actively resists your natural tendency to open the face.

It's called Forward Axis Balance. It's the core of Roll Straight Technology — a 7-feature system where every design choice is downstream of one principle: weight forward of the shaft.

Traditional putters fought you AND you fought yourself. LAB stopped the putter from fighting you — but you still fought yourself. The RS1 stops the fight entirely. It works WITH your natural stroke to keep the face square.

Square face AND natural stroke. Not one or the other. Both.

The era of face drift is over.
