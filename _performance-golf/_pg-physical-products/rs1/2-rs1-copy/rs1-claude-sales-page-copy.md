## Section 5: Mechanism Reveal — Roll Straight Technology

There are two things that open the face during a putting stroke.

The first is the equipment. In a traditional putter, the weight sits behind the shaft. That creates a pushing force — and pushed objects rotate. The face drifts open mechanically, before your hands even get involved.

The second is you. Your natural stroke arc wants to rotate the face open during the backswing. Your hands and wrists — the smallest, twitchiest muscles in your body — have to fight this rotation on every putt. Under pressure, they fail first.

For a hundred years, both problems compounded. Your putter fought you AND you fought yourself.

Then LAB Golf took a real step forward. They created zero-torque putters — perfectly neutral, with no tendency to rotate. They eliminated the equipment's contribution to face drift. And they deserve credit for that.

But LAB solved one problem and left the other on the table. A neutral putter doesn't fight you. It also doesn't help you. Your natural stroke still opens the face. Your small muscles still do all the work. Under pressure, you're on your own.

The RS1 is the next step.

Introducing Roll Straight Technology — a 7-feature system engineered by 25-year putter veteran Chris McGinley. The core principle: move the weight forward of the shaft.

This reverses the physics. Instead of pushing the face through impact (where it rotates open), the forward weight creates a pulling force — and pulled objects track straight. Think of towing a trailer forward versus backing one up. One tracks perfectly. The other jackknifes.

Forward Axis Balance doesn't just eliminate the equipment's tendency to open. It creates a counteracting force against your tendency to open the face too.

It's not neutral. It's helpful.

It's the first putter designed to fix both sides of the putting equation — the technology AND the human.

## Section 6: Technology Features (CPB)

Seven features. One principle. Every design choice in the RS1 is downstream of Forward Axis Balance — weight forward of the shaft.

---

**Forward Axis Balance**
*The Core of Roll Straight Technology*

The center of gravity in the RS1 sits in front of the shaft axis. Not behind it. Not centered. In front.

This creates face-down balance. Set the RS1 on a table with the head hanging off the edge, and the face points toward the ground. Your current putter probably flips face-up. That's the physics that's been drifting open on every putt you've ever hit.

Forward Axis Balance creates a pulling relationship between your hands and the putter head. Instead of pushing the face through impact and hoping it stays square, you're pulling it — and pulled objects track straight.

You stop fighting the head. You start flowing with it. Less guesswork. More fireworks.

---

**High Forward MOI**
*265 Grams of Face Stability*

75% of the RS1's head weight is packed into the front 25% of the putter head. The front section alone weighs 265 grams — roughly the same as a typical 7-iron head.

That's not perimeter weighting. That's a heavier face. And a heavier face resists twisting on off-center strikes. Your distance control stays consistent because the face simply doesn't give at impact.

You catch one toward the toe on a 30-footer. Instead of pulling up 5 feet short, the ball finishes close. The face held. Your lag putting just got more reliable — without changing a thing about your stroke.

---

**74-Degree Square-to-Square Lie Angle**
*The Straightest Path in Putting*

The industry standard lie angle is approximately 70 degrees. The RS1 ships at 74 — the most upright stock lie angle on the market.

A more upright lie keeps your hands higher and your eyes directly over the ball. It creates less stroke arc, which means less opportunity for the face to rotate. The result is a simpler, more repeatable motion.

Instead of timing a complex arc and hoping the face squares at impact, you rock your shoulders and the ball goes where you're looking. That's what a 74-degree lie does — it takes the wrist timing out of the equation.

And if 74 isn't your setup? Precision-milled interchangeable shaft spuds let you switch to 72 or 70 degrees in seconds.

Putting just got less complicated.

You look down, you see the line, you know. Square or not square. No adjusting, no second-guessing, no doubt. Just pull the trigger.

Three materials. Three jobs. CNC-machined steel in the front — that's where the mass lives for Forward Axis Balance. Lightweight carbon composite woven crown — keeps weight out of the top so it can live in the face. CNC-machined aluminum tail — provides the Bold T-Wing shape without shifting the CG backward.
