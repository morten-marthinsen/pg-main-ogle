### HERO MECHANISM: ROLL STRAIGHT TECHNOLOGY™ (7-Feature System)

Roll Straight Technology is a compound mechanism — a branded 7-feature system engineered to eliminate face drift. The core geometry is WEIGHT FORWARD OF THE SHAFT. Everything else is downstream.

1. Forward Axis Balance™ (Hero Sub-Mechanism) — Positions center of gravity IN FRONT of the shaft. Creates a pulling relationship (vs. pushing). Reverses the physics that causes face drift. Less than 5% of putters on market use this approach. This is the "special magical sauce."

2. High Forward MOI — 75%+ of the total 360g head weight packed in the front 25% of the putter head. 265g in the front section alone (the weight of a typical 7-iron head). The heavier face resists twisting during the stroke and at impact.

3. Forward Center of Gravity — Heaviest point sits forward of the shaft. Creates face-down balance (putter naturally sits face-down when placed on a table). Visible and demonstrable — the "table test."

4. 74° Square-to-Square Lie Angle — Most upright in the market (industry standard is ~70°). Creates less stroke arc for a straighter back-and-through motion. Enables a vertical, face-square release instead of rotational timing. Precision-milled interchangeable shaft spuds.

5. Face-Square Focus Alignment — Three long, parallel alignment lines (1-2-3mm wide). Tail-to-front lines draw focus to a perpendicular line at the face. Shows misalignment more effectively than single-line designs.

6. Face-Forward Shaft Position — Onset design gives full face visibility unbroken by shaft or hosel. Better pivot point for a pure pendulum stroke. Clear sight line at address.

7. Multi-Material Construction — Heavy CNC-machined steel front. Lightweight carbon composite woven crown. Lightweight CNC-machined aluminum tail. 5-axis precision-machined interchangeable shaft spuds.

A micro-script compresses stories into repeatable phrases. Built on a Dominant Selling Idea (DSI) — the ONE most important difference.

The 4½ Big Rules:
1. It's not what people hear, it's what they repeat
2. Every screen is a word-of-mouth machine
3. Story is everything
4. Give 'Em A Micro-Script — just enough information in the least number of words
5. Build on a DSI — the superlative claim (must pass: Superlative, Important, Believable, Measurable, Ownable)

Micro-Scripts Document Structure: Category → Binary Frame → DSI → Positioning Statement → Micro-Pitch (3-5 paragraphs) → Tagline → 50+ Micro-Scripts by type (A/B Equations, Stark Reminders, Unique Wordplay, Whole Micro Stories, Rhyme, Alliteration, Repetitive Sequences, Rule of Three) → Features CPB → Facts of Difference (30+ "ONLY" claims) → One-Page Brand Story.
