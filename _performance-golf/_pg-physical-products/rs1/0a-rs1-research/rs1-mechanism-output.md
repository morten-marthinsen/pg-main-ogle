#### CANDIDATE 1: Roll Straight Technology™ (RETAIN)

**Core Logic:** \> Face drift causes putts to miss because traditional
putters have their center of gravity behind the shaft --- creating a
pushing relationship that opens the face during every stroke. Roll
Straight Technology eliminates face drift through a 7-feature system
built around forward CG placement. Forward Axis Balance positions the
heaviest point of the putter in front of the shaft, so instead of your
hands fighting to keep the face square, the putter's physics does it for
you. Seven engineered features --- forward balance, forward CG, forward
MOI, upright lie angle, alignment system, onset shaft, and
multi-material construction --- all work together to keep the face
square from address through impact.

**Analogy --- Primary:** \> "Think about pulling a trailer down the
highway versus backing one up. Pull it forward --- it tracks perfectly
straight, no effort. Try pushing it backward --- it jackknifes
immediately. Your putter works the same way. When the center of gravity
is behind the shaft, you're pushing the face through your stroke. It
wants to open. Roll Straight Technology puts the weight in front, so
you're pulling the face through your stroke instead of pushing it. The
face stays square --- just like the trailer stays straight."

**Analogy --- Secondary:** \> "It's like the difference between steering
a shopping cart from the front versus the back. Push from behind and it
wobbles. Pull from the front and it tracks straight. Roll Straight
Technology makes your putter track straight by putting the weight where
physics says it belongs --- in front."

**Step-by-Step Explanation (The 7 Features):** 1. **Forward Axis
Balance™** --- The face naturally tilts forward instead of opening up.
This forward orientation resists drifting open during the stroke. 2.
**High Forward MOI** --- Weight packed into the front of the head makes
the face harder to twist, holding square position through the stroke. 3.
**Forward Center of Gravity** --- Heaviest point sits in front of the
shaft instead of behind it. Creates a pulling relationship that keeps
the face from turning open. 4. **Square-to-Square Lie Angle (74°)** ---
More upright lie angle creates a straighter back-and-through stroke with
less face rotation. 5. **Face-Square Focus Alignment System** --- Three
long lines and one short front line guide the eyes to square the face at
setup. When the face starts square, the forward-balanced design holds
it. 6. **Face-Forward Shaft Position** --- Onset design gives clear view
of the full hitting surface. Creates a true pendulum swing that reduces
twisting. 7. **Multi-Material Construction** --- Heavy steel front,
light carbon top, light aluminum back. Pushes mass toward the front and
supports all other face-drift-fighting features.

### 5.3 Core Explanation

#### CORE LOGIC (103 words)

> Face drift happens when your putter's center of gravity sits behind
> the shaft. That placement creates a pushing relationship --- and
> pushed objects rotate. Your putter face wants to open on every stroke.
> Roll Straight Technology reverses this by placing the center of
> gravity in front of the shaft. Now the stroke creates a pulling

> features work together: Forward Axis Balance, forward CG, forward MOI,

> pushing the face through your stroke. It wants to open. Roll Straight
> Technology puts the weight in front, so you're pulling the face
> through instead of pushing it. The face stays square --- just like the
> trailer stays straight."

#### ANALOGY --- SECONDARY (42 words)

> belongs."

#### STEP-BY-STEP EXPLANATION (The 7 Features)

**FEATURE 1: Forward Axis Balance™** ⭐ HERO \> The putter naturally
sits with the face tilted forward instead of opening up. This forward
orientation resists drifting open during the stroke, so the putter works
to stay square without the golfer having to fix it. This is the core
innovation --- the single feature that creates the pulling relationship.

**FEATURE 2: High Forward MOI** \> Most of the putter's weight is packed
into the very front of the head. With more weight in front, the face
becomes harder to twist, which holds a square position as the putter
moves through the stroke.

**FEATURE 3: Forward Center of Gravity** \> The heaviest point of the
putter sits in front of the shaft instead of behind it. When the CG is
forward, the putter naturally lines up with its path and keeps the face
from turning open. This is what creates the pulling motion --- the same
physics that makes a trailer track straight.

**FEATURE 4: Square-to-Square Lie Angle (74°)** \> A more upright lie
angle creates a stroke that moves straighter back and through with less
face rotation. Instead of an arcing stroke that opens and closes the
face, the 74° angle keeps the putter moving on a more linear path.

**FEATURE 5: Face-Square Focus Alignment System** \> Three long lines
and one short front line guide the eyes to square the face at setup.
When the face starts square, Roll Straight Technology's forward-balanced
design can hold that square position more easily through the stroke.

**FEATURE 6: Face-Forward Shaft Position** \> The shaft enters the head
from behind the face (onset design), giving a clear view of the full
hitting surface. This setup creates a true pendulum-style swing that
reduces twisting and keeps the face pointed where the golfer aims.

**FEATURE 7: Multi-Material Construction** \> A heavy steel front, light
carbon top, and light aluminum back push more mass toward the front of
the head. This front-biased build supports all other features that fight
face drift --- the materials are engineered to put weight where it
fights face drift most effectively.
