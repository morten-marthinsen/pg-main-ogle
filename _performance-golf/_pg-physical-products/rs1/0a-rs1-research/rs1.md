## **[Golfer Psychology (Putting)]{.underline}**

## **1. Beliefs About Self**

1.  **\"I\'m not a good putter\"** / **\"Putting is my weakness\"** -
    The most common self-limiting belief where golfers define themselves
    as inherently bad at putting, creating a negative identity

2.  **\"I can\'t make short putts\"** - Specific belief about inability
    to convert makeable putts inside 5-10 feet, often leading to anxiety
    on easier putts

3.  **\"I\'m great on the practice green but terrible on the
    course\"** - Belief that they lack the ability to transfer practice
    performance to real rounds

4.  **\"I don\'t have the mental game for putting\"** - Belief that they
    lack the psychological strength or focus required for good putting

5.  **\"I\'m just not naturally talented at putting\"** - Attribution of
    poor putting to lack of innate ability rather than skill development

## **2. Beliefs About Problems**

1.  **Poor distance control / Speed inconsistency** - Cannot judge the
    pace of putts, either leaving them drastically short or blowing them
    past the hole (most frequently mentioned problem)

2.  **Can\'t read greens properly** - Inability to accurately read
    break, slope, and green contours leading to wrong line selection

3.  **Lack of consistency** - Results vary wildly from round to round
    with no predictable performance pattern

4.  **Mental/psychological issues** - Anxiety, overthinking, fear of
    missing, and inability to commit to the line

5.  **Technical stroke flaws** - Issues with alignment, path, tempo, or
    making solid contact with the sweet spot

## **3. Beliefs About Solutions**

1.  **More practice is the answer** - Belief that simply spending more
    hours on the practice green will solve putting problems

2.  **Need better equipment** - Conviction that buying a new putter or
    training aid will fix their putting

3.  **Need a lesson from a professional** - Belief that expert
    instruction is required to improve

4.  **Mental game work / Sports psychology** - Belief that the solution
    lies in mindset, confidence building, and anxiety management

5.  **Developing a consistent routine** - Belief that having a
    repeatable pre-putt routine will bring consistency

## **4. Pain Points**

1.  **Three-putting** - The most frustrating and score-damaging outcome,
    especially on makeable distances

2.  **Inconsistent speed control on lag putts** - Cannot get long putts
    (20+ feet) close to the hole, leading to difficult second putts

3.  **Missing short \"gimme\" putts** - Embarrassing misses inside 3-5
    feet that should be automatic

4.  **Cannot transfer practice green performance to the course** -
    Putting well in practice but poorly under pressure

5.  **Inability to make critical putts under pressure** - Missing
    important putts for par, birdie, or in competitions when it matters
    most

## **5. Biggest Fears**

1.  **The Yips** - Involuntary muscle twitches and jerks during the
    stroke; the most terrifying condition for golfers

2.  **Embarrassment from missing short putts** - Fear of looking
    incompetent in front of playing partners

3.  **Losing tournaments/matches due to putting** - Fear that putting
    will cost them important competitions or good rounds

4.  **Developing anxiety that gets worse over time** - Fear that mental
    putting problems will spiral out of control

5.  **Never improving / Being stuck forever** - Fear that despite
    effort, they\'ll never become a competent putter

## **6. Desired Benefits**

1.  **Consistency and predictability** - Want repeatable performance
    they can count on round after round

2.  **Making more putts (especially 10-15 footers)** - Want to convert
    birdie opportunities and save par more often

3.  **Eliminating three-putts** - Want to avoid the score-killing
    three-putt and reliably two-putt from any distance

4.  **Confidence on the greens** - Want to feel assured and fearless
    when standing over any putt

5.  **Lower scores / Better handicap** - Want putting improvement to
    directly translate to shooting lower scores

## **7. Past Failures**

1.  **Countless training aids that didn\'t work** - Wasted money on
    gimmicky products that provided no lasting improvement

2.  **Practice that didn\'t translate to improvement** - Hours spent
    practicing with no results or inconsistent outcomes

3.  **Multiple putter purchases** - Bought numerous putters hoping
    equipment would solve the problem

4.  **Lessons that provided temporary fixes** - Instruction that helped
    briefly but improvements didn\'t last

5.  **Tips from friends/magazines that made things worse** -
    Well-intentioned advice that actually hurt their putting or created
    confusion

## **8. Consequences Of The Problem**

1.  **Significantly higher scores** - Poor putting directly adds 5-10+
    strokes per round, preventing handicap improvement

2.  **Lost confidence affecting entire game** - Putting struggles spread
    negativity to other parts of their game

3.  **Frustration and loss of enjoyment** - Bad putting ruins the fun of
    golf and creates anger/disappointment

4.  **Embarrassment in front of peers** - Social discomfort when putting
    poorly in front of friends or in competitions

5.  **Tournament losses and missed opportunities** - Inability to
    compete effectively or win due to putting failures

## **9. Objections To Other Solutions**

1.  **\"Training aids are gimmicks / waste of money\"** - Skepticism
    that putting aids actually work based on past disappointments

2.  **\"Lessons are too expensive\"** - Cost concerns about professional
    instruction, especially if past lessons didn\'t help

3.  **\"Don\'t have time to practice that much\"** - Belief that
    improvement requires more practice time than they can commit

4.  **\"My problem is mental, not physical\"** - Objection that
    technical solutions won\'t fix psychological issues

5.  **\"Nothing has worked before, why would this be different?\"** -
    Deep skepticism from repeated past failures

## **10. Trigger, Eureka, or Transformational Moments**

1.  **Simplifying the routine (e.g., no practice strokes)** -
    Discovering that eliminating practice strokes and trusting instinct
    dramatically improves putting

2.  **Finding the right grip style** - Switching to cross-hand, claw, or
    another grip that suddenly clicks

3.  **Reading greens with feet instead of eyes** - Learning to feel
    slope with feet provides breakthrough in green reading

4.  **Accepting that missing is okay** - Mental shift of detaching from
    outcome and focusing on process

5.  **Discovering proper putter fit** - Finding a putter (face-balanced
    vs. toe-hang) that matches their stroke type

## **11. What They Don\'t Want Or Refuse To Do**

1.  **Boring, repetitive putting practice** - Find traditional putting
    drills tedious and mind-numbing

2.  **Complex technical changes** - Don\'t want to overhaul their entire
    putting stroke with complicated mechanics

3.  **Slow play / Taking too long** - Refuse to slow down their group
    with elaborate green-reading routines

4.  **Looking foolish with unusual methods** - Won\'t use techniques
    that make them look strange (e.g., AimPoint, weird grips)

5.  **Admitting they need help** - Resist acknowledging the severity of
    their putting problems or seeking professional help

## **[Golfer Psychology (*Premium* Putters)]{.underline}**

## **TOP 5 THINGS PREMIUM PUTTER BUYERS CARE ABOUT FOR PUTTING PERFORMANCE:**

### **1. DISTANCE CONTROL & SPEED CONSISTENCY**

This is universally considered the **most important putting skill**.
Experienced golfers understand that controlling speed is more critical
than making long putts---it\'s about leaving shorter second putts and
eliminating three-putts. Speed control directly impacts proximity to the
hole and scoring. Expert fitters emphasize that improving speed
consistency can drop 2-3 strokes per round, which matters immensely to
skilled players tracking their handicaps.

### **2. FACE CONTACT CONSISTENCY & STRIKE QUALITY**

Premium golfers obsess over striking the ball on the same spot of the
face repeatedly. Inconsistent contact affects both direction *and*
distance. These players know that hitting the sweet spot produces
predictable roll, better feel feedback, and more accurate distance
control---all essential for lower scores.

### **3. LINE & ALIGNMENT ACCURACY**

The ability to start putts on the intended line combines green reading
skills with proper setup and alignment. Experienced golfers invest time
in reading greens correctly (slope, speed, grain) and want their setup
to support that read. They understand that even the best green read is
worthless if you can\'t align properly and execute the intended line.

### **4. ROLL QUALITY & BALL BEHAVIOR**

Better golfers care deeply about how the ball comes off the putter
face---they want immediate forward roll (not skid or hop), consistent
ball speed, and predictable behavior across different green speeds. They
notice when equipment produces a \"true roll\" versus when the ball
bounces or skids. This affects their ability to control outcomes,
especially on fast or undulating greens.

### **5. MENTAL CONFIDENCE & TRUST**

The psychological aspect is enormous for premium golfers. They need
complete confidence standing over a putt---trust in their read, their
stroke, and their equipment. This confidence comes from proven
performance and positive reinforcement. Low-handicap players understand
that golf is mostly mental, and any doubt on the greens destroys
performance under pressure. They want to feel like they\'re
\"automatic\" inside certain distances.

## **TOP 5 THINGS PREMIUM PUTTER BUYERS CARE ABOUT WHEN PURCHASING A PUTTER:**

### **1. FEEL & FEEDBACK**

This is likely the top priority for premium putter buyers. They want to
feel exactly what\'s happening at impact---whether they hit the sweet
spot, how solid the contact was, and the ball\'s response. Premium
players debate \"soft vs. firm\" endlessly. Most prefer putters that
provide clear tactile and auditory feedback so they can make stroke
adjustments. This is why milled putters (Scotty Cameron, Bettinardi)
command \$400-500+ prices---the precision milling and quality materials
(303 stainless steel, soft carbon steel) deliver a superior feel that
cheaper putters cannot match.

### **2. PROPER FITTING SPECIFICATIONS**

Experienced golfers increasingly understand that getting fitted is the
best equipment investment for lowering scores. They care about:

- **Weight & balance** (toe hang vs. face-balanced) matching their
  stroke type

- **Lie angle** ensuring the sole sits flat at address

- **Loft** for their stroke and green conditions

- **Length** matching their posture and setup

- **Grip size** for consistent hand position

Fitters report dramatic improvements when these specs are dialed
in---such as correcting a player who pulled every putt left by simply
adjusting weight distribution and lie angle.

### **3. HEAD DESIGN & ALIGNMENT AIDS**

Premium buyers are particular about head shape (blade vs. mallet vs.
\"spaceship\" designs) based on their stroke type and visual
preferences. They want:

- **MOI (stability)** appropriate for their consistency level

- **Alignment features** that inspire confidence without being
  distracting

- **Visual appeal** that looks \"right\" at address

- **Proven technology** (precision milling, insert materials, face
  technology)

Many stick with certain head shapes for years once they find what works,
while others experiment based on performance needs.

### **4. CRAFTSMANSHIP, QUALITY & BRAND HERITAGE**

These buyers appreciate and will pay for:

- **Precision milling** and manufacturing tolerances

- **Premium materials** (303 stainless steel, carbon steel,
  aerospace-grade aluminum)

- **Tour validation** (knowing pros use them)

- **Brand reputation** (Scotty Cameron, Bettinardi, L.A.B. Golf, etc.)

- **Customization options** (weights, finishes, stamping)

- **Durability** and resale value

They view premium putters as lifetime investments, not disposable
equipment. The fact that Bettinardi worked for Scotty Cameron before
starting his own company matters to these buyers---they care about the
craftsman\'s pedigree.

### **5. CONSISTENCY & REPEATABILITY**

More than any flashy feature, premium buyers want a putter that produces
**repeatable results**. This means:

- Consistent ball speed on similar-length strokes

- Predictable performance across the face (forgiveness on mishits)

- Reliable performance in pressure situations

- Elimination of variables so they can focus on execution

They\'re buying confidence through consistency. They know equipment
won\'t make putts for them, but the right putter eliminates excuses and

## ***[Pull vs. Push: Science]{.underline}***

## **Executive Summary**

Research evidence supports that **pulling motions are biomechanically
superior to pushing motions** in golf, including putting. When weight is
positioned **forward/on the face** of a putter (forward CG), it creates
conditions that favor a pulling motion and naturally improve stability
and consistency. Here\'s the comprehensive scientific evidence:

## **1. FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS: WHY PULLING IS SUPERIOR TO PUSHING**

### **The Truck and Trailer Principle (Core Physics Concept)**

From "Rotary Swing" Golf\'s research on golf physics:

**Pushing (Rear CG) Problems:**

- When you push an object, it tends to rotate around its own center of
  gravity

- The pushed object tries to move away from the line of force

- Example: Backing up a trailer - extremely difficult to control because
  pushing causes rotation

- In a putter: When the CG is behind the shaft axis, pushing forces
  cause the face to want to rotate open

**Pulling (Forward CG) Advantages:**

- When you pull an object, it aligns itself with the center of gravity
  of the pulling force

- Pulled objects naturally straighten and track straight

- Example: A truck pulling a trailer down the highway - the trailer
  stays perfectly straight with no effort

- In a putter: When the CG is forward/on the face, pulling forces keep
  the face stable and square

## **2. PUTTER-SPECIFIC PHYSICS: FORWARD CG vs REAR CG**

### **How Center of Gravity (CG) Position Affects Torque**

From *Dave Tutelman* on extensive putter design research:

**Forward CG Benefits:**

1.  **Reduces Torque**: When force is applied in front of the CG, it
    creates a stable clubface during the stroke

2.  **Face Stability**: Striking the ball directly in front of the CG
    prevents putter rotation

3.  **Self-Squaring**: Forward CG naturally squares the face at impact

**Rear CG Problems:**

1.  **Increases Torque**: When force is applied with CG behind the
    shaft, it creates rotational forces

2.  **Face Rotation**: The putter wants to open during the stroke

3.  **Requires Compensation**: Golfer must apply counter-torque with
    hands to keep face square

### **The Zero-Torque Revolution**

Modern putter companies (LAB Golf, Axis1) have revolutionized putting
with forward CG placement:

**LAB Golf DF3:**

- Places CG to eliminate torque completely

- \"Lie Angle Balance\" technology - shaft axis crosses directly through
  CG

- Result: Face stays square regardless of hand action

**Axis1 Putters:**

- Patents on placing CG on the center of the striking face and in-line
  with shaft axis

- Eliminates both static and dynamic torques

**TaylorMade Spider FCG (Forward CG):**

- Forward CG (closer to clubface) increases toe-hang, which is ideal for
  golfers that push putts

- Forward CG reduces face opening tendency

- Research shows: When it\'s a back CG with a moderate forward press
  putting style, the face tends to open to the right in reaction to the
  CG

## **3. BIOMECHANICS: BIG MUSCLES & PULLING MOTIONS**

### **Why \"Big Muscles\" Work Better with Pulling**

From biomechanics research and golf instruction:

**The Big Muscle Principle:**

- Modern putting instruction emphasizes using shoulders, back (lats),
  and core - not hands/wrists

- These large muscles work best with pulling motions, not pushing

- Large muscles produce smoother, more consistent motion with less
  twitchy movement

**The Pendulum Stroke & Pulling:**

- Ideal putting stroke is a \"rocking of the shoulders\" (pendulum
  motion)

- This creates a pulling action from the lead shoulder

- Large muscles = more stable, less affected by pressure/adrenaline

- Results in repeatable, consistent stroke

## **4. MUSCLE ACTIVATION STUDIES: PULLING VS PUSHING**

### **Scientific Evidence from Human Biomechanics Research**

From NIH/PMC study \"Human Body Mechanics of Pushing and Pulling\":

**Pulling Advantages (Task-Specific):**

1.  **Lower perceived exertion** (RPE) in some pulling tasks

2.  **Higher muscle activation** for pulling = more direct force
    application

3.  **Better control** when pulling toward center of mass

**Pushing Disadvantages:**

- Creates \"off-axis forces\" that must be stabilized

- Requires more complex muscle coordination

- Less natural alignment with body mechanics

**Golf-Specific Application:**

- The putting stroke is a subtle, precision movement

- Pulling engages larger, more stable muscle groups (shoulders, lats)

- Pushing engages smaller, twitchier muscles (wrists, forearms)

- Result: Pulling is more consistent and repeatable under pressure

## **5. FORWARD CG PUTTERS: PRACTICAL BENEFITS**

### **How Forward Weight Distribution Helps Golfers**

**1. Minimal Forward Shaft Lean Required:**

- Rear CG putters require forward press to prevent face opening

- Forward CG putters work with neutral or minimal forward press

**4. Better with \"Pulling\" Stroke:**

- **Benefit**: More consistent stroke, especially under pressure

**5. Forgiveness on Mis-Hits:**

- Forward CG reduces twisting even on off-center strikes

- **Benefit**: Fewer 3-putts from longer distances

- Consistent roll characteristics across various green speeds

- Less adjustment needed between practice green and course

### **1. Adjustment Period / Learning Curve**

- Feels significantly different from traditional putters

- Requires relearning distance control (especially on lag putts)

- Ball can roll out farther than expected on fast greens

- Requires recalibration of distance perception

- Downhill putts can be more challenging initially

### **4. Limited Availability & Higher Cost**

- Fewer models available compared to traditional putters

- Often premium pricing (\$300-\$500+)

- Limited customization options in some lines

### **5. Not for Everyone\'s Stroke Type**

- May not suit golfers with very wristy/hands-dominant strokes

- Some players with strong arc strokes prefer traditional heel-toe
  weighting

- Face-balanced forward CG putters don\'t suit all stroke paths

### **6. Aesthetic/Visual Concerns**

- Some designs look unconventional or \"ugly\" to traditionalists

- Offset/onset shaft positions can be visually distracting at address

- May not appeal to golfers who want classic blade appearance

### **7. Less Forgiveness on Toe/Heel Hits (Compared to Extreme MOI Mallets)**

- While forward CG reduces twisting, it doesn\'t maximize perimeter
  weighting

- High-MOI mallets with rear weighting may be more forgiving on severe
  mishits

- Trade-off: feel/roll quality vs. maximum forgiveness

## ***[MOI (moment of inertia)]{.underline}***

**Moment of Inertia = Resistance to Twisting (at impact)**

- **High MOI** = Putter head is **hard to twist** when you miss the
  sweet spot

- **Low MOI** = Putter head **twists easily** on off-center hits

There are **TWO main MOI measurements** for putters:

### **1. VERTICAL AXIS MOI (Most Common)**

- **What it measures:** Heel-to-toe stability (left-right twisting)

- **Why it matters:** Keeps face square on off-center hits

- **This is what manufacturers advertise**

### **2. HORIZONTAL AXIS MOI (Less Discussed) ←**Rare but has been marketed ([[https://argolf.com/blogs/tips-and-instructions/how-does-moi-affect-a-putter-s-forgiveness]{.underline}](https://argolf.com/blogs/tips-and-instructions/how-does-moi-affect-a-putter-s-forgiveness))

- **What it measures:** Front-to-back stability (high-low hits)

- **Why it matters:** Consistent ball speed on strikes above/below
  center

- **Rarely mentioned in marketing**

## ***[MOI (moment of inertia) vs. Forward CG]{.underline}***

***\* [MOI]{.underline} and [Forward CG]{.underline} in putters have an
inverse relationship: moving CG forward (toward the face) typically
reduces MOI, as mass concentrates closer to the rotation axis, lowering
resistance to twisting.***

**What It Is:**

**Characteristics:**

- Face stays square throughout the stroke

- Minimal face rotation during swing

- Works best with **straight-back, straight-through** putting strokes

- Center-shafted mallets

**What It Is:**

- When balanced on your finger, the toe hangs down toward the ground

**Characteristics:**

- Face wants to rotate open on backswing, closed on follow-through

- Natural arc to the putting stroke

- Requires more face rotation through impact

**Common Designs:**

- Traditional blade putters with heel-mounted shafts

- Anser-style putters

- Classic offset designs

**Popular Examples:**

- Scotty Cameron Newport

- Ping Anser

- TaylorMade TP Collection (blades)

- **Most traditional blade putters**

### **3. SLIGHT TOE-HANG (Moderate Balance)**

**What It Is:**

- When balanced, toe hangs down

- Middle ground between face-balanced and full toe-hang

**Characteristics:**

- Works with **slight arc strokes**

- Some natural face rotation but not extreme

- Versatile for different stroke types

**Common Designs:**

- Modified Anser designs

- Some mid-mallets

- Plumber\'s neck putters with moderate offset

**Popular Examples:**

- Odyssey Stroke Lab (some models)

- Cleveland Huntington Beach

- Ping Vault 2.0 (some models)

### **4. TOE-UP / FACE-DOWN ←**(RARE/SPECIALIZED)

**What It Is:**

- When balanced on your finger, the toe points UP (face points DOWN
  toward the ground)

- Opposite of traditional toe-hang

- CG is positioned significantly behind the shaft axis

**Why It Exists:**

- Designed for **extreme arc strokes**

- Players who have very strong hand/wrist rotation

- Theoretical benefit: Face naturally wants to close aggressively

stiffer shaft:

- Reduces face twisting on toe/heel hits

- Maintains ball speed better across the face

- Gives you more forgiveness

**In your game:** Mishits still go a reasonable distance and stay closer
to your line.

## **THE WEIGHT EFFICIENCY BONUS**

Stiffness increases faster than weight.

The engineering:

- Torsional stiffness increases with diameter⁴

- Weight only increases with diameter² (roughly)

**What this means:** You can get a MUCH stiffer shaft without adding
proportional weight. This is why carbon fiber putter shafts work so
well - larger diameter for massive stiffness gains, but lighter than
steel.

4.  **Supporting** results about **onset** (shaft behind like RS1)
    putters being preferable for alignment/vision, though downsides
    exist.

## **ONSET vs OFFSET Putter Shafts: Complete Comparison**

### **What Are They?**

**Offset Putters**: The shaft is positioned ahead of (in front of) the
putter face. The offset can be \"full shaft\" (equal to shaft width) or
\"half shaft\" (half the shaft width). This is the most common design in
putters.

**Onset Putters**: The shaft enters the head behind the putter face,
meaning the face is ahead of (closer to the target than) the leading
edge of the shaft. These are relatively rare and considered
unconventional.

## **PROS AND CONS**

### **OFFSET PUTTERS**

**Pros:**

- **Promotes forward hands at impact** - Encourages hands to stay
  slightly ahead of the ball through the stroke

- **Reduces wrist breakdown** - Helps maintain firmer wrists and
  prevents excessive hand action

- **More forgiving** - The design makes it easier to maintain
  consistency

- **Promotes upward strike** - Helps get the ball rolling smoothly with
  slight loft

- **Most common design** - Easy to find and widely available

**Cons:**

- **Affects aim to the left** - The more offset, the more it causes
  right-handed golfers to aim left.

- **Can obscure vision** - The shaft can block your view of the ball and
  alignment

- **Not ideal for straight-back-straight-through strokes** - Better for
  slight arc putters

- **May not suit dominant eye positioning** - Can interfere with eye
  dominance alignment

### **ONSET PUTTERS**

**Pros:**

- **Unobstructed vision** - You can see the entire top edge of the
  putter blade without the shaft blocking your view

- **Improved alignment for some golfers** - Most people aim further
  right with onset putters, which can correct leftward aiming tendencies

- **Better for dominant eye positioning** - Particularly good for
  right-handed golfers with a dominant left eye ←May only be 20%-30%
  generally

- **Cleaner visual appearance** - Simplifies the look at address

- **Prevents excessive forward shaft lean** - Good for golfers who
  don\'t want hands ahead

**Cons:**

- **Unconventional feel** - Takes adjustment if you\'re used to
  traditional designs

- **Less forgiving** - Doesn\'t promote the same hand-forward position
  that helps consistency

- **May encourage wrist breakdown** - Without the offset structure,
  easier to get \"handsy\"
