1. Product Overview
Product Name / Working Title: ONE.1
Category / Line: Wedges / Short Game Equipment
Launch Target: 2/16/2026
Product Type: Physical
Summary Statement:
ONE.1 is the first wedge engineered to solve short game struggles by letting the club do the hard work through the ground instead of requiring perfect technique from the golfer. Unlike traditional wedges that dig into turf and sand—causing chunks, blades, and fear around the green—ONE.1's patented ONE Wedge Technology™ features an Auto Glide Sole that moves smoothly through any lie (fairway, rough, sand, tight lies) with one simple swing. By managing ground contact automatically, ONE.1 makes short game shots easier, more predictable, and far less stressful, allowing golfers to get the ball on the green in one swing regardless of lie conditions.

2. Strategic Rationale
Business Objective:
[ASSUMPTION - Please confirm or revise:]

Consumer Insight:
Most golfers struggle around the green not because they lack skill or haven't had lessons, but because traditional wedges are fundamentally hard to use from the real-world lies they actually face. Tight lies punish slight mishits with thin shots. Thick rough grabs the hosel and causes chunks. Uneven turf creates unpredictable contact. Bunker sand requires precise speed and entry angle. Traditional wedges demand perfect technique and timing—qualities most recreational golfers don't possess consistently.
The result is a confidence crisis around the green. Golfers tense up on simple pitch shots, decelerate in fear of chunking, and develop anxiety about any lie that isn't perfect. They blame their swing when the real problem is equipment that doesn't forgive the small mistakes everyone makes. Research shows that short game inconsistency is the #1 frustration for mid-to-high handicap golfers, costing them 5-8 strokes per round. They want a solution that works WITH their current swing, not one that requires them to become more skilled.
Brand Fit:
ONE.1 aligns perfectly with Performance Golf's positioning as the brand that solves real golf problems with innovative technology rather than requiring golfers to change their swing or take more lessons. Just as 357 helped golfers without technical complexity, ONE.1 makes the short game accessible by engineering forgiveness into the club itself.
The "ONE" brand architecture creates opportunity for a family of products: ONE.1 (general short game), ONE.S (Super Sand for seniors), etc. This establishes a defensible category position around "equipment that does the work for you."
Market Opportunity:
The wedge market is mature but ripe for disruption. Traditional wedge design has focused on three areas: (1) spin technology for better players, (2) grind options for shot versatility, and (3) aesthetic preferences (blade vs. cavity). What's been ignored is the largest segment of golfers: those who struggle with basic contact from imperfect lies.
Current market dynamics favor ONE.1:
High sophistication + unmet need: Golfers have tried multiple wedges, lessons, and practice but still struggle around the green. They're ready for a different solution.
"Equipment blame" is justified here: Unlike full swing problems that are often technique-based, short game contact IS heavily influenced by sole design and club-ground interaction. This makes an equipment solution credible.
Premium pricing acceptance: Golfers who struggle around the green are desperate enough to invest in solutions. A wedge that actually delivers easier contact can command $150-250 pricing.
Competitive gap: Major OEMs (Titleist, Callaway, TaylorMade, Cleveland) focus on spin and grind versatility for better players. No one owns "forgiveness from bad lies" positioning.
The market is primed for a wedge that acknowledges most golfers don't play from perfect lies and need equipment that works with their limitations rather than exposing them.

3. Product Details
Product Description:
ONE.1 is a 50°, 56°, 60° wedge featuring ONE Wedge Technology™, a comprehensive ground-interaction system that ensures the club glides through turf and sand instead of digging. The heart of the technology is the Auto Glide Sole—a large, four-way cambered sole that increases ground contact surface area, resists downward force, and keeps the club moving forward through impact.
The system includes five integrated features:
Auto Glide Sole: Four-way cambered shape that increases surface area, reduces digging, and maintains club momentum through turf and sand
Pitch Control Weighting: Heavy sole wing + high toe weight placement that stabilizes off-center contact and preserves ball speed across the face
Square and Shoot Alignment: Straight leading edge + center-face alignment marks that make squaring the club simple at address
Spin-Friction Face: Traditional grooves for full shots combined with laser-etched surface texture for friction on partial swings
Motion Swing Weighted: Stepless steel shaft with 40g counterweight that improves balance and reduces flipping/deceleration
Key Benefits / Claims:
Functional Benefits:
Eliminate chunked shots from fairway, rough, and tight lies through automatic glide technology
Make clean contact from any lie with one simple swing (no manipulation required)
Get consistent distance control on off-center strikes through strategic weighting
Escape bunkers reliably without changing technique or opening the face
Generate predictable spin on both full wedge shots and short pitches
Reduce deceleration and flipping through improved club balance
Emotional Benefits:
Confidence to swing freely without fear of digging or chunking
Relief from short game anxiety and the tension that ruins simple shots
Pride in getting up and down consistently instead of making excuses
Freedom to play aggressively around the green without conservative bailouts
Enjoyment of short game shots instead of dreading them
Satisfaction from seeing immediate improvement without changing swing mechanics
Unique Selling Proposition (USP):
ONE.1 is the ONLY wedge with a four-way cambered Auto Glide Sole that manages ground contact automatically, making it the only wedge that lets you make the same simple swing from fairway, rough, sand, and tight lies with predictable results.
While traditional wedges require precise technique, perfect timing, and lie-specific adjustments—punishing small mistakes with chunks, blades, and inconsistent contact—ONE Wedge Technology™ does the hard work through the ground for you, so you can swing freely and trust the club to get the ball on the green regardless of the lie you face.

4. Target Consumer

Male and female golfers, ages 40-70, 12-28 handicap
Psychographic profile:
Frustrated by short game inconsistency despite practice and lessons
Blame themselves for chunking/blading but secretly suspect equipment doesn't help
Play 15-30 rounds per year, mostly on courses with challenging lies (rough, firm fairways, bunkers)
Willing to invest in equipment that delivers immediate, noticeable improvement
Skeptical of "magic club" claims but desperate enough to try if proof is credible
Want to enjoy golf more by eliminating the short game anxiety that ruins rounds

Internal dialogue: "I can't trust my wedge from anything but a perfect lie. If it's sitting down in the rough or on a tight fairway, I'm already thinking 'don't chunk it' before I even start my swing. And that tension makes me decelerate, which causes exactly what I'm afraid of. I've had lessons. I've practiced. But I still can't consistently get the ball on the green from 30-40 yards. It's embarrassing."
Motivations:
Want to stop losing 5-8 strokes per round around the green
Desire confidence to swing freely without fear of chunks/blades
Seeking equipment solution since swing changes haven't worked
Want to enjoy short game shots instead of dreading them
Need to eliminate the "one bad wedge shot" that ruins an otherwise good hole
Aspire to become the player in their foursome who "gets it up and down"
Barriers:

Confusion about whether this is "for beginners" vs. legitimate performance equipment
Fear that sole design changes will require learning new setup/swing adjustments
Uncertainty about whether it will work from ALL lies or just certain conditions
Usage Occasion:
Primary: All short game shots from 5-80 yards (full wedges to greenside pitches and chips)
Specific situations:
30-50 yard approach shots from fairway where tight lie creates chunk fear
Rough lies where traditional wedge would dig and grab
Greenside bunker shots requiring reliable escape
Tight lies around green where margin for error is minimal
Uneven lies (uphill/downhill/sidehill) where ground contact is unpredictable
Pre-round short game practice to build confidence
On-course use where forgiveness matters more than workability
Will likely stay in bag permanently once golfer experiences reliable contact improvement.

5. Competitive Landscape
Key Competitors:
Cleveland RTX ZipCore - $150-180, cavity back design, focus on spin + forgiveness, multiple grind options
Callaway Mack Daddy CB - $150, cavity back wedge, emphasis on forgiveness + spin, heel-toe weighting
Ping Glide 4.0 - $160-180, multiple sole grinds, focus on versatility, some forgiveness in cavity version
TaylorMade Hi-Toe 3 - $150, high MOI design, spin + forgiveness positioning
Cleveland CBX - $130, full cavity back, maximum forgiveness positioning, aimed at higher handicaps
Competitive Analysis:
ONE.1 Owns:
ONLY wedge positioning "ground interaction management" as the core benefit (competitors focus on spin, forgiveness, or versatility)
ONLY sole design specifically engineered to GLIDE vs. DIG (competitors have standard bounce/grind options but don't own the "anti-dig" positioning)
ONLY wedge addressing the "fear of chunking" problem directly (competitors assume technique is the issue)
ONLY product making "one swing from any lie" the central promise (competitors require lie-specific technique adjustments)
ONLY technology that removes the need for perfect technique (competitors still assume golfer skill as prerequisite)
Competitive Gaps/Opportunities:
All competitors focus features on SPIN TECHNOLOGY (grooves, face milling, materials) for better players
All competitors offer multiple GRIND OPTIONS assuming golfer knows which to use when
All competitors position forgiveness as "peripheral benefit" vs. core value proposition
No competitor acknowledges that most golfers CAN'T execute proper technique consistently
No competitor directly addresses the confidence/fear problem around digging
Category messaging assumes golfer wants shot-making versatility vs. reliable simplicity
Category Truth We Can Own:
"Traditional wedges are designed for perfect contact from perfect lies. ONE.1 is designed for real golfers hitting from real lies—and that changes everything."
Alternative: "Stop fighting the ground. Let the club glide."

8. Risks & Open Questions
Risks:
Sole Design Skepticism: Will golfers believe that sole design can actually solve contact problems, or will they assume it's still a technique issue requiring lessons?
"Beginner Club" Perception: Risk that "easy to use" and "forgiveness" positioning makes ONE.1 seem like a club for hackers rather than legitimate performance equipment, turning off better players who also struggle from bad lies.
Single-Wedge Limitation: Most golfers carry 2-3 wedges. If ONE.1 is positioned as "the wedge that does everything," does that limit expansion to multiple lofts/gapping options?
Proof Requirements: Short game improvement is highly visible and quantifiable. Will we have sufficient before/after data, testimonials, and instructor validation to overcome skepticism?
Spin Trade-off Concern: Will golfers fear that "easy contact" means sacrificing spin/control that good wedge players demand?
