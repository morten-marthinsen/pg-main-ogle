## Purpose

Market research interview with female golfer and long drive competitor Averee Dovsek to understand the female golf market and identify core messaging angles for the Women's Click Stick landing page.

---

## Key Insights

### Primary Messaging Angles for Women

1. **Power Transfer / Energy Transfer**
   - Women often feel powerless with a golf club despite being strong in other areas (gym, sports)
   - Key angle: "Do you feel strong in the gym but powerless with a golf club?"
   - The Click Stick helps women feel and understand how to transfer their power into the swing

2. **Feel-Based Learning (Critical for Women)**
   - Women are generally more feel-based than technical
   - Men tend to be more data/mechanics-driven
   - Messaging should emphasize FEEL over technical jargon
   - Potential hook: "What if all those swing tips could be condensed into one sound?"

3. **Distance / Keeping Up**
   - "Do you want to keep up with the guys off the tee?"
   - Distance matters to women who don't want to be far behind playing partners

4. **Simplicity Over Information Overload**
   - Women are often overloaded with swing tips and data they don't understand
   - Click Stick offers a simple solution: make it click = you're doing it right
   - "Are you tired of tons of swing tips and data being thrown at you?"

5. **The Release Point**
   - Beginner women especially struggle with the release and follow-through
   - Common issue: arm swing without proper weight transfer to lead side
   - Click Stick provides feedback on proper release timing

### Important Terminology Notes

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Weight transfer | Moving through your swing |
| Load, lag, release | Transferring power/energy |
| Technical mechanics | Feel-based descriptions |

**Reason:** Technical terms confuse beginners and even intermediate women. Terms like "weight transfer" cause swaying instead of rotation.

## Product Benefits to Highlight

### 1. Weighted Training Effect
- Click Stick is heavier than a standard club
- Builds strength with every swing
- When switching to regular club, it feels like a feather
- Results in increased swing speed through neural adaptation
- **Averee noted this was something she hadn't considered and needs to be explained in advertising**

### 2. Instant Feedback Through Sound
- One sound tells you if you're swinging correctly
- Eliminates need to understand complex mechanics
- Visual: slow-mo of magnet sliding down shaft and clicking at impact
- Represents the "visual representation of transferring power"

### 3. Confidence Building
- Helps women feel comfortable transferring power
- Builds muscle memory and strength simultaneously
- Simple success metric: if it clicks, you're doing it right

---

## Messaging Framework

### For Beginners
- Focus on simplicity and feel
- Avoid technical jargon entirely
- Emphasize the "one sound" feedback system
- Address the feeling of powerlessness with a golf club

### For Intermediate/Advanced
- Can use slightly more technical language
- Focus on refining the release point
- Swing speed and distance gains
- Strength building through weighted training
