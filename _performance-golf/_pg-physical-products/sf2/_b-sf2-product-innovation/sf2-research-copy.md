**NEW FEATURE: Speed Geometry Toe Slot**

*A brand-new, visually distinctive feature not found on SF1.*

***What It Is:***

An aggressive slot carved into the toe of the clubhead that removes material from the side wall.

***How It Works:***

The slot reduces aerodynamic drag on the toe, allowing it to "speed up" and catch the heel through impact. This directly counteracts the natural tendency for the toe to lag behind—ensuring a square face at the moment of truth.

***Why It's Different:***

Most drivers have big, blocky walls that create drag and make it harder to square the face. The Speed Geometry Toe Slot is a visible, functional feature that actively helps close the face while looking aggressive and tour-level.

**IMPROVED: Aero Square Crown Channels**

*Carried over from SF1 but significantly enhanced.*

***What It Is:***

Angled channels on the crown that guide airflow around the heel to promote face closure.

***What's Improved:***

• More aggressive channel angles for enhanced face closure

• Better visual alignment for inside-out path

• Improved topline-crown channel interaction for better setup appearance

***How It Works:***

As you swing, air flows over the head and through these channels, helping the heel rotate around the shaft axis to square the face. The channels also provide a visual guide for taking the club back on the right path.

**IMPROVED: Counter-Slice Keel**

*The primary weighting structure that powers the Slice-Fix Effect.*

***What It Is:***

A raised, angled structural element positioned low in the heel of the clubhead that creates deep, heel-biased weight placement.

***What's Improved:***

Thanks to titanium's weight savings, the SF2 keel is thicker and heavier than SF1's Fixed Counter-Slice Weights—delivering more aggressive dynamic face closure.

***How It Works:***

When the weight is positioned low and back in the heel, it wants to rotate around the shaft axis during your swing. This creates "dynamic face closure"—the clubhead naturally rotates to square the face at impact without you having to time it manually.

**IMPROVED: Draw Spin Face Bulge**

*Refined face curvature for better spin management across the face.*

***What It Is:***

Asymmetric bulge (curvature across the face) with different shapes on the heel vs. toe sides.

***How It Works:***

• Toe side: More rounded curvature \= increased gear effect \= MORE draw spin on toe hits

• Heel side: Flatter curvature \= reduced gear effect \= LESS cut spin on heel hits

***Why It Matters:***

Traditional drivers have symmetrical bulge, which means heel hits produce cut spin (making slices worse). SF2's asymmetric design reduces the slice penalty on heel hits while enhancing draw spin on toe hits.

**RETAINED: Draw-Calibrated Face Angle (3° Closed)**

***What It Is:***

The clubface is pre-set 3° closed at address, giving you built-in "face-closing insurance."

***How It Works:***

By starting the face slightly closed, you're already ahead in the fight to square it by impact. Even if your natural swing leaves the face slightly open, the pre-closed position gives you a buffer.

***What's Different in SF2:***

The topline-crown channel interaction has been engineered so that despite the 3° closed face angle, the driver looks SQUARE at address—not "hooked" like many draw-bias drivers.

**RETAINED: Takeaway Tracks**

***What It Is:***

A finely etched pattern on the crown that provides visual guidance for your takeaway path.

***How It Works:***

The tracks help you take the club back square, then bring it back on an inside-out path—the path that produces draws instead of slices.

**RETAINED: Counterbalanced Shaft**

***What It Is:***

A shaft with weight shifted toward the grip end, creating balance points near your hands.

***How It Works:***

With more weight in your hands (relatively less in the head), you gain better control and can more easily close the clubface through impact. The counterbalance also helps prevent "steering" and promotes natural lag.

***What's New in SF2:***

Added X-Stiff flex option for stronger players who want to take advantage of the titanium head's speed capabilities.

**DESIGN REFINEMENT: 445cc Anti-Slice Speed Geometry Head**

***What It Is:***

A purposefully compact 445cc head (vs. the 460cc maximum allowed by USGA) with sleek, aerodynamic shaping.

***Why Smaller Is Better for Slice-Fixing:***

Bigger, blockier heads are harder to square through impact. The 445cc design creates a sleeker profile that's easier to close while still providing plenty of forgiveness.

***The "Clamshell" Construction:***

Instead of big walls around the skirt, the SF2 features a clamshell design where the crown and sole come together—creating reduced drag and a faster-closing head.
