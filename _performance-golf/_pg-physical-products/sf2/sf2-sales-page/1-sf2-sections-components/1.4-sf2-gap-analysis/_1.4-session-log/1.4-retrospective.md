### 1.2 GAP Identification Method Was Rigorous

**What Happened:**
Applied systematic 3-step method to identify GAP components:

**Step 1:** Listed ALL 26 OEM baseline components (those with ≥50% usage across 12 OEMs):

| Category | Components Listed |
|----------|-------------------|
| Hero/ATF | Product Image Gallery, Product Title, Product Description, Navigation Bar, Configuration Selectors, Price Display, Add to Cart CTA, Breadcrumb Navigation, Promotional Banner |
| Technology | Technology Feature Blocks, Named Proprietary Tech, Face Technology, Weight/CG Technology, Materials Callouts, Technical Diagrams |
| Specifications | Specifications Table, Loft Options Listed, Shaft Options Table, Adjustability Info, Grip Specifications |
| Model Comparison | Model Comparison Chart, Launch/Spin Indicators, Target Player Description |
| Media | Multiple Product Views, Video Content |
| Purchase Enablers | Fitting CTA, Cross-sell/Related, Free Shipping Mention |

**Step 2:** Checked each of 26 components against PG pages (357 and SF1).

For each component, I asked:
- Does 357 have this component? (Y/N/Partial)
- Does SF1 have this component? (Y/N/Partial)
- If present, is it PROMINENT or MINIMAL?

**Step 3:** Marked as GAP only if BOTH conditions were true:
1. OEM baseline status (≥50% of 12 OEMs)
2. PG status = MISSING or MINIMAL

**The 8 GAPs Identified:**

| # | Component | OEM % | PG Status | Evidence |
|---|-----------|-------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Product Image Gallery | 100% | MINIMAL | Both 357 and SF1 show single product image, no multi-angle gallery, no zoom |
| 2 | Specifications Table | 100% | MINIMAL | SF1 has tab but buried; 357 lacks visible specs section entirely |
| 3 | Technical Diagrams/Cutaways | 92% | MINIMAL | Tech sections exist but lack OEM-level cross-section cutaway diagrams |
| 4 | Model Comparison Chart | 67% | MISSING | Neither 357 nor SF1 shows comparison to other models in lineup |
| 5 | Video Content (product demo) | 58% | PARTIAL | Both have testimonial videos but no product/technology demonstration video |
| 6 | Fitting CTA | 75% | MISSING | No "find your fit" tool; SF1 has flex guide but not interactive fitting |
| 7 | Cross-sell/Related | 67% | MISSING | No related products section on either page |
| 8 | Breadcrumb Navigation | 58% | MISSING | Neither page shows breadcrumb navigation |

**Why This Matters:**
A casual observer might say "PG has specs somewhere" or "PG shows the product." But the method required checking whether PG's implementation matched OEM PROMINENCE standards, not just existence. This revealed that PG's specs are buried, PG's product images are singular, and PG's tech visuals lack cutaways — all credibility gaps.

## PART 3: WHAT WAS LEARNED (DETAILED INSIGHTS)

### Insight 1: OEM "Table Stakes" Are CREDIBILITY Signals, Not Conversion Drivers

**The Observation:**
All 8 GAP components I identified share a common function — they answer visitor questions about LEGITIMACY, not DESIRE:

| GAP Component | Visitor Question Answered |
|---------------|---------------------------|
| Product Image Gallery | "Can I see this product properly?" |
| Specifications Table | "Is this a real, engineered product?" |
| Technical Diagrams | "How does the technology actually work?" |
| Model Comparison | "Which model is right for me?" |
| Video Content (demo) | "What does this look like in action?" |
| Fitting CTA | "Will this fit my swing?" |
| Cross-sell | "What else do they make?" (ecosystem) |
| Breadcrumbs | "Where am I on this site?" (navigation trust) |

**The Pattern:**
These are all CREDIBILITY signals. A visitor who doesn't see these components thinks "this feels cheap" or "this feels unprofessional" — NOT "I don't want this."

**The Implication for SF2:**
PG's conversion elements (🔶 PG-UNIQUE) are excellent. The problem is visitors who WANT to buy might hesitate because the page doesn't feel "legitimate" enough. Adding OEM table stakes isn't about selling harder — it's about removing credibility barriers.

**The Framework:**
- 🔶 PG-UNIQUE = DESIRE drivers (make them want it)
- OEM Table Stakes = CREDIBILITY signals (make them trust it)
- SF2 needs BOTH

### Insight 4: Reviews Are SF2's Biggest Competitive Wedge

**The Observation:**
OEMs dramatically under-index on customer reviews:

| Category | Customer Reviews % | Star Ratings % |
|----------|-------------------|----------------|
| OEM (12) | 42% (5/12) | 42% (5/12) |
| DTC (5) | 80% (4/5) | 80% (4/5) |
| DR (4) | ~50% (2/4) | ~50% (2/4) |

**Why OEMs Under-Index:**
1. OEMs rely on brand authority — they don't "need" reviews
2. OEM sites are more "catalog" than "sales page"
3. OEM reviews are often on retailer sites (Golf Galaxy, etc.), not OEM direct

**Why This Is SF2's Opportunity:**
Most golfers shopping for drivers visit OEM sites first (TaylorMade, Callaway). They see:
- Beautiful product imagery ✅
- Detailed tech specs ✅
- Professional presentation ✅
- Customer reviews ❌
- Social proof ❌

Then they visit PG/SF2 and see:
- Less polished imagery (CURRENT GAP)
- Less visible specs (CURRENT GAP)
- Customer reviews EVERYWHERE ✅
- Testimonials with names ✅
- "5,000+ Satisfied Players" ✅

**The Strategy:**
SF2 should close the credibility gaps (imagery, specs, diagrams) while MAINTAINING review prominence advantage. The result is a page that feels as "legitimate" as TaylorMade but has 10x the social proof.
