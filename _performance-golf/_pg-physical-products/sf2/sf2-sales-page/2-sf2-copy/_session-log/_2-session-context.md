## ENTRY 1: METHODOLOGY — BILL SCHLEY'S MICRO-SCRIPT RULES

### 1.1 Core Process Order (MUST FOLLOW PRECISELY)

Per Bill Schley (The Micro-Script Rules):

1. **DSI FIRST** — Know your Dominant Selling Idea before creating any micro-script
2. **BRAND STORY** — Write one-page problem/solution story only you can tell
3. **SYNTHESIZE** — Mine the brand story for micro-scripts
4. **COLLECT** — Gather 30+ micro-scripts (one becomes tagline, others used elsewhere)
5. **REFINE** — Test which ones get repeated back

### 1.2 DSI Criteria — ALL 5 MUST BE MET

| Criteria | Definition | Validation Question |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| **Superlative** | #1 at something | "What is SF2 the BEST/ONLY/MOST at?" |
| **Important** | Must-have, not nice-to-have | "Does this matter enough for customers to act?" |
| **Believable** | Credible, plausible reason | "Is there a logical reason WHY SF2 can claim this?" |
| **Measurable** | Specific, observable, provable | "Can customers SEE proof of this difference?" |
| **Ownable** | Not already claimed by competitor | "Does any competitor already own this position?" |

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 1: SOUND IS KING — Micro-Scripts Are for the EAR, Not the Eye

**The Error:** First batch was logical, clear, marketing-speak. Read okay, but didn't SOUND good when spoken aloud.

**The Fix:** Prioritize how it SOUNDS when spoken. Every candidate must pass the "say it out loud" test.

**What Worked (User-Kept):**

|--------------|--------------|
| "Swing Free. Less Trees." | Rhyme (Free/Trees) |

| "Stop Steering. Start Striping." | Alliteration (St sounds) + parallel |
| "Straighter, Farther, Faster." | Triple -er ending + rhythm |

**Rule:** If it doesn't have a sound device (rhyme, alliteration, rhythm, repetition), it probably won't stick.

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 2: CONTRAST CREATES CLARITY

**The Error:** First batch stated benefits directly without tension. "The face that fixes itself" is a statement. "Kill the slice. Keep the speed." is a contrast.

**The Fix:** Use A vs B structures, paradoxes, or parallel contrasts.

**What Worked:**
- "Kill The Slice. Keep The Speed." — Verb+Noun parallel with opposite outcomes
- "Right is wrong. Straight is long." — Paradox (right IS wrong) + contrast
- "Same Swing. No Slice." — Same A. Different B.

**Famous Examples (Training Doc):**
- "Tastes great. Less filling." (Miller Lite)
- "Melts in your mouth, not in your hand" (M&Ms)
- "Kills the weeds, not the grass" (Ortho)

**Rule:** Great micro-scripts often use "A, not B" or "A vs B" structures.

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 3: STUDY FAMOUS EXAMPLES, NOT JUST TEMPLATES

**The Error:** First batch applied templates mechanically without internalizing why famous examples work.

**The Fix:** Deep study of cliches/proverbs/taglines to understand the PATTERNS, not just the RULES.

**Patterns from Famous Examples (Training Doc Lines 12-106):**

| Pattern | Examples | Why It Works |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **If/Then Rhyme** | "If the glove doesn't fit, you acquit" | Conditional + rhyme = memorable logic |
| **Repetition + Rhyme** | "No pain, no gain" / "You snooze, you lose" | Parallel structure reinforces the rule |
| **Alliteration Chain** | "Loose lips sink ships" | Consonant sounds link ideas |
| **Triple Beat** | "Stop, drop, and roll" / "I came, I saw, I conquered" | Three-beat rhythm is deeply satisfying |
| **Paradox** | "Slow is smooth and smooth is fast" | Unexpected reversal creates intrigue |

**Rule:** Before writing micro-scripts, spend 30 minutes reading and SAYING famous examples aloud to internalize the patterns.

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 4: COMBINE TEMPLATES + INGREDIENTS

**The Error:** First batch used templates in isolation. "The club squares itself" uses A/B Equation template but lacks any sound ingredient.

**The Fix:** Layer templates WITH ingredients (rhyme, alliteration, rhythm, repetition).

**Example from Training Doc (Page 96):**
> "You snooze, you lose" uses: A/B Equation + Stark Reminder + rhyme + alliteration + repetitive sequence

**What Worked (Batch 2):**

| "Swing Free. Less Trees." | Stark Reminder (command) | + Rhyme |
| "Kill The Slice. Keep The Speed." | Stark Reminder (command) | + Alliteration + parallel |

**Rule:** Template alone = adequate. Template + 2 ingredients = memorable.

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 5: SHORTER FORCES BETTER

**The Error:** First batch had too many 7-8 word micro-scripts. Length = room for filler.

**The Fix:** Force brevity. The best ones are 3-5 words.

**User-Kept by Word Count:**

---

#### BREAKTHROUGH 6: MICRO-SCRIPTS MUST BE REPEATABLE (The "Bar Test")

**The Error:** First batch sounded like ad copy. "The only driver that gives you both" — no one says that to a friend.

**The Fix:** Ask: "Would someone repeat this at a bar or on the first tee?"
