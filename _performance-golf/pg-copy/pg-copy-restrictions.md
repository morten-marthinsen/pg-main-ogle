## Do NOT's In Copy

WATCH OUT for these in all copy, videos, and pages.

### 1. Hank Haney & Tiger Woods

- **No pictures** of Hank and Tiger together
- If Hank is mentioned, ALWAYS call him **Tiger's "former" coach**
- Alternative phrasings:
  - "the man who coached Tiger for 6 years"
  - "the man who coached Tiger during one of the most dominant runs in PGA history"
- **NEVER** say he is "Tiger's coach" (present tense)
- **AVOID** the term "ex-coach" due to their rocky split

## USGA Guidelines for Product Conformity

**After receiving official USGA approval** that product conforms to the rules of golf, we can state:
- **USGA Conforming**
- **Conforms to the Rules of Golf**

**NEVER use:**
- "USGA Approved"
- "Approved by the USGA"
- "authorized," "endorsed," or "sanctioned"

The USGA does not "approve" products—this would imply endorsement. They are a neutral ruling body.

**R&A Approval:**
Once the USGA approves our product as conforming, it is automatically approved by the R&A for all markets outside of North America.

**Pre-Approval Communication:**
If we need to send communications before receiving approval:
- "All Performance Golf clubs are designed to conform to the Rules of Golf."
- You may mention we are going through the approval process, but check with Donnie on exact language and timing.

**General Best Practice:**
It's generally better to refrain from making specific statements before receiving approval. However, to reassure customers, it's fine to express our intention of always offering conforming products.
